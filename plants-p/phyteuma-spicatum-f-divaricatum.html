Phyteuma spicatum f. divaricatum

Phyteuma spicatum f. divaricatum, commonly known as the branched phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that produces showy, spires of pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its branched inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "divaricatum.". It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant blooms.
