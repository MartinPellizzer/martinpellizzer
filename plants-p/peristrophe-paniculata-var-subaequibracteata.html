Peristrophe paniculata var. subaequibracteata

Peristrophe paniculata var. subaequibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-bract grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with bracts that are nearly equal in length to the flowers. Its botanical structure is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
