Potentilla pensylvanica f. sanguisorbifolia

Potentilla pensylvanica f. sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the pink potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
