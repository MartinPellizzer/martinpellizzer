Potentilla lipskyana

Potentilla lipskyana, commonly known as the Lipsky's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Potentilla lipskyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
