Psidium acunae

Psidium acunae, commonly known as the acuna guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
