Pachyrhizus angulatus var. integrifolius

Pachyrhizus angulatus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, entire leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to thrive in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
