Pleurothallis fernandiana

Pleurothallis fernandiana, commonly known as the Fernandian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its leaves are usually narrow and strap-like, adapted for efficient water absorption in its natural habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized root system for nutrient uptake.
