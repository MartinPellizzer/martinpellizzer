Pityrogramma dealbata

Pityrogramma dealbata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pityrogrammaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

The silver fern is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
