Prunus botan

Prunus botan, commonly known as the botanical cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by small, round fruits known as cherries. Prunus botan is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
