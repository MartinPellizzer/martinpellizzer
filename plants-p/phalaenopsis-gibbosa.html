Phalaenopsis gibbosa

Phalaenopsis gibbosa, commonly known as the wrinkled moth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping flower spikes that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and typically arranged in two rows along the stem.

Phalaenopsis gibbosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
