Pongamia caerulea

Pongamia caerulea, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive flat, pod-like fruits containing oily seeds.

Pongamia caerulea is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
