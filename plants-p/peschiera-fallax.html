Peschiera fallax

Peschiera fallax, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect, branching stems that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a tendency to form a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
