Piriqueta duarteana var. chrysotricha

Piriqueta duarteana var. chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
