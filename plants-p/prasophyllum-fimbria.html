Prasophyllum fimbria

Prasophyllum fimbria, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, upright stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a spike of white to pale pink flowers, which are often tinged with green at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in heathlands or open forests.

Prasophyllum fimbria is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and is often found in specific regions of New South Wales and Victoria.
