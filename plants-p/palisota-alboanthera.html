Palisota alboanthera

Palisota alboanthera, commonly known as the white anther fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, white spore cases called anthers, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Palisota alboanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
