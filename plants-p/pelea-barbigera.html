Pelea barbigera

Pelea barbigera, commonly known as the bearded pelea, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Pelea barbigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
