Pyrus buxifolia

Pyrus buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact foliage, with leaves that are small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
