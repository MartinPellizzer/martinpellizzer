Potentilla hookeriana subsp. chamissonis

Potentilla hookeriana subsp.

chamissonis, commonly known as Hooker's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically features wiry, many-branched stems covered with fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
