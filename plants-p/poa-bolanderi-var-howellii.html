Poa bolanderi var. howellii

Poa bolanderi var. howellii, commonly known as Howell's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, soft foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-rowed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
