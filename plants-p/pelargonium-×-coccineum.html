Pelargonium × coccineum

Pelargonium × coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of stems. The leaves are palmately lobed, often with a slightly hairy surface, and exhibit a range of green to reddish hues. Native to South Africa, this species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
