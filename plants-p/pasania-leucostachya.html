Pasania leucostachya

Pasania leucostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed pasania, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
