Prosopis denudans var. patagonica

Prosopis denudans var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically grayish-green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by long, thin pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in dry grasslands and scrublands.

The species is valued for its hardy nature and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
