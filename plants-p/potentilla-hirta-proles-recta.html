Potentilla hirta proles recta

Potentilla hirta proles recta, commonly known as the upright potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticulture.
