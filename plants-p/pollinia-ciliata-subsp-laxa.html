Pollinia ciliata subsp. laxa

Pollinia ciliata subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the soft-hairy orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America. It is characterized by its delicate, hairy sepals and petals, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories.

The pollinia are clustered in a unique structure, facilitating specific pollination mechanisms.
