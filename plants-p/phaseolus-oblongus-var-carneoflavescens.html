Phaseolus oblongus var. carneoflavescens

Phaseolus oblongus var. carneoflavescens, commonly known as the yellow wax bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong pods that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a waxy coating that gives it its common name. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces a moderate yield of pods that can be harvested for culinary use.

This variety is valued for its flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in home gardens and small-scale farming.
