Piper granatense

Piper granatense, commonly known as the granate pepper or red pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bright red, peppercorn-like fruits. These fruits are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.
