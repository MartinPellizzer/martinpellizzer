Pachyanthus oleifolius

Pachyanthus oleifolius, commonly known as the African olive, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, black fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
