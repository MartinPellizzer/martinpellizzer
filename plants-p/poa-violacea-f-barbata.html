Poa violacea f. barbata

Poa violacea f. barbata, commonly known as the barbed bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically pink to purplish in color. Its leaves are often covered with short, barbed hairs, giving it its distinguishing name.

This grass is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
