Poa annua var. elatior

Poa annua var. elatior, commonly known as the tall annual bluegrass, is a variant of the annual bluegrass species.

It is a low-growing grass with slender, dark green blades and is typically found in open, disturbed areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often considered a weed in turfgrass settings due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a wide range of environments, contributing to its widespread distribution.
