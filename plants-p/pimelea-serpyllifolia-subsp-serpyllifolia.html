Pimelea serpyllifolia subsp. serpyllifolia

Pimelea serpyllifolia subsp.

serpyllifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is notable for its longevity, with some individuals living for several decades.
