Pandanus spissus

Pandanus spissus, commonly known as the stiff pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are densely packed and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spiky inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and the leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

Pandanus spissus thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
