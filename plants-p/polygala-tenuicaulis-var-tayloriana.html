Polygala tenuicaulis var. tayloriana

Polygala tenuicaulis var. tayloriana, commonly known as Taylor's narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as sagebrush steppe and woodland edges. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
