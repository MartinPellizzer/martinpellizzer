Porana mairei var. holosericea

Porana mairei var. holosericea, commonly known as the Maire's porana, is a climbing shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
