Paphiopedilum viniferum

Paphiopedilum viniferum, commonly known as the grapevine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and long, pendulous inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that resembles a lip-shaped structure. It thrives in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Paphiopedilum viniferum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
