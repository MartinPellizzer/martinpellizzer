Potentilla speciosa var. rubriflora

Potentilla speciosa var. rubriflora, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which bloom in early summer and are borne on long, slender stalks. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
