Petrocoptis montserratii

Petrocoptis montserratii, commonly known as the Montserrat rockcress, is a small flowering plant native to the volcanic slopes of Montserrat in the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain the seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
