Parthenium rollinsianum

Parthenium rollinsianum, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, yellow to brownish flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Parthenium rollinsianum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
