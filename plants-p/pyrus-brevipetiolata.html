Pyrus brevipetiolata

Pyrus brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-stalked pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, deciduous leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round pomes that are typically edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blossoms.

Pyrus brevipetiolata is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
