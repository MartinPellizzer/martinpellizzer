Potentilla × malyschevii

Potentilla × malyschevii, commonly known as the Malyschevii cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a soft, white down. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating drought-tolerant plants.
