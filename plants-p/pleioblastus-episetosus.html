Pleioblastus episetosus

Pleioblastus episetosus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and dense, feathery foliage that gives it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ability to form clumps and its slow growth rate make it a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
