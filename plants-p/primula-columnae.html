Primula columnae

Primula columnae, commonly known as the columnar primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright, columnar growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically oval and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Primula columnae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
