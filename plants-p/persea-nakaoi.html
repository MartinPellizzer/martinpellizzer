Persea nakaoi

Persea nakaoi, commonly known as the Nakao's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries that are similar in appearance to persimmons. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
