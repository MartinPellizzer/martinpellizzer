Parodia microsperma var. opulenta

Parodia microsperma var. opulenta, commonly known as the opulent cactus or the yellow moon cactus, is a succulent species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or yellowish in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
