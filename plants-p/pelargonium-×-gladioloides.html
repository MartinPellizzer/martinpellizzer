Pelargonium × gladioloides

Pelargonium × gladioloides, commonly known as the angel wing begonia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are often heart-shaped or wing-like, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-draining soil.

Pelargonium × gladioloides is popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
