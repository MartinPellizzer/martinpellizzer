Physocarpus alternans subsp. panamintensis

Physocarpus alternans subsp.

panamintensis, commonly known as the Panamint serviceberry, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces edible berries that are dark blue to black in color, typically ripening in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh climates.
