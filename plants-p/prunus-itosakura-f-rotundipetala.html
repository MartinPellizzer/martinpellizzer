Prunus itosakura f. rotundipetala

Prunus itosakura f. rotundipetala, commonly known as the round-petaled Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus itosakura species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded petals that give it a unique appearance compared to other cherry blossoms. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It typically blooms in early spring with clusters of fragrant, pale pink flowers.

The leaves are oval-shaped and provide a lush green backdrop to the delicate blossoms.
