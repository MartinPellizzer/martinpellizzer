Potamogeton crispus f. longifolius

Potamogeton crispus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in shallow waters, such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
