Pyrus babauttiagi

Pyrus babauttiagi, commonly known as the Babautti's pear, is a rare and lesser-known species of the Pyrus genus.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears but have a distinct flavor profile. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a glossy green surface that provides visual interest in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
