Piper cascajalanum

Piper cascajalanum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
