Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. longipedicellatus

Phyllanthus chamaecerasus var. longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-peduncled phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, clustered leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Phyllanthaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
