Prunus maritima var. purpurea-parva

Prunus maritima var. purpurea-parva, commonly known as the dwarf purple sandcherry, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant produces small, showy flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for birds and mammals. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn reddish in the fall.

This variety is well adapted to sandy soils and salt spray, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments.
