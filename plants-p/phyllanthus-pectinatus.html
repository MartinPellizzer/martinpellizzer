Phyllanthus pectinatus

Phyllanthus pectinatus, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leafy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its fruit, which resembles a small, fleshy berry, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, including treating ailments related to the liver and urinary system.
