Pseudanamomis gibberosa

Pseudanamomis gibberosa, commonly known as the gibberous moss, is a rare and specialized moss species found in specific microhabitats.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often on rock surfaces or in crevices where humidity remains consistently high. The plant exhibits a dense, cushion-like growth form, with tightly packed, short, greenish-gray shoots. Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, are borne on short stalks and release spores when mature.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of environmental stability.
