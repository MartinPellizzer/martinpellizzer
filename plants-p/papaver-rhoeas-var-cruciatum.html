Papaver rhoeas var. cruciatum

Papaver rhoeas var. cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-shaped poppy, is a variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed, cross-shaped petals that give it its unique appearance. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It produces bright red or orange flowers that bloom in early summer.

The seeds of this variety are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread in various environments.
