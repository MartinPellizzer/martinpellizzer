Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. afrorientale

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

afrorientale, commonly known as the African green-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating.

The plant produces small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters, typically in spring. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that are yellow when ripe.

It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.
