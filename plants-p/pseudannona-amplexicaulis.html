Pseudannona amplexicaulis

Pseudannona amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping pseudannona, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features showy, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a spherical, fleshy berry that contains multiple seeds.

Pseudannona amplexicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
