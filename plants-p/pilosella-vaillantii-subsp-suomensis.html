Pilosella vaillantii subsp. suomensis

Pilosella vaillantii subsp.

suomensis, commonly known as the Finnish hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small yellow flowers.

The subspecies suomensis is distinguished by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to other subspecies. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
