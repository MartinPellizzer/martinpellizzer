Papaver nudicaule var. pilosum

Papaver nudicaule var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features erect, slender stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it its varietal name "pilosum.". The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often covered in a similar hairy coating.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
