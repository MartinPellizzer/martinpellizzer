Peperomia subcalvescens

Peperomia subcalvescens, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden beneath its foliage. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and ability to thrive in indirect light.
