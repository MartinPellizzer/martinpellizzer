Pinus pseudotaeda

Pinus pseudotaeda, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces cones that are typically small and cylindrical, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and cold, dry climates.

This species is notable for its longevity and resilience, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.
