Pilosella lactucella subsp. melanocalathia

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

melanocalathia, commonly known as the dark-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.
