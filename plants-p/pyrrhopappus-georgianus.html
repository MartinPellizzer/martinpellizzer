Pyrrhopappus georgianus

Pyrrhopappus georgianus, commonly known as the Georgian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pinkish in color.

Pyrrhopappus georgianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
