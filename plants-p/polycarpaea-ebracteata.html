Polycarpaea ebracteata

Polycarpaea ebracteata, commonly known as the leafy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters and are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
