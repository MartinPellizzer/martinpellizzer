Persea boldufolia

Persea boldufolia, commonly known as the Boldufolia avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its fruit is edible, with a creamy texture and a mild, nutty flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural use in tropical regions.
