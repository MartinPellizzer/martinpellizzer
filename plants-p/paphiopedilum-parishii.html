Paphiopedilum parishii

Paphiopedilum parishii, commonly known as the Parry's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or green with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Paphiopedilum parishii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
