Phyllanthus subcrenulatus

Phyllanthus subcrenulatus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
