Paspalum schesslii

Paspalum schesslii, commonly known as Schessler's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum schesslii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
