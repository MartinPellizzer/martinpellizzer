Phyteuma spicatum f. crenatoserratum

Phyteuma spicatum f. crenatoserratum, commonly known as the crenate-serrated phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive crenate-serrated margin, giving the plant its specific epithet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant summer blooms.
