Polygonum hickmanii

Polygonum hickmanii, commonly known as Hickman's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Hickman's knotweed is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
