Potamogeton lacunatus f. novae-angliae

Potamogeton lacunatus f. novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the Potamogeton genus. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a habitat and food source for various aquatic organisms.
