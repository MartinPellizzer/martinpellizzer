Peganum harmala

Peganum harmala, commonly known as Syrian rue, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and distinctive, branched seed pods. The plant contains various alkaloids, including harmine and harmaline, which have been studied for their potential medicinal and psychoactive properties. Its leaves and seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Peganum harmala thrives in poor soil conditions and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
