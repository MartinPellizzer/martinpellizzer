Phyllanthus simplex var. pratensis

Phyllanthus simplex var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in moist grasslands and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
