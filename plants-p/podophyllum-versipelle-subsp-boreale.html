Podophyllum versipelle subsp. boreale

Podophyllum versipelle subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern American mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada. It features a distinctive, umbrella-shaped leaf that is deeply lobed and often hairy.

The plant produces a showy, greenish-yellow flower that develops into a fleshy, berry-like fruit. Its root system contains toxic compounds, including podophyllotoxin, which has been used in medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is an important component of forest understory ecosystems.
