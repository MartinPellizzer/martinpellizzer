Polypodium suspensum var. mollissimum

Polypodium suspensum var. mollissimum, commonly known as the silky fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to its host, while its spore-producing structures are located on the underside of its fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
