Prostanthera pimeloides

Prostanthera pimeloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fragrant, mint-like aroma.

Prostanthera pimeloides is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
