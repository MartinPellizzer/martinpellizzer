Pastinaca absinthiifolia

Pastinaca absinthiifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
