Polypodium macleayi

Polypodium macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's shield fern, is a species of polypody that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and fronds that are typically leathery and divided into pinnate segments. Native to Australia, this fern thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests or along stream banks. The fronds are evergreen, with a distinctive appearance that includes a broad, shield-like shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
