Plantago oblonga

Plantago oblonga, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago oblonga have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
