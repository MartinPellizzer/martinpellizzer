Potentilla erecta var. latiloba

Potentilla erecta var. latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically palmately divided into five broad, ovate lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
