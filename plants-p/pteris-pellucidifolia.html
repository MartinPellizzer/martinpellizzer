Pteris pellucidifolia

Pteris pellucidifolia, commonly known as the translucent fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often translucent or semi-transparent, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The spores are released from the sori, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds, facilitating its reproduction.
