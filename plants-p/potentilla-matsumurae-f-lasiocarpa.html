Potentilla matsumurae f. lasiocarpa

Potentilla matsumurae f. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its densely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It is native to regions of East Asia, particularly China, and thrives in rocky or dry environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
