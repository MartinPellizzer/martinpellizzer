Polystachya rosea

Polystachya rosea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and its distinctive inflorescence of pink or white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Polystachya rosea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
