Pelargonium panduriforme

Pelargonium panduriforme, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed or toothed, and the plant often produces bright red or pink flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types when given adequate sunlight.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
