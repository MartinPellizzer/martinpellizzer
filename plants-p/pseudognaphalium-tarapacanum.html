Pseudognaphalium tarapacanum

Pseudognaphalium tarapacanum, commonly known as the Andean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions with well-drained soils.

The plant is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
