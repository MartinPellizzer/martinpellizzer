Pyrus rufoferruginea subvar. amoena

Pyrus rufoferruginea subvar. amoena, commonly known as the red-ironwood pear, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and reddish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
