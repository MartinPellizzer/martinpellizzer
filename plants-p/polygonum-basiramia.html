Polygonum basiramia

Polygonum basiramia, commonly known as the Basiramia knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a prominent midrib.

The small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters, and the plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
