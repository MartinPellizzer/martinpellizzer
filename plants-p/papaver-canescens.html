Papaver canescens

Papaver canescens, commonly known as the grey poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple petals and a distinctive, saucer-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, greyish-white down.

Papaver canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
