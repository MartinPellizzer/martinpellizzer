Peperomia santa-helenae

Peperomia santa-helenae, commonly known as the Santa Helena peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, typical of the Piperaceae family. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its compact size and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its unique leaf patterns and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
