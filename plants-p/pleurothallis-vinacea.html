Pleurothallis vinacea

Pleurothallis vinacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.

Pleurothallis vinacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
