Potentilla arenaria subvar. glandulosa

Potentilla arenaria subvar. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular sand cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
