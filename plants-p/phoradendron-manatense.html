Phoradendron manatense

Phoradendron manatense, commonly known as the Manatee's blessing, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Caribbean region.

It attaches itself to the stems of other plants, primarily in the genus Acrocomia, to obtain water and nutrients. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy coating.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
