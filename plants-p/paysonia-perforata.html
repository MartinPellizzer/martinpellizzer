Paysonia perforata

Paysonia perforata, commonly known as the leafy false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of many legumes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important part of the ecological landscape in its native range.
