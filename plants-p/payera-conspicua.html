Payera conspicua

Payera conspicua, commonly known as the showy payera, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically display vibrant colors such as red, orange, or yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or woolly coating.

Payera conspicua is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
