Podophyllum japonicum

Podophyllum japonicum, commonly known as Japanese spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a short, thick rhizome and produces erect, branched stems covered in small, waxy leaves. The plant is notable for its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are toxic and contain compounds used in medicinal preparations.

It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
