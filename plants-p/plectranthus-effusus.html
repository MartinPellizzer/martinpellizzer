Plectranthus effusus

Plectranthus effusus, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and Central America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and emit a strong, mint-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary use.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in both gardens and traditional medicine.
