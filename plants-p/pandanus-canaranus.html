Pandanus canaranus

Pandanus canaranus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for making thatch and baskets.

Pandanus canaranus thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
