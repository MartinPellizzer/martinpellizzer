Pelargonium thamnoides

Pelargonium thamnoides, commonly known as the sandstone geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural settings.
