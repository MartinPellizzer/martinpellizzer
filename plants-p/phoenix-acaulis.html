Phoenix acaulis

Phoenix acaulis, commonly known as the desert candle, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its thick, unbranched trunk covered in a layer of fibrous, waxy bark that helps it retain moisture in harsh desert conditions. Its leaves are reduced to small, spines, which minimize water loss and protect it from herbivores. The plant produces a single, tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, yellowish-white flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

After flowering, it develops a cluster of feather-like fruits that contain seeds, completing its life cycle in a remarkably compact form.
