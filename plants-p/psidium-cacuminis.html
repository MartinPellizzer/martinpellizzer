Psidium cacuminis

Psidium cacuminis, commonly known as the sour sop or custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It produces large, heart-shaped fruits with a thick, spiny rind that encases a sweet, creamy pulp. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale green, with five petals.

The tree is valued for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
