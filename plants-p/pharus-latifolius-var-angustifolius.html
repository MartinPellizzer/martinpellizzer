Pharus latifolius var. angustifolius

Pharus latifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pharus, is a variant of the broader-leaved pharus species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
