Pleroma bicolor

Pleroma bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a silvery underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pleroma bicolor is also valued for its aromatic foliage and resilience to drought conditions.
