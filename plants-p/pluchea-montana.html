Pluchea montana

Pluchea montana, commonly known as swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swamps and marshes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
