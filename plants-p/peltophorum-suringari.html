Peltophorum suringari

Peltophorum suringari, commonly known as the Indian peacock tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive peacock-like flower clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces long, pendulous inflorescences that bloom in spring. Its seeds are enclosed in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.
