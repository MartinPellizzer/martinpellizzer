Primula lepta

Primula lepta, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
