Pecteilis lucida

Pecteilis lucida, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
