Physospermopsis stricta

Physospermopsis stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf physosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
