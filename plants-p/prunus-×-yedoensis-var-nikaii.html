Prunus × yedoensis var. nikaii

Prunus × yedoensis var. nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a variety of cherry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus serrula and Prunus lusitanica, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark red fruits in the fall.

This species is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in Japan.
