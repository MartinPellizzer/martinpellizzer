Posoqueria insignis

Posoqueria insignis, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, floating leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has a thick, spongy stem that supports its massive leaves and helps it float on the water's surface. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with a distinctive shape that attracts pollinators like beetles.

Posoqueria insignis plays an important role in its ecosystem by stabilizing water bodies and offering shelter to aquatic life.
