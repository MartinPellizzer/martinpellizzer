Piptocarpha brownii

Piptocarpha brownii, commonly known as the brown's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically light green to brown in color. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Piptocarpha brownii is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
