Potentilla riparia var. miyajimensis

Potentilla riparia var. miyajimensis, commonly known as the Miyajima potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing riverbank ecosystems.
