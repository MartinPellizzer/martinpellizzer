Pygeum clementis

Pygeum clementis, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, almond-shaped fruits that contain hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
