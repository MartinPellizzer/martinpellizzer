Piper gerardoi

Piper gerardoi, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved fruits containing numerous tiny, dark seeds. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical features of Piper gerardoi make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
