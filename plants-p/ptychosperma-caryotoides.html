Ptychosperma caryotoides

Ptychosperma caryotoides, commonly known as the African palm or oil palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. Its fruits are typically clustered in spikes and contain a fleshy outer layer surrounding the hard, stony seeds.

Ptychosperma caryotoides is valued for its oil-rich seeds, which are used in various industrial and culinary applications.
