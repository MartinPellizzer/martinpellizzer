Pandanus tectorius var. douglasii

Pandanus tectorius var. douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous, spiny fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in dense stands.

Its adaptability to saline and brackish environments makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
