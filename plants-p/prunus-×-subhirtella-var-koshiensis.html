Prunus × subhirtella var. koshiensis

Prunus × subhirtella var. koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively low maintenance.
