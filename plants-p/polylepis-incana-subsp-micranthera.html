Polylepis incana subsp. micranthera

Polylepis incana subsp.

micranthera, commonly known as the Andean velvet tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is typically found at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
