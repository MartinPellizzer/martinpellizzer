Phoradendron fuscoerubescens

Phoradendron fuscoerubescens, commonly known as the red-brown mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually reddish-brown in color, contributing to its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or orange. It relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
