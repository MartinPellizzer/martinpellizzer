Pteris latifolia

Pteris latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Indian subcontinent. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are typically bright green and have a graceful, arching appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pteris latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
