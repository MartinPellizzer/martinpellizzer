Polygala raddiana var. raddiana

Polygala raddiana var. raddiana, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
