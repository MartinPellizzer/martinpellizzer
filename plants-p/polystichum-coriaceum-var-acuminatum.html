Polystichum coriaceum var. acuminatum

Polystichum coriaceum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acuminated shield fern, is a variant of the broad buckwheat fern.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China and Japan. The plant features sturdy, upright fronds that are dark green and leathery, with a distinctive shield-shaped appearance. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in cool, moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
