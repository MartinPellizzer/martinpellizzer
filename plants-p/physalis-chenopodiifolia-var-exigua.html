Physalis chenopodiifolia var. exigua

Physalis chenopodiifolia var. exigua, commonly known as the desert gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, featuring upright stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it thrives in well-drained soils with minimal water requirements.

The fruit is edible when ripe, though it is often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
