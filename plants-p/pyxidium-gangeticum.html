Pyxidium gangeticum

Pyxidium gangeticum, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.
