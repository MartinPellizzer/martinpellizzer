Potentilla oweriniana var. divina

Potentilla oweriniana var. divina, commonly known as the divine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
