Piper puberulibracteum

Piper puberulibracteum, commonly known as the pubescent-bract piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

Piper puberulibracteum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth.
