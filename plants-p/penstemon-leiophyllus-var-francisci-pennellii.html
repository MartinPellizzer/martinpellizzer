Penstemon leiophyllus var. francisci-pennellii

Penstemon leiophyllus var. francisci-pennellii, commonly known as the Franciscan penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
