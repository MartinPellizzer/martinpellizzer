Picris angustifolia subsp. carolorum-henricorum

Picris angustifolia subsp.

carolorum-henricorum, commonly known as the narrowleaf sickleweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

This subspecies is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of herbal remedies.
