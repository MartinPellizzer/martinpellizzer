Pittosporum takauele

Pittosporum takauele, commonly known as the Tahitian pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its fragrant white flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
