Phaius tankervilleae f. veronicae

Phaius tankervilleae f. veronicae, commonly known as the Veronica's orchid, is a unique hybrid orchid with distinctive botanical features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with a contrasting yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, depending on its habitat. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

The inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn.
