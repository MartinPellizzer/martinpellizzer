Primula cylindriflora

Primula cylindriflora, commonly known as the cylindrical primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical flower spikes that bear numerous small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a perennial herb with narrow, linear leaves that form a basal rosette.

The flowers bloom in early spring, making it a valuable addition to alpine gardens and rockeries.
