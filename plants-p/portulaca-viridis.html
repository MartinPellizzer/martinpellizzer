Portulaca viridis

Portulaca viridis, commonly known as the common purslane, is a succulent plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features fleshy, prostrate stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in gardens, lawns, and disturbed soils.

Portulaca viridis is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a slightly tangy flavor.
