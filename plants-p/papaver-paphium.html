Papaver paphium

Papaver paphium, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Cyprus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

Due to its cultural significance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in Cyprus.
