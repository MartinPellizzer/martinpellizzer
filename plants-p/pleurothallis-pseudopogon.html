Pleurothallis pseudopogon

Pleurothallis pseudopogon, commonly known as the false beard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which often resembles a beard, giving it its common name.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
