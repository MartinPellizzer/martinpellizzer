Pyrus bristoliensis

Pyrus bristoliensis, commonly known as Bristoli's pear, is a rare and critically endangered species native to a small area in southern England.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears but are less widely known. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is highly vulnerable to environmental changes and human activity.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
