Potentilla × chulensis

Potentilla × chulensis, commonly known as the Chul Mountains potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
