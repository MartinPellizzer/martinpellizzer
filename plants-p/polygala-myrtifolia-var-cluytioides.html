Polygala myrtifolia var. cluytioides

Polygala myrtifolia var. cluytioides, commonly known as the Cluytoides milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, winged seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
