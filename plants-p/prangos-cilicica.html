Prangos cilicica

Prangos cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various pollinators and wildlife.
