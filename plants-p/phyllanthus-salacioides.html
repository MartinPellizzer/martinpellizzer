Phyllanthus salacioides

Phyllanthus salacioides, commonly known as the creeping phyllanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive elliptical or ovate shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Phyllanthus salacioides is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
