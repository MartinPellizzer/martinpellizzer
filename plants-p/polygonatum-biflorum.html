Polygonatum biflorum

Polygonatum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Polygonatum biflorum thrives in shady, moist woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.
