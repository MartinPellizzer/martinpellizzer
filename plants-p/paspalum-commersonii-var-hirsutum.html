Paspalum commersonii var. hirsutum

Paspalum commersonii var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and erect, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

It is also valued for its use in pastureland due to its nutritional value for livestock.
