Portulaca elatior

Portulaca elatior, commonly known as the greater purslane, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. It has a creeping stem that allows it to spread across the ground, often forming dense mats.

The species is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient weed in many regions.
