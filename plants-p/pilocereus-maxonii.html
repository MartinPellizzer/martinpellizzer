Pilocereus maxonii

Pilocereus maxonii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a unique structure, with a colorful outer layer called the "pseudostem" and a green inner core that performs photosynthesis. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
