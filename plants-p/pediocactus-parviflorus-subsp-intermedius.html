Pediocactus parviflorus subsp. intermedius

Pediocactus parviflorus subsp.

intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate small-flowered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem and a dense covering of white to light green spines.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent in arid climates.
