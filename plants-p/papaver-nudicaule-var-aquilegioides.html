Papaver nudicaule var. aquilegioides

Papaver nudicaule var. aquilegioides, commonly known as the aquatic poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy characterized by its unique aquatic adaptations.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often floating or submerged, aiding in its aquatic lifestyle. Its flowers are showy, with petals that range from pale blue to violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is notable for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
