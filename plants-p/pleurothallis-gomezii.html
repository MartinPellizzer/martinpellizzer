Pleurothallis gomezii

Pleurothallis gomezii, commonly known as the Gomez's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis gomezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
