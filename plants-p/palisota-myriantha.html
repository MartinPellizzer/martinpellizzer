Palisota myriantha

Palisota myriantha, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro.

Palisota myriantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in certain regions.
