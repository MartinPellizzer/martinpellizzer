Polystachya cultriformis var. humblotii

Polystachya cultriformis var. humblotii, commonly known as the Humboldt's sword fern, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, sword-like leaves that are often arched or drooping, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
