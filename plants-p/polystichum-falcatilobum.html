Polystichum falcatilobum

Polystichum falcatilobum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It features long, lance-shaped, dark green fronds that are often arching or drooping, giving it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its fronds are divided into narrow, serrated segments, and it produces spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings.
