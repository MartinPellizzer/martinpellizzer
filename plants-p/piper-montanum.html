Piper montanum

Piper montanum, commonly known as the mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small berries that contain the seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.

Piper montanum is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
