Phaeopappus schimperi

Phaeopappus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and latex-producing tissues. The plant features a tall, hairy stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a prominent central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
