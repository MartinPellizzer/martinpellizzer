Piper infidele

Piper infidele, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by berries that range in color from green to red. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
