Potentilla gaudinii f. glandulosa

Potentilla gaudinii f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are usually hairy and glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in rock gardens and alpine settings.
