Poa nankoensis

Poa nankoensis, commonly known as the Nanko grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like China, Kazakhstan, and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Poa nankoensis is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates.
