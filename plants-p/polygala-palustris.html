Polygala palustris

Polygala palustris, commonly known as the marsh milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow up to 60 cm in height. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala palustris plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
