Passiflora dasyadenia

Passiflora dasyadenia, commonly known as the woolly passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking flowers with vibrant blue and white petals, surrounded by a crown of red filaments. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow capsule that matures into a reddish color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
