Polyscias chapelieri

Polyscias chapelieri, commonly known as the velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a prominent central spur. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Polyscias chapelieri is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
