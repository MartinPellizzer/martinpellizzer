Pseudosasa nanunica

Pseudosasa nanunica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. Pseudosasa nanunica typically grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in suitable habitats.
