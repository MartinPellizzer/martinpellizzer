Physalis megistocarpos

Physalis megistocarpos, commonly known as the giant husk tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange berries that are edible when ripe. Its foliage consists of broad, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit structure.
