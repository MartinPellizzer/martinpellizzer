Pandanus tectorius f. convexus

Pandanus tectorius f. convexus, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a variant of the common pandanus species.

It is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its wide, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional thatching and basket weaving. Its inflorescence consists of a branched, spiky structure with small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, coastal environments and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
