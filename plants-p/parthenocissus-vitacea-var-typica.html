Parthenocissus vitacea var. typica

Parthenocissus vitacea var. typica, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread over walls, trellises, and buildings.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide seasonal color in gardens and urban landscapes.
