Panicum papillosum

Panicum papillosum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinct hairy texture.

Panicum papillosum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
