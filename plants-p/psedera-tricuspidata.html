Psedera tricuspidata

Psedera tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of leaves with three distinct lobes, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to anchor it in nutrient-poor soils.

Psedera tricuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
