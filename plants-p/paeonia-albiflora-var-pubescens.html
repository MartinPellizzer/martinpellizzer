Paeonia albiflora var. pubescens

Paeonia albiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the white peony with hairy leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of large, white, single or semi-double flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features deeply lobed, hairy, and dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its pale blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its fragrance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
