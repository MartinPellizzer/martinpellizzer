Premna involucrata

Premna involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters.

Premna involucrata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
