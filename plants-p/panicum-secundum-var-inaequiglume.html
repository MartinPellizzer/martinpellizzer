Panicum secundum var. inaequiglume

Panicum secundum var. inaequiglume, commonly known as the unequal-glume panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, with the lower glumes being shorter than the upper ones, giving it its varietal name.

This variation in glume length is a distinguishing trait in its morphology.
