Polyscias confertifolia var. confertifolia

Polyscias confertifolia var. confertifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf holly or false holly, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shady conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is used in horticulture for landscape design.
