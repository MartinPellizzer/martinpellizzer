Pandanus incrassatus

Pandanus incrassatus, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, stiff, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. These flowers are followed by spiky, pineapple-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pandanus incrassatus is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
