Primula frenchii

Primula frenchii, commonly known as the French primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of France and Italy.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other early-season pollinators.
