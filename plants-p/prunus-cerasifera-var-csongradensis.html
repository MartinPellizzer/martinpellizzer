Prunus cerasifera var. csongradensis

Prunus cerasifera var. csongradensis, commonly known as the Hungarian cherry, is a variety of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically edible. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the fruiting season in summer. It is native to Hungary and parts of Central Europe, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
