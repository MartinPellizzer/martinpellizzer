Pulsatilla cernua var. koreana

Pulsatilla cernua var. koreana, commonly known as the Korean anemone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Korea.

It features a short, stout rhizome and produces basal leaves that are pinnately divided with a hairy surface. The plant blooms with showy, white to pale pink flowers that have a distinctive, rounded shape and a hairy center. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, making it an important plant for supporting pollinator populations.
