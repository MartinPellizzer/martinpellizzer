Phoenix hanceana var. philippinensis

Phoenix hanceana var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine date palm, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in its native range.
