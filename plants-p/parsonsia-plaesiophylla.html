Parsonsia plaesiophylla

Parsonsia plaesiophylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a red or orange calyx. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
