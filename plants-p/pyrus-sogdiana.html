Pyrus sogdiana

Pyrus sogdiana, commonly known as the Sogdian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions such as Tajikistan, Uzbekistan, and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, yellowish pear, is edible and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

The tree typically grows in mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, arid climates.
