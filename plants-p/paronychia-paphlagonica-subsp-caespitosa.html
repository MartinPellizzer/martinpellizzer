Paronychia paphlagonica subsp. caespitosa

Paronychia paphlagonica subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the Paphlagonian sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the rocky and sandy soils of southeastern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a compact rosette.
