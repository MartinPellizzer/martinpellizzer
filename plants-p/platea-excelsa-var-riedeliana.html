Platea excelsa var. riedeliana

Platea excelsa var. riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's giant flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has a straight, unbranched trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a dominant feature in its native habitat. Its leaves are compound, with long, narrow leaflets, and it produces woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates for its vibrant floral display.
