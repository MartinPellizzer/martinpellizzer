Pluchea lucens

Pluchea lucens, commonly known as the shining blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, with yellow central disks, and it produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Pluchea lucens is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.
