Physalis obscura var. glabra

Physalis obscura var. glabra, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. Its flowers are typically pink to purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous surface, contrasting with the more hairy forms of the species.
