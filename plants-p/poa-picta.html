Poa picta

Poa picta, commonly known as the painted grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southern regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Poa picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
