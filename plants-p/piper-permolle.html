Piper permolle

Piper permolle, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits.
