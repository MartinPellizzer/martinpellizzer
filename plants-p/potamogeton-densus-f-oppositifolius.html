Potamogeton densus f. oppositifolius

Potamogeton densus f. oppositifolius, commonly known as the dense pondweed with opposite leaves, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stem.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms. Its leaves are usually dark green and can vary in width depending on water conditions.

This fern-like plant is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, contributing to water clarity and supporting biodiversity.
