Polypodium angustum var. sectifrons

Polypodium angustum var. sectifrons, commonly known as the narrow polypody, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and delicate, pinnate fronds with a dark green coloration. The fronds are typically short and narrowly lanceolate, with finely serrated margins. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is distinguished by its narrow leaflets and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
