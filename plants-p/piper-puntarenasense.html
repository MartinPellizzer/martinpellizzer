Piper puntarenasense

Piper puntarenasense, commonly known as the Costa Rican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
