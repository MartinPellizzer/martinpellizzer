Polygonum caucanum

Polygonum caucanum, commonly known as the Caucanum knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and sometimes edible rhizomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring.

Polygonum caucanum is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
