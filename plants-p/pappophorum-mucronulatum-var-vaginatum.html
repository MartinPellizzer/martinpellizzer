Pappophorum mucronulatum var. vaginatum

Pappophorum mucronulatum var. vaginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, triangular stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, aiding in its spread.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
