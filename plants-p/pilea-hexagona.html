Pilea hexagona

Pilea hexagona, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Pilea hexagona is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental leaves and ease of care.
