Poa macrocalyx subsp. koriakiensis

Poa macrocalyx subsp.

koriakiensis, commonly known as the Koryak mountain grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae. It is native to the Russian Far East and parts of northern Japan, typically growing in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or red.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or well-drained soils.
