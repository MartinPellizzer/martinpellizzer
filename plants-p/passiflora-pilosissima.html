Passiflora pilosissima

Passiflora pilosissima, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
