Pisonia viscosa

Pisonia viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are usually white or pink in color.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
