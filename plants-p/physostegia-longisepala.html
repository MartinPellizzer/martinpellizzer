Physostegia longisepala

Physostegia longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, two-lipped flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
