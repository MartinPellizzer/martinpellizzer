Peperomia leonardii

Peperomia leonardii, commonly known as the Leonard's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a glossy surface and a variety of colors ranging from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia leonardii is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and occasional fertilization during the growing season.
