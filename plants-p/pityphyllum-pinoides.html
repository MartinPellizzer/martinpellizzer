Pityphyllum pinoides

Pityphyllum pinoides, commonly known as the slender leaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland forest environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in the North and South Islands of New Zealand, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens outside its native range.
