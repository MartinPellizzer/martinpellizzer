Phyllachne clavigera

Phyllachne clavigera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phyllachne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
