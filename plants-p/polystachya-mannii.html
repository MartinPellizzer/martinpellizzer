Polystachya mannii

Polystachya mannii, commonly known as the African forest ginger, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in the rainforests of West and Central Africa. The plant is characterized by its dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in the undergrowth of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
