Pterocactus valentini

Pterocactus valentini, commonly known as the Valentine's cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems that resemble a crown, which are covered in dense, white hairs. The plant produces small, vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring season, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a protected species in its native environment.
