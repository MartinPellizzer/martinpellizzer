Parodia gutekunstiana

Parodia gutekunstiana, commonly known as the Gutekunst's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is a solitary or occasionally clustering cactus with a short, cylindrical stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on areoles along the stem. Its spines are short and often whitish, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its compact size and attractive floral display.
