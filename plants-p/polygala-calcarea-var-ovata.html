Polygala calcarea var. ovata

Polygala calcarea var. ovata, commonly known as the smooth rock milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes. The plant typically grows on rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are often visited by insects such as bees and flies.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
