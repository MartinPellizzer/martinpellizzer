Pyrus estiensis

Pyrus estiensis, commonly known as the Estian pear, is a species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, with leaves that turn a vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, oval pear with a thin, smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Pyrus estiensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
