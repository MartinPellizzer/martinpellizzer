Piper trigonioideum

Piper trigonioideum, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or Trinidad nutmeg, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, known as a drupe, is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it distinct within its family, with unique growth habits and chemical compositions.
