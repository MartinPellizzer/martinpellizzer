Persicaria hydropiper f. angustissima

Persicaria hydropiper f. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, typically found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

This subspecies is often distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Persicaria hydropiper.
