Polygonum bistorta var. incanum

Polygonum bistorta var. incanum, commonly known as the white-haired bistort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems covered in white or pale hairs, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to reddish flowers in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tinge.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
