Persicaria neofiliformis

Persicaria neofiliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its small, clustered flowers are pink to red in color and bloom in late summer.

Persicaria neofiliformis is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a potential indicator of wetland health.
