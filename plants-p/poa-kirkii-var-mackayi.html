Poa kirkii var. mackayi

Poa kirkii var. mackayi, commonly known as the Mackay's tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
