Petalolepis rosmarinifolia

Petalolepis rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the mountain rosemary.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its foliage emits a strong, pleasant fragrance, reminiscent of rosemary, which is used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Petalolepis rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
