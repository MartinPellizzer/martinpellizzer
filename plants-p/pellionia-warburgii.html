Pellionia warburgii

Pellionia warburgii, commonly known as the silver-veined pellionia, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or pale green vein pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Pellionia warburgii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant.

Its ornamental value lies primarily in its attractive leaf structure rather than its flowers.
