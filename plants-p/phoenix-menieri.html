Phoenix menieri

Phoenix menieri, commonly known as the Menieri's date palm, is a species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and long, pinnate leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible dates, though it is less productive than other date palm species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates with well-drained soils.

The Menieri's date palm is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its economic importance in traditional agriculture.
