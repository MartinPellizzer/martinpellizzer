Phyllanthus minutiflorus var. gracillimus

Phyllanthus minutiflorus var. gracillimus, commonly known as the slender-flowered phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
