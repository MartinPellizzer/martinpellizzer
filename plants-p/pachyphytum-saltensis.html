Pachyphytum saltensis

Pachyphytum saltensis, commonly known as the Salt Valley Pachyphytum, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pachyphytum saltensis is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.
