Papyrius polymorphus

Papyrius polymorphus, commonly known as the variable papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters above the water.

Papyrius polymorphus plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic life and maintaining water quality.
