Piper villiramulum

Piper villiramulum, commonly known as the Bornean pepper or Borneo long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its elongated, sparsely branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that contain numerous tiny, black seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Piper villiramulum is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
