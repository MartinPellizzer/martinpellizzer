Phacelia congesta var. rupestris

Phacelia congesta var. rupestris, commonly known as the desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments.
