Plateana

Plateana, commonly known as the common fig tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique syconium fruits. The plant produces a sweet, edible fruit that is highly valued for its nutritional content and culinary uses. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

The tree is also notable for its ability to host wasps in its fruit, which play a crucial role in pollination.
