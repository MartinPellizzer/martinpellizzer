Pterocarpus draco

Pterocarpus draco, commonly known as the dragon's blood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive umbrella-shaped crown. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, winged fruits. Its bark and sap have been traditionally used in medicine and as a dye due to their rich red color.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
