Polygonatum multiflorum f. ramosum

Polygonatum multiflorum f. ramosum, commonly known as the branched Solomon's seal, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
