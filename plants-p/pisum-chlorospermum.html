Pisum chlorospermum

Pisum chlorospermum, commonly known as the golden pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential in ecological and agricultural applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and scientific research.
