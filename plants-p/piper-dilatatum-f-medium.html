Piper dilatatum f. medium

Piper dilatatum f. medium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the species Piper dilatatum, which belongs to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically larger than those of the standard species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, three-lobed berries. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine.
