Picea excelsa cincinnata

Picea excelsa cincinnata, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a coniferous tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The needles are long, flat, and dark green, with a slightly waxy coating. It produces small, cylindrical cones that hang downward from the branches.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
