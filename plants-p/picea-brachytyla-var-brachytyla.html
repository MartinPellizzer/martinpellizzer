Picea brachytyla var. brachytyla

Picea brachytyla var. brachytyla, commonly known as the dwarf white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its short, dense branches and small, triangular needles that are typically bluish-green in color. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its slow growth rate and compact form make it well-suited for ornamental landscaping.
