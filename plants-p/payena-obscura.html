Payena obscura

Payena obscura, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its density and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody capsules containing small, dark seeds.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in the region.
