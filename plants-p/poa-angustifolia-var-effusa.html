Poa angustifolia var. effusa

Poa angustifolia var. effusa, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bluegrass.

It is a cool-season grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This variant is noted for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
