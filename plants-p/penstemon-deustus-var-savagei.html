Penstemon deustus var. savagei

Penstemon deustus var. savagei, commonly known as the Sagebrush Penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple tubular flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
