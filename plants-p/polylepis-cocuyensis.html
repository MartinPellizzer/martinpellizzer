Polylepis cocuyensis

Polylepis cocuyensis, commonly known as the Cocuyensis Polylepis, is a species of tree native to the Andean highlands of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, small, elliptical leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. This tree is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Polylepis cocuyensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
