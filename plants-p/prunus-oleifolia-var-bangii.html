Prunus oleifolia var. bangii

Prunus oleifolia var. bangii, commonly known as the Bangi cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark red or black berries. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are edible but are typically used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
