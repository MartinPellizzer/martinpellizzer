Pouteria lifuana

Pouteria lifuana, commonly known as the Lifuana sapote, is a tropical tree native to the lowland rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces large, fleshy fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible and often used in local cuisines. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
