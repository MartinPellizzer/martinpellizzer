Polygonum islandicum

Polygonum islandicum, commonly known as the island knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, coastal environments and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
