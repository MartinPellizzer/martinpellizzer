Pleiotaxis sciaphila

Pleiotaxis sciaphila, commonly known as the shadow fern, is a unique fern species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often pale green with a bluish tinge. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, making it well-suited for forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

The plant's ability to tolerate low light conditions is a key adaptation to its ecological niche.
