Paronychia kapela subsp. aretioides

Paronychia kapela subsp.

aretioides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with four petals, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and ornamental addition to rock gardens and arid landscapes.
