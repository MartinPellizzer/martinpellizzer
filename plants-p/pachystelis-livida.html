Pachystelis livida

Pachystelis livida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a slightly succulent texture.

Pachystelis livida is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
