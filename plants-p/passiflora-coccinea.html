Passiflora coccinea

Passiflora coccinea, commonly known as the crimson passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with intricate, feathery structures and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora coccinea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
