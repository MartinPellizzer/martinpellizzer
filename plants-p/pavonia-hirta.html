Pavonia hirta

Pavonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and coastal areas. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic of the species, contributing to its adaptability in various environmental conditions.

Pavonia hirta is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
