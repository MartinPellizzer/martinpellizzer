Prostanthera denticulata var. latifolia

Prostanthera denticulata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow to broad lance-shaped leaves with a slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting native pollinators.
