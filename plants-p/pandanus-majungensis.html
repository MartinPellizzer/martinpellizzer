Pandanus majungensis

Pandanus majungensis, commonly known as the Majungensis pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the pandan family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the ends of its stems. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove areas.

The leaves of Pandanus majungensis are used in traditional crafts and for thatching in some local communities.
