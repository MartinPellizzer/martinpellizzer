Phebalium cicatricatum

Phebalium cicatricatum, commonly known as the scarred wattle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive scarred bark, which gives it its common name. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, narrow leaves and produces pods that contain seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
