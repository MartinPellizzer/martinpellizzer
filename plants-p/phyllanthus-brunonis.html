Phyllanthus brunonis

Phyllanthus brunonis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring erect, branching stems covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface.

Phyllanthus brunonis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
