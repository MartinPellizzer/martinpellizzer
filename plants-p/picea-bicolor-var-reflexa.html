Picea bicolor var. reflexa

Picea bicolor var. reflexa, commonly known as the white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-blue needles and distinctive cone shape. The foliage is typically dense and conical, with the needles arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. This variety is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a range of soil types.

It plays an important ecological role in boreal and subalpine forests.
