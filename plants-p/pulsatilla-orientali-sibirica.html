Pulsatilla orientali-sibirica

Pulsatilla orientali-sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates, often blooming in early spring. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs, which help protect it from harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
