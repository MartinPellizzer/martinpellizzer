Piper peltatum var. colombianum

Piper peltatum var. colombianum, commonly known as the Colombian pepper plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
