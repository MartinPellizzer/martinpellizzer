Patagonium peraltae

Patagonium peraltae, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes in Argentina.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Patagonium peraltae plays an important role in its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
