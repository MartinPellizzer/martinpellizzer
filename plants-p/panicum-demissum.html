Panicum demissum

Panicum demissum, commonly known as the drooping panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping inflorescences and fine, wiry leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing small, pale green to brown spikelets.

Panicum demissum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
