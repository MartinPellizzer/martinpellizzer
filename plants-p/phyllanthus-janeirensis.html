Phyllanthus janeirensis

Phyllanthus janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pear tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, green, pear-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in dense inflorescences.
