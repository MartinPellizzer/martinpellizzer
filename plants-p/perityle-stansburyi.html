Perityle stansburyi

Perityle stansburyi, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silky hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The small, yellow flowers bloom in the spring and are visited by various pollinators, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.
