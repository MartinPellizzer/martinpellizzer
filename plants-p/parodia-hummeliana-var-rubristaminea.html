Parodia hummeliana var. rubristaminea

Parodia hummeliana var. rubristaminea, commonly known as the red-stemmed pincushion cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense, spiny exterior with a pale green to blue-gray body, and its stems are often tinged with a reddish hue, particularly in sunlight. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its areoles, which bear the spines and flowers.
