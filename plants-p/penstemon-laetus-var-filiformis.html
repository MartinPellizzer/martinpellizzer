Penstemon laetus var. filiformis

Penstemon laetus var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
