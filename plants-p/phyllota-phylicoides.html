Phyllota phylicoides

Phyllota phylicoides, commonly known as the Mexican bluebonnet, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Phyllota phylicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in native gardens.
