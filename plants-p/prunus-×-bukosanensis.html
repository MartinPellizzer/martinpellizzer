Prunus × bukosanensis

Prunus × bukosanensis, commonly known as the Kishu cherry, is a hybrid species of cherry tree originating from Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, dark red fruits that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its beautiful blossoms and compact growth habit.

Prunus × bukosanensis thrives in temperate climates and is well-suited for gardens and landscapes.
