Pecluma celaquensis

Pecluma celaquensis, commonly known as the African water spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near water bodies. It has a shallow root system that allows it to spread rapidly in wet environments.

Pecluma celaquensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in some regions.
