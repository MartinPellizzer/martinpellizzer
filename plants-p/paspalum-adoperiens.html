Paspalum adoperiens

Paspalum adoperiens, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum adoperiens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate poor soil conditions.
