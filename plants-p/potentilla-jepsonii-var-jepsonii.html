Potentilla jepsonii var. jepsonii

Potentilla jepsonii var. jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with woody stems and small, palmate leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
