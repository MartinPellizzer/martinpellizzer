Piper scansum

Piper scansum, commonly known as the pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Piper scansum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
