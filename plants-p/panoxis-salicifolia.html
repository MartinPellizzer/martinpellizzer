Panoxis salicifolia

Panoxis salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf panoxis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
