Plantago tomentosa subsp. leiocalyx

Plantago tomentosa subsp.

leiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
