Ptilotus latifolius var. major

Ptilotus latifolius var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved ptilotus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
