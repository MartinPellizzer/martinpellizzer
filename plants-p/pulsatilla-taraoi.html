Pulsatilla taraoi

Pulsatilla taraoi, commonly known as the Taroai pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Pulsatilla taraoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique ecological adaptations.
