Polyalthia subsessilifolia

Polyalthia subsessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its striking, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in bright red or orange hues. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a short petiole, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological and medicinal properties.
