Piper flabellinerve

Piper flabellinerve, commonly known as the fan-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive, fan-like venation pattern. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
