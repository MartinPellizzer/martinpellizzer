Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. lowii

Parthenocissus tricuspidata f. lowii, commonly known as the variegated ivy creeper, is a climbing plant native to China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, lobed leaves that often display variegation with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage.

Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises, walls, and as a ground cover.
