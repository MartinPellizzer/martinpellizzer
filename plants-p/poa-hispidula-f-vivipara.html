Poa hispidula f. vivipara

Poa hispidula f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous shoots, which are capable of rooting and growing independently. This feature allows it to spread rapidly in suitable environments. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its small, narrow leaves and dense growth habit make it a common component of temperate grassland ecosystems.
