Prunus spinosa var. ovata

Prunus spinosa var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-shaped blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by dark purple berries in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedging and as a food source for wildlife.
