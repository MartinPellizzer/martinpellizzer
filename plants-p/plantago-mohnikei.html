Plantago mohnikei

Plantago mohnikei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by oval, three-lobed seed capsules.

This species is valued for its hardy nature and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
