Plagianthus pulchellus

Plagianthus pulchellus, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescences that appear in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique floral structure and seasonal bloom make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
