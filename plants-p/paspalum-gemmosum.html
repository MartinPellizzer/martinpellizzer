Paspalum gemmosum

Paspalum gemmosum, commonly known as the creeping paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers, typically in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Paspalum gemmosum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
