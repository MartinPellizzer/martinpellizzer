Piper bengalense

Piper bengalense, commonly known as Indian black pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody vines and small, peppery fruits. The plant produces tiny, dark-colored berries that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
