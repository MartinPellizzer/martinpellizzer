Pulsatilla ucrainica

Pulsatilla ucrainica, commonly known as the Ukrainian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles as its fruit type.

Pulsatilla ucrainica is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
