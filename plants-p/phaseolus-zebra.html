Phaseolus zebra

Phaseolus zebra, commonly known as the zebra bean, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus zebra is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is a source of protein and fiber.
