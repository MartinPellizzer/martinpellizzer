Prunus carmesina

Prunus carmesina, commonly known as the Mexican white cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by edible drupes that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
