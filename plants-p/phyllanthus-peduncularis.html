Phyllanthus peduncularis

Phyllanthus peduncularis, commonly known as the Indian cheesenut, is a shrub native to tropical and southern regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble tiny cheese-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional systems of medicine.
