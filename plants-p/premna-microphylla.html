Premna microphylla

Premna microphylla, commonly known as small-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. Its small, fragrant flowers are borne in clusters and are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
