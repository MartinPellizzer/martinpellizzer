Piper salentoi var. cajamarcanum

Piper salentoi var. cajamarcanum, commonly known as the Cajamarca pepper or Andean pepper, is a variant of the Piper salentoi species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its peppery flavor in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable plant in both ecological and culinary contexts.
