Phymatanthus elatus

Phymatanthus elatus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
