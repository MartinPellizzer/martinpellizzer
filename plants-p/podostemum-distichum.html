Podostemum distichum

Podostemum distichum, commonly known as the two-ranked water primrose, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, with small, opposite leaves that often appear in two rows along the stem. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, typically near the edges of rivers and lakes. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Podostemum distichum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
