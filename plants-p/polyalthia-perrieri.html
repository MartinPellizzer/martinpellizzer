Polyalthia perrieri

Polyalthia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with an odd number of leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Its bark is smooth and light grey, and it is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
