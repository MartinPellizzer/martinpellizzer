Pachycarpus insignis

Pachycarpus insignis, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
