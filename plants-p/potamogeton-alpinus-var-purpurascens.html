Potamogeton alpinus var. purpurascens

Potamogeton alpinus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, with a distinctive purplish tinge in some varieties. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
