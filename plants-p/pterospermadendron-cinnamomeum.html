Pterospermadendron cinnamomeum

Pterospermadendron cinnamomeum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a member of the Leguminosae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and bipinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing flat, elongated seeds.

It is valued for its aromatic bark, which contains compounds similar to cinnamon, and for its ecological role in tropical forests.
