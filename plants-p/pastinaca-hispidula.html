Pastinaca hispidula

Pastinaca hispidula, commonly known as the hairy burdock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pastinaca hispidula thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.
