Potentilla hippiana

Potentilla hippiana, commonly known as the Persian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to nine oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
