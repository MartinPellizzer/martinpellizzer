Piper admirabile

Piper admirabile, commonly known as the Admirable Pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its small greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, a small berry, turns red when mature and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.
