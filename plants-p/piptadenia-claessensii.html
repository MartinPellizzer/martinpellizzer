Piptadenia claessensii

Piptadenia claessensii, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often bipinnate, and its yellowish or greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and is often found in dry savanna habitats.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
