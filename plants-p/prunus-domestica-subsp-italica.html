Prunus domestica subsp. italica

Prunus domestica subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian prune, is a subspecies of the European plum. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is used in various culinary applications, including jams, preserves, and dried fruit.
