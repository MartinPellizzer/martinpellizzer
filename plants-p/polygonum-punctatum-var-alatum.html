Polygonum punctatum var. alatum

Polygonum punctatum var. alatum, commonly known as the winged knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
