Pterygota trinervia

Pterygota trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined winged plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves exhibit a trinervate venation pattern, with three primary veins branching from the base, giving it a delicate, feather-like appearance. The plant belongs to the group Pterygota, which includes plants with winged or membranous structures, typically associated with certain ferns and mosses. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying on spores for propagation rather than seeds.
