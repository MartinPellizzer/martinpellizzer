Piper glabrispica

Piper glabrispica, commonly known as the smooth-hairless pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and its use in local herbal remedies.
