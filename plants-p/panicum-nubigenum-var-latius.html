Panicum nubigenum var. latius

Panicum nubigenum var. latius, commonly known as the broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing panicles that are often visible during the dry season. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its growth habit makes it a useful ornamental plant in landscaping and a potential candidate for erosion control.

The species is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions, making it resilient in challenging climates.
