Pentas globifera

Pentas globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globe-shaped inflorescences composed of numerous small, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually bright red or orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
