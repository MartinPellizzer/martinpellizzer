Pandanus divergens

Pandanus divergens, commonly known as the broadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
