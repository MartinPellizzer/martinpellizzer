Paxiuscula

Paxiuscula, commonly known as the dwarf paxius, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the moist woodlands of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked due to their small size and dull coloration. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing in association with other understory plants.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
