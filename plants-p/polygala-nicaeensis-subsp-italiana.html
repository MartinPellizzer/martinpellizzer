Polygala nicaeensis subsp. italiana

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

italiana, commonly known as the Italian milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or pink, and it produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
