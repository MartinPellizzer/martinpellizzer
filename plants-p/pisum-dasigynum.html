Pisum dasigynum

Pisum dasigynum, commonly known as the wild pea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its pods contain several seeds, which are often used for forage or as a food source.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
