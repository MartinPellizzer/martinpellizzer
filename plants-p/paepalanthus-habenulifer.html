Paepalanthus habenulifer

Paepalanthus habenulifer, commonly known as the yellow cupflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The yellow cupflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
