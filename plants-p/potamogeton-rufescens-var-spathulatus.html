Potamogeton rufescens var. spathulatus

Potamogeton rufescens var. spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spathes, which are modified leaves that protect the reproductive structures. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
