Primula bella subsp. coryphaea

Primula bella subsp.

coryphaea, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
