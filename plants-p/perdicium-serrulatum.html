Perdicium serrulatum

Perdicium serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Perdicium serrulatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
