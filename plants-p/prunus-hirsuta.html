Prunus hirsuta

Prunus hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

Prunus hirsuta is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in various regions.
