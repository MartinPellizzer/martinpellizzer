Piper silvicola

Piper silvicola, commonly known as the forest pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Piper silvicola is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments.
