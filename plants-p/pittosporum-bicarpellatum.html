Pittosporum bicarpellatum

Pittosporum bicarpellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum bicarpellatum is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
