Pithecellobium cyclocarpum

Pithecellobium cyclocarpum, commonly known as the cyclocarpum acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spherical pods that encase its seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
