Paronychia rechingeri

Paronychia rechingeri, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Paronychia rechingeri is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
