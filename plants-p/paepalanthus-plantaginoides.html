Paepalanthus plantaginoides

Paepalanthus plantaginoides, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
