Pyrus rhaphiolepis

Pyrus rhaphiolepis, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in a variety of soil types.
