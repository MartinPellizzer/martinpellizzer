Pilostyles haussknechtii

Pilostyles haussknechtii, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique parasitic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, stems, and roots, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. The plant produces a single, elongated, white or pale green flower that resembles a ghostly shape, hence its common name. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found growing on the roots of other plants.

Due to its specialized parasitic lifestyle and rare occurrence, it is considered a fascinating but elusive botanical specimen.
