Pluchea tetranthera var. tomentosa

Pluchea tetranthera var. tomentosa, commonly known as silky thornweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
