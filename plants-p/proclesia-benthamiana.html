Proclesia benthamiana

Proclesia benthamiana, commonly known as the slender rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a short-lived lifecycle and is adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
