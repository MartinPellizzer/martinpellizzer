Posoqueria grandifructa

Posoqueria grandifructa, commonly known as the giant swamp taro, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stems. Its rhizomes are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

Posoqueria grandifructa thrives in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas.
