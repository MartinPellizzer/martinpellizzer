Penstemon moriahensis

Penstemon moriahensis, commonly known as Moriah beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as slopes and meadows. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
