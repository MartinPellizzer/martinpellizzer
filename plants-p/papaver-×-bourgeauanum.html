Papaver × bourgeauanum

Papaver × bourgeauanum, commonly known as the Bourgeau poppy, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a striking ornamental plant with historical significance in horticulture.
