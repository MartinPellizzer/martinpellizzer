Pulsatilla taraoi subsp. nipponica

Pulsatilla taraoi subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or subalpine regions. It has palmately divided leaves and produces follicular fruits that contain a single seed.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
