Pyrrosia tricholepis

Pyrrosia tricholepis, commonly known as the hairy rock fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

Pyrrosia tricholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
