Potentilla × solitaria

Potentilla × solitaria, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are dark green, finely divided, and arranged in a rosette pattern, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla × solitaria is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes due to its hardiness and aesthetic value.
