Phaseolus bicolor

Phaseolus bicolor, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red or yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds used in various cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

Phaseolus bicolor is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
