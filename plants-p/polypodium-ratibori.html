Polypodium ratibori

Polypodium ratibori, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly metallic sheen. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Polypodium ratibori is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
