Piper polystachyon

Piper polystachyon, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, thin fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in warm climates for both aesthetic and potential medicinal purposes.
