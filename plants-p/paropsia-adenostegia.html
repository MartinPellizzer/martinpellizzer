Paropsia adenostegia

Paropsia adenostegia, commonly known as the woolly ant plant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a perennial shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and often clustered at the base of the plant.

Paropsia adenostegia is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
