Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. viridis

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the Japanese hemlock, is a variant of the hemlock tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically a vibrant green, contributing to its ornamental value. This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
