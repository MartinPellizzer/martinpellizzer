Piper subrepens

Piper subrepens, commonly known as creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
