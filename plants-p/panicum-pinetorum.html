Panicum pinetorum

Panicum pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open woodlands and sandy soils, often found in association with pine trees. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds in late summer. Its flowering structure consists of spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem.

Panicum pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
