Potentilla × menziesii

Potentilla × menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, typically arranged in an alternate pattern along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
