Picris glomerata

Picris glomerata, commonly known as clustered toothworts, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
