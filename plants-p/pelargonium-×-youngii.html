Pelargonium × youngii

Pelargonium × youngii, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is often used in hanging baskets and as a decorative ground cover due to its compact form and ornamental appeal.

Pelargonium × youngii thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
