Phaseolus albescens

Phaseolus albescens, commonly known as the white bean plant, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces pods containing small, round beans that are typically white or cream in color. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This plant thrives in warm climates and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types.
