Paeonia delavayi subsp. angustiloba

Paeonia delavayi subsp.

angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan and Yunnan in China. It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional Chinese medicine.
