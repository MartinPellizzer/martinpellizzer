Papaver radicatum f. majus

Papaver radicatum f. majus, commonly known as the giant poppy, is a striking botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter, with vibrant red petals and a prominent black central stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in gardens for its dramatic floral display and is also studied for its potential in horticultural and ecological contexts.
