Pterostylis crassicaulis

Pterostylis crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its robust, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy greenish-white flower with a distinctive hood-like structure. It typically blooms in spring and is found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and understoreys.

Pterostylis crassicaulis is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique floral morphology and ecological significance.
