Pandanus pseudocollinus

Pandanus pseudocollinus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it thrives in coastal and wetland environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and ecological restoration projects.
