Polypodium atacamense

Polypodium atacamense, commonly known as the Atacama polypody, is a fern native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
