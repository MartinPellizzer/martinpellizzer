Puccinellia anisoclada subsp. melderisiana

Puccinellia anisoclada subsp.

melderisiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common species in desert and coastal environments.

Its botanical features reflect its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
