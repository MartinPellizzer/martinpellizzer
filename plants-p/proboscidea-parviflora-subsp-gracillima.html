Proboscidea parviflora subsp. gracillima

Proboscidea parviflora subsp.

gracillima, commonly known as the slender-flowered proboscidea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America. It features slender, upright stems with small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It blooms during the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
