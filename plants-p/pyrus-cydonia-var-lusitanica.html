Pyrus cydonia var. lusitanica

Pyrus cydonia var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lisbon pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with dark green, lobed leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and resistance to certain diseases.
