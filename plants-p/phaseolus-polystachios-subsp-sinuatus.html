Phaseolus polystachios subsp. sinuatus

Phaseolus polystachios subsp.

sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuate runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America. It produces long, slender pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or brown when mature.

The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive sinuate or wavy edge, contributing to its ornamental value. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, and it is valued both for its edible beans and as a decorative plant.

The subspecies is adapted to a variety of growing conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
