Polygonum ramosissimum var. prolificum

Polygonum ramosissimum var. prolificum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, spherical fruits. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
