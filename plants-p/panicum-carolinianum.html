Panicum carolinianum

Panicum carolinianum, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a grass species that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, silver-gray blades with a distinctive feathery plume at the top, which gives it a striking appearance. Its inflorescence is a panicle, consisting of numerous small spikelets that mature into seeds.

Panicum carolinianum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
