Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. micropterus

Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. micropterus, commonly known as the winged bean, is a leguminous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-winged pods that contain edible seeds and a starchy, tuberous root. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is cultivated for its nutritious seeds and roots.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
