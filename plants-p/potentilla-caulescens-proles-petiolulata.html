Potentilla caulescens proles petiolulata

Potentilla caulescens proles petiolulata, commonly known as the petiolulate potentilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and multiple stems. The leaves are typically compound, with small leaflets arranged along slender petioles, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
