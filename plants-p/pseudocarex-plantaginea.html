Pseudocarex plantaginea

Pseudocarex plantaginea, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a distinctive grass-like appearance, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Pseudocarex plantaginea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
