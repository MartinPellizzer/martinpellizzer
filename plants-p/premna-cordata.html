Premna cordata

Premna cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf or Indian nettle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna cordata is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
