Puya pectinata

Puya pectinata, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in cool, sunny conditions and is adapted to the harsh, mountainous environment.

Puya pectinata is known for its slow growth and long lifespan, often living for several decades.
