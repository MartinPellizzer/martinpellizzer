Phaulopsis glutinosa

Phaulopsis glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky phaulopsis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, sticky resinous secretions that coat its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
