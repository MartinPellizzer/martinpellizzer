Prunus persica f. pendula

Prunus persica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping peach, is a striking ornamental variety of the peach tree.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that drape gracefully from the main trunk, creating a distinctive, ethereal appearance. The plant produces showy pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its unique form.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and seasonal beauty.
