Pyrus kunoriana

Pyrus kunoriana, commonly known as the Kunor apple, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Pamir and Tien Shan ranges. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. Its fruit, a small apple-like berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Pyrus kunoriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
