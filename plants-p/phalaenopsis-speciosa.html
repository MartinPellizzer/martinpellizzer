Phalaenopsis speciosa

Phalaenopsis speciosa, commonly known as the Orchid of Beauty, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

Phalaenopsis speciosa is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
