Parasia primuliflora

Parasia primuliflora, commonly known as the spring bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting local pollinators.
