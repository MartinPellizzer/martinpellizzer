Pourouma mollis subsp. mollis

Pourouma mollis subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its growth habit resembles that of a palm, though it is botanically classified as a bamboo.

The species is notable for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
