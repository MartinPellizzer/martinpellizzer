Pleurothallis privigna

Pleurothallis privigna, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a unique combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Pleurothallis privigna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
