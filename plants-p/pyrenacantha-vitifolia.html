Pyrenacantha vitifolia

Pyrenacantha vitifolia, commonly known as the grapeleaf pyrenacantha, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The leaves are typically elliptical or oblong, with a glossy surface and a slight waxy coating. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries.

This plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
