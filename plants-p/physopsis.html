Physopsis

Physopsis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense stands in its native range.

Physopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
