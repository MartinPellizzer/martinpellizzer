Peperidia peltata

Peperidia peltata, commonly known as the broad-leaf peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically green or reddish and are borne in clusters.

Peperidia peltata is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
