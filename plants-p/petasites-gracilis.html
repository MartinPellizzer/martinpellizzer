Petasites gracilis

Petasites gracilis, commonly known as the slender edelweiss, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly white foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil. Its unique morphology includes a rosette of broad, spoon-shaped leaves that form a protective layer around the central stem.

Petasites gracilis is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.
