Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. sulivongii

Paphiopedilum barbigerum var. sulivongii, commonly known as the Sulivongii moth orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure, featuring a lip that resembles a moth's wing, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its rarity and aesthetic appeal.
