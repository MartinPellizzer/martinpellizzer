Papaver pinnatifidum

Papaver pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, vividly colored flowers that range from red to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple central spot. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance.

Papaver pinnatifidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
