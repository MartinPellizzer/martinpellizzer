Pleioblastus kodzumae

Pleioblastus kodzumae, commonly known as the Shikoku bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and finely serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage is a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Pleioblastus kodzumae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
