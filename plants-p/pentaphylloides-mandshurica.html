Pentaphylloides mandshurica

Pentaphylloides mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian five-leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pentaphylloideae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Korea, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
