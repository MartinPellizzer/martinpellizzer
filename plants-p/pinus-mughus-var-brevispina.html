Pinus mughus var. brevispina

Pinus mughus var. brevispina, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a variant of the Himalayan blue pine.

It is a tall, coniferous evergreen tree native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, particularly in regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan. The tree is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and its distinctive short, stout cones. Its needles are typically blue-green in color, and the bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh mountain climates.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to the local biodiversity.
