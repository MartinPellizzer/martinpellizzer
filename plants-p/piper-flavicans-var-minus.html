Piper flavicans var. minus

Piper flavicans var. minus, commonly known as the lesser yellow pepper, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish to orange-colored berries, which are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinct aromatic scent.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in both gardens and traditional cuisines.
