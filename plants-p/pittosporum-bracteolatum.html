Pittosporum bracteolatum

Pittosporum bracteolatum, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pittosporum bracteolatum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
