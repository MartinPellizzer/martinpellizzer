Plectranthus madagascariensis var. aliciae

Plectranthus madagascariensis var. aliciae, commonly known as the Madagascar betony, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It typically blooms in shades of purple and blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant flowers and fragrant foliage.
