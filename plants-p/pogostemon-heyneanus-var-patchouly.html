Pogostemon heyneanus var. patchouly

Pogostemon heyneanus var. patchouly, commonly known as patchouli, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green, and broad lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves, are widely used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Patchouli thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
