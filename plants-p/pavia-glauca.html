Pavia glauca

Pavia glauca, commonly known as the blue pavia, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pavia glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
