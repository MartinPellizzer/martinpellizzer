Poterium fontqueri

Poterium fontqueri, commonly known as the mountain huckleberry, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
