Plantago polita

Plantago polita, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface that reflects light, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

Its leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
