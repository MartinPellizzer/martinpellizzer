Physalis viscosa var. cinerascens

Physalis viscosa var. cinerascens, commonly known as the woolly groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit ripens. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique fruit.
