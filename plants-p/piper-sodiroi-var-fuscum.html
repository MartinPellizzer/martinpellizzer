Piper sodiroi var. fuscum

Piper sodiroi var. fuscum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

The fruit, which matures to a dark, almost black color, is the primary part used for its medicinal and culinary properties.
