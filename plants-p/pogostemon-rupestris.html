Pogostemon rupestris

Pogostemon rupestris, commonly known as the rock pogostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant features upright, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Pogostemon rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
