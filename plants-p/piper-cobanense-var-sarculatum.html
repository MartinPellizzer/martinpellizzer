Piper cobanense var. sarculatum

Piper cobanense var. sarculatum, commonly known as the Coban pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, green, and often spiny fruits. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and has a distinctive, pungent aroma. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

The fruit is typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its unique flavor profile.
