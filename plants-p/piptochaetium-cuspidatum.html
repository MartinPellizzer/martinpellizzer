Piptochaetium cuspidatum

Piptochaetium cuspidatum, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-green color, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Piptochaetium cuspidatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
