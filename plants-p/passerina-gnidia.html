Passerina gnidia

Passerina gnidia, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Passerina gnidia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
