Potamogeton × zizii f. lucescens

Potamogeton × zizii f. lucescens, commonly known as the silverleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that often exhibit a silvery or bluish sheen.

It is typically found in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a submersed growth habit, with its stems and leaves submerged in water, while its flowers emerge above the water surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a greenish-yellow color, and it produces small, elongated fruits.

This species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
