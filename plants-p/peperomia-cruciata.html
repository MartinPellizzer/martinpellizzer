Peperomia cruciata

Peperomia cruciata, commonly known as the Cross Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or bronze tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Peperomia cruciata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
