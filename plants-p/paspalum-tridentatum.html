Paspalum tridentatum

Paspalum tridentatum, commonly known as the three-toothed grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in various wetland ecosystems.
