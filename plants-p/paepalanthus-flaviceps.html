Paepalanthus flaviceps

Paepalanthus flaviceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed grass.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina. This species belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of many grass species.

It thrives in moist, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.
