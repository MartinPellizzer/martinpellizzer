Plectronia angustiflora

Plectronia angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectronia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, delicate white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting various pollinators like bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
