Potentilla salisburgensis gracilior

Potentilla salisburgensis gracilior, commonly known as the slender Salzburg cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
