Piper semicrudum

Piper semicrudum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Piper semicrudum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
