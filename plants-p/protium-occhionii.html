Protium occhionii

Protium occhionii, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a rough, woody husk. Its botanical features include a smooth, grayish bark and a tendency to grow in dense clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
