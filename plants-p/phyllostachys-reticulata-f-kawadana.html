Phyllostachys reticulata f. kawadana

Phyllostachys reticulata f. kawadana, commonly known as the Kawadana bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green culms that are adorned with a distinctive reticulate pattern. This ornamental bamboo is native to East Asia and is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The plant is known for its hardiness and adaptability, making it a popular choice for bamboo enthusiasts.
