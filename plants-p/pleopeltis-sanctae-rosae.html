Pleopeltis sanctae-rosae

Pleopeltis sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Santa Rosa rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are dark green and covered in fine, silky hairs. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing clusters, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
