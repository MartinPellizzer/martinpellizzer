Poa douglasii subsp. macrantha

Poa douglasii subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the Douglas's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
