Podagrostis aequivalvis

Podagrostis aequivalvis, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly rolled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
