Polystachya capensis

Polystachya capensis, commonly known as the Cape orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces tall, slender stems with whorled leaves and bears clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its unique floral structure includes specialized structures called labellum and column, which are characteristic of orchids.

Polystachya capensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
