Piper pergamentifolium

Piper pergamentifolium, commonly known as the parchment-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are thin and papery in texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are small, three-lobed berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
