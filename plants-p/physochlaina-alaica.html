Physochlaina alaica

Physochlaina alaica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers.
