Potentilla billotii

Potentilla billotii, commonly known as the Billot's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla billotii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
