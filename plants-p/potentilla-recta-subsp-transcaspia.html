Potentilla recta subsp. transcaspia

Potentilla recta subsp.

transcaspia, commonly known as the Transcaspian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky soils and along riverbanks.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, with several oval-shaped leaflets arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
