Piper artanthe var. grandifolium

Piper artanthe var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper artanthe species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and culinary applications.
