Pelargonium alatum

Pelargonium alatum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically lobed or serrated, often with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
