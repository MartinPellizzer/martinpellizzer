Pleioblastus akasiensis

Pleioblastus akasiensis, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream sides. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
