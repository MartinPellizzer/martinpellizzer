Pseuderanthemum laxiflorum

Pseuderanthemum laxiflorum, commonly known as the spreading false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pseuderanthemum laxiflorum is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its cascading form and vibrant blooms.
