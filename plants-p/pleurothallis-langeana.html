Pleurothallis langeana

Pleurothallis langeana, commonly known as the Lange's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette, helping it to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
