Porroglossum hystrix

Porroglossum hystrix, commonly known as the hooked porroglossum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is the spiny, hooked bracts that surround the flower head, giving it its common name.

Porroglossum hystrix is typically found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
