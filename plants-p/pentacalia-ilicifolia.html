Pentacalia ilicifolia

Pentacalia ilicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and drought tolerance.
