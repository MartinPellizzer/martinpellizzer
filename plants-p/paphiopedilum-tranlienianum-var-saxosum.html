Paphiopedilum tranlienianum var. saxosum

Paphiopedilum tranlienianum var. saxosum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure with a pouch-like lip that is typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, nutrient-poor environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, aiding in water retention.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in both greenhouse and outdoor settings.
