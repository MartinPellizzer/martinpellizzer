Potentilla caulescens var. sericea

Potentilla caulescens var. sericea, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
