Polygonatum alternicirrhosum var. piliferum

Polygonatum alternicirrhosum var. piliferum, commonly known as the hairy yellow flag.

This plant is a variant of the yellow flag iris, characterized by its distinctive hairy stems and yellow flowers. It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in moist, swampy environments. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
