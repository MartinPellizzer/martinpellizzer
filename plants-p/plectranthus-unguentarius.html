Plectranthus unguentarius

Plectranthus unguentarius, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Plectranthus unguentarius is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine.
