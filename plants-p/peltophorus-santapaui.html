Peltophorus santapaui

Peltophorus santapaui, commonly known as the Santapau's peacock fern, is a unique tropical plant belonging to the Davalliaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like fronds that resemble the tail of a peacock, hence its common name. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its leaves are often divided into narrow segments, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
