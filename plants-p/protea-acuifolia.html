Protea acuifolia

Protea acuifolia, commonly known as the needle protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like bracts that form a spiky, conical inflorescence, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or cream, with a central yellow or orange style, and it blooms during the summer months.

Protea acuifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
