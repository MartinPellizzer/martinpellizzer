Pelargonium tripartitum

Pelargonium tripartitum, commonly known as the three-lobed geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are deeply lobed into three segments. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens around the world.
