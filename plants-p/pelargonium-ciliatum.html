Pelargonium ciliatum

Pelargonium ciliatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rounded leaves and vibrant pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Pelargonium ciliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
