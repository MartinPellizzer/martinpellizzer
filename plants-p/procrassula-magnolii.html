Procrassula magnolii

Procrassula magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia crassula, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture, and its flowers range from white to pale pink.

Procrassula magnolii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
