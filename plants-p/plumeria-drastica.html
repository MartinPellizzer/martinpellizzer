Plumeria drastica

Plumeria drastica, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive, aromatic scent.

The plant is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
