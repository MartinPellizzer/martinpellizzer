Palaquium bintuluense

Palaquium bintuluense, commonly known as the Bintulu wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble apples in shape. The fruit has a thin, waxy skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp, making it a valuable species in local ecosystems and traditional uses.

It plays an important role in the biodiversity of its habitat and is often found in secondary forests.
