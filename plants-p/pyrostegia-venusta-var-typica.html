Pyrostegia venusta var. typica

Pyrostegia venusta var. typica, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in tropical and subtropical climates.
