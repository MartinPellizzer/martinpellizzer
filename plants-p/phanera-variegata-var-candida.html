Phanera variegata var. candida

Phanera variegata var. candida, commonly known as the white-veined phanera, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins against a deep green background. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
