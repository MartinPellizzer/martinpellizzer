Pseudolophanthus decolorans

Pseudolophanthus decolorans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
