Piper calvescens var. cangrejalense

Piper calvescens var. cangrejalense, commonly known as the Cangrejalense pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, characterized by its climbing or shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a distinct aromatic quality.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
