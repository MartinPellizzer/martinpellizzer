Passiflora kegeliana

Passiflora kegeliana, commonly known as the Kegel's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts, often in shades of red, yellow, and white. The plant is also known for its edible berries, which are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
