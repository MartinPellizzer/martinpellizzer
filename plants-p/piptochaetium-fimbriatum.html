Piptochaetium fimbriatum

Piptochaetium fimbriatum, commonly known as the hairy leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny spikelets.

Piptochaetium fimbriatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
