Pancratium amboinense

Pancratium amboinense, commonly known as the golden arum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow spatulate flowers that are surrounded by a purple bract, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are broad, arrow-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Pancratium amboinense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
