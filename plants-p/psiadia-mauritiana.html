Psiadia mauritiana

Psiadia mauritiana, commonly known as the Mauritius daisy, is a flowering plant native to the Mascarene Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It is often found in dunes and on rocky slopes, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Psiadia mauritiana plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
