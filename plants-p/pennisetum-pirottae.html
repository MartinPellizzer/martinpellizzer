Pennisetum pirottae

Pennisetum pirottae, commonly known as the Pirotta's fountain grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, silver-green plumes. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Pennisetum pirottae is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies due to its showy inflorescences.
