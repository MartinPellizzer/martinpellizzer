Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. flava

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. flava, commonly known as the Yellow-tinged Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight yellowish tinge. The flowers are predominantly white with a subtle yellow hue, often featuring a pale yellow lip that adds to its ornamental appeal. This variety thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

It is prized for its long-lasting blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
