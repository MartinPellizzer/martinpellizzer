Peplis timeroyi

Peplis timeroyi, commonly known as the Timbuktu thornbush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
