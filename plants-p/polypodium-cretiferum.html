Polypodium cretiferum

Polypodium cretiferum, commonly known as the Crete fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or along stream banks. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
