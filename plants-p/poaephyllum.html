Poaephyllum

Poaephyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Poaephyllum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
