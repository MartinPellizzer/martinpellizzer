Pseudohemipilia pilosa

Pseudohemipilia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pseudohemipilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pseudohemipilia pilosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
