Peperomia amphoricarpa

Peperomia amphoricarpa, commonly known as the bottle-shaped peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia amphoricarpa is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental leaves and ease of care.
