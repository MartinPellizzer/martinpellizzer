Pyrus assadii

Pyrus assadii, commonly known as the Assad's pear, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, clustered leaves and white, fragrant flowers. The fruit, which is a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated.

Due to habitat loss and limited natural distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
