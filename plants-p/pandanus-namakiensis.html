Pandanus namakiensis

Pandanus namakiensis, commonly known as the Namaki pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, which are leaf-like bracts. Its roots and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
