Piper amphoricarpum

Piper amphoricarpum, commonly known as the bladderfruit pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into distinctive bladder-like fruits. These fruits are inflated and contain a single seed, which is used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
