Potentilla comarum

Potentilla comarum, commonly known as common cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Potentilla comarum is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
