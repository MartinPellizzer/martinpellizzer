Pleiocarpidia holectomia

Pleiocarpidia holectomia, commonly known as the yellow-barked tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-brown bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique reproductive system involving multiple carpels.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
