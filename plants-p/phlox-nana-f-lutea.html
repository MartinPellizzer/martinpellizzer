Phlox nana f. lutea

Phlox nana f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow garden phlox, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features low-growing, spreading stems with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. The foliage is typically dark green and slightly hairy, adding texture to the plant's appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
