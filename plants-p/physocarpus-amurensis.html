Physocarpus amurensis

Physocarpus amurensis, commonly known as Amur beautybush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, panicle-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features narrow, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in the fall.

Physocarpus amurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
