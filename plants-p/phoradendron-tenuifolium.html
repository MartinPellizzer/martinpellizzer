Phoradendron tenuifolium

Phoradendron tenuifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded into the host's bark. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing food for various animals and contributing to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
