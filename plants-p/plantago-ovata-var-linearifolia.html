Plantago ovata var. linearifolia

Plantago ovata var. linearifolia, commonly known as slender plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features slender, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, with a hairy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Plantaginaceae family and shares similar medicinal and culinary uses.
