Piper boehmeriifolium

Piper boehmeriifolium, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant produces small, black peppercorns that are used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and local agriculture.
