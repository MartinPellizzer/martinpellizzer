Populus deltoides

Populus deltoides, commonly known as the eastern cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, triangular leaves and its ability to thrive in moist environments. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It has a fast growth rate and is often found near rivers and wetlands.

Botanically, it is part of the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars.
