Pneumonanthe dahurica

Pneumonanthe dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its blooming period occurs in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.
