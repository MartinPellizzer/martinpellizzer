Phyteuma ovale

Phyteuma ovale, commonly known as the common evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyteuma ovale is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
