Polypodium struthionis

Polypodium struthionis, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
