Passiflora glauca

Passiflora glauca, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Passiflora glauca is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
