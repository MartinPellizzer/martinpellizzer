Persicaria minor var. kawagoeana

Persicaria minor var. kawagoeana, commonly known as Kawagoe's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens.
