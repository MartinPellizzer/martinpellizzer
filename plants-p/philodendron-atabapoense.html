Philodendron atabapoense

Philodendron atabapoense, commonly known as the Atabapo philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Amazon Basin in South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
