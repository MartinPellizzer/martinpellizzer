Philadelphus scoparius var. linifolius

Philadelphus scoparius var. linifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow and elongated, giving the plant its varietal name, "linifolius," which translates to "linen-leaved.". It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is often used for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
