Polygala longicaulis var. major

Polygala longicaulis var. major, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive structure that resembles a spurred petal.
