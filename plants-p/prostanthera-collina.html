Prostanthera collina

Prostanthera collina, commonly known as narrow-leaved mintbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Prostanthera collina is also valued for its fragrant foliage and is used in traditional bush medicine.
