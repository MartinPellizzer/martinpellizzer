Pluchea paniculata var. muralis

Pluchea paniculata var. muralis, commonly known as the wall chaffflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and rocky areas, often found along walls or in urban environments. Its small, yellow flowers are followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
