Pternandra pachyphylla

Pternandra pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved pterandra, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.

Pternandra pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
