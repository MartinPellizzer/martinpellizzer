Pyrrocoma lyallii

Pyrrocoma lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's pygmy tea, is a small shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
