Prunus mira

Prunus mira, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn.

Prunus mira is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Japanese culture.
