Pterostylis macrosceles

Pterostylis macrosceles, commonly known as the large-lobed helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest floors and is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped flower. The plant has a single, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower with a greenish-yellow hue. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This orchid is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
