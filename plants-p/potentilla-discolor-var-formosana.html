Potentilla discolor var. formosana

Potentilla discolor var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, which helps in identifying the species.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
