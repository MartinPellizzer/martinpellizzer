Passiflora perlobata

Passiflora perlobata, commonly known as the silky passion flower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a vibrant mix of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a soft, silky texture.

Passiflora perlobata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
