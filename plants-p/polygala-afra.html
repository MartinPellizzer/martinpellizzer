Polygala afra

Polygala afra, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, round seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Polygala afra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
