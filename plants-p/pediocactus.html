Pediocactus

Pediocactus, commonly known as the "paddle cactus," is a genus of cacti native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

These plants are characterized by their flattened, paddle-shaped stems that store water, allowing them to survive in arid environments. Pediocactus species typically have spiny or woolly surfaces to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. They produce vibrant flowers, often in shades of yellow, red, or orange, during the spring and summer months.

Due to their unique morphology and ecological adaptations, they are popular among cactus enthusiasts and are often cultivated in desert gardens.
