Pelargonium × pulchrum

Pelargonium × pulchrum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including red, pink, purple, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with distinct zonation patterns, which give the plant its common name. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × pulchrum thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
