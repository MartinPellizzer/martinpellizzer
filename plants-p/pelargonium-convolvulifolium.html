Pelargonium convolvulifolium

Pelargonium convolvulifolium, commonly known as the convolvulifolium geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
