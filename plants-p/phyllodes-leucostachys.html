Phyllodes leucostachys

Phyllodes leucostachys, commonly known as the white-stemmed wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, flattened phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Phyllodes leucostachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
