Pleurothallis aurea var. asperilinguis

Pleurothallis aurea var. asperilinguis, commonly known as the waxy orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its unique appearance and growth habit make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The variety asperilinguis is distinguished by its rough, waxy texture on the leaves and stems.
