Pachypodium ramosum

Pachypodium ramosum, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick trunk with a crown of spiny, branching stems. The plant has small, spiny leaves that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Its flowers are yellowish-green and appear in clusters, often followed by small, red fruits.

Pachypodium ramosum is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
