Psidium coriaceum

Psidium coriaceum, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It produces fragrant, white flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and aromatic pulp. The leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a whitish, velvety pubescence. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for cultivation in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and ecological systems.
