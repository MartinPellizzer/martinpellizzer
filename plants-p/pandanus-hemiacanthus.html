Pandanus hemiacanthus

Pandanus hemiacanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in saline soils.

Pandanus hemiacanthus is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in many island communities.
