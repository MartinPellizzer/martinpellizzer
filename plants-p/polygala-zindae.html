Polygala zindae

Polygala zindae, commonly known as the Zindae milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Polygala zindae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
