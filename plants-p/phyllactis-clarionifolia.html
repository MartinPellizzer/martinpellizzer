Phyllactis clarionifolia

Phyllactis clarionifolia, commonly known as the Clarion leaf bladder, is a unique plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant belongs to the family Phyllactis, which is known for its specialized leaf structures. Its leaves are typically green and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Phyllactis clarionifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a subject of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology.
