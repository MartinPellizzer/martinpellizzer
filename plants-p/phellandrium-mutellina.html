Phellandrium mutellina

Phellandrium mutellina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honey mint, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
