Peschiera myriantha

Peschiera myriantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Peschiera myriantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
