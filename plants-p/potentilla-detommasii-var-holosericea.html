Potentilla detommasii var. holosericea

Potentilla detommasii var. holosericea, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which results from a thick layer of fine, white hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch of color to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a desirable species for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
