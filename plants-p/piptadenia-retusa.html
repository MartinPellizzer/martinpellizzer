Piptadenia retusa

Piptadenia retusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia retusa*, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
