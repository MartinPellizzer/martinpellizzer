Pilosella mayeri

Pilosella mayeri, commonly known as the Mayer's goat's beard, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy, yellow to white flower heads that resemble a goat's beard. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. Pilosella mayeri thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
