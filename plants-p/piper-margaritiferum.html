Piper margaritiferum

Piper margaritiferum, commonly known as the bead vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bead-like fruits that hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green flowers that develop into the distinctive bead-shaped berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The botanical features of Piper margaritiferum make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and botanical collections.
