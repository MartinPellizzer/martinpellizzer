Pseudanthus brunonis

Pseudanthus brunonis, commonly known as the New Zealand sunflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

The leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
