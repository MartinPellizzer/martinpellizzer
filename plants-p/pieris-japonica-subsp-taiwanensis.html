Pieris japonica subsp. taiwanensis

Pieris japonica subsp.

taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan rhododendron, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Taiwan. It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green with a glossy surface.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers in shades of pink, white, or red during late winter to early spring. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, humid climates.
