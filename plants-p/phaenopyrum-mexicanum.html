Phaenopyrum mexicanum

Phaenopyrum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice in tropical and subtropical landscapes.
