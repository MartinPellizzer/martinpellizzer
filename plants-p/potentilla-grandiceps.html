Potentilla grandiceps

Potentilla grandiceps, commonly known as the Sierra potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Potentilla grandiceps is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in full sun.
