Picea abies var. argentea

Picea abies var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the European spruce.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the needles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in coniferous forests and prefers cool, moist climates.

Its cones are cylindrical and contain small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
