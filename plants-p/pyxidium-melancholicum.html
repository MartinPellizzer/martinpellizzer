Pyxidium melancholicum

Pyxidium melancholicum, commonly known as the blackroot or melancholy root, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features a robust, tuberous root system that stores nutrients and moisture, enabling it to survive in harsh, high-altitude environments. The plant has dark, almost black, roots that are often harvested for their medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

Pyxidium melancholicum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
