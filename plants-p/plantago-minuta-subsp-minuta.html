Plantago minuta subsp. minuta

Plantago minuta subsp.

minuta, commonly known as the small plantain, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
