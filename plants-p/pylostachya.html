Pylostachya

Pylostachya, commonly known as the beardgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their slender, often hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. They are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Pylostachya species are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
