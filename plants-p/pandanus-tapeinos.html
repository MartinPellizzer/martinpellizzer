Pandanus tapeinos

Pandanus tapeinos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in spikes, which are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots and leaves are sometimes utilized in local cuisines and medicinal practices.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
