Poa levitskyi

Poa levitskyi, commonly known as the Levitsky's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Poa levitskyi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
