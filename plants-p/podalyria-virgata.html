Podalyria virgata

Podalyria virgata, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flower heads. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer.

Podalyria virgata is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
