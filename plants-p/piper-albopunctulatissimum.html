Piper albopunctulatissimum

Piper albopunctulatissimum, commonly known as the white-spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with distinct white spots. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
