Polygala rupestris var. juniperina

Polygala rupestris var. juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
