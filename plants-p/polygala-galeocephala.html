Polygala galeocephala

Polygala galeocephala, commonly known as the hooded milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hooded flowers that resemble a small bird's head. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
