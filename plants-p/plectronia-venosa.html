Plectronia venosa

Plectronia venosa, commonly known as the veined plectronia, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that exhibit a complex pattern of green and silver hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Plectronia venosa is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
