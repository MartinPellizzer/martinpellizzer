Phyllanthus indo-myanmarensis

Phyllanthus indo-myanmarensis, commonly known as the Burmese phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Myanmar and India. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is often grown for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
