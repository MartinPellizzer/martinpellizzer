Paspalum schultesii

Paspalum schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Paspalum schultesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
