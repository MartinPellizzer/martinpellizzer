Piper geniculatum var. coriaceum

Piper geniculatum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical areas for horticultural purposes.
