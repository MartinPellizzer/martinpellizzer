Piper nigropunctatum

Piper nigropunctatum, commonly known as black-spotted pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves with prominent black or purple spots, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
