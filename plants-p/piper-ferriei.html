Piper ferriei

Piper ferriei, commonly known as the golden pepper or ferrie's pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, yellowish fruits.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and for its mild peppery flavor.
