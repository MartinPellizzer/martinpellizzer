Prunus kaengkrachanensis

Prunus kaengkrachanensis, commonly known as the Thai cherry or Kaeng Krachan cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Thailand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red berries.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional Thai medicine.
