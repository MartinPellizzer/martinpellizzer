Phlomoides deserticola

Phlomoides deserticola, commonly known as the desert phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, tubular flowers that are often violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and resilience make it a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
