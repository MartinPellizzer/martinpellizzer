Ptilotus tetrandrus

Ptilotus tetrandrus, commonly known as the "blue mistflower," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, blue-violet bracts that resemble misty clouds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by these colorful, leaf-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Ptilotus tetrandrus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique visual display.
