Polygala albicans var. caracaensis

Polygala albicans var. caracaensis, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along forest edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators.
