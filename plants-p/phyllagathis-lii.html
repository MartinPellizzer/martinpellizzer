Phyllagathis lii

Phyllagathis lii, commonly known as the Malayan whiteleaf mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. It thrives in brackish water environments and is often found in mangrove forests.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
