Pseudopinanga maculata

Pseudopinanga maculata, commonly known as the spotted dwarf palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring a short trunk and a dense crown of narrow, leathery leaves. The leaves are usually dark green with distinctive white or yellow spots, which give the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of primary forests.
