Psoralea esculenta

Psoralea esculenta, commonly known as the broadleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and have been used in traditional diets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Psoralea esculenta is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
