Pinguicula grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Pinguicula grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, boggy environments and requires high humidity and acidic soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular species among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
