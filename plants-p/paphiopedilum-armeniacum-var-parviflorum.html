Paphiopedilum armeniacum var. parviflorum

Paphiopedilum armeniacum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the Armenian lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
