Plectronia velutina

Plectronia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual or perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-yellow, and are arranged in clusters.

Plectronia velutina is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
