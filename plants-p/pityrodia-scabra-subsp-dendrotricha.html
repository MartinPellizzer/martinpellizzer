Pityrodia scabra subsp. dendrotricha

Pityrodia scabra subsp.

dendrotricha, commonly known as the roughleaf lilly pilly, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its greyish-green, rough-textured leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
