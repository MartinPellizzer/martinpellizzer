Pogostemon hispidus

Pogostemon hispidus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pogostemon hispidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
