Piper orosianum

Piper orosianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the sacred pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the biodiversity in its native habitat.
