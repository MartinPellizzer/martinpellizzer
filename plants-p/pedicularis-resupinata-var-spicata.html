Pedicularis resupinata var. spicata

Pedicularis resupinata var. spicata, commonly known as the spicate monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces elongated, racemose inflorescences, with flowers that are often held upright on slender pedicels. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
