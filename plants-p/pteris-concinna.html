Pteris concinna

Pteris concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Pteris concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
