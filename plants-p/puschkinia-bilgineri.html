Puschkinia bilgineri

Puschkinia bilgineri, commonly known as the Turkish squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Puschkinia bilgineri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in mild climates.
