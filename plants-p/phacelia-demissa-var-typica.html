Phacelia demissa var. typica

Phacelia demissa var. typica, commonly known as the slender phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
