Potentilla caulescens var. subglabra

Potentilla caulescens var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that are covered with a smooth, glabrous surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
