Persicaria excurrens

Persicaria excurrens, commonly known as creeping smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless and have a slightly rough texture. Its small, pink to red flowers appear in dense spikes during the summer months.

Persicaria excurrens is known for its aggressive spreading habit through rhizomes, making it both a common weed and a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
