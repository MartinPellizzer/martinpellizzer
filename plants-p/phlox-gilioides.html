Phlox gilioides

Phlox gilioides, commonly known as the gilroy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, seed-like fruits.

Phlox gilioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
