Polygonum durum

Polygonum durum, commonly known as hardy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

Polygonum durum is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
