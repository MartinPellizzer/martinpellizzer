Psittacanthus bolivarensis

Psittacanthus bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian arrowhead, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
