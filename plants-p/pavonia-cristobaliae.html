Pavonia cristobaliae

Pavonia cristobaliae, commonly known as the golden pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to its habitat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia cristobaliae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
