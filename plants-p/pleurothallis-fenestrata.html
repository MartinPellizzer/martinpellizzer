Pleurothallis fenestrata

Pleurothallis fenestrata, commonly known as the window orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive window-like pattern on its petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
