Pseudotrimezia pusilla

Pseudotrimezia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf trimezia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are tiny, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale green in color.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
