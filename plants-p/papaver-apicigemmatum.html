Papaver apicigemmatum

Papaver apicigemmatum, commonly known as the Sierra Valley poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a short stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Papaver apicigemmatum is an annual herb that blooms in late spring to early summer, contributing to the region's biodiversity and ecological importance.
