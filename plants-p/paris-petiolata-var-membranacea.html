Paris petiolata var. membranacea

Paris petiolata var. membranacea, commonly known as the membranous-leaf Paris, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a dense, umbel-like cluster, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are narrow, membranous, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
