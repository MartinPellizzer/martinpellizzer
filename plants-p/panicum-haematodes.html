Panicum haematodes

Panicum haematodes, commonly known as crimson panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, reddish-brown inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum haematodes is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
