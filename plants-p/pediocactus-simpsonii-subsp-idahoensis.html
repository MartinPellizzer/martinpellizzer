Pediocactus simpsonii subsp. idahoensis

Pediocactus simpsonii subsp.

idahoensis, commonly known as the Idaho prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny, globular stems and is adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is a popular plant among cactus enthusiasts.
