Phyteuma tenerum f. pilosum

Phyteuma tenerum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Native to southern Europe, this perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from early summer to late autumn.
