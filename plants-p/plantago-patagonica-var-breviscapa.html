Plantago patagonica var. breviscapa

Plantago patagonica var. breviscapa, commonly known as the Patagonian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
