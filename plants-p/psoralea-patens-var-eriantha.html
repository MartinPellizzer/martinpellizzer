Psoralea patens var. eriantha

Psoralea patens var. eriantha, commonly known as the silky psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by silky, hairy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
