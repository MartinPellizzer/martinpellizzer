Physaria carinata

Physaria carinata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features distinctive, spiny, leaf-like structures called phyllaries that surround its seed pods. Its showy, orange to red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Physaria carinata is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
