Primula flavescens

Primula flavescens, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a serrated edge.

Primula flavescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
