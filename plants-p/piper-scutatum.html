Piper scutatum

Piper scutatum, commonly known as the shieldleaf pepper, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when ripe.

Piper scutatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
