Primula auriculata subsp. pycnorhiza

Primula auriculata subsp.

pycnorhiza, commonly known as the auriculate primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
