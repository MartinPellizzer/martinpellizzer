Pallasia intermedia

Pallasia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate pallasia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers bloom in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

Pallasia intermedia is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland plants.
