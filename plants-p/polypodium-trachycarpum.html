Polypodium trachycarpum

Polypodium trachycarpum, commonly known as the cliff fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds. The plant typically grows in rocky or shaded environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a rugged texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polypodium trachycarpum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to different soil types.
