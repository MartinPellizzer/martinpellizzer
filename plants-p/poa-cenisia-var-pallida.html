Poa cenisia var. pallida

Poa cenisia var. pallida, commonly known as the pale meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, pale green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale to whitish flowers.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in conservation areas and is valued for its role in maintaining biodiversity.
