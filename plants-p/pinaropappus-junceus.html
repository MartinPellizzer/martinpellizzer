Pinaropappus junceus

Pinaropappus junceus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water. Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional crafts and textiles.

Pinaropappus junceus plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators in its native range.
