Passerina hirsuta proles polygalifolia

Passerina hirsuta proles polygalifolia, commonly known as the hairy pasqueflower, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a basal rosette of hairy, ovate leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. Its stems are upright and bear few leaves, with the majority of the foliage concentrated at the base.

This species is often found in rocky soils and alpine regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
