Primula croftii var. scaposa

Primula croftii var. scaposa, commonly known as the Scaposa primula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically oval and slightly hairy, with a serrated edge. This variety is a member of the Primulaceae family, known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its delicate, early spring blooms.
