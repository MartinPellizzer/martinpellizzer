Phylica aemula

Phylica aemula, commonly known as the silver tree, is a unique evergreen shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems.

Phylica aemula is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
