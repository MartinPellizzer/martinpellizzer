Papaver calcareum

Papaver calcareum, commonly known as the limestone poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow stamens, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, calcareous soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Papaver calcareum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and limestone outcrops.
