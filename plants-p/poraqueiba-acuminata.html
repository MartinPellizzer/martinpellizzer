Poraqueiba acuminata

Poraqueiba acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a yellowish pulp. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly the resin extracted from its bark, which has anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
