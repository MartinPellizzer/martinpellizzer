Pelargonium × ludovicae

Pelargonium × ludovicae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often bicolor flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, adding ornamental value to the landscape.
