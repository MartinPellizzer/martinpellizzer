Peltophorum adnatum

Peltophorum adnatum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
