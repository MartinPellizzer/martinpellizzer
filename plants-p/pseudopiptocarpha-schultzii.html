Pseudopiptocarpha schultzii

Pseudopiptocarpha schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's false milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.
