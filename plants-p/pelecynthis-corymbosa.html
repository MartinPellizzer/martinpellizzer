Pelecynthis corymbosa

Pelecynthis corymbosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, oval fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
