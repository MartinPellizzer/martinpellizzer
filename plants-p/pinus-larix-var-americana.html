Pinus larix var. americana

Pinus larix var. americana, commonly known as the American larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles that turn golden yellow before falling off in the autumn. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and grayish when young, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
