Pitcairnia secunda

Pitcairnia secunda, commonly known as the silver leaf pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of southern Brazil, Paraguay, and northern Argentina. The plant is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage with a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its humid habitat. It produces inflorescences of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

Pitcairnia secunda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
