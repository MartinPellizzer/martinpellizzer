Pilosella macrostolona subsp. cernuiformis

Pilosella macrostolona subsp.

cernuiformis, commonly known as the woolly cat's-paw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming habit, characterized by its hairy, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its subspecies cernuiformis is distinguished by its drooping, elongated flower heads.

The species is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
