Peruviopuntia pachypus

Peruviopuntia pachypus, commonly known as the Peruvian prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its leaves are modified into spiny structures that provide protection from herbivores.

The plant's adaptability to harsh conditions makes it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
