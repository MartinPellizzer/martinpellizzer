Persea laevigata

Persea laevigata, commonly known as the Mexican avocado or sometimes the Guatemalan avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The tree's fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry that contains a single, large seed. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
