Plocama tinctoria

Plocama tinctoria, commonly known as the redroot or redroot aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant has a distinctive reddish-brown root system, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Plocama tinctoria is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
