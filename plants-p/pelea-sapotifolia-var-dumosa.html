Pelea sapotifolia var. dumosa

Pelea sapotifolia var. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf sapotilla, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible berries that are similar in appearance to those of the common sapotilla. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local cuisine.
