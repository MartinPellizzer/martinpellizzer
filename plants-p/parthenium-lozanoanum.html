Parthenium lozanoanum

Parthenium lozanoanum, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Parthenium lozanoanum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
