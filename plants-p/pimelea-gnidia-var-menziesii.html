Pimelea gnidia var. menziesii

Pimelea gnidia var. menziesii, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble bottlebrushes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodland or heathland environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
