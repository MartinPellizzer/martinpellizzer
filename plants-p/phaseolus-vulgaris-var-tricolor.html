Phaseolus vulgaris var. tricolor

Phaseolus vulgaris var. tricolor, commonly known as the three-color bean, is a striking variety of common bean characterized by its tri-colored foliage and pods.

This plant is native to the Americas and belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet displaying a different color, typically green, white, and purple. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant appearance.

The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which can be used in cooking similar to other bean varieties.
