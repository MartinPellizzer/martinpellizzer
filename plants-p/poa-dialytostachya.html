Poa dialytostachya

Poa dialytostachya, commonly known as the narrowleaf poa, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense tussocks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, Poa dialytostachya is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
