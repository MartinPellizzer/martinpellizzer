Petrorhagia peroninii

Petrorhagia peroninii, commonly known as the Peron's rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are small, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Petrorhagia peroninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
