Passiflora cuatrecasasii

Passiflora cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Passiflora cuatrecasasii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique botanical features.
