Polygala fernandesiana

Polygala fernandesiana, commonly known as the Cape Verde polygala, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. It is often found in coastal areas and is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
