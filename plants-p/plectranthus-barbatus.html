Plectranthus barbatus

Plectranthus barbatus, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and have a strong, mint-like aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for culinary and ornamental purposes.
