Poa acicularifolia

Poa acicularifolia, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
