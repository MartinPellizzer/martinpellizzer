Pyrus borosiana

Pyrus borosiana, commonly known as the Armenian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn.

Pyrus borosiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
