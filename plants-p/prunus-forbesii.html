Prunus forbesii

Prunus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Prunus forbesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
