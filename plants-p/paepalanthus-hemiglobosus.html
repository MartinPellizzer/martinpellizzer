Paepalanthus hemiglobosus

Paepalanthus hemiglobosus, commonly known as the globular cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a compact, rounded shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Paepalanthus hemiglobosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
