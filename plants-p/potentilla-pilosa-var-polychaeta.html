Potentilla pilosa var. polychaeta

Potentilla pilosa var. polychaeta, commonly known as the softleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive soft, fuzzy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
