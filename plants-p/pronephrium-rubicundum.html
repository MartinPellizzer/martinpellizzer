Pronephrium rubicundum

Pronephrium rubicundum, commonly known as the red kidney bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

Its structure supports both ecological and agricultural significance in its native habitat.
