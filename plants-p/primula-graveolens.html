Primula graveolens

Primula graveolens, commonly known as the stinking viora, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and is known for its strong, sometimes unpleasant, fragrance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique scent, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
