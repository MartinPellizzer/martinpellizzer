Phyllanthus nobilis var. antillanus

Phyllanthus nobilis var. antillanus, commonly known as the Antillean sandmat, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and produces tiny yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or dry habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it suitable for coastal landscaping.
