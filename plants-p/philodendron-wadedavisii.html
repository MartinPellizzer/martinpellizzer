Philodendron wadedavisii

Philodendron wadedavisii, commonly known as the Wadewasivii philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
