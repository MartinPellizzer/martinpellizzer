Pluchea obovata

Pluchea obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate sunflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branched stems and hairy foliage. Its leaves are broadly ovate, often with toothed margins, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Pluchea obovata is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
