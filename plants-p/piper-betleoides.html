Piper betleoides

Piper betleoides, commonly known as the Indian betel leaf plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional practices. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are aromatic and contain compounds such as eugenol and methyl eugenol.

Due to its medicinal and cultural significance, it is cultivated in various regions for both commercial and ritual purposes.
