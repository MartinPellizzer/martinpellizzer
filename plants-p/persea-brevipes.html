Persea brevipes

Persea brevipes, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to red berries that ripen in the fall. The fruit is edible when fully mature and has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
