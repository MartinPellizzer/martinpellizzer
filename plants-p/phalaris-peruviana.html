Phalaris peruviana

Phalaris peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian grass or Peruvian needlegrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Phalaris peruviana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.
