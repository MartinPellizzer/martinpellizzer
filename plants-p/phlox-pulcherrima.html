Phlox pulcherrima

Phlox pulcherrima, commonly known as the tropical phlox, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Phlox pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
