Pseudolobivia hamatacantha

Pseudolobivia hamatacantha, commonly known as the spiny lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and can withstand extreme temperature fluctuations. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
