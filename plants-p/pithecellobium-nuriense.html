Pithecellobium nuriense

Pithecellobium nuriense, commonly known as the Nuriense evergreen acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, evergreen foliage and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade plant in tropical landscapes.
