Potamogeton spirillus

Potamogeton spirillus, commonly known as the spiral pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, spiral-shaped stems that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, green to reddish-brown flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often spiraling around the stem.

Potamogeton spirillus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
