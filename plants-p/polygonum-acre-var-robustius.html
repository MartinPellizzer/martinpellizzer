Polygonum acre var. robustius

Polygonum acre var. robustius, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a robust annual herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base. The plant produces small, greenish to reddish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in agricultural fields.
