Piper labillardierei

Piper labillardierei, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper or Labillardiere's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and New Guinea. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, and it is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
