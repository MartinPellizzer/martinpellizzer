Pyrus × hippomenes

Pyrus × hippomenes, commonly known as the Persian white apple, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (common apple) and Pyrus amygdaliformis (Persian ironwood).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 4–8 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are small, white, and borne in clusters. The fruit is a small, yellow or green apple with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
