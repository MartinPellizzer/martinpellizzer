Panicum hippothrix

Panicum hippothrix, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with numerous small, spikelet-like flowers.

Panicum hippothrix is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
