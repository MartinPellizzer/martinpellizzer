Premna quadridentata

Premna quadridentata, commonly known as the four-toothed premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with four teeth on the margin. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody fruits. It is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing effects.
