Procopiania insularis

Procopiania insularis, commonly known as the island pomegranate, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean.

It features broad, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant bears red, fleshy fruits resembling pomegranates, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and island environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
