Psidium pyriferum

Psidium pyriferum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
