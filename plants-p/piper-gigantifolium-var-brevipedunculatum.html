Piper gigantifolium var. brevipedunculatum

Piper gigantifolium var. brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the giant-leaf pepper, is a variant of the giant pepper plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elongated, narrow berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high rainfall and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
