Polygala oppositifolia

Polygala oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters in height and thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
