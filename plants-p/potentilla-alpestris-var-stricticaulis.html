Potentilla alpestris var. stricticaulis

Potentilla alpestris var. stricticaulis, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It features narrow, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in rocky or gravelly gardens.

This variety is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
