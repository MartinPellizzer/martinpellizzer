Populus nigra

Populus nigra, commonly known as the black poplar, is a species of poplar native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It is a tall, deciduous tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a heart-shaped base, with a rough, hairy texture on the underside. Its bark is dark, fissured, and becomes flaky with age, while its flowers are small, yellowish-green catkins that appear in early spring.

The tree is often found near water sources and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
