Phacelia bicolor var. leibergii

Phacelia bicolor var. leibergii, commonly known as the Leiberg's blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped outline.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
