Piper latispicum

Piper latispicum, commonly known as the broad-spined pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent, waxy glands along the veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique aromatic properties.
