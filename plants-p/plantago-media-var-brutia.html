Plantago media var. brutia

Plantago media var. brutia, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
