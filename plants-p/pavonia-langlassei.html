Pavonia langlassei

Pavonia langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse's penta, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
