Passerina tinctoria

Passerina tinctoria, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Passerina tinctoria is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native gardens.
