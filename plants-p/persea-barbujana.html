Persea barbujana

Persea barbujana, commonly known as the Barbujan avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, yellowish skin and a creamy, oily flesh. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
