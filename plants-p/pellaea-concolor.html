Pellaea concolor

Pellaea concolor, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive silvery sheen. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, clustered leaves.

Pellaea concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
