Paphiopedilum × deleonii

Paphiopedilum × deleonii, commonly known as the De Leon's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, which resembles a pouch or a slipper. The plant typically features dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and longevity in floral arrangements.
