Polyosma mucronata

Polyosma mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.

Polyosma mucronata is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
