Paepalanthus albiceps

Paepalanthus albiceps, commonly known as the white-headed puya, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that collect rainwater. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
