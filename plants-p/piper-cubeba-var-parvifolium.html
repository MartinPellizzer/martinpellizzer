Piper cubeba var. parvifolium

Piper cubeba var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved long pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, aromatic fruits that resemble peppercorns. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and has alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, and the fruit develops in a drupe-like structure.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its pungent, spicy flavor.
