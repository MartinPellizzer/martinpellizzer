Phaseolus vulgaris var. subochraceus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. subochraceus, commonly known as the golden snake bean, is a variety of common bean native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden pod color, which becomes more intense as the pods mature. The plant typically grows as an upright annual vine, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and small white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are usually small and oblong, with a smooth texture and a range of colors from yellow to brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional cuisines.
