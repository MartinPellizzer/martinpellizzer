Prunus rubella

Prunus rubella, commonly known as the red cherry plum, is a small deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, red fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus rubella is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
