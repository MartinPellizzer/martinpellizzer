Plumbago maximowiczii

Plumbago maximowiczii, commonly known as silverleaf plumbago, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects light and gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
