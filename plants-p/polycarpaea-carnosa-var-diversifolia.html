Polycarpaea carnosa var. diversifolia

Polycarpaea carnosa var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated velvetleaf, is a variant of the species Polycarpaea carnosa, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on the upper leaf surfaces. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist woodland or forest understories.

The varietal name *diversifolia* refers to the diverse leaf patterns, which can vary in color and distribution.
