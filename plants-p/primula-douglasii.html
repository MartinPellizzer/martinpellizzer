Primula douglasii

Primula douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a dark green color.

Primula douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
