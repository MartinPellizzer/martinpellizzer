Peperomia samoensis

Peperomia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Samoa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, which are borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
