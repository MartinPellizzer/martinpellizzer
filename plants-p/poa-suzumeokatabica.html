Poa suzumeokatabica

Poa suzumeokatabica, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa suzumeokatabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
