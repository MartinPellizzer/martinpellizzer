Poa speluncarum

Poa speluncarum, commonly known as the cave bentgrass, is a rare and specialized grass species that thrives in dark, humid environments such as caves and rock crevices.

It is adapted to low light conditions and has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which is a common reproductive strategy among grasses. Its root system is dense and fibrous, allowing it to anchor firmly in nutrient-poor substrates.

Poa speluncarum is considered an indicator species for subterranean ecosystems due to its unique ecological niche.
