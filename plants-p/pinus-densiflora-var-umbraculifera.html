Pinus densiflora var. umbraculifera

Pinus densiflora var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically shorter and more clustered compared to the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is native to southern Japan and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
