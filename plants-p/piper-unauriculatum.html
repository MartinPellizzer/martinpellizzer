Piper unauriculatum

Piper unauriculatum, commonly known as the golden piper or the yellow pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or golden flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
