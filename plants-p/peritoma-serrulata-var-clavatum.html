Peritoma serrulata var. clavatum

Peritoma serrulata var. clavatum, commonly known as the Clavate Peritoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sunny environments, often found in coastal regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The variety clavatum is characterized by its unique, club-shaped seed pods, which are a notable botanical feature.
