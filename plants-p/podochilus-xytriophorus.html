Podochilus xytriophorus

Podochilus xytriophorus, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple veins on its leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
