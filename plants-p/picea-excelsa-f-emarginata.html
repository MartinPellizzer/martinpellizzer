Picea excelsa f. emarginata

Picea excelsa f. emarginata, commonly known as the Mongolian fir, is a distinctive variant of the Siberian fir.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical shape and slender, spreading branches. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Mongolia and northern China.

It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to harsh climatic conditions.
