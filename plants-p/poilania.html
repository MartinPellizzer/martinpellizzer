Poilania

Poilania, commonly known as the "scented orchid," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, and it produces small, clustered blooms that emit a sweet aroma.

Poilania is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
