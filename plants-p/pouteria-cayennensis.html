Pouteria cayennensis

Pouteria cayennensis, commonly known as the Cayenne sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, and creamy fruits. Its fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a soft, yellowish pulp that is rich in nutrients.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
