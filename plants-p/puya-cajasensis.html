Puya cajasensis

Puya cajasensis, commonly known as the Cajas puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its large, rosette-forming leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in cloud forests and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
