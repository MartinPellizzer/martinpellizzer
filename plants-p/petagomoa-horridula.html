Petagomoa horridula

Petagomoa horridula, commonly known as the hairy pteridium, is a fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

Petagomoa horridula plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to forest floor decomposition.
