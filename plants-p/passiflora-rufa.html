Passiflora rufa

Passiflora rufa, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces striking, complex flowers with a unique structure featuring a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its fruit, a red, fleshy berry, is edible and contains seeds.

Passiflora rufa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
