Panicum mapalense

Panicum mapalense, commonly known as the Mapalense grass, is a species native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

The botanical features of Panicum mapalense make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
