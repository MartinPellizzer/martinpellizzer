Pulvinaria stenoloba

Pulvinaria stenoloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf woolly aphid, is a species of aphid belonging to the family Aphididae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fluffy waxy coating that covers its body, providing protection against predators and environmental stressors. This plant pest is often found on the stems and leaves of various host plants, including roses and other ornamental shrubs. The wax secretions of Pulvinaria stenoloba can cause sooty mold growth, which may negatively impact the health and appearance of the host plant.

Its life cycle includes both sexual and asexual reproduction, allowing for rapid population growth under favorable conditions.
