Pyrus integerrima

Pyrus integerrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its rugged, thick-textured leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically not cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
