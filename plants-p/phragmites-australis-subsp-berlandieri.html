Phragmites australis subsp. berlandieri

Phragmites australis subsp.

berlandieri, commonly known as the western common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and dense, feathery plumes that produce a large amount of pollen.

This subspecies is native to North America and is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and other moist habitats. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and extensive rhizome system make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
