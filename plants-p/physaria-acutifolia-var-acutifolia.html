Physaria acutifolia var. acutifolia

Physaria acutifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear, often hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh, drought-prone habitats.
