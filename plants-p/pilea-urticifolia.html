Pilea urticifolia

Pilea urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle pilea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its stinging hairs, it requires careful handling to avoid irritation.
