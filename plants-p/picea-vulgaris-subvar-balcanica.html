Picea vulgaris subvar. balcanica

Picea vulgaris subvar. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan spruce, is a subspecies of the European spruce native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The bark is thin and reddish-brown, often flaking in small, irregular scales. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species.

This subspecies is adapted to mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.
