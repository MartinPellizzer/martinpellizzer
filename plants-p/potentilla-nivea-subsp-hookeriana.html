Potentilla nivea subsp. hookeriana

Potentilla nivea subsp.

hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers, which typically bloom in early summer.

The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky slopes.
