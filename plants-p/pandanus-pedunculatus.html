Pandanus pedunculatus

Pandanus pedunculatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, consisting of long, cylindrical bracts with small, clustered flowers. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts and construction.
