Pseudoraphis Squarrosa

Pseudoraphis squarrosa is a striking succulent known for its unique, spiky appearance and vibrant coloration. Native to arid regions of Africa, this plant thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal water. Its rosette-shaped leaves form a compact, bushy structure that resembles a cactus but is actually a member of the grass family. The plant produces delicate, feathery flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or orange during the growing season. Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, Pseudoraphis squarrosa has become a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
