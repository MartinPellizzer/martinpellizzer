Perdicesca

Perdicesca, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a rare and valuable tree native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bark and heart-shaped leaves, which are adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing a single seed. Due to overharvesting, it is listed as critically endangered on the IUCN Red List.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a subject of both ecological and cultural interest.
