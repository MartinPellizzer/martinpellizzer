Panicum villosissimum var. scoparioides

Panicum villosissimum var. scoparioides, commonly known as the silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its drought tolerance and ecological benefits.
