Phacelia cicutaria var. hubbyi

Phacelia cicutaria var. hubbyi, commonly known as the Hubby's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
