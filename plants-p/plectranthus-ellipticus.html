Plectranthus ellipticus

Plectranthus ellipticus, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it both aesthetically pleasing and easy to maintain.
