Pseudobromus tenuifolius

Pseudobromus tenuifolius, commonly known as the slender leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland and coastal habitats. The plant has slender, narrow leaves that are usually less than 2 mm wide, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, spike-like seed heads.

Pseudobromus tenuifolius is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.
