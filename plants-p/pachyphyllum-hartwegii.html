Pachyphyllum hartwegii

Pachyphyllum hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
