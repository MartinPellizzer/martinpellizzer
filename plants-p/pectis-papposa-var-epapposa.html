Pectis papposa var. epapposa

Pectis papposa var. epapposa, commonly known as the woolly papposa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are surrounded by pale green to white bracts. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is notable for its distinctive, fluffy pappus that aids in seed dispersal.
