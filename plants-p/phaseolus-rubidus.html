Phaseolus rubidus

Phaseolus rubidus, commonly known as the red runner bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its beans, which are used in various culinary traditions.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
