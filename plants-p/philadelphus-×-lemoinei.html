Philadelphus × lemoinei

Philadelphus × lemoinei, commonly known as the mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 6 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus × lemoinei is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
