Poa calamagrostoidea

Poa calamagrostoidea, commonly known as the Chilean meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Chile.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa calamagrostoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
