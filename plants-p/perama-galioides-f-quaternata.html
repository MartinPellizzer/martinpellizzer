Perama galioides f. quaternata

Perama galioides f. quaternata, commonly known as the four-leaved sorrel, is a variant of the sorrel plant characterized by its unique four-leaf clover-like foliage.

It belongs to the Oxalidaceae family and is native to certain regions of Australia. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that often form a cluster of four, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.
