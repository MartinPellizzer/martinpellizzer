Piper zeylanicum

Piper zeylanicum, commonly known as Indian pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries that grow in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for its pungent flavor.

The botanical features of Piper zeylanicum make it distinct from other pepper species, such as black pepper.
