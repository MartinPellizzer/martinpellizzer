Perezia gayana

Perezia gayana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, delicate blooms that are often clustered together.

Perezia gayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
