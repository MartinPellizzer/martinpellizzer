Piper viae-marginis

Piper viae-marginis, commonly known as the margin-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that often have a distinct margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
