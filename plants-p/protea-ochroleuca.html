Protea ochroleuca

Protea ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to yellowish flower heads surrounded by a cluster of bracts. The plant is native to the Cape Province and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to their nectar-rich blooms.
