Pyrus ringo var. kaido

Pyrus ringo var. kaido, commonly known as the Kaido apple, is a cultivated variety of the apple tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits with a distinctive red and yellow skin. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The Kaido apple is valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.
