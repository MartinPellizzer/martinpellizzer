Pseudagrostistachys africana subsp. humbertii

Pseudagrostistachys africana subsp.

humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grassland and savanna habitats, often in areas with well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a slight sheen. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
