Plantago asiatica var. yezoensis

Plantago asiatica var. yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo plantain, is a variant of the common plantain that is native to Japan.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
