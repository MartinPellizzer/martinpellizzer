Piper rasile

Piper rasile, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
