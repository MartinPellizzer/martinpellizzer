Pelargonium optimum

Pelargonium optimum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium optimum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
