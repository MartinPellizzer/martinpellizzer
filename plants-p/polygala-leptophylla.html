Polygala leptophylla

Polygala leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf milkwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
