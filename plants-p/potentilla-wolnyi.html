Potentilla wolnyi

Potentilla wolnyi, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Potentilla wolnyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and beauty.
