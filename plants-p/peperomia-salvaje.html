Peperomia salvaje

Peperomia salvaje, commonly known as the wild peperomia, is a herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often glossy and come in various shades of green, sometimes with reddish or purple tones. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish, clustered in spikes.

Peperomia salvaje is well adapted to shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
