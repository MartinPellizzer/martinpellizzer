Panicum lanceolatum

Panicum lanceolatum, commonly known as eastern dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive, drooping appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in hue, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
