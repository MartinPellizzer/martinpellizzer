Passiflora suberosa var. longipes

Passiflora suberosa var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruits are edible berries that turn from green to orange-red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
