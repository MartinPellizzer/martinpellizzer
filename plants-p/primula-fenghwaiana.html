Primula fenghwaiana

Primula fenghwaiana, commonly known as the Fenghwa primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in various shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
