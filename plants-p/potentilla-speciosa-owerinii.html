Potentilla speciosa owerinii

Potentilla speciosa owerinii, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
