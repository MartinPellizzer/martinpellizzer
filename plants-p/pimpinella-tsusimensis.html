Pimpinella tsusimensis

Pimpinella tsusimensis, commonly known as Japanese cow-weed, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and waste grounds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
