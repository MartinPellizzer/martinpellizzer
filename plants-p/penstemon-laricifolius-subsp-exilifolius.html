Penstemon laricifolius subsp. exilifolius

Penstemon laricifolius subsp.

exilifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in dry, sunny environments. It produces seed capsules that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
