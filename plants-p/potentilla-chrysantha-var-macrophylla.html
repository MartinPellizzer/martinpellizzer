Potentilla chrysantha var. macrophylla

Potentilla chrysantha var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are broad and serrated, providing a lush green backdrop to the bright flowers.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
