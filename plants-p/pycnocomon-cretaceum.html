Pycnocomon cretaceum

Pycnocomon cretaceum, commonly known as the Cretaceous spore, is a fossilized plant structure that provides insight into ancient flora.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical spore masses, which are typically found in Cretaceous sedimentary rocks. These spores are often preserved in fine-grained sediments, indicating their aquatic or wetland origin. The morphology of Pycnocomon cretaceum suggests it was produced by early land plants, possibly ferns or mosses.

Its study helps scientists understand the evolution of plant reproductive strategies during the Mesozoic era.
