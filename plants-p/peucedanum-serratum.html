Peucedanum serratum

Peucedanum serratum, commonly known as the serrated angelica, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of theApiaceae family. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Peucedanum serratum is valued for its potential health benefits and is used in herbal medicine for its antispasmodic and digestive properties.
