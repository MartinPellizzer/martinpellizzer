Pterocassia goldmanii

Pterocassia goldmanii, commonly known as the golden pterocassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into several lobes and often have a striking golden or yellow hue. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is not commonly found in cultivation outside of its native range.
