Pleiocarpidia lanaensis

Pleiocarpidia lanaensis, commonly known as the Lanaean pleiocarp, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that exhibit a complex venation pattern. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fruiting bodies that are distinctive for their multi-seeded capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
