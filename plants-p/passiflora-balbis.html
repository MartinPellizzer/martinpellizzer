Passiflora balbis

Passiflora balbis, commonly known as the Balbis passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent.

Passiflora balbis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
