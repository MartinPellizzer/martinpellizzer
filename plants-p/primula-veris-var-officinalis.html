Primula veris var. officinalis

Primula veris var. officinalis, commonly known as the lesser primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow with five petals. The plant produces a distinctive scent and blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and partial shade.
