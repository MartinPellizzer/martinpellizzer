Persea sassafras

Persea sassafras, commonly known as the sassafras tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries. Its leaves and fruits contain essential oils, contributing to its use in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
