Passerina campanulata

Passerina campanulata, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Passerina, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Passerina campanulata plays a role in supporting local pollinators with its nectar-rich flowers.
