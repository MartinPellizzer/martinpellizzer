Piper napo-pastazanum

Piper napo-pastazanum, commonly known as the Pastaza pepper, is a species of flowering vine native to the Amazon rainforest in South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy, aromatic compound similar to capsaicin. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and agricultural researchers.
