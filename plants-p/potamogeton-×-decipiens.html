Potamogeton × decipiens

Potamogeton × decipiens, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton przewalskii.

It is an aquatic plant characterized by its submerged leaves that are often arranged in whorls and have a distinctive ribbed structure. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically found in spikes, and it thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton × decipiens plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
