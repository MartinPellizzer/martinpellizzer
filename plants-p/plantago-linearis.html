Plantago linearis

Plantago linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It is highly adaptable, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
