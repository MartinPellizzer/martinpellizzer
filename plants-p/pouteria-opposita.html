Pouteria opposita

Pouteria opposita, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces oval, woody fruits that contain one or two large, edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, fiber, and essential minerals like selenium.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
