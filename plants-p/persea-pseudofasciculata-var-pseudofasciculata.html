Persea pseudofasciculata var. pseudofasciculata

Persea pseudofasciculata var. pseudofasciculata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in traditional culinary preparations.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
