Phalaris caroliniana

Phalaris caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads. It is often found in disturbed or flooded soils and can tolerate a range of moisture conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
