Pseudosamanea carbonaria

Pseudosamanea carbonaria, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are bipinnate, composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
