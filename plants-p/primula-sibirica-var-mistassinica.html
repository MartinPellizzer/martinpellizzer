Primula sibirica var. mistassinica

Primula sibirica var. mistassinica, commonly known as the Mistassinica Primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
