Pelargonium retinervium

Pelargonium retinervium, commonly known as the featherleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Pelargonium retinervium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
