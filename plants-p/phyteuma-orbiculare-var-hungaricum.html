Phyteuma orbiculare var. hungaricum

Phyteuma orbiculare var. hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian globe flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with a red or purple throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.
