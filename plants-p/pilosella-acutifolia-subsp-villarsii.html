Pilosella acutifolia subsp. villarsii

Pilosella acutifolia subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaves and compact growth habit. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes in mountainous areas.
