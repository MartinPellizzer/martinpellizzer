Phebalium ralstonii

Phebalium ralstonii, commonly known as the Ralston's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale yellow to cream flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phebalium ralstonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and conservation efforts.
