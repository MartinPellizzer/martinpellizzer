Pentacalia vaccinioides var. microdentata

Pentacalia vaccinioides var. microdentata, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf pentacalia, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size.

The variety microdentata is characterized by its finely toothed leaf margins, adding to its ornamental value.
