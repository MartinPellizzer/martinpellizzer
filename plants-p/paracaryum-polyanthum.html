Paracaryum polyanthum

Paracaryum polyanthum, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its attractive, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically deep green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Paracaryum polyanthum is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
