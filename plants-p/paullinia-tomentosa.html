Paullinia tomentosa

Paullinia tomentosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
