Pennisetum lanuginosum var. majus

Pennisetum lanuginosum var. majus, commonly known as the woolly finger grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It features tall, slender stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
