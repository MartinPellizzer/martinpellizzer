Pulmonaria azurea subsp. vulgaris

Pulmonaria azurea subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive rosette of velvety, spotted leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to purple.

The plant thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
