Pothos paucinervius

Pothos paucinervius, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a distinctive silvery-green coloration on the underside. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can climb using aerial roots that attach to surfaces. Its leaves are typically 5 to 10 centimeters in length and have a few prominent veins.

It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
