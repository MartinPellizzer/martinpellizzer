Prunus damascena var. hispanica

Prunus damascena var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish cherry or Spanish laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall, and it produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers in spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual interest and fragrance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental planting.
