Polyspora speciosa

Polyspora speciosa, commonly known as the showy polypore, is a striking fungal species belonging to the family Polyporaceae.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped or cushion-like basidiocarps that often display a vibrant coloration, ranging from yellow to orange or red. The fruiting bodies are typically non-stipitate, with a spongy or porous surface that facilitates spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, decaying wood, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

This species plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.
