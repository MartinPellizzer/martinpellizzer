Parodia concinna subsp. blaauwiana

Parodia concinna subsp.

blaauwiana, commonly known as the Blaauw's pincushion cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa. It features a dense covering of white to pale green spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
