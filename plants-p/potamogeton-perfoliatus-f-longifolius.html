Potamogeton perfoliatus f. longifolius

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often longer than they are wide.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a submersed growth habit, with its leaves often emerging from the water surface in a floating or semi-aquatic manner. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The subspecies longifolius is distinguished by its notably longer leaves compared to the typical form of Potamogeton perfoliatus.
