Penstemon angustifolius subsp. caudatus

Penstemon angustifolius subsp.

caudatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
