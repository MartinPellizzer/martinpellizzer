Plectranthus splendens

Plectranthus splendens, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to lavender, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Plectranthus splendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
