Poa angustifolia subsp. brizoides

Poa angustifolia subsp.

brizoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

The plant has slender, cylindrical leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant is known for its fine, dense growth habit.

It is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
