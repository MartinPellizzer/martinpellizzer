Potentilla fastigiata var. permollis

Potentilla fastigiata var. permollis, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a compact, shrubby plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its foliage. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
