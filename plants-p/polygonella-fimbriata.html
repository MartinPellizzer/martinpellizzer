Polygonella fimbriata

Polygonella fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed moss-rush, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Polygonella fimbriata is commonly found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms and small invertebrates.
