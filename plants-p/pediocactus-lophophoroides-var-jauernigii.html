Pediocactus lophophoroides var. jauernigii

Pediocactus lophophoroides var. jauernigii, commonly known as the Jauernig's prickly pear, is a compact cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It features a short, ribbed stem with prominent, spiny areoles, and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is adapted to extreme desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.

Its unique varietal characteristics make it a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.
