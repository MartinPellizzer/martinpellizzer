Pseudozygocactus epiphylloides subsp. bradei

Pseudozygocactus epiphylloides subsp.

bradei, commonly known as the Brade's false jointcactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of short, white spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
