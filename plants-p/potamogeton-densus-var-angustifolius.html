Potamogeton densus var. angustifolius

Potamogeton densus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
