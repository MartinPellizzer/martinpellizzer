Phyllanthus micrantheoides

Phyllanthus micrantheoides, commonly known as the small-fruited phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus micrantheoides is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
