Pilea bracteosa var. striolata

Pilea bracteosa var. striolata, commonly known as the Shiny Pilea, is a compact, evergreen herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with a striking light green to dark green coloration, often adorned with subtle white or silver veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or white, which appear during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it an excellent addition to tabletop displays or hanging baskets.
