Paullinia fistulosa

Paullinia fistulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Sapindaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemistry research.
