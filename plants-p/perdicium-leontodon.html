Perdicium leontodon

Perdicium leontodon, commonly known as the lion's tooth, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its common name derives from the shape of its leaves, which resemble the sharp teeth of a lion.

Perdicium leontodon is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
