Prunus × caproniana

Prunus × caproniana, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus serrula and Prunus avium.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark purple drupes that ripen in late summer.

Prunus × caproniana is native to the Mediterranean region and is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
