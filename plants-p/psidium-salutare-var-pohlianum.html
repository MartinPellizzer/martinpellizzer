Psidium salutare var. pohlianum

Psidium salutare var. pohlianum, commonly known as the Pohlian's guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a slightly pubescent underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries that range in color from yellow to red. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
