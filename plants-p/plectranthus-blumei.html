Plectranthus blumei

Plectranthus blumei, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in dense, showy clusters. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Plectranthus blumei is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
