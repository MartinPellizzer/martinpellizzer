Pouteria macrantha

Pouteria macrantha, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces a large, spiny fruit that is highly prized for its edible, sweet, and aromatic flesh. The fruit contains a single large seed and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
