Pisonia sylvestris

Pisonia sylvestris, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red, coral-like flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, round, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

Pisonia sylvestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
