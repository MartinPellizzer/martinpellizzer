Passiflora tessmannii

Passiflora tessmannii, commonly known as the Tessmann's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, yellow, and white hues, along with intricate filaments and a prominent crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Passiflora tessmannii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
