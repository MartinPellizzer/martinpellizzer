Persicaria hispida

Persicaria hispida, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its fibrous root system makes it a hardy and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
