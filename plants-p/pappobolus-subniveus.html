Pappobolus subniveus

Pappobolus subniveus, commonly known as the snowball grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, giving it its common name. The plant has fibrous roots and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its spread. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
