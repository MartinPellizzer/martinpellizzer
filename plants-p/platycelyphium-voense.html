Platycelyphium voense

Platycelyphium voense, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in wetland habitats and can thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a potential invasive species in some regions.
