Phyllanthus juglandifolius subsp. juglandifolius

Phyllanthus juglandifolius subsp.

juglandifolius, commonly known as the Indian chestnut or wild chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often glossy leaves that resemble those of a chestnut tree.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
