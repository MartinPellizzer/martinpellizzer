Podalyria burchellii var. albida

Podalyria burchellii var. albida, commonly known as the white-flowered rock heath.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy white blooms. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and hairy, providing a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
