Periploca chevalieri

Periploca chevalieri, commonly known as the Canary Island periploca, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
