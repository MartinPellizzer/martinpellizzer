Piptochaetium featherstonei

Piptochaetium featherstonei, commonly known as the featherstone grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, delicate foliage and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Piptochaetium featherstonei is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
