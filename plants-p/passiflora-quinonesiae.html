Passiflora quinonesiae

Passiflora quinonesiae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It features striking, five-petaled white and violet flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora quinonesiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
