Phoebe cathia

Phoebe cathia, commonly known as the silverleaf phoebe, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phoebe cathia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
