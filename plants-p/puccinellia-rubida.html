Puccinellia rubida

Puccinellia rubida, commonly known as redstem needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple at the base, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy texture.

Puccinellia rubida is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
