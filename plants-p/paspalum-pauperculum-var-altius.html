Paspalum pauperculum var. altius

Paspalum pauperculum var. altius, commonly known as the tall paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy ornamental and forage plant.
