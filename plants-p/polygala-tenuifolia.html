Polygala tenuifolia

Polygala tenuifolia, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
