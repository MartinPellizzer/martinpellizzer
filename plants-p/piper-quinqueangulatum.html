Piper quinqueangulatum

Piper quinqueangulatum, commonly known as the five-angled pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
