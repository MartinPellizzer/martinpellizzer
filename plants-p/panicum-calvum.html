Panicum calvum

Panicum calvum, commonly known as smooth panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often hairless. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Panicum calvum is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to warm, dry climates.
