Plectoma inflata

Plectoma inflata, commonly known as the inflated plectoma, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, textured surface.

The flowers are small and clustered, often in whorls, and can be brightly colored, attracting various pollinators.
