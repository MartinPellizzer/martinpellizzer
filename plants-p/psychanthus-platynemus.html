Psychanthus platynemus

Psychanthus platynemus, commonly known as the flatstem psychanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
