Peltophorum roxburghii

Peltophorum roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian peacock tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its distinctive feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy, pendulous inflorescences that resemble peacock tails. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine.
