Primula obtusifolia var. roylei

Primula obtusifolia var. roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has broad, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
