Paspalum pluriracemosum

Paspalum pluriracemosum, commonly known as the many-branched grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spreading blades that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
