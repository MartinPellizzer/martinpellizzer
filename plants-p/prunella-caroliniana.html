Prunella caroliniana

Prunella caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often hairy.

The flowering period occurs in early spring, making it a notable early bloom in forest ecosystems.
