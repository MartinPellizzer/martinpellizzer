Pinanga fractiflexa

Pinanga fractiflexa, commonly known as the flexible bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves that give it a graceful appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga fractiflexa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
