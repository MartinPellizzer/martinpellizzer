Pedicularis amoena var. argutiserrata

Pedicularis amoena var. argutiserrata, commonly known as the serrated beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive, elongated lower lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its specific epithet "argutiserrata.".

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
