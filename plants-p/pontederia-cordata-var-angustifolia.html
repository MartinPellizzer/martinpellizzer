Pontederia cordata var. angustifolia

Pontederia cordata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, with a waxy coating that helps repel water. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes above the water. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an effective indicator of nutrient-rich aquatic environments.
