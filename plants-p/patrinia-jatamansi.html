Patrinia jatamansi

Patrinia jatamansi, commonly known as Indian valerian, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also valued in traditional Ayurvedic and Tibetan medicine.
