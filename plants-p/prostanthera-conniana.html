Prostanthera conniana

Prostanthera conniana, commonly known as the broad-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes.

Prostanthera conniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrant foliage and nectar-rich blooms.
