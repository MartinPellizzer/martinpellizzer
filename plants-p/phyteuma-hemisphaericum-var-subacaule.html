Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. subacaule

Phyteuma hemisphaericum var. subacaule, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often slightly hairy, and it produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
