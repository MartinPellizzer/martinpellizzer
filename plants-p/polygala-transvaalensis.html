Polygala transvaalensis

Polygala transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish and are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods.

It is often found in open woodlands and on mountain slopes.
