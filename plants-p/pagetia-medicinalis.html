Pagetia medicinalis

Pagetia medicinalis, commonly known as the medicinal pagetia, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It features erect, slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
