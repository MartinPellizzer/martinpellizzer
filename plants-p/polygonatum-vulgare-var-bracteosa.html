Polygonatum vulgare var. bracteosa

Polygonatum vulgare var. bracteosa, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are typically eaten by birds.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
