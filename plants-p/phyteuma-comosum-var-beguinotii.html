Phyteuma comosum var. beguinotii

Phyteuma comosum var. beguinotii, commonly known as the Beguin's goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its showy inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
