Polychilos gigantea

Polychilos gigantea, commonly known as the giant polychilos, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that can reach several feet in height. Its leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a water-holding structure.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
