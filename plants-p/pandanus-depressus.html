Pandanus depressus

Pandanus depressus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to various parts of the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii, Tahiti, and the Cook Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in many island communities.
