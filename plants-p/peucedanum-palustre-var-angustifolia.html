Peucedanum palustre var. angustifolia

Peucedanum palustre var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved hogweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tint. The leaves are lance-shaped and narrow, with a distinctive pungent odor when crushed. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typically white or greenish-white in color.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
