Prostanthera cryptandroides

Prostanthera cryptandroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue or purple and are arranged in dense spikes.

Prostanthera cryptandroides is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.
