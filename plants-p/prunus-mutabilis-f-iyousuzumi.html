Prunus mutabilis f. iyousuzumi

Prunus mutabilis f. iyousuzumi, commonly known as the Iyousuzumi cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese flowering cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to white flowers that change color as they bloom, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the fruit is a small, dark purple drupe.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
