Peperomia castilloi

Peperomia castilloi, commonly known as the Castillo's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant features oval, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Peperomia castilloi prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
