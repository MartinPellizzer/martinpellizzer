Phaseolus obovatus

Phaseolus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil types.

Phaseolus obovatus is also studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
