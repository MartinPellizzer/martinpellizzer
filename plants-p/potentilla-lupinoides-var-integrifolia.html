Potentilla lupinoides var. integrifolia

Potentilla lupinoides var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, which distinguishes it from other varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
