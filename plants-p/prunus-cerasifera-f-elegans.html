Prunus cerasifera f. elegans

Prunus cerasifera f. elegans, commonly known as the velvet chokecherry, is a compact, ornamental form of the common chokecherry.

It features dark purple, velvety leaves that provide striking visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. It is typically grown as a shrub or small tree, valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

This cultivar is relatively low maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
