Pedilanthus cordatus

Pedilanthus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf poinsettia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and emit a mild fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pedilanthus cordatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular during the holiday season.
