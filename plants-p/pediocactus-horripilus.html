Pediocactus horripilus

Pediocactus horripilus, commonly known as the bristly barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, bristly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing, long-lived cactus that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations to arid environments, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and conservation efforts.
