Passiflora vernicosa

Passiflora vernicosa, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, waxy coating that gives it a silky appearance.

Passiflora vernicosa thrives in humid, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
