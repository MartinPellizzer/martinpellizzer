Pyrus pompejana

Pyrus pompejana, commonly known as the Pompeian pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the volcanic soils of Pompeii.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a rough, russet-colored skin. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The flowers are white and clustered, producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.
