Potentilla maculata var. pyrenaica

Potentilla maculata var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often in clusters of five, giving it its common name. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
