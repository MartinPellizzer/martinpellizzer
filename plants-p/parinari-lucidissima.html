Parinari lucidissima

Parinari lucidissima, commonly known as the shining parinari, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, yellow or orange fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.

The plant's rapid growth makes it suitable for reforestation efforts in degraded tropical areas.
