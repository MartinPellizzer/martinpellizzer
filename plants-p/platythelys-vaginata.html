Platythelys vaginata

Platythelys vaginata, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
