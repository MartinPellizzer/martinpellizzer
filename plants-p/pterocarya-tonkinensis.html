Pterocarya tonkinensis

Pterocarya tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin hickory, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate arrangement. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, followed by edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
