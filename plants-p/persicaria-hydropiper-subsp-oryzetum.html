Persicaria hydropiper subsp. oryzetum

Persicaria hydropiper subsp.

oryzetum, commonly known as water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats. It features upright stems covered with fine, stinging hairs that can cause irritation upon contact.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender spikes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.
