Phaseolus pulchellus

Phaseolus pulchellus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and long, edible pods. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The seeds within the pods are often used in culinary applications, adding a sweet and nutty flavor to dishes.
