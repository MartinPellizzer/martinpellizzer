Passiflora involucrata

Passiflora involucrata, commonly known as the purple crown passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and bright red filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora involucrata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and fragrance.
