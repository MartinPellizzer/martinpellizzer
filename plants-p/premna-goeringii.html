Premna goeringii

Premna goeringii, commonly known as Chinese nettle or blue mist vine, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rapid growth and dense foliage make it an effective ground cover but also a potential invasive species in some regions.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
