Podocarpus buchii

Podocarpus buchii, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, which is a typical feature of gymnosperms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Podocarpus buchii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its compact growth habit.
