Phyllorkis umbellata

Phyllorkis umbellata, commonly known as the broadleaf umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Phyllorkis umbellata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
