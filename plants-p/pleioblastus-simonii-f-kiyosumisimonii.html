Pleioblastus simonii f. kiyosumisimonii

Pleioblastus simonii f. kiyosumisimonii, commonly known as the Kiyosumi bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, gracefully arching stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in late spring, followed by tiny, triangular seeds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant.

This variety is cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.
