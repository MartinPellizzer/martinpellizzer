Paspalum richardii

Paspalum richardii, commonly known as Richard's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and grassy slopes.

Paspalum richardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
