Pancratium illyricum

Pancratium illyricum, commonly known as the Balkan torch lily, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a distinctive, elongated seed capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Pancratium illyricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
