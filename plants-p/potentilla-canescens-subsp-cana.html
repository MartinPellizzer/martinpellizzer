Potentilla canescens subsp. cana

Potentilla canescens subsp.

cana, commonly known as the grey cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging environments.
