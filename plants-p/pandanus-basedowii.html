Pandanus basedowii

Pandanus basedowii, commonly known as the New Guinea pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that contain numerous small, white flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

Pandanus basedowii thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
