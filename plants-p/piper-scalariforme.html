Piper scalariforme

Piper scalariforme, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white, and it produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper scalariforme make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
