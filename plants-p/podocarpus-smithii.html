Podocarpus smithii

Podocarpus smithii, commonly known as the silverwood tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Podocarpus smithii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
