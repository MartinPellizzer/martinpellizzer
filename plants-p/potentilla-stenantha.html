Potentilla stenantha

Potentilla stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. It produces small, oval fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

Potentilla stenantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
