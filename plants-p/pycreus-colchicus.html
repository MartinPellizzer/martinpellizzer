Pycreus colchicus

Pycreus colchicus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces a tall, slender stem topped with a cluster of tubular flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Pycreus colchicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
