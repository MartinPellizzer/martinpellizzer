Pilosella montiberica

Pilosella montiberica, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features dense, woolly gray-green foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Pilosella montiberica is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
