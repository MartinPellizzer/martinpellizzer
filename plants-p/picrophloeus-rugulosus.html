Picrophloeus rugulosus

Picrophloeus rugulosus, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
