Passerina uniflora var. purpurea

Passerina uniflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators in its native environment.
