Prunus serrulata var. sieboldii

Prunus serrulata var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or Yamazakura, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular floral display. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

This variety is closely related to the more commonly known Prunus serrulata, but it has distinct floral and foliage characteristics.
