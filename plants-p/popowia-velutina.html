Popowia velutina

Popowia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in a soft, velvety texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that contain a single seed.

Popowia velutina is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
