Plectranthus tatei

Plectranthus tatei, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and emit a strong, minty scent when crushed.

Plectranthus tatei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
