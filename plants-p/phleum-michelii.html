Phleum michelii

Phleum michelii, commonly known as Michel's oat, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, two-rowed flowers.

Phleum michelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
