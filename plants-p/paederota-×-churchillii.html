Paederota × churchillii

Paederota × churchillii, commonly known as the Churchill's pearly everlasting, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
