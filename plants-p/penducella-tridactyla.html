Penducella tridactyla

Penducella tridactyla, commonly known as the three-fingered lily, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, three-lobed leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in its wetland environment by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
