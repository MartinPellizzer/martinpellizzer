Potamogeton pusillus subsp. groenlandicus

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

groenlandicus, commonly known as the Greenland pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, temperate regions. It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

The plant has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are usually smooth and hairless. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to low light conditions and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
