Petroselinum peregrinum

Petroselinum peregrinum, commonly known as wild parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in culinary applications. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by small, round fruits.
