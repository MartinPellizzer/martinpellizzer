Perovskia artemisioides

Perovskia artemisioides, commonly known as Russian sage, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features aromatic, silver-green foliage and tall spikes of blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its essential oils contribute to its strong, minty fragrance, which is also used in aromatherapy.

Botanically, it belongs to the Lamiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like lavender and mint.
