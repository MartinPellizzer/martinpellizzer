Phalacraea callitricha

Phalacraea callitricha, commonly known as the African orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a compact, rosette-like growth habit, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
