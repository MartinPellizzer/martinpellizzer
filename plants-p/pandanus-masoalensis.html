Pandanus masoalensis

Pandanus masoalensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in local crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in humid, sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in a spadix, which can be used to produce a sweet, aromatic syrup.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its ornamental and practical uses.
