Peperomia pavasiana

Peperomia pavasiana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its drought tolerance.

It is also prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
