Polygonum samarense

Polygonum samarense, commonly known as the Samare knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sumbawa in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum samarense is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
