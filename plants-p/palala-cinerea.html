Palala cinerea

Palala cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf palala, is a rare and distinctive plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Palala cinerea is considered a species of conservation concern.
