Phyllanthus cicca

Phyllanthus cicca, commonly known as the Indian false brome, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Phyllanthus cicca is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
