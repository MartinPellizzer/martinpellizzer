Pollinia filifolia

Pollinia filifolia, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and distinctive floral structure. The plant produces pollinia, which are compact masses of pollen that are adapted for specific pollination mechanisms. Its botanical features include slender, lanceolate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

This orchid is often found in moist, shaded environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
