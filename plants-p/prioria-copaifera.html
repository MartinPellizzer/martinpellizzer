Prioria copaifera

Prioria copaifera, commonly known as Copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dense canopy of elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It is valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife in its native habitats.
