Potamogeton crispus var. phialiensis

Potamogeton crispus var. phialiensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a wavy or curled margin, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often emerging from the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in ponds and slow-moving streams.
