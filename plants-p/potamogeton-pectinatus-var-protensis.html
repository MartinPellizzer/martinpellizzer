Potamogeton pectinatus var. protensis

Potamogeton pectinatus var. protensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
