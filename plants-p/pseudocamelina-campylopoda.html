Pseudocamelina campylopoda

Pseudocamelina campylopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed camelina, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.

The species is of interest for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its oilseed properties.
