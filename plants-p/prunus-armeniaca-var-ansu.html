Prunus armeniaca var. ansu

Prunus armeniaca var. ansu, commonly known as the apricot or Chinese apricot, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It produces sweet, yellow or orange fruits with a fuzzy skin and a single seed inside. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing before the leaves in early spring.

The fruit is widely cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.
