Polystichum montevidense f. imbricata

Polystichum montevidense f. imbricata, commonly known as the imbricata fern, is a distinctive fern species characterized by its tightly overlapping, imbricated fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to South America, particularly Uruguay and southern Brazil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are lanceolate and have a dark green, leathery texture with a serrated margin.
