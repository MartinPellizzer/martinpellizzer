Pinus mugo

Pinus mugo, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a small evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with short, twisted branches and dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and slightly curved. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Pinus mugo is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous and alpine ecosystems.
