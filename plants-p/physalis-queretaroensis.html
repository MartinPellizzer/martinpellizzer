Physalis queretaroensis

Physalis queretaroensis, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inflated calyces that enclose its orange-yellow berries, which are edible when ripe. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
