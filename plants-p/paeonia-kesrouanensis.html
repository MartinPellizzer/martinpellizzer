Paeonia kesrouanensis

Paeonia kesrouanensis, commonly known as the Kesrouan peony, is a rare and endemic species native to Lebanon.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces distinctive, fragrant blooms in late spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Paeonia kesrouanensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
