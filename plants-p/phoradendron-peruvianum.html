Phoradendron peruvianum

Phoradendron peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the Amazon rainforest.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis using the limited chlorophyll it possesses. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the fruits, allowing the plant to spread to new hosts.

Phoradendron peruvianum plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, influencing host tree health and providing food for various wildlife species.
