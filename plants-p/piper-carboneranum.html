Piper carboneranum

Piper carboneranum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces tiny, berry-like fruits that mature to a dark purple color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include a woody stem, glandular hairs, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
