Pyrethrum decipiens

Pyrethrum decipiens, commonly known as the false pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Pyrethrum decipiens is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
