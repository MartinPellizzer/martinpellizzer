Pandanus stelliger

Pandanus stelliger, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of its stems. Its root system is fibrous and well adapted to the sandy soils of coastal areas.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various species.
