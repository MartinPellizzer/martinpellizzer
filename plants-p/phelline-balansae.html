Phelline balansae

Phelline balansae, commonly known as the Balansae phelline, is a flowering plant species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are often narrow and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
