Pasania dealbata

Pasania dealbata, commonly known as the white-flowered pasania, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Pasania dealbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
