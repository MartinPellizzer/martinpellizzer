Potamogeton × bennettii

Potamogeton × bennettii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it thrives in shallow, freshwater environments. Potamogeton × bennettii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.
