Pavonia insignis

Pavonia insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to Brazil.

It is a shrub that produces vibrant, showy red or orange bracts that resemble flowers, although they are actually modified leaves. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features include simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous true flowers that bloom beneath the colorful bracts.
