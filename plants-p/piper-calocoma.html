Piper calocoma

Piper calocoma, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage and small, spiky fruits that mature to a bright red color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Piper calocoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
