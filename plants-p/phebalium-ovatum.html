Phebalium ovatum

Phebalium ovatum, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are oval in shape and covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture and landscaping.
