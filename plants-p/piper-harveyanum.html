Piper harveyanum

Piper harveyanum, commonly known as Harvey's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
