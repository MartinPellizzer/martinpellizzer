Picotia nitida

Picotia nitida, commonly known as the silverleaf picotia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
