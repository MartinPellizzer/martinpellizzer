Poa grandiflora

Poa grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Poa grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
