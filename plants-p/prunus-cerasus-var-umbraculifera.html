Prunus cerasus var. umbraculifera

Prunus cerasus var. umbraculifera, commonly known as the Umbra cherry, is a variety of cherry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive dark purple to black fruit, which is typically smaller and more intensely flavored than standard cherries. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
