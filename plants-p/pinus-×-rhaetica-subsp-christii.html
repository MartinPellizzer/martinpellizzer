Pinus × rhaetica subsp. christii

Pinus × rhaetica subsp.

christii, commonly known as the Rhaetian pine subspecies Christi, is a hybrid conifer native to the Alps. It exhibits morphological characteristics intermediate between its parent species, Pinus cembra and Pinus mugo.

The plant is characterized by its dense, slow-growing foliage and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its cones are typically small and serotinous, releasing seeds in response to fire or mechanical disturbance.

This subspecies plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of alpine forests.
