Potentilla × zapalowiczi

Potentilla × zapalowiczi, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are compound, consisting of 5 to 7 oval-shaped leaflets, and are arranged alternately along the stems.

Potentilla × zapalowiczi is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
