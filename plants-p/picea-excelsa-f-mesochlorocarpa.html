Picea excelsa f. mesochlorocarpa

Picea excelsa f. mesochlorocarpa, commonly known as the Himalayan white fir, is a botanical variant of the blue spruce.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and silver-blue needles that are shorter than those of the typical species. The plant exhibits a unique chlorophyll distribution in its needles, giving it a distinct pale green to white appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in the Himalayas and other mountainous regions.

This form is adapted to cold climates and is relatively slow-growing, making it a valuable ornamental and ecological species.
