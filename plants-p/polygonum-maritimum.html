Polygonum maritimum

Polygonum maritimum, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a perennial shrub native to coastal regions of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has woody stems that are often covered in a layer of resin, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Polygonum maritimum thrives in saline and sandy soils, often growing in coastal dunes and along shorelines.
