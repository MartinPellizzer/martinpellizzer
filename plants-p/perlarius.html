Perlarius

Perlarius, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink petals that resemble translucent tissue, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its rarity, Perlarius is considered endangered and is protected in its natural habitat.
