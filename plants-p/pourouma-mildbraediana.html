Pourouma mildbraediana

Pourouma mildbraediana, commonly known as the mildbraed's areca, is a species of palm native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to those of the areca palm. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

The plant has cultural and potential medicinal significance in the regions where it is native.
