Pleurothallis manningiana

Pleurothallis manningiana, commonly known as the Manning's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated inflorescence bearing small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
