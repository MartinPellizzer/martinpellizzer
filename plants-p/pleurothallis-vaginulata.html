Pleurothallis vaginulata

Pleurothallis vaginulata, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the vast Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,500 species.

Due to its unique beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
