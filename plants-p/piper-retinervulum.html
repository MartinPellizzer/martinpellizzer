Piper retinervulum

Piper retinervulum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper or creeping pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely veined, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
