Piper variegatum

Piper variegatum, commonly known as the Variegated Pepper Plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by red, peppery fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
