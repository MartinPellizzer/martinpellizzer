Prunus debilis

Prunus debilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese white cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the emergence of leaves. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus debilis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
