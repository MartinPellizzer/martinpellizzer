Polylychnis

Polylychnis, commonly known as the silver grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, often forming dense clumps with slender, upright stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, which are often adorned with a fine, silky texture.

Polylychnis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and low-maintenance qualities.
