Poa laudanensis

Poa laudanensis, commonly known as the laudanum grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Poa laudanensis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
