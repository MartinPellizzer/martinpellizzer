Physaria alpina

Physaria alpina, commonly known as the alpine bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces distinctive seed pods that resemble bladders.

Physaria alpina is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season, blooming in late spring to early summer.
