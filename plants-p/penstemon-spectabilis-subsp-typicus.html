Penstemon spectabilis subsp. typicus

Penstemon spectabilis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet.

The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 3 meters and produces lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to both ecological and ornamental value.
