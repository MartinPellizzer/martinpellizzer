Paphiopedilum praestans f. striatum

Paphiopedilum praestans f. striatum, commonly known as the striated lady's slipper orchid, is a unique hybrid orchid characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often striped petals.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers, which are usually a mix of white and green, are notable for their intricate, slipper-shaped structure.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and rarity in horticultural collections.
