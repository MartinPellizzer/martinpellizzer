Potamogeton illinoensis f. rosulatus

Potamogeton illinoensis f. rosulatus, commonly known as the Illinois pondweed.

It is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America, characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
