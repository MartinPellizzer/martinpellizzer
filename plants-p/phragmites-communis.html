Phragmites communis

Phragmites communis, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and dense, feather-like plumes that appear in the fall. The plant is highly adaptable and can grow in a variety of water conditions, including brackish and freshwater habitats. Its rhizomes form extensive underground networks, allowing it to spread rapidly and dominate wetland ecosystems.

Common reed plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
