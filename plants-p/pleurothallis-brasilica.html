Pleurothallis brasilica

Pleurothallis brasilica, commonly known as the Brazilian pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted for efficient water absorption.

The flowers, which are often white or pale pink, are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the wet season.
