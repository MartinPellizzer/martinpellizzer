Palala gordoniifolia

Palala gordoniifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf palala, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
