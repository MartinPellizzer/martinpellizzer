Plectranthus seyanii

Plectranthus seyanii, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive purple underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.

Plectranthus seyanii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
