Piper curtisii

Piper curtisii, commonly known as Curtis's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, dark blue berries.

Piper curtisii is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
