Potentilla erecta var. macrophylla

Potentilla erecta var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
