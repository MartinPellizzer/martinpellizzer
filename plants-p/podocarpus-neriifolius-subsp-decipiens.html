Podocarpus neriifolius subsp. decipiens

Podocarpus neriifolius subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the False Cypresses, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This subspecies is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
