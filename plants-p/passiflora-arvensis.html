Passiflora arvensis

Passiflora arvensis, commonly known as the field passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking, colorful flowers with a unique structure. The plant is characterized by its tendrils that help it climb and its showy, five-lobed corona within the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Passiflora arvensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
