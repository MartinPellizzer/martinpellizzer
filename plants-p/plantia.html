Plantia

Plantia, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that reflect sunlight, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and help it thrive in well-drained soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
