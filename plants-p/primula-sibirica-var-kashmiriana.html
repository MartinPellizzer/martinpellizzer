Primula sibirica var. kashmiriana

Primula sibirica var. kashmiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Kashmir and northern India.

It features dense, low-growing foliage with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
