Pithecellobium trinitense

Pithecellobium trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidad acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
