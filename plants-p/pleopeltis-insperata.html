Pleopeltis insperata

Pleopeltis insperata, commonly known as the rare tree fern, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a unique structure with a central trunk covered in a thick, spongy layer of root-like structures called rhizomes. Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
