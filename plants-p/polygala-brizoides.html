Polygala brizoides

Polygala brizoides, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological niche.
