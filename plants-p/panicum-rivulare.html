Panicum rivulare

Panicum rivulare, commonly known as water panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist soils near rivers, streams, and marshes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to golden flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in waterlogged areas.

Panicum rivulare plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
