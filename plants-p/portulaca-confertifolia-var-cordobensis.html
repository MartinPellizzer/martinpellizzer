Portulaca confertifolia var. cordobensis

Portulaca confertifolia var. cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban moss rose, is a succulent plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It features fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it an ideal plant for arid or semi-arid climates.
