Pityrodia uncinata var. exserta

Pityrodia uncinata var. exserta, commonly known as the hooked pityrodia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, woody shrub form. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

The variety exserta is distinguished by its longer, more pronounced floral structures compared to the typical form.
