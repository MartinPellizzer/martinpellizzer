Peperomia purpurinervis

Peperomia purpurinervis, commonly known as the Purple-veined Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a distinctive purple or reddish vein pattern running through the leaf blade. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers, which are often hidden among the foliage. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia purpurinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
