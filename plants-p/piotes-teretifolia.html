Piotes teretifolia

Piotes teretifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf piotes, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
