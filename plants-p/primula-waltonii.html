Primula waltonii

Primula waltonii, commonly known as the Walton's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It has hairy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
