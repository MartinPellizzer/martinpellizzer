Primula rugosa

Primula rugosa, commonly known as the rough primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
