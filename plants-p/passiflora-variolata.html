Passiflora variolata

Passiflora variolata, commonly known as the Variable Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora variolata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
