Philadelphus sericanthus var. sericanthus

Philadelphus sericanthus var. sericanthus, commonly known as the sweet shrub or mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and ability to attract pollinators.
