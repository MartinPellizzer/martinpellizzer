Pachidendron ferox

Pachidendron ferox, commonly known as the fierce pachidendron, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its woody structure and compound leaves.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a thick, branching stem with small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a source of natural dyes.
