Potentilla drosocalyx

Potentilla drosocalyx, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
