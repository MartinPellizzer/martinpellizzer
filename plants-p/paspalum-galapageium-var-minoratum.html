Paspalum galapageium var. minoratum

Paspalum galapageium var. minoratum, commonly known as the Galápagos paspalum, is a grass species native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, adapting to the unique environmental conditions of the archipelago. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
