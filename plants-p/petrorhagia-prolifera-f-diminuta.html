Petrorhagia prolifera f. diminuta

Petrorhagia prolifera f. diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, woolly leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and its flowers are typically pale pink or white, appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
