Pyrus vallis

Pyrus vallis, commonly known as the valley pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-green pears in late summer. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which has been used in traditional cuisine and medicine.
