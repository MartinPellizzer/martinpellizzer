Pleurothallis loreae

Pleurothallis loreae, commonly known as the Lorea's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis loreae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
