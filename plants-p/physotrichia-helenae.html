Physotrichia helenae

Physotrichia helenae, commonly known as the Helen's hairgrass, is a small, delicate grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Physotrichia helenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
