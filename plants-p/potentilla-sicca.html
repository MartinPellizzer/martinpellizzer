Potentilla sicca

Potentilla sicca, commonly known as Sicilian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla sicca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in dry garden landscapes.
