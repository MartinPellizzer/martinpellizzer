Prasophyllum gracile

Prasophyllum gracile, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with a single, narrow leaf and produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. It is a perennial species, often flowering in its second year.

Prasophyllum gracile is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and degradation.
