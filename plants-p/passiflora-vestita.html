Passiflora vestita

Passiflora vestita, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with intricate patterns and a unique, fragrant scent. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Passiflora vestita thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
