Peschiera affinis var. campestris

Peschiera affinis var. campestris, commonly known as the field marshmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
