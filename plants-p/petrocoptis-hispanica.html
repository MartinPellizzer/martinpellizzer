Petrocoptis hispanica

Petrocoptis hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish rock-cress, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous or coastal regions. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, with small, bright yellow flowers arranged in racemes. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Petrocoptis hispanica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
