Polemonium humile var. macranthum

Polemonium humile var. macranthum, commonly known as the showy Jacob's ladder, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves that are basal and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
