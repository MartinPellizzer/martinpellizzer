Poa scabriuscula

Poa scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive hairy texture along their surfaces. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
