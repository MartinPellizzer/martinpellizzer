Phenax uliginosus

Phenax uliginosus, commonly known as the bog gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
