Pericycla penduliflora

Pericycla penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, pendulous flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
