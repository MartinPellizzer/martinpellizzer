Prostanthera galbraithiae

Prostanthera galbraithiae, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Prostanthera galbraithiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australians.
