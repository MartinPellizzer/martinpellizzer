Pyrus aria subvar. bellojocensis

Pyrus aria subvar. bellojocensis, commonly known as the Bellojocensis pear, is a rare subvariety of the pear tree native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that typically range in color from yellow to green when mature. The plant exhibits deciduous foliage, with leaves that are broadly ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface. It prefers well-drained soil and a temperate climate, often growing in mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique traits, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
