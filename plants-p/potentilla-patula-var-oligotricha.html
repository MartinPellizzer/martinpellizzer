Potentilla patula var. oligotricha

Potentilla patula var. oligotricha, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.
