Passiflora rugosa

Passiflora rugosa, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that ripens to a reddish color.

Passiflora rugosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
