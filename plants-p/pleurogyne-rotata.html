Pleurogyne rotata

Pleurogyne rotata, commonly known as the spiral waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves, which spiral around its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water, aiding in its photosynthetic process. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its morphology is adapted for efficient water absorption and light capture in aquatic habitats.
