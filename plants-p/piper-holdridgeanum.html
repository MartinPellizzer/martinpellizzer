Piper holdridgeanum

Piper holdridgeanum, commonly known as the Holdridge pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid forests of Central and South America, particularly in regions with high rainfall and warm temperatures. The plant is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, and they contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
