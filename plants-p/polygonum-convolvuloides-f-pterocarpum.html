Polygonum convolvuloides f. pterocarpum

Polygonum convolvuloides f. pterocarpum, commonly known as the winged knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum convolvuloides.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and Japan. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Its flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.
