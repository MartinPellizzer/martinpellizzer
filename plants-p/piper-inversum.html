Piper inversum

Piper inversum, commonly known as the inverted pepper plant, is a unique species within the Piper genus.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, downward-facing flowers and small, green berries that turn red when mature. Its foliage is typically glossy and dark green, with heart-shaped leaves.

Due to its unusual appearance and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
