Phoradendron paucifolium

Phoradendron paucifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are often inconspicuous. Its berries are typically red or orange and serve as a food source for birds.

Phoradendron paucifolium plays a role in forest ecosystems by influencing host tree growth and providing habitat for various organisms.
