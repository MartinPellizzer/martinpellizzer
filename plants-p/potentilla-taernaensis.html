Potentilla taernaensis

Potentilla taernaensis, commonly known as the Taernaean cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, with a serrated margin, and its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla taernaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
