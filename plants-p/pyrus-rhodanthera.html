Pyrus rhodanthera

Pyrus rhodanthera, commonly known as the Rhododendron pear, is a rare and unique plant that combines features of both pears and rhododendrons.

It is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
