Panicum hallii subsp. filipes

Panicum hallii subsp.

filipes, commonly known as silky grass or filiped panicum, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a silky texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system and rapid growth make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
