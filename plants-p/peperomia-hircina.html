Peperomia hircina

Peperomia hircina, commonly known as the hircine peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white or pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Peperomia hircina is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both decorative and medicinal purposes in traditional herbal practices.
