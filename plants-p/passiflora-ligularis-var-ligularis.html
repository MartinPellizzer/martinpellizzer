Passiflora ligularis var. ligularis

Passiflora ligularis var. ligularis, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
