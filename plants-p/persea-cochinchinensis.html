Persea cochinchinensis

Persea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by orange to reddish fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a valuable species in both horticulture and local economies.
