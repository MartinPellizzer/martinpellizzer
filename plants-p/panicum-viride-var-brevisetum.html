Panicum viride var. brevisetum

Panicum viride var. brevisetum, commonly known as green panic grass, is a variant of the green panic species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like growth habit and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage grass in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its use in erosion control and landscaping.
