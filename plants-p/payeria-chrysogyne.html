Payeria chrysogyne

Payeria chrysogyne, commonly known as the golden star ginger, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has strap-like, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to subtropical gardens.
