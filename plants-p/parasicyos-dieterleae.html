Parasicyos dieterleae

Parasicyos dieterleae, commonly known as the Dieterle's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is characterized by its rapid growth habit. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive if not controlled.
