Parodia exquisita

Parodia exquisita, commonly known as the "Spoon Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems that often resemble a spoon or a narrow cylinder. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
