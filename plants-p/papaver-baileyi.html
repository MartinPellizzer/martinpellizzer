Papaver baileyi

Papaver baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tian Shan and Pamir Mountains. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Papaver baileyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blossoms.
