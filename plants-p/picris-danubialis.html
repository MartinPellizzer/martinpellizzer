Picris danubialis

Picris danubialis, commonly known as the Danube wormwood, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in late summer, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry grasslands.
