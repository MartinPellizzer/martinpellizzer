Pterocaulon cordobense

Pterocaulon cordobense, commonly known as the Cordoba burr, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, woody pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
