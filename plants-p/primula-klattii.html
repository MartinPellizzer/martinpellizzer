Primula klattii

Primula klattii, commonly known as the Klatt's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring cool and moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal, broad, and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Primula klattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
