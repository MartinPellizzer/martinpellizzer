Potamogeton pusillus proles berchtoldii

Potamogeton pusillus proles berchtoldii, commonly known as the dwarf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 cm in length, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in stabilizing sediments.

Its rapid growth makes it an important component of aquatic habitats but can also lead to dense vegetation in some environments.
