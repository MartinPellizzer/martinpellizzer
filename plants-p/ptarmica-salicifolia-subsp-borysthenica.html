Ptarmica salicifolia subsp. borysthenica

Ptarmica salicifolia subsp.

borysthenica, commonly known as the Siberian fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of eastern Europe and western Siberia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and open tundra. Ptarmica salicifolia subsp.

borysthenica is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
