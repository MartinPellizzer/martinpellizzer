Pithecellobium macrandrium

Pithecellobium macrandrium, commonly known as the large-flowered acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for sustainable agriculture.
