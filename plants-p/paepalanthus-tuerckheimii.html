Paepalanthus tuerckheimii

Paepalanthus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's puya, is a species of flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its rosette of fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
