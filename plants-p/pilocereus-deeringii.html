Pilocereus deeringii

Pilocereus deeringii, commonly known as the Deering's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rains.

Pilocereus deeringii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
