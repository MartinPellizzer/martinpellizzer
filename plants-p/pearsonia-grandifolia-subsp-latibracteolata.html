Pearsonia grandifolia subsp. latibracteolata

Pearsonia grandifolia subsp.

latibracteolata, commonly known as the broad-bract pearsonia, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to New Zealand. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broader bracts compared to the typical form, which are often hairy and persistent. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.
