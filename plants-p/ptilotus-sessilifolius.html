Ptilotus sessilifolius

Ptilotus sessilifolius, commonly known as the "Desert Flax," is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant has narrow, sessile leaves that reduce water loss, adapting it to harsh, dry environments. It produces seeds in small, hairy pods that help in dispersal by wind.

Ptilotus sessilifolius is often found in sandy soils and is a common sight in desert landscapes.
