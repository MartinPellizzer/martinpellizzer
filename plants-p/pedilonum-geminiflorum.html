Pedilonum geminiflorum

Pedilonum geminiflorum, commonly known as the twin-flower orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral structure consists of two petals that appear to be joined at the base, giving it the common name.

Pedilonum geminiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
