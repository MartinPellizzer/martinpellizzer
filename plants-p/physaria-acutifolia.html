Physaria acutifolia

Physaria acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in elongated racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Physaria acutifolia is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that is well adapted to its native habitat.
