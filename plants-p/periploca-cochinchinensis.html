Periploca cochinchinensis

Periploca cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese periploca, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in axillary or terminal inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is noted for its ornamental value and medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
