Pedicularis sylvatica subsp. sylvatica

Pedicularis sylvatica subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or woodland habitats.

Its unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
