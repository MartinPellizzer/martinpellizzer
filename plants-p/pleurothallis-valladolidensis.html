Pleurothallis valladolidensis

Pleurothallis valladolidensis, commonly known as the Valladolid orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to the moist, nutrient-poor conditions of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis valladolidensis is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
