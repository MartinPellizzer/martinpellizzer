Primula × deschmannii

Primula × deschmannii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a hybrid between Primula sinensis and Primula vulgaris, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, featuring rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
