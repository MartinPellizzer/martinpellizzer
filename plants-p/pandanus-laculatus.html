Pandanus laculatus

Pandanus laculatus, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and can grow up to several meters in height. Its fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing small seeds, is adapted for water dispersal.

The leaves are also used in cooking and as a natural dye in various cultures.
