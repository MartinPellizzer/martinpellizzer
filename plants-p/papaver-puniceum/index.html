Papaver Puniceum

Papaver puniceum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its vibrant red flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, creating a dramatic visual impact in natural landscapes. The plant is often found in rocky soils and alpine environments where it thrives in harsh conditions. Despite its beauty, it is also known for its toxic properties, making it both a marvel of nature and a cautionary plant.
