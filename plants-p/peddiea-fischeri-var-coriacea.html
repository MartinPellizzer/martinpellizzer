Peddiea fischeri var. coriacea

Peddiea fischeri var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough peddiea, is a unique species within the genus Peddiea, characterized by its distinctive, rugged foliage.

This variant is native to specific regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a rough texture that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered near the base of the stems.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Peddiea fischeri var. coriacea is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
