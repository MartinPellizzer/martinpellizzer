Poa nemoralis var. bistricensis

Poa nemoralis var. bistricensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
