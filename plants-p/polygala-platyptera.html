Polygala platyptera

Polygala platyptera, commonly known as the flat-winged milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves that are usually hairy. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Polygala platyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
