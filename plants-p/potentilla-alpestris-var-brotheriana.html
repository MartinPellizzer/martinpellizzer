Potentilla alpestris var. brotheriana

Potentilla alpestris var. brotheriana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically features hairy stems and dark green, serrated leaves that are adapted to cold and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
