Potentilla argentea var. major

Potentilla argentea var. major, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
