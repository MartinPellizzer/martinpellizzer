Polygala malesiana

Polygala malesiana, commonly known as the male's galingale, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that are often clustered in loose spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
