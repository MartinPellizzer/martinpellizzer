Phaseolus compressus var. niger

Phaseolus compressus var. niger, commonly known as black bean plant, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are typically used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate structures and produces small, green pods that mature into the distinctive black beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its role in traditional agriculture and horticulture.
