Poa soczawae

Poa soczawae, commonly known as the Soczawa meadow grass, is a species of grass native to specific regions of Poland.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Poa soczawae is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
