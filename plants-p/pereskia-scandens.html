Pereskia scandens

Pereskia scandens, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often mistaken for leaves, which are actually modified stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Pereskia scandens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
