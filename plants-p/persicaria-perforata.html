Persicaria perforata

Persicaria perforata, commonly known as the punctured knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Asia and parts of Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of small holes, giving it its scientific name. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
