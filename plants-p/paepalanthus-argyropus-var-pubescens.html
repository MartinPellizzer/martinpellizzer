Paepalanthus argyropus var. pubescens

Paepalanthus argyropus var. pubescens, commonly known as the silver-haired puya, is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, particularly in South America. The plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, spike-like structure bearing numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers.

This species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its unique ornamental appearance.
