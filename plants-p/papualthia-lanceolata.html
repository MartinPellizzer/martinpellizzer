Papualthia lanceolata

Papualthia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed papua orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
