Poikilacanthus harleyi

Poikilacanthus harleyi, commonly known as the harley's spiny scale, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, spiny structures that cover its stems and leaves.

It is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in areas with dry to semi-arid climates. The plant exhibits a succulent growth habit, allowing it to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its leaves are typically small and often reduced, with the majority of photosynthesis occurring through the stems.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
