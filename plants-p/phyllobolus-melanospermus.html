Phyllobolus melanospermus

Phyllobolus melanospermus, commonly known as the black-seeded leaf beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy body and the presence of black seeds in its reproductive structures. The plant is often found in grassland and savanna habitats, where it plays a role in seed dispersal. Its botanical significance lies in its unique seed morphology and ecological interactions.

The species is of interest to botanists and entomologists studying plant-insect relationships.
