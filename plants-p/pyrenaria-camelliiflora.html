Pyrenaria camelliiflora

Pyrenaria camelliiflora, commonly known as the camellia-flowered pyrenaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Theaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and they bloom in the spring.

The leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.
