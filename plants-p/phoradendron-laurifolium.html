Phoradendron laurifolium

Phoradendron laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, simple leaves that are often mistaken for those of its host species, and it produces white, fleshy berries that are consumed by birds. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are embedded in the host tree's bark. It plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by providing food for wildlife and contributing to the natural cycle of host trees.

Its presence can sometimes lead to the decline of its host if the parasitic relationship becomes too burdensome.
