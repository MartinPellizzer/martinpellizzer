Peperomia futunaensis

Peperomia futunaensis, commonly known as the Futuna Island Peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Futuna in the Pacific Ocean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its native environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color.

Peperomia futunaensis is a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
