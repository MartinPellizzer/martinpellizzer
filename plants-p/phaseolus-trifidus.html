Phaseolus trifidus

Phaseolus trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, edible seeds that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

Phaseolus trifidus is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential as a crop in sustainable agriculture.
