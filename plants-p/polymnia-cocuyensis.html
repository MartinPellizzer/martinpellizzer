Polymnia cocuyensis

Polymnia cocuyensis, commonly known as the Cocuy Mountain polychrome, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often vibrant flowers. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, giving it a showy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
