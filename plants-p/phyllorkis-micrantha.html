Phyllorkis micrantha

Phyllorkis micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockheath, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
