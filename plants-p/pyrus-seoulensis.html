Pyrus seoulensis

Pyrus seoulensis, commonly known as Seoul pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South Korea and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its flowers are white and arranged in clusters.

The fruit ripens in late summer and is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp.
