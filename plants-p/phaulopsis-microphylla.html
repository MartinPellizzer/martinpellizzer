Phaulopsis microphylla

Phaulopsis microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved phaulopsis, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
