Pachyphyllum tenue

Pachyphyllum tenue, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, star-shaped flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage.

Pachyphyllum tenue is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
