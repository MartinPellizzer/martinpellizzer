Picea obovata var. argentea

Picea obovata var. argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a variant of the Japanese white fir.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue needles, which are shorter and more densely packed than those of the typical species. The tree has a conical shape with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes deeply furrowed with age.

This variety is native to Japan and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.
