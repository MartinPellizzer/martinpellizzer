Polypodium feei

Polypodium feei, commonly known as the Chinese shield fern, is a species of fern native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can spread to form dense colonies.

Polypodium feei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
