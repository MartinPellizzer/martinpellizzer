Peristeria selligera

Peristeria selligera, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. The plant has a succulent, spiny stem and produces small, yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed. The species is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique flowering behavior.
