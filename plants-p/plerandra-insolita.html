Plerandra insolita

Plerandra insolita, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

Plerandra insolita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
