Panicum amaroides

Panicum amaroides, commonly known as bitter panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
