Paris tetraphylla var. angustifolia

Paris tetraphylla var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white baneberry, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces distinctive red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
