Peperomia retusa var. bachmannii

Peperomia retusa var. bachmannii, commonly known as the Bachmann's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape, often with a waxy texture and a range of colors from deep green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, though they are not the primary ornamental feature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
