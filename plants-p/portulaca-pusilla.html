Portulaca pusilla

Portulaca pusilla, commonly known as the moss rose or dwarf portulaca, is a small, annual flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, short-lived flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for dry, rocky environments. Its compact size and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Portulaca pusilla is also known for its ability to self-seed, ensuring its propagation in suitable habitats.
