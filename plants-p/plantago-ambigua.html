Plantago ambigua

Plantago ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as those found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and healing properties.

The plant's ability to thrive in a variety of environments makes it a common sight in many temperate regions.
