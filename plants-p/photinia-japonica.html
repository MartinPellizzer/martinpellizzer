Photinia japonica

Photinia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese rose or red leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
