Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. praelongus

Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. praelongus, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its elongated, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often obscured by its dense foliage. It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
