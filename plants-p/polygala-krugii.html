Polygala krugii

Polygala krugii, commonly known as the Krug's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Polygala krugii typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its unique floral structure and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
