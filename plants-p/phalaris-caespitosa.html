Phalaris caespitosa

Phalaris caespitosa, commonly known as tufted bottlebrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are often bright red or orange in color. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Phalaris caespitosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its visual interest and drought tolerance.
