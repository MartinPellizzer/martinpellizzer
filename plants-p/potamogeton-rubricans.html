Potamogeton rubricans

Potamogeton rubricans, commonly known as the redstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It has slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its leaves are long and ribbon-like, with a smooth surface and a slight sheen.

Potamogeton rubricans plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
