Potentilla subpinnata

Potentilla subpinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Potentilla subpinnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
