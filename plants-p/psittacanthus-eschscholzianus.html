Psittacanthus eschscholzianus

Psittacanthus eschscholzianus, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and distinctive red veins. The plant produces a spadix inflorescence surrounded by a spathe, typical of many arum species. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy root vegetable, similar to taro.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
