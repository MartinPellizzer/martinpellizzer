Phlox × rugelii

Phlox × rugelii, commonly known as the rugelii phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Phlox × rugelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and fragrance.
