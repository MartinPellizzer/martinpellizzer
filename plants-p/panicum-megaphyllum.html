Panicum megaphyllum

Panicum megaphyllum, commonly known as the giant panicgrass, is a tall grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and distinctive, fluffy panicles that appear at the top of the plant. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other grasses and forbs in mixed vegetation.

Panicum megaphyllum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
