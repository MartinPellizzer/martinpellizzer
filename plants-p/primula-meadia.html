Primula meadia

Primula meadia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of funnel-shaped, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens during that time.
