Pinus fenzeliana subsp. varifolia

Pinus fenzeliana subsp.

varifolia, commonly known as the Fenzel's pine, is a rare and endemic species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and conical shape, typical of many pine species.

The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in bundles of two, and exhibit a distinctive variation in foliage, hence its subspecies name *varifolia*. The cones are woody and produce edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique morphological traits.
