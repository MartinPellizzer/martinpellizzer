Plantago valida

Plantago valida, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
