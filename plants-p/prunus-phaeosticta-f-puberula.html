Prunus phaeosticta f. puberula

Prunus phaeosticta f. puberula, commonly known as the hairy cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree or shrub, native to the eastern United States. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a pubescent (hairy) surface, giving it a unique appearance. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
