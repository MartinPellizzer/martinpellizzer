Paederia verticillata var. tomentella

Paederia verticillata var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy paederia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
