Pleurothallis magnicalcarata

Pleurothallis magnicalcarata, commonly known as the giant spurred orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, spurred flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its leaves are usually broad and leathery, adapted to the humid, shaded environments it inhabits.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
