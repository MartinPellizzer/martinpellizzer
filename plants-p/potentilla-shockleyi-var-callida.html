Potentilla shockleyi var. callida

Potentilla shockleyi var. callida, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

The species is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions, making it well-suited to its harsh native habitat.
