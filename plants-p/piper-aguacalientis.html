Piper aguacalientis

Piper aguacalientis, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the compound piperine, which contributes to its spicy flavor.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in culinary applications in some regions.
