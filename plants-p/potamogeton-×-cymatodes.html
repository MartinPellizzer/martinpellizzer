Potamogeton × cymatodes

Potamogeton × cymatodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parental species, Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton cymosus.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it thrives in shallow to moderately deep waters. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Potamogeton × cymatodes plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, as it tends to flourish in clean, well-oxygenated environments.
