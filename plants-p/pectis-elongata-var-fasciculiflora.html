Pectis elongata var. fasciculiflora

Pectis elongata var. fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered rat-tail bush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a grayish-green coloration.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls.
