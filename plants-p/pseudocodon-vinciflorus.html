Pseudocodon vinciflorus

Pseudocodon vinciflorus, commonly known as the grapeleaf puccoon, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.
