Philodendron pseudoundulatum

Philodendron pseudoundulatum, commonly known as the waxy leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are often variegated with light green and white patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
