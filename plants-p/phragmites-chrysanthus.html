Phragmites chrysanthus

Phragmites chrysanthus, commonly known as the golden reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces fluffy, golden-colored seed heads that give it its common name and aid in wind pollination. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
