Pavonia arabica

Pavonia arabica, commonly known as the Arabian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Pavonia arabica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
