Prunus × donarium f. sobanzakura

Prunus × donarium f. sobanzakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant is a deciduous tree with a compact, rounded shape and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
