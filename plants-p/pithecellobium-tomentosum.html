Pithecellobium tomentosum

Pithecellobium tomentosum, commonly known as the hairy pod bean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, hairy, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agroforestry systems.
