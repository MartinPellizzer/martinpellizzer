Phoradendron vernicosum

Phoradendron vernicosum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant produces small, white or green berries that are dispersed by birds. It is found in various parts of the Americas and plays a role in local ecosystems.

Its presence can affect the health of host plants and contribute to biodiversity.
