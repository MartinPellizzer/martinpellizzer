Polygonum raptae

Polygonum raptae, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often rhizomatous growth patterns. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, greenish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
