Pilocereus houlletii

Pilocereus houlletii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil. Its flowers typically last only one night, making it a rare and striking sight.

Pilocereus houlletii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
