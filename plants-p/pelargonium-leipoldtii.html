Pelargonium leipoldtii

Pelargonium leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant features serrated, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium leipoldtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
