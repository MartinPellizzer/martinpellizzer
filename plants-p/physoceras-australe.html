Physoceras australe

Physoceras australe, commonly known as the Australian bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Australia.

It is characterized by its tiny, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts, which help it float and absorb gases from the water. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It is a carnivorous plant that traps and digests small aquatic organisms.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
