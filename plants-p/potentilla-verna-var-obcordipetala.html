Potentilla verna var. obcordipetala

Potentilla verna var. obcordipetala, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, often with a distinctive heart-shaped base, and are covered in fine, soft hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
