Papaver bhutanicum

Papaver bhutanicum, commonly known as the Bhutanese poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Bhutan.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, cup-shaped seed capsule. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high elevations. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are known for their vibrant color and medicinal properties.

The seeds of Papaver bhutanicum are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
