Polyscias mayottensis

Polyscias mayottensis, commonly known as Mayotte glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small and clustered, often white or cream in color, and are followed by bright red berries that attract various bird species.
