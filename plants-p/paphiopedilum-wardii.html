Paphiopedilum wardii

Paphiopedilum wardii, commonly known as the Ward's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a distinctive, pouch-shaped lip that is a hallmark of the slipper orchid genus. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with broad, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with a deep red or orange lip, and it blooms in late spring or early summer.

Paphiopedilum wardii prefers cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated in shaded, well-drained soil.
