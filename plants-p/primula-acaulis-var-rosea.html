Primula acaulis var. rosea

Primula acaulis var. rosea, commonly known as the pink rock primrose, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to rocky habitats in Europe.

It features dense clusters of showy, pink or rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with few leaves, which are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and have a delicate, bell-shaped appearance.

This variety is well adapted to poor, well-drained soils and is often found growing on limestone or chalky substrates.
