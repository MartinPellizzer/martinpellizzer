Phlox nana var. triovulata

Phlox nana var. triovulata, commonly known as the dwarf phlox or three-lobed phlox, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. The plant has narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
