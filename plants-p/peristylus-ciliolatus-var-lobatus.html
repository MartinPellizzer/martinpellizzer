Peristylus ciliolatus var. lobatus

Peristylus ciliolatus var. lobatus, commonly known as the broad-lobed peristylus, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
