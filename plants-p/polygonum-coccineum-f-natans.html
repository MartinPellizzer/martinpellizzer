Polygonum coccineum f. natans

Polygonum coccineum f. natans, commonly known as the floating knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive red stems and heart-shaped leaves.

It is a variant of Polygonum coccineum, known for its unique growth habit of floating on the surface of water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest to aquatic environments. Its roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

This species is often found in shallow, still waters such as ponds and marshes.
