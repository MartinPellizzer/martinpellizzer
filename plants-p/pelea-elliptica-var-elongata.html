Pelea elliptica var. elongata

Pelea elliptica var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated pelea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically elongated, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
