Panicum pterigodium var. majus

Panicum pterigodium var. majus, commonly known as the giant beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a forage crop.
