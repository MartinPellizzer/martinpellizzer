Potentilla bolzanensiformis

Potentilla bolzanensiformis, commonly known as the Bolzanian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
