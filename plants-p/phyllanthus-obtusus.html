Phyllanthus obtusus

Phyllanthus obtusus, commonly known as the blunt-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often rounded or ovate in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
