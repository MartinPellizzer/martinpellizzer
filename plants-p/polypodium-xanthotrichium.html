Polypodium xanthotrichium

Polypodium xanthotrichium, commonly known as the golden hair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, golden hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Polypodium xanthotrichium make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to botanical gardens.
