Pyrus haljamovae

Pyrus haljamovae, commonly known as the Haljamovae pear, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in rocky soils at high altitudes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green leaves and clusters of white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small pear-like berry that ripens in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
