Pyrus lancasteri

Pyrus lancasteri, commonly known as the Lancaster pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 10-20 feet and features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
