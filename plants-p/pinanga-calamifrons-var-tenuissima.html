Pinanga calamifrons var. tenuissima

Pinanga calamifrons var. tenuissima, commonly known as the slender-leafed bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows as a clumping bamboo, with tall, narrow stems that can reach several meters in height. Its leaves are elongated and often have a smooth, waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
