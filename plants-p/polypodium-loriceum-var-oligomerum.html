Polypodium loriceum var. oligomerum

Polypodium loriceum var. oligomerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a serrated or toothed margin.

The spore-producing structures, called sporangia, are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.
