Plantago eriopoda f. latior

Plantago eriopoda f. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
