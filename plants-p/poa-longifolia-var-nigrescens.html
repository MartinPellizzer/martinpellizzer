Poa longifolia var. nigrescens

Poa longifolia var. nigrescens, commonly known as the dark-stemmed bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow with a bluish-gray sheen, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
