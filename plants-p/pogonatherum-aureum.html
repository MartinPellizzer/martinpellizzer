Pogonatherum aureum

Pogonatherum aureum, commonly known as golden beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces golden-yellow inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pogonatherum aureum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
