Pappobolus acutifolia

Pappobolus acutifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pappobolus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its seed dispersal mechanism involves lightweight, hairy seeds that are carried by the wind.

Pappobolus acutifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
