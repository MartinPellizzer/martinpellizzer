Piptatherum miliaceum subsp. thomasii

Piptatherum miliaceum subsp.

thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' millet, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Thomas' millet is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
