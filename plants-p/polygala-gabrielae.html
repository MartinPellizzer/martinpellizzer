Polygala gabrielae

Polygala gabrielae, commonly known as Gabriel's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that resemble small butterflies. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
