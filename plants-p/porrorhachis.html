Porrorhachis

Porrorhachis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, intricate flower structures that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments. Porrorhachis species often have strap-shaped leaves and produce small, fragrant flowers in various colors.

Due to their specific habitat requirements, they are often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized gardens.
