Pavonia paniculata f. hirsuta

Pavonia paniculata f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in soft, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
