Protea salicifolia

Protea salicifolia, commonly known as the silky protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a rounded, umbrella-like shape. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky down, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea salicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species for local biodiversity.
