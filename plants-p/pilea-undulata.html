Pilea undulata

Pilea undulata, commonly known as the peacock plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that resemble the tail of a peacock. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

Pilea undulata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
