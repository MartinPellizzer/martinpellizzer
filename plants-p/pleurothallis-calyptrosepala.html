Pleurothallis calyptrosepala

Pleurothallis calyptrosepala, commonly known as the hidden-lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining, acidic soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall understated appearance.

This orchid is often found in cloud forests, where it attaches itself to trees or rocks, showcasing its epiphytic growth habit.
