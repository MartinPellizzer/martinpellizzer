Pelargonium peltatum var. glabrum

Pelargonium peltatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often variegated with silver and green. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a garden plant and in hanging baskets.
