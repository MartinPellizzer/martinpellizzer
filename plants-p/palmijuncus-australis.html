Palmijuncus australis

Palmijuncus australis, commonly known as the Australian fan palm, is a unique species native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing the ecosystem. Its slow growth rate and ability to withstand salt spray make it well adapted to its coastal habitat.

However, it is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
