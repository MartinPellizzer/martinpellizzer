Pityranthus

Pityranthus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their striking yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. Pityranthus species often grow in moist, tropical environments and can be found in a variety of habitats, including forests and wetlands.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
