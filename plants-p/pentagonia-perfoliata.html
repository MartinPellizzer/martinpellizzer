Pentagonia perfoliata

Pentagonia perfoliata, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive central vein. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
