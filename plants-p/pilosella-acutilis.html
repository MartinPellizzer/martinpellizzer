Pilosella acutilis

Pilosella acutilis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance.
