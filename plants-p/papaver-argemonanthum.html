Papaver argemonanthum

Papaver argemonanthum, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and a distinctive, often lobed leaf structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
