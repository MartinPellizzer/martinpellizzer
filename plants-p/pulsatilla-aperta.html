Pulsatilla aperta

Pulsatilla aperta, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are palmately lobed. Its flowers typically consist of numerous petal-like sepals and numerous stamens, creating a distinctive, showy appearance.

Pulsatilla aperta is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
