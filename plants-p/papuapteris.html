Papuapteris

Papuapteris, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a genus of ferns native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with fine hairs. The plants typically grow in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Papuapteris species are known for their small, spore-bearing structures called sori, which are usually located on the underside of the fronds.

These ferns play an important role in their ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
