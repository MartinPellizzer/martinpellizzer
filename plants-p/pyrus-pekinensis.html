Pyrus pekinensis

Pyrus pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive, finely toothed leaves and showy white flowers in spring. The tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are prized in both culinary and medicinal contexts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and fruit yield.

Pyrus pekinensis is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
