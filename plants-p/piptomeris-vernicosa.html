Piptomeris vernicosa

Piptomeris vernicosa, commonly known as the sticky willow or silver willow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and small, oval leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces catkins, which are its primary means of seed dispersal, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. Its bark is often covered in a sticky resin, which gives it its common name.

Piptomeris vernicosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
