Pseuderanthemum polyanthum

Pseuderanthemum polyanthum, commonly known as the clustered bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Pseuderanthemum polyanthum is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
