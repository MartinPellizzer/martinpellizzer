Polygala sibirica var. latifolia

Polygala sibirica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid gardens.
