Psidium xidocarpum

Psidium xidocarpum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. The fruit has a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular fruit in its native region.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit yield.
