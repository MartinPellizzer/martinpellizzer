Physalis michoacanensis

Physalis michoacanensis, commonly known as the Michoacan husk tomato, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in the Sierra Madre mountains.

The fruit is edible, though it has a tart flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
