Paepalanthus venustus

Paepalanthus venustus, commonly known as the velvetleaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires careful management due to its aggressive growth habit.

The plant's ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems allows it to survive in arid conditions.
