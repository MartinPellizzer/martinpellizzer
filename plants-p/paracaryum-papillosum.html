Paracaryum papillosum

Paracaryum papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. Paracaryum papillosum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.

It is also considered a potential weed in some regions due to its aggressive spread.
