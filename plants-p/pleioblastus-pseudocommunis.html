Pleioblastus pseudocommunis

Pleioblastus pseudocommunis, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant is clump-forming, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for small garden spaces. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and ability to create a lush, textured appearance in landscapes.
