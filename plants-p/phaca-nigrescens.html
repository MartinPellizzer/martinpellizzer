Phaca nigrescens

Phaca nigrescens, commonly known as the blackening phaca, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black foliage, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
