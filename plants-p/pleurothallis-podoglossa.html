Pleurothallis podoglossa

Pleurothallis podoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lip pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
