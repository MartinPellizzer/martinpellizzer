Pinus zitacuarensis

Pinus zitacuarensis, commonly known as the Zitacuaro pine, is a species of conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color and contain seeds surrounded by a thin, papery covering. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems and is valued for its timber.
