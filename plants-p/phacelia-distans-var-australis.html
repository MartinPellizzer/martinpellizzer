Phacelia distans var. australis

Phacelia distans var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
