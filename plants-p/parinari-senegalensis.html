Parinari senegalensis

Parinari senegalensis, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
