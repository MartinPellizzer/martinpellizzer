Pitcairnia wendtiae

Pitcairnia wendtiae, commonly known as the Wendt's pitcairnia, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central water-holding tank, a common feature among bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences with showy, colorful bracts that attract pollinators. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns and can vary in color depending on environmental conditions.

Pitcairnia wendtiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique botanical structure.
