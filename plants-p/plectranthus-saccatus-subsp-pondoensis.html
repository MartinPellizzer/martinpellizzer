Plectranthus saccatus subsp. pondoensis

Plectranthus saccatus subsp.

pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
