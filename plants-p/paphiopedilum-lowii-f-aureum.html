Paphiopedilum lowii f. aureum

Paphiopedilum lowii f. aureum, commonly known as the golden lowii orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow petals and a velvety, dark green to purple-tinged sepals. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and lance-shaped leaves. It blooms with showy, lip-like flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a deep golden hue.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral display.
