Prangos pabularia subsp. lamellata

Prangos pabularia subsp.

lamellata, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
