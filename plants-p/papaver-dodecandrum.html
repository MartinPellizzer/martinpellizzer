Papaver dodecandrum

Papaver dodecandrum, commonly known as the twelve-petaled poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature 12 petals, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
