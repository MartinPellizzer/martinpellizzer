Platysepalum violaceum var. vanhouttei

Platysepalum violaceum var. vanhouttei, commonly known as the Vanhoutte's violet-leafed plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, violet-colored leaves and clusters of small, pale purple or white flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and is often grown as a decorative indoor plant. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
