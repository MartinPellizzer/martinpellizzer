Polygonum emarginatum

Polygonum emarginatum, commonly known as the margined smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Polygonum emarginatum thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It is a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
