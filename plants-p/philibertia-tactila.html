Philibertia tactila

Philibertia tactila, commonly known as the touch-me-not plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its common name comes from the way its leaves respond to touch, curling slightly when handled.
