Panmorphia comayaguensis

Panmorphia comayaguensis, commonly known as the Maya orchid, is a unique orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that display a range of colors, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
