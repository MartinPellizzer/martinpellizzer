Panicum regulare

Panicum regulare, commonly known as regular panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and use in ecological restoration projects.
