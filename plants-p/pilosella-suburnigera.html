Pilosella suburnigera

Pilosella suburnigera, commonly known as the silverleaf hawkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single inflorescence at the end of each stem. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pilosella suburnigera is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
