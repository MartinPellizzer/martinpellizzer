Potentilla sordida var. genuina

Potentilla sordida var. genuina, commonly known as the gloomy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It typically features dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in harsh, cold environments and is adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species suited to extreme climatic conditions.
