Piper cumbricola

Piper cumbricola, commonly known as the mountain pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round berries.

Piper cumbricola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
