Paspalum soboliferum

Paspalum soboliferum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are clustered in panicles, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum soboliferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
