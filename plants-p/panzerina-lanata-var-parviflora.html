Panzerina lanata var. parviflora

Panzerina lanata var. parviflora, commonly known as the woolly panzerina, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique morphology, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
