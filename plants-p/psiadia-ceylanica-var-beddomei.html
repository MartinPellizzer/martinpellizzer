Psiadia ceylanica var. beddomei

Psiadia ceylanica var. beddomei, commonly known as the Ceylon daisy, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and often branched.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
