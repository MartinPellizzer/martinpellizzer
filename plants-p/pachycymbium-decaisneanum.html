Pachycymbium decaisneanum

Pachycymbium decaisneanum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

Pachycymbium decaisneanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
