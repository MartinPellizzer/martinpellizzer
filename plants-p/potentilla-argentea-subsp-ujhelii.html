Potentilla argentea subsp. ujhelii

Potentilla argentea subsp.

ujhelii, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
