Persea pedunculosa

Persea pedunculosa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
