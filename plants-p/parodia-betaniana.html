Parodia betaniana

Parodia betaniana, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, silky hairs. The plant features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, adding a striking contrast to its textured foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
