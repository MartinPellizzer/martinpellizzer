Polygala thuringiaca

Polygala thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland or grassy areas and reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in moist to moderately dry habitats.
