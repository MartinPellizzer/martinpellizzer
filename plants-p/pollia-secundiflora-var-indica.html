Pollia secundiflora var. indica

Pollia secundiflora var. indica, commonly known as the Indian velvet tamarind, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant that produces small, clustered flowers with showy, fleshy petals. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
