Panicum macractinium

Panicum macractinium, commonly known as giant bristlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its distinctive, stiff, bristle-like inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
