Pelargonium × brevipes

Pelargonium × brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both container gardening and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, Pelargonium × brevipes is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, colorful addition to their landscape.
