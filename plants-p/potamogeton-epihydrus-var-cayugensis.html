Potamogeton epihydrus var. cayugensis

Potamogeton epihydrus var. cayugensis, commonly known as Cayuga pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality.
