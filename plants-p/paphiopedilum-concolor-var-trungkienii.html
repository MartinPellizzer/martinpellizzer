Paphiopedilum concolor var. trungkienii

Paphiopedilum concolor var. trungkienii, commonly known as the Trung Kien Orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after hybrid of the Paphiopedilum genus.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a distinctive lip that resembles a pouch, a defining feature of the orchid family. This variety is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, with dark green, leathery leaves and a compact growth habit.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring or early summer, adding to its ornamental value.
