Plantago pungens

Plantago pungens, commonly known as the horsehoof plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It is often found in moist environments such as meadows and wetlands.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
