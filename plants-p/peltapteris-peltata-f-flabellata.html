Peltapteris peltata f. flabellata

Peltapteris peltata f. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate fern, is a rare and distinctive fossil plant from the Devonian period.

It belongs to the order Psilophytales and is characterized by its unique, fan-shaped fronds with a distinctive, flattened structure. The plant exhibits a simple, dichotomous branching pattern, typical of early vascular plants. Its morphology suggests it was a primitive terrestrial plant adapted to moist, swampy environments.

Fossilized remains of this fern provide important insights into the early evolution of plant life during the Paleozoic era.
