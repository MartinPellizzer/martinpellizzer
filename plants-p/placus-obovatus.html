Placus obovatus

Placus obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate plant, is a species characterized by its distinctive leaf shape.

The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth, waxy surface that aids in water retention. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color.

Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
