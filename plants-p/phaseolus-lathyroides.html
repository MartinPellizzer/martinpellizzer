Phaseolus lathyroides

Phaseolus lathyroides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
