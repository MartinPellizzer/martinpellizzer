Phaeopappus amblyolepis

Phaeopappus amblyolepis, commonly known as the rough pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant is known for its hairy, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds.

Phaeopappus amblyolepis thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
