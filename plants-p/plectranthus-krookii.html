Plectranthus krookii

Plectranthus krookii, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue or purple flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Plectranthus krookii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
