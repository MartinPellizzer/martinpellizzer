Potentilla gordonii var. lycopodioides

Potentilla gordonii var. lycopodioides, commonly known as the Gordon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
