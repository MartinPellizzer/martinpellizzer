Phaseolus coccineus subsp. striatus

Phaseolus coccineus subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas. It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet having a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary use.
