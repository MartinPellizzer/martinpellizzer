Polypodium calirhiza

Polypodium calirhiza, commonly known as the rock fern or mountain fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to various regions of the world, including Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in rocky or shaded environments, often growing on cliffs or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to colonize difficult habitats.

Polypodium calirhiza is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
