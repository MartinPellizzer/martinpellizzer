Pandanus kaida

Pandanus kaida, commonly known as the Kaida pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural fiber source. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color.

Pandanus kaida thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
