Passiflora ita

Passiflora ita, commonly known as the Italian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant's flowers are typically white or pale blue with a prominent central cluster of stamens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The fruit, which develops after flowering, is a small, edible berry with a mild, sweet flavor.
