Polygonum quadrifidum

Polygonum quadrifidum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with reddish stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in dense clusters, which bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Polygonum quadrifidum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wetlands.
