Poa alberti var. vivipara

Poa alberti var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous albert grass, is a variant of the albert grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its unique feature is the presence of viviparous shoots, which can develop into new plants while still attached to the parent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its reproductive strategy and ecological adaptability.
