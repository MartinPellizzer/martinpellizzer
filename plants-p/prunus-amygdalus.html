Prunus amygdalus

Prunus amygdalus, commonly known as the almond tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain the edible seeds used for almond production. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy white or pink flowers. Its fruit develops in a hard, woody shell, and the tree is often cultivated for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.

The almond tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates.
