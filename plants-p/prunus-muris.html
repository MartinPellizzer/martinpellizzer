Prunus muris

Prunus muris, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible yellow or orange fruits. Prunus muris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
