Pyrenacantha acuminata

Pyrenacantha acuminata, commonly known as the African snowball, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
