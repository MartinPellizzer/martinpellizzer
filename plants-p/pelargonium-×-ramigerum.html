Pelargonium × ramigerum

Pelargonium × ramigerum, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically has rounded leaves with serrated edges and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × ramigerum is also known for its ability to produce fragrant foliage, which adds to its appeal in both landscape design and indoor gardening.
