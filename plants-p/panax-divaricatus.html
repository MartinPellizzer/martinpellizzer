Panax divaricatus

Panax divaricatus, commonly known as American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched root system. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with pinnately compound leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its roots are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.
