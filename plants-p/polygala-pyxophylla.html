Polygala pyxophylla

Polygala pyxophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
