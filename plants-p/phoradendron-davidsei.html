Phoradendron davidsei

Phoradendron davidsei, commonly known as the David's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Ficus*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also obtaining some nutrients through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

Its ecological role includes providing food for birds and insects, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
