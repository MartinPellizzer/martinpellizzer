Pseudogynoxys chiribogensis

Pseudogynoxys chiribogensis, commonly known as the Chiriboga's false yarrow, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Pseudogynoxys chiribogensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
