Passiflora trisecta

Passiflora trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral displays.
