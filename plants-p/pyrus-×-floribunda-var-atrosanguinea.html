Pyrus × floribunda var. atrosanguinea

Pyrus × floribunda var. atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis and Pyrus ussuriensis.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy clusters of pink to red flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically not suitable for commercial use but are appreciated for their unique flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
