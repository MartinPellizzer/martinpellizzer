Pholidota annamensis

Pholidota annamensis, commonly known as the Annam flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and along riverbanks.

The Annam flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
