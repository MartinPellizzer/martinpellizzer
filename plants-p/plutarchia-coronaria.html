Plutarchia coronaria

Plutarchia coronaria, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a basal rosette structure.

Plutarchia coronaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
