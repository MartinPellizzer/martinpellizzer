Pellionia umbellata

Pellionia umbellata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or umbrella fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and compound leaves that often form a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it its scientific name. Pellionia umbellata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
