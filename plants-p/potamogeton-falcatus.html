Potamogeton falcatus

Potamogeton falcatus, commonly known as the sickle pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically curved or sickle-shaped, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it is often found in shallow, freshwater habitats. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton falcatus is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
