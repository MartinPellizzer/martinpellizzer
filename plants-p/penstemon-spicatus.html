Penstemon spicatus

Penstemon spicatus, commonly known as the spiked beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. The plant features tall, erect stems with clusters of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon spicatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
