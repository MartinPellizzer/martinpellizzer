Polygala taquetii

Polygala taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, showy flowers.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
