Passiflora perfoliata var. perfoliata

Passiflora perfoliata var. perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive perfoliate base that appears to pass through the stem. This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
