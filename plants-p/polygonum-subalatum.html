Polygonum subalatum

Polygonum subalatum, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has erect, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer.

Polygonum subalatum is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
