Plantago hookeriana

Plantago hookeriana, commonly known as Hooker's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
