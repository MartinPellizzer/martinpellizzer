Pyrus cotoneaster

Pyrus cotoneaster, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits that are similar in appearance to pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit in certain regions.
