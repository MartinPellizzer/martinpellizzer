Plantago formosana

Plantago formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense cluster, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
