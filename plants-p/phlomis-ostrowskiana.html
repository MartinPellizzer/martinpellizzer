Phlomis ostrowskiana

Phlomis ostrowskiana, commonly known as the Persian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It features tall, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Phlomis ostrowskiana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
