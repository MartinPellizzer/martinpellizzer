Pavonia melhanioides

Pavonia melhanioides, commonly known as the Florida pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia melhanioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
