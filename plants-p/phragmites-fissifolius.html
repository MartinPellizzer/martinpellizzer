Phragmites fissifolius

Phragmites fissifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved reed, is a tall, perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctively serrated or toothed edge. This plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes produce fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its spread.

Phragmites fissifolius plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
