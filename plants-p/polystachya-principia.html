Polystachya principia

Polystachya principia, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow inflorescence that consists of multiple clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

Polystachya principia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting various pollinators.
