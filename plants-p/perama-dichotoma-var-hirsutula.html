Perama dichotoma var. hirsutula

Perama dichotoma var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy perama, is a variant of the perama plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and branched growth habit. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.

This variety is often cultivated for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
