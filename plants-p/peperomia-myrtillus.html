Peperomia myrtillus

Peperomia myrtillus, commonly known as the velvetleaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia myrtillus is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
