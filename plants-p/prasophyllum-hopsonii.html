Prasophyllum hopsonii

Prasophyllum hopsonii, commonly known as the Hopson's leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and a single, slender flower spike. The plant typically grows in heathland or open forest habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It blooms between September and November, producing small, white or pale pink flowers.

Prasophyllum hopsonii is listed as vulnerable due to habitat loss and land clearing.
