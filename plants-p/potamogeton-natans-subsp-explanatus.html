Potamogeton natans subsp. explanatus

Potamogeton natans subsp.

explanatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America. It is characterized by its long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences near the water's surface. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
