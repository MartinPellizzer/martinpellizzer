Premna suaveolens

Premna suaveolens, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweetroot, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, fleshy fruits.

Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
