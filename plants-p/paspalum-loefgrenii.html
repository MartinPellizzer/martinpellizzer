Paspalum loefgrenii

Paspalum loefgrenii, commonly known as the dwarf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover.
