Polygala lanceolata

Polygala lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala lanceolata is often found in the central and western United States, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
