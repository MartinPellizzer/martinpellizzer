Phyllanthus securinegioides

Phyllanthus securinegioides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
