Polygala exilis subsp. parviflora

Polygala exilis subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, arranged in loose spikes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
