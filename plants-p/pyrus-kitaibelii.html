Pyrus kitaibelii

Pyrus kitaibelii, commonly known as the Kitaibel's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus kitaibelii is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soils.
