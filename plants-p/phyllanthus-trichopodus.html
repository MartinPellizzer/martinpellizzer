Phyllanthus trichopodus

Phyllanthus trichopodus, commonly known as the Indian liverwort or stonecress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Phyllanthus trichopodus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
