Polygonum tataricum

Polygonum tataricum, commonly known as red root or tatarian knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant has reddish roots that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties. Its leaves are heart-shaped and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

Polygonum tataricum is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
