Persicaria macrantha

Persicaria macrantha, commonly known as rose campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features erect, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, which bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Persicaria macrantha is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
