Passiflora adiantum

Passiflora adiantum, commonly known as the silver leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
