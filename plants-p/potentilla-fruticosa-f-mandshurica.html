Potentilla fruticosa f. mandshurica

Potentilla fruticosa f. mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian cinquefoil, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cold climates.
