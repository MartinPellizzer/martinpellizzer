Pavonia multiflora

Pavonia multiflora, commonly known as the Florida pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist, sunny conditions.
