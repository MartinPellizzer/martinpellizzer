Psophocarpus longepedunculatus var. barteri

Psophocarpus longepedunculatus var. barteri, commonly known as the giant cowpea, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, pale yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its long peduncles, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds.
