Peperomia triplinervis

Peperomia triplinervis, commonly known as the Three-Nerved Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a short, upright stem with a rosette of leaves that helps it retain moisture. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or reddish and appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

Peperomia triplinervis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
