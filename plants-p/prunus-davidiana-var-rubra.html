Prunus davidiana var. rubra

Prunus davidiana var. rubra, commonly known as the red-leaf Chinese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes particularly striking in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and can reach up to 10 centimeters in length.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
