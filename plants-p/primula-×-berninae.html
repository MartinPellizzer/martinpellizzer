Primula × berninae

Primula × berninae, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 centimeters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The hybrid nature of Primula × berninae results from crossbreeding between different species of primroses, leading to a wide variety of cultivars.
