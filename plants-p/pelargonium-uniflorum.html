Pelargonium uniflorum

Pelargonium uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often grown as an ornamental in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium uniflorum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
