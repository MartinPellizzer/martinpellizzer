Pedilonum petiolatum

Pedilonum petiolatum, commonly known as the slender-leaved orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter, which is typical of many orchid species.

Due to habitat loss, Pedilonum petiolatum is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
