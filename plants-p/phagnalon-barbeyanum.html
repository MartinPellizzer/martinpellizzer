Phagnalon barbeyanum

Phagnalon barbeyanum, commonly known as the Barbey's phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Phagnalon barbeyanum is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
