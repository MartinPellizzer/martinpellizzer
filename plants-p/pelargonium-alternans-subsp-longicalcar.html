Pelargonium alternans subsp. longicalcar

Pelargonium alternans subsp.

longicalcar, commonly known as the long-spurred geranium, is a member of the Geraniaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals that give it its common name.

The plant typically has upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
