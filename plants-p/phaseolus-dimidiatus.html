Phaseolus dimidiatus

Phaseolus dimidiatus, commonly known as the dwarf bean or small-seeded bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically produces small, white or pale pink flowers and has compound leaves with three leaflets. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional agriculture and as a forage crop.
