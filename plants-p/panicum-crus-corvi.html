Panicum crus-corvi

Panicum crus-corvi, commonly known as the crow's foot grass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, tufted inflorescence that resembles a crow's foot. The plant typically grows in wet or disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Panicum crus-corvi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
