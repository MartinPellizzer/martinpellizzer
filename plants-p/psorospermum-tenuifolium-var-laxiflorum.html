Psorospermum tenuifolium var. laxiflorum

Psorospermum tenuifolium var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-leaved psorospermum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, contributing to its ornamental value. Its fruits are small drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody capsule.

This species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in horticulture and ecological restoration.
