Phreatia hollandiana

Phreatia hollandiana, commonly known as the Holland's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, deriving moisture from the air and rain. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding environment.

Phreatia hollandiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in humid, shaded environments.
