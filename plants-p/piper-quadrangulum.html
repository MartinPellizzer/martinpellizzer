Piper quadrangulum

Piper quadrangulum, commonly known as the four-sided pepper or quadrangular pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its quadrangular (four-sided) stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and the fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

Piper quadrangulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
