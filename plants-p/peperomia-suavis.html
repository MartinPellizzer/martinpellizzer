Peperomia suavis

Peperomia suavis, commonly known as the sweet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in clusters during the growing season. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
