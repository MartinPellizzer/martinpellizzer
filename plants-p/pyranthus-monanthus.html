Pyranthus monanthus

Pyranthus monanthus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pyranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of white or pale blue blooms.

Pyranthus monanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
