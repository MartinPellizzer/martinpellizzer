Polygala glaucoides var. triflora

Polygala glaucoides var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
