Potentilla chrysopetala

Potentilla chrysopetala, commonly known as the golden petal potentilla, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces showy yellow flowers in the summer months. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
