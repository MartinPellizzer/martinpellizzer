Phaca glabriuscula

Phaca glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth phaca, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and pale blue or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
