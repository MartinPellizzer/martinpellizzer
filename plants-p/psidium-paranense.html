Psidium paranense

Psidium paranense, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Psidium paranense make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
