Pectis linifolia var. marginalis

Pectis linifolia var. marginalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structure. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss. Its inflorescence consists of yellow disk flowers surrounded by pale green to white ray florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its succulent leaves and deep root system.
