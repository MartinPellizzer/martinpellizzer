Panicum uncinatum

Panicum uncinatum, commonly known as curly topgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, curly, barbed inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Curly topgrass plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
