Papaya papaya

Papaya papaya, commonly known as the pawpaw, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a large, succulent fruit that is typically harvested when it is fully ripe and slightly soft. The fruit contains a juicy, sweet pulp surrounding a central core of seeds.

Papaya is also valued for its latex, which has various industrial and medicinal applications.
