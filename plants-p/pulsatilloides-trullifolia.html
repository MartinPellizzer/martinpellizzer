Pulsatilloides trullifolia

Pulsatilloides trullifolia, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive purple or brown throat. The plant has a compact, clump-forming growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that bear opposite leaves. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
