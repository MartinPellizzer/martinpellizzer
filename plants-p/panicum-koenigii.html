Panicum koenigii

Panicum koenigii, commonly known as curry leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, elongated leaves that are widely used in South Asian cuisine. The plant belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is often grown as an ornamental and culinary species. Its leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and aroma.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
