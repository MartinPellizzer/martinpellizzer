Puccinellia skottsbergii

Puccinellia skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, hairless stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, exhibiting deep root systems and reduced leaf surface area.

Puccinellia skottsbergii plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
