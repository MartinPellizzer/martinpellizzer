Persea ichangensis

Persea ichangensis, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are typically orange to reddish in color when mature. The fruit has a sweet, nutty flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
