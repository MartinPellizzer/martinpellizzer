Pitcairnia cerrateana

Pitcairnia cerrateana, commonly known as the Cerrado pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect water, a common adaptation in tropical environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with long, spadix-like flowers, often in shades of red and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species within the Bromeliaceae family.
