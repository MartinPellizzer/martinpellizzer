Piptocarpha ramiflora var. gardneri

Piptocarpha ramiflora var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
