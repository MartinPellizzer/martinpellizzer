Polypodium subimmersum f. mengtzeanum

Polypodium subimmersum f. mengtzeanum, commonly known as the Mengtzeanum water fern, is a fern species characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It features delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically submerged or floating in water, adapting to its wetland or aquatic environments. The plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in slow-moving or still freshwater habitats. Its rhizomes are often rooted in the substrate, providing structural support and facilitating nutrient absorption.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in aquatic ecosystems.
