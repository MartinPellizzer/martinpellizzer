Piper amphioxys

Piper amphioxys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, dark blue berries.

The plant is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
