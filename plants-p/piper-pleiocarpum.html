Piper pleiocarpum

Piper pleiocarpum, commonly known as the many-seeded pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into elongated, multi-seeded fruits. Its botanical features make it distinct from other Piper species due to its unique fruit structure.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests where it thrives in partial shade.
