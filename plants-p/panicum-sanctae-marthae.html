Panicum sanctae-marthae

Panicum sanctae-marthae, commonly known as the sacred grass of Saint Martha, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and delicate, feathery panicles. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are arranged in loose, branching clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its historical and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and symbolic value.
