Phlomis aurea

Phlomis aurea, commonly known as golden beardgrass, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that turns reddish with age. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while its root system is fibrous and robust.

Phlomis aurea is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
