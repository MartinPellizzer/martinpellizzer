Polygonum subsagittatum

Polygonum subsagittatum, commonly known as the arrow-leaved dock, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features alternate, arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, typically blooming in late summer.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
