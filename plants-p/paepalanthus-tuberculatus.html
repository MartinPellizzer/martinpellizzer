Paepalanthus tuberculatus

Paepalanthus tuberculatus, commonly known as the spiked pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globular stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptations to arid environments include reduced leaves and thick, fleshy stems for water retention.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or desert regions.
