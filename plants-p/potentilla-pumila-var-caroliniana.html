Potentilla pumila var. caroliniana

Potentilla pumila var. caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has wiry, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
