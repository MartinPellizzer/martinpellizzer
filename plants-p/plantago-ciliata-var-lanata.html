Plantago ciliata var. lanata

Plantago ciliata var. lanata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender, erect stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is commonly used in traditional herbal remedies.
