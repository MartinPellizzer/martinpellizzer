Polygonum plebeium var. griffithii

Polygonum plebeium var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often zigzagging stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red or pink. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.
