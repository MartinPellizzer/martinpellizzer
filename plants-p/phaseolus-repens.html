Phaseolus repens

Phaseolus repens, commonly known as the prostrate runner bean, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. Its seeds are edible and often used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is closely related to other legumes such as common beans.
