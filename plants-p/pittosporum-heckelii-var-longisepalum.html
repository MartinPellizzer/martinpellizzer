Pittosporum heckelii var. longisepalum

Pittosporum heckelii var. longisepalum, commonly known as the long-sepal pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
