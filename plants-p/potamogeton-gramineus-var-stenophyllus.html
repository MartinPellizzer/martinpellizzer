Potamogeton gramineus var. stenophyllus

Potamogeton gramineus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used as a bioindicator in ecological studies.
