Pomaria canescens

Pomaria canescens, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in open habitats such as pine flatwoods and scrublands. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Pomaria canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
