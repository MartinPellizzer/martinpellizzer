Philodendron heterocraspedon

Philodendron heterocraspedon, commonly known as the red-veined satin pothos, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have a distinctive red and green veining pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, fleshy berries containing bright red seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

Philodendron heterocraspedon is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
