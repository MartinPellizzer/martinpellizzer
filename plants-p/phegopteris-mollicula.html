Phegopteris mollicula

Phegopteris mollicula, commonly known as the soft maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including China, Japan, and the Philippines. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a soft, velvety texture, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
