Pelea oblongifolia

Pelea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pelea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

Pelea oblongifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.
