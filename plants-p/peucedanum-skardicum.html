Peucedanum skardicum

Peucedanum skardicum, commonly known as the Skardic cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Peucedanum skardicum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
