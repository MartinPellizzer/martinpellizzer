Potentilla medioxima

Potentilla medioxima, commonly known as the broadleaf potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with multiple stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Potentilla medioxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
