Pollia miranda

Pollia miranda, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its long, climbing stems and showy, hooded flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Pollia miranda thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
