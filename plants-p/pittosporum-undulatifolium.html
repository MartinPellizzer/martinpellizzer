Pittosporum undulatifolium

Pittosporum undulatifolium, commonly known as the undulating-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a wavy, undulating margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, spherical berries. It is often found in coastal and lowland forests, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in temperate regions.
