Philotheca brucei subsp. brevifolia

Philotheca brucei subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain germander, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open woodlands and heathlands. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and small, ovate leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
