Potentilla argentea var. dissecta

Potentilla argentea var. dissecta, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white leaves that give it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in upright cymes. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
