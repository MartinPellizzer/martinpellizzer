Piptatherum coerulescens var. stramineum

Piptatherum coerulescens var. stramineum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, silver-green blades and produces fluffy, pale blue to white spikelets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive color and texture.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
