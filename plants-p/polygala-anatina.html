Polygala anatina

Polygala anatina, commonly known as the Anatolian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala anatina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
