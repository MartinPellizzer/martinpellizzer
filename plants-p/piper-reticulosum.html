Piper reticulosum

Piper reticulosum, commonly known as the net-veined pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries.

Piper reticulosum is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and berries, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
