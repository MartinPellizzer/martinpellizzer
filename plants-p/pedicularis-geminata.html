Pedicularis geminata

Pedicularis geminata, commonly known as the twinflower, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, pink or red bilaterally symmetrical flowers. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is known for its unique floral structure that resembles a pair of joined flowers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
