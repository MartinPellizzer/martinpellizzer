Phlox × oliverorum

Phlox × oliverorum, commonly known as the Oliver's phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Phlox × oliverorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
