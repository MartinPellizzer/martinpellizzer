Pelargonium × amabile

Pelargonium × amabile, commonly known as the double-flowered geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that resemble small roses, with numerous petals arranged in layers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, often with a hairy texture, and the stems are usually square in cross-section.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
