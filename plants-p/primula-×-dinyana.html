Primula × dinyana

Primula × dinyana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and pink, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, often found in woodland areas.
