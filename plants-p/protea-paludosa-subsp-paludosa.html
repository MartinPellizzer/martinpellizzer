Protea paludosa subsp. paludosa

Protea paludosa subsp.

paludosa, commonly known as the swamp protea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a dense cluster of small, papery flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape.

The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils near the coast, often in areas with high humidity and salt exposure. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom during the summer months.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
