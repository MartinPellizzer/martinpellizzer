Plantago pachyphylla

Plantago pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it well adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
