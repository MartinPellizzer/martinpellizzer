Pilosocereus pleurocarpus

Pilosocereus pleurocarpus, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, white spines and a rounded, ribbed stem. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
