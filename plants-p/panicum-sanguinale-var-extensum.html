Panicum sanguinale var. extensum

Panicum sanguinale var. extensum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to parts of North America.

It is a warm-season grass characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant typically produces inflorescences that are spike-like and often reddish in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for erosion control and ornamental use.

Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in soil stabilization.
