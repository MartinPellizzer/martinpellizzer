Panicum divaricatum var. stenostachyum

Panicum divaricatum var. stenostachyum, commonly known as narrow-stalked millet, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale to golden flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
