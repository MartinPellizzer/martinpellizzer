Phaeopappus gaubae

Phaeopappus gaubae, commonly known as the yellow pappus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
