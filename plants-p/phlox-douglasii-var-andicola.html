Phlox douglasii var. andicola

Phlox douglasii var. andicola, commonly known as the Andes phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
