Parthenium densipilum

Parthenium densipilum, commonly known as rough horseweed, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Parthenium densipilum is also known for its allergenic properties, contributing to hay fever in some individuals.
