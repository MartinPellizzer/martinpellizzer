Parnassia rhombipetala

Parnassia rhombipetala, commonly known as the diamondleaf parnassia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Parnassiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rhombic-shaped petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its flowers are white with a yellowish center, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are eaten by various bird species.

Parnassia rhombipetala is notable for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
