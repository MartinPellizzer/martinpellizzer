Pyrus euaria

Pyrus euaria, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that appear in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits known as pomes, which are typically yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus euaria is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ecological and historical significance.
