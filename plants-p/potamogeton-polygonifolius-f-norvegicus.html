Potamogeton polygonifolius f. norvegicus

Potamogeton polygonifolius f. norvegicus, commonly known as the Norwegian pondweed, is a freshwater plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The subspecies norvegicus is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to the typical form of P. polygonifolius.
