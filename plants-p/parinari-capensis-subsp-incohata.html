Parinari capensis subsp. incohata

Parinari capensis subsp.

incohata, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and savanna woodlands.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.
