Pandanus javanicus

Pandanus javanicus, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a unique and often striking appearance. The plant has a woody stem that is often covered in a thick, fibrous layer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and typically have a waxy texture.

Pandanus javanicus is often used in traditional crafts and can also be found growing near coastal areas.
