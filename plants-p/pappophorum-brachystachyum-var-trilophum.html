Pappophorum brachystachyum var. trilophum

Pappophorum brachystachyum var. trilophum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed pappophorum, is a grass species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in fine, hairy structures called pappi. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
