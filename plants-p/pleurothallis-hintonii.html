Pleurothallis hintonii

Pleurothallis hintonii, commonly known as the Hinton's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow throat. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
