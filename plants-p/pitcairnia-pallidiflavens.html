Pitcairnia pallidiflavens

Pitcairnia pallidiflavens, commonly known as the pale-yellow pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking inflorescences with flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on rock faces or in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
