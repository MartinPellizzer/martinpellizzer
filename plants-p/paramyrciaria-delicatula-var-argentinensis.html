Paramyrciaria delicatula var. argentinensis

Paramyrciaria delicatula var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine spider orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, intricate flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring or early summer, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
