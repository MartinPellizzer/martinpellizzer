Phacelia hastata var. leucophylla

Phacelia hastata var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the plant. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
