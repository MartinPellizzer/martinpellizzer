Pachyrhizus panamensis

Pachyrhizus panamensis, commonly known as the Panama bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural systems. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for its nutritional seeds.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable farming practices due to its adaptability and resilience.
