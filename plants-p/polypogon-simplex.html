Polypogon simplex

Polypogon simplex, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
