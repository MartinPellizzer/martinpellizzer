Pachylobus edulis var. mubafo

Pachylobus edulis var. mubafo, commonly known as the Mubafo tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellow or greenish hues. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
