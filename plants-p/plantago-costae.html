Plantago costae

Plantago costae, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and fibrous root system.
