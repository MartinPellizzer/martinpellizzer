Potentilla albertii

Potentilla albertii, commonly known as Albert's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided into five leaflets, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla albertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
