Podocarpus nagi var. caesius

Podocarpus nagi var. caesius, commonly known as the silver podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a silvery-blue sheen, giving it its common name. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It typically grows in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and ecological benefits.
