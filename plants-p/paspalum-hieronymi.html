Paspalum hieronymi

Paspalum hieronymi, commonly known as the Brazilian paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to a wide range of soil types and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.

Paspalum hieronymi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
