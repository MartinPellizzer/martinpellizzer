Pisum variegatum

Pisum variegatum, commonly known as the variable pea, is a species within the legume family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its variable leaf patterns, which can include mottled or spotted foliage, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, and it produces pods containing several seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf variegation.
