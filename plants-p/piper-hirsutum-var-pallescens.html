Piper hirsutum var. pallescens

Piper hirsutum var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale-hirsute pepper plant, is a variant of the creeping pepper vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
