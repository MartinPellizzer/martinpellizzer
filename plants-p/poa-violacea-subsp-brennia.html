Poa violacea subsp. brennia

Poa violacea subsp.

brennia, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces dense, tufted clumps of dark green to purplish foliage.

The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or violet hues. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
