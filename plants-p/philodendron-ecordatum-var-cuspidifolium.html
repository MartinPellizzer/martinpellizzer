Philodendron ecordatum var. cuspidifolium

Philodendron ecordatum var. cuspidifolium, commonly known as the Pointed-leaf Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a hanging plant.
