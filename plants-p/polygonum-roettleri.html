Polygonum roettleri

Polygonum roettleri, commonly known as the Red Polygonum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Polygonum roettleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
