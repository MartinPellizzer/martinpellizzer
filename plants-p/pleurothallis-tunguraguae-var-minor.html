Pleurothallis tunguraguae var. minor

Pleurothallis tunguraguae var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
