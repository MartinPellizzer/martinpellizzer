Phyllostachys rubicunda

Phyllostachys rubicunda, commonly known as the red bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern China and is characterized by its striking red stems and leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and forms dense clumps. Its foliage is often a deep green with a reddish tint, especially in the sun-exposed areas.

Phyllostachys rubicunda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
