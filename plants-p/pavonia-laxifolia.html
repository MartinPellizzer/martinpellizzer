Pavonia laxifolia

Pavonia laxifolia, commonly known as the slender pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia laxifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
