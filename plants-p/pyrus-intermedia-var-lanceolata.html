Pyrus intermedia var. lanceolata

Pyrus intermedia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a cultivar of the pear tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is typically small, sweet, and juicy, with a smooth, thin skin. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blossoms.

This variety is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a range of soil types, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
