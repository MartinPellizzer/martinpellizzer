Phaius daenikeri

Phaius daenikeri, commonly known as the Daeniker's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy floral structure. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its upright, strap-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive purple or brown lip.

Phaius daenikeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
