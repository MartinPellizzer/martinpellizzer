Peperomia umbellata

Peperomia umbellata, commonly known as the Umbellate Peperomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often display a vibrant green color. This plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

It is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in humid environments, though it is sensitive to overwatering.
