Prunus × lannesiana f. kirigaya

Prunus × lannesiana f. kirigaya, commonly known as the Kirigaya cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, double-flowered blooms that appear in shades of white and pink. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in its ornamental value. It is typically grown as a deciduous shrub or small tree, thriving in temperate climates.

The foliage is dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy flowers.
