Passiflora laticaulis

Passiflora laticaulis, commonly known as the broad-canal passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora laticaulis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
