Piper variipilum

Piper variipilum, commonly known as the variable pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Piper variipilum is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its medicinal properties.
