Plantago princeps

Plantago princeps, commonly known as the royal plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a medicinal plant for its potential health benefits.
