Peritassa myrsinoides

Peritassa myrsinoides, commonly known as the red-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and striking red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with a sticky coating, aiding in attachment to host plants. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
