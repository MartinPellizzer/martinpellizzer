Pleurothallis brachiloba

Pleurothallis brachiloba, commonly known as the short-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a slender, often branching stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts in controlled environments.
