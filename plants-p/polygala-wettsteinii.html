Polygala wettsteinii

Polygala wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and the flowers are often arranged in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

It is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
