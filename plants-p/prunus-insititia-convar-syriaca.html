Prunus insititia convar. syriaca

Prunus insititia convar. syriaca, commonly known as the Syrian blackthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by its distinctive fruit.

The shrub is often used in ornamental gardening and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
