Pleopeltis dulitensis

Pleopeltis dulitensis, commonly known as the Dullit's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic and lithophytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the leaves.

Pleopeltis dulitensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
