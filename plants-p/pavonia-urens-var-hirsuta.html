Pavonia urens var. hirsuta

Pavonia urens var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright, branching stems and hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
