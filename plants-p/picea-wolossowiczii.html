Picea wolossowiczii

Picea wolossowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows at high altitudes, often in subalpine or alpine zones, where it forms dense forests or stands. The tree has slender, upward-curving needles and produces cones that hang downward, characteristic of the fir genus. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and craftsmanship.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
