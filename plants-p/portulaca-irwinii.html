Portulaca irwinii

Portulaca irwinii, commonly known as the Mexican moss rose, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to yellow, often with a distinctive orange throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Portulaca irwinii is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
