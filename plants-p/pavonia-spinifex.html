Pavonia spinifex

Pavonia spinifex, commonly known as the prickly pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Pavonia spinifex is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
