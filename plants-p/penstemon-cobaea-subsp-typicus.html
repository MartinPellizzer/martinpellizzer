Penstemon cobaea subsp. typicus

Penstemon cobaea subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant produces numerous narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
