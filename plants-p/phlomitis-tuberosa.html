Phlomitis tuberosa

Phlomitis tuberosa, commonly known as the purple mountain phlomis, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, lavender-pink to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
