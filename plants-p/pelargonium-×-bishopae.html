Pelargonium × bishopae

Pelargonium × bishopae, commonly known as the bishop's geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × bishopae is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to various growing conditions.
