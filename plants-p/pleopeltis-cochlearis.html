Pleopeltis cochlearis

Pleopeltis cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged fronds that give it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a dense, velvety texture.

Pleopeltis cochlearis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to shaded conditions.
