Potamogeton coloratus var. siculus

Potamogeton coloratus var. siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often bright green and can exhibit a reddish tinge under certain environmental conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense spikes.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
