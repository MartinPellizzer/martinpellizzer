Potentilla persica minor

Potentilla persica minor, commonly known as Persian shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces yellow or white flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla persica minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
