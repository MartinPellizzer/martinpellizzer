Pterocephalus frutescens var. tomentellus

Pterocephalus frutescens var. tomentellus, commonly known as the woolly pterocephalus, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color and covered in fine, white pubescence.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
