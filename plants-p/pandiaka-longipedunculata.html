Pandiaka longipedunculata

Pandiaka longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed pandiaka, is a unique member of the Pandiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its elongated, branching inflorescences that bear small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are broad and slightly lobed, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species in its native range.
