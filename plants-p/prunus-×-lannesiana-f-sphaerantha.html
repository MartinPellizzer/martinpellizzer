Prunus × lannesiana f. sphaerantha

Prunus × lannesiana f. sphaerantha, commonly known as the globeflower cherry, is a unique hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globe-shaped inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dense, dark green foliage. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
