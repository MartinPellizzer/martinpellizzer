Phyllanthus antillanus var. glaucescens

Phyllanthus antillanus var. glaucescens, commonly known as the glossy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a waxy, bluish-green appearance, which gives it its common name. Its flowers are small and yellowish, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
