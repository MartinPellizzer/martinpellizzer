Pultenaea microphylla var. cinerascens

Pultenaea microphylla var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf pultenaea, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
