Primula valentiniana

Primula valentiniana, commonly known as Valentine's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous stamens that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and is also considered a symbol of love and fertility.
