Psidium myrsinites

Psidium myrsinites, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden landscapes.

However, it is not typically grown for its fruit, as the fruit is small and not particularly flavorful.
