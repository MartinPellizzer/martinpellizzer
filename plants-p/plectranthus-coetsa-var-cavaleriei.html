Plectranthus coetsa var. cavaleriei

Plectranthus coetsa var. cavaleriei, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
