Paspalum oaxacense

Paspalum oaxacense, commonly known as the Oaxacan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum oaxacense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
