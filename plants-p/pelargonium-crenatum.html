Pelargonium crenatum

Pelargonium crenatum, commonly known as the scalycup geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its scalloped, lobed leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers, which can range in color from pink to red and purple.
