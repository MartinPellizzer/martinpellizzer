Persea longipeda

Persea longipeda, commonly known as the long-leaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It produces large, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same plant.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its edible fruit, which has a creamy texture and a mild flavor.
