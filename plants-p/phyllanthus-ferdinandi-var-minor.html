Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. minor

Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces tiny, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
