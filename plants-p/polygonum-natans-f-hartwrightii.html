Polygonum natans f. hartwrightii

Polygonum natans f. hartwrightii, commonly known as the floating knotweed.

This plant is a variant of the common knotweed, characterized by its unique growth habit of floating on the water surface. It has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of pink or red.

It is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, contributing to aquatic ecosystems through its role in stabilizing water surfaces.
