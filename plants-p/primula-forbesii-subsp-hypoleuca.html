Primula forbesii subsp. hypoleuca

Primula forbesii subsp.

hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forest margins at high elevations. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
