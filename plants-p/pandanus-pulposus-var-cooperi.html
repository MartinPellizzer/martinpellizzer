Pandanus pulposus var. cooperi

Pandanus pulposus var. cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces fragrant, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental features. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring well-drained, sandy soils.

The species is notable for its resilience to salt spray and its ability to withstand periodic flooding.
