Potentilla glandulosa var. procera

Potentilla glandulosa var. procera, commonly known as the tall cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered in glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.
