Pouteria peruviana

Pouteria peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible fruits that have a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit's unique flavor and texture make it a valuable crop in certain regions.
