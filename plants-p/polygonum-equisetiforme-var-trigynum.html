Polygonum equisetiforme var. trigynum

Polygonum equisetiforme var. trigynum, commonly known as the three-styled smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as in marshes, ditches, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a distinctive white midrib.

The three-styled smartweed is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often found in agricultural areas.
