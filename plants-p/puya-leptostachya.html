Puya leptostachya

Puya leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stemmed puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a striking visual display. Its flowers are typically orange or red, and it blooms once in its lifetime before dying.

Puya leptostachya is adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
