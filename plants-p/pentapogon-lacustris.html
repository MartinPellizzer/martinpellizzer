Pentapogon lacustris

Pentapogon lacustris, commonly known as the lake sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and lake margins, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
