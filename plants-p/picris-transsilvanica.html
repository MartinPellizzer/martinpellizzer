Picris transsilvanica

Picris transsilvanica, commonly known as Transylvanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Eastern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
