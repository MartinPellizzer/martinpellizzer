Polygonum thymifolium

Polygonum thymifolium, commonly known as thyme-leaved polygonum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Polygonum thymifolium is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
