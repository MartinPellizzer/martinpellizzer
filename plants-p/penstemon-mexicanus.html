Penstemon mexicanus

Penstemon mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to red, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon mexicanus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
