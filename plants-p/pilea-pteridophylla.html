Pilea pteridophylla

Pilea pteridophylla, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to China.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its round, glossy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea pteridophylla is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
