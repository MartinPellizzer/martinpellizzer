Phaseolus vulgaris var. cardinalis

Phaseolus vulgaris var. cardinalis, commonly known as the cardinal bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and white flowers, which bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its trifoliate leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture.

The pods are usually green and contain several bean seeds that are edible when mature.
