Polygonella laetevirens

Polygonella laetevirens, commonly known as the green moss-like plant, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and distinctive leaf structures.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths on moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. The plant's leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a glossy appearance, with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.

Polygonella laetevirens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
