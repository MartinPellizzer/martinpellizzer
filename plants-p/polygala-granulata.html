Polygala granulata

Polygala granulata, commonly known as the granulate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala granulata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
