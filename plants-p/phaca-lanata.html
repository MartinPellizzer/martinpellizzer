Phaca lanata

Phaca lanata, commonly known as the woolly phaca, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
