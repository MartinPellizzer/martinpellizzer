Paspalum dilatatum

Paspalum dilatatum, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, blue to purple flowers. This plant is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions. It is widely used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Paspalum dilatatum is also valued for its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
