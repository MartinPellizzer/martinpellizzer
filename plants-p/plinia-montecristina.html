Plinia montecristina

Plinia montecristina, commonly known as the Brazilian white sapote, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, oblong fruits with a smooth, pale yellow to white skin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet, creamy flesh that is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
