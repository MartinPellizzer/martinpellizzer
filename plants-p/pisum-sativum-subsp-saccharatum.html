Pisum sativum subsp. saccharatum

Pisum sativum subsp.

saccharatum, commonly known as sugar pea, is a subspecies of the pea plant characterized by its edible pods and seeds. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant produces climbing vines with small, pea-shaped pods that contain sweet, edible seeds. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and white or pink flowers.

This subspecies is cultivated for both its seeds and pods, which are used in culinary and agricultural contexts.
