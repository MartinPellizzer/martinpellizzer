Phalaenopsis lobbii var. vietnamensis

Phalaenopsis lobbii var. vietnamensis, commonly known as the Vietnam phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant flower spikes that bear multiple showy, white to pale yellow blossoms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and commercial purposes.
