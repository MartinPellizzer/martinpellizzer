Persea malayana

Persea malayana, commonly known as the Malayan persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and potential economic importance make it a significant species in botanical studies.
