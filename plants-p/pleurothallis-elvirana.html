Pleurothallis elvirana

Pleurothallis elvirana, commonly known as the Elvirana orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and green.

Pleurothallis elvirana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
