Parrya nudicaulis subsp. rydbergii

Parrya nudicaulis subsp.

rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's nakedstem parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that rise from a basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
