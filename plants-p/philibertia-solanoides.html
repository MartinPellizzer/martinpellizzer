Philibertia solanoides

Philibertia solanoides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
