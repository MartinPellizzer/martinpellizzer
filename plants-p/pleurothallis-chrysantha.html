Pleurothallis chrysantha

Pleurothallis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its blooms are often clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, intricate structure.

Pleurothallis chrysantha is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
