Persicaria chinensis var. nicobarica

Persicaria chinensis var. nicobarica, commonly known as the Nicobar knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
