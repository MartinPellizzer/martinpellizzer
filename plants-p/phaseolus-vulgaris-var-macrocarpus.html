Phaseolus vulgaris var. macrocarpus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its large, thick pods and seeds that are typically larger than those of standard kidney beans. The plant is an annual herb that grows upright, with trifoliate leaves and tendrils for climbing. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.
