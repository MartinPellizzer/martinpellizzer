Pyrrhocactus pilispinus

Pyrrhocactus pilispinus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in spines that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
