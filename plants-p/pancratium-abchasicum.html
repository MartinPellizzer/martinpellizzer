Pancratium abchasicum

Pancratium abchasicum, commonly known as the Abchazian crown imperial, is a striking perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, red-orange flowers that resemble a crown. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a bulbous root system typical of many Amaryllidaceae species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside its native range.
