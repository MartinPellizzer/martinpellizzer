Piper mikanianum var. mikanianum

Piper mikanianum var. mikanianum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, elongated berries that transition from green to red as they mature. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement and possesses a distinctive, aromatic scent. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to other pepper species.
