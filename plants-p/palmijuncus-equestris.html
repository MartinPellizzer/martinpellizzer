Palmijuncus equestris

Palmijuncus equestris, commonly known as the horse palm, is a rare and unique plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes most palm species, but it exhibits characteristics more akin to a shrub than a typical palm. The plant has a short trunk and produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contrasting with the broad leaves of most palms.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
