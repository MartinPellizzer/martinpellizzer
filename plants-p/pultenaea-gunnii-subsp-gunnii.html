Pultenaea gunnii subsp. gunnii

Pultenaea gunnii subsp.

gunnii, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late winter to early spring, which are clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
