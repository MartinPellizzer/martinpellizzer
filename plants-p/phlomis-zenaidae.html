Phlomis zenaidae

Phlomis zenaidae, commonly known as the Armenian phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-white pubescence, and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy, pale purple to lilac flowers are arranged in dense terminal clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Phlomis zenaidae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
