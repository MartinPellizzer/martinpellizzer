Pyrus × magdeburgensis

Pyrus × magdeburgensis, commonly known as the Magdeburg pear, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (wood pear).

It is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a hardy species. The plant typically produces small, round fruits with a yellowish-green skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The Magdeburg pear is also valued for its resistance to certain diseases, which enhances its suitability for both garden and commercial cultivation.
