Polygala hyemalis

Polygala hyemalis, commonly known as the winter polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, often branched stems that grow up to 30 inches tall, adorned with small, delicate flowers in shades of pink to white. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The winter polygala blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle floral display to the landscape.
