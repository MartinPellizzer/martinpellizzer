Persicaria portoricensis

Persicaria portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts that are often reddish in color.

Persicaria portoricensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
