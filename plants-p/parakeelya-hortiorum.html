Parakeelya hortiorum

Parakeelya hortiorum, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that range in color from deep green to variegated yellow and white patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by ornamental red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Parakeelya hortiorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
