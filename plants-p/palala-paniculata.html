Palala paniculata

Palala paniculata, commonly known as the pink panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that form panicles. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats. It is adapted to warm climates and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Palala paniculata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small fauna.
