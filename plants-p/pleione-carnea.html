Pleione carnea

Pleione carnea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and flowering stems that bear clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Orchidaceae family.

Pleione carnea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
