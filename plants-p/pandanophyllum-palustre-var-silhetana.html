Pandanophyllum palustre var. silhetana

Pandanophyllum palustre var. silhetana, commonly known as the Silhet swamp pandan, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional cooking. The plant thrives in moist, swampy environments and is adapted to low-light conditions. Its leaves contain aromatic compounds that contribute to its distinctive fragrance.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
