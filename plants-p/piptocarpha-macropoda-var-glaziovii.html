Piptocarpha macropoda var. glaziovii

Piptocarpha macropoda var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, pale blue or white flowers clustered in dense heads. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xerophytic environments and it is of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
