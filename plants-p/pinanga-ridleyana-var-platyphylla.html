Pinanga ridleyana var. platyphylla

Pinanga ridleyana var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix and a spathe. The plant produces edible corms that are widely used in culinary applications. Its leaves are large, broad, and often have a waxy texture, which helps in water retention in its humid habitat.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in lowland forests.
