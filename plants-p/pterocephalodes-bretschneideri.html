Pterocephalodes bretschneideri

Pterocephalodes bretschneideri, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
