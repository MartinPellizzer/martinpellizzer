Phaseolus microspermus

Phaseolus microspermus, commonly known as the small-seeded bean, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, yellow flowers and pods containing tiny, dark seeds. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Due to its unique seed structure, it is of interest in botanical studies and has potential for agricultural use.
