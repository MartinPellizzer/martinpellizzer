Panicum peninsularum

Panicum peninsularum, commonly known as the Peninsular grass, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

Panicum peninsularum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in coastal areas.
