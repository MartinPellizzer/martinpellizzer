Primula limbata

Primula limbata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy coating.

Primula limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
