Priva virgata

Priva virgata, commonly known as the silverleaf privet, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually a deep green on top and silvery white underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. These flowers are followed by small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Priva virgata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
