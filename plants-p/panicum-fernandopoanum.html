Panicum fernandopoanum

Panicum fernandopoanum, commonly known as the dwarf panicgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
