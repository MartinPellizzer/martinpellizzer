Passiflora linda

Passiflora linda, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or black berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora linda is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
