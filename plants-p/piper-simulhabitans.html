Piper simulhabitans

Piper simulhabitans, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
