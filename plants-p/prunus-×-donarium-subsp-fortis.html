Prunus × donarium subsp. fortis

Prunus × donarium subsp.

fortis, commonly known as the Japanese black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and upright growth habit.

The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red or black berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant yellow in autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
