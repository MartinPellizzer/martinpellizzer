Pilosella dubia

Pilosella dubia, commonly known as the slender hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and lance-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella dubia is often found in open habitats and is considered a minor weed in some regions.
