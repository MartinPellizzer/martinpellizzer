Piper puberulibaccum

Piper puberulibaccum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
