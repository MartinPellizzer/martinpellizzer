Premna dubia

Premna dubia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves and stems contain mucilage, which has been used in traditional medicine for its soothing properties.

Premna dubia is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in forest edges and wastelands.
