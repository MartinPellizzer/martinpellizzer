Paspalum guaraniticum

Paspalum guaraniticum, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Guarani grass, is a perennial bunchgrass native to South America.

It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed areas, and is adapted to both wet and dry conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptation in tropical and subtropical environments.
