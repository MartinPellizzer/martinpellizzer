Penstemon cleburnei

Penstemon cleburnei, commonly known as Cleburne's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon cleburnei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
