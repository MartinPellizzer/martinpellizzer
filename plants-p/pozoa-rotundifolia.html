Pozoa rotundifolia

Pozoa rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf pozoa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with rounded tips, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental potential and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
