Plinia

Plinia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a genus of flowering plants in the Myrtaceae family.

It comprises several species, many of which are native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plants are shrubs or small trees, often characterized by their attractive, showy flowers and edible fruits. The fruit, similar to a small cherry, is typically red or yellow when ripe and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Botanically, Plinia species are notable for their aromatic leaves and their role in tropical ecosystems.
