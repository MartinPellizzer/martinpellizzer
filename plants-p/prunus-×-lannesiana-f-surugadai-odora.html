Prunus × lannesiana f. surugadai-odora

Prunus × lannesiana f. surugadai-odora, commonly known as the fragrant Japanese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate pink flowers that emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, making it a prized ornamental plant. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata, resulting in its distinct botanical features. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with lance-shaped leaves and a compact, rounded form.

This variety is well-suited for gardens and landscapes, where its aesthetic and aromatic qualities enhance the environment.
