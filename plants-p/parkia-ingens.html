Parkia ingens

Parkia ingens, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often resembling flames. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
