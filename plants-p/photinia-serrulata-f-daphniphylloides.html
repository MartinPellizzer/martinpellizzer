Photinia serrulata f. daphniphylloides

Photinia serrulata f. daphniphylloides, commonly known as the Daphniphylloides photinia, is a cultivar of the photinia genus native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental shrub. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that add seasonal interest.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
