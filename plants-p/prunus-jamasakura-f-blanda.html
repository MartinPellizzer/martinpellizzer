Prunus jamasakura f. blanda

Prunus jamasakura f. blanda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate aesthetic to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

The species is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
