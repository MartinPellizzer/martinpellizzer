Plantago tehuelcha

Plantago tehuelcha, commonly known as the Andean plantain or Chilean plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
