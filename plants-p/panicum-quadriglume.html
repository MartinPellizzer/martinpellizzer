Panicum quadriglume

Panicum quadriglume, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are encased in a hull that splits into four parts, giving it the species name "quadriglume.".

It is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
