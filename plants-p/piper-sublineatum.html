Piper sublineatum

Piper sublineatum, commonly known as the pepperleaf plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves contain compounds that have been used for their potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
