Platanthera peramoena f. doddsiae

Platanthera peramoena f. doddsiae, commonly known as the Dodds' lady's-sock, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinct, fragrant scent. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or along stream banks. It has a sympodial growth habit, with short, horizontal rhizomes and erect, leafy stems.
