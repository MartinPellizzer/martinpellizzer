Piper politaereum var. elongatum

Piper politaereum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper politaereum species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppery fruits, similar to other Piper species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
