Piper obliquum var. subeximium

Piper obliquum var. subeximium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper obliquum species native to the Andes region of South America.

This plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature, and they are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species within the Piper genus.
