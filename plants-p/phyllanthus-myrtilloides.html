Phyllanthus myrtilloides

Phyllanthus myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry myrtle, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are often a deep blue or purple when mature. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and medicinal properties.
