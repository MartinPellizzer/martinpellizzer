Piper ponapense

Piper ponapense, commonly known as the Ponape pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a peppery substance used in traditional cooking. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and cultural importance in the region.
