Pfaffia lanata var. peteriana

Pfaffia lanata var. peteriana, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces reddish-brown berries that contain the seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its roots, which are believed to have adaptogenic effects.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern.
