Phytolacca micrantha

Phytolacca micrantha, commonly known as the small-fruited lacca, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its succulent stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its roots and stems contain toxic compounds, making it a plant with both medicinal and cautionary significance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
