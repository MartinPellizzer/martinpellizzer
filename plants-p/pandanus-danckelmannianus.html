Pandanus danckelmannianus

Pandanus danckelmannianus, commonly known as the Danckelman's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
