Plantago stricta var. divaricata

Plantago stricta var. divaricata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained environments.

This species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
