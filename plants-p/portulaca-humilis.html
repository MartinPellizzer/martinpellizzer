Portulaca humilis

Portulaca humilis, commonly known as the common purslane, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Americas.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the sun. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in disturbed soils. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it highly drought-tolerant.

Portulaca humilis is also known for its edible parts, including the leaves and stems, which are used in various cuisines.
