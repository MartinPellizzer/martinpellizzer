Passiflora capsularis var. geminifolia

Passiflora capsularis var. geminifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces green, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically divided into two opposite lobes, giving it the "twin-leaf" appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
