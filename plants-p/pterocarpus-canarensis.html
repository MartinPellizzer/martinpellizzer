Pterocarpus canarensis

Pterocarpus canarensis, commonly known as the Karnataka rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for reforestation efforts.
