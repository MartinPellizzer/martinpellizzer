Paphiopedilum emersonii

Paphiopedilum emersonii, commonly known as the Emerson's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-shaped lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow with deep red or brown markings, and it blooms during the summer months.

Paphiopedilum emersonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
