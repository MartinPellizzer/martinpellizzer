Primula heucherifolia

Primula heucherifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals arranged in a bell shape. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom early in the growing season.

Its foliage and flowers contribute to its role in both wild and cultivated landscapes.
