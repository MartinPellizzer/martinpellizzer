Piper sandianum

Piper sandianum, commonly known as the Sandia pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing aromatic, peppery seeds. The seeds are harvested and used as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both culinary and traditional medicinal purposes.
