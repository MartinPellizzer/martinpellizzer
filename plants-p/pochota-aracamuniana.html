Pochota aracamuniana

Pochota aracamuniana, commonly known as the Andean cashew, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing edible, sweet, and nutty seeds. Its seeds are often used in traditional Andean cuisine and are also valued for their nutritional content.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.
