Potamogeton lucens f. coriaceus

Potamogeton lucens f. coriaceus, commonly known as the smooth pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its smooth, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant is submerged, with long, flexible stems that often grow in shallow waters. Its flowers are small and green, and it produces elliptical, greenish fruits.

This form is adapted to tolerate a range of water conditions and is often found in ponds and slow-moving streams.
