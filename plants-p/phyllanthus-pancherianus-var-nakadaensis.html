Phyllanthus pancherianus var. nakadaensis

Phyllanthus pancherianus var. nakadaensis, commonly known as the Nakada's spurge, is a variant of the spurge genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is notable for its unique morphological features and ecological adaptability.
