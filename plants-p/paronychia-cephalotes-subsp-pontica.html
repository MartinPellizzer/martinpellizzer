Paronychia cephalotes subsp. pontica

Paronychia cephalotes subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The plant features small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
