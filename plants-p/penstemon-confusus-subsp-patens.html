Penstemon confusus subsp. patens

Penstemon confusus subsp.

patens, commonly known as the western penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
