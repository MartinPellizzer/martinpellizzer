Pleroma chamaecistus

Pleroma chamaecistus, commonly known as the rock dudleya, is a succulent plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its dense, fleshy foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
