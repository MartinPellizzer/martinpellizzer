Pancratium fragrans

Pancratium fragrans, commonly known as the sweet cycad, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, palm-like leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, especially at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and aromatic blooms, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
