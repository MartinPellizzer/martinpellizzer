Phoradendron pellucidulum

Phoradendron pellucidulum, commonly known as the transparent mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are adapted for attachment to host plants. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

The species is notable for its unique ecological role in supporting biodiversity within forest ecosystems.
