Potamogeton praelongus subvar. elegans

Potamogeton praelongus subvar. elegans, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often slightly curled and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and streams.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
