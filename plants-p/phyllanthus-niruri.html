Phyllanthus niruri

Phyllanthus niruri, commonly known as cheesefruit or stonebreaker, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, green, round leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hard, green fruits that turn yellow or red when mature. It is often found growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability.

Botanically, it belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other plants with similar morphological features.
