Peucedanum coriaceum var. pospichalii

Peucedanum coriaceum var. pospichalii, commonly known as the Balkan cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered white flowers. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
