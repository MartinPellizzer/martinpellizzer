Pentachondra rubra

Pentachondra rubra, commonly known as the red pentachondra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a reddish tint. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
