Phyllanthus preissianus

Phyllanthus preissianus, commonly known as the African date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its palm-like leaves, which are alternate, simple, and often lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
