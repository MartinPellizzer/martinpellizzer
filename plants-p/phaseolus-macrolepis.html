Phaseolus macrolepis

Phaseolus macrolepis, commonly known as the large-leaf bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing small, kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for food use, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
