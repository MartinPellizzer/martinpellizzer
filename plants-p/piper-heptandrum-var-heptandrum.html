Piper heptandrum var. heptandrum

Piper heptandrum var. heptandrum, commonly known as the seven-threaded pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in herbal remedies.
