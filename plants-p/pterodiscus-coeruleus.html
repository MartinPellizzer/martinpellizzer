Pterodiscus coeruleus

Pterodiscus coeruleus, commonly known as the blue pterodiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and unique, disc-like floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Pterodiscus coeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
