Pilosella densiflora subsp. xanthothyrsa

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

xanthothyrsa, commonly known as the yellow-hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has small, palmately divided leaves and produces small, five-petaled flowers.
