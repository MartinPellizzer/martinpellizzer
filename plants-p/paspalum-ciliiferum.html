Paspalum ciliiferum

Paspalum ciliiferum, commonly known as the silky grass or silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, soft leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum ciliiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
