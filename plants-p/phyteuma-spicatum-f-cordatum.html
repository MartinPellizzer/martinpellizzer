Phyteuma spicatum f. cordatum

Phyteuma spicatum f. cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved phyteuma, is a decorative perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features striking, elongated leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces tall spikes of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
