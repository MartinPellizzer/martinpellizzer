Philadelphus lewisii subsp. californicus

Philadelphus lewisii subsp.

californicus, commonly known as California mock orange, is a shrub native to the western United States. It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early summer.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of habitats, including coastal bluffs and dry slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
