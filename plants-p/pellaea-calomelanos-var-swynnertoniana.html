Pellaea calomelanos var. swynnertoniana

Pellaea calomelanos var. swynnertoniana, commonly known as the Swynnerton's rock fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is typically found in mountainous or coastal regions. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the undersides of its fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
