Pentapetes coccinea

Pentapetes coccinea, commonly known as the red pentapetes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing species. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually elliptical in shape.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.
