Pelargonium × baumannianum

Pelargonium × baumannianum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as citrus, mint, or spice, depending on the cultivar. The plant typically has a bushy growth habit with lobed or serrated foliage and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative and aromatic plant.

The essential oils found in its leaves are widely used in perfumes, aromatherapy, and natural insect repellents.
