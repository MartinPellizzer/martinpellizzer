Persicaria grandifolia

Persicaria grandifolia, commonly known as the largeleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered in soft, hairy foliage. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a prominent midrib, and they vary in size along the stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are typically pink to red in color, appearing in late summer.

The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
