Plumeria phagedaenica

Plumeria phagedaenica, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces large, palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender stems and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
