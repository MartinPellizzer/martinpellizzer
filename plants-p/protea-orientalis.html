Protea orientalis

Protea orientalis, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous overlapping bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

The King Protea is also valued for its ornamental use in horticulture and floral arrangements.
