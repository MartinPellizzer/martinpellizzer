Polygonum subsessile

Polygonum subsessile, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its leaves are thin and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum subsessile is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a persistent weed in certain habitats.
