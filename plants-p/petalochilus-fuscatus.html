Petalochilus fuscatus

Petalochilus fuscatus, commonly known as the dark orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to brownish petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Petalochilus fuscatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
