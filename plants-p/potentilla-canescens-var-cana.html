Potentilla canescens var. cana

Potentilla canescens var. cana, commonly known as the silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
