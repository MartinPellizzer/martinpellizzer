Paspalum saurae

Paspalum saurae, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a grass species native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in dune environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and preventing erosion.

Paspalum saurae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
