Persoonia myrtilloides subsp. cunninghamii

Persoonia myrtilloides subsp.

cunninghamii, commonly known as the purple spike heath, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland environments, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
