Potentilla stipitata

Potentilla stipitata, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Potentilla stipitata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and other wetland habitats.
