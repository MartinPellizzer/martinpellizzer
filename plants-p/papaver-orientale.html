Papaver orientale

Papaver orientale, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking ornamental plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with palmate leaves that are hairy and lobed. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a dramatic visual impact in gardens.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has been adapted to various climates with proper care.
