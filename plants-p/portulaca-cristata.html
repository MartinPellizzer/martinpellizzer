Portulaca cristata

Portulaca cristata, commonly known as the crown of thorns or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close during the heat of the day. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a hardy plant for arid environments.

Portulaca cristata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
