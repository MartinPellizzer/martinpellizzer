Psyllium chamaepsyllium

Psyllium chamaepsyllium, commonly known as psyllium husk, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the plant family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a long, slender seed capsule that contains fine, fibrous husks, which are the primary part used in herbal remedies and dietary supplements. The plant is also valued for its ability to absorb water, making it useful in both culinary and medicinal applications.
