Paloveopsis emarginata

Paloveopsis emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-margin paloverde, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and pale green to yellowish bark. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by small, dry fruits. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a slow growth rate.

Paloveopsis emarginata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
