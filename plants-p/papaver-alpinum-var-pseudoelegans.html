Papaver alpinum var. pseudoelegans

Papaver alpinum var. pseudoelegans, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and a distinctive, tall, slender stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, high-altitude climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
