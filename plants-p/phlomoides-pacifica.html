Phlomoides pacifica

Phlomoides pacifica, commonly known as Pacific blueweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue, contributing to its striking appearance.

Phlomoides pacifica is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
