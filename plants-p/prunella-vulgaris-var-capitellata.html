Prunella vulgaris var. capitellata

Prunella vulgaris var. capitellata, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with square stems and opposite leaves that are hairy and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens.

Selfheal is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
