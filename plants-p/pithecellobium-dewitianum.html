Pithecellobium dewitianum

Pithecellobium dewitianum, commonly known as the dwarf acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow, pea-like flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains edible seeds, often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological contributions in its native habitat.
