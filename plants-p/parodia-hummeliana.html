Parodia hummeliana

Parodia hummeliana, commonly known as the Hummel's pincushion cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by cylindrical, green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
