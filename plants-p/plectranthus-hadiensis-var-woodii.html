Plectranthus hadiensis var. woodii

Plectranthus hadiensis var. woodii, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a compact, aromatic shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or purple flowers, which are highly fragrant and attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
