Phreatia falcata

Phreatia falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often near water sources such as swamps or marshes. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, which is crucial for its survival in wetland habitats.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
