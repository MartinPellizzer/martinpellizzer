Petunia axillaris subsp. subandina

Petunia axillaris subsp.

subandina, commonly known as the Andean petunia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the cool, high-altitude climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
