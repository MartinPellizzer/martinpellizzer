Paeonia trollioides

Paeonia trollioides, commonly known as the Tibetan peony, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green foliage and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Paeonia trollioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
