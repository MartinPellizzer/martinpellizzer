Psidium haitiense

Psidium haitiense, commonly known as Haitian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
