Paspalum foveolatum

Paspalum foveolatum, commonly known as the silvergrass or foveolate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, and thrives in full sun. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Paspalum foveolatum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.
