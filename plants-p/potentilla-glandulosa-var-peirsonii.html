Potentilla glandulosa var. peirsonii

Potentilla glandulosa var. peirsonii, commonly known as the Peirson's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in glandular dots, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
