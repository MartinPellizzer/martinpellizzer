Pandanus decipiens

Pandanus decipiens, commonly known as the false pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a pineapple, is edible and has a sweet, fibrous texture.

Pandanus decipiens thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas or in wetlands.
