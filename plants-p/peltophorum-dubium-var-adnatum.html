Peltophorum dubium var. adnatum

Peltophorum dubium var. adnatum, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often planted in urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
