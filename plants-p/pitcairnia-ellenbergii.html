Pitcairnia ellenbergii

Pitcairnia ellenbergii, commonly known as the Ellenberg's pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the humid tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This evergreen perennial is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces striking inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.
