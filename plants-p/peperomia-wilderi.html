Peperomia wilderi

Peperomia wilderi, commonly known as the wilderi peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, often adorned with subtle veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of greenish-white, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both home and office environments.
