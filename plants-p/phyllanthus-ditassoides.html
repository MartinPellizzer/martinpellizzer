Phyllanthus ditassoides

Phyllanthus ditassoides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
