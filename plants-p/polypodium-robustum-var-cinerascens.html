Polypodium robustum var. cinerascens

Polypodium robustum var. cinerascens, commonly known as the gray-leafed cliff fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically grayish in color due to a dense covering of trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients in arid conditions.

The spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
