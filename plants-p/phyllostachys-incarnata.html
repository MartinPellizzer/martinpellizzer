Phyllostachys incarnata

Phyllostachys incarnata, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to reddish-purple culms, which become more intense as the plant matures. This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting rapid growth and a clumping habit.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide privacy and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
