Parinari parvifolia

Parinari parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved parinari, is a tropical tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptic leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by round, yellowish fruits that split open to reveal sticky, fleshy arils. It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific, and thrives in humid, warm climates.

The tree is often found in secondary forests and is valued for its wood and potential medicinal properties.
