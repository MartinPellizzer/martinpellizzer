Pyrus communis var. hortensis

Pyrus communis var. hortensis, commonly known as the common pear, is a cultivar of the European pear tree.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in the autumn. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a spreading crown.

This variety is valued for its fruit quality and is widely cultivated in temperate regions for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
