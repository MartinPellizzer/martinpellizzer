Polygala viminalis var. casuarina

Polygala viminalis var. casuarina, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a silvery or grayish underside, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
