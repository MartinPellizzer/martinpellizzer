Phyllarthron multiflorum

Phyllarthron multiflorum, commonly known as the multiblade fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features multiple small, clustered leaves that grow in a dense, feathery pattern. Its reproductive structures are borne on specialized stalks, and it produces spores rather than seeds.

Phyllarthron multiflorum is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
