Potentilla pastoralis

Potentilla pastoralis, commonly known as the meadow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five to seven leaflets that are oval and serrated.

Potentilla pastoralis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
