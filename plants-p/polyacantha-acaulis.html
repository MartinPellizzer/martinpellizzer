Polyacantha acaulis

Polyacantha acaulis, commonly known as the stemless hibiscus, is a unique plant characterized by its lack of a distinct stem, with leaves directly emerging from the root system.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, often with a contrasting throat. Due to its striking appearance and compact growth habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
