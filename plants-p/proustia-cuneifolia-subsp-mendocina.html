Proustia cuneifolia subsp. mendocina

Proustia cuneifolia subsp.

mendocina, commonly known as the Mendocino pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its name reflects its historical association with the Mendocino region and its delicate, long-lasting flowers.
