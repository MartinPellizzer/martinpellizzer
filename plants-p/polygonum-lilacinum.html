Polygonum lilacinum

Polygonum lilacinum, commonly known as blue polygonum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a bluish hue, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pale blue to purple.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
