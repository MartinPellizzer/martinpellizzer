Pelargonium geranioides

Pelargonium geranioides, commonly known as the waxleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.

Pelargonium geranioides is typically grown as a houseplant or in gardens where it thrives in warm, sunny conditions.
