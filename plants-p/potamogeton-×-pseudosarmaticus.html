Potamogeton × pseudosarmaticus

Potamogeton × pseudosarmaticus, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton sarmaticus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. It has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes.

Its presence can indicate good water quality, as it thrives in clean, well-oxygenated habitats.
