Pulicaria aromatica

Pulicaria aromatica, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic foliage and long-lasting petals make it a popular choice for dried floral arrangements.

Pulicaria aromatica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
