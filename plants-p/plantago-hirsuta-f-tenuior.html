Plantago hirsuta f. tenuior

Plantago hirsuta f. tenuior, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its shorter, more compact stature.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in urban environments.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
