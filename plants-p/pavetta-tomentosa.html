Pavetta tomentosa

Pavetta tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its hairy, greyish leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Pavetta tomentosa is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks.
