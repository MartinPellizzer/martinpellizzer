Petiveria alliacea var. tetrandra

Petiveria alliacea var. tetrandra, commonly known as "chicory root" or "huitlacoche," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils. Its leaves are often used as a vegetable, while the roots are valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant also produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.
