Phellandrium divaricatum

Phellandrium divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed lemon myrtle, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant produces small white flowers followed by oval, lemon-scented fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and cliff faces.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is valued in both horticulture and traditional herbal practices.
