Penstemon cobaea subsp. purpureus

Penstemon cobaea subsp.

purpureus, commonly known as the purple giant penstemon, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 6 feet in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a pollinator attractor, supporting bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
