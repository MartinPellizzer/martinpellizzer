Passiflora tesserula

Passiflora tesserula, commonly known as the tesserula passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Passiflora tesserula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
