Phoenix humilis var. typica

Phoenix humilis var. typica, commonly known as the Arabian date palm, is a small, evergreen palm native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant is known for its hardiness in arid environments and its ability to tolerate drought. It bears small, edible dates that are typically harvested in the summer months.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical significance in desert regions.
