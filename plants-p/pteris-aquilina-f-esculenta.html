Pteris aquilina f. esculenta

Pteris aquilina f. esculenta, commonly known as the edible water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, bi-pinnate fronds that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are edible and have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

This fern is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for both culinary and decorative purposes.
