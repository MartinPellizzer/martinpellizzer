Pleioblastus oedogonatus

Pleioblastus oedogonatus, commonly known as the bamboo grass or the Japanese bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely textured leaves. Native to East Asia, it thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant exhibits clumping growth habits and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring.

Its foliage provides a delicate, feathery appearance that makes it popular in garden landscapes.
