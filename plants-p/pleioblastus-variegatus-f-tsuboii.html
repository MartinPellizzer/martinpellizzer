Pleioblastus variegatus f. tsuboii

Pleioblastus variegatus f. tsuboii, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a compact, clumping bamboo that exhibits striking variegated foliage.

Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and display a combination of green, white, and yellow hues, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Due to its slow growth and dense foliage, it is an excellent choice for erosion control and as a groundcover in shaded areas.
