Polygonum purpureonervosum

Polygonum purpureonervosum, commonly known as the purple-veined knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its stout stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with prominent purple veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, adding visual interest in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
