Polygonatum latifolium var. commutatum

Polygonatum latifolium var. commutatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with large, heart-shaped leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
