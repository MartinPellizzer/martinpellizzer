Prunus maximowiczii f. aperta

Prunus maximowiczii f. aperta, commonly known as the open-flowered Chinese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that open fully, contrasting with the more closed blooms of the typical species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with smooth, grey-barked stems and ovate, serrated leaves. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are typically pink to white and are followed by small, edible berries.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
