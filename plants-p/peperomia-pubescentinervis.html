Peperomia pubescentinervis

Peperomia pubescentinervis, commonly known as the velvety-veined peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and white, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

Peperomia pubescentinervis is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
