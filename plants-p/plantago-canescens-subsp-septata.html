Plantago canescens subsp. septata

Plantago canescens subsp.

septata, commonly known as the silver plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features a rosette of broad, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
