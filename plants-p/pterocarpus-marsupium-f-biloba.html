Pterocarpus marsupium f. biloba

Pterocarpus marsupium f. biloba, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-brown wood. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
