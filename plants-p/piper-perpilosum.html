Piper perpilosum

Piper perpilosum, commonly known as the woolly pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine.

Piper perpilosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
