Poa hauptiana

Poa hauptiana, commonly known as the Haupt's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Poa hauptiana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
