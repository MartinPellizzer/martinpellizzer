Platychilum

Platychilum, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their flat, strap-like leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. Platychilum species typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique morphology and ecological adaptations to humid, shaded environments.
