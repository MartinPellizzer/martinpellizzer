Primula magellanica

Primula magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and thrives in shaded or partially sunny environments. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
