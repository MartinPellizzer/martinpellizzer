Peperomia circumscissa

Peperomia circumscissa, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or green. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its minimal water requirements.

Its compact growth habit makes it an ideal choice for container gardening and as a ground cover in shaded areas.
