Pyrus yamatensis

Pyrus yamatensis, commonly known as the Yamatogami or Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by white to yellow fruits that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus yamatensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its fruit, though it is less commonly grown than other pear species.
