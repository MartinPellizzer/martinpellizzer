Polygala glochidiata var. pallida

Polygala glochidiata var. pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, spiny leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
