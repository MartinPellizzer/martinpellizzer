Piptochaetium tuberculatum

Piptochaetium tuberculatum, commonly known as the tuberculate grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
