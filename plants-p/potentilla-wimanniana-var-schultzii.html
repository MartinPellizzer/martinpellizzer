Potentilla wimanniana var. schultzii

Potentilla wimanniana var. schultzii, commonly known as the Schultze's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a coarse texture and a slightly hairy surface.

The species is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
