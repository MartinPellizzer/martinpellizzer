Prunus cerasifera var. iranica

Prunus cerasifera var. iranica, commonly known as the Iranian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountains of Iran.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The Iranian cherry is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
