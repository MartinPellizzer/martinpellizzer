Polypodium paradoxum

Polypodium paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds, which are divided into narrow, linear segments that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to substrates, while its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium paradoxum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
