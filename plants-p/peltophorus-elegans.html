Peltophorus elegans

Peltophorus elegans, commonly known as the elegant peacock fern, is a tropical climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its unique, feather-like fronds that resemble the tail of a peacock. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and climb along supports. Its leaves are bioluminescent in certain conditions, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
