Pogostemon strigosus

Pogostemon strigosus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves covered in fine hairs, and small, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.
