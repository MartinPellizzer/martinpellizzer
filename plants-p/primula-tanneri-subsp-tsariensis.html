Primula tanneri subsp. tsariensis

Primula tanneri subsp.

tsariensis, commonly known as the Tsarien primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
