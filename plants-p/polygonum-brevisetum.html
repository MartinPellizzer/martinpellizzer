Polygonum brevisetum

Polygonum brevisetum, commonly known as the short-stemmed knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple hue, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers in late summer.

Polygonum brevisetum is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of wetland habitats.
