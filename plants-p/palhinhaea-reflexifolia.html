Palhinhaea reflexifolia

Palhinhaea reflexifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds.

Palhinhaea reflexifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
