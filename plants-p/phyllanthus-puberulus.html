Phyllanthus puberulus

Phyllanthus puberulus, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

Phyllanthus puberulus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
