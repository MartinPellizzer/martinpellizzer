Pittosporum acutisepalum

Pittosporum acutisepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant features dark green, narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.
