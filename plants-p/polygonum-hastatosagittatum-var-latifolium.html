Polygonum hastatosagittatum var. latifolium

Polygonum hastatosagittatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from greenish-white to reddish-purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as stream banks and meadows.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
