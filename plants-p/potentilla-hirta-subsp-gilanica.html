Potentilla hirta subsp. gilanica

Potentilla hirta subsp.

gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan potentilla, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
