Phyllostachys nigra f. monstrosa

Phyllostachys nigra f. monstrosa, commonly known as the black bamboo with variegation, is a unique cultivar of the bamboo genus Phyllostachys.

It is characterized by its striking black stalks and variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. This plant is a form of the common black bamboo, Phyllostachys nigra, and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

The variegation in its leaves makes it a standout choice for adding visual interest to bamboo collections.
