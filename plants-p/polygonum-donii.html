Polygonum donii

Polygonum donii, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Polygonum donii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
