Pleurophora pungens f. imbricata

Pleurophora pungens f. imbricata, commonly known as the silverleaf pellitory, is a variant of the pungent pellitory species.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apiaceae, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs, giving the plant its distinctive silvery appearance. It produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
