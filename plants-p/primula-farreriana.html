Primula farreriana

Primula farreriana, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China and Korea.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation have made it a popular choice among gardeners.
