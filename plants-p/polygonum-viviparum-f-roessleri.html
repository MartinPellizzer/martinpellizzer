Polygonum viviparum f. roessleri

Polygonum viviparum f. roessleri, commonly known as the roessleri variant of the common knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit.

It features lanceolate to ovate leaves with prominent venation and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant is notable for its viviparous reproduction, where viable offspring develop on the mother plant before detaching. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
