Phaleria ambigua

Phaleria ambigua, commonly known as the white-berried champa, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
