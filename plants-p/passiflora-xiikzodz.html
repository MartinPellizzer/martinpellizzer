Passiflora xiikzodz

Passiflora xiikzodz, commonly known as the sacred passionflower, is a rare and striking species native to the highlands of Central America.

It is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural and medicinal significance in traditional practices.
