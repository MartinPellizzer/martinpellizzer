Potentilla rubripes var. saximontana

Potentilla rubripes var. saximontana, commonly known as the Sierra sandspurry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine, woolly hairs.

This variety is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.
