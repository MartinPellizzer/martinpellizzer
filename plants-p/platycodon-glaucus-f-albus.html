Platycodon glaucus f. albus

Platycodon glaucus f. albus, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a cultivar of the bellflower plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
