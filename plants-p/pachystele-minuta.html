Pachystele minuta

Pachystele minuta, commonly known as the dwarf pachystele, is a small, flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It is often found in rocky outcrops and dry, sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
