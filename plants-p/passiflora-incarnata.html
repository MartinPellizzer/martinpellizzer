Passiflora incarnata

Passiflora incarnata, commonly known as the sacred passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora incarnata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
