Poa nudata

Poa nudata, commonly known as the nakedleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. This plant is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

Poa nudata is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
