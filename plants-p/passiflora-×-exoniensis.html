Passiflora × exoniensis

Passiflora × exoniensis, commonly known as the Exeter passionflower, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces edible, orange-yellow berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Passiflora is known for its complex floral morphology and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
