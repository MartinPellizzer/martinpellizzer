Primulina modesta

Primulina modesta, commonly known as the modest primula, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
