Pyrus malus subsp. prasomila

Pyrus malus subsp.

prasomila, commonly known as the wild apple or crab apple, is a subspecies of the apple tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, often ornamental fruits that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pink and appear in clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its winter interest and fruit production.
