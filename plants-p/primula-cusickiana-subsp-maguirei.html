Primula cusickiana subsp. maguirei

Primula cusickiana subsp.

maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pink to white.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are broad and heart-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
