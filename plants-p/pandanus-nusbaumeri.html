Pandanus nusbaumeri

Pandanus nusbaumeri, commonly known as the Nusbaumer's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and have cultural significance in local communities.

The species is adapted to grow in coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
