Primula anisodora

Primula anisodora, commonly known as the Andes bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula anisodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
