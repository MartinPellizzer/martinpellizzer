Pelargonium pustulosum

Pelargonium pustulosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, almost woolly appearance. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Pelargonium pustulosum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
