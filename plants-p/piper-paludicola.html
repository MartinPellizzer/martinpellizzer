Piper paludicola

Piper paludicola, commonly known as the swamp pepper or water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a peppery aroma when crushed.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.
