Parietaria pensylvanica var. hespera

Parietaria pensylvanica var. hespera, commonly known as the western wall-rocket, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stinging hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences.

The species is known for its adaptability and ability to thrive in various soil types, making it a common sight in humid environments.
