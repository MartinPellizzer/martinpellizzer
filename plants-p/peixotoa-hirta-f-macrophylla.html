Peixotoa hirta f. macrophylla

Peixotoa hirta f. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved peixotoa, is a variant of the peixotoa genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, herbaceous growth habit and its distinctive, hairy foliage. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in dense inflorescences, and are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
