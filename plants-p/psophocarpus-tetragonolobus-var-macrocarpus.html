Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. macrocarpus

Psophocarpus tetragonolobus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces large, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, followed by distinctive, inflated pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with four leaflets and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its botanical features include hairy stems and stipules, which are small, leaf-like structures at the base of the leaflets.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cuisines.
