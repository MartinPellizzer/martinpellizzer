Phillyrea medianifolia subsp. vayedana

Phillyrea medianifolia subsp.

vayedana, commonly known as the Yemen phillyrea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark blue, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
