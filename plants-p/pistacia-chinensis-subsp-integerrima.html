Pistacia chinensis subsp. integerrima

Pistacia chinensis subsp.

integerrima, commonly known as the Chinese pistache, is a deciduous tree native to China and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are divided into 5-9 pairs of ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, orange-red drupes. Its bark is thick, fissured, and often reddish-brown, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly planted in urban landscapes for its drought tolerance and vibrant fall color.
