Pitcairnia ferruginea

Pitcairnia ferruginea, commonly known as the iron plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a water-holding structure at the center. Pitcairnia ferruginea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant floral display.
