Passiflora orbifolia

Passiflora orbifolia, commonly known as the orange passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange and red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces showy, intricate flowers with a unique structure featuring a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. Its fruits are edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

Passiflora orbifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
