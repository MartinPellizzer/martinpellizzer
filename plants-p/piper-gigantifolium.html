Piper gigantifolium

Piper gigantifolium, commonly known as the giant-leaf pepper, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens to a bright red, containing a single seed.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated in shaded gardens and greenhouses.
