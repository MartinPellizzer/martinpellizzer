Pyrus taochia

Pyrus taochia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. Pyrus taochia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
