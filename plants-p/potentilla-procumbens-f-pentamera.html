Potentilla procumbens f. pentamera

Potentilla procumbens f. pentamera, commonly known as the five-petaled potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and floral display.
