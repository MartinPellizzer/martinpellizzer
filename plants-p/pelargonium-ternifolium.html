Pelargonium ternifolium

Pelargonium ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
