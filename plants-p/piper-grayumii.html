Piper grayumii

Piper grayumii, commonly known as the gray's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture with a distinctive scent.

Piper grayumii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
