Pleurothallis claudii

Pleurothallis claudii, commonly known as the Claud's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often feature a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are usually white or pale green with intricate markings, and it blooms during the wet season.

Pleurothallis claudii is noted for its delicate appearance and is a popular species among orchid enthusiasts.
