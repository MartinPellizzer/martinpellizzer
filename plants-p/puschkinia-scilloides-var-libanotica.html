Puschkinia scilloides var. libanotica

Puschkinia scilloides var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon squill, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. Its bulbs are hardy and can naturalize over time, creating a striking display in gardens.

The flowers are attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
