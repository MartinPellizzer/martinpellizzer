Paracalia jungioides

Paracalia jungioides, commonly known as the Jungle Ghost Plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax, which helps to reduce water loss. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a sought-after specimen for collectors and enthusiasts of rare succulents.
