Prunus jamasakura f. densifolia

Prunus jamasakura f. densifolia, commonly known as the dense-flowered cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree characterized by its thick, dense clusters of flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant features small, oval leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its blossoms, which appear in early spring, are typically pale pink to white, creating a striking visual display.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and extended blooming period compared to other cherry species.
