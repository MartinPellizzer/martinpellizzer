Pilosocereus vilaboensis subsp. rizzoanus

Pilosocereus vilaboensis subsp.

rizzoanus, commonly known as the Rizzo's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, white to grayish spines and a ribbed, cylindrical stem.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand long periods of drought. It produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are often visited by nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
