Polygala pobeguinii

Polygala pobeguinii, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, spicate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, often blue or purple, flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
