Phyllomphax acuta

Phyllomphax acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are located on the underside of the fronds. Phyllomphax acuta thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique morphology and reproductive structures make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
