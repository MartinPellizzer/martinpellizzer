Plantago remota

Plantago remota, commonly known as the remote plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, producing wind-pollinated seeds.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential healing properties.
