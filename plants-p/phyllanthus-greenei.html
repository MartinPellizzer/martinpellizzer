Phyllanthus greenei

Phyllanthus greenei, commonly known as the Greene's phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Phyllanthus greenei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
