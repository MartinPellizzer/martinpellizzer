Pteris denticulata var. papyracea

Pteris denticulata var. papyracea, commonly known as the paper fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, papery fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with fronds that are typically bipinnate and covered in fine, tooth-like serrations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

The fronds are lightweight and can be used for decorative purposes or in traditional crafts.
