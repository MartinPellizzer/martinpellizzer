Primula rosea subsp. elegans

Primula rosea subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited for shady or partially shaded locations.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
