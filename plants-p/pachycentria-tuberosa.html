Pachycentria tuberosa

Pachycentria tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, clustered blue or purple flowers. The plant produces tuberous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and medicinal studies.
