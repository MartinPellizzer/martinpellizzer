Phebalium squamulosum subsp. longifolium

Phebalium squamulosum subsp.

longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved squamulous peacockwood, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, scale-like hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
