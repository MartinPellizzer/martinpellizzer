Pyrus pyraster f. curtipes

Pyrus pyraster f. curtipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a compact, shrub-like form of the common pear, characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, edible fruits. This variety is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by small, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
