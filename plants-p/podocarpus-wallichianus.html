Podocarpus wallichianus

Podocarpus wallichianus, commonly known as the Indian pine or Chinese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and durable wood.
