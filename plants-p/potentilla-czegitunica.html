Potentilla czegitunica

Potentilla czegitunica, commonly known as the Czegitun cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
