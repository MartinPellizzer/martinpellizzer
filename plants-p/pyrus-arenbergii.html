Pyrus arenbergii

Pyrus arenbergii, commonly known as the arenberg pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in jams and preserves.

It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
