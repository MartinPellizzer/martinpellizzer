Pinus tenuis

Pinus tenuis, commonly known as the slender pine, is a species of conifer native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches long. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often persist on the branches for several years. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, providing a distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
