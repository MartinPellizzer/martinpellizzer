Paspalum glabrum

Paspalum glabrum, commonly known as smooth paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in open fields.

Smooth paspalum is known for its ability to form dense vegetation, making it both a weed and a potential forage species.
