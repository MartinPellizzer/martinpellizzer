Pteriphis tripteris

Pteriphis tripteris, commonly known as the three-winged fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are divided into three distinct, narrow segments, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the undersides of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Overall, Pteriphis tripteris is a fascinating example of the diversity found within the fern group.
