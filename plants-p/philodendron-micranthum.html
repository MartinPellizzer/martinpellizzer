Philodendron micranthum

Philodendron micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron micranthum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
