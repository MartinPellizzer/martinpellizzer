Plumeria attenuata

Plumeria attenuata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and fragrance.

Plumeria attenuata is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal areas and is commonly used in landscaping in tropical regions.
