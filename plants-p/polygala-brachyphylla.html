Polygala brachyphylla

Polygala brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygala genus, which includes over 700 species, many of which are found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant typically grows as a small shrub, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
