Podalyria biflora

Podalyria biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered podalyria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family, characterized by its typical bean-like pods and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Podalyria biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
