Pipturus discolor

Pipturus discolor, commonly known as the variegated huckleberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded garden settings.
