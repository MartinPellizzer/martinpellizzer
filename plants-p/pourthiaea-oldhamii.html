Pourthiaea oldhamii

Pourthiaea oldhamii, commonly known as the oldham's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiky inflorescence that resembles a pincushion. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow or orange flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
