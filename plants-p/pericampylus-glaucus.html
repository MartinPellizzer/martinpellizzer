Pericampylus glaucus

Pericampylus glaucus, commonly known as the silverleaf pericampylus, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Pericampylus glaucus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
