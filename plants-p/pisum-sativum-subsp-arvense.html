Pisum sativum subsp. arvense

Pisum sativum subsp.

arvense, commonly known as field pea, is a subspecies of the pea plant native to temperate regions. It is characterized by its slender, trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, and the pods contain several oval, edible seeds. This subspecies is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is adapted to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It plays an important role in agricultural systems as a legume that fixes nitrogen in the soil.
