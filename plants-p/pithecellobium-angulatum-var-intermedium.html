Pithecellobium angulatum var. intermedium

Pithecellobium angulatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the angular pithecellobium, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with angular leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in disturbed soils and secondary forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
