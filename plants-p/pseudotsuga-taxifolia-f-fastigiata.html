Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. fastigiata

Pseudotsuga taxifolia f. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright form of the Japanese fir, is a compact, columnar evergreen conifer.

It features needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in a dense, vertical pattern. The plant produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It is native to Japan and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil.

This cultivar is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and structured appearance.
