Peristylus arachnoideus

Peristylus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spider-fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spiny leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
