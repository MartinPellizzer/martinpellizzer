Pimelea suaveolens subsp. flava

Pimelea suaveolens subsp.

flava, commonly known as the yellow pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a low-growing shrub with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and dry grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more intense yellow flower color compared to other varieties of Pimelea suaveolens.
