Picea koraiensis var. koraiensis

Picea koraiensis var. koraiensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Korean Peninsula.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is characterized by its conical shape and dense foliage. The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are small and cylindrical, maturing to a reddish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and reforestation efforts.
