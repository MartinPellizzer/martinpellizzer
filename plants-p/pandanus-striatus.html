Pandanus striatus

Pandanus striatus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus striatus is often grown for its ornamental value and cultural significance in island communities.
