Pouteria clementis

Pouteria clementis, commonly known as the Clementine sapodilla, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible fruits resembling small, round apples. The fruit has a sweet, creamy pulp with a mild, slightly acidic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
