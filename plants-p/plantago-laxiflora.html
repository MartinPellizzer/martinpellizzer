Plantago laxiflora

Plantago laxiflora, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its adaptability and resilience make it a common weed in various habitats.
