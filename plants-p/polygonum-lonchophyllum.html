Polygonum lonchophyllum

Polygonum lonchophyllum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are elongated with a smooth, narrow shape.

Polygonum lonchophyllum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
