Pinus pseudostrobus var. apulcensis

Pinus pseudostrobus var. apulcensis, commonly known as the Apulcensis Mexican Pine, is a rare and endemic variant of the Mexican pine found primarily in the Sierra Madre de Chiapas region of Mexico.

It is a tall, evergreen conifer with long, needle-like leaves and produces distinctive cone clusters. The tree exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to the humid, tropical montane climate of its native habitat. Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic makeup and ecological role within its restricted range.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
