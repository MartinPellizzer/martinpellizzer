Piratinera glaucifolia

Piratinera glaucifolia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells, which bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Piratinera glaucifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
