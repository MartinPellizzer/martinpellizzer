Pleurothallis linguifera

Pleurothallis linguifera, commonly known as the "tongue orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, strap-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its unique floral structure and leaf shape give it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
