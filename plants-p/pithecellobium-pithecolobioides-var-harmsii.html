Pithecellobium pithecolobioides var. harmsii

Pithecellobium pithecolobioides var. harmsii, commonly known as the Harms' velvet tamarind, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

The leaves are also used as a vegetable in some regions, adding to its ecological and cultural significance.
