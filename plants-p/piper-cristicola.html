Piper cristicola

Piper cristicola, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small drupe, contains the seeds used to produce white pepper.

The botanical features of Piper cristicola make it an important species in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
