Potamogeton proteus lucens

Potamogeton proteus lucens, commonly known as the bright pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly translucent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Potamogeton proteus lucens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
