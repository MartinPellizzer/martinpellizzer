Polygonum pacificum

Polygonum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features erect, somewhat woody stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Polygonum pacificum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
