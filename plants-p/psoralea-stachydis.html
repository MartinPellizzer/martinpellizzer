Psoralea stachydis

Psoralea stachydis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and characteristic pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers contribute to its ornamental value in gardens.

Psoralea stachydis is also known for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating skin conditions.
