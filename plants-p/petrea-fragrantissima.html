Petrea fragrantissima

Petrea fragrantissima, commonly known as the sweet shrub or fragrant petrea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is evergreen, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.

Petrea fragrantissima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
