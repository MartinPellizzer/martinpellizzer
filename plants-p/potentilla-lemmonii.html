Potentilla lemmonii

Potentilla lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla lemmonii thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
