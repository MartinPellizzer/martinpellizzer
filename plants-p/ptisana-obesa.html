Ptisana obesa

Ptisana obesa, commonly known as the fat-tailed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its tropical and subtropical habitats. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Ptisana obesa thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
