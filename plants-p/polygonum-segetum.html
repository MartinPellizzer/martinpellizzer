Polygonum segetum

Polygonum segetum, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its roots are often harvested for culinary use, while the leaves are also edible and used in various traditional dishes.

Polygonum segetum thrives in moist, tropical environments and is commonly cultivated in wetland areas.
