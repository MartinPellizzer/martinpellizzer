Podocarpus palawanensis

Podocarpus palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests. It plays a significant role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing habitat for various species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
