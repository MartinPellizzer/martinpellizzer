Prunus serotina var. serotina

Prunus serotina var. serotina, commonly known as black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Prunus serotina var. serotina is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional medicine.
