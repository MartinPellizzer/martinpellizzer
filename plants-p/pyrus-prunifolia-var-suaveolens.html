Pyrus prunifolia var. suaveolens

Pyrus prunifolia var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its fragrant flowers and edible fruits.

It is native to parts of Asia and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blossoms. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color. The fruit, which matures in late summer, is smaller than standard pears and has a sweet, delicate flavor.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible produce.
