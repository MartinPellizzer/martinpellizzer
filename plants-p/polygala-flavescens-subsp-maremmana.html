Polygala flavescens subsp. maremmana

Polygala flavescens subsp.

maremmana, commonly known as the Maremmans' milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of tiny, clustered flowers. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salty and arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
