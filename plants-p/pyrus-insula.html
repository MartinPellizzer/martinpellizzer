Pyrus insula

Pyrus insula, commonly known as the island pear, is a rare species native to the island of Socotra.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has distinctive, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, round pear-like fruit with a thick, leathery skin.

The species is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its native habitat, including high temperatures and seasonal rainfall.
