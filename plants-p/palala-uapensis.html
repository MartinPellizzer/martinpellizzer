Palala uapensis

Palala uapensis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a distinctive plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the Cyatheaceae family and is known for its slow growth rate and longevity. Its unique structure makes it a key component of the forest understory in its natural habitat.

Palala uapensis plays an important ecological role in providing shelter and habitat for various native species.
