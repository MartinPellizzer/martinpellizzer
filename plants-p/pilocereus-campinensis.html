Pilocereus campinensis

Pilocereus campinensis, commonly known as the Campinense Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny areoles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
