Paphiopedilum villosum f. affine

Paphiopedilum villosum f. affine, commonly known as the hairy villosum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spurred lip and velvety foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive, fleshy labellum that aids in pollination.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
