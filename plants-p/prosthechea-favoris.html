Prosthechea favoris

Prosthechea favoris, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces striking, orchid-like flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding its survival in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and fleshy, while its flowers bloom in clusters with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Prosthechea favoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
