Pereilema setarioides

Pereilema setarioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pereilema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
