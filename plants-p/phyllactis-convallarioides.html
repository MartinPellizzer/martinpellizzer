Phyllactis convallarioides

Phyllactis convallarioides, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
