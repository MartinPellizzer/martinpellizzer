Ponthieva multiflora

Ponthieva multiflora, commonly known as the multiflora ponthieva, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

However, it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
