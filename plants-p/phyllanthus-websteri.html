Phyllanthus websteri

Phyllanthus websteri, commonly known as the Webster's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Phyllanthus websteri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
