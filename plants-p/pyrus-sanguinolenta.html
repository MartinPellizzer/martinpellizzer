Pyrus sanguinolenta

Pyrus sanguinolenta, commonly known as the red-stemmed pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
