Potentilla peduncularis var. curta

Potentilla peduncularis var. curta, commonly known as the short-stemmed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, clustered leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its short, upright stems and dense foliage make it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
