Polygala × kotovii

Polygala × kotovii, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of alternate, narrow leaves, and it produces yellow or greenish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
