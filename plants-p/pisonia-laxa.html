Pisonia laxa

Pisonia laxa, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its drooping clusters of small, white flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or dense forests.

Its fruits are capsules that release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation.
