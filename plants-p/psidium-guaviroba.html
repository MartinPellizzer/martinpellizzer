Psidium guaviroba

Psidium guaviroba, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit has a sweet and acidic flavor, making it a popular ingredient in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
