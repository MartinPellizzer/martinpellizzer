Polyosma buxea

Polyosma buxea, commonly known as the Buxa boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage composed of small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers that are followed by red berries. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to form dense hedges.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Ericaceae and is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
