Potamogeton pectinatus f. zosteroides

Potamogeton pectinatus f. zosteroides, commonly known as the ribbonweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, mat-forming structure. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are smooth and have a distinctive pectinate (toothed) margin, which aids in its identification.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
