Pilosella subcymigera subsp. radiata

Pilosella subcymigera subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the starry hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which are typically covered in a dense layer of short, white to grayish hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
