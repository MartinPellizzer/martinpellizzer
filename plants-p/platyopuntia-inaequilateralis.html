Platyopuntia inaequilateralis

Platyopuntia inaequilateralis, commonly known as the unequal-flowered prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red-fleshed fruits. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
