Peperomia rupiceda

Peperomia rupiceda, commonly known as the rock peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Peperomia rupiceda is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and minimal care requirements.
