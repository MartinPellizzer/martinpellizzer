Populus afghanica var. tadshikistanica

Populus afghanica var. tadshikistanica, commonly known as the Tadjikistan poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. This plant is often found in river valleys and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its wood is lightweight and has been used traditionally for construction and furniture.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
