Potentilla anserina f. delitschiana

Potentilla anserina f. delitschiana, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the alpine regions of Europe. The plant features wiry, branched stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady, moist environments.
