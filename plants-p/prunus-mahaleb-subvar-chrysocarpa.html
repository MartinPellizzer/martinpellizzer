Prunus mahaleb subvar. chrysocarpa

Prunus mahaleb subvar. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cherry or golden cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves. The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer, often with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
