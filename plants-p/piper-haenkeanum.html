Piper haenkeanum

Piper haenkeanum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, pungent aroma. Its flowers are small and green, typically clustered in catkins, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit develops from the female flowers and is harvested when fully mature for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
