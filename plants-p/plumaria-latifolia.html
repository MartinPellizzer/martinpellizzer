Plumaria latifolia

Plumaria latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf plumaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
