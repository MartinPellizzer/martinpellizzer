Prunus serrulata f. moutan

Prunus serrulata f. moutan, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and public spaces.
