Peperomia parasitica var. longicaulis

Peperomia parasitica var. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed parasitic peperomia, is a unique species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a hemiparasitic plant, meaning it partially relies on host plants for water and nutrients while performing photosynthesis. The plant features short, thick stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive reddish or violet color.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
