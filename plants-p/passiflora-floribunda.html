Passiflora floribunda

Passiflora floribunda, commonly known as the fringed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora floribunda is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
