Pinguicula grandiflora var. rosea

Pinguicula grandiflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces striking pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for enthusiasts of carnivorous plants.
