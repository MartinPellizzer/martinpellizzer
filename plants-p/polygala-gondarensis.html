Polygala gondarensis

Polygala gondarensis, commonly known as the Natal milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a small helmet.

Polygala gondarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
