Plantago cornuti

Plantago cornuti, commonly known as the Cornut's plantain, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
