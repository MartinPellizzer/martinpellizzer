Protium amplum

Protium amplum, commonly known as the Indian tamarind or tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several flat, seed-containing pods.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and have various applications in local cuisines and crafts.
