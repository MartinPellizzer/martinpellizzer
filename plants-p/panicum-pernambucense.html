Panicum pernambucense

Panicum pernambucense, commonly known as Brazilian millet, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but has potential for use in erosion control and as a forage crop.
