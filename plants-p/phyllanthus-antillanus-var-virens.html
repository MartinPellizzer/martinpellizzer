Phyllanthus antillanus var. virens

Phyllanthus antillanus var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf spurge, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing minute seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
