Passiflora herbertiana

Passiflora herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora herbertiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
