Pseuderanthemum dispermum

Pseuderanthemum dispermum, commonly known as the two-seeded false heather, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and often have a distinctive blue or purple color.
