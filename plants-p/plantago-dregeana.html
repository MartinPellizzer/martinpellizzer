Plantago dregeana

Plantago dregeana, commonly known as the Desert Plantain, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
