Polystichum falcatum var. fortunei

Polystichum falcatum var. fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's deer fern, is a variant of the narrow-leaved wood fern.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often curved fronds that give it a distinctive, flowing appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are biennial, with fertile fronds appearing in the second year of growth.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
