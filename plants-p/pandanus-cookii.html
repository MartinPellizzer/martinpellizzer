Pandanus cookii

Pandanus cookii, commonly known as the Cook Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for making thatch, baskets, and other woven items.

Pandanus cookii thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing near the shoreline in humid climates.
