Piper hosokawae

Piper hosokawae, commonly known as the Hosokawa pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elongated, slender fruits. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, often found in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces a peppery flavor similar to other members of the Piper genus.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential culinary uses.
