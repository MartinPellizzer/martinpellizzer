Pentaptera roxburghii

Pentaptera roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are notable for having five lobes, which give the species its scientific name.

Pentaptera roxburghii is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
