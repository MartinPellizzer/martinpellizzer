Pseudopanax arboreus

Pseudopanax arboreus, commonly known as the lancewood, is a unique evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically narrow and pointed. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is often twisted and covered in a rough, flaky bark. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Pseudopanax arboreus is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in forested areas.
