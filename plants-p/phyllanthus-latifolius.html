Phyllanthus latifolius

Phyllanthus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is widely used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
