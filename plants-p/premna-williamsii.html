Premna williamsii

Premna williamsii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or silver vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that often have a silvery or white underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the summer months. Premna williamsii is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its use in traditional medicine.
