Polemonium brandegeei

Polemonium brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegee's Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. It is a perennial herb that blooms in early to mid-summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
