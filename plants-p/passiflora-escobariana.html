Passiflora escobariana

Passiflora escobariana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a vibrant array of colors, often in shades of red, yellow, and blue. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

Passiflora escobariana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
