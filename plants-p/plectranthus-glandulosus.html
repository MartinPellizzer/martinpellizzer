Plectranthus glandulosus

Plectranthus glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in glandular hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
