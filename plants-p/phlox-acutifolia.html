Phlox acutifolia

Phlox acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that bear opposite, narrow, and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Phlox acutifolia thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
