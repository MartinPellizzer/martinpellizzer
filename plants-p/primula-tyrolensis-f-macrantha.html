Primula tyrolensis f. macrantha

Primula tyrolensis f. macrantha, commonly known as the Tyrolean primrose, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the high elevations of the Eastern Alps.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive orange or red throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in specialized gardens due to its delicate beauty and challenging growing requirements.
