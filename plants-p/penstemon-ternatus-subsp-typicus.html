Penstemon ternatus subsp. typicus

Penstemon ternatus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces distinctive seed capsules that release small, hairy seeds. Penstemon ternatus subsp.

typicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
