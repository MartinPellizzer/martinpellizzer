Pseudalangium polyosmoides var. tomentosum

Pseudalangium polyosmoides var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
