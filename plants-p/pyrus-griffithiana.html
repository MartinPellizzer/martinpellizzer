Pyrus griffithiana

Pyrus griffithiana, commonly known as the Kashmir pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and yellowish-orange fruit. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found at high altitudes.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is used in traditional medicine and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
