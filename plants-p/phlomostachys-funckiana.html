Phlomostachys funckiana

Phlomostachys funckiana, commonly known as the Funck's panic grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
