Pteris microdonta

Pteris microdonta, commonly known as the small fern or lesser water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically less than 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pteris microdonta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
