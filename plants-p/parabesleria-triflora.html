Parabesleria triflora

Parabesleria triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered parabesleria, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, three-lobed flowers that bloom in a striking array of colors, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, typically arranged in a compact rosette.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Parabesleria triflora is considered a conservation concern.
