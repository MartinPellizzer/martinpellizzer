Pandanus meetiaensis

Pandanus meetiaensis, commonly known as the Moluccan pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Indonesia, particularly the Maluku Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to sandy soils.
