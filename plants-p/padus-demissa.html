Padus demissa

Padus demissa, commonly known as the white dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Padus demissa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for birds and wildlife.
