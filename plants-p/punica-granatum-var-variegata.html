Punica granatum var. variegata

Punica granatum var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated pomegranate, is a variant of the pomegranate tree characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves display a unique combination of green, white, and pink hues, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the characteristic red, fleshy fruits. While it is primarily grown for its ornamental value, it can also yield edible seeds under suitable conditions.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
