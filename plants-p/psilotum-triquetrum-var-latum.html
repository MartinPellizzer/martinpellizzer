Psilotum triquetrum var. latum

Psilotum triquetrum var. latum, commonly known as the broad-leafed whisk fern, is a rare and primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Psilotaceae.

It is characterized by its lack of true leaves and roots, instead having small, scale-like structures called rhizoids and sporangia that produce spores. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its evolutionary significance lies in its simplicity, representing an early stage in the development of vascular plants.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and its role in understanding plant evolution.
