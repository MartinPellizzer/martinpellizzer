Peperomia cuneifolia

Peperomia cuneifolia, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a compact, low-maintenance houseplant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive triangular or arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that emerge from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia cuneifolia is valued for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
