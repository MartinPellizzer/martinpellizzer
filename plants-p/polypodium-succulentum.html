Polypodium succulentum

Polypodium succulentum, commonly known as the creeping fern or Spanish moss, is a perennial epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It features a dense, mat-forming growth habit with long, thin, and often pendulous stems that can hang from trees or grow on the ground. The plant produces small, clustered spore-bearing structures called sporangia on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests and coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ability to grow on various surfaces make it a popular ornamental plant.
