Ptelea straminea

Ptelea straminea, commonly known as the green ash or slender ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the olive family (Olacaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

Ptelea straminea is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
