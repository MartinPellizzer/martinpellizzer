Plectranthus phulchokiensis

Plectranthus phulchokiensis, commonly known as the Nepal plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinct aromatic scent.

Plectranthus phulchokiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habits.
