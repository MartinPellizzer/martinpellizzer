Pisonia coronata

Pisonia coronata, commonly known as the Crown-of-Thorns, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are small, leathery, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Pisonia coronata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
