Panax integrifolius

Panax integrifolius, commonly known as the American ginseng, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves with entire margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red berries, which contain the seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is highly valued in traditional herbal medicine.
