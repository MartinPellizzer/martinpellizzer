Priva trachelioides

Priva trachelioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf privet, is a shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and as a hedge.
