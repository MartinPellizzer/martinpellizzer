Pulmonaria pilosa

Pulmonaria pilosa, commonly known as the woolly lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland environments and is often found in moist soils.

Pulmonaria pilosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
