Prosthechea allemanii

Prosthechea allemanii, commonly known as the alleman's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Prosthechea allemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
