Peucedanum dissectum

Peucedanum dissectum, commonly known as the dissected cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological habitats.
