Panicum nudiflorum

Panicum nudiflorum, commonly known as the nakedflower grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that lack bracts, giving it its common name. The plant produces delicate, white to pale green flowers that are clustered in dense, fluffy panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
