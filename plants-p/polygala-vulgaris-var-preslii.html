Polygala vulgaris var. preslii

Polygala vulgaris var. preslii, commonly known as the Presl's milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that often bear small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in globular inflorescences. The plant has pinnately divided leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
