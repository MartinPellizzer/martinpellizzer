Polyphlebium hymenophylloides

Polyphlebium hymenophylloides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-white fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments, often found in dense rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
