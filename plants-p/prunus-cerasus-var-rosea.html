Prunus cerasus var. rosea

Prunus cerasus var. rosea, commonly known as the Rosa–µ cherry or pink cherry, is a variety of the sweet cherry tree known for its ornamental value.

It produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and a dark green upper surface. The fruit is a small, sweet cherry with a pale red to pink color when ripe.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and mild, flavorful fruit.
