Prunus padus parviflora

Prunus padus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn yellow in autumn.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color, typically appearing in late summer.
