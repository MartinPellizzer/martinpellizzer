Phyllanthus rheedei var. hispidus

Phyllanthus rheedei var. hispidus, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The hairy phyllanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
