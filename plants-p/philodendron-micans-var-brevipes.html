Philodendron micans var. brevipes

Philodendron micans var. brevipes, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its deep green, velvety leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly notched base and can grow up to 12 inches in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
