Papaver nudicaule subsp. anomalum

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

anomalum, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
