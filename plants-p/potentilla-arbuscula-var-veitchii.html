Potentilla arbuscula var. veitchii

Potentilla arbuscula var. veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's shrub cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, compact growth with small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine plantings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in cooler climates.
