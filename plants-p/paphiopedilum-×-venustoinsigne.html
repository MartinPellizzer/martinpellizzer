Paphiopedilum × venustoinsigne

Paphiopedilum × venustoinsigne, commonly known as the golden winged orchid, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a blend of characteristics from its parent species, Paphiopedilum venustum and Paphiopedilum insigne.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive golden hue on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil, making it suitable for both greenhouse and outdoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and unique floral structure.
