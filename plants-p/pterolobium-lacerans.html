Pterolobium lacerans

Pterolobium lacerans, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
