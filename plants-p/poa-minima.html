Poa minima

Poa minima, commonly known as the little blue grass, is a small, tufted perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and woodland edges. The plant has fine, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist to dry soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Poa minima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecosystems.
