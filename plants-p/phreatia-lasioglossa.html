Phreatia lasioglossa

Phreatia lasioglossa, commonly known as the narrow-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive narrow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is notable for its intricate floral structure and specific pollination mechanisms.
