Papaver argemone f. immaculatum

Papaver argemone f. immaculatum, commonly known as the white poppy, is a cultivar of the Argemone poppy characterized by its striking white flowers.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to California, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The flowers are typically single, with five broad petals and a prominent yellow center, adding ornamental value to gardens. This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often used in floral arrangements.

However, it should be handled with care, as all parts of the plant contain toxic compounds.
