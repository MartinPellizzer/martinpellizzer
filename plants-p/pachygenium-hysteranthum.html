Pachygenium hysteranthum

Pachygenium hysteranthum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-like inflorescence that resembles a ghostly face, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are succulent and adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The ghost flower is also notable for its rapid growth and short blooming period, making it a rare and fascinating botanical specimen.
