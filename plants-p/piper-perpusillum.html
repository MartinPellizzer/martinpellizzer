Piper perpusillum

Piper perpusillum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
