Pulsatilla multiceps

Pulsatilla multiceps, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces palmately divided leaves that are hairy and covered in fine glands. Its flowers typically have five sepals and numerous stamens, and it is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Pulsatilla multiceps is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
