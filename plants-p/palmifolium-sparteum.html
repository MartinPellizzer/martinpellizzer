Palmifolium sparteum

Palmifolium sparteum, commonly known as the sparteum palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, palm-like leaves.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant features a slender trunk covered in a fibrous sheath, with compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
