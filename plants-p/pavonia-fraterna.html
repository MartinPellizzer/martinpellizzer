Pavonia fraterna

Pavonia fraterna, commonly known as the redstem false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically pink or red in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Pavonia fraterna is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
