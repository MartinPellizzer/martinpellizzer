Psiadia madagascariensis

Psiadia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar daisy, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.

Psiadia madagascariensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
