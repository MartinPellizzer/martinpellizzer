Plumeria velutina var. boliviana

Plumeria velutina var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.
