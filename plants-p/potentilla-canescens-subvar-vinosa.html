Potentilla canescens subvar. vinosa

Potentilla canescens subvar. vinosa, commonly known as rosebay willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in wet meadows.

This plant is also known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
