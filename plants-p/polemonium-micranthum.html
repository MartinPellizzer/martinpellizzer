Polemonium micranthum

Polemonium micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
