Psophocarpus golungensis

Psophocarpus golungensis, commonly known as the Golung bean, is a climbing legume native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are small, white or pale pink, clustered in axillary racemes. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several oval, edible seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often used in traditional cuisines.

Additionally, it has potential agricultural value due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
