Penstemon laetus subsp. typicus

Penstemon laetus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Penstemon laetus subsp.

typicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and pollinator attraction.
