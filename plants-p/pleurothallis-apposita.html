Pleurothallis apposita

Pleurothallis apposita, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green to orange flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
