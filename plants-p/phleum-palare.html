Phleum palare

Phleum palare, commonly known as red brome, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Phleum palare is often found in meadows and can tolerate a range of soil conditions.
