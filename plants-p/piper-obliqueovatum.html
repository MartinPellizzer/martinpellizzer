Piper obliqueovatum

Piper obliqueovatum, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the midrib. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its unique foliage.
