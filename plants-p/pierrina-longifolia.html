Pierrina longifolia

Pierrina longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pierrina, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring and early summer months. Its fibrous root system is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pierrina longifolia is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
