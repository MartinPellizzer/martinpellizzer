Peperomia affinis

Peperomia affinis, commonly known as the Watermelon Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
