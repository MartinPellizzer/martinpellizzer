Phaseolus oblongus var. saponaceus

Phaseolus oblongus var. saponaceus, commonly known as the soapbean plant, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods that contain smooth, white, edible seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are known for their mild, soapy taste, which gives the plant its common name.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in agricultural systems.
