Poa nemoralis subsp. glauca

Poa nemoralis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the glaucous bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single, ascending spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its role in maintaining soil stability in its natural habitat.
