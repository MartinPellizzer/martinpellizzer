Peperomia vitiana

Peperomia vitiana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or white, which are followed by round, red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia vitiana is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests, contributing to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
