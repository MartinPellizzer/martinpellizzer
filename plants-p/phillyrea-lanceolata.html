Phillyrea lanceolata

Phillyrea lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, spherical berries. Phillyrea lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also noted for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance plant for gardens.
