Pilosella brachiata

Pilosella brachiata, commonly known as the hairy leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with pale centers.

Pilosella brachiata is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.
