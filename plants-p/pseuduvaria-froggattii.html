Pseuduvaria froggattii

Pseuduvaria froggattii, commonly known as the African star orchid, is a tropical orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and have a waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Pseuduvaria froggattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
