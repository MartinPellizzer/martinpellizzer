Peiranisia viciifolia

Peiranisia viciifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by kidney-shaped seed pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Peiranisia viciifolia is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
