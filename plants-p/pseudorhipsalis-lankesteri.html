Pseudorhipsalis lankesteri

Pseudorhipsalis lankesteri, commonly known as the Lankester's cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its branched, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Native to Mexico, it thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
