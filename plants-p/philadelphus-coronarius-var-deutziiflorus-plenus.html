Philadelphus coronarius var. deutziiflorus-plenus

Philadelphus coronarius var. deutziiflorus-plenus, commonly known as the double-flowered weeping bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive, pendulous branches that create a weeping appearance. The plant produces clusters of double white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and provides attractive contrast to the showy blooms.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and landscapes.
