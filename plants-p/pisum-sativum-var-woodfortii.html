Pisum sativum var. woodfortii

Pisum sativum var. woodfortii, commonly known as the Woodfort pea, is a botanical variety of the pea plant characterized by its distinct morphological features.

It is a climbing legume with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The pods are usually green and contain several seeds, which are edible when mature. This variety is notable for its genetic diversity and is often studied for its potential in agricultural breeding programs.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
