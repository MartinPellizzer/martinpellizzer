Paspalum distichum var. microstachyum

Paspalum distichum var. microstachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, two-ranked spikelets. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
