Primula denticulata subsp. cachemeriana

Primula denticulata subsp.

cachemeriana, commonly known as the Cachemire primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely toothed leaf margins and compact growth habit.
