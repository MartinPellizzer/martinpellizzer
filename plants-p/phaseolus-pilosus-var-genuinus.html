Phaseolus pilosus var. genuinus

Phaseolus pilosus var. genuinus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with a hairy texture and produces elongated, green pods that mature to a yellowish hue. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and is often cultivated for its edible beans. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
