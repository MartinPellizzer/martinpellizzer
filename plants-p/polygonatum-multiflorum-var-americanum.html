Polygonatum multiflorum var. americanum

Polygonatum multiflorum var. americanum, commonly known as American Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. The plant produces tall, arching stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its most distinctive feature is the panicle of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant also produces red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
