Polygala spinosa

Polygala spinosa, commonly known as the spinous milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense, spikelike inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slight hairy texture.

Polygala spinosa is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
