Plantago novae-zelandiae

Plantago novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand plantain, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
