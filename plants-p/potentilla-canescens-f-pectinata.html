Potentilla canescens f. pectinata

Potentilla canescens f. pectinata, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the flowers are arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
