Pseudosabicea pedicellata

Pseudosabicea pedicellata, commonly known as the mountain snowberry, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are generally ovate and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

Pseudosabicea pedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
