Prunus verecunda var. compta

Prunus verecunda var. compta, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese apricot, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red fruits. The plant has a dense, rounded habit with dark green, glossy leaves that provide year-round visual interest. It is well-suited for small gardens or containers due to its modest size and hardy nature.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
