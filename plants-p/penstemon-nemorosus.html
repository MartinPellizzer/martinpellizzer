Penstemon nemorosus

Penstemon nemorosus, commonly known as the woodland penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red. It is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and well-drained soils.

Penstemon nemorosus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
