Parasenecio roborowskii

Parasenecio roborowskii, commonly known as the Tibetan groundsel, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, adapting to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, hairy, and arranged in a rosette, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its harsh habitat.
