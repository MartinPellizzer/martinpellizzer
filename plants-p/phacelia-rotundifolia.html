Phacelia rotundifolia

Phacelia rotundifolia, commonly known as blue flax, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Phacelia rotundifolia is also valued for its use in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
