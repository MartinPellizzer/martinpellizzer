Prunus × lannesiana f. ohnanden

Prunus × lannesiana f. ohnanden, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical cultivar characterized by its distinctive white petals and compact growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis. The plant typically produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly in spring when it displays its showy white blossoms.

This cultivar thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
