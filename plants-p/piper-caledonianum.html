Piper caledonianum

Piper caledonianum, commonly known as the Caledonian pepper or mountain pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaves, and small white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant's aromatic properties and medicinal uses make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
