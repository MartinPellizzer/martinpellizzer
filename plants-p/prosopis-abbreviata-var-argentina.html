Prosopis abbreviata var. argentina

Prosopis abbreviata var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate and composed of several leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
