Pilea sessiliflora

Pilea sessiliflora, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features round, waxy leaves that are often described as resembling small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in the spring. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea sessiliflora is known for its slow growth and ability to form dense, bushy foliage over time.
