Pappophorum caerulescens

Pappophorum caerulescens, commonly known as the bluegrass or blue false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides.

Pappophorum caerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
