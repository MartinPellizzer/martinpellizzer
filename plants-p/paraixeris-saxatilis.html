Paraixeris saxatilis

Paraixeris saxatilis, commonly known as the rock paraixeris, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and preference for rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Paraixeris saxatilis is adapted to thrive in full sun and is often found in mountainous or coastal environments.
