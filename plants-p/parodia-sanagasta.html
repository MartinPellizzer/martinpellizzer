Parodia sanagasta

Parodia sanagasta, commonly known as the Sanaga cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Parodia sanagasta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
