Prunus virginiana subvar. duerinckii

Prunus virginiana subvar. duerinckii, commonly known as the Duerrick chokecherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark red to black berries, which are typically edible but can be bitter. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated margins. It produces showy white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

This variety is often found in moist woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
