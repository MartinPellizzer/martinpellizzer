Passiflora serratodigitata

Passiflora serratodigitata, commonly known as the serrated digitate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its complex, showy flowers that feature a blend of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are palmately lobed with serrated edges, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
