Poidium latifolium

Poidium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf poidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage.
