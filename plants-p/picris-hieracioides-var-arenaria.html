Picris hieracioides var. arenaria

Picris hieracioides var. arenaria, commonly known as sand chicory, is a flowering plant native to arid and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh, coastal or desert conditions.

Its small, linear leaves and showy yellow blooms make it a distinctive and resilient plant in challenging habitats.
