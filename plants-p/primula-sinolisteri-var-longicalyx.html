Primula sinolisteri var. longicalyx

Primula sinolisteri var. longicalyx, commonly known as the longiflorous primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features rosettes of basal, woolly, and often hairy leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with five petals and a distinctive throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and conservationists.
