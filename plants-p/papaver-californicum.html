Papaver californicum

Papaver californicum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red petals and a distinctive saucer-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a basal rosette of smooth, waxy leaves and produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds.

The California poppy is also the state flower of California and is known for its medicinal properties.
