Penstemon hintonii

Penstemon hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon hintonii is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
