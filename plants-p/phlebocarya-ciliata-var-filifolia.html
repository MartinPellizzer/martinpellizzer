Phlebocarya ciliata var. filifolia

Phlebocarya ciliata var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically narrow and have a soft, velvety texture with fine hairs along the edges. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
