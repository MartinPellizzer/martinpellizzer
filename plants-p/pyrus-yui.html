Pyrus yui

Pyrus yui, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and slender, upright branches. The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface with a lighter underside. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, white to yellowish fruit.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
