Pandiaka confusa

Pandiaka confusa, commonly known as the confused pandiaka, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a distinctive fragrance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Due to habitat loss, Pandiaka confusa is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
