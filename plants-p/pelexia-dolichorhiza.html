Pelexia dolichorhiza

Pelexia dolichorhiza, commonly known as the long-rooted pelexia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has a fibrous root system that extends deep into the soil. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded, fertile soils.

Pelexia dolichorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
