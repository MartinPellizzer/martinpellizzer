Pelargonium × divergens

Pelargonium × divergens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium × divergens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
