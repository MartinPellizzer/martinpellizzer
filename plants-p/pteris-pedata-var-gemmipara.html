Pteris pedata var. gemmipara

Pteris pedata var. gemmipara, commonly known as the gemmipara water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near water bodies. The plant features fronds that are usually dark green and have a distinctive, creeping growth habit. It reproduces through spores and also exhibits gemmae, which are small plantlets that can develop into new individuals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
