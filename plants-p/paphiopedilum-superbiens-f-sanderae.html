Paphiopedilum superbiens f. sanderae

Paphiopedilum superbiens f. sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a unique pouch-shaped lip, a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
