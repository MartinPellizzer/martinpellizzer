Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. latifolius

Phaseolus longepedunculatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vigorous climbing habit. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that are typically green and edible when harvested young. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
