Psoralea psoralioides var. gracilis

Psoralea psoralioides var. gracilis, commonly known as slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
