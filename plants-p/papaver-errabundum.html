Papaver errabundum

Papaver errabundum, commonly known as the wandering poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Papaver errabundum is noted for its ephemeral blooming period, typically occurring in late spring to early summer.
