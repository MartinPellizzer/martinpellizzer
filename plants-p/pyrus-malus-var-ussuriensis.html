Pyrus malus var. ussuriensis

Pyrus malus var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussurian apple, is a variant of the apple tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in colder climates. The plant typically produces small, tart apples that are often used for culinary or medicinal purposes. Botanically, it has simple, alternate leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in challenging environments.
