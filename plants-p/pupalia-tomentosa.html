Pupalia tomentosa

Pupalia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pug or woolly pugflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, soft, grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pupalia tomentosa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
