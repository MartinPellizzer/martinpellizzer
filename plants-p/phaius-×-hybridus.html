Phaius × hybridus

Phaius × hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phaius flaviflorus and Phaius tankervillii.

It is a herbaceous perennial with large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow or orange flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This orchid thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.
