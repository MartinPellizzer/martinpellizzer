Prestonia succo

Prestonia succo, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to reddish-purple as it matures.

Prestonia succo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its striking foliage and floral display.
