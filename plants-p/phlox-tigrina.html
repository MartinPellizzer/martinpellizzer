Phlox tigrina

Phlox tigrina, commonly known as the tiger phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Phlox tigrina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
