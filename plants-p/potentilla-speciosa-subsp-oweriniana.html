Potentilla speciosa subsp. oweriniana

Potentilla speciosa subsp.

oweriniana, commonly known as the showy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
