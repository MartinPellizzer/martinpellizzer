Patersonia lanata

Patersonia lanata, commonly known as the woolly patersonia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats.

Patersonia lanata is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
