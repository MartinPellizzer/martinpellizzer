Pisum flavum

Pisum flavum, commonly known as the yellow pea, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several round, yellow seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

Pisum flavum is also valued for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.
