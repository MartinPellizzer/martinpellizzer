Polygala alata

Polygala alata, commonly known as the winged milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces distinctive, winged pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Polygala alata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
