Pharbitis fragrans

Pharbitis fragrans, commonly known as sweet morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink and violet. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and climbing habit.

However, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
