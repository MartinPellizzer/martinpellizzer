Pulicaria longifolia

Pulicaria longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Pulicaria longifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
