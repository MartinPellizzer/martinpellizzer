Pachygenium callosum

Pachygenium callosum, commonly known as the cushion plant, is a unique succulent native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming a low-lying cushion that helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid climates.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
