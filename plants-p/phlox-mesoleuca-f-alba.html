Phlox mesoleuca f. alba

Phlox mesoleuca f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the eastern phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that are typically white to pink in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in partial to full sun. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
