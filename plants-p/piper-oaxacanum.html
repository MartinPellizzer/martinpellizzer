Piper oaxacanum

Piper oaxacanum, commonly known as the Oaxacan pepper or wild chili, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, green to red berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

The fruit contains capsaicin, giving it a spicy flavor and making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
