Panicum plicatum

Panicum plicatum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swampy areas.

Silvergrass is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
