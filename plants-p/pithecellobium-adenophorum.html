Pithecellobium adenophorum

Pithecellobium adenophorum, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white flowers arranged in racemes, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
