Panicum milioides var. filifolium

Panicum milioides var. filifolium, commonly known as silky millet, is a grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-haired leaves and panicles that are often covered in soft, hairy structures. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient crop for marginal lands. It produces small, pale green grains that are used for food and animal feed.

Its botanical features make it an important species in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration.
