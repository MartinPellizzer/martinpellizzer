Plantago linearis var. villosa

Plantago linearis var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This variety is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
