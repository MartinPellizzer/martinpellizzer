Polygala rosei

Polygala rosei, commonly known as the rose spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that resemble those of orchids. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the warmer months.

Polygala rosei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
