Phalangium longifolium

Phalangium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved phalangium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
