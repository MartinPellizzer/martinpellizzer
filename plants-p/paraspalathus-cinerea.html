Paraspalathus cinerea

Paraspalathus cinerea, commonly known as the silver leaf spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves that reflect light, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Paraspalathus cinerea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
