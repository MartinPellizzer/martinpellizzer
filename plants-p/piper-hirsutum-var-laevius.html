Piper hirsutum var. laevius

Piper hirsutum var. laevius, commonly known as the smooth-hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
