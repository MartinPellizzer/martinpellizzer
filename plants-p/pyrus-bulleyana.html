Pyrus bulleyana

Pyrus bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyana pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and compact size.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
