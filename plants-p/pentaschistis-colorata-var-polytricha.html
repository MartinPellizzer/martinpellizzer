Pentaschistis colorata var. polytricha

Pentaschistis colorata var. polytricha, commonly known as the red-flowered rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are vibrant red or purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
