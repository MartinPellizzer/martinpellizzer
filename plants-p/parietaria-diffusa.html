Parietaria diffusa

Parietaria diffusa, commonly known as stinging nettle, is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Parietaria diffusa is known for its irritant hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact.
