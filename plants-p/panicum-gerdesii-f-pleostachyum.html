Panicum gerdesii f. pleostachyum

Panicum gerdesii f. pleostachyum, commonly known as the broad-stalked panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
