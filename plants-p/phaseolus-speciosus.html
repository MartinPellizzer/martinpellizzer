Phaseolus speciosus

Phaseolus speciosus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods containing seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both gardens and agricultural settings.
