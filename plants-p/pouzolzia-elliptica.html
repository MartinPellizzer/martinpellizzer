Pouzolzia elliptica

Pouzolzia elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pouzolzia elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
