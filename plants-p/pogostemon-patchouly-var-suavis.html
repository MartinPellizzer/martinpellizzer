Pogostemon patchouly var. suavis

Pogostemon patchouly var. suavis, commonly known as sweet patchouli, is a variant of the patchouli plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pale purple flowers. The plant is known for its strong, earthy fragrance, which comes from the essential oils produced in its leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its use in perfumery and traditional medicine.
