Persea oreophila

Persea oreophila, commonly known as the cloud forest avocado, is a species native to the humid highlands of Central America.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to yellow as it matures. Its habitat is typically found in misty, shaded environments at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Persea oreophila is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
