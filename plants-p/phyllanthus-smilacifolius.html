Phyllanthus smilacifolius

Phyllanthus smilacifolius, commonly known as creeping phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and waste grounds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
