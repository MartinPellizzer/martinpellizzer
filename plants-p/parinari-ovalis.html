Parinari ovalis

Parinari ovalis, commonly known as the oval mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, oval-shaped fruits that are similar in appearance to mangosteens. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and fruit.
