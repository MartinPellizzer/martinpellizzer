Pinus mughus var. longispina

Pinus mughus var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined pine, is a variant of the Mughal pine native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its long, stiff needles and distinctive, spiny cones. The tree typically grows in high-altitude regions, where it adapts to cold and arid conditions. Its foliage is dense, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

The longispina variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
