Philodendron bipinnatifidum var. lundii

Philodendron bipinnatifidum var. lundii, commonly known as the Congo heartleaf, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and often have a velvety texture. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture in its soil. Its foliage is prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening.

It is known for its ability to climb using aerial roots and can grow quite large if provided with adequate support.
