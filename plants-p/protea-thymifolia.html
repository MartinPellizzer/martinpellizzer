Protea thymifolia

Protea thymifolia, commonly known as the sweet thyme protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that form a rounded or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically pink to red in color, with a distinctive yellowish or orangeish center, and it blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Protea thymifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in local ecosystems.
