Polygonatum officinale var. papillosum

Polygonatum officinale var. papillosum, commonly known as the Chinese solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring. Its berries are dark blue and edible, often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
