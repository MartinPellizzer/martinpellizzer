Piper accrescens

Piper accrescens, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and have a spicy, peppery flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
