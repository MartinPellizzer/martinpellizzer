Protea hirta

Protea hirta, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Protea hirta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
