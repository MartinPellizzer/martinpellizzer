Pleuropteropyrum nakaii

Pleuropteropyrum nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese feather grass, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
