Platythelys paranaensis

Platythelys paranaensis, commonly known as the Paranaensis orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and the presence of pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
