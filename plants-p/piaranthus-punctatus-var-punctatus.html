Piaranthus punctatus var. punctatus

Piaranthus punctatus var. punctatus, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The Cape sugarbush is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
