Plectranthus marrubatus

Plectranthus marrubatus, commonly known as African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant has fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus marrubatus is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
