Physostemon lanceolatus subsp. paraguensis

Physostemon lanceolatus subsp.

paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan skullcap, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
