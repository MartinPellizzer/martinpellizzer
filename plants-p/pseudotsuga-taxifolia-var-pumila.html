Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. pumila

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese fir, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and dense, pyramidal shape, making it a popular ornamental plant. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and often retain their color throughout the winter. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, preferring cool, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its ability to form a neat, shrub-like form, suitable for rock gardens and small landscapes.
