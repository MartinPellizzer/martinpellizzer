Phillyrea subangustifolia var. oleastrifolia

Phillyrea subangustifolia var. oleastrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and woodland areas.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
