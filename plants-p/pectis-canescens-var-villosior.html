Pectis canescens var. villosior

Pectis canescens var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy pectis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
