Pilosella × derubella

Pilosella × derubella, commonly known as the hairy groundsel, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Pilosella × derubella is often considered an invasive species due to its rapid spread and ability to thrive in various soil types.
