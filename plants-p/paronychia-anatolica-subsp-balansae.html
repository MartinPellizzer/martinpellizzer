Paronychia anatolica subsp. balansae

Paronychia anatolica subsp.

balansae, commonly known as the Anatolian sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, and its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as steppes and coastal areas.
