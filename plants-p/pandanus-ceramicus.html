Pandanus ceramicus

Pandanus ceramicus, commonly known as the pandanus tree or hibiscus tree, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit, known as a "pandanus fruit," is edible and has a fibrous texture.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value in tropical landscapes.
