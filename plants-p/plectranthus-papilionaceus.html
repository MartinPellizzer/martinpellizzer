Plectranthus papilionaceus

Plectranthus papilionaceus, commonly known as the blue plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many herbs and ornamental plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile plant for both decorative and medicinal uses.
