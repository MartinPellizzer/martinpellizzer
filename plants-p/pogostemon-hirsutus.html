Pogostemon hirsutus

Pogostemon hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of purple or violet, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in traditional herbal practices.
