Parahyparrhenia siamensis

Parahyparrhenia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass, is a tropical perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in humid and moist environments. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Parahyparrhenia siamensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
