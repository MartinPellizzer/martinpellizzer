Phlox diffusa subsp. subcarinata

Phlox diffusa subsp.

subcarinata, commonly known as the smooth phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role in attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
