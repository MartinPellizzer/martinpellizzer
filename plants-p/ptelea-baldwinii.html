Ptelea baldwinii

Ptelea baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. Ptelea baldwinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
