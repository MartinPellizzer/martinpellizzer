Panicum neurodes var. kleinianum

Panicum neurodes var. kleinianum, commonly known as Klein's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces inflorescences that are typically spike-like or panicle-shaped. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage species in arid regions.
