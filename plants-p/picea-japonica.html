Picea japonica

Picea japonica, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones. The tree typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The bark of Picea japonica is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
