Passiflora schlimiana

Passiflora schlimiana, commonly known as the Schlim's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are compound, with three to five lobes, and the stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora schlimiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
