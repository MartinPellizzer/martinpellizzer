Pimelea clutioides

Pimelea clutioides, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of yellow flowers that resemble bottle brushes, blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and open forests. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Pimelea clutioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
