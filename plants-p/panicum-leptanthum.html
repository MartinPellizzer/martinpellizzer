Panicum leptanthum

Panicum leptanthum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum leptanthum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
