Pteris brooksiana

Pteris brooksiana, commonly known as the Brooks' water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous sporangia on the undersides of its leaves.

Pteris brooksiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
