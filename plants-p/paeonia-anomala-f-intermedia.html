Paeonia anomala f. intermedia

Paeonia anomala f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate peony, is a hybrid form of the Persian peony.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant is a deciduous shrub with a woody stem and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in horticulture.
