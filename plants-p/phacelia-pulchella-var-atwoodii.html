Phacelia pulchella var. atwoodii

Phacelia pulchella var. atwoodii, commonly known as Atwood's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
