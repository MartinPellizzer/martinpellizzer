Papaver fauriei

Papaver fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver fauriei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
