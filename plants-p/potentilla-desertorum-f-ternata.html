Potentilla desertorum f. ternata

Potentilla desertorum f. ternata, commonly known as the Desert Cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
