Petalodactylis

Petalodactylis, commonly known as the winged orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring elongated petals that resemble wings, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Petalodactylis species are known for their delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters.

These orchids are prized for their ornamental value and are often cultivated in greenhouses or botanical gardens.
