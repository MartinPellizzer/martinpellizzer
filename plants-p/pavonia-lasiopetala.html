Pavonia lasiopetala

Pavonia lasiopetala, commonly known as the woolly pavia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

Pavonia lasiopetala is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
