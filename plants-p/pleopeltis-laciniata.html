Pleopeltis laciniata

Pleopeltis laciniata, commonly known as the slit-leaf rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lanceolate leaves that give it a delicate, lacy appearance. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are often clustered and have a distinctive, segmented structure.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor settings.
