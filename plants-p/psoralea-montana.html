Psoralea montana

Psoralea montana, commonly known as the mountain psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous areas, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Psoralea montana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
