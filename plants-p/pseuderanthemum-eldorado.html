Pseuderanthemum eldorado

Pseuderanthemum eldorado, commonly known as the Eldorado pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
