Pithecellobium purpusii

Pithecellobium purpusii, commonly known as the Hawaiian acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
