Piper villosulum

Piper villosulum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant's leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity should be considered.
