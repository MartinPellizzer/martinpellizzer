Piper variabile var. obtentum

Piper variabile var. obtentum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that turn red when mature, containing the seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
