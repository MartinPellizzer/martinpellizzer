Parkia reticulata

Parkia reticulata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The tree has pinnate leaves and produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often planted for its ornamental value.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
