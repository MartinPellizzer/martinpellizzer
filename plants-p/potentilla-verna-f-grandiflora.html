Potentilla verna f. grandiflora

Potentilla verna f. grandiflora, commonly known as the spring cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, rounded leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and early blooming period, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
