Pseudosasa truncatula

Pseudosasa truncatula, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Pseudosasa truncatula is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
