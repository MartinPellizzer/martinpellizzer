Pyrus nivalis var. orientalis

Pyrus nivalis var. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
