Prunus serrulata f. dianthipetala

Prunus serrulata f. dianthipetala, commonly known as the pink-veined cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its striking pink veins that run through the leaves, contrasting with the pale green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically pink or white, which add to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
