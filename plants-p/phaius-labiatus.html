Phaius labiatus

Phaius labiatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as rainforests and marshlands. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, with a distinctive lip that is often broader than the other petals.

Phaius labiatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivation.
