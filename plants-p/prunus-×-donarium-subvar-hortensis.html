Prunus × donarium subvar. hortensis

Prunus × donarium subvar. hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters and has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, sweet plum that ripens in late summer, making it both ornamental and edible.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in horticultural breeding programs.
