Penducella composita

Penducella composita, commonly known as the composite penduncle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads made up of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters that resemble a composite structure.

Penducella composita is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.
