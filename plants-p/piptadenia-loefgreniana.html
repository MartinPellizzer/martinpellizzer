Piptadenia loefgreniana

Piptadenia loefgreniana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in reforestation efforts.
