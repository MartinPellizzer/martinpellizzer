Panicum bistipulatum

Panicum bistipulatum, commonly known as the two-leaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
