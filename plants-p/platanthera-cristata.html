Platanthera cristata

Platanthera cristata, commonly known as the cristate twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and bogs.

Platanthera cristata is known for its mycorrhizal associations, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.
