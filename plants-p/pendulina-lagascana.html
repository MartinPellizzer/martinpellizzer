Pendulina lagascana

Pendulina lagascana, commonly known as the hanging lily, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from slender stems, creating an elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental value.

Botanically, Pendulina lagascana is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms and ecological role in its native habitat.
