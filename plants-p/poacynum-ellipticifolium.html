Poacynum ellipticifolium

Poacynum ellipticifolium, commonly known as the elliptic-leaved milkweed, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has a milky sap that is toxic and serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
