Prunus incisa f. bellura

Prunus incisa f. bellura, commonly known as the bellura cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits. It is valued for its dense foliage and seasonal interest, making it suitable for small gardens or as a container plant.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
