Piper jubatum

Piper jubatum, commonly known as the golden pepper or yellow pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a climbing habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
