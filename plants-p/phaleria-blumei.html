Phaleria blumei

Phaleria blumei, commonly known as the white-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom throughout the year. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phaleria blumei is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
