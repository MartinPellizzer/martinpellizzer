Potamogeton berchtoldii f. ramosissimus

Potamogeton berchtoldii f. ramosissimus, commonly known as the branched pondweed, is a aquatic plant characterized by its dense, branching stems that often form dense underwater vegetation.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is notable for its extensive root system, which helps stabilize sediments and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
