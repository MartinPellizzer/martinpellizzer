Pleurothallis alexii

Pleurothallis alexii, commonly known as the Alexander's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features elongated, narrow pseudobulbs and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a faintly fragrant scent. Its botanical structure is adapted for nutrient absorption from the air and rain, typical of many orchids in its habitat.

Pleurothallis alexii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
