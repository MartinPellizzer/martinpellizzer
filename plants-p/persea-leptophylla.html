Persea leptophylla

Persea leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf avocado, is a species of evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a drupe, similar to other avocados, with a thick, leathery outer layer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use compared to other avocado species.
