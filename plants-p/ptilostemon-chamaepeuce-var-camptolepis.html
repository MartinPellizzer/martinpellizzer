Ptilostemon chamaepeuce var. camptolepis

Ptilostemon chamaepeuce var. camptolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ptilostemon, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
