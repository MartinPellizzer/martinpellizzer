Psidium coriaceum var. grandifolium

Psidium coriaceum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are typically larger than those of the common guava. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
