Phaseolus setulosus

Phaseolus setulosus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
