Penstemon giganteus

Penstemon giganteus, commonly known as the giant beardtongue, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, adorned with showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often bluish-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon giganteus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
