Pyrus pompeiana moschata

Pyrus pompeiana moschata, commonly known as the Pompeian pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the volcanic soil of Pompeii.

This plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, sweet fruits that were once cultivated in ancient Roman times. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with distinct seasonal changes.

Due to its historical significance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
