Paspalum racemosum

Paspalum racemosum, commonly known as the racemose paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Paspalum racemosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
