Plantago bidentata

Plantago bidentata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in open fields.

This species is known for its medicinal uses and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
