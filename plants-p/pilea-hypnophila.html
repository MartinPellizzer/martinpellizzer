Pilea hypnophila

Pilea hypnophila, commonly known as the "Chinese money plant," is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a short, upright stem and rosettes of heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea hypnophila is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
