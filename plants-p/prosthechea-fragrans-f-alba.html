Prosthechea fragrans f. alba

Prosthechea fragrans f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a variant of the fragrant orchid species Prosthechea fragrans.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with erect, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
