Pandanus dasystigma

Pandanus dasystigma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots can be used in some local medicinal practices.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it resilient to saltwater and flooding.
