Pluchea subdecurrens

Pluchea subdecurrens, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often serrated along the edges. Its small, yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Pluchea subdecurrens is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.
