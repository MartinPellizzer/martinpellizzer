Poa unispiculata

Poa unispiculata, commonly known as the single-bristle grass, is a species of grass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa unispiculata is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
