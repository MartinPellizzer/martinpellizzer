Papaver croceum subsp. tianschanicum

Papaver croceum subsp.

tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tianshan poppy, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly the Tianshan mountain range. It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange petals and a distinctive, elongated seed capsule.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its medicinal and ornamental value.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Papaver genus, sharing similar morphological traits but with unique adaptations to its high-altitude habitat.
