Pedilonum dichaeoides

Pedilonum dichaeoides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Pedilonum dichaeoides is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.
