Phyllanthus pilulifer

Phyllanthus pilulifer, commonly known as the bead plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bead-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are usually small and clustered, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma.
