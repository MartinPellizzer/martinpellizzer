Polygala sancti-georgii

Polygala sancti-georgii, commonly known as Saint George's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, adapting to the island's unique climate. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive structure that resembles a spurred shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
