Potentilla norvegica subsp. monspeliensis

Potentilla norvegica subsp.

monspeliensis, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features wiry, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are divided into five oval lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
