Paris henryi

Paris henryi, commonly known as the Henry's mouse-ear, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Parisaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in shaded, moist environments. It is a perennial herb that is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
