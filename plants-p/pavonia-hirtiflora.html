Pavonia hirtiflora

Pavonia hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are hairy and ovate, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia hirtiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
