Potamogeton × nitens f. involutus

Potamogeton × nitens f. involutus, commonly known as the curled pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tightly coiled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Its leaves are usually dark green and can be found submerged or partially emergent.

Potamogeton × nitens f. involutus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
