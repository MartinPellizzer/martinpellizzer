Peperomia elongata var. guianensis

Peperomia elongata var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan elongated peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are inconspicuous, appearing in clusters of tiny, greenish-white spathes.

This species is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.
