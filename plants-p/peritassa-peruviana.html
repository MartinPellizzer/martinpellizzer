Peritassa peruviana

Peritassa peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian lily, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
