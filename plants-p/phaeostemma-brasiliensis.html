Phaeostemma brasiliensis

Phaeostemma brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian ghost flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance with translucent, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
