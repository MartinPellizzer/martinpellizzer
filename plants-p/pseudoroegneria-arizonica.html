Pseudoroegneria arizonica

Pseudoroegneria arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry landscapes.
