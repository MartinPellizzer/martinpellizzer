Piper poiteanum

Piper poiteanum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the aromatic compound responsible for its spicy flavor.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine.
