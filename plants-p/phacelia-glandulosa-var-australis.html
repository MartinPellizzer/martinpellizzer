Phacelia glandulosa var. australis

Phacelia glandulosa var. australis, commonly known as the Australian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, glandular leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
