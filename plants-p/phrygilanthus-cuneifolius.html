Phrygilanthus cuneifolius

Phrygilanthus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phrygilanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or white in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
