Pyrus neopopovii

Pyrus neopopovii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in temperate climates.

Pyrus neopopovii is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
