Psilosiphon rivularis

Psilosiphon rivularis, commonly known as the river spongeplant, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, sponge-like structures that aid in water filtration and nutrient absorption. The plant typically grows in slow-moving streams and rivers, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. Its leaves are thin and translucent, allowing for efficient light penetration for photosynthesis.

Psilosiphon rivularis plays a vital role in maintaining water quality by reducing sediment and pollutants in its habitat.
