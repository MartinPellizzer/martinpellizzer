Prunus ilicifolia subsp. lyonii

Prunus ilicifolia subsp.

lyonii, commonly known as the Lyon's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental landscaping.
