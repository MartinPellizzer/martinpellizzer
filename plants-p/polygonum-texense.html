Polygonum texense

Polygonum texense, commonly known as Texas knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible parts. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wetlands, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygonum texense is noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in wet environments, making it a common sight in riparian zones.
