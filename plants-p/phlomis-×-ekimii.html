Phlomis × ekimii

Phlomis × ekimii, commonly known as the hybrid hybrid mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of showy, yellow to orange-red flowers, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Phlomis × ekimii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
