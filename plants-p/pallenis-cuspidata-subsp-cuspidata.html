Pallenis cuspidata subsp. cuspidata

Pallenis cuspidata subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, typically blue or violet, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
