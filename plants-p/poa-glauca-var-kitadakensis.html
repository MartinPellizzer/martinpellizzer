Poa glauca var. kitadakensis

Poa glauca var. kitadakensis, commonly known as the Kitadakensis bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, which makes it well-suited for ornamental landscaping. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually tinged with purple or reddish hues.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
