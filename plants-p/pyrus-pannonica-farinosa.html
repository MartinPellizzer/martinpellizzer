Pyrus pannonica farinosa

Pyrus pannonica farinosa, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear-like berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
