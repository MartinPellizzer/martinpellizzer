Packera bellidifolia

Packera bellidifolia, commonly known as the showy golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The showy golden aster produces bright yellow flowers in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
