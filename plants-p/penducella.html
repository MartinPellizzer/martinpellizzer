Penducella

Penducella, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that often hang in clusters, giving it its common name. The plants typically have slender stems and heart-shaped leaves, with flowers that range in color from blue to purple. Penducella species are native to regions in Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

They are popular in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
