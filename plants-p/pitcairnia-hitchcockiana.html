Pitcairnia hitchcockiana

Pitcairnia hitchcockiana, commonly known as the Hitchcock's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous covering, which helps in water retention. The plant produces showy, long-lasting inflorescences that can be pink, red, or orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
