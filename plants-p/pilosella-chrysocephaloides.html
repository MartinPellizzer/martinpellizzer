Pilosella chrysocephaloides

Pilosella chrysocephaloides, commonly known as the golden-head pilosella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features wiry, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its most distinctive feature is the bright yellow flower head, which consists of numerous small florets.

Pilosella chrysocephaloides typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
