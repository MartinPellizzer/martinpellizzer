Phillyrea angustifolia var. lanceolata

Phillyrea angustifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically evergreen and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
