Phrynium rheedei

Phrynium rheedei, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix enclosed by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
