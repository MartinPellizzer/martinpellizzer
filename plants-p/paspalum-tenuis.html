Paspalum tenuis

Paspalum tenuis, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils and its tolerance to periodic flooding.

Paspalum tenuis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
