Piper aequale var. ellipticolanceolatum

Piper aequale var. ellipticolanceolatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppercorns, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a climbing habit.

The varietal form ellipticolanceolatum distinguishes it by its broader, more elongated leaf shape compared to the typical species.
