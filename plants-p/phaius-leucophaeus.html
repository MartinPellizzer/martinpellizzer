Phaius leucophaeus

Phaius leucophaeus, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows in swampy or wetland habitats, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often clustered and can form dense mats in the soil.

The orchid is noted for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it a common sight in marshy areas.
