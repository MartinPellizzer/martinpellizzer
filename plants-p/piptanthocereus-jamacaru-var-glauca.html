Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. glauca

Piptanthocereus jamacaru var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-tipped columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a dense covering of blue-gray spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This variety is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
