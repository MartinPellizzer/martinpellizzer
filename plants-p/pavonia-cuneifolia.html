Pavonia cuneifolia

Pavonia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a pointed tip and a rough, textured surface. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
