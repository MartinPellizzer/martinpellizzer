Phyllanthus nutans var. purdiaeanus

Phyllanthus nutans var. purdiaeanus, commonly known as the creeping false sandwort, is a variant of the species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptical, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
