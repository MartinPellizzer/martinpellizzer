Polypodium crassinerve

Polypodium crassinerve, commonly known as the broad-veined water fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizome from which fronds emerge, with a distinctive broad, parallel-veined structure on its leaves. Its fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

Polypodium crassinerve is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
