Pilea capitata subsp. capitata

Pilea capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to China. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick, glossy, and rounded, resembling small coins.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters at the center of the rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Its unique appearance and ease of care have contributed to its widespread cultivation.
