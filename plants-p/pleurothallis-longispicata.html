Pleurothallis longispicata

Pleurothallis longispicata, commonly known as the long-spurred pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
