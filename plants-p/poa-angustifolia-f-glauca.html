Poa angustifolia f. glauca

Poa angustifolia f. glauca, commonly known as the blue mountain grass, is a variant of the narrowleaf bluegrass.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine blue-gray bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in cooler, moist environments such as meadows and stream banks. Its compact growth habit and silvery foliage make it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia.
