Panderia

Panderia, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Anacardiaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Panderia tonkinensis, which is native to Southeast Asia. The plants are characterized by their aromatic, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. They produce small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Panderia species are valued for their unique fragrance and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
