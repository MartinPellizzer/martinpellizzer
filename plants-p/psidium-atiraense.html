Psidium atiraense

Psidium atiraense, commonly known as the Atiraense guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest in northern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries similar to those of the common guava. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
