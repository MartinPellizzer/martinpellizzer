Pelargonium × smyrniifolium

Pelargonium × smyrniifolium, commonly known as the smokeplant, is a species native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage often emits a mild, spicy scent when crushed, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Pelargonium.
