Potamogeton × decipiens f. torssanderi

Potamogeton × decipiens f. torssanderi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has a submerged growth habit, with its stems and leaves often found in the water column. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters near the water surface.

This variant is notable for its morphological distinctiveness compared to its parent species, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
