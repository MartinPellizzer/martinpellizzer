Piper perareolatum

Piper perareolatum, commonly known as the hairy sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
