Parashorea parvifolia

Parashorea parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved dipterocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dipterocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically small and elliptical. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by woody fruits containing winged seeds. It plays an important ecological role in rainforest ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.

Due to its economic value, it is also subject to habitat loss and conservation concerns.
