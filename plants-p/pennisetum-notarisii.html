Pennisetum notarisii

Pennisetum notarisii, commonly known as the golden beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive golden inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Pennisetum notarisii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
