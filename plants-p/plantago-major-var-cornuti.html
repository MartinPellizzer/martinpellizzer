Plantago major var. cornuti

Plantago major var. cornuti, commonly known as the cornutus plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its distinctive seed structure.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, producing seeds with a unique, elongated shape.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and resilience, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
