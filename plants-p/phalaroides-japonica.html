Phalaroides japonica

Phalaroides japonica, commonly known as Japanese phalaris, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and adaptability to moist environments.
