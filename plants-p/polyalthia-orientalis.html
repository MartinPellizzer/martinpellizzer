Polyalthia orientalis

Polyalthia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves in the spring. The plant has compound leaves with several leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
