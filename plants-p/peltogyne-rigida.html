Peltogyne rigida

Peltogyne rigida, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered a species of concern for conservation.
