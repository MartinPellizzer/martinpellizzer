Pattara basal

Pattara basal, commonly known as the basal leaf of the *Pattara* genus, is a unique plant structure characterized by its position at the base of the plant.

It typically serves as a support system, anchoring the plant to the soil and facilitating nutrient absorption. The basal leaves are often larger and more robust compared to the upper foliage, adapting to environmental conditions. These leaves may also play a role in photosynthesis, especially in shaded environments.

Botanically, the basal leaf is an important feature for identifying and classifying species within the *Pattara* family.
