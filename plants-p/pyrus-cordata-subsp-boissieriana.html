Pyrus cordata subsp. boissieriana

Pyrus cordata subsp.

boissieriana, commonly known as the Armenian pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
