Piper tortivenulosum f. glabrum

Piper tortivenulosum f. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth piper or glabrous piper, is a variant of the species Piper tortivenulosum, which belongs to the Piperaceae family.

This plant is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy surface. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forest understories.
