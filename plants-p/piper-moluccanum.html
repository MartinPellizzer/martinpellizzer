Piper moluccanum

Piper moluccanum, commonly known as the Moluccan pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, peppery fruits. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. These berries contain numerous tiny seeds and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
