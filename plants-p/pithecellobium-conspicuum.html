Pithecellobium conspicuum

Pithecellobium conspicuum, commonly known as the showy poinsettia, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Pithecellobium conspicuum is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
