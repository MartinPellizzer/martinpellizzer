Phalaenopsis × bertii

Phalaenopsis × bertii, commonly known as the hybrid moth orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis amabilis and Phalaenopsis aphrodite.

It is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of showy, moth-like flowers. The plant typically has broad, dark green leaves and produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

It is popular in horticulture for its longevity and ease of care, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
