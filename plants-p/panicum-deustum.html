Panicum deustum

Panicum deustum, commonly known as dwarf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture.

Panicum deustum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
