Pelargonium × schulzianum

Pelargonium × schulzianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming throughout the summer months. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover due to its ability to spread vigorously.

Pelargonium × schulzianum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
