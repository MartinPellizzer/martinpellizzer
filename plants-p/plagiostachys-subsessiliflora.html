Plagiostachys subsessiliflora

Plagiostachys subsessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly pointed. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
