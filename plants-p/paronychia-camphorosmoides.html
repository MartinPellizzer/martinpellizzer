Paronychia camphorosmoides

Paronychia camphorosmoides, commonly known as the camphor-like mossy tamarisk, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and produces small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
