Premna crassa

Premna crassa, commonly known as the broadleaf premna, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Premna crassa is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
