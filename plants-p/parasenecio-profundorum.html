Parasenecio profundorum

Parasenecio profundorum, commonly known as the deep-sea senecio, is a rare flowering plant found in the deep ocean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique adaptations to high-pressure, low-light environments. The plant has specialized structures that allow it to anchor itself to the seafloor and absorb nutrients from the surrounding water. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are adapted for pollination by deep-sea organisms.

Due to its extreme habitat, it remains one of the least studied plants in the world.
