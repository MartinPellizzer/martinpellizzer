Phlox longifolia var. linearifolia

Phlox longifolia var. linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stem. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
