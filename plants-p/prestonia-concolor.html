Prestonia concolor

Prestonia concolor, commonly known as the white prestonia, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae, which includes milkweeds and related plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

Prestonia concolor is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
