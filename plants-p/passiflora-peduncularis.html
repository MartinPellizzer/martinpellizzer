Passiflora peduncularis

Passiflora peduncularis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora peduncularis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
