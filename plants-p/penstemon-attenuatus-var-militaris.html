Penstemon attenuatus var. militaris

Penstemon attenuatus var. militaris, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
