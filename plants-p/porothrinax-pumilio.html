Porothrinax pumilio

Porothrinax pumilio, commonly known as the dwarf palm, is a small, cold-tolerant palm native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features a slender, unbranched trunk that grows slowly and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
