Plantago arborescens subsp. maderensis

Plantago arborescens subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the tree plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, covered with dense, grayish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, pale green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its leaves are characterized by their broad, oval shape with a wavy margin and a hairy surface.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
