Piper tjambae

Piper tjambae, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The plant produces a pungent, aromatic compound called piperylene, which contributes to its distinctive flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

Piper tjambae is cultivated for its spicy fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
