Placus grandis

Placus grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stem. Its roots are fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Placus grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
