Prunus serrulata f. shujaku

Prunus serrulata f. shujaku, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree with white flowers, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive white blooms that contrast with its dark green foliage, making it a striking addition to gardens. This form is typically found in specific regions of Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant is closely related to other Prunus species, sharing similar growth habits and ecological requirements.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral display and its role in the broader Prunus genus.
