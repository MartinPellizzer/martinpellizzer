Penstemon spectabilis subsp. subinteger

Penstemon spectabilis subsp.

subinteger, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, enhancing its ecological value.
