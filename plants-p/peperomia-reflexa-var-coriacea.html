Peperomia reflexa var. coriacea

Peperomia reflexa var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive corrugated or rugose surface, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge, while the plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to any plant collection.
