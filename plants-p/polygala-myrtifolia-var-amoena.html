Polygala myrtifolia var. amoena

Polygala myrtifolia var. amoena, commonly known as the sweet flag or false sweet flag, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
