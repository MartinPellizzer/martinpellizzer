Protea grandiceps

Protea grandiceps, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of many small, tubular flowers that are typically white or cream in color. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure and striking appearance make it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea grandiceps is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
