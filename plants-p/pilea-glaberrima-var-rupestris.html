Pilea glaberrima var. rupestris

Pilea glaberrima var. rupestris, commonly known as the stone polka, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Mexico.

It features dense clusters of small, rounded, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant has a short, thick stem and is well adapted to rocky or dry environments. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This variety is prized for its unique foliage and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with moderate light.
