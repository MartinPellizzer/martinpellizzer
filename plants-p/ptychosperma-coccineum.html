Ptychosperma coccineum

Ptychosperma coccineum, commonly known as the red-fruit palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to New Guinea and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces striking red fruits that are clustered in panicles, which are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
