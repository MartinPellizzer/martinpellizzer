Phyllostachys aurea

Phyllostachys aurea, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is characterized by its rapid growth and clumping habit. The plant features long, slender culms that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues, especially in the sun-exposed areas. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Phyllostachys aurea is widely cultivated for landscaping and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
