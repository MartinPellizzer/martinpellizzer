Pectis incisifolia

Pectis incisifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features linear, serrated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis incisifolia is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
