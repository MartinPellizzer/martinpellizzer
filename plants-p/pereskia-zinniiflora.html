Pereskia zinniiflora

Pereskia zinniiflora, commonly known as the Zinnia-like Pereskia, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, broad leaves that are typically covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in clusters at the ends of the branches. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sunny, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its unique combination of succulent stems and ornamental flowers, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.
