Passiflora thaumasiantha

Passiflora thaumasiantha, commonly known as the Thai passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
