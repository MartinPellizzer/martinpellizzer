Pulicaria revoluta

Pulicaria revoluta, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Pulicaria revoluta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
