Piper bartlingianum var. parimanum

Piper bartlingianum var. parimanum, commonly known as the Parimanum pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries are used both as a spice and for ornamental purposes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
