Polygonum balansae subsp. induratum

Polygonum balansae subsp.

induratum, commonly known as the hardy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its stout, often hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its robust growth habit and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
