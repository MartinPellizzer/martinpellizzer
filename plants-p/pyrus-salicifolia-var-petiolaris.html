Pyrus salicifolia var. petiolaris

Pyrus salicifolia var. petiolaris, commonly known as the petioled pear, is a unique variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are elongated and have petioles that are notably longer than those of the typical Pyrus salicifolia, giving it a striking appearance. The plant is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial shade.

This variety is also valued for its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
