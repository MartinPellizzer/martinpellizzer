Potentilla viridescens

Potentilla viridescens, commonly known as greenish potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, wiry stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with a waxy coating. The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
