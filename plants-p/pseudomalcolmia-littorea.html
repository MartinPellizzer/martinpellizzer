Pseudomalcolmia littorea

Pseudomalcolmia littorea, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime climates with high salt exposure. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Pseudomalcolmia littorea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
