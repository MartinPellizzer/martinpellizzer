Prasophyllum exile

Prasophyllum exile, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike that bears small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, often in association with waterlogged soils. Prasophyllum exile is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its delicate and ephemeral blooms.
