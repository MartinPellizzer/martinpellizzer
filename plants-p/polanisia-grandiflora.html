Polanisia grandiflora

Polanisia grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora polanisia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
