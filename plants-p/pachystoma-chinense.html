Pachystoma chinense

Pachystoma chinense, commonly known as the Chinese stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the summer months. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
