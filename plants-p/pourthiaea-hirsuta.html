Pourthiaea hirsuta

Pourthiaea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild state.
