Pteris hexagona var. multifida

Pteris hexagona var. multifida, commonly known as the feather-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with multiple divisions creating a complex, lace-like pattern.

The spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.
