Polygala neurocarpa

Polygala neurocarpa, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and it has palmately lobed leaves. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Polygala neurocarpa is also noted for its milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus.
