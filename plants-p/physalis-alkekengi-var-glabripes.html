Physalis alkekengi var. glabripes

Physalis alkekengi var. glabripes, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or winter cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped, lobed leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose the seeds, which are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. These fruits are typically harvested in late autumn and are prized for their ornamental value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
