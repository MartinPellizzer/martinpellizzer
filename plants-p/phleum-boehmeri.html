Phleum boehmeri

Phleum boehmeri, commonly known as Timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures.

Timothy grass is valued for its use in forage and as a component in ecological restoration projects.
