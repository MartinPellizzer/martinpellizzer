Phlox variabilis

Phlox variabilis, commonly known as the variable phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlox variabilis thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
