Pfaffia paniculata var. intermedia

Pfaffia paniculata var. intermedia, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is often cultivated for use in herbal remedies.
