Pleurothallis triptera

Pleurothallis triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
