Poa trivialis var. sudetica

Poa trivialis var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic meadowgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a low-growing perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. The plant typically forms dense, mat-like tufts and has fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a powdery bloom. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
