Picea australis

Picea australis, commonly known as the southern fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the stems. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. Picea australis is often found in temperate rainforests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
