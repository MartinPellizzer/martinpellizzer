Panicum maximum var. altissimum

Panicum maximum var. altissimum, commonly known as giant Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The leaves are long, narrow, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color. It produces panicles of small, loose spikes that are typically brownish when mature.

This plant is highly invasive and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete other vegetation.
