Puccinellia kashmiriana

Puccinellia kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia kashmiriana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
