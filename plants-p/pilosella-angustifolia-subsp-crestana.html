Pilosella angustifolia subsp. crestana

Pilosella angustifolia subsp.

crestana, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
