Pertya bodinieri var. berberidoides

Pertya bodinieri var. berberidoides, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage that forms a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in cool, humid environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
