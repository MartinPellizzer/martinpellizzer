Pyrus communis var. immatura

Pyrus communis var. immatura, commonly known as the "nashi" or "Chinese white pear," is a cultivar of the European pear tree.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and crisp fruit that ripens early in the season. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, including jams and desserts, due to its unique flavor.

The plant is relatively hardy and can thrive in temperate climates with well-drained soil.
