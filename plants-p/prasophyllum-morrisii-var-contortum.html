Prasophyllum morrisii var. contortum

Prasophyllum morrisii var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted leek orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a single, slender flower spike that bears a single, pale green or cream-colored flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates. It is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Its unique morphology and restricted range make it a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
