Poa atropidiformis var. patagonica

Poa atropidiformis var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, fine-textured blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in grasslands and mountainous areas. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often tightly clustered.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
