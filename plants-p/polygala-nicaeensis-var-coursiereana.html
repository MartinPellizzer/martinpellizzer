Polygala nicaeensis var. coursiereana

Polygala nicaeensis var. coursiereana, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble insects. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and mountainous regions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
