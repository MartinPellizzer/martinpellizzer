Pipturus micronesicus

Pipturus micronesicus, commonly known as the Caroline Islands tree fern, is a distinctive member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is native to the Micronesian islands and thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features a tall, unbranched trunk with a crown of large, pinnately compound fronds. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its dramatic appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native range.
