Papaver radicatum f. pygmaeum

Papaver radicatum f. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a compact variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens or small spaces. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
