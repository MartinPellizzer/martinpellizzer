Piper glanduligerum var. subcoriaceum

Piper glanduligerum var. subcoriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the black pepper species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into the familiar black, white, and green peppercorns used in culinary applications. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and stimulant properties.

The variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and adaptability to various growing conditions.
