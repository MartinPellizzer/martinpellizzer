Potentilla microphylla var. latifolia

Potentilla microphylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, narrow to broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
