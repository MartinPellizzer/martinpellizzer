Pseudodatura arborea

Pseudodatura arborea, commonly known as tree jimsonweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers. The plant produces smooth, green berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds within a fleshy pulp. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Pseudodatura arborea is known for its toxic properties, containing tropane alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
