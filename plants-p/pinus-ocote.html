Pinus ocote

Pinus ocote, commonly known as the Mexican pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and temperate climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

Pinus ocote plays an important role in local ecosystems, offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
