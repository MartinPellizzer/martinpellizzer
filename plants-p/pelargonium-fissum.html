Pelargonium fissum

Pelargonium fissum, commonly known as the fissured geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
