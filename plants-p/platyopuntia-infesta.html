Platyopuntia infesta

Platyopuntia infesta, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Platyopuntia infesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh conditions.
