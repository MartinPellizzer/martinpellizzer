Piper margaritatum

Piper margaritatum, commonly known as the pearl vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits containing a single seed. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas of rainforests.
