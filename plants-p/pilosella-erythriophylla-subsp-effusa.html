Pilosella erythriophylla subsp. effusa

Pilosella erythriophylla subsp.

effusa, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude ecosystems and is often found in regions with limited vegetation.
