Phlomis polymorpha

Phlomis polymorpha, commonly known as spiked speedwell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Phlomis polymorpha is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils.
