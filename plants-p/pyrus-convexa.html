Pyrus convexa

Pyrus convexa, commonly known as the rough pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
