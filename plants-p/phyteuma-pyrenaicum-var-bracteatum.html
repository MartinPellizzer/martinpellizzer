Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. bracteatum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing vivid purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
