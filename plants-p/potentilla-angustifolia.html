Potentilla angustifolia

Potentilla angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters of five, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
