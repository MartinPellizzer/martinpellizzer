Phlox pilosa var. detonsa

Phlox pilosa var. detonsa, commonly known as the rough phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features hairy, gray-green foliage that adds texture to the landscape. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular selection for rock gardens and borders.
