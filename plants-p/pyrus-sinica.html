Pyrus sinica

Pyrus sinica, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellow-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by white to yellow fruits. The fruit has a sweet, juicy pulp and a hard, brown core, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and seasonal display of flowers and fruit.
