Pinus tabuliformis subsp. umbraculifera

Pinus tabuliformis subsp.

umbraculifera, commonly known as the Chinese fir or umbrella pine, is a variant of the Chinese white pine. It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown, which gives it its common name.

The tree has long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. It produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is native to eastern China and is valued for its ornamental and timber qualities.
