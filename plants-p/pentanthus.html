Pentanthus

Pentanthus, commonly known as the five-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that typically display shades of blue or purple. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is native to Europe and prefers moist, shady environments in woodlands.

Pentanthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
