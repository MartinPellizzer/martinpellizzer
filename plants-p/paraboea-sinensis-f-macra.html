Paraboea sinensis f. macra

Paraboea sinensis f. macra, commonly known as the giant paraboea, is a unique variant of the paraboea species native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink to red. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem, adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for its ecological benefits.
