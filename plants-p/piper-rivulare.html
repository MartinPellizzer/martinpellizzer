Piper rivulare

Piper rivulare, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
