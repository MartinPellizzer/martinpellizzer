Pectis multifida

Pectis multifida, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive, divided appearance.

Pectis multifida is known for its hard, heavy wood, which has been traditionally used for making tools and ornaments.
