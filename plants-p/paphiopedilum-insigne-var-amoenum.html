Paphiopedilum insigne var. amoenum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. amoenum, commonly known as the Amoenum Lady's Slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, showy flower with a pouch-like lip that is typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces a single, fragrant bloom during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
