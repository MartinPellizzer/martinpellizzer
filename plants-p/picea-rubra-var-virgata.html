Picea rubra var. virgata

Picea rubra var. virgata, commonly known as the red spruce, is a variant of the red spruce species native to the eastern United States.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in forest ecosystems and is sensitive to environmental changes.
