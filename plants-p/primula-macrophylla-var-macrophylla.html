Primula macrophylla var. macrophylla

Primula macrophylla var. macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese primrose or the Japanese primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a popular rock garden and container plant.
