Potentilla minor f. erythrodes

Potentilla minor f. erythrodes, commonly known as the redstem cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bright orange to red flowers.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are distinctly red, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
