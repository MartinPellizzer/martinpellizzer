Petrorhagia candica

Petrorhagia candica, commonly known as the white sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces vibrant clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy, blue-green leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Petrorhagia candica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
