Platanthera grandiflora f. carnea

Platanthera grandiflora f. carnea, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, spirally arranged inflorescences. The flowers are typically pink to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often more prominently colored in this form. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows, often growing in rich, organic soil.

This orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
