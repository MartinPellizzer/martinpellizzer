Piper subantillanum

Piper subantillanum, commonly known as the Surinam pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, reddish berries. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.
