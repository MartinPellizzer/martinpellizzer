Potentilla effusa var. gossypina

Potentilla effusa var. gossypina, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly gray foliage that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
