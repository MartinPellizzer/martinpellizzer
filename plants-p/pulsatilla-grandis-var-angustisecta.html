Pulsatilla grandis var. angustisecta

Pulsatilla grandis var. angustisecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and basal leaves that are narrow and divided into segments. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value in gardens.
