Poga oleosa

Poga oleosa, commonly known as the oil plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its most notable feature is the production of a valuable oil, which is extracted from its seeds.

This oil is used in both culinary and industrial applications due to its unique properties.
