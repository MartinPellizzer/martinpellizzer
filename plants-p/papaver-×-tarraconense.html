Papaver × tarraconense

Papaver × tarraconense, commonly known as the Tarraconense poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm and features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and landscapes.
