Phragmites tzvelevii

Phragmites tzvelevii, commonly known as the Tzvelev's reed, is a tall, perennial grass species native to parts of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, lakeshores, and riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant forms dense stands through its rhizomatous growth, which allows it to spread rapidly and dominate its environment. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its flowering spikes are tall and cylindrical.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
