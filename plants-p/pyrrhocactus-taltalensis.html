Pyrrhocactus taltalensis

Pyrrhocactus taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive green body and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring season, which are clustered at the ends of its stems. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
