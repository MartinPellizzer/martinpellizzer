Pollia omeiensis

Pollia omeiensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is typically evergreen, with leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Pollia omeiensis is also noted for its ability to produce vibrant, long-lasting blooms throughout the growing season.
