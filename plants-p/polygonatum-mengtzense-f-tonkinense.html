Polygonatum mengtzense f. tonkinense

Polygonatum mengtzense f. tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate leaves arranged in a basal rosette and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
