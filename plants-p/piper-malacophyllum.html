Piper malacophyllum

Piper malacophyllum, commonly known as the Malacophyllum pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
