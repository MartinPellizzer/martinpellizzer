Pleurothallis marthae

Pleurothallis marthae, commonly known as the Marthas' orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis marthae is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure and ease of cultivation under proper conditions.
