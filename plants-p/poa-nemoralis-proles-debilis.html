Poa nemoralis proles debilis

Poa nemoralis proles debilis, commonly known as the slender wood sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
