Phaseolus linearis f. transiens

Phaseolus linearis f. transiens, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover or trellis support. The plant produces small, elongated beans that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible pods, making it a versatile choice for both gardens and agriculture.
