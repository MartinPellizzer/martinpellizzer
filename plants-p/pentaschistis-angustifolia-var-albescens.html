Pentaschistis angustifolia var. albescens

Pentaschistis angustifolia var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered narrowleaf pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
