Pseudolaelia vasconcelosiana

Pseudolaelia vasconcelosiana, commonly known as the Vasconcelosian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Brazil.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a short, underground rhizome with a few erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty orchid collections.
