Polystachya reichenbachiana

Polystachya reichenbachiana, commonly known as the Reichenbach's spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Polystachya reichenbachiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
