Plectranthus natalensis

Plectranthus natalensis, commonly known as Natal hedge or Natal mint, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring aromatic, hairy leaves that emit a mint-like scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal use.

Its adaptability and unique fragrance contribute to its value in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
