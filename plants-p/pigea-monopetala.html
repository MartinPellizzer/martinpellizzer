Pigea monopetala

Pigea monopetala, commonly known as the single-petaled pigeonwings, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated petals that resemble the wings of a pigeon, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant colors that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Pigea monopetala is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
