Pyrethrum gracile

Pyrethrum gracile, commonly known as slender pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often grown for its ornamental value.

Pyrethrum gracile is also noted for its potential use in natural pest control due to the presence of pyrethrins in its flowers.
