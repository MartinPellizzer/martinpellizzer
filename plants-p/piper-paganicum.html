Piper paganicum

Piper paganicum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper paganicum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
