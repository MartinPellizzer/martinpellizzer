Potentilla chrysantha var. typica

Potentilla chrysantha var. typica, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has slender, woody stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
