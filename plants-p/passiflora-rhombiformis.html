Passiflora rhombiformis

Passiflora rhombiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rhomboid-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora rhombiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
