Potentilla bifurca var. monstruosa

Potentilla bifurca var. monstruosa, commonly known as the monstrous potentilla, is a striking ornamental shrub characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and unique, often twisted growth habit.

Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces showy, daisy-like blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and borders in temperate climates.
