Parinari floribunda

Parinari floribunda, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by globose, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
