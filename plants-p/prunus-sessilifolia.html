Prunus sessilifolia

Prunus sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are attached directly to the stem, lacking petioles. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, sweet-tasting fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Prunus sessilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
