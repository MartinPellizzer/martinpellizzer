Passerina dodecandra

Passerina dodecandra, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple florets that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Passerina dodecandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
