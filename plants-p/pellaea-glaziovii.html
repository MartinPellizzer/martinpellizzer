Pellaea glaziovii

Pellaea glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's maidenhair, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive appearance reminiscent of maidenhair ferns.

Pellaea glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
