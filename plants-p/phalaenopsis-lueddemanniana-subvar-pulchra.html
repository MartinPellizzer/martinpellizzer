Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana subvar. pulchra

Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana subvar. pulchra, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant flower spikes that bear numerous showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically has broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and slightly pubescent. It blooms once a year, usually during the winter months, and requires moderate light and consistent humidity to thrive.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
