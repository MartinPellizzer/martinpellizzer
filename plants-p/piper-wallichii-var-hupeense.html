Piper wallichii var. hupeense

Piper wallichii var. hupeense, commonly known as the Hupeh pepper or Chinese pepper, is a variant of the Piper wallichii species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to other pepper species.
