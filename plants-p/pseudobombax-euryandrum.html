Pseudobombax euryandrum

Pseudobombax euryandrum, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
