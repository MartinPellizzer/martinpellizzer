Penstemon albrightii

Penstemon albrightii, commonly known as the Albright's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.
