Potamogeton rufescens f. angustifolius

Potamogeton rufescens f. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct midrib, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
