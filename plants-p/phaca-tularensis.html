Phaca tularensis

Phaca tularensis, commonly known as the Tulare mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its small, tubular flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.
