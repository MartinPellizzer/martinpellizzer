Primula nivalis lineariloba

Primula nivalis lineariloba, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and other mountainous areas.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing, mat-forming habit, often found in rocky or alpine meadows. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Primula nivalis lineariloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
