Pitcairnia philippii

Pitcairnia philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's pitcairnia, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. The inflorescence consists of a tall, branched stalk bearing numerous small, white or pale pink flowers.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.
