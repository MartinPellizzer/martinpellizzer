Pentameris grandiflora

Pentameris grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered pentameris, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
