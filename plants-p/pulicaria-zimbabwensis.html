Pulicaria zimbabwensis

Pulicaria zimbabwensis, commonly known as the Zimbabweal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grey-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small yellow flowers arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grassland and savanna environments.

Pulicaria zimbabwensis is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it a resilient species in its native range.
