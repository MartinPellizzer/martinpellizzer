Penstemon cyananthus subsp. compactus

Penstemon cyananthus subsp.

compactus, commonly known as the blue beard penstemon, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
