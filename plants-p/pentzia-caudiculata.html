Pentzia caudiculata

Pentzia caudiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock fig, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Pentzia caudiculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
