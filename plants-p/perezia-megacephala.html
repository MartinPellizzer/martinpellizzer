Perezia megacephala

Perezia megacephala, commonly known as the giant helmet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a helmet, giving it its common name. The plant produces vividly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and wasps. Its leaves are long and strap-like, adapted to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

Perezia megacephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
