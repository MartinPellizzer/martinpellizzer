Pastinaca sativa subsp. divaricata

Pastinaca sativa subsp.

divaricata, commonly known as the curly top celery. This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like leaves that are deeply lobed and often curl upwards, giving it a unique appearance.

It is a biennial plant that thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained, loamy soil. The plant produces a stalk that is typically shorter and more robust compared to common celery.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, sharing features such as hollow stems and aromatic compounds.
