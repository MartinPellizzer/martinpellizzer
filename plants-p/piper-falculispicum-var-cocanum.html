Piper falculispicum var. cocanum

Piper falculispicum var. cocanum, commonly known as the Cocanum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper falculispicum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique flavor profile due to its high capsaicin content.

This variety is valued for both its medicinal properties and its role in regional culinary practices.
