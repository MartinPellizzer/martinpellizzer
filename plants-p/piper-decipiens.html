Piper decipiens

Piper decipiens, commonly known as the false pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as forests and undergrowth.
