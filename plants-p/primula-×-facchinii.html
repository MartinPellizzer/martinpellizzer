Primula × facchinii

Primula × facchinii, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a hardy perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily.

Primula × facchinii is also valued for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to attract pollinators.
