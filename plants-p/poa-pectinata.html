Poa pectinata

Poa pectinata, commonly known as the pectinata grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Poa pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
