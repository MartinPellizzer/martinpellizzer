Psydrax capensis

Psydrax capensis, commonly known as the Cape tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its pinnate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The Cape tamarind is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native habitats.
