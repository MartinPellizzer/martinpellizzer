Passiflora alata var. brasiliana

Passiflora alata var. brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that display a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The plant produces bright orange or yellow berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
