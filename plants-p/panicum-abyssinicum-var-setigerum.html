Panicum abyssinicum var. setigerum

Panicum abyssinicum var. setigerum, commonly known as African millet, is a grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy crop.

Its seeds are used for food, animal feed, and traditional crafts in various regions of Africa.
