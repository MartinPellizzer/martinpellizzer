Parrya menziesii var. lanuginosa

Parrya menziesii var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly parrya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

The botanical features of this species make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
