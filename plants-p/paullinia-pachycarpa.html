Paullinia pachycarpa

Paullinia pachycarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian peppercorn, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy fruits that resemble small peppers, which contain numerous seeds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Paullinia pachycarpa is also known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
