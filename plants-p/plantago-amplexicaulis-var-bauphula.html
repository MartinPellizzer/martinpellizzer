Plantago amplexicaulis var. bauphula

Plantago amplexicaulis var. bauphula, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. The plant is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields, due to its tolerance for harsh conditions.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and can also be consumed as a leafy vegetable.
