Populus puschkinii

Populus puschkinii, commonly known as the Puschkin's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the Russian Far East and parts of Northeast China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive white bark. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by the wind. Populus puschkinii is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
