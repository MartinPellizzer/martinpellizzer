Pothomorphe umbellata var. glabrior

Pothomorphe umbellata var. glabrior, commonly known as the smoothleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions and extreme temperatures.
