Potentilla bifida

Potentilla bifida, commonly known as the divided cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which are divided into five narrow lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Potentilla bifida is a perennial shrub that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
