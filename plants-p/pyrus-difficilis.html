Pyrus difficilis

Pyrus difficilis, commonly known as the difficult pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy white flowers and attractive foliage. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its fruit is a small, edible pear-like berry.

Pyrus difficilis is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
