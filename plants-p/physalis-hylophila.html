Physalis hylophila

Physalis hylophila, commonly known as the tropical ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
