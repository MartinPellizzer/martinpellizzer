Pilea pollicaris

Pilea pollicaris, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, glossy, waxy leaves that are typically round or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea pollicaris is known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings, which contributes to its widespread cultivation.
