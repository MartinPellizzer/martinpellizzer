Polystachya tricruris

Polystachya tricruris, commonly known as the three-footed sword fern, is a member of the Polystachyaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several feet in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruiting bodies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
