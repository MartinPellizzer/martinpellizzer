Platyopuntia orurensis

Platyopuntia orurensis, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in dense clusters of spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise desolate habitat. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
