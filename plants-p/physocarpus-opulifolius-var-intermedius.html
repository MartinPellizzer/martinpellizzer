Physocarpus opulifolius var. intermedius

Physocarpus opulifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as Smith's ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves that are pinnately arranged with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental value, hardiness, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and winter interest.
