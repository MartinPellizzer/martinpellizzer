Paepalanthus xiphophyllus

Paepalanthus xiphophyllus, commonly known as the swordleaf cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that branch out into a dense, shrub-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by fruiting bodies that disperse seeds. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Paepalanthus xiphophyllus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, thriving in full sun and low rainfall conditions.
