Phlox divaricata

Phlox divaricata, commonly known as wild blue phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems covered in opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
