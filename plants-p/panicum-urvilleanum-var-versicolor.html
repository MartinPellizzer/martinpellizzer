Panicum urvilleanum var. versicolor

Panicum urvilleanum var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its variable leaf coloration, which can range from green to red or purple, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray seed heads that are visually striking and often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

Its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions makes it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
