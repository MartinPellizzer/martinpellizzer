Pinalia pandurata

Pinalia pandurata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that hang in clusters, resembling small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green and are opposite in arrangement. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.

Pinalia pandurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
