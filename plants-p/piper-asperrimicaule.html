Piper asperrimicaule

Piper asperrimicaule, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rugged, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
