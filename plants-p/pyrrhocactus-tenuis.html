Pyrrhocactus tenuis

Pyrrhocactus tenuis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pyrrhocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
