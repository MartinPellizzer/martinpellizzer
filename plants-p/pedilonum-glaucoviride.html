Pedilonum glaucoviride

Pedilonum glaucoviride, commonly known as the blue-green pedilonum, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
