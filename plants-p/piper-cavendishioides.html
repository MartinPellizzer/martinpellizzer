Piper cavendishioides

Piper cavendishioides, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper-like plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, similar to a soursop, is edible and has a sweet, creamy texture with a mild, aromatic flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its potential medicinal properties.
