Potentilla sericea var. genuina

Potentilla sericea var. genuina, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a low-growing, spreading habit with hairy stems and leaves.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
