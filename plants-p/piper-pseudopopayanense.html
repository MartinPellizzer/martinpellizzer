Piper pseudopopayanense

Piper pseudopopayanense, commonly known as the Popayan pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, specifically Ecuador and northern Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
