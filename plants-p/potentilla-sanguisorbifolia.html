Potentilla sanguisorbifolia

Potentilla sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the bloodroot potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
