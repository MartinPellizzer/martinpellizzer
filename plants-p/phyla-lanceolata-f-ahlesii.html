Phyla lanceolata f. ahlesii

Phyla lanceolata f. ahlesii, commonly known as the Ahlesii form of the lanceolate phyla, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive leaf shape and structure.

This fern-like plant exhibits a lanceolate (spear-shaped) foliage, which is a defining feature of its morphology. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, favoring rich, well-drained soils. The plant's reproductive structures include sporangia, which produce spores for propagation.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and ecological adaptations.
