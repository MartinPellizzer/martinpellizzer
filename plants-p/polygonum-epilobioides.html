Polygonum epilobioides

Polygonum epilobioides, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or upright growth habit, with slender stems that often turn reddish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to red, and it produces small, oval berries that are a food source for birds.

Polygonum epilobioides is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
