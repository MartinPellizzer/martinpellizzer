Passiflora trifoliata var. tarmensis

Passiflora trifoliata var. tarmensis, commonly known as the Tarmian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, colorful flowers that resemble the structure of a passion fruit. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
