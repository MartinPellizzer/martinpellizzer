Psorothamnus thompsoniae var. thompsoniae

Psorothamnus thompsoniae var. thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's greasewood, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to extreme environmental conditions.
