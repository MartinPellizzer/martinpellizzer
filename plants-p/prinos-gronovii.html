Prinos gronovii

Prinos gronovii, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that turns silver-white on the underside of the leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Prinos gronovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
