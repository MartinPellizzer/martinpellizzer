Polemonium hultenii

Polemonium hultenii, commonly known as Hulten's Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
