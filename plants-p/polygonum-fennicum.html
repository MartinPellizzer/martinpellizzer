Polygonum fennicum

Polygonum fennicum, commonly known as the Finnish knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a resilient species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often edible parts.
