Phylogyne × broteri

Phylogyne × broteri, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two distinct orchid species.

It exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent plants, often displaying vibrant flowers with intricate patterns. The botanical characteristics of Phylogyne × broteri include a sympodial growth habit and specialized roots adapted for nutrient absorption. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters, showcasing a variety of colors and forms.

This hybrid is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and genetic diversity.
