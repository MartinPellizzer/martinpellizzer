Pithecellobium unguis-cati var. forfex

Pithecellobium unguis-cati var. forfex, commonly known as the cat's claw or cat's paw, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its unique, claw-like seed pods that encase the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
