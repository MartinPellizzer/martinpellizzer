Papaver cassandrinum

Papaver cassandrinum, commonly known as the Cassandria poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Papaver cassandrinum is typically found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Papaveraceae family.
