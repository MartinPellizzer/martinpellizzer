Podophyllum emodi var. jaeschkei

Podophyllum emodi var. jaeschkei, commonly known as the Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem with large, palmately lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
