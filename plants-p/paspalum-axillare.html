Paspalum axillare

Paspalum axillare, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum axillare is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and periodic flooding.
