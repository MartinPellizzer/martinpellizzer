Poa smirnowii subsp. mariae

Poa smirnowii subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Marie's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its foliage is often bluish-green, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and genetic distinctiveness.
