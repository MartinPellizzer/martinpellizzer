Piper atrobaccum var. pubinervulum

Piper atrobaccum var. pubinervulum, commonly known as the black-stemmed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its dark, glossy stems and compound leaves with prominently veined, pubescent surfaces.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple to black berries, which are edible but have a sharp, peppery taste.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique spices in traditional cuisines.
