Persicaria mitis var. pubescens

Persicaria mitis var. pubescens, commonly known as softleaf knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are softly hairy, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
