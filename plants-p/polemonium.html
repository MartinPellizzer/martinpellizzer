Polemonium

Polemonium, commonly known as Jacob's ladder, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polemoniaceae.

These plants are characterized by their cascading clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, purple, or white. They are native to North America and are often found in woodland or rocky habitats. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polemonium species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are commonly cultivated in gardens for their vibrant blooms.
