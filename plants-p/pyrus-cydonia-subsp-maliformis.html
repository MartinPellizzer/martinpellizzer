Pyrus cydonia subsp. maliformis

Pyrus cydonia subsp.

maliformis, commonly known as the sand pear, is a subspecies native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe.

The plant produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible pomes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
