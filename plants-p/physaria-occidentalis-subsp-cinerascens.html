Physaria occidentalis subsp. cinerascens

Physaria occidentalis subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and distinctive inflated seed pods.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptation to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
