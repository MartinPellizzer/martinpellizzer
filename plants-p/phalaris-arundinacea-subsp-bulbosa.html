Phalaris arundinacea subsp. bulbosa

Phalaris arundinacea subsp.

bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous reed, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, greenish flowers.

The plant produces bulbous structures at the base, which serve as storage organs and aid in its survival during adverse conditions. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along water edges.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes enable it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.
