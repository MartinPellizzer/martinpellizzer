Paullinia eriocarpa var. mollis

Paullinia eriocarpa var. mollis, commonly known as the velvet passionfruit, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow-orange flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a thick, spiky rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp that is edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique flavor in culinary applications.
