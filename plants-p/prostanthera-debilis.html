Prostanthera debilis

Prostanthera debilis, commonly known as the slender honeysuckle, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive mint-like scent.

Prostanthera debilis is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
