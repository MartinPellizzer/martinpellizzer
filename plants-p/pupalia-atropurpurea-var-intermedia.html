Pupalia atropurpurea var. intermedia

Pupalia atropurpurea var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, cone-shaped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, blooming in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
