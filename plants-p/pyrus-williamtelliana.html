Pyrus williamtelliana

Pyrus williamtelliana, commonly known as the William Tell pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is often grown in gardens and botanical collections.
