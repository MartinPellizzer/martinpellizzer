Piper tumidinodum

Piper tumidinodum, commonly known as the swollen node pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stems, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to red when mature.
