Polygonatum japonicum var. variegatum

Polygonatum japonicum var. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with distinct variegation patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique flowering display.
