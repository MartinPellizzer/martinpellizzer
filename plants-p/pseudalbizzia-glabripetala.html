Pseudalbizzia glabripetala

Pseudalbizzia glabripetala, commonly known as the glabrous petal acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange-pink inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.
