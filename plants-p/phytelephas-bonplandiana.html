Phytelephas bonplandiana

Phytelephas bonplandiana, commonly known as the Areca palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which eventually develop into elongated, spiny fruits. Its trunk is typically unbranched and covered with a fibrous, spongy layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for landscaping purposes.
