Paepalanthus pilosus var. pilosus

Paepalanthus pilosus var. pilosus, commonly known as the silky pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to pale green hairs that cover its stems and flowers. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a popular plant among cactus enthusiasts and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
