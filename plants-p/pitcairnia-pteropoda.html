Pitcairnia pteropoda

Pitcairnia pteropoda, commonly known as the feather-leaf pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are long, narrow, and often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces vibrant orange or red flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bats. Its unique leaf structure helps it collect and store water in its natural habitat, which is often humid and shaded.

Pitcairnia pteropoda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
