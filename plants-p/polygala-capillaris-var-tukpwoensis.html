Polygala capillaris var. tukpwoensis

Polygala capillaris var. tukpwoensis, commonly known as the Tukpwo milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, bean-like fruits.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
