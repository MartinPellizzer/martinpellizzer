Priva flabelliformis

Priva flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf privia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fan-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in rock gardens.
