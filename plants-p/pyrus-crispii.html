Pyrus crispii

Pyrus crispii, commonly known as the crisped pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that give it a unique appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sweet. Its foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Pyrus crispii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
