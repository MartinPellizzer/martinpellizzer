Pipturus pullei

Pipturus pullei, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-green foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has a rounded crown and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elliptical, leathery fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
