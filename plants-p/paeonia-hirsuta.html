Paeonia hirsuta

Paeonia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.

Paeonia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
