Polygala angulata var. diffusa

Polygala angulata var. diffusa, commonly known as the angular milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability.
