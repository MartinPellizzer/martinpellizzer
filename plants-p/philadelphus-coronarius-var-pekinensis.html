Philadelphus coronarius var. pekinensis

Philadelphus coronarius var. pekinensis, commonly known as the Chinese Mock Orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features showy, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
