Physurus bicalcaratus

Physurus bicalcaratus, commonly known as the two-shouldered fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Physurus bicalcaratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
