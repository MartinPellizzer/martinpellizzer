Phaseolus pulniensis

Phaseolus pulniensis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Phaseolus pulniensis is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious beans.
