Pelargonium atrofuscum

Pelargonium atrofuscum, commonly known as the dark-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
