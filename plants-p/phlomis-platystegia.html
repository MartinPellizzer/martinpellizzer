Phlomis platystegia

Phlomis platystegia, commonly known as the broad-stemmed mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to xeriscaped gardens and a valuable species for ecological studies.
