Paeonia delavayi subsp. lutea

Paeonia delavayi subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has large, palmately divided leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.
