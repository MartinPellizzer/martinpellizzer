Poa araratica subsp. psilolepis

Poa araratica subsp.

psilolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats or cushions.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinct midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and biodiversity.
