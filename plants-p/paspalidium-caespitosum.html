Paspalidium caespitosum

Paspalidium caespitosum, commonly known as tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Paspalidium caespitosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
