Peristeria humboldtii var. fulva

Peristeria humboldtii var. fulva, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring a fleshy, yellowish pseudobulb and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with white petals and a golden-yellow lip, which are highly prized in horticulture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
