Pilocereus russelianus subsp. margaritensis

Pilocereus russelianus subsp.

margaritensis, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering cactus native to South America. It is characterized by its swollen, rounded stems that resemble a moon, giving it its common name.

The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
