Physaria acutifolia var. purpurea

Physaria acutifolia var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often display a distinctive purple or reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and compact growth habit.
