Phyla lanceolata var. recognita

Phyla lanceolata var. recognita, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyla, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The species is often used in herbal medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
