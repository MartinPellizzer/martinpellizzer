Paeonia mascula var. corsica

Paeonia mascula var. corsica, commonly known as Corsican peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in a distinctive, thick layer of bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The Corsican peony is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
