Piper angulatum

Piper angulatum, commonly known as the angular pepper or black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with adventitious roots and dense, compound leaves.

Piper angulatum is cultivated for its essential oil and medicinal properties, as well as its use in traditional medicine.
