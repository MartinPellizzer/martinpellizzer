Potentilla frigida var. brauneana

Potentilla frigida var. brauneana, commonly known as the Arctic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, helping to retain heat in cold environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, cold climates.

This hardy species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand extreme weather conditions.
