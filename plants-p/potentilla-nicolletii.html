Potentilla nicolletii

Potentilla nicolletii, commonly known as Nicollet's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and is typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits known as achenes. It is often used in native plant gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Potentilla nicolletii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
