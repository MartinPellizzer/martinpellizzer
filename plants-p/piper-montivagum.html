Piper montivagum

Piper montivagum, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature, though they may change color as they ripen. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Piper montivagum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
