Pyrus atrovirens

Pyrus atrovirens, commonly known as the blackleaf pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and potential for hybridization with other pear species.
