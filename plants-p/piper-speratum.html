Piper speratum

Piper speratum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits resembling berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, peppery aroma.

Piper speratum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
