Podostemum subulatum

Podostemum subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf water fern, is a aquatic plant found in fast-flowing streams and rivers.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, rocky areas where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures are unique, with flowers that are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

Podostemum subulatum plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
