Primula brandisii

Primula brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

Primula brandisii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
