Piper calanyanum var. sardinanum

Piper calanyanum var. sardinanum, commonly known as Sardinian pepper, is a variant of the Piper calanyanum species native to the island of Sardinia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries are typically harvested when ripe, turning from green to a bright red or orange hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal practices.
