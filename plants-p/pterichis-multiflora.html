Pterichis multiflora

Pterichis multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese lantern vine, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptical in shape.
