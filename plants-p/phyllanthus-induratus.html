Phyllanthus induratus

Phyllanthus induratus, commonly known as the stone apple or Indian date, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, yellow or orange berries that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Phyllanthus induratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
