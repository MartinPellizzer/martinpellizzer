Phlegmatospermum Cochlearinum Var. Ochranthum

Ochre-flowered phlegmatospermum, scientifically known as Phlegmatospermum cochlearinum var. ochranthum, is a rare and striking plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeast Asia. Its most notable feature is its vibrant ochre-colored blooms, which resemble tiny suns against the lush green foliage. This unique variant of the phlegmatospermum genus is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance. Despite its beauty, it remains a little-known species, often overlooked in mainstream botanical discussions. Conservation efforts are increasingly important to protect this delicate plant from habitat loss and over-collection.
