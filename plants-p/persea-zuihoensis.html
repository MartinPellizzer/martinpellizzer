Persea zuihoensis

Persea zuihoensis, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern Japan and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
