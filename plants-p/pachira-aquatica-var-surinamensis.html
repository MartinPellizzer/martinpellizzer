Pachira aquatica var. surinamensis

Pachira aquatica var. surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam water chestnut, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in moist, well-draining soil and prefers partial to full sunlight. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is valued for its ability to purify water in aquatic environments.
