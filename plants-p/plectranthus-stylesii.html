Plectranthus stylesii

Plectranthus stylesii, commonly known as the Cape nettle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or violet in color and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Plectranthus stylesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight.
