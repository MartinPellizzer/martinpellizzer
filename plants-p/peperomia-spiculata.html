Peperomia spiculata

Peperomia spiculata, commonly known as the spike peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by small, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia spiculata is valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
