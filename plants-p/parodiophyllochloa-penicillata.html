Parodiophyllochloa penicillata

Parodiophyllochloa penicillata, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a species of grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
