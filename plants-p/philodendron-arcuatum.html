Philodendron arcuatum

Philodendron arcuatum, commonly known as the silver-veined philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a striking silver-veined pattern that contrasts with its deep green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and can reach lengths of several meters when given adequate support. Its leaves are often waxy and have a slightly rough texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Philodendron arcuatum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
