Persica vulgaris var. ruberrima

Persica vulgaris var. ruberrima, commonly known as the red persicaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
