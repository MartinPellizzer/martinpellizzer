Pneumatopteris Ligulata

Pneumatopteris ligulata is a unique fern species known for its delicate, lace-like fronds. Native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. This plant is often found in rocky crevices and forest floors where it can absorb moisture from the air. Its structure resembles a miniature tree, with a short trunk and branching fronds. Despite its fragile appearance, Pneumatopteris ligulata is remarkably resilient and has adapted to survive in challenging conditions.
