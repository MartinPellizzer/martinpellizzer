Puya macrura

Puya macrura, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and vibrant, showy inflorescences. The plant produces large, colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, often resembling a torch, hence its common name. Puya macrura is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, sunny conditions with well-drained soil.

It is a long-lived perennial, often surviving for decades, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in botanical gardens.
