Pycnarrhena montana

Pycnarrhena montana, commonly known as the mountain pycnarrhena, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy pink to purple hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Pycnarrhena montana is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
