Polypodium quadripartitum

Polypodium quadripartitum, commonly known as the four-parted sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that are divided into four distinct segments, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are also sometimes used as ornamental plants in horticulture due to their attractive, feathery appearance.
