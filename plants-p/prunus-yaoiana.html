Prunus yaoiana

Prunus yaoiana, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red drupes that are typically edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
