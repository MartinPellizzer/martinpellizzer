Primula cordifolia

Primula cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or alpine meadows. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Primula cordifolia is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
