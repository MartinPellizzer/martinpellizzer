Prestonia calycina

Prestonia calycina, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are typically clustered in racemes. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and emits a sweet fragrance, especially in the evening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Prestonia calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
