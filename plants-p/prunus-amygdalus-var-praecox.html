Prunus amygdalus var. praecox

Prunus amygdalus var. praecox, commonly known as the early almond.

This variety is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its edible, sweet kernels. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in commercial almond cultivation.

Its botanical features include compound leaves and a woody structure adapted to arid climates.
