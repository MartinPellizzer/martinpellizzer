Physaria kingii subsp. latifolia

Physaria kingii subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The species is known for its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.
