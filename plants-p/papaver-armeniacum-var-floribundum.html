Papaver armeniacum var. floribundum

Papaver armeniacum var. floribundum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a blackish central blotch. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit and distinctive foliage.
