Philodice hoffmannseggii var. compacta

Philodice hoffmannseggii var. compacta, commonly known as the compact pincushion flower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, grayish-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens and alpine collections.
