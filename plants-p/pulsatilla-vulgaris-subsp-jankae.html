Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp. jankae

Pulsatilla vulgaris subsp.

jankae, commonly known as the Janka's pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms consisting of pink to purple petals and yellow stamens.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its foliage consists of basal leaves divided into lobes, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early blooming flowers.
