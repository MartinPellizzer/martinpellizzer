Picea mariana f. grisea

Picea mariana f. grisea, commonly known as the gray spruce, is a naturally occurring variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-blue foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to the typical white spruce. This form is native to boreal and subarctic regions of North America, where it thrives in cold, harsh climates. The plant is evergreen, with needle-like leaves that are short and stiff, and it produces small, cone-like fruits.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
