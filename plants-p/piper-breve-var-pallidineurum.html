Piper breve var. pallidineurum

Piper breve var. pallidineurum, commonly known as the pale-leaf pepper plant, is a variant of the short pepper species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are pale green in color, distinguishing it from the typical green leaves of the standard variety. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorns, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, warm environments with partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique foliage and peppercorn production make it a notable ornamental and aromatic plant in botanical collections.
