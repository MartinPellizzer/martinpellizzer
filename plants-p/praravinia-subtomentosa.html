Praravinia subtomentosa

Praravinia subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky prairie violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, often bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, showy violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Praravinia subtomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
