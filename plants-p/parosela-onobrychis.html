Parosela onobrychis

Parosela onobrychis, commonly known as the Scotch broom, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is native to Europe and parts of Western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Parosela onobrychis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
