Pueraria seguinii

Pueraria seguinii, commonly known as the yellow cowpea, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, vine-like stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Pueraria seguinii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
