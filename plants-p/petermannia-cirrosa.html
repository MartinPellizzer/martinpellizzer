Petermannia cirrosa

Petermannia cirrosa, commonly known as the fleshy leaf ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. It has a low-growing habit with sprawling stems that can root at the nodes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, rocky soils and full sun exposure.
