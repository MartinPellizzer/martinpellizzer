Pyrus suavis

Pyrus suavis, commonly known as the sweet pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The leaves are broad and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Pyrus suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to both culinary and horticultural practices.
