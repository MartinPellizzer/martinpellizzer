Pachyphyllum foreroi

Pachyphyllum foreroi, commonly known as the Forero's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
