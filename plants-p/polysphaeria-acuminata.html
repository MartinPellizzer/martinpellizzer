Polysphaeria acuminata

Polysphaeria acuminata, commonly known as the spike moss, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Lepidosporales.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and a distinct sporophyte structure that consists of a stem with whorls of spore-bearing leaves. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Polysphaeria acuminata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
