Primulina glandaceistriata

Primulina glandaceistriata, commonly known as the glandular-striated primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically bright yellow or orange, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
