Phyllostachys reticulata var. castilloni-inversa

Phyllostachys reticulata var. castilloni-inversa, commonly known as the reverse variegated bamboo, is a cultivar of the giant bamboo species characterized by its unique foliage.

Its leaves exhibit a striking reverse variegation pattern, with green veins contrasting against a lighter green or white background. This variation is due to a genetic mutation that affects chlorophyll distribution. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow to significant heights, adding a dramatic visual impact to gardens.
