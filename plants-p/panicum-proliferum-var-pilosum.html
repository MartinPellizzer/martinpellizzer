Panicum proliferum var. pilosum

Panicum proliferum var. pilosum, commonly known as silky foxtail, is a grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender, silky-haired inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage.

However, it can also be considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its aggressive spreading nature.
