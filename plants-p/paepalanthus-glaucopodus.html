Paepalanthus glaucopodus

Paepalanthus glaucopodus, commonly known as the blue-veined puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, waxy coating, giving them a distinctive blue or grayish hue. The plant produces an inflorescence of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are adapted to collect and store water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
