Paeonia monticola

Paeonia monticola, commonly known as the alpine peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces large, palmately divided leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. Paeonia monticola thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine meadows at high elevations.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
