Pandanus parachevalieri

Pandanus parachevalieri, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in panicles, which are dense clusters. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal or wetland areas.

The red pandanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
