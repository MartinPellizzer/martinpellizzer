Peperomia arifolioides

Peperomia arifolioides, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically silver-white to gray-green in color, with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or red, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia arifolioides is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
