Pilosella brachiata subsp. tapina

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

tapina, commonly known as the slenderleaf mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Pilosella brachiata subsp.

tapina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
