Prunus sativa proles congestiflora

Prunus sativa proles congestiflora, commonly known as the clustered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red or black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
