Polystachya amazonica

Polystachya amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian spike grass, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in the undergrowth of dense forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor in the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Polystachya amazonica plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
