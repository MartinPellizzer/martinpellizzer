Potentilla clusiana subsp. triphylla

Potentilla clusiana subsp.

triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia and the Americas. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets, and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features include woody stems and a compact growth habit, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
