Phlox condensata subsp. condensata

Phlox condensata subsp.

condensata, commonly known as smooth phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, showy clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
