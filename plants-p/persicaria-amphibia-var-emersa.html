Persicaria amphibia var. emersa

Persicaria amphibia var. emersa, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent, ribbed stems that can root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a waxy coating.

This versatile species is often found in wet or marshy habitats, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
