Polygonum quarrei

Polygonum quarrei, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to red. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and meadows.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize areas quickly, making it both a weed and a pioneer species in ecological succession.
