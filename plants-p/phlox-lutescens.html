Phlox lutescens

Phlox lutescens, commonly known as the yellow phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Phlox lutescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
