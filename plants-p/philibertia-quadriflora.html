Philibertia quadriflora

Philibertia quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered filbert, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Philibertia quadriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
