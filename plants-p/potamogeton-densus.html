Potamogeton densus

Potamogeton densus, commonly known as dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant is characterized by its dense growth pattern, which can form thick mats on the water surface or along the bottom. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton densus is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its ability to absorb pollutants from the water.
