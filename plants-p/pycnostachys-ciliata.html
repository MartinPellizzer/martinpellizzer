Pycnostachys ciliata

Pycnostachys ciliata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pycnostachys ciliata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
