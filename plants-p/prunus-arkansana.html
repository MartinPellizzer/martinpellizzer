Prunus arkansana

Prunus arkansana, commonly known as the Arkansas cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have finely serrated edges.

Prunus arkansana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife and supporting local biodiversity.
