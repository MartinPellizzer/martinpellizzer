Podochilus rhombeus

Podochilus rhombeus, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features a slender, erect stem with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in a raceme, with a distinctive rhombic shape, giving the species its common name.

Podochilus rhombeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
