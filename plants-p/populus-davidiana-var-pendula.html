Populus davidiana var. pendula

Populus davidiana var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous branches that droop gracefully, creating a weeping appearance. The tree has heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is often valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique visual appeal.
