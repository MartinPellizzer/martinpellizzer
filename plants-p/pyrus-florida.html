Pyrus florida

Pyrus florida, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. Its fruit is a small, edible apple-like berry that ripens in late summer.

Pyrus florida is often found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.
