Prosthechea ebanii

Prosthechea ebanii, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the wet season. Prosthechea ebanii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
