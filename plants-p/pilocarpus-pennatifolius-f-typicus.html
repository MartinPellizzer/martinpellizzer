Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. typicus

Pilocarpus pennatifolius f. typicus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellowish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Rubiaceae family.
