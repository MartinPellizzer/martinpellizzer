Phoradendron lingulatum

Phoradendron lingulatum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, evergreen leaves and produces white or yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while deriving its own energy through photosynthesis. Its berries, which are often red, are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats.
