Phoradendron tetrastachyum var. spathulifolium

Phoradendron tetrastachyum var. spathulifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to host trees through its haustoria.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually clustered at the ends of its branches. Its flowers are typically white or pale green, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays an important ecological role by providing food for various wildlife species.
