Pecluma ptilotos var. caespitosa

Pecluma ptilotos var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered pecluma, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a compact growth habit with dense, tufted foliage that often forms a low, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its textured appearance.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.
