Phrynium thyrsiflorum

Phrynium thyrsiflorum, commonly known as the flame bamboo or torch plant, is a tropical perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cane-like stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces striking, spiky inflorescences that resemble torches, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phrynium thyrsiflorum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
