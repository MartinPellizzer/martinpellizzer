Papaver tauricum

Papaver tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that surround a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in early summer, adding a vivid splash of color to the landscape.

The Tauric poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been the subject of botanical study due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
