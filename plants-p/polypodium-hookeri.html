Polypodium hookeri

Polypodium hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, deeply lobed fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is thick and robust, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The fronds are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat.
