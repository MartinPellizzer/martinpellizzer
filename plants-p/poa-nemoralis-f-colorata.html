Poa nemoralis f. colorata

Poa nemoralis f. colorata, commonly known as the pink meadow-grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

This variant is distinguished by its striking pinkish-red inflorescence, which contrasts with the typical green color of the species. It is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant's foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscape design.
