Potentilla ascendens

Potentilla ascendens, commonly known as the ascending cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its low-growing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla ascendens thrives in harsh, cold environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
