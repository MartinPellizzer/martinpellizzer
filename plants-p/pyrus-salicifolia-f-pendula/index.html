Pyrus Salicifolia F. Pendula

Pyrus salicifolia f. pendula is a unique cultivar of the common pear tree known for its striking weeping form. This ornamental plant features long, cascading branches that create a dramatic visual effect in gardens and landscapes. Its delicate, lance-shaped leaves add a soft, feathery texture to its overall appearance. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by ornamental fruit that adds color throughout the growing season. While it shares characteristics with its parent species, this form stands out for its graceful, flowing silhouette.
