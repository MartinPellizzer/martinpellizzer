Perezia capito

Perezia capito, commonly known as the Cape town daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Perezia capito is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
