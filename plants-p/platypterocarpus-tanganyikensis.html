Platypterocarpus tanganyikensis

Platypterocarpus tanganyikensis, commonly known as the Lake Tanganyika maple, is a deciduous tree native to the rocky shores of Lake Tanganyika in East Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climate of its native region.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
