Primula franchetii var. tibetica

Primula franchetii var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow or white petals, often with a contrasting purple or reddish throat. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its alpine habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
