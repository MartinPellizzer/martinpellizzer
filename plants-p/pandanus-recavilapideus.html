Pandanus recavilapideus

Pandanus recavilapideus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional crafts.
