Potamogeton amplifolius

Potamogeton amplifolius, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically broader at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical fruits containing single seeds. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton amplifolius is often found in quiet waters such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
