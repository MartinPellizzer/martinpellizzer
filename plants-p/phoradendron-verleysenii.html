Phoradendron verleysenii

Phoradendron verleysenii, commonly known as the Verleysen's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Neotropics.

It has small, leathery leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent. Its berries are typically red or orange and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by influencing host tree health and providing resources for wildlife.
