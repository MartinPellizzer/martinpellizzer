Pyrus chamaemespilus var. glabra

Pyrus chamaemespilus var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features smooth, grayish-brown bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, subglobose berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native environment.
