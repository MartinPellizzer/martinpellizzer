Picea brachytyla f. rhombisquamea

Picea brachytyla f. rhombisquamea, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a variant of the brachytyla spruce, characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America. The leaves are short, scale-like, and arranged in crosses, giving the tree a distinctive, quilt-like appearance. This form is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique morphology and hardiness.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a landscape specimen.
