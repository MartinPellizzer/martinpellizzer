Pulsatilla pratensis var. albida

Pulsatilla pratensis var. albida, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmate leaves that are divided into three to five lobes. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat.
