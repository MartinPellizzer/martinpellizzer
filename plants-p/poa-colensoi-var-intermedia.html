Poa colensoi var. intermedia

Poa colensoi var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hill grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue when fresh. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
