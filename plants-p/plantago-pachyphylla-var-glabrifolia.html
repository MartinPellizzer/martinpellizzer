Plantago pachyphylla var. glabrifolia

Plantago pachyphylla var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the hairyleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, a feature adapted to its drought-prone environment. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence compared to other varieties of Plantago pachyphylla.
