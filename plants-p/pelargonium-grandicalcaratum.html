Pelargonium grandicalcaratum

Pelargonium grandicalcaratum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers range in color from pink to red. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Pelargonium grandicalcaratum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
