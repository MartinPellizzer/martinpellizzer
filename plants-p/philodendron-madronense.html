Philodendron madronense

Philodendron madronense, commonly known as the Madrone Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
