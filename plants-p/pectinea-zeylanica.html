Pectinea zeylanica

Pectinea zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and wetlands. The plant features arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a fibrous root system. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Pectinea zeylanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens.
