Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. rubroaurantiacum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

rubroaurantiacum, commonly known as the red-orange poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in late summer.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.
