Plantago major var. rosea

Plantago major var. rosea, commonly known as the red plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its distinctive pink to reddish flower spikes.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant features broad, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are arranged in dense, upright spikes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both garden use and medicinal purposes.
