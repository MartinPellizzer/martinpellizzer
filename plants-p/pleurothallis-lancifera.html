Pleurothallis lancifera

Pleurothallis lancifera, commonly known as the lancifera orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features linear, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a faint fragrance. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is part of the Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
