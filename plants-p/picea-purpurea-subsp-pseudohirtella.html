Picea purpurea subsp. pseudohirtella

Picea purpurea subsp.

pseudohirtella, commonly known as the purple spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark blue-green foliage that turns a striking purplish-red in the fall.

The needles are short, stiff, and often have a slightly waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its cold, arid environment. It produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds.

This subspecies is well adapted to harsh mountain conditions, thriving in poor soils and high elevations.
