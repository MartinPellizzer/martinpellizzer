Puccinellia kjellmannii

Puccinellia kjellmannii, commonly known as the Kjellmann's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in steppes and coastal dunes, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of dryland ecosystems.
