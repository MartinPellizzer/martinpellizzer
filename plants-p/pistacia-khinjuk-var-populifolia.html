Pistacia khinjuk var. populifolia

Pistacia khinjuk var. populifolia, commonly known as the Persian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
