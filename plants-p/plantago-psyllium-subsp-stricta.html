Plantago psyllium subsp. stricta

Plantago psyllium subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psyllium, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing tiny, white seeds. These seeds are rich in soluble fiber and are often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or cultivated areas.
