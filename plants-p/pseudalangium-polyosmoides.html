Pseudalangium polyosmoides

Pseudalangium polyosmoides, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant has a slender, twining stem with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The fruit is a follicle that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.
