Plantago robusta

Plantago robusta, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
