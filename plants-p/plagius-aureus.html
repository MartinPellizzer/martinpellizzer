Plagius aureus

Plagius aureus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters at the top of the stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Plagius aureus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
