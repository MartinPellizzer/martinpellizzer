Piper parmatum

Piper parmatum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical structure of Piper parmatum supports its role in both culinary and medicinal applications.
