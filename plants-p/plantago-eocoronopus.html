Plantago eocoronopus

Plantago eocoronopus, commonly known as the ancient plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are clustered near the top of the stem. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been cultivated for its potential therapeutic properties.
