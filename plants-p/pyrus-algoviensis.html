Pyrus algoviensis

Pyrus algoviensis, commonly known as the Algoviensis pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Balkan Peninsula, particularly found in Serbia and Bosnia and Herzegovina. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with compound leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like apple, is typically edible and has a sweet flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
