Potamogeton × pseudolacunatus

Potamogeton × pseudolacunatus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton lucens and Potamogeton natans, exhibiting traits from both parent species. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often found on separate plants, a feature typical of dioecious species.

Potamogeton × pseudolacunatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through oxygen production.
