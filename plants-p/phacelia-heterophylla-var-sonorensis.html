Phacelia heterophylla var. sonorensis

Phacelia heterophylla var. sonorensis, commonly known as the Sonoran phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and variable leaf shapes, which range from lobed to linear, adapting to arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suited to desert environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various desert wildlife.
