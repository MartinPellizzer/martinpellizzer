Petrophytum caespitosum subsp. acuminatum

Petrophytum caespitosum subsp.

acuminatum, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its gray-green, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spiny tubercles, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The species is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
