Panicum bourgaei

Panicum bourgaei, commonly known as the golden panicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of bright yellow inflorescences that resemble tufts of golden hair. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, adding a delicate texture to the landscape.

Panicum bourgaei is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
