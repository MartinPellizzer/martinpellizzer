Pulegium micranthum

Pulegium micranthum, commonly known as narrow-leaved pulegium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and lavender. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Pulegium micranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
