Paeonia obovata var. amurensis

Paeonia obovata var. amurensis, commonly known as the Amur peony, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with ovate to obovate leaflets and produces showy, pink to red flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its robust root system and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in colder climates.
