Pilea tatamensis

Pilea tatamensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea tatamensis prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
