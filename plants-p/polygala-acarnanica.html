Polygala acarnanica

Polygala acarnanica, commonly known as the Acarnanian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala acarnanica is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
