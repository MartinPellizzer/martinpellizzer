Pandanus acanthostylus

Pandanus acanthostylus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescences are terminal and consist of a spicate axis with numerous small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
