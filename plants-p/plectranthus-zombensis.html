Plectranthus zombensis

Plectranthus zombensis, commonly known as the zombie plant, is a unique member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its striking, velvety foliage.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in warm, humid conditions and prefers well-draining soil. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its unusual appearance and folklore surrounding its name, it has gained attention in both horticultural and scientific communities.
