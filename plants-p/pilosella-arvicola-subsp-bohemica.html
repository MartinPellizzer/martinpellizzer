Pilosella arvicola subsp. bohemica

Pilosella arvicola subsp.

bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian cat's ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically thrives in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and geographic distribution compared to the main species.
