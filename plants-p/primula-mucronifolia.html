Primula mucronifolia

Primula mucronifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, or pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers partial shade and well-drained soil.
