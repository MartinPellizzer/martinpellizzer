Pleurothallis pertenuis

Pleurothallis pertenuis, commonly known as the slender pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is known for its unique floral structure and is often found in association with specific host trees.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
