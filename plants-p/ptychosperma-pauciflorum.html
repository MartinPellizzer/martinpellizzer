Ptychosperma pauciflorum

Ptychosperma pauciflorum, commonly known as the slender cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects, and its seed cones are enclosed in a fibrous, woody structure. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Ptychosperma pauciflorum is considered critically endangered and is threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.
