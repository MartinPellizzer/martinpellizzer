Potentilla geranioides f. urmiensis

Potentilla geranioides f. urmiensis, commonly known as the Persian cinquefoil, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has rounded, leathery leaves with a slightly hairy texture, typical of the Potentilla genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
