Pyrus insolita

Pyrus insolita, commonly known as the rare or unusual pear.

It is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are not typically consumed by humans but are of interest to botanists. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus insolita is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
