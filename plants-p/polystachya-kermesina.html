Polystachya kermesina

Polystachya kermesina, commonly known as the Kermesina bamboo, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Polystachya kermesina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
