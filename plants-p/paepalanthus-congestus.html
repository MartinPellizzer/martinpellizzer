Paepalanthus congestus

Paepalanthus congestus, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract various pollinators. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with reduced leaves and modified stems that store water.

Paepalanthus congestus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
