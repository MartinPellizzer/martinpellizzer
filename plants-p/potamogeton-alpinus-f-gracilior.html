Potamogeton alpinus f. gracilior

Potamogeton alpinus f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender alpine pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Potamogetonaceae family. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in alpine lakes and streams.

Its morphology reflects adaptations to cold, oligotrophic conditions, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems in high-altitude regions.
