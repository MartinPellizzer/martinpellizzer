Panicum boscianum

Panicum boscianum, commonly known as Boscian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, feathery appearance and ability to provide habitat for small wildlife.
