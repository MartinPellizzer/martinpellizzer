Polystichum platyphyllum f. mettenii

Polystichum platyphyllum f. mettenii, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped, dark green fronds that are densely covered with small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, evergreen foliage.
