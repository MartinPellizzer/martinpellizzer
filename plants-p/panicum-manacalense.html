Panicum manacalense

Panicum manacalense, commonly known as Manacalense grass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is adapted to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in open habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
