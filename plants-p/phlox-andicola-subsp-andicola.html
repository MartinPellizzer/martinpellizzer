Phlox andicola subsp. andicola

Phlox andicola subsp.

andicola, commonly known as the Andicola phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
