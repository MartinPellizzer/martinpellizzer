Polygala exasperata

Polygala exasperata, commonly known as the devil's-bit scabious, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala exasperata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
