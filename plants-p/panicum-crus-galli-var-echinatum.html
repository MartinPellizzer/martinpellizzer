Panicum crus-galli var. echinatum

Panicum crus-galli var. echinatum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its striking purple to pink flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
