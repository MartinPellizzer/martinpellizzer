Pandanus diodon

Pandanus diodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots and leaves have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The species is well adapted to coastal and humid environments, thriving in sandy soils.
