Pouteria mammosa

Pouteria mammosa, commonly known as the cupuacu, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit has a creamy, acidic pulp that is rich in nutrients and antioxidants. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
