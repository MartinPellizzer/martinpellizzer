Persea microphylla

Persea microphylla, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, yellow-orange berry with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

Persea microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, tropical climates.
