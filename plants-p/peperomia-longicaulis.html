Peperomia longicaulis

Peperomia longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly waxy appearance. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spadices, typically appearing in shades of white or pale purple.

Peperomia longicaulis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.
