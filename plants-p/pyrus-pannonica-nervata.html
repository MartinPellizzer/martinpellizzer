Pyrus pannonica nervata

Pyrus pannonica nervata, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pannonian Basin in Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pome fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional agriculture and horticulture.
