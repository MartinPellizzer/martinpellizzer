Platonia insignis var. insignis

Platonia insignis var. insignis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *P. insignis*, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, round fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of natural dye.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
