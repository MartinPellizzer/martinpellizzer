Paurotis androsana

Paurotis androsana, commonly known as the Androsan rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or reddish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its foliage consists of narrow, upright leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Paurotis androsana is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and arid environments.
