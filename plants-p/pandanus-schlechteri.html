Pandanus schlechteri

Pandanus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's pandanus, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in swampy or saline soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional crafts in its native regions.
