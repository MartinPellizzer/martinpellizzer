Pisum sativum convar. xiphocarpum

Pisum sativum convar. xiphocarpum, commonly known as the wild pea, is a botanical variant of the pea plant characterized by its smaller, more robust pods and seeds.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and it produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds.

This variety is often studied for its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.
