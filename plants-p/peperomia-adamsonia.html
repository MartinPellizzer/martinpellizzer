Peperomia adamsonia

Peperomia adamsonia, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters along the stems. It is a succulent-like plant with thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in dry conditions.

Peperomia adamsonia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it an ideal choice for indoor cultivation.
