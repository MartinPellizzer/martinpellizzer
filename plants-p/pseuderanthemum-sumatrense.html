Pseuderanthemum sumatrense

Pseuderanthemum sumatrense, commonly known as the Sumatran false pomegranate, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, followed by round, fleshy fruits that resemble pomegranates but are not related. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
