Peponidium boinense

Peponidium boinense, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical vine belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests or along coastal areas.

Peponidium boinense is also known for its ability to climb using aerial roots and can be invasive in certain regions.
