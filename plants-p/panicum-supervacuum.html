Panicum supervacuum

Panicum supervacuum, commonly known as the vacuum panic grass, is a unique species of grass that thrives in extreme low-pressure environments.

It possesses specialized cellular structures that allow it to retain moisture in arid, high-altitude regions. Its leaves are narrow and densely packed, reducing water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, delicate seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Due to its adaptability, it is of interest to botanists studying extremophiles and plant resilience.
