Prunella petiolaris

Prunella petiolaris, commonly known as narrow-leaved heal-all, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Prunella petiolaris is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
