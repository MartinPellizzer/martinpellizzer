Prangos hissarica

Prangos hissarica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, adorned with lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers in late summer, which are arranged in terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
