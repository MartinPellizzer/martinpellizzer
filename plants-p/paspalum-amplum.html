Paspalum amplum

Paspalum amplum, commonly known as the broadleaf grass or broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, and it blooms during the summer months. Paspalum amplum is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.

It is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along the edges of water bodies.
