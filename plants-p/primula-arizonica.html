Primula arizonica

Primula arizonica, commonly known as Arizona primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Primula arizonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
