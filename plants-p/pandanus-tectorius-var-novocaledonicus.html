Pandanus tectorius var. novocaledonicus

Pandanus tectorius var. novocaledonicus, commonly known as the New Caledonian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are aromatic and contain a high concentration of volatile oils, which contribute to its distinctive scent.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
