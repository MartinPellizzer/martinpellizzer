Poa nannfeldtii

Poa nannfeldtii, commonly known as the Nannfeldt's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region of Chile and Argentina. This perennial grass typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous areas. It has slender, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
