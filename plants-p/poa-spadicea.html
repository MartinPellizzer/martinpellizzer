Poa spadicea

Poa spadicea, commonly known as the purple grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in open, disturbed habitats. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are typically purple to pink in color.

Poa spadicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flower heads.
