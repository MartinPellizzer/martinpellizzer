Philadelphus gattingeri

Philadelphus gattingeri, commonly known as the Gattenger's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and pleasant fragrance.
