Pelargonium multibracteatum

Pelargonium multibracteatum, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features hairy leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium multibracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
