Primula yunnanensis subsp. fragilis

Primula yunnanensis subsp.

fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant features a low-growing rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its alpine habitat. It blooms in spring, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
