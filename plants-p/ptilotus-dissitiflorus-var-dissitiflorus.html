Ptilotus dissitiflorus var. dissitiflorus

Ptilotus dissitiflorus var. dissitiflorus, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
