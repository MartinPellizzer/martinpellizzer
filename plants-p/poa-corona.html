Poa corona

Poa corona, commonly known as the crowned grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive crown-like structure at the base of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Poa corona typically blooms in the spring, producing small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are adapted to its alpine environment.
