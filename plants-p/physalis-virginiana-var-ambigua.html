Physalis virginiana var. ambigua

Physalis virginiana var. ambigua, commonly known as the common groundcherries or the husk tomato, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, egg-shaped fruits that enclose the edible seeds, turning orange-red when mature. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields, roadsides, and forest edges.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and use in culinary applications.
