Pelargonium angustissimum

Pelargonium angustissimum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
