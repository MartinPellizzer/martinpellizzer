Polystachya costaricensis

Polystachya costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican featherplant, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
