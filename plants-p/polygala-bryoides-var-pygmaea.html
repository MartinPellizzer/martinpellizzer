Polygala bryoides var. pygmaea

Polygala bryoides var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, bottle-shaped inflorescences that resemble small bottles. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
