Polygala vulgaris var. alba

Polygala vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
