Parastranthus luteus var. capitatus

Parastranthus luteus var. capitatus, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a bushy growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
