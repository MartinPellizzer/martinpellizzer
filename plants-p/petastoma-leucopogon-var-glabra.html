Petastoma leucopogon var. glabra

Petastoma leucopogon var. glabra, commonly known as the smoothleaf bluebells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves that are lance-shaped and often clustered at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
