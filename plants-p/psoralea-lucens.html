Psoralea lucens

Psoralea lucens, commonly known as the shining psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Psoralea lucens is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
