Potamogeton × bifrons

Potamogeton × bifrons, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, slender stems that often bear whorled leaves, which are typically narrow and strap-like. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers usually occurring on separate plants.

Potamogeton × bifrons plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
