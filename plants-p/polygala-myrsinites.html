Polygala myrsinites

Polygala myrsinites, commonly known as the redstem milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, and its leaves are small, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
