Polygala calvipes

Polygala calvipes, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and smooth, and the flowers are often pink or white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
