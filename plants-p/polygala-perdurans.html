Polygala perdurans

Polygala perdurans, commonly known as the everlasting polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. Its name "everlasting" refers to the durability of its dried flowers, which retain their color and form for extended periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
