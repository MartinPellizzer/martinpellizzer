Psidium guayabita var. oblongatum

Psidium guayabita var. oblongatum, commonly known as the oblongata guayaba, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically oblong in shape, with a smooth, waxy skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its nutritional content.
