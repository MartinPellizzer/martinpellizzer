Polystachya pamelae

Polystachya pamelae, commonly known as Pamelae's sword fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Polystachyaceae and is characterized by its elegant, arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the undersides of its leaves, and it reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
