Pavetta montana var. multiflora

Pavetta montana var. multiflora, commonly known as the mountain pavetta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
