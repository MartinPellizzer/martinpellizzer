Prenanthes purpurea var. intermedia

Prenanthes purpurea var. intermedia, commonly known as the purple coneflower or purple ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of lavender to purple flowers, which bloom from late summer into early fall. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
