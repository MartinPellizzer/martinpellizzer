Primula bellidifolia subsp. boreiocalliantha

Primula bellidifolia subsp.

boreiocalliantha, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinct yellow or green throat. Its leaves are usually basal, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a fine, hairy layer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
