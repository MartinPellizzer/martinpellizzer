Prostanthera subalpina

Prostanthera subalpina, commonly known as the Alpine mint bush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Prostanthera subalpina produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
