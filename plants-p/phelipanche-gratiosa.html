Phelipanche gratiosa

Phelipanche gratiosa, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often opening in the morning. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
