Pavia khassyana

Pavia khassyana, commonly known as the Kashmiri pavia, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring. Its botanical features include a woody stem and aromatic leaves, which are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Pavia khassyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
