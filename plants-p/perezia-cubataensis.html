Perezia cubataensis

Perezia cubataensis, commonly known as the Cuban perezia, is a striking orchid native to the island of Cuba.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often purple or violet-colored flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Perezia cubataensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
