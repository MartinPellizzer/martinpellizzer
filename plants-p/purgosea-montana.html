Purgosea montana

Purgosea montana, commonly known as the mountain purgative, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a robust root system and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its most notable botanical feature is its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it a hardy species.

The plant is also recognized for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments.
