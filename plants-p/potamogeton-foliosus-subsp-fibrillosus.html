Potamogeton foliosus subsp. fibrillosus

Potamogeton foliosus subsp.

fibrillosus, commonly known as the fibrous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in fine, fibrous strands, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its reproductive structures, including small, green flowers, are usually found in clusters near the water surface.
