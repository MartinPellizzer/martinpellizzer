Podolepis kendallii var. nanus

Podolepis kendallii var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf podolepis, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, mat-like formation. The plant produces tiny, clustered white flowers during the cooler months, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
