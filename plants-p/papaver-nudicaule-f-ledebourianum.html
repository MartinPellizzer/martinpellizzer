Papaver nudicaule f. ledebourianum

Papaver nudicaule f. ledebourianum, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a botanical variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in alpine and rocky environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of basal, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
