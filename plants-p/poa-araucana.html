Poa araucana

Poa araucana, commonly known as the Araucaria grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in mountainous or alpine environments. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring.

Poa araucana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
