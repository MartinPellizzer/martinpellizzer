Phyllanthus leai

Phyllanthus leai, commonly known as the creeping phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, prostrate stems that form a mat-like structure. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture with prominent veins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
