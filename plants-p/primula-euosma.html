Primula euosma

Primula euosma, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces showy blooms in early spring.

Primula euosma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
