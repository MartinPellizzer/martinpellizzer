Phyllanthus huallagensis

Phyllanthus huallagensis, commonly known as the Andean spurge, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often surrounded by bracts, and its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

It is typically found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to the high-altitude environments of the Andes.
