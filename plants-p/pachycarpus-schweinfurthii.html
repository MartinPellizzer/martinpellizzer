Pachycarpus schweinfurthii

Pachycarpus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. It produces small, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
