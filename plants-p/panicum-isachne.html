Panicum isachne

Panicum isachne, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden to bronze-colored inflorescence, which resembles a foxtail in appearance. The plant typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum isachne is often considered a weed but is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
