Petrophile incurvata

Petrophile incurvata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer seasons.

Petrophile incurvata is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.
