Plocoglottis lacuum

Plocoglottis lacuum, commonly known as the Lake Swamp Fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the wetlands of South America.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in swampy areas with rich, organic soil. The plant has slender, arching fronds that are typically less than a meter in length, with a distinctive, finely divided leaf structure. Its spore-producing structures are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic feature of the fern family.

Due to habitat loss, Plocoglottis lacuum is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
