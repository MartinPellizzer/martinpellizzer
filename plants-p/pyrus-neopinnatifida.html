Pyrus neopinnatifida

Pyrus neopinnatifida, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears but have a more delicate flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
