Pistolochia pseudoalpestris

Pistolochia pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine false spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cold temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora and plant adaptations.
