Pinus sylvestris var. norvegica

Pinus sylvestris var. norvegica, commonly known as the Norwegian spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous or boreal forest regions.

This variety is valued for its timber and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its hardiness and adaptability.
