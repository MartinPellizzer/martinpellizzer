Pyrus pyrifolia f. discolor

Pyrus pyrifolia f. discolor, commonly known as the white-fleshed pear, is a cultivar of the Japanese pear.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white flesh and a mild, sweet flavor. The plant produces small, yellow-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible fruit.
