Picconia

Picconia, commonly known as the pink bottlebrush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plants typically have narrow, leathery leaves and produce vibrant red or pink flowers. Picconia species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

They prefer well-drained soils and full sun, making them suitable for coastal and tropical climates.
