Panicum lindenbergianum var. pallidum

Panicum lindenbergianum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces fluffy, pale golden to silver-green inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dry, open habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and drought tolerance.
