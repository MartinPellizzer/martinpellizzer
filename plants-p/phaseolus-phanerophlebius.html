Phaseolus phanerophlebius

Phaseolus phanerophlebius, commonly known as the yellow snake bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It produces elongated, yellow pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is characterized by its vigorous vine growth habit. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The botanical features of this species make it a valuable crop in regions with suitable growing conditions.
