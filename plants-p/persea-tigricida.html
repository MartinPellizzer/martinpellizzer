Persea tigricida

Persea tigricida, commonly known as the tiger-leaf persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown venation pattern. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into edible, orange-red berries.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruit.
