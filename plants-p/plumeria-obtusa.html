Plumeria obtusa

Plumeria obtusa, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant is also notable for its milky sap, which can cause skin irritation in some individuals.
