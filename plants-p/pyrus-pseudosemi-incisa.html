Pyrus pseudosemi-incisa

Pyrus pseudosemi-incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are edible pomes, similar to apples, though they are less commonly cultivated.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential horticultural value.
