Piper rubiginosum

Piper rubiginosum, commonly known as the crimson pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, red, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its fruits contain essential oils and alkaloids, contributing to its medicinal properties.

Piper rubiginosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.
