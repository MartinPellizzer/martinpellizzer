Piper salentoi

Piper salentoi, commonly known as the Salento pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Piper salentoi thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical research.
