Paphiopedilum hookerae var. volonteanum

Paphiopedilum hookerae var. volonteanum, commonly known as the Volonteanum lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with a subtle reddish tinge. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific cultivation needs, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
