Phlox dasyphylla

Phlox dasyphylla, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Phlox dasyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
