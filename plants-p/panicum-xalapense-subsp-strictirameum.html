Panicum xalapense subsp. strictirameum

Panicum xalapense subsp.

strictirameum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its striking purple to reddish inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its rapid establishment.
