Potentilla × cavarnana

Potentilla × cavarnana, commonly known as the hybrid shrub or sometimes the golden cinquefoil, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are typical of the Potentilla genus. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and they turn red or orange in the fall.

Potentilla × cavarnana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
