Phaseolus crotalarioides

Phaseolus crotalarioides, commonly known as the arrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a climbing habit with tendrils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
