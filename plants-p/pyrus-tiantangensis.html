Pyrus tiantangensis

Pyrus tiantangensis, commonly known as the Tiantang pear, is a species of pear native to China.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy white flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in the autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable addition to botanical collections.
