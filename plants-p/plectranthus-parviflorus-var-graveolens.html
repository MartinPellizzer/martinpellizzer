Plectranthus parviflorus var. graveolens

Plectranthus parviflorus var. graveolens, commonly known as the lemon verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both garden beds and containers.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for herbal gardens and aromatherapy applications.
