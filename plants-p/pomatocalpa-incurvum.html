Pomatocalpa incurvum

Pomatocalpa incurvum, commonly known as the curved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a unique pouch-like structure at the base of the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
