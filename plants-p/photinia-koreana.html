Photinia koreana

Photinia koreana, commonly known as the Korean rose.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Korea, characterized by its vibrant red leaves that turn crimson in autumn. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and features small white flowers that bloom in spring. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking coloration.

Photinia koreana prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
