Philibertia tomentella

Philibertia tomentella, commonly known as the woolly filbert, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Philibertia tomentella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
