Plantago weldenii

Plantago weldenii, commonly known as Welden's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. Its seed capsules are distinctive, containing numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it is characterized by its basal rosette growth habit and is part of the Plantaginaceae family.
