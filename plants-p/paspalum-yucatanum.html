Paspalum yucatanum

Paspalum yucatanum, commonly known as Yucatan grass, is a perennial grass native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Paspalum yucatanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
