Primula × brevistyla

Primula × brevistyla, commonly known as the short-styled primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in early spring, and its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
