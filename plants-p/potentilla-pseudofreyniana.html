Potentilla pseudofreyniana

Potentilla pseudofreyniana, commonly known as the Mexican cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems arising from the base.
