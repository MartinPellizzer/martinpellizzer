Pandanus amboinensis

Pandanus amboinensis, commonly known as the screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiral-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used to make ropes and mats.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
