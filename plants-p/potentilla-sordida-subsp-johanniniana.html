Potentilla sordida subsp. johanniniana

Potentilla sordida subsp.

johanniniana, commonly known as the dark potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its botanical features include hairy stems and finely divided foliage, which help it adapt to harsh environmental conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
