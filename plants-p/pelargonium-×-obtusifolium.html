Pelargonium × obtusifolium

Pelargonium × obtusifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its long-lasting blooms.
