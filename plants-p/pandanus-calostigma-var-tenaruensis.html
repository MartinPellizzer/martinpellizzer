Pandanus calostigma var. tenaruensis

Pandanus calostigma var. tenaruensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that grow in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
