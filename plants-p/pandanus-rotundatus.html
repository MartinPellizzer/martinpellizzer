Pandanus rotundatus

Pandanus rotundatus, commonly known as the round-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the palm family.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences with both male and female flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts. Its roots and leaves have been utilized by indigenous communities for various purposes, including food and construction.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid and saline conditions.
