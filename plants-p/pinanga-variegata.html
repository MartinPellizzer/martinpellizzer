Pinanga variegata

Pinanga variegata, commonly known as the variegated bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, slender stems and striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Arecaceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Its growth habit is clumping, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
