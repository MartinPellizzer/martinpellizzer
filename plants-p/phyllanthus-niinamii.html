Phyllanthus niinamii

Phyllanthus niinamii, commonly known as the Japanese wax plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including Japan, where it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Phyllanthus niinamii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
