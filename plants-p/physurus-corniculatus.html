Physurus corniculatus

Physurus corniculatus, commonly known as the horned violets, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Violaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Physurus corniculatus is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
