Piper atropremnon

Piper atropremnon, commonly known as black pepper vine, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its climbing habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-chambered berries. The berries contain tiny, dark seeds that are the source of the spice known as black pepper.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and ornamental value.
