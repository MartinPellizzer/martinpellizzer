Prunus persica f. mollis

Prunus persica f. mollis, commonly known as the velvet peach, is a unique cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and fruit.

This variety is distinguished by its dense, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance compared to typical peach trees. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit is typically smaller and sweeter than standard peaches. It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to produce optimal fruit.
