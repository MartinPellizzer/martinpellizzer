Plantago macrostachys f. brachypus

Plantago macrostachys f. brachypus, commonly known as the short-stalked plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. Its flowers are arranged in spikes, with the inflorescence being shorter and more compact compared to the typical form.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
