Prunus insititia var. villosa

Prunus insititia var. villosa, commonly known as the woolly cherry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible, red or purple berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
