Paphiopedilum insigne var. mooreanum

Paphiopedilum insigne var. mooreanum, commonly known as the Moorean Lady's Slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the island of Moorea in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in the humid, tropical rainforests of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making it a prized and protected plant in botanical collections.
