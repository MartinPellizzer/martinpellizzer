Phacelia lyonii

Phacelia lyonii, commonly known as Lyon's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Lyon's phacelia is valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
