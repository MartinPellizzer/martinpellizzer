Phalangium hirsutum

Phalangium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy phalangium, is a species of plant belonging to the family Phalangiaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, leafy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are often pale green or white, and they bloom in late spring or early summer.
