Plectranthus aromaticus

Plectranthus aromaticus, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus aromaticus is often used in herbal teas and as a culinary herb due to its pleasant fragrance and medicinal properties.
