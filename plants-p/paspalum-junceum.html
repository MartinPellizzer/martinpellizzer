Paspalum junceum

Paspalum junceum, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum junceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
