Poa laxiuscula

Poa laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, pale green blades. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as lawns, roadsides, and meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers.

Poa laxiuscula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate various environmental conditions.
