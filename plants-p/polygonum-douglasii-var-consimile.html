Polygonum douglasii var. consimile

Polygonum douglasii var. consimile, commonly known as the Douglas's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Polygonum douglasii var. consimile is known for its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
