Potentilla aurea subvar. pusilla

Potentilla aurea subvar. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf goldmink, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This subvariety is adapted to harsh, cold environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
