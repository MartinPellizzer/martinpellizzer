Pisum viride

Pisum viride, commonly known as the green pea plant, is a member of the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, green pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support itself.

Botanically, it is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
