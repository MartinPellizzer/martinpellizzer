Pinus sylvestris var. phaeosperma

Pinus sylvestris var. phaeosperma, commonly known as the dark cone pine, is a variant of the Scots pine.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish cones and darker bark compared to the typical Scots pine. This variety is native to parts of Europe and is often found in mountainous regions. The leaves are needle-like, typically growing in clusters of five, and are a deep green color.

It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
