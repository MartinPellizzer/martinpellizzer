Polybotrya gaudichaudiana

Polybotrya gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the golden torch fern, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange fronds that resemble torches. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce adventitious roots. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a distinctive glossy appearance and a long, narrow shape.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
