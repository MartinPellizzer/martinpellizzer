Phlomis samia subsp. bovei

Phlomis samia subsp.

bovei, commonly known as the Balkan torchflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange composite flowers.

The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
