Piper flexicaule

Piper flexicaule, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features slender, trailing stems that often grow along the ground or climb supports, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface. Small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, followed by small, peppery berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
