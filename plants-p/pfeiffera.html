Pfeiffera

Pfeiffera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often fragrant, white or pale-colored flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. Pfeiffera species typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and produce small, clustered inflorescences. They are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

The genus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
