Panicum jurgensenii

Panicum jurgensenii, commonly known as Jurgensen's bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing a clump-forming habit with stiff, upright stems. The plant produces small, blue-green spikelets that are often tinged with purple, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
