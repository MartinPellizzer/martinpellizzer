Potentilla grenieriana

Potentilla grenieriana, commonly known as Grenier's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually palmately divided into five leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla grenieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
