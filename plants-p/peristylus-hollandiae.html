Peristylus hollandiae

Peristylus hollandiae, commonly known as the Dutch peristylus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their shape and nectar-rich structure.

Peristylus hollandiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
