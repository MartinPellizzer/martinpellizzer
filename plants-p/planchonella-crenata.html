Planchonella crenata

Planchonella crenata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with five petals and a prominent central stamen.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
