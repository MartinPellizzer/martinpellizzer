Pinus densiflora f. congesta

Pinus densiflora f. congesta, commonly known as the dense-flowered Japanese red pine, is a naturally occurring variant of the Japanese red pine.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and closely packed needles, which give it a distinctive, bushy appearance. This form typically exhibits shorter, more clustered foliage compared to the typical variety, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique visual characteristics.
