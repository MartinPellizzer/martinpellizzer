Paphiopedilum ciliolare

Paphiopedilum ciliolare, commonly known as the Ciliated Slipper Orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a feature typical of many slipper orchids. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a mix of white, green, and yellow hues, often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a prized species among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
