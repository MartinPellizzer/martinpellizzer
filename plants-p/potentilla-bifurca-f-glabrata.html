Potentilla bifurca f. glabrata

Potentilla bifurca f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth potentilla, is a compact, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically glabrous, or smooth, giving it its specific epithet "glabrata.". The plant produces clusters of delicate, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular plant in horticultural settings.
