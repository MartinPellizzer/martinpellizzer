Polygala latifolia

Polygala latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The plant's nectar is a valuable food source for various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
