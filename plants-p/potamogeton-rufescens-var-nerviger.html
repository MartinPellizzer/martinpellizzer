Potamogeton rufescens var. nerviger

Potamogeton rufescens var. nerviger, commonly known as the hairy water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
