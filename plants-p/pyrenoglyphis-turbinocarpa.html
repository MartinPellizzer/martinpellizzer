Pyrenoglyphis turbinocarpa

Pyrenoglyphis turbinocarpa, commonly known as the turbinocarpic fern, is a rare and distinctive member of the Osmundaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy structures. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sporangia, which are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. Its habitat is typically moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
