Prunus insititia var. juliana

Prunus insititia var. juliana, commonly known as the Japanese black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, dark red to black fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by its distinctive berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
