Pseuderanthemum latifolium var. levicaspa

Pseuderanthemum latifolium var. levicaspa, commonly known as the broadleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The leaves are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, depending on the variety. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
