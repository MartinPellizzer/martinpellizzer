Panicum marginatum var. majus

Panicum marginatum var. majus, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, purple-tinted seed heads that add visual interest to gardens. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance landscapes. Its feathery appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental use.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
