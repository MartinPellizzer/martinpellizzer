Pelargonium hypoleucum

Pelargonium hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-veined geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white veins on dark green leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pelargonium hypoleucum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
