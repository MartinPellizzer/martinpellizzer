Parinari obtusifolia

Parinari obtusifolia, commonly known as the African apple or wild mango, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and usually elliptical or ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, orange-red fruits that are highly valued in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.
