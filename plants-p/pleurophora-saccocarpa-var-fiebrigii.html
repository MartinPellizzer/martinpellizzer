Pleurophora saccocarpa var. fiebrigii

Pleurophora saccocarpa var. fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated pods that resemble bladders. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces seed pods that split open when mature to release seeds.
