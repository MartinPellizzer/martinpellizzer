Protium montanum

Protium montanum, commonly known as mountain satinash, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests. The plant has simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by small, fleshy fruits.

The bark and leaves of Protium montanum contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and phytochemical research.
