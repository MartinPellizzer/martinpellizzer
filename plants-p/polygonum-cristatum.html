Polygonum cristatum

Polygonum cristatum, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are pinnately arranged and its showy, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum cristatum is sometimes used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to colonize areas quickly.
