Polygala albicans var. silvae

Polygala albicans var. silvae, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
