Pelargonium × notatum

Pelargonium × notatum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. Native to southern Africa, it is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
