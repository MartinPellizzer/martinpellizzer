Panicum virgatum var. pilosum

Panicum virgatum var. pilosum, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, brown seed heads in late summer, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for native landscapes.

Its deep root system also makes it drought-tolerant and useful for erosion control.
