Phaseolus capensis

Phaseolus capensis, commonly known as the African bean or Cape bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and is often cultivated for both its seeds and ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and nitrogen-fixing ability.
