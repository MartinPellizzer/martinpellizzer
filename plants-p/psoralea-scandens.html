Psoralea scandens

Psoralea scandens, commonly known as the climbing psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its twining stems that climb along other vegetation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Psoralea scandens is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
