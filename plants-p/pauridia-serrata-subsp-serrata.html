Pauridia serrata subsp. serrata

Pauridia serrata subsp.

serrata, commonly known as the serrated pauridia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America. It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are distinctly serrated along the edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its fibrous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained soils, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
