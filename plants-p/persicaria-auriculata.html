Persicaria auriculata

Persicaria auriculata, commonly known as the earleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Persicaria auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
