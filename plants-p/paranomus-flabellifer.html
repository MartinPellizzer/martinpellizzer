Paranomus flabellifer

Paranomus flabellifer, commonly known as the fan-leaf parasol, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble a fan, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often as a parasite on other plants.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
