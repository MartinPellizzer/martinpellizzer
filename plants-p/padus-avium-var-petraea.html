Padus avium var. petraea

Padus avium var. petraea, commonly known as the rock crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple native to rocky and mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its small, often red or yellow fruits and dense, compact growth habit. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, rocky environments. Its leaves are typically oval with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces showy white or pink flowers in spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
