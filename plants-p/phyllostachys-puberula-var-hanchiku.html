Phyllostachys puberula var. hanchiku

Phyllostachys puberula var. hanchiku, commonly known as the Hanchiku bamboo, is a variety of the fargesia bamboo group.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter green underside. This bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
