Petagnia

Petagnia, commonly known as the common petagnia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Petagnia is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
