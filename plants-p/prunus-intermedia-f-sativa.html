Prunus intermedia f. sativa

Prunus intermedia f. sativa, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Chinese date, is a deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial in various climates.
