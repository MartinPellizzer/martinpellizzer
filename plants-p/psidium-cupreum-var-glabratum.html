Psidium cupreum var. glabratum

Psidium cupreum var. glabratum, commonly known as the glabrous guava, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has smooth, glabrous bark and dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary and medicinal uses.
