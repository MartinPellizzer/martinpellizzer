Piper asterotrichum var. asterotrichum

Piper asterotrichum var. asterotrichum, commonly known as the star-haired pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous small, dark seeds. Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.

The varietal name "asterotrichum" refers to the star-like arrangement of its fine hairs on the leaves.
