Potentilla chrysanthoides

Potentilla chrysanthoides, commonly known as golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and showy blooms.
