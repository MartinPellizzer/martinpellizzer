Platycodon grandiflorus f. leucanthus

Platycodon grandiflorus f. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a variant of the Chinese bellflower.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant is a perennial herb native to East Asia, particularly China and Korea. It features sturdy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
