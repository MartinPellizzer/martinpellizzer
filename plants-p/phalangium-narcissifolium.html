Phalangium narcissifolium

Phalangium narcissifolium, commonly known as the golden leaf phalangium, is a rare and intriguing plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, and glossy leaves that resemble the petals of a narcissus flower, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as dense forests or along stream banks. Its foliage is often a vibrant yellow or gold, which stands out against the greenery of its natural habitat.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it remains a relatively understudied species in botanical research.
