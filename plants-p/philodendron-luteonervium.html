Philodendron luteonervium

Philodendron luteonervium, commonly known as the golden-veined philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that have a vibrant green color with prominent yellow veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring white or yellow markings that enhance its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
