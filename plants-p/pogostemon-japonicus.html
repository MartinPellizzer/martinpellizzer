Pogostemon japonicus

Pogostemon japonicus, commonly known as Japanese mint, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It has square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its aromatic leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
