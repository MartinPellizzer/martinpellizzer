Phoradendron dunstervillorum

Phoradendron dunstervillorum, commonly known as the Mexican mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on oak trees in Mexico.

It has small, elliptical leaves and produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while conducting photosynthesis to supplement its energy needs. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological relationships and limited distribution.
