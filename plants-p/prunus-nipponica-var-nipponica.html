Prunus nipponica var. nipponica

Prunus nipponica var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and its bark is smooth and reddish-brown when young. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators during the spring bloom.
