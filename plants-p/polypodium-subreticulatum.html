Polypodium subreticulatum

Polypodium subreticulatum, commonly known as the reticulate holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are typically bright green and covered with a dense layer of tiny, scale-like structures. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

Polypodium subreticulatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
