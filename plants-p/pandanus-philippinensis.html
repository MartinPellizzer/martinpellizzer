Pandanus philippinensis

Pandanus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by insects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local communities.
