Pieris forrestii

Pieris forrestii, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen or forest pieris, is a species of evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture, and they remain evergreen throughout the year. It produces small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
