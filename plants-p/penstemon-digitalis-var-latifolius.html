Penstemon digitalis var. latifolius

Penstemon digitalis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are broader than those of the typical Penstemon digitalis. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
