Protea venulosa

Protea venulosa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it its common name.

Protea venulosa is a popular ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
