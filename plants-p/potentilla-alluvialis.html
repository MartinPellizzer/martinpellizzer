Potentilla alluvialis

Potentilla alluvialis, commonly known as the alluvial cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and floodplains, showcasing its adaptability to alluvial environments. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Potentilla alluvialis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in riparian ecosystems.
