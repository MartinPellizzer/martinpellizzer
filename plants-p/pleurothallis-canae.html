Pleurothallis canae

Pleurothallis canae, commonly known as the Cane's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found attached to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette, which helps it conserve moisture.

Pleurothallis canae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
