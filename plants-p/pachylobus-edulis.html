Pachylobus edulis

Pachylobus edulis, commonly known as the edible lichen, is a unique organism that belongs to the lichen group, which is a symbiotic combination of a fungus and an alga.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found on tree trunks and rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that can vary in color from green to yellowish depending on the season and environmental conditions. It is valued for its nutritional content, providing a source of vitamins and minerals in certain indigenous communities.

However, its use is limited due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements.
