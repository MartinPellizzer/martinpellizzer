Prunus myrobalana var. gigantea

Prunus myrobalana var. gigantea, commonly known as the Persian cherry or Chinese date, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces clusters of small, pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellow fruits resembling dates. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
