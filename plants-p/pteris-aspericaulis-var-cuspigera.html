Pteris aspericaulis var. cuspigera

Pteris aspericaulis var. cuspigera, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in small, rough spines. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
