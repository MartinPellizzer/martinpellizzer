Plantago bracteosa

Plantago bracteosa, commonly known as the bracted plantain, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
