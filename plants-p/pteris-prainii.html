Pteris prainii

Pteris prainii, commonly known as the Prain's brake, is a fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of brownish, hairy tissue.

Pteris prainii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
