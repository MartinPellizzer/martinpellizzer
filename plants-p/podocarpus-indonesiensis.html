Podocarpus indonesiensis

Podocarpus indonesiensis, commonly known as the Java yellowwood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits. The tree typically grows in humid tropical and subtropical climates, often in mountainous regions. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
