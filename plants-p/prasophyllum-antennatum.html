Prasophyllum antennatum

Prasophyllum antennatum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or well-drained soils. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, slender spike of white or pale pink flowers. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in regions of New South Wales and Victoria.

Prasophyllum antennatum is also noted for its ecological significance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
