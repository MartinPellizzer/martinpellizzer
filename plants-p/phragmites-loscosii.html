Phragmites loscosii

Phragmites loscosii, commonly known as the Loscosii reed, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant forms dense stands with long, narrow leaves that are often coated in a fine layer of silky hairs. Its flowering stems produce fluffy, white plumes that are characteristic of the genus Phragmites.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
