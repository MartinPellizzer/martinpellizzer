Potentilla australis subsp. malyana

Potentilla australis subsp.

malyana, commonly known as the Malyana cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a sprawling or upright growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
