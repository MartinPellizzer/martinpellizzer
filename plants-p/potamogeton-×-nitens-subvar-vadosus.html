Potamogeton × nitens subvar. vadosus

Potamogeton × nitens subvar. vadosus, commonly known as the aquatic arrowhead, is a hybrid plant species that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets. It is commonly found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
