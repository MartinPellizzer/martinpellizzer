Pseudatalaya

Pseudatalaya, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air humidity and moderate temperatures.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pseudatalaya is considered a conservation concern.
