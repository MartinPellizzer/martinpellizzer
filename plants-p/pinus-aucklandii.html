Pinus aucklandii

Pinus aucklandii, commonly known as the Auckland pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often forming dense stands in its native habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable species in New Zealand's flora.
