Piper carnibracteum

Piper carnibracteum, commonly known as the Brazil pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark red color, often containing a single seed.

Piper carnibracteum is typically found in tropical rainforests and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
