Paspalum semialatum var. ecklonii

Paspalum semialatum var. ecklonii, commonly known as Ecklon's paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
