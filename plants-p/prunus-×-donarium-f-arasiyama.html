Prunus × donarium f. arasiyama

Prunus × donarium f. arasiyama, commonly known as the Arasiyama cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its striking floral display.

It is a hybrid between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus padus, resulting in a plant with distinctive features. The tree typically produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. Its foliage is dark green and provides a lush backdrop during the growing season.

The Arasiyama cherry is prized in horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
