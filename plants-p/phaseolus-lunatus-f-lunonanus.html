Phaseolus lunatus f. lunonanus

Phaseolus lunatus f. lunonanus, commonly known as the moon bean or white kidney bean, is a unique variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive white, kidney-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with climbing vines, producing small, pale yellow flowers. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets and a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
