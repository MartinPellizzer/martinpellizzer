Primula latifolia subsp. elisabethae

Primula latifolia subsp.

elisabethae, commonly known as the Elisabeth's broadleaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It features broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
