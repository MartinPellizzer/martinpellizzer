Periploca angustifolia

Periploca angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf periploca, is a shrub native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in rocky or limestone soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
