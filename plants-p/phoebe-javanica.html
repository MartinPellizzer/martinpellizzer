Phoebe javanica

Phoebe javanica, commonly known as the Java phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Phoebe javanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
