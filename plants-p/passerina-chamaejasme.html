Passerina chamaejasme

Passerina chamaejasme, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its prolonged flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
