Philcoxia goiasensis

Philcoxia goiasensis, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is notable for its ability to survive in extreme environments, such as arid regions with minimal rainfall. The plant exhibits a specialized adaptation called CAM photosynthesis, which allows it to conserve water efficiently. Its leaves are small and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Despite its resilience, Philcoxia goiasensis is considered rare and is of significant interest to botanists due to its unusual morphology and ecological adaptations.
