Pelexia gutturosa

Pelexia gutturosa, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and often displays distinctive spots on its stems and leaves. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Pelexia gutturosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
