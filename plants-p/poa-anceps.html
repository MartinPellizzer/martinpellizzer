Poa anceps

Poa anceps, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and fine, dark green foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, cool climates and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa anceps plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
