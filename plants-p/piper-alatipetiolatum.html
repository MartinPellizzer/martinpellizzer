Piper alatipetiolatum

Piper alatipetiolatum, commonly known as the winged petiole pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged petioles, which give it its common name and aid in identification. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticulture.
