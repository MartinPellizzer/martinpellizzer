Pleurothallis geminiflora

Pleurothallis geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Pleurothallis geminiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
