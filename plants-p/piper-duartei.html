Piper duartei

Piper duartei, commonly known as the Duarte's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been studied for its potential pharmacological applications.
