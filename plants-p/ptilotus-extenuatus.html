Ptilotus extenuatus

Ptilotus extenuatus, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in harsh environments.

Ptilotus extenuatus is often found in sandy soils and is well-suited to drought conditions.
