Pollinia polystachya

Pollinia polystachya, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and unique pollination mechanism involving pollinia, which are sticky masses of pollen. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces long, slender spikes of flowers.

Pollinia polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouse settings.
