Pelargonium damarense

Pelargonium damarense, commonly known as the Damara geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens across the world.
