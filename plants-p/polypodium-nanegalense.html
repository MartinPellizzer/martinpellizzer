Polypodium nanegalense

Polypodium nanegalense, commonly known as the Nanegalense fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
