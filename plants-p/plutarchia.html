Plutarchia

Plutarchia, commonly known as the golden crown plant, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the dry season, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's adaptability to harsh environments makes it an important component of its native ecosystem.
