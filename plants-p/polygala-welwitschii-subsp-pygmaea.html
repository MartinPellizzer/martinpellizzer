Polygala welwitschii subsp. pygmaea

Polygala welwitschii subsp.

pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant features small, delicate flowers that are often pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
