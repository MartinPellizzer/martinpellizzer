Poa solida

Poa solida, commonly known as solid grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its rhizomatous growth and ability to form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for soil stabilization.
