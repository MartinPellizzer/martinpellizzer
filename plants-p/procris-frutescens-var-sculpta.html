Procris frutescens var. sculpta

Procris frutescens var. sculpta, commonly known as the sculpted procris, is a unique and ornamental plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, sculptural inflorescences that resemble intricate floral sculptures, making it a popular choice for horticultural displays. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering during its growing season.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
