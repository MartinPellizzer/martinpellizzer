Pleurothallis trialata

Pleurothallis trialata, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking silver or white coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and glossy, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis trialata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique and elegant appearance.
