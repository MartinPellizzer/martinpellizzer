Pulicaria menachensis

Pulicaria menachensis, commonly known as the Jordanian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with hairy, narrow leaves.

Pulicaria menachensis is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its role in local ecosystems.
