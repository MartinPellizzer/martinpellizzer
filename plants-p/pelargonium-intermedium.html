Pelargonium intermedium

Pelargonium intermedium, commonly known as the Zonal Geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves with distinct color zones, typically in shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in clusters, often in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is popular in gardens for its ornamental value.

Pelargonium intermedium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
