Phaseolus radiatus

Phaseolus radiatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and long, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil.

The beans are rich in protein and are commonly used in various cuisines around the world.
