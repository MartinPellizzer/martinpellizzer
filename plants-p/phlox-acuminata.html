Phlox acuminata

Phlox acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Phlox acuminata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
