Piper subtrinerve var. harveyanum

Piper subtrinerve var. harveyanum, commonly known as Harvey's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elliptical, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The varietal form, harveyanum, is distinguished by specific leaf and fruit morphology compared to the typical subspecies.
