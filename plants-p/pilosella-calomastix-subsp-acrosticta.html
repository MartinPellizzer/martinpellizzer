Pilosella calomastix subsp. acrosticta

Pilosella calomastix subsp.

acrosticta, commonly known as the alpine groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually hairy and lobed, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and provides nectar for pollinators.
