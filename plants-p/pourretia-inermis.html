Pourretia inermis

Pourretia inermis, commonly known as the smooth porcupine plant, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.
