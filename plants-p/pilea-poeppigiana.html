Pilea poeppigiana

Pilea poeppigiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features round, waxy, succulent leaves that are often described as resembling coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea poeppigiana is known for its easy care and ability to propagate through stem cuttings.
