Papaya peltata

Papaya peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf papaya, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are typically pear-shaped and contain a juicy, sweet pulp. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant.

Papaya peltata is often found in moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions.
