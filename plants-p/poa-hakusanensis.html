Poa hakusanensis

Poa hakusanensis, commonly known as the Hakusan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Poa hakusanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
