Polypodium fuscopetiolatum

Polypodium fuscopetiolatum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It features a compact, creeping rhizome with dark, leathery leaves that are typically divided into lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Its fronds are evergreen, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

This fern is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
