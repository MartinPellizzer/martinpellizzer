Prasophyllum concinnum

Prasophyllum concinnum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an herbaceous plant native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and grassy slopes. Prasophyllum concinnum is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with well-drained sandy or gravelly substrates.

Due to habitat loss and land clearing, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
