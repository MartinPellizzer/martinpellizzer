Patagonium divaricatum

Patagonium divaricatum, commonly known as the Patagonian thornbush, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various wildlife species. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually small and leathery, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Patagonium divaricatum plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in its native ecosystems.
