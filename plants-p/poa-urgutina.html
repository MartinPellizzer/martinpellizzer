Poa urgutina

Poa urgutina, commonly known as the Urgut grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in mountainous and alpine regions, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Poa urgutina plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
