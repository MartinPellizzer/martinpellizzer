Primula roylei subsp. dianae

Primula roylei subsp.

dianae, commonly known as the Dianae primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China. It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive yellow throat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
