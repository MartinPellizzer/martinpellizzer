Polypodiodes amoena var. duclouxii

Polypodiodes amoena var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that resemble those of other maidenhair ferns. The plant produces small, dark green to brown spore cases called sporangia, which are typically found on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and botanists alike.
