Prunus vinariensis

Prunus vinariensis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The tree's leaves are alternate, simple, and lanceolate, turning bright yellow in autumn. Its fruits are small drupes that ripen to a dark red or black color.

Prunus vinariensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
