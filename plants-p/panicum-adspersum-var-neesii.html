Panicum adspersum var. neesii

Panicum adspersum var. neesii, commonly known as Nees' panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often produce dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced under drought conditions. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient and widely distributed species.
