Pleurothallis convoluta

Pleurothallis convoluta, commonly known as the curled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate, spiral-like inflorescence.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
