Pentasacme

Pentasacme, commonly known as the five-lobed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, five-lobed flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic to some animals, including humans. Its leaves are typically opposite and oval-shaped, with a hairy texture.
