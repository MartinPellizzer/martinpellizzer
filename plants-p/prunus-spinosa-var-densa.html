Prunus spinosa var. densa

Prunus spinosa var. densa, commonly known as the dense blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are a food source for birds and are used in traditional herbal remedies.
