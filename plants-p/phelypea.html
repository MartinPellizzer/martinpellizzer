Phelypea

Phelypea, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are characterized by their delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Phelypea species are typically found in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors and stream banks. They have a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing them to spread and form dense mats.
