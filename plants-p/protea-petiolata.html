Protea petiolata

Protea petiolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that bear numerous small, white to pink flowers. The plant is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers attract pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Protea petiolata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
