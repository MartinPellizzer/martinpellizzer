Piper inebrians

Piper inebrians, commonly known as the "drunken pepper," is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported stimulant properties.
