Pteris undulata

Pteris undulata, commonly known as the wavy fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that have a wavy or undulating appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often covered in a dense layer of brown, hairy roots.

Pteris undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
