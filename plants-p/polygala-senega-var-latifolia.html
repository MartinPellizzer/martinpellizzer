Polygala senega var. latifolia

Polygala senega var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges, often in dry to moderately moist soils.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
