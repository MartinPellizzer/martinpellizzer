Potentilla anserina f. longissima

Potentilla anserina f. longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed silverweed, is a variant of the silverweed species native to alpine and subalpine regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
