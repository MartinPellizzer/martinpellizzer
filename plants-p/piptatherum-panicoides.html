Piptatherum panicoides

Piptatherum panicoides, commonly known as switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is widely recognized for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both wet and dry conditions. Switchgrass is also valued for its ecological benefits, including erosion control and wildlife habitat.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense stands, making it an effective forage crop and a candidate for biofuel production.
