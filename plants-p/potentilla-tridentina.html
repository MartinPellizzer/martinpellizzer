Potentilla tridentina

Potentilla tridentina, commonly known as the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having three pointed lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Potentilla tridentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
