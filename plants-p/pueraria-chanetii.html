Pueraria chanetii

Pueraria chanetii, commonly known as the wild soybean vine, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, white or purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system and the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil. Its seeds are encased in hairy pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Pueraria chanetii is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its growth habits and nutritional properties.
