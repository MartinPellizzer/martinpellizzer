Primula sikkimensis var. hopeana

Primula sikkimensis var. hopeana, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayan region.

It features rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is adapted to cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
