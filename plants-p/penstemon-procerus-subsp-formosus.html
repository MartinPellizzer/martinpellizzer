Penstemon procerus subsp. formosus

Penstemon procerus subsp.

formosus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
