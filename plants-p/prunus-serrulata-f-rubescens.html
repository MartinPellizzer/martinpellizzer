Prunus serrulata f. rubescens

Prunus serrulata f. rubescens, commonly known as the pink flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and they turn a striking red or orange in the fall.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and along streets for its seasonal display.
