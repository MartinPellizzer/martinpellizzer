Primula dyeriana

Primula dyeriana, commonly known as the Dyer's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.

The species is also noted for its resilience to cold temperatures and its ability to bloom even in shaded conditions.
