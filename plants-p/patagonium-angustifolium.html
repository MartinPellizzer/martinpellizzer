Patagonium angustifolium

Patagonium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Patagonia plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
