Pluchea rubelliflora

Pluchea rubelliflora, commonly known as red-flowered groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy red flowers bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pluchea rubelliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
