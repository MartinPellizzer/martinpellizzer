Polygonum hydropiper var. fastigiatum

Polygonum hydropiper var. fastigiatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a common pioneer species in disturbed aquatic environments.
