Parmentiera foliolosa

Parmentiera foliolosa, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has distinctive silver-green foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, often blooming in spring.

Parmentiera foliolosa is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
