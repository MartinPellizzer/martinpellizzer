Polygonum equisetiforme var. graecum

Polygonum equisetiforme var. graecum, commonly known as Greek knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, green, ovate leaves arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive species within the Polygonaceae family.
