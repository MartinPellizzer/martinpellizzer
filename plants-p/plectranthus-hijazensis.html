Plectranthus hijazensis

Plectranthus hijazensis, commonly known as the Hijaz plectranthus, is a perennial shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
