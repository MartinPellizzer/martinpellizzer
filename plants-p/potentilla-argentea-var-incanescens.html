Potentilla argentea var. incanescens

Potentilla argentea var. incanescens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
