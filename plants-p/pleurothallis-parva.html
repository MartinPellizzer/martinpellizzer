Pleurothallis parva

Pleurothallis parva, commonly known as the small pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, fragrant structure.

Pleurothallis parva is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
