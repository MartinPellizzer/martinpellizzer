Pinus ponderosa subsp. critchfieldiana

Pinus ponderosa subsp.

critchfieldiana, commonly known as the Critchfield's ponderosa pine, is a subspecies native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically two to three inches in length.

The cones of this subspecies are generally smaller and more elongated compared to those of other ponderosa pine varieties. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
