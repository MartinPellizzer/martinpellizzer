Puccinellia deschampsioides

Puccinellia deschampsioides, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and silver-gray foliage, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of salt marsh and coastal dune ecosystems. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia deschampsioides plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
