Pilosella apargioides

Pilosella apargioides, commonly known as the hairy leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. The plant features small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale blue petals. Its leaves are typically hairy, which gives it its common name, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pilosella apargioides is often considered a weed but can be grown as a ornamental plant in gardens.
