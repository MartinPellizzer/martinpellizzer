Panicum glabrinode

Panicum glabrinode, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Panicum glabrinode is a hardy perennial that is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
