Pilosella infidula

Pilosella infidula, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a woolly texture, and the flowers are small, white or pale yellow.

Pilosella infidula is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
