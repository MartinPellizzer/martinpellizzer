Peperomia coroicoensis

Peperomia coroicoensis, commonly known as the Coroico peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Andean region of Bolivia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and often displays striking red or purple veins on its leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia coroicoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
