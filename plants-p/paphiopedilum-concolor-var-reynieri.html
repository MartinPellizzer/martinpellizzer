Paphiopedilum concolor var. reynieri

Paphiopedilum concolor var. reynieri, commonly known as the Red-tipped Paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a deep red lip and greenish-yellow petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. It has a short rhizome and produces a single, upright flower spike.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
