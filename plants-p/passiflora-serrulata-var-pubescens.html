Passiflora serrulata var. pubescens

Passiflora serrulata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. Its fruit is a edible, fleshy berry that ripens to a yellow or orange color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
