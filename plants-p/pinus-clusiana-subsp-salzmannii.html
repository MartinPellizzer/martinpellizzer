Pinus clusiana subsp. salzmannii

Pinus clusiana subsp.

salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's pine, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the Andes of South America. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, needle-like foliage.

The leaves are typically dark green and occur in fascicles of five, giving the tree a distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is adapted to high-altitude environments and plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
