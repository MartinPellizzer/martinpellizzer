Potentilla kurdica

Potentilla kurdica, commonly known as Kurdish cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal leaves with five leaflets and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in high altitude areas, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained conditions.

Potentilla kurdica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
