Panicum adspersum

Panicum adspersum, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically greenish-brown in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its invasive nature, it can become a significant weed in agricultural fields and natural habitats.
