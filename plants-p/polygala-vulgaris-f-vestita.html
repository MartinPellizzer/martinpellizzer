Polygala vulgaris f. vestita

Polygala vulgaris f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a soft, velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassy areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
