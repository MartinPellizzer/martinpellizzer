Pleopeltis iboensis

Pleopeltis iboensis, commonly known as the Ibo's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its creeping rhizomes and dense, compact fronds. The fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a leathery texture and a dark green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in rainforest ecosystems.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
