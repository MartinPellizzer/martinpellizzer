Polygala glomerata var. langbianensis

Polygala glomerata var. langbianensis, commonly known as the Langbian clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, bean-like pods containing seeds.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
