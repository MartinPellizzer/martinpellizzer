Persicaria filiformis f. albiflora

Persicaria filiformis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered filiform hogweed, is a variant of the filiform hogweed species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, characterized by its slender, elongated stems. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.
