Piper pyrifolium

Piper pyrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese betel pepper, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-purple berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
