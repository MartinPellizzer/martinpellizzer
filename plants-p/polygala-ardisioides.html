Polygala ardisioides

Polygala ardisioides, commonly known as the red-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
