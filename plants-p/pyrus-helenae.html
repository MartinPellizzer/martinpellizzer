Pyrus helenae

Pyrus helenae, commonly known as the Helen's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, particularly in Greece and the Balkans. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
