Pera membranacea

Pera membranacea, commonly known as the membranous pera, is a unique plant species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, thin leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for medicinal use.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
