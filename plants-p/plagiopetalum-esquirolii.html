Plagiopetalum esquirolii

Plagiopetalum esquirolii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, contributing to its unique appearance.

Plagiopetalum esquirolii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
