Plectaneia inutilis

Plectaneia inutilis, commonly known as the useless plectane, is a rare and enigmatic plant species found in specific microhabitats within tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-arranged leaves that exhibit a striking iridescence under sunlight. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by a specialized subset of nocturnal moths. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with certain mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient absorption in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Plectaneia inutilis remains a subject of ongoing botanical research.
