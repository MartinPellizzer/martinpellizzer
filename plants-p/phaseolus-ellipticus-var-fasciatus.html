Phaseolus ellipticus var. fasciatus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. fasciatus, commonly known as the streaked runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and distinctive white and green streaking on its pods. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible pods. Its flowers are typically pale purple or white, and it produces elongated, curved pods that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
