Polygonum gentilianum

Polygonum gentilianum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters.

Polygonum gentilianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
