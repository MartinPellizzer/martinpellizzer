Pyrularia bullata

Pyrularia bullata, commonly known as the globular bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive swollen base. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a desirable plant for rock gardens and alpine collections.
