Pisum sativum subsp. biflorum

Pisum sativum subsp.

biflorum, commonly known as the twin-flowered pea, is a subspecies of the pea plant characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescences. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to its cultivated relative, the common garden pea. Its foliage is typically trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it exhibits a climbing habit through tendrils.

This subspecies is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
