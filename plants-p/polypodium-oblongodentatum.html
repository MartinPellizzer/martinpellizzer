Polypodium oblongodentatum

Polypodium oblongodentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into segments with broad, tooth-like lobes. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
