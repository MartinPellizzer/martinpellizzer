Pitcairnia macrochlamys

Pitcairnia macrochlamys, commonly known as the giant pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height, forming a dense canopy. The plant produces showy, violet-colored inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
