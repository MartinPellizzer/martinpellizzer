Polypodium papilligerum

Polypodium papilligerum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that are covered in small, papillate (bump-like) structures, giving it a distinctive texture. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, making it a striking addition to humid gardens.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to specific ecological niches make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
