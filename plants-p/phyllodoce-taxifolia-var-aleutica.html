Phyllodoce taxifolia var. aleutica

Phyllodoce taxifolia var. aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian mountain moss, is a variant of the broadleaf mountain moss.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming evergreen moss that thrives in cool, moist environments, typically found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, capsule-like sporangia for reproduction. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native habitat.

This moss is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in shaded, damp conditions.
