Piaranthus decorus

Piaranthus decorus, commonly known as the ornamental dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, which are typical of the family. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Piaranthus decorus is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
