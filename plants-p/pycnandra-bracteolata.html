Pycnandra bracteolata

Pycnandra bracteolata, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
