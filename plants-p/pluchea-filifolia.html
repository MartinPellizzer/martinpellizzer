Pluchea filifolia

Pluchea filifolia, commonly known as silky weed or filifolium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is recognized for its soft, silvery-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Pluchea filifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
