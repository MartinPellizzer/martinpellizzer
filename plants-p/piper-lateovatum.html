Piper lateovatum

Piper lateovatum, commonly known as the late ovate pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper lateovatum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
