Pella ribesioides

Pella ribesioides, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth surface, and the stems are often hairy.

Pella ribesioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
