Populus × barnesii

Populus × barnesii, commonly known as the Barnes poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the United States.

It is a cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus deltoides, resulting in a species with unique botanical characteristics. The tree typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, featuring a broad, rounded crown and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, while its flowers are catkin-like and appear in early spring.

This species is valued for its fast growth and adaptability to various soil types, making it a useful plant in reforestation and ornamental horticulture.
