Passiflora amethystina

Passiflora amethystina, commonly known as the amethyst passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that feature intricate structures including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Passiflora amethystina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
