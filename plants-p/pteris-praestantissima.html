Pteris praestantissima

Pteris praestantissima, commonly known as the elegant water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slightly wavy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction.

Pteris praestantissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or as a houseplant.
