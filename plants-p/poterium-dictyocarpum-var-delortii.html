Poterium dictyocarpum var. delortii

Poterium dictyocarpum var. delortii, commonly known as the Delort's net-veined fern, is a variant of the net-veined fern, characterized by its intricate leaf structure.

The plant exhibits a delicate, feather-like frond with a complex network of veins that give it its common name. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with each leaflet bearing a distinct, reticulate venation pattern.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
