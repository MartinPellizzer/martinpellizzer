Polypodium dicranophyllum

Polypodium dicranophyllum, commonly known as the slender wall fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, thriving in humid climates. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are protected by indusia, a feature common among ferns.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its elegant foliage.
