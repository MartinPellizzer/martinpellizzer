Passiflora banksii var. brachystephanea

Passiflora banksii var. brachystephanea, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central calyx. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in native Australian flora.
