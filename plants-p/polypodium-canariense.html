Polypodium canariense

Polypodium canariense, commonly known as the Canary fern, is a species of polypody that is native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the production of fiddlehead-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often on rocky or wooded slopes. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance, and it produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds.

Polypodium canariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
