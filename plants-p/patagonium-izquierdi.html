Patagonium izquierdi

Patagonium izquierdi, commonly known as the Izcuerdi's dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and it produces a distinctive seed head that aids in wind dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
