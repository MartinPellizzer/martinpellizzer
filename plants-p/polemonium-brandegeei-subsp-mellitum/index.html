Polemonium Brandegeei Subsp. Mellitum

Blue poppy, scientifically known as Polemonium brandegeei subsp. mellitum, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. Its vivid blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring, adding a splash of color to rocky slopes and forested areas. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and shaded woodlands. With its delicate foliage and showy blossoms, it is a favorite among gardeners and botanists alike. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively rare and elusive species in the wild.
