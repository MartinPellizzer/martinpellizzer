Piper trichopus

Piper trichopus, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
