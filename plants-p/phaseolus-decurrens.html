Phaseolus decurrens

Phaseolus decurrens, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible, elongated bean pods. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are rich in protein and nutrients, making them a valuable addition to both gardens and diets.
