Phaseolus candidus var. membranaceus

Phaseolus candidus var. membranaceus, commonly known as the white bean plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a pale green color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated pods containing oval, white seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
