Phalaenopsis schilleriana var. immaculata

Phalaenopsis schilleriana var. immaculata, commonly known as the Schiller's Phalaenopsis, is a type of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of white, lip-like flowers with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
