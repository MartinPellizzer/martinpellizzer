Potentilla argentea var. septenata

Potentilla argentea var. septenata, commonly known as silver potentilla or silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
