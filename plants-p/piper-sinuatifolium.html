Piper sinuatifolium

Piper sinuatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaved pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its distinctive wavy, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
