Potamogeton alpinus var. angustifolius

Potamogeton alpinus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, often forming dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually less than 1 cm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. Its small, green flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
