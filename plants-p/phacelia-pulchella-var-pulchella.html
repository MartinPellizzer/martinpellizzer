Phacelia pulchella var. pulchella

Phacelia pulchella var. pulchella, commonly known as the showy phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
