Piper rudgeanum

Piper rudgeanum, commonly known as the Rudge's pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It produces small, round berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine.
