Pilosella brachiata subsp. nematocauloides

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

nematocauloides, commonly known as the woolly leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this subspecies thrives in meadows and grassy areas. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in sunny, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
