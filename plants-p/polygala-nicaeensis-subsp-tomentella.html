Polygala nicaeensis subsp. tomentella

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

tomentella, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
