Piper vermiculatum

Piper vermiculatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some culinary uses in local cuisines.
