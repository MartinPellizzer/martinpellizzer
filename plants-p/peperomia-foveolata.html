Peperomia foveolata

Peperomia foveolata, commonly known as the "Peperomia with the pits," is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia foveolata is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
