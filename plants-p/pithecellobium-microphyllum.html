Pithecellobium microphyllum

Pithecellobium microphyllum, commonly known as the dwarf pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a legume containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and potential medicinal properties.
