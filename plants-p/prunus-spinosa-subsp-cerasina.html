Prunus spinosa subsp. cerasina

Prunus spinosa subsp.

cerasina, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries in late summer. These berries are edible but have a tart, sharp flavor.

The plant is often used in hedgerows and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
