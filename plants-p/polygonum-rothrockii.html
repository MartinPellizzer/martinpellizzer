Polygonum rothrockii

Polygonum rothrockii, commonly known as Rothrock's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
