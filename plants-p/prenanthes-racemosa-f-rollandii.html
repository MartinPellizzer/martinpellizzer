Prenanthes racemosa f. rollandii

Prenanthes racemosa f. rollandii, commonly known as the rolled-leaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often curled or rolled, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Its unique foliage and ecological role make it a notable species in botanic gardens and native plant habitats.
