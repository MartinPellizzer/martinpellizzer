Passiflora anastomosans

Passiflora anastomosans, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central calyx. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora anastomosans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
