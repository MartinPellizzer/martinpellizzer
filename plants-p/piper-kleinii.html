Piper kleinii

Piper kleinii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its restricted range and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
