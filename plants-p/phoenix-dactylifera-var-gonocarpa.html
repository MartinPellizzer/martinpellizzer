Phoenix dactylifera var. gonocarpa

Phoenix dactylifera var. gonocarpa, commonly known as the red date palm, is a variant of the date palm native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink fruit, which differs from the typical brown dates of the main variety. The plant has a tall, slender trunk with a crown of pinnate leaves that are dark green and often covered in a whitish bloom. Its fruit develops in clusters and is valued for its unique color and flavor.

This variety is primarily cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a specialty fruit in certain regions.
