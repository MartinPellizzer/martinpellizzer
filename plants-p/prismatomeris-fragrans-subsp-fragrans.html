Prismatomeris fragrans subsp. fragrans

Prismatomeris fragrans subsp.

fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forested areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
