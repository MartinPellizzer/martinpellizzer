Potentilla brandisiana

Potentilla brandisiana, commonly known as the Brandis cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces red or orange berries in the autumn.

Potentilla brandisiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
