Pithecellobium leucosericeum

Pithecellobium leucosericeum, commonly known as the white silky acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, often giving it a delicate appearance. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with hairy surfaces that contribute to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
