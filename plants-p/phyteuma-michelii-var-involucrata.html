Phyteuma michelii var. involucrata

Phyteuma michelii var. involucrata, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, with a distinctive central cone-shaped disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
