Polygala lutea var. nana

Polygala lutea var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf yellow milkvetch, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features small, yellow flowers clustered in spicate inflorescences, which bloom in early spring. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
