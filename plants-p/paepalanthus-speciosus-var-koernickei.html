Paepalanthus speciosus var. koernickei

Paepalanthus speciosus var. koernickei, commonly known as the showy pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a perennial cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
