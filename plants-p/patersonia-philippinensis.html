Patersonia philippinensis

Patersonia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Philippines.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Patersonia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
