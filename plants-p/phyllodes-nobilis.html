Phyllodes nobilis

Phyllodes nobilis, commonly known as the silky habit of the acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique phyllodes, which are flattened, modified leaves that serve as the primary photosynthetic structures. These phyllodes are typically narrow, elongated, and covered in a fine, silky hair that gives the plant its common name. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
