Potentilla kionaea

Potentilla kionaea, commonly known as the Kionaea cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla kionaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
