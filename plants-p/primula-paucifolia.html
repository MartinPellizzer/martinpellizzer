Primula paucifolia

Primula paucifolia, commonly known as the few-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features a compact rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that are densely packed at the base. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
