Pandanus beguinii

Pandanus beguinii, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is valued both ornamentally and for its practical uses.
