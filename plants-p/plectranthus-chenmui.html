Plectranthus chenmui

Plectranthus chenmui, commonly known as the Chinese mint or purple mint, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly fuzzy leaves that range from green to purple in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is widely cultivated for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
