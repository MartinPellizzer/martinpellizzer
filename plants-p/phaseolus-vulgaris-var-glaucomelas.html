Phaseolus vulgaris var. glaucomelas

Phaseolus vulgaris var. glaucomelas, commonly known as the dark green bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its deep green foliage and elongated, narrow pods.

This plant is typically cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The leaves are broad and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually pale purple or white.

It is an annual legume that plays a significant role in agricultural systems due to its nutritional value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
