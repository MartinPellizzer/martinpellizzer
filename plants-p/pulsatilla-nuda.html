Pulsatilla nuda

Pulsatilla nuda, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves that are typically lobed and hairy as well. Its flowers consist of numerous petal-like sepals and are often surrounded by a crown of white bracts.

Pulsatilla nuda is typically found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
