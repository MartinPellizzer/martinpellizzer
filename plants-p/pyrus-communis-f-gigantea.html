Pyrus communis f. gigantea

Pyrus communis f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant common pear, is a cultivar of the common pear tree characterized by its exceptionally large size and robust growth.

It features broad, glossy leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in early spring. The fruit is typically larger and more elongated than standard pears, with a firm, sometimes aromatic flesh. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential for fruit production in suitable climates.

This form is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact and historical significance.
