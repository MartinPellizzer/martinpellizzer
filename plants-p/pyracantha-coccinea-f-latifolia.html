Pyracantha coccinea f. latifolia

Pyracantha coccinea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf red firethorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy orange-red berries.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is notable for its dense, thorny branches and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and as a hedge plant.

The f. latifolia form differs from the typical species by having broader leaves and a more spreading growth habit.
