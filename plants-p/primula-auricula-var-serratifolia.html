Primula auricula var. serratifolia

Primula auricula var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated auricula, is a striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns and a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
