Polypodium huegelii

Polypodium huegelii, commonly known as the Hugel's polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or on rocky outcrops. Its rhizomes are wiry and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.

Polypodium huegelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with the right conditions.
