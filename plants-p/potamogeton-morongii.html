Potamogeton morongii

Potamogeton morongii, commonly known as Morong's pondweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with small, green flowers that appear in spikes. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
