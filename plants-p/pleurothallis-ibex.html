Pleurothallis ibex

Pleurothallis ibex, commonly known as the ibex orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and colors that range from white to pale pink.

Pleurothallis ibex is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique growth habits.
