Pharnaceum holosteoides

Pharnaceum holosteoides, commonly known as the African waterwheel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a wheel-like pattern, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
