Premna corymbosa

Premna corymbosa, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves and stems are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
