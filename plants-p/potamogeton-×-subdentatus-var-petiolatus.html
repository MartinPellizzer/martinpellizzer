Potamogeton × subdentatus var. petiolatus

Potamogeton × subdentatus var. petiolatus, commonly known as the petiolated pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its presence often indicates good water quality and can play a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic life.
