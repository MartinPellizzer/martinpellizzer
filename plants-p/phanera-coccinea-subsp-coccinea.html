Phanera coccinea subsp. coccinea

Phanera coccinea subsp.

coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered phanera, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
