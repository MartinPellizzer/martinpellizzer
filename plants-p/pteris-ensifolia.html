Pteris ensifolia

Pteris ensifolia, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of fern native to Asia, including regions like China, Japan, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that resemble a sword, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Pteris ensifolia reproduces through spores and is a member of the family Pteridaceae.
