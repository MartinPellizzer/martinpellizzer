Penstemon colvillii

Penstemon colvillii, commonly known as Colvill's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance, with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon colvillii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
