Parasenecio

Parasenecio, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plants typically grow in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Parasenecio species are often found in open habitats and are adapted to dry conditions.

They are known for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
