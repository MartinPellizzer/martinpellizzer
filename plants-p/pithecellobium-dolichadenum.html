Pithecellobium dolichadenum

Pithecellobium dolichadenum, commonly known as the longpod velvetbean, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
