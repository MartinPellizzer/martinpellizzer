Populus pseudosimonii f. bisexualis

Populus pseudosimonii f. bisexualis, commonly known as the bisexual poplar, is a unique variant of the poplar tree characterized by its ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same inflorescence.

This trait, known as monoecy, is relatively rare in the Populus genus and contributes to its reproductive flexibility. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its genetic distinctiveness, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists studying plant adaptation and hybridization.
