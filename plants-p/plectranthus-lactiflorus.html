Plectranthus lactiflorus

Plectranthus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milky weed or African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature and can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Plectranthus lactiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
