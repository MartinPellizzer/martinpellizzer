Peucedanum laevigatum

Peucedanum laevigatum, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, licorice-scented seeds, which are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Peucedanum laevigatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
