Prunus × nudiflora

Prunus × nudiflora, commonly known as the Chinese wild cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that appear before the leaves in early spring. These flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic value.

It is relatively easy to grow and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun to partial shade.
