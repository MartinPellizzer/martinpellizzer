Pilosella prussica subsp. eisbergensis

Pilosella prussica subsp.

eisbergensis, commonly known as the Eisberg pilosella, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb with a compact growth habit and hairy leaves, giving it its species name "prussica.".

The subspecies eisbergensis is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other subspecies of Pilosella prussica.
