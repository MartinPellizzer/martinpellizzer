Pinus zitacuarii

Pinus zitacuarii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth, longevity, and distinctive bark that ranges from reddish-brown to grayish in color. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing edible seeds that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its needle-like leaves are dark green and arranged in clusters of five, contributing to its unique appearance.

This species is adapted to harsh, rocky environments and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
