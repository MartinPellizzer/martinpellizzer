Passiflora fruticosa

Passiflora fruticosa, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Passiflora fruticosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
