Polygonum somdevae

Polygonum somdevae, commonly known as Somdeva's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and flowering shoots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and resilience in challenging conditions.
