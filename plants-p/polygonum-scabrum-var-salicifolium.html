Polygonum scabrum var. salicifolium

Polygonum scabrum var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and alternate, willow-like leaves that are lanceolate and often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and roadside areas.

Polygonum scabrum var. salicifolium is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
