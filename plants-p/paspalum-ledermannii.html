Paspalum ledermannii

Paspalum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has slender, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brown spikelets. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Paspalum ledermannii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
