Pilosella sigmoidea

Pilosella sigmoidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

Pilosella sigmoidea is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
