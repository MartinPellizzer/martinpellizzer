Passiflora ciliata var. riparia

Passiflora ciliata var. riparia, commonly known as the river passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with finely toothed margins, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral display.
