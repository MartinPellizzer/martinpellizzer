Phyllanthus manono

Phyllanthus manono, commonly known as the African star apple or matoke, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed raw or cooked.

Its botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and local agriculture.
