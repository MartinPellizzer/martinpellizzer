Phaseolus xuarezii

Phaseolus xuarezii, commonly known as the Huastec bean, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within the Phaseolus genus.
