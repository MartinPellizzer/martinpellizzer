Penstemon adamsianus

Penstemon adamsianus, commonly known as the Adams' beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon adamsianus is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
