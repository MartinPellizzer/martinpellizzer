Prosthechea baculus

Prosthechea baculus, commonly known as the candlestick orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a tall, slender stem that resembles a candlestick, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Prosthechea baculus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
