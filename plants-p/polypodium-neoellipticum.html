Polypodium neoellipticum

Polypodium neoellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant features sporangia that are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like China and India.

Polypodium neoellipticum is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded, moist forest environments.
