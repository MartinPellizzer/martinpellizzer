Phlox omniflora

Phlox omniflora, commonly known as the New England phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soil.

Phlox omniflora is valued for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
