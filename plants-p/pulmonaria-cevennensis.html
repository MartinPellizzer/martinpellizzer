Pulmonaria cevennensis

Pulmonaria cevennensis, commonly known as the Welsh lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the UK.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to blue. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
