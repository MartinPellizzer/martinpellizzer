Platanocarpum

Platanocarpum, commonly known as the sacred fig or Bodhi tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, fig-like fruits that are edible. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The tree also holds significant cultural and religious importance, particularly in Buddhist traditions.
