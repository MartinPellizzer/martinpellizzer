Prunus mutabilis f. radiata

Prunus mutabilis f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to Japan.

This ornamental plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that change color from white to pink to red as they bloom. The flowers are typically borne in dense clusters, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape. The plant has a compact, rounded form and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth and flowering.
