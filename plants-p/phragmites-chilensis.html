Phragmites chilensis

Phragmites chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant forms dense stands with long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, grayish-green spikes of flowers in the summer. Its rhizomes are extensive, allowing it to spread rapidly and dominate aquatic habitats.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife.
