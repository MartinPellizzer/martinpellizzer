Piptadenia micracantha

Piptadenia micracantha, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Piptadenia* tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive glandular appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
