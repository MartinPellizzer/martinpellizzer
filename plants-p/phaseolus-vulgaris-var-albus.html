Phaseolus vulgaris var. albus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. albus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a cultivar of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, round, white seeds that are typically harvested when mature and fully developed. The plant is an annual legume that produces pods containing the edible seeds, which are often used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both food and agricultural purposes.
