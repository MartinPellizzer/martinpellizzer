Pappobolus stuebelii

Pappobolus stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical seed heads that resemble small burrs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are encased in a pappus, which aids in wind dispersal.

Pappobolus stuebelii is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid environments.
