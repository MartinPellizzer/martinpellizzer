Pelargonium ribifolium

Pelargonium ribifolium, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Pelargonium ribifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
