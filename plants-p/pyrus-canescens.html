Pyrus canescens

Pyrus canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and sweet. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

The plant has historical uses in horticulture and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
