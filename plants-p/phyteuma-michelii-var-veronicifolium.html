Phyteuma michelii var. veronicifolium

Phyteuma michelii var. veronicifolium, commonly known as the Veronica-leaved Phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellow-orange flowers with purple markings, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its foliage and flowers add both visual interest and ecological value to gardens.
