Prunus jamasakura f. imperialis

Prunus jamasakura f. imperialis, commonly known as the Imperial cherry, is a unique horticultural form of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its exceptionally large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 centimeters in diameter. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to Japan, and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, creating a striking visual display.

The f. imperialis form is particularly noted for its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
