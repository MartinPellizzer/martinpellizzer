Psidium

Psidium, commonly known as the guava, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It includes approximately 100 species, most of which are native to the Americas. The plants are typically small to medium-sized trees or shrubs, characterized by their simple, opposite leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The fruit, which is the most economically important part, is a berry that can vary in size and color depending on the species.

Psidium species are valued for their edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
