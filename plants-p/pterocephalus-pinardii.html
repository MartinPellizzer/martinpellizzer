Pterocephalus pinardii

Pterocephalus pinardii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces a distinctive, elongated fruit that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
