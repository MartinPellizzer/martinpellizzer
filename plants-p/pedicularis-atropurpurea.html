Pedicularis atropurpurea

Pedicularis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features striking purple to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows in rocky or alpine habitats. It is part of the Orobanchaceae family, which includes many parasitic and hemiparasitic species.

Its unique floral structure and vivid coloration make it a notable species in botanical gardens and alpine flora studies.
