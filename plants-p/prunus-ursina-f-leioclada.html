Prunus ursina f. leioclada

Prunus ursina f. leioclada, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus ursina species.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. This plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
