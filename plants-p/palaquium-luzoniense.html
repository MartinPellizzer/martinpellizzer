Palaquium luzoniense

Palaquium luzoniense, commonly known as the Luzon persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruit is edible, though typically consumed when fully ripe, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value in agroforestry systems.
