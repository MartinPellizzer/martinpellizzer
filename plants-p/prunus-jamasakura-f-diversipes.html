Prunus jamasakura f. diversipes

Prunus jamasakura f. diversipes, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with diverse petals, is a botanical curiosity within the Rosaceae family.

This form exhibits a unique variation in petal morphology, showcasing a broader range of colors and shapes compared to the typical Prunus jamasakura. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, are characterized by their delicate, layered petals that contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic diversity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and scientific value.
