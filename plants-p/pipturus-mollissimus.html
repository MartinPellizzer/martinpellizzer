Pipturus mollissimus

Pipturus mollissimus, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several elliptical seeds.

Pipturus mollissimus is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
