Paeonia barrii

Paeonia barrii, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink and purple. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces fruit that consists of a hairy capsule containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Paeonia barrii is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
