Pulsatilla dacica

Pulsatilla dacica, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy areas and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces a cluster of follicles containing numerous seeds.

Pulsatilla dacica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
