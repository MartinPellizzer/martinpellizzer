Polemonium caeruleum var. gracile

Polemonium caeruleum var. gracile, commonly known as bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
