Prunus ruiziana

Prunus ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Prunus ruiziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
