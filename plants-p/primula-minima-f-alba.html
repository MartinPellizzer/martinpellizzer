Primula minima f. alba

Primula minima f. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This variety is a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.
