Picea abies var. cupressina

Picea abies var. cupressina, commonly known as the Serbian fir, is a rare and unique variant of the European fir.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and a conical shape that resembles a cypress, giving it its common name. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans, particularly in Serbia and Montenegro. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The Serbian fir is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
