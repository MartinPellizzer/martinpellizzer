Paspalum oligostachyum

Paspalum oligostachyum, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Paspalum oligostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
