Piper subfuscum

Piper subfuscum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark red color, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical structure includes aerial stems, adventitious roots, and a complex system of leaves and flowers that support its reproductive cycle.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its economic importance as a spice.
