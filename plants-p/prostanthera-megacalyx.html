Prostanthera megacalyx

Prostanthera megacalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically clustered in dense spikes, producing a fragrant aroma.

Prostanthera megacalyx is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
