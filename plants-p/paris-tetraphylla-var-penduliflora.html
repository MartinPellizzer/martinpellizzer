Paris tetraphylla var. penduliflora

Paris tetraphylla var. penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower Paris, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the plant like pendant blossoms. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
