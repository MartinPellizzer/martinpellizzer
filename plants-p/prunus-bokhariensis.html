Prunus bokhariensis

Prunus bokhariensis, commonly known as the Afghan cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature.

Prunus bokhariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
