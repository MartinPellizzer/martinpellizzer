Polygonum amplexicaule var. speciosum

Polygonum amplexicaule var. speciosum, commonly known as the creeping knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features succulent, cordate leaves that often wrap around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it both a resilient species and a potential invasive weed.
