Podocarpus brevifolius

Podocarpus brevifolius, commonly known as the dwarf podocarp or small-leaved podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are typically short and dense, providing a compact appearance. Its small, cone-like fruits contain edible seeds, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Podocarpus brevifolius thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
