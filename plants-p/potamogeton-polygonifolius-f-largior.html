Potamogeton polygonifolius f. largior

Potamogeton polygonifolius f. largior, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its leaves are often used as a food source by various aquatic herbivores and can contribute to water oxygenation through photosynthesis.
