Pinus picea f. pyramidalis

Pinus picea f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal spruce, is a cultivated form of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dense foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting. The plant retains its needle-like leaves throughout the year, providing evergreen coverage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, exhibiting moderate growth rates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its structured appearance and adaptability to various climates.
