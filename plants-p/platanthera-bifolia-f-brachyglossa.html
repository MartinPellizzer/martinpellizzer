Platanthera bifolia f. brachyglossa

Platanthera bifolia f. brachyglossa, commonly known as the short-lipped marsh orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to wetlands and bogs in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale green flowers with a distinctive, reduced labellum. The plant relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological dependence on symbiotic relationships. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and requires consistent moisture, often found in sphagnum moss-dominated habitats.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this subspecies is considered endangered in many regions.
