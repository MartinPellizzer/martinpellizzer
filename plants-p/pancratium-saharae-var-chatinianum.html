Pancratium saharae var. chatinianum

Pancratium saharae var. chatinianum, commonly known as the Saharan yellow daffodil, is a striking bulbous plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center. The plant is adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its fleshy bulb. It blooms during the rainy season, typically in spring, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and valuable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
