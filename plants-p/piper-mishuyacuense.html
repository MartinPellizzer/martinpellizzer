Piper mishuyacuense

Piper mishuyacuense, commonly known as the Mishuyacu pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforests of South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, elongated leaves. Its fruit is a tiny, berry-like structure that contains aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper mishuyacuense make it an important component of the region's biodiversity and ecological systems.
