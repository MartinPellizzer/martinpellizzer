Protea aurea subsp. potbergensis

Protea aurea subsp.

potbergensis, commonly known as the Potberg protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers surrounded by bracts.

The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
