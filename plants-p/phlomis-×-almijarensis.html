Phlomis × almijarensis

Phlomis × almijarensis, commonly known as the Almería hybrid globemallow, is a hybrid plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and showy, globular flower heads that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Phlomis × almijarensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display and drought tolerance.
