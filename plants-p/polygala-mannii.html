Polygala mannii

Polygala mannii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts specific pollinators.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
