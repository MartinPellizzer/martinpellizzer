Phlox sibirica subsp. alaskensis

Phlox sibirica subsp.

alaskensis, commonly known as Alaska phlox, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Alaska phlox is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
