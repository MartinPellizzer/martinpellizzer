Plectranthus forskalaei

Plectranthus forskalaei, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It produces violet or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Plectranthus forskalaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
