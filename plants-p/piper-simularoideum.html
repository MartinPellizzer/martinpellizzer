Piper simularoideum

Piper simularoideum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
