Pilosella verruculata subsp. verruculata

Pilosella verruculata subsp.

verruculata, commonly known as the woolly goatherb, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this perennial herb thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant has a low-growing habit with numerous stolons, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional herbal practices for various ailments.
