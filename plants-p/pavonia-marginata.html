Pavonia marginata

Pavonia marginata, commonly known as the silverleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-gray underside. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Pavonia marginata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
