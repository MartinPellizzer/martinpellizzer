Pinalia extinctoria

Pinalia extinctoria, commonly known as the fire-extinguishing plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions with high alkaline soils.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that contain high levels of natural compounds with fire-retardant properties. The plant's flowers are typically small and clustered, often appearing in shades of deep red or orange. Its growth is slow and it thrives in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unusual properties, it has attracted interest for both scientific research and potential ecological applications.
