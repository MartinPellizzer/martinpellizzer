Pilocereus gounellei var. zehntneri

Pilocereus gounellei var. zehntneri, commonly known as the Zehntner's night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is known for its nocturnal flowering habit. The flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow, open at night and emit a sweet fragrance. This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in arid gardens.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic landscapes.
