Polypodium blepharodes var. microlepis

Polypodium blepharodes var. microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaf wall fern, is a variant of the wall fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its compact, dense fronds that grow close to the ground. The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, with small, closely spaced pinnae. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
