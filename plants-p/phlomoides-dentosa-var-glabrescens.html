Phlomoides dentosa var. glabrescens

Phlomoides dentosa var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish-red flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered with fine, smooth hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
