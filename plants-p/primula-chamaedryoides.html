Primula chamaedryoides

Primula chamaedryoides, commonly known as the rock primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with five petals and a distinctive, hairy calyx.

Primula chamaedryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
