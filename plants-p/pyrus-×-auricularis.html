Pyrus × auricularis

Pyrus × auricularis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus.

It is characterized by its small, round, white fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to Japan and has been cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The leaves are dark green and glossy, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
