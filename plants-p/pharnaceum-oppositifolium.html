Pharnaceum oppositifolium

Pharnaceum oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf pharnaceum, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves are paired along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
