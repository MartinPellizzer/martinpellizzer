Persoonia salicina var. muelleri

Persoonia salicina var. muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's salmonberry, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, salmon-pink berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
