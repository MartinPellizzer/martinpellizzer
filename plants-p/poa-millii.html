Poa millii

Poa millii, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a species of grass native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
