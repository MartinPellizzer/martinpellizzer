Piper tinctum

Piper tinctum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Malabar Coast of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and dark, aromatic berries. The fruit, known as a peppercorn, undergoes a transformation from green to red and finally to black as it matures. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically cultivated for its pungent, spicy fruit.

Its botanical features make it a significant crop in both culinary and medicinal applications.
