Philodendron linnaei var. linnaei

Philodendron linnaei var. linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaeus's Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often adorned with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by spathate fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is popular as a houseplant and in ornamental horticulture.
