Phylica brachycephala

Phylica brachycephala, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, cone-like inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Phylica brachycephala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
