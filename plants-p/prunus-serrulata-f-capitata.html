Prunus serrulata f. capitata

Prunus serrulata f. capitata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a cultivated form of the common cherry.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic value. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

This variety is particularly noted for its ornamental appeal during the blooming season.
