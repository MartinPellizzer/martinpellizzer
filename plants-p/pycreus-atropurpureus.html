Pycreus atropurpureus

Pycreus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers with prominent white fringes. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pycreus atropurpureus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
