Pfaffia sarcophylla

Pfaffia sarcophylla, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Compositae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flower heads. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, red berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pfaffia sarcophylla is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
