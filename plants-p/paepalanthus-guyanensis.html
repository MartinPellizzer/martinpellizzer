Paepalanthus guyanensis

Paepalanthus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the wetlands of Guyana and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant thrives in waterlogged soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered at the base of the plant, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
