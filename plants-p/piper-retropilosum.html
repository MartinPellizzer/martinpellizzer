Piper retropilosum

Piper retropilosum, commonly known as the backward-hair pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
