Potentilla inclinata glabrior

Potentilla inclinata glabrior, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rounded, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, palmately compound leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Potentilla inclinata glabrior is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky areas.
