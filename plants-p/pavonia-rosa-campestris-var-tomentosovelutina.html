Pavonia rosa-campestris var. tomentosovelutina

Pavonia rosa-campestris var. tomentosovelutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape and covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
