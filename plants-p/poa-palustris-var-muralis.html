Poa palustris var. muralis

Poa palustris var. muralis, commonly known as the wall meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as stream banks and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in compacted soils and is often found in urban or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability and resilience in challenging environmental conditions.
