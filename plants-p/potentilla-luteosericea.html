Potentilla luteosericea

Potentilla luteosericea, commonly known as the golden-hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellowish stems and leaves with a distinctive five-lobed shape. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
