Pachystela pobeguiniana

Pachystela pobeguiniana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and grows up to 2 feet tall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
