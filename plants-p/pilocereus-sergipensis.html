Pilocereus sergipensis

Pilocereus sergipensis, commonly known as the sergipense cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and produces night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and fragrance.

Pilocereus sergipensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
