Poa callichroa

Poa callichroa, commonly known as the golden tuft grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant golden-green foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near freshwater sources. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Poa callichroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
