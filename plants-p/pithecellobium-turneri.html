Pithecellobium turneri

Pithecellobium turneri, commonly known as the white-flowered acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in ecological systems.

Pithecellobium turneri is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
