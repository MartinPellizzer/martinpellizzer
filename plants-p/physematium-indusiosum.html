Physematium indusiosum

Physematium indusiosum, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, often with a rough texture, and the plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Physematium indusiosum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
