Pectis odorata f. major

Pectis odorata f. major, commonly known as the fragrant pectis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The fragrance of its flowers attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
