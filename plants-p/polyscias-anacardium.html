Polyscias anacardium

Polyscias anacardium, commonly known as the Brazilian holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, compound leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by berry-like fruits that contain a single seed. Due to its aesthetic appeal and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
