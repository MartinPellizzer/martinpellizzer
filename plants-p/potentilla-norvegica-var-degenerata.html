Potentilla norvegica var. degenerata

Potentilla norvegica var. degenerata, commonly known as the Norwegian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
