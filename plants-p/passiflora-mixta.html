Passiflora mixta

Passiflora mixta, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of colors, including blue, purple, and white, with intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora mixta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.
