Poraqueiba paraensis

Poraqueiba paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of essential oils. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Poraqueiba paraensis plays a vital role in the ecological balance of its habitat and is also valued for its timber and traditional uses.
