Physurus plantagineus

Physurus plantagineus, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Physurus plantagineus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
