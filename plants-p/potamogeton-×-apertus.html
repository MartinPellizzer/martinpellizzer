Potamogeton × apertus

Potamogeton × apertus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is an aquatic plant characterized by its submerged leaves that are often elongated and have a distinct midrib. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.

Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual reproduction through seeds and vegetative propagation through stolons.
