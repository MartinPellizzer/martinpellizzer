Prunus × donarium var. compta

Prunus × donarium var. compta, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub valued for its delicate flowers and foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a hybrid between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus serrulata. The plant typically grows to a height of 1–2 meters, featuring small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red fruits.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
