Picris lyrata

Picris lyrata, commonly known as the lyre's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust stem that is often branched and covered in stiff, grayish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with a distinctive lyre-shaped outline, giving it its common name.

The plant is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
