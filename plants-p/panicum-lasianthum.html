Panicum lasianthum

Panicum lasianthum, commonly known as blue panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in wetland landscapes.

Panicum lasianthum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
