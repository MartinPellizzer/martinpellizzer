Parivoa

Parivoa, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Parivoa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value and historical medicinal uses have contributed to its enduring presence in horticulture.
