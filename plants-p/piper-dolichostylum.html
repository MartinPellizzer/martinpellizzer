Piper dolichostylum

Piper dolichostylum, commonly known as the long-stalked pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow when mature.

The plant is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
