Penstemon dolius

Penstemon dolius, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon dolius is a perennial species that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
