Piptochaetium stipoides var. purpurascens

Piptochaetium stipoides var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple muhly, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
