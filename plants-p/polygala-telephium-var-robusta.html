Polygala telephium var. robusta

Polygala telephium var. robusta, commonly known as the red or pink milkvetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces slender, hairy stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flower color and drought tolerance.
