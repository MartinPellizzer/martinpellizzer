Pseudodictamnus hirsutus subsp. hirsutus

Pseudodictamnus hirsutus subsp.

hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy dictamnus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces cylindrical, woody stems that are often covered in fine, hairy structures, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
