Pyrus arachnoidea

Pyrus arachnoidea, commonly known as the spider apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, woolly appearance, with fuzzy leaves and a branched, often spiny stem. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by apple-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
