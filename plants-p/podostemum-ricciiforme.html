Podostemum ricciiforme

Podostemum ricciiforme, commonly known as the laceleaf water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its intricate, thread-like leaves that form a dense, carpet-like structure on the surface of water. The plant thrives in fast-flowing streams and rivers, where it attaches itself to rocks using its specialized root system. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically emerging above the water surface.

Podostemum ricciiforme plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic invertebrates and serving as a food source for certain fish species.
