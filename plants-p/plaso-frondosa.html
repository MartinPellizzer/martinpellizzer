Plaso frondosa

Plaso frondosa, commonly known as the leafy spore plant, is a unique species belonging to the fern family.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that unfurl in a distinctive spiral pattern. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are released into the air for propagation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate rainforests.

Its intricate structure and reproductive method make it an important species in ecological studies.
