Polygonum camporum var. australe

Polygonum camporum var. australe, commonly known as the Australian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces distinctive, bead-like fruits.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
