Paepalanthus flaccidus

Paepalanthus flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid puya, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, often in high-altitude areas. The plant features rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves that store water, adapted to its arid habitat. It produces showy, inflorescences with small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
