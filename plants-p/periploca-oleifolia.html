Periploca oleifolia

Periploca oleifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white, woolly flowers that resemble snowballs, blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Periploca oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
