Penicillaria italica

Penicillaria italica, commonly known as the Italian penicillaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
