Phyllanthus lacunarius

Phyllanthus lacunarius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
