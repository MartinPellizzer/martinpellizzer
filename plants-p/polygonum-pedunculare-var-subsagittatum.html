Polygonum pedunculare var. subsagittatum

Polygonum pedunculare var. subsagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped to arrowhead-shaped leaves with a distinctive deep green color and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. These flowers give way to orange to red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
