Piper brittonorum

Piper brittonorum, commonly known as the Britton's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
