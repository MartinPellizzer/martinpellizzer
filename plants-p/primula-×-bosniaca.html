Primula × bosniaca

Primula × bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian primrose, is a hybrid plant that originated from crosses between Primula vulgaris and Primula sinensis.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm and prefers cool, moist environments. It has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is native to the Balkans and is well-suited for shady, well-drained soils.
