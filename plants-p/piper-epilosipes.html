Piper epilosipes

Piper epilosipes, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are elongated berries that turn red when mature, attracting various birds and small mammals.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
