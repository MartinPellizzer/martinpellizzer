Potamogeton × decipiens f. major

Potamogeton × decipiens f. major, commonly known as the major form of the hybrid water plant, is a perennial aquatic plant characterized by its submerged leaves and flowering stems.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often have a distinct midrib, while its flowers are small and arranged in spikelets.

This form is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for aquatic invertebrates and fish.
