Pseudopogonatherum trispicatum

Pseudopogonatherum trispicatum, commonly known as the three-spined grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
