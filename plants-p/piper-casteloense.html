Piper casteloense

Piper casteloense, commonly known as the Casteloense pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
