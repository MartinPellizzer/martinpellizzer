Papaver croceum var. glabrum

Papaver croceum var. glabrum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow flowers with a papery, saucer-shaped corona and a central stigma that is often reddish in color. The plant has smooth, hairless stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The smooth poppy is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
