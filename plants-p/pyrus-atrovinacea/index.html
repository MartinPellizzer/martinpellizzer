Pyrus Atrovinacea

Pyrus atrovinacea is a rare and enigmatic species of apple tree native to the mountainous regions of China. This small to medium-sized tree is known for its dark violet to black fruit, which sets it apart from other apple varieties. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in forested areas at high elevations. Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research. Despite its beauty and ecological importance, Pyrus atrovinacea remains largely overlooked by the general public.
