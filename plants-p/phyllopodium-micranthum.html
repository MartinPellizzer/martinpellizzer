Phyllopodium micranthum

Phyllopodium micranthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllopodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during the late spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
