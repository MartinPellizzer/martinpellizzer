Panicum nematostachyum

Panicum nematostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
