Polygala paludosa var. myurus

Polygala paludosa var. myurus, commonly known as the swamp milk-vine, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from rhizomes and typically reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is adapted to moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.
