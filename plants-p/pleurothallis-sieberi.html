Pleurothallis sieberi

Pleurothallis sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging species to grow in non-tropical climates.
