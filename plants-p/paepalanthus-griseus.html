Paepalanthus griseus

Paepalanthus griseus, commonly known as the gray cholla, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive and resilient member of the cactus family.
