Pteronia fastigiata

Pteronia fastigiata, commonly known as the Cape rosemary, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, which bloom throughout the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
