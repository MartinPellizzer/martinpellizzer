Phalaenopsis amabilis var. fuscata

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. fuscata, commonly known as the dark phalaenopsis orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage and long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically have a white or pale pink hue with a subtle, dark veining, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in controlled environments.

It requires moderate light, consistent moisture, and good air circulation to flourish.
