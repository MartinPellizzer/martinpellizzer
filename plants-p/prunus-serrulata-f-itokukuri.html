Prunus serrulata f. itokukuri

Prunus serrulata f. itokukuri, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, double flowers that bloom in early spring, producing a profusion of pink and white petals. The plant is native to Japan and is highly valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens and parks. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.

The foliage turns vibrant green in summer, adding to its seasonal appeal.
