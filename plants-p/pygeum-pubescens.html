Pygeum pubescens

Pygeum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing pale seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
