Piper triquetrofructum

Piper triquetrofructum, commonly known as the three-lobed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves with three lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed berries, which are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary spices.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
