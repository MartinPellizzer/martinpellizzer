Prunella browniana

Prunella browniana, commonly known as Brown's dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant produces small, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Prunella browniana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
