Parosela enneandra

Parosela enneandra, commonly known as the nine-leaf parosela, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features finely divided, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are small and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal collections.
