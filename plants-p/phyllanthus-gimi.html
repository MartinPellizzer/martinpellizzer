Phyllanthus gimi

Phyllanthus gimi, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish sarcocarp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

Phyllanthus gimi is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
