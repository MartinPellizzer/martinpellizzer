Piptomeris restioides

Piptomeris restioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates. Its rhizomes form dense mats that can help stabilize sediments and improve water quality.

Piptomeris restioides is often found in wetlands, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
