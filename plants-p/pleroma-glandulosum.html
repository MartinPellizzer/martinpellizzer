Pleroma glandulosum

Pleroma glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky pleroma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
