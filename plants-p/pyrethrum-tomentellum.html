Pyrethrum tomentellum

Pyrethrum tomentellum, commonly known as the woolly pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, white to greyish pubescent foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic properties. Its leaves and flowers contain natural pyrethrins, which are used in organic pesticides.

Pyrethrum tomentellum thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
