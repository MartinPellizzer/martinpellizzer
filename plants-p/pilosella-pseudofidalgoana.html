Pilosella pseudofidalgoana

Pilosella pseudofidalgoana, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the spring and early summer.
