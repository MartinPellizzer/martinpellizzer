Pelargonium meyeri

Pelargonium meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features upright, branched stems and lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is often cultivated as a garden plant or in containers due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Pelargonium meyeri prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
