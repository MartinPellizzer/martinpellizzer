Passiflora marmorea

Passiflora marmorea, commonly known as the marble passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with a mix of white, blue, and purple hues, often featuring a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora marmorea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
