Pandanus tiassaleensis

Pandanus tiassaleensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into fibrous fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
