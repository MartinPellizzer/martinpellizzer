Psiloesthes elongata

Psiloesthes elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed psiloesthes, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with finely divided leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ecological importance and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
