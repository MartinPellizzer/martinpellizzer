Phippsia borealis subsp. neglecta

Phippsia borealis subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the northern phippsia, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils in tundra and alpine environments.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
