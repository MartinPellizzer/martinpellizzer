Podalyria laevigata

Podalyria laevigata, commonly known as the smooth podalyria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Podalyria laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and flowering habit.
