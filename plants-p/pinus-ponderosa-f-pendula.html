Pinus ponderosa f. pendula

Pinus ponderosa f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping ponderosa pine, is a unique variant of the ponderosa pine characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This ornamental form exhibits a distinct, weeping habit that sets it apart from the upright growth of the typical ponderosa pine. Botanically, it retains the same needle-like leaves and cone structure as its parent species, though the branching pattern is more pronounced and pendulous. The plant is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for landscaping and botanical gardens.
