Peperomia megalepis

Peperomia megalepis, commonly known as the large-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia megalepis is also valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
