Parodia penicillata var. fulviceps

Parodia penicillata var. fulviceps, commonly known as the golden spindles, is a cacti species native to southern Africa.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem with distinctive yellowish spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
