Papaver officinale var. monstrosum

Papaver officinale var. monstrosum, commonly known as the giant poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring vibrant red petals with a dark red center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant.
