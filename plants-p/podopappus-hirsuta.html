Podopappus hirsuta

Podopappus hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy podopappus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces bract-like structures that give it a distinctive appearance.

Podopappus hirsuta is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often thriving in sandy or rocky soils.
