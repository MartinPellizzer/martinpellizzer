Pseudolepanthes ramosii

Pseudolepanthes ramosii, commonly known as the ramosa slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they attach to trees without parasitizing them.

Pseudolepanthes ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
