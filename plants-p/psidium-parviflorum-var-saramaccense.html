Psidium parviflorum var. saramaccense

Psidium parviflorum var. saramaccense, commonly known as the Saramacca guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, which are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and a compact growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense forests.

The fruit is valued for its unique flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
