Prunus cerasifera f. plano-subglobosa-drupacea

Prunus cerasifera f. plano-subglobosa-drupacea, commonly known as the flat-globular cherry, is a compact, ornamental form of the common cherry tree.

It features small, dark red to black fruits that are typically borne in clusters. The plant has a rounded, bushy growth habit with dense, dark green foliage. It is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is well-suited for container growing and as a specimen plant due to its compact size.
