Phaseolus galactioides

Phaseolus galactioides, commonly known as the yellow bean or cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its yellow pods and small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and clusters of small white or purple flowers. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is widely cultivated for both human consumption and animal feed in tropical and subtropical regions.
