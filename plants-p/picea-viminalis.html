Picea viminalis

Picea viminalis, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The Siberian pine is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh, snowy environments.
