Pavonia morii

Pavonia morii, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy, bright red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often has a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia morii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
