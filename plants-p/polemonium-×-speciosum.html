Polemonium × speciosum

Polemonium × speciosum, commonly known as the Eastern Pewbeak, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces seed capsules that disperse its seeds in the fall.

Polemonium × speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
