Phlegmariurus foliaceus

Phlegmariurus foliaceus, commonly known as the common haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, greenish to yellowish stems that are often covered in a dense layer of hair-like structures. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinct midrib and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky habitats.

This moss plays a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
