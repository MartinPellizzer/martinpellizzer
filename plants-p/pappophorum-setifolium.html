Pappophorum setifolium

Pappophorum setifolium, commonly known as the hairy grass or slender hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Pappophorum setifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
