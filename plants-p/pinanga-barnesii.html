Pinanga barnesii

Pinanga barnesii, commonly known as the Barnes' bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features slender, clustered stems with long, arching leaves that give it a graceful appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing at the base of the leaves.

Pinanga barnesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
