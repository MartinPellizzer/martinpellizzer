Parinari polyandra var. pleiocarpa

Parinari polyandra var. pleiocarpa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elliptical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
