Phyllanthus spinulosus

Phyllanthus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny gooseberry, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
