Pinus radiata var. binata

Pinus radiata var. binata, commonly known as the silver pine, is a variant of the radiata pine native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive, graceful appearance. The leaves are needle-like, typically arranged in bundles of five, and are a glossy green color. This variety is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

It also plays a role in ecological systems, providing habitat for native wildlife.
