Pandanus hemisphaericus

Pandanus hemisphaericus, commonly known as the globe pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence that resembles a globe, giving it its common name. The plant produces both male and female flowers, which are typically clustered in the central core of the inflorescence. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This species is often used in traditional crafts and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
