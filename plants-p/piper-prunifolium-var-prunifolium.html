Piper prunifolium var. prunifolium

Piper prunifolium var. prunifolium, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive purple underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that transition from white to purple as they mature, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
