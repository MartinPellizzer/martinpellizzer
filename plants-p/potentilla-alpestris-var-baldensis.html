Potentilla alpestris var. baldensis

Potentilla alpestris var. baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to alpine regions of North America.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has wiry, often hairy stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing alpine soils.
