Plantago carnosa var. hirsuta

Plantago carnosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
