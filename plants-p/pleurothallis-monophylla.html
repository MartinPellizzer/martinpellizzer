Pleurothallis monophylla

Pleurothallis monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, drawing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant features a single, fleshy leaf that is often dark green in color, and its flowers are usually small and pale in hue. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its unique morphology and adaptability to humid, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
