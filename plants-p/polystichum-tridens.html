Polystichum tridens

Polystichum tridens, commonly known as the three-toothed wood fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds with a distinctive arrangement of pinnae. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polystichum tridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
