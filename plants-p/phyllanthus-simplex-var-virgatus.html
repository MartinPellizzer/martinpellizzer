Phyllanthus simplex var. virgatus

Phyllanthus simplex var. virgatus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often adorned with a waxy coating, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.
