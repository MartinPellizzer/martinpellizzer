Pyrethrum pourretii

Pyrethrum pourretii, commonly known as the Pourret's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain potent insecticidal compounds known as pyrethrins. These compounds make it a valuable source for natural pest control in agriculture.

Pyrethrum pourretii typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
