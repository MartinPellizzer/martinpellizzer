Potentilla pedata var. tuberosa

Potentilla pedata var. tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and have been used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often branched.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
