Poterium sanguisorba var. puberulum

Poterium sanguisorba var. puberulum, commonly known as red sanders, is a variant of the genus Poterium within the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including parts of China and Korea, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
