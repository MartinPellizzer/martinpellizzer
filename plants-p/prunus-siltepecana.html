Prunus siltepecana

Prunus siltepecana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or siltepecan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern Mexico and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit is usually red when ripe and contains a single seed, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and potential culinary uses.
