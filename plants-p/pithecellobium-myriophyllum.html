Pithecellobium myriophyllum

Pithecellobium myriophyllum, commonly known as the water fig or water hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
