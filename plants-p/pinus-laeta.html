Pinus laeta

Pinus laeta, commonly known as the white pine, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically 2 to 4 inches in length and have a silvery-white underside. The tree produces cones that range from 2 to 5 inches in length, containing edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife species. Pinus laeta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Its growth habit is typically tall and straight, reaching heights of up to 100 feet.
