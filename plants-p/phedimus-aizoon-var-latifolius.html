Phedimus aizoon var. latifolius

Phedimus aizoon var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phedimus, is a perennial evergreen subshrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, mat-forming growth with thick, fleshy leaves that are broad and rounded, often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
