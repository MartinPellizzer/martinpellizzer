Pabstiella analoga

Pabstiella analoga, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale purple, often blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich substrates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pabstiella analoga is considered a conservation concern.
