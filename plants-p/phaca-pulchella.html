Phaca pulchella

Phaca pulchella, commonly known as the beautiful phaca, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Phaca pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
