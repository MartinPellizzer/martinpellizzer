Piper crassinervium var. guilleminianum

Piper crassinervium var. guilleminianum, commonly known as the Guilleminianum pepper plant, is a variant of the broad-veined pepper species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves with prominent veins, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into berries, which are typically green when immature and turn red upon maturity. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in both horticulture and ethnobotany.
