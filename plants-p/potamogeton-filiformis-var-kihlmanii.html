Potamogeton filiformis var. kihlmanii

Potamogeton filiformis var. kihlmanii, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, contributing to its delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the Potamogetonaceae family. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with sandy or muddy substrates.

This variety is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-flowing rivers, playing a role in aquatic habitat structure and water quality regulation.
