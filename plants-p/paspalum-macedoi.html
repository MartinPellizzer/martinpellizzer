Paspalum macedoi

Paspalum macedoi, commonly known as Macedonian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm. The leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a smooth surface and a prominent midrib. Its small, spike-like inflorescences bear tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This grass is well adapted to dry and rocky environments, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid regions.
