Piper tumidum

Piper tumidum, commonly known as the inflated pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are berry-like, turning from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
