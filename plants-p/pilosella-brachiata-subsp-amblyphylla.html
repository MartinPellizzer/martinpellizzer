Pilosella brachiata subsp. amblyphylla

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, with ray florets that are shorter than the central disk florets.
