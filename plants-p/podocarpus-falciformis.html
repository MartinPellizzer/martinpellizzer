Podocarpus falciformis

Podocarpus falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically narrow and curved. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
