Pteris longifolia f. bipinnata

Pteris longifolia f. bipinnata, commonly known as the bipinnate water fern, is a fern species characterized by its finely divided, bipinnate fronds.

It features a compact rosette of leaves that radiate from a central point, creating a dense, bushy appearance. The fronds are typically light green and have a feathery texture, with small, serrated pinnae. This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
