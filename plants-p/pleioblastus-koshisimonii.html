Pleioblastus koshisimonii

Pleioblastus koshisimonii, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. It grows in dense clumps and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
