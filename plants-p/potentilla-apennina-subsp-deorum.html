Potentilla apennina subsp. deorum

Potentilla apennina subsp.

deorum, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
