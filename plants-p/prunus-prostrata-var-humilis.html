Prunus prostrata var. humilis

Prunus prostrata var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color and turn bright red in the fall. The plant produces delicate pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
