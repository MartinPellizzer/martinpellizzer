Periploca latifolia

Periploca latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf periploca, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. Its latex is milky and contains alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
