Plectranthus hirtellus

Plectranthus hirtellus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to blue. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Plectranthus hirtellus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
