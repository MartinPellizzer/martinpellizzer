Pelargonium gramineum

Pelargonium gramineum, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with succulent-like leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium gramineum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
