Piper allardii

Piper allardii, commonly known as the allard's pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its long, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
