Pindarea fastuosa

Pindarea fastuosa, commonly known as the showy pindara, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its flowers are often visited by specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Pindarea fastuosa is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
