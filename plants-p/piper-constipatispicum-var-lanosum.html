Piper constipatispicum var. lanosum

Piper constipatispicum var. lanosum, commonly known as the woolly pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by dark, peppery berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
