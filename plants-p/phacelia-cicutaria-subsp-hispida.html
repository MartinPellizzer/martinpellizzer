Phacelia cicutaria subsp. hispida

Phacelia cicutaria subsp.

hispida, commonly known as hairy phacelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also bear a hairy texture, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.
