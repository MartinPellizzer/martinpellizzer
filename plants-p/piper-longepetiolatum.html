Piper longepetiolatum

Piper longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
