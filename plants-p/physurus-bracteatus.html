Physurus bracteatus

Physurus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive velvety texture.

It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Physurus bracteatus is also known to be an invasive species in some areas, outcompeting native flora.
