Pedalium muricatum

Pedalium muricatum, commonly known as the rough pedalium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
