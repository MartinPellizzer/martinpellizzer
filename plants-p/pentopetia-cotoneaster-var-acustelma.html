Pentopetia cotoneaster var. acustelma

Pentopetia cotoneaster var. acustelma, commonly known as the Acustelma cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Chile.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to temperate climates.
