Papaver orientale var. parviflora

Papaver orientale var. parviflora, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and distinctive small flowers, which are a key feature of this cultivar.
