Palaua lanceolata

Palaua lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf palaua, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Palaua lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
