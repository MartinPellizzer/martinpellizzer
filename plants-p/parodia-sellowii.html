Parodia sellowii

Parodia sellowii, commonly known as the Sellow's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and smooth, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Parodia sellowii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
