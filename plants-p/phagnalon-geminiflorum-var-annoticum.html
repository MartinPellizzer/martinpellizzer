Phagnalon geminiflorum var. annoticum

Phagnalon geminiflorum var. annoticum, commonly known as the coastal flax or sand flax, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help stabilize coastal dunes.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal habitats and providing habitat for native fauna.
