Pleurothallis albopurpurea

Pleurothallis albopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-white pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The flowers are small, typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often marked with contrasting colors. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and moderate light for optimal growth.

This orchid is prized for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
