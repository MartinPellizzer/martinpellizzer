Papaver × concinnum

Papaver × concinnum, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to pale pink, with a distinctive saucer-shaped corona. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky down. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.

Papaver × concinnum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
