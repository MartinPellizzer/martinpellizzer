Piptatherum verticillatum

Piptatherum verticillatum, commonly known as the slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are a defining feature of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
