Piptadenia pteroclada var. klugii

Piptadenia pteroclada var. klugii, commonly known as the Brazilian wild bean, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a leguminous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
