Pappophorum pappiferum var. alopecuroides

Pappophorum pappiferum var. alopecuroides, commonly known as the woolly grass, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture that aids in water retention.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
