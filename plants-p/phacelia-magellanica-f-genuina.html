Phacelia magellanica f. genuina

Phacelia magellanica f. genuina, commonly known as the Magellanic phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting local pollinators.
