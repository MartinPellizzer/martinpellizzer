Piper phthinotrichum var. san-roqueanum

Piper phthinotrichum var. san-roqueanum, commonly known as the San Roque pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper phthinotrichum species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, peppery fruits that are used in local cuisines. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
