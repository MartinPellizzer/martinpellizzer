Phryne vulgaris

Phryne vulgaris, commonly known as the common moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable habitats.
