Petunia integrifolia subsp. inflata

Petunia integrifolia subsp.

inflata, commonly known as the inflated petunia, is a species native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated flower buds that resemble small, rounded balloons.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ornamental and botanical collections.
