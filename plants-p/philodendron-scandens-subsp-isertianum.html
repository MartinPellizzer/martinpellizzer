Philodendron scandens subsp. isertianum

Philodendron scandens subsp.

isertianum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its vining habit makes it popular as a houseplant or for use in vertical gardens.
