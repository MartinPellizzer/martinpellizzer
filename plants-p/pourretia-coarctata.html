Pourretia coarctata

Pourretia coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf porcupine plant, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with its succulent leaves and deep root system, allowing it to store water efficiently. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

The plant is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
