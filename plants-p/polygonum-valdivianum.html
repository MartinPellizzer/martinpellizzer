Polygonum valdivianum

Polygonum valdivianum, commonly known as the Valdivian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy coating.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in the spring and early summer.
