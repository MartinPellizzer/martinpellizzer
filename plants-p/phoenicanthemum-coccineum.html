Phoenicanthemum coccineum

Phoenicanthemum coccineum, commonly known as the scarlet sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to thrive in arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value in native gardens.
