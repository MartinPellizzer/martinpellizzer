Pluchea kelleri

Pluchea kelleri, commonly known as Keller's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped, with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface.

Pluchea kelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
