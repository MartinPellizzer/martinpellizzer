Potentilla wiemanniana

Potentilla wiemanniana, commonly known as Wiemann's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and compound leaves. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature, thriving in harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers bloom in early summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
