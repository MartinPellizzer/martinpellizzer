Polycardia aquifolium var. ilicifolia

Polycardia aquifolium var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaf holly, is a species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hedges and ornamental gardens.

Its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in landscaping.
