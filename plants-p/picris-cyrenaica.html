Picris cyrenaica

Picris cyrenaica, commonly known as the desert chicory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
