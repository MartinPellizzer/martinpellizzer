Prunus lamottii

Prunus lamottii, commonly known as the Lamott cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Prunus lamottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
