Peucedanum aegopoides

Peucedanum aegopoides, commonly known as love parsley or bishop's weed, is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its seeds are small, ovoid, and have a distinctive, slightly sweet aroma.

Peucedanum aegopoides is often found in moist, calcareous soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
