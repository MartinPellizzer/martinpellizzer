Piper marturetense

Piper marturetense, commonly known as the Marturet's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
