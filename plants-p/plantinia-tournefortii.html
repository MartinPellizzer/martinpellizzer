Plantinia tournefortii

Plantinia tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's plantain, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tall, erect stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal remedies.
