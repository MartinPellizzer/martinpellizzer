Polygonum lapathifolium var. incarnatum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. incarnatum, commonly known as the red polygonum or crimson knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it can be invasive in some regions.
