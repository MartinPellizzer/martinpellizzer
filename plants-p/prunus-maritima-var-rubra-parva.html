Prunus maritima var. rubra-parva

Prunus maritima var. rubra-parva, commonly known as the dwarf red cherry, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, compact growth and attractive red foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red or black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
