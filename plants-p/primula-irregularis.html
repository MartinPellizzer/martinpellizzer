Primula irregularis

Primula irregularis, commonly known as the irregular primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, irregularly shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are usually broad and hairy, while the flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Primula irregularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
