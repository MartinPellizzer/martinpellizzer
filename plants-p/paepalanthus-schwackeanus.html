Paepalanthus schwackeanus

Paepalanthus schwackeanus, commonly known as the Schwacke's candelabra cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that branch out into a candelabra-like structure, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
