Phaseolus oblongus var. sargentone

Phaseolus oblongus var. sargentone, commonly known as the Sargent's broad bean, is a variety of the broad bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or brown when mature. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers and has compound leaves with multiple leaflets. Its seeds are usually oval and can be harvested when the pods are dry and crisp.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
