Parkia africana

Parkia africana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a tropical leguminous tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red and orange. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces flat, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The tree also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
