Pilosella pseudofidalgoana subsp. pseudofidalgoana

Pilosella pseudofidalgoana subsp.

pseudofidalgoana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawkweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences.

The plant is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, displaying a low-growing habit.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
