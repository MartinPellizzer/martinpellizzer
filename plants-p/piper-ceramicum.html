Piper ceramicum

Piper ceramicum, commonly known as the ceramic pepper or porcelain pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
