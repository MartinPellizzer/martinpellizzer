Plantago radicata subsp. granatensis

Plantago radicata subsp.

granatensis, commonly known as the granatense plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces erect, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
