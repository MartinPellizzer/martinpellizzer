Prunus spinosa f. genuina

Prunus spinosa f. genuina, commonly known as the common blackthorn.

It is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and pale underneath. It flowers with clusters of white or pale green flowers in early spring, before the leaves appear.

The berries, which ripen in late summer, are used in traditional foods and herbal remedies.
