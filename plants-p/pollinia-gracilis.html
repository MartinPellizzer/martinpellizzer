Pollinia gracilis

Pollinia gracilis, commonly known as the slender pollinia orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique pollinia, which are small, compact masses of pollen that are typically attached to a viscid disk. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with intricate structures that facilitate specific pollination mechanisms.

Due to its specialized biology, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic orchids.
