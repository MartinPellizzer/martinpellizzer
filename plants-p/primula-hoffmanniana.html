Primula hoffmanniana

Primula hoffmanniana, commonly known as the Hoffmann's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
