Papaver pseudo-orientale

Papaver pseudo-orientale, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, with a distinctive central stigma. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
