Petalochilus coactilis

Petalochilus coactilis, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Petalochilus coactilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
