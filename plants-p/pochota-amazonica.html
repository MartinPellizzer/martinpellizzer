Pochota amazonica

Pochota amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian chocolate tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in nutrients and have a chocolate-like flavor. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source by indigenous communities.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various rainforest species and its contribution to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
