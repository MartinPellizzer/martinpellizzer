Potentilla × ternata

Potentilla × ternata, commonly known as silver weigela, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a silvery-white underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
