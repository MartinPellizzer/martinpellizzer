Poinciana cassioides

Poinciana cassioides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red-orange inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
