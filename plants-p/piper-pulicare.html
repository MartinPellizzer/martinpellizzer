Piper pulicare

Piper pulicare, commonly known as the aquatic pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the seeds.

Piper pulicare is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
