Penstemon linarioides var. viridis

Penstemon linarioides var. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
