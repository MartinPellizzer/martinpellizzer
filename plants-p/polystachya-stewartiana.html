Polystachya stewartiana

Polystachya stewartiana, commonly known as Stewart's spike grass, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Polystachya stewartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
