Polygonum albanicum

Polygonum albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as smartweeds or knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Polygonum albanicum is often noted for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate a variety of soil conditions.
