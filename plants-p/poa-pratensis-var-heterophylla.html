Poa pratensis var. heterophylla

Poa pratensis var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable meadowgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its variation in leaf morphology, with some leaves being broader and shorter than others. This plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
