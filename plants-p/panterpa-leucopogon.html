Panterpa leucopogon

Panterpa leucopogon, commonly known as the white-flowered panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panterpa leucopogon plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various insect species.
