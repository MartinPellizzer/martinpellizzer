Pilea longipedunculata

Pilea longipedunculata, commonly known as the "Chinese money plant," is a tropical houseplant native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, fruit-like structures resembling small, green balls. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea longipedunculata is relatively easy to care for and is often propagated through stem cuttings.
