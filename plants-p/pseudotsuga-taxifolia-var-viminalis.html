Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. viminalis

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. viminalis, commonly known as the Japanese white fir, is a variant of the Japanese fir, characterized by its slender, weeping growth habit.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant features needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a distinctive whitish underside. Its cones are small and typically hang downward, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its unique, flowing form and adaptability to various soil conditions.
