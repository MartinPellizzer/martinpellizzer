Penstemon floridus

Penstemon floridus, commonly known as the Florida beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and striking violet to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon floridus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
