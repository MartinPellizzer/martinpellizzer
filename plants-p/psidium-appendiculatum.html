Psidium appendiculatum

Psidium appendiculatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or appendiculate guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

The botanical features of Psidium appendiculatum make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
