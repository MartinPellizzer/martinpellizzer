Piptochaetium pallidum

Piptochaetium pallidum, commonly known as the pale grass or slender grass, is a species of grass native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively.

Piptochaetium pallidum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
