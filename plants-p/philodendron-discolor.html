Philodendron discolor

Philodendron discolor, commonly known as the Variegated Philodendron, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its heart-shaped foliage and climbing habit. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
