Primula jesoana f. pubescens

Primula jesoana f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy foliage, which gives it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Native to the mountainous regions of East Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
