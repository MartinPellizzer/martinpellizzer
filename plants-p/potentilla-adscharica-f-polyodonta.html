Potentilla adscharica f. polyodonta

Potentilla adscharica f. polyodonta, commonly known as the many-flowered cinquefoil, is a botanical variant of the species Potentilla adscharica.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub native to Central Asia, characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in rocky or well-drained soil conditions.
