Pheidochloa vulpioides

Pheidochloa vulpioides, commonly known as foxtail grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical spikelets that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small fauna.
