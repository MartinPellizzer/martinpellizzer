Pavonia sulphurea

Pavonia sulphurea, commonly known as the sulfur pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Pavonia sulphurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, eye-catching blooms.
