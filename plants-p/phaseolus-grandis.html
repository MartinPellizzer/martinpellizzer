Phaseolus grandis

Phaseolus grandis, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces strikingly colorful, showy flowers that are typically violet or blue in hue. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are edible when fully mature.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential as a food crop.
