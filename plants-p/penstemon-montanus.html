Penstemon montanus

Penstemon montanus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine and subalpine environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

Penstemon montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
