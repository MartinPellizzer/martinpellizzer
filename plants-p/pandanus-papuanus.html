Pandanus papuanus

Pandanus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, spiraling leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the tips of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and can form a dense network, helping to stabilize the soil in coastal areas.

The leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, showcasing its cultural and practical significance.
