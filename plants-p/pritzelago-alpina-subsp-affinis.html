Pritzelago alpina subsp. affinis

Pritzelago alpina subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features erect stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and sensitive species in its native range.
