Phyllanthus eglandulosus

Phyllanthus eglandulosus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1 meter in height, featuring simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
