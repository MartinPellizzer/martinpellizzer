Paspalum paniculatum var. rigidum

Paspalum paniculatum var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The plant produces inflorescences that are panicles, which are often stiff and rigid, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with temperate climates.
