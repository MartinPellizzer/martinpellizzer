Poicilla mollis

Poicilla mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fibrous roots and robust stems make it a hardy species capable of thriving in a variety of soil conditions.

Poicilla mollis is often found in disturbed habitats, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields.
