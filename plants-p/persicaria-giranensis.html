Persicaria giranensis

Persicaria giranensis, commonly known as the Giran knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant features upright stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer.

Persicaria giranensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
