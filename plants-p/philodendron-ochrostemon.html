Philodendron ochrostemon

Philodendron ochrostemon, commonly known as the white-veined philodendron, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy green leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron ochrostemon is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
