Polyscalis sequax

Polyscalis sequax, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
