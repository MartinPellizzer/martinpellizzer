Panicum pradanum

Panicum pradanum, commonly known as pradanum grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
