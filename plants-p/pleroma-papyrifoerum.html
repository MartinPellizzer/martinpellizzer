Pleroma papyrifoerum

Pleroma papyrifoerum, commonly known as the paper-leaf pleroma, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, papery leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Pleroma papyrifoerum requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
