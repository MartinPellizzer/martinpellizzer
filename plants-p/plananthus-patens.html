Plananthus patens

Plananthus patens, commonly known as the spreading plananthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading shrub with narrow, silver-gray leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.

Plananthus patens is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
