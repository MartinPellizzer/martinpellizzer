Phalaris arundinacea f. ramifera

Phalaris arundinacea f. ramifera, commonly known as the ramifera form of soft rush, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of dark green, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, pond edges, and damp meadows.

Its fibrous stems and rhizomatous growth habit make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
