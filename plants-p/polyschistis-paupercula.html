Polyschistis paupercula

Polyschistis paupercula, commonly known as the poor man's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
