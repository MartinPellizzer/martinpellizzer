Plumeria tenuifolia

Plumeria tenuifolia, commonly known as the sickle pod plumeria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that remain on the tree for an extended period. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
