Pinus muricata var. stantonii

Pinus muricata var. stantonii, commonly known as the Stanton's lodgepole pine, is a variant of the lodgepole pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The cones of this species are typically small and woody, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in subalpine or alpine regions.

This variant is adapted to harsh climates and is an important component of mountain ecosystems.
