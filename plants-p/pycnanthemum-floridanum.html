Pycnanthemum floridanum

Pycnanthemum floridanum, commonly known as Florida mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a grayish-white underside, and it produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Additionally, it has been used historically by Native American tribes for its medicinal properties.
