Pseudosasa victorialis

Pseudosasa victorialis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or golden bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking golden-green color. This plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Pseudosasa victorialis is a clump-forming plant that can grow up to several meters in height, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
