Pollinia versicolor

Pollinia versicolor, commonly known as the variable spore plant, is a rare and intriguing species within the fungal kingdom.

It is characterized by its unique spore structures that exhibit a wide range of colors, contributing to its common name. The plant's spores are typically found in specialized structures called pollinia, which are adapted for efficient dispersal. Its habitat is often limited to specific microclimates, making it a challenging species to study.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, Pollinia versicolor is of interest to mycologists and conservationists alike.
