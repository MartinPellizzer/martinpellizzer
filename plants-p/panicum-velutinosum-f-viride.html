Panicum velutinosum f. viride

Panicum velutinosum f. viride, commonly known as green silky grass, is a variant of the silky grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and densely packed leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, drooping spikelets that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
