Persea pyriformis

Persea pyriformis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by fruit that matures from orange to deep red, with a sweet, non-astringent flavor. The fruit is typically pear-shaped, which gives the species its scientific name, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
