Poterium magnolii

Poterium magnolii, commonly known as the magnolia vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Magnoliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with multiple petals and a prominent, showy stamen.

Poterium magnolii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in warm, humid climates.
