Paspalum cartilagineum

Paspalum cartilagineum, commonly known as the cartilage grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
