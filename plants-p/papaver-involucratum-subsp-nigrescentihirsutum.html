Papaver involucratum subsp. nigrescentihirsutum

Papaver involucratum subsp.

nigrescentihirsutum, commonly known as the black-hirsute poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, dark purple to black stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name.

The plant produces striking, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
