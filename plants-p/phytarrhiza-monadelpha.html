Phytarrhiza monadelpha

Phytarrhiza monadelpha, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to South America.

It has broad, floating leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud at the bottom of lakes and rivers. It produces showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting pollinators such as moths.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by stabilizing waterways and supporting biodiversity.
