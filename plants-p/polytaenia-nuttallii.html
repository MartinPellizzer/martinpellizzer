Polytaenia nuttallii

Polytaenia nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's polytaenia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
