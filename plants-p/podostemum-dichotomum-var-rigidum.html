Podostemum dichotomum var. rigidum

Podostemum dichotomum var. rigidum, commonly known as the rigid forked water plant, is a rare aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem splits into two equal parts. The plant typically grows along the edges of fast-flowing streams and rivers, anchoring itself to rocks with its specialized roots. Its small, opposite leaves are usually submerged, while its flowers emerge above the water surface.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality through nutrient cycling.
