Piper telanum

Piper telanum, commonly known as the Java pepper or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berries, which contain the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem covered in tiny, glandular hairs.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
