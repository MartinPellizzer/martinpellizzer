Penstemon humboldtii

Penstemon humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon humboldtii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
