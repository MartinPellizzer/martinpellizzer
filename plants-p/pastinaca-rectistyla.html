Pastinaca rectistyla

Pastinaca rectistyla, commonly known as the straight-styled parsley.

It is a flowering plant in the carrot family, Apiaceae, characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It is native to Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates.
