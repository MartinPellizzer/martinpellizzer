Physostegia truncata

Physostegia truncata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, tubular blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
