Pleurothallis laxa

Pleurothallis laxa, commonly known as the slender pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Pleurothallis laxa is notable for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
