Pinus fischeri

Pinus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the high elevations of the Sierra Nevada and Cascade Ranges in California.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long, containing winged seeds adapted for wind dispersal. Its cones open to release seeds, which are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its limited geographic distribution.

Due to its restricted habitat and threats from climate change, Pinus fischeri is of significant conservation interest.
