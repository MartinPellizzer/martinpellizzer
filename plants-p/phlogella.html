Phlogella

Phlogella, commonly known as the "fire plant," is a rare and enigmatic species with unique botanical features.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange foliage, which resembles flames, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in volcanic soils and is native to certain regions with high mineral content.

Due to its rarity and unusual appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its visual appeal and scientific interest.
