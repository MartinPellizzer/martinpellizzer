Prunus brachypetala

Prunus brachypetala, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits known as cherries, which are typically edible but less commonly consumed compared to other cherry species. Prunus brachypetala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.
