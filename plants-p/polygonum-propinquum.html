Polygonum propinquum

Polygonum propinquum, commonly known as smooth knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems that are smooth and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Polygonum propinquum thrives in moist habitats such as wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed soils.
