Piper scutilimbum

Piper scutilimbum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal applications.
