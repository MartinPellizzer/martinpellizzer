Persicaria subsessilis

Persicaria subsessilis, commonly known as narrowleaf hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features upright stems that are hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with toothed margins.

It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
