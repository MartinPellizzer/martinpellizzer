Polygonum punctatum var. breviciliatum

Polygonum punctatum var. breviciliatum, commonly known as the short-stalked knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to red, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
