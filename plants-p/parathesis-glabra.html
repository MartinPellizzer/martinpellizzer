Parathesis glabra

Parathesis glabra, commonly known as the smooth parathesis, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
