Polemonium viscosum f. leucanthum

Polemonium viscosum f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered sticky bluebell, is a botanical variant of the sticky bluebell.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white flowers, which contrast with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant is native to North America and thrives in moist, woodland environments. It features lance-shaped leaves with a sticky texture, a trait common to the species.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to natural habitats.
