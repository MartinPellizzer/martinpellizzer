Piper ruizianum

Piper ruizianum, commonly known as the Ruiz's pepper or the Amazonian black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple to black berries. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.

Piper ruizianum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
