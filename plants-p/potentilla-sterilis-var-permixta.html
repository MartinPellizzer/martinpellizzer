Potentilla sterilis var. permixta

Potentilla sterilis var. permixta, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
