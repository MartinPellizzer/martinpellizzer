Potentilla trifida

Potentilla trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets, each usually lobed or notched. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spherical inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
