Penstemon richardsonii var. richardsonii

Penstemon richardsonii var. richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, seed-like fruits called capsules.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
