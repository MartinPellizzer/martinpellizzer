Physurus bidentiferus

Physurus bidentiferus, commonly known as the two-toothed grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers.

Physurus bidentiferus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
