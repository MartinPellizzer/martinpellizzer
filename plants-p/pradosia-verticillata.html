Pradosia verticillata

Pradosia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered pradosia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Pradosia verticillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
