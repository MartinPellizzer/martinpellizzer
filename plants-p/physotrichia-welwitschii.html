Physotrichia welwitschii

Physotrichia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its two large, leathery leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a woody stem that can reach several meters in height, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deep underground.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a flagship species of the Namib Desert.
