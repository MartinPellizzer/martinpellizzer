Primula macrophylla subsp. aitchisonii

Primula macrophylla subsp.

aitchisonii, commonly known as the Aitchison's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive dark center.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or alpine environments. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
