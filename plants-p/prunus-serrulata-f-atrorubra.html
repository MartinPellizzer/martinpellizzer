Prunus serrulata f. atrorubra

Prunus serrulata f. atrorubra, commonly known as the Crimson Cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a natural variant of the Japanese cherry blossom, characterized by its deep crimson to almost black flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage is dark green, providing a striking contrast to the intense red blooms. This variety is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces, often planted for its seasonal display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various climates.
