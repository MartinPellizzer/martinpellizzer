Primula domensis

Primula domensis, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes where temperatures are low. It is a member of the Primulaceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Primula and Myrsine.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
