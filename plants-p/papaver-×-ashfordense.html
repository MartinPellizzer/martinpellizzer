Papaver × ashfordense

Papaver × ashfordense, commonly known as the Ashford poppy, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its striking, large, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Botanically, it is a cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver bracteatum (bracted poppy), resulting in its unique morphological features.
