Polygala brizoides var. latifolia

Polygala brizoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually pale pink to white in color. This species thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
