Payena paralleloneura

Payena paralleloneura, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
