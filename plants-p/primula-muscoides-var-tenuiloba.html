Primula muscoides var. tenuiloba

Primula muscoides var. tenuiloba, commonly known as the slender-leaved primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
