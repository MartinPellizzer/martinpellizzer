Pouteria torta subsp. gallifructa

Pouteria torta subsp.

gallifructa, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow custard apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, pale bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and aromatic pulp.

Its fruit is highly valued for its unique flavor and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
