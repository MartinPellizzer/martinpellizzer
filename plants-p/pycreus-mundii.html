Pycreus mundii

Pycreus mundii, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, green, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native environment.

Pycreus mundii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
