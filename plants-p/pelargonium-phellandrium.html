Pelargonium phellandrium

Pelargonium phellandrium, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-scented leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit with rounded foliage and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for containers and garden beds.

Its aromatic properties make it popular for use in aromatherapy and as a natural pest repellent.
