Platytheca galioides var. pubescens

Platytheca galioides var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy false goldenweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety *pubescens* is distinguished by its dense pubescence, which gives it a fuzzy appearance.
