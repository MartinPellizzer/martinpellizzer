Potentilla crantzii var. firma

Potentilla crantzii var. firma, commonly known as the compact potentilla, is a low-growing, flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, rounded foliage composed of small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, hardy plants.
