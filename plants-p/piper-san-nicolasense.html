Piper san-nicolasense

Piper san-nicolasense, commonly known as the San Nicolas pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit, which is a small, round berry, is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild, peppery flavor.
