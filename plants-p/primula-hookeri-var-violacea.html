Primula hookeri var. violacea

Primula hookeri var. violacea, commonly known as the violet primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
