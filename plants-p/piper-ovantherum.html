Piper ovantherum

Piper ovantherum, commonly known as the oval-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

Piper ovantherum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
