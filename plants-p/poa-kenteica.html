Poa kenteica

Poa kenteica, commonly known as Kente grass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Poa kenteica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments and supporting local biodiversity.
