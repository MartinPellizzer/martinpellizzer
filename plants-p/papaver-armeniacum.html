Papaver armeniacum

Papaver armeniacum, commonly known as Armenian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice in gardens and botanical collections.
