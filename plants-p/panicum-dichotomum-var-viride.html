Panicum dichotomum var. viride

Panicum dichotomum var. viride, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its rapid growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant or used in ecological restoration projects.
