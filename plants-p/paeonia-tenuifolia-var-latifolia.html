Paeonia tenuifolia var. latifolia

Paeonia tenuifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved peony, is a cultivar of the peony plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
