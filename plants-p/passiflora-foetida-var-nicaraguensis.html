Passiflora foetida var. nicaraguensis

Passiflora foetida var. nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
