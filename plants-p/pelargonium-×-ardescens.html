Pelargonium × ardescens

Pelargonium × ardescens, commonly known as the water geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to dark purple. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to early summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal contribute to its widespread cultivation in gardens around the world.
