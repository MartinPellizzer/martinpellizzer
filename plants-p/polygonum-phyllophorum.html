Polygonum phyllophorum

Polygonum phyllophorum, commonly known as the broad-leaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant features smooth, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base, with slender, ascending stems. It produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygonum phyllophorum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
