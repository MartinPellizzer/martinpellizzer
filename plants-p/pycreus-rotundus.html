Pycreus rotundus

Pycreus rotundus, commonly known as the roundleaf mallow, is a flowering plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Pycreus rotundus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
