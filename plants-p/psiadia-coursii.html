Psiadia coursii

Psiadia coursii, commonly known as the Coursii's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It is part of the milkweed family, Apocynaceae, and contains latex sap that can be toxic.

Psiadia coursii is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
