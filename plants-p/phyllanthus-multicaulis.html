Phyllanthus multicaulis

Phyllanthus multicaulis, commonly known as the African buttertree, is a shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is small and capsule-like, contains seeds that are dispersed by animals.
