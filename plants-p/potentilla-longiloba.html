Potentilla longiloba

Potentilla longiloba, commonly known as the long-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in early summer.

Potentilla longiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
