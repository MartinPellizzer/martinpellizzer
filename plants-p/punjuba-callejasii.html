Punjuba callejasii

Punjuba callejasii, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its low water requirements and ability to withstand harsh conditions, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
