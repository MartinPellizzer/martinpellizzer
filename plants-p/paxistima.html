Paxistima

Paxistima, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique succulent that belongs to the family Crassulaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is characterized by its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by curling into a compact ball. The plant's leaves are fleshy and thick, adapted to store water efficiently. Paxistima reproduces through seeds and can also propagate vegetatively from its stems.

When water becomes available, it "resurrects," uncurling and regaining its green appearance.
