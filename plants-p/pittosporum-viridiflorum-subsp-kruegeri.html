Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. kruegeri

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

kruegeri, commonly known as the Krueger's Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that turn red as they mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
