Primula glutinosa var. exilis

Primula glutinosa var. exilis, commonly known as the slender sticky primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which is a distinctive botanical characteristic. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Primulaceae family.
