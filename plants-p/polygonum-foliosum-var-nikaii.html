Polygonum foliosum var. nikaii

Polygonum foliosum var. nikaii, commonly known as the Nikai's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, creeping stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
