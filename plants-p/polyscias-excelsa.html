Polyscias excelsa

Polyscias excelsa, commonly known as the giant polycarpic, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Polyscias excelsa is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its potential medicinal properties.
