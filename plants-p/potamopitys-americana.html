Potamopitys americana

Potamopitys americana, commonly known as the American water pine.

It is a coniferous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thin and scaly, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
