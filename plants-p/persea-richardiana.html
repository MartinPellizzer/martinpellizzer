Persea richardiana

Persea richardiana, commonly known as the Mexican black sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a pear-shaped fruit with a smooth, dark purple skin and a creamy, sweet flesh. Its botanical features include a thick, aromatic bark and a shallow root system adapted to well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
