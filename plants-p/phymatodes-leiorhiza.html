Phymatodes leiorhiza

Phymatodes leiorhiza, commonly known as the narrow-rooted phymatodes, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
