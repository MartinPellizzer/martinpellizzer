Pectis subeglandulosa

Pectis subeglandulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces showy yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
