Passiflora foetida var. sericea

Passiflora foetida var. sericea, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The fruit is a small, edible berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
