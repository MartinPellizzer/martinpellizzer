Psychotria rubriceps

Psychotria rubriceps, commonly known as the red-headed psychotria, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking red calyx and small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Psychotria rubriceps is also known for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as dimethyltryptamine.
