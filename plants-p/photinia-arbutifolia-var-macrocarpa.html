Photinia arbutifolia var. macrocarpa

Photinia arbutifolia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the glossyleaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy green on top and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedging and landscape design.
