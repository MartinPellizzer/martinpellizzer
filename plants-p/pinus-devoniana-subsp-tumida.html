Pinus devoniana subsp. tumida

Pinus devoniana subsp.

tumida, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its slow growth, twisted trunk, and dense foliage, which contribute to its longevity and unique appearance.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust and widely spaced branches compared to other members of the *Pinus devoniana* species complex. Its cones are typically small and reddish-brown, containing two to three seeds.

This plant plays a vital ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
