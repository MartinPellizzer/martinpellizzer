Pachystigma burttii

Pachystigma burttii, commonly known as the Burtt's rock daisy, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a dense rosette, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with prominent, showy stamens, which attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
