Pleopeltis soromanes

Pleopeltis soromanes, commonly known as the Bornean rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are short and often covered with a thick layer of moss-like tissue.

Pleopeltis soromanes is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
