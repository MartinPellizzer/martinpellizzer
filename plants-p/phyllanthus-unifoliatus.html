Phyllanthus unifoliatus

Phyllanthus unifoliatus, commonly known as the cheesewood or single-leaved phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is used in traditional medicine for various health conditions.
