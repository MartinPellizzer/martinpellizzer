Panicum betafoense

Panicum betafoense, commonly known as the Betafoense Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in grasses. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
