Pothos grandis

Pothos grandis, commonly known as the giant pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pothos grandis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
