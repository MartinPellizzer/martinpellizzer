Pekea villosa

Pekea villosa, commonly known as the hairy pekea, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits.

Pekea villosa is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
