Pappobolus hypargyreus

Pappobolus hypargyreus, commonly known as the golden burr grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose the flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that are surrounded by a dense, hairy pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
