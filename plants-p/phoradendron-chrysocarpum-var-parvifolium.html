Phoradendron chrysocarpum var. parvifolium

Phoradendron chrysocarpum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved golden-fruited mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on a variety of host trees.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-orange berries that contain sticky seeds. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, inconspicuous white flowers. It is native to parts of Central and South America and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to the ecological dynamics of its environment.
