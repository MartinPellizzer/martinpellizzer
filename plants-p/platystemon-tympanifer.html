Platystemon tympanifer

Platystemon tympanifer, commonly known as the drumleaf cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its leaves are often lobed or divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that dehisces to release tiny seeds, aiding in its dispersal.
