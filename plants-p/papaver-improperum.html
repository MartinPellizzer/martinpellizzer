Papaver improperum

Papaver improperum, commonly known as the western poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with a dark central blotch. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Papaver improperum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
