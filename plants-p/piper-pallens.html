Piper pallens

Piper pallens, commonly known as the pale pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
