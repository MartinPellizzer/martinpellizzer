Psidium cuneatum

Psidium cuneatum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Psidium cuneatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
