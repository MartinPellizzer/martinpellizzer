Phragmipedium caudatum var. roseum

Phragmipedium caudatum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseum phragmipedium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, velvety petals that range from pale pink to deep rose hues, giving it a dramatic and showy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring or early summer, are known for their intricate structure and vibrant coloration.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
