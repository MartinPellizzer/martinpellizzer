Plinthine

Plinthine, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a flowering plant belonging to the genus Oxalis.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to various regions in the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Plinthine contains oxalic acid, which can be toxic in large quantities.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.
