Psilotrichum erythrostachyum

Psilotrichum erythrostachyum, commonly known as the red-stalked psilotrichum, is a member of the Psilotaceae family, characterized by its unique, primitive structure.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with a short, creeping stem and delicate, branching leaves. The plant produces small, reddish spore-bearing structures called strobili, which are clustered at the tips of its branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests.

Its simple morphology and ancient lineage make it an important subject in the study of early land plant evolution.
