Pseudopilocereus multicostatus

Pseudopilocereus multicostatus, commonly known as the night-blooming star cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and often covered with spines. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This cactus is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
