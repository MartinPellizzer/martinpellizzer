Passiflora pedata subsp. stipularis

Passiflora pedata subsp.

stipularis, commonly known as the stipular passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown.

The flowers are typically blue or purple with a central crown of filaments, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
