Passiflora acuminata

Passiflora acuminata, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in tendrils that help it climb.

Passiflora acuminata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
