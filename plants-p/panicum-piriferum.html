Panicum piriferum

Panicum piriferum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, red-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more reddish in maturity.

Panicum piriferum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
