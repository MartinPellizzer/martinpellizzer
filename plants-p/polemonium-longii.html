Polemonium longii

Polemonium longii, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically features dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with well-drained soil.

Polemonium longii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
