Potamogeton octandrus var. mizuhikimo

Potamogeton octandrus var. mizuhikimo, commonly known as Japanese pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls of eight, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and rivers.

Its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic life but may also become invasive in certain regions.
