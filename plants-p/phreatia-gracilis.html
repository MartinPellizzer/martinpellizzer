Phreatia gracilis

Phreatia gracilis, commonly known as the slender phreatia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season.

Phreatia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
