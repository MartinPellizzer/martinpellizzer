Peperomia albonervosa

Peperomia albonervosa, commonly known as the White-veined Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a striking, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance, it is often used as a decorative plant in both homes and offices.
