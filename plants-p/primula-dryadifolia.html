Primula dryadifolia

Primula dryadifolia, commonly known as the dryad's ghost, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in spring, and its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine layer of down. Primula dryadifolia is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific moisture and light conditions.
