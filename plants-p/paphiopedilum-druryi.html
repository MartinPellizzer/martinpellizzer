Paphiopedilum druryi

Paphiopedilum druryi, commonly known as the Drury's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a characteristic feature of the orchid family. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with intricate patterns, and it produces a single, showy bloom per stem.

Paphiopedilum druryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
