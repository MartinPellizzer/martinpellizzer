Pavetta polyantha

Pavetta polyantha, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and have a sweet fragrance.

Pavetta polyantha is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
