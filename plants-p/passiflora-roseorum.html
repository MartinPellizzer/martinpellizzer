Passiflora roseorum

Passiflora roseorum, commonly known as the rose passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink and white flowers, which feature intricate patterns and a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora roseorum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
