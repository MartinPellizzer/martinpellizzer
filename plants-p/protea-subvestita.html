Protea subvestita

Protea subvestita, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white to pale pink, with a distinctive silver or grayish hue on the outer bracts.

Protea subvestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's unique flora.
