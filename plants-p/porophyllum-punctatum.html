Porophyllum punctatum

Porophyllum punctatum, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has ecological importance as a food source for certain insects.
