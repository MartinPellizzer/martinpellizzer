Prismatomeris

Prismatomeris, commonly known as the glassy fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, glassy appearance due to the presence of silica in its cell walls, which gives it a crystalline look. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, often with a waxy coating. These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
