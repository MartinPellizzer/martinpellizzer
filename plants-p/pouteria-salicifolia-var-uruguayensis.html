Pouteria salicifolia var. uruguayensis

Pouteria salicifolia var. uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay pouteria, is a species native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, orange-yellow berries that are a key food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of the understory vegetation in tropical and subtropical forests.
