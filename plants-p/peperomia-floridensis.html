Peperomia floridensis

Peperomia floridensis, commonly known as the Florida peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia floridensis is also valued for its role in ecological habitats, providing shelter and food for small insects and animals.
