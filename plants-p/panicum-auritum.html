Panicum auritum

Panicum auritum, commonly known as the ear-shaped grass or red panicum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, reddish-brown inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish hue that adds visual interest.

Panicum auritum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
