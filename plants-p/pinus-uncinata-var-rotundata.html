Pinus uncinata var. rotundata

Pinus uncinata var. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded pine, is a variant of the Spanish pine characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features short, twisted needles that are typically bluish-green in color, giving the plant a unique appearance. The cones are small and usually remain attached to the branches for a long time, contributing to its ornamental value. This variety is native to the Iberian Peninsula and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
