Pleurothallis pleurothalloides

Pleurothallis pleurothalloides, commonly known as the pink pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It features small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color, with a distinctive lip that is usually adorned with intricate patterns. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its unique appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
