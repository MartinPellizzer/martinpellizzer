Pandanus occultus

Pandanus occultus, commonly known as the ghost pandanus, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to certain parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often unbranched inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically grayish-green in color, with a waxy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or mangrove areas.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
