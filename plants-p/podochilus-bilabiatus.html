Podochilus bilabiatus

Podochilus bilabiatus, commonly known as the bilabiatus orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, bloom in clusters and have a distinctive bilabiate (two-lip) structure.
