Phoradendron hawksworthii

Phoradendron hawksworthii, commonly known as the Hawaiian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is a symbol of the unique flora of Hawaii.
