Passiflora garckei subsp. garckei

Passiflora garckei subsp.

garckei, commonly known as the Garcke's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface and prominent veins. This species produces edible berries that are yellow or orange when mature, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
