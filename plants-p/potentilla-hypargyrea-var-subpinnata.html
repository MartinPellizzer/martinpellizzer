Potentilla hypargyrea var. subpinnata

Potentilla hypargyrea var. subpinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, whitish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
