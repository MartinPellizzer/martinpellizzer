Populus tremuloides var. aurea

Populus tremuloides var. aurea, commonly known as the golden trembling aspen, is a variant of the trembling aspen characterized by its striking golden foliage.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a vibrant yellow color during the autumn season. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
