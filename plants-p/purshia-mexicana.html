Purshia mexicana

Purshia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican bitterbrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and gray-green foliage, which provide important habitat and food for wildlife. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Purshia mexicana plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in its native range.
