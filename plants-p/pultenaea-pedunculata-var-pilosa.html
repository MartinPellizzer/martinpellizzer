Pultenaea pedunculata var. pilosa

Pultenaea pedunculata var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
