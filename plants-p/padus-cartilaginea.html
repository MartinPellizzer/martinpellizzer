Padus cartilaginea

Padus cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilage maple, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, red-orange flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with cool, moist climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
