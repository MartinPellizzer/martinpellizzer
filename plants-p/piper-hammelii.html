Piper hammelii

Piper hammelii, commonly known as the Hammel's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface, while the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature, and it is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cultures.
