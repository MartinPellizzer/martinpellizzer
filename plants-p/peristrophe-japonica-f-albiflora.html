Peristrophe japonica f. albiflora

Peristrophe japonica f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese peristrophe, is a rare and striking variant of the original species.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast sharply with its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is native to southern Japan and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique coloration.
