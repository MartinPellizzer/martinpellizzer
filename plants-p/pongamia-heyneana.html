Pongamia heyneana

Pongamia heyneana, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, oily substance. It is often found in dry tropical and subtropical regions, growing in various soil types.

The plant has potential for use in biofuel production due to its high oil content.
