Platymerium glomeratum

Platymerium glomeratum, commonly known as the clustered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
