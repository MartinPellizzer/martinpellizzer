Pittosporum sulcatum

Pittosporum sulcatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pittosporum sulcatum is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
