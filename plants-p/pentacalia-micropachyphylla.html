Pentacalia micropachyphylla

Pentacalia micropachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pentacalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, narrow leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
