Piper relictum

Piper relictum, commonly known as betel quill, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant in chewing practices.

The botanical features of Piper relictum make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
