Pygeum nervosum

Pygeum nervosum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
