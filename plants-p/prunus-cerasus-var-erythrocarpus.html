Prunus cerasus var. erythrocarpus

Prunus cerasus var. erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-cherry plum, is a variant of the European plum species.

It is characterized by its small, vibrant red fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing before the leaves in early spring.
