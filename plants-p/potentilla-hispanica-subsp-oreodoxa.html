Potentilla hispanica subsp. oreodoxa

Potentilla hispanica subsp.

oreodoxa, commonly known as Spanish cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are usually divided into five lobes. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
