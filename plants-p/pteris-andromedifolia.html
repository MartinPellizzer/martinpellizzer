Pteris andromedifolia

Pteris andromedifolia, commonly known as the Andromeda brake, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green to yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are arranged in a distinctive, fan-like pattern.

Pteris andromedifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
