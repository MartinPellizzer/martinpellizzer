Piper leticianum var. longispicum

Piper leticianum var. longispicum, commonly known as the longispiculate pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, spiculate calyces that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, peppercorn-like berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
