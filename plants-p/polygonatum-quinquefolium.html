Polygonatum quinquefolium

Polygonatum quinquefolium, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous underground stem that produces clusters of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant bears panicles of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. Its berries are red and edible, often consumed by birds and small mammals.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
