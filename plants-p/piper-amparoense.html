Piper amparoense

Piper amparoense, commonly known as the Amparo pepper or Amparo chili, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, reddish berry that contains several seeds.

Piper amparoense is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in traditional cooking and herbal medicine.
