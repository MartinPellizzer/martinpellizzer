Plantago alata

Plantago alata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Plantago alata is also known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for skin irritations and digestive issues.
