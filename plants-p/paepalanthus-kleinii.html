Paepalanthus kleinii

Paepalanthus kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
