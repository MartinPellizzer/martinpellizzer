Panicum amphistemon

Panicum amphistemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike that bears small, two-lobed spikelets.

Panicum amphistemon plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
