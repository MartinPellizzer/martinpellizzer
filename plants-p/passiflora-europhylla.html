Passiflora europhylla

Passiflora europhylla, commonly known as the European passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, five-petaled flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments, typical of the Passifloraceae family. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
