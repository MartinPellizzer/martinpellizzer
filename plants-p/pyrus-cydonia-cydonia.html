Pyrus-cydonia cydonia

Pyrus-cydonia cydonia, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces a distinctive, fragrant fruit that is typically harvested when it is fully mature and dry. The fruit has a rough, pale yellow skin and a dense, aromatic pulp with a sweet-tart flavor. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible fruit.
