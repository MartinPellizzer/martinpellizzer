Piper oparumaense

Piper oparumaense, commonly known as the Oparuma pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are typically green berries that turn red when mature, and they are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
