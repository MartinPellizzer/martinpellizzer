Potamogeton leschenaultii

Potamogeton leschenaultii, commonly known as Leschenault's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to various regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
