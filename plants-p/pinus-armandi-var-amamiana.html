Pinus armandi var. amamiana

Pinus armandi var. amamiana, commonly known as the Amami pine, is a variant of the Chinese white pine native to the Amami Islands of Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and cone-like structures. The plant exhibits a distinct growth habit with a conical shape and is adapted to the humid, subtropical climate of its native habitat. Its foliage provides ecological benefits, supporting local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Japanese landscapes.
