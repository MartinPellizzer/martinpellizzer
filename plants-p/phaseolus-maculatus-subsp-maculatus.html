Phaseolus maculatus subsp. maculatus

Phaseolus maculatus subsp.

maculatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species native to the Americas. It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds.

The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in height, supported by its tendrils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in warm climates and is commonly cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
