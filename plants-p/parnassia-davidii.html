Parnassia davidii

Parnassia davidii, commonly known as the David's fairy cup, is a striking member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a greenish or reddish tint at the base. The plant features a short, hairy stem that supports the single, prominent flower, which is surrounded by broad, lance-shaped leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species among plant enthusiasts and botanists.
