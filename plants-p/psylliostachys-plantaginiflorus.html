Psylliostachys plantaginiflorus

Psylliostachys plantaginiflorus, commonly known as the blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often forming dense stands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
