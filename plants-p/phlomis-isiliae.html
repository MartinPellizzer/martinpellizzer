Phlomis isiliae

Phlomis isiliae, commonly known as the Cyprus phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple or pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Phlomis isiliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
