Pelargonium barbatum

Pelargonium barbatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. Its leaves are rounded with serrated edges and are often covered in a similar fuzzy coating.

Pelargonium barbatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
