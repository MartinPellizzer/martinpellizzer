Polianthes lata

Polianthes lata, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Polianthes lata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.
