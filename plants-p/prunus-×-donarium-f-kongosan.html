Prunus × donarium f. kongosan

Prunus × donarium f. kongosan, commonly known as the Korean cherry, is a botanical hybrid belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring. The plant is native to Korea and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture, and it produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in traditional cuisine.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
