Potamogeton × nitens var. curvifolius

Potamogeton × nitens var. curvifolius, commonly known as the curved-leaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often curve at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. It produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spikelets, and it is often found in dense vegetation.

This species plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
