Peucedanum meifolium

Peucedanum meifolium, commonly known as meadow angelica, is a member of the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a biennial or perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish-yellow flowers in umbrella-shaped inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its aromatic seeds are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including volatile oils and flavonoids, contributing to its medicinal properties.
