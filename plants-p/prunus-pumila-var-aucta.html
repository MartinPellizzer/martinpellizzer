Prunus pumila var. aucta

Prunus pumila var. aucta, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry or the snowflake cherry, is a compact shrub native to Japan.

It features small, delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has a low-growing habit with arching branches and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover. Its foliage turns a vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shady garden areas.
