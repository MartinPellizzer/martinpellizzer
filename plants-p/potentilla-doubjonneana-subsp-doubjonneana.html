Potentilla doubjonneana subsp. doubjonneana

Potentilla doubjonneana subsp.

doubjonneana, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with bright yellow flowers.

The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
