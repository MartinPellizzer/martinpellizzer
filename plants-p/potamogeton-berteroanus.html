Potamogeton berteroanus

Potamogeton berteroanus, commonly known as the Bertero's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, flexible stems that are often branching and bear small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
