Polygonatum humile var. krylovii

Polygonatum humile var. krylovii, commonly known as the Siberian Solomon's Seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, rhizomatous stems that produce opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant bears small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
