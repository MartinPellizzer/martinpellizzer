Potamogeton americanus subsp. mascarensis

Potamogeton americanus subsp.

mascarensis, commonly known as the Mascarene pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean. It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various waterborne organisms.
