Paepalanthus elongatus var. glabrescens

Paepalanthus elongatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous elongated papyrus, is a variant of the papyrus plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or wetland environments.
