Ponera pleurostachys

Ponera pleurostachys, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with a distinct scent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
