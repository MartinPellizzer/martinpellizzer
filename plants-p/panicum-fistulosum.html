Panicum fistulosum

Panicum fistulosum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed but can also serve as a valuable forage plant for livestock.
