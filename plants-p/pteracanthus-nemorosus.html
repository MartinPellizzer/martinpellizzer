Pteracanthus nemorosus

Pteracanthus nemorosus, commonly known as the forest thornbush, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in dense, shaded environments, such as forests and thickets. The plant features woody stems with thorny branches, and its leaves are usually small, simple, and opposite in arrangement. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in panicles, and it produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
