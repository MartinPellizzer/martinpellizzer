Poagris

Poagris, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Africa. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are often overlooked due to their modest size.

Poagris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
