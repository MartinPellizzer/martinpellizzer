Primula balfourii

Primula balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats and prefers cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Primula balfourii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
