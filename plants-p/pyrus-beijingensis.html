Pyrus beijingensis

Pyrus beijingensis, commonly known as the Beijing pear, is a deciduous tree native to northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears but with a unique, slightly tart flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
