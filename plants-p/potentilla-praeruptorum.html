Potentilla praeruptorum

Potentilla praeruptorum, commonly known as Chinese potentilla, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Potentilla praeruptorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
