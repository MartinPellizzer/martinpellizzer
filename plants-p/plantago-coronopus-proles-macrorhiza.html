Plantago coronopus proles macrorhiza

Plantago coronopus proles macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-rooted plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has a distinctive rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense spikes, which appear in late summer.

The species is notable for its robust root system, which contributes to its ability to thrive in wet environments.
