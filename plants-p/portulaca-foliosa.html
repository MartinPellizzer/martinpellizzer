Portulaca foliosa

Portulaca foliosa, commonly known as the leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green or red in color, depending on the species and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close during the heat of the day. It is adapted to survive in poor soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Portulaca foliosa is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, which helps it endure long periods of drought.
