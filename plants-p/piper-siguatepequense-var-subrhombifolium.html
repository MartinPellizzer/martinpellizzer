Piper siguatepequense var. subrhombifolium

Piper siguatepequense var. subrhombifolium, commonly known as the Guayacan de Cuenca, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its broad, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
