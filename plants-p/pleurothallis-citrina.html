Pleurothallis citrina

Pleurothallis citrina, commonly known as the yellow pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Pleurothallis citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
