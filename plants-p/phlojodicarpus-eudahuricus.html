Phlojodicarpus eudahuricus

Phlojodicarpus eudahuricus, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban conditions.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and use in windbreaks and reforestation projects.
