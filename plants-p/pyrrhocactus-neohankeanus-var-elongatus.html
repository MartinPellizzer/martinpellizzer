Pyrrhocactus neohankeanus var. elongatus

Pyrrhocactus neohankeanus var. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated pyrrhocactus, is a unique cactus species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features areolate, spiny leaves that are adapted for water storage and reduce transpiration. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native environment.
