Piper velayudhanii

Piper velayudhanii, commonly known as the Velayudhan's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India, where it thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
