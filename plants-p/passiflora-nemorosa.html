Passiflora nemorosa

Passiflora nemorosa, commonly known as the woodland passionflower, is a climbing perennial plant native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The flowers are typically white or pale blue with a central red or orange corona, which is unique to the Passifloraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The plant is also known for its edible berries, which are small, yellow, and have a tart flavor.
