Perriera orientalis

Perriera orientalis, commonly known as the oriental perriera, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it stores water in its thick, waxy leaves. Perriera orientalis is often found growing in rocky soils and along dry riverbeds.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
