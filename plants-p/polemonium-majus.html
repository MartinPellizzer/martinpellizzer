Polemonium majus

Polemonium majus, commonly known as the Eastern blueflag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Polemonium majus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to support local wildlife.
