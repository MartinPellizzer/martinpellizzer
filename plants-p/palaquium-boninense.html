Palaquium boninense

Palaquium boninense, commonly known as the Bonin persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Bonin Islands.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a smooth, round berry that turns from green to orange-red when ripe. The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
