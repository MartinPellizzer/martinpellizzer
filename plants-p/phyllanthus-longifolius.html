Phyllanthus longifolius

Phyllanthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
