Polygala blepharotropis

Polygala blepharotropis, commonly known as the winged milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually small and alternate, while the fruit is a dry, dehiscent capsule that releases seeds upon maturity.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
