Parkia filicina

Parkia filicina, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a popular ornamental species. The plant produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Parkia filicina thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and ecological benefits.
