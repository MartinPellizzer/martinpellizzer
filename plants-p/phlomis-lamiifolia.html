Phlomis lamiifolia

Phlomis lamiifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features tall, erect stems with gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pale yellow to orange, blooming in summer.

Phlomis lamiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
