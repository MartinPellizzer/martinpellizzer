Prunus sativa var. praecociflora

Prunus sativa var. praecociflora, commonly known as the early-flowering almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender branches, serrated leaves, and fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom early in the spring. The plant produces small, edible drupes with a sweet, nutty flavor, often harvested before the fruit fully ripens. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth and flowering.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
