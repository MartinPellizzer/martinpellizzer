Phellosperma guelzowiana

Phellosperma guelzowiana, commonly known as the Guelzow's phellosperma, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phellospermataceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its succulent leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
