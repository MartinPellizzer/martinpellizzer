Philibertia longifolia

Philibertia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved filbert, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Philibertia longifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native range.
