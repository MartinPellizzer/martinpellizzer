Phyllanthus scoparius

Phyllanthus scoparius, commonly known as the African date tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fleshy, edible fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in dry climates.
