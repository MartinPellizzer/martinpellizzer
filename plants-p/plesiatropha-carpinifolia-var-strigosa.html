Plesiatropha carpinifolia var. strigosa

Plesiatropha carpinifolia var. strigosa, commonly known as the woolly alder, is a shrub native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found near streams and wet meadows. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, oval-shaped berries.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
