Pilosella neogelmii

Pilosella neogelmii, commonly known as the Neogelm's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, silvery appearance. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Pilosella neogelmii is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
