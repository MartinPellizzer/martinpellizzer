Psoralea polysticta

Psoralea polysticta, commonly known as the spotted psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in shrubland or open woodlands. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

Psoralea polysticta is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in local communities for various health purposes.
