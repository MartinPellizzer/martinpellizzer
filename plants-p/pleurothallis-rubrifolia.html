Pleurothallis rubrifolia

Pleurothallis rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or pink hues, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white and yellow, with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis rubrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
