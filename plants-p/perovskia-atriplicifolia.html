Perovskia atriplicifolia

Perovskia atriplicifolia, commonly known as Russian sage, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and spikes of lavender-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, and its stems are square and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and garden borders.

The botanical features of Russian sage contribute to its adaptability and ornamental value in various climates.
