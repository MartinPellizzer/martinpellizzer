Pittosporum parvifolium var. parvifolium

Pittosporum parvifolium var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
