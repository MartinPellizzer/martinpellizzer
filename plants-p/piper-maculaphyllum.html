Piper maculaphyllum

Piper maculaphyllum, commonly known as the spotted pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its dark green, slightly hairy leaves with distinctive white or yellow spots. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by black, peppery fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
