Psephellus simplicicaulis

Psephellus simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-stemmed rock cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically has a basal rosette of leaves and produces racemes of bright yellow flowers in the spring. Its leaves are simple and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
