Persea parviflora

Persea parviflora, commonly known as the little-flowered avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thin, smooth skin and a creamy, edible pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
