Plantago macrantha var. argentea

Plantago macrantha var. argentea, commonly known as silver plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces upright spikes of small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
