Parodia variicolor

Parodia variicolor, commonly known as the variegated pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a spiny exterior and a dense arrangement of tubercles. The plant's surface is often covered with white or pale blue spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are typically yellow or orange and bloom in clusters at the top of the plant.

Parodia variicolor is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
