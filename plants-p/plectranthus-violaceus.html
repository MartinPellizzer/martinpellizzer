Plectranthus violaceus

Plectranthus violaceus, commonly known as violetleaf false mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually violet or purple in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus violaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
