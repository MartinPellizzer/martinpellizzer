Pulicaria chrysantha var. oligochaeta

Pulicaria chrysantha var. oligochaeta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tansy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
