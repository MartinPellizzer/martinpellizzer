Peritassa bullata

Peritassa bullata, commonly known as the bullate peritassa, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often covered in a waxy bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
