Puya llatensis

Puya llatensis, commonly known as the Llay Llay plant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Puya llatensis is also notable for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native ecosystem.
