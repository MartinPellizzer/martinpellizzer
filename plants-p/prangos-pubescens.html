Prangos pubescens

Prangos pubescens, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy stems and large, spiny leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy purple to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Prangos pubescens typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to rock gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
