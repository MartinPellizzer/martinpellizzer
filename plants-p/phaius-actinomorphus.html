Phaius actinomorphus

Phaius actinomorphus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble sunbursts, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, with each bloom featuring three sepals and three petals.

The botanical features of Phaius actinomorphus make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and among orchid enthusiasts.
