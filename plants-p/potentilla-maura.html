Potentilla maura

Potentilla maura, commonly known as the common potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring many slender, woody stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
