Pariti pernambucense

Pariti pernambucense, commonly known as the Pernambuco orchid, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and researchers.
