Paphiopedilum virens

Paphiopedilum virens, commonly known as the green-flowered slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, saddle-shaped lip that is typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces showy, green or white flowers with a prominent spur, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and prefers well-draining soil.

Paphiopedilum virens is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking foliage and delicate blooms.
