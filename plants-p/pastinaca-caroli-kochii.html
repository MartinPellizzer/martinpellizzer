Pastinaca caroli-kochii

Pastinaca caroli-kochii, commonly known as the Caroli-Koch's scorzonera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers with prominent central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its root system is fibrous, and the roots are often harvested for their edible qualities.

It is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
