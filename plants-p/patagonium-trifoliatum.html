Patagonium trifoliatum

Patagonium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaf Patagonia plant, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three lance-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its harsh, high-altitude habitat.
