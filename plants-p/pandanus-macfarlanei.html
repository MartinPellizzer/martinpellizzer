Pandanus macfarlanei

Pandanus macfarlanei, commonly known as the Macfarlane's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to salty and humid conditions.

Its unique morphology and cultural significance make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
