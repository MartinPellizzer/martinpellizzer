Piper fontium

Piper fontium, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a strong, spicy aroma. Its flowers are small and green, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Piper fontium is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
