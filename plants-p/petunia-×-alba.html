Petunia × alba

Petunia × alba, commonly known as the white petunia, is a hybrid flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that typically range in color from white to pale purple. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. Petunia × alba thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for container gardening and bedding plants.

Its ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season adds to its appeal in horticultural settings.
