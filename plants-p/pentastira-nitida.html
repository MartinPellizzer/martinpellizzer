Pentastira nitida

Pentastira nitida, commonly known as the slender holly or white holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, bell-shaped flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
