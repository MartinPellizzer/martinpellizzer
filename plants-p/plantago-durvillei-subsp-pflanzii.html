Plantago durvillei subsp. pflanzii

Plantago durvillei subsp.

pflanzii, commonly known as the Durville's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
