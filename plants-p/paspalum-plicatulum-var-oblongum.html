Paspalum plicatulum var. oblongum

Paspalum plicatulum var. oblongum, commonly known as the broadleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly folded appearance, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle structure. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it well adapted to moist environments. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical characteristics make it a useful plant in restoration projects and as a forage crop.
