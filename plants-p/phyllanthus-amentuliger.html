Phyllanthus amentuliger

Phyllanthus amentuliger, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in some regions.
