Potentilla californica var. elata

Potentilla californica var. elata, commonly known as the tall potentilla, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. It produces small, oval leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often woody at the base.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
