Pothos ovatus

Pothos ovatus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a popular houseplant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically solid green with a prominent yellow vein. The plant is a trailing vine that can be grown in hanging baskets or trained to climb. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires moderate watering.

Its adaptability and ease of care make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
