Parapolystichum acuminatum

Parapolystichum acuminatum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically 30 to 60 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to the forest floor, while its spore-producing structures are located on the undersides of its leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
