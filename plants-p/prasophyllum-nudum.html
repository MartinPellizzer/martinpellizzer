Prasophyllum nudum

Prasophyllum nudum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in southern and eastern Australia, including regions like New South Wales and Victoria.

Prasophyllum nudum is a herbaceous perennial, with its flowering period occurring during the summer months.
