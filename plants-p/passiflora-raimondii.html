Passiflora raimondii

Passiflora raimondii, commonly known as the Raimondi passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and intricate patterns. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
