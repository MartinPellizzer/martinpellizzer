Paris hexaphylla var. manshurica

Paris hexaphylla var. manshurica, commonly known as the Manshurian lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of northeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a dense, spiraled inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in shaded forest understories. It is classified as a terrestrial orchid and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered in its natural range.
