Polypodium regulare

Polypodium regulare, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Polypodium regulare is widely distributed across temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
