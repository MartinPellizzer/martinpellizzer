Pioctonon ternatum

Pioctonon ternatum, commonly known as the three-leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, each consisting of three lobes.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae, showcasing its evolutionary adaptations to humid climates.
