Pterostylis despectans

Pterostylis despectans, commonly known as the despised orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, often strikingly shaped flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant produces a single, erect stem bearing a single or clustered inflorescence with small, intricate flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas.

The orchid's pollination mechanism involves specific insect species, highlighting its ecological significance in native ecosystems.
