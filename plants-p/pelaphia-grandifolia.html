Pelaphia grandifolia

Pelaphia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pelaphia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it remains relatively rare in horticultural trade.
