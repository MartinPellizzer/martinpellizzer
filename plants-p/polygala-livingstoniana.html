Polygala livingstoniana

Polygala livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, dry conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a unique structure that resembles a spurred shape.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
