Polygonum aviculare var. laxum

Polygonum aviculare var. laxum, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems that are often covered with fine hairs. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.
