Polystichopsis bella

Polystichopsis bella, commonly known as the golden fern, is a rare and striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow fronds, which contrast sharply with its dark, leathery texture. Its unique coloration makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.

Due to habitat loss, Polystichopsis bella is considered endangered, highlighting the need for continued research and protection.
