Passiflora herbertiana subsp. herbertiana

Passiflora herbertiana subsp.

herbertiana, commonly known as Herbert's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures.

The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
