Potentilla argentea var. typica

Potentilla argentea var. typica, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
