Phoenix siamensis

Phoenix siamensis, commonly known as the Siam date palm, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender palm with a tall, unbranched stem and a crown of long, arching leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
