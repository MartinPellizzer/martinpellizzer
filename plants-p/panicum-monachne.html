Panicum monachne

Panicum monachne, commonly known as monk's panicum, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop in certain regions.
