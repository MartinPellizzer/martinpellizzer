Panicum dichotomum var. yadkinense

Panicum dichotomum var. yadkinense, commonly known as the Yadkin panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with narrow, blade-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, two-branched spikelets.

This variety is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
