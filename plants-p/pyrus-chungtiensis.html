Pyrus chungtiensis

Pyrus chungtiensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, white to yellow fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production, thriving in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Pyrus chungtiensis is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
