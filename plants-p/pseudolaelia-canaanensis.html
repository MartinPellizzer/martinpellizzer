Pseudolaelia canaanensis

Pseudolaelia canaanensis, commonly known as the Canaan orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of Israel and the West Bank.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow blooms during the spring season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation status.
