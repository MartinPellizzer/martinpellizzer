Podochilus lancilabris

Podochilus lancilabris, commonly known as the slender-lipped orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a raceme. Its roots are adapted for aerial attachment to trees, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Podochilus lancilabris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
