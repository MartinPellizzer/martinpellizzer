Potentilla reptans f. microphylla

Potentilla reptans f. microphylla, commonly known as creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter, giving it a dense, carpet-like appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
