Pyrus sinensis var. maximowicziana

Pyrus sinensis var. maximowicziana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variety of pear native to China.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers and edible, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in the fall. The plant is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
