Polygala densifolia var. angustifolia

Polygala densifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, often clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, spicate inflorescences with tiny, pinkish to white flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
