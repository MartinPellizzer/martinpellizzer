Phacelia cryptantha

Phacelia cryptantha, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

Phacelia cryptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
