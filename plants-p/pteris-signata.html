Pteris signata

Pteris signata, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

Pteris signata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
