Philodendron devianum

Philodendron devianum, commonly known as the Devil's Ivy, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant is characterized by its ability to climb using aerial roots, which it uses to attach itself to tree trunks or other surfaces. Its foliage can sometimes exhibit variegation, though this is less common in most cultivated forms.

Philodendron devianum is a popular houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
