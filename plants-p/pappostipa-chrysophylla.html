Pappostipa chrysophylla

Pappostipa chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden feather grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, golden-yellow inflorescence and slender, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Pappostipa chrysophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
