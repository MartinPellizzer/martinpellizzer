Poa nemoralis var. rariflora

Poa nemoralis var. rariflora, commonly known as the rare wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed. Its flowering heads are small and clustered, producing pale blue or greenish flowers.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
