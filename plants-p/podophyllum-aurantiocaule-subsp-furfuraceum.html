Podophyllum aurantiocaule subsp. furfuraceum

Podophyllum aurantiocaule subsp.

furfuraceum, commonly known as the golden-stemmed podophyllum, is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia. It is characterized by its bright orange stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces distinctive, rounded, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, with a waxy surface.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.
