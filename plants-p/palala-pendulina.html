Palala pendulina

Palala pendulina, commonly known as the hanging palala, is a unique plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous growth habit, with long, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant's leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Palala pendulina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
