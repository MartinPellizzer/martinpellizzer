Phagnalon umbelliforme

Phagnalon umbelliforme, commonly known as the woolly tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a soft, fuzzy texture, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Phagnalon umbelliforme is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
