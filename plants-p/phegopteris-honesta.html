Phegopteris honesta

Phegopteris honesta, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bi-pinnate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris honesta plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
