Pedicularis caespitosa

Pedicularis caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that produces striking, spurred flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, with a unique structure that facilitates nectar access.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
