Philadelphus gracilis

Philadelphus gracilis, commonly known as the slender mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches and dark green, ovate leaves that are often hairy beneath. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and pleasant scent, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
