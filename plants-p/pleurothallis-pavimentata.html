Pleurothallis pavimentata

Pleurothallis pavimentata, commonly known as the pavement orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis pavimentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
