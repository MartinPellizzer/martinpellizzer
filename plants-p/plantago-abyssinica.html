Plantago abyssinica

Plantago abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
