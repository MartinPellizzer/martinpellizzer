Phleum praecox var. delphinense

Phleum praecox var. delphinense, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in turfgrass mixtures.
