Prosopis reptans var. cinerascens

Prosopis reptans var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf mesquite, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by long, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

This species is often used for erosion control and as a source of forage for wildlife and livestock.
