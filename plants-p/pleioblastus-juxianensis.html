Pleioblastus juxianensis

Pleioblastus juxianensis, commonly known as the Juxian bamboo, is a species native to China, specifically found in the provinces of Sichuan and Yunnan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters, with slender, greenish-yellow culms that have a smooth, slightly ribbed surface. The leaves are lance-shaped, dark green, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

It is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in landscaping and traditional Chinese gardens.
