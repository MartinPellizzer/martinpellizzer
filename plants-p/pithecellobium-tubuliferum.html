Pithecellobium tubuliferum

Pithecellobium tubuliferum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are often arranged in long, drooping clusters. Its roots are known to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
