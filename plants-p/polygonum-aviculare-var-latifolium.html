Polygonum aviculare var. latifolium

Polygonum aviculare var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf knotweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions, including North America. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water bodies.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems.
