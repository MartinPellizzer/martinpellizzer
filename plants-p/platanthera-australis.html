Platanthera australis

Platanthera australis, commonly known as the Australian lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a distinctive inflorescence of white or pale pink flowers arranged in a loose, trichotomy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Platanthera australis is an important component of the local flora and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its habitat.
