Picea alba var. coerulea

Picea alba var. coerulea, commonly known as the Blue Spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the northern regions of North America.

This coniferous tree is characterized by its striking blue-gray needles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The foliage is dense and pyramidal, with a slow growth rate that allows for a compact, elegant form. It thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The tree is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity and reliability in cultivation.
