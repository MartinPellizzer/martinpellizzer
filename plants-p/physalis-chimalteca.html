Physalis chimalteca

Physalis chimalteca, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Central America and parts of Mexico, where it typically grows in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, resembling a lantern. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and it has erect, branched stems.

Physalis chimalteca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
