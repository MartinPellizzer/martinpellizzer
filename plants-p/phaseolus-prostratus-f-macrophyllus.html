Phaseolus prostratus f. macrophyllus

Phaseolus prostratus f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant leaf scarlet runner bean, is a cultivar characterized by its large, broad leaves and vibrant red flowers.

It is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family, native to the Americas, and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible beans. The plant typically grows as a sprawling vine, reaching lengths of several meters, with trifoliate leaves and pods that mature into edible beans.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and agricultural use.
