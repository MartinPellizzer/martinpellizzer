Phaseolus schottii

Phaseolus schottii, commonly known as the Schott's bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant typically grows as an annual herb.

Phaseolus schottii is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
