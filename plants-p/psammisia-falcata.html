Psammisia falcata

Psammisia falcata, commonly known as the sandspoon plant, is a species of flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and helps it survive in sandy soils by anchoring it firmly and accessing moisture deep underground.

Psammisia falcata is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
