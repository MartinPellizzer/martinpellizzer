Perlarius incanus

Perlarius incanus, commonly known as the silver-haired fern, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that turn a silvery-white color as they mature. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Perlarius incanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments with minimal maintenance.
