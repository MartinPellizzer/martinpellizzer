Pleurothallis × subversa

Pleurothallis × subversa, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structures. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in clusters. Its pseudobulbs are slender and help store water and nutrients, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitats.

Pleurothallis × subversa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
