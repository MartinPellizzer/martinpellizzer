Poa annua

Poa annua, commonly known as annual bluegrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including lawns, fields, and disturbed soils, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. Poa annua has a short rhizome system and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist conditions.

Its life cycle is annual, meaning it completes its growth, reproduction, and death within a single growing season.
