Paspalum singulare

Paspalum singulare, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Paspalum singulare is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and adaptability to various wetland conditions.
