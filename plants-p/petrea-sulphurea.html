Petrea sulphurea

Petrea sulphurea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Petrea sulphurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in landscaping in suitable climates.
