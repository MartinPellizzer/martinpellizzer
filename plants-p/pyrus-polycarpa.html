Pyrus polycarpa

Pyrus polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus polycarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
