Prunus saxicola

Prunus saxicola, commonly known as the saxifrage cherry, is a small shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Prunus saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a hardy landscape plant.
