Phyllanthus quintuplinervis

Phyllanthus quintuplinervis, commonly known as the five-veined phyllanthus, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with five distinct veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its fruits are capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
