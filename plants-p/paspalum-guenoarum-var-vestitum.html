Paspalum guenoarum var. vestitum

Paspalum guenoarum var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
