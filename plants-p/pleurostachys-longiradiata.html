Pleurostachys longiradiata

Pleurostachys longiradiata, commonly known as the long-rayed feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive, feathery inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Pleurostachys longiradiata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native region.
