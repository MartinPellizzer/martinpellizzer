Piptadenia adiantoides var. peruviana

Piptadenia adiantoides var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian spider plant, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features pinnate leaves with slender, linear leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its seeds are encased in pods that split open to release the seeds, facilitating wind dispersal.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
