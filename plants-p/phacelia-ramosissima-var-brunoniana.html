Phacelia ramosissima var. brunoniana

Phacelia ramosissima var. brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces numerous small seeds in its fruiting structures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
