Phaseolus vulgaris var. cruentus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. cruentus, commonly known as the red runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and long, slender pods that turn red as they mature. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by the characteristic red pods. The seeds inside are typically small and oblong, with a smooth texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible beans in certain regions.
