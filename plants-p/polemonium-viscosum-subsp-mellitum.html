Polemonium viscosum subsp. mellitum

Polemonium viscosum subsp.

mellitum, commonly known as sticky bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a sticky, glandular coating. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
