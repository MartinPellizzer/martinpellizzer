Pseudolaelia lyman-smithii

Pseudolaelia lyman-smithii, commonly known as the Lyman-Smith's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces intricate, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and moths.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
