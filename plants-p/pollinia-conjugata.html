Pollinia conjugata

Pollinia conjugata, commonly known as the paired pollinia orchid, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral structure, which includes two fused pollinia that are attached to a single viscid disc. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with intricate petal arrangements typical of the orchid family. Its reproductive strategy relies on specific pollinators, such as bees and wasps, which are attracted by its scent and color.

The botanical features of Pollinia conjugata make it an important subject in the study of orchid pollination mechanisms and plant adaptation.
