Pupalia subfusca

Pupalia subfusca, commonly known as the pale spurge or white spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pupalia subfusca is native to parts of Africa and is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
