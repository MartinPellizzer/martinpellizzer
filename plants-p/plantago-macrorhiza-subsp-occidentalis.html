Plantago macrorhiza subsp. occidentalis

Plantago macrorhiza subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America. It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces erect flowering stems with clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized by indigenous peoples for centuries.
