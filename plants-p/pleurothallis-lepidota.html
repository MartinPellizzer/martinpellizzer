Pleurothallis lepidota

Pleurothallis lepidota, commonly known as the velvet slipper orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble miniature slippers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are fragrant and come in shades of white and pale pink.

Pleurothallis lepidota is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
