Phyllopentas elata

Phyllopentas elata, commonly known as the spreading pentafoliate, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom throughout the dry season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing along riverbanks and in open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in warm climates.
