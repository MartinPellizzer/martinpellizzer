Parahebe derwentiana

Parahebe derwentiana, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Parahebe derwentiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
