Polemonium pulcherrimum f. candidum

Polemonium pulcherrimum f. candidum, commonly known as the white Jacob's ladder, is a cultivar of the Jacob's ladder plant.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, woodland environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The white variegation of its foliage makes it a striking addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
