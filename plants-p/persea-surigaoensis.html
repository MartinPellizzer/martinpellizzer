Persea surigaoensis

Persea surigaoensis, commonly known as the Surigao persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries that are orange-red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a fruit crop.
