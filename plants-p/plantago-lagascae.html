Plantago lagascae

Plantago lagascae, commonly known as the Armenian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
