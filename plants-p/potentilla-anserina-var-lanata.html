Potentilla anserina var. lanata

Potentilla anserina var. lanata, commonly known as the downy cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

It is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
