Phymatopsis incurvata

Phymatopsis incurvata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in desert or semi-desert environments. The plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions by accessing deep water sources. Its flowers are yellow and resemble those of the common dandelion, and it produces a capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

The species is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.
