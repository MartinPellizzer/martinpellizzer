Pectis texana

Pectis texana, commonly known as Texas pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in a cylindrical shape, often with bracts that resemble leaves. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Pectis texana is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
