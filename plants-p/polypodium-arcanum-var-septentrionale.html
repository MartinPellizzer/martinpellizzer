Polypodium arcanum var. septentrionale

Polypodium arcanum var. septentrionale, commonly known as the northern rock fern, is a rare and specialized fern species native to specific regions of North America.

It thrives in rocky, shaded environments, often growing on cliffs or in crevices where moisture is retained. The plant features a compact, evergreen frond with a distinctive, lance-shaped appearance. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
