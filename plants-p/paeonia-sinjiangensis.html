Paeonia sinjiangensis

Paeonia sinjiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid regions of Xinjiang, China.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces striking, double or semi-double flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, with deeply lobed petals. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Paeonia sinjiangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
