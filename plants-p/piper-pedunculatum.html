Piper pedunculatum

Piper pedunculatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of culinary spice.
