Phyllanthus hirtellus var. ledifolius

Phyllanthus hirtellus var. ledifolius, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy foliage. The leaves are typically opposite or whorled, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs. This plant often grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
