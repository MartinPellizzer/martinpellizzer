Polypodium brooksiae

Polypodium brooksiae, commonly known as the Brooks' shield fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than a foot in length. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

The fern's intricate morphology and ecological niche make it an important component of its native habitat.
