Phaseolus vulgaris var. praecox

Phaseolus vulgaris var. praecox, commonly known as early bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and early maturing traits, making it suitable for shorter growing seasons. The plant produces bushy foliage and bears small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and flowers that are typically white or pale purple.

This variety is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various climates.
