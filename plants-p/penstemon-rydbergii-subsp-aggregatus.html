Penstemon rydbergii subsp. aggregatus

Penstemon rydbergii subsp.

aggregatus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
