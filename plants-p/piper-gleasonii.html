Piper gleasonii

Piper gleasonii, commonly known as the Gleason's pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
