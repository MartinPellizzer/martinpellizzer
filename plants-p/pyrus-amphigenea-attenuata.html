Pyrus amphigenea attenuata

Pyrus amphigenea attenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East, and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or brown when mature. It prefers cool, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and orchards.
