Pleurothallis barbacenensis var. angustifolia

Pleurothallis barbacenensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are small, fragrant, and typically appear in clusters, with a delicate white or pale pink coloration. This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
