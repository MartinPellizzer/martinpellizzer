Panicum concinnum

Panicum concinnum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland vegetation and a subject of ecological study.
