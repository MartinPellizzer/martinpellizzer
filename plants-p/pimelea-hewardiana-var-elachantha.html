Pimelea hewardiana var. elachantha

Pimelea hewardiana var. elachantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or pink in color, which give it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

This species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
