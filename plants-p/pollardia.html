Pollardia

Pollardia, commonly known as the white lily, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Pollardia has lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
