Parietaria soleirolii

Parietaria soleirolii, commonly known as the rock pepper or wall pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and arranged in clusters, typically blooming from spring to early summer.

Parietaria soleirolii is often found growing on rocky soils and walls, thriving in sunny to partially shaded conditions.
