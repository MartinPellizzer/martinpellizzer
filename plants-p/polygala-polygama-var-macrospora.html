Polygala polygama var. macrospora

Polygala polygama var. macrospora, commonly known as the large-spored milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated pods that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts in arid regions.
