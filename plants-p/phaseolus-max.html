Phaseolus max

Phaseolus max, commonly known as the common bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its erect growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely cultivated for food. Phaseolus max is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

It thrives in temperate climates and is an important crop for both human consumption and animal feed.
