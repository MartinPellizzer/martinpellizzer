Pronephrium × pseudoliukiuense

Pronephrium × pseudoliukiuense, commonly known as the hybrid water fern, is a rare and intriguing plant that combines traits from its parent species, Pronephrium and Pseudoliukiuens.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically light green to bluish in color, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes, where it can anchor itself using its rhizomes. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are often found on the underside of its fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, Pronephrium × pseudoliukiuense is of interest to botanists studying aquatic plant evolution and hybridization.
