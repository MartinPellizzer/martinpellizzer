Polygonum cyanandrum

Polygonum cyanandrum, commonly known as blue false flax, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum cyanandrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
