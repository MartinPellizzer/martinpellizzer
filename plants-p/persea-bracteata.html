Persea bracteata

Persea bracteata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Persea bracteata is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value in tropical and subtropical regions.
