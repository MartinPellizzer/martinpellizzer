Paullinia sericea

Paullinia sericea, commonly known as the silky sarsaparilla, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used traditionally in folk medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
