Pyrus eburnea

Pyrus eburnea, commonly known as the white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, including regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and is edible.
