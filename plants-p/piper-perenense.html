Piper perenense

Piper perenense, commonly known as the evergreen pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper perenense is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.
