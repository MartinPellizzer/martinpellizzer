Polygonum asclepiadeum

Polygonum asclepiadeum, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or rocky habitats.

Polygonum asclepiadeum is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
