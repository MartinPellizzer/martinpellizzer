Ptilotus gomphrenoides var. conglomeratus

Ptilotus gomphrenoides var. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered blusher, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes. It produces vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.
