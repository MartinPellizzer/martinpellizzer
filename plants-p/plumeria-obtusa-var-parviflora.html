Plumeria obtusa var. parviflora

Plumeria obtusa var. parviflora, commonly known as the common frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and are used in floral arrangements and traditional ceremonies.
