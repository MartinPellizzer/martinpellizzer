Papaver litwinowii

Papaver litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting black or dark purple center. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The seeds of Papaver litwinowii are small and contain a high oil content, making them valuable for both ornamental and potential industrial uses.
