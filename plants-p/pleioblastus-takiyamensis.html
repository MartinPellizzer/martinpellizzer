Pleioblastus takiyamensis

Pleioblastus takiyamensis, commonly known as the Takiyamensis bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely textured leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, typical of many bamboo species.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its graceful appearance and hardiness.
