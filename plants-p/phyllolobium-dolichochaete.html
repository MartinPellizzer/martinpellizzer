Phyllolobium dolichochaete

Phyllolobium dolichochaete, commonly known as the long-hair phyllodinea, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions through its deep root system. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that reduce water loss, and its flowers are usually small and clustered. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
