Pelargonium acerinum

Pelargonium acerinum, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptive features, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
