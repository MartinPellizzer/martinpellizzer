Physalis chenopodiifolia var. glandulosa

Physalis chenopodiifolia var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are enclosed in a fleshy, inflated calyx. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central and South America.

This species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
