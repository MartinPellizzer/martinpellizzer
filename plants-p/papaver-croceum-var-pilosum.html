Papaver croceum var. pilosum

Papaver croceum var. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking yellow flowers with a dark red center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosum.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
