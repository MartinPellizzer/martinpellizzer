Podochilus bicolor

Podochilus bicolor, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive golden hue. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing in shaded forest areas.

Podochilus bicolor is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional medicine.
