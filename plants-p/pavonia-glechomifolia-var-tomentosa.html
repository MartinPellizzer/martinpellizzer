Pavonia glechomifolia var. tomentosa

Pavonia glechomifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
