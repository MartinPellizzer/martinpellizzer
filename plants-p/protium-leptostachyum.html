Protium leptostachyum

Protium leptostachyum, commonly known as the Philippine mahogany or white ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing smooth, black seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also a species of concern for conservation efforts.
