Periploca graeca var. vestita

Periploca graeca var. vestita, commonly known as the white periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a slightly waxy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
