Pseuderanthemum lanceophyllum

Pseuderanthemum lanceophyllum, commonly known as the lanceleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or vine, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
