Panicum issongense

Panicum issongense, commonly known as the Issonge grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically in shades of brown or pale yellow. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum issongense is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
