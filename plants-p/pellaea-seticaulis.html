Pellaea seticaulis

Pellaea seticaulis, commonly known as the hooked spleenwort, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors, where it can thrive in low light conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable plant in various habitats.
