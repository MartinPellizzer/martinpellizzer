Phanera bidentata var. kingii

Phanera bidentata var. kingii, commonly known as the King's phanera, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is adapted to dry conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
