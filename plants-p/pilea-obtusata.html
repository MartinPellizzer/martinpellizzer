Pilea obtusata

Pilea obtusata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often hidden beneath the leaves, and are usually green or white in color.

Pilea obtusata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
