Pityrodia puberula

Pityrodia puberula, commonly known as the hairy pittosporum, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Pityrodia puberula is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
