Phyllanthus cyrtophyllus

Phyllanthus cyrtophyllus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hanging clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Phyllanthus cyrtophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
