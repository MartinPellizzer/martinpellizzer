Primula officinalis var. longistyla

Primula officinalis var. longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern and eastern Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, with the notable elongated stamen that gives it its botanical name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
