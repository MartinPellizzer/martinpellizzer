Panicum megiston

Panicum megiston, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicles, which are open, branched clusters of flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
