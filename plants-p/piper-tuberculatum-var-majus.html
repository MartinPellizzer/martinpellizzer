Piper tuberculatum var. majus

Piper tuberculatum var. majus, commonly known as the giant wakerobin, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, attracting various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in suitable climates.
