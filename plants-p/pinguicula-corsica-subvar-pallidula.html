Pinguicula corsica subvar. pallidula

Pinguicula corsica subvar. pallidula, commonly known as the Corsican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor habitats such as bogs and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually small and yellow, arranged in a single stem, and bloom in late spring or early summer.

The subvariety pallidula is distinguished by its pale green leaves and reduced floral structures compared to the typical form.
