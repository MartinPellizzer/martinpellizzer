Porana megathyrsa

Porana megathyrsa, commonly known as the giant porana, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, showcasing a vibrant green color with a waxy texture. The plant produces impressive, clustered inflorescences bearing yellow-pink flowers, which attract various pollinators. Its robust trunk and slow growth rate make it a long-lived species, often found in humid, lowland environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections worldwide.
