Phyllanthus touranensis

Phyllanthus touranensis, commonly known as the Touranensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, pale green fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Phyllanthus touranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
