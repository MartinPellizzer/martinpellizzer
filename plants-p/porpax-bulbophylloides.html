Porpax bulbophylloides

Porpax bulbophylloides, commonly known as the bulbophyll orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
