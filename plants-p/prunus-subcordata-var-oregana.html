Prunus subcordata var. oregana

Prunus subcordata var. oregana, commonly known as the Oregon serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall, and its clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color, appearing in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
