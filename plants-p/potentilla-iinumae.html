Potentilla iinumae

Potentilla iinumae, commonly known as the Japanese potentilla, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
