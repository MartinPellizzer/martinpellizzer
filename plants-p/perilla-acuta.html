Perilla acuta

Perilla acuta, commonly known as blunt perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Perilla acuta is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which have a mild, slightly spicy flavor.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
