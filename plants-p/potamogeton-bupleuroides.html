Potamogeton bupleuroides

Potamogeton bupleuroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, often with a slightly pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This species is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
