Platanus algeriensis

Platanus algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Platanaceae family and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Platanus algeriensis is often used in urban landscaping due to its tolerance of pollution and ability to grow in urban environments.
