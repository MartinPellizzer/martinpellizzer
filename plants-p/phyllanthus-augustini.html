Phyllanthus augustini

Phyllanthus augustini, commonly known as African date tree or wild date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe.
