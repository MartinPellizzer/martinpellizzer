Phyllanthus rubens

Phyllanthus rubens, commonly known as the red phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a reddish tint on the underside.

Phyllanthus rubens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
