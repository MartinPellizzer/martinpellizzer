Parodia pluricentralis var. xanthoflora

Parodia pluricentralis var. xanthoflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered parodia, is a cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to grayish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which bloom in clusters at the stem tips. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
