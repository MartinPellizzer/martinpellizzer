Phlox austromontana subsp. prostrata

Phlox austromontana subsp.

prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically forms dense mats of foliage with dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to lavender, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland edges.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover with seasonal color.
