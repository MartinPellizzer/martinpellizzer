Podocarpus distichus

Podocarpus distichus, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp or the common podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It has a dense, compact crown with short, stiff, dark green needles that are arranged in two vertical rows. The tree produces small, yellowish-green cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.

Podocarpus distichus is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
