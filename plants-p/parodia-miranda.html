Parodia miranda

Parodia miranda, commonly known as the Miranda pincushion cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and is covered in short, spiny bristles. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
