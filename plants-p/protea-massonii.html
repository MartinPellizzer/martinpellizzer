Protea massonii

Protea massonii, commonly known as the Masson's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowering period typically occurs from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
