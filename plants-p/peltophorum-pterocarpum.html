Peltophorum pterocarpum

Peltophorum pterocarpum, commonly known as the Indian peacock tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive peacock-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree has bipinnate leaves and produces woody pods containing flat, seed-rich fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
