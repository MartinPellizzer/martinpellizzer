Pygeum ovalifolium

Pygeum ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaved African chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are arranged in panicles, followed by woody, spiny pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
