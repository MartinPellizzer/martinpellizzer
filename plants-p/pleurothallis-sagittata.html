Pleurothallis sagittata

Pleurothallis sagittata, commonly known as the arrow-shaped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, arrow-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized for its unique floral structure and ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
