Proboscidea fallax

Proboscidea fallax, commonly known as the false arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a single flower, though it is actually composed of many small, clustered blooms. The plant features a distinctive spathe, a modified leaf that encloses the spadix, which contains the true flowers. Proboscidea fallax thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in moist, shaded areas near streams or in dense undergrowth.

Its botanical structure makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of interest for its unique reproductive biology.
