Paronychia argentea var. rotundata

Paronychia argentea var. rotundata, commonly known as silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a dense mat of silver-gray, fleshy leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its silver foliage makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
