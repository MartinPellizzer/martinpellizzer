Paspalum longifolium

Paspalum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are usually clustered in loose panicles.

Paspalum longifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
