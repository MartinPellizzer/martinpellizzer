Pseudopilocereus flavipulvinatus

Pseudopilocereus flavipulvinatus, commonly known as the yellow pulvinated cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and yellow spines. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
