Prunus spinosa f. rupestris

Prunus spinosa f. rupestris, commonly known as the rock cherry or stone cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the common blackthorn.

It features small, dark purple to black berries that mature in late summer. The plant is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are pale green with a slight pinkish tinge.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
