Potentilla pilosa var. viscidula

Potentilla pilosa var. viscidula, commonly known as sticky potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in dense, glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

It is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species suited to arid or semi-arid environments.
