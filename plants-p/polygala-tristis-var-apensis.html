Polygala tristis var. apensis

Polygala tristis var. apensis, commonly known as the Apennine milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

This variety is noted for its low-growing habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
