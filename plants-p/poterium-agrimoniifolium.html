Poterium agrimoniifolium

Poterium agrimoniifolium, commonly known as the agrimony leaf poterium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caprifoliaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, opposite leaves that are often serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its historical use in herbal medicine, it is considered a valuable plant in traditional botanical practices.
