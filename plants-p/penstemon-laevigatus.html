Penstemon laevigatus

Penstemon laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
