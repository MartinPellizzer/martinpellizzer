Pouzolzia microphylla

Pouzolzia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved pouzolzia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
