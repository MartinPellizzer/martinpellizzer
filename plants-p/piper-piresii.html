Piper piresii

Piper piresii, commonly known as the Piresii pepper or Piresii long pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, peppercorn-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic fruits.
