Pogostemon szemaoensis

Pogostemon szemaoensis, commonly known as the Sichuan betel plant, is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

Native to China, it is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, tubular flowers in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its traditional use in herbal medicine and its role in local ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential pharmacological properties.
