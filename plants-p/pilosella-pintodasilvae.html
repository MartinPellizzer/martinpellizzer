Pilosella pintodasilvae

Pilosella pintodasilvae, commonly known as the Pintos' silvery weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has basal leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Pilosella pintodasilvae is often found in disturbed soils and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
