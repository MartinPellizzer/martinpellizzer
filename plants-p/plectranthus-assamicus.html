Plectranthus assamicus

Plectranthus assamicus, commonly known as the Indian balm, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Plectranthus assamicus thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
