Pyrus oblonga

Pyrus oblonga, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces round, yellow to green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

Pyrus oblonga prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
