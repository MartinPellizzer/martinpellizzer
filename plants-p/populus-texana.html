Populus texana

Populus texana, commonly known as Texas poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and open woodlands.

Populus texana is valued for its fast growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
