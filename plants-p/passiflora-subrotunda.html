Passiflora subrotunda

Passiflora subrotunda, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, waxy texture.

Passiflora subrotunda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
