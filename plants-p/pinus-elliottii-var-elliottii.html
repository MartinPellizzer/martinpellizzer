Pinus elliottii var. elliottii

Pinus elliottii var. elliottii, commonly known as the slash pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that grow in bundles of two, and its cones are typically reddish-brown with a distinctive shape. The tree thrives in wetland and coastal areas, often forming dense stands in swampy habitats. It produces a valuable timber and is also important for wildlife habitat and erosion control.

The species is well adapted to humid climates and can tolerate seasonal flooding.
