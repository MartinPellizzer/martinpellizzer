Panicum roseum f. hirtum

Panicum roseum f. hirtum, commonly known as the hairy pink panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and its distinctive pinkish-red inflorescence that resembles a panicle. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it its specific epithet "hirtum.". This ornamental grass is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
