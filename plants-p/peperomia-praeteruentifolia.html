Peperomia praeteruentifolia

Peperomia praeteruentifolia, commonly known as the veinal peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central and South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with prominent, parallel veins that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale green, typically emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.

Peperomia praeteruentifolia is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance, making it an ideal plant for beginners.
