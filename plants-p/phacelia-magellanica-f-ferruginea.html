Phacelia magellanica f. ferruginea

Phacelia magellanica f. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty phacelia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small blue to purple flowers. This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ecological benefits.
