Piptolepis ericoides

Piptolepis ericoides, commonly known as the heathwort, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant has compact, dense foliage with small, leathery leaves that are usually green or reddish in color. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are often white or pink, blooming in the spring.

Piptolepis ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
