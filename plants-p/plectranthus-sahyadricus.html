Plectranthus sahyadricus

Plectranthus sahyadricus, commonly known as the Kerala plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.

Plectranthus sahyadricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
