Polygonon

Polygonon, commonly known as the common polygonum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It includes both annual and perennial species, often characterized by their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, usually pink or red flowers. Many species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in wetlands, meadows, or disturbed soils. Some species are cultivated for their ornamental value, while others are considered weeds.

The genus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
