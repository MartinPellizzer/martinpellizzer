Pyrus communis var. latifrons

Pyrus communis var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear, is a variant of the common pear species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically oval, with a smooth skin and a sweet, juicy pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
