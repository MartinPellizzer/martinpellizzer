Potentilla × nebulosa

Potentilla × nebulosa, commonly known as clouded potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
