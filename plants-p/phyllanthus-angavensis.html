Phyllanthus angavensis

Phyllanthus angavensis, commonly known as the Angava plant, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Phyllanthus angavensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
