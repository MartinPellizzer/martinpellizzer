Phlox pilosa var. typica

Phlox pilosa var. typica, commonly known as the hairy phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall textured foliage.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
