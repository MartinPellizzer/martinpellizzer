Proustia domingensis

Proustia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican proustia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow with a central disk, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
