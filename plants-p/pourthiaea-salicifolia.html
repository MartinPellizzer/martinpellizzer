Pourthiaea salicifolia

Pourthiaea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaved pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
