Pseudolobivia wilkeae

Pseudolobivia wilkeae, commonly known as the Wilke's pseudolobivia, is a rare and unique cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh desert environment.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered an endangered species.
