Plantago hirtella var. denticulata

Plantago hirtella var. denticulata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
