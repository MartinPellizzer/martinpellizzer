Ptychocarpus

Ptychocarpus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of fleshy, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. Ptychocarpus species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soils. They are popular as houseplants due to their compact size and ability to bloom year-round under proper care.

The genus includes several species, such as Ptychocarpus eugeniifolius, which is prized for its ornamental value.
