Pilea unciformis

Pilea unciformis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It features round, glossy, succulent leaves that resemble small coins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in a compact rosette form, with leaves that are thick and fleshy to store water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually greenish-white in color.

Pilea unciformis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.
