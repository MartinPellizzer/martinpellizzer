Phaenosperma

Phaenosperma, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Acanthaceae.

These plants are characterized by their unique, often pale or translucent flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence their common name. Phaenosperma species are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. They produce small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The genus is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking floral displays.
