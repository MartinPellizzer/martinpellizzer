Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. obtusus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. obtusus, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, cylindrical stems that often form dense underwater stands, and its leaves are broad, thick, and pointed at the tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable ecological conditions.
