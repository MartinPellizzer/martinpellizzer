Passiflora warei

Passiflora warei, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora warei is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
