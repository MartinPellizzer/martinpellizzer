Phyllanthus eutaxioides

Phyllanthus eutaxioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
