Papaver hyoscyamifolium

Papaver hyoscyamifolium, commonly known as the Turkish poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It features striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive black center. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it both a visually appealing and ecologically valuable species in its native habitat.
