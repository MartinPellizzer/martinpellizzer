Peucedanum ovinum

Peucedanum ovinum, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, ribbed fruits that contain tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Peucedanum ovinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
