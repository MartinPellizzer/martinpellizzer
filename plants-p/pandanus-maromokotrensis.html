Pandanus maromokotrensis

Pandanus maromokotrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar pandanus, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many pandanus species. Its leaves are often used in local crafts and traditional medicine, highlighting its cultural and economic significance.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to humid and saline conditions.
