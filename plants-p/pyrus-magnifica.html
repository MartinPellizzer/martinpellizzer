Pyrus magnifica

Pyrus magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent pear, is a deciduous tree native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The tree produces pome fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, though they are not typically cultivated for commercial use. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and the tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters.
