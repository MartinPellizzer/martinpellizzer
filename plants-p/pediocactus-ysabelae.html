Pediocactus ysabelae

Pediocactus ysabelae, commonly known as the Ysabel's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
