Pentas magnifica

Pentas magnifica, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Pentas magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
