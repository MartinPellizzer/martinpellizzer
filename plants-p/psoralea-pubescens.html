Psoralea pubescens

Psoralea pubescens, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has small, compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea pubescens is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
