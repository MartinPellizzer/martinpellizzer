Paronychia arabica subsp. arabica

Paronychia arabica subsp.

arabica, commonly known as the Arabian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula. It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, often found in coastal and desert environments.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh conditions.
