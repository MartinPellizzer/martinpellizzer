Pseudodichanthium

Pseudodichanthium, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. They are characterized by their slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

Pseudodichanthium species are important for soil stabilization and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
