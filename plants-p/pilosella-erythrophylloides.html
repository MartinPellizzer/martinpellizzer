Pilosella erythrophylloides

Pilosella erythrophylloides, commonly known as the red-leaved pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are often tinged with red, giving it its common name. It is native to regions of Europe and is typically found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Pilosella erythrophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
