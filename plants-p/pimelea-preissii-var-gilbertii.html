Pimelea preissii var. gilbertii

Pimelea preissii var. gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in coastal and sandy soils.
