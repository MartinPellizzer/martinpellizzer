Pyrus pyraster

Pyrus pyraster, commonly known as the common medlar, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen in the fall. The fruit has a unique, mealy texture and is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in hedgerows.
