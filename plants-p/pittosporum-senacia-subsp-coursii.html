Pittosporum senacia subsp. coursii

Pittosporum senacia subsp.

coursii, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in a variety of habitats, including coastal areas and forests.
