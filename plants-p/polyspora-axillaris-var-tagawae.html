Polyspora axillaris var. tagawae

Polyspora axillaris var. tagawae, commonly known as the Tagawa's polypody, is a fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lobed fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The varietal form, tagawae, is distinguished by specific morphological features in its fronds and spore structures.
