Phyllostachys pubescens var. biconvexa

Phyllostachys pubescens var. biconvexa, commonly known as the Biconvex bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, smooth, and often silver-colored culms that exhibit a unique biconvex shape, meaning they are curved outward on both sides. The plant grows in dense clumps and is well-suited for ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, and it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This bamboo variety is also valued for its ability to provide privacy and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
