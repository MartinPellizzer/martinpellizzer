Physalis macrosperma

Physalis macrosperma, commonly known as the giant Cape gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its large, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its fruit, which is a yellow-orange berry, is edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
