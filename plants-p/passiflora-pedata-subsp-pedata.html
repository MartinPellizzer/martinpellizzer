Passiflora pedata subsp. pedata

Passiflora pedata subsp.

pedata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens.

The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture and a distinctive scent.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates.
