Pleurothallis richteri

Pleurothallis richteri, commonly known as the Richter's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native environment.
