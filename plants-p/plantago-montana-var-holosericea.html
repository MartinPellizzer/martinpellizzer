Plantago montana var. holosericea

Plantago montana var. holosericea, commonly known as the Sierra Plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, silver-white hairs. The plant produces upright spikes of small, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves and seeds have been used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
