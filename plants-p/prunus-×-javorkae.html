Prunus × javorkae

Prunus × javorkae, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Prunus mandshurica and Prunus serrulata.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and is native to Japan and Korea.
