Parodia spanisa

Parodia spanisa, commonly known as the Spanish cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper part of the stem. Parodia spanisa is a succulent plant, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
