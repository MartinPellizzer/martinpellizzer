Poinsettia pulcherrima var. major

Poinsettia pulcherrima var. major, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a tropical shrub native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that serve as the plant's showy floral display. The true flowers are small, yellowish cyathia surrounded by colorful leaves. This plant thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

It is widely cultivated as a holiday plant due to its striking appearance and seasonal availability.
