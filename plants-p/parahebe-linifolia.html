Parahebe linifolia

Parahebe linifolia, commonly known as the silver parahebe, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, dark green foliage that is covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting foliage.
