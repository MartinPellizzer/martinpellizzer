Pothuava alopecurus

Pothuava alopecurus, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It features small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Its leaves and flowers are edible and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
