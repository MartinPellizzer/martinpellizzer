Piptocarpha axillaris subsp. graciellae

Piptocarpha axillaris subsp.

graciellae, commonly known as the slender leaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, hairy stems, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence, aiding in water conservation in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
