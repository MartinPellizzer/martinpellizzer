Potentilla recta proles sulphurea

Potentilla recta proles sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and serrated leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves and flowers contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
