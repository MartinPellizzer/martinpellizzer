Panax gomphophyllus

Panax gomphophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy roots, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small greenish-white flowers. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a short, woody stem.

The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.
