Potentilla velutina

Potentilla velutina, commonly known as the velvet cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Potentilla velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
