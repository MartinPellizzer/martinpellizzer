Phellandrium tabernaemontani

Phellandrium tabernaemontani, commonly known as the Narrow-leafed Honey Myrtle, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
