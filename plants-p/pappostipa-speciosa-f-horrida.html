Pappostipa speciosa f. horrida

Pappostipa speciosa f. horrida, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a variant of the false brome grass.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to brown flowers in late summer.

This form is notable for its distinctive appearance and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
