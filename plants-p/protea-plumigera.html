Protea plumigera

Protea plumigera, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

Protea plumigera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
