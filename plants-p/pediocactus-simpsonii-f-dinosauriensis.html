Pediocactus simpsonii f. dinosauriensis

Pediocactus simpsonii f. dinosauriensis, commonly known as the dinosaurian cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, thick stems and prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its fragile nature and habitat specificity, it is often found in protected environments or cultivated in specialized gardens.
