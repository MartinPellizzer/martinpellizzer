Pleioblastus nakashimae

Pleioblastus nakashimae, commonly known as the Nakashima's bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, where it thrives in temperate climates. This bamboo is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, linear leaves that often have a silvery-blue or grayish-green hue. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.

Pleioblastus nakashimae is also valued for its use in traditional Japanese gardens and as a decorative plant in landscaping.
