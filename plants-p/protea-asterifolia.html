Protea asterifolia

Protea asterifolia, commonly known as the starry protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble star-like clusters, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure and vibrant colors.

Protea asterifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
