Pastinaca rectistylis

Pastinaca rectistylis, commonly known as the straight-styled scorzonera, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its thick, tuberous root system. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves with a rough texture and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and daisy-like, typically blooming in late summer.

Pastinaca rectistylis is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
