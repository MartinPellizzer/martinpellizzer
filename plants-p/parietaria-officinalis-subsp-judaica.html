Parietaria officinalis subsp. judaica

Parietaria officinalis subsp.

judaica, commonly known as Jewish nettle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is often found growing in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in urban areas. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

However, it is considered a weed in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
