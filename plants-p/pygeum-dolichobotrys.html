Pygeum dolichobotrys

Pygeum dolichobotrys, commonly known as the long-stalked African privet, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, spherical fruits. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
