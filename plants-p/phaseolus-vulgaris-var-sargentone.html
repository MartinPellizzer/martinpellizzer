Phaseolus vulgaris var. sargentone

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sargentone, commonly known as the Sargent bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves, typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content.
