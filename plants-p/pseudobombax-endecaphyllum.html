Pseudobombax endecaphyllum

Pseudobombax endecaphyllum, commonly known as the pink star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces striking, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a fleshy, star-shaped berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
