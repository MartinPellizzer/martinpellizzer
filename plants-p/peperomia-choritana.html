Peperomia choritana

Peperomia choritana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for novice plant enthusiasts.
