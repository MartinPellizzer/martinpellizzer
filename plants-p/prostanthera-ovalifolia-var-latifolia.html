Prostanthera ovalifolia var. latifolia

Prostanthera ovalifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mint bush, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, oval-shaped leaves that are broader than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and heathlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Indigenous medicine.
