Pseudo-fumaria alba

Pseudo-fumaria alba, commonly known as the white fumaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseudo-fumaria alba is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
