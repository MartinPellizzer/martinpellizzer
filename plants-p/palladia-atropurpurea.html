Palladia atropurpurea

Palladia atropurpurea, commonly known as the purplestem coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stem that turns a deep purple hue as it matures. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Palladia atropurpurea is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
