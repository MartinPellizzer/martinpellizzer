Papaver rogersii

Papaver rogersii, commonly known as Rogers' poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
