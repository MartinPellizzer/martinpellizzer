Paralbizzia platycarpa

Paralbizzia platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited paralbizzia, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its fruit, a flattened pod, contains several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in its native habitat.
