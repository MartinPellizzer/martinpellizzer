Phlox roemeriana

Phlox roemeriana, commonly known as Roemer's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and vibrant pink to purple flower clusters. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
