Phyllanthus niruri var. baronianus

Phyllanthus niruri var. baronianus, commonly known as the stonebreaker or mouse ear, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, elliptical seeds.

This species is notable for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
