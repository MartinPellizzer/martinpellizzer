Polygonum pallidiflorum

Polygonum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color. Its small, pale pink to white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Polygonum pallidiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
