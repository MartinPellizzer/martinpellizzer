Pseudagrostistachys ugandensis

Pseudagrostistachys ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda grass, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
