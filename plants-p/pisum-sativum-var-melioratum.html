Pisum sativum var. melioratum

Pisum sativum var. melioratum, commonly known as the edible pea, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its pod-like structures that enclose the edible seeds, which are typically green and round. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually blue or violet, and it is cultivated for both its seeds and pods.

The botanical features of this variety make it well-suited for agricultural use and home gardening.
