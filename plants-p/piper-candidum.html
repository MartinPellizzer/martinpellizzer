Piper candidum

Piper candidum, commonly known as white pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to black pepper. The plant produces small, white berries that are harvested and dried to make white pepper. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
