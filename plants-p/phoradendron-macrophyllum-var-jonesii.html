Phoradendron macrophyllum var. jonesii

Phoradendron macrophyllum var. jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It attaches to the branches of host trees, such as oaks and hickories, using a specialized root system called a haustellum to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects while also influencing the growth of its host trees.
