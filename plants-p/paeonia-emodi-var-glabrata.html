Paeonia emodi var. glabrata

Paeonia emodi var. glabrata, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a variant of the Himalayan peony native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant is a herbaceous perennial with thick, fleshy rhizomes that store nutrients for growth. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated edges, and it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with adequate sunlight and protection from extreme temperatures.
