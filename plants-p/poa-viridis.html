Poa viridis

Poa viridis, commonly known as green moss grass, is a species of grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically forms dense mats on moist, shady soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in woodland floors and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in shaded conditions and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.
