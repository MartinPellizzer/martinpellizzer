Plantago barbata subvar. rigidula

Plantago barbata subvar. rigidula, commonly known as rigid-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, rigid stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
