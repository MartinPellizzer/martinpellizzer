Paronychia velata var. subvelata

Paronychia velata var. subvelata, commonly known as the velvet leaf carnation, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Carnation family (Caryophyllaceae) and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
