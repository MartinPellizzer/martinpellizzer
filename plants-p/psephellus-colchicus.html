Psephellus colchicus

Psephellus colchicus, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It features slender, upright stems and produces striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Psephellus colchicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
