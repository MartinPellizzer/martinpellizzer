Polypompholyx laciniata var. rubrocalcarata

Polypompholyx laciniata var. rubrocalcarata, commonly known as the red-spurred polypompholyx, is a striking member of the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely lobed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers with prominent red stipules that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique floral features.
