Pladera perfoliata

Pladera perfoliata, commonly known as the perforate platter, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves with distinctive perforations along the margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its unique leaf structure, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
