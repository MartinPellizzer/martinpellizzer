Pulsatilla vernalis var. ajanensis

Pulsatilla vernalis var. ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajanensis Pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a short, upright stem with few leaves, typically arranged in a rosette at the base. Its flowers have five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals, and it produces a single, rounded, greenish-brown achene with a tuft of white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.
