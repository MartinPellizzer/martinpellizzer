Palala bivalvis

Palala bivalvis, commonly known as the spiral bivalve, is a unique marine mollusk characterized by its distinctive spiral shell.

It belongs to the family Pinnotheridae and is typically found in tropical and subtropical waters. The shell is often elongated and has a complex, coiled structure that provides protection. The plant-like appearance of its shell has led to its classification in the bivalve family, despite not being a true plant.

Its habitat and ecological role are still subjects of ongoing research in marine biology.
