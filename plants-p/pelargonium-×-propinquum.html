Pelargonium × propinquum

Pelargonium × propinquum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated as a bedding plant due to its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
