Prunus mitjurinii

Prunus mitjurinii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, often preceding the foliage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Prunus mitjurinii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators during the spring season.
