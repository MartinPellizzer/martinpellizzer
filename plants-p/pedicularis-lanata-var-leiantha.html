Pedicularis lanata var. leiantha

Pedicularis lanata var. leiantha, commonly known as the woolly monkeyflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
