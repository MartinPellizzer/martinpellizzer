Piper fulgidum

Piper fulgidum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small pepper, turns a striking purple color when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
