Piper perverrucosum

Piper perverrucosum, commonly known as the rough-hairy pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
