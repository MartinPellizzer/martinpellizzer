Pyrus herefordensis

Pyrus herefordensis, commonly known as Hereford pear, is a deciduous tree native to the UK.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that are typically small and sweet. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its fruit.

The species is relatively rare and is considered a valuable part of the UK's botanical heritage.
