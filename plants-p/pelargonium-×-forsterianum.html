Pelargonium × forsterianum

Pelargonium × forsterianum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species of the geranium genus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × forsterianum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
