Pseudonoseris discolor

Pseudonoseris discolor, commonly known as the variable dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and heart-shaped, toothed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, enclosed in a fluffy pappus, allowing for wide distribution.

The species is known for its variability in leaf shape and flower color, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
