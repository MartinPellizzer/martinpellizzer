Primula calycina

Primula calycina, commonly known as the rock primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers a cool, humid climate. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Primula calycina is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in early spring.
