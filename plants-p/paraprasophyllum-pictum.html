Paraprasophyllum pictum

Paraprasophyllum pictum, commonly known as the painted paraprasophyllum, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ornate leaf patterns that resemble intricate illustrations, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is known for its delicate, grass-like appearance with narrow, ribbed leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous forests.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
