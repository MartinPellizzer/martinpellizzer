Pouteria sebertii

Pouteria sebertii, commonly known as the Natal apple or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often used in local cuisines.

It also has a high nutritional value, containing significant amounts of vitamin C and other essential nutrients.
