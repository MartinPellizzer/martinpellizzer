Ptychosema trifoliolatum

Ptychosema trifoliolatum, commonly known as the three-leaf ptychosema, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
