Piper flavicans var. flavicans

Piper flavicans var. flavicans, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. These berries are typically harvested when mature, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
