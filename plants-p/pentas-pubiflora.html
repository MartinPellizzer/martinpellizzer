Pentas pubiflora

Pentas pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically pink, red, or white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pentas pubiflora is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
