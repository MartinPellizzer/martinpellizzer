Pariti gangeticum

Pariti gangeticum, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pink, and it blooms during the monsoon season.

Pariti gangeticum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
