Pyrethrum multifidum

Pyrethrum multifidum, commonly known as the multifidum pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that contain numerous fine, hair-like structures called phylloides. Pyrethrum multifidum is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Additionally, it is sometimes used in the production of natural insecticides due to the presence of pyrethrins in its flowers.
