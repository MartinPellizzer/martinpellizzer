Pachidendron × principis

Pachidendron × principis, commonly known as the hybrid pachidendron, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

Pachidendron × principis plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes and provides habitat for small desert animals.
