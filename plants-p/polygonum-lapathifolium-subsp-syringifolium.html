Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. syringifolium

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

syringifolium, commonly known as the purpleflower knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
