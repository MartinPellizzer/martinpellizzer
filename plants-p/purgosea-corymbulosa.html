Purgosea corymbulosa

Purgosea corymbulosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The botanical features of Purgosea corymbulosa make it an attractive addition to native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
