Plumeria angustiflora

Plumeria angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are used in cultural and ceremonial contexts in various regions.
