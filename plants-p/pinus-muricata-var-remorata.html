Pinus muricata var. remorata

Pinus muricata var. remorata, commonly known as the coastal red pine, is a variant of the coastal red pine native to the western coast of North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its reddish-barked trunk and needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits a distinct morphology adapted to coastal environments, including salt tolerance and resistance to wind. Its cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
