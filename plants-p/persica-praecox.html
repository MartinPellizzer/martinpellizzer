Persica praecox

Persica praecox, commonly known as the early peach.

It is a species of the Prunus genus, characterized by its deciduous nature and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and produces small, sweet fruits that ripen early in the growing season. Its botanical features include simple leaves and a branching structure that supports its fruiting habit.

Persica praecox is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible yield.
