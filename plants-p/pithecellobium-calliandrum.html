Pithecellobium calliandrum

Pithecellobium calliandrum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow, pendant flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
