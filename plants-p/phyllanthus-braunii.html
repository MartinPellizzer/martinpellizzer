Phyllanthus braunii

Phyllanthus braunii, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble beans, which are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture and is often found in moist, shaded environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable plant.
