Prunus spinosa var. erythrocalyx

Prunus spinosa var. erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red-flowered blackthorn, is a variant of the common blackthorn, characterized by its striking red or pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically growing in hedgerows and woodland edges. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its flowers appear in early spring, preceding the leaves, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is grey and fissured.
