Potentilla explanata

Potentilla explanata, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and pinnately compound leaves that are typically broad and lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Potentilla explanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
