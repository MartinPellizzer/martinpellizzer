Primula chionota

Primula chionota, commonly known as the snow primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with clusters of showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Primula chionota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
