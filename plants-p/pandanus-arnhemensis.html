Pandanus arnhemensis

Pandanus arnhemensis, commonly known as the Northern Territory pandanus, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a dense rosette, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used by Indigenous communities for weaving and other traditional crafts.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, showing resilience to salt spray and wind.
