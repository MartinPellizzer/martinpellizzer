Potamogeton nodosus f. latifolius

Potamogeton nodosus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are broader than those of the typical species, often reaching up to 2 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various organisms.
