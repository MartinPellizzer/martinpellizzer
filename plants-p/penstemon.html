Penstemon

Penstemon, commonly known as beardtongue, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Plantaginaceae.

These plants are native to North America and are known for their showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, purple, and yellow. Penstemon species are typically perennial, with some varieties thriving in rocky or arid environments. They are popular in gardens for their drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

The leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to their distinctive appearance.
