Procris latifolia

Procris latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf procris, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Malvaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, pentagonal flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or white. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
