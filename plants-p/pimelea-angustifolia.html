Pimelea angustifolia

Pimelea angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf riceflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, simple leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is commonly found in coastal and sandy environments, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
