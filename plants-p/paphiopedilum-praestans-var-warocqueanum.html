Paphiopedilum praestans var. warocqueanum

Paphiopedilum praestans var. warocqueanum, commonly known as the Warocqueanum orchid, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendulous flowers that often display a vibrant pink or purple coloration. The plant typically grows as a lithophyte, anchoring itself to rock surfaces in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its native range.

This variety is highly valued by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and rarity in cultivation.
