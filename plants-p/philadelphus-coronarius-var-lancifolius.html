Philadelphus coronarius var. lancifolius

Philadelphus coronarius var. lancifolius, commonly known as the lanceleaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its showy blossoms and pleasant scent.
