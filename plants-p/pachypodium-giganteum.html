Pachypodium giganteum

Pachypodium giganteum, commonly known as the giant pachypodium or elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, columnar stem and a dense cluster of fleshy, spiny leaves at the base. The plant stores water in its swollen stem, allowing it to survive in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the evening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xerophytic gardens.
