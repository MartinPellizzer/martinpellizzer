Podocarpus spathoides

Podocarpus spathoides, commonly known as the white-leaf podocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
