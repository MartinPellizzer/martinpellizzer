Polylepis ochreata

Polylepis ochreata, commonly known as the yellow polylepis, is a species of tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and lanceolate leaves. This tree thrives in high-altitude environments, typically found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Polylepis ochreata plays a vital role in its ecosystem, providing habitat for various endemic species.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it an important component of the region's biodiversity.
