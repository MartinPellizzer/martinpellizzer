Pararistolochia meridionaliana

Pararistolochia meridionaliana, commonly known as the southern arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hooded flowers that resemble a pipe or a bird's beak. The plant's leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture. It produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
