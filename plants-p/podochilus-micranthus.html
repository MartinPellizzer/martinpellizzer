Podochilus micranthus

Podochilus micranthus, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and often clustered at the base, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
