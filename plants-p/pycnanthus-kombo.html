Pycnanthus kombo

Pycnanthus kombo, commonly known as the African starfruit or African yellowwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
