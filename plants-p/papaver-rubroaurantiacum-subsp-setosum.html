Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. setosum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

setosum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family. It is characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers with a distinctive yellow center.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Native to parts of Central Asia, it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
