Polycarpon loeflingiae

Polycarpon loeflingiae, commonly known as the Loefling's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
