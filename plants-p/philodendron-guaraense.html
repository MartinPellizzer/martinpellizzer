Philodendron guaraense

Philodendron guaraense, commonly known as the Guaraense philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its growth habit involves twining around supports, making it suitable for vertical gardening.

It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.
