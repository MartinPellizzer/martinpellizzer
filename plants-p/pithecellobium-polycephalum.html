Pithecellobium polycephalum

Pithecellobium polycephalum, commonly known as the hairy pod podocarp, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruits are elongated pods that mature into a dark brown color, containing several seeds.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
