Piofontia violacea

Piofontia violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often tinged with a reddish hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Piofontia violacea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
