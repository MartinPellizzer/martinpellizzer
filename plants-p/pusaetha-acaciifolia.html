Pusaetha acaciifolia

Pusaetha acaciifolia, commonly known as the acacia-leaved daisies, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically has narrow, feather-like leaves that resemble those of acacia trees, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are often clustered in spherical or disc-like formations.

Pusaetha acaciifolia is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
