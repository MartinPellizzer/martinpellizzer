Phytoxis sideritifolia

Phytoxis sideritifolia, commonly known as the ironstone fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its intricate frond structure and adaptive features.

It typically thrives in nutrient-poor, calcareous soils, showcasing its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions. The plant's leaves are divided into delicate, feathery segments, which aid in maximizing light absorption in its often shaded habitat. Its root system is specialized for extracting essential minerals from rocky substrates, contributing to its survival in such environments.

This species plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
