Pithecellobium clypearia var. velutinum

Pithecellobium clypearia var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
