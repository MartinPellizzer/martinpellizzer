Pescatoria violacea

Pescatoria violacea, commonly known as the violet sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are adapted to salt spray and sandy soils. It thrives in moist, well-drained environments and is often found near the shoreline.

Pescatoria violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting coastal biodiversity.
