Pandanus mollifoliaceus

Pandanus mollifoliaceus, commonly known as the soft-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, smooth, and often waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, which eventually develop into edible fruits. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in various cultures.
