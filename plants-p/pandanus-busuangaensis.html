Pandanus busuangaensis

Pandanus busuangaensis, commonly known as the Busuanga pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in local crafts and traditional practices. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which are borne on long, branching stalks. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
