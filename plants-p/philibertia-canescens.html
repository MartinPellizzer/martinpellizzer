Philibertia canescens

Philibertia canescens, commonly known as the woolly filbert, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Philibertia canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
