Pulicaria somalensis subsp. somalensis

Pulicaria somalensis subsp.

somalensis, commonly known as the Somali pulicaria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall. Its leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, while the flowers are small and yellowish.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest for its potential in arid land restoration.
