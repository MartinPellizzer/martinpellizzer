Potentilla kingii

Potentilla kingii, commonly known as King's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Potentilla kingii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
