Pelargonium terebinthinaceum

Pelargonium terebinthinaceum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
