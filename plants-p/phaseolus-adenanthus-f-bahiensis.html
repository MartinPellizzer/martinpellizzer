Phaseolus adenanthus f. bahiensis

Phaseolus adenanthus f. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped seeds and hairy, compound leaves. This plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, tropical climates. It is typically grown as an annual crop and is valued for its nutritional content.

The plant's botanical features make it suitable for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
