Potentilla sylvestris subvar. hirsuta

Potentilla sylvestris subvar. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This subshrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscaping.
