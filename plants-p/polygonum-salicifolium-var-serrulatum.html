Polygonum salicifolium var. serrulatum

Polygonum salicifolium var. serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated willow-leaf polygonum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, commonly referred to as the buckwheat family. The plant is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as stream sides and wet meadows.
