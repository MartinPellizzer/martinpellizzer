Polygala rhynchosperma

Polygala rhynchosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight pubescence, while the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
