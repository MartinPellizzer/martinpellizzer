Phaseolus debouckii

Phaseolus debouckii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian runner bean, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a climbing legume that produces elongated, edible pods similar to common beans. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate structures and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are typically small and oval, with a smooth, pale brown surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a food crop in certain regions.
