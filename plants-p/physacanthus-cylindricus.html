Physacanthus cylindricus

Physacanthus cylindricus, commonly known as the cylindrical spongeplant, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spongy, root-like structures that help it anchor in wet soil or submerged environments. The plant has a cylindrical stem with small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and marshes.

Its botanical structure supports its role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
