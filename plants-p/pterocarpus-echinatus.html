Pterocarpus echinatus

Pterocarpus echinatus, commonly known as the African teak or blackwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It is characterized by its dark, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and aesthetic appeal. The plant has compound leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna or woodland habitats.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
