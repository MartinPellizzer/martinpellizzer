Pieris formosa var. forrestii

Pieris formosa var. forrestii, commonly known as the Forrest's Japanese Pieris, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often adorned with white or pale markings. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is prized for its attractive foliage and mild fragrance, though it is relatively slow-growing.
