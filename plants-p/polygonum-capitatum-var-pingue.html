Polygonum capitatum var. pingue

Polygonum capitatum var. pingue, commonly known as the swollen knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, fleshy stems that often appear swollen, especially at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with a waxy coating.

This species is often found in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
