Prunus salicina

Prunus salicina, commonly known as the European cherry or Japanese cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves that are typically serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red or black berries. Prunus salicina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in the production of cherries for culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
