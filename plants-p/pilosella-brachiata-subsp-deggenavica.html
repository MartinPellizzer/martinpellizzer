Pilosella brachiata subsp. deggenavica

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

deggenavica, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold climates.

The species is notable for its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months.
