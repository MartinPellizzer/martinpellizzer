Philodendron hopkinsianum

Philodendron hopkinsianum, commonly known as the silver velvet philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a houseplant.
