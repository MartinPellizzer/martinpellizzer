Pastinaca boissieriana

Pastinaca boissieriana, commonly known as the Boissier's burdock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lobed leaves and produces purple to brown flowers that attract pollinators. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is relatively rare in the wild.
