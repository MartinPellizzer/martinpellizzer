Passiflora rohrii

Passiflora rohrii, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora rohrii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
