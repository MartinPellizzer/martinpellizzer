Parashorea globosa

Parashorea globosa, commonly known as the globular tamanu, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive globose fruit, which is covered in a thick, waxy coating. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its bark and seeds have been traditionally used in indigenous medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
