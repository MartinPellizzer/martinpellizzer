Persicaria amphibia subsp. laevimarginata

Persicaria amphibia subsp.

laevimarginata, commonly known as the water pepper, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments. It features erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have smooth margins, distinguishing it from other subspecies. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes, which are typically borne in the upper leaf axils.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.
