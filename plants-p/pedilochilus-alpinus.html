Pedilochilus alpinus

Pedilochilus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine pedilochilus, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth in high-altitude environments. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip that is often marked with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and delicately adapted species.
