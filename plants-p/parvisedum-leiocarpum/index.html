Parvisedum Leiocarpum

Leiocarpus wood, scientifically known as Parvisedum leiocarpum, is a rare and unique plant species found in specific tropical regions. This plant is characterized by its distinctive wood structure and unusual growth patterns. Despite its limited distribution, it holds significant ecological importance in its native habitat. Its name derives from the Greek words for "smooth" and "carp," referring to its smooth seed pods. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this species faces increasing threats to its survival.
