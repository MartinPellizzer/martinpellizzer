Pancratium rotatum var. pluriflorum

Pancratium rotatum var. pluriflorum, commonly known as the Cape lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces a tall, narrow stem with multiple racemes of flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively low maintenance.
