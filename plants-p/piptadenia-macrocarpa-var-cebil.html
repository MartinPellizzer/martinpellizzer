Piptadenia macrocarpa var. cebil

Piptadenia macrocarpa var. cebil, commonly known as cebil or cebil tree, is a species native to tropical South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately veined, often with a rough texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces large, woody pods containing edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
