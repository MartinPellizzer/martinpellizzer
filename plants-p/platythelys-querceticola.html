Platythelys querceticola

Platythelys querceticola, commonly known as the oakleaf orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the understory of temperate forests.

It is characterized by its unique, broad, oak-like leaves and intricate floral structures that mimic the appearance of oak leaves. This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its reproductive cycle is closely tied to the seasonal availability of light and moisture.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
