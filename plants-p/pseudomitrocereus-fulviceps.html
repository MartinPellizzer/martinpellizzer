Pseudomitrocereus fulviceps

Pseudomitrocereus fulviceps, commonly known as the yellow-headed cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
