Pandanus foetidus var. racemosus

Pandanus foetidus var. racemosus, commonly known as the stinking pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its roots and leaves have been utilized in various cultural practices, including as a source of fiber and for medicinal purposes.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
