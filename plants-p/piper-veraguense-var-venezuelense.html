Piper veraguense var. venezuelense

Piper veraguense var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically harvested when ripe for culinary or medicinal use. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
