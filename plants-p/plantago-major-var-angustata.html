Plantago major var. angustata

Plantago major var. angustata, commonly known as narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped, hairy leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
