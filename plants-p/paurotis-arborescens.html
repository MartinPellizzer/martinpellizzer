Paurotis arborescens

Paurotis arborescens, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Paurotis arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
