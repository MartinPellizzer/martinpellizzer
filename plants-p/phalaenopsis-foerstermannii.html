Phalaenopsis foerstermannii

Phalaenopsis foerstermannii, commonly known as the Foerstermann's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, making it suitable for hanging or mounted displays.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
