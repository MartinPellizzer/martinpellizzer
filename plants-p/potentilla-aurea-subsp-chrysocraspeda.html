Potentilla aurea subsp. chrysocraspeda

Potentilla aurea subsp.

chrysocraspeda, commonly known as golden-hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy foliage, which give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is often found in temperate regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral and foliar features compared to other Potentilla species.
