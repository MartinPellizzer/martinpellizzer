Pharnaceum thunbergii

Pharnaceum thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pharnaceum thunbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
