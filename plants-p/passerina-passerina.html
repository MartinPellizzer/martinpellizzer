Passerina passerina

Passerina passerina, commonly known as the European goldfinch, is a small passerine bird with distinctive yellow plumage and black markings.

Its botanical aspects are closely tied to its diet, which primarily consists of seeds from plants such as thistles, teasels, and other flowering plants. The bird's beak is adapted for efficiently extracting seeds from these plant structures. It often forages in open fields and gardens where its host plants are abundant.

The presence of Passerina passerina can indicate a healthy ecosystem with diverse plant life.
