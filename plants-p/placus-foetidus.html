Placus foetidus

Placus foetidus, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and oval, often with a rough texture.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in bloom.
