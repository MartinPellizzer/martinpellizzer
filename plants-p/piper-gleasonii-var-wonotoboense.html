Piper gleasonii var. wonotoboense

Piper gleasonii var. wonotoboense, commonly known as the Wonotobo pepper, is a climbing vine native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, spindle-shaped fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

The species is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices in its native region.
