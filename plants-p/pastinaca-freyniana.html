Pastinaca freyniana

Pastinaca freyniana, commonly known as the Freyn's bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lobed and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and can be somewhat succulent.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright blooms.
