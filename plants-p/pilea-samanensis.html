Pilea samanensis

Pilea samanensis, commonly known as the Saman pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle plants, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the spring. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

Pilea samanensis thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
