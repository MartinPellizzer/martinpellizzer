Pittosporum wallichii

Pittosporum wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features aromatic, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and visual appeal contribute to its use in horticulture across various climates.
