Pandanus heronensis

Pandanus heronensis, commonly known as the Heron's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by spiky, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and can be processed into materials used for weaving.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
