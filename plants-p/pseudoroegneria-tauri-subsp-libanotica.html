Pseudoroegneria tauri subsp. libanotica

Pseudoroegneria tauri subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters.

The leaves are typically narrow, flat, and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and cliffs.
