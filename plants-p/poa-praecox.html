Poa praecox

Poa praecox, commonly known as early bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, ditches, and along roadsides.

Poa praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
