Phyllocyclus petelotii

Phyllocyclus petelotii, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaves.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively uncommon in cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.
