Phacelia magellanica f. ballii

Phacelia magellanica f. ballii, commonly known as the Magellan phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
