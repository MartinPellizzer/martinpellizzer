Phaseolus prostratus var. longipedunculatus

Phaseolus prostratus var. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-pedunculate runner bean, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple. The plant bears elongated, cylindrical pods that mature to a yellowish-green color, containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and requires support for its vigorous climbing growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown for its edible beans.
