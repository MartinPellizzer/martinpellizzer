Potentilla impolita subsp. tenerrima

Potentilla impolita subsp.

tenerrima, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage composed of small, ovate to elliptic leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in cultivating native or hardy plants.
