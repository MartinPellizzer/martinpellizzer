Plesioneuron tahitense

Plesioneuron tahitense, commonly known as the Tahitian fern, is a species of tree fern native to the island of Tahiti in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize the soil, making it ecologically significant in its native habitat.

The fronds are used in traditional Polynesian crafts and are also valued for their ornamental appearance.
