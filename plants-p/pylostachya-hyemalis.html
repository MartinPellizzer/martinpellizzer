Pylostachya hyemalis

Pylostachya hyemalis, commonly known as the winter switchgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes appear in late summer to early fall, producing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Pyrostachya hyemalis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
