Panicum alopecuros

Panicum alopecuros, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stalks produce fluffy, panicle-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
