Pachyrhizus

Pachyrhizus, commonly known as the giant root or sweet potato vine, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family.

These plants are native to South America and are characterized by their vigorous growth and trailing habit. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can be either simple or compound, depending on the species. Pachyrhizus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Their tuberous roots are also edible and can be used in culinary applications.
