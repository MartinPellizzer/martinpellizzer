Potentilla subglobosa

Potentilla subglobosa, commonly known as the globe cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. The flowers are usually bright yellow, with five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant, hardy plants.
