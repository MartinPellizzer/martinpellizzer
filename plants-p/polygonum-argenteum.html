Polygonum argenteum

Polygonum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
