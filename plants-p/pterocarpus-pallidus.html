Pterocarpus pallidus

Pterocarpus pallidus, commonly known as the pale pterocarpus, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, woody pods containing rounded seeds. It is valued for its durable, aromatic wood used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
