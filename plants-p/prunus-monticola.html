Prunus monticola

Prunus monticola, commonly known as the mountain cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape with a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Prunus monticola plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various animal species.
