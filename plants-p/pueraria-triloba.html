Pueraria triloba

Pueraria triloba, commonly known as the Indian bean or Kwao Krua, is a climbing legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and large, showy purple or white flowers. The plant contains high levels of protein and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Its roots are often harvested and processed into various medicinal formulations.

Pueraria triloba is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
