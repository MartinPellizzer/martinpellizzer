Paspalum vinosum

Paspalum vinosum, commonly known as purple paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and pastures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum vinosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
