Phaseolus vulgaris var. nankinensis

Phaseolus vulgaris var. nankinensis, commonly known as the Chinese kidney bean, is a variety of the common bean plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically beige to brown in color and have a smooth, firm texture. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and is typically grown as an annual crop.

The variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content in traditional cuisines.
