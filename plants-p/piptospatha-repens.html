Piptospatha repens

Piptospatha repens, commonly known as the creeping pterostemon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
