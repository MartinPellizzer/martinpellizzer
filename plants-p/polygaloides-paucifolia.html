Polygaloides paucifolia

Polygaloides paucifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of pink to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
