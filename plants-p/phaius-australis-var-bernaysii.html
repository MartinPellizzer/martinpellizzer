Phaius australis var. bernaysii

Phaius australis var. bernaysii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
