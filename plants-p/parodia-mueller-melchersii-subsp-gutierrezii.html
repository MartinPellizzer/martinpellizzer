Parodia mueller-melchersii subsp. gutierrezii

Parodia mueller-melchersii subsp.

gutierrezii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Mexico. It features a swollen, globular stem that is often brightly colored, with a distinct, rounded shape.

The plant produces small, white flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ease of care.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and attractive addition to succulent collections.
