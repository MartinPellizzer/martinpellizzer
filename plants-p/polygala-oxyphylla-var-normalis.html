Polygala oxyphylla var. normalis

Polygala oxyphylla var. normalis, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
