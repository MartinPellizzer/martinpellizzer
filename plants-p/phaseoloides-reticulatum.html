Phaseoloides reticulatum

Phaseoloides reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, and its small, pea-like flowers are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential agricultural uses.
