Picea fricksii

Picea fricksii, commonly known as the Fricks spruce, is a rare and endangered conifer native to a small region in western China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are densely packed along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments at high altitudes, often in mountainous regions with poor soil conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
