Prunus racemosa var. glaucescens

Prunus racemosa var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blueleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often a bluish-green color. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by small, dark red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it suitable for use in gardens and as a hedge.
