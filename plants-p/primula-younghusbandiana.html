Primula younghusbandiana

Primula younghusbandiana, commonly known as the Tibetan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Primula younghusbandiana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological significance in its native habitat.
