Pleurothallis alpina

Pleurothallis alpina, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and humid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
