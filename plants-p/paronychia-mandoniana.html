Paronychia mandoniana

Paronychia mandoniana, commonly known as the African hairless goosefoot, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a farina. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Paronychia mandoniana is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
