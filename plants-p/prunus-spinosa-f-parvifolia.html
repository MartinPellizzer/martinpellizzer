Prunus spinosa f. parvifolia

Prunus spinosa f. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf blackthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, dark green leaves and pale blue to white flowers.

It is a compact form of Prunus spinosa, featuring smaller foliage compared to the typical species. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in hedging or as a ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is valued for its dense growth habit and seasonal interest throughout the year.
