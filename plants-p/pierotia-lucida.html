Pierotia lucida

Pierotia lucida, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its hollow, cylindrical stems are often used in floral arrangements due to their unique appearance.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads.
