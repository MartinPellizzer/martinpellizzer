Protea compar

Protea compar, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its resilience. Its flowers are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
