Polygonella polygama

Polygonella polygama, commonly known as the golden club, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and has grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

Polygonella polygama is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
