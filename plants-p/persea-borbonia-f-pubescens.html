Persea borbonia f. pubescens

Persea borbonia f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy soursop, is a variant of the soursop tree characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

This plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically large, ovate, and covered with fine pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, spiky fruits.

It is valued both for its ornamental qualities and for the potential medicinal properties of its fruit.
