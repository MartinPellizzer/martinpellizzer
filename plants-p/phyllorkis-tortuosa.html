Phyllorkis tortuosa

Phyllorkis tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that release spores for propagation.

The zigzag fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
