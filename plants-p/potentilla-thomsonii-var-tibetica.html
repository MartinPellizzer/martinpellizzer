Potentilla thomsonii var. tibetica

Potentilla thomsonii var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and small, five-petaled yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
