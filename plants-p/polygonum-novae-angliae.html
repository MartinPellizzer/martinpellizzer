Polygonum novae-angliae

Polygonum novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged in water, adapted for aquatic environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist conditions make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
