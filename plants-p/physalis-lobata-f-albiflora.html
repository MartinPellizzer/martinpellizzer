Physalis lobata f. albiflora

Physalis lobata f. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower gooseberry, is a cultivar of the common groundcherry.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which contrast with its green, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, orange berries enclosed in a papery husk, similar to other Physalis species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in temperate climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
