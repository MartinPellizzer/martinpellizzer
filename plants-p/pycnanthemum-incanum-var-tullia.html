Pycnanthemum incanum var. tullia

Pycnanthemum incanum var. tullia, commonly known as silverblue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a slightly aromatic scent when crushed.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist to dry prairies and woodlands.
