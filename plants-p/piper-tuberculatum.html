Piper tuberculatum

Piper tuberculatum, commonly known as the warty pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often have a warty texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
