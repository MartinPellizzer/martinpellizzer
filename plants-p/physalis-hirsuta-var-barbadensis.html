Physalis hirsuta var. barbadensis

Physalis hirsuta var. barbadensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It produces small, inflated, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, typical of the Solanaceae family. The fruit is edible, with a tart flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for both ornamental and nutritional purposes.
