Piper neurostachyum

Piper neurostachyum, commonly known as the creeping piper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, fleshy berries.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates as a ground cover or ornamental plant.
