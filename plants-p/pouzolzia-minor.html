Pouzolzia minor

Pouzolzia minor, commonly known as the lesser pouzolzia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

Pouzolzia minor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
