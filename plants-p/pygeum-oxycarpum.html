Pygeum oxycarpum

Pygeum oxycarpum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing hard, angular seeds. It is often found in coastal and mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
