Paracaryum reuteri var. leiocarpum

Paracaryum reuteri var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the silky capsicum, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturation. Its flowers are usually yellow with white markings, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
