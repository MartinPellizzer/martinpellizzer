Pterichis meirax

Pterichis meirax, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pterichis meirax is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
