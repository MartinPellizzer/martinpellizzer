Paeonia mascula subsp. hellenica

Paeonia mascula subsp.

hellenica, commonly known as the Greek peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for garden cultivation.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often grown for its vibrant floral displays.
