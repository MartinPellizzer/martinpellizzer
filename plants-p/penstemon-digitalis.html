Penstemon digitalis

Penstemon digitalis, commonly known as the digital penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon digitalis is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
