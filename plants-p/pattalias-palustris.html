Pattalias palustris

Pattalias palustris, commonly known as the marsh pattia, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its root system helps stabilize soil and improve water filtration in wetland ecosystems.

Pattalias palustris plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
