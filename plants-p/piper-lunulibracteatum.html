Piper lunulibracteatum

Piper lunulibracteatum, commonly known as the moon-leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green midrib, giving it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by elliptical berries that mature to a dark red color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
