Phyllanthus nummularioides

Phyllanthus nummularioides, commonly known as the dollar plant or pancake plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy, round leaves that resemble coins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique leaf shape and ability to thrive in various soil types.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
