Peucedanum acaule

Peucedanum acaule, commonly known as the slender cowparsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often aromatic and have a mild licorice-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Peucedanum acaule is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along woodland edges.
