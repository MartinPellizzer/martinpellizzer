Pomatocalpa brachybotryum

Pomatocalpa brachybotryum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed lady's slipper, is an orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flower structure known as a labellum, which is typically pink or purple in color. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
