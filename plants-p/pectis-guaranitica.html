Pectis guaranitica

Pectis guaranitica, commonly known as the Guarani dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in open fields and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Pectis guaranitica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in local communities for its healing benefits.
