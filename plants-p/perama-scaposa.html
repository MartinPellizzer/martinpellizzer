Perama scaposa

Perama scaposa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed scaposa, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, linear leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to sandy or gravelly soils.

Perama scaposa is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in full sun.
