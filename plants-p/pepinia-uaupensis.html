Pepinia uaupensis

Pepinia uaupensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive nature, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
