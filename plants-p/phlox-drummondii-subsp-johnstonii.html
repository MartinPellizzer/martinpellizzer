Phlox drummondii subsp. johnstonii

Phlox drummondii subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's garden phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters.

The plant produces showy clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
