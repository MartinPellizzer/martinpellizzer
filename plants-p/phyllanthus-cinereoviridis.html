Phyllanthus cinereoviridis

Phyllanthus cinereoviridis, commonly known as the silver-green phyllanthus, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white, followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high light exposure.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
