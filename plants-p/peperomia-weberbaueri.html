Peperomia weberbaueri

Peperomia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, followed by tiny, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia weberbaueri is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
