Primula pedemontana subsp. iberica

Primula pedemontana subsp.

iberica, commonly known as the Iberian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with hairy surfaces, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, contributing to the floral diversity of its habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
