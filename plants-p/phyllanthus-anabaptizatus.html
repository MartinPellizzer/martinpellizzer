Phyllanthus anabaptizatus

Phyllanthus anabaptizatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
