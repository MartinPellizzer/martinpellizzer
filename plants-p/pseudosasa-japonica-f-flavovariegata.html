Pseudosasa japonica f. flavovariegata

Pseudosasa japonica f. flavovariegata, commonly known as the golden variegated bamboo, is a varietal form of the Japanese bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green variegated leaves, which add visual interest to gardens and landscapes. This plant is a clump-forming bamboo that grows in dense, upright clusters, reaching moderate heights. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in both residential and commercial settings.
