Piptochaetium cucullatum

Piptochaetium cucullatum, commonly known as the hooded grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive hood-like auricles at the base of the leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-rowed spikelets.

Piptochaetium cucullatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
