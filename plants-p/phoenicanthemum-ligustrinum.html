Phoenicanthemum ligustrinum

Phoenicanthemum ligustrinum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like flower heads that range in color from purple to pink, surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phoenicanthemum ligustrinum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
