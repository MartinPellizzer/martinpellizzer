Polygonum hirtovaginum

Polygonum hirtovaginum, commonly known as the hairy bladderworts, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It is native to regions of North America, particularly in the western United States and parts of Canada.

Polygonum hirtovaginum is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in disturbed or dry habitats.
