Protea alba

Protea alba, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy white inflorescence that resembles a cloud, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by birds and insects, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The protea alba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
