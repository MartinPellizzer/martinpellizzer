Pyrus ussuriensis var. obovata

Pyrus ussuriensis var. obovata, commonly known as the Ussuri pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in suitable climates.
