Prunus × sieboldii f. watereri

Prunus × sieboldii f. watereri, commonly known as the Watereri cherry, is a hybrid plant that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with dense, upright growth habit and bright red or orange flowers in spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy dark green upper surface. Its fruit is a small, sweet, and edible drupe, typically red or yellow when mature.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
