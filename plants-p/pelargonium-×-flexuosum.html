Pelargonium × flexuosum

Pelargonium × flexuosum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a hybrid species in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture. Native to South Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is often used in hanging baskets and as a ground cover due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal.
