Pleiostemon verrucosus

Pleiostemon verrucosus, commonly known as the roughleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include a trailing or ascending stem and small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.
