Piper guayranum var. humilius

Piper guayranum var. humilius, commonly known as the dwarf guayran, is a tropical vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
