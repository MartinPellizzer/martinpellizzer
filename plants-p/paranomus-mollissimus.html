Paranomus mollissimus

Paranomus mollissimus, commonly known as the silky fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that give it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Paranomus mollissimus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add a lush, green texture to gardens.
