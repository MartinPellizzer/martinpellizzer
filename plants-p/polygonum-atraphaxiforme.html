Polygonum atraphaxiforme

Polygonum atraphaxiforme, commonly known as the black nightshade or wild spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, greenish flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Polygonum atraphaxiforme is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and occasionally invasive in certain regions.
