Pseuderanthemum nanum

Pseuderanthemum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf false jasmine, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
