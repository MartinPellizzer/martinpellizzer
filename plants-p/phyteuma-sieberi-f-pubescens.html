Phyteuma sieberi f. pubescens

Phyteuma sieberi f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy or pubescent leaves, which give it its specific epithet "pubescens.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
