Psorospermum citrifolium

Psorospermum citrifolium, commonly known as the citrifolium psorospermum, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
