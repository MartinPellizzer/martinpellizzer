Podianthus

Podianthus, commonly known as the Christmas tree or the Christmas rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rhamnaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrubs with small, leathery leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom during the winter season. The plant is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and seasonal beauty. Podianthus species are typically drought-tolerant and thrive in well-drained soils, making them suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique flowering period makes it a popular choice for holiday decorations and garden displays.
