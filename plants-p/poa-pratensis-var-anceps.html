Poa pratensis var. anceps

Poa pratensis var. anceps, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
