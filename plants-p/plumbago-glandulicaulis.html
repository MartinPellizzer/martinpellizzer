Plumbago glandulicaulis

Plumbago glandulicaulis, commonly known as the glandular plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
