Pithecellobium lusorium

Pithecellobium lusorium, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its hardy nature makes it a common weed in agricultural areas.
