Paspalum digitarioides

Paspalum digitarioides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and tufted inflorescences that resemble a brush. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, thriving in moist to saturated soils. Its rapid growth and dense coverage make it an effective pioneer species in ecological restoration projects.

Paspalum digitarioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
