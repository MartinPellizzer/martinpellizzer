Piper pseudodilatatum

Piper pseudodilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or creeping pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its elongated, opposite leaves that are often broad and glossy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
