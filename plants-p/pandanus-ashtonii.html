Pandanus ashtonii

Pandanus ashtonii, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the palm family.

It is native to the islands of French Polynesia, including Tahiti and Moorea. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to humid, tropical climates.
