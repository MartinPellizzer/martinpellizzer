Podocarpus zamiifolius

Podocarpus zamiifolius, commonly known as the African snowflake or African holly, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Podocarpus zamiifolius is also valued for its hardy nature and use in landscaping and as a hedge.
