Pandanus odontoides

Pandanus odontoides, commonly known as the toothed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often lined with small teeth or spines along the edges. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in large, branched inflorescences. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional crafts and for medicinal purposes.

Pandanus odontoides thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in shallow water or on sandy soils.
