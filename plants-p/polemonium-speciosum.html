Polemonium speciosum

Polemonium speciosum, commonly known as the showy lady's slippers, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
