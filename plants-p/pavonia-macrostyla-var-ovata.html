Pavonia macrostyla var. ovata

Pavonia macrostyla var. ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf pavia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright red or pink bract-like flowers that are clustered at the ends of its stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they often have a reddish or purple tinge when young. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and seasonal interest.
