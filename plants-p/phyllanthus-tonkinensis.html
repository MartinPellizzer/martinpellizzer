Phyllanthus tonkinensis

Phyllanthus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
