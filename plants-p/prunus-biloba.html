Prunus biloba

Prunus biloba, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, round fruits that resemble pears. The tree has a compact growth habit with smooth, grey bark and dark green, serrated leaves. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Prunus biloba is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soils.
