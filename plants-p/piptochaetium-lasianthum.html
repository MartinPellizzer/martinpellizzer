Piptochaetium lasianthum

Piptochaetium lasianthum, commonly known as the tallgrass bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and savanna ecosystems. It plays an important role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife and livestock.

Piptochaetium lasianthum is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and grazing.
