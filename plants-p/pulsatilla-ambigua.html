Pulsatilla ambigua

Pulsatilla ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-white petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
