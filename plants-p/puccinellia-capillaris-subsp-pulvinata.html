Puccinellia capillaris subsp. pulvinata

Puccinellia capillaris subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as the slender needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions. It typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in dunes and coastal areas, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
