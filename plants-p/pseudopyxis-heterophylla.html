Pseudopyxis heterophylla

Pseudopyxis heterophylla, commonly known as the variable pseudopyxis, is a unique plant species native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a valuable component of riparian ecosystems.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
