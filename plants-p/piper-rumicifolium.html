Piper rumicifolium

Piper rumicifolium, commonly known as the wild pepper or Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical regions of Mexico, Central America, and parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
