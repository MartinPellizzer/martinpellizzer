Piper cyanophyllum

Piper cyanophyllum, commonly known as the blue-leaved pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking blue-green leaves, which are covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically greenish-white in color and are followed by small, round berries.

Piper cyanophyllum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
