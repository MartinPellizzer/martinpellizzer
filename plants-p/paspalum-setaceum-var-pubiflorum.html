Paspalum setaceum var. pubiflorum

Paspalum setaceum var. pubiflorum, commonly known as the pubiflorous paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue that enhances its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
