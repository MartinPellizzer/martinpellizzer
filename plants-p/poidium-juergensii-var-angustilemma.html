Poidium juergensii var. angustilemma

Poidium juergensii var. angustilemma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved poidium, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
