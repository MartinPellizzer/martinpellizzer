Platycarphella carlinoides

Platycarphella carlinoides, commonly known as the carlinoid false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
