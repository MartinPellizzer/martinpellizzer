Piper segoviarum

Piper segoviarum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or the sacred pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elongated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries. The berries are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices due to their pungent flavor and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
