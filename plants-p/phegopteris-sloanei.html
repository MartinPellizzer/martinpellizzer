Phegopteris sloanei

Phegopteris sloanei, commonly known as the Sloane's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are typically divided into small, lance-shaped segments. Its rhizomes are often submerged, anchoring the plant and facilitating nutrient absorption.

Phegopteris sloanei plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
