Panicum ferventicola

Panicum ferventicola, commonly known as the feverfew panicum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant produces light green to yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Panicum ferventicola is typically found in open grasslands and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sunlight.
