Pleione pinkepankii

Pleione pinkepankii, commonly known as the pink-flowered pleione, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China.

It is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in shaded, moist environments, and is adapted to cool, humid climates. Its flowers are notable for their intricate petal structure and vibrant coloration, making it a prized species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native range.
