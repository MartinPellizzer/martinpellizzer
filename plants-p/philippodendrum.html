Philippodendrum

Philippodendrum, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and aerial roots that help it climb towards light. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Philippodendrum species are also valued for their ability to purify air and enhance the aesthetic of indoor spaces.
