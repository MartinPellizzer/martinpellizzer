Piper caballetense

Piper caballetense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
