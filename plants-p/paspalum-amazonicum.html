Paspalum amazonicum

Paspalum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian grass or giant grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stature and dense, tufted growth habit, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, demonstrating adaptability to moist, tropical environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
