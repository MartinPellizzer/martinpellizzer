Pulsatilla donetzica

Pulsatilla donetzica, commonly known as the Donetsky pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, solitary, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant has a short, hairy stem and produces follicles as its fruit type. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
