Physaria montana

Physaria montana, commonly known as the mountain flax or desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Physaria montana is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and rocky slopes.
