Piaranthus comptus var. ciliatus

Piaranthus comptus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the Cape candelabra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem with cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, often blooming in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
