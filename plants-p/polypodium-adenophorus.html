Polypodium adenophorus

Polypodium adenophorus, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, glandular hairs. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Polypodium adenophorus is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
