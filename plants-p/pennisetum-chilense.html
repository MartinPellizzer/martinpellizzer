Pennisetum chilense

Pennisetum chilense, commonly known as Chilean fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, arching stems that bear panicles of small, fluffy flowers. The plant typically reaches heights of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces showy, pinkish to reddish flower spikes in late summer.

Pennisetum chilense is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
