Paepalanthus reflexus

Paepalanthus reflexus, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and spiny stems, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
