Primula zeylanica

Primula zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, five-petaled flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
