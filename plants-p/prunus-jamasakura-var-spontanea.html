Prunus jamasakura var. spontanea

Prunus jamasakura var. spontanea, commonly known as the wild cherry blossom, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
