Primula dentiflora

Primula dentiflora, commonly known as the toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it the common name "toothed.".

Primula dentiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
