Prunus cuthbertii

Prunus cuthbertii, commonly known as the Cuthbert cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Prunus cuthbertii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes grown in botanical gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
