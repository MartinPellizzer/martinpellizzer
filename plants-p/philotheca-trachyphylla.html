Philotheca trachyphylla

Philotheca trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved philotheca, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

Philotheca trachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
