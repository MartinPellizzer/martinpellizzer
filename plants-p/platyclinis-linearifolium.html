Platyclinis linearifolium

Platyclinis linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved platyclinis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
