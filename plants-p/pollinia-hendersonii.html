Pollinia hendersonii

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, Pollinia hendersonii is considered a rare and endangered species.
