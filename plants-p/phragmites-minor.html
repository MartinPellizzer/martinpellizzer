Phragmites minor

Phragmites minor, commonly known as narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It has narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, white plumes that are characteristic of the genus Phragmites. This plant is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in other moist soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its growth is often influenced by water levels and soil salinity.
