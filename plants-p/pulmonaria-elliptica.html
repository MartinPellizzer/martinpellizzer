Pulmonaria elliptica

Pulmonaria elliptica, commonly known as the lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often spotted with purple or silver. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Pulmonaria elliptica is valued in gardens for its ornamental foliage and early spring blooms.
