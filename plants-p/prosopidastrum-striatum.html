Prosopidastrum striatum

Prosopidastrum striatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spurge.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a distinctive striated pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.

Its latex contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
