Phragmipedium longifolium var. gracile

Phragmipedium longifolium var. gracile, commonly known as the slender-leafed phragmipedium, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with pale yellow petals and a prominent, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively ease of cultivation.
