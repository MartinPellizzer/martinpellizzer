Polypodium clathratum var. lobatum

Polypodium clathratum var. lobatum, commonly known as the basket fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a lush, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a unique, segmented structure that resembles a woven basket.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
