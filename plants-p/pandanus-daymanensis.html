Pandanus daymanensis

Pandanus daymanensis, commonly known as the Malabar pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the coastal regions of the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
