Phaseolus vulgaris var. carneoflavescens

Phaseolus vulgaris var. carneoflavescens, commonly known as the yellow wax bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange pod color and its slightly hairy, elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves and axial inflorescences. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the beans are edible when harvested young.

This variety is valued for its flavor and nutritional content, making it a popular choice in culinary applications.
