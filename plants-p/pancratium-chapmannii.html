Pancratium chapmannii

Pancratium chapmannii, commonly known as the Chapman's yellow nutmeg, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves and develops a bulbous stem that stores water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer and are pollinated primarily by insects.

Pancratium chapmannii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
