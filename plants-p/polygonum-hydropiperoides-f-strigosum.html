Polygonum hydropiperoides f. strigosum

Polygonum hydropiperoides f. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a variant of the polygonum species characterized by its hairy foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to Asia, particularly found in regions of China and Korea. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in spikes, and bloom in late summer.
