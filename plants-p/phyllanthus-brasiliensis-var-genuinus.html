Phyllanthus brasiliensis var. genuinus

Phyllanthus brasiliensis var. genuinus, commonly known as Brazilian starfruit or *carambola*, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, star-shaped fruits that are edible and widely cultivated for their nutritional value.

Its botanical features include a woody stem, hairy young shoots, and a distinctive fruit shape that resembles a star when cut.
