Petrea racemosa

Petrea racemosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

Petrea racemosa is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun and its resilience to drought conditions.
