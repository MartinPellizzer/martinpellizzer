Platycentrum annulatum

Platycentrum annulatum, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features long, narrow fronds that spiral around the central axis, creating a visually striking appearance. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy environments.

Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns.
