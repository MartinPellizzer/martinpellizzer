Protium decandrum

Protium decandrum, commonly known as the five-toothed or five-lobed Protium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with five lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
