Piper tapianum

Piper tapianum, commonly known as the Tapianum pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green to yellowish flowers that are followed by berry-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are hairy.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
