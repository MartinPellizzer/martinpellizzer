Phleum pratense var. maximum

Phleum pratense var. maximum, commonly known as tall sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This variety is known for its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and adaptability.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ecological value.
