Polygonum tenuinaevum

Polygonum tenuinaevum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its thin, erect stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Polygonum tenuinaevum is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
