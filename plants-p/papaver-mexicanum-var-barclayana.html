Papaver mexicanum var. barclayana

Papaver mexicanum var. barclayana, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red flowers with a distinctive black center, which bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its beauty and potential medicinal properties.
