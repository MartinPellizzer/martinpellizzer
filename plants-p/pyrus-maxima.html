Pyrus maxima

Pyrus maxima, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a large, sweet, and juicy pear with a thin, crisp skin. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in temperate climates.
