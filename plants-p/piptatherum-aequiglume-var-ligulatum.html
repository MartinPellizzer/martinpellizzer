Piptatherum aequiglume var. ligulatum

Piptatherum aequiglume var. ligulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly compressed, with a smooth texture.

The inflorescence consists of two to four spikelets, each bearing two to three small, awned florets.
