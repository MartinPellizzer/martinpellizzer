Poa coarctata

Poa coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or alpine soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Poa coarctata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous habitats.
