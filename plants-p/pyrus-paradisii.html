Pyrus paradisii

Pyrus paradisii, commonly known as the European pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces fruits that are typically harvested in late summer and early autumn. The plant has alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is characterized by its crisp, juicy flesh and a distinctive aroma.
