Phaulopsis longifolia

Phaulopsis longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf phaulopsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
