Prunus tomentosa var. insularis

Prunus tomentosa var. insularis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, white-pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has a compact growth habit with a rounded form and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are ovate with a hairy underside, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
