Pterocelastrus burmanni

Pterocelastrus burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's boxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
