Prunus serrulata f. communis

Prunus serrulata f. communis, commonly known as the common cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a variety of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a small deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
