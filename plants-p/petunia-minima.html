Petunia minima

Petunia minima, commonly known as the dwarf petunia, is a compact flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and violet. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it ideal for rock gardens, containers, and borders. Its foliage is heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and branching.

Petunia minima is known for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
