Protea sceptrum-gustavianum

Protea sceptrum-gustavianum, commonly known as the King Protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flower heads composed of numerous individual flowers, which are typically pink or red in color. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic appeal, it is also a symbol of South Africa's floral heritage.
