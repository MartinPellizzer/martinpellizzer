Phyllanthus gaertneri

Phyllanthus gaertneri, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, characterized by its small, thick, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Phyllanthus gaertneri is also notable for containing various bioactive compounds, which have been studied for potential medicinal uses.
