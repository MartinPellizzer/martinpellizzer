Passiflora pulchella var. bifidata

Passiflora pulchella var. bifidata, commonly known as the bifid passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This species produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
