Potentilla pensylvanica var. pinnatifida

Potentilla pensylvanica var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically serrated and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
