Passiflora vitifolia var. minor

Passiflora vitifolia var. minor, commonly known as the lesser vine passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with a distinctive central cluster of stamens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
