Paepalanthus elongatus var. barbulatus

Paepalanthus elongatus var. barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy long-stemmed puya, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its rosette of grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
