Piptadenia rigida

Piptadenia rigida, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain elliptical seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood and traditional medicinal uses.
