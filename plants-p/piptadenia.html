Piptadenia

Piptadenia, commonly known as the "Brazil nut tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. Its most notable feature is the fruit, which contains the edible Brazil nuts.

Piptadenia species are also valued for their nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
