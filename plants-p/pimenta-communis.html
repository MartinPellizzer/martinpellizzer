Pimenta communis

Pimenta communis, commonly known as the allspice tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark red berries that are harvested when fully mature. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that develop into the spice commonly used in cooking. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
