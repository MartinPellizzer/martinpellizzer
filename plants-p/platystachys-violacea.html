Platystachys violacea

Platystachys violacea, commonly known as the purple flame bamboo, is a striking ornamental plant native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its striking purple to lavender-colored inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, adding to its visual appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, hardy plant.
