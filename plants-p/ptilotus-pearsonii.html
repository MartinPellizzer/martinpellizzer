Ptilotus pearsonii

Ptilotus pearsonii, commonly known as the pearson's spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance. Its small, clustered inflorescences are often mistaken for weeds due to their appearance.

Ptilotus pearsonii plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
