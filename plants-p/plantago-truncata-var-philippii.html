Plantago truncata var. philippii

Plantago truncata var. philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its fibrous roots and robust growth make it a resilient plant adapted to arid environments.
