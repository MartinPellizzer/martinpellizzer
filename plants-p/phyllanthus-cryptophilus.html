Phyllanthus cryptophilus

Phyllanthus cryptophilus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus cryptophilus is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional properties.
