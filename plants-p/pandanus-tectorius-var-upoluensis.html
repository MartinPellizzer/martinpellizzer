Pandanus tectorius var. upoluensis

Pandanus tectorius var. upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, spadix-like structures. Its leaves are fibrous and are used for making thatch, mats, and other woven items.

This variety is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
