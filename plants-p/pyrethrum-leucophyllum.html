Pyrethrum leucophyllum

Pyrethrum leucophyllum, commonly known as the white pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, flat clusters.

Pyrethrum leucophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insecticidal properties.
