Penstemon fruticosus subsp. scouleri

Penstemon fruticosus subsp.

scouleri, commonly known as Scouler's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and woodlands.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
