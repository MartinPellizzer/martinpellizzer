Passiflora hollrungii

Passiflora hollrungii, commonly known as the Hollrung's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits similar to those of other passionfruit species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora hollrungii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
