Philodendron albovaginatum

Philodendron albovaginatum, commonly known as the white-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent white veins that create a striking visual contrast. The plant has a vining growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when grown in suitable conditions. Its leaves are often glossy and can vary in size depending on light exposure and care.

Philodendron albovaginatum is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
