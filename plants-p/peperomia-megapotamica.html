Peperomia megapotamica

Peperomia megapotamica, commonly known as the Brazilian peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and compact form, it is often used as a decorative foliage plant in collections.
