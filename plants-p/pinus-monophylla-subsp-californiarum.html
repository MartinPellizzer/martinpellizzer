Pinus monophylla subsp. californiarum

Pinus monophylla subsp.

californiarum, commonly known as the California whitebark pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive white bark and needle-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in high-elevation areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its cones are serotinous, meaning they remain closed until triggered by heat from a fire, allowing seeds to be released.

This adaptation aids in its regeneration in fire-prone ecosystems.
