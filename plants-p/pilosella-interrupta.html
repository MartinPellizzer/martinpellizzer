Pilosella interrupta

Pilosella interrupta, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, and the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Pilosella interrupta is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
