Primula calderiana subsp. bawaii

Primula calderiana subsp.

bawaii, commonly known as the Hawaiian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Hawaiian Islands. It features succulent, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
