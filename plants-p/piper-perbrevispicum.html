Piper perbrevispicum

Piper perbrevispicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
