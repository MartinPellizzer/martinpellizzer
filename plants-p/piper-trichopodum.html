Piper trichopodum

Piper trichopodum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, which eventually develop into small, round berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden areas or hanging baskets.
