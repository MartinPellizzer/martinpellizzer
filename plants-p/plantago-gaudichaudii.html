Plantago gaudichaudii

Plantago gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Cook Islands plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in saline and well-drained soils.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
