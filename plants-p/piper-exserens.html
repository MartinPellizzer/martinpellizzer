Piper exserens

Piper exserens, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, round berries. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to spread along the ground or climb supports.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
