Pterocarpus roxburghii

Pterocarpus roxburghii, commonly known as Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its density and rich reddish-brown color. Its bark is smooth and often fissured, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
