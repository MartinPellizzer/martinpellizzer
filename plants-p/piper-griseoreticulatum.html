Piper griseoreticulatum

Piper griseoreticulatum, commonly known as the gray reticulated pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with a distinctive grayish reticulation pattern on the underside. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
