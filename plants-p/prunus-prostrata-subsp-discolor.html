Prunus prostrata subsp. discolor

Prunus prostrata subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the Japanese cherry laurel, is a low-growing shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in early spring.

The subspecies discolor is distinguished by its variegated leaves, which often display white or pale green markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal blooms make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
