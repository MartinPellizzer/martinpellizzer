Perityle leptoglossa subsp. palmeri

Perityle leptoglossa subsp.

palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's perityle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are small and clustered in a spherical shape.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological adaptations.
