Persea pauhoi

Persea pauhoi, commonly known as the Hawaiian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, orange to red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its bark is thick and rugged, often covered in lichen, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Persea pauhoi plays an important ecological role in Hawaii's native ecosystems.
