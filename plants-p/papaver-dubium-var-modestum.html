Papaver dubium var. modestum

Papaver dubium var. modestum, commonly known as the modest poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features delicate, nodding flowers with bright orange to red petals and a prominent, saucer-shaped seed capsule. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The modest poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
