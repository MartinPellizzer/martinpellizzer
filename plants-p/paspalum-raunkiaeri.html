Paspalum raunkiaeri

Paspalum raunkiaeri, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Paspalum raunkiaeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
