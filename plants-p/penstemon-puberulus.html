Penstemon puberulus

Penstemon puberulus, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Penstemon puberulus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
