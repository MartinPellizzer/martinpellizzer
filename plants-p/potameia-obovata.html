Potameia obovata

Potameia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate marsh plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland and marsh habitats in southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of lakes. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to aquatic environments, with rhizomes that help it anchor and absorb nutrients from the surrounding water.

Potameia obovata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
