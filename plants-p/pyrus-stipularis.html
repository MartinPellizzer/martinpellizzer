Pyrus stipularis

Pyrus stipularis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruit in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
