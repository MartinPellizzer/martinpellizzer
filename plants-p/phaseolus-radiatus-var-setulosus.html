Phaseolus radiatus var. setulosus

Phaseolus radiatus var. setulosus, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a tropical legume characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and elongated, bean-like pods.

This variety belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to Central and South America. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in tough, fibrous pods that mature to a yellowish or brownish color.

The hairy runner bean is valued for its edible pods and seeds, which are commonly used in culinary applications.
