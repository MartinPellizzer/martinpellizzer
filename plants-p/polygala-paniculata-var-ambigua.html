Polygala paniculata var. ambigua

Polygala paniculata var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often have a slight hairy texture.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
