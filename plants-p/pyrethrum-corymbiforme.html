Pyrethrum corymbiforme

Pyrethrum corymbiforme, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pink petals. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by the wind. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Pyrethrum corymbiforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
