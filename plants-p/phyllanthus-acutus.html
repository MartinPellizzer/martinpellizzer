Phyllanthus acutus

Phyllanthus acutus, commonly known as the sharpleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Phyllanthus acutus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
