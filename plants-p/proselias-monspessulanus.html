Proselias monspessulanus

Proselias monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
