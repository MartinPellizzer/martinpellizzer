Peucedanum insulare

Peucedanum insulare, commonly known as the island angelica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features large, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, white to greenish-yellow flowers in umbels. Its seeds are enclosed in a ribbed, dry fruit known as a schizocarp.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
