Pilocarpus demerarae

Pilocarpus demerarae, commonly known as the Amazonian prickly ash, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its most notable feature is the presence of alkaloids, particularly pilocarpine, which has medicinal applications.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of primary forests.
