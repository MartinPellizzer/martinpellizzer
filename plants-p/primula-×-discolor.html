Primula × discolor

Primula × discolor, commonly known as the spotted violet, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety leaves with a bluish-green hue and prominent purple spots. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and early blooming period.
