Polygonum microcephalum var. sphaerocephalum

Polygonum microcephalum var. sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the globe-headed smartweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, red-tipped flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
