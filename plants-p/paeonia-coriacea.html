Paeonia coriacea

Paeonia coriacea, commonly known as the sand peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its stems are woody at the base and produce multiple flowering shoots each season.

Paeonia coriacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional horticulture.
