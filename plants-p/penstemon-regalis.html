Penstemon regalis

Penstemon regalis, commonly known as the royal penstemon, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like flower clusters that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Penstemon regalis thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, contributing to its ecological value.
