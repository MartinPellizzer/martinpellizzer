Pedicularis semibarbata var. charlestonensis

Pedicularis semibarbata var. charlestonensis, commonly known as the Charleston beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, downward-facing flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or swampy areas. It features lance-shaped leaves and produces tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
