Panargyrus densifolius

Panargyrus densifolius, commonly known as the dense-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers surrounded by overlapping, narrow leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
