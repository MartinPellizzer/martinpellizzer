Primula mistassinica var. intercedens

Primula mistassinica var. intercedens, commonly known as the Mistassini primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canadian province of Quebec.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
