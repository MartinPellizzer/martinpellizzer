Phyllostegia grandiflora var. majoricalycis

Phyllostegia grandiflora var. majoricalycis, commonly known as the broad-leaved strobilant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

This variety is distinguished by its larger calyces compared to the typical form of Phyllostegia grandiflora.
