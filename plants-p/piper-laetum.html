Piper laetum

Piper laetum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, yellow berries that are edible and have a spicy, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
