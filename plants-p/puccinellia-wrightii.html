Puccinellia wrightii

Puccinellia wrightii, commonly known as Wright's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, two-lobed flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Puccinellia wrightii is adapted to harsh environments, making it an important component of stabilized dunes and sandy soils.
