Paepalanthus bryoides

Paepalanthus bryoides, commonly known as the moss-like cactus, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leafless stems that resemble moss or lichens. The plant has small, green, spiny structures that serve as modified leaves, performing photosynthesis. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unusual appearance, it is often mistaken for a moss or lichen, but it is indeed a flowering plant.
