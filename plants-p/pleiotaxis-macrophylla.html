Pleiotaxis macrophylla

Pleiotaxis macrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf pleiotaxis, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
