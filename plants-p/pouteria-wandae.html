Pouteria wandae

Pouteria wandae, commonly known as the Cameroon oil palm, is a tropical tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces a valuable oil-rich fruit, which is used in both culinary and industrial applications. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species.

However, due to habitat loss and overharvesting, Pouteria wandae is considered vulnerable to extinction.
