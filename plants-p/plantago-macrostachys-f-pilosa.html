Plantago macrostachys f. pilosa

Plantago macrostachys f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense spike of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Its hairy foliage and robust growth make it a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for ornamental and medicinal uses.
