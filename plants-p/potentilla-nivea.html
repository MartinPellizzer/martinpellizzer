Potentilla nivea

Potentilla nivea, commonly known as snowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and its berries are small, white, and toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Potentilla nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
