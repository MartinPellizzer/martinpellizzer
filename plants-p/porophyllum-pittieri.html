Porophyllum pittieri

Porophyllum pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow to white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Porophyllum pittieri is also known for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
