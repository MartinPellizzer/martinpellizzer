Physalis peruviana var. esculenta

Physalis peruviana var. esculenta, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It produces small, lantern-shaped fruits that are encased in a papery husk. The fruit is rich in vitamin C, dietary fiber, and antioxidants, making it nutritionally valuable. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

Its unique appearance and nutritional profile make it a popular choice in both culinary and horticultural contexts.
