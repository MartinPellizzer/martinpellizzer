Paspalum rosei

Paspalum rosei, commonly known as the rose lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is adapted to both full sun and partial shade.

Paspalum rosei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
