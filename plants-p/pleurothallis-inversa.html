Pleurothallis inversa

Pleurothallis inversa, commonly known as the upside-down orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where the flowers face downward, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually small, with vivid colors such as yellow, orange, or red, and are often fragrant.

Pleurothallis inversa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
