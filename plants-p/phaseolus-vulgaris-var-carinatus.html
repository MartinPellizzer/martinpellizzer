Phaseolus vulgaris var. carinatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. carinatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and often colorful pod structure, which can range from bright red to yellow or orange. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing edible beans within its elongated pods. Its botanical features include compound leaves and flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various cuisines.
