Phaca picta

Phaca picta, commonly known as the painted phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in inflorescences that add to its ornamental appeal.

Phaca picta is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and adaptability to various garden conditions.
