Parodia papagayana

Parodia papagayana, commonly known as the Papagayo Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Parodia papagayana is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
