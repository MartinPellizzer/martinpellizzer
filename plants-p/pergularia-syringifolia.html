Pergularia syringifolia

Pergularia syringifolia, commonly known as the cherry leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and simple, alternate leaves. Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
