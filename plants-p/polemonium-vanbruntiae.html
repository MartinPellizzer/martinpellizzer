Polemonium vanbruntiae

Polemonium vanbruntiae, commonly known as Vanbrunt's bluebeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces showy, beard-like seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
