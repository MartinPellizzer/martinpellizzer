Potentilla parviflora subsp. goldbachii

Potentilla parviflora subsp.

goldbachii, commonly known as the goldbach's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
