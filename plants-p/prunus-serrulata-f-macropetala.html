Prunus serrulata f. macropetala

Prunus serrulata f. macropetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with large petals, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrowly ovate leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its flowers are notable for their large, showy petals, which contribute to its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

The leaves are serrated, giving the plant its specific epithet "serrulata.".
