Prunus mahaleb var. transsilvanica

Prunus mahaleb var. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian cherry, is a variety of the European cherry species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring. The fruit is a dark red to black cherry, typically harvested in late summer, and is valued for its flavor and ornamental appeal. This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and its edible fruit.
