Pachycarpus mildbraedii

Pachycarpus mildbraedii, commonly known as the mildbraed's pachycarpus, is a flowering plant native to the South African region of KwaZulu-Natal.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
