Pyrus turkestanica

Pyrus turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges.

Pyrus turkestanica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
