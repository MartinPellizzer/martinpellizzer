Piper hirsutum var. jamaicense

Piper hirsutum var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican pepper or wild pepper, is a perennial shrub native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clustered, three-lobed berries. The berries are typically bright red when mature and contain a single seed.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
