Pappobolus lanatus var. sanctae

Pappobolus lanatus var. sanctae, commonly known as the sacred grass, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its cultural significance, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and rituals.
