Potentilla chrysantha var. coronensis

Potentilla chrysantha var. coronensis, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is noted for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce numerous blooms throughout the growing season.
