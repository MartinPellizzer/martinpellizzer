Podanthus gratus

Podanthus gratus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor collections.
