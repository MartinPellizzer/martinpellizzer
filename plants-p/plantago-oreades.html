Plantago oreades

Plantago oreades, commonly known as the mountain plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant has erect, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
