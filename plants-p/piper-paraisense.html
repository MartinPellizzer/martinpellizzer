Piper paraisense

Piper paraisense, commonly known as the "Brazilian pepper tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge for ecological management.
