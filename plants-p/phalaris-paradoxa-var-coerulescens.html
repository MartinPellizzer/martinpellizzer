Phalaris paradoxa var. coerulescens

Phalaris paradoxa var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered love grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and striking clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions and its resistance to drought.
