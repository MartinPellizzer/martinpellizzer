Pulsatilla dahurica

Pulsatilla dahurica, commonly known as the European pasqueflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its foliage consists of palmately divided leaves that are hairy and dark green.

Pulsatilla dahurica is known for its early spring blooming habit, often emerging before the forest canopy fully develops.
