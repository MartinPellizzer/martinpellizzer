Piper cispontinum

Piper cispontinum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or cayenne pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the spicy compound capsaicin. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The botanical features of Piper cispontinum include opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into the characteristic peppery fruits.
