Polypodium musifolium

Polypodium musifolium, commonly known as the banana fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching fronds that resemble banana leaves, which can grow up to several feet in length. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with a central stem that produces new fronds from the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
