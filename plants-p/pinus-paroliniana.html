Pinus paroliniana

Pinus paroliniana, commonly known as the Parolin's pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of southern Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, elongated cones and needle-like leaves. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its high-altitude habitat, with a slow growth rate and specialized root systems for nutrient absorption in poor soils. Its cones are typically small and woody, releasing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
