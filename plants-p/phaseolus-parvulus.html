Phaseolus parvulus

Phaseolus parvulus, commonly known as the small bean or dwarf bean, is a species of legume native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant produces small, edible beans that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Phaseolus parvulus is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the vicinity.
