Phymatopsis stewartii var. nigropaleacea

Phymatopsis stewartii var. nigropaleacea, commonly known as the black paleaceous phymatopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark green, glabrous leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variant is distinguished by its darker foliage compared to other varieties of Phymatopsis stewartii.
