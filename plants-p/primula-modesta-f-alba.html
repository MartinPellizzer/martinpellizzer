Primula modesta f. alba

Primula modesta f. alba, commonly known as the white primrose, is a cultivar of the primrose species native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-20 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

This cultivar is valued for its delicate appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
