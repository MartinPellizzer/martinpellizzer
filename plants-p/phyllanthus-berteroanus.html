Phyllanthus berteroanus

Phyllanthus berteroanus, commonly known as the Brazilian pear, is a shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
