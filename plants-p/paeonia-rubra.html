Paeonia rubra

Paeonia rubra, commonly known as the red peony, is a species of flowering plant in the family Paeoniaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Russia and China. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to pink, with a diameter of up to 20 centimeters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

Paeonia rubra is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
