Pinelia lehmanniana

Pinelia lehmanniana, commonly known as Lehmann's pinelike plant, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family, which includes various pine and fir species. The plant is characterized by its coniferous appearance, with needle-like leaves and a compact, shrub-like growth habit. It typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
