Pilidiostigma tetramerum

Pilidiostigma tetramerum, commonly known as the four-lobed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the Apocynaceae family and can be toxic. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a distinct lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pilidiostigma tetramerum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats.
