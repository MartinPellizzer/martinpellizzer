Platea excelsa var. excelsa

Platea excelsa var. excelsa, commonly known as the towering sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, often peeling bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a habitat provider and its potential medicinal uses.
