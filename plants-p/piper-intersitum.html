Piper intersitum

Piper intersitum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale green flowers that develop into small, round berries. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental use in humid climates.
