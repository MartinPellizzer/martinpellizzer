Physalis urceolata

Physalis urceolata, commonly known as the inflated gooseberry or winter cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn bright red when mature, encased in a papery, inflated calyx. Its foliage consists of oval, hairy leaves with a distinctive scent when crushed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
