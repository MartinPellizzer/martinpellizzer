Pyrus amygdaliformis var. eriopleura

Pyrus amygdaliformis var. eriopleura, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its broad, oval leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fruit. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of climates.
