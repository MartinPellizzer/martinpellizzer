Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. livida

Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. livida, commonly known as the lavender phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid with unique botanical characteristics.

It features lance-shaped, light green leaves that are often tinged with a pale lavender hue, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces elegant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white to pale lavender, typically arranged in a raceme. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in both indoor and greenhouse settings.
