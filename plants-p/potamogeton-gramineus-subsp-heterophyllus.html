Potamogeton gramineus subsp. heterophyllus

Potamogeton gramineus subsp.

heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments. It is characterized by its flexible, grass-like leaves that can vary in shape and size depending on environmental conditions.

The plant typically grows in shallow waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and clustered, with both male and female parts present on the same inflorescence.

This subspecies plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
