Phaseolus × dumosus

Phaseolus × dumosus, commonly known as the bush bean, is a perennial legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and the production of small, edible pods containing round, tender seeds. The plant typically features trifoliate leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the familiar bean pods. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and culinary uses.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it a beneficial companion plant in agricultural settings.
