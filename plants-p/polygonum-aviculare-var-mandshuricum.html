Polygonum aviculare var. mandshuricum

Polygonum aviculare var. mandshuricum, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
