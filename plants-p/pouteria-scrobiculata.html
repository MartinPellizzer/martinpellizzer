Pouteria scrobiculata

Pouteria scrobiculata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods. The tree is well-known for its edible seeds, which are harvested as Brazil nuts. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pouteria scrobiculata plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
