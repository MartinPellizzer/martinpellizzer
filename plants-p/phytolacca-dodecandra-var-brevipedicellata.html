Phytolacca dodecandra var. brevipedicellata

Phytolacca dodecandra var. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed Indian mallow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
