Prunus divaricata var. pontica

Prunus divaricata var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, often twisted branches and small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
