Peperomia melanokirrocarpa

Peperomia melanokirrocarpa, commonly known as the black-veined peperomia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with prominent, contrasting veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia melanokirrocarpa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
