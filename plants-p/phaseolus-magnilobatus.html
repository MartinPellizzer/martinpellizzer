Phaseolus magnilobatus

Phaseolus magnilobatus, commonly known as the giant lima bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Andean region of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to produce sizable, bean-like pods. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds of this plant are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.
