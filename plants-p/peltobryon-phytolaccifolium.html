Peltobryon phytolaccifolium

Peltobryon phytolaccifolium, commonly known as the phytolaccifolium liverwort, is a type of non-vascular plant belonging to the family Peltobryaceae.

It is characterized by its leafy structure, with leaves that are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest floors or on rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures include capsules that release spores, a common feature among liverworts.

Peltobryon phytolaccifolium plays a role in soil stabilization and provides microhabitats for various small organisms.
