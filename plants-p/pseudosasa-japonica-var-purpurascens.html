Pseudosasa japonica var. purpurascens

Pseudosasa japonica var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a variant of the Japanese bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking purple stems and leaves, which add a unique ornamental value to gardens and landscapes. This plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It grows as a clump-forming grass, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its foliage turns a vibrant green in summer and may exhibit reddish hues in autumn, making it a visually appealing choice for horticultural use.
