Pandanus neomecklenburgensis

Pandanus neomecklenburgensis, commonly known as the Mecklenburg pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly in parts of Australia and New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color.

This species thrives in coastal and wetland environments, where it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
