Phyllanthus welwitschianus

Phyllanthus welwitschianus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a tropical shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments. Its fruit, which is a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
