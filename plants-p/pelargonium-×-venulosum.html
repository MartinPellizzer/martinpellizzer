Pelargonium × venulosum

Pelargonium × venulosum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its attractive, lobed leaves that often display variegation in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically in clusters at the ends of stems. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Pelargonium × venulosum is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
