Pelargonium angustifolium

Pelargonium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or red flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Pelargonium angustifolium is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
