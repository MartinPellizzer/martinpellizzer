Pleurothallis caudatisepala

Pleurothallis caudatisepala, commonly known as the caudate-sepaled orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its sepals are distinctive, with a long, tail-like extension that gives it its scientific name.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
