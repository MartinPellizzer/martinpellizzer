Phippsia vilfoidea subsp. sibirica

Phippsia vilfoidea subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian phippsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. It is adapted to withstand harsh winters and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
