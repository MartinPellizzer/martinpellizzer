Phegopteris fluminensis

Phegopteris fluminensis, commonly known as the river maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along riverbanks and in dense forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fronds are bioluminescent in some varieties, adding to its unique botanical features.
