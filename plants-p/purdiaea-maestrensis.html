Purdiaea maestrensis

Purdiaea maestrensis, commonly known as the Maestrense bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

Purdiaea maestrensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
