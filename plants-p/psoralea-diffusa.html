Psoralea diffusa

Psoralea diffusa, commonly known as the broadleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Psoralea diffusa is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
