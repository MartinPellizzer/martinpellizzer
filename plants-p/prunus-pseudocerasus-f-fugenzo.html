Prunus pseudocerasus f. fugenzo

Prunus pseudocerasus f. fugenzo, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry or the loquat, is a botanical variant of the loquat species.

It is characterized by its small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to China, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and its flowers are usually white or pink.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
