Pteridium aquilinum var. pinetorum

Pteridium aquilinum var. pinetorum, commonly known as the western bracken, is a variant of the common bracken that thrives in forested areas, particularly in coniferous and mixed woodlands.

This fern is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that can reach up to two meters in length, with a dark green, leathery texture. It reproduces via spores, which are released from the spore-bearing structures called sori located on the underside of the fronds. The plant is adapted to a wide range of soil types and can tolerate both acidic and alkaline conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
