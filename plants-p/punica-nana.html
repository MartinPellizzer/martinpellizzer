Punica nana

Punica nana, commonly known as dwarf pomegranate, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It features small, bright red flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by orange-red, spiky fruits. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in warm climates.
