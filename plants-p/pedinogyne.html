Pedinogyne

Pedinogyne, commonly known as the "spider plant," is a flowering plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its rosette of sword-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soil with moderate sunlight. It is drought-tolerant and often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pedinogyne is also noted for its ability to propagate through offsets, making it easy to cultivate.
