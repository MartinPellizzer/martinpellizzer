Pachira oleagina

Pachira oleagina, commonly known as the Mexican money plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers followed by distinctive red, spiny fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

Pachira oleagina is also popular for its symbolic association with good fortune and prosperity.
