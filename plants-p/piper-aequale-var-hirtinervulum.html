Piper aequale var. hirtinervulum

Piper aequale var. hirtinervulum, commonly known as the hairy-veined pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with hairy veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, peppery fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The variety hirtinervulum is distinguished by its dense, hairy veins, which set it apart from other Piper species.
