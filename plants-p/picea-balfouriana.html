Picea balfouriana

Picea balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subalpine and alpine zones.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
