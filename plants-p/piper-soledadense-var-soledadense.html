Piper soledadense var. soledadense

Piper soledadense var. soledadense, commonly known as the Soledad pepper or Soldean pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature. The berries are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
