Prometheum sempervivoides

Prometheum sempervivoides, commonly known as the Flameleaf Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or orange hues, especially under sunny conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to red during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
