Pueraria warburgii

Pueraria warburgii, commonly known as the Indian bean or velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant produces pods containing flat, bean-like seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems.

Pueraria warburgii also contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and flavonoids, which contribute to its medicinal properties.
