Piper tacamahaca

Piper tacamahaca, commonly known as the "Chilca" or "Peruvian pepper," is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its small, green, and often spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers that develop into small, round, and often peppery fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
