Platystele taylorii

Platystele taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's ghost plant, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate, and often translucent leaves. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme, and it is adapted to thrive in high humidity and shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss and its limited distribution, Platystele taylorii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
