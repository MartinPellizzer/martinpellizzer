Piper bolanicum

Piper bolanicum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a mild peppery aroma.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
