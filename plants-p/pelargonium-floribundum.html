Pelargonium floribundum

Pelargonium floribundum, commonly known as the sweet geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant produces a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium floribundum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
