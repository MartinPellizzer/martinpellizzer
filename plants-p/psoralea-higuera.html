Psoralea higuera

Psoralea higuera, commonly known as the Higuera plant, is a flowering species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss.

Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable for several years, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.
