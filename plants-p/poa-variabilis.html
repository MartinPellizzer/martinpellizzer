Poa variabilis

Poa variabilis, commonly known as variable bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to blue in color.

Poa variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
