Polygala subspicata

Polygala subspicata, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in dry, open habitats. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, leathery fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala subspicata is often found in grasslands and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
