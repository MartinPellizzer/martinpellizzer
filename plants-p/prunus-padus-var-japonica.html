Prunus padus var. japonica

Prunus padus var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features slender, arching branches and produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
