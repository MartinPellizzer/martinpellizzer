Pelargonium × megalanthum

Pelargonium × megalanthum, commonly known as the giant geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 5 inches in diameter, often in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, eye-catching blooms.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping and as a cut flower.
