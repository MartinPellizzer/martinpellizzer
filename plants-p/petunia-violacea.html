Petunia violacea

Petunia violacea, commonly known as the violet petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant violet to purple flowers, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant is a herbaceous annual, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Petunia violacea thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, easy-to-grow plants.
