Primula elatior subsp. corcontica

Primula elatior subsp.

corcontica, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lanceolate and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and woodland settings.
