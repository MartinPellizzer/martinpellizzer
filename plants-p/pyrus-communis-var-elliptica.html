Pyrus communis var. elliptica

Pyrus communis var. elliptica, commonly known as the spindle apple or spindle pear, is a cultivar of the common pear.

It is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped fruit that typically measures between 5 to 10 centimeters in length. The fruit has a smooth, thin skin that ranges in color from yellow to reddish-brown when ripe. It is native to Europe and is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
