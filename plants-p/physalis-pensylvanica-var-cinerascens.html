Physalis pensylvanica var. cinerascens

Physalis pensylvanica var. cinerascens, commonly known as the Pennsylvania groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyx that persists on the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.
