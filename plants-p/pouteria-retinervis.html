Pouteria retinervis

Pouteria retinervis, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a segmented, juicy interior with a sweet, slightly tart flavor.

Pouteria retinervis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
