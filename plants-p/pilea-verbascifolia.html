Pilea verbascifolia

Pilea verbascifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to dark purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Pilea verbascifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
