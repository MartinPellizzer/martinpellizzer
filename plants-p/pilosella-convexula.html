Pilosella convexula

Pilosella convexula, commonly known as the convexleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and often have a convex shape, giving it its scientific name.

Pilosella convexula produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
