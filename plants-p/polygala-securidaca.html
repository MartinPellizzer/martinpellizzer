Polygala securidaca

Polygala securidaca, commonly known as the golden mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems that often grow on trees, relying on its host for some of its nutrients. Its leaves are alternate and simple, typically appearing in pairs along the stems.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting various insect and bird species.
