Potentilla adscendens subsp. polyodonta

Potentilla adscendens subsp.

polyodonta, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged alternately along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
