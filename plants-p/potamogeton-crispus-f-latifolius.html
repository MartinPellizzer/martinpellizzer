Potamogeton crispus f. latifolius

Potamogeton crispus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has a submerged stem with numerous branches, and its leaves are usually smooth and slightly wavy in appearance. It produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spikes, and it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variant of Potamogeton crispus is notable for its distinctive foliage compared to other forms of the species.
