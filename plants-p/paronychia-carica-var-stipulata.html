Paronychia carica var. stipulata

Paronychia carica var. stipulata, commonly known as the stipulate goosefoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have prominent stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its foliage and resilience.
