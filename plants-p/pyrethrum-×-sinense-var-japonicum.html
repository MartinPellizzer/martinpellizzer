Pyrethrum × sinense var. japonicum

Pyrethrum × sinense var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its foliage is finely divided and often covered in a powdery coating, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is widely cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
