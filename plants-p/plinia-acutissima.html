Plinia acutissima

Plinia acutissima, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges and a glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
