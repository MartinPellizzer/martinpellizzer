Phyllomphax obcordata

Phyllomphax obcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental leaves, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticulture.
