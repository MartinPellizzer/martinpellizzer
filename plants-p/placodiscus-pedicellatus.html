Placodiscus pedicellatus

Placodiscus pedicellatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet or purple flowers that are borne on long pedicels, hence its scientific name. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Placodiscus pedicellatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
