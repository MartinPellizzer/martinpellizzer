Placodiscus angustifolius

Placodiscus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf placodiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are typically white or pale pink in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
