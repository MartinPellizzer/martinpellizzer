Piaranthus fuscatus

Piaranthus fuscatus, commonly known as the dark sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Piaranthus fuscatus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
