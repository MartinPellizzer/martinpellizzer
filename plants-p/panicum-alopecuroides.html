Panicum alopecuroides

Panicum alopecuroides, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features fine, silver-green leaves with a silky texture, and its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscapes for its attractive appearance.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
