Paspalum huberi

Paspalum huberi, commonly known as the Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum huberi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
