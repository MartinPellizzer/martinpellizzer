Podochilus vieillardii

Podochilus vieillardii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, often as an epiphyte on trees. Its botanical features include long, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
