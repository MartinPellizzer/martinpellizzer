Piper umbellatum var. vestitum

Piper umbellatum var. vestitum, commonly known as the velvet pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by elongated, narrow berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
