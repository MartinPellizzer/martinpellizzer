Paspalum griffithianum

Paspalum griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum griffithianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
