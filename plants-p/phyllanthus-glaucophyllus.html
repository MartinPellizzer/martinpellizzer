Phyllanthus glaucophyllus

Phyllanthus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the blue-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that often exhibit a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
