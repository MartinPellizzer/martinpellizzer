Plectranthus moslifolius

Plectranthus moslifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to violet. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.

Plectranthus moslifolius is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant.
