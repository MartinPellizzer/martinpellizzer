Porophyllum ruderale var. macrolepidium

Porophyllum ruderale var. macrolepidium, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy yellow-orange flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces seeds in elongated, slender capsules, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
