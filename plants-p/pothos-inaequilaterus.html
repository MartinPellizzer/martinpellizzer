Pothos inaequilaterus

Pothos inaequilaterus, commonly known as the Variegated Pothos, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that display a striking variegation of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden beneath leaf-like bracts. It thrives in low-light conditions and is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and ornamental appeal.

Pothos inaequilaterus is also known for its ability to purify the air and is often used in interior design to enhance both aesthetics and air quality.
