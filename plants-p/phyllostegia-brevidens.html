Phyllostegia brevidens

Phyllostegia brevidens, commonly known as the narrowleaf stiltgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
