Piper pallidum

Piper pallidum, commonly known as the pale pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is characterized by its pale-colored fruits and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
