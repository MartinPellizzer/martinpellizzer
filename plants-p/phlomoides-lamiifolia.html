Phlomoides lamiifolia

Phlomoides lamiifolia, commonly known as silverleaf phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
