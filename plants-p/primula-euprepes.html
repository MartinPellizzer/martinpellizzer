Primula euprepes

Primula euprepes, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
