Philadelphus satsumi f. nikoensis

Philadelphus satsumi f. nikoensis, commonly known as the Nikko mock orange, is a cultivar of the Philadelphus genus native to Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1.5 to 3 meters and features simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
