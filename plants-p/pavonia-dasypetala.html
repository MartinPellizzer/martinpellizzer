Pavonia dasypetala

Pavonia dasypetala, commonly known as the roughleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pavonia dasypetala is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
