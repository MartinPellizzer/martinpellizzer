Piper laxum

Piper laxum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper laxum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
