Primula warshenewskiana subsp. olgae

Primula warshenewskiana subsp.

olgae, commonly known as the Olga's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, clustered in cymes, and bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable microclimates.
