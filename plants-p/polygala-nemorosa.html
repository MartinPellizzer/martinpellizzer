Polygala nemorosa

Polygala nemorosa, commonly known as the wood spurge or lesser polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in woodlands, hedgerows, and rocky areas, preferring moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelike inflorescences, often with a distinctive, spurred structure.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
