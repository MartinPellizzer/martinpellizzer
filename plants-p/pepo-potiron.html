Pepo potiron

Pepo potiron, commonly known as pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its thick, hard outer rind and fleshy interior.

It is a type of summer squash that typically grows on a vine, producing fruits that vary in shape, size, and color. The plant has large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. The fruit's outer layer, or pepo, is a modified berry that develops from the flower's ovary.

Pepo potiron is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and versatility in culinary uses.
