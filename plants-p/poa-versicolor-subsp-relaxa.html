Poa versicolor subsp. relaxa

Poa versicolor subsp.

relaxa, commonly known as the variable bluegrass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The plant typically produces inflorescences of spikelets that are blue-green in color, which contribute to its common name. It is native to parts of Europe and has been introduced to other regions, including North America.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils.
