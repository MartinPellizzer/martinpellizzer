Piptocoma schomburgkii

Piptocoma schomburgkii, commonly known as the Schomburgk's pittosporum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

Piptocoma schomburgkii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
