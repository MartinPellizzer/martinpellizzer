Pelargonium × eryngii

Pelargonium × eryngii, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping and floral arrangements.
