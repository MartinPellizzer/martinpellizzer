Potentilla sylvestris subvar. integristipula

Potentilla sylvestris subvar. integristipula, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are finely divided, giving the plant a delicate, feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
