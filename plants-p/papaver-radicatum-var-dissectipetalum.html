Papaver radicatum var. dissectipetalum

Papaver radicatum var. dissectipetalum, commonly known as the dissect-leaved poppy, is a variant of the Persian poppy characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves.

It produces striking, showy flowers with bright red petals that are often adorned with a dark purple or black central blotch. The plant is native to regions of Central Asia and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant and unique floral display.
