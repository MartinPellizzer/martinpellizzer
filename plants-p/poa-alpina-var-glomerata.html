Poa alpina var. glomerata

Poa alpina var. glomerata, commonly known as the Alpine bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted shoots and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered together, giving it its varietal name "glomerata.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its foliage is typically dark green and remains dense throughout the growing season.
