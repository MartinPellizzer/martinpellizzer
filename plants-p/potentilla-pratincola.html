Potentilla pratincola

Potentilla pratincola, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a splash of color to its alpine habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

Potentilla pratincola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in mountainous terrain.
