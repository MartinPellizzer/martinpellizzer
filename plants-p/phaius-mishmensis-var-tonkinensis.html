Phaius mishmensis var. tonkinensis

Phaius mishmensis var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green, with a unique, intricate pattern on the petals. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along riverbanks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in conservation efforts.
