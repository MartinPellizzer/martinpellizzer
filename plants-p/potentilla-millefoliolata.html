Potentilla millefoliolata

Potentilla millefoliolata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
