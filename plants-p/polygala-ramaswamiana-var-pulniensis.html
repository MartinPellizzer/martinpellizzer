Polygala ramaswamiana var. pulniensis

Polygala ramaswamiana var. pulniensis, commonly known as the Nilgiri milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
