Prunus domestica var. brignola

Prunus domestica var. brignola, commonly known as the Brignola plum, is a cultivar of the European plum.

It is characterized by its medium to large, oval-shaped fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a deep red to purple skin with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It is typically used for fresh consumption, jams, and preserves.

This variety is well-suited to temperate climates and requires a chilling period for proper fruit development.
