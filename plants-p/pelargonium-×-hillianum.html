Pelargonium × hillianum

Pelargonium × hillianum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant typically has upright stems and serrated, lobed leaves that are often fuzzy or hairy. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.

Pelargonium × hillianum is often cultivated as a bedding plant or in containers due to its compact growth habit.
