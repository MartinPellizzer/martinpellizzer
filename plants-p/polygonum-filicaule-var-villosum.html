Polygonum filicaule var. villosum

Polygonum filicaule var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a variant of the common polygonum species.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, velvety texture.

It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer.
