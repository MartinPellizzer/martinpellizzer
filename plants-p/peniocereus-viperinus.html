Peniocereus viperinus

Peniocereus viperinus, commonly known as the night-blooming star cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 inches. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that open at night. These flowers emit a sweet fragrance and are pollinated by nocturnal insects such as moths.

The plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
