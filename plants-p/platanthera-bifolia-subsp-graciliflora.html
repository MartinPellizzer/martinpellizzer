Platanthera bifolia subsp. graciliflora

Platanthera bifolia subsp.

graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered twayblade, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a perennial herb with a slender, leafless stem that often grows in shaded, moist environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It lacks true leaves, relying instead on its underground rhizomes for photosynthesis.

This subspecies is found in temperate regions of North America and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
