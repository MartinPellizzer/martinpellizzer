Potamogeton zosterifolius subsp. zosteriformis

Potamogeton zosterifolius subsp.

zosteriformis, commonly known as the ribbonweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and have a distinct midrib.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
