Plumbago scandens

Plumbago scandens, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a vigorous climber with slender, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
