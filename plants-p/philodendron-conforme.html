Philodendron conforme

Philodendron conforme, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with light green or white markings. The plant typically grows horizontally along the forest floor, using its aerial roots to climb and support itself. Its stems are thick and fibrous, allowing it to spread rapidly in its natural habitat.

Philodendron conforme is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
