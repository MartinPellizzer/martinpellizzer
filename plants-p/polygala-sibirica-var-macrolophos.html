Polygala sibirica var. macrolophos

Polygala sibirica var. macrolophos, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
