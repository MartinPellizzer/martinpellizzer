Phragmites australis subsp. maritimus

Phragmites australis subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as salt marsh cordgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height.

This subspecies is highly adapted to saline and brackish wetland environments. Its rhizomes form dense stands that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant also provides habitat and food for various wildlife species.
