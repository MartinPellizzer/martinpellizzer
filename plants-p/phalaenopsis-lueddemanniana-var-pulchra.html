Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. pulchra

Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. pulchra, commonly known as the Orchid of the Moon, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant flower spikes that bear numerous white to pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant has broad, elliptical leaves that are typically a deep green color with a waxy texture. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

This variety is known for its relatively long blooming period, often lasting several months.
