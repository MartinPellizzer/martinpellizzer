Potentilla recta subsp. tuberosa

Potentilla recta subsp.

tuberosa, commonly known as the tuberous cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has distinctive, five-lobed leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. Its root system includes tuberous structures, which are adapted for storage and propagation.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.
