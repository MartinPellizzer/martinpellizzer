Papaver balangense

Papaver balangense, commonly known as the Balangeng poppy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange petals that resemble those of other poppy species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers bloom in early spring and are followed by seed capsules that release small, black seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
