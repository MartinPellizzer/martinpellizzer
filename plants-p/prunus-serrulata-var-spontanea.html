Prunus serrulata var. spontanea

Prunus serrulata var. spontanea, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or some varieties as the mountain cherry, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The species is well adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
