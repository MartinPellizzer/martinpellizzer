Pilosella × obornyana

Pilosella × obornyana, commonly known as the European mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Pilosella × obornyana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
