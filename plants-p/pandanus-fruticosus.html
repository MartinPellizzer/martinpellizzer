Pandanus fruticosus

Pandanus fruticosus, commonly known as the pandanus shrub, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in local cuisines and crafts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
