Polypodium blanchetii

Polypodium blanchetii, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect sunlight. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies over time.

Polypodium blanchetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
