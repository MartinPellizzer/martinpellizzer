Procris piperifolia

Procris piperifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant is characterized by its broad, smooth leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, aquatic or semi-aquatic environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Procris piperifolia is valued for its edible leaves, which have a peppery taste similar to watercress.
