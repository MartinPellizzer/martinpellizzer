Phyllanthus mimosifolius

Phyllanthus mimosifolius, commonly known as the mimosa-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that resemble those of the mimosa tree, with multiple small leaflets arranged along a central axis. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
