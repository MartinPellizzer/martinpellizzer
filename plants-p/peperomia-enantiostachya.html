Peperomia enantiostachya

Peperomia enantiostachya, commonly known as the zigzag peperomia, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or purple, which are followed by tiny, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique leaf patterns and ease of care contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
