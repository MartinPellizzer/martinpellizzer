Panicum nemorosum

Panicum nemorosum, commonly known as wood panicgrass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, typically found in moist woodland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental grass.

Panicum nemorosum plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
