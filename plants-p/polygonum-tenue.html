Polygonum tenue

Polygonum tenue, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly toothed margin, and they are often hairy underneath.

Polygonum tenue is commonly found in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas.
