Plantago setacea var. lanosa

Plantago setacea var. lanosa, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces upright flowering stalks bearing small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in gardens and along roadsides.
