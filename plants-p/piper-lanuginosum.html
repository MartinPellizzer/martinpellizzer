Piper lanuginosum

Piper lanuginosum, commonly known as the silky pipevine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species known for their aromatic properties. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The berries it produces are typically bright red and serve as a food source for various bird species.
