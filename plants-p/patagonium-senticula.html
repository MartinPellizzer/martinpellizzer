Patagonium senticula

Patagonium senticula, commonly known as the scentless Patagonian grass, is a perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized ecological requirements, Patagonium senticula is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
