Pandanus aragoensis

Pandanus aragoensis, commonly known as the Arago pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the island of Arago in the Indian Ocean, specifically the Comoros archipelago. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the plant.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
