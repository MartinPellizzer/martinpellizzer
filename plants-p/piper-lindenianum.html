Piper lindenianum

Piper lindenianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper lindenianum make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological studies.
