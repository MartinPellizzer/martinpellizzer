Polygonum criopolitanum

Polygonum criopolitanum, commonly known as the creeping knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
