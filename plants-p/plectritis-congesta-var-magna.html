Plectritis congesta var. magna

Plectritis congesta var. magna, commonly known as the giant clustered filaree, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The species is notable for its showy inflorescences and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.
