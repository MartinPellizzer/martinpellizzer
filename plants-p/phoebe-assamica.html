Phoebe assamica

Phoebe assamica, commonly known as the Assam beech, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate forests.

Phoebe assamica is valued for its ecological role and is also used in traditional medicine.
