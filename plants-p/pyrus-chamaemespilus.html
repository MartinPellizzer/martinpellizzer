Pyrus chamaemespilus

Pyrus chamaemespilus, commonly known as the creeping whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Pyrus chamaemespilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
