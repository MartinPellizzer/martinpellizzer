Palicourea pentandra

Palicourea pentandra, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple passionvine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, often clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Palicourea pentandra is valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
