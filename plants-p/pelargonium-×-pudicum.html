Pelargonium × pudicum

Pelargonium × pudicum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, velvety leaves that emit a variety of scents, such as citrus, mint, or spice, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its aromatic foliage also makes it a valuable plant for herbal and essential oil extraction.
