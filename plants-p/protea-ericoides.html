Protea ericoides

Protea ericoides, commonly known as the silver leaf protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower, composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with mild, moist winters and dry summers. Its leaves are silvery-gray, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
