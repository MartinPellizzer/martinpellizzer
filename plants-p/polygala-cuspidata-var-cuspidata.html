Polygala cuspidata var. cuspidata

Polygala cuspidata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and forest edges. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or water.
