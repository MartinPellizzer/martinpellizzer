Peritoma aurea

Peritoma aurea, commonly known as the golden shower vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or vine that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers bloom in summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.
