Prunus iwagiensis

Prunus iwagiensis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that turns red when mature.

Prunus iwagiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
