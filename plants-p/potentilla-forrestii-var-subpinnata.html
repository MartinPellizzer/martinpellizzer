Potentilla forrestii var. subpinnata

Potentilla forrestii var. subpinnata, commonly known as the subpinnate potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with finely divided leaflets that give it a delicate appearance. It produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
