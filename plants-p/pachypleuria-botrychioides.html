Pachypleuria botrychioides

Pachypleuria botrychioides, commonly known as the clustered bramble, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered together, giving it its species name "botrychioides," which refers to its grape-like inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
