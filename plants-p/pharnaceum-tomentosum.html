Pharnaceum tomentosum

Pharnaceum tomentosum, commonly known as the wooly pharnaceum, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
