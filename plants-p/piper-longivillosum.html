Piper longivillosum

Piper longivillosum, commonly known as the long-hair pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its finely pubescent stems and leaves, which have a distinctive, rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
