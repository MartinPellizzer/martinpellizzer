Potentilla cinerea proles velutina

Potentilla cinerea proles velutina, commonly known as the silky cinerea potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in a fine, silky layer of hair, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a soft, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
