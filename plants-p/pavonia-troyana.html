Pavonia troyana

Pavonia troyana, commonly known as the Troyan pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a slight pubescence.

Pavonia troyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
