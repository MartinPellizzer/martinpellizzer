Pseuderanthemum sylvestre

Pseuderanthemum sylvestre, commonly known as wild pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
