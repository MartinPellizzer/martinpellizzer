Pourretia bonplandiana

Pourretia bonplandiana, commonly known as the Bonplandian star-of-Bahia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in sandy soils along the shoreline, exhibiting succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
