Primula cusickiana subsp. domensis

Primula cusickiana subsp.

domensis, commonly known as the Domestic primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at elevations above 2,000 meters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
