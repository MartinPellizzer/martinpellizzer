Primula vialii

Primula vialii, commonly known as the Vial's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

Primula vialii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
