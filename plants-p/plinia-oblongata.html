Plinia oblongata

Plinia oblongata, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dark, edible berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit, which is small and dark purple when ripe, is prized for its intense flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
