Piper cubeba f. cambodiana

Piper cubeba f. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian black pepper, is a variant of the black pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family, characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

The botanical features of this plant make it distinct from other Piper species, particularly in its fruit morphology and habitat preferences.
