Pilosocereus superbus

Pilosocereus superbus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that form a dense, rounded structure. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the night, which are pollinated by moths. Its adaptations to extreme desert conditions include a thick waxy cuticle and the ability to store water in its stems.

Pilosocereus superbus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
