Parapholis gracilis

Parapholis gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as along stream banks and in marshy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and often hairy.

Parapholis gracilis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
