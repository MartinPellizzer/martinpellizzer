Pseudopilocereus pernambucoensis

Pseudopilocereus pernambucoensis, commonly known as the Pernambuco cactus, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its structure consists of cylindrical stems with areoles, from which spines and flowers emerge.

Pseudopilocereus pernambucoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to harsh conditions.
