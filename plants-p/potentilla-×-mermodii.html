Potentilla × mermodii

Potentilla × mermodii, commonly known as the Mermod's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has dark green, serrated leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
