Prunus jamasakura f. grandis

Prunus jamasakura f. grandis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are dark green and turn vibrant red in the fall.

This variety is highly valued for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in Japan.
