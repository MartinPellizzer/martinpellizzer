Pelargonium caylae

Pelargonium caylae, commonly known as Cayla's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are usually rounded and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium caylae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
