Phaius flavus f. punctatus

Phaius flavus f. punctatus, commonly known as the spotted white orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with distinctive dark purple or brown spots on the petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a compact, pseudobulbous stem structure.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or specialized collections.
