Pothos rumphii

Pothos rumphii, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is known for its rapid growth, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pothos rumphii is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive with minimal care.
