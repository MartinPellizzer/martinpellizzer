Prunus pojarkovii

Prunus pojarkovii, commonly known as the Siberian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East of Russia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed structure and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red berries that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Prunus pojarkovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
