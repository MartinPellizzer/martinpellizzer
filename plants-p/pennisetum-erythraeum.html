Pennisetum erythraeum

Pennisetum erythraeum, commonly known as bloodgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It features striking red to purple flower plumes that contrast with its green foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pennisetum erythraeum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
