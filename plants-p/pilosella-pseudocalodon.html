Pilosella pseudocalodon

Pilosella pseudocalodon, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-ear, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
