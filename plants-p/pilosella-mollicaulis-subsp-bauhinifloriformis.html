Pilosella mollicaulis subsp. bauhinifloriformis

Pilosella mollicaulis subsp.

bauhinifloriformis, commonly known as the woolly cat's-paw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
