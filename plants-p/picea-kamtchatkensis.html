Picea kamtchatkensis

Picea kamtchatkensis, commonly known as the Kamchatka spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and its needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The Kamchatka spruce plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
