Pyrus longepedunculata

Pyrus longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated peduncles that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus longepedunculata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive floral display and compact growth habit.
