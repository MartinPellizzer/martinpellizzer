Polythecium macranthum

Polythecium macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered polythecium, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Polytheciaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, large petals that are typically a vibrant shade of pink or red. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

The flowers are borne in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
