Potamogeton perversus

Potamogeton perversus, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and marshes.

Potamogeton perversus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
