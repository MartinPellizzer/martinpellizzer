Pleopeltis valida

Pleopeltis valida, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually bright green with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, contributing to its ornamental value.

It prefers indirect light and consistently moist soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
