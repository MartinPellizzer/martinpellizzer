Piper epigynium

Piper epigynium, commonly known as the epigynous pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it bears small, round berries that turn red when mature. The berries contain a single seed and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in tropical flora and ecological studies.
