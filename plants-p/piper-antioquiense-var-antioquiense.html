Piper antioquiense var. antioquiense

Piper antioquiense var. antioquiense, commonly known as the Antioquian pepper, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
