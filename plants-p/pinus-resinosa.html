Pinus resinosa

Pinus resinosa, commonly known as the pitch pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in pairs. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Pitch pine is adapted to fire-prone environments and can regenerate from heat-treated seeds.

Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against forest fires.
