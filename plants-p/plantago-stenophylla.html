Plantago stenophylla

Plantago stenophylla, commonly known as narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
