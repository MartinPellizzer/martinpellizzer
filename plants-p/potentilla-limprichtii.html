Potentilla limprichtii

Potentilla limprichtii, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually spiny.

Potentilla limprichtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
