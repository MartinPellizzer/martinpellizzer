Pelargonium buffonianum

Pelargonium buffonianum, commonly known as the Buffoon's geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
