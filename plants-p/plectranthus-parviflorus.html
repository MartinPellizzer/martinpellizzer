Plectranthus parviflorus

Plectranthus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flower plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. The plant has aromatic leaves that release a mint-like scent when crushed, making it popular in horticulture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used as a decorative ground cover or in containers.

Plectranthus parviflorus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
