Polygonum hermonis

Polygonum hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon polygonum, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Polygonum hermonis typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.
