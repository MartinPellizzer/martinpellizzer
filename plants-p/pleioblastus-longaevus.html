Pleioblastus longaevus

Pleioblastus longaevus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy seed heads.

Pleioblastus longaevus is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover, providing both aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
