Phaseolus helvolus var. missouriensis

Phaseolus helvolus var. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri lima bean, is a wild legume native to the United States, particularly Missouri.

It is characterized by its indeterminate growth habit and large, edible pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. Its botanical features include hairy stems, compound leaves, and a fibrous root system.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability to local climates.
