Phagnalon purpurascens

Phagnalon purpurascens, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-tinged leaves and showy, yellow flower heads that bloom in summer. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits that contain a single seed, facilitating wind dispersal.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
