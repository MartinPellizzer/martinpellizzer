Paphiopedilum singchii

Paphiopedilum singchii, commonly known as the Sing Chi's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a prominent pouch-like lip that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are dark green and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Paphiopedilum singchii blooms with colorful flowers, usually in shades of pink, white, and red, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
