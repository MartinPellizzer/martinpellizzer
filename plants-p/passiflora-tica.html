Passiflora tica

Passiflora tica, commonly known as the Ticos passionflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The Ticos passionflower is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
