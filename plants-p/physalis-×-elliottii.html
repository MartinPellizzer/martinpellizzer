Physalis × elliottii

Physalis × elliottii, commonly known as the Elliot's groundcherry, is a hybrid species within the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyces that enclose the small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with spreading stems and lobed leaves. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its fruits are often used in culinary applications, similar to other Physalis species.
