Palladia

Palladia, commonly known as the white hellebore, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a toxic sap containing cardiac glycosides, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Palladia is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties, though its use is limited due to its toxicity.
