Parinari barbata

Parinari barbata, commonly known as the hairy parinari, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, lowland areas and is often found in secondary forests.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has some traditional medicinal uses.
