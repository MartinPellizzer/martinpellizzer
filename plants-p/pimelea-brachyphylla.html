Pimelea brachyphylla

Pimelea brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved riceflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native gardens.
