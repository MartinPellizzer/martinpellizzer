Poa vallesiana

Poa vallesiana, commonly known as the Valles grass, is a species of perennial grass native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes.

Poa vallesiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
