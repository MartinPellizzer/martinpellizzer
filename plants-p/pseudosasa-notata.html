Pseudosasa notata

Pseudosasa notata, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or Japanese sweet bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to East Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to form dense clumps.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
