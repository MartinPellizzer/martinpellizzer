Polygonum confertum

Polygonum confertum, commonly known as the broadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late summer and early fall.
