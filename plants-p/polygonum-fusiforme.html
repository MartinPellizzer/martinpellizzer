Polygonum fusiforme

Polygonum fusiforme, commonly known as the spindle-shaped knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense stands, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Polygonum fusiforme is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it both a common weed and an important ecological indicator species.
