Prunus jamasakura f. shogetsu

Prunus jamasakura f. shogetsu, commonly known as the late blooming cherry tree, is a unique variation of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its delayed flowering period, typically occurring later in the spring compared to other Prunus species. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring, creating a striking visual display. Its foliage is dark green and dense, providing an attractive backdrop during the growing season.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
