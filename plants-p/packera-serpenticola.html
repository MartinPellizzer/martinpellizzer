Packera serpenticola

Packera serpenticola, commonly known as the snake's head ragwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowering stems bear small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Packera serpenticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
