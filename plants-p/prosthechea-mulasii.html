Prosthechea mulasii

Prosthechea mulasii, commonly known as the Mulas' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique rosette-like growth pattern and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the soil.

Prosthechea mulasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
