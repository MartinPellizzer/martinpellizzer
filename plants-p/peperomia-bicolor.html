Peperomia bicolor

Peperomia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and reddish or pinkish on the underside, creating a striking visual contrast. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale green, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia bicolor is popular for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a favored choice for tabletop displays and hanging baskets.
