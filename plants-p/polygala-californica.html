Polygala californica

Polygala californica, commonly known as the California milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
