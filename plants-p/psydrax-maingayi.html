Psydrax maingayi

Psydrax maingayi, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive fruits and foliage.
