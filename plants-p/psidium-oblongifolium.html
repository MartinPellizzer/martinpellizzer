Psidium oblongifolium

Psidium oblongifolium, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or oblong-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, fragrant fruits. The fruit is typically oblong in shape, with a smooth skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
