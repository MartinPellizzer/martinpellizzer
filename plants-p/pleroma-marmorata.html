Pleroma marmorata

Pleroma marmorata, commonly known as the marble orchid, is a striking species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, spurred flowers that display a unique marbled pattern of white and purple. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with a slender stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its blooming period occurs during the winter months, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Pleroma marmorata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
