Prunus crataegifolia

Prunus crataegifolia, commonly known as the common dogwood, is a small deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually arranged opposite on the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Prunus crataegifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
