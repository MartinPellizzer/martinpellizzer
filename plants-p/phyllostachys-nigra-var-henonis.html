Phyllostachys nigra var. henonis

Phyllostachys nigra var. henonis, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variety of bamboo characterized by its dark, almost black stems and dense, upright growth habit.

It is native to China and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color, adding a striking visual contrast to its dark stems. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows relatively slowly, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to urban conditions.
