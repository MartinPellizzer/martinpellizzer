Plantago sericea var. congesta

Plantago sericea var. congesta, commonly known as silverleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
