Pandanus megacarpus

Pandanus megacarpus, commonly known as the bottle palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The species is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
