Peperomia praeruptorum

Peperomia praeruptorum, commonly known as the orange peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that can vary in color from deep green to reddish tones. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, often arranged in clusters.

Peperomia praeruptorum is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.
