Pilosocereus laniflorus

Pilosocereus laniflorus, commonly known as the woolly cactus or laniflorous cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy succulent.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a distinctive and interesting botanical specimen.
