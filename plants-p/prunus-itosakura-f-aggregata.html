Prunus itosakura f. aggregata

Prunus itosakura f. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered Japanese apricot, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use. Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage and flowering display, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
