Piper paraguassuanum

Piper paraguassuanum, commonly known as the Paraguassu pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and pungent, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This plant is valued both for its medicinal properties and as a spice in traditional cuisines.
