Piper azupizuanum

Piper azupizuanum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, peppery berry that contains a single seed, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
