Persea hemsleyi

Persea hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's avocado, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a smooth, egg-shaped berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in commercial cultivation.
