Penstemon miniatus subsp. apateticus

Penstemon miniatus subsp.

apateticus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky, dry habitats such as meadows and slopes.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, tubular, and typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal in gardens.
