Piper espejuelanum

Piper espejuelanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into peppery, edible fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.

The plant's botanical structure includes a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it attach to supports as it grows.
