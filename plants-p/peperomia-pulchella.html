Peperomia pulchella

Peperomia pulchella, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are often a deep green with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive appearance make it a favored plant for both novice and experienced growers.
