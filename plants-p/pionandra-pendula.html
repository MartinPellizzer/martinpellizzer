Pionandra pendula

Pionandra pendula, commonly known as the weeping lily, is a striking flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, drooping stems that bear clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense basal rosette.

Pionandra pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
