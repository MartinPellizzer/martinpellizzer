Papaver alaskanum var. latilobum

Papaver alaskanum var. latilobum, commonly known as the Alaska poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features showy, bright orange to red flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped structure. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
