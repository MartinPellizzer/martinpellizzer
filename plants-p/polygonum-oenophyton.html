Polygonum oenophyton

Polygonum oenophyton, commonly known as red burreed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species with distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a rough, sandpapery texture.

Polygonum oenophyton is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.
