Prunus × lannesiana f. gosiozakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. gosiozakura, commonly known as the Yoshino cherry, is a hybrid cultivar of Prunus species, notable for its ornamental value.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom profusely in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and smooth, light grey bark. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
