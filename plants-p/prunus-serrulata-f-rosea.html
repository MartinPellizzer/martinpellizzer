Prunus serrulata f. rosea

Prunus serrulata f. rosea, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with pink flowers, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink-pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to gardens and landscapes. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 5 to 10 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, red fruits in the fall.

This variety is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in parks and along streets for its seasonal display.
