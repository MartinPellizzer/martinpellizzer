Polygala spinescens var. aspalathoides

Polygala spinescens var. aspalathoides, commonly known as the aspalathoid false milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which is often narrow and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
