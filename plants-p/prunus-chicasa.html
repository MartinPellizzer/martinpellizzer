Prunus chicasa

Prunus chicasa, commonly known as the Mexican wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen to a dark red or black color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands or rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
