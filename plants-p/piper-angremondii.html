Piper angremondii

Piper angremondii, commonly known as the Amazonian black pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces tiny, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with adventitious roots and a complex vascular system.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high rainfall for optimal growth.
