Panicum helopus var. glabrescens

Panicum helopus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are generally smooth to the touch. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are often tinged with red or purple hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
