Poa eminens var. alexejii

Poa eminens var. alexejii, commonly known as the Alexejov's tall bluegrass, is a variant of the tall bluegrass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This grass is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
