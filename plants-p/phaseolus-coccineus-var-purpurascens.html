Phaseolus coccineus var. purpurascens

Phaseolus coccineus var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple stems and leaves, which contrast with its bright red, edible pods. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its seeds are enclosed within the fleshy, pod-like fruits that mature to a yellow or orange hue.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
