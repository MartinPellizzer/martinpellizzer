Piper genuflexum

Piper genuflexum, commonly known as the hanging pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery aroma when crushed.

Piper genuflexum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
