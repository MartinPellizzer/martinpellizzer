Prunus communis var. spinosa

Prunus communis var. spinosa, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in cooking. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and autumn foliage.
