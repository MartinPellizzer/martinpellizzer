Pyrrhocactus curvispinus

Pyrrhocactus curvispinus, commonly known as the curved-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in short, hooked spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh environments make it a notable species in desert flora.
