Pisum sativum var. dicksonii

Pisum sativum var. dicksonii, commonly known as the Dickson pea, is a botanical variety of the pea plant characterized by its distinctive pod and seed morphology.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates, exhibiting a climbing growth habit with tendrils that aid in its ascent. The plant produces small, typically green pods that contain several edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic and edible qualities.
