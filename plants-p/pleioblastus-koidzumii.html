Pleioblastus koidzumii

Pleioblastus koidzumii, commonly known as the Japanese silvergrass, is a deciduous grass native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching blades with a silvery-blue sheen, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are followed by feathery seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its foliage turns a vibrant gold in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
