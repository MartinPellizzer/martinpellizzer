Pentatropis senegalensis

Pentatropis senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in panicles. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
