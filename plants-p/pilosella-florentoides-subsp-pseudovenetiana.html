Pilosella florentoides subsp. pseudovenetiana

Pilosella florentoides subsp.

pseudovenetiana, commonly known as the false Venetian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies.

The plant features finely hairy stems and leaves, with small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological and horticultural interest.
