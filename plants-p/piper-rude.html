Piper rude

Piper rude, commonly known as roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

Piper rude is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various herbal remedies.
