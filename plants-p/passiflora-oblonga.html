Passiflora oblonga

Passiflora oblonga, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, blue, and purple hues, with intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Passiflora oblonga is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
