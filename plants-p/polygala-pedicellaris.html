Polygala pedicellaris

Polygala pedicellaris, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive spurred petal. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Polygala pedicellaris is valued in traditional medicine and is also appreciated for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
