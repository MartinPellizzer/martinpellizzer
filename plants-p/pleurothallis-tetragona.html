Pleurothallis tetragona

Pleurothallis tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, often epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive four-angled stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and growth pattern, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
