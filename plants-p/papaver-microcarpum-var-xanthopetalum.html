Papaver microcarpum var. xanthopetalum

Papaver microcarpum var. xanthopetalum, commonly known as the yellow-petaled poppy, is a variant of the lesser poppy native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
