Plantago daltonii

Plantago daltonii, commonly known as Dalton's plantain, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a resilient species adapted to arid environments.
