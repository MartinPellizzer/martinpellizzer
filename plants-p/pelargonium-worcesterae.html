Pelargonium worcesterae

Pelargonium worcesterae, commonly known as the Worcester geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with upright, hairy stems and clusters of small, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a fuzzy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
