Pseudolobivia boyuibensis

Pseudolobivia boyuibensis, commonly known as the Boyuibensis lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and spiny stems. The plant features small, paddle-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, adapted to store water efficiently. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
