Pimelea cracens subsp. glabra

Pimelea cracens subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

The plant has smooth, often grey-green leaves that are lance-shaped and densely arranged along the stems. It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal and inland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
