Pereskia pititache

Pereskia pititache, commonly known as the pititache cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that are often mistaken for shrubs rather than typical cacti. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season, adding a striking visual element to its structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Pereskia pititache is also notable for its ability to store water in its stems, making it well-adapted to its native habitat.
