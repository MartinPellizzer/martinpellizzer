Piper longifilamentum

Piper longifilamentum, commonly known as the long-filament pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that contains several seeds, and it is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
