Phlomis muliensis

Phlomis muliensis, commonly known as the Muli phlomis, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
