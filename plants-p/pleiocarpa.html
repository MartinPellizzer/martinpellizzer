Pleiocarpa

Pleiocarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Its fruits are typically capsules that split open to release numerous small seeds.
