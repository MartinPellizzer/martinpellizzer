Pyrus kitaibeliana

Pyrus kitaibeliana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine white pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, green fruits that ripen to a yellowish-orange color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant form and seasonal interest.

The species is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
