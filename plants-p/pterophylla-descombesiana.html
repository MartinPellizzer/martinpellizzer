Pterophylla descombesiana

Pterophylla descombesiana, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Pterophylla descombesiana is often found in coastal areas and is a symbol of resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
