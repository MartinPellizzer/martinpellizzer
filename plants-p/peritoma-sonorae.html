Peritoma sonorae

Peritoma sonorae, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and trailing.

Peritoma sonorae is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
