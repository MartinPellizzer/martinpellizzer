Phalaenopsis stuartiana

Phalaenopsis stuartiana, commonly known as the Stuart's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms once a year, with flowers lasting several months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis stuartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
