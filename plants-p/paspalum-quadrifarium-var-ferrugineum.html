Paspalum quadrifarium var. ferrugineum

Paspalum quadrifarium var. ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
