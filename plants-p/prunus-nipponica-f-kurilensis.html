Prunus nipponica f. kurilensis

Prunus nipponica f. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril cherry, is a flowering shrub native to the Japanese archipelago and the Russian island of Sakhalin.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, red drupes. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The Kuril cherry is also valued for its hardy nature and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
