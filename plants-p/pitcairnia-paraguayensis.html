Pitcairnia paraguayensis

Pitcairnia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay airplant, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique structure and ability to store water make it well adapted to its ecological niche.
