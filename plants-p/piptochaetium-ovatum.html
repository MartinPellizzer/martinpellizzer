Piptochaetium ovatum

Piptochaetium ovatum, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland environments. Its botanical features include narrow leaves with a hairy texture and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Piptochaetium ovatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
