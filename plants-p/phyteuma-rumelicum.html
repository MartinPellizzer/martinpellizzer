Phyteuma rumelicum

Phyteuma rumelicum, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and branched.

Phyteuma rumelicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
