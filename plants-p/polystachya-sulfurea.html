Polystachya sulfurea

Polystachya sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and orange inflorescences, which resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on long, branched stems and are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Polystachya sulfurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant, eye-catching display.
