Phlox stansburyi subvar. viridis

Phlox stansburyi subvar. viridis, commonly known as the greenleaf phlox, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and low water requirements make it a desirable plant for arid landscapes.
