Primula dryadifolia subsp. chrysophylla

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. This subspecies is notable for its striking golden foliage, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.
