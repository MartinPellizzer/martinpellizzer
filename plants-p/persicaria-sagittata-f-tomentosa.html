Persicaria sagittata f. tomentosa

Persicaria sagittata f. tomentosa, commonly known as the fuzzy arrowleaf plant, is a herbaceous perennial belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
