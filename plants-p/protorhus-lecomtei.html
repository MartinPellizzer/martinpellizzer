Protorhus lecomtei

Protorhus lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's protea, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae, which is characterized by its showy, often colorful inflorescences. The plant features a distinctive, rounded head of flowers surrounded by leaf-like bracts, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
