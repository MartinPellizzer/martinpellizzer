Prunus avium proles austera

Prunus avium proles austera, commonly known as the dwarf European sweet cherry, is a compact cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small, rounded growth habit and produces small, sweet, dark red fruits. The plant is typically grown in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is a deciduous shrub or tree that blooms in spring with white flowers before fruiting.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and suitability for small gardens or container planting.
