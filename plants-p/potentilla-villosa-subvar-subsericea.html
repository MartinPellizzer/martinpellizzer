Potentilla villosa subvar. subsericea

Potentilla villosa subvar. subsericea, commonly known as the silky cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features wiry, arching stems covered in dense, silky white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and are often covered with fine, silvery hairs.

This subshrub thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
