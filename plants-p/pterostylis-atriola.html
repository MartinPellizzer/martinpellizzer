Pterostylis atriola

Pterostylis atriola, commonly known as the black-tongued spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance with a single, slender flower spike bearing a single, dark brown to black, hooded flower. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insect species, showcasing a specialized ecological relationship.

The orchid's morphology reflects adaptations to its habitat, with leaves that are often covered in a dense, woolly coating.
