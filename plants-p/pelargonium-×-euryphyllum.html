Pelargonium × euryphyllum

Pelargonium × euryphyllum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its widespread use in gardens and landscapes.
