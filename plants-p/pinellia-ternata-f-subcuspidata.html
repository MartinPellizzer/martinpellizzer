Pinellia ternata f. subcuspidata

Pinellia ternata f. subcuspidata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf Pinellia, is a variant of the Chinese windball plant.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tuberous rhizomes and erect, herbaceous stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers arranged in spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a slightly pointed apex, giving it its specific epithet.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
