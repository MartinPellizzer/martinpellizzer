Pararistolochia meridionaliana subsp. popondettensis

Pararistolochia meridionaliana subsp.

popondettensis, commonly known as the Popondetta grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of New Guinea. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, intricate flowers that resemble a snake's body.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that mature to a dark purple color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found along stream banks and in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
