Parosela argyrostachya

Parosela argyrostachya, commonly known as silver-stemmed parosela, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
