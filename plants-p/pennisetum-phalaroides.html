Pennisetum phalaroides

Pennisetum phalaroides, commonly known as flatgrass or purpletop, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that can range in color from green to purple, depending on the variety. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Pennisetum phalaroides is a hardy species that is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
