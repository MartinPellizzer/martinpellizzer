Primula kweichouensis

Primula kweichouensis, commonly known as the Kweichou primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, particularly in the provinces of Guizhou and Sichuan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture.

Primula kweichouensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
