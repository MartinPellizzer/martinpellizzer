Phaeopappus microlonchoides

Phaeopappus microlonchoides, commonly known as the red pappus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant is notable for its pale yellow to orange flowers, which are surrounded by reddish, papery bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens for its unique appearance.
