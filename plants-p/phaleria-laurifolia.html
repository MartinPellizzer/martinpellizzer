Phaleria laurifolia

Phaleria laurifolia, commonly known as the sacred fig or the Indian laurier, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are arranged in a compact, almost circular pattern at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The tree is also significant in traditional medicine and cultural practices across its native regions.
