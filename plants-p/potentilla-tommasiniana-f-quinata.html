Potentilla tommasiniana f. quinata

Potentilla tommasiniana f. quinata, commonly known as the Quinata cinquefoil, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, featuring multiple stems covered in dense, glandular hairs. The leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give the plant its common name. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained, rocky soils.
