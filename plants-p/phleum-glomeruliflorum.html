Phleum glomeruliflorum

Phleum glomeruliflorum, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in landscaping and as forage for livestock.
