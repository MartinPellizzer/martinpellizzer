Phlomis biloba

Phlomis biloba, commonly known as narrowleaf mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features tall, erect stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, and it produces showy, pale purple to yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.
