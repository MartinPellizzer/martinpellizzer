Pultenaea rosmarinifolia

Pultenaea rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, pea-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically appearing in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandplain regions.

Pultenaea rosmarinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
