Persea similis

Persea similis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe, though they can be astringent when unripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
