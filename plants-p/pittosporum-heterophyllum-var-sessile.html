Pittosporum heterophyllum var. sessile

Pittosporum heterophyllum var. sessile, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries, which are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
