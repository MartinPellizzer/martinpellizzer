Pilosocereus swartzii

Pilosocereus swartzii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cacti species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a swollen, colorful crown atop a slender, green stem. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stem. Its vibrant flowers, which appear in shades of yellow, orange, or red, bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Despite its small size, Pilosocereus swartzii is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique beauty and relatively low maintenance requirements.
