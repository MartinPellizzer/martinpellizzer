Pygeum koordersianum

Pygeum koordersianum, commonly known as the Koord's African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a resinous sap.

Pygeum koordersianum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
