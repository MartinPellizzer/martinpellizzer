Piper planadosense

Piper planadosense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in yellow or green hues. Its fruits are small berries that contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
