Pelexia magdalenensis

Pelexia magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalen Islands sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Magdalen Islands in the Gulf of St. Lawrence.

It is a hardy, herbaceous perennial that thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a characteristic species of maritime habitats.

Pelexia magdalenensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
