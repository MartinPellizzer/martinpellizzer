Pothos warburgii

Pothos warburgii, commonly known as the silver pothos, is a popular houseplant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are predominantly silver or variegated with white and green patterns. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and can be used for propagation.

Pothos warburgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care, making it a favorite among indoor gardeners.
