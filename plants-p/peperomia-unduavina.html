Peperomia unduavina

Peperomia unduavina, commonly known as the unduavina peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that often display variegation. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically green or red in color.

Peperomia unduavina is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.
