Pomatocalpa latifolium

Pomatocalpa latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique, often showy structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The orchid is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
