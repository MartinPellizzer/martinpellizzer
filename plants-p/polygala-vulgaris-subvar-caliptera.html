Polygala vulgaris subvar. caliptera

Polygala vulgaris subvar. caliptera, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, pinnately divided leaves. It is part of the Polygalaceae family, known for its unique floral structures.

The subspecies caliptera is distinguished by its hairy or woolly stems and leaves, giving it its common name.
