Psychotria rubiginosa

Psychotria rubiginosa, commonly known as the "red-eyed beauty," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its striking red calyx that resembles eyes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various animals.

Psychotria rubiginosa is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
