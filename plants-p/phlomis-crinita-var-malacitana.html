Phlomis crinita var. malacitana

Phlomis crinita var. malacitana, commonly known as the Malacitan beardgrass, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features tall, upright stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a silvery-blue hue due to dense pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.
