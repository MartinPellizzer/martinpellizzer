Penstemon miniatus var. apateticus

Penstemon miniatus var. apateticus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its unique floral structure makes it an attractive choice for native gardens and pollinator habitats.
