Pinguicula vulgaris f. bicolor

Pinguicula vulgaris f. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, glandular leaves that secrete digestive enzymes to trap small insects. The plant's most distinctive feature is its bicolor flowers, which typically display a combination of white and pink or purple hues. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to maintain its carnivorous adaptations.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular specimen among carnivorous plant enthusiasts.
