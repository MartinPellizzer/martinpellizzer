Pachycereus marginatus var. oaxacensis

Pachycereus marginatus var. oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan columnar cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has a thick, ribbed structure with prominent areoles that bear spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the ends of the stems during the warmer months.

This cactus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
