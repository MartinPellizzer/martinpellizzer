Piper crassicaule

Piper crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper crassicaule make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
