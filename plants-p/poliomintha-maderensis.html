Poliomintha maderensis

Poliomintha maderensis, commonly known as Mader's poliomintha, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant typically features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters.

Poliomintha maderensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils in arid and semi-arid environments.
