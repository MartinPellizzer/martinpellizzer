Polanisia oxyphylla

Polanisia oxyphylla, commonly known as the red-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red foliage and delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
