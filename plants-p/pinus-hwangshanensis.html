Pinus hwangshanensis

Pinus hwangshanensis, commonly known as the Hwangshan pine, is a rare and endemic species native to China.

It is a coniferous tree characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and dense, pyramidal crown. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to its mountainous habitat, with a slow growth rate and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its cones are small and woody, containing two to three seeds, and it reproduces primarily through wind pollination.

The Hwangshan pine plays a significant ecological role in its native environment, contributing to forest biodiversity and soil stability.
