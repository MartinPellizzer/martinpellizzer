Prenanthes paniculata

Prenanthes paniculata, commonly known as the arrowhead or arrowhead weed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in an upright inflorescence called a panicle. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors.

Prenanthes paniculata is often considered an invasive species in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
