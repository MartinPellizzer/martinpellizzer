Pyrus carolinensis var. ellipsoidalis

Pyrus carolinensis var. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the elliptic-flowered serviceberry, is a variant of the Carolina serviceberry.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. The plant features elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer, providing food for wildlife.
