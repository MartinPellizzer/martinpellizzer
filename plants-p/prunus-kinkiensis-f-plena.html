Prunus kinkiensis f. plena

Prunus kinkiensis f. plena, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its abundant, double flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking ornamental display. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy green surface and turn reddish in the fall.

The fruit is small, sweet, and edible, though often not harvested for consumption.
