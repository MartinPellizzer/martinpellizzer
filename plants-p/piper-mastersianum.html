Piper mastersianum

Piper mastersianum, commonly known as the Mastersia pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and are followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
