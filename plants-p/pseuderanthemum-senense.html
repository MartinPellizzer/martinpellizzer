Pseuderanthemum senense

Pseuderanthemum senense, commonly known as the Brazilian flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble traditional chrysanthemums. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pseuderanthemum senense is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
