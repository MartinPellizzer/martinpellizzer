Polystichum oreopteris

Polystichum oreopteris, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, silver-white hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in cool and humid conditions. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 1 meter, and they unfurl slowly over time, giving the plant a unique, elegant appearance.

Polystichum oreopteris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
