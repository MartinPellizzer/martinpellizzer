Polypodium nephrolepioides

Polypodium nephrolepioides, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its creeping rhizome and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as rocky crevices or forest floors. Its fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Polypodium nephrolepioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, green foliage.
