Petrophytum caespitosum var. elatius

Petrophytum caespitosum var. elatius, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit with dense, spiny stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are usually small and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
