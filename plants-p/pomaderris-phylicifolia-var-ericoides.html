Pomaderris phylicifolia var. ericoides

Pomaderris phylicifolia var. ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoid Pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, narrow, glossy leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring, which are arranged in umbels. It is typically found in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The variety ericoides is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and smaller leaves compared to the typical form.
