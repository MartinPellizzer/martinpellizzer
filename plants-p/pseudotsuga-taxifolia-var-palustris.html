Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. palustris

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. palustris, commonly known as the Sitka fir, is a variant of the Sitka fir native to the coastal regions of Alaska and the Aleutian Islands.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits adaptations to its coastal environment, including resistance to salt spray and strong winds. Its foliage is typically dark green and dense, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its timber and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
