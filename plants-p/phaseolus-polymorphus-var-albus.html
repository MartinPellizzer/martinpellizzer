Phaseolus polymorphus var. albus

Phaseolus polymorphus var. albus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, white pods that mature into dry, flat beans. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces compound leaves and bears small, yellow flowers typical of the Fabaceae family.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds and is valued for its adaptability and yield in agricultural settings.
