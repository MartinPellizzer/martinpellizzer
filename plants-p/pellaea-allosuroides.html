Pellaea allosuroides

Pellaea allosuroides, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a species of fern native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clustered fronds that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs.

The botanical features of Pellaea allosuroides make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
