Pitcairnia bifurcatispina

Pitcairnia bifurcatispina, commonly known as the bifurcated-spined pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red flowers, which are typically borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collections.
