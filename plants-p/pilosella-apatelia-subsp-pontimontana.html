Pilosella apatelia subsp. pontimontana

Pilosella apatelia subsp.

pontimontana, commonly known as the narrowleaf pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like flowers.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers with a central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
