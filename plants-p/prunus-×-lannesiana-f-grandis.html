Prunus × lannesiana f. grandis

Prunus × lannesiana f. grandis, commonly known as the large-flowered cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, with a compact growth habit and smooth, reddish bark. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green surface and serrated edges.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its ornamental flowers and seasonal foliage.
