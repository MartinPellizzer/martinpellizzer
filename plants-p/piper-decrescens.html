Piper decrescens

Piper decrescens, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Piper decrescens is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
