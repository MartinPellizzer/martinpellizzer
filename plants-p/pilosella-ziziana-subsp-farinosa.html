Pilosella ziziana subsp. farinosa

Pilosella ziziana subsp.

farinosa, commonly known as the farinose hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features a low-growing rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely pubescent foliage and its preference for arid environments.
