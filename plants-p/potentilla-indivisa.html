Potentilla indivisa

Potentilla indivisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla indivisa is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
