Plumbago zeylanica var. dawei

Plumbago zeylanica var. dawei, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valuable addition to both tropical and temperate landscapes.
