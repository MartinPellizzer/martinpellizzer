Piptadenia flabellata

Piptadenia flabellata, commonly known as the Brazilian fan pod, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warmer regions.
