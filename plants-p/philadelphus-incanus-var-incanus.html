Philadelphus incanus var. incanus

Philadelphus incanus var. incanus, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
