Prunus chinensis

Prunus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Chinese apricot, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that ripen in late summer, containing a single hard pit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy and thrives in well-drained soil.
