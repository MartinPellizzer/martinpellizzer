Pithecellobium costaricense

Pithecellobium costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in native habitats.
