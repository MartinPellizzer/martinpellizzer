Pisum formosum var. pubescens

Pisum formosum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pea, is a variant of the pea species characterized by its hairy foliage and stems.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, pea-shaped pods that contain several seeds, which are typically used for culinary purposes. Its botanical features include compound leaves with trifoliate structures and small, white or pale pink flowers.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible seeds.
