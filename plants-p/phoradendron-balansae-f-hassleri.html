Phoradendron balansae f. hassleri

Phoradendron balansae f. hassleri, commonly known as the Hassler's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has a dense, spherical shape with small, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers, followed by berries that are typically red or orange in color. It relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.

This species is often found in the canopy of forests, contributing to the ecological dynamics of its environment.
