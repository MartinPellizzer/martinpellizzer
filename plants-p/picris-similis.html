Picris similis

Picris similis, commonly known as the common goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and its yellow flower heads consist of many small florets.

Picris similis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
