Poa glaucescens

Poa glaucescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades that often have a bluish-gray hue, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, lawns, and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
