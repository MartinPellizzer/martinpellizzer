Plectronia junodii

Plectronia junodii, commonly known as the Madagascar violet, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of violet and white. The plant has a short, erect stem with a few leaves and produces a single spike of flowers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories, where it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.
