Persea surinamensis

Persea surinamensis, commonly known as the Surinam cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces a large, spiny fruit that contains a creamy, sweet pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer. The fruit is highly valued for its unique flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is notable for its aromatic compounds and medicinal properties.
