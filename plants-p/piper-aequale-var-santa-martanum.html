Piper aequale var. santa-martanum

Piper aequale var. santa-martanum, commonly known as Santa Marta pepper, is a variant of the Piper aequale species native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow or red, indicating maturity.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and peppery flavor, often used in traditional cuisines and herbal preparations.
