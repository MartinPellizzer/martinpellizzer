Panicum germanicum

Panicum germanicum, commonly known as German millet, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

Panicum germanicum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
