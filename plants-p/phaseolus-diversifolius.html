Phaseolus diversifolius

Phaseolus diversifolius, commonly known as the variable-leaf bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with varying numbers of leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its nutritional seeds and has been studied for its potential in agricultural systems.
