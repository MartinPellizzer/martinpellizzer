Planchonella lamprophylla

Planchonella lamprophylla, commonly known as the glossy hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, spherical fruits that are often eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
