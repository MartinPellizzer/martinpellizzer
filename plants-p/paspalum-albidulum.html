Paspalum albidulum

Paspalum albidulum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Paspalum albidulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
