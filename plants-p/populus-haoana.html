Populus haoana

Populus haoana, commonly known as the Hawaiian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive bark. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and lowland areas. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Populus haoana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native wildlife species.
