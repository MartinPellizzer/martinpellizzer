Peperomia emarginatifolia

Peperomia emarginatifolia, commonly known as the notched-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate to elliptical leaves with a distinctive notched or serrated margin, often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both homes and offices.
