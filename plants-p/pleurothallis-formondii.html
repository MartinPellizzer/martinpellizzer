Pleurothallis formondii

Pleurothallis formondii, commonly known as the Formondii orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It prefers high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
