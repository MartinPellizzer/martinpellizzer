Polygonum tianschanicum

Polygonum tianschanicum, commonly known as the Tian Shan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia, particularly the Tian Shan mountain range.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its root system is fibrous and extensively branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Polygonum tianschanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
