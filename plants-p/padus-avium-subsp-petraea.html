Padus avium subsp. petraea

Padus avium subsp.

petraea, commonly known as the rock crabapple, is a subspecies of the crabapple tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its small, often red or yellowish flowers and oval, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically sour but can be used in jams or wines. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
