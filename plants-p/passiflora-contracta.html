Passiflora contracta

Passiflora contracta, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate structures. The flowers feature five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments around the reproductive organs. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that ripens to a deep purple color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
