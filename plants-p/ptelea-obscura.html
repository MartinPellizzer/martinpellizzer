Ptelea obscura

Ptelea obscura, commonly known as the darkleaf ptelea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by small, round berries that are dark purple when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
