Prasophyllum filiforme

Prasophyllum filiforme, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern regions of Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils within open forests and heathlands. The plant features a slender, grass-like stem with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in a loose spike. It is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Prasophyllum filiforme is a member of the orchid family and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor environments.
