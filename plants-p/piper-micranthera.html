Piper micranthera

Piper micranthera, commonly known as the small-fruited pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tiny and greenish-white, often clustered in spikes, while the fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded gardens.
