Phalangium niveum

Phalangium niveum, commonly known as the white legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is often used in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
