Panicum latifolium var. clandestinum

Panicum latifolium var. clandestinum, commonly known as the secret flat-leaf panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, grass-like flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying grassland ecosystems.
