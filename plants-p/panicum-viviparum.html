Panicum viviparum

Panicum viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous panic grass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble small clouds. The plant produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, allowing it to colonize open areas quickly. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural contexts.
