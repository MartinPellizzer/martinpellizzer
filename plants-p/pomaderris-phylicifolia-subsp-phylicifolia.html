Pomaderris phylicifolia subsp. phylicifolia

Pomaderris phylicifolia subsp.

phylicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pomaderris, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the autumn and winter seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued species in native gardens and conservation efforts.
