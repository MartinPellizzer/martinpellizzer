Phaseolus vulgaris var. lilacinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. lilacinus, commonly known as the purple hull pea, is a variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive purplish-blue pods and seeds, which are typically harvested when young and tender. The plant is a climbing legume that produces trifoliate leaves and flowers in shades of white or pale purple. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
