Populus gamblei

Populus gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier poplar, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its growth habit is typically tall and slender, with a pyramidal shape when young.

Populus gamblei is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and open woodlands.
