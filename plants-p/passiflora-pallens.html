Passiflora pallens

Passiflora pallens, commonly known as the pale passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of white, blue, and purple hues, with a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora pallens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
