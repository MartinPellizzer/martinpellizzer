Pyrus mokpoensis

Pyrus mokpoensis, commonly known as the Mokpo pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South Korea and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears in appearance and texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
