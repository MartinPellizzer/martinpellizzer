Paspalum dilatatum var. eriophorum

Paspalum dilatatum var. eriophorum, commonly known as the woolly paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or marshy areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
