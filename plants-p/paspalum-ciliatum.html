Paspalum ciliatum

Paspalum ciliatum, commonly known as slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum ciliatum is often found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.
