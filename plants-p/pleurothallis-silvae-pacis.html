Pleurothallis silvae-pacis

Pleurothallis silvae-pacis, commonly known as the Peace Forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, a common trait among orchids.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to deforestation and habitat loss.
