Potamogeton leptanthus

Potamogeton leptanthus, commonly known as slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still waters, such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its rhizomes form dense mats that can stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton leptanthus plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in freshwater habitats.
