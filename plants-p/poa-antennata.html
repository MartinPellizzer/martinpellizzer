Poa antennata

Poa antennata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often hairless blades. The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, pastures, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often drooping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Poa antennata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
