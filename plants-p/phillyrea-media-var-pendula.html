Phillyrea media var. pendula

Phillyrea media var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, drooping branches that give it a graceful, weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by small, leathery fruits. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.
