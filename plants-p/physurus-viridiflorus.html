Physurus viridiflorus

Physurus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. Physurus viridiflorus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and garden cultivation.
