Panicum lutescens

Panicum lutescens, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces fluffy, yellowish to brownish seed heads. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.

Its growth habit is typically clump-forming, with rhizomes that help it spread and establish quickly.
