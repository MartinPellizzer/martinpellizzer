Potentilla pensylvanica var. strigosa

Potentilla pensylvanica var. strigosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are usually alternate, with five leaflets that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
