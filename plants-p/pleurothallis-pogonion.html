Pleurothallis pogonion

Pleurothallis pogonion, commonly known as the hairy lip orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive hairy lip, which is a notable botanical feature. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, typical of epiphytic orchids.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
