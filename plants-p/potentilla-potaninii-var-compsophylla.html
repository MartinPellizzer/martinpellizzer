Potentilla potaninii var. compsophylla

Potentilla potaninii var. compsophylla, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variant is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand cold climates, contributing to its popularity among horticulturists.
