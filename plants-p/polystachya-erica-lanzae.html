Polystachya erica-lanzae

Polystachya erica-lanzae, commonly known as the Lanza's polystachya, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
