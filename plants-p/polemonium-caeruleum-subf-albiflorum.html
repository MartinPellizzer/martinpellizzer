Polemonium caeruleum subf. albiflorum

Polemonium caeruleum subf.

albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a ladder-like pattern along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
