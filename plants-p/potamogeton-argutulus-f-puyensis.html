Potamogeton argutulus f. puyensis

Potamogeton argutulus f. puyensis, commonly known as the Puyensian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in South America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as lakes and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
