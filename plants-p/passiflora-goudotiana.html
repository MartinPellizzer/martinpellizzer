Passiflora goudotiana

Passiflora goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora goudotiana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
