Philodendron glanduliferum subsp. glanduliferum

Philodendron glanduliferum subsp.

glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular philodendron, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical regions of South America. It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
