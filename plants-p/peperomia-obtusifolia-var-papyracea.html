Peperomia obtusifolia var. papyracea

Peperomia obtusifolia var. papyracea, commonly known as the paper-leaf peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a subtle sheen, giving it a unique, almost papery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and hanging baskets.
