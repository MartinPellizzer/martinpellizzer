Picris spitzelioides

Picris spitzelioides, commonly known as the Spitzel's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has linear, aromatic leaves with a strong, bitter scent. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental or medicinal plant.
