Pomaria brachycarpa

Pomaria brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited pomaria, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, leathery berry that ripens in the fall.

Pomaria brachycarpa is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland habitats.
