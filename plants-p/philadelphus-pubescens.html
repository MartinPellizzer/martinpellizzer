Philadelphus pubescens

Philadelphus pubescens, commonly known as the Downy Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets that are often covered in fine, downy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable to local wildlife.
