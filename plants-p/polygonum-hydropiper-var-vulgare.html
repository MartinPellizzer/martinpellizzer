Polygonum hydropiper var. vulgare

Polygonum hydropiper var. vulgare, commonly known as water pepper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, translucent glands. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in terminal spikes.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other damp environments.
