Plectranthus flavidus

Plectranthus flavidus, commonly known as the yellow plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
