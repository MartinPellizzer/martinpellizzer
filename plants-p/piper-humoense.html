Piper humoense

Piper humoense, commonly known as the swamp pepper or humid pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, reddish berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and are believed to have various therapeutic properties.
