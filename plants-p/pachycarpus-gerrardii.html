Pachycarpus gerrardii

Pachycarpus gerrardii, commonly known as the desert lily, is a striking plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting to its harsh, dry environment. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Pachycarpus gerrardii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various desert animals.
