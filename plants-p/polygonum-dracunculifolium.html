Polygonum dracunculifolium

Polygonum dracunculifolium, commonly known as the dragon's tail plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rugged appearance. Its small, clustered flowers typically bloom in shades of pink or red, adding visual interest in the late spring to early summer.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
