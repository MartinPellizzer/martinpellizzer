Persea punctata

Persea punctata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a rough texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by orange to reddish fruit that ripens in the fall. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor when fully mature and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
