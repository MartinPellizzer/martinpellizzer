Protea australis

Protea australis, commonly known as the Australian protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, known as a protea. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Protea australis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
