Phyllanthus vacciniifolius subsp. vinillaensis

Phyllanthus vacciniifolius subsp.

vinillaensis, commonly known as the Vinillaensis Phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
