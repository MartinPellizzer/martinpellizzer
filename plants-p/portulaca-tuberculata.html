Portulaca tuberculata

Portulaca tuberculata, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant with fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Portulaca tuberculata is also known for its ability to survive in arid conditions due to its succulent nature.
