Potentilla dichroa

Potentilla dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Potentilla dichroa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
