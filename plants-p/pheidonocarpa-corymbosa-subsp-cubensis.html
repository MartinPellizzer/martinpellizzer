Pheidonocarpa corymbosa subsp. cubensis

Pheidonocarpa corymbosa subsp.

cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban starfruit, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba. It is a small shrub or tree that produces bright yellow, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a thin, edible pericarp and is rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
