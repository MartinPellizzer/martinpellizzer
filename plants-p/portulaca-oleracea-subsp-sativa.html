Portulaca oleracea subsp. sativa

Portulaca oleracea subsp.

sativa, commonly known as garden purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to warm temperate regions. It features succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning.

The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are edible and often used in salads or cooked dishes due to their mild flavor and nutritional value.

It is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in harsh environments.
