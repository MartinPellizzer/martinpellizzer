Patrisa chocoensis

Patrisa chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocoan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of the rainforest. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by attracting specific insects.

Due to habitat destruction, Patrisa chocoensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
