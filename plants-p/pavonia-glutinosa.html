Pavonia glutinosa

Pavonia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. The plant features upright stems covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which is a distinguishing botanical characteristic. Its showy, pink to red bracts attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, contributing to its unique appearance in the botanical world.
