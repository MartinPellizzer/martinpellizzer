Pavonia urens var. irakuensis

Pavonia urens var. irakuensis, commonly known as the Irakuenis pavonia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a distinctive scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
