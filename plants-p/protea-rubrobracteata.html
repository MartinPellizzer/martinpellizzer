Protea rubrobracteata

Protea rubrobracteata, commonly known as the red bract protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small white flowers surrounded by vibrant red bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
