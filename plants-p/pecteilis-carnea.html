Pecteilis carnea

Pecteilis carnea, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pecteilis carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
