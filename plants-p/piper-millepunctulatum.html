Piper millepunctulatum

Piper millepunctulatum, commonly known as the "spotted pepper plant," is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that have a speckled or dotted appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, red berries that are edible but have a sharp, peppery flavor. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
