Philadelphus pallidus

Philadelphus pallidus, commonly known as the white mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, reddish-brown berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its showy flowers and pleasant fragrance.
