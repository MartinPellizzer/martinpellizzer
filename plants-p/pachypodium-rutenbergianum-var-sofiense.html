Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. sofiense

Pachypodium rutenbergianum var. sofiense, commonly known as the Sofiense Pachypodium, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the dry season, which are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, serving as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

This species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience to drought.
