Pedicularis bambusetorum

Pedicularis bambusetorum, commonly known as the bamboo orchid, is a rare and unique plant that combines features of both orchids and bamboo.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes over 450 species of flowering plants, many of which are native to the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and delicate, orchid-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
