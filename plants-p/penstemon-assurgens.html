Penstemon assurgens

Penstemon assurgens, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its lance-shaped leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the stems are erect and branched.

Penstemon assurgens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
