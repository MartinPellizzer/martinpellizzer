Porpax papillosa

Porpax papillosa, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to efficiently absorb water from the soil.

Porpax papillosa is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
