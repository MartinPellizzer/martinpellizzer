Paullinia rubiginosa subsp. rubiginosa

Paullinia rubiginosa subsp.

rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-staining soursop, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that have a rough texture.

The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits with a distinctive aroma. Its fruit is edible, though it contains seeds and is often used in local traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
