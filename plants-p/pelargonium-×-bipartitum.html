Pelargonium × bipartitum

Pelargonium × bipartitum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features a mix of green and white or cream-colored patterns. The plant typically produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
