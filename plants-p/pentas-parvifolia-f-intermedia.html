Pentas parvifolia f. intermedia

Pentas parvifolia f. intermedia, commonly known as the Intermediate Fiveflower, is a variant of the Pentas parvifolia species.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its vibrant blooms and relatively low maintenance requirements.
