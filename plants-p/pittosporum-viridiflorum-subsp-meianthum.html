Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp. meianthum

Pittosporum viridiflorum subsp.

meianthum, commonly known as the silver-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are highly fragrant, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
