Polygonum kuekenthalii

Polygonum kuekenthalii, commonly known as the Kuekenthal's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
