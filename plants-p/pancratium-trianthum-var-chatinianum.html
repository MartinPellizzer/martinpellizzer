Pancratium trianthum var. chatinianum

Pancratium trianthum var. chatinianum, commonly known as the Chatinian yellow lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are evergreen and can grow up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to withstand coastal conditions, including salt spray and sandy soils.
