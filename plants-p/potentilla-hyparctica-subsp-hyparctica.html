Potentilla hyparctica subsp. hyparctica

Potentilla hyparctica subsp.

hyparctica, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks and marshes.

The plant features compound leaves with five elliptic leaflets and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in open, sunny environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
