Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. pratensis

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a single, hairy fruit that disperses its seeds by wind.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
