Podocoma foliosa

Podocoma foliosa, commonly known as the leafy podocarp, is a unique evergreen shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that forms a compact, cushion-like structure, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by the development of woody, cone-like structures. Its root system is extensive and adapted to absorb moisture from deep underground, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Podocoma foliosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
