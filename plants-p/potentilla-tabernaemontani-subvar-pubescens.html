Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. pubescens

Potentilla tabernaemontani subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its "pubescens" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
