Pittosporum rhytidocarpum

Pittosporum rhytidocarpum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Pittosporum rhytidocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal interest.
