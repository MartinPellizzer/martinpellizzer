Primula septemloba

Primula septemloba, commonly known as the seven-lobed primrose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, seven-lobed petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in woodland and rocky habitats, preferring cool, moist environments. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
