Pleurothallis reymondii

Pleurothallis reymondii, commonly known as the Reymond's pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. It produces small, intricate flowers that are usually greenish-white with a faintly colored lip, adapted for specific pollinators. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
