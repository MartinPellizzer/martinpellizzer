Plantago major var. minima

Plantago major var. minima, commonly known as the common plantain, is a small variant of the widespread plantain species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters. The plant features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, greenish-brown, and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils, such as lawns, roadsides, and gardens, and is known for its medicinal properties.
