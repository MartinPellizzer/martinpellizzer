Pleurothallis ospinae

Pleurothallis ospinae, commonly known as the Ospina's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Pleurothallis ospinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
