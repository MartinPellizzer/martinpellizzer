Piper pendens

Piper pendens, commonly known as the hanging pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes several species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential medicinal properties.
