Papaver nudicaule subsp. kvaenagense

Papaver nudicaule subsp.

kvaenagense, commonly known as the Norwegian poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Scandinavia. It features striking bright red petals that are typically arranged in a single, showy bloom.

The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers open in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
