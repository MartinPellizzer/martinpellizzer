Pharbitis scabra

Pharbitis scabra, commonly known as hairy nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes.

Pharbitis scabra is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
