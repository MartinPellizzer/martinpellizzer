Plexipus latifolius

Plexipus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf plexipus, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
