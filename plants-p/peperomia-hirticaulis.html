Peperomia hirticaulis

Peperomia hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, slightly hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves with a textured surface. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and silver, which add visual interest.

Peperomia hirticaulis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
