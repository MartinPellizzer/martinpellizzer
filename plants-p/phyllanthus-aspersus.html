Phyllanthus aspersus

Phyllanthus aspersus, commonly known as the sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Africa, southern Europe, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in tiny, translucent bumps, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Phyllanthus aspersus is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
