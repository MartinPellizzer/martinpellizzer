Peschiera apoda

Peschiera apoda, commonly known as the naked root orchid, is a unique orchid species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of visible roots, which is an unusual feature among orchids, and its striking, elongated flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture and indirect light. Its botanical structure includes a short, slender stem and a single, showy flower that blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Peschiera apoda is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
