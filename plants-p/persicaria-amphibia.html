Persicaria amphibia

Persicaria amphibia, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its root system is adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils, making it a common sight in muddy or aquatic habitats.

Persicaria amphibia is also known for its ability to exude a peppery scent when the leaves are crushed.
