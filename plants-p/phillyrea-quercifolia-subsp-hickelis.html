Phillyrea quercifolia subsp. hickelis

Phillyrea quercifolia subsp.

hickelis, commonly known as Hickel's Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside.

The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid landscapes.
