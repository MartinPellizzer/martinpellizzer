Paepalanthus compactus

Paepalanthus compactus, commonly known as the compact dwarf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with dense clusters of small, spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry gardens and rockeries.
