Pelargonium crucifolium

Pelargonium crucifolium, commonly known as the crucifix geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Pelargonium crucifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.
