Primula variabilis

Primula variabilis, commonly known as the variable primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and cool climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
