Pistacia narbonnensis

Pistacia narbonnensis, commonly known as the Pyrenean pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Pyrenees.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. This species is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a resilient plant in mountainous regions.
