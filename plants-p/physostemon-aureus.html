Physostemon aureus

Physostemon aureus, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant produces long, slender leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and its stems are often hairy. It is a vigorous climber, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
