Panicum sparsicomum

Panicum sparsicomum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of numerous small spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Panicum sparsicomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
