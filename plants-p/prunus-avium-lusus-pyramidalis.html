Prunus avium lusus pyramidalis

Prunus avium lusus pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit.

It produces sweet, yellow-orange fruits with a firm flesh and a high sugar content, typically harvested in late summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and structured form.

The pyramidal peach is also valued for its fruit quality, making it suitable for both ornamental and commercial cultivation.
