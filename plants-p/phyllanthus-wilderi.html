Phyllanthus wilderi

Phyllanthus wilderi, commonly known as the African date tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its small yellowish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in such conditions.
