Piper lenormandianum

Piper lenormandianum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, dark berries that contain the seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
