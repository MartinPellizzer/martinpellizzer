Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens f. hinkulii

Phyllostachys viridiglaucescens f. hinkulii, commonly known as the Hinku bamboo, is a variant of the greenish-blue bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant stalks that exhibit a distinctive bluish-green sheen. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often a lighter green than the stems, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a clump-forming bamboo that grows relatively slowly, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.

This cultivar is prized for its aesthetic value and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
