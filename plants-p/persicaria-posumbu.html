Persicaria posumbu

Persicaria posumbu, commonly known as the swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in eastern Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with reddish hues. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in moist, poorly drained soils and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
