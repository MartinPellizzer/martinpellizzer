Pleurothallis bolsanelloi

Pleurothallis bolsanelloi, commonly known as the Bolivian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, narrow lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its short, wiry stems and clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
