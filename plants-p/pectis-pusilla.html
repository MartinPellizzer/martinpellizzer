Pectis pusilla

Pectis pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf pectis, is a small, flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Pectis pusilla is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
