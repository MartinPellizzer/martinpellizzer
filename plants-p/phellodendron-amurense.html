Phellodendron amurense

Phellodendron amurense, commonly known as the Amur corktree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thick, cork-like bark that peels off in strips. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in axillary racemes, while the fruit is a reddish-brown drupe.

The tree is valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing compounds used in traditional Chinese medicine.
