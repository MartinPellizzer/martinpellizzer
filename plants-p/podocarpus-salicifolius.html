Podocarpus salicifolius

Podocarpus salicifolius, commonly known as the silky pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are leathery and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
