Passiflora giberti

Passiflora giberti, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a sweet, fleshy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
