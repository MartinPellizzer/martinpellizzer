Primula microdonta var. alpicola

Primula microdonta var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
