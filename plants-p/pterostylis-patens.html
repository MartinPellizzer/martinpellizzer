Pterostylis patens

Pterostylis patens, commonly known as the slender greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique hood-like labellum and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish-green, with a single prominent spur.

Pterostylis patens is a terrestrial orchid that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
