Pleurothallis infundibulosa

Pleurothallis infundibulosa, commonly known as the cup-shaped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers that are often arranged in a raceme, with a distinctive cup-like structure at the base of the petals. It has fibrous roots that help it absorb moisture and nutrients from its surroundings.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
