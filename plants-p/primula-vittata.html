Primula vittata

Primula vittata, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
