Panicum dioecum

Panicum dioecum, commonly known as little barley, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers on separate plants. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum dioecum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
