Pyrus hugh-mcallisteri

Pyrus hugh-mcallisteri, commonly known as the Hugh McAllister pear, is a rare and ornamental species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens with specific conditions.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and historical significance.
