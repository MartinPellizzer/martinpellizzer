Pothos australasicus

Pothos australasicus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with shades of yellow and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is more valued for its ornamental foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is known for its ability to purify indoor air.

Pothos australasicus is a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens due to its vigorous growth and low maintenance requirements.
