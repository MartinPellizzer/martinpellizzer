Prenanthes mira

Prenanthes mira, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple hue. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Prenanthes mira is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.
