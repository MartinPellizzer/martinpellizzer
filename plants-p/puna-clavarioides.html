Puna clavarioides

Puna clavarioides, commonly known as the Andean coral fungus, is a unique fungal species found in the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Clavariaceae and is characterized by its branching, coral-like structure. The fungus thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found growing on decaying wood or soil in alpine meadows. Its color ranges from pale yellow to orange, and it is known for its delicate, gelatinous texture.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of high-altitude ecosystems.
