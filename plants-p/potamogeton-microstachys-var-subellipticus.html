Potamogeton microstachys var. subellipticus

Potamogeton microstachys var. subellipticus, commonly known as the narrow-elliptic pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain tiny, greenish flowers that are adapted for underwater pollination.

This variety is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
