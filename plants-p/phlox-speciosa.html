Phlox speciosa

Phlox speciosa, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its floral display attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
