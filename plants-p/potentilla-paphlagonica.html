Potentilla paphlagonica

Potentilla paphlagonica, commonly known as the Paphlagonian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Balkans.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

Potentilla paphlagonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
