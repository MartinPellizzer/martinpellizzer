Polygonum patulum f. transsilvanicum

Polygonum patulum f. transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streams and in meadows.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
