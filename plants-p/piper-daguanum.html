Piper daguanum

Piper daguanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that resemble black pepper. The plant produces a pungent, spicy compound called piperine, similar to that found in black pepper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal and culinary potential, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotanists alike.
