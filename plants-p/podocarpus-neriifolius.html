Podocarpus neriifolius

Podocarpus neriifolius, commonly known as the black-leaved podocarp, is a evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and reforestation projects.
