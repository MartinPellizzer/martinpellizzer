Phrynium chrysantha

Phrynium chrysantha, commonly known as the golden phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with striking yellow or orange markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens due to its aesthetic appeal.
