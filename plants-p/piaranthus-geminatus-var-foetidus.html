Piaranthus geminatus var. foetidus

Piaranthus geminatus var. foetidus, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in grassy or disturbed areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

The species is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is most noticeable when the flowers are in full bloom.
