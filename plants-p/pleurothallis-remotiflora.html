Pleurothallis remotiflora

Pleurothallis remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
