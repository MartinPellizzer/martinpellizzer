Phaiosperma

Phaiosperma, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and unique epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its delicate appearance and elusive nature make it a subject of fascination among botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and illegal collection, Phaiosperma is critically endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
