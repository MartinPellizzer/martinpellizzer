Polypodium brasiliense var. gladiatum

Polypodium brasiliense var. gladiatum, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, and its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
