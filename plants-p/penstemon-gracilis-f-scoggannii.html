Penstemon gracilis f. scoggannii

Penstemon gracilis f. scoggannii, commonly known as the Scoggan's narrowleaf beardtongue, is a cultivar of the narrowleaf beardtongue, a species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
