Porophyllum ruderale var. ruderale

Porophyllum ruderale var. ruderale, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and prairies. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a sprawling growth habit.

Its leaves are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, which contribute to its common name.
