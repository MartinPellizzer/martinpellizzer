Potentilla × jaeggiana

Potentilla × jaeggiana, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a hybrid shrub belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
