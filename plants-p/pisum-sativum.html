Pisum sativum

Pisum sativum, commonly known as the garden pea, is an annual legume belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are the edible part consumed by humans. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it has small, pink or white flowers.

Pisum sativum is cultivated worldwide for its nutritional value and is a key crop in agriculture.
