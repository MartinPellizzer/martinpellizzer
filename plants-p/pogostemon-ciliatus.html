Pogostemon ciliatus

Pogostemon ciliatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. Pogostemon ciliatus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses have made it a subject of interest in horticulture and botanical research.
