Pyrus communis var. hiemalis

Pyrus communis var. hiemalis, commonly known as the winter pear, is a cold-hardy variety of the common pear.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that ripen in late autumn or early winter. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and winter fruiting habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
