Podospermum decumbens var. angustifolia

Podospermum decumbens var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its growth habit is prostrate or decumbent, allowing it to spread along the ground.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
