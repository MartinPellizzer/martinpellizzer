Plectranthus atroviolaceus

Plectranthus atroviolaceus, commonly known as black viola plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
