Plantago aschersonii

Plantago aschersonii, commonly known as the Ascherson's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used traditionally for their healing effects.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
