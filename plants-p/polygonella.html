Polygonella

Polygonella, commonly known as the moss campion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It consists of several species that are typically found in arctic and alpine regions, where they thrive in harsh, cold environments. These plants are characterized by their low-growing, mat-forming structure and small, often clustered flowers. Polygonella species are often used in ecological studies due to their role in stabilizing soil in extreme climates.

Their botanical significance lies in their adaptation to nutrient-poor soils and their resilience to environmental stress.
