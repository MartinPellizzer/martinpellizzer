Poa ligulata var. djurdjurae

Poa ligulata var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit, which helps it thrive in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-ranked flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is well-adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
