Paspalum floribundum

Paspalum floribundum, commonly known as the showy grass or bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue to purple in color. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture that contributes to its ornamental appeal.

Paspalum floribundum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
