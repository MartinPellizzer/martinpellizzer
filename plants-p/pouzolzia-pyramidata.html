Pouzolzia pyramidata

Pouzolzia pyramidata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.

The leaves are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its hardy and resilient nature.
