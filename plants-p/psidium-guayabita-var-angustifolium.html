Psidium guayabita var. angustifolium

Psidium guayabita var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guayaba, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
