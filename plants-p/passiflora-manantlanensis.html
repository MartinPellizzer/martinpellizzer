Passiflora manantlanensis

Passiflora manantlanensis, commonly known as the Manantlan passionflower, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing vine with striking, five-petaled flowers that display a mix of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tropical flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
