Plumeria warmingii

Plumeria warmingii, commonly known as the white frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces woody, branched stems. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles and bloom during the dry season.
