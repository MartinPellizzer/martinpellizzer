Pterocarpus paniculatus

Pterocarpus paniculatus, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces flat, bean-like pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is thick and often flaky.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
