Pergularia japonica

Pergularia japonica, commonly known as Japanese spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pergularia japonica is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
