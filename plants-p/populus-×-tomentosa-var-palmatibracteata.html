Populus × tomentosa var. palmatibracteata

Populus × tomentosa var. palmatibracteata, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its broad, palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
