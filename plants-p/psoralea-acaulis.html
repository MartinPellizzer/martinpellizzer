Psoralea acaulis

Psoralea acaulis, commonly known as the stemless psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is herbaceous, with no true stem, and features a basal rosette of leaves that are pinnately lobed. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
