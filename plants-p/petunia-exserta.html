Petunia exserta

Petunia exserta, commonly known as the skyflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, elongated fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Petunia exserta is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
