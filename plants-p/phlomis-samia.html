Phlomis samia

Phlomis samia, commonly known as the Greek fireweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tall, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered with dense, woolly hair. The leaves are lance-shaped, gray-green in color, and often have a fuzzy texture. It produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late summer, which are attractive to pollinators.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy addition to rocky or sandy gardens.
