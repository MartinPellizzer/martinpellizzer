Pseudolarix amabilis

Pseudolarix amabilis, commonly known as the golden larch, is a deciduous conifer native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage in autumn, which turns to a vibrant orange before shedding. The tree has a tall, slender trunk with a conical shape and produces small, woody cones. Its leaves are needle-like and turn bright yellow in the fall, making it a popular ornamental tree.

The species is also valued for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
