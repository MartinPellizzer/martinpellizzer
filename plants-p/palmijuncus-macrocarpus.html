Palmijuncus macrocarpus

Palmijuncus macrocarpus, commonly known as the giant fan palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that are about the size of a small apple. Its trunk is slender and covered with persistent leaf bases, which contribute to its unique visual appeal.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its majestic form and resilience.
