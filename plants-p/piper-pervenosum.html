Piper pervenosum

Piper pervenosum, commonly known as the pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-chambered berries. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

Piper pervenosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
