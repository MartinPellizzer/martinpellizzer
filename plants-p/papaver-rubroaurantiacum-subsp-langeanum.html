Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. langeanum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

langeanum, commonly known as the Lange's poppy, is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking red and orange petals that form a cup-shaped corona, which is a distinctive feature of the species.

The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers bloom in the spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
