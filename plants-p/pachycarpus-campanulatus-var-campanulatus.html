Pachycarpus campanulatus var. campanulatus

Pachycarpus campanulatus var. campanulatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscape designs.
