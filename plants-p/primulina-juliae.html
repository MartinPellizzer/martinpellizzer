Primulina juliae

Primulina juliae, commonly known as the Julia's gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil to flourish.
