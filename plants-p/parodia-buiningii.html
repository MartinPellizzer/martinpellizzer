Parodia buiningii

Parodia buiningii, commonly known as the Buning's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Parodia buiningii is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance needs.
