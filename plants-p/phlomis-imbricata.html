Phlomis imbricata

Phlomis imbricata, commonly known as the wrinkled mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
