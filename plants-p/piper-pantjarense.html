Piper pantjarense

Piper pantjarense, commonly known as the Pantjarense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy green surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The small, green fruits are typically harvested when mature and are used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.
