Polygonum obliquum

Polygonum obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks.

Polygonum obliquum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
