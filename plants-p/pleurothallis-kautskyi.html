Pleurothallis kautskyi

Pleurothallis kautskyi, commonly known as the Kautsky's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
