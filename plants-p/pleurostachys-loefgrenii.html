Pleurostachys loefgrenii

Pleurostachys loefgrenii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre grass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
