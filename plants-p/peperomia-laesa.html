Peperomia laesa

Peperomia laesa, commonly known as the Shiny Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers in terminal clusters. The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care, requiring bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a versatile choice for indoor gardening.
