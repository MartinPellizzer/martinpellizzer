Piper duchassaingii

Piper duchassaingii, commonly known as the "Java pepper" or "long pepper," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. These berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

The botanical characteristics of Piper duchassaingii make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
