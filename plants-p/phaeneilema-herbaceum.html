Phaeneilema herbaceum

Phaeneilema herbaceum, commonly known as the silverleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous habit and features silvery-white leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Phaeneilema herbaceum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
