Piper adscendens

Piper adscendens, commonly known as the climbing pepper or sky pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, narrow fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's ornamental value is also appreciated due to its attractive foliage and unique growth pattern.
