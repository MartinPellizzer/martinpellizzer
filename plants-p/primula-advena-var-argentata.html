Primula advena var. argentata

Primula advena var. argentata, commonly known as the silver-leaved primrose, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
