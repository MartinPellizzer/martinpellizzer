Piper acutibracteum

Piper acutibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-bract pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppercorn-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.

The plant's morphology and growth habits are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
