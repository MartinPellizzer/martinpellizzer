Pisum sativum var. rourkeanum

Pisum sativum var. rourkeanum, commonly known as the Rourke's pea, is a rare and less commonly cultivated variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring larger and more showy flowers compared to standard garden peas. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing pods that contain a few seeds. Botanically, it exhibits traits that make it of interest for genetic studies due to its distinct morphological features.

Its cultivation is limited, primarily due to its ornamental value and specific growing requirements.
