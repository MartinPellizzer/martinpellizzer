Ptilotus Pyramidatus

Ptilotus pyramidatus, also known as the 'Cocky' plant, is a unique and striking species native to Australia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble a cock's head, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions. Its vibrant color and unusual shape make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections. Ptilotus pyramidatus is not only visually appealing but also holds cultural significance in Indigenous Australian communities.
