Piper sumideranum

Piper sumideranum, commonly known as the Sumatra pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in local cuisines for their pungent flavor.

The botanical features of Piper sumideranum make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
