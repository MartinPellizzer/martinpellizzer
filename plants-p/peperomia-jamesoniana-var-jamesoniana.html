Peperomia jamesoniana var. jamesoniana

Peperomia jamesoniana var. jamesoniana, commonly known as the Jameson's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
