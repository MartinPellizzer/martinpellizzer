Poa laxa var. caesioglauca

Poa laxa var. caesioglauca, commonly known as the blue-green meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish tint that becomes more pronounced in cooler conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude environments.
