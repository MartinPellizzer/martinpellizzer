Pseudoblepharis heinsenii

Pseudoblepharis heinsenii, commonly known as the Heinsen's false heather, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
