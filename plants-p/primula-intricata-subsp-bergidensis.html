Primula intricata subsp. bergidensis

Primula intricata subsp.

bergidensis, commonly known as the Bergidensis primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and have a hairy texture that helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
