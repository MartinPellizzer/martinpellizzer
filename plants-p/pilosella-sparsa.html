Pilosella sparsa

Pilosella sparsa, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is low-growing, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella sparsa is often found in dry grasslands, rocky areas, and disturbed soils, where it thrives in full sun.
