Phymatopsis albopes

Phymatopsis albopes, commonly known as the white-leaf phymatopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically white or pale green, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phymatopsis albopes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
