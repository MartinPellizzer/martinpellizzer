Phrygia major

Phrygia major, commonly known as the giant phrygia, is a striking member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves.

It produces umbel-shaped inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
