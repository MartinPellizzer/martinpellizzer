Patagonium graminideum

Patagonium graminideum, commonly known as the Patagonian grass.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to the temperate regions of South America, particularly in Patagonia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Patagonium graminideum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
