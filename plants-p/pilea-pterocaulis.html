Pilea pterocaulis

Pilea pterocaulis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea pterocaulis is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated easily through leaf cuttings.
