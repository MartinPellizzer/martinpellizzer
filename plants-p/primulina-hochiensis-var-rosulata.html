Primulina hochiensis var. rosulata

Primulina hochiensis var. rosulata, commonly known as the Rosulate Primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
