Protea tortuosa

Protea tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence that resembles a twisted tower, giving it its common name. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Protea tortuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in the conservation of South Africa's biodiversity.
