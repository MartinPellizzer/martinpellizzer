Pithecellobium dinklagei

Pithecellobium dinklagei, commonly known as the Dinklage's acacia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in dense inflorescences. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
