Phyllanthus diversifolius var. longifolius

Phyllanthus diversifolius var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its foliage.
