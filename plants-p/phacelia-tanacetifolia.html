Phacelia tanacetifolia

Phacelia tanacetifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Phacelia tanacetifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in herbal medicine.
