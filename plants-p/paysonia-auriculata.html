Paysonia auriculata

Paysonia auriculata, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, spike-like cluster, and the plant produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds.

Paysonia auriculata is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
