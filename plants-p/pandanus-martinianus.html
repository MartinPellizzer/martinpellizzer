Pandanus martinianus

Pandanus martinianus, commonly known as the Martin's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of French Polynesia and the Cook Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, fibrous fruits.

The plant is adapted to coastal environments and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils.
