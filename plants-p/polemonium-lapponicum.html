Polemonium lapponicum

Polemonium lapponicum, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in the study of plant adaptation to extreme environments.
