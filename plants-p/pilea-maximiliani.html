Pilea maximiliani

Pilea maximiliani, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, shield-shaped leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathaceous berries. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea maximiliani thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.
