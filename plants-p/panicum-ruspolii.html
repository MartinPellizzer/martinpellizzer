Panicum ruspolii

Panicum ruspolii, commonly known as the Ruspoli panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
