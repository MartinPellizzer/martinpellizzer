Paspalum virletii

Paspalum virletii, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum virletii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
