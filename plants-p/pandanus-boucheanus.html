Pandanus boucheanus

Pandanus boucheanus, commonly known as the Bouchean pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used for culinary and medicinal purposes in local cultures.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it resilient to saltwater and flooding.
