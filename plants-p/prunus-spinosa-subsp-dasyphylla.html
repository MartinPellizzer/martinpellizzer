Prunus spinosa subsp. dasyphylla

Prunus spinosa subsp.

dasyphylla, commonly known as the bramble cherry, is a shrub native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, red or black berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is adaptable to different light conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common species in hedgerows and woodland edges.
