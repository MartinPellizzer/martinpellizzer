Premna interrupta

Premna interrupta, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-winged fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
