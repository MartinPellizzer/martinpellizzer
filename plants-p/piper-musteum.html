Piper musteum

Piper musteum, commonly known as the wild pepper or musteum pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the common black pepper plant. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
