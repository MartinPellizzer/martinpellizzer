Populus angulata var. serotina

Populus angulata var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering aspen, is a variant of the aspen species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its late summer flowering period, which occurs after most other aspen varieties. The plant has finely serrated leaves that turn yellow in the fall, contributing to its seasonal aesthetic. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
