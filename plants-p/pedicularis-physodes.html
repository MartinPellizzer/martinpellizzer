Pedicularis physodes

Pedicularis physodes, commonly known as the purple gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Pedicularis physodes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
