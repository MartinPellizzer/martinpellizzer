Physaria valida

Physaria valida, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy species adapted to harsh desert conditions.
