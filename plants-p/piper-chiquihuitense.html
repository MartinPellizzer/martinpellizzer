Piper chiquihuitense

Piper chiquihuitense, commonly known as the Chiquihuitl pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the state of Jalisco, where it grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a peppery berry that is used in traditional Mexican cuisine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
