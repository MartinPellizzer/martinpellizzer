Portulaca caryophylloides

Portulaca caryophylloides, commonly known as the Indian cress or rock purslane, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates. It has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Its seeds are often used in spice blends, contributing a peppery flavor.
