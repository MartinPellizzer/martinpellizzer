Parthenium incanum

Parthenium incanum, commonly known as rough hound's tongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to brown flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Parthenium incanum is also known for its allergenic properties, causing skin irritation in some individuals.
