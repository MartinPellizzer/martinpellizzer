Plectranthus coerulescens

Plectranthus coerulescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
