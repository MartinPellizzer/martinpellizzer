Pulsatilla × jolanthae

Pulsatilla × jolanthae, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Pulsatilla vulgaris and Pulsatilla patens, characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant features a short, hairy stem that bears a single, large, cup-shaped flower with yellow stamens and a distinctive central style. Its leaves are basal, palmately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
