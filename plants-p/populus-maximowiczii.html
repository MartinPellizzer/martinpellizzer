Populus maximowiczii

Populus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

Populus maximowiczii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
