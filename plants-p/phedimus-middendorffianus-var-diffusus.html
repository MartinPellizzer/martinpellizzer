Phedimus middendorffianus var. diffusus

Phedimus middendorffianus var. diffusus, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a dense mat of rounded, succulent leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels and attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
