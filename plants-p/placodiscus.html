Placodiscus

Placodiscus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosette-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. Placodiscus species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and blooms.

They prefer bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
