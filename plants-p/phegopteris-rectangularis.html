Phegopteris rectangularis

Phegopteris rectangularis, commonly known as the broad buckwheat fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
