Potentilla madrensis

Potentilla madrensis, commonly known as the Madrean potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Potentilla madrensis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
