Poa gracillima var. vaseyochloa

Poa gracillima var. vaseyochloa, commonly known as Vasey's meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
