Pseudolobivia wilkeae var. carminata

Pseudolobivia wilkeae var. carminata, commonly known as the Carminata Lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features spiny, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and are relatively short-lived.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
