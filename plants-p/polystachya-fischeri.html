Polystachya fischeri

Polystachya fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's torch orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it can thrive in the dappled light. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it often forms symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

The orchid's unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural interest.
