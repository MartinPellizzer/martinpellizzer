Papaver carmeli

Papaver carmeli, commonly known as the Carmel poppy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, rounded seed pod. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.

The Carmel poppy is often cultivated for its ornamental value and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
