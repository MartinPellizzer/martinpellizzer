Prunus fremontii subsp. pilulata

Prunus fremontii subsp.

pilulata, commonly known as the Fremont's cherry or the desert cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin and produces white or pinkish flowers in spring. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

The subspecies pilulata is distinguished by its more rounded fruit and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Prunus fremontii.
