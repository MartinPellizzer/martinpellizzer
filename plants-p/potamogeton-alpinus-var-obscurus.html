Potamogeton alpinus var. obscurus

Potamogeton alpinus var. obscurus, commonly known as the dark pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold, freshwater environments.

It typically grows in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has slender, branching stems with small, oval leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, and it plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
