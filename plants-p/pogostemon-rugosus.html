Pogostemon rugosus

Pogostemon rugosus, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
