Populus simonii var. breviamenta

Populus simonii var. breviamenta, commonly known as the short-bract poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, broad leaves and distinctive bracts that are shorter than those of the typical Populus simonii. The plant belongs to the Salicaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
