Pseudopilocereus fulvilanatus

Pseudopilocereus fulvilanatus, commonly known as the yellow-barked cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines and a distinctive yellowish-brown bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the evening, typically during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This cactus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
