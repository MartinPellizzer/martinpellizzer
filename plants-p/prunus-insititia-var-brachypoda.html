Prunus insititia var. brachypoda

Prunus insititia var. brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf black-cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptical in shape with a pointed tip. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
