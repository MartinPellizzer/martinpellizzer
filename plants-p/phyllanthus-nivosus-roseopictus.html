Phyllanthus nivosus roseopictus

Phyllanthus nivosus roseopictus, commonly known as the snowflake phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a delicate and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and potential medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a subject of botanical study.
