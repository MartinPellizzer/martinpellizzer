Panicum carinifolium

Panicum carinifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds, which are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and sandy soils.

Its drought tolerance and rapid growth make it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
