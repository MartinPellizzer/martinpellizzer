Pyrus andreanszkyana

Pyrus andreanszkyana, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor with a slightly tart finish.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional horticulture.
