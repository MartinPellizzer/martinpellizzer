Psedera quinquefolia

Psedera quinquefolia, commonly known as five-leafed psedera, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of five leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
