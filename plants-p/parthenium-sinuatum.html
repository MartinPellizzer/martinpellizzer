Parthenium sinuatum

Parthenium sinuatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open fields and along roadsides, preferring full sun and well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, while its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters.

Parthenium sinuatum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
