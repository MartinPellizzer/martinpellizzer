Packera sanguisorbae

Packera sanguisorbae, commonly known as the showy mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in full sunlight. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Packera sanguisorbae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
