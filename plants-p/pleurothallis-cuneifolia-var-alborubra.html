Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. alborubra

Pleurothallis cuneifolia var. alborubra, commonly known as the red-veined pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is distinguished by its slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a reddish veining, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is a terrestrial orchid that prefers well-draining soil and consistent moisture.
