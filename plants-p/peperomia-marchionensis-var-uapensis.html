Peperomia marchionensis var. uapensis

Peperomia marchionensis var. uapensis, commonly known as the Uapensian peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, elongated berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
