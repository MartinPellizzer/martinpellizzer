Pistacia chia

Pistacia chia, commonly known as chia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped seeds that are rich in omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, and antioxidants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has a shrub-like growth habit. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications and are also valued for their nutritional benefits.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid or semi-arid climates.
