Portulaca grandis

Portulaca grandis, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is an annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its succulent leaves and vibrant flowers. The plant produces small, showy blooms in shades of red, pink, and yellow, typically during the summer months. Its fleshy stems and leaves store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Portulaca grandis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
