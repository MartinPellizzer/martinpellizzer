Pseudocaryophyllus theifer

Pseudocaryophyllus theifer, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that typically open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that contain toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to conserve water and withstand high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
