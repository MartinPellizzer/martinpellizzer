Parthenocissus inserta f. dubia

Parthenocissus inserta f. dubia, commonly known as the variegated ivy grape.

This climbing vine is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often display variegation of white, yellow, and green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for trellises or walls.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage rather than its flowers or fruit.
