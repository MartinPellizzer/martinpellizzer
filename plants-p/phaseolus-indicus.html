Phaseolus indicus

Phaseolus indicus, commonly known as the Indian pea or hill pea, is a species of legume native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and purple or white flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown for its edible seeds.

Phaseolus indicus is valued both for its nutritional content and its role in traditional agriculture.
