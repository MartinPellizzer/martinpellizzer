Pterocarpus grandiflorus

Pterocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Java wood or the yellow-wood tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. Its fruit is a woody pod that contains several seeds.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
