Phaseolus schimperi

Phaseolus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian bean, is a leguminous plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its indeterminate growth habit and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and it flowers with small, white or pale yellow blooms. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to poor soils, making it an important crop in arid regions.

Its seeds are rich in protein and are a staple food in Ethiopian cuisine.
