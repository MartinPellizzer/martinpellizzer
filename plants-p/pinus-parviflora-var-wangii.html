Pinus parviflora var. wangii

Pinus parviflora var. wangii, commonly known as the Wangi pine, is a variant of the dwarf Japanese pine.

It is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Japan.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardiness.
