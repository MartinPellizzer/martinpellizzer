Piper mazamariense

Piper mazamariense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin. The plant is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
