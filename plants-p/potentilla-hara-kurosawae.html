Potentilla hara-kurosawae

Potentilla hara-kurosawae, commonly known as the Hara-Kurosawa potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like form with silver-gray foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and attractive plant for horticultural collections.
