Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp. lanatum

Pittosporum abyssinicum subsp.

lanatum, commonly known as the Abyssinian pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
