Polycarpaea depressa

Polycarpaea depressa, commonly known as the small-flowered polycarpaea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is not widely cultivated but is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
