Poa acroleuca var. spiciformis

Poa acroleuca var. spiciformis, commonly known as the spiciform bentgrass, is a variety of bentgrass native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptation to temperate climates and its use in ecological restoration projects.
