Persica flava

Persica flava, commonly known as the yellow peach, is a species of peach native to certain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow fruit, which is typically smaller and less sweet compared to the common peach. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white with a slight pink tinge, and they bloom in early spring.

The fruit is usually harvested in late summer, making it a valuable crop in its native habitat.
