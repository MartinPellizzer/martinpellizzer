Persea indecora

Persea indecora, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Persea indecora is often cultivated in warm climates for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit.
