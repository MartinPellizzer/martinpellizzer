Pseudoparis cauliflora var. tsaratananae

Pseudoparis cauliflora var. tsaratananae, commonly known as the Tsaratanana orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, where flowers are arranged in a compact, clustered pattern along the stem. The plant produces strikingly colorful blooms, often in shades of pink, purple, and white, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the rainforests of the Tsaratanana massif.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
