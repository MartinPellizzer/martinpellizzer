Phalaris ammophila

Phalaris ammophila, commonly known as the sand reed, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is well adapted to sandy and coastal environments, often found in dunes and other coastal habitats. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes through its extensive root system.

Phalaris ammophila is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
