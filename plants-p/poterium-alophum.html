Poterium alophum

Poterium alophum, commonly known as the African fireplant, is a striking member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, orb-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling small flames. The plant has a low-growing, succulent habit with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are borne on erect, branching stems and bloom during the cooler months, typically from spring to early summer.

Poterium alophum is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
