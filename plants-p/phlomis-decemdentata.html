Phlomis decemdentata

Phlomis decemdentata, commonly known as the ten-toothed mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flower clusters in late summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
