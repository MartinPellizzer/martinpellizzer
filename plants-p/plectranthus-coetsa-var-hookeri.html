Plectranthus coetsa var. hookeri

Plectranthus coetsa var. hookeri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces showy, showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
