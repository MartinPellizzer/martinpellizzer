Peperomia procumbens var. brachystachya

Peperomia procumbens var. brachystachya, commonly known as the dwarf peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically light green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spathes that enclose tiny, clustered flowers, which are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its compact size and ease of care.
