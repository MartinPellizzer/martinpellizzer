Pimpinella saxifraga f. dissecta

Pimpinella saxifraga f. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected cow parsley, is a botanical variant of the cow parsley species.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.
