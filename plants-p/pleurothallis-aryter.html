Pleurothallis aryter

Pleurothallis aryter, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis aryter is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
