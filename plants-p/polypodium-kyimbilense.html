Polypodium kyimbilense

Polypodium kyimbilense, commonly known as the Kyimbilense fern, is a species of polypody found in tropical regions.

It is a terrestrial fern with a distinctive frond structure characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium kyimbilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
