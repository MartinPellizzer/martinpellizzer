Poa lineata

Poa lineata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Poa lineata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
