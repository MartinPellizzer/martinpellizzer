Palaquium sambasense

Palaquium sambasense, commonly known as the Sambas wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits with a waxy coating. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and economic significance.
