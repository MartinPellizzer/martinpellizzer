Philydrum lanuginosum

Philydrum lanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly yellowroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a thick, yellowish rootstock with a distinct, fleshy texture. Its showy, yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the leaves are ovate with a soft, hairy surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
