Piper grandipedunculum

Piper grandipedunculum, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, trailing stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

The fruit, which is a small, three-lobed berry, contains the seeds and is often used in traditional medicine.
