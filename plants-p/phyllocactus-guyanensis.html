Phyllocactus guyanensis

Phyllocactus guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana candle cactus, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Phyllocactus guyanensis is often cultivated for its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
