Pentagramma triangularis subsp. rebmanii

Pentagramma triangularis subsp.

rebmanii, commonly known as the Rebman's starflower, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States. It features small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
