Piper aequale var. glanduligerum

Piper aequale var. glanduligerum, commonly known as the glandular pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and fruits contain various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

The glandular structures on its leaves and stems contribute to its unique chemical profile and medicinal properties.
