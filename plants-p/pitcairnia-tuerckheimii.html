Pitcairnia tuerckheimii

Pitcairnia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that can reach several feet in height, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, creating a natural reservoir for its epiphytic growth habit. Its flowers are typically long-lasting, making it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments, contributing to its ecological and horticultural significance.
