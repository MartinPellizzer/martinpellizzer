Pleurothallis aveniformis

Pleurothallis aveniformis, commonly known as the "sickle-shaped pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and are noted for their intricate, delicate structure.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and somewhat elusive species within the orchid family.
