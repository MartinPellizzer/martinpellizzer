Polycodium floridanum

Polycodium floridanum, commonly known as the Florida polycodium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
