Psidium richardianum

Psidium richardianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are rich in vitamin C and used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit is also used in culinary applications, particularly in jams and beverages.
