Pleocarphus dentatus

Pleocarphus dentatus, commonly known as the toothed fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The leaves are serrated, giving the plant its common name and contributing to its distinctive appearance.
