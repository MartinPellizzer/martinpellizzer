Pachystachys nutans

Pachystachys nutans, commonly known as the hanging bamboo or hanging grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that give it a unique, cascading appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its aesthetic appeal, it is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
