Polemonium caeruleum var. nipponicum

Polemonium caeruleum var. nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese bluebells, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polemonium genus within the family Polemoniaceae. The plant features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
