Pseudolysimachion ovatum var. villosum

Pseudolysimachion ovatum var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy ovate pseudolysimachion, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its botanical features include a compact stature and a preference for well-drained, humus-rich soil.

The variety 'villosum' is distinguished by its dense, velvety pubescence on the leaves and stems.
