Potamogeton marinus var. caespitosus

Potamogeton marinus var. caespitosus, commonly known as sea pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal regions.

It typically grows in brackish or saltwater environments, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves and produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of water quality and ecological health in coastal habitats.
