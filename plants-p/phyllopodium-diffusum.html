Phyllopodium diffusum

Phyllopodium diffusum, commonly known as the spreading leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that resemble the structure of a fern.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are typically broad and segmented, with a soft, velvety texture that distinguishes it from other fern species. It reproduces through spores, which are released from small, clustered structures on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
