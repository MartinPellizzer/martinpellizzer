Pseudosasa orthotropa

Pseudosasa orthotropa, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or clumping bamboo, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, followed by triangular, feather-like seeds. Its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly curved, often with a waxy coating.

Pseudosasa orthotropa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
