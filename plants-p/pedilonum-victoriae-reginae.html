Pedilonum victoriae-reginae

Pedilonum victoriae-reginae, commonly known as the Victoria's slipper orchid, is a rare and exquisite member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America, where it thrives in cool, humid environments. The plant is distinguished by its unique, slipper-shaped flower that resembles a small cup, typically in shades of pink and white. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the rocky soil in which it grows.

Due to its endangered status, it is highly sought after by botanists and conservationists alike.
