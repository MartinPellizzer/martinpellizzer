Pisum sativum var. debelii

Pisum sativum var. debelii, commonly known as the mountain pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, edible pods containing several seeds, similar to other pea varieties. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to harsh climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
