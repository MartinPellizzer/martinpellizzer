Phalacrodiscus lanceolatus

Phalacrodiscus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate phalacrodiscus, is a member of the Phalacrodiscaceae family.

It is a small, aquatic plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette formation. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for water dispersal.

The species is characterized by its delicate structure and preference for nutrient-rich, calm waters.
