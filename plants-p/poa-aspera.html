Poa aspera

Poa aspera, commonly known as rough bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelets.

Poa aspera is often found in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
