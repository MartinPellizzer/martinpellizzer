Piper hippocrepiforme

Piper hippocrepiforme, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, heart-shaped leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
