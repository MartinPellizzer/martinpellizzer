Puccinellia manchuriensis

Puccinellia manchuriensis, commonly known as Manchurian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Puccinellia manchuriensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
