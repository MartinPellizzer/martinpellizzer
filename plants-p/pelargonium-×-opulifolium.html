Pelargonium × opulifolium

Pelargonium × opulifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often with a contrasting throat. It is typically grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and containers due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium × opulifolium prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
