Polygonum cardiophyllum

Polygonum cardiophyllum, commonly known as the heartleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and often edible parts. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Polygonum cardiophyllum is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
