Potamogeton alpinus f. ovatifolius

Potamogeton alpinus f. ovatifolius, commonly known as the alpine pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to cold, freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves that are typically submerged, aiding in photosynthesis in aquatic settings. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often found near the water's surface. It thrives in nutrient-rich, shallow waters and is an important part of the aquatic ecosystem.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often found in ponds and lakes in mountainous regions.
