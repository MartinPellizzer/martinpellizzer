Populus balsamifera var. simonii

Populus balsamifera var. simonii, commonly known as the Chinese balsam poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The tree produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, cone-like fruits that release seeds with feathery appendages. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
