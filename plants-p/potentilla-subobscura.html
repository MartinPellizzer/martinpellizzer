Potentilla subobscura

Potentilla subobscura, commonly known as the darkleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Potentilla subobscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
