Plantago zerpentina

Plantago zerpentina, commonly known as creeping plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a prostrate growth habit with slender, branching stems that root at the nodes. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture and a prominent central vein. It produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
