Polypodium pleopeltidis

Polypodium pleopeltidis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a creeping rhizome with sporangia clustered on the underside of the fronds. Its fronds are pinnate, with lanceolate to ovate pinnae that are often covered in fine hairs.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
