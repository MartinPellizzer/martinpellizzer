Pfaffia paniculata var. glabrata

Pfaffia paniculata var. glabrata, commonly known as Paraguay tea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It is commonly cultivated for its leaves, which are used to make a herbal tea.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though scientific evidence supporting these claims remains limited.
