Pimelea octophylla var. behrii

Pimelea octophylla var. behrii, commonly known as the Behri's candlebush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand salt spray and strong winds.
