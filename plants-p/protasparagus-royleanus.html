Protasparagus royleanus

Protasparagus royleanus, commonly known as the Kashmir asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
