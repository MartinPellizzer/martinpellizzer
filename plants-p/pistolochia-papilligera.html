Pistolochia papilligera

Pistolochia papilligera, commonly known as the prickly gooseberry, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often reddish in color and contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
