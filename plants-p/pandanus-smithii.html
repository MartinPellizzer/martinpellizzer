Pandanus smithii

Pandanus smithii, commonly known as the Smith's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly found in parts of Hawaii and other island chains. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescences, or flower clusters, are typically borne at the ends of the stems and consist of small, fragrant flowers.

Pandanus smithii is often used in traditional Hawaiian culture for its ornamental value and has also been utilized in various practical applications.
