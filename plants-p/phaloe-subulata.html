Phaloe subulata

Phaloe subulata, commonly known as the slender phaloe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among plants in its ecological niche.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
