Platytaenia thomsonii

Platytaenia thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's flatleaf, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Platytaenia thomsonii is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
