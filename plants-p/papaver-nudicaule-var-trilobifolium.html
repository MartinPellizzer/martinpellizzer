Papaver nudicaule var. trilobifolium

Papaver nudicaule var. trilobifolium, commonly known as the three-lobed poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three distinct lobes, giving it its specific name.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
