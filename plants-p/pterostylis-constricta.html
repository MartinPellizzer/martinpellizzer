Pterostylis constricta

Pterostylis constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a distinctive greenish-brown flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate markings that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pterostylis constricta is a rare and endangered species, requiring careful conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
