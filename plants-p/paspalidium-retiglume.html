Paspalidium retiglume

Paspalidium retiglume, commonly known as the narrow-leaved grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Paspalidium retiglume is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
