Polygonum aviculare subvar. humifusum

Polygonum aviculare subvar. humifusum, commonly known as creeping knotweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features prostrate or decumbent stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread rapidly along the ground. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a slightly hairy surface, and the small, greenish-white flowers appear in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its resilience and ability to colonize areas quickly, making it both a common weed and a useful pioneer species in ecological succession.
