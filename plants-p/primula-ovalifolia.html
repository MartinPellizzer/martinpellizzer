Primula ovalifolia

Primula ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
