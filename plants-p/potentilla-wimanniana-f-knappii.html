Potentilla wimanniana f. knappii

Potentilla wimanniana f. knappii, commonly known as the Knapp's potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with finely divided leaves that help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in challenging climates.
