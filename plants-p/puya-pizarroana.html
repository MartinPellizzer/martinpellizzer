Puya pizarroana

Puya pizarroana, commonly known as the Pizarro's puya, is a striking member of the bromeliad family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, bearing vibrant red and orange flowers. The plant produces large, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a unique and dramatic appearance. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, humid climates and well-drained soil.

Puya pizarroana is also notable for its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months.
