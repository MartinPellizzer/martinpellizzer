Phenax pentlandianus

Phenax pentlandianus, commonly known as the Pentland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Scottish Highlands.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland or heathland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

The species is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
