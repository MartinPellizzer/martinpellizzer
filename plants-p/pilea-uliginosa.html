Pilea uliginosa

Pilea uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp pilea, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in wetland ecosystems.
