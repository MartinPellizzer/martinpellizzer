Pandanus mareensis

Pandanus mareensis, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into fibrous fruits. Its root system is adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for both its ornamental beauty and practical uses in local cultures.
