Pavonia decora

Pavonia decora, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white eye, and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia decora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
