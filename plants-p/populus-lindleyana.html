Populus lindleyana

Populus lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Populus lindleyana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
