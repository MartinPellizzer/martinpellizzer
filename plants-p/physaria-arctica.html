Physaria arctica

Physaria arctica, commonly known as the Arctic bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, dry environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, and its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Physaria arctica plays a role in the ecological balance of its harsh environment, supporting various insect and animal life.
