Potentilla tobolensis

Potentilla tobolensis, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a hairy texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Potentilla tobolensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh, temperate conditions.
