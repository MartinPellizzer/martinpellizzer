Pandanus horneinsularum

Pandanus horneinsularum, commonly known as the island pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly found in the islands of Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional weaving practices. Its inflorescences consist of clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its native habitats.
