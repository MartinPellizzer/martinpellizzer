Passiflora pentagona

Passiflora pentagona, commonly known as the five-angled passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and showy, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its foliage consists of palmately lobed leaves with a rough texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Passiflora pentagona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
