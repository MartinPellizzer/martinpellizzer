Pilosella piloselloides subsp. albidobractea

Pilosella piloselloides subsp.

albidobractea, commonly known as the white bract pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its species name "pilosella.".

The subspecies albidobractea is distinguished by its pale, almost white bracts that surround the flower heads.
