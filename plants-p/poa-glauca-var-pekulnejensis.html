Poa glauca var. pekulnejensis

Poa glauca var. pekulnejensis, commonly known as the Pekulnejensis bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and pale blue-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact structure that helps it withstand environmental stress.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and ecological adaptations.
