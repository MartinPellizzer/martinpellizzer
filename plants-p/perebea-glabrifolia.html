Perebea glabrifolia

Perebea glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved perebea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
