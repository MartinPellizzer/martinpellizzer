Pinanga stylosa

Pinanga stylosa, commonly known as the slender bamboo palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clustered, pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a smooth, waxy surface.

Pinanga stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
