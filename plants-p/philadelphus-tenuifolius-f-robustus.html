Philadelphus tenuifolius f. robustus

Philadelphus tenuifolius f. robustus, commonly known as the robust form of the narrow-leaved mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching branches with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are smaller than those of the species. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its showy floral display.

This cultivar is relatively hardy and adaptable, making it suitable for a variety of garden conditions.
