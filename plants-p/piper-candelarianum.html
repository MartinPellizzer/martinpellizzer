Piper candelarianum

Piper candelarianum, commonly known as the flame vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated, red to orange berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its vigorous growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid climates.

The botanical features of Piper candelarianum make it an attractive ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
