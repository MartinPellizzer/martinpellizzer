Papaver postii

Papaver postii, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange petals that resemble those of the common poppy. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, high-altitude climates. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, often in clusters, and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Papaver postii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
