Penstemon shantzii var. incognitus

Penstemon shantzii var. incognitus, commonly known as the Shantz's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
