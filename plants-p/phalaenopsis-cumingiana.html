Phalaenopsis cumingiana

Phalaenopsis cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers with a velvety texture. It blooms once a year, usually in the spring or early summer.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its relatively low maintenance requirements.
