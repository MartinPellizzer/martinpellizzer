Prunus incisa var. bellura

Prunus incisa var. bellura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish-brown when young and turn dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically white or pale pink, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
