Polygala luzoniensis

Polygala luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as montane forests and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
