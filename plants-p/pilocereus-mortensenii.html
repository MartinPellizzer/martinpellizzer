Pilocereus mortensenii

Pilocereus mortensenii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering cactus species native to South America.

It features a swollen, colorful tuberous stem that resembles a flower, while its true stem is a slender, green rootstock. The plant produces showy, white or yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
