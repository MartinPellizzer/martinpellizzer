Phyllanthus assimilis

Phyllanthus assimilis, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and is typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Phyllanthus assimilis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
