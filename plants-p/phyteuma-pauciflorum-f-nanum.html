Phyteuma pauciflorum f. nanum

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf phyteuma, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of no more than 15 centimeters, making it ideal for rock gardens or alpine settings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
