Podocarpus macrostachys

Podocarpus macrostachys, commonly known as the Hawaiian Christmas tree, is a coniferous evergreen shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a landscape plant in warm climates.
