Plumeria hypoleuca var. angustifolia

Plumeria hypoleuca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, producing showy, waxy petals that emit a sweet, citrus-like scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental foliage.
