Potentilla recta subsp. pilosa

Potentilla recta subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia and North America. It features a mat-forming growth habit with numerous slender, upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky slopes.
