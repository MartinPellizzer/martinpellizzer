Panicum barbipulvinatum

Panicum barbipulvinatum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive hairy structures on its leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of bracts, which are often hairy and contribute to its common name.

Panicum barbipulvinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
