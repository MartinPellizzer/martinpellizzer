Philodendron pinnatifidum

Philodendron pinnatifidum, commonly known as the giant philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, with a deep green color and prominent veins. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil. It has a rapid growth rate and is often used as a decorative foliage plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.

Philodendron pinnatifidum is also notable for its ability to produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, berry-like fruits.
