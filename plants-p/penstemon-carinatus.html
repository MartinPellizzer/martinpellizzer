Penstemon carinatus

Penstemon carinatus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Penstemon carinatus plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
