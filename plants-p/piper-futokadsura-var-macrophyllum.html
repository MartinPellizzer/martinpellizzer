Piper futokadsura var. macrophyllum

Piper futokadsura var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
