Paspalum remotum

Paspalum remotum, commonly known as Florida grass or carpet grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, making it a dioecious species. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to various soil types and tolerance to flooding make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
