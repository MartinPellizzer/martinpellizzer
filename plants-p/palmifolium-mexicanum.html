Palmifolium mexicanum

Palmifolium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, coastal climates and is often found in sandy soils along the Gulf Coast. Its leaves are typically light green and can grow up to several feet in diameter, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its use in landscaping.
