Platypetalum dubium

Platypetalum dubium, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Platypetalum dubium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
