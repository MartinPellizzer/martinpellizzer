Persea leiogyna

Persea leiogyna, commonly known as the smooth-leafed persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are leathery and have a smooth texture, contributing to its common name.

Persea leiogyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit in warm climates.
