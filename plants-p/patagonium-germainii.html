Patagonium germainii

Patagonium germainii, commonly known as the Patagonian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh, cold climates of Patagonia. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Patagonium germainii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
