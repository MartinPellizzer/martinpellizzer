Pseudolysimachion euxinum

Pseudolysimachion euxinum, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of southern Ukraine and the Black Sea region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late summer, which are clustered in terminal racemes. It has fibrous roots and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the region's semi-arid grassland ecosystems.
