Pyrus acutiloba

Pyrus acutiloba, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are elongated, serrated, and turn yellow in autumn.

Pyrus acutiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
