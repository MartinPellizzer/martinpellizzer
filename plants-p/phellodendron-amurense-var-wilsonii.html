Phellodendron amurense var. wilsonii

Phellodendron amurense var. wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's Amur corktree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that peels in thin layers, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange to red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its medicinal properties.
