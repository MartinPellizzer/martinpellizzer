Phlox longifolia subsp. calva

Phlox longifolia subsp.

calva, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and open woodland habitats, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant produces showy clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
