Pultenaea vrolandii

Pultenaea vrolandii, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, wiry stems and bipinnate leaves, which are divided into small, leaflets. It is often found in sandy soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
