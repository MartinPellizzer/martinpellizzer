Piper bredemeyeri var. flexuosum

Piper bredemeyeri var. flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and green walls in tropical gardens.
