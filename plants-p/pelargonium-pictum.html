Pelargonium pictum

Pelargonium pictum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, green, and pink, often with a distinctive pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium pictum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
