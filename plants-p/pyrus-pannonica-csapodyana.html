Pyrus pannonica csapodyana

Pyrus pannonica csapodyana, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Carpathian Basin and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white to pink flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in Central European horticulture.
