Passiflora silvicola

Passiflora silvicola, commonly known as the forest passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, pulpy interior with numerous seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora silvicola is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and other wildlife.
