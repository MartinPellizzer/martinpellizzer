Potamogeton × pseudolucens

Potamogeton × pseudolucens, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton lucens and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, often found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Its morphology reflects a combination of traits from both parent species, making it a notable example of hybridization in aquatic flora.
