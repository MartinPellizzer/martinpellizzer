Peperomia arabica

Peperomia arabica, commonly known as the African violet, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and ability to thrive in humid environments. It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.

Peperomia arabica is often propagated through leaf cuttings or division, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
