Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. hirsuta

Parthenocissus quinquefolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-leafed foliage, with the leaves often appearing fuzzy or hairy, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its vigorous growth habit and attractive autumn coloration make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

However, it can be invasive in some regions and requires careful management to prevent it from overtaking native vegetation.
