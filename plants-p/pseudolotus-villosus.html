Pseudolotus villosus

Pseudolotus villosus, commonly known as the hairy lotus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which help it adapt to high-altitude environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Pseudolotus villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
