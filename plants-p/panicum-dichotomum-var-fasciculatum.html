Panicum dichotomum var. fasciculatum

Panicum dichotomum var. fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters, giving it a compact appearance. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This variant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
