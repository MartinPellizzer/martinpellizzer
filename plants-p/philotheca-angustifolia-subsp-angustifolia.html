Philotheca angustifolia subsp. angustifolia

Philotheca angustifolia subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually gray-green in color and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

This species is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
