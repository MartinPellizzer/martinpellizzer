Phalangium annuum

Phalangium annuum, commonly known as the annual phalangium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

While not widely cultivated, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in phytoremediation.
