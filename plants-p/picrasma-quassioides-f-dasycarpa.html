Picrasma quassioides f. dasycarpa

Picrasma quassioides f. dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited quassia, is a variant of the quassia tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive, rough-textured fruits. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The f. dasycarpa form is notable for its unique fruit morphology compared to the typical quassia species.
