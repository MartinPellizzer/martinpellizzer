Pandanus fragrans f. koidzumii

Pandanus fragrans f. koidzumii, commonly known as the fragrant pandanus, is a variant of the pandanus genus native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires ample sunlight to grow optimally.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in local communities.
