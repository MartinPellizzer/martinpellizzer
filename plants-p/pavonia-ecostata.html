Pavonia ecostata

Pavonia ecostata, commonly known as the coastal pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavonia ecostata is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal environments.
