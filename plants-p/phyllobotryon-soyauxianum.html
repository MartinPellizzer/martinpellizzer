Phyllobotryon soyauxianum

Phyllobotryon soyauxianum, commonly known as the leaf blister fungus, is a plant pathogen that primarily affects the leaves of various plants, particularly those in the Asteraceae family.

It causes distinctive leaf blisters that form due to the proliferation of its microscopic spores within the plant tissue. The fungus produces a unique structure called a pycnidium, which serves as a site for spore production and release. It thrives in moist and humid conditions, often leading to significant damage in agricultural and ornamental settings.

Due to its impact on plant health, it is a subject of study in both botanical and agricultural sciences.
