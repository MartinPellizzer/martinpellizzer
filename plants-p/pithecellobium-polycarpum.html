Pithecellobium polycarpum

Pithecellobium polycarpum, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
