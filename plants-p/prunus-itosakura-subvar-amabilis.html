Prunus itosakura subvar. amabilis

Prunus itosakura subvar. amabilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
