Papaver variegatum

Papaver variegatum, commonly known as the variegated poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant features smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
