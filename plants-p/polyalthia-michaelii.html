Polyalthia michaelii

Polyalthia michaelii, commonly known as the Malabar flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, greyish bark and pinnate leaves that are alternate and compound. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its showy floral display.
