Pandanus vitiensis

Pandanus vitiensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, often growing in saline or brackish environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in the region's biodiversity.
