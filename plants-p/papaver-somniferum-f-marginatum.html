Papaver somniferum f. marginatum

Papaver somniferum f. marginatum, commonly known as the opium poppy with margins, is a botanical variant of the opium poppy.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale pink flowers with prominent red or pink margins. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough texture.

This cultivar is notable for its historical and medicinal significance, as it is the source of opium and other alkaloids.
