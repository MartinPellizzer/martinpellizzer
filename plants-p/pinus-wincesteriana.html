Pinus wincesteriana

Pinus wincesteriana, commonly known as the Wincester pine, is a rare and endangered species native to the high elevations of the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils. The tree has slender, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its needles are long and slender, often retaining their color through winter. It is characterized by its slow growth rate and unique ecological niche, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is critically endangered and requires specialized conservation efforts.
