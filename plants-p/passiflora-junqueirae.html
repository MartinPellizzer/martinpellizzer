Passiflora junqueirae

Passiflora junqueirae, commonly known as the Junqueira passionflower, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with heart-shaped leaves and tendrils for support. Its fruit is a greenish-yellow berry that matures to a red color, containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
