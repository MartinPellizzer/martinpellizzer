Phyllostegia cernua

Phyllostegia cernua, commonly known as the weeping strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of violet or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its unique, drooping inflorescence gives it the common name "weeping strobilanthes.".

Phyllostegia cernua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.
