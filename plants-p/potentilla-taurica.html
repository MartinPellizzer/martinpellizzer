Potentilla taurica

Potentilla taurica, commonly known as the Crimean cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has slender, woody stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Potentilla taurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
