Plantago hirtella var. pachystachys

Plantago hirtella var. pachystachys, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features dense, hairy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a rough texture. The plant produces tall, spikelike inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
