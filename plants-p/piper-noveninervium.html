Piper noveninervium

Piper noveninervium, commonly known as the nine-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves with nine distinct veins. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
