Porophyllum pygmaeum

Porophyllum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats. Its small, blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has a low, spreading growth habit and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.
