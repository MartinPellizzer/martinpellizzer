Populus arizonica var. jonesii

Populus arizonica var. jonesii, commonly known as the Arizona poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in early spring, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in riparian zones and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
