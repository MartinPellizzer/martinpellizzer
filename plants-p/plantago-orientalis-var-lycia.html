Plantago orientalis var. lycia

Plantago orientalis var. lycia, commonly known as the Turkish plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been utilized in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
