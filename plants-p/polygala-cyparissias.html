Polygala cyparissias

Polygala cyparissias, commonly known as the cypress-leaved milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of a cypress tree. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
