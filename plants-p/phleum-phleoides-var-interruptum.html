Phleum phleoides var. interruptum

Phleum phleoides var. interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its distinctive interrupted growth pattern, which gives it its scientific name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
