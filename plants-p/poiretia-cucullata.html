Poiretia cucullata

Poiretia cucullata, commonly known as the hooded bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure on the petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poiretia cucullata is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and adaptability to humid climates.
