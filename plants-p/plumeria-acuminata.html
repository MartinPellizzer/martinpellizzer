Plumeria acuminata

Plumeria acuminata, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that come in various colors such as white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces woody, spiny capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Plumeria acuminata is well adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
