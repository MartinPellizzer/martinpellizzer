Pteris semipinnata var. aequata

Pteris semipinnata var. aequata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the common water fern.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. The plant has a delicate, feathery frond structure with a distinct, narrow pinnate arrangement. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
