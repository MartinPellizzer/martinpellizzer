Phymatopsis oxyloba

Phymatopsis oxyloba, commonly known as the redroot false nettle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often have a reddish hue, particularly at the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on leafless stalks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a hairy surface.

Phymatopsis oxyloba thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
