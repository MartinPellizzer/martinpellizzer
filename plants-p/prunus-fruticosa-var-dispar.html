Prunus fruticosa var. dispar

Prunus fruticosa var. dispar, commonly known as the variable cherry or shrub cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and turn red or yellow in the fall.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
