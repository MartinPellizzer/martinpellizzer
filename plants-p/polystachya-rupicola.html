Polystachya rupicola

Polystachya rupicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with several linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in dense, branched inflorescences.

Polystachya rupicola is adapted to cool, humid conditions and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude habitats.
