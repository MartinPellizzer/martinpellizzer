Polygala arillata f. chartacea

Polygala arillata f. chartacea, commonly known as the chartaceous polygala, is a unique variant of the polygala genus native to southern Africa.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, chartaceous (paper-like) leaves, which are often pale green to whitish in color. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native range.
