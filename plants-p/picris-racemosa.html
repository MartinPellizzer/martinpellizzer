Picris racemosa

Picris racemosa, commonly known as goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant features a tall, branching stem with narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. Its showy yellow flowers appear in clusters, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Picris racemosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.
