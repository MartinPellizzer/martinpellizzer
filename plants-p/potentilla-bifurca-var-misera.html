Potentilla bifurca var. misera

Potentilla bifurca var. misera, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
