Polygala gomphrenoides

Polygala gomphrenoides, commonly known as the flame flower or red flame grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent that attracts pollinators.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting floral display.
