Parsonsia ciliata

Parsonsia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild bitterness. Parsonsia ciliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and horticultural collections.
