Pavonia baillonii

Pavonia baillonii, commonly known as the Baillon's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pavonia baillonii is characterized by its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
