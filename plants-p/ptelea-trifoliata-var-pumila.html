Ptelea trifoliata var. pumila

Ptelea trifoliata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf silky dogwood, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
