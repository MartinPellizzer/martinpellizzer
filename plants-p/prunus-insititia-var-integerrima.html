Prunus insititia var. integerrima

Prunus insititia var. integerrima, commonly known as the wild cherry or black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red, edible drupes. Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
