Polygala rosea subsp. boissieri

Polygala rosea subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features delicate, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and capsule fruits that release tiny seeds with silky appendages.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
