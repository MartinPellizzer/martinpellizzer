Pollinia eriopoda

Pollinia eriopoda, commonly known as the woolly pollinia orchid, is a unique species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pollinia, which are clustered and covered in a fine, hairy layer, aiding in efficient pollination. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with specialized structures that facilitate the attachment of pollinia to visiting insects.

The botanical features of Pollinia eriopoda make it an interesting subject for studies on plant pollination mechanisms and orchid evolution.
