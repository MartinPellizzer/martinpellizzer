Phaeopappus chrysocollus

Phaeopappus chrysocollus, commonly known as the golden collared pappus, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellowish-orange bracts that surround the small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a distinctive pappus, a feathery structure that aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecology.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
