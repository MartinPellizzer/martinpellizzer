Pilea dispar

Pilea dispar, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its round, succulent leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea dispar is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
