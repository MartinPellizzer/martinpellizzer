Polanisia diandra

Polanisia diandra, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces striking white flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit, which is typically yellow or red when ripe, contains a white, juicy pulp surrounded by a thick, inedible rind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
