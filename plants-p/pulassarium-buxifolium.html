Pulassarium buxifolium

Pulassarium buxifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is glossy and dark green with a silvery underside. The plant has small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive leaves make it suitable for hedges and topiary.
