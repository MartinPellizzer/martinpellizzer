Phymatodes geminata

Phymatodes geminata, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of small, spiny fruits. It has adapted to harsh environments through deep root systems and a waxy cuticle that reduces water loss.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for survival in dry, nutrient-poor soils.
