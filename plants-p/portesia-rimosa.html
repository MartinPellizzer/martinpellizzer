Portesia rimosa

Portesia rimosa, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is typically found in moist, disturbed soils. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a basal rosette of leaves and a single stem that supports the flowering head.

Portesia rimosa plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
