Philadelphus serpyllifolius var. intermedius

Philadelphus serpyllifolius var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
