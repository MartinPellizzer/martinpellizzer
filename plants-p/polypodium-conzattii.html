Polypodium conzattii

Polypodium conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's shield fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a distinctive, shield-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in rows along the margins of the fronds.

Polypodium conzattii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its unique foliage.
