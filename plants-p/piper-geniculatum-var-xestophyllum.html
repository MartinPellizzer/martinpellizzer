Piper geniculatum var. xestophyllum

Piper geniculatum var. xestophyllum, commonly known as the yellow betel vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional practices, though its medicinal uses are less well-documented compared to other Piper species.
