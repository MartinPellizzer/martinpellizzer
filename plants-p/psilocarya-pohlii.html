Psilocarya pohlii

Psilocarya pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's psilocybe, is a species of psychedelic mushroom native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Psilocybe and is characterized by its small, cap-like structure that ranges from white to light brown in color. The fruiting bodies typically grow on decaying wood, often in humid and shaded environments. This species contains psilocybin and psilocin, psychoactive compounds that induce hallucinogenic effects when consumed.

Due to its potent psychoactive properties, it is often sought after by mycologists and psychotropic enthusiasts.
