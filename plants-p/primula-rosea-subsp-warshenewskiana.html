Primula rosea subsp. warshenewskiana

Primula rosea subsp.

warshenewskiana, commonly known as the Warshenewskiana primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, with a prominent yellow throat.

The plant produces basal leaves that are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Primulaceae family and is known for its horticultural value in gardens and rockeries.
