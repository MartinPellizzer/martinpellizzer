Platycodon grandiflorus f. subasepalus

Platycodon grandiflorus f. subasepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal bellflower, is a botanical variant of the Chinese bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant features narrow, lanceolate leaves and a short, upright stem. It is native to regions of East Asia, including China and Korea, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
