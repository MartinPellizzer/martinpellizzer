Potentilla curviseta

Potentilla curviseta, commonly known as the curling potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and small, rounded leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla curviseta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
