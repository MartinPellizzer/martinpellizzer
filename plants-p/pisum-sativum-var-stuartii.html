Pisum sativum var. stuartii

Pisum sativum var. stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more rounded pods compared to standard peas. The plant produces edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in root nodules.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various climates and its use in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
