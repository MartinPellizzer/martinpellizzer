Pachycereus ruficeps

Pachycereus ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed cactus, is a columnar cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant has pale green, spiny stems with prominent areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance.
