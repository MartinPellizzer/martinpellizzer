Pyrus × fallax

Pyrus × fallax, commonly known as the hybrid pear.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (wild pear). This plant is characterized by its vigorous growth and variable morphology, often displaying traits from both parent species. The leaves are typically lobed and have a rough texture, while the fruit can vary in size and shape.

It is primarily found in temperate regions and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
