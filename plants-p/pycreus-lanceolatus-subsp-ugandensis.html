Pycreus lanceolatus subsp. ugandensis

Pycreus lanceolatus subsp.

ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda lanceleaf, is a rare and endemic plant species found primarily in the mountainous regions of Uganda. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the specific microclimates of its native habitat.
