Pleuroplitis schimperi

Pleuroplitis schimperi, commonly known as the African orchid or Schimper's orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
