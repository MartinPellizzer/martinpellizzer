Piper puente-altoanum

Piper puente-altoanum, commonly known as the Puerto Rican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Puerto Rico and the Virgin Islands. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
