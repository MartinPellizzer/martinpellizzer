Pandanus calostigma f. samoanus

Pandanus calostigma f. samoanus, commonly known as the Samoan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a variant of the common pandanus species, distinguished by its unique foliage and growth characteristics. The plant features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, adapting well to sandy soils and salt spray.

Its resilience and ornamental value make it a significant species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
