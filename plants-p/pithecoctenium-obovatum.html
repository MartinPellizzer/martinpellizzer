Pithecoctenium obovatum

Pithecoctenium obovatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad-ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, often followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
