Passiflora suberosa var. pallida

Passiflora suberosa var. pallida, commonly known as the pale passion vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central calyx. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

The pale passion vine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
