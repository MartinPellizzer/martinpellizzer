Prunus × yedoensis f. mishimazakura

Prunus × yedoensis f. mishimazakura, commonly known as the Mishima cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its exceptionally pale pink to almost white flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus yedoensis and Prunus lannesiana, resulting in its distinctive floral coloration. It is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and during cherry blossom festivals.

The foliage is dark green, and the tree has a compact, rounded shape.
