Plantago subulata var. atlantis

Plantago subulata var. atlantis, commonly known as the Atlantis plantain, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage with sharp, spiny leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
