Pilderia urticifolia

Pilderia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous species characterized by its erect, hairy stems and alternate leaves.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges and are covered in fine, stinging hairs that release irritants when touched. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. The plant is native to temperate regions and thrives in moist, shady environments.

It is often found in woodland areas and along the edges of streams.
