Polygonum chinense var. malaicum

Polygonum chinense var. malaicum, commonly known as the Malacca knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.
