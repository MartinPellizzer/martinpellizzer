Paepalanthus sericifolius

Paepalanthus sericifolius, commonly known as the silky leaf cactus, is a unique plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white hairs, which helps it conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a short, thick stem that stores water.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
