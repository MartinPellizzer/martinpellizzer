Potamogeton mandschuriensis

Potamogeton mandschuriensis, commonly known as the Manchurian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in East Asia.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, rivers, and wetlands.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
