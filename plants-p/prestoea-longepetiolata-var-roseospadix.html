Prestoea longepetiolata var. roseospadix

Prestoea longepetiolata var. roseospadix, commonly known as the rose-spadix poinsettia, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous true flowers surrounded by the showy spadix. Its leaves are elongated and have a waxy texture, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
