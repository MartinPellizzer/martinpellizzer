Pyrethrum parthenium

Pyrethrum parthenium, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its daisy-like white flowers with yellow centers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves contain compounds such as parthenin, which are responsible for its medicinal properties.

Pyrethrum parthenium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
