Passiflora suberosa var. angustifolia

Passiflora suberosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated margin. This species produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
