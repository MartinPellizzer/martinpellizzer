Poikilacanthus tweedieanus

Poikilacanthus tweedieanus, commonly known as the Tweedie's spurge, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy stems that can vary in color from green to reddish. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a waxy surface, adapting to arid environments.

Poikilacanthus tweedieanus is notable for its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
