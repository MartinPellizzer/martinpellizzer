Podocarpus brassii var. brassii

Podocarpus brassii var. brassii, commonly known as the silver fir or yellow wood, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
