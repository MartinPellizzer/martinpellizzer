Purgobulea × serpentaria

Purgobulea × serpentaria, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a central cone-shaped flower head. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Purgobulea × serpentaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
