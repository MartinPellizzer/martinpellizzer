Pseudophoenix sargentii var. navassana

Pseudophoenix sargentii var. navassana, commonly known as the Navassa palm, is a rare and unique species native to the Navassa Island in the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like fronds that give it a distinctive, palm-like appearance. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive, including well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of several feet, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution and conservation status, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
