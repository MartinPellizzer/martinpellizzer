Pediocactus bradyi var. winkleri

Pediocactus bradyi var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's barrel cactus, is a rare and compact cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines and a dense, woolly coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
