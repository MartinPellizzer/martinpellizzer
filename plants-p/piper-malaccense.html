Piper malaccense

Piper malaccense, commonly known as the Bornean pepper or Malacca pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and dark, peppery fruits. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
