Pernettya purpurea var. pentlandii

Pernettya purpurea var. pentlandii, commonly known as the Scottish heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It features small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine conditions.
