Piper quintuplinervium

Piper quintuplinervium, commonly known as the five-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, ovate leaves with five distinct veins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn red when mature. Native to the rainforests of Central and South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
