Polium pratense

Polium pratense, commonly known as field panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with whorled leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, greenish-brown florets.

Polium pratense is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local insect populations.
