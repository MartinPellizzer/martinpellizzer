Psidiomyrtus

Psidiomyrtus, commonly known as the myrtle or myrtle tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

These plants are characterized by their aromatic leaves, which often emit a fragrant scent when crushed. They typically have small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The fruit is a small, edible berry that contains several seeds.

Psidiomyrtus species are native to various regions in the Southern Hemisphere, including South America and Australia.
