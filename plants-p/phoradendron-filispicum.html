Phoradendron filispicum

Phoradendron filispicum, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in a dense, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat the berries, contributing to its spread across different host species.

The plant's parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
