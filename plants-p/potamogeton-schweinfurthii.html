Potamogeton schweinfurthii

Potamogeton schweinfurthii, commonly known as Schweinfurth's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Its rhizomes help anchor it to the substrate, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic sediments.
