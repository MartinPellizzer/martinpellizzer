Podocarpus agathifolius

Podocarpus agathifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape, with needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Podocarpus agathifolius is also valued for its durability and is used in landscaping and as a windbreak in coastal areas.
