Penstemon lentus var. albiflorus

Penstemon lentus var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
