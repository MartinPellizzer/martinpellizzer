Pleurospermum decurrens

Pleurospermum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and sunny environments. The plant features a sprawling, low-growing habit with numerous small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while the stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Pleurospermum decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and prolonged flowering period.
