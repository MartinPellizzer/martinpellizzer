Phaseolus pictus

Phaseolus pictus, commonly known as the painted bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes red or purple hues. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are edible when mature. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add visual interest to their landscapes.

Additionally, like other legumes, it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, benefiting soil fertility.
