Putterlickia neglecta

Putterlickia neglecta, commonly known as the neglected putterlick, is a small, herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain areas.
