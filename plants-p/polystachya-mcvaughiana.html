Polystachya mcvaughiana

Polystachya mcvaughiana, commonly known as the McVaugh's bromeliad, is a species of flowering plant in the Bromeliaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central pool to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in tropical flora.
