Piper valdivianum

Piper valdivianum, commonly known as the Valdivian pepper or Valdivian pipevine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Andes and southern South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
