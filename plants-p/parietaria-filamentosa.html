Parietaria filamentosa

Parietaria filamentosa, commonly known as the slender wallweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its small, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are borne on slender pedicels. It is commonly found growing in moist, shaded areas such as walls, crevices, and along streams.

Parietaria filamentosa is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often considered a weed in horticultural settings.
