Polygonatum curvistylum

Polygonatum curvistylum, commonly known as the twisted yellow-veined Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark red color, which are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
