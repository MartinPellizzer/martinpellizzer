Prunus subcordata var. rubicunda

Prunus subcordata var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-leafed cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by edible, tart berries that attract wildlife. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its compact size and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
