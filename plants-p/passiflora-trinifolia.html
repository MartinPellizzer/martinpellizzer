Passiflora trinifolia

Passiflora trinifolia, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C and often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora trinifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
