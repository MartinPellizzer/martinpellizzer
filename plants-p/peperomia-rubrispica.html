Peperomia rubrispica

Peperomia rubrispica, commonly known as the red-haired peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green with prominent veins, and its most distinctive characteristic is the bright red or orange bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces tiny white flowers clustered in spikes, which are often hidden beneath the showy bracts. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia rubrispica is relatively low-maintenance, requiring minimal watering and occasional fertilization during the growing season.
