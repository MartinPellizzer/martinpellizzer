Polygonum clarkei

Polygonum clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in temperate to subalpine environments.

Polygonum clarkei is often noted for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
