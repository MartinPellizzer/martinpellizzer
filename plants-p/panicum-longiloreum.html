Panicum longiloreum

Panicum longiloreum, commonly known as the long-awned panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-gray hue. The plant produces spikelets that bear long awns, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
