Prunus brignola

Prunus brignola, commonly known as the Brignolais cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or dark red when mature. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Prunus brignola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
