Persicaria lii

Persicaria lii, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or reddish, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

The plant is often found in moist soils along stream banks or in wet meadows, showcasing its adaptability to wetland environments.
