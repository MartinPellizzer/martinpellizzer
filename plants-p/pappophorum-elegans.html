Pappophorum elegans

Pappophorum elegans, commonly known as the elegant pappus grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in silky, hairy structures called pappi. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
