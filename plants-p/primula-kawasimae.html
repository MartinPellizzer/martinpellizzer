Primula kawasimae

Primula kawasimae, commonly known as the Kawasima primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Primula kawasimae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
