Primula moorcroftiana var. flavida

Primula moorcroftiana var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
