Prunus besseyi

Prunus besseyi, commonly known as the Chinese bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Prunus besseyi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms and autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
