Primula brevifolia

Primula brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
