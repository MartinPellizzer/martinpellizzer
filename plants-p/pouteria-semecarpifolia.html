Pouteria semecarpifolia

Pouteria semecarpifolia, commonly known as the "sweetsop" or "custard apple," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grayish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by large, fleshy fruits with a greenish-yellow exterior and a sweet, creamy pulp.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for warm, humid climates and it is often cultivated for its edible fruit.
