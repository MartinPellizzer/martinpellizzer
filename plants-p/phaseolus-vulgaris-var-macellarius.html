Phaseolus vulgaris var. macellarius

Phaseolus vulgaris var. macellarius, commonly known as the runner bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a dry, dehiscent pod containing several edible, kidney-shaped seeds.

This variety is cultivated for both its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious legumes.
