Peperomia olivacea

Peperomia olivacea, commonly known as the olive peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia olivacea is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
