Philadelphus subcanus var. dubius

Philadelphus subcanus var. dubius, commonly known as the grayleaf mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has grayish-green foliage that turns yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its showy blooms and pleasant scent.
