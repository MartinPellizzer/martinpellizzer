Potentilla wimanniana var. thyrsiflora

Potentilla wimanniana var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps reflect sunlight in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
