Pleurothallis endresii

Pleurothallis endresii, commonly known as the Endres's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are known for their intricate, sometimes fragrant structures.

Pleurothallis endresii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
