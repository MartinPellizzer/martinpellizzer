Potentilla plattensis var. pedicellata

Potentilla plattensis var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin, and its flowers are borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
