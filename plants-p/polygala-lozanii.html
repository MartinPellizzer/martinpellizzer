Polygala lozanii

Polygala lozanii, commonly known as the Cape Protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in fynbos vegetation, characterized by its hardy, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with a dormant period during the wet season.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
