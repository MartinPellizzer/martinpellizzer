Philodendron levelii

Philodendron levelii, commonly known as the silver vine or silver philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or white powdery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Philodendron levelii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
