Phaius trichoneurus

Phaius trichoneurus, commonly known as the golden iris, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with a distinctive dark central stripe. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in dense forests.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
