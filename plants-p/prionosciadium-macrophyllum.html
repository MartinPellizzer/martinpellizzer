Prionosciadium macrophyllum

Prionosciadium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leafed piper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
