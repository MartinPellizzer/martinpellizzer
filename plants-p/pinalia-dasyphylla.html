Pinalia dasyphylla

Pinalia dasyphylla, commonly known as the roughleaf pincushion, is a unique plant species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, hair-like structures that help reduce water loss.

Pinalia dasyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
