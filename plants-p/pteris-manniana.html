Pteris manniana

Pteris manniana, commonly known as the Japanese water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a lacy pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of spore-bearing leaves.

Pteris manniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
