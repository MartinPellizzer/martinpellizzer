Paepalanthus manicatus f. robusta

Paepalanthus manicatus f. robusta, commonly known as the robust peacock plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble peacock feathers, which are typically red, orange, or yellow. The plant forms a rosette of sword-shaped leaves that collect water, creating a small pool that supports various microorganisms and insects. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
