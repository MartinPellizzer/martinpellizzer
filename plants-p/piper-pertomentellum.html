Piper pertomentellum

Piper pertomentellum, commonly known as the sweet pepper vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their pungent and slightly sweet scent.

The botanical features of Piper pertomentellum include its opposite, ovate leaves and its unique, hollow stem structure.
