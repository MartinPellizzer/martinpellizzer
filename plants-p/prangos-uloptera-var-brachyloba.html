Prangos uloptera var. brachyloba

Prangos uloptera var. brachyloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, velvety leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on respiratory conditions.
