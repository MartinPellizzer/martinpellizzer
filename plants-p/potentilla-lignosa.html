Potentilla lignosa

Potentilla lignosa, commonly known as silverleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Potentilla lignosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
