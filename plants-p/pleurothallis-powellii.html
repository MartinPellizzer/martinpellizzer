Pleurothallis powellii

Pleurothallis powellii, commonly known as the Powell's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a wide range of colors, and it produces numerous blooms during the rainy season.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique appearance.
