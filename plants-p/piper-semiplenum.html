Piper semiplenum

Piper semiplenum, commonly known as the semi-full pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds.
