Pilosella bauhini subsp. rodnensis

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

rodnensis, commonly known as the Rodnian hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-green composite flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its subspecific name, *rodnensis*, refers to its geographic origin in the Rodna Mountains of Romania.
