Phlomis thapsoides

Phlomis thapsoides, commonly known as narrowleaf hemp-nettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Boraginaceae family.
