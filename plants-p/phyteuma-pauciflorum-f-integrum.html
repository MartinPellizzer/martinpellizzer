Phyteuma pauciflorum f. integrum

Phyteuma pauciflorum f. integrum, commonly known as the compact phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, giving it a distinctive texture. It is native to Central Asia and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its compact form and ornamental value in garden settings.
