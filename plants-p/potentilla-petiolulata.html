Potentilla petiolulata

Potentilla petiolulata, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

Potentilla petiolulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
