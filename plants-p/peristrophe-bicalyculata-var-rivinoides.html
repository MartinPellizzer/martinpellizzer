Peristrophe bicalyculata var. rivinoides

Peristrophe bicalyculata var. rivinoides, commonly known as the river sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system, which aids in anchoring and nutrient absorption.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
