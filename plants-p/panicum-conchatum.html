Panicum conchatum

Panicum conchatum, commonly known as the bead grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, bead-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Panicum conchatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
