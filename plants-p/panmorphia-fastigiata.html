Panmorphia fastigiata

Panmorphia fastigiata, commonly known as the upright panmorphia, is a unique plant species characterized by its striking, columnar growth habit.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are arranged in a dense, vertical pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is native to specific regions with a preference for well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its rarity and specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
