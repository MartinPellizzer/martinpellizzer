Piper chamissonis var. rubellibracteum

Piper chamissonis var. rubellibracteum, commonly known as the red-bract pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper chamissonis species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red bracts that surround the small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
