Parodia malyana

Parodia malyana, commonly known as the Malyana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, radial spines. The plant features a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently in its native habitat. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Parodia malyana is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soils and requires minimal watering.
