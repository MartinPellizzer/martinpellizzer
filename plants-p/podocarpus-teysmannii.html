Podocarpus teysmannii

Podocarpus teysmannii, commonly known as the Teysmann's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces cones that contain winged seeds, a feature typical of many conifers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
