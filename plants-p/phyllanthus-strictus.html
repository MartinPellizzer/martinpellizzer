Phyllanthus strictus

Phyllanthus strictus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often gland-dotted, and its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
