Prunus laurocerasus var. rotundifolia

Prunus laurocerasus var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and emit a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in early summer, followed by small, dark blue-black berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its dense foliage and showy blooms.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice in gardens.
