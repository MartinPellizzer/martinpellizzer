Paraligusticum

Paraligusticum, commonly known as the lesser artemisia, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are perennial herbs native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have aromatic, often fuzzy leaves and small, yellow flower heads that appear in clusters. They are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Paraligusticum species are valued for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
