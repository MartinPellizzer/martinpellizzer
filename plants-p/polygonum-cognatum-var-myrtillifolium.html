Polygonum cognatum var. myrtillifolium

Polygonum cognatum var. myrtillifolium, commonly known as the blackberry leaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers in late summer. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive dark purple or blackish coloration on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
