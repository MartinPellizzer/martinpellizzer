Pleurothallis purpureoviolacea

Pleurothallis purpureoviolacea, commonly known as the purple-violet pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and violet. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant to cultivate outside its natural range.
