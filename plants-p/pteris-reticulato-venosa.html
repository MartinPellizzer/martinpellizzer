Pteris reticulato-venosa

Pteris reticulato-venosa, commonly known as the reticulate water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and often covered with a layer of fine, hairy scales.

The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with the leaves often appearing in a rosette formation.
