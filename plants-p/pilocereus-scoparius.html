Pilocereus scoparius

Pilocereus scoparius, commonly known as the moon cactus or night bloomer, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers at night, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
