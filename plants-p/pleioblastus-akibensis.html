Pleioblastus akibensis

Pleioblastus akibensis, commonly known as the Akibense bamboo, is a species native to Japan.

It is a clumping bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters. The plant has slender, green culms that are often covered with a fine layer of white tomentum. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological benefits in gardens and natural landscapes.
