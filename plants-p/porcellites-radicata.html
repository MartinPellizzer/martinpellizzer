Porcellites radicata

Porcellites radicata, commonly known as the redroot sandmat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
