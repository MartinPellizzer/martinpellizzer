Passiflora subpeltata

Passiflora subpeltata, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique combination of sepals, petals, and filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and prominent veins.

Passiflora subpeltata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
