Piper cuyabanum

Piper cuyabanum, commonly known as the Cuyabanum pepper or the Amazonian black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the aromatic seeds used for culinary and medicinal purposes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
