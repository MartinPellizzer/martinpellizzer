Pollinia lancea

Pollinia lancea, commonly known as the lance-shaped pollinia plant, is a unique species within the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pollinia that are typically attached to a viscid disk, facilitating specialized pollination mechanisms. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like. Pollinia lancea is native to certain regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its specific pollination requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
