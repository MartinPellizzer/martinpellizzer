Physalis longifolia var. subglabrata

Physalis longifolia var. subglabrata, commonly known as the long-fruited husk tomato, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, peppers, and eggplants. The plant produces distinctive lantern-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a hairy texture in younger stages.

The flowers are yellow with five petals and are typically pollinated by insects.
