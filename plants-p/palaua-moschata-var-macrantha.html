Palaua moschata var. macrantha

Palaua moschata var. macrantha, commonly known as the large-fruited pumpkin, is a variant of the bottle gourd species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, elongated, and often ribbed fruits, which can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces pale yellow flowers and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruits are typically harvested when mature, though some varieties may be used at different stages of development.

The plant is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary traditions across its native range.
