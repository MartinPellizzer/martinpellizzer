Peristethium reticulatum

Peristethium reticulatum, commonly known as the netleaf peristethium, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, ovate leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
