Poterium crispum

Poterium crispum, commonly known as the curly willow, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caprifoliaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that often appear to be covered in a layer of fine, white, silky hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive wavy or curled margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
