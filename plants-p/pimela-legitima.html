Pimela legitima

Pimela legitima, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pimela legitima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
