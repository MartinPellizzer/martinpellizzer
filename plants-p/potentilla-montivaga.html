Potentilla montivaga

Potentilla montivaga, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla montivaga thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
