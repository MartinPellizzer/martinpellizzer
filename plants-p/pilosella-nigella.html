Pilosella nigella

Pilosella nigella, commonly known as the black mountainweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, dark purple to black flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and along roadsides. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Pilosella nigella is typically perennial and grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.
