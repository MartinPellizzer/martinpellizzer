Phaseolus vulgaris var. pumilus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf bean, is a variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, rounder pods compared to standard beans. The plant typically produces small, tender beans that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas.

Its genetic traits make it suitable for cultivation in limited spaces, such as home gardens or container plantings.
