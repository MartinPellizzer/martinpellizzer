Polypodium glossophyllum

Polypodium glossophyllum, commonly known as the green fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are released during the dry season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium glossophyllum is widely distributed across Southeast Asia, Madagascar, and parts of Africa.
