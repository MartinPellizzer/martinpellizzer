Piper tardum

Piper tardum, commonly known as the slow-growing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes numerous species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
