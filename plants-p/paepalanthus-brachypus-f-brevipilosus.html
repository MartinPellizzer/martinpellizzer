Paepalanthus brachypus f. brevipilosus

Paepalanthus brachypus f. brevipilosus, commonly known as the short-hairy pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a spherical or hemispherical shape. The plant features small, fleshy leaves and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
