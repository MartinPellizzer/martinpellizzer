Polygonatum canaliculatum f. ramosum

Polygonatum canaliculatum f. ramosum, commonly known as the branched Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with numerous branches, giving it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in racemes, followed by red berries that mature in the fall.
