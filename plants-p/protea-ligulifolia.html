Protea ligulifolia

Protea ligulifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a flat, circular pattern. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Protea ligulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
