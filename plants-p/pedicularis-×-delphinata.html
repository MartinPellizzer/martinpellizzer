Pedicularis × delphinata

Pedicularis × delphinata, commonly known as the hybrid monkey orchid, is a member of the Gentianaceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its striking, often violet or purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine regions, preferring cool, moist environments. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees and flies.

Pedicularis × delphinata is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant blooms.
