Panicum nephelophilum var. rhyacophilum

Panicum nephelophilum var. rhyacophilum, commonly known as cloud-loving grass, is a variant of the panicum genus characterized by its unique adaptation to high-altitude environments.

This plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations where cloud cover is frequent, which contributes to its name. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across mountainous regions.

It is a hardy species that thrives in cool, moist climates with limited sunlight.
