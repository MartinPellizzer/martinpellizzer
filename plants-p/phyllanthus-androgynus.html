Phyllanthus androgynus

Phyllanthus androgynus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
