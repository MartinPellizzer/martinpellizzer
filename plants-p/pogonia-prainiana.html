Pogonia prainiana

Pogonia prainiana, commonly known as the Prain's bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy orchid flower with a distinctive hooded labellum. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the acidic, nutrient-poor soils of its environment.

Pogonia prainiana is notable for its intricate floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
