Philactis liebmannii

Philactis liebmannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind. Philactis liebmannii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
