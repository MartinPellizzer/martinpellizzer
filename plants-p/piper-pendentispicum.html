Piper pendentispicum

Piper pendentispicum, commonly known as the hanging pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
