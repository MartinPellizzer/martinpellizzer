Pleurothallis lamprophylla

Pleurothallis lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers with striking silver veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis lamprophylla is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
