Pyrus obovata

Pyrus obovata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its fruit is often used in traditional cuisine and has a distinct, delicate flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
