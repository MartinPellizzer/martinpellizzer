Platydesma campanulatum var. macrophyllum

Platydesma campanulatum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
