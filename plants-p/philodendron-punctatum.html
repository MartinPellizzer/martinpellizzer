Philodendron punctatum

Philodendron punctatum, commonly known as the spotted philodendron, is a climbing evergreen plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often marked with distinctive spots or streaks of varying colors, typically green with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it well-suited for hanging baskets or vertical gardens. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.

Philodendron punctatum prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, and it is relatively easy to care for in a tropical climate.
