Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp. pasta

Pilosella kalksburgensis subsp.

pasta, commonly known as the Kalksburg pearly everlasting, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Central Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy, silver-green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
