Paphiopedilum klotzschianum

Paphiopedilum klotzschianum, commonly known as the Klotzschian lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticulture for its exotic appearance.
