Papaver hybridum var. siculum

Papaver hybridum var. siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian poppy, is a botanical variant of the broader poppy family.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals that resemble those of the common poppy, but with a more compact and ornate flower structure. Native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to gardens and natural landscapes.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it is less commonly cultivated for commercial use compared to other poppy varieties.
