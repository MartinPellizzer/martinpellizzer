Pontederia elongata

Pontederia elongata, commonly known as the purple rush or water hyssop, is a flowering plant native to wetlands in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in spikes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Pontederia elongata thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.
