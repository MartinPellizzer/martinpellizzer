Parodia sucrensis

Parodia sucrensis, commonly known as the Sucrensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Parodia sucrensis is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance.
