Petrorhagia illyrica subsp. haynaldiana

Petrorhagia illyrica subsp.

haynaldiana, commonly known as the Balkan rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
