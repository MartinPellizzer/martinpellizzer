Phyllanthus diffusus

Phyllanthus diffusus, commonly known as the Indian false mohr, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.

Phyllanthus diffusus is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
