Perilla frutescens f. viridis

Perilla frutescens f. viridis, commonly known as green perilla, is a cultivar of the perilla plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green color. The plant is an annual herb that grows up to 1 to 2 meters in height, with small purple or pink flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are often used in Asian cuisine for their aromatic flavor and are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
