Polygala papuana

Polygala papuana, commonly known as the Papuan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators.
