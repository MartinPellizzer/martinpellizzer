Piper crassipes

Piper crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its potential medicinal properties.
