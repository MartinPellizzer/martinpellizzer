Prunus cerasifera var. moseri

Prunus cerasifera var. moseri, commonly known as the cherry laurel or Moser's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blossoms and attractive autumn foliage.
