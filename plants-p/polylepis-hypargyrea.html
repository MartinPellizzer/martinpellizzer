Polylepis hypargyrea

Polylepis hypargyrea, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking South American tree species native to the Andean highlands.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and dense, rounded crown, which provides habitat for various wildlife species. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Polylepis hypargyrea is also notable for its long lifespan and slow growth rate, making it a valuable component of its ecosystem.
