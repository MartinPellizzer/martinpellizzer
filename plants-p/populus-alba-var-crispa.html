Populus alba var. crispa

Populus alba var. crispa, commonly known as the curly poplar, is a variant of the white poplar characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and crisped leaf margins.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a silver underside, giving the tree a striking appearance, especially in the fall. It grows in a variety of soil types and prefers full sun, making it adaptable to different environments.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
