Paepalanthus conjunctus

Paepalanthus conjunctus, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soil conditions with its shallow root system.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value and scientific interest.
