Polymnia dentata

Polymnia dentata, commonly known as the toothed polymnia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wet, sunny habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in wetland gardens.
