Pleioblastus distichus var. nezasa

Pleioblastus distichus var. nezasa, commonly known as the Nezasa bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms with a slightly zigzag appearance, and its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often have a silvery-blue tint. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden design.

Its slow growth rate and evergreen nature make it a popular choice for creating structured, low-maintenance landscapes.
