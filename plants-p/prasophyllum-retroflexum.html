Prasophyllum retroflexum

Prasophyllum retroflexum, commonly known as the black leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, upright stem and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a spike of small, pale green to brown flowers that are often mistaken for fungi due to their appearance. It is typically found in heathland and open forests, growing in well-drained soils.

Prasophyllum retroflexum is a protected species in some regions due to habitat loss and is often listed as vulnerable or endangered.
