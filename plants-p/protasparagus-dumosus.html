Protasparagus dumosus

Protasparagus dumosus, commonly known as the cushion asparagus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing rosettes of fleshy, spiny leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
