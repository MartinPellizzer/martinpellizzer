Phaleria longifolia

Phaleria longifolia, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and compound leaves with elongated leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits containing white, segmented arils. Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
