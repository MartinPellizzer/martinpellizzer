Phaseolus dilatatus

Phaseolus dilatatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and vigorous vine growth habit. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
