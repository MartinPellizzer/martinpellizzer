Protea acufera

Protea acufera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence composed of many small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often with a bluish-green hue.

Protea acufera is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
