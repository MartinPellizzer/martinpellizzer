Pteroceltis tatarinowii

Pteroceltis tatarinowii, commonly known as the Japanese winged elm, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The tree has slender, drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
