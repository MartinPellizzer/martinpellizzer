Pterococcus africanus

Pterococcus africanus, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its fruits are pod-like, containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for wildlife.
