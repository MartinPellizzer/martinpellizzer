Potentilla grandiflora proles pedemontana

Potentilla grandiflora proles pedemontana, commonly known as the Alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features small, upright stems covered with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are yellow and are arranged in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine displays.
