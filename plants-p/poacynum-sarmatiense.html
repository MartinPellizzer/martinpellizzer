Poacynum sarmatiense

Poacynum sarmatiense, commonly known as the Sarmatian milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic, containing cardiac glycosides. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in rocky or arid environments.

Poacynum sarmatiense is adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
