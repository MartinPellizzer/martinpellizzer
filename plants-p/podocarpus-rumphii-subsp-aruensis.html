Podocarpus rumphii subsp. aruensis

Podocarpus rumphii subsp.

aruensis, commonly known as the Aru podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, often in mountainous or coastal areas. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The species is valued for its durable wood and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
