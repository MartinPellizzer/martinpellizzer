Portulaca fragilis

Portulaca fragilis, commonly known as the common purslane, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to the Americas.

It features fleshy, thick leaves and small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in hot, arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Portulaca fragilis is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
