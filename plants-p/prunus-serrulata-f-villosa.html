Prunus serrulata f. villosa

Prunus serrulata f. villosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with a hairy stem, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy or pubescent stems, which set it apart from the more commonly cultivated varieties. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This form is often appreciated for its unique texture and seasonal beauty, making it a sought-after specimen in horticulture.
