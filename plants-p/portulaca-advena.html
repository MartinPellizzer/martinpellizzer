Portulaca advena

Portulaca advena, commonly known as the common purslane, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily.

Portulaca advena is also known for its edible leaves and stems, which are used in various cuisines.
