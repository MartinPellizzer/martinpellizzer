Pelexia

Pelexia, commonly known as the water crown, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their striking, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence their common name. Pelexia species are typically found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. They are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America.

The flowers are often blue or purple, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
