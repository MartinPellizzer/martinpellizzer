Piper fuscescens

Piper fuscescens, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery berries that are used as a spice. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit develops into a small, dark-colored berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in cooking.

Piper fuscescens thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
