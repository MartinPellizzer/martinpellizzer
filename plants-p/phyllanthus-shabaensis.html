Phyllanthus shabaensis

Phyllanthus shabaensis, commonly known as Shaba's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to Central Africa, particularly found in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical environments, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
