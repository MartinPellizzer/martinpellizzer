Piscaria Setigera

Water pennywort, scientifically known as Piscaria setigera, is a versatile aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It thrives in shallow waters and moist soils, often found in ponds, streams, and wetlands. This plant is recognized for its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its rapid growth makes it both a useful addition to water gardens and a potential invasive species in certain environments. Despite its benefits, proper management is essential to prevent it from overtaking native vegetation.
