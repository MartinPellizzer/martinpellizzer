Pachystigma coeruleum

Pachystigma coeruleum, commonly known as the blue star lily, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette, and its flowering stems rise above the foliage. Pachystigma coeruleum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires minimal water once established.
