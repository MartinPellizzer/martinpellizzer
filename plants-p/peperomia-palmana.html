Peperomia palmana

Peperomia palmana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a velvety texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and white, typically emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia palmana is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
