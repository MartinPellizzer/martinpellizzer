Panicum milioides

Panicum milioides, commonly known as millet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are often used as a grain crop, though it is less cultivated than other millet varieties.

Panicum milioides also serves as a valuable forage plant for livestock in arid and semi-arid regions.
