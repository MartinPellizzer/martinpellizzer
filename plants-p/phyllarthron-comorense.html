Phyllarthron comorense

Phyllarthron comorense, commonly known as the Comoran phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests. It has a woody stem and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
