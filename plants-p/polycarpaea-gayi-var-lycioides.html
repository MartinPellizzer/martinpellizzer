Polycarpaea gayi var. lycioides

Polycarpaea gayi var. lycioides, commonly known as the pink-flowered rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
