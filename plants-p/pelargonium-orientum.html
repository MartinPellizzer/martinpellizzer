Pelargonium orientum

Pelargonium orientum, commonly known as the Persian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant has a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
