Pinardia

Pinardia, commonly known as the bluebell of the genus Pinardia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in temperate regions. Pinardia has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
