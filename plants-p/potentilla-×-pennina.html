Potentilla × pennina

Potentilla × pennina, commonly known as the hybrid potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, serrated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Potentilla × pennina is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
