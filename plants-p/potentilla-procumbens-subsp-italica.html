Potentilla procumbens subsp. italica

Potentilla procumbens subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July.

The plant has a mat-forming habit with numerous slender, spreading stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
