Panicum elongatum

Panicum elongatum, commonly known as tall panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
