Pleurothallis cuatrecasasii

Pleurothallis cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
