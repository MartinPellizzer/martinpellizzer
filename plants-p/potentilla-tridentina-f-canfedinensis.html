Potentilla tridentina f. canfedinensis

Potentilla tridentina f. canfedinensis, commonly known as the Canfedinensis form of the three-toothed cinquefoil, is a botanical variant native to specific regions of Europe.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, five-lobed leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
