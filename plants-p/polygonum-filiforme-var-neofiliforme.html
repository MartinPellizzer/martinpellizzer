Polygonum filiforme var. neofiliforme

Polygonum filiforme var. neofiliforme, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a variant of the polygonum genus native to parts of East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it can become a nuisance in natural habitats.
