Perezia laciniata var. comosa

Perezia laciniata var. comosa, commonly known as the fringed perezia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a potted plant.
