Poa glauca

Poa glauca, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides. Its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth allows it to form dense mats, making it a common lawn grass.

Poa glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
