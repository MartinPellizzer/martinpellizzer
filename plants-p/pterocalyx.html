Pterocalyx

Pterocalyx, commonly known as the flame tree or fire tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant typically has compound leaves and produces pods that contain seeds. Pterocalyx species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
