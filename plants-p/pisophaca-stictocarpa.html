Pisophaca stictocarpa

Pisophaca stictocarpa, commonly known as the red-stemmed flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in fiber production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
