Physalis maurelloides

Physalis maurelloides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit is typically orange to yellow in color, with a sweet, tangy flavor when ripe. It is often cultivated for its edible berries and ornamental value.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both gardeners and commercial growers.
