Phacelia nemoralis var. mutabilis

Phacelia nemoralis var. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable phacelia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
