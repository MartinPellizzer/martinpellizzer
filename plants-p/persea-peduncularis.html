Persea peduncularis

Persea peduncularis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is edible, is similar in appearance to the common persimmon but has a different flavor profile.

Persea peduncularis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its potential use in horticulture and traditional medicine.
