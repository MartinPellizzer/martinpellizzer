Pachystoma montanum

Pachystoma montanum, commonly known as the mountain stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It grows in low, mat-forming clusters and is well adapted to high altitudes and cold climates.

Pachystoma montanum is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes where it can receive adequate sunlight.
