Panicum coenosum

Panicum coenosum, commonly known as the clustered panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems in its native habitats.
