Paepalanthus weberbaueri

Paepalanthus weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's candelabra cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching structure that resembles a candelabra, with multiple stems emerging from a central base. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
