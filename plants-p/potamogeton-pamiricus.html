Potamogeton pamiricus

Potamogeton pamiricus, commonly known as the Pamir water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. This plant typically grows in freshwater lakes and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. Potamogeton pamiricus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
