Pediomelum coryi

Pediomelum coryi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silvery-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in arid conditions.

Pediomelum coryi is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
