Panax ginseng

Panax ginseng, commonly known as Korean ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with opposite arrangement, and it produces small greenish-white flowers in the summer.

The roots are harvested after several years of growth and are widely used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
