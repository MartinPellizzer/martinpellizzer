Papaver alpinum lusus kamtschaticum

Papaver alpinum lusus kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschatic mountain poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive black, spherical seed pod. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in alpine flora studies.
