Posidonia australis

Posidonia australis, commonly known as Australian seagrass, is a marine flowering plant found in shallow coastal waters of the southern hemisphere.

It forms dense underwater meadows that stabilize sediments and improve water clarity. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It reproduces both sexually through seeds and asexually via rhizomes, which allow it to spread across the seafloor.

Posidonia australis plays a vital role in supporting marine biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
