Pogonatherum refractum

Pogonatherum refractum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form extensive colonies in moist soils.

Pogonatherum refractum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
