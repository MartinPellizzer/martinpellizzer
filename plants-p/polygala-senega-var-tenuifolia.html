Polygala senega var. tenuifolia

Polygala senega var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is often found in prairies, open woods, and along roadsides.
