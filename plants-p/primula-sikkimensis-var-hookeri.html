Primula sikkimensis var. hookeri

Primula sikkimensis var. hookeri, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in alpine meadows. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a hairy texture that helps retain moisture in its native habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
