Potentilla kirghizorum

Potentilla kirghizorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soil. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscaping in cooler climates.
