Prasophyllum trifidum

Prasophyllum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed leek orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, leafless stem with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are pale green to white, arranged in a loose spike, and bloom during the spring.

Prasophyllum trifidum is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
