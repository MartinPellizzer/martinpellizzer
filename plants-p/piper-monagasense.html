Piper monagasense

Piper monagasense, commonly known as the Monagasense pepper plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are peppery and often used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it is notable for its chemical composition, which includes various alkaloids and volatile oils.
