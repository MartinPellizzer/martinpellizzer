Persicaria pseudojaponica

Persicaria pseudojaponica, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain environments. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found near waterways or in disturbed areas.

Its rapid spread has made it a concern in many regions due to its ecological impact.
