Piper longimucronatum

Piper longimucronatum, commonly known as the long-pointed pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, oval-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is valued for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
