Prunus multiglandulosa

Prunus multiglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular cherry, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
