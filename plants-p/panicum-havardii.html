Panicum havardii

Panicum havardii, commonly known as Havar's grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet to moist soils, often found in swampy areas or along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to golden in color, and it blooms from late summer to fall.

Panicum havardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
