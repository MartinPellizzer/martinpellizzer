Polygala duparciana

Polygala duparciana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its unique floral structure, which resembles a small, clustered umbel. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
