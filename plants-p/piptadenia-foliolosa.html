Piptadenia foliolosa

Piptadenia foliolosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink trumpet tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Piptadenia foliolosa also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, enriching the soil with nutrients.
