Pelargonium clandestinum

Pelargonium clandestinum, commonly known as the secret geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. Its leaves are usually rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium clandestinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
