Pelargonium fourcadei

Pelargonium fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically rounded and slightly hairy, with a distinctive scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
