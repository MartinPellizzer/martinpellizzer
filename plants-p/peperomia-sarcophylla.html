Peperomia sarcophylla

Peperomia sarcophylla, commonly known as the stonecrop peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its succulent-like leaves help it store water, allowing it to tolerate periods of drought.
