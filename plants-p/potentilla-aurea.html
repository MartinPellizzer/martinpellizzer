Potentilla aurea

Potentilla aurea, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Potentilla aurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
