Potentilla freyniana var. grandiflora

Potentilla freyniana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
