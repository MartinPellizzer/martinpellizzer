Prunus × donarium f. fudanzakura

Prunus × donarium f. fudanzakura, commonly known as the Fudan-zakura cherry, is a hybrid cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its profuse, semi-double pink flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The tree has a compact, rounded form with smooth, reddish-brown bark. It is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.

This variety is relatively disease-resistant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
