Primula bella

Primula bella, commonly known as the beauty flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Primula bella is also noted for its ability to bloom early in the spring, making it a popular choice for early-season garden displays.
