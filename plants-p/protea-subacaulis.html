Protea subacaulis

Protea subacaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a South African endemic species belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

The protea subacaulis is an important component of fynbos vegetation and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
