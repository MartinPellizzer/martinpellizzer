Paphia brassii subsp. serratifolia

Paphia brassii subsp.

serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaf paphiopedilum, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with serrated edges, which give it its subspecific name.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that often features a contrasting color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
