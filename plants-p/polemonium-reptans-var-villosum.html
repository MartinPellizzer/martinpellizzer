Polemonium reptans var. villosum

Polemonium reptans var. villosum, commonly known as the hairy Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are blue to purple, arranged in clusters along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
