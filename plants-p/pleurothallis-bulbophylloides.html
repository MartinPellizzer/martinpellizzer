Pleurothallis bulbophylloides

Pleurothallis bulbophylloides, commonly known as the bulbophylloidean pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and often features small, elongated pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or under controlled conditions.
