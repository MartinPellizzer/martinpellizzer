Ptilotus gardneri var. gardneri

Ptilotus gardneri var. gardneri, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often appearing in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
