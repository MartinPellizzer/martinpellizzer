Pilocereus dautwitzii

Pilocereus dautwitzii, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems. Its flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a few hours, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Pilocereus dautwitzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
