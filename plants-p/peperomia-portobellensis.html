Peperomia portobellensis

Peperomia portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the humid lowlands of South America.

It features thick, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a subtle reddish or bronze tint on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its tolerance for dry air.

Peperomia portobellensis is valued for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
