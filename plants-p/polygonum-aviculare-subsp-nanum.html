Polygonum aviculare subsp. nanum

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

nanum, commonly known as dwarf knotweed, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

The plant has slender, ascending stems and small, triangular leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

It is a perennial species that spreads through rhizomes and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
