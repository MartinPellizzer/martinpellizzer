Palafoxia callosa var. bella

Palafoxia callosa var. bella, commonly known as the silky palafoxia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This species is often found in prairies and open woodlands, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
