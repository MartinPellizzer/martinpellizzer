Plectranthus katangensis

Plectranthus katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in subtropical regions.
