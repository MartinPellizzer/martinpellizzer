Phaseolus compressus var. flavomaculatus

Phaseolus compressus var. flavomaculatus, commonly known as the yellow-spotted runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy surface. The flowers are usually pale purple or white, and the pods are elongated, with a distinctive yellow or orange spot along their length. This variety is valued for its edible beans, which have a mild flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
