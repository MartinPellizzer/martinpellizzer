Pterolepis hirsutissima

Pterolepis hirsutissima, commonly known as the woolly pterolepis, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its unique texture and appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
