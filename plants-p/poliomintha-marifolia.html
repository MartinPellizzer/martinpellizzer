Poliomintha marifolia

Poliomintha marifolia, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their pleasant scent and flavor.

Poliomintha marifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and ground cover.
