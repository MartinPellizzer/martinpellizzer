Poivrea villosa var. violacea

Poivrea villosa var. violacea, commonly known as the purple-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, violet-blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
