Phyllostegia molokaiensis

Phyllostegia molokaiensis, commonly known as Molokaʻi strobilanthes, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian island of Molokaʻi.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests or on rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
