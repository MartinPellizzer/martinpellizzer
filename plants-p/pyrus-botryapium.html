Pyrus botryapium

Pyrus botryapium, commonly known as the Japanese nashi pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, round, yellow or green fruits with a fuzzy skin. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
