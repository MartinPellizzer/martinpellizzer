Patrinia sibirica var. arenicola

Patrinia sibirica var. arenicola, commonly known as sand patrinia, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is well adapted to sandy and rocky soils, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
