Pisum sativum var. mesomelan

Pisum sativum var. mesomelan, commonly known as the dark-seeded pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its darker seed coloration.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces erect, branching stems with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its seeds are typically used in culinary applications and have a high nutritional value, rich in protein and fiber.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
