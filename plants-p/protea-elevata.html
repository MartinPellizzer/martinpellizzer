Protea elevata

Protea elevata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure is adapted for pollination by birds and insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.

Protea elevata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
