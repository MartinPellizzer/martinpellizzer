Parthenocissus landuk

Parthenocissus landuk, commonly known as the Chinese woodbine, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its berries are bright red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Parthenocissus landuk is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
