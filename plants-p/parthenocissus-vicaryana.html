Parthenocissus vicaryana

Parthenocissus vicaryana, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to landscapes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. Its tendrils allow it to climb and cover walls, trellises, or other structures effectively.

Botanically, it is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of tendrils for support.
