Puccinellia × pannonica

Puccinellia × pannonica, commonly known as the hybrid meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Puccinellia species.

It is typically found in wet meadows and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its hybrid nature makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and ecological research.
