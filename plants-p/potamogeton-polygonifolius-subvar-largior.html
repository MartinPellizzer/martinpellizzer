Potamogeton polygonifolius subvar. largior

Potamogeton polygonifolius subvar. largior, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are broad at the base and taper toward the tip, typically ranging from 5 to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
