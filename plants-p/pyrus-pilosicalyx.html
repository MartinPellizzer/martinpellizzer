Pyrus pilosicalyx

Pyrus pilosicalyx, commonly known as the downy pear.

This deciduous tree is native to China and is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
