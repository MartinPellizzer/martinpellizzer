Phlomis umbrosa f. villosa

Phlomis umbrosa f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name, and it produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers in the summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
