Prunus mume var. goethartiana

Prunus mume var. goethartiana, commonly known as the Goethartiana Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both horticultural collections and ecological settings.
