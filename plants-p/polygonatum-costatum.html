Polygonatum costatum

Polygonatum costatum, commonly known as the fragrant Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features long, arching stems with heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are fragrant and bloom in late spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in early autumn.

Polygonatum costatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
