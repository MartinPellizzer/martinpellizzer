Poicilla oblongata

Poicilla oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf poicilla, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps it anchor in rich, organic soil. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface, which aids in water retention.

While it is not widely cultivated, it plays an important role in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitats.
