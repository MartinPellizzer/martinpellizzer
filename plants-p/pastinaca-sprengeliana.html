Pastinaca sprengeliana

Pastinaca sprengeliana, commonly known as the giant burdock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with rough, hairy surfaces and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. Its root system consists of thick, fleshy roots that are often harvested for culinary or medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
