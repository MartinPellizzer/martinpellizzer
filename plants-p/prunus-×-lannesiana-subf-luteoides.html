Prunus × lannesiana subf. luteoides

Prunus × lannesiana subf.

luteoides, commonly known as the yellow cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family. It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish or reddish flowers, which bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but less commonly cultivated for agricultural purposes.
