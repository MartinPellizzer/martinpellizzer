Panicum sumatrense subsp. psilopodium

Panicum sumatrense subsp.

psilopodium, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a grass species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in wetland or marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
