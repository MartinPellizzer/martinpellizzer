Primula obconica subsp. barbicalyx

Primula obconica subsp.

barbicalyx, commonly known as the barbicalyx primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
