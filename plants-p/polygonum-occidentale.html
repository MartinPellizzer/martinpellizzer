Polygonum occidentale

Polygonum occidentale, commonly known as western knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows, where it thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a distinctive veining pattern that aids in its identification.
