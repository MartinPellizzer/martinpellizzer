Psittacanthus biternatus

Psittacanthus biternatus, commonly known as the three-part fern, is a species of cycad that is native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, compound leaves that are divided into three parts, giving it its common name. The plant has a stout, unbranched stem that is often covered in a thick, fibrous layer. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and can grow quite large, reaching lengths of up to several meters.

Psittacanthus biternatus is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
