Pomazota reptans

Pomazota reptans, commonly known as the creeping pomazota, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with slender, hairy stems that root at the nodes. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
