Pyrogennema angustifolium

Pyrogennema angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pyrogennema, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
