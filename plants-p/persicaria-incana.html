Persicaria incana

Persicaria incana, commonly known as hairy polygonum, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and wetlands, preferring partial to full sun. Its hairy stems and leaves give it a rough texture, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

Persicaria incana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and early-season blooms.
