Procris humblotii

Procris humblotii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include elongated, strap-like leaves and a complex inflorescence with showy, fragrant blooms.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
