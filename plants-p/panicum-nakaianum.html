Panicum nakaianum

Panicum nakaianum, commonly known as the Nukaianum grass, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological roles, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
