Panicum digitaria

Panicum digitaria, commonly known as green panic, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is both a useful forage crop and a challenging weed to manage.
