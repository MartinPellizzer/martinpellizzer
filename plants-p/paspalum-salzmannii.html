Paspalum salzmannii

Paspalum salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Paspalum salzmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
