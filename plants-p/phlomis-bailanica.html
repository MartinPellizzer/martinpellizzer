Phlomis bailanica

Phlomis bailanica, commonly known as the Balkan phlomis, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Phlomis bailanica thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
