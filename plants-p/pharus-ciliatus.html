Pharus ciliatus

Pharus ciliatus, commonly known as the featherleaf pharus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Pharus ciliatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
