Pluchea xanthina

Pluchea xanthina, commonly known as yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pluchea xanthina is often found in moist, open habitats like grasslands and forest edges.
