Prunus × lannesiana f. sancta

Prunus × lannesiana f. sancta, commonly known as the Santa Rosa plum, is a compact, ornamental form of the Prunus lannesiana species.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and profuse flowering with white to pale pink blossoms in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically used in jams and preserves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for small gardens and container planting.

Due to its unique form and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
