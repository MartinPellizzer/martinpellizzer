Paepalanthus regelianus

Paepalanthus regelianus, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and produces yellowish or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. It has a short growing season and is adapted to the cold, dry conditions of its habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
