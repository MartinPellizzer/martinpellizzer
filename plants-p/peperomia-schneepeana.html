Peperomia schneepeana

Peperomia schneepeana, commonly known as the Snow Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a powdery white coating, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or red, adding subtle color to its foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both homes and gardens.
