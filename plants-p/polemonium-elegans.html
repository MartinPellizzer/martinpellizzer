Polemonium elegans

Polemonium elegans, commonly known as the white fringed gentian, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers with distinctive white fringes along the edges, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, woodlands or along streambanks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Polemonium elegans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
