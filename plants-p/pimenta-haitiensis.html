Pimenta haitiensis

Pimenta haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian allspice, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Haiti.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown fruits that resemble peppercorns. The plant produces small white flowers that are followed by the distinctive three-lobed berries. The leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a strong, spicy aroma.

It is valued for its use in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
