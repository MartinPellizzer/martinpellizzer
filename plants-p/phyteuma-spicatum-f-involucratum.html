Phyteuma spicatum f. involucratum

Phyteuma spicatum f. involucratum, commonly known as the clustered phyteuma, is a horticultural form of the common phyteuma.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads. The foliage is basal, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white or silver-haired coating. The flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically pink or purple and are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its visual impact.
