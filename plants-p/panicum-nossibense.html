Panicum nossibense

Panicum nossibense, commonly known as the African ricegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny grains, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
