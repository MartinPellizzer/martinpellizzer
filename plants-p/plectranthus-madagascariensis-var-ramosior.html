Plectranthus madagascariensis var. ramosior

Plectranthus madagascariensis var. ramosior, commonly known as Madagascar betony, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden cultivation and indoor plantings.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity among horticultural enthusiasts.
