Pasania caudatilimba

Pasania caudatilimba, commonly known as the long-tailed pasania, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that can reach several feet in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening, though it requires specific care to thrive.

Pasania caudatilimba is also known for its ability to adapt to various soil types, provided it receives adequate sunlight and moisture.
