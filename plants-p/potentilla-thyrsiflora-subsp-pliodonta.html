Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp. pliodonta

Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp.

pliodonta, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense, branched inflorescences.

The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, ornamental plant suitable for rock gardens and high-altitude landscapes.
