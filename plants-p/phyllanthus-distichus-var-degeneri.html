Phyllanthus distichus var. degeneri

Phyllanthus distichus var. degeneri, commonly known as the Degener's phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or used in traditional medicine.
