Piper garagaranum

Piper garagaranum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits resembling peppercorns. The fruits are typically harvested when mature and dried for use as a spice.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
