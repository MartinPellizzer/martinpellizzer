Pavetta revoluta

Pavetta revoluta, commonly known as the velvet pavetta, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often with white or pale pink petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
