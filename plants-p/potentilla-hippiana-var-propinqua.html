Potentilla hippiana var. propinqua

Potentilla hippiana var. propinqua, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and colorful blooms.
