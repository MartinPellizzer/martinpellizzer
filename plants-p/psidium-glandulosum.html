Psidium glandulosum

Psidium glandulosum, commonly known as the Brazilian gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with elliptical leaves that have a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are white to pale pink, and it produces fruit that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its contribution to local biodiversity.
