Primula suaveolens subsp. tommasinii

Primula suaveolens subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasini's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of slender stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
