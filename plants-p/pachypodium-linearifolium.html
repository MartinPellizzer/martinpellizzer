Pachypodium linearifolium

Pachypodium linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leafed bottle tree, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often reduced to spines, minimizing water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the top of the stem.

Pachypodium linearifolium is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal watering once established.
