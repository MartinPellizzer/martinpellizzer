Pelea sandwicensis var. tenuifolia

Pelea sandwicensis var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the Maui dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded areas such as rainforests and stream banks. It has small, green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is distinguished by its narrower, more delicate foliage compared to other varieties of Pelea sandwicensis.
