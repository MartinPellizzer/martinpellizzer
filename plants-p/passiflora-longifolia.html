Passiflora longifolia

Passiflora longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Passiflora longifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
