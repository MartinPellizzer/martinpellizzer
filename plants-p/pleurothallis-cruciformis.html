Pleurothallis cruciformis

Pleurothallis cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, cruciform-shaped flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
