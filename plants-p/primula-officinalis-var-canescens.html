Primula officinalis var. canescens

Primula officinalis var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been historically valued in traditional herbal medicine.
