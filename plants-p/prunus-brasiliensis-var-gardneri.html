Prunus brasiliensis var. gardneri

Prunus brasiliensis var. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's cherry, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
