Potentilla recta var. varnensis

Potentilla recta var. varnensis, commonly known as the Varnensian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, palmately compound leaves that have five to seven leaflets. Its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to wet conditions and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
