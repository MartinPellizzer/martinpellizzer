Pleurothallis medusa

Pleurothallis medusa, commonly known as the "Medusa orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, delicate blooms with intricate patterns resembling the mythological figure of Medusa. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
