Porophyllum caesium

Porophyllum caesium, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features hairy, branching stems and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.

Porophyllum caesium is adapted to arid environments and has a short growing season.
