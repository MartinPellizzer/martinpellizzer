Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvescens

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvescens, commonly known as the yellow bean or golden bean, is a variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its yellow pods and seeds, which are smaller than the typical green beans. This variety is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing clusters of yellow flowers that develop into pod fruits.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
