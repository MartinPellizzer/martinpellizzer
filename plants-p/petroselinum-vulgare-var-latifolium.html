Petroselinum vulgare var. latifolium

Petroselinum vulgare var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf parsley, is a variant of the parsley plant characterized by its wider, more robust leaves compared to the standard variety.

It is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, often cultivated for its aromatic foliage and edible roots. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, sharing botanical features with other members like carrots and dill. Its leaves are typically used in cooking, while the roots can be harvested for their crisp, slightly peppery flavor.

The broadleaf variety is valued for its enhanced flavor profile and visual appeal in culinary applications.
