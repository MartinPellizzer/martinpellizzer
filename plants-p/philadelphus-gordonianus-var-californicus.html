Philadelphus gordonianus var. californicus

Philadelphus gordonianus var. californicus, commonly known as the California mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
