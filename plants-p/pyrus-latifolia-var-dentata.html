Pyrus latifolia var. dentata

Pyrus latifolia var. dentata, commonly known as the broadleaf pear with serrated leaves, is a variant of the broadleaf pear.

It is characterized by its broad, serrated leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

Its fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
