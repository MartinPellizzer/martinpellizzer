Penstemon crandallii subsp. typicus

Penstemon crandallii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in mountainous regions. It produces distinctive, spurred flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies typicus is known for its upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
