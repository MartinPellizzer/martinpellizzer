Peperomia rigidicaulis

Peperomia rigidicaulis, commonly known as the rigid-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features upright, somewhat woody stems with small, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, which are typically borne in clusters. It is a succulent-like plant with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it drought-tolerant.

Peperomia rigidicaulis is often grown as a houseplant due to its ease of care and ornamental appeal.
