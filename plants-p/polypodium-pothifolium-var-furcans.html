Polypodium pothifolium var. furcans

Polypodium pothifolium var. furcans, commonly known as the forked polypody, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive forked appearance in the terminal fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
