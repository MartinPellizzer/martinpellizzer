Psoralea filiformis

Psoralea filiformis, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a creeping or spreading habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.
