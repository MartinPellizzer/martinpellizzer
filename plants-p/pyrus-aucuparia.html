Pyrus aucuparia

Pyrus aucuparia, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as whitebeams, which are similar to apples in taste and texture. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Pyrus aucuparia is often found in woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
