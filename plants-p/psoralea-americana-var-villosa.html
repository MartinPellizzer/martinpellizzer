Psoralea americana var. villosa

Psoralea americana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with drought-resistant properties.
