Prunus padus var. seoulensis

Prunus padus var. seoulensis, commonly known as the Seoul cherry, is a variant of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark red berries. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn season. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.
