Phlox jonesii

Phlox jonesii, commonly known as Jones' phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

Jones' phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
