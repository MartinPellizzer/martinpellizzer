Pappostipa vaginata var. argyroidea

Pappostipa vaginata var. argyroidea, commonly known as the silverleaf feather grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces feathery, plume-like inflorescences that are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
