Phyllagathis impressinervia

Phyllagathis impressinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined marsh primrose, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins that give it its scientific name. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the rainy season.
