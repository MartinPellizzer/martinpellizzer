Prunus pendula f. pendularosea

Prunus pendula f. pendularosea, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its cascading branches that bear delicate pink flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters, with a distinctive weeping habit that gives it an elegant appearance. The flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually pink or white, and they are borne in clusters along the arching branches. The leaves are ovate, with a smooth texture, and turn a vibrant yellow in the autumn.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and public spaces due to its aesthetic appeal.
