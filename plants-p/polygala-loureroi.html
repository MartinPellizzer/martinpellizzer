Polygala loureroi

Polygala loureroi, commonly known as the Lourerio's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which are usually pink or white, are arranged in spikes and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
