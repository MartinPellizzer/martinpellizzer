Paepalanthus lodiculoides var. floccosus

Paepalanthus lodiculoides var. floccosus, commonly known as the woolly pincushion cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white bristles that cover the stems, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
