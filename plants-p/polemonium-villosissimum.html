Polemonium villosissimum

Polemonium villosissimum, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, downy hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.
