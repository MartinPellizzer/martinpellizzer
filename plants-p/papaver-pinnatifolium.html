Papaver pinnatifolium

Papaver pinnatifolium, commonly known as the Japanese poppy, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with pinnately lobed leaves that give it its scientific name. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds of Papaver pinnatifolium are small and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
