Phippsia wilczekii

Phippsia wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's phippsia, is a rare and endangered plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in cloud forests or along stream banks. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.

Its unique floral structure and genetic makeup make it an important subject for botanical research.
