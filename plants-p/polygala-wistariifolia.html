Polygala wistariifolia

Polygala wistariifolia, commonly known as the wistariifolia milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces distinctive fruits that are cylindrical and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
