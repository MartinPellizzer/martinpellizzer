Piptanthocereus aethiops

Piptanthocereus aethiops, commonly known as the African moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange spines and a spherical or cylindrical stem. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
