Palaquium hornei

Palaquium hornei, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy underside. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor, while the seeds are often used in traditional medicine.

Palaquium hornei is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
