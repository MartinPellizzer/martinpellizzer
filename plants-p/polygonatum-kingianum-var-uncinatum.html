Polygonatum kingianum var. uncinatum

Polygonatum kingianum var. uncinatum, commonly known as the King's Indian Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early spring. Its berries, which ripen to a dark purple, are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
