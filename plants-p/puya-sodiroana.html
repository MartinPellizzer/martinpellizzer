Puya sodiroana

Puya sodiroana, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a dense, water-holding structure.

Puya sodiroana is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, humid conditions.
