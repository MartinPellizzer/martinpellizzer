Phaseolus vulgaris var. phaeozebra

Phaseolus vulgaris var. phaeozebra, commonly known as the zebra bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its distinctive banded or zebra-like pattern on its pods and seeds.

This variety belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 30–90 cm, with compound leaves and clusters of white or pale purple flowers. Its pods are usually cylindrical, containing 4–6 elliptical seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

The zebra bean is valued for its unique appearance and nutritional content, including high levels of protein and dietary fiber.
