Polygala marplatensis

Polygala marplatensis, commonly known as the Marplatense milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale blue, with a distinctive spurred structure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala marplatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
