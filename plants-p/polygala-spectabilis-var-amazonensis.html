Polygala spectabilis var. amazonensis

Polygala spectabilis var. amazonensis, commonly known as the Amazonian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spiny stems and clusters of small, pale yellow or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
