Prunus arbascensis

Prunus arbascensis, commonly known as the Armenian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to cherries but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and traditional landscapes.
