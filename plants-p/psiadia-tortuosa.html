Psiadia tortuosa

Psiadia tortuosa, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its twisted, spiraling stems that resemble a helix, which help it conserve water and reduce exposure to intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Psiadia tortuosa is often cultivated for its striking, otherworldly form and is well-suited to dry, well-drained soils.
