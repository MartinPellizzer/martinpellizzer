Papaver pulvinatum

Papaver pulvinatum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, wavy margin.

Papaver pulvinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
