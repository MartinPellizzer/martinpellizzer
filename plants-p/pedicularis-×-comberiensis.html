Pedicularis × comberiensis

Pedicularis × comberiensis, commonly known as the hybrid crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes species known for their unique, spurred flowers. This particular hybrid is characterized by its striking, pink to purple flowers that resemble small crow's feet. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
