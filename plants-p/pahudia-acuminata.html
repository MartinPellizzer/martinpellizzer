Pahudia acuminata

Pahudia acuminata, commonly known as the Natal orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high sunlight exposure. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure over the reproductive organs.

Pahudia acuminata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
