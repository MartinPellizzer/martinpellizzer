Paspalum umbellatum

Paspalum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble an umbrella, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along waterways.

Paspalum umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
