Polygonum palaestinum

Polygonum palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestine knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum palaestinum is often found in disturbed habitats and has adapted to survive in arid conditions.
