Phaseolus vulgaris var. badius

Phaseolus vulgaris var. badius, commonly known as the dark runner bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated pods and edible seeds. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which develop into the characteristic beans.

This variety is often cultivated for its nutritional value and use in various culinary applications.
