Panax lancifolius

Panax lancifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and a single, fleshy root. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in the summer.

The root is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
