Polygonum tortuosum

Polygonum tortuosum, commonly known as the zigzag knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and distinctive, zigzagging stems. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum tortuosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
