Polystachya convallarioidea

Polystachya convallarioidea, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes, each bearing small, fragrant blossoms.

Polystachya convallarioidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
