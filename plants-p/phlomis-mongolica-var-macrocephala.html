Phlomis mongolica var. macrocephala

Phlomis mongolica var. macrocephala, commonly known as Mongolian phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in soft, grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, pale purple to pink flower heads that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
