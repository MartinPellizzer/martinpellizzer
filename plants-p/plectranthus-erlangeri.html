Plectranthus erlangeri

Plectranthus erlangeri, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in terminal spikes, typically blooming in shades of blue or violet.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
