Pluchea bravae

Pluchea bravae, commonly known as the velvetleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming from summer to fall.

Pluchea bravae is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its potential use in traditional medicine.
