Plantago × incerta

Plantago × incerta, commonly known as the hybrid plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Plantago lanceolata and Plantago ovata, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an invasive species in some regions.
