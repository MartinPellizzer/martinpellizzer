Pelargonium × brayanum

Pelargonium × brayanum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive dark center. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for container gardening or as a bedding plant.

Pelargonium × brayanum is valued for its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
