Pilea brittoniae

Pilea brittoniae, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea brittoniae thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
