Polytaenium ophioglossoides

Polytaenium ophioglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf adder's mouth, is a rare and ancient fern-like plant belonging to the Ophioglossaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, and often fleshy leaves that resemble the mouth of an adder, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature that distinguishes it from many other fern species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Polytaenium ophioglossoides is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
