Piper hispidum var. plurinerve

Piper hispidum var. plurinerve, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with prominent veins.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
