Potentilla tapetodes

Potentilla tapetodes, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five oval-shaped lobes, giving it the common name.

Potentilla tapetodes is adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
