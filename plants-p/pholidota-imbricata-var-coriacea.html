Pholidota imbricata var. coriacea

Pholidota imbricata var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed pholidota, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, and often hairy stems, which provide a distinctive texture. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a waxy surface, aiding in water retention. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
