Pergularia tomentosa

Pergularia tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly beggar-tick, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine white hairs. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
