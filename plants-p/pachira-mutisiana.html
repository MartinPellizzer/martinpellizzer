Pachira mutisiana

Pachira mutisiana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
