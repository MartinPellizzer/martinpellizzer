Plumeria mollis

Plumeria mollis, commonly known as the sweet sandlewood tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its bark is smooth and pale, and it produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Plumeria mollis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
