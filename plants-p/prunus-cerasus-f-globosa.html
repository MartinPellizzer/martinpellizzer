Prunus cerasus f. globosa

Prunus cerasus f. globosa, commonly known as the globe cherry, is a unique cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit, which gives it a spherical appearance. The plant produces small, sweet cherries that are typically harvested in late summer. Its foliage is dense and provides excellent shade, making it suitable for ornamental purposes.

The globe cherry is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its edible fruit.
