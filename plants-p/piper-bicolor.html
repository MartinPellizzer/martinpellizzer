Piper bicolor

Piper bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored leaves, which are typically green on the upper surface and purple or reddish on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by berries that range in color from yellow to red. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
