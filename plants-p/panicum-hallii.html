Panicum hallii

Panicum hallii, commonly known as Hall's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Panicum hallii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
