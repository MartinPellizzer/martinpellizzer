Pyrrhopappus geiseri

Pyrrhopappus geiseri, commonly known as the Geiser's goldenaster, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces showy yellow-orange flowers in the summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Pyrrhopappus geiseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
