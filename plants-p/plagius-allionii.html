Plagius allionii

Plagius allionii, commonly known as the Allion's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of white or grayish hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Plagius allionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
