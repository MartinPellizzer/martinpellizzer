Poa scaligera

Poa scaligera, commonly known as the scalycorn grass, is a species of grass native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Poa scaligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
