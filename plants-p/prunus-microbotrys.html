Prunus microbotrys

Prunus microbotrys, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is renowned for its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular visual display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval leaves and small, dark purple berries that follow the flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The botanical features of Prunus microbotrys make it a symbol of renewal and the changing seasons in Japanese culture.
