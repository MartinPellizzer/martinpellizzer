Polygonatum laoticum

Polygonatum laoticum, commonly known as the Laotian Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Polygonatum laoticum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
