Potentilla tatsienluensis

Potentilla tatsienluensis, commonly known as the Tatsienlu cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cooler climates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, aggregate fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
