Phaseolus heterophyllus subsp. rotundifolius

Phaseolus heterophyllus subsp.

rotundifolius, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to red.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its nutritional contribution to diets in various regions.
