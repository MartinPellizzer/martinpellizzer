Pilosella callimorphoides subsp. pseudoschultesiiformis

Pilosella callimorphoides subsp.

pseudoschultesiiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale blue ray florets.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is native to parts of southern Africa, where it thrives in temperate climates.
