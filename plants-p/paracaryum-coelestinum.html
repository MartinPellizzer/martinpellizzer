Paracaryum coelestinum

Paracaryum coelestinum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a striking perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of vivid blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical name reflects its striking blue petals and its grass-like appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
