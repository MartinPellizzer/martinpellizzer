Prunella pienina

Prunella pienina, commonly known as narrow-leaved gentian, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Prunella pienina is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
