Piper glandulosissimum

Piper glandulosissimum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, black, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its stems are often covered in glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive aroma.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
