Plantago recurvata subsp. humilis

Plantago recurvata subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Asia. It features a basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface and a prominent midrib.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
