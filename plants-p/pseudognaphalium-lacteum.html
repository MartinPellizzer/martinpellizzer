Pseudognaphalium lacteum

Pseudognaphalium lacteum, commonly known as the milkweed aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used for medicinal purposes by indigenous peoples. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Its leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
