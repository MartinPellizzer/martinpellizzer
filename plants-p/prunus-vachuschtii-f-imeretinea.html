Prunus vachuschtii f. imeretinea

Prunus vachuschtii f. imeretinea, commonly known as the Imereti cherry, is a rare and ornamental shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
