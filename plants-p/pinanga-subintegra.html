Pinanga subintegra

Pinanga subintegra, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feathery, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
