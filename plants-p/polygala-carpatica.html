Polygala carpatica

Polygala carpatica, commonly known as the Carpathian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
