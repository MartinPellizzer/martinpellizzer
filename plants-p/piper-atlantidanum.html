Piper atlantidanum

Piper atlantidanum, commonly known as the Atlantic pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical regions.
