Pleopeltis phlebodes

Pleopeltis phlebodes, commonly known as the veined rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit on tree trunks and rocks. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
