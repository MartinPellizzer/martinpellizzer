Polypodium denticulatum

Polypodium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or on rocky surfaces, thriving in humid environments. Its leaves are divided into small, serrated segments, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Polypodium denticulatum is often found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
