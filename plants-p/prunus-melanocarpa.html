Prunus melanocarpa

Prunus melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that turn dark purple when mature.

Its fruits are often used in jams and pies, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
