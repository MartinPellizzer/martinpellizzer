Philoxerus portulacoides var. commersonii

Philoxerus portulacoides var. commersonii, commonly known as the Commerson's ice plant, is a succulent species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
