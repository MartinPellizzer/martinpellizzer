Pyrus semilobata

Pyrus semilobata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for ornamental and fruit cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
