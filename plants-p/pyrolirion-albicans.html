Pyrolirion albicans

Pyrolirion albicans, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are white to pale pink in color. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a subtle beauty to alpine meadows.

Pyrolirion albicans is relatively low maintenance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
