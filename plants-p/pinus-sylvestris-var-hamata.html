Pinus sylvestris var. hamata

Pinus sylvestris var. hamata, commonly known as the scaly pine, is a variant of the Scots pine native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, scaly bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The cones of this species are usually pendulous and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, acidic conditions.

The plant is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cold climates.
