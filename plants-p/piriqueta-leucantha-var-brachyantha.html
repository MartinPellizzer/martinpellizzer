Piriqueta leucantha var. brachyantha

Piriqueta leucantha var. brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved piriqueta, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
