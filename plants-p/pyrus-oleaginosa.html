Pyrus oleaginosa

Pyrus oleaginosa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, white to yellow fruits in autumn. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both its fruit and its use in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
