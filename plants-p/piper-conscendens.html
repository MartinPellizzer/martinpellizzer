Piper conscendens

Piper conscendens, commonly known as the climbing pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries are covered in tiny, rough bumps and contain a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
