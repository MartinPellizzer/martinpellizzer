Pyrostegia lauta

Pyrostegia lauta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
