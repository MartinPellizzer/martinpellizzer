Phlogacanthus thyrsiflorus

Phlogacanthus thyrsiflorus, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features a stout, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. Its bark and latex contain compounds used for treating ailments like malaria and wounds.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
