Phalaris vaginiflora

Phalaris vaginiflora, commonly known as the bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Phalaris vaginiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
