Paphia brassii subsp. brassii

Paphia brassii subsp.

brassii, commonly known as the Brassii Paphiopedilum, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, dark green to purple foliage and its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink.

The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are spurred at the base, a distinctive feature of the Paphiopedilum genus.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts.
