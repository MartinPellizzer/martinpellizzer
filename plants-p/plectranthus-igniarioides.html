Plectranthus igniarioides

Plectranthus igniarioides, commonly known as the flame bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowering display and drought tolerance.
