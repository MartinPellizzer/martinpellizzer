Phyllanthus macgregorii

Phyllanthus macgregorii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various soil types.
