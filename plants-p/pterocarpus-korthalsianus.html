Pterocarpus korthalsianus

Pterocarpus korthalsianus, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that resembles ebony. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits. Its root system is extensive, and it thrives in well-drained soils in humid climates.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
