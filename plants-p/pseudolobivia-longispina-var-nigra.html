Pseudolobivia longispina var. nigra

Pseudolobivia longispina var. nigra, commonly known as the black-spined lobivia, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces striking, spiky flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its dark, sharply defined spines are a distinguishing characteristic, providing protection from herbivores.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
