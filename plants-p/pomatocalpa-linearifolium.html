Pomatocalpa linearifolium

Pomatocalpa linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
