Polygonum florulentum

Polygonum florulentum, commonly known as the showy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 2 meters in height. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Polygonum florulentum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
