Plinia parvifolia

Plinia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red or yellow fruits.

Plinia parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
