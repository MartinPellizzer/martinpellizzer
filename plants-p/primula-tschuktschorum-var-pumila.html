Primula tschuktschorum var. pumila

Primula tschuktschorum var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
