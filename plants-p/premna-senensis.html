Premna senensis

Premna senensis, commonly known as the white-flowered premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
