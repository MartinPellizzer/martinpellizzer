Parodia catarinensis

Parodia catarinensis, commonly known as the Catarinensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Parodia catarinensis is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
