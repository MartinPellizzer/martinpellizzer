Pelargonium quinquelobatum

Pelargonium quinquelobatum, commonly known as the five-lobed geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically lobed, with five distinct lobes, giving the plant its common name. It produces flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often in late spring to early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and borders due to its compact size and ornamental value.
