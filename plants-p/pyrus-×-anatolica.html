Pyrus × anatolica

Pyrus × anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian pear, is a hybrid species native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its medium-sized, often fragrant, and sweet fruits. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, with compound leaves and white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit is valued for culinary use and has been cultivated for its flavor and nutritional content.

The species is also appreciated for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
