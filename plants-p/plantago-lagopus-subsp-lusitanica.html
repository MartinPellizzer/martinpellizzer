Plantago lagopus subsp. lusitanica

Plantago lagopus subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the lusitanian plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
