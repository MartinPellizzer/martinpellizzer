Polembryum castaneaecarpum

Polembryum castaneaecarpum, commonly known as the chestnut-carpus polembryum, is a unique plant species with specialized botanical features.

It belongs to the family Polembryaceae and is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and reproductive structures. The plant exhibits a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, which plays a crucial role in its nutrient absorption. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in late spring.

This species is native to temperate regions and thrives in moist, shaded environments.
