Pterostylis obtusa

Pterostylis obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved duck orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, grass-like leaves and a single, erect flower spike that bears a single, greenish-yellow orchid flower. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It has a short-lived rhizome and produces small, white to cream-colored flowers during the spring.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms.
