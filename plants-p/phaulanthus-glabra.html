Phaulanthus glabra

Phaulanthus glabra, commonly known as the smooth phaulanthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils in desert regions. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are typically pale green to yellowish in color.

Phaulanthus glabra is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
