Pithecellobium oppositum

Pithecellobium oppositum, commonly known as the velvet bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines.

It also has a fibrous root system that helps prevent soil erosion and can fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
