Pedicularis chamissonis var. hokkaidoensis

Pedicularis chamissonis var. hokkaidoensis, commonly known as the Hokkaido lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Hokkaido, Japan.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family, characterized by its parasitic or hemiparasitic growth habit. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive bilabiate structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of hemiparasitic plants and alpine flora.
