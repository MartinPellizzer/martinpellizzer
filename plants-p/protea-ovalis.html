Protea ovalis

Protea ovalis, commonly known as the oval protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or oval shape. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly the sugarbird, which is native to the area.

The protea ovalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
