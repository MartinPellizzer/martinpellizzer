Papaver indehiscens

Papaver indehiscens, commonly known as the Turk's Cap Papaver, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces capsule fruits that remain closed until maturity, a feature known as indehiscence. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Papaver indehiscens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
