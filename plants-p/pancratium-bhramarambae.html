Pancratium bhramarambae

Pancratium bhramarambae, commonly known as the Bhramarambae yellow lily, is a striking flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often spotted with white or silver markings.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
