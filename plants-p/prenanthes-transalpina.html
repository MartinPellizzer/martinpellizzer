Prenanthes transalpina

Prenanthes transalpina, commonly known as the transalpine water parsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, hollow stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually greenish-white in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
