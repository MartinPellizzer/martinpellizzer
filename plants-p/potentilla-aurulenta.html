Potentilla aurulenta

Potentilla aurulenta, commonly known as golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of silky white hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla aurulenta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
