Pseudovigna sulaensis

Pseudovigna sulaensis, commonly known as the Sulaensis cowpea, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with trifoliate leaves and cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Due to its nutritional value, it is cultivated for both food and forage in various parts of its native range.
