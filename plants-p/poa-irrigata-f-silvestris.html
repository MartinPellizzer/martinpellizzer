Poa irrigata f. silvestris

Poa irrigata f. silvestris, commonly known as the wild bluegrass, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in native plant gardens.
