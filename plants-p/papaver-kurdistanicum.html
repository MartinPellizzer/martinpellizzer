Papaver kurdistanicum

Papaver kurdistanicum, commonly known as the Kurdish poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a prominent, centrally located stigma. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its capsule-shaped seed pod contains numerous small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
