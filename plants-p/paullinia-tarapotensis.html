Paullinia tarapotensis

Paullinia tarapotensis, commonly known as the Amazonian soursop or cupuacu, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit with a thick, spiny rind. The fruit contains a white, aromatic pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a slightly acidic taste. The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting biodiversity and contributing to forest structure.
