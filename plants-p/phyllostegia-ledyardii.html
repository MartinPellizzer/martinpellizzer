Phyllostegia ledyardii

Phyllostegia ledyardii, commonly known as the ledyard's strobilant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
