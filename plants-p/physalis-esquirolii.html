Physalis esquirolii

Physalis esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, yellow-orange calyx that encloses the small, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to Mexico and Central America. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, and the fruit ripens to a bright orange-red color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food plant in some regions.
