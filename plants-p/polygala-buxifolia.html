Polygala buxifolia

Polygala buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and dense, foliage resembling boxwood leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala buxifolia is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
