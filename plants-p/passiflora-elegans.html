Passiflora elegans

Passiflora elegans, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora elegans thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
