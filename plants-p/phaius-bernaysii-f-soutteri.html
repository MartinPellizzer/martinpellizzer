Phaius bernaysii f. soutteri

Phaius bernaysii f. soutteri, commonly known as the Soutter's orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pale yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive green throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
