Pyrus communis f. serotina

Pyrus communis f. serotina, commonly known as the late-blooming pear.

This is a cultivar of the common pear, characterized by its delayed flowering compared to other varieties. It typically produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen later in the season. The plant retains the typical morphology of the common pear, with a spreading canopy and edible fruit.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and extended fruiting period.
