Piper tacananum

Piper tacananum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or Tacana pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
