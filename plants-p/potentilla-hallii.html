Potentilla hallii

Potentilla hallii, commonly known as Hall's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla hallii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
