Piper maagnasanum

Piper maagnasanum, commonly known as the West African pepper or African long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor and potential medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and cultural contexts across its native range.
