Piper tacanaense

Piper tacanaense, commonly known as the Tacana pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the cloud forests of Bolivia and Peru.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, green to yellowish fruits, which are typically harvested when mature. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are hairy and woody.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
