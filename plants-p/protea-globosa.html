Protea globosa

Protea globosa, commonly known as the globular protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in areas with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are pollinated by insects and birds.

Protea globosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important component of local biodiversity.
