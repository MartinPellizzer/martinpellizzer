Phyllopodium krebsianum

Phyllopodium krebsianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, warm environments and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.
