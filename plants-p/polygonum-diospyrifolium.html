Polygonum diospyrifolium

Polygonum diospyrifolium, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish stems. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, serrated margin. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white and appear in late summer.

Polygonum diospyrifolium is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in partial shade to full sun.
