Phaseolus monophyllus var. rufus

Phaseolus monophyllus var. rufus, commonly known as the red runner bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, hairy pods and small, edible beans that are typically consumed when young and tender. The plant grows as a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces flowers that range from white to pale purple. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary use.

The seeds are rich in protein and can be used in various traditional dishes across its native regions.
