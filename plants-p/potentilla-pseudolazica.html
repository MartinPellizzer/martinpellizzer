Potentilla pseudolazica

Potentilla pseudolazica, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the local flora.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
