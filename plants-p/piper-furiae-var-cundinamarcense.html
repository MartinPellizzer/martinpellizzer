Piper furiae var. cundinamarcense

Piper furiae var. cundinamarcense, commonly known as the Cundinamarca pepper, is a variant of the Piper furiae species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its aromatic, pungent fruits that are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a strong, spicy aroma when crushed.

The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully mature, contains a small, edible seed enclosed in a thin, papery pericarp.
