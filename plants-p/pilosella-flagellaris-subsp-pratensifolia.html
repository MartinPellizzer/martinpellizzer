Pilosella flagellaris subsp. pratensifolia

Pilosella flagellaris subsp.

pratensifolia, commonly known as the meadow pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
