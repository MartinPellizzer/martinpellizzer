Piper mystaciferum

Piper mystaciferum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are harvested for their aromatic seeds. The plant has opposite, simple, and often glossy leaves, and its stems are usually hairy. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

The fruit develops in clusters and is typically ready for harvest several months after flowering.
