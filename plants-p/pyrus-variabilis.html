Pyrus variabilis

Pyrus variabilis, commonly known as the variable pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Pyrus variabilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
