Piper arieianum f. puberulum

Piper arieianum f. puberulum, commonly known as the hairy sylvan pepper, is a variant of the sylvan pepper plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticultural and ecological studies.
