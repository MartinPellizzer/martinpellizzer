Panicum colonum var. bussei

Panicum colonum var. bussei, commonly known as the Bussei panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often visible during the late summer and early autumn months. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects and as a ornamental grass in landscaping.
