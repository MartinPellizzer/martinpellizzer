Prunus sogdiana var. eximia

Prunus sogdiana var. eximia, commonly known as the Sogdian cherry, is a rare and ancient species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, thorny branches. Its leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
