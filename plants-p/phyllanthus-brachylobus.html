Phyllanthus brachylobus

Phyllanthus brachylobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Phyllanthus brachylobus is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.
