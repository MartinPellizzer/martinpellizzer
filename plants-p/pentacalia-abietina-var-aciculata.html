Pentacalia abietina var. aciculata

Pentacalia abietina var. aciculata, commonly known as the pineleaf pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that resemble those of pine trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
