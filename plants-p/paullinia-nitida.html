Paullinia nitida

Paullinia nitida, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop or yellow soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage. Its fruits, while not commonly consumed, have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
