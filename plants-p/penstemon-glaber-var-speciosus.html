Penstemon glaber var. speciosus

Penstemon glaber var. speciosus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It produces showy, pinkish-white bracts that resemble fuzzy hairs, giving it its common name.

Penstemon glaber var. speciosus is a perennial species valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
