Pituranthos chloranthus subsp. cossonianus

Pituranthos chloranthus subsp.

cossonianus, commonly known as the Cossonian sandmat, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
