Penstemon laetus var. sagittatus

Penstemon laetus var. sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
