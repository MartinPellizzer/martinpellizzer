Polygala apopetala

Polygala apopetala, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its unique, delicate inflorescence that resembles a cluster of tiny, translucent flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.
