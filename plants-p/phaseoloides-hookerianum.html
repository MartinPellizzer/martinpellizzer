Phaseoloides hookerianum

Phaseoloides hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It has a trailing habit and can grow up to several meters in length.

The seeds are enclosed in a fibrous, hairy pod that matures in late summer.
