Pyrola rotundifolia var. serotina

Pyrola rotundifolia var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering wintergreen, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are rounded and glossy, and the flowers are usually pink to reddish in color.
