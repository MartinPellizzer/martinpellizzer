Poa glauca var. strictior

Poa glauca var. strictior, commonly known as the bluegrass or slender bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and a tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and form dense turf.

Its compact form and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for landscaping and turfgrass applications.
