Phalangium graminifolium

Phalangium graminifolium, commonly known as the grass-leaf phalangium, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a smooth texture and a tendency to form dense mats.

Phalangium graminifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
