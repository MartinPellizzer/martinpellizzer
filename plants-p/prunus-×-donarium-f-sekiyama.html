Prunus × donarium f. sekiyama

Prunus × donarium f. sekiyama, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elegant, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular floral display. The tree has a rounded canopy and smooth, light gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are ovate and turn vibrant yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in Japanese tradition.
