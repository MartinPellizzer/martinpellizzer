Pleurothallis foetens

Pleurothallis foetens, commonly known as the stinking orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant or foul-smelling flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a wide range of colors.

Pleurothallis foetens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
