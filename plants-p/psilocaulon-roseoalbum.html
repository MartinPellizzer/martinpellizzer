Psilocaulon roseoalbum

Psilocaulon roseoalbum, commonly known as the pink-white sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that mature into a hard, woody structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along coastal areas.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
