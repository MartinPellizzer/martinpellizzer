Pleurothallis pilosissima

Pleurothallis pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, hairy texture, which gives it its common name.

Pleurothallis pilosissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
