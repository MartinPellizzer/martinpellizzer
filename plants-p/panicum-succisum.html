Panicum succisum

Panicum succisum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikelets that are often wind-dispersed. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wetland biodiversity.
