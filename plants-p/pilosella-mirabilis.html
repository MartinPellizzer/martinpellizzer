Pilosella mirabilis

Pilosella mirabilis, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that resemble miniature dusters, hence its common name. Its leaves are hairy and narrow, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pilosella mirabilis is often found in open habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
