Podophyllum aurantiocaule var. aurantiocaule

Podophyllum aurantiocaule var. aurantiocaule, commonly known as the orange-stemmed podophyllum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a stout, reddish-orange stem that is often hairy and becomes more prominent with age. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

This variety is notable for its striking coloration and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in shaded gardens.
