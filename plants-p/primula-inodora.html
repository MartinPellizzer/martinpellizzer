Primula inodora

Primula inodora, commonly known as the scentless primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow and bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy leaves that are oval to lance-shaped, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
