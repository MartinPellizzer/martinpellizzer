Pavonia microphylla

Pavonia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved penta, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

Pavonia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
