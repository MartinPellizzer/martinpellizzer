Poa bannatica

Poa bannatica, commonly known as Bann's bluegrass, is a species of grass native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Poa bannatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
