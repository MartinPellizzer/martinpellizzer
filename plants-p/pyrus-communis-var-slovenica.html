Pyrus communis var. slovenica

Pyrus communis var. slovenica, commonly known as the Slovenian pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Slovenia and the surrounding areas of the Balkans. This variety is characterized by its small, often ornamental fruits and its ability to thrive in cooler climates. The leaves are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued both for its horticultural appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
