Polycnemum heuffelii var. paradoxum

Polycnemum heuffelii var. paradoxum, commonly known as the paradox variety of Heuffel's polycnemum, is a rare and lesser-known fern species.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
