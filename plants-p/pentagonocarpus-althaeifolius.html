Pentagonocarpus althaeifolius

Pentagonocarpus althaeifolius, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable, heavy wood used in furniture and construction.

The tree also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
