Pleopeltis fallax

Pleopeltis fallax, commonly known as the Hawaiian rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its humid, tropical environment. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pleopeltis fallax is considered a rare and endangered species.
