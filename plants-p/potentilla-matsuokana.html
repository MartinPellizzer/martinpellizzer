Potentilla matsuokana

Potentilla matsuokana, commonly known as the Matsuoka cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically features upright stems with palmate leaves, which are divided into five lobes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla matsuokana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural gardens.
