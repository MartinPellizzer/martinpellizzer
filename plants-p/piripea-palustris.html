Piripea palustris

Piripea palustris, commonly known as the marsh piripea, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, typically emerging from the water's surface.

Piripea palustris plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
