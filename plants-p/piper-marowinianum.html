Piper marowinianum

Piper marowinianum, commonly known as the Marowini pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.

The leaves are also used in traditional remedies for their purported health benefits.
