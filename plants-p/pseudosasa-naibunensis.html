Pseudosasa naibunensis

Pseudosasa naibunensis, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pseudosasa naibunensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
