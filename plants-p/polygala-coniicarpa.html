Polygala coniicarpa

Polygala coniicarpa, commonly known as the clustered milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
