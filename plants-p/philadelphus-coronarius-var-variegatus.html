Philadelphus coronarius var. variegatus

Philadelphus coronarius var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated weeping bergamot, is a deciduous shrub native to the United Kingdom.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features white and green markings on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its growth habit is typically rounded and weeping, with branches that hang gracefully.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
