Penstemon rydbergii var. oreocharis

Penstemon rydbergii var. oreocharis, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, sunny conditions.
