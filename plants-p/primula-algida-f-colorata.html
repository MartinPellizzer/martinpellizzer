Primula algida f. colorata

Primula algida f. colorata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

The f. colorata form is distinguished by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
