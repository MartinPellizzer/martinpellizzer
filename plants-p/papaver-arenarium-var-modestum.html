Papaver arenarium var. modestum

Papaver arenarium var. modestum, commonly known as the desert poppy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
