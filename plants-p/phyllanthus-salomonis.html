Phyllanthus salomonis

Phyllanthus salomonis, commonly known as Solomon's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring succulent, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits containing seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine systems.
