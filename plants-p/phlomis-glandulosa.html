Phlomis glandulosa

Phlomis glandulosa, commonly known as woolly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance and contributing to its aromatic properties.

Phlomis glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
