Potamogeton lonchites

Potamogeton lonchites, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and rivers. The plant has long, slender leaves that are arranged spirally around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
