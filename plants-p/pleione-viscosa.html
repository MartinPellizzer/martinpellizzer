Pleione viscosa

Pleione viscosa, commonly known as the sticky pleione, is a species of orchid native to the Himalayas and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen rhizomes and its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent yellow lip petals, which are often fragrant. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts.
