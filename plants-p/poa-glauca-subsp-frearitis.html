Poa glauca subsp. frearitis

Poa glauca subsp.

frearitis, commonly known as the bluegrass or frear's bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
