Pinanga stricta

Pinanga stricta, commonly known as the bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Pinanga stricta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor landscaping.
