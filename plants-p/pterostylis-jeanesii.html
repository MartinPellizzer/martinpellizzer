Pterostylis jeanesii

Pterostylis jeanesii, commonly known as the Jeane's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single flower that is often hooded in appearance. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
