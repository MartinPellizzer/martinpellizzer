Polygala fragilis

Polygala fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, often hairy stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and the flowers are arranged in loose, branched inflorescences.

Polygala fragilis is known for its fragrant scent, which attracts various pollinators like bees and flies.
