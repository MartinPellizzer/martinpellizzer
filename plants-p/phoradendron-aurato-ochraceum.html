Phoradendron aurato-ochraceum

Phoradendron aurato-ochraceum, commonly known as the golden-ochre mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the neotropical region.

It possesses a dense, leafy crown with small, elliptical leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries containing sticky seeds. Its parasitic nature allows it to draw water and nutrients from its host, while also contributing to ecological interactions through seed dispersal by birds.

The species is notable for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
