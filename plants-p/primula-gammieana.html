Primula gammieana

Primula gammieana, commonly known as the Gammie's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Primula gammieana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
