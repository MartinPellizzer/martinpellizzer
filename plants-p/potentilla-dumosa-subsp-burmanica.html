Potentilla dumosa subsp. burmanica

Potentilla dumosa subsp.

burmanica, commonly known as Burman's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant features wiry, thorny stems and small, palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are yellow, occurring in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
