Pastinaca apula

Pastinaca apula, commonly known as the Apulian burdock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pastinaca apula is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
