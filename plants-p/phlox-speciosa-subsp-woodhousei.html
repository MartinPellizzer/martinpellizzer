Phlox speciosa subsp. woodhousei

Phlox speciosa subsp.

woodhousei, commonly known as the Woodhouse's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces numerous stamens in its flower clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
