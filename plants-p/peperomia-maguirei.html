Peperomia maguirei

Peperomia maguirei, commonly known as the Guatemalan peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with subtle variegation, often displaying shades of silver or white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia maguirei is prized for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
