Paphiopedilum insigne var. pynaertii

Paphiopedilum insigne var. pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's Lady's Slipper, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure, featuring a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, which is a defining trait of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and requires specific conditions to thrive, including high humidity and indirect light. Its flowers, which can be pink, white, or pale yellow, are often fragrant and bloom during the spring or early summer.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
