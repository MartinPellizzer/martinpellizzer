Pseuderanthemum coudercii

Pseuderanthemum coudercii, commonly known as the purple-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
