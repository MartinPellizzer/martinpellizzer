Pogonia scottii

Pogonia scottii, commonly known as the Scott's pink, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and wetlands. It has a slender stem with a single, showy flower that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
