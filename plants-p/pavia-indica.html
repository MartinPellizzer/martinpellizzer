Pavia indica

Pavia indica, commonly known as Indian clover, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassy areas.

Pavia indica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
