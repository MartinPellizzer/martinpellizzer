Phacelia tanacetifolia var. cinerea

Phacelia tanacetifolia var. cinerea, commonly known as silver phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
