Pithecellobium cochleatum

Pithecellobium cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features spirally arranged leaves, which give it its common name, and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
