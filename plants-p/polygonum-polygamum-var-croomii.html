Polygonum polygamum var. croomii

Polygonum polygamum var. croomii, commonly known as the Croom's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinct, bead-like fruits and simple, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes, which appear in early summer.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
