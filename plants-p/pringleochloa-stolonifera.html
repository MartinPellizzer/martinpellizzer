Pringleochloa stolonifera

Pringleochloa stolonifera, commonly known as the stoloniferous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its creeping growth habit through stolons. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential applications in horticulture.
