Phlox drummondii subsp. mcallisteri

Phlox drummondii subsp.

mcallisteri, commonly known as the McAllister's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The subspecies is typically found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of cultivation.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for garden use in arid and semi-arid regions.
