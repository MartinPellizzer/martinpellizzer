Panicum dichotomum var. mattamuskeetense

Panicum dichotomum var. mattamuskeetense, commonly known as Mattamuskeet grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
