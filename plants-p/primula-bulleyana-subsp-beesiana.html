Primula bulleyana subsp. beesiana

Primula bulleyana subsp.

beesiana, commonly known as the bees' primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated corolla.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its early spring blooms and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
