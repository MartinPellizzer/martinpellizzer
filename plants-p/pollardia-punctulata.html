Pollardia punctulata

Pollardia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with dark green coloration and yellow or brown spots.

Pollardia punctulata is often found in temperate climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
