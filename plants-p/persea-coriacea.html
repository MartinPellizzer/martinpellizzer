Persea coriacea

Persea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-skin avocado, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a pear-shaped berry with a rough, greenish-brown skin and a creamy, oily pulp. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
