Phylloctenium bernieri

Phylloctenium bernieri, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, which give it a dandelion-like appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Phylloctenium bernieri is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
