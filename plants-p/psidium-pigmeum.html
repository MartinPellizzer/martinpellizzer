Psidium pigmeum

Psidium pigmeum, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for container gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, requiring moderate watering.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in subtropical regions.
