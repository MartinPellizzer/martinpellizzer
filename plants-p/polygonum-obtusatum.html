Polygonum obtusatum

Polygonum obtusatum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas.

Polygonum obtusatum is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife and ecological diversity.
