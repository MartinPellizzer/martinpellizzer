Pavetta comostyla subsp. nyassica

Pavetta comostyla subsp.

nyassica, commonly known as the East African pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks.

The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with smooth or slightly hairy surfaces. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
