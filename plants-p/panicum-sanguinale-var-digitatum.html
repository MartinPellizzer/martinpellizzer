Panicum sanguinale var. digitatum

Panicum sanguinale var. digitatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant red to purple flower spikes, which add visual interest to gardens and landscapes.
