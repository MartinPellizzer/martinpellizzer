Panicum ankarense

Panicum ankarense, commonly known as the African grass or red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Panicum ankarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
