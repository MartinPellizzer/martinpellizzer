Protea strobilina

Protea strobilina, commonly known as the sugar bush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features dense, leathery foliage and produces intricate, cone-like flower heads that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Protea strobilina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
