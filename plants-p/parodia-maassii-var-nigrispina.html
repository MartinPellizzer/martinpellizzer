Parodia maassii var. nigrispina

Parodia maassii var. nigrispina, commonly known as the Black Thorn Cactus, is a rare and striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, dark purple to black spines, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its dark foliage. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
