Polystachya orophila

Polystachya orophila, commonly known as the mountain polystachya, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in cloud forests or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, adapted to the humid and shaded conditions of its habitat.

Polystachya orophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
