Potentilla procumbens mixta

Potentilla procumbens mixta, commonly known as the mixed creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that spread along the ground and produce clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant collections.
