Passiflora pomifera

Passiflora pomifera, commonly known as the giant granadilla, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces large, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color when ripe. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
