Piper holguinianum

Piper holguinianum, commonly known as the Holguin's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often glandular-dotted. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in axillary clusters, and it produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
