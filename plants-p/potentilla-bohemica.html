Potentilla bohemica

Potentilla bohemica, commonly known as Bohemian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. The plant features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla bohemica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
