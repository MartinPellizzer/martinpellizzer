Poranthera oreophila

Poranthera oreophila, commonly known as the mountain larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at higher elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Poranthera oreophila is part of the tribe Lophopyreae within the genus Poranthera, which is closely related to the genus Delphinium.
