Pyrus hypocarpa

Pyrus hypocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray-brown bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits.

The fruit is typically pear-shaped, with a thin, smooth skin and a juicy, white flesh that is prized for its flavor and culinary use.
