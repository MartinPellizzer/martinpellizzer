Potentilla × sericantissima

Potentilla × sericantissima, commonly known as silver weeping potentilla, is a hybrid shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and cascading growth habit.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact form and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a border plant.

The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, contributing to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
