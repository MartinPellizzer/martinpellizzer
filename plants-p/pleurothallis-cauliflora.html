Pleurothallis cauliflora

Pleurothallis cauliflora, commonly known as the calyptrochilum orchid, is a species native to the humid tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and growth habits. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in the canopy layer of rainforests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive calyptra that covers the ovary, giving it its scientific name.

Pleurothallis cauliflora is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
