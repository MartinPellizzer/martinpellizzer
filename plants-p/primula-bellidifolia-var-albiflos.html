Primula bellidifolia var. albiflos

Primula bellidifolia var. albiflos, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. It prefers cool, moist habitats and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its delicate and early blooming flowers.
