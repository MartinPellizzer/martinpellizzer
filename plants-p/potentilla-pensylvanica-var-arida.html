Potentilla pensylvanica var. arida

Potentilla pensylvanica var. arida, commonly known as sandstone cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in poor, sandy soils. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy ornamental plant for xeriscapes.

The species is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil in erosion-prone areas.
