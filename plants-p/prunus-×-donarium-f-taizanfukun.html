Prunus × donarium f. taizanfukun

Prunus × donarium f. taizanfukun, commonly known as the Taizanfukun cherry, is a unique ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus, known for its showy, double flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
