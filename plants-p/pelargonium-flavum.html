Pelargonium flavum

Pelargonium flavum, commonly known as the yellow geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. It prefers full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pelargonium flavum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
