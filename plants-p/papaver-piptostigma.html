Papaver piptostigma

Papaver piptostigma, commonly known as the desert poppy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, dry seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Papaver piptostigma is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
