Plantago major subsp. cornuti

Plantago major subsp.

cornuti, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are borne on erect, branched stems. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils, due to its hardy nature.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
