Pedicularis groenlandica subsp. surrecta

Pedicularis groenlandica subsp.

surrecta, commonly known as the Greenland betony, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and alpine regions of northern Europe and North America. It is a perennial herb that grows in rocky and open habitats, often in tundra or mountainous areas.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip-shaped petal. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is adapted to cold climates and has a long flowering period during the short summer season.
