Philodendron huaynacapacense

Philodendron huaynacapacense, commonly known as the Huaynacapacense Philodendron, is a rare and striking tropical plant native to the cloud forests of Peru.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berries containing seeds. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a challenging but rewarding species for experienced plant enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
