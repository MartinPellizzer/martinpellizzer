Passiflora tryphostemmatoides

Passiflora tryphostemmatoides, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and botanical studies.
