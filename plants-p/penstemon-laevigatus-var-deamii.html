Penstemon laevigatus var. deamii

Penstemon laevigatus var. deamii, commonly known as Deam's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
