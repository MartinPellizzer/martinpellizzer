Paspalum furcatum var. fissum

Paspalum furcatum var. fissum, commonly known as the fissured paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with distinctive, three-lobed glumes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and cultivated settings.
