Pachyrhizus angulatus f. integrifolius

Pachyrhizus angulatus f. integrifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian bean or angular cowpea, is a variant of the angular cowpea species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, angular pods and trifoliate leaves with entire margins. The plant is a legume, capable of fixing nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

This variety is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
