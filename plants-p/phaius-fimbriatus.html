Phaius fimbriatus

Phaius fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaius fimbriatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and private collections.
