Paspalum laeve var. altissimum

Paspalum laeve var. altissimum, commonly known as tall silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and soft, silky blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to bluish, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
