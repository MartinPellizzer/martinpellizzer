Pleurothallis balaeniceps

Pleurothallis balaeniceps, commonly known as the whale's head orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated flower structure that resembles a whale's head. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, often in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Pleurothallis balaeniceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance.
