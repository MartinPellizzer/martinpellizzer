Polygonum aviculare subsp. aviculare

Polygonum aviculare subsp.

aviculare, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It typically features prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are small, rounded, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, lawns, and along pathways.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
