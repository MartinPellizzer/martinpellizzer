Pancratium verecundum

Pancratium verecundum, commonly known as the yellow lily or golden arum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble lilies, with a spatulate-shaped petal that curves backward. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It produces a showy inflorescence with a spathe that is usually yellow or orange in color.

Pancratium verecundum is a perennial herb that thrives in warm, sunny environments.
