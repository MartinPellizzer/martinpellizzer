Penstemon nitidus subsp. polyphyllus

Penstemon nitidus subsp.

polyphyllus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces numerous spikes of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
