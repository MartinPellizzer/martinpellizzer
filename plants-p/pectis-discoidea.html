Pectis discoidea

Pectis discoidea, commonly known as the "Cape daisy," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow composite flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Pectis discoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
