Potentilla husztensis

Potentilla husztensis, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Hungary and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Potentilla husztensis is also valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
