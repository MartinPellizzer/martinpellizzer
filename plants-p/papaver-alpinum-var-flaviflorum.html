Papaver alpinum var. flaviflorum

Papaver alpinum var. flaviflorum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy with yellow flowers, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a tall, slender stem that supports its delicate blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role in mountain ecosystems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
