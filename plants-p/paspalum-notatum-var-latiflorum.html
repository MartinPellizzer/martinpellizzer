Paspalum notatum var. latiflorum

Paspalum notatum var. latiflorum, commonly known as broadflower hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, white or pale blue flowers, which are borne on slender stems. It is often found in wet or seasonally flooded areas, making it adaptable to a variety of soil conditions.

Its ability to thrive in moist environments makes it a common species in wetlands and along waterways.
