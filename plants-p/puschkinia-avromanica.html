Puschkinia avromanica

Puschkinia avromanica, commonly known as the Persian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of eastern Turkey and northern Iran.

It produces clusters of striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base, and it thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Puschkinia avromanica is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a range of growing conditions.
