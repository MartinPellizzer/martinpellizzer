Piper acutibaccum

Piper acutibaccum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries. Its fruit is typically harvested when it turns a bright red or yellow, depending on the variety.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
