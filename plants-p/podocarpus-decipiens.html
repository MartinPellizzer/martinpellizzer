Podocarpus decipiens

Podocarpus decipiens, commonly known as the false cypress or decipiens podocarp, is a evergreen coniferous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves arranged in compact clusters. The plant produces small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a landscape plant in many regions.
