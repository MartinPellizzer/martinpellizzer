Phylocarpos

Phylocarpos, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. Its botanical features include small, clustered inflorescences and aromatic leaves.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
