Potentilla leuconota var. corymbosa

Potentilla leuconota var. corymbosa, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
