Pithecellobium acacioides

Pithecellobium acacioides, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
