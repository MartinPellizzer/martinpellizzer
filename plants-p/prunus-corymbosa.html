Prunus corymbosa

Prunus corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese date or Indian date, is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet, and sticky fruits in summer. The fruit, similar in appearance to a date, is rich in sugars and has a distinctive aroma. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
