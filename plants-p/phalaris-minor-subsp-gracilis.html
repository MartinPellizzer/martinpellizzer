Phalaris minor subsp. gracilis

Phalaris minor subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Europe. It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
