Pagamea capitata subsp. capitata

Pagamea capitata subsp.

capitata, commonly known as the Cape mountain cedar, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
