Phalaenopsis ochracea

Phalaenopsis ochracea, commonly known as the yellow phalaenopsis orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity.

Phalaenopsis ochracea is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
