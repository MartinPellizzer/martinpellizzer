Passiflora ceratosepala

Passiflora ceratosepala, commonly known as the horned sepaled passionflower, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers with five sepals that are prominently horned. The plant produces showy, blue or purple flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

The fruit, which develops after flowering, is a small, edible berry with a sweet and tart flavor.
