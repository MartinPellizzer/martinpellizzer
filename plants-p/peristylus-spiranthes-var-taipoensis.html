Peristylus spiranthes var. taipoensis

Peristylus spiranthes var. taipoensis, commonly known as the Taipo spider-orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to specific regions of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of flowers, which gives it its scientific name "spiranthes," meaning "spiral flower.". The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are small, delicate, and often pale green or white.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
