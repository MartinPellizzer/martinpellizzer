Prunus myrobalana f. cerasifera

Prunus myrobalana f. cerasifera, commonly known as the Persian lilac or Persian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round, orange-red fruits. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
