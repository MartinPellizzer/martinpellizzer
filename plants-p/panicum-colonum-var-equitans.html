Panicum colonum var. equitans

Panicum colonum var. equitans, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy panicles that produce a reddish tinge during the flowering season. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
