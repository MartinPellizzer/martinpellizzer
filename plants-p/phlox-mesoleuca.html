Phlox mesoleuca

Phlox mesoleuca, commonly known as the white phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers range from white to pale pink and are arranged in terminal or axillary clusters.

Phlox mesoleuca thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
