Phyllostachys puberula var. boryana

Phyllostachys puberula var. boryana, commonly known as the Boryana bamboo, is a cold-hardy variety native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

This bamboo is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant growth habit and resilience.
