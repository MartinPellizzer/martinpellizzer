Pleurothallis fusca

Pleurothallis fusca, commonly known as the dark pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, and it prefers high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its striking appearance and unique growth patterns, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
