Phyllanthus golonensis

Phyllanthus golonensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Phyllanthus golonensis is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
