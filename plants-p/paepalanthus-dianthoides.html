Paepalanthus dianthoides

Paepalanthus dianthoides, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with a distinctive silver-gray foliage that helps it reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
