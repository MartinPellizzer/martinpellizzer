Piper aequale var. fimbribracteatum

Piper aequale var. fimbribracteatum, commonly known as the fimbriated pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its unique foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

However, it is important to note that all parts of the plant contain irritants and should be handled with care.
