Pavonia ramosissima

Pavonia ramosissima, commonly known as the scarlet bower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branching habit and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Pavonia ramosissima is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
