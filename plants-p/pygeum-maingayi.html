Pygeum maingayi

Pygeum maingayi, commonly known as African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and smooth, grey bark. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a distinctive elliptical shape with a pointed tip.

Pygeum maingayi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
