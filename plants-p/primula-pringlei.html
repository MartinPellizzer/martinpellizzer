Primula pringlei

Primula pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's primrose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Primula pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
