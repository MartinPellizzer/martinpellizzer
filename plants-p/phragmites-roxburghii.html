Phragmites roxburghii

Phragmites roxburghii, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense growth habit and hollow, jointed stems that can reach heights of up to six meters. The plant produces fluffy, feathery panicles of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly and form extensive colonies.

Phragmites roxburghii plays a crucial role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
