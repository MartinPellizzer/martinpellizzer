Pycreus laxespicatus

Pycreus laxespicatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf pycreus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
