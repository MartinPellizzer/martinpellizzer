Puccinellia distans var. pichaueri

Puccinellia distans var. pichaueri, commonly known as the Pichauer's saltbush, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert and steppe ecosystems.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
