Prasophyllum anticum

Prasophyllum anticum, commonly known as the Antigonish leek, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the coastal regions of Nova Scotia, Canada.

It is a low-growing plant with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green or white flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, acidic soils and is often found in dune environments. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in ecological and floral botany.
