Pinus montezumae var. hartwegii

Pinus montezumae var. hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's Mexican pine, is a variant of the Montezuma pine native to high-altitude regions of central Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive cone morphology. The species thrives in temperate to subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, a conical crown, and a slow growth rate, making it a valuable component of montane ecosystems.

This variety is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
