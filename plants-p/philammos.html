Philammos

Philammos, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural propagation.

Philammos is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance.
