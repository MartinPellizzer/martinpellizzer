Prostanthera cotinifolia

Prostanthera cotinifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Prostanthera cotinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional bush medicine.
