Piper ponendum

Piper ponendum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, peppercorn-like berries. The plant produces elongated, dark reddish-brown fruits that are harvested when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
