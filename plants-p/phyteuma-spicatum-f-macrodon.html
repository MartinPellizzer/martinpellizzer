Phyteuma spicatum f. macrodon

Phyteuma spicatum f. macrodon, commonly known as the giant spikephyte, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
