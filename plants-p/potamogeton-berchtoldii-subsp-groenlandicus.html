Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp. groenlandicus

Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp.

groenlandicus, commonly known as Greenland pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to cold freshwater environments in the Arctic and subarctic regions. It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically unbranched and bear small, whorled leaves.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or partially emergent. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of clean, well-oxygenated water.
