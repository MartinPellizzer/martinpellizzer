Primula shikokiana

Primula shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
