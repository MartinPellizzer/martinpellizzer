Panicum caesioglaucum

Panicum caesioglaucum, commonly known as blue glaucous panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-green foliage and produces fluffy, pale blue to white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive bluish hue that contributes to its ornamental value.

Panicum caesioglaucum is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
