Peucedanum angustifolium

Peucedanum angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved hogweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a tall, hollow stem that is often reddish in color and covered with coarse hairs. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are narrow and deeply lobed. Its flowers are small, white to pale green, and arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
