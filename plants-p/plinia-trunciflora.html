Plinia trunciflora

Plinia trunciflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia trunciflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible red berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy green appearance.

Plinia trunciflora is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
