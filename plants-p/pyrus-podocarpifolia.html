Pyrus podocarpifolia

Pyrus podocarpifolia, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.
