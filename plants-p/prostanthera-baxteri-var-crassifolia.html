Prostanthera baxteri var. crassifolia

Prostanthera baxteri var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Prostanthera baxteri var. crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
