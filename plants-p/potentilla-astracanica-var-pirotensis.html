Potentilla astracanica var. pirotensis

Potentilla astracanica var. pirotensis, commonly known as the Pirot Peninsula potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, woody stems and serrated, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
