Piper flavicans

Piper flavicans, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, yellow fruits. These fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
