Pyrus vajdae

Pyrus vajdae, commonly known as the Vajda pear, is a rare and little-studied species of pear native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, rugged fruit with a thick, sometimes hairy skin. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pyrus vajdae is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
