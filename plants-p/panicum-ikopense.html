Panicum ikopense

Panicum ikopense, commonly known as the Nigerian millet, is a grass species native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soil conditions.

Its seeds are used as a traditional food source in some African communities.
