Puccinellia fasciculata var. pseudodistans

Puccinellia fasciculata var. pseudodistans, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dunes. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are closely arranged along the stem, giving it a dense appearance.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
