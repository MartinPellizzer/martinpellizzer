Persicaria brittingeri

Persicaria brittingeri, commonly known as the Brittinger's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Persicaria brittingeri is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and blooms, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
