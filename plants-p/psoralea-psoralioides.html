Psoralea psoralioides

Psoralea psoralioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
