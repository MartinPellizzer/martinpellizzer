Petiveria hexandria

Petiveria hexandria, commonly known as the Mexican mint or Mexican marigold, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and herbal remedies.
