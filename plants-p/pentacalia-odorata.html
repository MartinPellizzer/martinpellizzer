Pentacalia odorata

Pentacalia odorata, commonly known as the fragrant pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb with slender stems and opposite leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its fragrance is a notable feature, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
