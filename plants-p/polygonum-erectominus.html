Polygonum erectominus

Polygonum erectominus, commonly known as the upright knotweed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are typically ovate with a distinctive veining pattern, and the plant can grow up to 1 meter in height.

Polygonum erectominus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
