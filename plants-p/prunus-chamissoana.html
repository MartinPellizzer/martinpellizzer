Prunus chamissoana

Prunus chamissoana, commonly known as the Chilean white cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of Chile and Argentina, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Prunus chamissoana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in suitable regions.
