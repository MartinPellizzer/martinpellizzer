Picris atlantica

Picris atlantica, commonly known as Atlantic wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Picris atlantica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
