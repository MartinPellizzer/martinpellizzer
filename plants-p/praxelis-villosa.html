Praxelis villosa

Praxelis villosa, commonly known as the hairy praxelis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Praxelis villosa is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
