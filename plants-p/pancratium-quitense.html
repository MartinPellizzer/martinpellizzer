Pancratium quitense

Pancratium quitense, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy yellow flower at the top. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

The flower blooms in late summer to early autumn, adding a dramatic touch to the landscape.
