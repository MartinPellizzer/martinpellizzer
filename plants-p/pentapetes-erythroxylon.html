Pentapetes erythroxylon

Pentapetes erythroxylon, commonly known as the redwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red to brown bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
