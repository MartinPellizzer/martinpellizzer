Pithecoctenium vitalba f. intermedium

Pithecoctenium vitalba f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate form of the common nettle, is a variant of the species with distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas. The plant features serrated leaves and produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikes. Its stems are covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system.
