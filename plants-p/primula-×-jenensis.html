Primula × jenensis

Primula × jenensis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Primula vulgaris and Primula auricula. The plant typically produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive greenish or purplish throat. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy surface, and the plant prefers cool, moist environments.
