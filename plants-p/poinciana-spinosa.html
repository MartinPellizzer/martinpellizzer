Poinciana spinosa

Poinciana spinosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange feathery flowers that appear in the summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Poinciana spinosa is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
