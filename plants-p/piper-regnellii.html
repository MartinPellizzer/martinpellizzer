Piper regnellii

Piper regnellii, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, curved fruits that contain numerous tiny, peppercorn-like seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often grown in shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
