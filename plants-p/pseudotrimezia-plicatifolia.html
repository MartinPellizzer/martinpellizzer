Pseudotrimezia plicatifolia

Pseudotrimezia plicatifolia, commonly known as the pleated fern, is a unique member of the fern family with distinctive, pleated fronds.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's fronds are characterized by their intricate, overlapping patterns that give it a textured appearance. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
