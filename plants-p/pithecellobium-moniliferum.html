Pithecellobium moniliferum

Pithecellobium moniliferum, commonly known as the clusterpod or coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its clustered, pod-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its root system is often used in traditional medicine, and it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The species is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
