Polystachya rigidula

Polystachya rigidula, commonly known as the rigid clubmoss, is a member of the family Polystachyaceae.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has whorled leaves that are stiff and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its reproductive structures consist of small, cone-like strobili that produce spores.

Polystachya rigidula is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in forest understory ecosystems.
