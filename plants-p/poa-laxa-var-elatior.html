Poa laxa var. elatior

Poa laxa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of woodlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

Poa laxa var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators and soil stabilization.
