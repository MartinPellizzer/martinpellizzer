Phaiophleps acaulis

Phaiophleps acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its sturdy, unbranched stem that is often leafless except for a few basal leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
