Podanthum canescens

Podanthum canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf podanthum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its succulent leaves are adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Podanthum canescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
