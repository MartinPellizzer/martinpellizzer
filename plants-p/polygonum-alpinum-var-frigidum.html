Polygonum alpinum var. frigidum

Polygonum alpinum var. frigidum, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variant is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resilience to cold and strong winds.
