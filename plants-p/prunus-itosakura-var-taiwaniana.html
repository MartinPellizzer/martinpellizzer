Prunus itosakura var. taiwaniana

Prunus itosakura var. taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan cherry or Taiwan peach tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red-orange cherries that are similar in appearance to Prunus persica.

It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
