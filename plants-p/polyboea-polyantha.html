Polyboea polyantha

Polyboea polyantha, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, flat seeds.
