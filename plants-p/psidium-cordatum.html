Psidium cordatum

Psidium cordatum, commonly known as the passionfruit vine, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive flowers with a complex structure. The plant is known for its fragrant, five-petaled flowers that are often associated with religious symbolism. Its fruit, which is typically orange or red in color, contains numerous seeds and is widely cultivated for its edible pulp.

The botanical features of Psidium cordatum make it an important species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
