Paronychia capitata var. imbricata

Paronychia capitata var. imbricata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense, showy heads. This plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
