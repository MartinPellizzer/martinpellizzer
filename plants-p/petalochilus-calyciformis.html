Petalochilus calyciformis

Petalochilus calyciformis, commonly known as the calyx-shaped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a calyx-like appearance in its floral bracts. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a variety of colors ranging from white to pink.

Petalochilus calyciformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
