Paraphlomis rugosa var. coronata

Paraphlomis rugosa var. coronata, commonly known as the crowned parrot's bill, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin and a pale green to bluish-green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in spikes, typically in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both horticultural and ecological studies.
