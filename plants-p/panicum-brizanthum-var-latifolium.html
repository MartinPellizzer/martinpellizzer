Panicum brizanthum var. latifolium

Panicum brizanthum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat blades and a tufted growth habit, making it a distinctive feature in native grasslands. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowering heads are cylindrical and produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for wildlife.
