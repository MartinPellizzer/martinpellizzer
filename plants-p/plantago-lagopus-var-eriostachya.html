Plantago lagopus var. eriostachya

Plantago lagopus var. eriostachya, commonly known as the Arctic plantain, is a variant of the common plantain that thrives in cold, arctic environments.

It is characterized by its rosette growth habit and silver-green leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces a tall, slender flowering stalk topped with a dense cluster of small, pale green flowers. It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

This variant is often found in tundra and alpine regions across the northern hemisphere.
