Palaquium gutta f. vrieseanum

Palaquium gutta f. vrieseanum, commonly known as the Vrieseanum gutta, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
