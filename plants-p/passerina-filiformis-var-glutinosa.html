Passerina filiformis var. glutinosa

Passerina filiformis var. glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky threadleaf, is a variant of the threadleaf plant characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves.

The leaves are covered in a sticky substance, which is a notable botanical feature of this variety. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its reduced leaf surface. The plant belongs to the Passerina genus, which is part of the Amaranthaceae family.

Its unique morphology and adaptation strategies make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
