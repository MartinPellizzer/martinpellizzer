Pistacia atlantica subsp. cabulica

Pistacia atlantica subsp.

cabulica, commonly known as Persian ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, fissured bark and small, leathery leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, resinous fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of gum. It is adapted to harsh environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
