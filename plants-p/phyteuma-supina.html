Phyteuma supina

Phyteuma supina, commonly known as the lying phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pale blue to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Phyteuma supina is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
