Portulaca multistaminata

Portulaca multistaminata, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant produces numerous stamens, giving it the species name "multistaminata," and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The flowers typically bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
