Piper pseudorubeba

Piper pseudorubeba, commonly known as the purple pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, purple flowers that give way to reddish-brown berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The berries are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though the plant is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes.
