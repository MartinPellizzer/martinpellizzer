Potamogeton lucens var. longifolius

Potamogeton lucens var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It features narrow, linear leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, giving it a distinctive appearance in water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters near the water surface. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and healthy wetland habitats.
