Pluchea eyrea var. major

Pluchea eyrea var. major, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. The plant features small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
