Plantago monosperma subsp. discolor

Plantago monosperma subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the white plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, demonstrating a degree of adaptability to different environmental conditions.
