Pothomorphe peltata var. hypoleuca

Pothomorphe peltata var. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-veined pothomorphe, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
