Pristimera celastroides

Pristimera celastroides, commonly known as the Chinese ghost pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique fruiting habit.
