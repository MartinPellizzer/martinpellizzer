Pyrrosia tonkinensis

Pyrrosia tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery fronds that often form a compact rosette. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Pyrrosia tonkinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in horticulture.
