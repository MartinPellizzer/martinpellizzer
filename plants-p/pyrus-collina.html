Pyrus collina

Pyrus collina, commonly known as the Chilean white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its small, white flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

Pyrus collina is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
