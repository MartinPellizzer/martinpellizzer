Prunus jamasakura f. glandulifera

Prunus jamasakura f. glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which give it its specific epithet. The plant is known for its delicate pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice in horticultural settings.
