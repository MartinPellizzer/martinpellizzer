Phalaroides arundinacea subsp. rotgesii

Phalaroides arundinacea subsp.

rotgesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved jointvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, ascending stems with small, lanceolate leaves that are usually glabrous. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are typically pale green to yellowish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow leaves and is often distinguished from other members of the genus by its specific morphological traits.
