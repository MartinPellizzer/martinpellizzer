Phyllanthus boehmii var. humilis

Phyllanthus boehmii var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Indian cheeseplant, is a low-growing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along its stems. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule containing several small, yellowish seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
