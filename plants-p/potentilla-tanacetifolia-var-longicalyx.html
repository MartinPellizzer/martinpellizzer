Potentilla tanacetifolia var. longicalyx

Potentilla tanacetifolia var. longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens with a preference for dry, sunny conditions.
