Pollinia birmanica

Pollinia birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique pollination mechanism. The plant produces specialized structures called pollinia, which are masses of pollen grains that adhere to the insect's body. These pollinia are typically found in clusters and are adapted for efficient transfer between flowers.

Pollinia birmanica is valued for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
