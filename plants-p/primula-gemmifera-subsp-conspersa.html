Primula gemmifera subsp. conspersa

Primula gemmifera subsp.

conspersa, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, hairy stems that bear oval, dark green leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for alpine and rock garden settings.
