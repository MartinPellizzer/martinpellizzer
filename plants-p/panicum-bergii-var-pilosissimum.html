Panicum bergii var. pilosissimum

Panicum bergii var. pilosissimum, commonly known as silky finger grass, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and stems, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
