Prunus × keredjensis

Prunus × keredjensis, commonly known as the Keredjensky cherry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir ranges. The plant is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it produces white or pink flowers in early spring.

The Keredjensky cherry is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential as a fruit crop in suitable climates.
