Piper nitidulum

Piper nitidulum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
