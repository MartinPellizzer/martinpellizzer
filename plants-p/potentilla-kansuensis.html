Potentilla kansuensis

Potentilla kansuensis, commonly known as the Kansu cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid regions.

Potentilla kansuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
