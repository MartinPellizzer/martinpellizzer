Pilea purpurella

Pilea purpurella, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.

Pilea purpurella is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
