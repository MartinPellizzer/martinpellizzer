Pyrus obsoletidentata

Pyrus obsoletidentata, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
