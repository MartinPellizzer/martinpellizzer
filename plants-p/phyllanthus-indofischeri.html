Phyllanthus indofischeri

Phyllanthus indofischeri, commonly known as the Indian false nutmeg, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by distinctive three-lobed capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
