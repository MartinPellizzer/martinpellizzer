Pandanus kuepferi

Pandanus kuepferi, commonly known as the Kuepfer's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are fibrous and can be woven into mats and other utilitarian items.

Pandanus kuepferi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
