Pilosella peteriana

Pilosella peteriana, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy, grass-like leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Pilosella peteriana is a hardy perennial that is adapted to cold climates and is often used in alpine gardens for its ornamental value.
