Phyllanthus inaequaliflorus

Phyllanthus inaequaliflorus, commonly known as the unequal-flowered phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
