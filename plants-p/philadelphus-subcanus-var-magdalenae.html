Philadelphus subcanus var. magdalenae

Philadelphus subcanus var. magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalene Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy underside, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
