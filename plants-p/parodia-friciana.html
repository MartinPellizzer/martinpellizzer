Parodia friciana

Parodia friciana, commonly known as the Friciana cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and ribbed structure. The plant features white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
