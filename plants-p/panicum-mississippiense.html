Panicum mississippiense

Panicum mississippiense, commonly known as Mississippi panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 feet. The plant produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that are often visible in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are narrow and have a blue-green hue, which distinguishes it from other grasses in the Panicum genus.

This species is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wetland areas.
