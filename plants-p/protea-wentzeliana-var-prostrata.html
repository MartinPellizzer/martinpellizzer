Protea wentzeliana var. prostrata

Protea wentzeliana var. prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has leathery, narrow leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the region's flora.
