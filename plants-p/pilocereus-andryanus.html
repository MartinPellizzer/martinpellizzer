Pilocereus andryanus

Pilocereus andryanus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is often grafted onto a different cactus species for structural support. Its botanical name reflects its association with the genus Pilocereus, which includes many night-blooming cacti.

This plant is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
