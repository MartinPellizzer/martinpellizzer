Pinus cembroides var. llaveana

Pinus cembroides var. llaveana, commonly known as the Laveana mountain pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a rugged, drought-resistant morphology adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and extreme temperatures.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
