Panicum longisetum

Panicum longisetum, commonly known as the long-haired panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, hairy leaves that give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Panicum longisetum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environments.
