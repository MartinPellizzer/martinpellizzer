Palaquium acuminatum

Palaquium acuminatum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a round, segmented berry that turns from green to reddish when mature. It is valued for its edible, sweet and tangy pulp, which contains high levels of antioxidants.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
