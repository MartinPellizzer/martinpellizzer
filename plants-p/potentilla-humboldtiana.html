Potentilla humboldtiana

Potentilla humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features wiry, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

This species is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
