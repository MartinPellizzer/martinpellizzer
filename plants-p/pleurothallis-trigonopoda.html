Pleurothallis trigonopoda

Pleurothallis trigonopoda, commonly known as the three-angled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant features elongated, narrow pseudobulbs and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually clustered and covered in a fine, velvety pubescence.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
