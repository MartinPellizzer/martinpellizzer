Polygala karensium var. obcordata

Polygala karensium var. obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant restoration projects.
