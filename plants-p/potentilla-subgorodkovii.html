Potentilla subgorodkovii

Potentilla subgorodkovii, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval-shaped and slightly toothed.

Potentilla subgorodkovii is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
