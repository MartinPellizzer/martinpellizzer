Pimelea calcicola

Pimelea calcicola, commonly known as the limestone pimelea, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a member of the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
