Pelargonium dondiifolium

Pelargonium dondiifolium, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and summer months. It is a compact shrub that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
