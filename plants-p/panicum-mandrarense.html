Panicum mandrarense

Panicum mandrarense, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive red or purple inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Panicum mandrarense is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
