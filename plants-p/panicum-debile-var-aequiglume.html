Panicum debile var. aequiglume

Panicum debile var. aequiglume, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a low-growing grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a slightly hairy surface. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to moist, sandy soils and its use in ornamental landscaping.
