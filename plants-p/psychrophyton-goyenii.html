Psychrophyton goyenii

Psychrophyton goyenii, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in the harsh conditions of the continent's cold and dry climate. The plant has small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It produces tiny white flowers that bloom during the brief Antarctic summer.

Psychrophyton goyenii plays a vital role in the fragile ecosystem of the region, contributing to soil stabilization and providing food for local invertebrates.
