Parastyrax macrophyllus

Parastyrax macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf parastyrax, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its botanical classification places it within the family Lauraceae, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
