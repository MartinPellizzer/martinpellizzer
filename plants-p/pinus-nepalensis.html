Pinus nepalensis

Pinus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in fascicles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a thin, papery wing. It thrives in temperate climates with high altitude and well-drained soils.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
