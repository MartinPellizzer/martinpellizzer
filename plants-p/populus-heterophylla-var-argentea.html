Populus heterophylla var. argentea

Populus heterophylla var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

This plant is native to parts of Asia and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the bark is smooth and gray in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
