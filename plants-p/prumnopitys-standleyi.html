Prumnopitys standleyi

Prumnopitys standleyi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in clusters. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability in construction.
