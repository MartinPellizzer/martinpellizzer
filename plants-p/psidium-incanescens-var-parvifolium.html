Psidium incanescens var. parvifolium

Psidium incanescens var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish bark and elliptical, narrow leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
