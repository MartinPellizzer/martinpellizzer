Poa sublanata var. vivipara

Poa sublanata var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus Poa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. One of its notable features is the production of viviparous shoots, which can develop into small plants while still attached to the parent plant.

Its botanical structure supports its adaptation to temperate climates and contributes to its ecological role in various habitats.
