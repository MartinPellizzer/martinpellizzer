Paphiopedilum armeniacum var. undulatum

Paphiopedilum armeniacum var. undulatum, commonly known as the undulating lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This variant is characterized by its unique, undulating pouch-shaped lip, which is a defining feature of the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid with thick, fleshy roots adapted to its rocky or forested habitats. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with intricate patterns that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its conservation.
