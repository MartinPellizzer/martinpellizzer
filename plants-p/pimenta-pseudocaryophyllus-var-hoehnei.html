Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. hoehnei

Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. hoehnei, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, red, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture, while its flowers are small and white, clustered in panicles.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
