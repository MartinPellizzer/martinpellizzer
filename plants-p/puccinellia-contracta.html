Puccinellia contracta

Puccinellia contracta, commonly known as the narrowleaf saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a common component of salt desert ecosystems.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important forage plant for livestock in dryland environments.
