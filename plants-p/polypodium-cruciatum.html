Polypodium cruciatum

Polypodium cruciatum, commonly known as the cross-ferns, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cross-shaped sori that are arranged in a unique pattern on the underside of its fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its fronds are usually slender and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

Polypodium cruciatum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid conditions.
