Polygonum janatae

Polygonum janatae, commonly known as the Indian knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and distinctive leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by bracts.

Polygonum janatae is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
