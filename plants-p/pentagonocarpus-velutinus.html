Pentagonocarpus velutinus

Pentagonocarpus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of food.
