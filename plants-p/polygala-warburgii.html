Polygala warburgii

Polygala warburgii, commonly known as the Warburg's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

It is often found in open vegetation types such as savannas and shrublands.
