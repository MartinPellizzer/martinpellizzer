Persica vulgaris var. alba

Persica vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white peach, is a variety of peach characterized by its pale yellow to white flesh and mild flavor.

It is a deciduous tree that produces small, smooth, white or yellow fruits with a fuzzy skin. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Asia, particularly China. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically pink to white, and they bloom in the spring before the fruit develops.
