Potentilla × piersii

Potentilla × piersii, commonly known as the evergold shrub, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and vibrant golden-yellow foliage that remains colorful throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
