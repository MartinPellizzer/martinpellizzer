Pseuderanthemum fruticosum

Pseuderanthemum fruticosum, commonly known as the creeping pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum fruticosum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to humid environments.
