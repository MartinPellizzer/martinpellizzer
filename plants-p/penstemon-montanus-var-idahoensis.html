Penstemon montanus var. idahoensis

Penstemon montanus var. idahoensis, commonly known as the Idaho beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces tall spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
