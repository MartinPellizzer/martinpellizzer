Polygonum lugdunense

Polygonum lugdunense, commonly known as the Lyon's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Polygonum lugdunense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
