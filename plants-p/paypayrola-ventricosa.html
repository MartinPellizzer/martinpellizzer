Paypayrola ventricosa

Paypayrola ventricosa, commonly known as the balloon flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its inflated, balloon-like seed pods that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, white or yellow flowers in the spring.

Paypayrola ventricosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
