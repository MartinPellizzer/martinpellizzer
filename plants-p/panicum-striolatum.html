Panicum striolatum

Panicum striolatum, commonly known as silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often lined with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue-gray florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Panicum striolatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
