Phlomoides umbrosa

Phlomoides umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pale blue to purple.

It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to thrive in low-light conditions.
