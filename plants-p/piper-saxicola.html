Piper saxicola

Piper saxicola, commonly known as the rock pepper or stone pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper saxicola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
