Pectis purpurea

Pectis purpurea, commonly known as the purple pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Pectis purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
