Pellaea regnelliana

Pellaea regnelliana, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a silvery appearance due to the presence of trichomes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and the plant reproduces through spores rather than seeds.

Pellaea regnelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
