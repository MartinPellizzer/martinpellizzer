Peucedanum wildemanianum

Peucedanum wildemanianum, commonly known as wildeman's angelica, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. Its roots are often harvested for their medicinal properties, containing compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
