Peperomia glabella var. caulibarbis

Peperomia glabella var. caulibarbis, commonly known as the woolly peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, velvety foliage with a distinctive waxy texture, and its leaves are often covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
