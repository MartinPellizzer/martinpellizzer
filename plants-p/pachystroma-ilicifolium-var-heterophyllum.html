Pachystroma ilicifolium var. heterophyllum

Pachystroma ilicifolium var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable holly-leaf pachystroma, is a rare and distinctive shrub native to specific regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leaves that vary in shape and size, ranging from narrow, holly-like leaves to broader, more rounded ones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade, making it a unique addition to specialized gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
