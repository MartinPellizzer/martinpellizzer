Panicum lineare var. mississippiensis

Panicum lineare var. mississippiensis, commonly known as the Mississippi sandreed, is a native grass species of the United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact, panicle-like inflorescences. This plant is typically found in sandy or well-drained soils, often in wetland or marsh environments. It is well adapted to coastal and tidal areas, making it a valuable species for erosion control.

The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread and survival in various habitats.
