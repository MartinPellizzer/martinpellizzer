Parodia thionantha

Parodia thionantha, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has a unique appearance due to its grafted base, which is typically a different species, such as Gymnocalycium, providing the necessary root system. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
