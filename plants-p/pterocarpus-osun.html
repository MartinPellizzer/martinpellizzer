Pterocarpus osun

Pterocarpus osun, commonly known as African rosewood, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in the furniture and musical instrument industries. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
