Persea amplexicaulis

Persea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the emerald ash borer, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green.

The fruit is a drupe, which contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish pulp.
