Piper henryei

Piper henryei, commonly known as Henry's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Piper henryei is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
