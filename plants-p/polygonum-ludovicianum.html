Polygonum ludovicianum

Polygonum ludovicianum, commonly known as the redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, streambanks, and prairies. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish underneath, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red. Its stems are reddish and often have a distinct, woody base.

Polygonum ludovicianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
