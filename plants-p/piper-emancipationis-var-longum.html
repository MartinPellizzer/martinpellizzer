Piper emancipationis var. longum

Piper emancipationis var. longum, commonly known as the long-fruited pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper emancipationis species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that hang in clusters, often reaching lengths of up to 15 centimeters. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil, typically growing as a vine or shrub. Its leaves are broad and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, white flowers that develop into the distinctive long fruits.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor profile, which combines spicy and citrus notes, making it a sought-after ingredient in regional cuisines.
