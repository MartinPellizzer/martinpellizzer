Pseudoprosopis fischeri

Pseudoprosopis fischeri, commonly known as the Fisher's mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, deciduous leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is well adapted to sandy or rocky soils and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

Its deep root system allows it to access groundwater, making it a hardy component of desert ecosystems.
