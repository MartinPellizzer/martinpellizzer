Potentilla crantzii subsp. serpentini

Potentilla crantzii subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as serpent's root, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America. It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

The plant has slender, woody stems and hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
