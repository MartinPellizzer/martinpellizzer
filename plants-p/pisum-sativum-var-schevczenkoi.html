Pisum sativum var. schevczenkoi

Pisum sativum var. schevczenkoi, commonly known as the Ukrainian pea, is a variety of the common pea characterized by its unique botanical features.

It typically produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant is an annual legume with compound leaves and tendrils that aid in climbing. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its genetic lineage and specific morphological traits that set it apart from other pea varieties.
