Physalodes physalodes

Physalodes physalodes, commonly known as the Jerusalem artichoke, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its edible tubers that resemble ginger in texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by seed heads that disperse its seeds by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its potential use in sustainable agriculture.
