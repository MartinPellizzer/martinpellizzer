Pentanema verbascifolium

Pentanema verbascifolium, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
