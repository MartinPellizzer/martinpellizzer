Prunus communis var. domestica

Prunus communis var. domestica, commonly known as the domestic cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from red to black. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is used in culinary and commercial applications. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Prunus communis var. domestica is also cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to various climates.
