Polystachya melliodora

Polystachya melliodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like aroma. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple racemes of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and botanical research.
