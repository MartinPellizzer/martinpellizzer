Potentilla rigida

Potentilla rigida, commonly known as hardy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with five oval leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla rigida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
