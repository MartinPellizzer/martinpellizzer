Pyrus major

Pyrus major, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its bark is often fissured and becomes flaky with age, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Pyrus major is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
