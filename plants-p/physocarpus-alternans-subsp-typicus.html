Physocarpus alternans subsp. typicus

Physocarpus alternans subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged, with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, clustered white to pink flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color in the fall.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal, hardiness, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
