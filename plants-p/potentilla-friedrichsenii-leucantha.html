Potentilla friedrichsenii leucantha

Potentilla friedrichsenii leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers with five petals, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
