Parahebe lithophila

Parahebe lithophila, commonly known as the rock parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub that typically thrives in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense, foliage cover.

Parahebe lithophila is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
