Pseudopilocereus hapalacanthus

Pseudopilocereus hapalacanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It has a shallow root system and requires minimal water, making it drought-tolerant.

Its growth is typically slow, and it thrives in well-draining soil.
