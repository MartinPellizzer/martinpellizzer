Pittosporum tobira

Pittosporum tobira, commonly known as the sweet box, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It features fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile plant for landscaping.
