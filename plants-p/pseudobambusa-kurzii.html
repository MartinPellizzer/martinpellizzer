Pseudobambusa kurzii

Pseudobambusa kurzii, commonly known as the Kuru bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to eastern India and is characterized by its slender, green culms that grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in panicles during the summer months. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a slight waxy coating.

Pseudobambusa kurzii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
