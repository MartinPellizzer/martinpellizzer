Paspalum ovatum var. parviflorum

Paspalum ovatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
