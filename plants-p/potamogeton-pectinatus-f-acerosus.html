Potamogeton pectinatus f. acerosus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. acerosus, commonly known as the rigid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its stiff, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and light penetration.
