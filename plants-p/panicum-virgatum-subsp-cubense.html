Panicum virgatum subsp. cubense

Panicum virgatum subsp.

cubense, commonly known as switchgrass, is a native North American grass species characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery plumes. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is highly adaptable to different environmental conditions.

The plant is known for its drought tolerance and ability to regrow from its root system after disturbances. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization and erosion control.

It is widely used in ecological restoration and as a renewable energy source due to its high biomass yield.
