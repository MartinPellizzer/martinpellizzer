Polygonum paucifoliatum

Polygonum paucifoliatum, commonly known as the few-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wetlands, and roadside ditches. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple. Its leaves are narrow, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum paucifoliatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
