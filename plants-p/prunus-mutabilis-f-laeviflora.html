Prunus mutabilis f. laeviflora

Prunus mutabilis f. laeviflora, commonly known as the white-flowered cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
