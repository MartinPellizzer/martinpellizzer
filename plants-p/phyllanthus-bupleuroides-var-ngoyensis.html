Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. ngoyensis

Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. ngoyensis, commonly known as the Ngoyensis phyllanthus, is a shrub native to central Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
