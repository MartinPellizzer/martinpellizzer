Pilogyne

Pilogyne, commonly known as the silky glume grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Pilogyne is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open fields or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common component of native grassland ecosystems.
