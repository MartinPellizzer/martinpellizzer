Piper praeacutilimbum

Piper praeacutilimbum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its fruit, which contains a single seed, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
