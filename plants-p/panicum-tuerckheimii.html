Panicum tuerckheimii

Panicum tuerckheimii, commonly known as Tuerckheim's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
