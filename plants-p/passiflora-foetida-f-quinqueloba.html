Passiflora foetida f. quinqueloba

Passiflora foetida f. quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed passionflower, is a variant of the passionvine genus native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, complex flowers with five prominent lobes on the corona, which are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.

The foliage is heart-shaped with a rough texture, adding to its visual appeal in gardens.
