Punica granatum var. flava

Punica granatum var. flava, commonly known as the yellow pomegranate, is a variant of the pomegranate plant characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange fruit.

This variety is native to the Middle East and South Asia, where it has been cultivated for centuries. The fruit is smaller than the typical red pomegranate and has a more acidic flavor. It is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its high antioxidant content.

The plant thrives in warm, arid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
