Populus argentea

Populus argentea, commonly known as silver poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its white-veined, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common sight in riparian zones.
