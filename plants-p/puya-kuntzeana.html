Puya kuntzeana

Puya kuntzeana, commonly known as the Kuntze's puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces a tall inflorescence bearing showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple band. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
