Passiflora pinnatifolia

Passiflora pinnatifolia, commonly known as the pinnate passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Passiflora pinnatifolia is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators and its potential medicinal properties.
