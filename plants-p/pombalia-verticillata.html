Pombalia verticillata

Pombalia verticillata, commonly known as the whorled pombalia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with multiple stems emerging from a single base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Pombalia verticillata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
