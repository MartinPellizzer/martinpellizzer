Piper durilignum

Piper durilignum, commonly known as the hard-wooded pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

Piper durilignum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
