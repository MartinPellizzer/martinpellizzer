Panicum reversipilum

Panicum reversipilum, commonly known as the reverse-hair grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of loosely arranged spikelets that often curve backward, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Panicum reversipilum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
