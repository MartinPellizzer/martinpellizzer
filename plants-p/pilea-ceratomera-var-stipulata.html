Pilea ceratomera var. stipulata

Pilea ceratomera var. stipulata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are often described as resembling silver coins, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, upright stem with dense foliage and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care, making it a popular houseplant.
