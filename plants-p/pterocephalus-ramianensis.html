Pterocephalus ramianensis

Pterocephalus ramianensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
