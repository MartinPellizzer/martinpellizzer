Phyllagathis gigantifolia

Phyllagathis gigantifolia, commonly known as giant leathery moss or giant phyllagathis, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a perennial evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Phyllagathis gigantifolia is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
