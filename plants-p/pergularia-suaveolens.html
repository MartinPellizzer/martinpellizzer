Pergularia suaveolens

Pergularia suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented beggar's-ticks, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant has slender, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is known for its mild, fragrant aroma, which is released when the leaves are crushed.

Pergularia suaveolens is often found in moist, shaded areas and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
