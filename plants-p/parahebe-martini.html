Parahebe martini

Parahebe martini, commonly known as the silverleaf parahebe, is a perennial shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
