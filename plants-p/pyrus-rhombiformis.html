Pyrus rhombiformis

Pyrus rhombiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, rhomboid-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Pyrus rhombiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
