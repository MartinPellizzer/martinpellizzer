Pyrus bucharica subsp. daschtidshumica

Pyrus bucharica subsp.

daschtidshumica, commonly known as the Dashtidshum pear, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The fruit is typically small, with a thin skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique adaptation to arid environments.
