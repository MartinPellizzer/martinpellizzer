Pyrethrum pseudanthemis

Pyrethrum pseudanthemis, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow ray florets and purple disk florets. The plant produces small, spherical flower heads that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight hairy texture.

Pyrethrum pseudanthemis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insecticidal properties.
