Phyllodoce decipiens

Phyllodoce decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive phyllodoce, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in early spring.

This species is adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
