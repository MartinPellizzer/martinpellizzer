Polygala vulgaris var. obtusifolia

Polygala vulgaris var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported tonic properties.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
