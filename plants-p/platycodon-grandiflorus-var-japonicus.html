Platycodon grandiflorus var. japonicus

Platycodon grandiflorus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features robust, upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical and have a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a bell or a tube.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
