Piper costaricense

Piper costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
