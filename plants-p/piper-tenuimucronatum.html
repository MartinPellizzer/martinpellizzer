Piper tenuimucronatum

Piper tenuimucronatum, commonly known as the slender-tipped pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of Piperaceae and its ecological roles.
