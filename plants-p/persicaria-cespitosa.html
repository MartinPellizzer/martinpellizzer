Persicaria cespitosa

Persicaria cespitosa, commonly known as redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 30 inches tall, featuring alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a reddish tinge. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and disturbed sites.

Persicaria cespitosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
