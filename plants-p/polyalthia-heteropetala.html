Polyalthia heteropetala

Polyalthia heteropetala, commonly known as the variable flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland areas with well-drained soil.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
