Primula × pruhonicensis

Primula × pruhonicensis, commonly known as the Puhonice primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is native to the Czech Republic and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is primarily found in its native habitat.
