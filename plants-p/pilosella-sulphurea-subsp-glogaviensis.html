Pilosella sulphurea subsp. glogaviensis

Pilosella sulphurea subsp.

glogaviensis, commonly known as the yellow globeflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence composed of numerous small, yellowish to golden flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are generally basal, forming a rosette, and are covered with dense, white or grayish hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
