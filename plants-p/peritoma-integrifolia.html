Peritoma integrifolia

Peritoma integrifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf peritoma, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are arranged oppositely along the stem.

Peritoma integrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
