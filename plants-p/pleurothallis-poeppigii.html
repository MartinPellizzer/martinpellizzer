Pleurothallis poeppigii

Pleurothallis poeppigii, commonly known as the Poeppig's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often found in tropical cloud forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
