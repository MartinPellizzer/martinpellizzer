Portlandia speciosa

Portlandia speciosa, commonly known as the giant pincushion plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow to orange flower heads that resemble pincushions, surrounded by dense, woolly foliage. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh coastal climate with strong winds and salt spray. It produces small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation along the coastline.

Portlandia speciosa is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
