Piper mutabile

Piper mutabile, commonly known as the variable pepper or the variable pipevine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique variability in leaf shape and coloration makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
