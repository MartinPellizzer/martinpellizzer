Prunus grandissima

Prunus grandissima, commonly known as the sugar maple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and orange in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Prunus grandissima is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
