Pithecellobium lindseifolium

Pithecellobium lindseifolium, commonly known as the Lindseifolia or Lindseifoliate acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in a pinnate pattern. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes and are followed by elongated, bean-like pods.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
