Pinguicula hirtiflora var. decipiens

Pinguicula hirtiflora var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive hairy butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often mistaken for flowers of similar species, hence its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it a unique and intriguing subject for both horticultural enthusiasts and scientific study.
