Polyscias tricochleata

Polyscias tricochleata, commonly known as the broadleaf sycamore, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
