Puccinellia asperifolia

Puccinellia asperifolia, commonly known as roughleaf saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its wiry, often hairy stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Puccinellia asperifolia plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
