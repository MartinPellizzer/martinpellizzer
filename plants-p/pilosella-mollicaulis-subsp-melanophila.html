Pilosella mollicaulis subsp. melanophila

Pilosella mollicaulis subsp.

melanophila, commonly known as the black-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features small, hairy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly velvety texture. Its flowers are usually yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
