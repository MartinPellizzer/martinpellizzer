Pyrola winkleriana

Pyrola winkleriana, commonly known as the Winkler's wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that remain throughout the year, and its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically pink or white. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Pyrola winkleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
