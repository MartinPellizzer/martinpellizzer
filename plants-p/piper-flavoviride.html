Piper flavoviride

Piper flavoviride, commonly known as yellow-green pepper vine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that mature to a bright yellow or orange color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
