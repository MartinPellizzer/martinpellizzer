Psilochenia modocensis subsp. subacaulis

Psilochenia modocensis subsp.

subacaulis, commonly known as the Modoc Valley spikerush, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the Modoc Plateau in California. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This subspecies thrives in wetland and riparian habitats, often found in areas with seasonal flooding. Its morphology reflects adaptations to its specific ecological niche, including a robust root system for anchoring in saturated soils.
