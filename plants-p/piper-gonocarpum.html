Piper gonocarpum

Piper gonocarpum, commonly known as the Australian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests and along stream banks.
