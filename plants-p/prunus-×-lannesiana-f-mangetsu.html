Prunus × lannesiana f. mangetsu

Prunus × lannesiana f. mangetsu, commonly known as the Mangetsu cherry, is a unique ornamental cultivar prized for its striking floral display.

This variety is a hybrid of Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parent species. Its flowers are notable for their deep pink to almost white hues, appearing in clusters that create a dramatic visual effect. The plant is typically deciduous, with small, elliptical leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
