Prunus × pugetensis

Prunus × pugetensis, commonly known as the Washington hawthorn, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its visual interest throughout the growing season.
