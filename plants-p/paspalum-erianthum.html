Paspalum erianthum

Paspalum erianthum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray, making it a common species in estuarine habitats.

Its ability to form dense mats makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
