Phlox carolina var. angusta

Phlox carolina var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf false bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in early summer. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
