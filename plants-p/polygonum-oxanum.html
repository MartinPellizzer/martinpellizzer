Polygonum oxanum

Polygonum oxanum, commonly known as the Oxanum knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and jointed stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late summer.

Polygonum oxanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
