Peutalis linearis

Peutalis linearis, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring and early summer.

Peutalis linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
