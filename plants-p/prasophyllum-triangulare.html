Prasophyllum triangulare

Prasophyllum triangulare, commonly known as the triangle leek, is an evergreen perennial plant native to the temperate regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a spike, blooming in late winter to early spring. Prasophyllum triangulare thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
