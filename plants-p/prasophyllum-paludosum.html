Prasophyllum paludosum

Prasophyllum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp leek, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or seasonally waterlogged soils, thriving in environments with high moisture levels. It is a perennial herb that often forms dense mats, contributing to the vegetation structure of its wetland habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened in some regions, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
