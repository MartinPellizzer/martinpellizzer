Prostanthera patula

Prostanthera patula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mintbush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Prostanthera patula is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
