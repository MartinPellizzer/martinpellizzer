Ptelea isophylla

Ptelea isophylla, commonly known as the smoothleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically hairless. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for various bird species, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Ptelea isophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
