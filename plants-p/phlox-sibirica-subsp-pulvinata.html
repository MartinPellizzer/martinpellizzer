Phlox sibirica subsp. pulvinata

Phlox sibirica subsp.

pulvinata, commonly known as Siberian phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia. It features upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or borders.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
