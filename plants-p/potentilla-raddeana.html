Potentilla raddeana

Potentilla raddeana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are usually trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated at the edges.

Potentilla raddeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic value.
