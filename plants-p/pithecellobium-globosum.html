Pithecellobium globosum

Pithecellobium globosum, commonly known as the globular burr acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional plant use.
