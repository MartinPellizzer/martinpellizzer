Physalis arenicola var. ciliosa

Physalis arenicola var. ciliosa, commonly known as the hairy gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its hairy, spiny calyx that encloses the fruit, which resembles a small, inflated berry. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
