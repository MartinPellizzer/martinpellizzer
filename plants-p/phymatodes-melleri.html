Phymatodes melleri

Phymatodes melleri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a taproot system and produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are basal, alternate, and have a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The plant is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, making it a hardy species in desert ecosystems.
