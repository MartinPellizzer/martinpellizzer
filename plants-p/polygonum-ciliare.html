Polygonum ciliare

Polygonum ciliare, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are lance-shaped with silky, hairy surfaces that give the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in wet meadows.
