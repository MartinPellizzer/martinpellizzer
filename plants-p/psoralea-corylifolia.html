Psoralea corylifolia

Psoralea corylifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed psoralea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules and trifoliate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by pods that contain several seeds. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
