Polygala setacea

Polygala setacea, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, feathery leaves that give it its common name. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, arranged in small clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
