Petrosimonia monandra

Petrosimonia monandra, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of yellow to orange. The plant has succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.
