Primulina linearifolia

Primulina linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
