Polypodium petrarchae

Polypodium petrarchae, commonly known as the Petrarch's polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is a small, epiphytic fern that typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are usually less than 30 centimeters in length. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium petrarchae is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
