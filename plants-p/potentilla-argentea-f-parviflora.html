Potentilla argentea f. parviflora

Potentilla argentea f. parviflora, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer. This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has a low-growing habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage.

The subspecies parviflora is distinguished by its smaller flowers compared to the typical form of Potentilla argentea.
