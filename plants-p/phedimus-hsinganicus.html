Phedimus hsinganicus

Phedimus hsinganicus, commonly known as the Hsian stonecrop, is a low-growing succulent plant native to China.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
