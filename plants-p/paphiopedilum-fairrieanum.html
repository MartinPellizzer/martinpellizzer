Paphiopedilum fairrieanum

Paphiopedilum fairrieanum, commonly known as the Fairrie's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a unique feature among orchids. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
