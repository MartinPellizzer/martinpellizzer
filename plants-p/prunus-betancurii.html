Prunus betancurii

Prunus betancurii, commonly known as the Betancurii cherry, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, Prunus betancurii is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
