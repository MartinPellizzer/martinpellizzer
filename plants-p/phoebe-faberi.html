Phoebe faberi

Phoebe faberi, commonly known as the Chinese emerald ash borer, is a species of tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by dark, spherical fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
