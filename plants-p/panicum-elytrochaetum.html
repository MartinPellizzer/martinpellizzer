Panicum elytrochaetum

Panicum elytrochaetum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and hairy leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and natural ecosystems.
