Pseudorosularia sempervivoides

Pseudorosularia sempervivoides, commonly known as the rock rose, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette shape, adapted for water storage. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
