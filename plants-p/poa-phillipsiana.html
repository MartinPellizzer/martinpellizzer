Poa phillipsiana

Poa phillipsiana, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and coastal areas. Its flowering heads are small, spike-like clusters that produce light green to brown seeds.

Poa phillipsiana plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
