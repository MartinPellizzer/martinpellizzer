Phyllostachys mitis var. heterocycla

Phyllostachys mitis var. heterocycla, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black culms that contrast with its lighter, often greenish leaves. This plant is a clumping bamboo, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. Its foliage is narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a tendency to droop slightly at the tips.

The black bamboo is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
