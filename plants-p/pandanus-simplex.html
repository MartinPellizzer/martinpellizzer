Pandanus simplex

Pandanus simplex, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and culinary practices.

Pandanus simplex thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in dense stands along shorelines.
