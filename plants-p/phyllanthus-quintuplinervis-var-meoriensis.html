Phyllanthus quintuplinervis var. meoriensis

Phyllanthus quintuplinervis var. meoriensis, commonly known as the Meoriensis false crossbar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
