Puya warszewiczii

Puya warszewiczii, commonly known as the Andean grass of warshaw, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family, characterized by its rosette of long, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces tall, spire-like inflorescences bearing numerous small, spathaceous flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. Puya warszewiczii is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and is often found growing in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the study of Andean flora.
