Polygonum multangulare

Polygonum multangulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base, and it produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters. Its stems are usually reddish or purple, and they can become woody with age.

Polygonum multangulare is known for its adaptability and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
