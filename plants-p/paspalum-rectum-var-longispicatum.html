Paspalum rectum var. longispicatum

Paspalum rectum var. longispicatum, commonly known as the long-spined paspalum, is a grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and turfgrass settings.
