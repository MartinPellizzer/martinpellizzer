Plantago aundensis

Plantago aundensis, commonly known as the Indian plantain or narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Plantago aundensis is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its healing benefits.
