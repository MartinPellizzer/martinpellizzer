Piper nuncupatum

Piper nuncupatum, commonly known as the "false pepper" or "wild pepper," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species like black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are often hairy.

Piper nuncupatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
