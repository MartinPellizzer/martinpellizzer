Pombalia potosina var. pennellii

Pombalia potosina var. pennellii, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers range in color from white to purple.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
