Paeonia obovata var. alba

Paeonia obovata var. alba, commonly known as the white peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a member of the芍药 family, known for its medicinal and horticultural value.
