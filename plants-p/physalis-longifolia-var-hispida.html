Physalis longifolia var. hispida

Physalis longifolia var. hispida, commonly known as the rough-hairied groundcherry, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
