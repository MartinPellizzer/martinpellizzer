Phyllanthus chrysanthus var. deverdensis

Phyllanthus chrysanthus var. deverdensis, commonly known as the golden phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional medicine practices.
