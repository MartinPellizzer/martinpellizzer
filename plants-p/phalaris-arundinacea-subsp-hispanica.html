Phalaris arundinacea subsp. hispanica

Phalaris arundinacea subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as Spanish reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
