Pera coccinea

Pera coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered pera, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals that stand out against its green foliage. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
