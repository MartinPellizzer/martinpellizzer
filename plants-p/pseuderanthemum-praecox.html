Pseuderanthemum praecox

Pseuderanthemum praecox, commonly known as the early morning flower or false anthemum, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and typically have two lips, with the lower lip being more prominent.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
