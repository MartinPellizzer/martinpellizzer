Psidium hians

Psidium hians, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or hians guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries similar to guavas. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in various culinary applications.
