Phragmites communis var. isiacus

Phragmites communis var. isiacus, commonly known as the narrowleaf common reed, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. This variety thrives in brackish or saline water, making it a common inhabitant of coastal marshes and estuaries. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.

The plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by filtering water and supporting biodiversity.
