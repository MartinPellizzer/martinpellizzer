Phyllodes macrostachya

Phyllodes macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-leafed acacia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like structures called phyllodes, which are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, slender pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
