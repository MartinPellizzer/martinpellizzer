Pithecellobium bijugatum

Pithecellobium bijugatum, commonly known as the yellow pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with pairs of leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional benefits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
