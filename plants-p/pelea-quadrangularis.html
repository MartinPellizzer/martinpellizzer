Pelea quadrangularis

Pelea quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angle plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its square-shaped stems, which give it its scientific name. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically green with a slightly rough texture. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
