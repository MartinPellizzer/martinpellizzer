Piqueria ovata

Piqueria ovata, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers and hairy, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

Piqueria ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
