Paphiopedilum josianae

Paphiopedilum josianae, commonly known as the Josian's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch, which is a distinctive feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in the undergrowth. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and green hues, with a striking contrast between the petals and the velvety pouch.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and is highly sought after in the orchid trade.
