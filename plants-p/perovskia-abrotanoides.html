Perovskia abrotanoides

Perovskia abrotanoides, commonly known as Russian lavender or blue mist shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and showy, fragrant blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens. Its essential oils contain compounds like linalool and camphor, which contribute to its strong scent and medicinal uses.

Perovskia abrotanoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
