Peperomia carpapatana

Peperomia carpapatana, commonly known as the Carpathian peperomia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub with upright, branching stems and oval, waxy leaves that are often dark green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a suitable houseplant for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental foliage and adaptability, it is appreciated in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
