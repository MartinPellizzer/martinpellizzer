Potamogeton gramineus f. longipedunculatus

Potamogeton gramineus f. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stalked pondweed, is a variant of the pondweed species native to freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which bear clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater vegetation. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating at the water surface.

This subspecies plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
