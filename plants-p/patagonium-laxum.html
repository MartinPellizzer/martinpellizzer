Patagonium laxum

Patagonium laxum, commonly known as the Patagonian lax plant, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cold, high-altitude climate of the Patagonian region.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
