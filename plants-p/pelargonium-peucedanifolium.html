Pelargonium peucedanifolium

Pelargonium peucedanifolium, commonly known as the cow parsley geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are finely divided and resemble those of cow parsley, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
