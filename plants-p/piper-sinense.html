Piper sinense

Piper sinense, commonly known as the Chinese pepper or Vietnamese pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, three-lobed berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
