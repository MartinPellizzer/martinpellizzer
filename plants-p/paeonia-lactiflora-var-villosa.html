Paeonia lactiflora var. villosa

Paeonia lactiflora var. villosa, commonly known as the fuzzy peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or villose, stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges and emits a sweet, spicy fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
