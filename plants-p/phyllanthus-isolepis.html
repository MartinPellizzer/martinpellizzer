Phyllanthus isolepis

Phyllanthus isolepis, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, greenish fruits.

Phyllanthus isolepis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
