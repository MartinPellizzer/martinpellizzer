Picea mariana var. pendula-variegata

Picea mariana var. pendula-variegata, commonly known as the weeping white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce tree native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that create a cascading effect. The foliage is typically a mix of white and green, giving it a variegated appearance. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in boreal forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
