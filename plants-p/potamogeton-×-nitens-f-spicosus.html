Potamogeton × nitens f. spicosus

Potamogeton × nitens f. spicosus, commonly known as the spiked pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating at the water's surface or partially submerged.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
