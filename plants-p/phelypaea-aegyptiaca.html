Phelypaea aegyptiaca

Phelypaea aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian water clover, is a species of aquatic plant native to the Nile River region.

It is characterized by its dense network of floating, leaf-like structures that form a mat on the water surface. The plant has small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne on short stalks above the water. Its leaves are thin and broad, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in aquatic environments.

Phelypaea aegyptiaca plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems by reducing currents and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
