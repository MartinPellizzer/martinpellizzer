Plantago americana

Plantago americana, commonly known as American plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
