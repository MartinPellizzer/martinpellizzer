Primula conjugens var. conjugens

Primula conjugens var. conjugens, commonly known as the Pacific Coast primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
