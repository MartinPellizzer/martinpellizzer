Parietaria lusitanica subsp. serbica

Parietaria lusitanica subsp.

serbica, commonly known as the Serbica wall-rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and the Middle East. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in waste areas, and is often found in temperate climates. It has a hairy stem and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The subspecies serbica is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other subspecies of Parietaria lusitanica.
