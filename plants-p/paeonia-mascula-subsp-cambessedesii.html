Paeonia mascula subsp. cambessedesii

Paeonia mascula subsp.

cambessedesii, commonly known as the Cambessedes peony, is a rare and distinctive subspecies native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its large, showy, single or semi-double pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, spicy fragrance. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical conservation efforts.
