Podocarpus idenburgensis

Podocarpus idenburgensis, commonly known as the Idenburg's podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
