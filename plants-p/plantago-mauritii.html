Plantago mauritii

Plantago mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius plantain, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
