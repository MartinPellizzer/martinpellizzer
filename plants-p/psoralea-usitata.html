Psoralea usitata

Psoralea usitata, commonly known as the common psoralea, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It produces small, lens-shaped seeds that are adapted for dispersal by wind.

Psoralea usitata is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
