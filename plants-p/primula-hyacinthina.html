Primula hyacinthina

Primula hyacinthina, commonly known as the fairybells, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Primula hyacinthina is often found in forest margins and rock outcrops, where it forms dense, attractive colonies.
