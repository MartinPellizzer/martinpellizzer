Pachyphyllum idroboi

Pachyphyllum idroboi, commonly known as the Idrobo's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soil, often in mountainous areas with high altitudes. Its leaves are usually blue-green in color, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss.

Pachyphyllum idroboi is a slow-growing species, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
