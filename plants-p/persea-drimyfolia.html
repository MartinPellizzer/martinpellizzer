Persea drimyfolia

Persea drimyfolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruit that is sweet and sometimes astringent.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes in its native habitat.
