Pauletia angulicaulis

Pauletia angulicaulis, commonly known as the angular-stemmed pauletia, is a unique species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its angular, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
