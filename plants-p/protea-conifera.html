Protea conifera

Protea conifera, commonly known as the coniferous protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a pine cone, hence its common name. The plant features showy, brightly colored bracts that surround the true flowers, which are small and clustered. Protea conifera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
