Plinia rufiflora

Plinia rufiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered guava, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the Myrtaceae family. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe.

Plinia rufiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
