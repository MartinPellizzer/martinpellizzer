Panisea demissa

Panisea demissa, commonly known as the slender lily, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a slight fragrance. Panisea demissa is adapted to cool, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in temperate climates.
