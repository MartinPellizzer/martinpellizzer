Phyllocephalum phyllolaenum

Phyllocephalum phyllolaenum, commonly known as the leafy head plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes cucumbers and squash.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, and deeply lobed leaves that resemble the structure of a head, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruits are elongated and covered in a rough, warty texture, resembling a miniature cucumber.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid climates.
