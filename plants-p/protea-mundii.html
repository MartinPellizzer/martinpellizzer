Protea mundii

Protea mundii, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or cylindrical shape. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It produces a distinctive, long-lasting bloom that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter.

Protea mundii is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
