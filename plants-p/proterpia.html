Proterpia

Proterpia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are often followed by distinctive, triangular-shaped seed pods. Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
