Prosthechea tripunctata

Prosthechea tripunctata, commonly known as the three-spotted orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features long, slender leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its roots are adapted for aerial attachment to trees, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Prosthechea tripunctata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
