Pleurothallis lateralis

Pleurothallis lateralis, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hanging inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
