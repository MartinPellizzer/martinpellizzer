Piper rupestre

Piper rupestre, commonly known as the rock pepper or stone pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Piper rupestre is often found growing in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.
