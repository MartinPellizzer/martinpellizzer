Passiflora ovalis

Passiflora ovalis, commonly known as the oval passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of colors and structures, including a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by various animals and birds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong scent.

Passiflora ovalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential calming properties.
