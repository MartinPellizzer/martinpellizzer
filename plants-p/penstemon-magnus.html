Penstemon magnus

Penstemon magnus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon magnus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
