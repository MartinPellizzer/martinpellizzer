Primula stricta

Primula stricta, commonly known as the upright primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow or white flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of down. Primula stricta thrives in well-drained, moist soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom early in the season.
