Phaseolus anisotrichos subsp. incisus

Phaseolus anisotrichos subsp.

incisus, commonly known as the incised runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves.

The plant produces elongated, hairy pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, and it thrives in warm, temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and agricultural significance.
