Pittosporum lenticellatum

Pittosporum lenticellatum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dark green, leathery leaves with prominent white lenticels that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
