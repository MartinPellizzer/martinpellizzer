Pueraria ficifolia

Pueraria ficifolia, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu or giant pueraria, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing several bean-like seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
