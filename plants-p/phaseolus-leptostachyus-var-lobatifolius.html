Phaseolus leptostachyus var. lobatifolius

Phaseolus leptostachyus var. lobatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with a lobed or trifoliate structure, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that eventually develop into elongated, bean-like pods. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and a fibrous root system typical of the Phaseolus genus.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible pods, which are used in various cuisines.
