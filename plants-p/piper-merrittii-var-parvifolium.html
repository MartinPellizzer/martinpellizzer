Piper merrittii var. parvifolium

Piper merrittii var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, dark blue berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The varietal name 'parvifolium' refers to its diminutive leaf size, distinguishing it from other varieties of Piper merrittii.
