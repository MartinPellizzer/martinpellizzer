Pelargonium × involucratum

Pelargonium × involucratum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often branched.

Pelargonium × involucratum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
