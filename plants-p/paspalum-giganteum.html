Paspalum giganteum

Paspalum giganteum, commonly known as giant paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its vigorous growth, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
