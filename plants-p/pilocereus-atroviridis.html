Pilocereus atroviridis

Pilocereus atroviridis, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
