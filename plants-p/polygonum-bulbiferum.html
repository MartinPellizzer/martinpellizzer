Polygonum bulbiferum

Polygonum bulbiferum, commonly known as the bulbous knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Polygonum bulbiferum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
