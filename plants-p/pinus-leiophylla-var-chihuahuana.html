Pinus leiophylla var. chihuahuana

Pinus leiophylla var. chihuahuana, commonly known as the Chihuahua white pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Chihuahuan Desert region of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated cones and needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more rigid compared to other pine species. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to low rainfall and high temperatures through its deep root system and water-conserving features. Its cones mature over several years and release seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its limited geographic distribution.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, this variety is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
