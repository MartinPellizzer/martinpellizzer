Phylloboea

Phylloboea, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens.

However, it can become invasive if not properly managed.
