Piper pullibracteatum

Piper pullibracteatum, commonly known as the broad-bract pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
