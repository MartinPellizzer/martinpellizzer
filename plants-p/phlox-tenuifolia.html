Phlox tenuifolia

Phlox tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and aromatic foliage.
