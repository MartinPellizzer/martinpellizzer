Plukenetia tamnoides

Plukenetia tamnoides, commonly known as the African butternut, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small shrub that produces small, yellowish flowers and is characterized by its alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant is notable for its edible seeds, which are rich in oil and have been used traditionally for food and oil extraction. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
