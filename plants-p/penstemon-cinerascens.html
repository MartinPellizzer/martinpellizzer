Penstemon cinerascens

Penstemon cinerascens, commonly known as the gray penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers. The plant has gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Penstemon cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
