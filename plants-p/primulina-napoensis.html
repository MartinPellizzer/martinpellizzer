Primulina napoensis

Primulina napoensis, commonly known as the Napo gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, with thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.
