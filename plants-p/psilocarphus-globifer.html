Psilocarphus globifer

Psilocarphus globifer, commonly known as the globe-fruit coralplant, is a unique member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like inflorescences that produce small, fleshy fruits. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, often with a waxy texture.

While not psychoactive, it shares some morphological traits with hallucinogenic fungi, leading to occasional confusion in botanical classification.
