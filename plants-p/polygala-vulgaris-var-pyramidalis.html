Polygala vulgaris var. pyramidalis

Polygala vulgaris var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, characterized by its unique, often intricate floral structures. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in spicate inflorescences, and can range in color from white to pink.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
