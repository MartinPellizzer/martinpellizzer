Panicum didistichum

Panicum didistichum, commonly known as the two-ranked panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-ranked spikelets. Its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Panicum didistichum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
