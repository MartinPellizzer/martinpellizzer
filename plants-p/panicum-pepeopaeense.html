Panicum pepeopaeense

Panicum pepeopaeense, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal lands.

Panicum pepeopaeense is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
