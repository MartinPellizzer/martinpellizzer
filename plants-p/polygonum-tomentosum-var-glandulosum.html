Polygonum tomentosum var. glandulosum

Polygonum tomentosum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit and hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and horticultural studies.
