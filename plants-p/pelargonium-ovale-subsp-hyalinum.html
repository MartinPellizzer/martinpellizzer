Pelargonium ovale subsp. hyalinum

Pelargonium ovale subsp.

hyalinum, commonly known as the silver-leaf geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to South Africa. It features succulent, ovate leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
