Pinguicula bohemica

Pinguicula bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in early summer.

Pinguicula bohemica is a low-growing plant with a rosette structure, adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
