Pilosella plaicensis

Pilosella plaicensis, commonly known as the plains mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and meadows. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
