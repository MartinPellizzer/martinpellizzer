Phyllanthus excisus

Phyllanthus excisus, commonly known as the cut-leaved spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and distinctive foliage. The plant typically has palmate leaves with lobed or cut edges, which give it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus excisus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
