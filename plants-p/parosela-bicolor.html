Parosela bicolor

Parosela bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored parosela, is a striking member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its vibrant blue and white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Parosela bicolor thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
