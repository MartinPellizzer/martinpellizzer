Pelargonium tetragonum

Pelargonium tetragonum, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a trailing growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Pelargonium tetragonum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
