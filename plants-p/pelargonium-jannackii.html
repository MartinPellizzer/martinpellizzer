Pelargonium jannackii

Pelargonium jannackii, commonly known as the Jannack's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and they emit a mild fragrance.

Pelargonium jannackii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
