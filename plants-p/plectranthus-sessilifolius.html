Plectranthus sessilifolius

Plectranthus sessilifolius, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus sessilifolius is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
