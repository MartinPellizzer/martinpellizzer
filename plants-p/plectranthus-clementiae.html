Plectranthus clementiae

Plectranthus clementiae, commonly known as the Woolly Mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features soft, fuzzy, silver-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus clementiae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and mild mint-like scent.
