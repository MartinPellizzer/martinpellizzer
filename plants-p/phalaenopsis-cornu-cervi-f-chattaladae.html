Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. chattaladae

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. chattaladae, commonly known as the Chattaladae phalaenopsis, is a rare and hybrid orchid variety.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, elongated flower spikes. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a distinctive white to pale pink coloration. It is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid environments.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
