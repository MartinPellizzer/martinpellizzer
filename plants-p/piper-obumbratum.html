Piper obumbratum

Piper obumbratum, commonly known as the shadow pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elliptical, dark purple berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Piper obumbratum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
