Pachystigma fuchsioides

Pachystigma fuchsioides, commonly known as the red-flowered pachystigma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Pachystigma fuchsioides is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
