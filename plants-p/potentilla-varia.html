Potentilla varia

Potentilla varia, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla varia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and vibrant floral display.
