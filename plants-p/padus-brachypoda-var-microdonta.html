Padus brachypoda var. microdonta

Padus brachypoda var. microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf bird cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
