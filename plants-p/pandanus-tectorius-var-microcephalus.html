Pandanus tectorius var. microcephalus

Pandanus tectorius var. microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its roots and leaves contain aromatic compounds, making it valuable for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in dense stands along shorelines.
