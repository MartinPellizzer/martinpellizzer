Polygonatum grandicaule

Polygonatum grandicaule, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, dark green leaves arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Polygonatum grandicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
