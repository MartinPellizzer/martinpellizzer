Poa candamoana

Poa candamoana, commonly known as the Canary Islands grass, is a species of grass native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Poa candamoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or sandy environments.
