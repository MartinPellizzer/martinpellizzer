Polygala affinis

Polygala affinis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Polygala affinis is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
