Phlox missoulensis

Phlox missoulensis, commonly known as the Missoula phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and meadows. The plant features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox missoulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
