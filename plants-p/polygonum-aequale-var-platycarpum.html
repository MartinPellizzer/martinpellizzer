Polygonum aequale var. platycarpum

Polygonum aequale var. platycarpum, commonly known as the broad-fruited smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bead-like fruits and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, thriving in temperate climates. Its stems are usually reddish or purple, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety platycarpum is distinguished by its broader, more robust fruits compared to the typical form of Polygonum aequale.
