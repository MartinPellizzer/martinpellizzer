Phyllanthus subemarginatus

Phyllanthus subemarginatus, commonly known as the African pear or false kola, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with entire margins. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
