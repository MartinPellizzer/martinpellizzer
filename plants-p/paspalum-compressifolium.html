Paspalum compressifolium

Paspalum compressifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, thriving in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Paspalum compressifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
