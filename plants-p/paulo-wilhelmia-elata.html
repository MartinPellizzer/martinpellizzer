Paulo-wilhelmia elata

Paulo-wilhelmia elata, commonly known as the showy poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the center of the bracts, which are typically white or yellow. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
