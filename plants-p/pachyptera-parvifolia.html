Pachyptera parvifolia

Pachyptera parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pachyptera, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and attractive foliage.
