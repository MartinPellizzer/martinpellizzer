Piptadenia robusta

Piptadenia robusta, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces elongated pods that contain multiple seeds, which are the edible Brazil nuts. Its root system is extensive, and it plays a vital role in the rainforest ecosystem.

The plant is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
