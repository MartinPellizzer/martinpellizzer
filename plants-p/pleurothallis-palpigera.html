Pleurothallis palpigera

Pleurothallis palpigera, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis palpigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
