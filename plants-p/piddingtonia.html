Piddingtonia

Piddingtonia, commonly known as the pink bloodwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often displays a reddish or pinkish hue, giving it its common name. The leaves are small, narrow, and typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. Piddingtonia produces small, pale green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
