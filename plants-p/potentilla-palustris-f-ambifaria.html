Potentilla palustris f. ambifaria

Potentilla palustris f. ambifaria, commonly known as the marsh cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves with finely serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
