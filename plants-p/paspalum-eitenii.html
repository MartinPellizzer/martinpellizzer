Paspalum eitenii

Paspalum eitenii, commonly known as the Eiten's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and marshy areas.

Paspalum eitenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
