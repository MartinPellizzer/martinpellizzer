Phyllanthus angustifolius var. elongatus

Phyllanthus angustifolius var. elongatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along riverbanks.
