Physalis missouriensis

Physalis missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into orange-red, inflated capsules containing kidney-shaped berries. These berries are edible when fully ripe and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woods and along streambanks.
