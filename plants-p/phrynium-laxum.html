Phrynium laxum

Phrynium laxum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spatheous inflorescences with bright yellow spadices, which are both showy and attractive. Its rhizomes are edible and are used as a starchy food source in some regions.

Phrynium laxum thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
