Peltophorum massaiense

Peltophorum massaiense, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The tree produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
