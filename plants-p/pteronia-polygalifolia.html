Pteronia polygalifolia

Pteronia polygalifolia, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Pteronia polygalifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
