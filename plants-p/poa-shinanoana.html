Poa shinanoana

Poa shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Japan and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa shinanoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
