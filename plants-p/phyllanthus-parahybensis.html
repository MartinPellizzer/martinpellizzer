Phyllanthus parahybensis

Phyllanthus parahybensis, commonly known as the Brazilian gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, green, fleshy fruits that resemble gooseberries, which are typically eaten raw or used in culinary applications. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
