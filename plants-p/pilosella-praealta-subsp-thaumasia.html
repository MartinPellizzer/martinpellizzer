Pilosella praealta subsp. thaumasia

Pilosella praealta subsp.

thaumasia, commonly known as the tall hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and yellow composite flowers that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

This subspecies is native to Europe and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.
