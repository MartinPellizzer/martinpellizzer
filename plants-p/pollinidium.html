Pollinidium

Pollinidium, commonly known as the "flower of the wind," is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized floral structure.

It features a compact, spherical inflorescence that resembles a small, closed globe, with tiny flowers clustered closely together. The plant's botanical structure is adapted for wind pollination, with stamens and pistils arranged in a way that facilitates the dispersal of pollen. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense mats near the ground.

Pollinidium thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, where it plays a minor but ecologically significant role in local ecosystems.
