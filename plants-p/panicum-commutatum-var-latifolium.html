Panicum commutatum var. latifolium

Panicum commutatum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce fluffy seeds. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has a clumping growth habit and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
