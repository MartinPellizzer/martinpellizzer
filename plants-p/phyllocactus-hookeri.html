Phyllocactus hookeri

Phyllocactus hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's phyllocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, leaf-like stems that are often mistaken for leaves, a feature typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a decorative element to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
