Phlox douglasii var. austromontana

Phlox douglasii var. austromontana, commonly known as the coastal phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and displays clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
