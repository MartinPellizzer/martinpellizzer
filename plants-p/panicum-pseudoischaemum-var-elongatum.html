Panicum pseudoischaemum var. elongatum

Panicum pseudoischaemum var. elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed bluestem, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open woodlands and prairies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
