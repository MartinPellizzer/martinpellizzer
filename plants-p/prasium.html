Prasium

Prasium, commonly known as the common thyme, is a small, flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Prasium has a creeping growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Its essential oils are also used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
