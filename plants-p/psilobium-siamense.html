Psilobium siamense

Psilobium siamense, commonly known as Siam's psilobium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Psilobium siamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
