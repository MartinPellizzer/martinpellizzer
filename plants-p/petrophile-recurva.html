Petrophile recurva

Petrophile recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Petrophile recurva is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in full sun.
