Petesia carnea

Petesia carnea, commonly known as the red spiderflower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red, spider-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
