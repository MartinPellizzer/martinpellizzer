Phreatia obtusa

Phreatia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and tall, branched inflorescence. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Phreatia obtusa is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soil and is appreciated for its ornamental value in cultivation.
