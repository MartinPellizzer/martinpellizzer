Physaria wardii

Physaria wardii, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid environments. Its leaves are usually linear and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

The yellow flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
