Poa moabitica

Poa moabitica, commonly known as the Moabitic grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It thrives in moist, temperate environments and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Poa moabitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
