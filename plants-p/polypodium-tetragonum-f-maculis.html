Polypodium tetragonum f. maculis

Polypodium tetragonum f. maculis, commonly known as the spotted cliff fern, is a variant of the common cliff fern.

It is a perennial fern that typically grows in rocky, shaded environments. The plant features fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin and a unique spotted pattern on its leaves. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and preference for humid, well-drained soil.

This fern is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
