Pyrus rubra

Pyrus rubra, commonly known as the red crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits that range in color from red to yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 10 meters, with a rounded crown and smooth, dark bark. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, turning yellow in the fall.

The flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by the showy, edible fruits that attract birds and other wildlife.
