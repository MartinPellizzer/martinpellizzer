Phyteuma charmelii var. serratum

Phyteuma charmelii var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, spires of yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving it its common name.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
