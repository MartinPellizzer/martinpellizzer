Pyrus bucharica var. diversifolia

Pyrus bucharica var. diversifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely toothed leaves that often display variegation with white or yellow margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart fruits known as whitebeams. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or rocky habitats.
