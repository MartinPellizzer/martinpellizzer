Premna amplectens

Premna amplectens, commonly known as the creeping premna, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a woody vine characterized by its opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
