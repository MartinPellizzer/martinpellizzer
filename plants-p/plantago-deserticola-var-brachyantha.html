Plantago deserticola var. brachyantha

Plantago deserticola var. brachyantha, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically borne on tall, slender stalks. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

This variety is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in areas with low rainfall and high temperatures.
