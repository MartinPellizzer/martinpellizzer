Piper pseudovulgatum

Piper pseudovulgatum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor, making them useful in culinary applications.
