Pseudomertensia moltkioides

Pseudomertensia moltkioides, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
