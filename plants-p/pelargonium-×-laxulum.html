Pelargonium × laxulum

Pelargonium × laxulum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or containers due to its cascading foliage.

Pelargonium × laxulum is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.
