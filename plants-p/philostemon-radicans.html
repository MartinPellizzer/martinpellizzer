Philostemon radicans

Philostemon radicans, commonly known as the trailing philostemon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Philostemon radicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
