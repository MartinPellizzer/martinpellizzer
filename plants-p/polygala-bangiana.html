Polygala bangiana

Polygala bangiana, commonly known as the Bangi polygala, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
