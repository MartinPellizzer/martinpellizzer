Passiflora oblongifolia

Passiflora oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a unique, colorful pattern resembling a passion symbol. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in juices and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and twining.

Passiflora oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
