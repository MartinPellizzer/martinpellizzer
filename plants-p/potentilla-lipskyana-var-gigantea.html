Potentilla lipskyana var. gigantea

Potentilla lipskyana var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant potentilla, is a variant of the Potentilla genus native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
