Pyrus aria var. graeca

Pyrus aria var. graeca, commonly known as the Greek pear, is a cultivar of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped fruits with a thin, smooth skin that ranges from yellow to greenish in color. The tree is deciduous, producing clusters of white flowers in the spring before the fruit develops. It prefers well-drained soil and a temperate climate, making it suitable for cultivation in regions with mild winters.

The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor.
