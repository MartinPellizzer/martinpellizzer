Passiflora platystyla

Passiflora platystyla, commonly known as the broad-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
