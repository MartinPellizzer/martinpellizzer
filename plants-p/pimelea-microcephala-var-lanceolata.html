Pimelea microcephala var. lanceolata

Pimelea microcephala var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waratah, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange-red, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. This variety is closely related to other species in the Proteaceae family, sharing similar adaptations to nutrient-poor soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is found in various habitats, including heathlands and open forests.
