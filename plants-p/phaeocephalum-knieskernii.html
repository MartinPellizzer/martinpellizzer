Phaeocephalum knieskernii

Phaeocephalum knieskernii, commonly known as the silverleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive silvery-gray to pale greenish appearance due to its dense covering of minute, white to pale yellowish vegetative layers.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on bark, stone, or soil in temperate regions. The thallus is typically leathery and flat, with a complex structure that includes a medulla, cortex, and algal layers. This lichen is known for its slow growth and is often used as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its contribution to the study of lichen symbiosis.
