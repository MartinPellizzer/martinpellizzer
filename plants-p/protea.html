Protea

Protea, commonly known as the sugarbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Proteaceae, native to southern Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, often brightly colored inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaf structures called bracts. Protea species typically have a wide range of leaf shapes, from needle-like to broad and leathery, adapted to various environmental conditions. They are often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Many protea species are cultivated for their ornamental value and are popular in horticulture due to their long-lasting blooms.
