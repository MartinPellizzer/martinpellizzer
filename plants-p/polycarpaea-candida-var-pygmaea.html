Polycarpaea candida var. pygmaea

Polycarpaea candida var. pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf white milkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that typically reach only a few inches in height, making it well-suited for rock gardens or woodland settings. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
