Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. barrerae

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

barrerae, commonly known as the Barrera's heartleaf Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, elliptical to heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. These flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
