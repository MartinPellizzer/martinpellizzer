Polanisia graveolens var. simplex

Polanisia graveolens var. simplex, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in eastern North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in dense, spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, and they are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its historical use in herbal remedies.
