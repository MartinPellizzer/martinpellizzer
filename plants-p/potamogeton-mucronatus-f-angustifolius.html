Potamogeton mucronatus f. angustifolius

Potamogeton mucronatus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in still or slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and clarity.
