Pleurandra microphylla

Pleurandra microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf pleurandra, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
