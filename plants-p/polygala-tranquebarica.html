Polygala tranquebarica

Polygala tranquebarica, commonly known as the Tranquebar's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern India.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive hooded structure that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
