Panicum retroflexum

Panicum retroflexum, commonly known as the dwarf panic grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale green to reddish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight sheen.

Panicum retroflexum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
