Penstemon gentryi

Penstemon gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon gentryi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
