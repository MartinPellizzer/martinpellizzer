Parodia maassii

Parodia maassii, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, rounded stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant typically has a bright yellow or orange coloration, which is due to the presence of carotenoids. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in the axils of the stems during the growing season.

Parodia maassii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
