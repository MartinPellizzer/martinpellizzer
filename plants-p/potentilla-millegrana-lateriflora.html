Potentilla millegrana lateriflora

Potentilla millegrana lateriflora, commonly known as the fragrant cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrant blooms, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
