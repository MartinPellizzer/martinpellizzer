Protea dodonaeifolia

Protea dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant is native to the fynbos biome and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It produces distinctive bracts that enclose the actual flowers, giving it a unique appearance.

Protea dodonaeifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
