Phyllorkis longiflora

Phyllorkis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered phyllorkis, is a member of the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests or heathlands.

Phyllorkis longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
