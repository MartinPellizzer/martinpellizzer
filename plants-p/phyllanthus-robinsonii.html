Phyllanthus robinsonii

Phyllanthus robinsonii, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical shrub native to Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Sudan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
