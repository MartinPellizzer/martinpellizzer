Pollalesta argentea

Pollalesta argentea, commonly known as silverleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water environments.

Pollalesta argentea plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
