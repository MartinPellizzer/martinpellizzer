Piper opinatum

Piper opinatum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by elongated, slender fruits that turn red when mature. These fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
