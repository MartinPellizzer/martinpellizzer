Pycreus antsirabensis var. densior

Pycreus antsirabensis var. densior, commonly known as the dense pycreus, is a unique variant of the pycreus genus native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the dry season. Its foliage is often covered in fine, short hairs, providing additional protection against desiccation.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is of interest to botanists studying succulent adaptations.
