Placus angustifolius

Placus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in regions with moderate to high sunlight exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
