Platylobium triangulare

Platylobium triangulare, commonly known as the triangular wattle, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small, herbaceous shrub that typically grows in sandy soils and open habitats. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in whorls around the stems.

Platylobium triangulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
