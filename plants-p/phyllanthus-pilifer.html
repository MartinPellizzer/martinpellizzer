Phyllanthus pilifer

Phyllanthus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Phyllanthus pilifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
