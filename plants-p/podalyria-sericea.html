Podalyria sericea

Podalyria sericea, commonly known as silky podalyria, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Podalyria sericea thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
