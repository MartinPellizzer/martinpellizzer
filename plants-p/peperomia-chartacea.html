Peperomia chartacea

Peperomia chartacea, commonly known as the paperleaf peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that resemble parchment, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is a popular houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia chartacea prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
