Phlox ingramiana

Phlox ingramiana, commonly known as Ingram's phlox, is a striking perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed capsules that are often used in seed propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
