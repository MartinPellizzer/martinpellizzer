Pseudophoenix ekmanii

Pseudophoenix ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's date palm, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its unique, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk, which are adapted to the arid climate of its native habitat. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and has a distinctive, fibrous fruit that resembles a date. Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it faces significant conservation challenges.

Efforts are underway to protect and propagate this remarkable species to ensure its survival.
