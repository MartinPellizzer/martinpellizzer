Petrorhagia velutina

Petrorhagia velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandthrift, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in a fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name.

Petrorhagia velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
