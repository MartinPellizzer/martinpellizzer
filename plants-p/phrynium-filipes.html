Phrynium filipes

Phrynium filipes, commonly known as the arrowleaf plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by broad, colorful bracts. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Phrynium filipes is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
