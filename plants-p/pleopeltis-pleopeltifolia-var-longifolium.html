Pleopeltis pleopeltifolia var. longifolium

Pleopeltis pleopeltifolia var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved rock fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid tropical climates. Its fronds are usually clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
