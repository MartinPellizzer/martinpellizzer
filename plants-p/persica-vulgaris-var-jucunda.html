Persica vulgaris var. jucunda

Persica vulgaris var. jucunda, commonly known as the dwarf peach.

This variety is a compact form of the peach tree, characterized by its small size and vigorous growth. It produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for smaller gardens or container cultivation.

Its flowers are pink and attract pollinators, contributing to its ornamental value.
