Phyllanthus nobilis var. pavonianus

Phyllanthus nobilis var. pavonianus, commonly known as the Pavonian gooseberry, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, resembling tiny berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
