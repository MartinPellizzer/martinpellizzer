Potentilla sachalinensis

Potentilla sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Far East, including regions of Russia, China, and Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces distinctive, five-lobed flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla sachalinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.
