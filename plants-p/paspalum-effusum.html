Paspalum effusum

Paspalum effusum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and slender, hair-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Paspalum effusum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
