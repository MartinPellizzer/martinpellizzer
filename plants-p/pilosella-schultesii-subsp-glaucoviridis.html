Pilosella schultesii subsp. glaucoviridis

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

glaucoviridis, commonly known as the glaucous pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
