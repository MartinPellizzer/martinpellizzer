Philodendron copense

Philodendron copense, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical evergreen climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron copense is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
