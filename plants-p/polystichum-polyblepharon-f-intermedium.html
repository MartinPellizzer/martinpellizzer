Polystichum polyblepharon f. intermedium

Polystichum polyblepharon f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate holly fern, is a fern species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green fronds with a distinctive holly-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with fertile sori arranged in two rows on the underside of the pinnae.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
