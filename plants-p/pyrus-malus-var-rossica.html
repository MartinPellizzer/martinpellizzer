Pyrus malus var. rossica

Pyrus malus var. rossica, commonly known as the Russian apple, is a variant of the domestic apple that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a deciduous tree with an upright growth habit, featuring alternate, simple, and ovate leaves with a smooth margin. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring. The fruit is a pome, usually round or oblong, with a thin skin and a firm, juicy flesh.

This variety is known for its cold hardiness and is often cultivated in temperate regions for its ornamental and edible qualities.
