Pyrus aucuparia var. glabrata

Pyrus aucuparia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple species.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and relatively small, ornamental flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit, which is typically a small, yellow or red apple, is edible but often tart. This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to temperate landscapes.
