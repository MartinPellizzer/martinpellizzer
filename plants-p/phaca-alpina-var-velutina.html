Phaca alpina var. velutina

Phaca alpina var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet alpine phaca, is a perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in loose inflorescences.

It is adapted to cold climates and has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and specialized species.
