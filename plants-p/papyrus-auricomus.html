Papyrus auricomus

Papyrus auricomus, commonly known as the golden ear papyrus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, broad, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically with a yellowish hue.
