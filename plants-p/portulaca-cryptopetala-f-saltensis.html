Portulaca cryptopetala f. saltensis

Portulaca cryptopetala f. saltensis, commonly known as the saltbush portulaca, is a succulent plant native to arid and saline environments.

It features fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, drought-prone conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the heat of the day. Its compact growth habit and ability to tolerate high salinity make it well-suited for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

This variety is a valuable example of adaptive botanical resilience in extreme climates.
