Phlox piperi

Phlox piperi, commonly known as the mountain phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Phlox piperi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
