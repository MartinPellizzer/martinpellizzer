Pavonia meyeri

Pavonia meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's false mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy, pink to red bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Pavonia meyeri thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
