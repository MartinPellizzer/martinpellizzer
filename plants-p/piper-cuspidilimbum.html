Piper cuspidilimbum

Piper cuspidilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-pointed pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
