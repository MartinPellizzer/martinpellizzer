Philodendron selloanum

Philodendron selloanum, commonly known as the Selloanum philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
