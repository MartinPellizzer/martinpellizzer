Piper amazonicum

Piper amazonicum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn dark purple when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used as a spice. The essential oil of Piper amazonicum contains compounds like piperine, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
