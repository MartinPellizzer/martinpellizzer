Persicaria × brauniana

Persicaria × brauniana, commonly known as the hybrid persicaria, is a perennial plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria × brauniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
