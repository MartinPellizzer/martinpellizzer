Pentacaena

Pentacaena, commonly known as the five-leaved fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive leaves that are divided into five narrow lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small figs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, Pentacaena is cultivated in various regions with a subtropical climate.
