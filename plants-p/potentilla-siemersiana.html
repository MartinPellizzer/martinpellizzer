Potentilla siemersiana

Potentilla siemersiana, commonly known as Siemers' cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
