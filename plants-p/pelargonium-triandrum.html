Pelargonium triandrum

Pelargonium triandrum, commonly known as the Zanzibar geranium, is a flowering plant native to the island of Zanzibar.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has serrated leaves and produces small, rounded fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
