Polypodium urophyllum

Polypodium urophyllum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that are typically less than a foot in length. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium urophyllum is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role.
