Ptilagrostis mongholica subsp. minutiflora

Ptilagrostis mongholica subsp.

minutiflora, commonly known as the Mongolian hairgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and limited water availability. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its ecological zone.
