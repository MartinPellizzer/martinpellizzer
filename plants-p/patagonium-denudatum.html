Patagonium denudatum

Patagonium denudatum, commonly known as the naked patagonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered an important species for studying plant resilience in extreme climates.
