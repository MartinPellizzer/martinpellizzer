Piper viridescens

Piper viridescens, commonly known as the greenbark pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
