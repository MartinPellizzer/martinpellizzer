Plantago barbata subsp. austroandina

Plantago barbata subsp.

austroandina, commonly known as the Andean plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine pubescence.

The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is adapted to grow in rocky or dry habitats, showing a preference for high-altitude environments.

Its fibrous roots and robust foliage make it a hardy species well-suited to arid and semi-arid conditions.
