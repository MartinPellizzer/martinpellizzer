Panicum lagascae

Panicum lagascae, commonly known as the Lagasce grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown bracts. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in dry environments.
