Podochilus pachyrhizus

Podochilus pachyrhizus, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. The plant produces edible tubers that are rich in starch and are used in various culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional agriculture.
