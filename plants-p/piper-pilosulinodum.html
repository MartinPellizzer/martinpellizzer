Piper pilosulinodum

Piper pilosulinodum, commonly known as the hairy-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.
