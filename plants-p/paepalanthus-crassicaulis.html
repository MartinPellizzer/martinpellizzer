Paepalanthus crassicaulis

Paepalanthus crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-stemmed puya, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique botanical features, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
