Phacelia californica f. egena

Phacelia californica f. egena, commonly known as the Egena phacelia, is a perennial herb native to California.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
