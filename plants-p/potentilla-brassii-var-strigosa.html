Potentilla brassii var. strigosa

Potentilla brassii var. strigosa, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
