Pentameris longiglumis

Pentameris longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume pentameris, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

The inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is elongated and covered with long, silky hairs, giving it its common name.
