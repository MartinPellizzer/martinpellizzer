Piper percostatum

Piper percostatum, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that give way to bright red, peppery fruits resembling tiny peppers. These fruits are not only ornamental but also edible, offering a spicy flavor used in various culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and culinary use in tropical climates.
