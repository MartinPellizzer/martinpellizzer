Pulsatilla albana var. parviflora

Pulsatilla albana var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

It is a perennial species that blooms in early spring, often around the time of the vernal equinox.
