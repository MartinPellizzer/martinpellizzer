Pachycereus schottii f. mieckleyanus

Pachycereus schottii f. mieckleyanus, commonly known as the Mieckley's barrel cactus, is a columnar cactus with a distinctive, ribbed, cylindrical stem.

It originates from the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in full sun and well-draining soil. The plant features spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing species that is well adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
