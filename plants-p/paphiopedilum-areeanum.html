Paphiopedilum areeanum

Paphiopedilum areeanum, commonly known as the Arean Paphiopedilum, is a rare and elegant orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, with dark green, velvety leaves and pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
