Pilosella callicyma

Pilosella callicyma, commonly known as the showy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pilosella callicyma is a perennial herb that is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
