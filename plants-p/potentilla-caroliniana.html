Potentilla caroliniana

Potentilla caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features wiry, thorny stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
