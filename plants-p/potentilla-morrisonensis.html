Potentilla morrisonensis

Potentilla morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
