Pelargonium heckmannianum

Pelargonium heckmannianum, commonly known as the Heckmann's geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically features serrated, lobed leaves and produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in subtropical regions.
