Pomaderris elliptica var. ferruginea

Pomaderris elliptica var. ferruginea, commonly known as rusty pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered in rusty-brown hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
