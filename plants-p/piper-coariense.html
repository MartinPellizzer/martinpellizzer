Piper coariense

Piper coariense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, attracting various bird species.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
