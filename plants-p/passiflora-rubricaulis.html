Passiflora rubricaulis

Passiflora rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature intricate patterns of violet, yellow, and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. Its fruit, which resembles a small, green berry, contains edible seeds.

Passiflora rubricaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
