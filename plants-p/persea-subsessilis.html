Persea subsessilis

Persea subsessilis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its fruit is a smooth, orange to reddish berry that ripens in the fall.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
