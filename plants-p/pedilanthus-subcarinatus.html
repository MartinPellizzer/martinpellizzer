Pedilanthus subcarinatus

Pedilanthus subcarinatus, commonly known as the "false arrowroot," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
