Psoralea pubescens var. potens

Psoralea pubescens var. potens, commonly known as the hairy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly observed in its natural environment.
