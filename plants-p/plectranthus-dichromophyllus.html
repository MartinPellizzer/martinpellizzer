Plectranthus dichromophyllus

Plectranthus dichromophyllus, commonly known as the red-veined nettle, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that have a deep green upper surface and a bright red underside, creating a dramatic visual contrast. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Plectranthus dichromophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
