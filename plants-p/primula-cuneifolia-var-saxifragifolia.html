Primula cuneifolia var. saxifragifolia

Primula cuneifolia var. saxifragifolia, commonly known as the saxifrage primrose, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features compact, low-growing foliage with dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
