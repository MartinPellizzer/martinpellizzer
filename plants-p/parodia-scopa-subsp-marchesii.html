Parodia scopa subsp. marchesii

Parodia scopa subsp.

marchesii, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America. It features a compact, globular stem with a dense covering of white, woolly bristles that help reflect sunlight and reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. Its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems make it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This subspecies is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its distinctive appearance and resilience.
