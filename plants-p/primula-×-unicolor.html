Primula × unicolor

Primula × unicolor, commonly known as the white primrose, is a hybrid species within the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers, which typically bloom in early spring. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Primula × unicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
