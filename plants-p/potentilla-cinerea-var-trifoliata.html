Potentilla cinerea var. trifoliata

Potentilla cinerea var. trifoliata, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for garden use.
