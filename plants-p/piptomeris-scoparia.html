Piptomeris scoparia

Piptomeris scoparia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallow, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides. The plant has slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pale pink to white, with five petals and a distinctive central column, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Piptomeris scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
