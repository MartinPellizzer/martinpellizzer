Pavonia flavoferruginea var. flavoferruginea

Pavonia flavoferruginea var. flavoferruginea, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange-pink bract-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features include small, clustered flowers and a compact growth habit that adds visual interest to landscapes.
