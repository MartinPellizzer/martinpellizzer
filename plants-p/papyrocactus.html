Papyrocactus

Papyrocactus, commonly known as the paper cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in soft, papery-like leaves that are often mistaken for petals. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native desert environment.

Despite its name, it is not a true cactus but belongs to the genus Pereskia, which is closely related to cacti.
