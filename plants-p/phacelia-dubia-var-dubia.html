Phacelia dubia var. dubia

Phacelia dubia var. dubia, commonly known as the pale phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, fuzzy leaves. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species for ecological diversity.
