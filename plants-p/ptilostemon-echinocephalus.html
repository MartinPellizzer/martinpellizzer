Ptilostemon echinocephalus

Ptilostemon echinocephalus, commonly known as the spiny-headed ptilostemon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a pair of opposite, fleshy, spiny leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
