Pectis ciliaris

Pectis ciliaris, commonly known as the fringe daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves to reduce water loss. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, with white or yellow ray florets that give it a distinctive appearance.

Pectis ciliaris is known for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
