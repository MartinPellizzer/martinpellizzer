Pilocopiapoa hypogaea

Pilocopiapoa hypogaea, commonly known as the underground cactus, is a unique species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces short, spiny stems and features small, white flowers that bloom during the brief rainy season. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in its arid environment.

This plant is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a remarkable example of desert flora.
