Psila brachylaenoides var. ligustrina

Psila brachylaenoides var. ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincushion cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It features cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that are often reduced to spines, typical of many cacti in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
