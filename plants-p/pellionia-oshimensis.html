Pellionia oshimensis

Pellionia oshimensis, commonly known as the silver velvetleaf, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive silver-gray underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pellionia oshimensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
