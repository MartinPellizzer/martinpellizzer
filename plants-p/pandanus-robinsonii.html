Pandanus robinsonii

Pandanus robinsonii, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, which eventually develop into fruiting structures. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are utilized in cooking and weaving.

The plant thrives in wet, coastal environments and is adapted to saline conditions.
