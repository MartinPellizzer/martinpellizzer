Potentilla potaninii

Potentilla potaninii, commonly known as the Khabarovsk cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla potaninii is typically found in moist, rocky soils along stream banks and in mountainous regions.
