Phyllanthus poueboensis

Phyllanthus poueboensis, commonly known as the Cameroon gooseberry, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and smooth, greenish-yellow fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.
