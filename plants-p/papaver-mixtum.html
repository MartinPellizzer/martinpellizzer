Papaver mixtum

Papaver mixtum, commonly known as the mixed poppy, is a hybrid species belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers bloom in early summer and are often used in ornamental gardening for their striking appearance.

Papaver mixtum prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, hardy plants.
