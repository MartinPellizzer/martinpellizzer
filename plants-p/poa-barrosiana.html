Poa barrosiana

Poa barrosiana, commonly known as the Barros's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets, which are wind-pollinated.

Poa barrosiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
