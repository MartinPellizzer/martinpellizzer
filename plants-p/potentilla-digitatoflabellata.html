Potentilla digitatoflabellata

Potentilla digitatoflabellata, commonly known as the flabellate cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate, five-lobed leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
