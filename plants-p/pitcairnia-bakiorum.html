Pitcairnia bakiorum

Pitcairnia bakiorum, commonly known as the Bakiorum air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy coating, aiding in moisture retention.

Pitcairnia bakiorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
