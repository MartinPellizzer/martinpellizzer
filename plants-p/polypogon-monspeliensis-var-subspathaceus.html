Polypogon monspeliensis var. subspathaceus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. subspathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf basketgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like blades with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
