Pyrus rubricata

Pyrus rubricata, commonly known as the red-flowering pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its fruits are small, edible pomes that ripen in late summer.

Pyrus rubricata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
