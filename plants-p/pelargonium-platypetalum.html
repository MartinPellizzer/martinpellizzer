Pelargonium platypetalum

Pelargonium platypetalum, commonly known as the flat-petaled geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals that are broader than they are long, giving the plant its common name.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
