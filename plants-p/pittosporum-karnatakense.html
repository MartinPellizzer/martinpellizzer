Pittosporum karnatakense

Pittosporum karnatakense, commonly known as the Karnataka pittosporum, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
