Pandanus kerchovei

Pandanus kerchovei, commonly known as the Bismarck palm, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or nutrient-poor soils.

Pandanus kerchovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in the region.
