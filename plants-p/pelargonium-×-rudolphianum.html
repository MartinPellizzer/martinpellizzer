Pelargonium × rudolphianum

Pelargonium × rudolphianum, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium graveolens.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with serrated edges and a velvety texture. This plant is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and vibrant blooms.
