Pueraria composita

Pueraria composita, commonly known as the Chinese kudzu, is a climbing legume native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to seven oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can grow deep and is known for its rapid growth rate. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Pueraria composita is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
