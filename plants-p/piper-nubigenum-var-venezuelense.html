Piper nubigenum var. venezuelense

Piper nubigenum var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the humid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antiseptic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and adaptability to shaded environments.
