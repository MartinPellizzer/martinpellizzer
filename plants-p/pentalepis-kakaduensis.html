Pentalepis kakaduensis

Pentalepis kakaduensis, commonly known as the Kakadu pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and produces small, woody cones. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in local crafts and construction.
