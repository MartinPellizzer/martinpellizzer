Pulmonaria hirta subsp. apennina

Pulmonaria hirta subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Alpina lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or pink.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in woodland gardens.
