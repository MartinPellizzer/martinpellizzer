Prunus zippeliana var. sphaerocarpa

Prunus zippeliana var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and turn vibrant yellow in the fall.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact form and seasonal interest.
