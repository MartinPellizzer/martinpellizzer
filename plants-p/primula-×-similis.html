Primula × similis

Primula × similis, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Primulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, with oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is native to East Asia, particularly Japan and Korea, and is well adapted to temperate climates.
