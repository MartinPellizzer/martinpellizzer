Portuna floribunda

Portuna floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Portuna floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
