Planchonella costata var. umbonata

Planchonella costata var. umbonata, commonly known as the broad-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating and prominent midribs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellow throat. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
