Primula advena var. euprepes

Primula advena var. euprepes, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and the stems are usually upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
