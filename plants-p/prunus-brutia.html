Prunus brutia

Prunus brutia, commonly known as the Persian apricot, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to apricots in taste and texture. Prunus brutia thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, dry climate to flourish.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable regions.
