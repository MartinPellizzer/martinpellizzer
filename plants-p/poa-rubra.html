Poa rubra

Poa rubra, commonly known as red grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its reddish hue, which is most prominent in its inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to various regions of Europe, Asia, and North America, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Its slender leaves and compact growth habit make it a common component of natural and managed grasslands.
