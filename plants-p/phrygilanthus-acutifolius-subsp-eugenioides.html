Phrygilanthus acutifolius subsp. eugenioides

Phrygilanthus acutifolius subsp.

eugenioides, commonly known as the slender-leaved phrygilanthus, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
