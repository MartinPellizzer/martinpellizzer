Poa costata

Poa costata, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Poa costata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
