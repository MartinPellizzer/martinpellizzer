Piptospatha deceptrix

Piptospatha deceptrix, commonly known as the deceptive piper, is a striking member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This species is notable for its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, though it remains relatively understudied.
