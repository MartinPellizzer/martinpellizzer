Pleomele reflexa var. angustifolia

Pleomele reflexa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleomele, is a striking member of the Agavaceae family native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth pattern, with long, narrow, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces inflorescences of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is often appreciated as a decorative plant in both tropical and subtropical gardens.
