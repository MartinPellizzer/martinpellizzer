Passiflora racemosa

Passiflora racemosa, commonly known as the clustered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora racemosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
