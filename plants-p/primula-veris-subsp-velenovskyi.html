Primula veris subsp. velenovskyi

Primula veris subsp.

velenovskyi, commonly known as the spring primrose, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
