Parmena palmata

Parmena palmata, commonly known as the slender palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes various types of palms. The plant is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing at the base of the leaves.

Parmena palmata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.
