Passiflora verruculosa

Passiflora verruculosa, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in vitamin C. Its leaves are usually ovate with a rough, waxy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora verruculosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
