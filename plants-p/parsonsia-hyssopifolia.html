Parsonsia hyssopifolia

Parsonsia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Parsonsia hyssopifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
