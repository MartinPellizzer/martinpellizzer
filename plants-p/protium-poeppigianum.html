Protium poeppigianum

Protium poeppigianum, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
