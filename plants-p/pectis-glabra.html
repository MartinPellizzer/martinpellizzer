Pectis glabra

Pectis glabra, commonly known as the smooth pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a sprawling or shrub-like growth form. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a soft appearance.

Pectis glabra produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
