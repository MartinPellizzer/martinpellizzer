Pyrus costata

Pyrus costata, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge.

Pyrus costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
