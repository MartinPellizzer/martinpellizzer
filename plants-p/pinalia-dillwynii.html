Pinalia dillwynii

Pinalia dillwynii, commonly known as the Dillwyn's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Pinalia dillwynii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
