Paspalum firmum

Paspalum firmum, commonly known as Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
