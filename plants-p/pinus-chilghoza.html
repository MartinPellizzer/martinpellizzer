Pinus chilghoza

Pinus chilghoza, commonly known as the Chilgoza pine, is a species of conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, tall trunk and needle-like leaves that grow in clusters of five. The tree produces distinctive cones that are often used for ornamental purposes and in traditional medicine. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
