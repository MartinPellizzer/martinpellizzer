Pleurothallis purpurella

Pleurothallis purpurella, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping it to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis purpurella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
