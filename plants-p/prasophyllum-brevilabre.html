Prasophyllum brevilabre

Prasophyllum brevilabre, commonly known as the small-lipped leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forest or heathland environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few flowering stems that bear pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often clustered at the base.

Prasophyllum brevilabre is a short-lived perennial, with a life cycle that includes a dormant phase during unfavorable conditions.
