Penaea mundii

Penaea mundii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant has smooth, often hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers emit a strong, sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.
