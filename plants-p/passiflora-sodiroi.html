Passiflora sodiroi

Passiflora sodiroi, commonly known as the Crimson Passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a vibrant crimson hue with intricate patterns. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora sodiroi is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and local biodiversity.
