Prunella vulgaris var. violacea

Prunella vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as blue dead-nettle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are usually hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
