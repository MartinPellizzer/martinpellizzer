Phleum gerardii

Phleum gerardii, commonly known as Timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it is often used as forage for livestock.

Timothy grass is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat for insects and birds.
