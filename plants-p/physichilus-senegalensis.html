Physichilus senegalensis

Physichilus senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that thrives in sandy and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Physichilus senegalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
