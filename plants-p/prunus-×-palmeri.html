Prunus × palmeri

Prunus × palmeri, commonly known as the palmer cherry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring smooth, gray bark and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. Its fruit, a small cherry-like drupe, is edible but often used more for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

Prunus × palmeri is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to provide shade in challenging climates.
