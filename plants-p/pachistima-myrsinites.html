Pachistima myrsinites

Pachistima myrsinites, commonly known as silver cholla, is a succulent shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, branching stems covered in small, spiny clusters of modified leaves. The plant has a distinctive silvery-blue coloration due to a dense coating of fine, white hairs. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Pachistima myrsinites is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
