Paeonia mascula subsp. mascula

Paeonia mascula subsp.

mascula, commonly known as the tree peony, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces yellowish-green, fragrant flowers with numerous stamens. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
