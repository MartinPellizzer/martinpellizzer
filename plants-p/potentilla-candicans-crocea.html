Potentilla candicans crocea

Potentilla candicans crocea, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance.
