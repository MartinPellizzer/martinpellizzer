Prunus avium proles duracina

Prunus avium proles duracina, commonly known as the Duracina cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its large, firm fruits with a deep red color and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates, producing fruit in late spring to early summer. It has a compact growth habit and is relatively resistant to common cherry diseases.

The leaves are broad and lobed, with a glossy green surface that turns vibrant yellow in autumn.
