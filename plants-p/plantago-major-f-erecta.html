Plantago major f. erecta

Plantago major f. erecta, commonly known as the upright plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its more upright growth habit.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein and produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like clusters. It is known for its medicinal properties, with the leaves used in traditional remedies for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
