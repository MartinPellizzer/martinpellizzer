Podanthum woronowii

Podanthum woronowii, commonly known as the silver leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique leaf patterns and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
