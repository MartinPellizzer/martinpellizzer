Pyrola rotundifolia f. rubella

Pyrola rotundifolia f. rubella, commonly known as the red-flowered wintergreen, is a variant of the broad-leafed wintergreen.

It is a low-growing evergreen herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere. The plant features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red or pink in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and is often found in temperate and subarctic regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in woodland ecosystems.
