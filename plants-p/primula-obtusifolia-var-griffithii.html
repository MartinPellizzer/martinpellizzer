Primula obtusifolia var. griffithii

Primula obtusifolia var. griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high elevations. The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that are usually pink or white, and they bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
