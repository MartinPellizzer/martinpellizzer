Polypodium niponicum

Polypodium niponicum, commonly known as the Japanese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, ovate leaves.

Polypodium niponicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its lush, dense foliage.
