Paspalum dactylon

Paspalum dactylon, commonly known as perennial grass or cogongrass, is a tropical and subtropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that forms dense, spreading colonies through its rhizomes and stolons. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive nature, it is considered a noxious weed in many regions.
