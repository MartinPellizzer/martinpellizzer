Panicum curranii

Panicum curranii, commonly known as Curran's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, bristly flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Panicum curranii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
