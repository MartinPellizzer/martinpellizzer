Polygonum restionoides

Polygonum restionoides, commonly known as the New Zealand knotweed, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

Polygonum restionoides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
