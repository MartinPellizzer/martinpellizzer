Prunus × orthosepala

Prunus × orthosepala, commonly known as the hybrid cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species, Prunus serrulata and Prunus lusitanica.

It features showy, double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface. The fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in late summer.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its ornamental flowers and foliage.
