Primula tschuktschorum subsp. eximia

Primula tschuktschorum subsp.

eximia, commonly known as the Tschuktschorum primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
