Polygonum hydropiper f. ciliare

Polygonum hydropiper f. ciliare, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum hydropiper, characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive nodes and bracts. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
