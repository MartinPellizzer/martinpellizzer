Phillyrea longifolia subsp. maireana

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

maireana, commonly known as the Mairean Phillyrea, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
