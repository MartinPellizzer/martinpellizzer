Potentilla tollii

Potentilla tollii, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Tibet and adjacent areas in China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving at high altitudes with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla tollii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
