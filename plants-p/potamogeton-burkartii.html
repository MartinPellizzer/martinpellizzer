Potamogeton burkartii

Potamogeton burkartii, commonly known as Burkart's pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in two rows along the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Potamogeton burkartii plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
