Parodia weskampiana

Parodia weskampiana, commonly known as the Weskamp's cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is a small, globular plant with a ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Parodia weskampiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
