Petunia serrulata

Petunia serrulata, commonly known as the serrated petunia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and clusters of trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. It typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil.

Petunia serrulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a bedding plant.
