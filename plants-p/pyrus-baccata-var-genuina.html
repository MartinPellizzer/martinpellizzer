Pyrus baccata var. genuina

Pyrus baccata var. genuina, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, yellowish berries in the fall. The plant has a rugged, twisted trunk and dark green, serrated leaves that turn red in autumn. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows.

The species is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
