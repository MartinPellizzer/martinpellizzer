Pleurothallis bahorucensis

Pleurothallis bahorucensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
