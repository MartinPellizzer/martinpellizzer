Pithecellobium valerioi

Pithecellobium valerioi, commonly known as the velvet pod acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, velvety pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
