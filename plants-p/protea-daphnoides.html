Protea daphnoides

Protea daphnoides, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flower heads, which are surrounded by a dense, woolly involucre. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The species is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
