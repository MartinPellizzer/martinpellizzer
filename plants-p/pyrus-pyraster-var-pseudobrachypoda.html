Pyrus pyraster var. pseudobrachypoda

Pyrus pyraster var. pseudobrachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a variant of the common pear species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is typically a small, edible pear with a mild flavor, though it is less commonly cultivated than other pear varieties. This plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural and ecological studies.
