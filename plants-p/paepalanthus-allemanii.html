Paepalanthus allemanii

Paepalanthus allemanii, commonly known as the Alleman's pincushion cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny, globe-shaped stems that resemble a pincushion, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its striking appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
