Paepalanthus albidus

Paepalanthus albidus, commonly known as the white tawny grass, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow florets that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
