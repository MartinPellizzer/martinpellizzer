Prosthechea vespa subsp. duartiana

Prosthechea vespa subsp.

duartiana, commonly known as the Duartian spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America. It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees in humid tropical environments.

The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and produces striking, spider-like flowers with bright yellow and red markings. Its flowers are usually arranged in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique coloration and morphology compared to other members of the Prosthechea genus.
