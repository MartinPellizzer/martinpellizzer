Polygala furcata

Polygala furcata, commonly known as the forked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family, which includes various species with distinctive floral structures. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky soils, and is adapted to drought conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.
