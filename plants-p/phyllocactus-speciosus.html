Phyllocactus speciosus

Phyllocactus speciosus, commonly known as the showy phyllocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the growing season. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a common adaptation for arid environments.

Phyllocactus speciosus thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
