Pyrostria revoluta

Pyrostria revoluta, commonly known as the white sand pine, is a small evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twisted trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by cone-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

Pyrostria revoluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal ecosystems.
