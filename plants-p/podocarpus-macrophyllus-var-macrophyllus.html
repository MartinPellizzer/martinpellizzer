Podocarpus macrophyllus var. macrophyllus

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense or the Indian snow fir, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It features a conical shape with dense, dark green foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant has needle-like leaves that are thick and leathery, providing a textured appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain the aromatic resin used in incense and traditional medicine.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
