Pastinaca pimpinellifolia

Pastinaca pimpinellifolia, commonly known as the lesser burdock.

It is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae, characterized by its tall, reddish-purple stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, bur-like fruits. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.
