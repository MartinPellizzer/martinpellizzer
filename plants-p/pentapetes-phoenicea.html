Pentapetes phoenicea

Pentapetes phoenicea, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
