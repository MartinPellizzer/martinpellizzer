Piper arboreum var. giganteum

Piper arboreum var. giganteum, commonly known as the giant sarsaparilla, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouses.
