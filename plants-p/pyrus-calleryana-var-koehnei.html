Pyrus calleryana var. koehnei

Pyrus calleryana var. koehnei, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

This variety is closely related to the species used in the development of the Bradford pear, a popular ornamental tree.
