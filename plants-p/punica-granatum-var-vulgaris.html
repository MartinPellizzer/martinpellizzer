Punica granatum var. vulgaris

Punica granatum var. vulgaris, commonly known as the pomegranate, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the region of Persia.

It is characterized by its thick, red bark and spiny branches, with elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers in spring, which give way to the iconic, seed-filled, jewel-like fruits. Pomegranates are valued for their ornamental beauty and their edible, nutrient-rich seeds.

They thrive in warm climates and require well-drained soil for optimal growth.
