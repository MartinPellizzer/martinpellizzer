Phyllanthus tritepalus

Phyllanthus tritepalus, commonly known as the three-lobed phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves that are often hairy or glandular. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf structure and resilience to various environmental conditions.
