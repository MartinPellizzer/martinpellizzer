Perama dichotoma

Perama dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched perama, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the winter months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in pairs along the stems.

Perama dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
