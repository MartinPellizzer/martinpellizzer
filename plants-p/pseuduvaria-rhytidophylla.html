Pseuduvaria rhytidophylla

Pseuduvaria rhytidophylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved pseuduvaria, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive, waxy appearance.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits.
