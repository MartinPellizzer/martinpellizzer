Panicum mananarense

Panicum mananarense, commonly known as the Mananarensis grass, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and coastal dunes.

Due to its unique ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
