Plantago media var. urvilleana

Plantago media var. urvilleana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on tall, slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
