Plantago major f. maxima

Plantago major f. maxima, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a robust perennial herb characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained environments, making it a common sight in lawns, gardens, and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in spikes, which are followed by oval, nut-like seeds. Its extensive root system allows it to spread easily, often forming dense mats that can suppress other vegetation.

This species is known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves traditionally used to treat wounds and digestive issues.
