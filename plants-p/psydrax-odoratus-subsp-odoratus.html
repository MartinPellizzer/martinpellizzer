Psydrax odoratus subsp. odoratus

Psydrax odoratus subsp.

odoratus, commonly known as the sweet-scented psyllium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well-known for its seed capsules, which contain the fibrous material used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
