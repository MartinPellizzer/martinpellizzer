Prunus gymnodonta

Prunus gymnodonta, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and turn vibrant shades of red or yellow in autumn.
