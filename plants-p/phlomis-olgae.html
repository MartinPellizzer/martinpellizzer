Phlomis olgae

Phlomis olgae, commonly known as the Crimean phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, demonstrating its adaptability to harsh environments.

Phlomis olgae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
