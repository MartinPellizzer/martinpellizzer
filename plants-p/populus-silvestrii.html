Populus silvestrii

Populus silvestrii, commonly known as the wild poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive yellowish-green bark. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Populus silvestrii is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ecological benefits.
