Pyrus sabina

Pyrus sabina, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. Pyrus sabina is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.
