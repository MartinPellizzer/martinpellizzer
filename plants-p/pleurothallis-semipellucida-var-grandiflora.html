Pleurothallis semipellucida var. grandiflora

Pleurothallis semipellucida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid forest environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a unique, delicate appearance. It prefers indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens and private collections.
