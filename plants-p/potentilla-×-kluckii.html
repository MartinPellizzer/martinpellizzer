Potentilla × kluckii

Potentilla × kluckii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of soft, silvery hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, Potentilla × kluckii is widely cultivated in gardens across temperate climates.
