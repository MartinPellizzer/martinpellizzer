Phaseolus polystachios var. sinuatus

Phaseolus polystachios var. sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous climbing bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, trailing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive sinuous margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a fibrous root system typical of legumes.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds and is valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions.
