Phalocallis geniculata

Phalocallis geniculata, commonly known as the zigzag cactus, is a succulent plant native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, twisting stems that form a zigzag pattern, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. It has spines and is adapted to survive in hot, dry climates with minimal rainfall.

Due to its striking form and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
