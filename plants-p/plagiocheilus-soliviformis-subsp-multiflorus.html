Plagiocheilus soliviformis subsp. multiflorus

Plagiocheilus soliviformis subsp.

multiflorus, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
