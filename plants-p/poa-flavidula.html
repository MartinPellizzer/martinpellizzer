Poa flavidula

Poa flavidula, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in maintaining grassland ecosystems.
