Potamogeton juncifolius var. amphibius

Potamogeton juncifolius var. amphibius, commonly known as the amphibious pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in both water and moist soil, making it a versatile species in wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface.

This variant is often used as a food source for aquatic herbivores and plays a role in stabilizing sediment in shallow water areas.
