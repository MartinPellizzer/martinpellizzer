Potamogeton gramineus var. lacustris

Potamogeton gramineus var. lacustris, commonly known as the lake pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in dense clusters near the water surface. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This variant is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes and ponds.
