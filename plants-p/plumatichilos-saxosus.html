Plumatichilos saxosus

Plumatichilos saxosus, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found growing on limestone or sandstone outcrops.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
