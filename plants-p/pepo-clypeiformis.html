Pepo clypeiformis

Pepo clypeiformis, commonly known as the shield-shaped pepo, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, shield-like fruit that is typically green when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor, making it a valuable crop in certain regions.
