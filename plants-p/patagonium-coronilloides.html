Patagonium coronilloides

Patagonium coronilloides, commonly known as the Patagonian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Patagonium coronilloides is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in its native range.
