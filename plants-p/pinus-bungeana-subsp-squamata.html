Pinus bungeana subsp. squamata

Pinus bungeana subsp.

squamata, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a subspecies of the Chinese pine native to parts of China. It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue needles that are bundled in pairs, giving it a unique appearance.

The tree produces cones that are typically elongated and cylindrical, with prickly bracts. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stress.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
