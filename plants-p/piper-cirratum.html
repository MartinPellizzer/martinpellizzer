Piper cirratum

Piper cirratum, commonly known as the curly pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value.
