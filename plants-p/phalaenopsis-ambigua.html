Phalaenopsis ambigua

Phalaenopsis ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or other structures rather than in soil. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it is known for its relatively easy care requirements.

Phalaenopsis ambigua is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper conditions.
