Pentachondra mucronata

Pentachondra mucronata, commonly known as the blunt-tipped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
