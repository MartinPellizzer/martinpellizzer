Pinalia rosea

Pinalia rosea, commonly known as the roseate pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white petals and a compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its dry environment.

Pinalia rosea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
