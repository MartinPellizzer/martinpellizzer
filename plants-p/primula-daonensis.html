Primula daonensis

Primula daonensis, commonly known as the Daon's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of flowers at the top of its stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
