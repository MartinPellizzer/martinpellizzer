Pyrus atalantae

Pyrus atalantae, commonly known as the Atalanta pear, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pyrus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with attractive foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small pears, typically yellow or green, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The Atalanta pear is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
