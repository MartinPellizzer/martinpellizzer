Polygala sinaica var. glabrescens

Polygala sinaica var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Sinai milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are often pale pink or white.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to the typical form of Polygala sinaica.
