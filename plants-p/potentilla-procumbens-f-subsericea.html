Potentilla procumbens f. subsericea

Potentilla procumbens f. subsericea, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with thin, wiry stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.
