Potentilla pensylvanica subsp. oreodoxa

Potentilla pensylvanica subsp.

oreodoxa, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States. It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
