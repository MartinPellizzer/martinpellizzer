Polypogon higegaweri

Polypogon higegaweri, commonly known as the Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
