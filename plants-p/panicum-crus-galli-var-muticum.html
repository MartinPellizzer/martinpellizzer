Panicum crus-galli var. muticum

Panicum crus-galli var. muticum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces spikes of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or open areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its attractive, feathery appearance.

The variety 'muticum' is distinguished by its more compact growth and vivid flower color compared to other forms of Panicum crus-galli.
