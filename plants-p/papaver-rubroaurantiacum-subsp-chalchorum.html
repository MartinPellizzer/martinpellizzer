Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp. chalchorum

Papaver rubroaurantiacum subsp.

chalchorum, commonly known as the red-orange poppy, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking red-orange petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are relatively small compared to other poppy species, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
