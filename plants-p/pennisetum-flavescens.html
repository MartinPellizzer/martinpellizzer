Pennisetum flavescens

Pennisetum flavescens, commonly known as yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typically in shades of yellow or brown. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Pennisetum flavescens is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for wildlife and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
