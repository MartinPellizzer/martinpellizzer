Potentilla pensylvanica var. atrovirens

Potentilla pensylvanica var. atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in the fall, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
