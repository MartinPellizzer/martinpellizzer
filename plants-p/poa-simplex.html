Poa simplex

Poa simplex, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a cool-season grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Poa simplex is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
