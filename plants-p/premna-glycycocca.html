Premna glycycocca

Premna glycycocca, commonly known as sweetleaf or Indian nettle.

It is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
