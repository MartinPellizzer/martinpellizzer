Polystachya parviflora

Polystachya parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered bamboo orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and compact, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Polystachya parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
