Pedilonum inamoenum

Pedilonum inamoenum, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pedilonum inamoenum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
