Pygeum forbesii

Pygeum forbesii, commonly known as the African prickly pear, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Pygeum forbesii is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
