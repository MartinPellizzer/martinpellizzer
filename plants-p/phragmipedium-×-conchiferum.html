Phragmipedium × conchiferum

Phragmipedium × conchiferum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which resemble a lady's slipper, and its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces large, showy blooms with a distinctive pouch-like structure, which is a defining feature of the Phragmipedium genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

This orchid is popular among horticulturists for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
