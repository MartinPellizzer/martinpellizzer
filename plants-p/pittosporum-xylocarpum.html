Pittosporum xylocarpum

Pittosporum xylocarpum, commonly known as the red-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum xylocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
