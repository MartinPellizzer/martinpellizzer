Portulaca diversifolia var. angustata

Portulaca diversifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf portulaca, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and produce small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged oppositely along the stems, adapting to conserve water in harsh environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
