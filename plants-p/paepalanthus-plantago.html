Paepalanthus plantago

Paepalanthus plantago, commonly known as the silverleaf plantain, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
