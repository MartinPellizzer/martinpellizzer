Pleurostima riparia

Pleurostima riparia, commonly known as the riverbank pleurostima, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting well to riparian environments. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough or hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
