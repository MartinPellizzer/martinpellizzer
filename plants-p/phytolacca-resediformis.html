Phytolacca resediformis

Phytolacca resediformis, commonly known as the red-rooted pokeweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Phytolaccaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing a milky sap that can cause skin irritation.

Phytolacca resediformis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
