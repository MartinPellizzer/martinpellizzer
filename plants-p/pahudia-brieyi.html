Pahudia brieyi

Pahudia brieyi, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a striking member of the Iridaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, fragrant flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Pahudia brieyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements.
