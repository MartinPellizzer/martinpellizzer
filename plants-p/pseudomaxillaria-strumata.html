Pseudomaxillaria strumata

Pseudomaxillaria strumata, commonly known as the Strumate Orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often sought after by orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
