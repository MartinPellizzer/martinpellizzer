Panicularia pulchella

Panicularia pulchella, commonly known as the pretty panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its delicate, branching inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in paniculate arrangements, giving it its scientific name.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for pollinators.
