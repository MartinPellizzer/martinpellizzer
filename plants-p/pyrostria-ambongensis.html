Pyrostria ambongensis

Pyrostria ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis palm, is a rare and endemic species native to the lowland rainforests of western New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its botanical features make it an important species for studying tropical palm diversity and ecological adaptation.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
