Passiflora candida

Passiflora candida, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with intricate patterns of filaments and a central crown. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture.

Passiflora candida is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
