Passiflora multiflora var. multiflora

Passiflora multiflora var. multiflora, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate patterns of red, purple, and white. The plant produces small, round, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture and dark green upper surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
