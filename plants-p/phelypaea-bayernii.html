Phelypaea bayernii

Phelypaea bayernii, commonly known as the Bavarian fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, delicate fronds that are often submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures include sori, which are clusters of spores located on the underside of the fronds.

Phelypaea bayernii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
