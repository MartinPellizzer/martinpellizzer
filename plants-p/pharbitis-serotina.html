Pharbitis serotina

Pharbitis serotina, commonly known as morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using tendrils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in gardens and wild areas.

Its seeds are encased in a fibrous, spiny capsule that disperses when mature.
