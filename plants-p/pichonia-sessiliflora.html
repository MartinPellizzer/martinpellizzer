Pichonia sessiliflora

Pichonia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a glossy appearance.

Pichonia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
