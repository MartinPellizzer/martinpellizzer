Polygonatum vulgare

Polygonatum vulgare, commonly known as the Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark purple color, typically appearing in late summer. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in woodland habitats.

Polygonatum vulgare is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
