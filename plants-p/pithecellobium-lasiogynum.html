Pithecellobium lasiogynum

Pithecellobium lasiogynum, commonly known as the velvet pod acacia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets and produces distinctive, bean-like pods. The plant flowers in clusters, producing yellowish to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
