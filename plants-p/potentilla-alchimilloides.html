Potentilla alchimilloides

Potentilla alchimilloides, commonly known as alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, delicate appearance.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
