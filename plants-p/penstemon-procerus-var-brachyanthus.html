Penstemon procerus var. brachyanthus

Penstemon procerus var. brachyanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both garden and natural settings.
