Puccinellia distans subsp. convoluta

Puccinellia distans subsp.

convoluta, commonly known as twisted spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or twisted.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and saline environments, showcasing its ecological versatility.
