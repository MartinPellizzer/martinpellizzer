Peucedanum macrophyllum

Peucedanum macrophyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaved angelica, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers in late summer, which are followed by spherical, brown seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.
