Pleopeltis ensata

Pleopeltis ensata, commonly known as the ear-shaped fern, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments with a waxy texture.
