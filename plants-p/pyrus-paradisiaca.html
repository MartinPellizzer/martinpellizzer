Pyrus paradisiaca

Pyrus paradisiaca, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It produces large, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by sweet, juicy fruits in the summer. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in cooking and preserves.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and its contribution to both culinary and horticultural practices.
