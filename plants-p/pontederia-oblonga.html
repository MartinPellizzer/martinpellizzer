Pontederia oblonga

Pontederia oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often above the water. It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands and marshes.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through vegetative reproduction make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
