Physalis hederifolia var. comata

Physalis hederifolia var. comata, commonly known as the hairy ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features hairy, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that develop into lantern-shaped, orange-red fruits. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its botanical characteristics include a sprawling growth habit and a distinct pubescent texture on its foliage.

The fruits are edible, often used in jams and desserts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.
