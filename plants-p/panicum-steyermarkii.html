Panicum steyermarkii

Panicum steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that are typically slender and spike-like, with small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
