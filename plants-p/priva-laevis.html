Priva laevis

Priva laevis, commonly known as the smooth privet, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically smooth and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Priva laevis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.
