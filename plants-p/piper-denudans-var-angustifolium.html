Piper denudans var. angustifolium

Piper denudans var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small green flowers followed by black peppercorns, which are used as a spice. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.

The variety angustifolium is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to the typical form of Piper denudans.
