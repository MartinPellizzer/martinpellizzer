Philoxerus aggregatus

Philoxerus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick stems.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
