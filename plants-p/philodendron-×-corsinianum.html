Philodendron × corsinianum

Philodendron × corsinianum, commonly known as the Corsican philodendron, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Philodendron bipinnatifidum and Philodendron selloum.

It is native to the island of Corsica and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its foliage is often adorned with a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Philodendron × corsinianum is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ability to purify indoor air.
