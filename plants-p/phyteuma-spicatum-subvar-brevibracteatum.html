Phyteuma spicatum subvar. brevibracteatum

Phyteuma spicatum subvar. brevibracteatum, commonly known as the short-bracted phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a reddish tinge beneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
