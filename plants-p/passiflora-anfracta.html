Passiflora anfracta

Passiflora anfracta, commonly known as the "Coral Vine," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that display a mix of red, yellow, and blue hues, with a unique corona structure. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Passiflora anfracta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
