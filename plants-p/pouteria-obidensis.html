Pouteria obidensis

Pouteria obidensis, commonly known as the Obidensis Sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, yellow fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a flavor similar to that of a persimmon.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
