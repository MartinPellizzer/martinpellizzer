Polygala alba var. leptostachya

Polygala alba var. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with distinctive, thread-like petals that give it a delicate appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
