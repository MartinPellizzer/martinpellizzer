Phleum boehmeri var. maximum

Phleum boehmeri var. maximum, commonly known as tall oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences composed of small, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

This plant is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in soil stabilization and forage for livestock.
