Ptelea trifoliata f. pentaphylla

Ptelea trifoliata f. pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf boxwood, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage, which consists of small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in clusters of five, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its unique leaf structure, it is often used as a specimen plant in gardens and as a hedge.
