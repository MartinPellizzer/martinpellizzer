Picris koreana

Picris koreana, commonly known as Korean picris, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, and the plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
