Physalis laurifolia

Physalis laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel-leafed groundcherry, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and vibrant orange berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and yellow, followed by the distinctive, inflated calyces that enclose the fruit.

Physalis laurifolia is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.
