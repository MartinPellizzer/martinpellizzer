Picea wilsonii f. mastersii

Picea wilsonii f. mastersii, commonly known as the Wilson's spruce, is a subspecies of the spruce tree native to high-altitude regions of China.

It is characterized by its conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly twisted. The cones are woody and often hang downward, containing small, winged seeds. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential ornamental use.
