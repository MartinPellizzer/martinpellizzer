Pedicularis myriophylla var. tatarinowii

Pedicularis myriophylla var. tatarinowii, commonly known as the Tatarian lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
