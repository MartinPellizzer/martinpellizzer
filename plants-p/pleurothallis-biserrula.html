Pleurothallis biserrula

Pleurothallis biserrula, commonly known as the serrated pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts and botanists alike.
