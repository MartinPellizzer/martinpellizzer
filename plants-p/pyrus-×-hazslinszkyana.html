Pyrus × hazslinszkyana

Pyrus × hazslinszkyana, commonly known as the hybrid pear, is a cross between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (bitter pear).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 4–8 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the autumn. Its flowers are white and borne in clusters, followed by edible fruits that are similar to pears but often smaller and more acidic.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
