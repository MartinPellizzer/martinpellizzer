Polypogon monspeliensis subsp. subspathaceus

Polypogon monspeliensis subsp.

subspathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
