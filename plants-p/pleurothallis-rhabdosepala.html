Pleurothallis rhabdosepala

Pleurothallis rhabdosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are known for their intricate, spiraling structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
