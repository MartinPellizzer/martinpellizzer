Podococcus acaulis

Podococcus acaulis, commonly known as the barren podocarp, is a unique plant species belonging to the Podocarpaceae family.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its lack of leaves, with only small, scale-like structures covering its stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures called strobili, which contain the reproductive organs. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and adaptation.
