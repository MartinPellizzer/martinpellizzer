Podocarpus usambarensis var. dawei

Podocarpus usambarensis var. dawei, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody cones. The plant has a deep root system, making it drought-resistant and suitable for rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
