Philodendron armigerum

Philodendron armigerum, commonly known as the creeping philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with lighter green or white markings. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.

Its ability to climb makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a trailing plant in indoor settings.
