Potentilla peduncularis var. vittata

Potentilla peduncularis var. vittata, commonly known as the variegated cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant in landscaped settings.
