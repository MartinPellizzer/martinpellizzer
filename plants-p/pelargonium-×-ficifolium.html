Pelargonium × ficifolium

Pelargonium × ficifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a unique combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, bright pink or red flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for container gardening and hanging baskets.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
