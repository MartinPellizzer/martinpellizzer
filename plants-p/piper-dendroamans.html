Piper dendroamans

Piper dendroamans, commonly known as the tree pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper dendroamans make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
