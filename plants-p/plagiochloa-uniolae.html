Plagiochloa uniolae

Plagiochloa uniolae, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage and delicate, slender inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Plagiochloa uniolae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
