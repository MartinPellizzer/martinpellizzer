Psammogeton diffusum

Psammogeton diffusum, commonly known as the widespread sandspurry, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, oval seeds.

Psammogeton diffusum is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture.
