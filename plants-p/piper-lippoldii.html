Piper lippoldii

Piper lippoldii, commonly known as the Lippold's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper lippoldii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
