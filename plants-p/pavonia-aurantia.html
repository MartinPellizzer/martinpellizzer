Pavonia aurantia

Pavonia aurantia, commonly known as the scarlet bower vine, is a tropical perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, winged fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking floral display.

Pavonia aurantia is also valued for its ability to climb or trail, making it suitable for trellises and hanging baskets.
