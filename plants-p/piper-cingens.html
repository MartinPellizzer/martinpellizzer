Piper cingens

Piper cingens, commonly known as the Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible.

Piper cingens is valued for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
