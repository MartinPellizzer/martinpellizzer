Pyrus maia

Pyrus maia, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits resembling apples.

Pyrus maia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively disease-resistant, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
