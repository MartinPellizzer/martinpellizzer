Passiflora hassleriana var. grandifolia

Passiflora hassleriana var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or in jams. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

This species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
