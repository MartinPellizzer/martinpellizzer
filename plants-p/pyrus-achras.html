Pyrus achras

Pyrus achras, commonly known as the African white pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically yellow or orange when ripe and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in arid regions.
