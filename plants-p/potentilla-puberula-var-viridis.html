Potentilla puberula var. viridis

Potentilla puberula var. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact form and long-lasting blooms.
