Polypodium ciliiferum

Polypodium ciliiferum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its fronds with hairy, ciliate margins. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine.

The fronds are pinnately compound, with a distinctive appearance that makes it easily identifiable.
