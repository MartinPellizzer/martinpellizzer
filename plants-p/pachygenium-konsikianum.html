Pachygenium konsikianum

Pachygenium konsikianum, commonly known as the Konsikian giant fern, is a rare and unique species native to the dense rainforests of Central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, robust fronds that can grow up to three meters in length, making it one of the tallest ferns in the world. The plant has a distinctive rhizome system that allows it to anchor deeply into the soil and absorb nutrients efficiently. Its leaves are bioluminescent, a rare trait in ferns, which helps it attract nocturnal pollinators.

Due to habitat destruction, Pachygenium konsikianum is critically endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
