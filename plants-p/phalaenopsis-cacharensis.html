Phalaenopsis cacharensis

Phalaenopsis cacharensis, commonly known as the Cachar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous flowers arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
