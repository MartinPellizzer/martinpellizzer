Primula brachystoma

Primula brachystoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces violet to purple blooms that appear in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, often covered in a fine, downy coating.

Primula brachystoma thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
