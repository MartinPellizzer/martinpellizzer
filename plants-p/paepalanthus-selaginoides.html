Paepalanthus selaginoides

Paepalanthus selaginoides, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves and produces flowering stems that rise above the foliage. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
