Pandanus marquesasensis

Pandanus marquesasensis, commonly known as the Marquesan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Marquesas Islands in French Polynesia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the plant itself is adapted to coastal and limestone soils.

Pandanus marquesasensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various local wildlife.
