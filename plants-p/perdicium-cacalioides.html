Perdicium cacalioides

Perdicium cacalioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Perdicium cacalioides is often found in open habitats such as meadows and forest edges.
