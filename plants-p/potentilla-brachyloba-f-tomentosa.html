Potentilla brachyloba f. tomentosa

Potentilla brachyloba f. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique texture and color.
