Paspalum polystachyum

Paspalum polystachyum, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or for erosion control.
