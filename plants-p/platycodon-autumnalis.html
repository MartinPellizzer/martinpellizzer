Platycodon autumnalis

Platycodon autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking autumn color.

Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
