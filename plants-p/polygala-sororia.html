Polygala sororia

Polygala sororia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows in woodland habitats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala sororia is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments and is an important species for native pollinators.
