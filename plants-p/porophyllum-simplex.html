Porophyllum simplex

Porophyllum simplex, commonly known as the plains milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and prairies. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Porophyllum simplex is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
