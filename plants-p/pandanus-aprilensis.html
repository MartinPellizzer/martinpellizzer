Pandanus aprilensis

Pandanus aprilensis, commonly known as the April pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, broad leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fibrous leaves are valued for their durability and are used in making thatch and baskets.

Pandanus aprilensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil.
