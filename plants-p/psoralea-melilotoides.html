Psoralea melilotoides

Psoralea melilotoides, commonly known as the sweet pea bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
