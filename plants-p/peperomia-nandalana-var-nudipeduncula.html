Peperomia nandalana var. nudipeduncula

Peperomia nandalana var. nudipeduncula, commonly known as the bare-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy texture and a range of colors from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its short, unbranched stems. Its succulent-like leaves help it store water, making it adaptable to dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
