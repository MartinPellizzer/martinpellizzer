Patagonium villosum var. typica

Patagonium villosum var. typica, commonly known as the hairy Patagonia plant, is a flowering shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
