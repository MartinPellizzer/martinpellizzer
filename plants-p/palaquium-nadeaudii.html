Palaquium nadeaudii

Palaquium nadeaudii, commonly known as the Nadeaud's wax apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a waxy, yellow or orange skin. Its fruit contains a single seed and is often used in local cuisines for its sweet, tangy flavor.

Palaquium nadeaudii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
