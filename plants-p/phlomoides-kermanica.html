Phlomoides kermanica

Phlomoides kermanica, commonly known as Kerman false panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are often pink or purple in color. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in desert landscapes and xeriscaped gardens.
