Platostoma menthoides var. longiracemosum

Platostoma menthoides var. longiracemosum, commonly known as the long-peduncled mintleaf, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a strong mint-like scent. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, two-lipped flowers, typically purple or blue in color.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
