Potentilla × vallei

Potentilla × vallei, commonly known as the valley cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on slender, arching stems. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Potentilla × vallei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
