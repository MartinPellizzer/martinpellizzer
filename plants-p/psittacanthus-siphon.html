It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, which are essential for its propagation.

The siphon fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
