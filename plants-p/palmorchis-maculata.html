Palmorchis maculata

Palmorchis maculata, commonly known as the spotted palm orchid, is a species of orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, stocky rhizome and a single, broad, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a spike of small, pale green or white flowers with a distinctive purple or brown marking on the dorsal sepal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in hardwood forests.

The orchid is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
