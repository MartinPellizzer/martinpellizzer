Peperomia ciliosa

Peperomia ciliosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia ciliosa is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its drought tolerance, making it a low-maintenance plant for both novice and experienced growers.
