Pseudomaxillaria conferta

Pseudomaxillaria conferta, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
