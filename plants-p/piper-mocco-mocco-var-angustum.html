Piper mocco-mocco var. angustum

Piper mocco-mocco var. angustum, commonly known as narrow-leaf mocco-mocco, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of dense vegetation.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying plant diversity in arid and semi-arid regions.
