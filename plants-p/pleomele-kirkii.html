Pleomele kirkii

Pleomele kirkii, commonly known as the Kauri pleomele, is a striking member of the Agavaceae family native to the tropical rainforests of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and its rosette of broad, fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces striking white or pale green flowers in long, branched inflorescences, which are followed by small, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
