Polygonum pteropus

Polygonum pteropus, commonly known as the flame bush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and is often found in moist, tropical environments. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
