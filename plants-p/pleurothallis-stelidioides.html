Pleurothallis stelidioides

Pleurothallis stelidioides, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is noted for its intricate floral structure and is often found in mixed orchid populations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
