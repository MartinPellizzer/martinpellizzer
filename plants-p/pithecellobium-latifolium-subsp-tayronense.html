Pithecellobium latifolium subsp. tayronense

Pithecellobium latifolium subsp.

tayronense, commonly known as the Tayrona pithecellobium, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America. It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and humid valleys.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
