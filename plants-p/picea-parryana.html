Picea parryana

Picea parryana, commonly known as Parry's spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its pyramidal shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones that hang downward. The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cold, moist conditions. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather.

Picea parryana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest ecosystems.
