Piper schottii var. schottii

Piper schottii var. schottii, commonly known as Schott's pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
