Polypodium articulatum

Polypodium articulatum, commonly known as the segmented fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its rhizomatous growth and fronds that are divided into segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its fronds are usually pinnately compound, with a distinctive articulation at the base of each segment.

Polypodium articulatum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
