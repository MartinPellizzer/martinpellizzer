Poa cusickii subsp. purpurascens

Poa cusickii subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple-tinged inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
