Pholidia exotrachys

Pholidia exotrachys, commonly known as the broadleaf hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pholidia exotrachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
