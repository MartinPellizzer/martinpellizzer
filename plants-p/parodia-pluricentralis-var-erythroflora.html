Parodia pluricentralis var. erythroflora

Parodia pluricentralis var. erythroflora, commonly known as the Red-flowered Pincushion Cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular stem with a spiny, ribbed structure that helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces striking, vibrant red flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

The varietal name "erythroflora" refers to its distinctive red floral coloration.
