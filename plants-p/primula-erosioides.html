Primula erosioides

Primula erosioides, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clustered flowers. The plant typically produces showy, bell-shaped blooms in shades of pink, white, or purple, which appear in early spring. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, serrated edge.

Primula erosioides thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
