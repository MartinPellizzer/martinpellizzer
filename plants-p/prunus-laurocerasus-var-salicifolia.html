Prunus laurocerasus var. salicifolia

Prunus laurocerasus var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elliptical and have a serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The variety is notable for its graceful appearance and adaptability to various garden conditions.
