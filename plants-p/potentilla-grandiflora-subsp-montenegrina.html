Potentilla grandiflora subsp. montenegrina

Potentilla grandiflora subsp.

montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its silver-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine displays.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in colder climates.
