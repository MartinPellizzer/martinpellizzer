Primula × goeppertiana

Primula × goeppertiana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a popular ornamental plant valued for its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Primula vulgaris and Primula veris, resulting in a diverse range of cultivars with varying flower colors and forms. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which bloom in early spring, come in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with a distinctive central blotch.

Primula × goeppertiana thrives in cool, moist environments and is often used in garden borders and rock gardens.
