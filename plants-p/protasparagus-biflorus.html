Protasparagus biflorus

Protasparagus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered asparagus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces tiny, blue-black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
