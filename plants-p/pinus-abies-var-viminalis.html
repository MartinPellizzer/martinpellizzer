Pinus abies var. viminalis

Pinus abies var. viminalis, commonly known as the European silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its wood is lightweight and has been historically used for construction and paper production.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
