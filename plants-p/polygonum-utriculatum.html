Polygonum utriculatum

Polygonum utriculatum, commonly known as bladder campion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers. The plant is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often rounded or ovate. Its flowers are typically pink or red, arranged in dense, terminal clusters, and it produces small, bladder-like seed pods that aid in seed dispersal.

Bladder campion is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
