Platonia elata

Platonia elata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The tree has a rounded crown and can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark that peels in thin strips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Platonia elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
