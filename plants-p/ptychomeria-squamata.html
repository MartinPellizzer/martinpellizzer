Ptychomeria squamata

Ptychomeria squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf ptychomeria, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its woody stems are often covered in a scaly or flaky texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ptychomeria squamata is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
