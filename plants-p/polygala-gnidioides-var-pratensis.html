Polygala gnidioides var. pratensis

Polygala gnidioides var. pratensis, commonly known as the meadow milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts specific pollinators.
