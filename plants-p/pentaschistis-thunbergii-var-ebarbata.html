Pentaschistis thunbergii var. ebarbata

Pentaschistis thunbergii var. ebarbata, commonly known as the Japanese stonecress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, and the plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.
