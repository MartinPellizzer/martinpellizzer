Prosthechea venosa

Prosthechea venosa, commonly known as the veined orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its reproductive success.

Prosthechea venosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitats.
