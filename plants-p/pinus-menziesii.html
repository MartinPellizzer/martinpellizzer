Pinus menziesii

Pinus menziesii, commonly known as the Douglas fir, is a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and distinctive cones that hang downward. The tree can reach heights of up to 100 meters, making it one of the tallest conifer species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fires and pests.

The Douglas fir is valued for its durable wood and plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems.
