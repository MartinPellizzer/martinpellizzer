Pleurothallis applanata

Pleurothallis applanata, commonly known as the flat-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and flat, contributing to its common name, while its flowers are usually pale green or white with a subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis applanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
