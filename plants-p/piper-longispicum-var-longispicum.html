Piper longispicum var. longispicum

Piper longispicum var. longispicum, commonly known as the long-spurred pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated, and often spicily flavored berries. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, spiculate fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape with a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential use in traditional medicine.
