Phyllarthron madagascariensis

Phyllarthron madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and branched stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive dry conditions.

Phyllarthron madagascariensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils in dry regions of Madagascar.
