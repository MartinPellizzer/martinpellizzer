Potentilla Pensylvanica Var. Hippiana

Potentilla pensylvanica var. hippiana is a lesser-known yet fascinating member of the rose family. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, woodsy environments. This variant is distinguished by its delicate yellow flowers and compact growth habit. Though not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance in its native habitat. Its resilience and beauty make it a valuable subject for both botanists and nature enthusiasts.
