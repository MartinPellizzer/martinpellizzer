Poterium sanguisorba subsp. muricatum

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

muricatum, commonly known as red sanders, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red, velvety stems that become more pronounced with age.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, with serrated edges.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
