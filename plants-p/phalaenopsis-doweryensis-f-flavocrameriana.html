Phalaenopsis doweryensis f. flavocrameriana

Phalaenopsis doweryensis f. flavocrameriana, commonly known as the Yellow-veined Phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, stocky rhizome and produces fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with intricate veining, and they bloom in clusters along the stem. This cultivar is prized for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its natural habitat is limited to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
