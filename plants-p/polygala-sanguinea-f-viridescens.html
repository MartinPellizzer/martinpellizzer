Polygala sanguinea f. viridescens

Polygala sanguinea f. viridescens, commonly known as the green-flowered milkvetch, is a variant of the species Polygala sanguinea, characterized by its distinctive greenish floral hues.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color, though the varietal form exhibits a more subdued greenish tint. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
