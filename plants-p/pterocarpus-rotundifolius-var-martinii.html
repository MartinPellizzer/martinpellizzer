Pterocarpus rotundifolius var. martinii

Pterocarpus rotundifolius var. martinii, commonly known as the Malabar kino or Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, kidney-shaped pods containing flat, dark seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
