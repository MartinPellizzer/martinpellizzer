Pitcairnia bracteata var. gireoudiana

Pitcairnia bracteata var. gireoudiana, commonly known as the Gireoud's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and vibrant, long-lasting inflorescences that display a range of colors from red to orange. The plant features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability and visual impact.
