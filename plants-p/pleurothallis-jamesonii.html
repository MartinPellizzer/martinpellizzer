Pleurothallis jamesonii

Pleurothallis jamesonii, commonly known as the Jameson's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Pleurothallis jamesonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouse environments due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
