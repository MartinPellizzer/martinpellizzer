Piper pubipes

Piper pubipes, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
