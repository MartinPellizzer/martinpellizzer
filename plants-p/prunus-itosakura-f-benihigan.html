Prunus itosakura f. benihigan

Prunus itosakura f. benihigan, commonly known as the Benihigan cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and vibrant pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its foliage turns a striking red in the autumn, adding seasonal interest to gardens.

The Benihigan cherry is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in landscapes for its beauty and fragrance.
