Pombalia insignis

Pombalia insignis, commonly known as the golden bell orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pombalia insignis is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
