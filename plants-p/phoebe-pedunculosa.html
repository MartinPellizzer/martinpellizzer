Phoebe pedunculosa

Phoebe pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered phoebe, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features dark green, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Its dense growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
