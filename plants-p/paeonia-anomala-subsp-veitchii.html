Paeonia anomala subsp. veitchii

Paeonia anomala subsp.

veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has palmately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

The subspecies is closely related to other peony species but is distinguished by its unique floral characteristics and habitat preferences.
