Papuasicyos arfakensis

Papuasicyos arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfakensis bottle tree, is a unique species native to the arid regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk that stores water, a feature adapted to its harsh, dry environment. The plant has small, narrow leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration. It produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
