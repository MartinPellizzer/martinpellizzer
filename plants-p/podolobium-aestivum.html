Podolobium aestivum

Podolobium aestivum, commonly known as the summer podolobium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
