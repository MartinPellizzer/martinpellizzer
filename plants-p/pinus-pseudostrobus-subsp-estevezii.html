Pinus pseudostrobus subsp. estevezii

Pinus pseudostrobus subsp.

estevezii, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a subspecies native to Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

The tree produces long, slender cones and has needle-like leaves that are arranged in pairs. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its wood is valued for its strength and is used in construction and furniture-making.
