Plantago alopecurus

Plantago alopecurus, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. The plant features upright, grass-like leaves that are narrow and slightly curled at the edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom in the summer months.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in many temperate regions.
