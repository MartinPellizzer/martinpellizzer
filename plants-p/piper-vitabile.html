Piper vitabile

Piper vitabile, commonly known as the pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
