Psorothamnus arborescens var. minutifolius

Psorothamnus arborescens var. minutifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved burrobrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
