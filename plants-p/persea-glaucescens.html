Persea glaucescens

Persea glaucescens, commonly known as the white spondias, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a drupe, typically yellow or orange when ripe, and is edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The tree is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
