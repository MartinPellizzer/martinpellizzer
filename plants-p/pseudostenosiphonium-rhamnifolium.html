Pseudostenosiphonium rhamnifolium

Pseudostenosiphonium rhamnifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It possesses small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 2 centimeters in length, arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red berries that contain the seeds. Its roots form haustoria, specialized structures that penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients.

The plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
