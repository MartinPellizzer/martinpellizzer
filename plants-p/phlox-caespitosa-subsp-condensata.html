Phlox caespitosa subsp. condensata

Phlox caespitosa subsp.

condensata, commonly known as dense phlox, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its compact, clump-forming growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in partial to full sun, preferring well-drained soil. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
