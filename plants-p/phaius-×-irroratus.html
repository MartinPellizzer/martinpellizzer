Phaius × irroratus

Phaius × irroratus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter.

Phaius × irroratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
