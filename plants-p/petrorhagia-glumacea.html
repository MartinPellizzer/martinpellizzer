Petrorhagia glumacea

Petrorhagia glumacea, commonly known as the woolly rock-mustard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is part of the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other mustard plants.
