Ptelea caroliniana

Ptelea caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in landscaping.
