Philibertia gilliesii

Philibertia gilliesii, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
