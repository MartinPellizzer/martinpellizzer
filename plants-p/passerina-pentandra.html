Passerina pentandra

Passerina pentandra, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
