Phrynium capitatum

Phrynium capitatum, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, umbrella-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its foliage is also utilized in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
