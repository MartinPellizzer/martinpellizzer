Polycarpaea eriantha

Polycarpaea eriantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf sida, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas, making it a common weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes cotton and hibiscus.
