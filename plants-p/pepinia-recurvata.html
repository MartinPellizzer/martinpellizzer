Pepinia recurvata

Pepinia recurvata, commonly known as the curly peperomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and fleshy, waxy leaves that often have a distinctive curling or twisted appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Due to its unique foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
