Pimelea lehmanniana var. ligustrinoides

Pimelea lehmanniana var. ligustrinoides, commonly known as the lemon scented pimelea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its habitat. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive lemony scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
