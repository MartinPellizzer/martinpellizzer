Pleurothallis queremalensis

Pleurothallis queremalensis, commonly known as the Queremal orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, adapted to the humid and shaded conditions of its environment.

Pleurothallis queremalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
