Pilosella floribunda subsp. atrocrocea

Pilosella floribunda subsp.

atrocrocea, commonly known as the dark yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
