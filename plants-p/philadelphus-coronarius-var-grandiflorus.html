Philadelphus coronarius var. grandiflorus

Philadelphus coronarius var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the grandiflorus mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and ability to attract pollinators.
