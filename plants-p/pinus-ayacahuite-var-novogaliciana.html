Pinus ayacahuite var. novogaliciana

Pinus ayacahuite var. novogaliciana, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a variant of the Ayacahuite pine native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, fast-growing form and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. The tree produces cones that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a conical shape that becomes more spreading with age.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and ornamental appeal in horticulture.
