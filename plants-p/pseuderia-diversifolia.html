Pseuderia diversifolia

Pseuderia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable-leaf pseuderia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes and sizes, which vary depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant adaptation and morphological variation.
