Psidium acutatum

Psidium acutatum, commonly known as the passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its fruit has a distinctive appearance with a bright yellow or orange rind and a juicy, aromatic interior.

The botanical features of Psidium acutatum make it a popular ornamental and commercial crop.
