Pernettya marginata

Pernettya marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bright red berries. The plant thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

Pernettya marginata is adapted to cold climates and plays an important role in its ecosystem.
