Pelargonium marmoratum

Pelargonium marmoratum, commonly known as the marble geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that feature a mix of green, white, and red patterns, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium marmoratum is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticultural displays.
