Psittacanthus calyculatus

Psittacanthus calyculatus, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features a dense rosette of narrow, leathery fronds that are typically a deep green color. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.

The fronds are arranged in a star-like pattern, giving the plant its common name.
