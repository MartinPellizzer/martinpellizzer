Phegopteris barbata

Phegopteris barbata, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

The plant plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small organisms in its native ecosystems.
