Phyllanthus glaucophyllus var. alpestris

Phyllanthus glaucophyllus var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine phyllanthus, is a variant of the glaucophyllus phyllanthus species.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, waxy, and often bluish-green leaves. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

This species is adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
