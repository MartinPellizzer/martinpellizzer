Pieris japonica subsp. formosa

Pieris japonica subsp.

formosa, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea. It is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its foliage and flowering habits make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
