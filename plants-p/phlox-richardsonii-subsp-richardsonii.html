Phlox richardsonii subsp. richardsonii

Phlox richardsonii subsp.

richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
