Pharbitis falkioides

Pharbitis falkioides, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and long, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The species is related to other members of the Fabaceae family, known for their nitrogen-fixing ability.
