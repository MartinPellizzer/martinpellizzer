Pittosporum ochrosiifolium var. amygdaloides

Pittosporum ochrosiifolium var. amygdaloides, commonly known as the sweet-smelling pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with dark green, leathery leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, black, berry-like drupe that contains several seeds.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
