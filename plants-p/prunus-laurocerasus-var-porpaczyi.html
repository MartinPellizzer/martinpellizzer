Prunus laurocerasus var. porpaczyi

Prunus laurocerasus var. porpaczyi, commonly known as the Porpaczyi cherry laurel, is a variant of the cherry laurel shrub native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to almost black in color. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are typically dark red or black. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature in gardens and landscapes.
