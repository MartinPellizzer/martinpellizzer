Pittosporum samoense

Pittosporum samoense, commonly known as the Tahitian pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the islands of French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or on exposed sites.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant in various regions.
