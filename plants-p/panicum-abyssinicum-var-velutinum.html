Panicum abyssinicum var. velutinum

Panicum abyssinicum var. velutinum, commonly known as velvet foxtail, is a grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and drooping inflorescences that give it a distinctive, foxtail-like appearance. The plant thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural fields. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, velvety texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

Velvet foxtail is sometimes considered a weed but is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in some regions.
