Pleurothallis patula

Pleurothallis patula, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically display shades of pink and white. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often clustered in loose inflorescences, and the leaves are usually elongated and lance-shaped.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
