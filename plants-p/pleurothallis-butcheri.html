Pleurothallis butcheri

Pleurothallis butcheri, commonly known as the Butcher's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its botanical features include narrow, strap-like leaves and a delicate, pendulous inflorescence that enhances its ornamental appeal.
