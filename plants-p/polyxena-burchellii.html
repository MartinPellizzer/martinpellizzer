Polyxena burchellii

Polyxena burchellii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Polyxena burchellii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
