Pentagonanthus grandiflorus

Pentagonanthus grandiflorus, commonly known as the five-angle flower or grandiflorous pentagonanthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
