Prunus chamaecerasus var. elatior

Prunus chamaecerasus var. elatior, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by sweet, juicy red or yellow fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible, flavorful fruits.
