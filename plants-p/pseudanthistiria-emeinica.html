Pseudanthistiria emeinica

Pseudanthistiria emeinica, commonly known as the Emei mountain aster, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from white to purple.

Pseudanthistiria emeinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
