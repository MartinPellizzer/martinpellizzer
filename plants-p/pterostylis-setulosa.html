Pterostylis setulosa

Pterostylis setulosa, commonly known as the hairy-veined greenhood, is an orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, often hairy stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single, greenish-brown flower with a distinctive hood-like structure, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and woodlands.

The species is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving native bees.
