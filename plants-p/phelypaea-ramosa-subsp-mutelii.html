Phelypaea ramosa subsp. mutelii

Phelypaea ramosa subsp.

mutelii, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It has finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, aiding in light absorption.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Its morphology is adapted for efficient nutrient uptake and photosynthesis in aquatic settings.
