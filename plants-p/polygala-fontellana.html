Polygala fontellana

Polygala fontellana, commonly known as the Fontellana milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Polygala fontellana is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
