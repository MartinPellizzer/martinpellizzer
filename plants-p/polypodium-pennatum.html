Polypodium pennatum

Polypodium pennatum, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with underground stems that allow it to spread and form dense colonies. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium pennatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
