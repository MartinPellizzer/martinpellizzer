Picris coronopifolia

Picris coronopifolia, commonly known as crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a flat, disc-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Picris coronopifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
