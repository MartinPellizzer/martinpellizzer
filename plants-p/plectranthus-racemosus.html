Plectranthus racemosus

Plectranthus racemosus, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
