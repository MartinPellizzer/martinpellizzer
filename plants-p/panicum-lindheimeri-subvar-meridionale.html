Panicum lindheimeri subvar. meridionale

Panicum lindheimeri subvar. meridionale, commonly known as the Texas panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems that often produce panicles of small, greenish-brown spikelets. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest in landscapes.

It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
