Plerandra leptophylla

Plerandra leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arum, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, spadix-like inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
