Prasophyllum hectorii

Prasophyllum hectorii, commonly known as Hector's leek, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or white flowers. This plant typically grows in damp, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. It is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in the South Island of New Zealand.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a threatened species.
