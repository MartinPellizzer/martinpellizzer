Pipturus argenteus var. lanosus

Pipturus argenteus var. lanosus, commonly known as the silverleaf holly, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is typically covered in a silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
