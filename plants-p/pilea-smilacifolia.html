Pilea smilacifolia

Pilea smilacifolia, commonly known as the creeping pilea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking white spadix inflorescence. The plant typically grows as a low, spreading ground cover, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is glossy and can vary in color from light green to deep emerald.

Pilea smilacifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
