Paradrymonia conferta

Paradrymonia conferta, commonly known as the broad-leafed daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are leathery, elliptical, and often have a bluish-green hue with a waxy coating. It produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in spring.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and winter hardiness.
