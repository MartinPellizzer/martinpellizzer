Potentilla tormentilla-elatior

Potentilla tormentilla elatior, commonly known as the tall cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rocky gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
