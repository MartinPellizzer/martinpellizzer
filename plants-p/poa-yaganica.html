Poa yaganica

Poa yaganica, commonly known as Yaganica grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and thrives in temperate climates. This perennial grass is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Poa yaganica is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
