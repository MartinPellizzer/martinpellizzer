Prumnopitys ferruginea

Prumnopitys ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are needle-like, arranged in spirals along the branches, and have a distinctive reddish-brown color when young. The tree produces small, woody cones that mature to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
