Prinos padifolius

Prinos padifolius, commonly known as the red-flowered prinos, is a shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
