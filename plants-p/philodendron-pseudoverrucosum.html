Philodendron pseudoverrucosum

Philodendron pseudoverrucosum, commonly known as the warty philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in warty or bumpy protrusions. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light. Its stems are typically thick and can climb using aerial roots.

Philodendron pseudoverrucosum is popular as a houseplant due to its striking foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
