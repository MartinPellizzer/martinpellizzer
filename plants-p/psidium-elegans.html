Psidium elegans

Psidium elegans, commonly known as the elegant guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and edible, aromatic fruits. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a yellow or red color, depending on the variety.
