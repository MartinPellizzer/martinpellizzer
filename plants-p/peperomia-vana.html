Peperomia vana

Peperomia vana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a distinctive silvery sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia vana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
