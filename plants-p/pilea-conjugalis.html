Pilea conjugalis

Pilea conjugalis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by berries containing seeds. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying properties.

Pilea conjugalis is often grown for its ornamental value and ease of care.
