Protea cyanoides

Protea cyanoides, commonly known as the blue protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The flowers are typically blue or purple in color, with a distinctive central style that protrudes from the center. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to regions with a Mediterranean climate.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of the Cape Floral Kingdom.
