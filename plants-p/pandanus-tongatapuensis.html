Pandanus tongatapuensis

Pandanus tongatapuensis, commonly known as the Tongatapu pandanus, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Tongatapu in the Tonga archipelago.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff leaves that form a rosette, often reaching heights of several meters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, which are clustered in a spadix surrounded by a leaf-like bract. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to the sandy soils of its native habitat.

This species is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance among the local communities.
