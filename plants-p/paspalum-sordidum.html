Paspalum sordidum

Paspalum sordidum, commonly known as the dirty grass or sordid paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

Paspalum sordidum is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and persistence.
