Peperomia sepicola

Peperomia sepicola, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light and minimal water conditions.

Peperomia sepicola is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for container gardening.
