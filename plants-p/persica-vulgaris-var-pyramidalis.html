Persica vulgaris var. pyramidalis

Persica vulgaris var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its upright, narrow growth habit.

It produces medium to large, sweet fruits with a firm flesh and a mild aroma. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in full sunlight with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive, compact form.

This variety is also valued for its fruit quality and adaptability to various climates.
