Pouteria gardneriana

Pouteria gardneriana, commonly known as the "Brazil nut tree," is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its capsule fruit, which contains the edible Brazil nuts. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in humid, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays a crucial role in the ecosystem by supporting a wide range of wildlife and contributing to the biodiversity of the rainforest.
