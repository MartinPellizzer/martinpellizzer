Pseudolophanthus pharica

Pseudolophanthus pharica, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Pseudolophanthus pharica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
