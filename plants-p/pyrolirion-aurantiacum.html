Pyrolirion aurantiacum

Pyrolirion aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange flame iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spike-like cluster and are known for their showy, spathaceous bracts.

Pyrolirion aurantiacum thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
