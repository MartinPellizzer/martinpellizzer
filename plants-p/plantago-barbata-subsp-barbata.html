Plantago barbata subsp. barbata

Plantago barbata subsp.

barbata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features a rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often ribbed and have a rough texture.

The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
