Polygonum langloisii

Polygonum langloisii, commonly known as the Langlois knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum langloisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
