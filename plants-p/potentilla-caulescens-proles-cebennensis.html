Potentilla caulescens proles cebennensis

Potentilla caulescens proles cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna shrub, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, compact form with numerous small, yellow flowers. The plant typically features wiry, branched stems covered in fine hairs and has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
