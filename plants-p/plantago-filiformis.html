Plantago filiformis

Plantago filiformis, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful in erosion control.
