Pleurophyllum splendens

Pleurophyllum splendens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and somewhat woody.

Pleurophyllum splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
