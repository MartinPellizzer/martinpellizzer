Paepalanthus vestitus var. caulescens

Paepalanthus vestitus var. caulescens, commonly known as the hairy pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with dense clusters of spiny, woolly stems that give it a pincushion appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh climates make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
