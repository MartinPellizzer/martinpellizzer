Papaya tunariensis

Papaya tunariensis, commonly known as the Tunari papaya, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruit. The fruit is typically oblong, with a soft, sweet pulp and black seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its edible fruit and potential medicinal properties.
