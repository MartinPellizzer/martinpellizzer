Pachycarpus viridiflorus

Pachycarpus viridiflorus, commonly known as the green-flowered thick-fruit plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in inflorescences.

It is primarily valued for its unique fruit structure and its role in local ecosystems.
