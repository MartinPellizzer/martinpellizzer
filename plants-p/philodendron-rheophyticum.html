Philodendron rheophyticum

Philodendron rheophyticum, commonly known as the stream philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. This species thrives in humid, shaded environments near water sources, often found in rainforests. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures.

The plant is well adapted to high moisture levels and is often found growing along streams or in wet, tropical climates.
