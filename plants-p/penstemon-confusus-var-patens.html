Penstemon confusus var. patens

Penstemon confusus var. patens, commonly known as the western penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in appearance.
