Peliosanthes altissima

Peliosanthes altissima, commonly known as the giant arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, waxy spathe. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Peliosanthes altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
