Panicum capillare var. miliaceum

Panicum capillare var. miliaceum, commonly known as millet grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, hair-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
