Penstemon eriantherus var. cleburnei

Penstemon eriantherus var. cleburnei, commonly known as Cleburne's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It produces attractive foliage with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
