Penstemon laetus var. roezlii

Penstemon laetus var. roezlii, commonly known as the Roezli's tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
