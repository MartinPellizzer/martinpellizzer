Pleopeltis linguiformis

Pleopeltis linguiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often divided into segments. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing soruses, are located on the underside of the leaves.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
