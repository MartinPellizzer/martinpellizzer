Piper amboinense var. latifolium

Piper amboinense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional cooking and herbal remedies.

The varietal form, latifolium, is distinguished by its broader foliage compared to the typical species.
