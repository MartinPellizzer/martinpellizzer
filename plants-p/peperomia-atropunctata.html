Peperomia atropunctata

Peperomia atropunctata, commonly known as the Black Dot Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with distinctive black or purple spots. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia atropunctata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
