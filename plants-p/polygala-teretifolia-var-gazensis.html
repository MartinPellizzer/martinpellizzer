Polygala teretifolia var. gazensis

Polygala teretifolia var. gazensis, commonly known as the Gazen's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.
