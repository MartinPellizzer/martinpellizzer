Pleurothallis eccentrica

Pleurothallis eccentrica, commonly known as the eccentric orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter in its natural habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
