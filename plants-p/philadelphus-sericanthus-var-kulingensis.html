Philadelphus sericanthus var. kulingensis

Philadelphus sericanthus var. kulingensis, commonly known as the Kuling honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, greyish-barked trunk. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its historical use in traditional Chinese medicine adds to its botanical and cultural significance.
