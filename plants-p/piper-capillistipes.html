Piper capillistipes

Piper capillistipes, commonly known as the slender-hairpipe pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and small, opposite leaves with fine, hair-like structures along the veins. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

While not widely cultivated for its fruit, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
