Prunella vulgaris f. taiwanalpina

Prunella vulgaris f. taiwanalpina, commonly known as the Taiwan alpine verbena, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in mountainous regions of Taiwan.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
