Paspalum malacophyllum var. glabrescens

Paspalum malacophyllum var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless or nearly so. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
