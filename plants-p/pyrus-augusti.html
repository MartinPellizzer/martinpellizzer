Pyrus augusti

Pyrus augusti, commonly known as the August pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, the August pear, is typically small, sweet, and edible, though it is not as widely cultivated as other pear varieties.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential in horticulture and plant breeding.
