Parashorea

Parashorea, commonly known as the cinnamom tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lauraceae.

These trees are native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. They are characterized by their aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Parashorea species typically grow as evergreen trees, reaching heights of up to 30 meters.

The genus includes several species, with Parashorea macrophylla being one of the most well-known for its valuable timber and aromatic properties.
