Papaver rhaeticum

Papaver rhaeticum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a black center, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stem is sturdy and often branched.

Papaver rhaeticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
