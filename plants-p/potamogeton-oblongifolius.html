Potamogeton oblongifolius

Potamogeton oblongifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender stems that often branch and bear small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that develop into dark, spherical fruits, which are dispersed by water currents. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton oblongifolius is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.
