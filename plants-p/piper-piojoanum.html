Piper piojoanum

Piper piojoanum, commonly known as the "pinworm pepper," is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green to yellowish fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is characterized by its unique ability to produce tiny, worm-like seeds, which give it its common name.
