Phyllanthus lanceolarius

Phyllanthus lanceolarius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy, with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, reddish berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
