Pimelea villosa subsp. villosa

Pimelea villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the silky bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of pink or red flowers that resemble bottlebrushes.

The plant has narrow, grey-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests. Pimelea villosa subsp.

villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
