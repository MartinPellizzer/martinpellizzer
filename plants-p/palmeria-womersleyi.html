Palmeria womersleyi

Palmeria womersleyi, commonly known as the Womersley's palmeria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are usually small and fleshy, helping to conserve water in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to extreme climates.
