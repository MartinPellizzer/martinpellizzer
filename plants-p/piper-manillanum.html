Piper manillanum

Piper manillanum, commonly known as the Manila pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries containing aromatic seeds. The berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
