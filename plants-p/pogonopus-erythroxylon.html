Pogonopus erythroxylon

Pogonopus erythroxylon, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its thick, dark bark and dense, fibrous roots.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing its seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay, making it important in traditional construction and crafts.

The plant also plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
