Poa versicolor var. araratica

Poa versicolor var. araratica, commonly known as the Araratico bluegrass, is a variant of the Poa versicolor species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled, with a dense, compact structure that helps it thrive in competitive environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
