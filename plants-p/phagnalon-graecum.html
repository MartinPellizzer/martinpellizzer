Phagnalon graecum

Phagnalon graecum, commonly known as Greek phagnalon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered leaves, with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
