Pseudorlaya maritima

Pseudorlaya maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in salty, windy environments. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, providing protection against harsh maritime conditions.

Pseudorlaya maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
