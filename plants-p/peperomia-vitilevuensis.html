Peperomia vitilevuensis

Peperomia vitilevuensis, commonly known as the Viti Levu peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Viti Levu in Fiji.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, typically in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
