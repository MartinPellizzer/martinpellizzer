Passiflora karsteniana

Passiflora karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a distinctive flavor. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Passiflora karsteniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
