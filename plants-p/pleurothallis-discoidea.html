Pleurothallis discoidea

Pleurothallis discoidea, commonly known as the disc-shaped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a unique growth habit, often found in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It is notable for its intricate floral structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
