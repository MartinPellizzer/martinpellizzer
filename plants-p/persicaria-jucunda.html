Persicaria jucunda

Persicaria jucunda, commonly known as swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetlands and marshy areas in North America.

It features erect, slender stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on tall, branching inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they are often covered in fine hairs.

Persicaria jucunda thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds.
