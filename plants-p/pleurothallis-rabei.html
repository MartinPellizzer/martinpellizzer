Pleurothallis rabei

Pleurothallis rabei, commonly known as the Rabei's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help it thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis rabei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
