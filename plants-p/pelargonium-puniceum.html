Pelargonium puniceum

Pelargonium puniceum, commonly known as the red geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Pelargonium puniceum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
