Polygala macrobotrya

Polygala macrobotrya, commonly known as the broad-leaf milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and shrubland habitats. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
