Pleurospermum capillaceum

Pleurospermum capillaceum, commonly known as the silky pleurospermum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
