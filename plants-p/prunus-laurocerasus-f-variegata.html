Prunus laurocerasus f. variegata

Prunus laurocerasus f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry laurel, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage with striking white or yellow variegation on the leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature, it is widely cultivated for landscaping purposes.
