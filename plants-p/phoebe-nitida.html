Phoebe nitida

Phoebe nitida, commonly known as the glossy phoebe, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Phoebe nitida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
