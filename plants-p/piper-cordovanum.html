Piper cordovanum

Piper cordovanum, commonly known as the Cordovan pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, peppery fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, ribbed berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
