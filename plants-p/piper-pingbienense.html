Piper pingbienense

Piper pingbienense, commonly known as the Guangxi pepper or Pingbien pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
