Pterocarpus cambodianus

Pterocarpus cambodianus, commonly known as Cambodian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its beauty and durability. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, flat pods containing seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, ovate leaflets.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
