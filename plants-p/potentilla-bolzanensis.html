Potentilla bolzanensis

Potentilla bolzanensis, commonly known as the Bolzanian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Potentilla bolzanensis thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.
