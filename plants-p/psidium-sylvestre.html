Psidium sylvestre

Psidium sylvestre, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a juicy pulp and numerous small seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
