Piper perditum

Piper perditum, commonly known as the lost pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many pepper species, but it is less commonly cultivated due to its rarity and limited commercial use. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Despite its botanical interest, it remains a relatively obscure species in both horticulture and scientific research.
