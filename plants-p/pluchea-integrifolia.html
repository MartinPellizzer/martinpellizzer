Pluchea integrifolia

Pluchea integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have smooth, uninterrupted margins, giving the plant its species name "integrifolia.". Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family.

The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions.
