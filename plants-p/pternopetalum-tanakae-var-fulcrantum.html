Pternopetalum tanakae var. fulcrantum

Pternopetalum tanakae var. fulcrantum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy dark green surface with a slightly waxy texture.

The tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and urban landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
