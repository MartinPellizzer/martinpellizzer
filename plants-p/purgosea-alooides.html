Purgosea alooides

Purgosea alooides, commonly known as the Cape mountain aloes, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
