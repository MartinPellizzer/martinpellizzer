Piper ripicola

Piper ripicola, commonly known as the rock pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, Piper ripicola is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
