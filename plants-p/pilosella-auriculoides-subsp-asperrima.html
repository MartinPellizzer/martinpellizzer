Pilosella auriculoides subsp. asperrima

Pilosella auriculoides subsp.

asperrima, commonly known as the rough pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The subspecies asperrima is distinguished by its rough, hairy texture and slightly elongated leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its drought tolerance and long-lasting blooms.
