Pelargonium × anacampton

Pelargonium × anacampton, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × anacampton prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for container gardening and hanging baskets.
