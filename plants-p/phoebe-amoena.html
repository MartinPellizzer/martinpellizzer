Phoebe amoena

Phoebe amoena, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Phoebe amoena is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.
