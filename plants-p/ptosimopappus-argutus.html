Ptosimopappus argutus

Ptosimopappus argutus, commonly known as the rough pappus bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Ptosimopappus argutus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
