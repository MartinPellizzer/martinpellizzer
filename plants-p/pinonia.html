Pinonia

Pinonia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to Asia, particularly found in regions like India and China.

It belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks. The plant has a unique structure with floating leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and religious rituals. Its roots, stems, and flowers are all utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices.

The botanical features of pinonia make it an important symbol in many Eastern cultures.
