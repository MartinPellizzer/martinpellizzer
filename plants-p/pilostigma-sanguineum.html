Pilostigma sanguineum

Pilostigma sanguineum, commonly known as the red pilostigma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in savanna and woodland habitats. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is often fissured and rough.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
