Panicum deschampsioides

Panicum deschampsioides, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
