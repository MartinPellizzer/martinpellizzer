Potentilla simulans

Potentilla simulans, commonly known as the mock sorrel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. Potentilla simulans thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and hardy addition to many garden landscapes.
