Pyrus aria var. silvestrii

Pyrus aria var. silvestrii, commonly known as the wild pear, is a variant of the pear tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
