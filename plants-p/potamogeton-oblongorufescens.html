Potamogeton oblongorufescens

Potamogeton oblongorufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and reddish on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, spongy fruits. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes, ponds, and lakes.

Potamogeton oblongorufescens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
