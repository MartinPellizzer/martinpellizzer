Peristrophe pubigera

Peristrophe pubigera, commonly known as the hairy peristrophe, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted grass-like appearance with hairy leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture.

Peristrophe pubigera is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
