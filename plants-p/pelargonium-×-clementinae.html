Pelargonium × clementinae

Pelargonium × clementinae, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a cross between species of the genus Pelargonium, resulting in a variety that exhibits a range of flower colors and forms. The plant typically features broad, lobed leaves and produces flowers in clusters, often in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a favored choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, Pelargonium × clementinae is widely cultivated for both decorative and horticultural purposes.
