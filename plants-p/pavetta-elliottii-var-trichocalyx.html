Pavetta elliottii var. trichocalyx

Pavetta elliottii var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a woolly appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
