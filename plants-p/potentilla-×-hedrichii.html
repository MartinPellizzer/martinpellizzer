Potentilla × hedrichii

Potentilla × hedrichii, commonly known as the hedrichii cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, compact foliage that often forms a rounded or conical shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in heads and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface, and it is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Potentilla × hedrichii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
