Plantago latibracteata

Plantago latibracteata, commonly known as the broad bract plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and prominent veins. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by bracts.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
