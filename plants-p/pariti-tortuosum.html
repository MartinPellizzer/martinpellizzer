Pariti tortuosum

Pariti tortuosum, commonly known as the wandering begonia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, trailing stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Pariti tortuosum is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor environments.
