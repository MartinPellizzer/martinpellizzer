Phagnalon × montserratense

Phagnalon × montserratense, commonly known as the hybrid meadow grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in open, sunny habitats like meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
