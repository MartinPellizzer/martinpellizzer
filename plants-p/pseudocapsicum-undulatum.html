Pseudocapsicum undulatum

Pseudocapsicum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating pseudocapsicum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its unique, undulating leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
