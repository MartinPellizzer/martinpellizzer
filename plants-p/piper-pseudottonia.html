Piper pseudottonia

Piper pseudottonia, commonly known as the African pepper or African piper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
