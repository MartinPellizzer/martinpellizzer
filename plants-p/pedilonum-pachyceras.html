Pedilonum pachyceras

Pedilonum pachyceras, commonly known as the broad-lobed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are sturdy and branching. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
