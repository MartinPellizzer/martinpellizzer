Panicum plenum

Panicum plenum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fluffy, purple-tinted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense panicles, which are its primary reproductive structures. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it blend into its natural habitat.

Panicum plenum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
