Phyllanthus crotalaroides

Phyllanthus crotalaroides, commonly known as the Indian mallow or false mallow, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.
