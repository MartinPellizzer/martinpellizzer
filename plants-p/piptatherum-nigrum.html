Piptatherum nigrum

Piptatherum nigrum, commonly known as black millet, is an annual grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark-colored grains and small, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in warm, dry climates. Its seeds are used as a staple food in some regions, valued for their nutritional content and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The plant's botanical features make it an important crop in arid agricultural systems.
