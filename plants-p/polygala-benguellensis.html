Polygala benguellensis

Polygala benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguela milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish and are arranged in clusters.

It is a herbaceous perennial that plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
