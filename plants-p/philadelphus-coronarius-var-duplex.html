Philadelphus coronarius var. duplex

Philadelphus coronarius var. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered tree of the mountain, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, double-flowered blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, white to pale yellow flower cluster. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators.
