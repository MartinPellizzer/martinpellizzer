Pleurothallis calcarata

Pleurothallis calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green. Its unique morphology and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is often found in high-altitude cloud forests.
