Pachysa formosa

Pachysa formosa, commonly known as the showy pachysandra, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense ground cover.

Pachysa formosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
