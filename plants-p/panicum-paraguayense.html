Panicum paraguayense

Panicum paraguayense, commonly known as Paraguay grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
