Platyzamia rigida

Platyzamia rigida, commonly known as the rigid fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, well-drained soils.

Platyzamia rigida plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
