Pleurothallis tuzae

Pleurothallis tuzae, commonly known as the Tuzae orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Pleurothallis tuzae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
