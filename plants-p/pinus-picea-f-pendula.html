Pinus picea f. pendula

Pinus picea f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a distinctive cultivar of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a unique, weeping appearance. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color, and it produces small, cone-shaped fruits. It thrives in cooler climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking visual form.

This variety is relatively hardy but requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
