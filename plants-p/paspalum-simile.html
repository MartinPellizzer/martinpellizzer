Paspalum simile

Paspalum simile, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Paspalum simile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
