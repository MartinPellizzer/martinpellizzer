Pyrethrum minimum

Pyrethrum minimum, commonly known as dwarf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in garden landscapes. Its flowers contain pyrethrins, natural compounds used in insecticides.

Pyrethrum minimum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
