Polymnia pyramidalis

Polymnia pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features upright, pyramid-shaped clusters of small, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Polymnia pyramidalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
