Pyrus wuzhishanensis

Pyrus wuzhishanensis, commonly known as the Wuzhishan pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing ornamental foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and temperate climates. Its fruits, though not widely cultivated, are prized for their unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
