Polygala retusa

Polygala retusa, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are arranged in clusters.

Polygala retusa is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
