Pelargonium × candidum

Pelargonium × candidum, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant for gardens and indoor spaces.

Pelargonium × candidum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
