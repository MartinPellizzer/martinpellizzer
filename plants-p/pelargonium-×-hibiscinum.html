Pelargonium × hibiscinum

Pelargonium × hibiscinum, commonly known as the scented geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its fragrant leaves, which emit a variety of scents such as citrus, mint, or rose. The plant typically has rounded, lobed leaves and produces showy, hibiscus-like flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms.

Pelargonium × hibiscinum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.
