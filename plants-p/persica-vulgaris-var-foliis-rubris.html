Persica vulgaris var. foliis-rubris

Persica vulgaris var. foliis-rubris, commonly known as the red-leaf peach.

This variant is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts vividly with its typical peach-like flowers and fruit. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cultivar of the domesticated peach tree, Prunus persica. Its leaves turn a deep crimson in the fall, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
