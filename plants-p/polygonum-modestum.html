Polygonum modestum

Polygonum modestum, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum modestum is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
