Perymenium sedasanum

Perymenium sedasanum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
