Pleopeltis × tricholepis

Pleopeltis × tricholepis, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pleopeltis and Tricholepis.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are usually long and arching, with a light green to bluish-green coloration.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in shaded areas.
