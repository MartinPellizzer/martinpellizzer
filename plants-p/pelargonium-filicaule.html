Pelargonium filicaule

Pelargonium filicaule, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have contributed to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
