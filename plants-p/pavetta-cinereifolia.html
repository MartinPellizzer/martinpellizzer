Pavetta cinereifolia

Pavetta cinereifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pavetta, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features simple, opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, with white or pale pink petals.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
