Pepo melopepo

Pepo melopepo, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its berry-like fruit, which is typically green when unripe and turns red or yellow when mature. The plant produces small, white or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Pepo melopepo is native to Central and South America and is often found in dry, open habitats.
