Pongatium indicum

Pongatium indicum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant has alternate, simple, and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in various cuisines.
