Prunus spachiana var. koshiensis

Prunus spachiana var. koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red drupes. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
