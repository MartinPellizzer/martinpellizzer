Primula hirtella

Primula hirtella, commonly known as the hairy primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
