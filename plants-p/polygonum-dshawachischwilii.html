Polygonum dshawachischwilii

Polygonum dshawachischwilii, commonly known as the Tibetan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stems. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
