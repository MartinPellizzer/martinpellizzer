Paphiopedilum rhizomatosum

Paphiopedilum rhizomatosum, commonly known as the wrinkled lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate lip that resembles a pouch. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a rosette around the rhizome.

This species is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
