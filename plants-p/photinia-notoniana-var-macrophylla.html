Photinia notoniana var. macrophylla

Photinia notoniana var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf photinia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually white or pink, followed by red or orange berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
