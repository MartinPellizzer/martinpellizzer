Photinia parvifolia var. tenuipes

Photinia parvifolia var. tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved photinia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually a glossy green, with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and hedging.
