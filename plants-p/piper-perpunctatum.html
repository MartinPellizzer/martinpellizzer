Piper perpunctatum

Piper perpunctatum, commonly known as the spotted pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white spots, and its small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Piper perpunctatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
