Piper glandulosum

Piper glandulosum, commonly known as the rough pepper or Indian pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and stimulant properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and medicinal uses.
