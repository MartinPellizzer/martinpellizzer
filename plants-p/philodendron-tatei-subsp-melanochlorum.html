Philodendron tatei subsp. melanochlorum

Philodendron tatei subsp.

melanochlorum, commonly known as the Black Chlorophyll Philodendron, is a rare and intriguing species native to the rainforests of Ecuador. It is characterized by its dark green, almost black leaves that give it its distinctive appearance.

The plant has a climbing habit and features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its foliage is often adorned with a waxy bloom, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant for cultivation.
