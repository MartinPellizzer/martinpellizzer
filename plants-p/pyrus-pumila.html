Pyrus pumila

Pyrus pumila, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or sand pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a small, edible pear that is typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
