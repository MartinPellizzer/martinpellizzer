Piper fragiliforme

Piper fragiliforme, commonly known as the fragrant vine or fragiliforme pepper, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, flexible stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, pubescent coating. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
