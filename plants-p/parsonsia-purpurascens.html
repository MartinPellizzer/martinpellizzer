Parsonsia purpurascens

Parsonsia purpurascens, commonly known as the red-flowered moonflower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color, which open at night. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a waxy texture.

Parsonsia purpurascens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flowering habit.
