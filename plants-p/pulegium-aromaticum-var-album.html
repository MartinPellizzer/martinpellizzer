Pulegium aromaticum var. album

Pulegium aromaticum var. album, commonly known as white heartwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.
