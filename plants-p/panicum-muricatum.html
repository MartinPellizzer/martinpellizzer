Panicum muricatum

Panicum muricatum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and vibrant purple or red bracts that give it its striking appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value or as a forage crop.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in various environmental conditions.
