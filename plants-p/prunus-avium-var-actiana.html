Prunus avium var. actiana

Prunus avium var. actiana, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy, single or double pink to white flowers in early spring. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a glossy green upper surface. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe that ripens in late summer.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
