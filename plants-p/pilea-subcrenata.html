Pilea subcrenata

Pilea subcrenata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, waxy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Pilea subcrenata is relatively low maintenance and is often appreciated for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
