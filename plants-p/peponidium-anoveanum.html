Peponidium anoveanum

Peponidium anoveanum, commonly known as the Anovean pumpkin, is a tropical plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and vibrant orange to yellow fruits that mature in the fall. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are typically unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is edible, often used in both culinary and medicinal applications in its native regions.
