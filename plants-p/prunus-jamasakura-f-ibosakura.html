Prunus jamasakura f. ibosakura

Prunus jamasakura f. ibosakura, commonly known as the Ibosakura cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white petals that bloom in early spring, creating a delicate and ethereal floral display. The plant is a compact shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy green color during the growing season.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
