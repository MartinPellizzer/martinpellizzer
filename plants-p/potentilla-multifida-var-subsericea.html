Potentilla multifida var. subsericea

Potentilla multifida var. subsericea, commonly known as the silky potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
