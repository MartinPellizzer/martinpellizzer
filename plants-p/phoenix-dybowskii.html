Phoenix dybowskii

Phoenix dybowskii, commonly known as the giant date palm, is a striking species native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though the fruit is not as widely cultivated as other date palms. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in warm climates around the world.
