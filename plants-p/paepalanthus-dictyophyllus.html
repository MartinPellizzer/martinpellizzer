Paepalanthus dictyophyllus

Paepalanthus dictyophyllus, commonly known as the spiny pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features a globular shape with dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a unique addition to desert flora.
