Polygala purpurea

Polygala purpurea, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala purpurea is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
