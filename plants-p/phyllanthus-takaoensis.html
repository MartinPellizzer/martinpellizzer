Phyllanthus takaoensis

Phyllanthus takaoensis, commonly known as the Takao phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the family Phyllanthaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is often found in subtropical climates and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
