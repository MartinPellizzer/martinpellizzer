Psephellus abchasicus

Psephellus abchasicus, commonly known as the Abkhazian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
