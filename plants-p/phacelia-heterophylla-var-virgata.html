Phacelia heterophylla var. virgata

Phacelia heterophylla var. virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's ghost or blue ghost, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
