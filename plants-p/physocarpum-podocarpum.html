Physocarpum podocarpum

Physocarpum podocarpum, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a succulent plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. Its fruit, known as a tunaf, is edible and has a sweet, sometimes tart flavor.

The species is also valued for its ornamental use and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
