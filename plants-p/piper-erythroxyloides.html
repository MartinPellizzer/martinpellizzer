Piper erythroxyloides

Piper erythroxyloides, commonly known as the redwood pepper or redwood vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by dark purple berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
