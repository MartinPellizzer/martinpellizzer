Plumbago lapathifolia

Plumbago lapathifolia, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Plumbago lapathifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
