Paspalum chapmanii

Paspalum chapmanii, commonly known as Chapman's grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates.

Paspalum chapmanii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife.
