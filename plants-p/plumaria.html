Plumaria

Plumaria, commonly known as the "flowering plum tree," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rutaceae.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and India, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible fruits resembling small plums. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge.

Plumaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which can be used in jams, desserts, and traditional medicine.
