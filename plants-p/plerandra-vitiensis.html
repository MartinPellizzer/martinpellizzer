Plerandra vitiensis

Plerandra vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Arum, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Vanuatu and Fiji.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively.

Plerandra vitiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, tropical environments.
