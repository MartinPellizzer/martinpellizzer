Penstemon triflorus var. integrifolius

Penstemon triflorus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped and smooth, with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, enhancing its ecological value.
