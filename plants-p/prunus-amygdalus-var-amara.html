Prunus amygdalus var. amara

Prunus amygdalus var. amara, commonly known as bitter almond.

This variant of the almond tree is characterized by its small, hard-shelled nuts that contain a high concentration of toxic hydrocyanic acid. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, while its flowers are typically pale pink or white.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in traditional medicine.
