Phyllanthus maritimus

Phyllanthus maritimus, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves and bright orange to red berries that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its berries are used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oils.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
