Pseudogynoxys Neovolubilis

Pseudogynoxys neovolubilis is a unique flowering plant native to the highlands of Mexico. It is known for its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. This species thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in open areas with ample sunlight. Its distinctive appearance has made it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively unknown to the general public.
