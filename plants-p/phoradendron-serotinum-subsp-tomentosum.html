Phoradendron serotinum subsp. tomentosum

Phoradendron serotinum subsp.

tomentosum, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of various host trees. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a silvery appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in humid, forested environments.

Its botanical structure includes both chlorophyll-containing leaves and specialized structures for absorbing nutrients from its host.
