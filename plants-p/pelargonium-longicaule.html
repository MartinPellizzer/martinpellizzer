Pelargonium longicaule

Pelargonium longicaule, commonly known as the tall geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated stems, which can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium longicaule is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
