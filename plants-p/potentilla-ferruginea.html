Potentilla ferruginea

Potentilla ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with five oval-shaped leaflets, often covered in a rusty-colored down. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in corymbs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
