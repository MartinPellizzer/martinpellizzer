Paronychia arabica var. longifolia

Paronychia arabica var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is typically found in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
