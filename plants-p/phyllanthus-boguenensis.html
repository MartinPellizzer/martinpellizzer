Phyllanthus boguenensis

Phyllanthus boguenensis, commonly known as the boguen's spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and latex production. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Phyllanthus boguenensis is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
