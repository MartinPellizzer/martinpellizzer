Piper gegarvum

Piper gegarvum, commonly known as the Malayan pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into berry-like fruits, which are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
