Potentilla egedii subsp. pacifica

Potentilla egedii subsp.

pacifica, commonly known as Pacific potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically features wiry, many-branched stems that are covered in fine, silvery hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface and a serrated margin.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks.
