Pinus × parapumila

Pinus × parapumila, commonly known as the cross between Pinus parviflora and Pinus mugo, is a hybrid conifer native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and short, twisted shoots. The needles are typically blue-green in color, often with a whitish or blue waxy coating, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and unique foliage.
