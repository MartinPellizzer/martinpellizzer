Pleurothallis hirtzii

Pleurothallis hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blooms that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are usually leathery and covered with fine, short hairs, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis hirtzii is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
