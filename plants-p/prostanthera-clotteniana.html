Prostanthera clotteniana

Prostanthera clotteniana, commonly known as the Clotted Anise or Clotted Mint, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pale violet to white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in herbal teas due to their mint-like scent.

Prostanthera clotteniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
