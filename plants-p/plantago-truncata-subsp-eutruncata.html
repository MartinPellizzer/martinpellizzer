Plantago truncata subsp. eutruncata

Plantago truncata subsp.

eutruncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is characterized by its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
