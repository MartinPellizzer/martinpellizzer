Plantago lanceolata var. bakeri

Plantago lanceolata var. bakeri, commonly known as Baker's plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive silver-white veins.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, typically found in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, clustered greenish flowers that develop into capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and role in ecological succession.
