Polygonum × bohemicum

Polygonum × bohemicum, commonly known as Bohemian knotweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish and can become woody with age, while its leaves are broad and have a smooth, glossy surface.

It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.
