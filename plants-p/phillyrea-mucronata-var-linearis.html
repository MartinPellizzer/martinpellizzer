Phillyrea mucronata var. linearis

Phillyrea mucronata var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
