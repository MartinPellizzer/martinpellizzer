Paepalanthus maculatus

Paepalanthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted puya, is a striking member of the Xerophytes family native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, yellow, and orange. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry climates and well-drained soils. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its arid habitat.

Paepalanthus maculatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and resilience.
