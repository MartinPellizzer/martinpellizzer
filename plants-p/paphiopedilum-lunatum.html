Paphiopedilum lunatum

Paphiopedilum lunatum, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, elongated flower with a distinctive, moon-shaped lip that is often pale green or white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

Paphiopedilum lunatum is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
