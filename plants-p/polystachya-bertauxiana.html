Polystachya bertauxiana

Polystachya bertauxiana, commonly known as the Bertaux's bamboo orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which helps it thrive in competitive habitats.

Polystachya bertauxiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
