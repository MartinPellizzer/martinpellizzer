Pseudodichanthium serrafalcoides

Pseudodichanthium serrafalcoides, commonly known as the serrated speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is occasionally used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
