Podistera albensis

Podistera albensis, commonly known as the white-flowered podistera, is a rare and endemic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and a unique pod-like structure that develops after flowering. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Due to habitat loss, Podistera albensis is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
