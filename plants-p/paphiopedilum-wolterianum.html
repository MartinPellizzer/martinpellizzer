Paphiopedilum wolterianum

Paphiopedilum wolterianum, commonly known as the Wolter's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-shaped lip, a defining feature of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow with a contrasting red or orange lip, and it blooms during the summer months.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
