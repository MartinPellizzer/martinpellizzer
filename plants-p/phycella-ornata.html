Phycella ornata

Phycella ornata, commonly known as the ornate phycella, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and yellow. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
