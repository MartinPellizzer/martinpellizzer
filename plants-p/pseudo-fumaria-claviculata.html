Pseudo-fumaria claviculata

Pseudo-fumaria claviculata, commonly known as the clustered fumaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing in woodland or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners interested in cultivating native flora.
