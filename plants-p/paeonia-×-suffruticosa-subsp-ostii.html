Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp. ostii

Paeonia × suffruticosa subsp.

ostii, commonly known as the Turkish peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its robust, multi-stemmed growth habit and produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its stems are covered in a thick layer of bark. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens.
