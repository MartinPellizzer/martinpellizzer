Potentilla verna f. platypetala

Potentilla verna f. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled sandwort, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is distinguished by its flattened petals, which give it its specific epithet "platypetala.".
