Poaephyllum selebicum

Poaephyllum selebicum, commonly known as the Sunda mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at elevations above 1,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of clustered, daisy-like flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
