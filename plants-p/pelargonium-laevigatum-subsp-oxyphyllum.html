Pelargonium laevigatum subsp. oxyphyllum

Pelargonium laevigatum subsp.

oxyphyllum, commonly known as the glossy leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its broad, dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white.

The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
