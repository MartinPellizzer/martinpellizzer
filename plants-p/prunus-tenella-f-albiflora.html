Prunus tenella f. albiflora

Prunus tenella f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its small, rounded form and profuse white flowers.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a naturally occurring form of Prunus tenella, which is native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy white blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for small gardens and containers.

The flowers appear in early spring, adding a delicate, fragrant touch to the landscape.
