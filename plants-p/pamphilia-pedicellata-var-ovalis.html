Pamphilia pedicellata var. ovalis

Pamphilia pedicellata var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet. The leaves are ovate and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
