Pourthiaea arguta var. latifolia

Pourthiaea arguta var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pourthiaea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers full sun and minimal water.
