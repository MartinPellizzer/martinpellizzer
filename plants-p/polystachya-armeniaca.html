Polystachya armeniaca

Polystachya armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian torchroot, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright orange or yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces tuberous roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Polystachya armeniaca is valued for its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in its native region.
