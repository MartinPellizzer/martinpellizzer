Peperomia huanucoana

Peperomia huanucoana, commonly known as the Huanuco peperomia, is a species native to the Peruvian Andes.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, ovate leaves. The leaves are typically green to reddish in color, often with a waxy texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the base of the plant.

This species is well adapted to high-altitude environments and requires well-drained soil and bright, indirect light.
