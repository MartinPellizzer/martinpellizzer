Pittosporum lacrymasepalum

Pittosporum lacrymasepalum, commonly known as the weeping sepal pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its drooping, white or cream-colored flowers that resemble small bells, often appearing in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and emit a fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum lacrymasepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
