Pandanus leptocaulis

Pandanus leptocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender, leafless stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The species is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
