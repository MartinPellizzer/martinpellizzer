Pelargonium zonale foliis-variegatis

Pelargonium zonale foliis-variegatis, commonly known as the variegated zonal geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive zonal patterns on the leaves, which feature contrasting color bands of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This ornamental plant is popular for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and containers.
