Potentilla arenaria subvar. eglandulosa

Potentilla arenaria subvar. eglandulosa, commonly known as sand cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arid and sandy environments.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has wiry, branched stems and small, oval leaves with a rough texture. It is well adapted to poor soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for rocky or sandy gardens.
