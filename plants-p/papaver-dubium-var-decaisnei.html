Papaver dubium var. decaisnei

Papaver dubium var. decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne's poppy, is a variant of the lesser poppy native to southern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its capsule fruits release seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.

The foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, adding to its distinctive botanical appearance.
