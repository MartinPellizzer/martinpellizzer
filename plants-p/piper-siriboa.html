Piper siriboa

Piper siriboa, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild long pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a drupe that contains a single seed, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
