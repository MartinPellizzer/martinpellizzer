Phaseolus caracalla

Phaseolus caracalla, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus caracalla is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its culinary uses.
