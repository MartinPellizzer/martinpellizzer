Pitcairnia harlingii

Pitcairnia harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
