Prunus brigantina

Prunus brigantina, commonly known as the Italian cherry or sweet cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by sweet, red or dark red fruits. Prunus brigantina is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruits, making it a popular choice in gardens and orchards.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
