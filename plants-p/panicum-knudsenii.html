Panicum knudsenii

Panicum knudsenii, commonly known as Knudsen's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences that produce wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it blend into its wetland habitat.

Panicum knudsenii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
