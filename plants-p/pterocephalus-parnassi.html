Pterocephalus parnassi

Pterocephalus parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often branched.

Pterocephalus parnassi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
