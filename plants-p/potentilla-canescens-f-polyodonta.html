Potentilla canescens f. polyodonta

Potentilla canescens f. polyodonta, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a variant of the silverleaf cinquefoil.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.

The inflorescence consists of multiple small flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, enhancing its visual appeal.
