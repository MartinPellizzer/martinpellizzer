Piper fistulosum

Piper fistulosum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically pollinated by insects.

Piper fistulosum is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
