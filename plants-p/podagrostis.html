Podagrostis

Podagrostis, commonly known as the grass of the genus Podagrostis, is a group of flowering plants belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are typically found in temperate regions and are characterized by their fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. Podagrostis species are often found in moist, disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in meadows. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny florets, typical of many grasses.

Due to their delicate appearance, they are sometimes cultivated in ornamental gardens for their visual appeal.
