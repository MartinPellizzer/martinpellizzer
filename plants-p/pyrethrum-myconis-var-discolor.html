Pyrethrum myconis var. discolor

Pyrethrum myconis var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its strikingly variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and natural insect-repelling properties, it is both aesthetically pleasing and functional in horticulture.
