Phyllanthus decipiens f. trilocularis

Phyllanthus decipiens f. trilocularis, commonly known as the three-chambered stonecrop, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to arid regions of southern Africa. The plant's unique feature is its trilocular fruit, which consists of three chambers, a trait that distinguishes this form from other Phyllanthus species. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental leaves make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
