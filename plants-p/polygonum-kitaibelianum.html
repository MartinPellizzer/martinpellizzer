Polygonum kitaibelianum

Polygonum kitaibelianum, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible parts. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Polygonum kitaibelianum is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
