Paraspalathus filicaulis

Paraspalathus filicaulis, commonly known as the slender-leafed spathaceous plant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that form a rosette structure, which helps in water retention. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that emerge from the center of the rosette. It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
