Philadelphus pulchellus

Philadelphus pulchellus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green and hairy beneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Philadelphus pulchellus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
