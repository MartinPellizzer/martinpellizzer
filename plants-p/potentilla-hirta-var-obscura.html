Potentilla hirta var. obscura

Potentilla hirta var. obscura, commonly known as the dark hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features dense, hairy foliage that is typically a dark green color, with small, rounded leaves arranged in clusters of five. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
