Pseuderanthemum pihuamoense

Pseuderanthemum pihuamoense, commonly known as the Pihuamo beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-blue bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
