Polygonum persicaria subsp. agreste

Polygonum persicaria subsp.

agreste, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family. It is characterized by its upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive reddish sheen.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions. Polygonum persicaria subsp.

agreste is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.
