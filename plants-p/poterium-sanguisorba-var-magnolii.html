Poterium sanguisorba var. magnolii

Poterium sanguisorba var. magnolii, commonly known as the Japanese bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, reddish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that resemble blood droplets, which appear in early spring. Its root system is thick and fleshy, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
