Polypodium fimbriatum

Polypodium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed maidenhair fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a feathery appearance, with a distinctive fringed margin.

Polypodium fimbriatum thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.
