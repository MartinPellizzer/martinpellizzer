Ptilotus pseudohelipteroides

Ptilotus pseudohelipteroides, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its unique, small, clustered inflorescences that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plant is an annual herb with hairy, branching stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions and drought.

The ghost flower is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
