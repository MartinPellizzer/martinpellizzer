Penstemon amplus

Penstemon amplus, commonly known as the broadleaf penstemon, is a species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a grayish or blue-green hue due to a waxy coating.

Penstemon amplus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
