Platylepis leucantha var. minor

Platylepis leucantha var. minor, commonly known as the lesser white-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
