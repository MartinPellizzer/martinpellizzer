Pycreus niger subsp. elegantulus

Pycreus niger subsp.

elegantulus, commonly known as the black velvet orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its dark, velvety petals that range from deep black to dark purple, giving it a dramatic appearance.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in dense clusters that enhance its visual impact.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its unique and exotic appearance.
