Pomaderris velutina

Pomaderris velutina, commonly known as silky pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, soft, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry to wet sclerophyll forests and woodlands. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, hairy fruits.

Pomaderris velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
