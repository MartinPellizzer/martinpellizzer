Piddingtonia nummularia

Piddingtonia nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features rounded, coin-shaped leaves that are typically a silvery-gray color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
