Pennisetum aristidoides

Pennisetum aristidoides, commonly known as blue fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that forms dense, compact clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking color and drought tolerance.
