Panicum simpliciflorum

Panicum simpliciflorum, commonly known as flatflower grass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, spike-like inflorescence that consists of tightly packed, narrow spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are often slightly hairy.

Panicum simpliciflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
