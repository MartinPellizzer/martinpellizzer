Phymatodes triloba

Phymatodes triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed phymatode, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
