Polygala membranacea

Polygala membranacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the flowers are often pink or purple in color.

Polygala membranacea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and small animals.
