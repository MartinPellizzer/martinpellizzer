Piper massiei

Piper massiei, commonly known as the Massai pepper, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, curved berries.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and have a peppery flavor, making them valuable in local cuisines.
