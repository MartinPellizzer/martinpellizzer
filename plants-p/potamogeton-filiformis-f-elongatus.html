Potamogeton filiformis f. elongatus

Potamogeton filiformis f. elongatus, commonly known as the elongated pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, thread-like leaves.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has a slender stem that can extend several meters, supporting its elongated leaves which are often spirally arranged. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its morphology is adapted to low-light conditions, allowing it to thrive in deeper water bodies.
