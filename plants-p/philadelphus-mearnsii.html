Philadelphus mearnsii

Philadelphus mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and showy blooms.
