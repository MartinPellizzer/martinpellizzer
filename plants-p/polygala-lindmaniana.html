Polygala lindmaniana

Polygala lindmaniana, commonly known as the Lindman's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with distinctive, spurred petals.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
