Phragmites karka var. cinctus

Phragmites karka var. cinctus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland environments.

It is a tall, grass-like perennial plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The leaves are narrower and more rigid compared to the typical variety, with a distinctive grayish-white stripe running down the central vein. It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along shorelines.

This plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial species.
