Passiflora ligularis var. lobata

Passiflora ligularis var. lobata, commonly known as the lobate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and showy, colorful flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers typically feature five sepals, five petals, and a central crown of filaments, which are adorned with bright blue and violet markings. It produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
