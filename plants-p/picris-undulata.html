Picris undulata

Picris undulata, commonly known as the wavy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant features yellow composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, with ray flowers surrounding a central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a wavy margin, giving it its common name.

The species is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
