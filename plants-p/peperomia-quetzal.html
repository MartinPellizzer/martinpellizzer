Peperomia quetzal

Peperomia quetzal, commonly known as the Quetzal peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of pink or purple, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
