Parosela lagopina

Parosela lagopina, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions.

Parosela lagopina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
