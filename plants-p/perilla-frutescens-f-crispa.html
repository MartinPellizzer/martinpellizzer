Perilla frutescens f. crispa

Perilla frutescens f. crispa, commonly known as curled perilla or Japanese shiso, is a cultivar of the perilla plant characterized by its crinkled, lobed leaves.

It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant is an annual herb that grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are rich in essential oils, giving them a distinctive flavor and aroma.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and small-scale cultivation.
