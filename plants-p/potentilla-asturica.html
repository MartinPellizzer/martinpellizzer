Potentilla asturica

Potentilla asturica, commonly known as the Asturian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and rugged, shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and it produces fruits that are aggregate berries.

Potentilla asturica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
