Passiflora aetheoantha

Passiflora aetheoantha, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with intricate patterns of red and yellow, which are adorned with silky, velvety filaments. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora aetheoantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
