Phlomoides impressa

Phlomoides impressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually violet or blue in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
