Pisonia rotundata subsp. acutiuscula

Pisonia rotundata subsp.

acutiuscula, commonly known as the sharp-toothed pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indian Ocean and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white bract-like flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture and is often found growing in coastal or mangrove habitats. It produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
