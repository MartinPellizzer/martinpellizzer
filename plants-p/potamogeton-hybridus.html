Potamogeton hybridus

Potamogeton hybridus, commonly known as the hybrid pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are often submerged or partially emergent. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and light penetration.
