Piper septemnerve

Piper septemnerve, commonly known as the seven-veined pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive seven main veins in its leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and practical uses.
