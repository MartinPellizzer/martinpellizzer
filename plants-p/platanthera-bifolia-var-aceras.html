Platanthera bifolia var. aceras

Platanthera bifolia var. aceras, commonly known as the fragrant lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It features a slender, branching stem with numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose, loose spiral or raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight sheen.

It is known for its subtle, sweet fragrance, which is most noticeable in the evening.
