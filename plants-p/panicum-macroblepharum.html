Panicum macroblepharum

Panicum macroblepharum, commonly known as the large-eyed panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, hairy bracts that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Panicum macroblepharum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
