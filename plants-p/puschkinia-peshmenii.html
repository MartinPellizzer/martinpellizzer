Puschkinia peshmenii

Puschkinia peshmenii, commonly known as the Armenian squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale blue flowers in early spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens. It is relatively low maintenance and thrives in full sun.

Puschkinia peshmenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize over time.
