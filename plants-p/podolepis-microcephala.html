Podolepis microcephala

Podolepis microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed podolepis, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a short, fibrous root system.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
