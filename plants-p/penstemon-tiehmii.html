Penstemon tiehmii

Penstemon tiehmii, commonly known as Tiehm's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Penstemon tiehmii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
