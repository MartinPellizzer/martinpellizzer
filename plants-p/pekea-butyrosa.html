Pekea butyrosa

Pekea butyrosa, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to small apples, with a crisp, juicy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pekea butyrosa is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible yield.
