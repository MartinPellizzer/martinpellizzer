Pilosella nigriseta

Pilosella nigriseta, commonly known as black-hairy groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils, thriving in open habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Pilosella nigriseta produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators.
