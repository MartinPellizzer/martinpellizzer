Poa chaixii var. purpurascens

Poa chaixii var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flowering meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, purple to pink flowers during the summer months, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various pollinators and small mammals.
