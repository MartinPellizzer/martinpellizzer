Psilochilus tuerckheimii

Psilochilus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's psilochilus, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
