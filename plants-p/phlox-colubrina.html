Phlox colubrina

Phlox colubrina, commonly known as Carolina phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
