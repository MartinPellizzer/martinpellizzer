Piper candollei

Piper candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's pepper or the Mexican pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ethnobotanical research.
