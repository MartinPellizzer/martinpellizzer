Potamogeton × nitens f. flumineus

Potamogeton × nitens f. flumineus, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early summer. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters such as rivers, lakes, and ponds. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
