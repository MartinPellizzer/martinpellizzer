Peperomia ukingensis

Peperomia ukingensis, commonly known as the Ukingensis Peperomia, is a species native to East Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and display a range of colors, from deep green to reddish hues. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal water requirements.
