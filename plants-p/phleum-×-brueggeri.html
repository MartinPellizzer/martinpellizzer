Phleum × brueggeri

Phleum × brueggeri, commonly known as Timothy grass, is a hybrid grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, blue-green leaves, which are typically found in temperate regions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Timothy grass is often used in forage agriculture due to its high nutritional value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological settings by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
