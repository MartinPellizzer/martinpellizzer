Picris tenuis

Picris tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in a spherical shape.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
