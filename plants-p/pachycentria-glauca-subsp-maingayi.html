Pachycentria glauca subsp. maingayi

Pachycentria glauca subsp.

maingayi, commonly known as the Maingay's bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
