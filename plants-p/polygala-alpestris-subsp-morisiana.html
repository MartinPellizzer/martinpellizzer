Polygala alpestris subsp. morisiana

Polygala alpestris subsp.

morisiana, commonly known as the Alpine milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rugged terrains.
