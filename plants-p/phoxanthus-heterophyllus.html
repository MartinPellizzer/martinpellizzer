Phoxanthus heterophyllus

Phoxanthus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable phlox, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on environmental conditions.

Phoxanthus heterophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
