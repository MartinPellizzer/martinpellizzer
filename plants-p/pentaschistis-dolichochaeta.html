Pentaschistis dolichochaeta

Pentaschistis dolichochaeta, commonly known as the long-hair grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control and soil stabilization.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.
