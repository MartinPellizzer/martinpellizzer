Phalaris arundinacea var. variegata

Phalaris arundinacea var. variegata, commonly known as variegated reed canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. Its fibrous roots make it a valuable plant for erosion control and habitat restoration.

The flowers are small and green, arranged in dense spikelets, and it blooms in late summer to early autumn.
