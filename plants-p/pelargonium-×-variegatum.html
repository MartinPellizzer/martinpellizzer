Pelargonium × variegatum

Pelargonium × variegatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its colorful, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with white, yellow, or cream markings. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for gardens and containers due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
