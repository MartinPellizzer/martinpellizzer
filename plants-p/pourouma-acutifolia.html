Pourouma acutifolia

Pourouma acutifolia, commonly known as the "palm tree of the Amazon," is a tall, slender palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, pinnate leaves and smooth, grayish trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small, round oranges. Its botanical features make it well adapted to the humid, shaded environments of the rainforest.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
