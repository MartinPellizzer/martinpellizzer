Pimelea nana

Pimelea nana, commonly known as dwarf geebung, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is typically found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It is well adapted to coastal and dry environments, showing resilience to salt spray and drought.

Pimelea nana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
