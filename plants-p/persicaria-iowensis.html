Persicaria iowensis

Persicaria iowensis, commonly known as Iowa knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria iowensis is often found in moist to wet habitats such as prairies, marshes, and along stream banks.
