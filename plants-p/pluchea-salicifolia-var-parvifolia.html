Pluchea salicifolia var. parvifolia

Pluchea salicifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its preference for wetland environments.
