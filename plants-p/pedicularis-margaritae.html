Pedicularis margaritae

Pedicularis margaritae, commonly known as the pearl gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are tubular and often have a distinctive white or yellow throat, which is a key identifying feature.

Pedicularis margaritae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
