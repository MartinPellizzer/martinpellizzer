Picridium cernuum

Picridium cernuum, commonly known as the drooping picrid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Picridaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The drooping picrid is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens.
