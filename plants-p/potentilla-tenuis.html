Potentilla tenuis

Potentilla tenuis, commonly known as slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically has wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky slopes.

Potentilla tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and early blooming habit.
