Polypogon viridis

Polypogon viridis, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
