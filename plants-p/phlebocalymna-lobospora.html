Phlebocalymna lobospora

Phlebocalymna lobospora, commonly known as the coral-like lichen, is a foliose lichen that exhibits a distinctive, branched thallus with a pale to reddish coloration.

It typically grows on bark and soil in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The thallus is composed of a symbiotic association between a photosynthetic alga and a fungal partner, which contributes to its unique structure and color. The reproductive structures, called soredia, are dispersed by wind and aid in the lichen's propagation.

This species is notable for its intricate morphology and ecological role in nutrient cycling within forest ecosystems.
