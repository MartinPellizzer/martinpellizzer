Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. meoriensis

Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. meoriensis, commonly known as the Meoriensis Phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
