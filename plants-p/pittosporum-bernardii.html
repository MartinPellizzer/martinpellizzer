Pittosporum bernardii

Pittosporum bernardii, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum bernardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
