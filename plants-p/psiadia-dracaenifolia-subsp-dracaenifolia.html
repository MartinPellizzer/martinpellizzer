Psiadia dracaenifolia subsp. dracaenifolia

Psiadia dracaenifolia subsp.

dracaenifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf false arum, is a species native to the Canary Islands. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
