Piptocarpha lechleri

Piptocarpha lechleri, commonly known as the caapi vine, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is notable for its use in traditional medicine, particularly in the preparation of Ayahuasca, a psychoactive brew. Its leaves and stems contain alkaloids that contribute to its psychoactive properties.

The botanical structure of Piptocarpha lechleri supports its role in both ecological and cultural contexts within the Amazon region.
