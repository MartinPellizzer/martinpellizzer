Polypodium forsythianum

Polypodium forsythianum, commonly known as the Chinese cliff fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, mat-like growth. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes or cliffs, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a distinctive, segmented appearance.

Polypodium forsythianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
