Plectranthus bolusii

Plectranthus bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus sedge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
