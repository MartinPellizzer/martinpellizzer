Petroselinum trifoliatum

Petroselinum trifoliatum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like carrots and dill. Its leaves are used extensively in cooking for their fresh, slightly peppery flavor.

Parsley also has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
