Prinodia sideroxyloides

Prinodia sideroxyloides, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
