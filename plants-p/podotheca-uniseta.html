Podotheca uniseta

Podotheca uniseta, commonly known as the single-set pod plant, is a unique species within the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, single-seeded pods that remain attached to the plant for an extended period. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and slightly hairy.

The flowers are small and usually pale in color, contributing to its subtle appearance in its natural habitat.
