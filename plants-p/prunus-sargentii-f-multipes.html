Prunus sargentii f. multipes

Prunus sargentii f. multipes, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a unique hybrid form of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible cherries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, offering genetic diversity within the Prunus family.
