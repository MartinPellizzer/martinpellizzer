Piper simulans

Piper simulans, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supports. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while the small green flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit, a small, dark purple berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
