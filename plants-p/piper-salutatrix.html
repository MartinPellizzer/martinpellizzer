Piper salutatrix

Piper salutatrix, commonly known as the sacred pepper or holy pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
