Phyllanthus oblongifolius

Phyllanthus oblongifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, and elliptical-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a round, yellowish berry that turns red when ripe.

The plant is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and traditional medicinal uses.
