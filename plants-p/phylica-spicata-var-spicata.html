Phylica spicata var. spicata

Phylica spicata var. spicata, commonly known as the spiked bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange floral spikes that resemble bottle brushes, which are particularly striking during the flowering season. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal and garden landscapes.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
