Parietaria judaica var. brevipetiolata

Parietaria judaica var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled Jewish wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or wall crevices, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with short petioles, and it produces tiny, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to urban environments.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
