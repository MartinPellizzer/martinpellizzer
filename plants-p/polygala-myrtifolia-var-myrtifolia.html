Polygala myrtifolia var. myrtifolia

Polygala myrtifolia var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that often branch near the base. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
