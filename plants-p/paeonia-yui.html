Paeonia yui

Paeonia yui, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its lush, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a compact, bushy form. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and red, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

Paeonia yui prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
