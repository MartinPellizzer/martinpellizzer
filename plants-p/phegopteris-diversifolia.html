Phegopteris diversifolia

Phegopteris diversifolia, commonly known as the variegated water fern, is a species of fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Phegopteris diversifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
