Paeonia daurica subsp. daurica

Paeonia daurica subsp.

daurica, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia. It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring.

The plant has palmately lobed leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit and reliable flowering in suitable climates.
