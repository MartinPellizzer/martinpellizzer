Penstemon clevelandii subsp. connatus

Penstemon clevelandii subsp.

connatus, commonly known as the Cleveland's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
