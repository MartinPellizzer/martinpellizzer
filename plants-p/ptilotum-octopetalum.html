Ptilotum octopetalum

Ptilotum octopetalum, commonly known as the eight-petaled flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with eight petals, which are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
