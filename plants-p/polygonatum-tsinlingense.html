Polygonatum tsinlingense

Polygonatum tsinlingense, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or greenish flowers in clusters. Its berries are typically red or black and contain several seeds.

Polygonatum tsinlingense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
