Paspalum helleri

Paspalum helleri, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months.

Paspalum helleri is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
