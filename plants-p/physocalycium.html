Physocalycium

Physocalycium, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. They have simple, alternate leaves and produce berries that contain toxic compounds.

Physocalycium species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in warm climates.
