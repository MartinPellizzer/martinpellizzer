Potamogeton machianus

Potamogeton machianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and slow-moving rivers.
