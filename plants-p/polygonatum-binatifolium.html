Polygonatum binatifolium

Polygonatum binatifolium, commonly known as the twin-leaved Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features grass-like leaves that are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are typically white or greenish-white, arranged in loose racemes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The berries that follow are small, red, and edible, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
