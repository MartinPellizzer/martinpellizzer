Psilosiphon schimperi

Psilosiphon schimperi, commonly known as the African ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic fern species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with spores dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Psilosiphon schimperi is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
