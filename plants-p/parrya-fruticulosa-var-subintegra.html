Parrya fruticulosa var. subintegra

Parrya fruticulosa var. subintegra, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, spiny foliage with dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to rocky and dry environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
