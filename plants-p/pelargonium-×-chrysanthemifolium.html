Pelargonium × chrysanthemifolium

Pelargonium × chrysanthemifolium, commonly known as the chrysanthemum-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from different pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The leaves are distinctive, with a deep green color and a crinkled, fern-like appearance that resembles chrysanthemum foliage. This plant is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental, thriving in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil.

It is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners and indoor plant enthusiasts.
