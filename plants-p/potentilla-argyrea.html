Potentilla argyrea

Potentilla argyrea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders.

Potentilla argyrea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
