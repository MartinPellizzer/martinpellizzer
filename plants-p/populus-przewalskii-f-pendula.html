Populus przewalskii f. pendula

Populus przewalskii f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a distinctive cultivar of the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that droop gracefully, creating a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green during the growing season. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming more rugged with age.
