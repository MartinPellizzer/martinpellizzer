Panicum spiciforme

Panicum spiciforme, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or reddish in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season.

Panicum spiciforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
