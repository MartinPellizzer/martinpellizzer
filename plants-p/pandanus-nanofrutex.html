Pandanus nanofrutex

Pandanus nanofrutex, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a small shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and stiff, fibrous leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often growing in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
