Potamogeton pseudopolygonus

Potamogeton pseudopolygonus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water quality and navigation.
