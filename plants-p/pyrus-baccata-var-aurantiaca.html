Pyrus baccata var. aurantiaca

Pyrus baccata var. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The fruit is typically orange or red, adding visual interest in the fall. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
