Pterolobium abyssinicum

Pterolobium abyssinicum, commonly known as the Abyssinian senna, is a leguminous shrub native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, hard seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
