Phyllodoce nipponica subsp. tsugifolia

Phyllodoce nipponica subsp.

tsugifolia, commonly known as the Japanese mountain sorrel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the forests of Japan. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
