Panicum silvestre

Panicum silvestre, commonly known as wild panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant produces slender, cylindrical spikes of small, spikelet-like flowers that are often tinged with red or purple. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.

Panicum silvestre is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
