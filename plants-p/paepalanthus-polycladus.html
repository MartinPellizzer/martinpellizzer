Paepalanthus polycladus

Paepalanthus polycladus, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, edible fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
