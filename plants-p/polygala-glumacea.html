Polygala glumacea

Polygala glumacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pale blue or purple, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters.

It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
