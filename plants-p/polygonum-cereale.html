Polygonum cereale

Polygonum cereale, commonly known as prostrate knotweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is characterized by its prostrate or decumbent growth habit. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum cereale is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of streams, where it thrives in humid conditions.
