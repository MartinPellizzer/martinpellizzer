Papaya pubescens

Papaya pubescens, commonly known as the hairy papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are typically smaller and more fibrous compared to common papayas. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
