Parakeelya creethiae

Parakeelya creethiae, commonly known as the rainbow plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble a rainbow, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture.

It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.
