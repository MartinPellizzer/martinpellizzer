Piptochaetium hackelii

Piptochaetium hackelii, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to low rainfall conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that bears small, two-lobed glumes.

Piptochaetium hackelii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
