Psilostrophe tagetina var. cerifera

Psilostrophe tagetina var. cerifera, commonly known as the candleplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a waxy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its ornamental value in gardens.
