Phalaenopsis esmeralda

Phalaenopsis esmeralda, commonly known as the Emerald Phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous flower spikes that bear clusters of fragrant, white or pale green flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that help it store water in its native tropical habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

This orchid is prized for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
