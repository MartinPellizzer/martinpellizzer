Poa aulacosperma

Poa aulacosperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often densely tufted at the base, with narrow, linear leaves that are usually hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Poa aulacosperma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
