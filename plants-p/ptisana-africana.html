Ptisana africana

Ptisana africana, commonly known as African star grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, often in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators.

Ptisana africana is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
