Prescottia colorans

Prescottia colorans, commonly known as the rainbow flower, is a unique member of the Amaryllis family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of strap-shaped leaves that are often variegated with green and white markings.

Prescottia colorans is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
