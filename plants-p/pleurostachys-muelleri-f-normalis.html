Pleurostachys muelleri f. normalis

Pleurostachys muelleri f. normalis, commonly known as the Mueller's pleurostachys, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family.

It is a terrestrial bromeliad characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and strap-like. The plant produces striking, spathaceous inflorescences that are usually brightly colored, often in shades of red or orange. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention and protection against herbivores.

This species is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
