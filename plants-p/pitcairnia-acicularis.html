Pitcairnia acicularis

Pitcairnia acicularis, commonly known as the needle-leaf pitcairnia, is a striking epiphytic bromeliad native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and long, narrow leaves that resemble needles, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks. Its flowers, which are typically violet or blue, are highly ornamental and attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
