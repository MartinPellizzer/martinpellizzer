Panicum mucronatum

Panicum mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects or as a forage plant for livestock.
