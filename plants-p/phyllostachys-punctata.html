Phyllostachys punctata

Phyllostachys punctata, commonly known as the bamboo fern, is a type of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems and fern-like leaves. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its graceful appearance.

Phyllostachys punctata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
