Phaca hypoglottis

Phaca hypoglottis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed phaca, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, a common feature of the Apiaceae family.
