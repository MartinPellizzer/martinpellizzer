Pilea tobagensis

Pilea tobagensis, commonly known as the Toba pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Pilea tobagensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
