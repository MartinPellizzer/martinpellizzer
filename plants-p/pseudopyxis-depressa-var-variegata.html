Pseudopyxis depressa var. variegata

Pseudopyxis depressa var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than its fruit.
