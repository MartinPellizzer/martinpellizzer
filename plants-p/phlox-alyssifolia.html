Phlox alyssifolia

Phlox alyssifolia, commonly known as the lesser phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are slender and often branched.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and blooms.
