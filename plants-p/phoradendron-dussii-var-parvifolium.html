Phoradendron dussii var. parvifolium

Phoradendron dussii var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually clustered at the tips of its branches. The plant produces white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. It relies on its host for water and nutrients but can photosynthesize to some extent.

This species plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and other animals.
