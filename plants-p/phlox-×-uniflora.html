Phlox × uniflora

Phlox × uniflora, commonly known as the garden phlox, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink, red, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with upright, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Botanically, it is a member of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
