Pentagonia pachiteana

Pentagonia pachiteana, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescences that resemble small bells, which appear in clusters during the flowering season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pentagonia pachiteana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
