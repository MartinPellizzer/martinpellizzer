Piscidia havanensis

Piscidia havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
