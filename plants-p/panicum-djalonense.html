Panicum djalonense

Panicum djalonense, commonly known as the West African millet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers. It is adapted to grow in dry, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Panicum djalonense is an important forage plant for livestock and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
