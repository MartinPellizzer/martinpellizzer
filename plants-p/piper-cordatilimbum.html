Piper cordatilimbum

Piper cordatilimbum, commonly known as the heart-shaped pepper or broad-leafed pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

Piper cordatilimbum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
