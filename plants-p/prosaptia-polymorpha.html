Prosaptia polymorpha

Prosaptia polymorpha, commonly known as the variable tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots form a deep taproot, allowing it to access groundwater and survive prolonged droughts.

Prosaptia polymorpha plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
