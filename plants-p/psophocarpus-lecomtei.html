Psophocarpus lecomtei

Psophocarpus lecomtei, commonly known as the Lecomte's climbing bean, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
