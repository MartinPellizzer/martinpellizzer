Pseuderanthemum tunicatum var. tunicatum

Pseuderanthemum tunicatum var. tunicatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a prominent central vein and a waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.
