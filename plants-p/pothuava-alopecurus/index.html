Pothuava Alopecurus

Pothuava alopecurus is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Asia. It is known for its small, star-shaped blooms that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in forest understories. Due to habitat loss, it is now classified as endangered by conservation organizations. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
