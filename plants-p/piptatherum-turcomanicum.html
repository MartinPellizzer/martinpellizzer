Piptatherum turcomanicum

Piptatherum turcomanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Piptatherum turcomanicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
