Polystichopsis mutica

Polystichopsis mutica, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are short and stout, supporting the growth of its fertile and sterile fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
