Polygonum meissneri

Polygonum meissneri, commonly known as Meissner's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it is often found in moist, shady environments.

Polygonum meissneri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.
