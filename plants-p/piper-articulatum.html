Piper articulatum

Piper articulatum, commonly known as the articulated pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twisting stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often containing a single seed.
