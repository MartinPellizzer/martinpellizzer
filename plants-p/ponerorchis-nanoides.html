Ponerorchis nanoides

Ponerorchis nanoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to wetland and marsh habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces greenish-yellow or brownish-purple flowers arranged in a spike. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, nutrient-poor soils.

Ponerorchis nanoides is an ephemeral species, with a short growing season and a reliance on specific environmental conditions for successful reproduction.
