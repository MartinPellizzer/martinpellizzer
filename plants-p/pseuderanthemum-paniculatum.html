Pseuderanthemum paniculatum

Pseuderanthemum paniculatum, commonly known as the panicled pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, two-lipped flowers in shades of white and purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
