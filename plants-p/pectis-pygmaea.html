Pectis pygmaea

Pectis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a small flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 inches, with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pectis pygmaea is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
