Phlox cortezana

Phlox cortezana, commonly known as the Mexican phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring upright stems and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.
