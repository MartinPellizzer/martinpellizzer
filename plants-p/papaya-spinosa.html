Papaya spinosa

Papaya spinosa, commonly known as the spiny papaya, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and yellow-orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing before the fruit development.

The fruit is not only edible but also contains enzymes that aid in digestion, making it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
