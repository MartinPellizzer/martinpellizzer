Potentilla alpina

Potentilla alpina, commonly known as alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, typically reaching heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features palmate leaves composed of five to seven lobes, and it produces yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are usually solitary or in small clusters, and they bloom from June to August depending on the region.

Potentilla alpina is adapted to harsh alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
