Plowmanianthus grandifolius subsp. robustus

Plowmanianthus grandifolius subsp.

robustus, commonly known as the robust giantleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces striking yellow flowers in panicles during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
