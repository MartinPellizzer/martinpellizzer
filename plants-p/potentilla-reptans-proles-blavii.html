Potentilla reptans proles blavii

Potentilla reptans proles blavii, commonly known as Blavie's creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The plant features serrated, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
