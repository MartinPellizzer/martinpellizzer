Pseuderanthemum affine

Pseuderanthemum affine, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Pseuderanthemum affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
