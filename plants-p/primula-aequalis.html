Primula aequalis

Primula aequalis, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and are covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Primula aequalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
