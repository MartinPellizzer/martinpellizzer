Paeonia officinalis subsp. humilis

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

humilis, commonly known as the meadow peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmately divided, dark green leaves and grows from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical use in traditional medicine and its ornamental value in horticulture.
