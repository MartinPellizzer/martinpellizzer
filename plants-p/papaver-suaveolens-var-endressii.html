Papaver suaveolens var. endressii

Papaver suaveolens var. endressii, commonly known as the Endress's fumitory, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often branching towards the top. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four petals, typically in shades of pink or purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
