Protea magnoliifolia

Protea magnoliifolia, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a flower but is actually a cluster of small, specialized flowers called proteoids. The plant produces striking white to pink bracts that can reach up to 30 cm in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and floral arrangements.
