Phaseolus occidentalis

Phaseolus occidentalis, commonly known as the common bean or Phaseolus vulgaris, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and yellow flowers. Its pods contain several seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules makes it an important crop for sustainable agriculture.
