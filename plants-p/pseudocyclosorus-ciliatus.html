Pseudocyclosorus ciliatus

Pseudocyclosorus ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some cultures.

The plant's reproductive structures, including sori, are typically located on the underside of its fronds.
