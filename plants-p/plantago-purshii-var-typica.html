Plantago purshii var. typica

Plantago purshii var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It features upright, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows and along roadsides.
