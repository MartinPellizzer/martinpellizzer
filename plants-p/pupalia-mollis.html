Pupalia mollis

Pupalia mollis, commonly known as the sweet shrub or sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. Pupalia mollis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and useful in culinary applications.
