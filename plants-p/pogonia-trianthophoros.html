Pogonia trianthophoros

Pogonia trianthophoros, commonly known as the pink and white lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a distinctive pink or white pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, which is a characteristic feature of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic wetlands and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it is considered an indicator species for healthy wetland ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is listed as endangered in several states.
