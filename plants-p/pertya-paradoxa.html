Pertya paradoxa

Pertya paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical pertya, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-like arrangement of leaves, which is a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish in color, and it produces berries that are edible but not widely consumed.
