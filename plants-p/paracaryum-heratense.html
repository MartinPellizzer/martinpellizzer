Paracaryum heratense

Paracaryum heratense, commonly known as the Herat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its root system is fibrous, which helps stabilize soil in its natural habitat.

Paracaryum heratense plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
