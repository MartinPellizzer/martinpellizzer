Prunus × tajimensis

Prunus × tajimensis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a hybrid species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe.

Prunus × tajimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
