Piper auriculiferum

Piper auriculiferum, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper or kawa, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that develop into orange or red berries. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is closely related to other pepper species. Its leaves are often used in traditional Hawaiian medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is an important component of the native flora of Hawaii.
