Polystichum bonapartii

Polystichum bonapartii, commonly known as the Bonaparte's wood fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with a distinctive, serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rocky or forested slopes. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through spore dispersal rather than seeds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded gardens.
