Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. cordifolia

Pleurogyne carinthiaca var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf pleurogyne, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it is relatively rare in the wild.
