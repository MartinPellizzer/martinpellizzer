Peperomia conocarpa

Peperomia conocarpa, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy, dark green surface.

Peperomia conocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
