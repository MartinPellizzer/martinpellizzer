Pleurothallis neojordanensis

Pleurothallis neojordanensis, commonly known as the Jordan's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid tropical forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and compact growth habit.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
