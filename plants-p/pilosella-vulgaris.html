Pilosella vulgaris

Pilosella vulgaris, commonly known as common hawk's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow flowers with ray florets and a central disk. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Pilosella vulgaris is often found in temperate climates and is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind.
