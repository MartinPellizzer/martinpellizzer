Pennisetum pedicellatum

Pennisetum pedicellatum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that are often pink or purple in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pennisetum pedicellatum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
