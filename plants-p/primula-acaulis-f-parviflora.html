Primula acaulis f. parviflora

Primula acaulis f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf primrose, is a compact, low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, dark green leaves that are densely packed at the base. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, moist soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
