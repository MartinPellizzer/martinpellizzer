Phleum pratense subsp. microstachyum

Phleum pratense subsp.

microstachyum, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, two-lobed glumes and bisexual florets. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its forage quality and ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
