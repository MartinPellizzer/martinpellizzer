Pandanus prainii

Pandanus prainii, commonly known as the white pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are typically white or light green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near mangrove forests.
