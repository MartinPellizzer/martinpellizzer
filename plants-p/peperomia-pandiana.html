Peperomia pandiana

Peperomia pandiana, commonly known as the velvet leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and often velvety foliage that comes in shades of green, red, and purple, depending on the variety. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by bright red berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.

Peperomia pandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
