Podocarpus dispermus

Podocarpus dispermus, commonly known as the two-seeded podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant produces small, woody cones that contain two seeds, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
