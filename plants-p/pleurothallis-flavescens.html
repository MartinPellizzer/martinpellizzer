Pleurothallis flavescens

Pleurothallis flavescens, commonly known as the golden pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green to orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
