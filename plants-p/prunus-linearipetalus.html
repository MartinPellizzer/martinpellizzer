Prunus linearipetalus

Prunus linearipetalus, commonly known as the linear-petaled cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are linear in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus linearipetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
