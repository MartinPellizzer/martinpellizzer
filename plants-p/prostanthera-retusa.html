Prostanthera retusa

Prostanthera retusa, commonly known as the slender mintbush, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple. Prostanthera retusa is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
