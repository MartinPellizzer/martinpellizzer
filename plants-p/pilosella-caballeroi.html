Pilosella caballeroi

Pilosella caballeroi, commonly known as the Caballero's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are often surrounded by pappus-like bracts, giving it a fluffy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Pilosella caballeroi is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
