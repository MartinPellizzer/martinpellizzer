Prunella elongata

Prunella elongata, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.

Prunella elongata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.
