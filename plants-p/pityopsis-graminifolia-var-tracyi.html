Pityopsis graminifolia var. tracyi

Pityopsis graminifolia var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

This variety is named after the botanist Tracy, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
