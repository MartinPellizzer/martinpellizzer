Panicum hondurense

Panicum hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often used in traditional practices. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
