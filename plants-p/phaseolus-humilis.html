Phaseolus humilis

Phaseolus humilis, commonly known as the common bean or small bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing edible seeds. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its nutritional seeds.

Phaseolus humilis plays an important role in traditional agriculture and is valued for its protein-rich beans.
