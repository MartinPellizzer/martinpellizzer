Pleurothallis sicula

Pleurothallis sicula, commonly known as the Sicilian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the island of Sicily.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features delicate, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is part of the Pleurothallis genus, which includes many epiphytic and terrestrial species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
