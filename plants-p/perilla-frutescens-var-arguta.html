Perilla frutescens var. arguta

Perilla frutescens var. arguta, commonly known as Japanese perilla or shiso, is an aromatic annual herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers and is often cultivated for its flavorful leaves used in cooking. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
