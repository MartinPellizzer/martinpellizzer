Phalaris arundinacea f. pallida

Phalaris arundinacea f. pallida, commonly known as the silver reed, is a variant of the common reed that exhibits a distinctive silvery-blue hue due to its glaucous leaf surfaces.

This plant is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, cylindrical stems can grow up to 2 meters tall, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, flat, and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in moist environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
