Piper pseudofallens

Piper pseudofallens, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its vining growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, round berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Piper pseudofallens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
