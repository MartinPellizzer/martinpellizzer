Psidium angustifolium

Psidium angustifolium, commonly known as the strawberry guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, edible fruits that resemble strawberries in appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are narrow and pointed, and it produces white flowers with a strong, pleasant fragrance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in both culinary and medicinal uses.
