Pilosella officinarum subsp. trichoscapa

Pilosella officinarum subsp.

trichoscapa, commonly known as the hairy cat's-paw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more densely hairy stems and leaves compared to other forms of Pilosella.
