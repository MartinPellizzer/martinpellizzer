Patrinia diandra

Patrinia diandra, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces spikes of blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Patrinia diandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
