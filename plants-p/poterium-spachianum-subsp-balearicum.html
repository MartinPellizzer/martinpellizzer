Poterium spachianum subsp. balearicum

Poterium spachianum subsp.

balearicum, commonly known as the Balearic thyme, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
