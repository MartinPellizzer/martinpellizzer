Placus laciniatus

Placus laciniatus, commonly known as the fringed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap aquatic insects. The plant's leaves are thin and often divided, with delicate, fringed edges that aid in capturing prey. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters, where it can float or anchor itself to submerged surfaces.

Its unique adaptations allow it to supplement its nutrient intake in nutrient-poor environments.
