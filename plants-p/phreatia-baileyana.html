Phreatia baileyana

Phreatia baileyana, commonly known as the Bailey's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less hospitable conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts and horticulturists.
