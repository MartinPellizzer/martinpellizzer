Phaseolus gonospermos

Phaseolus gonospermos, commonly known as the Gonospermos bean, is a species native to South America.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps in nitrogen fixation. Its seeds are used for culinary purposes and are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant's botanical features make it an important crop in certain regions of its native habitat.
