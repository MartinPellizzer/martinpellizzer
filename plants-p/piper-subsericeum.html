Piper subsericeum

Piper subsericeum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into red, berry-like fruits. Its vines can spread along the ground or climb supports, making it a common ornamental plant in warm climates.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
