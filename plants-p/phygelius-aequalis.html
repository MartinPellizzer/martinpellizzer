Phygelius aequalis

Phygelius aequalis, commonly known as the yellow oxalis, is a South African shrub belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers. The plant is native to the coastal regions of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Phygelius aequalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
