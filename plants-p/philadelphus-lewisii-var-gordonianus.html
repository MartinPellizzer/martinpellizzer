Philadelphus lewisii var. gordonianus

Philadelphus lewisii var. gordonianus, commonly known as Gordon's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem structure and showy floral displays that attract pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
