Pelargonium × impurum

Pelargonium × impurum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white, often with contrasting markings. The leaves are typically rounded and may have a velvety texture, and they often feature distinctive spots or mottling. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value has led to the cultivation of numerous cultivars with varying flower colors and leaf patterns.
