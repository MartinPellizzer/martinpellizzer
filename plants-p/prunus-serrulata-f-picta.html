Prunus serrulata f. picta

Prunus serrulata f. picta, commonly known as the painted cherry or variegated Japanese maple, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive, variegated foliage that features white, yellow, and green streaks on the leaves, creating a striking visual effect. The leaves are typically oval or elliptical with finely serrated edges, and they turn vibrant red or orange in the autumn. It produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange fruit.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
