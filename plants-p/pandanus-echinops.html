Pandanus echinops

Pandanus echinops, commonly known as the spiny pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its stiff, spiny leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, white flowers.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
