Pandanus salailuaensis

Pandanus salailuaensis, commonly known as the Salailua pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions such as Samoa and Tonga. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color.

It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, playing a significant role in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
