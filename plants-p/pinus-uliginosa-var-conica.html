Pinus uliginosa var. conica

Pinus uliginosa var. conica, commonly known as the cone-shaped black spruce, is a variant of the black spruce native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its conical shape, which distinguishes it from the more typical broad, bushy form of the species. The tree produces small, dark cones that mature and release seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle. Its needles are short and dark green, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss in cold, wet environments.

This variant is adapted to grow in peat bogs and wetlands, where it plays a vital role in ecosystem structure and carbon storage.
