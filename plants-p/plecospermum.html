Plecospermum

Plecospermum, commonly known as the "silver vine," is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and distinctive silvery-green leaves that have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Plecospermum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
