Primula yunnanensis subsp. maikhaensis

Primula yunnanensis subsp.

maikhaensis, commonly known as the Maikha primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China. It is characterized by its rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at elevations above 2,500 meters. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
