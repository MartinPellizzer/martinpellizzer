Podosemum distichophyllum

Podosemum distichophyllum, commonly known as the two-ranked fern, is a rare and delicate plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bi-directionally arranged fronds that give it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or rocky crevices. Its spores are dispersed through the wind, contributing to its limited distribution.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
