Pouzolzia walkeriana

Pouzolzia walkeriana, commonly known as the Walker's pouzolzia, is a low-growing, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically reaches a height of about 1 to 2 feet and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, woodland environments.

Pouzolzia walkeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
