Phyllostachys makinoi f. tanakae

Phyllostachys makinoi f. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming bamboo species native to Japan.

It features slender, erect culms that are typically green, with a smooth, almost metallic sheen. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a bright green color and a slightly hairy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscape design.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and containers.
