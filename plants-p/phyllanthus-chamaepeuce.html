Phyllanthus chamaepeuce

Phyllanthus chamaepeuce, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its upright, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, green leaves with a waxy texture and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
