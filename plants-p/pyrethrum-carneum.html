Pyrethrum carneum

Pyrethrum carneum, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its flowers are rich in pyrethrins, which are natural insecticides used in organic pest control.

Pyrethrum carneum is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its medicinal properties.
