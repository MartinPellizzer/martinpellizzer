Potentilla ancistrifolia var. concolor

Potentilla ancistrifolia var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth or only slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is usually low and spreading, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in well-drained soils.
