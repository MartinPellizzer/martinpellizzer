Potentilla forrestii var. segmentata

Potentilla forrestii var. segmentata, commonly known as the Chinese cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to China.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically oval and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
