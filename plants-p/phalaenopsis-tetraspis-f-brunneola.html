Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. brunneola

Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. brunneola, commonly known as the brown-veined phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with prominent brown veins. The plant produces pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a subtle brownish tinge. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
