Piper planipes var. amplum

Piper planipes var. amplum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The varietal form 'amplum' distinguishes it from other Piper species by its broader leaves and distinct growth habit.
