Paspalum aspidiotes

Paspalum aspidiotes, commonly known as the spikeless paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It is adapted to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
