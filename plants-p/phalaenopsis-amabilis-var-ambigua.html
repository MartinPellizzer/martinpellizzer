Phalaenopsis amabilis var. ambigua

Phalaenopsis amabilis var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous moth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees or other structures rather than in soil. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
