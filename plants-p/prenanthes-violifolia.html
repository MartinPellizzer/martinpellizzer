Prenanthes violifolia

Prenanthes violifolia, commonly known as violetleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Prenanthes violifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
