Persicaria birmanica

Persicaria birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge. Its small, white to pinkish flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
