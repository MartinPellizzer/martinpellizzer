Plectranthus triangularis

Plectranthus triangularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, often with a compact and bushy appearance. It is known for its aromatic foliage, which can emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

Plectranthus triangularis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
