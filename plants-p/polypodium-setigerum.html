Polypodium setigerum

Polypodium setigerum, commonly known as the hairy cliff fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is typically found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium setigerum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in specific ecological conditions.
