Piper graciliramosum

Piper graciliramosum, commonly known as the slender-branch pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with both male and female flowers occurring on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, and it is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
