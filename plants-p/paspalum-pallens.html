Paspalum pallens

Paspalum pallens, commonly known as the silver paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist and well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Paspalum pallens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife.
