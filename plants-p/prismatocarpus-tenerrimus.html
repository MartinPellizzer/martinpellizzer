Prismatocarpus tenerrimus

Prismatocarpus tenerrimus, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and evergreen foliage, which contributes to its ecological significance. The plant has a distinctive branching pattern and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain multiple seeds.

This species is valued for its durable timber and plays an important role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
