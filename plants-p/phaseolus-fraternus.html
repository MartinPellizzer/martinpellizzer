Phaseolus fraternus

Phaseolus fraternus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a species of flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

Phaseolus fraternus is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its nutritional content.
