Podochilus uncifer

Podochilus uncifer, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers with a unique coloration pattern. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
