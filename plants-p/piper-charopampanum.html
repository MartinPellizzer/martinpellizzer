Piper charopampanum

Piper charopampanum, commonly known as the Charopanum pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, thin fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a distinct aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
