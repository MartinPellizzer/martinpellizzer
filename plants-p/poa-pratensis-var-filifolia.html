Poa pratensis var. filifolia

Poa pratensis var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a variant of the common bluegrass species.

It is characterized by its fine, soft blades that give it a silky texture, making it visually appealing. This grass thrives in temperate climates and is often found in well-drained soils. It has a tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in lawns and gardens.
