Potamogeton drupaceus

Potamogeton drupaceus, commonly known as the clustered pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, branched stems that often form dense clusters, and its leaves are long, narrow, and slightly ribbed. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters.
