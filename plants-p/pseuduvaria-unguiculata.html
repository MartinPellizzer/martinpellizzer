Pseuduvaria unguiculata

Pseuduvaria unguiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pseuduvaria, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly pointed tip. It produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that are often fragrant.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.
