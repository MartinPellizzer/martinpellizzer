Polygala pulchra

Polygala pulchra, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

Polygala pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
