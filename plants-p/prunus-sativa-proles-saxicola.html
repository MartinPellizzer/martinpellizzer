Prunus sativa proles saxicola

Prunus sativa proles saxicola, commonly known as the saxicola cherry, is a rare and hardy species adapted to rocky and arid environments.

It is characterized by its small, dark red fruits and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface, and its flowers are typically pale pink or white. It is native to mountainous regions in Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental and ecological resilience.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare species in botanical collections.
