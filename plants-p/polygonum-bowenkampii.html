Polygonum bowenkampii

Polygonum bowenkampii, commonly known as the Bornean knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often edible rhubarb-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth margin and can reach up to 15 cm in length.

Polygonum bowenkampii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
