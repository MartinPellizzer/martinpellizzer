Protea echinulata

Protea echinulata, commonly known as the white sugar bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its unique, urn-shaped inflorescence. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. It produces small, white flowers that are clustered around a central stem.

Protea echinulata is adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
