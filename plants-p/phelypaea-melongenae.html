Phelypaea melongenae

Phelypaea melongenae, commonly known as the purple pitcher plant, is a carnivorous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple to reddish-brown pitchers that secrete a sticky liquid to trap insects. The plant's leaves are modified into tubular structures that serve as both a trap and a digestive system. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to maintain its specialized adaptations.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an intriguing subject for botanical study.
