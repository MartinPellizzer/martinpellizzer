Pterygodium catholicum

Pterygodium catholicum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive white, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
