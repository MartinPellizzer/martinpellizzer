Penstemon glaucus

Penstemon glaucus, commonly known as the blue curl penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
