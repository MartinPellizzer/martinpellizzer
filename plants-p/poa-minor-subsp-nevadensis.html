Poa minor subsp. nevadensis

Poa minor subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and open slopes.
