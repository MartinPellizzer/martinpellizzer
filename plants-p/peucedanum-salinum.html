Peucedanum salinum

Peucedanum salinum, commonly known as salt wormwood, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in saline or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, salty soils. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Peucedanum salinum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
