Polypodium glycirrhiza

Polypodium glycirrhiza, commonly known as the Indian kidney fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped fronds with a deep green color and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is valued for its ornamental appearance and medicinal properties.
