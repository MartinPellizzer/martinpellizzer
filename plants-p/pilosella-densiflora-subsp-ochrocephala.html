Pilosella densiflora subsp. ochrocephala

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

ochrocephala, commonly known as the yellowhead pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance.

The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are yellow at the base and transition to white toward the tips, creating a striking visual contrast.
