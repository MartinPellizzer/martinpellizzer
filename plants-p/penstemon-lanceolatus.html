Penstemon lanceolatus

Penstemon lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Penstemon lanceolatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
