Pachystelma cordatum

Pachystelma cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pachystelma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to succulent collections.
