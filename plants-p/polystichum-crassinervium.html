Polystichum crassinervium

Polystichum crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The fronds are bipinnate, with broad, flat pinnae that have prominent, parallel veins. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests and along stream banks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is commonly cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
