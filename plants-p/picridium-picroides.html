Picridium picroides

Picridium picroides, commonly known as the common picrid, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Picridaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a basal rosette.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
