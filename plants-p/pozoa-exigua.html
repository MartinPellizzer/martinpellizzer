Pozoa exigua

Pozoa exigua, commonly known as the small pozoa, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the summer months. Pozoa exigua is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.

Its short life cycle and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
