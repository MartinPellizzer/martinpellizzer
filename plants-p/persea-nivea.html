Persea nivea

Persea nivea, commonly known as the white persimmon, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow or orange when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
