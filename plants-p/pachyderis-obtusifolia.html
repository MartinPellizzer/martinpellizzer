Pachyderis obtusifolia

Pachyderis obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pachyderis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
