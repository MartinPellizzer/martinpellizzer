Prunus × lannesiana f. chosiuhizakura

Prunus × lannesiana f. chosiuhizakura, commonly known as the Choshiuhizakura cherry, is a unique floral form of the Prunus lannesiana species.

It is characterized by its double flowers, which consist of numerous small petals arranged in a compact, fluffy cluster. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its ornamental value in traditional Japanese gardens. It typically blooms in early spring, producing a profusion of white or pale pink flowers.

This cultivar is often used in bonsai and as a decorative plant due to its delicate appearance and seasonal beauty.
