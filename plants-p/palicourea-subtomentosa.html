Palicourea subtomentosa

Palicourea subtomentosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf palicourea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
