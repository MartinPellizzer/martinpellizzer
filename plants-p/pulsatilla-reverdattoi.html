Pulsatilla reverdattoi

Pulsatilla reverdattoi, commonly known as the Italian pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, yellow or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, fluffy center. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a velvety texture.

Pulsatilla reverdattoi is noted for its early blooming habit, often appearing in spring before many other wildflowers.
