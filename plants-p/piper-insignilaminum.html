Piper insignilaminum

Piper insignilaminum, commonly known as the pink-veined pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive pink veins, which give it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by black, peppery fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
