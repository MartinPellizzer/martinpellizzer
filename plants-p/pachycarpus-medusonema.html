Pachycarpus medusonema

Pachycarpus medusonema, commonly known as the star-shaped spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, star-like formations. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to low-water conditions through its thick, water-storing tissues.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
