Physacanthus talbotii

Physacanthus talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's ghost fern, is a unique fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its common name. The plant has a creeping rhizome that produces small, spore-bearing sorus clusters on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
