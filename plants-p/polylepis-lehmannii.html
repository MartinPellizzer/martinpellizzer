Polylepis lehmannii

Polylepis lehmannii, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its dense, wax-coated foliage and distinctive, often pendulous, fruiting structures. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at elevations between 3,000 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a unique appearance in the mountainous landscape.

Polylepis lehmannii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
