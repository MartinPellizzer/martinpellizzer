Prunus serrulata f. hakkasan

Prunus serrulata f. hakkasan, commonly known as the Hakkasan cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese maple.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely serrated leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its unique foliage makes it a prized addition to horticultural collections.
