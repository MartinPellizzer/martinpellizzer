Paeonia mlokosewitschii

Paeonia mlokosewitschii, commonly known as the Chinese peony, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has palmate leaves composed of 5–7 lobes, and its stems are covered in thorns. It belongs to the芍药属 (Paeonia) genus within the family Ranunculaceae.

Paeonia mlokosewitschii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.
