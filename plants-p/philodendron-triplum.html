Philodendron triplum

Philodendron triplum, commonly known as the three-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often exhibit a striking variegation of green and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Philodendron triplum thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
