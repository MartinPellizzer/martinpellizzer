Pleiocardia

Pleiocardia, commonly known as the heartleaf or heartleaf fig, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and is native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia. The plants typically grow as shrubs or small trees, with smooth, often greyish bark. They produce small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

The genus is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its unique leaf shapes.
