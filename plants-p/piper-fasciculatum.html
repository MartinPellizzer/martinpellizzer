Piper fasciculatum

Piper fasciculatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its botanical structure supports its climbing habit, with adventitious roots emerging from its stems.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
