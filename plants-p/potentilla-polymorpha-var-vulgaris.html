Potentilla polymorpha var. vulgaris

Potentilla polymorpha var. vulgaris, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and wiry, sprawling growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
