Pfaffia cipoana

Pfaffia cipoana, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Pfaffia cipoana thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
