Parodiochloa trachyantha

Parodiochloa trachyantha, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along riverbanks, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Parodiochloa trachyantha is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
