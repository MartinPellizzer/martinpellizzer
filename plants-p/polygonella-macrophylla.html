Polygonella macrophylla

Polygonella macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved polygonella, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands and stream banks of North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

Polygonella macrophylla plays an important role in stabilizing soil in moist environments and serves as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
