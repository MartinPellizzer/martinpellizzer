Potentilla erecta var. herminii

Potentilla erecta var. herminii, commonly known as the Herminii cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, upright leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
