Pseudopavonia

Pseudopavonia, commonly known as the false pavonia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

These plants are native to the Americas, particularly South America, and are characterized by their showy, often pink or purple flowers. Pseudopavonia species typically have compound leaves and produce small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. They are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value due to their vibrant floral displays.
