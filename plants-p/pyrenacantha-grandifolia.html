Pyrenacantha grandifolia

Pyrenacantha grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered pyrenacantha, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage, which provide habitat for various small animals and insects. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a slightly rough texture, while the fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

Pyrenacantha grandifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.
