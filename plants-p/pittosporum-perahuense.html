Pittosporum perahuense

Pittosporum perahuense, commonly known as the Perahuense Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Pittosporum perahuense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
