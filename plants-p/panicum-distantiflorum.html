Panicum distantiflorum

Panicum distantiflorum, commonly known as the distant-flowered panicgrass, is a species of grass native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are arranged in panicles, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Panicum distantiflorum is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
