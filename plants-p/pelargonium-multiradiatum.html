Pelargonium multiradiatum

Pelargonium multiradiatum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium multiradiatum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.
