Pelargonium tongaense

Pelargonium tongaense, commonly known as the Tonga geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy flowers. The plant produces small, clustered blooms in shades of pink and red, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium tongaense is often grown as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
