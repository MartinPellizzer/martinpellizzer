Potentilla gracilis flabelliformis

Potentilla gracilis flabelliformis, commonly known as the flabelliform rose.

This perennial shrub is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers. The leaves are typically divided into fine, feathery segments, giving the plant a graceful appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and showy blooms.
