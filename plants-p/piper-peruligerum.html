Piper peruligerum

Piper peruligerum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or black pepper vine, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, dark purple berries. The berries contain small, white seeds that are used as a spice and are known for their pungent flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
