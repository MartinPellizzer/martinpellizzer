Pilosella promeces

Pilosella promeces, commonly known as the creeping mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features silvery-white hairs covering its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Its small yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pilosella promeces typically thrives in moist, shaded environments like woodlands and stream banks.
