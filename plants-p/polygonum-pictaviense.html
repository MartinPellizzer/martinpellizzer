Polygonum pictaviense

Polygonum pictaviense, commonly known as the Picardy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, knotted stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Polygonum pictaviense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
