Podophyllum veitchii

Podophyllum veitchii, commonly known as Veitch's bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Podophyllum veitchii is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
