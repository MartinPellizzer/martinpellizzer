Panax pseudoginseng var. bipinnatifidus

Panax pseudoginseng var. bipinnatifidus, commonly known as the bipinnatifid ginseng, is a variant of the pseudoginseng species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive bipinnate leaves, which are deeply divided into two sets of smaller leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in traditional Chinese medicine and botanical research.
