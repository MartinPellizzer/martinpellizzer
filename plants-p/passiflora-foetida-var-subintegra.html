Passiflora foetida var. subintegra

Passiflora foetida var. subintegra, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that matures to a reddish color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
