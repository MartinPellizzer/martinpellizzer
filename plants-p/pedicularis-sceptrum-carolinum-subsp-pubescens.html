Pedicularis sceptrum-carolinum subsp. pubescens

Pedicularis sceptrum-carolinum subsp.

pubescens, commonly known as the sticky monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its subspecies name "pubescens.".

The plant produces showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
