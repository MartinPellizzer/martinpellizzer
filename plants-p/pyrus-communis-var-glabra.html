Pyrus communis var. glabra

Pyrus communis var. glabra, commonly known as the common pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in autumn. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The variety is noted for its compact growth habit and resistance to certain pests and diseases.
