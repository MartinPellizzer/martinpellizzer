Piper subasperifolium

Piper subasperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. Its fruits are small berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.
