Plantago major var. megastachya

Plantago major var. megastachya, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a robust perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, roadsides, and waste areas.

This variety is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
