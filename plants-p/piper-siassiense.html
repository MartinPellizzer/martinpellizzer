Piper siassiense

Piper siassiense, commonly known as the Siassi pepper or Siassi long pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, green flowers followed by the distinctive, spiky fruits. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a strong, pungent aroma.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns from green to red, indicating maturity.
