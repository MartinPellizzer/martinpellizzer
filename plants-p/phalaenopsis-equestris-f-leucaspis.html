Phalaenopsis equestris f. leucaspis

Phalaenopsis equestris f. leucaspis, commonly known as the white-veined phalaenopsis, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its distinctive white and green veined leaves.

It originates from the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid and warm climates. The plant produces elegant, long-lasting flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically growing in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
