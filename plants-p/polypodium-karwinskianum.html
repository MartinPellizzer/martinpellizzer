Polypodium karwinskianum

Polypodium karwinskianum, commonly known as the Karwinski's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of brownish spores. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, anchoring itself with its rhizomes while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
