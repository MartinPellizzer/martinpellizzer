Polygala vulgaris var. pubescens

Polygala vulgaris var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
