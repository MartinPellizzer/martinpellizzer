Pilosella caespitosa subsp. caespitosa

Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, tufted growth habit, forming compact, cushion-like clusters.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense heads, resembling dandelion flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy and linear, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
