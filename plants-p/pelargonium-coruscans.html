Pelargonium coruscans

Pelargonium coruscans, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium coruscans is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
