Polygonum setaceum

Polygonum setaceum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright stems covered with tiny, hair-like structures called setae, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
