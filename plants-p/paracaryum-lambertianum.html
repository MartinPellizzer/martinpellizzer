Paracaryum lambertianum

Paracaryum lambertianum, commonly known as the Lambert's spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its milky sap and distinctive, alternate leaves. The plant typically features yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a complex inflorescence called a cyathium. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
