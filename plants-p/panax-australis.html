Panax australis

Panax australis, commonly known as the southern ginseng, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is protected in many regions.
