Phyllanthus macrosepalus

Phyllanthus macrosepalus, commonly known as the large-sepal phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with five sepals that are prominently enlarged.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.
