Pladera

Pladera, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the genus Tithonia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
