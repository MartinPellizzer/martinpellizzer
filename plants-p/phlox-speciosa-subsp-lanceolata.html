Phlox speciosa subsp. lanceolata

Phlox speciosa subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are lance-shaped, hence the subspecies name, and are arranged oppositely along the stems. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
