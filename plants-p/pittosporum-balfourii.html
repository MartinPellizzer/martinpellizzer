Pittosporum balfourii

Pittosporum balfourii, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Pittosporum balfourii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
