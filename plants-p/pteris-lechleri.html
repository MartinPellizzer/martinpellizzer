Pteris lechleri

Pteris lechleri, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Colombia.

It is a member of the fern family, characterized by its large, shield-shaped fronds that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The botanical structure of Pteris lechleri includes a well-developed root system and a complex system of vascular tissues that support its rapid growth.
