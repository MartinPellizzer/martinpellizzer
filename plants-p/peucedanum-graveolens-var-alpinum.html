Peucedanum graveolens var. alpinum

Peucedanum graveolens var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine carrot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and cooler climates, making it suitable for alpine gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
