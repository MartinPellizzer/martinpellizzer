Pinellia

Pinellia, commonly known as the pinellia tuber, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its underground rhizomes, which are the primary part used in traditional medicine. Pinellia contains various bioactive compounds, including alkaloids and saponins, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is widely used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
