Potentilla flabellifolia var. yuparensis

Potentilla flabellifolia var. yuparensis, commonly known as the Yuparensis cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid environment. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in drought-tolerant plants.
