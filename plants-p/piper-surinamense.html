Piper surinamense

Piper surinamense, commonly known as Surinam pepper or black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature, resembling black pepper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and potential medicinal studies.
