Pyrola rotundifolia subsp. dahurica

Pyrola rotundifolia subsp.

dahurica, commonly known as the Dahurian roundleaf wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the boreal and subarctic regions of Asia. It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed along the stems.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Pyrolaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
