Phlomoides boraldaica

Phlomoides boraldaica, commonly known as the Boralda false foxglove, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.

Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.
