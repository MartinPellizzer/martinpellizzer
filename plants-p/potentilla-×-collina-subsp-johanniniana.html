Potentilla × collina subsp. johanniniana

Potentilla × collina subsp.

johanniniana, commonly known as the Johanninian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features dense, upright growth with narrow, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
