Paphiopedilum fowliei f. christianae

Paphiopedilum fowliei f. christianae, commonly known as the Christian's lady's slipper, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple petals and a distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Paphiopedilum genus. The plant typically grows as a lithophyte or epiphyte, thriving in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, producing a fragrant display that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and horticultural value, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
