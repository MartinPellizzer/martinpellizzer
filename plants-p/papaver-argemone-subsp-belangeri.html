Papaver argemone subsp. belangeri

Papaver argemone subsp.

belangeri, commonly known as the Belanger's poppy, is a species native to western North America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers and capsule-like fruits.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and roadsides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
