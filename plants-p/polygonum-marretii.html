Polygonum marretii

Polygonum marretii, commonly known as the Korean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by three-lobed seed capsules.

Polygonum marretii is often found in moist habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges.
