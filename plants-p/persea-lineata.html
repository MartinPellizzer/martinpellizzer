Persea lineata

Persea lineata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a smooth, green berry that contains a single large seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
