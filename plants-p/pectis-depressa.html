Pectis depressa

Pectis depressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, producing small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pectis depressa is adapted to arid environments and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
