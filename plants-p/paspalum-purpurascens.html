Paspalum purpurascens

Paspalum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and clay soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It is often found in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is sometimes used in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
