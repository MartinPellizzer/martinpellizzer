Phalaris minor var. nepalensis

Phalaris minor var. nepalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are usually greenish or pale blue in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
