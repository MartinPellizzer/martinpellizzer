Pouzolzia hirta var. quinquenervis

Pouzolzia hirta var. quinquenervis, commonly known as the hairy pouzolzia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are three-angled berries that turn red when mature.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.
