Potamogeton punensis

Potamogeton punensis, commonly known as the Andean water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbon-like in shape, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Potamogeton punensis is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb pollutants from the water.
