Pieris divaricata

Pieris divaricata, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
