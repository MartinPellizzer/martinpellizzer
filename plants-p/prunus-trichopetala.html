Prunus trichopetala

Prunus trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its finely hairy leaves, which give it its scientific name, and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Prunus trichopetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
