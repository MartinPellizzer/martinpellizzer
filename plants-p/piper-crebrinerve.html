Piper crebrinerve

Piper crebrinerve, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with prominent, parallel veins. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, three-lobed berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
