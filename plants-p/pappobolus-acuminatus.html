Pappobolus acuminatus

Pappobolus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tailed grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in fluffy, parachute-like structures called pappi. These adaptations allow it to thrive in harsh environments and efficiently spread its seeds over long distances.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for ecological restoration and soil stabilization in degraded landscapes.
