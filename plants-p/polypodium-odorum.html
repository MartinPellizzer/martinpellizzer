Polypodium odorum

Polypodium odorum, commonly known as the fragrant polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often covered in fine, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows on rock surfaces or in shaded forest environments, thriving in humid conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's unique aromatic properties have also made it a subject of interest in botanical research.
