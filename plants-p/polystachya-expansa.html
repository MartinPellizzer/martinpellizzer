Polystachya expansa

Polystachya expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed rattlesnake plant, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its long, arching leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in length, often with a waxy texture and a distinctive, variegated coloration. It produces striking, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically pollinated by insects.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
