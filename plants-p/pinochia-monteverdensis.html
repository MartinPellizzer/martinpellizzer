Pinochia monteverdensis

Pinochia monteverdensis, commonly known as the Monteverde pine, is a species of conifer native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and its ability to thrive in high humidity and cool temperatures. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found growing at elevations between 1,200 and 2,200 meters. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
