Paraquilegia Caespitosa

Crested bellflower, scientifically known as Paraquilegia caespitosa, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is prized for its unique appearance, featuring rosettes of velvety blue-green leaves and spikes of delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays. Its ability to form dense clumps gives it a compact and ornamental look, enhancing its appeal to gardeners. With its elegant form and vibrant blooms, the crested bellflower is a standout addition to any botanical collection.
