Piper excelsum subsp. peltatum

Piper excelsum subsp.

peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by the characteristic peppery berries. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species known for their aromatic properties.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
