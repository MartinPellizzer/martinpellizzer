Psittacanthus barlowii

Psittacanthus barlowii, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in a dense layer of brown, fuzzy hairs. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in the understory of tropical forests. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among fern enthusiasts and botanists.
