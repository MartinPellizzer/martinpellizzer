Palmorchis valdiviesoana

Palmorchis valdiviesoana, commonly known as the Valdivia orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a slender, palm-like pseudobulb and a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence, typical of many orchid species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a conservation concern.
