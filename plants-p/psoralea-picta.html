Psoralea picta

Psoralea picta, commonly known as the painted psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Psoralea picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
