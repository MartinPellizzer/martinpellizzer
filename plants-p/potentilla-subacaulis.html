Potentilla subacaulis

Potentilla subacaulis, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has wiry, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Potentilla subacaulis plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments.
