Picea excelsa subvar. columnaris

Picea excelsa subvar. columnaris, commonly known as the slender white spruce, is a variant of the white spruce native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow growth habit and relatively slow growth rate, making it a striking ornamental plant. The foliage consists of dense, dark green needles that are typically shorter than those of the typical white spruce. It produces small, cone-like fruits that contain winged seeds, typical of coniferous species.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a windbreak due to its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
