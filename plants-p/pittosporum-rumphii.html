Pittosporum rumphii

Pittosporum rumphii, commonly known as the white pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a pleasant aromatic scent.

Pittosporum rumphii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
