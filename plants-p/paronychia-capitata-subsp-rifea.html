Paronychia capitata subsp. rifea

Paronychia capitata subsp.

rifea, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
