Premna attenuata

Premna attenuata, commonly known as the slender leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, opposite leaves, and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Premna attenuata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
