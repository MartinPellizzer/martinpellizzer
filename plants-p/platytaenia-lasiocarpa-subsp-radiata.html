Platytaenia lasiocarpa subsp. radiata

Platytaenia lasiocarpa subsp.

radiata, commonly known as the radiate flat-leafed broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and leaves that are typically flat and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
