Phelline comosa var. vieillardii

Phelline comosa var. vieillardii, commonly known as the velvety phelline, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers dry, arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, adding subtle color to its otherwise muted foliage.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
