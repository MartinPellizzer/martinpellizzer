Polygonum lapathifolium var. xanthophyllum

Polygonum lapathifolium var. xanthophyllum, commonly known as yellow polygonum, is a flowering plant species belonging to the buckwheat family, Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist environments, including wet meadows and streambanks, and is native to regions of Asia. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

This variant is distinguished by its more vibrant yellow floral display compared to other varieties of Polygonum lapathifolium.
