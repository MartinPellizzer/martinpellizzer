Papaver chelidoniifolium var. pinnatum

Papaver chelidoniifolium var. pinnatum, commonly known as the pinnatecelandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinnate leaves, which are finely divided and resemble those of the commoncelandine. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a central greenish area, typical of the Papaver genus. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
