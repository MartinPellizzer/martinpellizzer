Pachira paraensis

Pachira paraensis, commonly known as the Amazonian money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pachira paraensis is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
