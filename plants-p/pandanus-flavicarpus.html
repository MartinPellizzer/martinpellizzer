Pandanus flavicarpus

Pandanus flavicarpus, commonly known as the yellow fruit pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Its fibrous leaves and fruits are valued for both culinary and ornamental purposes in its native habitat.
