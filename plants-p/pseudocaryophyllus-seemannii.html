Pseudocaryophyllus seemannii

Pseudocaryophyllus seemannii, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months, which attract various pollinators. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

Due to its ecological importance and unique morphology, it is often studied for its role in desert ecosystems.
