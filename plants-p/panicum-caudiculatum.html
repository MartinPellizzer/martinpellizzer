Panicum caudiculatum

Panicum caudiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, delicate panicles of pale green to golden flowers in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a low-maintenance ornamental grass.

Due to its compact size and attractive foliage, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.
