Polygala zeyheri

Polygala zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Polygala zeyheri is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.
