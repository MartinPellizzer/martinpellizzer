Pinus excelsa var. chinensis

Pinus excelsa var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pine, is a variant of the Chinese white pine species native to eastern Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slow growth and elegant, pyramidal shape. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of five, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
