Potentilla heptaphylla var. goldbachii

Potentilla heptaphylla var. goldbachii, commonly known as the Goldbach's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly toothed margin, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact size and long blooming period.
