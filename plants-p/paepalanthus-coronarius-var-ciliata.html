Paepalanthus coronarius var. ciliata

Paepalanthus coronarius var. ciliata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
