Plantago major f. bifida

Plantago major f. bifida, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its broader, more lance-shaped leaves.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and it thrives in a variety of habitats, including lawns, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
