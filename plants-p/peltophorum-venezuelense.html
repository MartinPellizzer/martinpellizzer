Peltophorum venezuelense

Peltophorum venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red and yellow. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
