Pinus sylvestris var. patula

Pinus sylvestris var. patula, commonly known as the Mexican stone pine, is a variant of the Scots pine.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dense, dark green needles and distinctive, edible cones. The tree typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a slow-growing evergreen with a pyramidal shape that becomes more irregular with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to produce a unique, aromatic resin.
