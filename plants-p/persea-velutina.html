Persea velutina

Persea velutina, commonly known as the velvet avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, velvety leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
