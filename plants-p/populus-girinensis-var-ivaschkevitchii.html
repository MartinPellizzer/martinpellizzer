Populus girinensis var. ivaschkevitchii

Populus girinensis var. ivaschkevitchii, commonly known as the Ivaschkevitch poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to specific regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically medium to dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and supporting local biodiversity.
