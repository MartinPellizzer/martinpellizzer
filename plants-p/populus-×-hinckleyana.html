Populus × hinckleyana

Populus × hinckleyana, commonly known as the Hinckley Poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its rapid growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and reforestation projects.
