Piper smitinandianum

Piper smitinandianum, commonly known as the Smitinanda pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, peppery fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. The plant has dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Its flowering occurs in clusters of small, greenish-white flowers, which eventually develop into the characteristic peppery berries.

The botanical features of Piper smitinandianum make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
