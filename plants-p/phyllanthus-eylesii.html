Phyllanthus eylesii

Phyllanthus eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Phyllanthus eylesii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
