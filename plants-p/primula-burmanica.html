Primula burmanica

Primula burmanica, commonly known as the Burmese primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly in China, Nepal, and India.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
