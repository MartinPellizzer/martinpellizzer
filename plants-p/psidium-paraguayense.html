Psidium paraguayense

Psidium paraguayense, commonly known as the Paraguay guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a fragrant, sweet aroma.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
