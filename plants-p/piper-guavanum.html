Piper guavanum

Piper guavanum, commonly known as the Guiana pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are aromatic and contain essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

Piper guavanum is valued for its potential health benefits and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
