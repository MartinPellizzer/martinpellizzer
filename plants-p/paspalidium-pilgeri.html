Paspalidium pilgeri

Paspalidium pilgeri, commonly known as the Pilger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping and restoration projects.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
