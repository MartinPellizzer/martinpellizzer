Prunella laciniata subsp. subintegra

Prunella laciniata subsp.

subintegra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered with opposite, lanceolate leaves that are finely toothed. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in dense whorls along the stem.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
