Physalis minima var. indica

Physalis minima var. indica, commonly known as the Indian groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It produces small, edible berries enclosed in a papery calyx that turns bright orange when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has hairy, opposite leaves. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has mild, sweet flavors.
