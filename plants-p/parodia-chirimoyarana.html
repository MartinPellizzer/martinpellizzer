Parodia chirimoyarana

Parodia chirimoyarana, commonly known as the "Chirimoya Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its stems are fleshy and store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
