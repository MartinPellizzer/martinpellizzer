Potentilla micrantha subsp. carniolica

Potentilla micrantha subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as the Carpathian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, five-lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
