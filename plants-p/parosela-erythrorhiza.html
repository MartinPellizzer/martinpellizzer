Parosela erythrorhiza

Parosela erythrorhiza, commonly known as the red-rooted parosela, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a robust rhizome system, with reddish roots that give it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Parosela erythrorhiza is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
