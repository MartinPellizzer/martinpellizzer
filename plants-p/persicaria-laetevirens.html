Persicaria laetevirens

Persicaria laetevirens, commonly known as the gladwillow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinctive bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an effective plant for erosion control.
