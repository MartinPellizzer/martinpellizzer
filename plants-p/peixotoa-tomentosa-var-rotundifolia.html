Peixotoa tomentosa var. rotundifolia

Peixotoa tomentosa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf peixotoa, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain areas.
