Psidium guayabita

Psidium guayabita, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or guayaba de monte, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil.
