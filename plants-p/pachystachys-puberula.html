Pachystachys puberula

Pachystachys puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
