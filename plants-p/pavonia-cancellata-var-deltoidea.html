Pavonia cancellata var. deltoidea

Pavonia cancellata var. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant features pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
