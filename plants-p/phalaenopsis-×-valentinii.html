Phalaenopsis × valentinii

Phalaenopsis × valentinii, commonly known as the Valentine orchid, is a hybrid orchid that results from the cross between Phalaenopsis aphrodis and Phalaenopsis equestris.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or white with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It has thick, fleshy roots that help it store water, and its leaves are broad and leathery.

This orchid is popular in horticulture for its long-lasting blooms and elegant appearance.
