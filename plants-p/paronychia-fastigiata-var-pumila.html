Paronychia fastigiata var. pumila

Paronychia fastigiata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush plant, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bright red flowers that resemble bottle brushes, blooming from late spring to early autumn. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its compact size and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
