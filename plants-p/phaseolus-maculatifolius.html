Phaseolus maculatifolius

Phaseolus maculatifolius, commonly known as the spotted leaf bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus maculatifolius is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
