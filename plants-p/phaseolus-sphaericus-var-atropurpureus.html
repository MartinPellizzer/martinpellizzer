Phaseolus sphaericus var. atropurpureus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking purplish-pink flowers and edible, cylindrical pods that turn green as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces beans that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and culinary uses.
