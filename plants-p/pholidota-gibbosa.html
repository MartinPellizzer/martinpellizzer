Pholidota gibbosa

Pholidota gibbosa, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and pairs of opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds. Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of food in some regions.
