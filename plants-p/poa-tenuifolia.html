Poa tenuifolia

Poa tenuifolia, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Poa tenuifolia is often found in nutrient-rich soils and can form dense mats in suitable conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
