Potentilla patens

Potentilla patens, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters and features wiry, sprawling stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five oval lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla patens thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
