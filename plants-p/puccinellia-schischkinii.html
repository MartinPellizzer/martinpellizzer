Puccinellia schischkinii

Puccinellia schischkinii, commonly known as the Schischkin's needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and saline soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
