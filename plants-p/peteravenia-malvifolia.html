Peteravenia malvifolia

Peteravenia malvifolia, commonly known as the white mallows, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
