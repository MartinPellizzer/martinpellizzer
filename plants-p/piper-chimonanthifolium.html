Piper chimonanthifolium

Piper chimonanthifolium, commonly known as the goldenleaf pepper, is a tropical plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a vibrant green, with some varieties displaying golden or variegated patterns. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by elongated, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
