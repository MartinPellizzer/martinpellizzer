Potentilla adscendens var. subbiserrata

Potentilla adscendens var. subbiserrata, commonly known as the serrated potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that often grow along the ground or climb slightly, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with finely serrated margins that give the plant its distinguishing name. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.
