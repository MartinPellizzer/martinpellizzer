Pelargonium hepaticifolium

Pelargonium hepaticifolium, commonly known as the liverleaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
