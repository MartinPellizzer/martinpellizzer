Piper timothianum

Piper timothianum, commonly known as the Timothian pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
