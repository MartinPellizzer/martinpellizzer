Pyrenaria diospyricarpa var. diospyricarpa

Pyrenaria diospyricarpa var. diospyricarpa, commonly known as the Chinese persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ebenaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, followed by fruits that are typically orange to red in color when mature. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicines.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
