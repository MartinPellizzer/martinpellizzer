Physaria lepidota

Physaria lepidota, commonly known as the sticky phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its scientific name reflects its unique botanical traits, including the presence of glandular structures.

Physaria lepidota is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
