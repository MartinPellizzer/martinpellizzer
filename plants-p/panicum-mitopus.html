Panicum mitopus

Panicum mitopus, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow with a distinctive reddish hue. The plant produces fluffy, spike-like inflorescences that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for native plant gardens.
