Piliostigma malabaricum var. acidum

Piliostigma malabaricum var. acidum, commonly known as the sour acacia, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
