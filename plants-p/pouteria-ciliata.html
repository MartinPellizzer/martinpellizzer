Pouteria ciliata

Pouteria ciliata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces a heavy crop of fruit, which contains the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ecological and economic importance is significant, particularly in the Amazon rainforest.
