Pandanus oviger

Pandanus oviger, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiny, woody fruits. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense thickets in mangrove areas.
