Piper concinnum var. trinitense

Piper concinnum var. trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or Trinitarian pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago, and grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is a climbing vine with small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature. The berries are used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for their pungent, spicy flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary applications in the Caribbean.
