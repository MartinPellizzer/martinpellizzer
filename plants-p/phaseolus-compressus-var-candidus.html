Phaseolus compressus var. candidus

Phaseolus compressus var. candidus, commonly known as the white runner bean, is a variant of the common runner bean characterized by its pale to white pod color.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is an annual herb that produces climbing vines with trifoliate leaves and showy flowers. Its pods are typically elongated and can be harvested when young and tender for culinary use.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild flavor, making it popular in both gardens and kitchens.
