Preonanthus occidentalis

Preonanthus occidentalis, commonly known as the western false indigo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
