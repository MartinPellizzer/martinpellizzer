Procris curtisii

Procris curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's spider orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and a prominent, forward-facing lip. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires specific microclimatic conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, Procris curtisii is considered a conservation concern.
