Pittosporum sahnianum

Pittosporum sahnianum, commonly known as the sahnian pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
