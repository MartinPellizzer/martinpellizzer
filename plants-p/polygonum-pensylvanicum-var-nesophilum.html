Polygonum pensylvanicum var. nesophilum

Polygonum pensylvanicum var. nesophilum, commonly known as the eastern purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive seed heads and jointed stems. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers form showy, purple-tinged clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variety is distinguished by its more slender form and slightly different coloration compared to the typical varietal form.
