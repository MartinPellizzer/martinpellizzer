Pelargonium × patens

Pelargonium × patens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × patens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
