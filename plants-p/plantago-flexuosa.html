Plantago flexuosa

Plantago flexuosa, commonly known as creeping plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a robust root system and produces rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
