Phyteuma spicatum subsp. betonicifolium

Phyteuma spicatum subsp.

betonicifolium, commonly known as the betonicifolium phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pale blue to lavender flowers.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive floral display.
