Parasenecio auriculatus var. bulbiferus

Parasenecio auriculatus var. bulbiferus, commonly known as the bulbous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value contribute to its appeal among horticulturists.
