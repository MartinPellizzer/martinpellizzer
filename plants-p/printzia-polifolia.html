Printzia polifolia

Printzia polifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf printzia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and often have a silvery or grayish hue due to the fine hairs covering them.

Printzia polifolia is a perennial shrub that is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
