Piper hastularum var. puberulentum

Piper hastularum var. puberulentum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or *Piper puberulentum*, is a tropical shrub native to Central America.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are pubescent, giving the plant its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue, peppery berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The berries contain capsaicin-like compounds, making them useful in traditional medicine.
