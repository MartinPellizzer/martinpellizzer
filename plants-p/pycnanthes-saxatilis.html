Pycnanthes saxatilis

Pycnanthes saxatilis, commonly known as the rock tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes or in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
