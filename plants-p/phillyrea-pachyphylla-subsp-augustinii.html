Phillyrea pachyphylla subsp. augustinii

Phillyrea pachyphylla subsp.

augustinii, commonly known as the Mediterranean Phillyrea, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Phillyrea pachyphylla subsp.

augustinii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
