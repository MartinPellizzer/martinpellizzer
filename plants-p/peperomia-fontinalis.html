Peperomia fontinalis

Peperomia fontinalis, commonly known as the water peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features dense, waxy foliage with a unique rosette shape, often displaying shades of green, silver, and variegation. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers consistently moist, well-draining soil. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne on short spikes and are not the primary ornamental feature.

Peperomia fontinalis is prized for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
