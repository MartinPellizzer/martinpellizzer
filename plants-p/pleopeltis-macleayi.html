Pleopeltis macleayi

Pleopeltis macleayi, commonly known as the Macleay's pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizome is creeping, and it produces small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves.
