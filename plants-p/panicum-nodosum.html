Panicum nodosum

Panicum nodosum, commonly known as nodding grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often nodding inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when young.

Panicum nodosum is often used in landscaping for erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
