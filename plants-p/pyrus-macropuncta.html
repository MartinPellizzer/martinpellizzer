Pyrus macropuncta

Pyrus macropuncta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by clusters of small, round, green fruits. These fruits are edible when ripe and have a sweet, tart flavor.

Pyrus macropuncta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
