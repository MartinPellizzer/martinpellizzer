Pourouma cordata

Pourouma cordata, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are also used in traditional crafts and as thatch materials in some regions.

The areca palm is relatively low-maintenance and adapts well to various soil types, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
