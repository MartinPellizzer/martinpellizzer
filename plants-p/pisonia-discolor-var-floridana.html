Pisonia discolor var. floridana

Pisonia discolor var. floridana, commonly known as the Florida pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink to red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils along the Florida coast.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
