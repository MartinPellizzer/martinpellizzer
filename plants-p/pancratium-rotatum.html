Pancratium rotatum

Pancratium rotatum, commonly known as the sea daffodil, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in dune environments. It produces a bulbous stem that stores nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh coastal conditions.

Pancratium rotatum is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
