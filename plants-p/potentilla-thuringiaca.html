Potentilla thuringiaca

Potentilla thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, which are typically oval-shaped and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Potentilla thuringiaca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
