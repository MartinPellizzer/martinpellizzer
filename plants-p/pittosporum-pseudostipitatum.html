Pittosporum pseudostipitatum

Pittosporum pseudostipitatum, commonly known as the New Zealand Pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a slight pubescence on the underside. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
