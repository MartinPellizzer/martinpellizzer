Phalaenopsis finleyi

Phalaenopsis finleyi, commonly known as the Finley's moth orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis finleyi is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for indoor gardening and floral arrangements.
