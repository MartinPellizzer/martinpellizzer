Pseudosedum ferganense subsp. ferganense

Pseudosedum ferganense subsp.

ferganense, commonly known as the Fergana sedum, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features fleshy, often blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in survival during dry periods. Its flowers are small and usually yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
