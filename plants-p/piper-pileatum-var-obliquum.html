Piper pileatum var. obliquum

Piper pileatum var. obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by peppery, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for both horticultural displays and traditional medicine.
