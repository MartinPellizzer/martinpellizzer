Pimpinella magna var. rosea

Pimpinella magna var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored angelica, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, loamy soils in full sun to partial shade. Its seeds are small and have a distinctive, ridged surface, and the plant is known for its aromatic, slightly spicy scent.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
