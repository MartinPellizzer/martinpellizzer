Potentilla holopetala var. gordiaginii

Potentilla holopetala var. gordiaginii, commonly known as the Armenian rose, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, arching stems and dark green, elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
