Plumeria floribunda

Plumeria floribunda, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is toxic if ingested. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, making it a striking addition to gardens and landscapes.
