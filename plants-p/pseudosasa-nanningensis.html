Pseudosasa nanningensis

Pseudosasa nanningensis, commonly known as Nanning bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangxi and Guangdong provinces, where it grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
