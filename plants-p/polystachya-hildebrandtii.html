Polystachya hildebrandtii

Polystachya hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polystachya hildebrandtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
