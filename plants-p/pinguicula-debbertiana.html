Pinguicula debbertiana

Pinguicula debbertiana, commonly known as the Debber's butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity.

Its botanical features make it a unique and fascinating subject for study in the field of carnivorous plants.
