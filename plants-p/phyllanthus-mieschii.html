Phyllanthus mieschii

Phyllanthus mieschii, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phyllanthus mieschii is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
