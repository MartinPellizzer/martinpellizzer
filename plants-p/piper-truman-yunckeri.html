Piper truman-yunckeri

Piper truman-yunckeri, commonly known as the Truman-Yuncker's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often trail along the ground or climb supports. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
