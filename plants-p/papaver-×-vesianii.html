Papaver × vesianii

Papaver × vesianii, commonly known as the Himalayan poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver bracteanum and Papaver orientale.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan, where it thrives in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
