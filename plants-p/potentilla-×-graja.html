Potentilla × graja

Potentilla × graja, commonly known as the hybrid shrub, is a deciduous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Potentilla × graja is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
