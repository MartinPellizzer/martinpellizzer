Pelargonium cuspidatum

Pelargonium cuspidatum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant features serrated leaves and produces vibrant blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Pelargonium cuspidatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
