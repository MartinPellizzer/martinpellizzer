Panicum vescum

Panicum vescum, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant native to warm temperate and tropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small grains, which are often used as a grain crop in some parts of the world. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for various ecological conditions.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it a valuable forage crop and a pioneer species in disturbed soils.
