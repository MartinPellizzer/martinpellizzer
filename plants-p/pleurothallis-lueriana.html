Pleurothallis lueriana

Pleurothallis lueriana, commonly known as the Lueriana orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps in conserving moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis lueriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
