Phyllanthus echinatus

Phyllanthus echinatus, commonly known as the spiny goosefoot, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a rough, spiny texture on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Phyllanthus echinatus is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
