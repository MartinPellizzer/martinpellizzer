Plantago media var. nevadensis

Plantago media var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States, particularly found in Nevada and surrounding regions.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and rocky areas. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
