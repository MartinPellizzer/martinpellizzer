Pterostylis ciliata

Pterostylis ciliata, commonly known as the fringed finger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a single, pendulous flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow color and intricate veining. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The orchid's structure is typical of the Orchidaceae family, featuring specialized pollination mechanisms.
