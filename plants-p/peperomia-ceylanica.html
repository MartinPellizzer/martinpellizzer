Peperomia ceylanica

Peperomia ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Sri Lanka and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or a trailing vine, with thick, fleshy stems and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia ceylanica is known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
