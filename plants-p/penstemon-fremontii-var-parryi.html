Penstemon fremontii var. parryi

Penstemon fremontii var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces showy, glandular-hairy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in xeriscaped gardens.
