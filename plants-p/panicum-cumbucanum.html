Panicum cumbucanum

Panicum cumbucanum, commonly known as the Cumbucan grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branching stalks that produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
