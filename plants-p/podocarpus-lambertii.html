Podocarpus lambertii

Podocarpus lambertii, commonly known as the Lambert's podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and narrow, needle-like leaves. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are often consumed by various bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
