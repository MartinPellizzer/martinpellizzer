Petunia parviflora

Petunia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered petunia, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of purple, blue, and white, depending on the variety. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
