Pseudognaphalium yalaense

Pseudognaphalium yalaense, commonly known as the Yala everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Yala National Park in Sri Lanka.

The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.
