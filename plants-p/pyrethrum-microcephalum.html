Pyrethrum microcephalum

Pyrethrum microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a strong, aromatic scent.

Pyrethrum microcephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in natural pest control due to its bioactive compounds.
