Potentilla aristata

Potentilla aristata, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, five-petaled flowers in early summer.

Potentilla aristata is often used in rock gardens and alpine gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
