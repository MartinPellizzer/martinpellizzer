Phyteuma campanuloides

Phyteuma campanuloides, commonly known as the bell-shaped phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both wildflower gardens and ecological landscapes.
