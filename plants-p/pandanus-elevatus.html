Pandanus elevatus

Pandanus elevatus, commonly known as the high pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the Pandanus genus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the tips of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, demonstrating adaptability to humid and saline conditions.
