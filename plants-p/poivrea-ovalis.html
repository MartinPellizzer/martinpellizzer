Poivrea ovalis

Poivrea ovalis, commonly known as the oval honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow to orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are red and juicy, mature in the fall and are an important food source for birds.

Poivrea ovalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
