Pilosella kumbelica

Pilosella kumbelica, commonly known as the Kumbelika's pappus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow central disk. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its hairy stems and leaves are adapted to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions.

Pilosella kumbelica is often found in mountainous areas of Central and Eastern Europe.
