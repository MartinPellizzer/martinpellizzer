Polygala × pawlowskii

Polygala × pawlowskii, commonly known as the hybrid milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale blue or pink, with a unique structure that resembles a small, spiny capsule.

Polygala × pawlowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
