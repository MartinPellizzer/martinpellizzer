Primula maximowiczii

Primula maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountain meadows. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture, while its stems are usually upright and branching.

Primula maximowiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
