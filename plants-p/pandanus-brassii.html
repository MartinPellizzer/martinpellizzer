Pandanus brassii

Pandanus brassii, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as roofing material in various island cultures.

Pandanus brassii thrives in humid, coastal environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
