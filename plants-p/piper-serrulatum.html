Piper serrulatum

Piper serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, serrated leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
