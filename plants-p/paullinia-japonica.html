Paullinia japonica

Paullinia japonica, commonly known as Japanese sarsaparilla, is a woody vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Sarsaparillaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitats.
