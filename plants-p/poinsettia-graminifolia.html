Poinsettia graminifolia

Poinsettia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it its common name, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

Poinsettia graminifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly during the holiday season.
