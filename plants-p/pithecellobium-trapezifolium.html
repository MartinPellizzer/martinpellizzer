Pithecellobium trapezifolium

Pithecellobium trapezifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pita, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and pod-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
