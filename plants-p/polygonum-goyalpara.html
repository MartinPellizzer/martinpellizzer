Polygonum goyalpara

Polygonum goyalpara, commonly known as the Gwalior knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive bead-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in riverine areas and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
