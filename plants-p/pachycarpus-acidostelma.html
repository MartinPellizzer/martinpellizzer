Pachycarpus acidostelma

Pachycarpus acidostelma, commonly known as the sourstemmed pachycarpus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
