Pileostegia urceolata

Pileostegia urceolata, commonly known as the false arum, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its unique inflorescence.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, which resembles a flower but is actually a modified leaf. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and deep green.

Pileostegia urceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
