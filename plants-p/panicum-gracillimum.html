Panicum gracillimum

Panicum gracillimum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, delicate spikelets that are often arranged in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Panicum gracillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
