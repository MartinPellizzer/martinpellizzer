Polygonum tibeticum

Polygonum tibeticum, commonly known as Tibetan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its swollen nodes and distinctive leaf patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum tibeticum is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Tibetan medicine.
