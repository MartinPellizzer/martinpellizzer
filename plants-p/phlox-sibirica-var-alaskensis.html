Phlox sibirica var. alaskensis

Phlox sibirica var. alaskensis, commonly known as Alaska phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Arctic tundra and alpine regions of northern North America.

It produces dense clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold climates with long, harsh winters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environmental conditions.
