Pteris scabra

Pteris scabra, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, serrated fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant features a creeping rhizome with dark brown, hairy roots, and its fronds are typically bipinnate with a rough, textured surface. Pteris scabra thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest undergrowth.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and horticultural collections.
