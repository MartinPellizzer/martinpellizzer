Phlox pilosa subsp. detonsa

Phlox pilosa subsp.

detonsa, commonly known as the hairy phlox or mountain phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands or along stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
