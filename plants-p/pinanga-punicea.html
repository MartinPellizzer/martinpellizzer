Pinanga punicea

Pinanga punicea, commonly known as the red-pink bamboo, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its striking red and pink inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by a colorful spathe. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pinanga punicea is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
