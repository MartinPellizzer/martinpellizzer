Premna obtusifolia var. serratifolia

Premna obtusifolia var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated-leaf glory vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Verbenaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often with a reddish calyx. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
