Primula sinomollis

Primula sinomollis, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

Primula sinomollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
