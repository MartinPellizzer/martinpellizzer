Polygonum pangianum

Polygonum pangianum, commonly known as the Philippine knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its rhombic leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and streambanks. Its stems are often reddish or purplish, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Polygonum pangianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
