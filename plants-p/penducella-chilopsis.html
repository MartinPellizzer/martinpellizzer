Penducella chilopsis

Penducella chilopsis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white powder.

Penducella chilopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
