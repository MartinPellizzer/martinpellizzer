Persicaria maculosa var. maculosa

Persicaria maculosa var. maculosa, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems, which are often covered in small, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along creek banks.

Persicaria maculosa var. maculosa is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within one growing season.
