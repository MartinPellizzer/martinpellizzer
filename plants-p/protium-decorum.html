Protium decorum

Protium decorum, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

Protium decorum also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
