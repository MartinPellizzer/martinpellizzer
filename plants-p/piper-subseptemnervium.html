Piper subseptemnervium

Piper subseptemnervium, commonly known as the seven-veined pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with seven distinct veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
