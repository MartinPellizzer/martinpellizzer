Paeonia × moutan var. humei

Paeonia × moutan var. humei, commonly known as the Hume's tree peony, is a variant of the tree peony native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, double flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its spring blooming period.
