Paeonia emodi subsp. sterniana

Paeonia emodi subsp.

sterniana, commonly known as the Himalayan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has deeply lobed leaves and produces yellow anthers that release pollen. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
