Piper cordulatum

Piper cordulatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pepper or heartleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Piper cordulatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
