Pleiotaxis subscaposa

Pleiotaxis subscaposa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pleiotaxis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stem and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
