Pastinaca kochii

Pastinaca kochii, commonly known as the Koch's burdock, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its thick, hollow stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by prickly, spherical fruits. Its root system consists of long, fibrous roots that are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Pastinaca kochii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
