Pouzolzia lucida

Pouzolzia lucida, commonly known as the shining pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pouzolzia lucida is often found in woodland edges and wetlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
