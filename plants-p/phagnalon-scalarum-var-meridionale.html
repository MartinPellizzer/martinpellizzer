Phagnalon scalarum var. meridionale

Phagnalon scalarum var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in early summer.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature.
