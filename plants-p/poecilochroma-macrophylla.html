Poecilochroma macrophylla

Poecilochroma macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant has showy, white to pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor in the substrate, while its stems spread across the water.

Poecilochroma macrophylla plays a vital role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
