Potentilla sericea var. sericea

Potentilla sericea var. sericea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it suitable for a variety of ecological and ornamental applications.
