Polygala sibirica lusus chinensis

Polygala sibirica lusus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that contain a single seed, and it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
