Pandanus polyglossus

Pandanus polyglossus, commonly known as the common pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or saline soils. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.
