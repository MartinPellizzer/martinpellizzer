Puya coerulea var. violacea

Puya coerulea var. violacea, commonly known as the violet puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-forming structure with vibrant violet-blue bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique and colorful display.
