Pyrus krymica

Pyrus krymica, commonly known as the Crimean pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad ovate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear with a sweet and tangy flavor, typically harvested in late summer. It is adapted to the temperate climate of its native region and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
