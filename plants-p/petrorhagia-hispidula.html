Petrorhagia hispidula

Petrorhagia hispidula, commonly known as the hairy rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Petrorhagia hispidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in arid environments.
