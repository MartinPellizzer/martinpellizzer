Protea capitata

Protea capitata, commonly known as the sugarbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often mistaken for flowers, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers.

Protea capitata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
