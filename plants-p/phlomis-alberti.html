Phlomis alberti

Phlomis alberti, commonly known as Albert's blazingstar, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green fuzzy hairs. Its showy, yellow-orange flower clusters appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlomis alberti is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
