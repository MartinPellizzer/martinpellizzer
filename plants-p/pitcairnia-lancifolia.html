Pitcairnia lancifolia

Pitcairnia lancifolia, commonly known as the sword fern, is a striking bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its long, sword-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant forms a rosette structure that holds water, creating a microhabitat for small organisms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Pitcairnia lancifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in tropical gardens worldwide.
