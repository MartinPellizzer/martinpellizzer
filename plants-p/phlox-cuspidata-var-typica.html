Phlox cuspidata var. typica

Phlox cuspidata var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, pink to lavender-colored flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a pointed tip, giving the plant its specific epithet "cuspidata.". It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
