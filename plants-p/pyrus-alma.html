Pyrus alma

Pyrus alma, commonly known as the Alma pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with clusters of white flowers appearing in early spring. Its fruit, the Alma pear, is small, sweet, and has a crisp texture, making it a valued fruit in local cuisines.

The tree typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters and is well adapted to cold climates.
