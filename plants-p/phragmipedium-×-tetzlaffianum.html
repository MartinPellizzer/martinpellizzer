Phragmipedium × tetzlaffianum

Phragmipedium × tetzlaffianum, commonly known as the hybrid phragmipedium, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from crosses between Phragmipedium kovachii and Phragmipedium sanderianum.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive lip, and it blooms in late winter to early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific humidity and light requirements.
