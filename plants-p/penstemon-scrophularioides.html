Penstemon scrophularioides

Penstemon scrophularioides, commonly known as the tall bearded penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant purple or red flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular plant among gardeners and botanists alike.
