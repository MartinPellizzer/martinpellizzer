Pimelea imbricata var. crinita

Pimelea imbricata var. crinita, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance in cultivation.
