Pulsatilla alpina subsp. austriaca

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian pasqueflower, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, basal leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers with numerous stamens.

Native to the alpine regions of Central Europe, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears the flowers in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
