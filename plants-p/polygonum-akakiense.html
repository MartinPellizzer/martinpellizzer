Polygonum akakiense

Polygonum akakiense, commonly known as the Japanese knotweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.

Polygonum akakiense is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
