Pittosporum timorerse var. teysmannianum

Pittosporum timorerse var. teysmannianum, commonly known as the Timor Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in regions with suitable climates.
