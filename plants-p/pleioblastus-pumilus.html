Pleioblastus pumilus

Pleioblastus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, dark green culms that grow in dense clusters, making it ideal for small garden spaces. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Pleioblastus pumilus is valued for its low maintenance and ability to add a touch of elegance to rock gardens or as a container plant.
