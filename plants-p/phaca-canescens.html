Phaca canescens

Phaca canescens, commonly known as the woolly phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid conditions.

Phaca canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
