Premna corymbosa var. madagascariensis

Premna corymbosa var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically pink to red, with tubular corollas that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
