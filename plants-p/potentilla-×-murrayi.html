Potentilla × murrayi

Potentilla × murrayi, commonly known as the Murray's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Potentilla × murrayi is also valued for its ability to tolerate dry conditions and partial shade.
