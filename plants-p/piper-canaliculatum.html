Piper canaliculatum

Piper canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
