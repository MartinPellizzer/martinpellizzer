Persicaria punctata var. eciliata

Persicaria punctata var. eciliata, commonly known as the spotted knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a slightly serrated edge and often bear small, raised dots. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
