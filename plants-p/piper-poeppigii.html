Piper poeppigii

Piper poeppigii, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, peppercorn-like fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper poeppigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild peppery flavors, making it useful in some culinary applications.
