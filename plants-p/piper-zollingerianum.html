Piper zollingerianum

Piper zollingerianum, commonly known as Zollinger's pepper or the Amazonian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit, a small, spherical berry, contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
