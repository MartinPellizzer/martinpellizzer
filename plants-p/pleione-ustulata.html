Pleione ustulata

Pleione ustulata, commonly known as the dragon's tongue orchid, is a species of terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in spring. The plant prefers cool, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its flowers are often pink or purple with a distinctive yellow or orange lip.

Pleione ustulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
