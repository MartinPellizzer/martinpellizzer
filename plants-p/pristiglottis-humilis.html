Pristiglottis humilis

Pristiglottis humilis, commonly known as the small leech plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that resemble the appearance of leeches.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in marshy or wetland environments. The plant is notable for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its small size and specialized structure allow it to anchor itself in soft substrates while filtering water effectively.

Pristiglottis humilis plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for microorganisms.
