Polygonum × pseudolapathum

Polygonum × pseudolapathum, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a hybrid perennial plant characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and often edible seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and roadside areas. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a bluish-green hue, and it produces pink to reddish flowers in late summer.

Polygonum × pseudolapathum is often considered a weed but can serve as a food source for certain insects and birds.
