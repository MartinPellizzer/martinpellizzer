Polyouratea serratula

Polyouratea serratula, commonly known as the serrated sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
