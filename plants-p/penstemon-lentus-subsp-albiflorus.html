Penstemon lentus subsp. albiflorus

Penstemon lentus subsp.

albiflorus, commonly known as the whiteflower beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
