Pterocaulon interruptum

Pterocaulon interruptum, commonly known as the interrupted pterocaulon, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a shrub or subshrub characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink. Pterocaulon interruptum is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
