Primula longipilosa

Primula longipilosa, commonly known as the long-hair primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense, woolly foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
