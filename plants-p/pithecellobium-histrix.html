Pithecellobium histrix

Pithecellobium histrix, commonly known as the rough pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is distinguished by its rough, thorn-covered stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in racemes, while the fruits are elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
