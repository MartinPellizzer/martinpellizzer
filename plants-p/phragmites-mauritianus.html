Phragmites mauritianus

Phragmites mauritianus, commonly known as the Mauritius reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It forms dense stands and is often found in marshes, along lake shores, and in brackish water areas. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces fluffy, feathery seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its rhizomes spread underground, allowing it to colonize large areas quickly.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
