Plectranthus selukwensis

Plectranthus selukwensis, commonly known as the Selukwensi mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink in color, often clustered in whorls along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
