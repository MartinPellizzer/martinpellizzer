Portulaca confertifolia

Portulaca confertifolia, commonly known as the clustered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to drought conditions, with a short life cycle that allows it to complete its growth and reproduction quickly.
