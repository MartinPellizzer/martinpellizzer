Panicum trichopodon

Panicum trichopodon, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often clustered at the base of the plant. It is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.
