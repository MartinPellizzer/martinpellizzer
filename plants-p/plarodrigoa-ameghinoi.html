Plarodrigoa ameghinoi

Plarodrigoa ameghinoi, commonly known as the Ameghino's plarodrigo, is a fossilized plant genus discovered in South America.

It belongs to the group of extinct gymnosperms, which were dominant during the Mesozoic era. The plant's fossils exhibit features typical of early coniferous structures, including needle-like leaves and reproductive structures. Its discovery provides insights into the evolutionary transition from primitive to more complex plant forms.

The genus is named in honor of the paleontologist Florentino Ameghino, who made significant contributions to the study of South American fossils.
