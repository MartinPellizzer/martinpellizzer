Piper hirsutissimum

Piper hirsutissimum, commonly known as the hairy pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are peppery and used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The botanical features of Piper hirsutissimum make it an important species in ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
