Passiflora × munroii

Passiflora × munroii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the development of many passionflower hybrids.
