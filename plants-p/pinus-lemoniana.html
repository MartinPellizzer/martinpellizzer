Pinus lemoniana

Pinus lemoniana, commonly known as the lemon pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The tree produces cones that are often yellow or reddish in hue, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid and semi-arid environments.

The lemon pine is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
