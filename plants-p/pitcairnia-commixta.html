It is native to the rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescence that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its flowers are typically white or pink and are pollinated by various insects and birds.

Pitcairnia commixta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
