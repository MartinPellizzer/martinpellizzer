Polypodium vulgare var. japonicum

Polypodium vulgare var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern native to eastern Asia.

It is a perennial fern characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds that form a compact, shield-like rosette. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, curled appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
