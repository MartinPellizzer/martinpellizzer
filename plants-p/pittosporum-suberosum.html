Pittosporum suberosum

Pittosporum suberosum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its rough, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum suberosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
