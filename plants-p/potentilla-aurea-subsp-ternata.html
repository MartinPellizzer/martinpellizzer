Potentilla aurea subsp. ternata

Potentilla aurea subsp.

ternata, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
