Petrophile acicularis

Petrophile acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, bright red or pink flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

Petrophile acicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
