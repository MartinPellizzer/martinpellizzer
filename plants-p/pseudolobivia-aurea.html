Pseudolobivia aurea

Pseudolobivia aurea, commonly known as the golden lobivia, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract various pollinators. Its adaptations to desert conditions include reduced leaf surfaces and the ability to store water in its stems.

Pseudolobivia aurea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
