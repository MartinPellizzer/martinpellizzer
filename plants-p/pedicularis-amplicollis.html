Pedicularis amplicollis

Pedicularis amplicollis, commonly known as the broad-necked lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the genus Pedicularis, which includes many species of louseworts known for their unique, often bioluminescent flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in rocky and acidic soils. Its flowers are usually red or orange, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a louse, hence its common name.

Pedicularis amplicollis is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in pollination by attracting various insects, including bees and flies.
