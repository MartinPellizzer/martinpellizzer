Penstemon gairdneri subsp. typicus

Penstemon gairdneri subsp.

typicus, commonly known as Gairdner's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
