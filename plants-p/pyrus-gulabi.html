Pyrus gulabi

Pyrus gulabi, commonly known as the Kashmiri pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in traditional cuisines.
