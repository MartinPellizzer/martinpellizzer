Papaver sherriffii

Papaver sherriffii, commonly known as Sherriff's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that resemble those of other poppy species. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.

The seeds of Papaver sherriffii are small and can be dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across suitable environments.
