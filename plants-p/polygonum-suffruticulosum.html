Polygonum suffruticulosum

Polygonum suffruticulosum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and hairy stems that often turn reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in hue, blooming from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum suffruticulosum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, pond edges, and disturbed soils.
