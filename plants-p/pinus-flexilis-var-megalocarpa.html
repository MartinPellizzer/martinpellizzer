Pinus flexilis var. megalocarpa

Pinus flexilis var. megalocarpa, commonly known as the giant ponderosa pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and thick, scaly bark that provides protection against fire. The cones of this species are large and can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters, containing numerous seeds. Its needles are long and slender, typically growing in bundles of five, and are often covered with a whitish band.

This variety is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high elevation.
