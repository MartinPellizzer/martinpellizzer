Poincianella eriostachys

Poincianella eriostachys, commonly known as the silky poinciana, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that produce clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with delicate, thread-like leaflets that give it a lacy appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Poincianella eriostachys is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
