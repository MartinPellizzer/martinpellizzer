Pleurothallis caulescens

Pleurothallis caulescens, commonly known as the stalked pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant features a short, stout stem with numerous small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are often arranged in a rosette pattern and are covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy tissue.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique floral structure and ease of care.
