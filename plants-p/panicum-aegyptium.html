Panicum aegyptium

Panicum aegyptium, commonly known as Egyptian millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed seeds that are often used as a grain in various parts of the world. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for cultivation in arid regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
