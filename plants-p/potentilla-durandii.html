Potentilla durandii

Potentilla durandii, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, which are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, mountainous climates.

Potentilla durandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
