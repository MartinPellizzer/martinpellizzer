Pselium

Pselium, commonly known as the common senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and parts of southern Europe. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are pinnately compound. Pselium is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its laxative effects.

The seeds are often used in herbal remedies and are rich in compounds like sennosides.
