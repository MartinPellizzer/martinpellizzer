Primula pauciflora var. cusickii

Primula pauciflora var. cusickii, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high elevations of California and Nevada.

It features rosettes of narrow, silvery-green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
