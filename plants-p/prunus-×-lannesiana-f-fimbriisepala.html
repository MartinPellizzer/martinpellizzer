Prunus × lannesiana f. fimbriisepala

Prunus × lannesiana f. fimbriisepala, commonly known as the Japanese apricot with fringed sepals, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that feature distinctive, fringed sepals, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is typically deciduous, with small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental choice in gardens.

Due to its hybrid nature, it may exhibit variability in growth habits and flowering patterns.
