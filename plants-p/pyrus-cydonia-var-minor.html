Pyrus cydonia var. minor

Pyrus cydonia var. minor, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous fruit tree native to the Middle East and Southwestern Asia.

It produces a fragrant, yellowish fruit that is typically harvested in late autumn. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The fruit is often used in cooking, preserving, and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and nutritional content.
