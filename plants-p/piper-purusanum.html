Piper purusanum

Piper purusanum, commonly known as the Indian pepper plant, is a climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its aromatic leaves and fruits are also utilized in culinary applications in some local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
