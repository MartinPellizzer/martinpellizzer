Pleurothallis phoxophylla

Pleurothallis phoxophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Pleurothallis phoxophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
