Papaver lapponicum subsp. jugoricum

Papaver lapponicum subsp.

jugoricum, commonly known as the Arctic poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates. It has a short growing season and blooms early in the summer.

The subspecies jugoricum is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology and geographic distribution compared to other subspecies of Papaver lapponicum.
