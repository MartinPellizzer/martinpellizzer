Passiflora pediculata

Passiflora pediculata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central reproductive structure. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures.

Passiflora pediculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
