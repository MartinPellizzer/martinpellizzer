Potamogeton natans subsp. vulgaris

Potamogeton natans subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia. It has long, slender stems that often form dense underwater vegetation, and its leaves are broad, oval, and slightly notched at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

It plays a vital role in water filtration and provides habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
