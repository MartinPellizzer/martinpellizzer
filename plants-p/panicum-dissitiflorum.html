Panicum dissitiflorum

Panicum dissitiflorum, commonly known as the scattered-flower panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces loose, open inflorescences with small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when fresh.

Panicum dissitiflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
