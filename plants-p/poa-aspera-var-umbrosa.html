Poa aspera var. umbrosa

Poa aspera var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. This variety is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. It thrives in shady, moist environments, often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and spikelet-based inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
