Plantago trichophora

Plantago trichophora, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Its robust growth and adaptability make it a common weed in many areas.
