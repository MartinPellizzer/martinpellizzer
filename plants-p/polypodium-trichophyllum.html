Polypodium trichophyllum

Polypodium trichophyllum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds that typically grow in dense clusters on rocky surfaces. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in temperate and tropical regions. Its rhizomes are covered in fine, soft hairs, which contribute to its common name.

Polypodium trichophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
