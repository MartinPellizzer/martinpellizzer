Penstemon coeruleus

Penstemon coeruleus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, tubular blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are evergreen in mild climates.

Penstemon coeruleus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
