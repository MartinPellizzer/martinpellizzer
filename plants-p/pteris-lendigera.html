Pteris lendigera

Pteris lendigera, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are often used for propagation, and the fronds can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Pteris lendigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
