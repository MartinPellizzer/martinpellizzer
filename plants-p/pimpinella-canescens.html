Pimpinella canescens

Pimpinella canescens, commonly known as hoary senna, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its finely hairy, grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its seeds are enclosed in narrow, slender pods that split open when mature.

Pimpinella canescens is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
