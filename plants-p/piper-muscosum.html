Piper muscosum

Piper muscosum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or kaphal, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture with a distinct, pungent aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
