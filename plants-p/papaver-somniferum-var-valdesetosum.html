Papaver somniferum var. valdesetosum

Papaver somniferum var. valdesetosum, commonly known as the Valdesetosum poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy characterized by its distinctive floral morphology and medicinal properties.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and features striking, large, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and pink. The plant produces capsules containing opium latex, which has been historically used for its analgesic and sedative effects. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, alternate leaves, and a taproot system.

Due to its association with narcotics, it is subject to strict legal regulations in many countries.
