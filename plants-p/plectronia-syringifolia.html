Plectronia syringifolia

Plectronia syringifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange or red berries. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in small, hooked spines. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
