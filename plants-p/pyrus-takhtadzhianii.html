Pyrus takhtadzhianii

Pyrus takhtadzhianii, commonly known as the Armenian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and is characterized by its deciduous nature and distinctive foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by pear-shaped fruits that ripen in late summer. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
