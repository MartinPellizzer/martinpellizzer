Polygala glomerata var. pedunculosa

Polygala glomerata var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental potential in native plant gardens.
