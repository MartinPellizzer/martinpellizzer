Polygonum milletii

Polygonum milletii, commonly known as Chinese milk vetch, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate regions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its roots are also utilized in some agricultural practices for soil stabilization.
