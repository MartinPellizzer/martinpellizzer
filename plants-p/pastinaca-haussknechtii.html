Pastinaca haussknechtii

Pastinaca haussknechtii, commonly known as the Hausknecht's burdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Pastinaca haussknechtii thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
