Picea canadensis

Picea canadensis, commonly known as the Canadian spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Canada and the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

Picea canadensis plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
