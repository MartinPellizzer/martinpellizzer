Pseudomaxillaria virescens

Pseudomaxillaria virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant features a pseudobulb-like structure and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white pubescence. It blooms during the warmer months and is valued for its unique coloration and ornamental appeal.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
