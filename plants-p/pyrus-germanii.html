Pyrus germanii

Pyrus germanii, commonly known as the European pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a crisp, juicy texture and a sweet to tart flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types and climates.
