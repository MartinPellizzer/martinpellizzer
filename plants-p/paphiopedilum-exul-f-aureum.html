Paphiopedilum exul f. aureum

Paphiopedilum exul f. aureum, commonly known as the golden-lip orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, strap-like leaves and distinctive, showy flowers with a yellowish-orange lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure and vibrant coloration, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

The f. aureum is a naturally occurring variant that exhibits enhanced yellow pigmentation, setting it apart from the typical form of the species.
