Polygala stenoclada var. typica

Polygala stenoclada var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish to greenish flowers that are often arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

It is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
