Pyrus chuana

Pyrus chuana, commonly known as the Chilean white pear, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar in appearance to pears.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its fruit in some regions.
