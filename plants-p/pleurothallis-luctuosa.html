Pleurothallis luctuosa

Pleurothallis luctuosa, commonly known as the mournful orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, where it thrives in the understory of rainforests. It produces small, delicate flowers that are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species. The plant has a compact growth habit and possesses pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Due to its habitat requirements and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
