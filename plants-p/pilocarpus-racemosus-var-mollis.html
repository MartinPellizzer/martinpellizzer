Pilocarpus racemosus var. mollis

Pilocarpus racemosus var. mollis, commonly known as the silky pilocarpus, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, softly hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
