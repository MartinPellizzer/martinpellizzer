Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp. gemmiparus

Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp.

gemmiparus, commonly known as the gemmiparous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant reproduces both sexually through flowers and asexually via gemmae, which are small vegetative propagules that form on the stems. It typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and plays a role in stabilizing sediment and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
