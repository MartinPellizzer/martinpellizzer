Pandanus andersonii

Pandanus andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically grouped in spikes at the top of the plant. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some island communities.
