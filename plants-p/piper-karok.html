Piper karok

Piper karok, commonly known as the Karok pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, narrow berries containing a single seed. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, spicy aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
