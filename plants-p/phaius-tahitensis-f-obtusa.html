Phaius tahitensis f. obtusa

Phaius tahitensis f. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a unique variant of the Phaius tahitensis species.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often wider than those of the typical form. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically arranged in a spike. It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
