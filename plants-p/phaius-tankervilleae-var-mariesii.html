Phaius tankervilleae var. mariesii

Phaius tankervilleae var. mariesii, commonly known as the Mariesii Orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a contrasting lip. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in moist, shaded environments with well-draining soil. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
