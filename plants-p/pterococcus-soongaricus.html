Pterococcus soongaricus

Pterococcus soongaricus, commonly known as the Mongolian tamarind, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
