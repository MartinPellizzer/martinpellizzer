Protea radiata

Protea radiata, commonly known as the sugarbowl protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy, spherical inflorescence that resembles a sugar bowl, hence its common name. The flowers are typically pink to red in color, with numerous small petals arranged in a dense, rounded shape. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
