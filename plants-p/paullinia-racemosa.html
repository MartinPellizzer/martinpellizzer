Paullinia racemosa

Paullinia racemosa, commonly known as the Brazilian sarsaparilla, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
