Plocama puberula

Plocama puberula, commonly known as the silky willow or silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silky appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and are pollinated by wind.

Plocama puberula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
