Polemonium pulcherrimum var. berryi

Polemonium pulcherrimum var. berryi, commonly known as the eastern bluebell, is a striking perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lanceolate, with a bluish-green hue and a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded, well-drained soils.

The plant is notable for its early spring blooming period and its role in supporting pollinators.
