Pholidota imbricata var. papuana

Pholidota imbricata var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to New Guinea and parts of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the tree a dramatic appearance during the flowering season. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a deep green color. The plant thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or near water sources.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
