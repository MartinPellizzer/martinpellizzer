Ptelea simplicifolia

Ptelea simplicifolia, commonly known as western ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands and chaparral habitats.

Ptelea simplicifolia is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife species.
