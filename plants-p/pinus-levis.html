Pinus levis

Pinus levis, commonly known as the white pine, is a species of coniferous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cone-shaped seed-bearing structures that mature over several years. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, becoming fissured with age.

Pinus levis plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ecological benefits.
