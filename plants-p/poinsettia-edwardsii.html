Poinsettia edwardsii

Poinsettia edwardsii, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The true flowers are small and inconspicuous, located in the center of the plant. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is popular as a decorative species, especially during the holiday season.
