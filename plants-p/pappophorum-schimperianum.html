Pappophorum schimperianum

Pappophorum schimperianum, commonly known as the Schimper's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of many grasses. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, open habitats.

Pappophorum schimperianum is often used in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
