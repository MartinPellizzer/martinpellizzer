Philodendron callosum subsp. ptarianum

Philodendron callosum subsp.

ptarianum, commonly known as the Brazilian Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Brazil. It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
