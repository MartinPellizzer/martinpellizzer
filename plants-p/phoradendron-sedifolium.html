Phoradendron sedifolium

Phoradendron sedifolium, commonly known as the mistletoe of the genus Phoradendron, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of trees and shrubs.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are modified to form haustoria. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical studies.
