Panicum sanguinale chloranthum

Panicum sanguinale chloranthum, commonly known as the bloodgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink inflorescences that resemble blood droplets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish hue that intensifies during flowering.

The species is often used in ornamental horticulture for its visual appeal and ecological value in supporting local wildlife.
