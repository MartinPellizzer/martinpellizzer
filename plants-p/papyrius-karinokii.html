Papyrius karinokii

Papyrius karinokii, commonly known as the Karinokii papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of East Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length, often floating on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its rhizomes are fibrous and can form dense mats, which help stabilize the sediment in shallow water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often found in marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers.
