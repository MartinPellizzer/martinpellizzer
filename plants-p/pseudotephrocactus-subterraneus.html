Pseudotephrocactus subterraneus

Pseudotephrocactus subterraneus, commonly known as the underground cactus, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its subterranean growth habit, with most of its structure hidden beneath the soil surface. The plant produces small, spiny stems that grow close to the ground, and it has adapted to extreme drought conditions by storing water in its fleshy tissues. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, emerge above the soil during the rainy season.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological adaptations.
