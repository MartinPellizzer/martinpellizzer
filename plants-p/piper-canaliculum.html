Piper canaliculum

Piper canaliculum, commonly known as the narrow-veined pepper or false black pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and heart-shaped leaves that have a distinct waxy coating. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, slender berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper canaliculum make it an important species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
