Phytelephas humboldtiana

Phytelephas humboldtiana, commonly known as the Huayna Picchu palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. This palm is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
