Pandanus brevifrugalis

Pandanus brevifrugalis, commonly known as the short-fruited pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, clustered fruit that resembles a pineapple, which is edible and often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove swamps or near the shoreline.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
