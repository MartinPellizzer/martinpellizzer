Paspalum solitarium

Paspalum solitarium, commonly known as the solitary paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum solitarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
