Potamogeton × fluitans var. americanus

Potamogeton × fluitans var. americanus, commonly known as the American water plantain, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface or emerge from the water. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

The species is often found in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense stands, influencing aquatic ecosystems.
