Paspalum macrospermum

Paspalum macrospermum, commonly known as giant paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a valuable ecological plant and a potential invasive species in non-native regions.
