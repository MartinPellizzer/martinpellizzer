Piper linguifolium

Piper linguifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental foliage.
