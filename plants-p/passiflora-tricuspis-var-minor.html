Passiflora tricuspis var. minor

Passiflora tricuspis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser threecorners passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
