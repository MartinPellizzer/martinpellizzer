Packera pauciflora

Packera pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered fleabane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist meadows, prairies, and open woodlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer.

Packera pauciflora is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
