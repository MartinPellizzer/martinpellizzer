Pilosocereus lanuginosus subsp. moritzianus

Pilosocereus lanuginosus subsp.

moritzianus, commonly known as the Moritz's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
