Pisum sativum var. cerocarpum

Pisum sativum var. cerocarpum, commonly known as the purple-seeded pea, is a botanical variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple seed coat, which sets it apart from the typical green-seeded peas. This variety is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seed color. The plant exhibits the typical growth habits of the pea species, including climbing vines and trifoliate leaves.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown in gardens for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
