Palmijuncus concinnus

Palmijuncus concinnus, commonly known as the slender palm, is a rare and unique species of palm native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are pollinated by insects. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
