Phreatia oreogena

Phreatia oreogena, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil and air.

Phreatia oreogena is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
