Paphiopedilum liemianum var. primulinum

Paphiopedilum liemianum var. primulinum, commonly known as the golden-lip paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and fleshy pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive lip that is yellow or orange. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in two rows, contributing to its dense foliage.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
