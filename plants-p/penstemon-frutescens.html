Penstemon frutescens

Penstemon frutescens, commonly known as tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon frutescens is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.
