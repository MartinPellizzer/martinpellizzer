Persicaria coccinea subsp. pratincola

Persicaria coccinea subsp.

pratincola, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive red stems that become more prominent as the plant matures.

The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, branched inflorescences.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and roadside settings.
