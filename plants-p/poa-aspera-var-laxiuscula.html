Poa aspera var. laxiuscula

Poa aspera var. laxiuscula, commonly known as the slender rough bentgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native environment.
