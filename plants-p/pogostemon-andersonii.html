Pogostemon andersonii

Pogostemon andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
