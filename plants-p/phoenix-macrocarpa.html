Phoenix macrocarpa

Phoenix macrocarpa, commonly known as the bottle tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fluted trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often shed during periods of drought.

The bottle tree is popular as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
