Pelargonium × heteromallum

Pelargonium × heteromallum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × incompatibilis, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically features rounded, lobed leaves and flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often with a distinctive double or semi-double petal structure. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden beds and containers.

Pelargonium × heteromallum is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
