Puccinellia kengiana

Puccinellia kengiana, commonly known as Keng's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in low-nutrient soils.

Puccinellia kengiana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
