Pfaffia acutifolia

Pfaffia acutifolia, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is notable for its underground tubers, which are rich in bioactive compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
