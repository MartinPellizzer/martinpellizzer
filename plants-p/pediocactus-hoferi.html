Pediocactus hoferi

Pediocactus hoferi, commonly known as the Hofer's cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly hairs that help protect it from intense sunlight and water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its stem is ribbed and typically ranges in color from green to reddish-brown.

Pediocactus hoferi is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
