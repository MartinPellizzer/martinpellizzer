Pleurothallis declivis

Pleurothallis declivis, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
