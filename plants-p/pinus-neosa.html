Pinus neosa

Pinus neosa, commonly known as the New Zealand white pine, is a coniferous tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in pairs. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The wood of Pinus neosa is valued for its strength and durability.
