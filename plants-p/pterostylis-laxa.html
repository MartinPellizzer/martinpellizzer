Pterostylis laxa

Pterostylis laxa, commonly known as the slender spider-orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows on tree trunks or branches in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with a single, narrow leaf and a small, greenish-yellow flower. Its floral structure is characteristic of the orchid family, with a labellum that resembles a spider's leg, aiding in pollination.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in rainforest habitats.
