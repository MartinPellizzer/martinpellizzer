Pulsatilla subslavica

Pulsatilla subslavica, commonly known as the Slavonic pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces follicles that contain numerous seeds.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in mountainous regions.
