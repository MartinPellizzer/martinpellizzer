Patrinia rupestris f. intermedia

Patrinia rupestris f. intermedia, commonly known as the rock patrinia, is a variant of the rock betony, native to China.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often grow along rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
