Pilosella erythrodonta

Pilosella erythrodonta, commonly known as the red-toothed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with reddish-brown markings on the petals, contributing to its common name.

Pilosella erythrodonta is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
