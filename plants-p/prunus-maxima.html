Prunus maxima

Prunus maxima, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces edible, sweet fruits similar to cherries, which ripen in late summer. Prunus maxima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
