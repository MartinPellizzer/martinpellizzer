Pinus koraiensis subsp. prokoraiensis

Pinus koraiensis subsp.

prokoraiensis, commonly known as the Korean fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, containing seeds enclosed in winged structures. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
