Parosela procumbens

Parosela procumbens, commonly known as the creeping parosela, is a small flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

Parosela procumbens is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is relatively uncommon in the wild.
