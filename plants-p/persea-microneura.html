Persea microneura

Persea microneura, commonly known as the dwarf avocado or Mexican avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, smooth skin that turns from green to yellow when ripe. Its fruit contains a single large seed surrounded by a creamy, nutrient-rich pulp.

Persea microneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is often used in local cuisines.
