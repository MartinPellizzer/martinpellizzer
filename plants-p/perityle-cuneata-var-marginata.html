Perityle cuneata var. marginata

Perityle cuneata var. marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf perityle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive margin that is often white or pale in color.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
