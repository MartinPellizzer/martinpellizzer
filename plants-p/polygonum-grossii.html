Polygonum grossii

Polygonum grossii, commonly known as the Indian cress or wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and Nepal.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive bead-like seeds and often succulent stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters.

Polygonum grossii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
