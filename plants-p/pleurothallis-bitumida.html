Pleurothallis bitumida

Pleurothallis bitumida, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
