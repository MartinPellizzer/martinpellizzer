Pygeum gazelle-peninsulum

Pygeum gazelle-peninsulum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to the dry regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties, with extracts used in traditional remedies for various health conditions.
