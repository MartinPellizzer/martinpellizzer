Penstemon digitalis var. albidus

Penstemon digitalis var. albidus, commonly known as the white beardplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species for pollinators in its native range.
