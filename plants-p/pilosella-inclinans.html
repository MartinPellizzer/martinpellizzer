Pilosella inclinans

Pilosella inclinans, commonly known as the bentleaf hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and often curved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella inclinans produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
