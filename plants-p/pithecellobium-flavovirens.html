Pithecellobium flavovirens

Pithecellobium flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green velvet tamarind, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, yellow-green flowers. The plant bears fruit that resembles a legume pod, containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
