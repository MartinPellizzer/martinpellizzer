Pasania companoana

Pasania companoana, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and unique scent. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
