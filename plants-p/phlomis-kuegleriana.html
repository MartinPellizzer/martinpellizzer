Phlomis kuegleriana

Phlomis kuegleriana, commonly known as Kuegler's beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a strong aromatic scent.

Phlomis kuegleriana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
