Pseudomaxillaria exigua

Pseudomaxillaria exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, hairy growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pseudomaxillaria exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
