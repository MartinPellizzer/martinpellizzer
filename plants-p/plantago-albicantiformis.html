Plantago albicantiformis

Plantago albicantiformis, commonly known as the white-haired plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in temperate regions.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
