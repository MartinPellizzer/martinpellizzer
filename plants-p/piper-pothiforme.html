Piper pothiforme

Piper pothiforme, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper pothiforme make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
