Persea bilocularis

Persea bilocularis, commonly known as the two-seeded avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a rough, green skin that contains a single or two large, oily seeds. The fruit is valued for its nutrient-rich flesh, which is high in healthy fats and vitamins.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique seed structure and its role in both ecological and agricultural systems.
