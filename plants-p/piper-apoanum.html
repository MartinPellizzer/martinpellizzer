Piper apoanum

Piper apoanum, commonly known as the West Indian pepper or piper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, containing three seeds.

Piper apoanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
