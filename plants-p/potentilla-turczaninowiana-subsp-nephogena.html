Potentilla turczaninowiana subsp. nephogena

Potentilla turczaninowiana subsp.

nephogena, commonly known as the Mongolian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets that are hairy on the underside. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and adaptability.
