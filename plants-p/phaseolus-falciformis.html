Phaseolus falciformis

Phaseolus falciformis, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, curved pods that contain small, yellowish seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Due to its unique morphology and nutritional value, it holds cultural and agricultural significance in certain regions.
