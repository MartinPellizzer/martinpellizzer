Phaseolus multiflorus var. bicolor

Phaseolus multiflorus var. bicolor, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and white flowers and long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being heart-shaped and slightly hairy.

The variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
