Pulicaria arabica

Pulicaria arabica, commonly known as the Arabian groundsel, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in its dry habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Pulicaria arabica is often found in rocky or sandy landscapes, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
