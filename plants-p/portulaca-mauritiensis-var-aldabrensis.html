Portulaca mauritiensis var. aldabrensis

Portulaca mauritiensis var. aldabrensis, commonly known as Aldabra prickly pear, is a succulent plant native to the Aldabra Atoll in the Seychelles.

It is characterized by its spiny, paddle-shaped leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a resilient species in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of the local flora.
