Patagonium hispidulum

Patagonium hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy Patagonian grass, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves. Patagonium hispidulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
