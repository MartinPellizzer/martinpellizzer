Pentaphyllon lupinaster

Pentaphyllon lupinaster, commonly known as the lupine aster, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of many small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces showy, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
