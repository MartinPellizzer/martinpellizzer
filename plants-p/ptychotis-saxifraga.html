Ptychotis saxifraga

Ptychotis saxifraga, commonly known as the rock saxifrage, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pink flowers in the summer. This species is typically found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil. Ptychotis saxifraga is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical features make it a notable example of adaptation to harsh, mountainous habitats.
