Phyllostegia macrophylla

Phyllostegia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Phyllostegia macrophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
