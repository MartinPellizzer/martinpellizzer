Parodia mammulosa subsp. eugeniae

Parodia mammulosa subsp.

eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia Parodia, is a cacti species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It has a compact growth habit and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more rounded tubercles compared to other Parodia species.
