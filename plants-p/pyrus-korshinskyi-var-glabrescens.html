Pyrus korshinskyi var. glabrescens

Pyrus korshinskyi var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Korshinsky pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features alternate, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, sweet to subacid pomes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and potential as a fruit-bearing plant in suitable climates.
