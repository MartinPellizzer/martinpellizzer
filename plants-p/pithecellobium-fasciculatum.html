Pithecellobium fasciculatum

Pithecellobium fasciculatum, commonly known as the clustered pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal.

The plant is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
