Pulsatilla violacea

Pulsatilla violacea, commonly known as the meadow buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive crown of petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodlands, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately divided, and it produces achenes (small, dry fruits) that are dispersed by wind.

Pulsatilla violacea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
