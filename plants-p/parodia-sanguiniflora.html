Parodia sanguiniflora

Parodia sanguiniflora, commonly known as the blood-flowered cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, spherical cactus with a dense covering of spines. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and striking color make it a popular choice for succulent collectors.
