Panax major

Panax major, commonly known as American ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of North America.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and hairy roots. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is known for its complex chemical composition, including ginsenosides, which are believed to contribute to its medicinal properties.
