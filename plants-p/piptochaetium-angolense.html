Piptochaetium angolense

Piptochaetium angolense, commonly known as the Angola grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Piptochaetium angolense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
