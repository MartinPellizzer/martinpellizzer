Phyllodoce nipponica subsp. nipponica

Phyllodoce nipponica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese mountain heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Japan. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature in cold climates.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.
