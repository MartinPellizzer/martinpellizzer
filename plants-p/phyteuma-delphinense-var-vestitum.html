Phyteuma delphinense var. vestitum

Phyteuma delphinense var. vestitum, commonly known as the blue harebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, short hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
