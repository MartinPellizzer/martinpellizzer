Prosaptia reineckei

Prosaptia reineckei, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
