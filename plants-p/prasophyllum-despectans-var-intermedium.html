Prasophyllum despectans var. intermedium

Prasophyllum despectans var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, erect stem bearing a single flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. It is a short-lived perennial, with its flowering period occurring during the cooler months.

The species is noted for its pale green to brownish flower, which is often adorned with faint markings.
