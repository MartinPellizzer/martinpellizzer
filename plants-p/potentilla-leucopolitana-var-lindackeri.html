Potentilla leucopolitana var. lindackeri

Potentilla leucopolitana var. lindackeri, commonly known as the Lindacker's white potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered with a dense layer of fine pubescence.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
