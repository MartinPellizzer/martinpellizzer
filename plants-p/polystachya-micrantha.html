Polystachya micrantha

Polystachya micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered pinhao, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically pale green to white in color and are borne on long, slender pedicels. The plant prefers humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
