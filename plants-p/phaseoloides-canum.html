Phaseoloides canum

Phaseoloides canum, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Phaseoloides canum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.
