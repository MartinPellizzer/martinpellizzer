Potentilla clevelandii var. brevibracteata

Potentilla clevelandii var. brevibracteata, commonly known as the Cleveland's cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter bracts compared to other Potentilla species, giving it a unique appearance.
