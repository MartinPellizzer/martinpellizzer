Perdicium brasiliense

Perdicium brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian partridge pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by animals or wind.

Perdicium brasiliense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
