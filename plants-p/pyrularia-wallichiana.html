Pyrularia wallichiana

Pyrularia wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps protect it from harsh weather conditions.

Pyrularia wallichiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
