Physalis Viscosa Var. Fuscomaculata

Physalis viscosa var. fuscomaculata is a unique variant of the common groundcherry plant known for its distinctive appearance and ecological significance. This plant is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and fruit that encase a cluster of bright orange or red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along the edges of forests. The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local pollinators. Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically important plant in certain regions.
