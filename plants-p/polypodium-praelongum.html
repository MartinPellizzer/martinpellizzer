Polypodium praelongum

Polypodium praelongum, commonly known as the long-leaf polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, elongated shape.
