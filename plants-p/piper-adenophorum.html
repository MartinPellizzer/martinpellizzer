Piper adenophorum

Piper adenophorum, commonly known as the Indian pepper plant, is a climbing shrub belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tiny, three-lobed berries. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well-adapted to tropical climates.
