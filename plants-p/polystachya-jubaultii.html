Polystachya jubaultii

Polystachya jubaultii, commonly known as the Jubault's bamboo orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and erect, branching inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Polystachya jubaultii is also valued for its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
