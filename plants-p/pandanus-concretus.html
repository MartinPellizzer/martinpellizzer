Pandanus concretus

Pandanus concretus, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, which is both ornamental and functional. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pandanus concretus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and is also used in traditional crafts and ceremonies in its native regions.
