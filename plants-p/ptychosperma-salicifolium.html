Ptychosperma salicifolium

Ptychosperma salicifolium, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
