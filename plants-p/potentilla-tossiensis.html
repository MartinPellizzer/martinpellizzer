Potentilla tossiensis

Potentilla tossiensis, commonly known as the Tossi cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, which give it its common name.

Potentilla tossiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
