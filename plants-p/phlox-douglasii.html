Phlox douglasii

Phlox douglasii, commonly known as Douglas phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and woodlands. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Phlox douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
