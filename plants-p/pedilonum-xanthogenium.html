Pedilonum xanthogenium

Pedilonum xanthogenium, commonly known as the golden foot plant, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and its distinctive, foot-like appearance of its leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its botanical features include hairy stems and elongated, lobed leaves that contribute to its ornamental value.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
