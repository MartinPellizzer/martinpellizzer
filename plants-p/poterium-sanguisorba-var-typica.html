Poterium sanguisorba var. typica

Poterium sanguisorba var. typica, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a distinctive red-orange sap that oozes from its rhizomes, giving it its common name. The plant has a single, smooth, reddish stem that supports a cluster of palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a dense umbel at the top of the stem.

The plant is often found in moist woodlands and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
