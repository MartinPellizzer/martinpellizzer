Piper pedunculare

Piper pedunculare, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
