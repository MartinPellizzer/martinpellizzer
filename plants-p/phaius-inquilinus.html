Phaius inquilinus

Phaius inquilinus, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and swamps. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture efficiently.

Phaius inquilinus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with proper humidity and light conditions.
