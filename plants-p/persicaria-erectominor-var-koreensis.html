Persicaria erectominor var. koreensis

Persicaria erectominor var. koreensis, commonly known as the Korean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common but sometimes invasive species in its native range.
