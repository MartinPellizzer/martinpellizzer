Pistacia lentiscus subsp. emarginata

Pistacia lentiscus subsp.

emarginata, commonly known as the Greek mastic tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces a sticky resin called mastic, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and as a chewing gum. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
