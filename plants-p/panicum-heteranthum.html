Panicum heteranthum

Panicum heteranthum, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescence. The plant produces small, two-leaved spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Panicum heteranthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
