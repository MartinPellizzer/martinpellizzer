Pseudolobivia calorubra

Pseudolobivia calorubra, commonly known as the red-tinted lobivia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant red or pinkish hues on its stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
