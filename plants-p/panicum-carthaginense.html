Panicum carthaginense

Panicum carthaginense, commonly known as Carthaginian grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
