Pelargonium × variifolium

Pelargonium × variifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a wide range of foliage shapes and colors.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped or lobed leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its ornamental value.

Pelargonium × variifolium is native to South Africa and is adapted to warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
