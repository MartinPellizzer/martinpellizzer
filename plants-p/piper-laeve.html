Piper laeve

Piper laeve, commonly known as the straightleaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, thin fruits. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Piper laeve is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has mild medicinal uses in traditional practices.
