Piper seducentifolium

Piper seducentifolium, commonly known as the seductive-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
