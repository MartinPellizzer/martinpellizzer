Pyrus mespilocarpa

Pyrus mespilocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits in late summer. The fruit is typically pear-shaped and has a crisp, juicy texture with a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
