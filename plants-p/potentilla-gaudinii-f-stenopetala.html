Potentilla gaudinii f. stenopetala

Potentilla gaudinii f. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-petaled potentilla, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. The flowers are usually yellow, appearing in clusters and blooming during the summer months. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens, particularly in rockeries and alpine settings.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant landscapes.
