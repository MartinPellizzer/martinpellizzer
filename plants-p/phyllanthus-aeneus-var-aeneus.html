Phyllanthus aeneus var. aeneus

Phyllanthus aeneus var. aeneus, commonly known as the copperleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red or orange fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
