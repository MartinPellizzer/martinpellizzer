Pentameris pictigluma var. mannii

Pentameris pictigluma var. mannii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, creating a visually dramatic display. The plant belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates with well-draining soil. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet.

Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves that are often dark green with a glossy texture.
