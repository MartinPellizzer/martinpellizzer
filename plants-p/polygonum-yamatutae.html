Polygonum yamatutae

Polygonum yamatutae, commonly known as the Yamatotai knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are characterized by their broad, heart-shaped bases and smooth, often reddish undersides.

Polygonum yamatutae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
