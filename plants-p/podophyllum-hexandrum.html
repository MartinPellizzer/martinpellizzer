Podophyllum hexandrum

Podophyllum hexandrum, commonly known as the Himalayan mayapple, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It features a distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaf that is often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer and is edible for humans and animals.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
