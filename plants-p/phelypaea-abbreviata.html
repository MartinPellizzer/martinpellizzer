Phelypaea abbreviata

Phelypaea abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. The plant has delicate, floating leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a clover-like appearance. Its roots are often submerged, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Phelypaea abbreviata is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to help maintain water clarity.
