Pauletia burchellii

Pauletia burchellii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, typical of its legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Pauletia burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
