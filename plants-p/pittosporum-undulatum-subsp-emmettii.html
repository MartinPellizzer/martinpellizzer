Pittosporum undulatum subsp. emmettii

Pittosporum undulatum subsp.

emmettii, commonly known as the Emmett's pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly wavy margin.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, followed by bright orange berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
