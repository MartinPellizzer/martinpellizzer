Potentilla kelloggii

Potentilla kelloggii, commonly known as Kellogg's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Potentilla kelloggii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
