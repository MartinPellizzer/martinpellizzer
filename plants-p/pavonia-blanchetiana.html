Pavonia blanchetiana

Pavonia blanchetiana, commonly known as the Brazilian velvetbean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
