Prunus salicina var. cordata

Prunus salicina var. cordata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, dark red fruits known as cherries, which are edible and used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

The variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
