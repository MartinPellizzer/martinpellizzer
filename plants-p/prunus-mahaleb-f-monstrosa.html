Prunus mahaleb f. monstrosa

Prunus mahaleb f. monstrosa, commonly known as the twisted cherry or mock cherry, is a decorative cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its gnarled, twisted branches that create an unusual and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, followed by red or black berries in autumn. It is typically grown as a specimen tree or hedge due to its unique form.

This variety thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
