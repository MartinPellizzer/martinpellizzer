Platanthera mandarinorum var. ophryodes

Platanthera mandarinorum var. ophryodes, commonly known as the Mandarin Orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pale yellow to greenish-white blossoms with a distinctive, sweet scent. It thrives in moist, shaded forest environments and requires specific humidity and light conditions for successful growth.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in several regions.
