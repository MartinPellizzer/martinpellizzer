Potentilla laresciae

Potentilla laresciae, commonly known as the Andean cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern and are covered with a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

Potentilla laresciae is well adapted to harsh mountain environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions.
