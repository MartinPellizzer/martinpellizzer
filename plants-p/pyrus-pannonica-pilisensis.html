Pyrus pannonica pilisensis

Pyrus pannonica pilisensis, commonly known as the Pannonian pear, is a rare and endemic species native to Hungary.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its elliptical leaves and white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is typically small, yellowish, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in river valleys and forested areas.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
