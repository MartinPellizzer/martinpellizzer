Pictetia angustifolia

Pictetia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pictetia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean climates.

Pictetia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
