Pachira arenaria

Pachira arenaria, commonly known as the areca palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves that form a crown at the top. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into three-winged fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pachira arenaria is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
