Phlox violacea

Phlox violacea, commonly known as blue phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in early to mid-summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.

Phlox violacea is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
