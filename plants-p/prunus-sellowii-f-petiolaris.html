Prunus sellowii f. petiolaris

Prunus sellowii f. petiolaris, commonly known as the Sellow's cherry or petioled cherry, is a botanical variant of the Prunus sellowii species.

It is characterized by its distinctive elongated petioles, which are longer than those of the typical form. This species belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Brazil. Its foliage consists of oval-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart berries that are often used in local cuisines.
