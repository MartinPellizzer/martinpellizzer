Peperomia conulifera var. stehleae

Peperomia conulifera var. stehleae, commonly known as the Stehle's pepper plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy and have a unique, waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and ability to thrive in moderate humidity.

Its ornamental value lies in its attractive foliage rather than its flowers.
