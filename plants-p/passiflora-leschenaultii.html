Passiflora leschenaultii

Passiflora leschenaultii, commonly known as the Leschenault's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins.

Passiflora leschenaultii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
