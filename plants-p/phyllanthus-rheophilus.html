Phyllanthus rheophilus

Phyllanthus rheophilus, commonly known as the water phyllanthus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands, showcasing its rheophilous habitat preference. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Phyllanthus rheophilus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its wetland habitats.
