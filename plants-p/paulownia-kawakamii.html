Paulownia kawakamii

Paulownia kawakamii, commonly known as the Japanese paulownia, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Paulowniaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are typically pink or purple in color. Its wood is highly valued for its lightweight and strength, making it popular in woodworking.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions due to its aggressive growth habit.
