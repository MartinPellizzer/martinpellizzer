Poa nemoralis var. kumgansanii

Poa nemoralis var. kumgansanii, commonly known as the Korean wood sedge, is a variant of the common wood sedge native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
