Pedicularis elegans

Pedicularis elegans, commonly known as the lady's slippers, is a striking member of the Orobanchaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, slipper-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
