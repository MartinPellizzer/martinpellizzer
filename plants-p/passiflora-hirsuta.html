Passiflora hirsuta

Passiflora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, waxy leaves and striking, colorful flowers that resemble a crown, often with blue and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.

Passiflora hirsuta also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including bees and birds, due to its nectar-rich blooms.
