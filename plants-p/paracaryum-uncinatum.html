Paracaryum uncinatum

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that produce aerial roots. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with small, hooked spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

Paracaryum uncinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded gardens and as a houseplant.
