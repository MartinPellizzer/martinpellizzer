Physalis cardiophylla

Physalis cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf physalis, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-like fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Physalis cardiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit structure.
