Pinus montezumae subsp. lindleyi

Pinus montezumae subsp.

lindleyi, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a subspecies native to high-altitude regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically shorter and more rigid compared to other pine species.

The cones of this plant are small and often have a distinctive bristle-like structure at the base. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its longevity and ecological significance in mountainous ecosystems.
