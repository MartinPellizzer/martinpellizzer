Penstemon paniculatus

Penstemon paniculatus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to six feet. The plant produces showy, spike-like clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon paniculatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
