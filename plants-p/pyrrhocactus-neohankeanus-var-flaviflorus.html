Pyrrhocactus neohankeanus var. flaviflorus

Pyrrhocactus neohankeanus var. flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered pyrrhocactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Mexico.

It features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the growing season, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are typically small and spiny, providing some protection against herbivores.

The species is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
