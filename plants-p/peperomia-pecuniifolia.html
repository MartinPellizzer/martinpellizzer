Peperomia pecuniifolia

Peperomia pecuniifolia, commonly known as the money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features oval, waxy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a silvery or white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red or pink, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia pecuniifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
