Piper tristigmum

Piper tristigmum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is cultivated for its dark, aromatic berries, which are used as a spice and in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive, pungent aroma.

The fruit develops from a single ovary and is typically harvested when fully mature.
