Psoralea lateralis

Psoralea lateralis, commonly known as the narrowleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, pinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Psoralea lateralis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
