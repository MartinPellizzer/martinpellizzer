Pterostylis xerampelina

Pterostylis xerampelina, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower spike that typically blooms during the winter months. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, often hooded shape. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and aesthetic features.
