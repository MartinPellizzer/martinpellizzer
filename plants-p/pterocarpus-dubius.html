Pterocarpus dubius

Pterocarpus dubius, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, pale bark that peels in thin strips. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of elliptical leaflets.

The wood of Pterocarpus dubius is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, making it a sought-after material in carpentry and furniture making.
