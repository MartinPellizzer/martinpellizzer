Paronychia argentea var. suffruticosa

Paronychia argentea var. suffruticosa, commonly known as silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial plant native to arid and rocky environments.

It features silvery-white, woolly foliage that helps reflect sunlight and reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, compact inflorescences. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or desert regions.
