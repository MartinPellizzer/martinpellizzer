Pachygenium xerophyticum

Pachygenium xerophyticum, commonly known as the desert thickstem, is a xerophytic plant adapted to arid environments.

It possesses thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in regions with limited rainfall. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, minimizing water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often adapted for pollination by wind or specialized insects.

It is found in rocky soils and dry landscapes across several desert regions.
