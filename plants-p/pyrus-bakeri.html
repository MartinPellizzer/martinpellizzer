Pyrus bakeri

Pyrus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit, a pear-like berry, is typically small and yellowish, maturing in late summer.

Pyrus bakeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
