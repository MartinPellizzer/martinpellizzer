Potamogeton interruptus

Potamogeton interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often interrupted by a narrow, leafless section near the base. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. It produces small, greenish flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and its rhizomes help it spread vegetatively.

Potamogeton interruptus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
