Psittacanthus longibracteatus

Psittacanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract siphonostele, is a species of cycad native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stem and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Psittacanthus longibracteatus is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
