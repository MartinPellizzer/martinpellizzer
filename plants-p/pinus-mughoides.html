Pinus mughoides

Pinus mughoides, commonly known as the Kashmir pine, is a coniferous tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and needle-like leaves arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain winged seeds. It thrives in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.

The Kashmir pine is valued for its timber and is also an important species in mountain ecosystems.
