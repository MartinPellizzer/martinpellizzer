Polygonum fallacinum

Polygonum fallacinum, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly wavy margin.

Polygonum fallacinum is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
