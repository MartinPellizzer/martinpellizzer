Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. supra-axillaris

Phyllanthus ferdinandi var. supra-axillaris, commonly known as the dwarf Indian madder, is a shrub native to southern India.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces tiny, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, spherical capsules containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
