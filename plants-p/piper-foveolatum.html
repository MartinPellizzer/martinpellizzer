Piper foveolatum

Piper foveolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, narrow leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
