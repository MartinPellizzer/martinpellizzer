Potentilla sibirica var. conferta

Potentilla sibirica var. conferta, commonly known as the denseflower cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has wiry, upright stems covered with fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in rock gardens and as a ground cover.
