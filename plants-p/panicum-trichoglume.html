Panicum trichoglume

Panicum trichoglume, commonly known as little barley, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a rough texture, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum trichoglume is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
