Poa trivialis var. holsatia

Poa trivialis var. holsatia, commonly known as the Holsatian meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense mats in meadows and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in grassy habitats.
