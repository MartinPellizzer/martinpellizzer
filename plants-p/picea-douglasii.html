Picea douglasii

Picea douglasii, commonly known as Douglas fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. Douglas fir is valued for its strong, durable wood and is often used in construction and furniture-making.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
