Primula macrophylla var. moorcroftiana

Primula macrophylla var. moorcroftiana, commonly known as the Moorcroft's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a fine pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
