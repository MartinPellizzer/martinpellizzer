Piptatherum hilariae subsp. badachschanicum

Piptatherum hilariae subsp.

badachschanicum, commonly known as the Badakhshan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting a low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in its native habitat.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions.
