Polypodium chrysoconion

Polypodium chrysoconion, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bright green with a slight golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or tree trunks in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and release spores for propagation.

Polypodium chrysoconion is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
