Polygonum sagittatum f. aestivum

Polygonum sagittatum f. aestivum, commonly known as the summer arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The species is known for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
