Pelargonium × gruinale

Pelargonium × gruinale, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its showy, colorful flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × inodorum, combining traits from both parents. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy, adding to its visual appeal.

Pelargonium × gruinale thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens and containers for its vibrant display.
