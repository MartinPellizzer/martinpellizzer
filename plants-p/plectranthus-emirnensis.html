Plectranthus emirnensis

Plectranthus emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis mint, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
