Polyachyrus poeppigii subsp. poeppigii

Polyachyrus poeppigii subsp.

poeppigii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features showy, trumpet-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
