Polypodium papillatum

Polypodium papillatum, commonly known as the hairy polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is wiry and covered with small, hairy projections, giving it its common name.

The fronds are usually evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinctive, serrated margin.
