Piptomeris stackhousei

Piptomeris stackhousei, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
