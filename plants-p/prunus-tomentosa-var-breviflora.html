Prunus tomentosa var. breviflora

Prunus tomentosa var. breviflora, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, grayish-white pubescent bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
