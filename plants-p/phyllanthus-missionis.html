Phyllanthus missionis

Phyllanthus missionis, commonly known as the Mission Hill goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.

Phyllanthus missionis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
