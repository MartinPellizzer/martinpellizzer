Papuodendron

Papuodendron, commonly known as the New Guinea broadleaf, is a member of the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of New Guinea and is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a fragrant aroma, and it produces edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
