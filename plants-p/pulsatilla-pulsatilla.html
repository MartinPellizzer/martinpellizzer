Pulsatilla pulsatilla

Pulsatilla pulsatilla, commonly known as the pasque flower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rootstock and hairy, lobed leaves that emerge after the flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Pulsatilla pulsatilla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
