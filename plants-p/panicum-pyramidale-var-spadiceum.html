Panicum pyramidale var. spadiceum

Panicum pyramidale var. spadiceum, commonly known as the purple panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to pinkish flower spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in ecological restoration projects.
