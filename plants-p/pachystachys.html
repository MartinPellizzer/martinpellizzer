Pachystachys

Pachystachys, commonly known as the spike grass or pachystachys lutea, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like clusters of small, often yellow or golden flowers. They are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Pachystachys species are often used in ornamental horticulture for their striking floral displays.

Their growth habit and botanical features make them popular in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
