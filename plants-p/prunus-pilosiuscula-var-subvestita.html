Prunus pilosiuscula var. subvestita

Prunus pilosiuscula var. subvestita, commonly known as the silky cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red to black berries. Native to southern China, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
