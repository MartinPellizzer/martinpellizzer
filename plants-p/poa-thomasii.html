Poa thomasii

Poa thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing stems.

Poa thomasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
