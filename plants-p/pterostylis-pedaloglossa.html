Pterostylis pedaloglossa

Pterostylis pedaloglossa, commonly known as the boot-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as under dense forest canopies. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that resembles a boot, giving it its common name.

The orchid relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological interdependence.
