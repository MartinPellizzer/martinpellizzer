Paphiopedilum johorense

Paphiopedilum johorense, commonly known as the Johore Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a lip that is often brightly colored and modified for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it forms dense clusters. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
