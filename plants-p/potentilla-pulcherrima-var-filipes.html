Potentilla pulcherrima var. filipes

Potentilla pulcherrima var. filipes, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its narrow, lance-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
