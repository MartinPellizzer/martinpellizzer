Paracaleana brockmanii

Paracaleana brockmanii, commonly known as the Western Australian orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of bright pink or purple petals with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
