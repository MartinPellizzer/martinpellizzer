Potentilla spoliata

Potentilla spoliata, commonly known as the silver potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-white foliage that gives it a striking appearance, especially in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, visually appealing plants.
