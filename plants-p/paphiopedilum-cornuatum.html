Paphiopedilum cornuatum

Paphiopedilum cornuatum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, leathery leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive, horn-like spur. The flower is typically pink or purple with a yellow lip, and it blooms during the rainy season. It prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is cultivated for ornamental purposes.
