Pomaria rubicunda var. rubicunda

Pomaria rubicunda var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered pomaria, is a striking species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
