Potamogeton lacunatus

Potamogeton lacunatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, and it is often found in shallow, still or slow-moving waters. Potamogeton lacunatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
