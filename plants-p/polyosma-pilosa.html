Polyosma pilosa

Polyosma pilosa, commonly known as the hairy polyosma, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Polyosma pilosa is often found in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
