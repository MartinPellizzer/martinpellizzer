Plerandra jatrophifolia

Plerandra jatrophifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by edible tubers that are similar in texture to yams. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible tubers, it is sometimes cultivated for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
