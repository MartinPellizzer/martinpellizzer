Phaeosphaerion persicariifolium

Phaeosphaerion persicariifolium, commonly known as the Persian rose or Persian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the stems are sturdy and often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
