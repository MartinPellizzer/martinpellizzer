Potamogeton alpinus f. viridis

Potamogeton alpinus f. viridis, commonly known as the green alpine pondweed, is a variant of the alpine pondweed species native to cold, freshwater environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

This aquatic plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are usually green in color. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms. The plant is often found in mountain lakes and streams, where it thrives in nutrient-poor conditions.

Its presence can indicate good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
