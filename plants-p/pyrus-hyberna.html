Pyrus hyberna

Pyrus hyberna, commonly known as the Irish pear, is a deciduous tree native to western Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grey bark. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in cymes. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional horticulture.
