Phagnalon rupestre var. illyricum

Phagnalon rupestre var. illyricum, commonly known as the Illyrian phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in dry, sunny habitats such as slopes and cliffs. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads.

This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
