Punica granatum var. flavescens

Punica granatum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pomegranate, is a variant of the pomegranate tree native to regions of the Middle East and South Asia.

This cultivar is distinguished by its vibrant yellow fruit, which matures from green to a striking orange-yellow color. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and dark green, while its flowers are usually red or orange, adding ornamental value.

The fruit is valued not only for its unique color but also for its tart flavor and nutritional content.
