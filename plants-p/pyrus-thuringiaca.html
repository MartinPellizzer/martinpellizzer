Pyrus thuringiaca

Pyrus thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Europe and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with serrated leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or reddish in color, similar to other pear varieties. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for gardens and orchards.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both aesthetic and agricultural purposes.
