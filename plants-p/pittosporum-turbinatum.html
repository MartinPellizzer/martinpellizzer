Pittosporum turbinatum

Pittosporum turbinatum, commonly known as the Turban Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red or orange berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pittosporum turbinatum is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and partial shade.
