Pseuderanthemum leptostachyum

Pseuderanthemum leptostachyum, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive red calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
