Pilosella alpicola subsp. furcotae

Pilosella alpicola subsp.

furcotae, commonly known as the Alpine pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy, leaf-like bracts and its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments. It is often found in rocky soils and meadows, where it contributes to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable indicator species for alpine habitats.
