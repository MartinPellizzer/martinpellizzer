Pithecellobium salutare

Pithecellobium salutare, commonly known as the sacred acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow or white flowers. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
