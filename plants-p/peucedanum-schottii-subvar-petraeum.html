Peucedanum schottii subvar. petraeum

Peucedanum schottii subvar. petraeum, commonly known as the rock angelica, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
