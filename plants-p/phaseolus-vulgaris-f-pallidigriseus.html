Phaseolus vulgaris f. pallidigriseus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. pallidigriseus, commonly known as the pale gray bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean.

It is characterized by its distinctive pale gray to greenish pod color and smaller, rounded seeds compared to typical beans. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its edible seeds.
