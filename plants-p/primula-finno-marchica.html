Primula finno-marchica

Primula finno-marchica, commonly known as the Finnish primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in alpine and tundra environments. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula finno-marchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, humid conditions.
