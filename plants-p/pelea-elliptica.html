Pelea elliptica

Pelea elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical pelea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth, waxy surface and a bluish-green hue. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a border plant.
