Pitcairnia patentiflora var. subintegra

Pitcairnia patentiflora var. subintegra, commonly known as the Crimson Paddle Plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Brazilian Atlantic Forest.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant red or orange, providing a dramatic visual impact. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
