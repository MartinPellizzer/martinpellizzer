Peperomia celiae

Peperomia celiae, commonly known as the Celia's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or heart-shaped, with a glossy texture and a range of colors from deep green to reddish tones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pale pink, though they are not the primary ornamental feature. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.

Peperomia celiae is valued for its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.
