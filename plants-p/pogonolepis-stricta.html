Pogonolepis stricta

Pogonolepis stricta, commonly known as the rigid pogonolepis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of southern Africa, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
