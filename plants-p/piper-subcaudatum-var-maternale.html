Piper subcaudatum var. maternale

Piper subcaudatum var. maternale, commonly known as the Maternal Pepper, is a tropical vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its long, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by red, peppercorn-like fruits. It is notable for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
