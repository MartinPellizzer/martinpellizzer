Passiflora serratistipula

Passiflora serratistipula, commonly known as the serrated-stipule passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central reproductive cluster. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically lobed and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Passiflora serratistipula is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
