Piptadenia killipii var. killipii

Piptadenia killipii var. killipii, commonly known as the Amazonian ironwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The tree has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its durable timber and is used in traditional medicine.
