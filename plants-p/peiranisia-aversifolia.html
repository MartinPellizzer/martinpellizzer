Peiranisia aversifolia

Peiranisia aversifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf peiranisia, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Peiranisia aversifolia is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
