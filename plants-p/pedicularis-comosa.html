Pedicularis comosa

Pedicularis comosa, commonly known as the woolly monkeyflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically pink or red in color, often resembling those of the genus *Phlox*. It thrives in alpine and subalpine habitats, often growing in rocky or moist soil conditions.

Pedicularis comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and vibrant blooms.
