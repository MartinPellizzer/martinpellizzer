Pilocarpus pauciflorus subsp. clavatus

Pilocarpus pauciflorus subsp.

clavatus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Brazil. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, often clustered leaves.

The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
