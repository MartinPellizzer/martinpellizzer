Puya thomasiana

Puya thomasiana, commonly known as the Thomasiana torchflower, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in a spiral pattern along the central stem. Puya thomasiana is a perennial plant that thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
