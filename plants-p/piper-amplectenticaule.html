Piper amplectenticaule

Piper amplectenticaule, commonly known as the climbing pepper or velvet pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that give way to elongated, peppery fruits. Its growth habit involves climbing or trailing along supports, making it suitable for shaded or humid environments.

The botanical features of Piper amplectenticaule make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
