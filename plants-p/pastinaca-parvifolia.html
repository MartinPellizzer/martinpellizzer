Pastinaca parvifolia

Pastinaca parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spiky flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Pastinaca parvifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
