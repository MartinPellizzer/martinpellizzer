Prunus × subhirtella f. oblongifolia

Prunus × subhirtella f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblongleaf cherry, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for both landscape design and container gardening.
