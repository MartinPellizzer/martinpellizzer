Potentilla laeta var. pinnatifida

Potentilla laeta var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems arising from a central rootstock.

Its flowers bloom in early summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
