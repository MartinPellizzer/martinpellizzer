Paspalum quadrifarium var. majus

Paspalum quadrifarium var. majus, commonly known as the giant panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
