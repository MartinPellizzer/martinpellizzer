Perymenium buphthalmoides

Perymenium buphthalmoides, commonly known as the false globe thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped heads of white or pale yellow flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
