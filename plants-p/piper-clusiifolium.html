Piper clusiifolium

Piper clusiifolium, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and stimulant properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
