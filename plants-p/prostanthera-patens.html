Prostanthera patens

Prostanthera patens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mintbush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue. Prostanthera patens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its fragrant foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native gardens.
