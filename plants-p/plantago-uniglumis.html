Plantago uniglumis

Plantago uniglumis, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces a single, slender flowering stem that bears a dense spike of small, greenish flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
