Polygala propinqua

Polygala propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The plant has slender, branching stems and linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in low-water environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
