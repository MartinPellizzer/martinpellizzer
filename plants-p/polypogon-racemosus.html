Polypogon racemosus

Polypogon racemosus, commonly known as the racemose grass or slender racemose grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
