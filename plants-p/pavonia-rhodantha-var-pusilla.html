Pavonia rhodantha var. pusilla

Pavonia rhodantha var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf scarlet pimpernel, is a compact shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. The plant produces clusters of bright red, spurred flowers that are highly visible and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a colorful, low-maintenance plant.
