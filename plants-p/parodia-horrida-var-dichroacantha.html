Parodia horrida var. dichroacantha

Parodia horrida var. dichroacantha, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often hidden among the spines. It is a slow-growing, compact plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
