Pseudolaelia oliveirana

Pseudolaelia oliveirana, commonly known as the Oliveira's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features linear to lanceolate leaves and produces inflorescences with showy, spurred flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
