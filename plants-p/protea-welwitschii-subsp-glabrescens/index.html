Protea Welwitschii Subsp. Glabrescens

Protea welwitschii subsp. glabrescens is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to southern Africa. This sub-species is known for its unique, large, and often brightly colored inflorescences that resemble pom-poms. It thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, typically found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife. Its resilience and adaptability make it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
