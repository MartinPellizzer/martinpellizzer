Platytaenia tripartita

Platytaenia tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnately divided fronds that typically grow in a compact, upright form. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
