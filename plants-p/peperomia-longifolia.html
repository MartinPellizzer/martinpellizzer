Peperomia longifolia

Peperomia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features compact, upright growth with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that have a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by red berries, which are edible but not commonly consumed. Peperomia longifolia thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
