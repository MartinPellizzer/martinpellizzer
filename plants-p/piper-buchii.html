Piper buchii

Piper buchii, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the spicy compound piperine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a distinct, aromatic scent.

Piper buchii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
