Pachysandra axillaris

Pachysandra axillaris, commonly known as the creeping pachysandra, is a low-growing evergreen groundcover plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a compact, mat-like growth pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring season. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to suppress weeds.

Pachysandra axillaris is also valued for its durability and adaptability to various soil types.
