Populus × smithii

Populus × smithii, commonly known as the Smith's poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. The plant typically features heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin and produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring. Its bark is smooth and often exhibits a pale gray or greenish hue.

Populus × smithii is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
