Piper pallidirameum var. subcrassifolium

Piper pallidirameum var. subcrassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for researchers studying plant diversity.
