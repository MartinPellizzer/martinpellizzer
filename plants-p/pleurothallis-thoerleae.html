Pleurothallis thoerleae

Pleurothallis thoerleae, commonly known as the Thoerle's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and produces delicate, greenish-white or pale yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
