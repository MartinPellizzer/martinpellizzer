Panicum leandrii

Panicum leandrii, commonly known as the Leandrii grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, fluffy panicles that produce small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Panicum leandrii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
