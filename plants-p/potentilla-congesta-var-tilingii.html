Potentilla congesta var. tilingii

Potentilla congesta var. tilingii, commonly known as the Tilingii cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to nine oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
