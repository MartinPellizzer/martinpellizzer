Pilosella cymosa subsp. regelii

Pilosella cymosa subsp.

regelii, commonly known as Regel's hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
