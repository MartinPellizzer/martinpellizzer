Pentaphorus pyrifolius

Pentaphorus pyrifolius, commonly known as the fireleaf tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that resemble the shape of a fire's spread, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
