Prunus serrulata var. praecox

Prunus serrulata var. praecox, commonly known as the early cherry or Japanese early cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom early in the spring. The leaves are oval, serrated, and turn a vibrant red or orange in the fall. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for spring displays.
