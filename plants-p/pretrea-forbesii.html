Pretrea forbesii

Pretrea forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in the Carolinas and Georgia. The plant has small, opposite leaves and produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are dark blue and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Pretrea forbesii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.
