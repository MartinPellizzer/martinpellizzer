Pellaea fendleri

Pellaea fendleri, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its rhizomes are short and horizontal, allowing it to spread slowly through vegetative reproduction.

Pellaea fendleri is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
