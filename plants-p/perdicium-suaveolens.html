Perdicium suaveolens

Perdicium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented piper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and emit a mild, pleasant aroma.

Perdicium suaveolens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fragrant essential oils.
