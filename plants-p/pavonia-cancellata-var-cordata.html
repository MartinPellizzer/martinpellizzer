Pavonia cancellata var. cordata

Pavonia cancellata var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf penta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring attractive, palmately lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a versatile choice for landscaping in warm climates.
