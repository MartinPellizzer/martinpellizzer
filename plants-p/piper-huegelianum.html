Piper huegelianum

Piper huegelianum, commonly known as the Hugel's pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery scent when crushed.

Piper huegelianum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
