Paullinia elegans subsp. neglecta

Paullinia elegans subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant has simple, alternate, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a red, fleshy drupe.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and berries.
