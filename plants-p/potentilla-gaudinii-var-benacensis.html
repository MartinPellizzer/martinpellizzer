Potentilla gaudinii var. benacensis

Potentilla gaudinii var. benacensis, commonly known as the Balkan cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils.

It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.
