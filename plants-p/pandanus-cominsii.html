Pandanus cominsii

Pandanus cominsii, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, three-lobed fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and its leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid environments.
