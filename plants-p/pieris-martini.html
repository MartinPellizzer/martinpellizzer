Pieris martini

Pieris martini, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Pieris martini is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its foliage and seasonal blooms.
