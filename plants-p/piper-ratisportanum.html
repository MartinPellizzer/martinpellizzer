Piper ratisportanum

Piper ratisportanum, commonly known as the Ratisbona pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a pungent aroma when crushed.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
