Pouteria rhopalocarpa

Pouteria rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the red mangaba tree, is a tropical species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by large, fleshy fruits with a sweet, reddish pulp.

Its fruit is valued for its edible qualities and is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
