Piper yaguarundi

Piper yaguarundi, commonly known as the Yaguarundi pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses by indigenous communities.
