Phoradendron psittacanthobium

Phoradendron psittacanthobium, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of its host trees, primarily in the genus *Erythroxylum*.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually green, and its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal.

It is native to Central and South America and is known for its unique ecological role in supporting avian populations.
