Phrynium venustum

Phrynium venustum, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that resemble torches, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Phrynium venustum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
