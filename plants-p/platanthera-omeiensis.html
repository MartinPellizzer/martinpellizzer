Platanthera omeiensis

Platanthera omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei Orchid, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a slender, leafless stem that bears a single, showy flower. The plant has a unique morphology, with a dark purple to brownish flower that has a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments, typically in shaded forest understories.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
