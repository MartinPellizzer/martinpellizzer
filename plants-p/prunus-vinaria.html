Prunus vinaria

Prunus vinaria, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart and not commonly consumed. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a generally compact growth habit.
