Phyllanthus tabularis

Phyllanthus tabularis, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in diverse environmental conditions.
