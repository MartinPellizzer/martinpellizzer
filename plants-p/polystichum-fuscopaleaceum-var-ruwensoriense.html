Polystichum fuscopaleaceum var. ruwensoriense

Polystichum fuscopaleaceum var. ruwensoriense, commonly known as the Ruwensoriense Shield Fern, is a rare and distinctive fern species native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, and deeply lobed fronds that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in forest understories where it receives dappled sunlight. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in rows along the margins of the fronds.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and ecological significance.
