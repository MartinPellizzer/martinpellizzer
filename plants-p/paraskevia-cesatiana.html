Paraskevia cesatiana

Paraskevia cesatiana, commonly known as the Mediterranean sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous annual with delicate, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal areas or along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Paraskevia cesatiana is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
