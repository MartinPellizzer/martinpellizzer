Prunus × yedoensis f. taizanfukun

Prunus × yedoensis f. taizanfukun, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree with a unique growth form, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and is often cultivated as a specimen tree. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped leaves. It is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appearance in gardens and landscapes.

This form is particularly noted for its slow growth and ability to develop a dense, bushy structure over time.
