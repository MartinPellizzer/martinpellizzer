Podocarpus gracillimus

Podocarpus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender podocarp or Chinese podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in spirals along the stems. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds, a feature typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a landscape plant in various regions.
