Phyllanthus papuanus

Phyllanthus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan gooseberry, is a small shrub native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits resembling berries. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
