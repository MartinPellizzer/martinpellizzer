Pinalia albidotomentosa

Pinalia albidotomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose pinalia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale in color, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.
