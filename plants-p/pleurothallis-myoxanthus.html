Pleurothallis myoxanthus

Pleurothallis myoxanthus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a striking yellow or orange hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to maximize light absorption in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis myoxanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
