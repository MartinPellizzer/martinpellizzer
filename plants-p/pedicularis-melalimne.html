Pedicularis melalimne

Pedicularis melalimne, commonly known as the purple flame gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Pedicularis melalimne is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
