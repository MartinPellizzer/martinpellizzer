Pueraria lobata var. chinensis

Pueraria lobata var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale purple flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and extensive root system, which can spread aggressively. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its invasive nature, it is sometimes considered a problematic weed in agricultural and natural settings.
