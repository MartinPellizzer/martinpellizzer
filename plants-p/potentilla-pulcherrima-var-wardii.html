Potentilla pulcherrima var. wardii

Potentilla pulcherrima var. wardii, commonly known as the Ward's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
