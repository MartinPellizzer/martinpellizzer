Polemonium viscosum subsp. elegans

Polemonium viscosum subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America. It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically hairy and have a glandular texture.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
