Pandanus parhamii

Pandanus parhamii, commonly known as the Parham's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific islands, particularly parts of French Polynesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of flowers, which are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Pandanus parhamii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
