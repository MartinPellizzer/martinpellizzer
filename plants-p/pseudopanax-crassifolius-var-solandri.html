Pseudopanax crassifolius var. solandri

Pseudopanax crassifolius var. solandri, commonly known as the Southern rata, is a unique evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including forest understories and rocky areas.

Its ecological importance is significant, as it provides food and shelter for native wildlife.
