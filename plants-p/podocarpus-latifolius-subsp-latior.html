Podocarpus latifolius subsp. latior

Podocarpus latifolius subsp.

latior, commonly known as the broadleaf podocarp, is a evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, orange-red aril. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
