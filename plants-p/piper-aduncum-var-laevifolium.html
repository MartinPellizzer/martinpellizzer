Piper aduncum var. laevifolium

Piper aduncum var. laevifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforest understories.

The leaves and fruits are valued for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
