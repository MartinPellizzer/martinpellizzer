Paepalanthus divaricatus

Paepalanthus divaricatus, commonly known as the variable cholla, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that branch out in a bushy pattern. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
