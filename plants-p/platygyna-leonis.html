Platygyna leonis

Platygyna leonis, commonly known as the lion's balm, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Platygyna leonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
