Primula modesta var. fauriei

Primula modesta var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces showy inflorescences that are often clustered at the top of its slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly serrated margin, and the plant thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
