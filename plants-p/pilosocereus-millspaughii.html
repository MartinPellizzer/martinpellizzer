Pilosocereus millspaughii

Pilosocereus millspaughii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has spiny areoles covered with white, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This drought-tolerant plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
