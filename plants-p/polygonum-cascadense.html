Polygonum cascadense

Polygonum cascadense, commonly known as the cascade knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, rocky habitats in mountainous regions.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Polygonum cascadense is valued for its ability to thrive in challenging environments and its aesthetic value in garden settings.
