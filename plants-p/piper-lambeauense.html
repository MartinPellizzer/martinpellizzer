Piper lambeauense

Piper lambeauense, commonly known as the Lambeau pepper or Brazilian pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a distinct serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates.
