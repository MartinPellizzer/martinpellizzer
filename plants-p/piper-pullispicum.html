Piper pullispicum

Piper pullispicum, commonly known as the purple pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit, which is a small, purple berry, is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
