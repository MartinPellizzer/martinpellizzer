Primula rosea subsp. harrissii

Primula rosea subsp.

harrissii, commonly known as the Harriss's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky habitats. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a pointed tip, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
