Paeonia tenuifolia var. plena

Paeonia tenuifolia var. plena, commonly known as the Japanese peony or the narrow-leaf peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of double, semi-double, or single flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its botanical features include showy, petal-rich flowers and a robust root system that helps it thrive in well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
