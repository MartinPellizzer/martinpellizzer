Poa sinattenuata var. breviligula

Poa sinattenuata var. breviligula, commonly known as the short-leaved bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, creeping stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are shorter than those of the typical species. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden among the foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
