Polypogon viridis subsp. pauciflorus

Polypogon viridis subsp.

pauciflorus, commonly known as the slender green panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in dense, compact clusters. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in ecological and botanical research.
