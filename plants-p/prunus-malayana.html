Prunus malayana

Prunus malayana, commonly known as the Malay cherry or Malayan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its flowers are white or pale pink.

The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
