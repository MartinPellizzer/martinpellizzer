Pisum sativum var. humile

Pisum sativum var. humile, commonly known as the dwarf pea, is a compact variety of the pea plant that exhibits shorter growth habits compared to its standard counterpart.

It typically features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of small, edible pods containing 2-5 seeds. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its compact size and attractive foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of leguminous nutrients.
