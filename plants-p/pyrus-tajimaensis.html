Pyrus tajimaensis

Pyrus tajimaensis, commonly known as the Japanese pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its genetic diversity in pear cultivation.
