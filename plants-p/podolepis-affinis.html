Podolepis affinis

Podolepis affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has small, linear leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, which is common among plants in its habitat.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
