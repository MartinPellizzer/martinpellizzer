Pterogastra major var. angustifolia

Pterogastra major var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pterogastra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
