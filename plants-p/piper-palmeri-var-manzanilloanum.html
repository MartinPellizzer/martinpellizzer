Piper palmeri var. manzanilloanum

Piper palmeri var. manzanilloanum, commonly known as the Manzanillo pepper plant, is a variant of the piper species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The varietal distinction highlights its unique adaptation to specific ecological conditions in its native habitat.
