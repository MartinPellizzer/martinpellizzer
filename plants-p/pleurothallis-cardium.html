Pleurothallis cardium

Pleurothallis cardium, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its overall aesthetic appeal.

Pleurothallis cardium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
