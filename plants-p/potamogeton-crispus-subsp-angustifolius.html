Potamogeton crispus subsp. angustifolius

Potamogeton crispus subsp.

angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia. It has long, slender, and often narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which float at the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and canals.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
