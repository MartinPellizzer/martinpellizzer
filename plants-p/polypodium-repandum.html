Polypodium repandum

Polypodium repandum, commonly known as the common huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and rhizomatous growth habit, often forming thick mats on forest floors. The leaves are pinnately compound, with a distinct midrib and small, ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate and tropical regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in various cultures.
