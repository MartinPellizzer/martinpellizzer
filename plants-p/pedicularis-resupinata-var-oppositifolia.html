Pedicularis resupinata var. oppositifolia

Pedicularis resupinata var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive opposite leaf arrangement and its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its tubular flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
