Pleurothallis cocornaensis

Pleurothallis cocornaensis, commonly known as the Cocora orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often blooming during the rainy season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
