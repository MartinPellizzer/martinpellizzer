Polygonum molle var. frondosum

Polygonum molle var. frondosum, commonly known as softleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its stems are usually prostrate or ascending, and the leaves are soft and fleshy, giving it its common name.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
