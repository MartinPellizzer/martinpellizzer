Pleiocardia orbiculata

Pleiocardia orbiculata, commonly known as the orbicular pleiocardium, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, orbicular-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers that bloom during the wet season. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful preservation efforts.
