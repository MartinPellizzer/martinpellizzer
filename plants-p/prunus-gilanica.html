Prunus gilanica

Prunus gilanica, commonly known as the Gilan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the forests of Gilan province in Iran.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-bark trunk and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart red berries. Prunus gilanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
