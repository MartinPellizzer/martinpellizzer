Pulicaria antiatlantica

Pulicaria antiatlantica, commonly known as the Canary Islands groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Pulicaria antiatlantica is adapted to arid conditions and is considered a characteristic species of the island's unique flora.
