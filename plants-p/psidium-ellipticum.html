Psidium ellipticum

Psidium ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but are less commonly cultivated. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a woody structure typical of many Myrtaceae species.

Psidium ellipticum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
