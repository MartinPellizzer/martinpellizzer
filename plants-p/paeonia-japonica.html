Paeonia japonica

Paeonia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a deciduous perennial plant native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing nutrients and water.

The flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are prized for their fragrance and ornamental value.
