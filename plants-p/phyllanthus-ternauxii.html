Phyllanthus ternauxii

Phyllanthus ternauxii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Phyllanthus ternauxii is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
