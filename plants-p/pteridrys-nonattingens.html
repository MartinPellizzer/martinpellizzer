Pteridrys nonattingens

Pteridrys nonattingens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous short, upright shoots.

The fronds are usually bipinnate, with a finely divided structure that enhances its ornamental appeal.
