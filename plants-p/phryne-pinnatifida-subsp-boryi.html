Phryne pinnatifida subsp. boryi

Phryne pinnatifida subsp.

boryi, commonly known as the Bory's flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to parts of Australia. It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
