Protea cneorifolia

Protea cneorifolia, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by many small, petal-like bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Protea cneorifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
