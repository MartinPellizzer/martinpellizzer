Paeonia mascula subsp. russoi

Paeonia mascula subsp.

russoi, commonly known as the Russo's peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and deep red.

The plant features a rhizomatous root system and possesses compound, bipinnate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
