Primula grignensis

Primula grignensis, commonly known as the Grignan primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southeastern France.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
