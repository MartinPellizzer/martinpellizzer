Pyrus meridionalis subvar. dilatata

Pyrus meridionalis subvar. dilatata, commonly known as the Southern pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern China, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is typically a small, yellowish pear with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to mild frost.
