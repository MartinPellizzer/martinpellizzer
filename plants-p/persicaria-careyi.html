Persicaria careyi

Persicaria careyi, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters tall, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria careyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist environments.
