Polygonum braunii

Polygonum braunii, commonly known as the Braun's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum braunii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
