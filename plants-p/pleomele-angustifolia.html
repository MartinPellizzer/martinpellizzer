Pleomele angustifolia

Pleomele angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pleomele, is a striking member of the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine and horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pleomele angustifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
