Piper sarmentosum

Piper sarmentosum, commonly known as creeping pepper or wild betel, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when mature, containing tiny seeds.

Piper sarmentosum is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
