Piper ornithorhynchum

Piper ornithorhynchum, commonly known as the duckbill pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is notable for its unique shape, with a swollen, duckbill-like structure at the base of the fruit.
