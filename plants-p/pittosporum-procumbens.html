Pittosporum procumbens

Pittosporum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features dense, leathery foliage with dark green leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum procumbens is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in ecological restoration projects.
