Piper aguilanum

Piper aguilanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinct, aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by bright red berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
