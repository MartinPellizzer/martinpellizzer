Phyteuma intermedium

Phyteuma intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. Phyteuma intermedium is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
