Protea rhodantha var. falcata

Protea rhodantha var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugarbush, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
