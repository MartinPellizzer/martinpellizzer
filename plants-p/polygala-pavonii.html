Polygala pavonii

Polygala pavonii, commonly known as the peacock spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble tiny peacocks. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

Polygala pavonii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
