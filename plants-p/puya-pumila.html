Puya pumila

Puya pumila, commonly known as the dwarf puya, is a perennial plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, colorful flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
