Phacelia patuliflora var. patuliflora

Phacelia patuliflora var. patuliflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered phacelia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
