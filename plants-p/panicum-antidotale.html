Panicum antidotale

Panicum antidotale, commonly known as the antidote grass, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Panicum antidotale is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
