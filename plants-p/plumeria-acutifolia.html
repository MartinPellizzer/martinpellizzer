Plumeria acutifolia

Plumeria acutifolia, commonly known as the Hawaiian plumeria or the frangipani, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in cultural and ceremonial contexts.
