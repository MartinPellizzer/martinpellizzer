Phymatopteris pellucidifolia

Phymatopteris pellucidifolia, commonly known as the transparent fern, is a species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The plant's botanical structure reflects its adaptation to humid, temperate climates.
