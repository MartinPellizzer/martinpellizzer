Piper perpuberulum

Piper perpuberulum, commonly known as the pubescent pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
