Poa lilloi

Poa lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with delicate blue to purple flowers.

Poa lilloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in low-maintenance landscaping.
