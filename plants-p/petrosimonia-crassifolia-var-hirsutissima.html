Petrosimonia crassifolia var. hirsutissima

Petrosimonia crassifolia var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the hairy petrosimonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are densely covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
