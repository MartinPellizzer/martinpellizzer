Padus eglandulosa

Padus eglandulosa, commonly known as the smooth white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to North America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and floodplains.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
