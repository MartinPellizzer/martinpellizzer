Poa extremiorientalis

Poa extremiorientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Asian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool climates. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
