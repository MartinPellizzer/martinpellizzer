Primula petelotii

Primula petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps to retain moisture.

Primula petelotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
