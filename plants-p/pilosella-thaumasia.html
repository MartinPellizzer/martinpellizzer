Pilosella thaumasia

Pilosella thaumasia, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that resemble miniature dusters, giving it its common name. Pilosella thaumasia is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and delicate appearance.
