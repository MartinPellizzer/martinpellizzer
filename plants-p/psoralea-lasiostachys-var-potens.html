Psoralea lasiostachys var. potens

Psoralea lasiostachys var. potens, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are yellowish-green and are arranged in dense, terminal spikes, which attract various pollinators.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
