Piper jaliscanum

Piper jaliscanum, commonly known as the Jalisco pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the black pepper plant. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and these berries contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include heart-shaped leaves and a climbing habit, making it a distinctive addition to botanical gardens and greenhouses.
