Pyrus melanocarpa var. subpubescens

Pyrus melanocarpa var. subpubescens, commonly known as the blackcap pear, is a variant of the European pear species.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fruit that ripens in late summer. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually dark green with a rough texture and are covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
