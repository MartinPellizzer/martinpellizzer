Polygonum oxyspermum subsp. raii

Polygonum oxyspermum subsp.

raii, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a pubescent surface, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
