Paullinia macrophylla

Paullinia macrophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is also known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
