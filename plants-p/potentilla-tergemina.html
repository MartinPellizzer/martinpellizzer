Potentilla tergemina

Potentilla tergemina, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five pointed lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla tergemina thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
