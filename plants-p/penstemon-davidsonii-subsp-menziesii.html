Penstemon davidsonii subsp. menziesii

Penstemon davidsonii subsp.

menziesii, commonly known as the Menzies' beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers.

The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
