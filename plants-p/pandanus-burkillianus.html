Pandanus burkillianus

Pandanus burkillianus, commonly known as the Burkill's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
