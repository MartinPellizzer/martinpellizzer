Pollia minor

Pollia minor, commonly known as the small orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. Pollia minor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
