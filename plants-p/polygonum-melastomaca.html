Polygonum melastomaca

Polygonum melastomaca, commonly known as the redroot sedum, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides. Its flowers are small, pink to red, and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
