Plantago kuntzei

Plantago kuntzei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically utilized in traditional remedies.
