Primula farinosa f. nivea

Primula farinosa f. nivea, commonly known as the snow primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, woolly foliage covered in a fine, white down, which gives it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
