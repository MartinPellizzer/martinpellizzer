Poa anceps var. gracilis

Poa anceps var. gracilis, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to the western United States.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or pink hues.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
