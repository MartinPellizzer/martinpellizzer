Polemonium archibaldiae

Polemonium archibaldiae, commonly known as Archibald's Jacob's ladder, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a valuable addition to woodland gardens.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a popular choice for native plant enthusiasts and conservation efforts.
