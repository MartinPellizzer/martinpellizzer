Passiflora erosa

Passiflora erosa, commonly known as the passionflower or maypop, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Passiflora erosa is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in traditional medicine.
