Prunus insititia var. cinerascens

Prunus insititia var. cinerascens, commonly known as the blackthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark blue berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
