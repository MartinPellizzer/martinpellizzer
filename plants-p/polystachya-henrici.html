Polystachya henrici

Polystachya henrici, commonly known as the Henry's bamboo orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and long, narrow leaves that resemble bamboo. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, typical of the polystachya genus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
