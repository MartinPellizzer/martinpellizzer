Paullinia mexicana

Paullinia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican sarsaparilla, is a climbing plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
