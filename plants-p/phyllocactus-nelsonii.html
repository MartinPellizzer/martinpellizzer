Phyllocactus nelsonii

Phyllocactus nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's phyllocactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Phyllocactus nelsonii thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.
