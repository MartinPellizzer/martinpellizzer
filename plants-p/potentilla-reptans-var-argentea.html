Potentilla reptans var. argentea

Potentilla reptans var. argentea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its silver-white, fuzzy foliage.

It produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its silver leaves provide ornamental value in gardens, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
