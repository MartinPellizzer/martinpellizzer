Paederia hirsuta

Paederia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy paederia, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
