Panicum ephemeroides

Panicum ephemeroides, commonly known as the ephemeral grass, is a short-lived perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, typically found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, narrow leaves and clusters of delicate, greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its rapid growth and short lifespan are adaptations to its harsh, dry habitat.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
