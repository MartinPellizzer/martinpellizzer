Plantago media var. dentata

Plantago media var. dentata, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on both sides, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
