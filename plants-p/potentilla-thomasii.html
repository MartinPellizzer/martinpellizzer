Potentilla thomasii

Potentilla thomasii, commonly known as Thomas' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, arranged in clusters of five, giving it its common name. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

Potentilla thomasii thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
