Phaseolus vulgaris var. oreolinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. oreolinus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and long, slender pods that contain edible beans. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to several meters. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and edible produce.
