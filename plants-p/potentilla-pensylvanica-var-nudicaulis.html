Potentilla pensylvanica var. nudicaulis

Potentilla pensylvanica var. nudicaulis, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance plant for native gardens.
