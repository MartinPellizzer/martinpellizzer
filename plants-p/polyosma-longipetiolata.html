Polyosma longipetiolata

Polyosma longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with thick, leathery foliage that provides dense cover. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
