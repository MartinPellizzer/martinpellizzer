Pinalia annulata

Pinalia annulata, commonly known as the ringed pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered with dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Pinalia annulata is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
