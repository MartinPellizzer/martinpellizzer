Pitcairnia maguirei

Pitcairnia maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, which forms a dense, water-holding structure at the center. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant purple and red flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are typically broad and covered in a fine layer of trichomes, which aid in water retention.

Pitcairnia maguirei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
