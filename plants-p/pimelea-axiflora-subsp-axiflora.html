Pimelea axiflora subsp. axiflora

Pimelea axiflora subsp.

axiflora, commonly known as the yellow bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive bright yellow flowers that are clustered in cylindrical spikes, resembling a bottlebrush.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
