Phacelia patuliflora

Phacelia patuliflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender stems and hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
