Potentilla biflora var. stenopetala

Potentilla biflora var. stenopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, with a dense arrangement along the stems. This variety is often found in rocky or alpine environments where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
