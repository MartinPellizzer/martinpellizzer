Piriqueta cistoides

Piriqueta cistoides, commonly known as the bladder cistus, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Piriqueta cistoides plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in Mediterranean scrublands.
