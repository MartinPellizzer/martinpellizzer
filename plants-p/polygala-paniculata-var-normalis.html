Polygala paniculata var. normalis

Polygala paniculata var. normalis, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the Polygala genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
