Populus major

Populus major, commonly known as the big poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. Its bark is smooth and grayish-white, often peeling in thin layers.

Populus major is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
