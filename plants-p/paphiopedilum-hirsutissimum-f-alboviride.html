Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum f. alboviride

Paphiopedilum hirsutissimum f. alboviride, commonly known as the white-green hirsute orchid, is a rare and highly sought-after phalaenopsis orchid species.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with a pale green and white coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with a short, thick rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its foliage. Its leaves are usually dark green and lance-shaped, while the flowers bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive lip that is broader than the other petals.

This orchid is native to Southeast Asia and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.
