Protea hirsuta

Protea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by a dense, hairy bract. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Protea hirsuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
