Pinus × sondereggeri

Pinus × sondereggeri, commonly known as the Sonderberg pine, is a hybrid conifer resulting from the cross between Pinus patula and Pinus ponderosa.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of two. The tree produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain edible seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

This hybrid species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and parks.
