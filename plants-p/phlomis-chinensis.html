Phlomis chinensis

Phlomis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese torchflower, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
