Polygala nicaeensis

Polygala nicaeensis, commonly known as the "Fairy Flax" or "Lady's Mantle," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spire-like inflorescences that bear small, often white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal areas or limestone habitats.

Polygala nicaeensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
