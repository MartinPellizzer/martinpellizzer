Plantago serraria var. laciniata

Plantago serraria var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, serrated leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
