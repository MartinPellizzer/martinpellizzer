Phalangium ramosissimum

Phalangium ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched whip fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching fronds that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
