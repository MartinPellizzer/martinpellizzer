Plinia strigipes

Plinia strigipes, commonly known as the roughleaf guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a yellow or red color.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
