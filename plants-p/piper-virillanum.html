Piper virillanum

Piper virillanum, commonly known as the golden piper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
