Passiflora inca

Passiflora inca, commonly known as the Inca Passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora inca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
