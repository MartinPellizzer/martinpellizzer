Piper jacquinii

Piper jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem with aerial roots and opposite, glossy foliage.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
