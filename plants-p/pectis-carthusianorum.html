Pectis carthusianorum

Pectis carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, woolly leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its robust, drought-resistant nature. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple branches forming a dense, thorny shrub.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
