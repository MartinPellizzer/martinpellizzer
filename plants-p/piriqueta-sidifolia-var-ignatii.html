Piriqueta sidifolia var. ignatii

Piriqueta sidifolia var. ignatii, commonly known as the Ignatius' spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water and withstand drought. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season, which are often borne on short, branching stems. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

The leaves are coated with a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss and protect against intense sunlight.
