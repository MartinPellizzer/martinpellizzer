Polemonium caeruleum var. piliferum

Polemonium caeruleum var. piliferum, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces showy, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
