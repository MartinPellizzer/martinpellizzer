Phalaris mauritii

Phalaris mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius grass, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or on rocky slopes.

Phalaris mauritii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
