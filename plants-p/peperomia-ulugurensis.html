Peperomia ulugurensis

Peperomia ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru peperomia, is a species native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a compact, low-growing shrub with thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance for low light and minimal water requirements.

Peperomia ulugurensis is valued for its ornamental foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for collectors and houseplant enthusiasts.
