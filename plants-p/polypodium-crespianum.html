Polypodium crespianum

Polypodium crespianum, commonly known as the crested polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crisped fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
