Phyllostachys edulis f. obliguinoda

Phyllostachys edulis f. obliguinoda, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a variant of the common bamboo species.

It is characterized by its striking purplish hue in young shoots and leaves, which gradually fades to a more typical green as the plant matures. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional crafts and as a source of edible shoots in certain regions.
