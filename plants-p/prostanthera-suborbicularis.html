Prostanthera suborbicularis

Prostanthera suborbicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honey mint, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or mauve, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Prostanthera suborbicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and scent.
