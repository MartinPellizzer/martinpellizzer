Passiflora quadrangularis var. decaisneana

Passiflora quadrangularis var. decaisneana, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its rough, quadrangular stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that ripens to a bright yellow or orange hue.

The fruit is edible, with a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp containing numerous seeds.
