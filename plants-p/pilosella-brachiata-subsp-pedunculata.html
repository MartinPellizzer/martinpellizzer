Pilosella brachiata subsp. pedunculata

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

pedunculata, commonly known as the bracted cat's-paw, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Europe. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves and produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

The subspecies pedunculata is distinguished by its elongated peduncles, which support the flowering heads.
