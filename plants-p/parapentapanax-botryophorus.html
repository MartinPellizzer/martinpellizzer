Parapentapanax botryophorus

Parapentapanax botryophorus, commonly known as the clustered pentapanax, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple leaflets arranged in a distinctive, clustered pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Araliaceae family.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
