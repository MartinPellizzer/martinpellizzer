Phyllanthus faguetii var. rhombifolius

Phyllanthus faguetii var. rhombifolius, commonly known as the rhombifoliate phyllanthus, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive rhombic-shaped leaves, which are often pubescent and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests or riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
