Parosela citriodora

Parosela citriodora, commonly known as lemon-scented parosela, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
