Passiflora pterocarpa

Passiflora pterocarpa, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white, often with a crown-like structure at the center. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora pterocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruits and medicinal properties.
