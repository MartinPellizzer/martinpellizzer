Plantago macrostachys var. typica

Plantago macrostachys var. typica, commonly known as the large-stalked plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces tall, erect flowering stems topped with a spike of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
