Poiretia erecta

Poiretia erecta, commonly known as the upright poiretia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, upright cluster. The plant produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptability, Poiretia erecta is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
