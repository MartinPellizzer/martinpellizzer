Psittacanthus guianensis

Psittacanthus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon basin. The plant features a stout, underground rhizome that gives rise to a rosette of lanceolate, glossy leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is valued for its starchy roots, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
