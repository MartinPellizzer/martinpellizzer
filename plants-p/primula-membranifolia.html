Primula membranifolia

Primula membranifolia, commonly known as the membranous primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a touch of color to the landscape during the cooler months.

Primula membranifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
