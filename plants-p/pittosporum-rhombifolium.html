Pittosporum rhombifolium

Pittosporum rhombifolium, commonly known as the diamond-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its broad, rhombic-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
