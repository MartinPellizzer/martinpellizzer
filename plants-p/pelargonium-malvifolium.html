Pelargonium malvifolium

Pelargonium malvifolium, commonly known as the Malva-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and prolonged blooming period.
