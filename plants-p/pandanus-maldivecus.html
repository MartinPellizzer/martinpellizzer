Pandanus maldivecus

Pandanus maldivecus, commonly known as the Maldives pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Maldives and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near the shoreline or in wetlands. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for making thatched roofs.

The plant also plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
