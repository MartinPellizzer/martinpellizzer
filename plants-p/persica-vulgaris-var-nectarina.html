Persica vulgaris var. nectarina

Persica vulgaris var. nectarina, commonly known as the nectarine.

This variant of the peach tree is characterized by its smooth, fuzzy skin and vibrant colors, typically ranging from yellow to deep red. The fruit is typically smaller than a peach, with a sweeter, more intense flavor due to its higher sugar content. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Asia, with cultivation spreading to Europe and the Americas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its edible fruit.
