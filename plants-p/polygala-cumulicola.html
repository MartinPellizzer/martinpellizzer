Polygala cumulicola

Polygala cumulicola, commonly known as the mountain polygala, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiraled inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, showy flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
