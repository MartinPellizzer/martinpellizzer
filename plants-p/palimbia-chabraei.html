Palimbia chabraei

Palimbia chabraei, commonly known as the Chabraei palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are consumed by both humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
