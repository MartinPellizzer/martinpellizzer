Pothos undatus

Pothos undatus, commonly known as the marble queen pothos, is a trailing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are variegated with creamy white and green patterns. The plant thrives in low-light conditions and is known for its ability to purify indoor air. It produces small, pale green flowers that are not typically visible above the foliage.

Pothos undatus is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
