Polembryum

Polembryum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant that thrives in stagnant water.

It is characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that resemble ghostly appendages, giving it its eerie appearance. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its leaf-like structures for anchoring and nutrient absorption. It is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it can float or partially submerge.

Despite its delicate appearance, Polembryum plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients.
