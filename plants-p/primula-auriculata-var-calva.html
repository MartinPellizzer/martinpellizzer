Primula auriculata var. calva

Primula auriculata var. calva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features basal leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
