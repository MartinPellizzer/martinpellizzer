Peperomia esquirolii

Peperomia esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquirolii Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or bronze tinge on the underside. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spadices, a characteristic feature of the Piperaceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia esquirolii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
