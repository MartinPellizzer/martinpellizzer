Passiflora hastata

Passiflora hastata, commonly known as the waxy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The flowers feature a unique structure with a crown of filaments and a central corona, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit, a red, fleshy berry, contains inedible seeds and is often consumed by birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
