Pterocarpus ecastaphyllum

Pterocarpus ecastaphyllum, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
