Prionachne dentata

Prionachne dentata, commonly known as the toothed prionachne, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive toothed margin. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, two-ranked flowers.

Prionachne dentata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
