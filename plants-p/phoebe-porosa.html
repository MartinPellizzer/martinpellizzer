Phoebe porosa

Phoebe porosa, commonly known as the porous phoebe, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular-dotted and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces dark, glossy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Phoebe porosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
