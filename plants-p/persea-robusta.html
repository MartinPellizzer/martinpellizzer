Persea robusta

Persea robusta, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully mature, though they can be astringent when unripe. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in craftsmanship and construction.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
