Phytelephas macrocarpa

Phytelephas macrocarpa, commonly known as the areca palm, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces large, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The areca palm is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
