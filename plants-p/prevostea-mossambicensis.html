Prevostea mossambicensis

Prevostea mossambicensis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a rare and striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant produces violet-colored berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to animals and humans. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
