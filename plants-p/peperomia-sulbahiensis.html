Peperomia sulbahiensis

Peperomia sulbahiensis, commonly known as the Sulbahi peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, succulent-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, making it an attractive ornamental plant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Peperomia sulbahiensis is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
