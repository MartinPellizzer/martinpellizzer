Pilosella pawlowskiellum

Pilosella pawlowskiellum, commonly known as the Polish mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, low-growing rosette of leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer. Native to central and eastern Europe, it thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant's compact size and distinctive foliage make it a notable species in its native habitat.

It is generally not considered invasive and plays a minor role in local ecosystems.
