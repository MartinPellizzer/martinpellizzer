Pseudodichanthium cookei

Pseudodichanthium cookei, commonly known as Cook's false needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland ecosystems.
