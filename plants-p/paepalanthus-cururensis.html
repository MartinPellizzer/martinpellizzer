Paepalanthus cururensis

Paepalanthus cururensis, commonly known as the Cururu cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with succulent stems that store water to survive drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
