Psidium jakucsianum

Psidium jakucsianum, commonly known as the jakucsian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by edible, berry-like fruits. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
