Pieris sinensis

Pieris sinensis, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is an evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green oval leaves that remain on the plant throughout the year. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The foliage and flowers provide seasonal interest, enhancing the visual appeal of gardens throughout the year.
