Penstemon amplexicaulis

Penstemon amplexicaulis, commonly known as the broadleaf beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and pairs of broad, overlapping leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.

Penstemon amplexicaulis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
