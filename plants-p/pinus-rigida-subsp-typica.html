Pinus rigida subsp. typica

Pinus rigida subsp.

typica, commonly known as the eastern white pine, is a coniferous tree native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its long, slender needles that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The tree produces cones that are usually 4 to 8 inches long, with scaly, reddish-brown bracts. Its bark is thin and scaly when young, becoming thicker and furrowed with age.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forests and woodlands.
