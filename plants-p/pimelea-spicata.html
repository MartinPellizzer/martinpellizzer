Pimelea spicata

Pimelea spicata, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is part of the family Thymelaeaceae and is known for its long-lasting flowers that retain their color when dried.
