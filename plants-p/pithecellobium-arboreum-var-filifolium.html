Pithecellobium arboreum var. filifolium

Pithecellobium arboreum var. filifolium, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for erosion control, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
