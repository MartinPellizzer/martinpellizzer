Pithecellobium prancei

Pithecellobium prancei, commonly known as the Mexican velvet tamarind, is a species of legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a woody stem that can grow into a shrub or small tree.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
