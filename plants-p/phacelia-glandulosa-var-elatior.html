Phacelia glandulosa var. elatior

Phacelia glandulosa var. elatior, commonly known as the tall glandular phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, ovate leaves with glandular dots, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
