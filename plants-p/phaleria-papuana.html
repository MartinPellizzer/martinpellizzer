Phaleria papuana

Phaleria papuana, commonly known as the Papuan white sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma, while its flowers are small and white, often clustered in inflorescences.

The wood of Phaleria papuana is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and perfumery.
