Pterocephalus frutescens var. angustissimus

Pterocephalus frutescens var. angustissimus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes or as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
