Prunus grayana var. fauriei

Prunus grayana var. fauriei, commonly known as the Faurie cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with serrated edges. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in summer, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
