Papuzilla

Papuzilla, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a member of the Campanulaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from blue to purple, and they bloom in the summer months. Papuzilla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
