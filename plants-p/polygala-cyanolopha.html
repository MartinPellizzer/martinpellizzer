Polygala cyanolopha

Polygala cyanolopha, commonly known as the blue-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
