Polythecium inaequale

Polythecium inaequale, commonly known as the unequal thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, this plant thrives in dry, sandy soils. The plant's name refers to the variation in the size and shape of its bracts.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and can be invasive in certain areas.
