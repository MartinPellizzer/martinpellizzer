Phymatosorus powellii

Phymatosorus powellii, commonly known as Powell's holly fern, is a species of fern native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its leathery, evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The botanical features of Phymatosorus powellii make it a distinctive and resilient member of the fern family.
