Pavonia hastata var. grandiflora

Pavonia hastata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and prairies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
