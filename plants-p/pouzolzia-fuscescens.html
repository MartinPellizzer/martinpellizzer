Pouzolzia fuscescens, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.
