Phrynium fasciculatum

Phrynium fasciculatum, commonly known as the arrow leaf plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Phrynium fasciculatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
