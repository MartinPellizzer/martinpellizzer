Papaver hortense

Papaver hortense, commonly known as the garden poppy, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Papaveraceae.

It is characterized by its large, showy petals that range in color from red to pink and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces striking, cup-shaped flowers. Its leaves are dark green, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The garden poppy is native to Europe and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
