Phyllostachys congesta

Phyllostachys congesta, commonly known as the black bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It is a clump-forming bamboo that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense stands in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllostachys congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscape design.
