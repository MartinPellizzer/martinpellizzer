Prunus maackii f. oblonga

Prunus maackii f. oblonga, commonly known as the Maack's cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus maackii species, which is native to East Asia.

This plant is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are narrower than those of the typical species. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. It produces edible berries that are typically red or black when mature, which can be used in culinary applications.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and spring blossoms.
