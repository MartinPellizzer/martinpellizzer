Parodia augustinii

Parodia augustinii, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors.
