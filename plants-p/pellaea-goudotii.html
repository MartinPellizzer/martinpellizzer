Pellaea goudotii

Pellaea goudotii, commonly known as the Goudot's maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are bipinnate, with a distinctive zigzag pattern along the rachis.

Pellaea goudotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
