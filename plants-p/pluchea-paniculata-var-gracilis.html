Pluchea paniculata var. gracilis

Pluchea paniculata var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators.
