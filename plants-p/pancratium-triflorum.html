Pancratium triflorum

Pancratium triflorum, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters of three, giving it its scientific name. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves and produces a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats.

Pancratium triflorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
