Piper elongatum f. cordulatum

Piper elongatum f. cordulatum, commonly known as the cordate pepper plant, is a unique cultivar of the pepper vine.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into peppery berries.

This ornamental and edible plant is often grown for its aesthetic value and mild flavor.
