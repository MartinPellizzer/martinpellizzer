Pancheria communis

Pancheria communis, commonly known as the common pancheria, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, often pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, with a branching structure that allows it to thrive in harsh environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Pancheria communis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
