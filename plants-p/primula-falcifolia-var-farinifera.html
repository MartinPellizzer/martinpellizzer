Primula falcifolia var. farinifera

Primula falcifolia var. farinifera, commonly known as the farinifera primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often curved leaves that give it its species name "falcifolia," meaning "sickle-shaped leaf.". The flowers are typically pink or white, with five petals and a showy, rounded shape. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
