Pithecellobium mekongense

Pithecellobium mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong pita, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is of interest in horticulture and botanical research.
