Passiflora insueta

Passiflora insueta, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces bright orange or red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Passiflora insueta is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
