Persea pseudolongifolia

Persea pseudolongifolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and lance-shaped. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that can be yellow or red when mature, and it is edible, though sometimes astringent.
