Petalochilus aurantiacus

Petalochilus aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers, which are often clustered in inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
