Pharus micranthus var. concolor

Pharus micranthus var. concolor, commonly known as the smoothleaf pharus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The leaves are typically narrow and smooth, with a lanceolate shape, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
