Portulaca oleracea var. alata

Portulaca oleracea var. alata, commonly known as the fat hen or blite, is an annual flowering plant native to warm temperate regions.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often hairy and can vary in color from green to reddish. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along roadsides.

Its edible leaves and seeds have been used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
