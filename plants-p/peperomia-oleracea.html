Peperomia oleracea

Peperomia oleracea, commonly known as the edible peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and compact growth habit. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a vegetable in some regions.

Peperomia oleracea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
