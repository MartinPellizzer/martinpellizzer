Pedicularis muscoides var. rosea

Pedicularis muscoides var. rosea, commonly known as the rosepink lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
