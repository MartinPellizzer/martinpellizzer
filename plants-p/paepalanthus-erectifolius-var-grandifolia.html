Paepalanthus erectifolius var. grandifolia

Paepalanthus erectifolius var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved puya, is a striking member of the bromeliad family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy bracts that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its visual impact in botanical collections.
