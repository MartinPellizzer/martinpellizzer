Picea likiangensis subsp. montigena

Picea likiangensis subsp.

montigena, commonly known as the Yunnan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinct cone structures.

The tree thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in temperate forests. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing protection against environmental stress.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting local biodiversity and contributing to forest ecosystems.
