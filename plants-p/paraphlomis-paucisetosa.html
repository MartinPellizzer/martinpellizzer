Paraphlomis paucisetosa

Paraphlomis paucisetosa, commonly known as the sparse-haired paraphlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, commonly known as the mint family, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the apex of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
