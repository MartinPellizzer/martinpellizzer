Prunella alba var. violacea

Prunella alba var. violacea, commonly known as violet loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to deep violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Violet loosestrife thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
