Philodendron ampamii

Philodendron ampamii, commonly known as the Ampa Mountain Philodendron, is a rare and unique species native to the high-altitude regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage is often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is a sought-after species among botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
