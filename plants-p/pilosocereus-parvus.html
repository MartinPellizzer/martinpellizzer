Pilosocereus parvus

Pilosocereus parvus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically red, orange, or yellow, with five to ten petals.

Pilosocereus parvus is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
