Potentilla × collina subsp. alpicola

Potentilla × collina subsp.

alpicola, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and serrated leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh mountain conditions. It typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils at high elevations.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.
