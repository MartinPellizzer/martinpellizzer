Plerandra paucidens

Plerandra paucidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lily, is a rare and striking orchid native to the wet tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing in rainforests or along stream banks. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, arranged in a loose spike that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Plerandra paucidens is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
