Piptatherum flaccidum

Piptatherum flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Piptatherum flaccidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
