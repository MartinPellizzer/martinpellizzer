Polygala molluginifolia

Polygala molluginifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in compact clusters. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sun-exposed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
