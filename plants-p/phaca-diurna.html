Phaca diurna

Phaca diurna, commonly known as the morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening, hence its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its vines can grow rapidly, often climbing supports or other structures.
