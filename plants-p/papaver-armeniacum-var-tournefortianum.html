Papaver armeniacum var. tournefortianum

Papaver armeniacum var. tournefortianum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
