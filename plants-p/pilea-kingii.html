Pilea kingii

Pilea kingii, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a subtle sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate moisture.

Pilea kingii is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
