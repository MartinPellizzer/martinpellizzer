Philadelphus stamineus

Philadelphus stamineus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Philadelphus stamineus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
