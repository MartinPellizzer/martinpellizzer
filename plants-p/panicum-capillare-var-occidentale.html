Panicum capillare var. occidentale

Panicum capillare var. occidentale, commonly known as western silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, hair-like leaves and produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads. It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
