Pimelea hirsuta subsp. elliptifolia

Pimelea hirsuta subsp.

elliptifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mistflower, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive greyish-green hue. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or heathland environments.

This subspecies is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
