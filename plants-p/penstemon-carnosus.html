Penstemon carnosus

Penstemon carnosus, commonly known as the woolly penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in dry, sunny environments. It produces numerous small, seed-bearing flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon carnosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
