Phyllanthus delpyanus

Phyllanthus delpyanus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional medicinal use.
