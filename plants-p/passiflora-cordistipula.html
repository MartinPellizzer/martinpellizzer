Passiflora cordistipula

Passiflora cordistipula, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and contain seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora cordistipula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
