Potentilla arenaria f. parvula

Potentilla arenaria f. parvula, commonly known as the sandstone cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and mountainous areas.

Its compact form and resilience make it a hardy choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
