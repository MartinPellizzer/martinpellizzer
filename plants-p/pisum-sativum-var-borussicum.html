Pisum sativum var. borussicum

Pisum sativum var. borussicum, commonly known as the Borussia pea, is a variant of the common pea that is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It is native to Central Europe and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive, trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, which can be harvested for culinary use. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its role in ecological gardening, as it attracts pollinators and supports biodiversity.
