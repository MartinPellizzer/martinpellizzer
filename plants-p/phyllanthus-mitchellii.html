Phyllanthus mitchellii

Phyllanthus mitchellii, commonly known as Mitchell's phyllanthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.
