Pterostylis pearsonii

Pterostylis pearsonii, commonly known as the Pearson's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and its distinctive hooded labellum, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it blooms during the cooler months of the year.

Pterostylis pearsonii is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
