Polyandrococos caudescens

Polyandrococos caudescens, commonly known as the hairy desert bean, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, hairy leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
