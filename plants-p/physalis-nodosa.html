Physalis nodosa

Physalis nodosa, commonly known as the clustered husk tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that develop into inflated, papery calyces. Its fruit, often called a "husk tomato," is typically orange to red in color and contains small, edible seeds.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
