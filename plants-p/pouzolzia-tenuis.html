Pouzolzia tenuis

Pouzolzia tenuis, commonly known as the slender pouzolzia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length, with a smooth, waxy surface. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by small, red berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
