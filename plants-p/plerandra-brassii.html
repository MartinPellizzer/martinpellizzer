Plerandra brassii

Plerandra brassii, commonly known as the golden torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange-red bracts that resemble torches. The plant features a short, stout rhizome with dense, glossy green leaves that are broad and ovate. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy spathe, which enhances its ornamental appeal.

Plerandra brassii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its striking visual display in gardens and indoor spaces.
