Pyrus fauriei

Pyrus fauriei, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, aromatic fruit in late summer. The fruit is typically eaten fresh or used in jams and preserves.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in traditional Chinese cuisine.
