Phaseolus seleri

Phaseolus seleri, commonly known as the Seler's bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional content, it is occasionally grown in gardens and agricultural settings.
