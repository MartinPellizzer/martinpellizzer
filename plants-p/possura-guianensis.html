Possura guianensis

Possura guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese tamarind, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its hard wood and has ecological importance in its native habitats.
