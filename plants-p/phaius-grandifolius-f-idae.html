Phaius grandifolius f. idae

Phaius grandifolius f. idae, commonly known as the Idaea phaius, is a striking orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to orange flowers with prominent purple veins, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique and vibrant floral display.
