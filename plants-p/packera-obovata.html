Packera obovata

Packera obovata, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and oval, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
