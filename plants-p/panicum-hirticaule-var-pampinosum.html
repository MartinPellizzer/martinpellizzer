Panicum hirticaule var. pampinosum

Panicum hirticaule var. pampinosum, commonly known as pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and large, feathery plumes that are often used in floral arrangements. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
