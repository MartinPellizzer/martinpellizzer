Plantago obtusata

Plantago obtusata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
