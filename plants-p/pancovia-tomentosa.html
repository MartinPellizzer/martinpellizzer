Pancovia tomentosa

Pancovia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pancovia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Pancovia tomentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
