Potentilla leschenaultiana var. major

Potentilla leschenaultiana var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
