Phyllostachys bambusoides f. castilloni-inversa

Phyllostachys bambusoides f. castilloni-inversa, commonly known as the reverse-veined bamboo, is a unique variant of the bamboo species characterized by its distinctive reverse vein pattern on the leaf blades.

This ornamental bamboo is native to China and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings. The leaves exhibit a striking contrast between the light green midribs and the darker green laminae, creating a visually appealing effect. It is a clump-forming plant that grows to a moderate height, making it suitable for garden landscapes and container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes rather than for commercial bamboo products.
