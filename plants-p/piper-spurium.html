Piper spurium

Piper spurium, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. Its small, ovate leaves are typically glossy and dark green, while the flowers are unisexual and clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to red as it matures.
