Peperomia ponapensis var. ponapensis

Peperomia ponapensis var. ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponapé peperomia, is a unique species native to the island of Ponapé in the Caroline Islands.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species, making it a subject of interest for botanical conservation efforts.
