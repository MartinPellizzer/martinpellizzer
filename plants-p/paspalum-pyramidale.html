Paspalum pyramidale

Paspalum pyramidale, commonly known as the pyramid grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Paspalum pyramidale is also valued for its use in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
