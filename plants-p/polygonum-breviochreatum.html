Polygonum breviochreatum

Polygonum breviochreatum, commonly known as the short-stemmed knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Polygonum breviochreatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
