Passiflora bicornis

Passiflora bicornis, commonly known as the two-horned passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a waxy texture.

Passiflora bicornis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in humid, shaded environments.
