Plectrocarpa arborea

Plectrocarpa arborea, commonly known as the broad-leaved waratah, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a prominent midrib. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Plectrocarpa arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
