Paronychia capitata var. rifea

Paronychia capitata var. rifea, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
