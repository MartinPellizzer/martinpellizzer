Photinia salicifolia

Photinia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elliptical and have a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and attractive foliage make it a versatile plant for hedges and screens.
