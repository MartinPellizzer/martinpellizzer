Potamogeton setaceus

Potamogeton setaceus, commonly known as the tufted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often tufted at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Potamogeton setaceus is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and marshes.
