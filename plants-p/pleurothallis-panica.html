Pleurothallis panica

Pleurothallis panica, commonly known as the panicle orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, branching inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a panicle-like structure. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis panica is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
