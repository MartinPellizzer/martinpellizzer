Pygeum coriifolium

Pygeum coriifolium, commonly known as the African wild olive, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
