Pyrus lemoinei

Pyrus lemoinei, commonly known as the Lemoine pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically not suitable for eating but are valued for their ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various climates.
