Panicum lagascae var. quadrifarium

Panicum lagascae var. quadrifarium, commonly known as the four-leaf panicgrass, is a variant of the panicgrass species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically arranged in a panicle structure. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained sandy soils.

This plant is often found in grassland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
