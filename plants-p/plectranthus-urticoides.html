Plectranthus urticoides

Plectranthus urticoides, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a member of the Lamiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa, characterized by its fuzzy, velvety leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
