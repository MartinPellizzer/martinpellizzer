Psidium erosum

Psidium erosum, commonly known as the rough passionfruit, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It produces small, edible fruits with a rough, hairy exterior and a fragrant, sweet-tart pulp. The plant has opposite, elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine, whitish hairs. Its flowers are fragrant and typically have five petals, with a distinctive corona of filaments.

The plant is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.
