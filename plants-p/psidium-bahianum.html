Psidium bahianum

Psidium bahianum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or *cação*, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary preparations. Its botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and white, clustered inflorescences.

Psidium bahianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield in suitable climates.
