Placodiscus letestui

Placodiscus letestui, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries, and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in subtropical regions.
