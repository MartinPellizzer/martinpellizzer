Punica granatum var. acidula

Punica granatum var. acidula, commonly known as the sour pomegranate, is a botanical variant of the pomegranate plant characterized by its smaller, more acidic fruits.

This variety is native to regions with temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a glossy green surface, while the stems are woody and branched. The flowers are bright red, and the fruit develops from a single flower, containing numerous seeds encased in a juicy, red aril.

It is valued for its tart flavor, making it suitable for culinary uses and traditional medicine.
