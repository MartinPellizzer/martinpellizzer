Pandiaka involucrata var. megastachya

Pandiaka involucrata var. megastachya, commonly known as the giant redflowered pandiaka, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters, making it a visually impressive plant. The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare and exotic flora.
