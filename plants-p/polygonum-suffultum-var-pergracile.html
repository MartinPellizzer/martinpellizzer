Polygonum suffultum var. pergracile

Polygonum suffultum var. pergracile, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, soft hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This plant is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
