Potentilla astragalifolia var. latisecta

Potentilla astragalifolia var. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer months. The leaves are broadly divided into lobes, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
