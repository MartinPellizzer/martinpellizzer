Protea laxa

Protea laxa, commonly known as the soft-bracted protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with a dry winter. Its unique floral structure attracts various pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea laxa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
