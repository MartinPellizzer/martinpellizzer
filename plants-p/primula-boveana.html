Primula boveana

Primula boveana, commonly known as the Bovean primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula boveana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
