Polygala gymnosepala

Polygala gymnosepala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, often pale green or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
