Pelargonium citronellum

Pelargonium citronellum, commonly known as lemon-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in perfumes and as a natural insect repellent.
