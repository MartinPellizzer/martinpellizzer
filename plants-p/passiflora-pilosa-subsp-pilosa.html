Passiflora pilosa subsp. pilosa

Passiflora pilosa subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the woolly passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing numerous small seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its unique floral display.
