Piper scabricaule

Piper scabricaule, commonly known as rough-stemmed pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a bright red color.
