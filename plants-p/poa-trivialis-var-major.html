Poa trivialis var. major

Poa trivialis var. major, commonly known as the tall meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and sandy soils. It has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ecological and ornamental value.
