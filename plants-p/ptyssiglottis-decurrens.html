Ptyssiglottis decurrens

Ptyssiglottis decurrens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dittany, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are aromatic and have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Ptyssiglottis decurrens is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a hardy species found in Mediterranean climates.
