Phaulopsis barteri var. pauciglandula

Phaulopsis barteri var. pauciglandula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phaulopsis, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces pale yellow to orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
