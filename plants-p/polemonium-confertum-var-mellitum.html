Polemonium confertum var. mellitum

Polemonium confertum var. mellitum, commonly known as the sweet pipsissewa, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces red berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
