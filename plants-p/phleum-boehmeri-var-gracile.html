Phleum boehmeri var. gracile

Phleum boehmeri var. gracile, commonly known as slender goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, often in temperate climates. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow leaves that are usually hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
