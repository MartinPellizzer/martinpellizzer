Ponerorchis tetraloba

Ponerorchis tetraloba, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, four-lobed floral structure. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Ponerorchis tetraloba is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
