Ptychosperma burretianum

Ptychosperma burretianum, commonly known as the Burret's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants, a feature typical of cycads. Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring protection.
