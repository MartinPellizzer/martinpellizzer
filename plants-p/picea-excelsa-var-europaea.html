Picea excelsa var. europaea

Picea excelsa var. europaea, commonly known as the European white spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, becoming more fissured with age.

This variant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions across Europe.
