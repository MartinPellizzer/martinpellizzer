Primula polyneura subsp. veitchii

Primula polyneura subsp.

veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan, China. It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, fragrant blooms.
