Polygonatum × buschianum

Polygonatum × buschianum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asparagaceae family.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and form a dense rosette, while its berries are red and contain seeds.

Polygonatum × buschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
