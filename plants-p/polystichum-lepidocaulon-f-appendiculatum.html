Polystichum lepidocaulon f. appendiculatum

Polystichum lepidocaulon f. appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its dense, evergreen fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China and Vietnam. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a leathery texture, and its sori are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
