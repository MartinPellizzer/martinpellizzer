Pithecellobium confine

Pithecellobium confine, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional properties.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for shade gardens and reforestation projects.
