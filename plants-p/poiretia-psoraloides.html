Poiretia psoraloides

Poiretia psoraloides, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Poiretia psoraloides is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments.
