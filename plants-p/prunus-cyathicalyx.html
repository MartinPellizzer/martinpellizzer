Prunus cyathicalyx

Prunus cyathicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese plum or Japanese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically purple or red when ripe. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Prunus cyathicalyx is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional Chinese medicine.
