Polygonum setosum subsp. luzuloides

Polygonum setosum subsp.

luzuloides, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracts and flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or streambanks. Its stems are often hairy, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
