Panicum monachnoides

Panicum monachnoides, commonly known as the monastic panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it conserve moisture.

Panicum monachnoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
