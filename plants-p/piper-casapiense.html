Piper casapiense

Piper casapiense, commonly known as the Casapiense pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and chili peppers. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
