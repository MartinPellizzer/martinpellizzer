Polypodium grisebachii

Polypodium grisebachii, commonly known as the gray fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, dark green fronds that often have a bluish or grayish tint. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium grisebachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
