Pleioblastus actinotrichus

Pleioblastus actinotrichus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and finely textured, gray-green foliage that gives it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Pleioblastus actinotrichus is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in forested areas.
