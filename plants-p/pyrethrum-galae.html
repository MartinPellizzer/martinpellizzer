Pyrethrum galae

Pyrethrum galae, commonly known as the Galae pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are surrounded by a ring of pale green to white ray florets. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pyrethrum galae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insect-repelling properties.
