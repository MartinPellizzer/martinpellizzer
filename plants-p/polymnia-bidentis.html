Polymnia bidentis

Polymnia bidentis, commonly known as the two-toothed polymnia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive two-toothed appearance along the margins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Polymnia bidentis is adapted to moist, sunny habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
