Prunus jamasakura f. plena

Prunus jamasakura f. plena, commonly known as the Full-flowered Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that appear in early spring, giving it a particularly showy appearance. The plant is native to Japan and is closely related to other Prunus species, including the common cherry. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.

The tree has a moderate growth rate and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
