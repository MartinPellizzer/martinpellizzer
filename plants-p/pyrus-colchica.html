Pyrus colchica

Pyrus colchica, commonly known as the Colchic pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round pomes that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and horticulture.
