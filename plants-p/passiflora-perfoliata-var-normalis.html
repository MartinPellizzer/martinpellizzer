Passiflora perfoliata var. normalis

Passiflora perfoliata var. normalis, commonly known as the common passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive leaflet structure, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
