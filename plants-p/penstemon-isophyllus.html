Penstemon isophyllus

Penstemon isophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Penstemon isophyllus is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
