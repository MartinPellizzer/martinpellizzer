Potamogeton × prussicus

Potamogeton × prussicus, commonly known as the European pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Potamogeton × prussicus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its ability to thrive in a range of water conditions makes it a common and adaptable species in shallow, still waters.
