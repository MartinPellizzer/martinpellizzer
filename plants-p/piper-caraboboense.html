Piper caraboboense

Piper caraboboense, commonly known as the Carabobo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Venezuela and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its unique flavor and is used in traditional culinary practices.

The botanical features of Piper caraboboense make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical studies.
