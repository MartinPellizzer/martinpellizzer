Penstemon gentianoides

Penstemon gentianoides, commonly known as the blue gentian penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hair.

Penstemon gentianoides is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
