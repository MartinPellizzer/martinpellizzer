Pionandra

Pionandra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Pionandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
