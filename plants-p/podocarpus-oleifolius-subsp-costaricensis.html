Podocarpus oleifolius subsp. costaricensis

Podocarpus oleifolius subsp.

costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Central America. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like fruits.

The plant is typically found in humid, tropical lowland forests, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. It has aromatic leaves that are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain.

This subspecies is an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitat.
