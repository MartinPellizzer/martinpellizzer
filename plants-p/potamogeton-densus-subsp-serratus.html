Potamogeton densus subsp. serratus

Potamogeton densus subsp.

serratus, commonly known as the serrated dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It is characterized by its dense, ribbon-like leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and can form dense underwater stands. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
