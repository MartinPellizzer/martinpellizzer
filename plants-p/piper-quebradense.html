Piper quebradense

Piper quebradense, commonly known as the Quebradense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
