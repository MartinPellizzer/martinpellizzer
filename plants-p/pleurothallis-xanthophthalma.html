Pleurothallis xanthophthalma

Pleurothallis xanthophthalma, commonly known as the golden-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate veining. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water and nutrients, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitats.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or terrariums.
