Pavonia regnelliana

Pavonia regnelliana, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and restoration projects.
