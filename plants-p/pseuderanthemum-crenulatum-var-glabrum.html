Pseuderanthemum crenulatum var. glabrum

Pseuderanthemum crenulatum var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, trailing growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly toothed along the edges. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and borne in clusters, often in shades of pink or white.
