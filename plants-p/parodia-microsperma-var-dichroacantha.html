Parodia microsperma var. dichroacantha

Parodia microsperma var. dichroacantha, commonly known as the Dichroacantha cactus, is a unique species native to South America, specifically Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in a dense layer of white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its growth habit is slow, making it a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of succulents.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled settings to ensure its survival.
