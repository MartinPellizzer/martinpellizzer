Papaver tubuliferum

Papaver tubuliferum, commonly known as the tubular poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Papaver tubuliferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
