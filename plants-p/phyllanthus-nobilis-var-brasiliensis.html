Phyllanthus nobilis var. brasiliensis

Phyllanthus nobilis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pear tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are fleshy, indehiscent capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
