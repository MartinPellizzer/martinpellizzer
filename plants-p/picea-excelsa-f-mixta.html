Picea excelsa f. mixta

Picea excelsa f. mixta, commonly known as the mixed white spruce, is a botanical variant of the white spruce, characterized by its unique combination of traits.

It exhibits a mix of morphological features from different parent species, leading to a diverse range of foliage and growth patterns. The plant typically grows in colder climates, thriving in boreal and subarctic regions where it forms dense forests. Its needle-like leaves are short and stiff, providing efficient photosynthesis in low-light conditions.

This hybrid form is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its adaptability and potential for ecological resilience.
