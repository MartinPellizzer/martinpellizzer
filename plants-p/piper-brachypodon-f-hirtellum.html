Piper brachypodon f. hirtellum

Piper brachypodon f. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper brachypodon species.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into clusters of three-winged berries. It is native to tropical regions of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
