Pteropepon oleiferum

Pteropepon oleiferum, commonly known as the oil-fruit vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash, and is characterized by its tendrils and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to oval, fleshy fruits containing a high oil content. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible oil.

The species is valued for both its agricultural and medicinal properties.
