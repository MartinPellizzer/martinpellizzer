Pectis coulteri

Pectis coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its yellow-orange flowers are arranged in flat-topped clusters, and the leaves are usually narrow and hairy.

It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
