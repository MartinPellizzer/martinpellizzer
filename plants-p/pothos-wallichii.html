Pothos wallichii

Pothos wallichii, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing or trailing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is known for its adaptability, thriving in low-light conditions and requiring minimal care. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Pothos wallichii is widely popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
