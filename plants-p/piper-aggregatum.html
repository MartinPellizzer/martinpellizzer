Piper aggregatum

Piper aggregatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, opposite leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its aromatic properties.
