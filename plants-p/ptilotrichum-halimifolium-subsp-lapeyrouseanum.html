Ptilotrichum halimifolium subsp. lapeyrouseanum

Ptilotrichum halimifolium subsp.

lapeyrouseanum, commonly known as the Lapeyrouse's ptilotrichum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos vegetation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is adapted to mild, Mediterranean-type climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more open growth habit compared to other members of the species.
