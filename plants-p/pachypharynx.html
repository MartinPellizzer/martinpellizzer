Pachypharynx

Pachypharynx, commonly known as the thick-throated orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. They are characterized by their thick, fleshy stems and showy, often fragrant flowers. The leaves are typically broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pachypharynx species are prized by horticulturists for their unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
