Polianthes tuberosa f. plena

Polianthes tuberosa f. plena, commonly known as the fringed nightshade or night-blooming cereus, is a hybrid cultivar of the tuberous-rooted species Polianthes tuberosa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers with prominent fringed petals that bloom at night. The plant is native to Mexico and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, making it a popular choice for evening floral displays.
