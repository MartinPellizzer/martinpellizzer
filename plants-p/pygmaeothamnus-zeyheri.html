Pygmaeothamnus zeyheri

Pygmaeothamnus zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's pygmy thornbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, exhibiting a compact, rounded form with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its small size and unique appearance make it a notable component of local flora.
