Pleopeltis pleopeltidis

Pleopeltis pleopeltidis, commonly known as the broad-leafed rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, broad, and often glossy appearance.

Pleopeltis pleopeltidis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
