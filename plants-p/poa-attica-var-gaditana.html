Poa attica var. gaditana

Poa attica var. gaditana, commonly known as the Iberian meadow grass, is a variant of the species Poa attica native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect culms. The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

This plant plays an important role in maintaining soil stability and supporting local biodiversity.
