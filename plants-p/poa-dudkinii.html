Poa dudkinii

Poa dudkinii, commonly known as Dudkin's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and often bluish-green, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

Poa dudkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
