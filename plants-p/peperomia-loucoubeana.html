Peperomia loucoubeana

Peperomia loucoubeana, commonly known as the Loucoube's pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia loucoubeana is known for its drought tolerance and ability to adapt to a variety of environmental conditions.
