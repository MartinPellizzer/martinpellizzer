Pistolochia nivalis

Pistolochia nivalis, commonly known as the snow pistolplant, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble small pistols, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in its harsh habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
