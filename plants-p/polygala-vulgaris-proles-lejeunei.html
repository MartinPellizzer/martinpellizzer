Polygala vulgaris proles lejeunei

Polygala vulgaris proles lejeunei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive flowers and often small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its flowers are usually small and either white or pale yellow, arranged in spike-like clusters.

It is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
