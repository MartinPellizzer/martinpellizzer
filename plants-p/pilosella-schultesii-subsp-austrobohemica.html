Pilosella schultesii subsp. austrobohemica

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

austrobohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typical of the genus Pilosella. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
