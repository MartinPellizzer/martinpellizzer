Populus × glandulosa

Populus × glandulosa, commonly known as the Chinese tamarisk.

It is a hybrid species belonging to the Salicaceae family, characterized by its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions. The plant typically exhibits gray-green foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in catkins. It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some areas, it is sometimes considered a problematic species in ecological contexts.
