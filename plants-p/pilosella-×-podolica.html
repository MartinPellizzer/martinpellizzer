Pilosella × podolica

Pilosella × podolica, commonly known as the meadow bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "pilosella.". The plant produces small, yellowish to white flowers that are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate regions.

Pilosella × podolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
