Psylliostachys × androssovii

Psylliostachys × androssovii, commonly known as the hybrid millet, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Psylliostachys huicui and Psylliostachys sibirica.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant is primarily cultivated for its potential as a biofuel crop due to its high biomass yield and adaptability to marginal soils. However, its sterility limits its use in traditional agricultural practices.

Despite this, it remains of interest in botanical research for its genetic diversity and ecological resilience.
