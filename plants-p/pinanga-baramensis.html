Pinanga baramensis

Pinanga baramensis, commonly known as the Baram Mountain bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo, specifically found in the state of Sarawak. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in lowland rainforests.
