Pouteria kaniensis

Pouteria kaniensis, commonly known as the Kaniensis Sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange to red fruits with a sweet, custard-like pulp. The fruit's exterior is covered in a thick, leathery skin, and it contains a single large seed.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential economic use in both horticulture and food production.
