Primula capitata subsp. lacteocapitata

Primula capitata subsp.

lacteocapitata, commonly known as the milkcap primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features dense clusters of small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive milky sap that gives it its common name.

The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with fleshy, basal leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
