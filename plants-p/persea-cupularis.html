Persea cupularis

Persea cupularis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-colored fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor when ripe.

Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine, while the tree itself is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
