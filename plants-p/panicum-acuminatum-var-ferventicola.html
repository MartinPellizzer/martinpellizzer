Panicum acuminatum var. ferventicola

Panicum acuminatum var. ferventicola, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, upright stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues, especially under stress or during flowering. The plant produces small, fluffy spikelets that give it a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental and functional landscaping in arid climates.
