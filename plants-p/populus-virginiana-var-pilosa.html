Populus virginiana var. pilosa

Populus virginiana var. pilosa, commonly known as the silky poplar or cottonwood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its soft, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, giving it the common name "cottonwood.". The tree has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
