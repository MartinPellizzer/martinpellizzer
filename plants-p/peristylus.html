Peristylus

Peristylus, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a genus of flowering plants in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with small, leathery leaves. The flowers are typically bell-shaped and occur in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, or red. Peristylus species are adapted to acidic soils and are often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
