Pterolobium indicum

Pterolobium indicum, commonly known as the Indian pterolobium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, fibrous pods, and it is often found in moist, tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has traditional uses in local medicine.
