Pedicularis armata

Pedicularis armata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are often adorned with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are usually basal and lance-shaped, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Pedicularis armata is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
