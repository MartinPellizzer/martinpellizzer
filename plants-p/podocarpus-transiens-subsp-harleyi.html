Podocarpus transiens subsp. harleyi

Podocarpus transiens subsp.

harleyi, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by woody, cone-like structures.

The plant has a slow growth rate and exhibits a typical podocarp morphology with spirally arranged leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
