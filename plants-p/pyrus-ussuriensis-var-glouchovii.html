Pyrus ussuriensis var. glouchovii

Pyrus ussuriensis var. glouchovii, commonly known as the Ussuri pear, is a variant of the Ussuri pear native to eastern Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in mountainous regions of China, Russia, and Korea. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. Its flowers are white and clustered, followed by small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
