Plumeria conspicua

Plumeria conspicua, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. Its ornamental value makes it popular in landscaping and as a cut flower.

The species is also valued for its cultural significance in various regions.
