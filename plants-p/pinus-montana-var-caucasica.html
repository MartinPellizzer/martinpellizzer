Pinus montana var. caucasica

Pinus montana var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian fir, is a variety of the mountain fir native to the Caucasus region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cones. The plant typically grows in mountainous areas with high altitudes and cool climates. Its botanical features include a straight trunk, dense foliage, and a conical shape.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
