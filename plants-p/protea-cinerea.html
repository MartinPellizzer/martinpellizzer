Protea cinerea

Protea cinerea, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, fluffy, white to pale pink flower heads, which are actually composed of many tiny flowers clustered together. The plant thrives in the fynbos biome, where it is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and frequent fires. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, although it is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.

Protea cinerea is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions of South Africa.
