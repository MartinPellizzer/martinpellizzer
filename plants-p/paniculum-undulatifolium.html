Paniculum undulatifolium

Paniculum undulatifolium, commonly known as the wavy-leaf panicum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit. The plant features narrow, undulating leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as savannas and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and resilience.
