Poa diaboli

Poa diaboli, commonly known as devil's grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to southern South America and is characterized by its dark purple to blackish stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

Poa diaboli is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking appearance.
