Phaeonychium albiflorum

Phaeonychium albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered phaeonychium, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the wet season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation areas.
