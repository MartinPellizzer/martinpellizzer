Pachira calophylla

Pachira calophylla, commonly known as the Mexican money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, broad leaves and striking, waxy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
