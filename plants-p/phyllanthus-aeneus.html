Phyllanthus aeneus

Phyllanthus aeneus, commonly known as the copperleaf, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and small, clustered leaves that often have a metallic or copper-like sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish or red fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Phyllanthus aeneus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various cultures.
