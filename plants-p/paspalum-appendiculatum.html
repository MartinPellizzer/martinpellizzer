Paspalum appendiculatum

Paspalum appendiculatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Paspalum appendiculatum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
