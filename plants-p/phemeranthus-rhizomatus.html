Phemeranthus rhizomatus

Phemeranthus rhizomatus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ephemeral blooming habit, where flowers open only briefly at night and close by dawn. The plant has a rhizomatous growth system, allowing it to spread vegetatively and establish colonies in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in its humid habitat.

Despite its short-lived flowers, it plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native ecosystem.
