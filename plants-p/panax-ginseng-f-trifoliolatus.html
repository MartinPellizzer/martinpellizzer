Panax ginseng f. trifoliolatus

Panax ginseng f. trifoliolatus, commonly known as the three-leaf ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng plant characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

This botanical form is native to regions of East Asia, including China, Korea, and Russia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with roots containing ginsenosides that are valued in traditional medicine. It typically grows in shaded, forested areas with well-drained soil.

The trifoliate foliage sets it apart from the more common two-leaf form of Panax ginseng.
