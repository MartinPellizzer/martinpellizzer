Poa nemoralis subsp. caespitosa

Poa nemoralis subsp.

caespitosa, commonly known as the common bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It forms dense, compact clumps and is often found in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant has slender, slightly hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its tolerance to partial shade.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
