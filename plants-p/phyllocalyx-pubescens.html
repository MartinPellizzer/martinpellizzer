Phyllocalyx pubescens

Phyllocalyx pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calyx orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, leaf-like bracts that encase the flower cluster. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually small and colorful, with a distinctive calyx-like structure that gives it its scientific name.

The plant's unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
