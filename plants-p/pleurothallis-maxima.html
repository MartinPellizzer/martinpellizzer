Pleurothallis maxima

Pleurothallis maxima, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often on rocky substrates. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with vibrant colors ranging from white to deep red.

Pleurothallis maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
