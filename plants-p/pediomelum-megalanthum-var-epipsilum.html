Pediomelum megalanthum var. epipsilum

Pediomelum megalanthum var. epipsilum, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
