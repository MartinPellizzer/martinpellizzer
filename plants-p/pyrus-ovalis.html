Pyrus ovalis

Pyrus ovalis, commonly known as the oval pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, juicy flavor.

Pyrus ovalis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
