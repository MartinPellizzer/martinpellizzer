Piper decumanum

Piper decumanum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
