Pyrus aucuparia dirkeni

Pyrus aucuparia dirkeni, commonly known as the Dirken's crabapple, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental fruits and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinct appearance.

The fruit, which is edible when ripe, is often used in jams and preserves due to its tart flavor.
