Piper lappaceum

Piper lappaceum, commonly known as the Malabar pepper or Indian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
