Peperomia ternata

Peperomia ternata, commonly known as the Three-leaf Peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with three leaflets per leaf, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia ternata is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
