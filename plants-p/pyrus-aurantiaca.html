Pyrus aurantiaca

Pyrus aurantiaca, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy yellow flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, yellow or golden fruits that are edible. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated edge.

Pyrus aurantiaca is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
