Piper pseudojalapense

Piper pseudojalapense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elongated, reddish berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
