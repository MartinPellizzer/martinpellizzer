Pyrrocoma hirta

Pyrrocoma hirta, commonly known as the hairy pyrrocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually pink or purple and appear in clusters.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and also hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
