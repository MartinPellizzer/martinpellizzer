Phaseolus leiospermus

Phaseolus leiospermus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and edible pods that contain seeds used in various cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often grown for both ornamental and culinary purposes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
