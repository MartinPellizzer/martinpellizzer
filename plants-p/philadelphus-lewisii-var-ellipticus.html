Philadelphus lewisii var. ellipticus

Philadelphus lewisii var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and long-lasting blooms.
