Poa capillata

Poa capillata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows, roadsides, and pastures. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Poa capillata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
