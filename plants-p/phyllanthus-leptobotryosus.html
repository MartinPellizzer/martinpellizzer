Phyllanthus leptobotryosus

Phyllanthus leptobotryosus, commonly known as the slender-fruit phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often red or yellow, which are an important food source for various animals. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
