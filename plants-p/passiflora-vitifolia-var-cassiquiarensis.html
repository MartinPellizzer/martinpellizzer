Passiflora vitifolia var. cassiquiarensis

Passiflora vitifolia var. cassiquiarensis, commonly known as the Cassiquiare passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding a central cluster of stamens. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. This species is notable for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
