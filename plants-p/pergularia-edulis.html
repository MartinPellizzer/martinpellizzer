Pergularia edulis

Pergularia edulis, commonly known as the edible bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It possesses small, bladder-like structures that trap and digest small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in freshwater environments, often found in slow-moving streams and ponds. Its leaves are thin and delicate, adapted for efficient nutrient absorption.

Despite its carnivorous nature, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic habitats.
