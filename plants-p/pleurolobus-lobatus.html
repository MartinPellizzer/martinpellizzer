Pleurolobus lobatus

Pleurolobus lobatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pleurolobus, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are often hairy.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in racemose inflorescences, and are typically pale yellow to greenish in color.
