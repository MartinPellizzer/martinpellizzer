Penstemon humilis subsp. obtusifolius

Penstemon humilis subsp.

obtusifolius, commonly known as the bluntleaf penstemon, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are blunt at the tips, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
