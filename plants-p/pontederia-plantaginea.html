Pontederia plantaginea

Pontederia plantaginea, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyacinth, is a semi-aquatic plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water, often forming dense mats. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in shallow water and facilitating nutrient absorption.

However, it is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to disrupt aquatic ecosystems.
