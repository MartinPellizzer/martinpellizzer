Plumeria bicolor

Plumeria bicolor, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central America, the Caribbean, and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, with a distinctive bicolor pattern. The plant has large, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty and longevity.

Plumeria bicolor is also valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and as a symbol in various cultures.
