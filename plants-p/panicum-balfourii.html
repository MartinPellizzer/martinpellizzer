Panicum balfourii

Panicum balfourii, commonly known as Balfour's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
