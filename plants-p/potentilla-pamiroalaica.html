Potentilla pamiroalaica

Potentilla pamiroalaica, commonly known as the Pamir cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several elliptical leaflets, and it produces fruit in the form of a dry, capsule.

Potentilla pamiroalaica is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
