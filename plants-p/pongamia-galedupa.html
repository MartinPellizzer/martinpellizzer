Pongamia galedupa

Pongamia galedupa, commonly known as the Java pongam, is a leguminous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into pod-like fruits containing elliptical seeds. The plant is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Pongamia galedupa is also valued for its potential in biofuel production due to its high oil content in its seeds.
