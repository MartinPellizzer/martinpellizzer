Pittosporum senacia subsp. senacia

Pittosporum senacia subsp.

senacia, commonly known as the coastal pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in coastal areas, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, edible berries that are a food source for native birds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
