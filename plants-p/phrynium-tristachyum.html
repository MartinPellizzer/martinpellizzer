Phrynium tristachyum

Phrynium tristachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1.5 meters in height, featuring broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch, making it economically important in certain regions.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
