Pelargonium primuliforme

Pelargonium primuliforme, commonly known as the African geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and often have a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in spring and summer, producing flowers in shades of pink, red, and white.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
