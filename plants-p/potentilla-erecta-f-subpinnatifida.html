Potentilla erecta f. subpinnatifida

Potentilla erecta f. subpinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It features compound leaves with a sub-pinnate arrangement, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to regions of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
