Pelargonium × callodon

Pelargonium × callodon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Pelargonium × callodon thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets, containers, and garden borders.
