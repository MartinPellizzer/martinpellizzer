Pandorea dendrophila

Pandorea dendrophila, commonly known as the wisteria vine, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Pandorea dendrophila is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to cover trellises or walls.
