Polygala congoensis

Polygala congoensis, commonly known as the Congo milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and are pollinated by insects.
