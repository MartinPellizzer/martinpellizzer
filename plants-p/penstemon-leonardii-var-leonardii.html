Penstemon leonardii var. leonardii

Penstemon leonardii var. leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
