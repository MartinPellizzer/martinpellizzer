Protea thesiifolia

Protea thesiifolia, commonly known as the coastal protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to red flowers, which are arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant is adapted to coastal environments, thriving in sandy soils and experiencing salt spray. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is typical of the Cape Floristic Region.

Protea thesiifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects.
