Pyrenoglyphis mattogrossensis

Pyrenoglyphis mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Amazonian leaf litter fern, is a tropical fern native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of the Amazon basin. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that produce numerous fronds. Its fronds are typically bioluminescent, a unique adaptation that may aid in attracting pollinators or deterring herbivores.

This species plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing shelter for small organisms.
