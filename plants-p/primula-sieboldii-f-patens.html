Primula sieboldii f. patens

Primula sieboldii f. patens, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or the snow bell, is a hybrid form of the Primula sieboldii species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and charming appearance.
