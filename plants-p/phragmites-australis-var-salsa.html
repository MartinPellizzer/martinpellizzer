Phragmites australis var. salsa

Phragmites australis var. salsa, commonly known as the saltmarsh reed, is a variant of the common reed that thrives in saline wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand saltwater exposure. This plant plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and avian species. It reproduces through rhizomes and seed dispersal, allowing it to spread across marshes and tidal flats.

Its presence is often an indicator of brackish or saline water conditions in wetland ecosystems.
