Passiflora verecunda

Passiflora verecunda, commonly known as the "Crimson Crown," is a striking species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador and Peru.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane regions with high rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is increasingly sought after by horticulturists and conservationists alike.
