Pyrus aucuparia monstr. pendula

Pyrus aucuparia monstr.

pendula, commonly known as the weeping crabapple, is a cultivar of the crabapple tree characterized by its pendulous branches that cascade with white or pink flowers in spring. It is a deciduous tree with a rounded, often weeping form, and its leaves are ovate with serrated edges.

The fruit is small, apple-like, and typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The plant is relatively hardy and can tolerate urban environments, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.
