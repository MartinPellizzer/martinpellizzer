Perezia kingii

Perezia kingii, commonly known as the King's perezia, is a rare and striking member of the orchid family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Perezia kingii is considered a conservation concern.
