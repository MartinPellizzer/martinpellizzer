Piper cubataonum

Piper cubataonum, commonly known as the Cuban pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruits are berries that mature to a bright red color, containing small, hard seeds.

Piper cubataonum is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and potential medicinal properties.
