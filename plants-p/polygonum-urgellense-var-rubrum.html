Polygonum urgellense var. rubrum

Polygonum urgellense var. rubrum, commonly known as the red-veined sorrel, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves that are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors or stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
