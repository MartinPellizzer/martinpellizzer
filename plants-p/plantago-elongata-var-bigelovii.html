Plantago elongata var. bigelovii

Plantago elongata var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
