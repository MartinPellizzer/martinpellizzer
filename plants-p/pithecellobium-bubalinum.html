Pithecellobium bubalinum

Pithecellobium bubalinum, commonly known as the water pea or wild tamarind, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in agroforestry systems.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
