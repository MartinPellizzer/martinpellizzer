Primula flava subsp. citrina

Primula flava subsp.

citrina, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features showy, clustered flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it prefers well-drained, moist soils in partial shade. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the floral diversity of its ecosystem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
