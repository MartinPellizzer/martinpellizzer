Potentilla balkharica

Potentilla balkharica, commonly known as the Balkh rose or Kashmir rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its drought resistance.

Potentilla balkharica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
