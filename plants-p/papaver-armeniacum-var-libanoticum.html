Papaver armeniacum var. libanoticum

Papaver armeniacum var. libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanese poppy, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black central stigma. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
