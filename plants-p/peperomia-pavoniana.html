Peperomia pavoniana

Peperomia pavoniana, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often display a velvety texture and vibrant coloration. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with some varieties exhibiting a striking purplish or reddish hue. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, arranged in spikes along the stems.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in indirect light conditions.
