Piptadenia flava

Piptadenia flava, commonly known as yellow angico, is a deciduous tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and traditional uses.
