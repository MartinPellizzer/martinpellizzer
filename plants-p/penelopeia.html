Penelopeia

Penelopeia, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Penelopeia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
