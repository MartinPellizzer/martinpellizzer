Primulina laxiflora

Primulina laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered gloxinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink and purple. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Primulina laxiflora prefers well-draining soil and indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
