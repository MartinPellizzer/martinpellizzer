Platanthera calderoniae

Platanthera calderoniae, commonly known as the Calderonia lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as pine forests and wetlands. It relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is crucial for its survival.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
