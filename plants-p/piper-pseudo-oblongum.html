Piper pseudo-oblongum

Piper pseudo-oblongum, commonly known as the long-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, elongated berries that contain the aromatic compound responsible for its spicy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires partial shade for optimal growth.
