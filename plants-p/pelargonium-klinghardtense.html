Pelargonium klinghardtense

Pelargonium klinghardtense, commonly known as the Klinghardt's geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
