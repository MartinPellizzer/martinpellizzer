Papaver radicatum subsp. subglobosum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

subglobosum, commonly known as the globular poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features showy, orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a compact, globular shape with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are surrounded by a series of bracts, which are modified leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
