Pilea dendrophila

Pilea dendrophila, commonly known as the Shingle Plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular houseplant among indoor gardeners.
