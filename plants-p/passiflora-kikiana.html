Passiflora kikiana

Passiflora kikiana, commonly known as the Kikiana passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces striking, colorful flowers with a distinctive crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The fruit, which is edible, develops from the flower and contains numerous seeds.
