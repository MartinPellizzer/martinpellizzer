Potentilla congesta var. nemorosa

Potentilla congesta var. nemorosa, commonly known as the shrubby cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features dense, upright stems covered in small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven oval leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
