Piper corcovadense var. petiolatum

Piper corcovadense var. petiolatum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is part of the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its berries are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it useful in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
