Paeonia potaninii var. trollioides

Paeonia potaninii var. trollioides, commonly known as the Tibetan peony, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive double petal structure. The plant features thick, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its rarity and beauty, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
