Protea obtusifolia

Protea obtusifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sugar bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant produces inflorescences that consist of a central cone surrounded by bracts, with numerous small, tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

Protea obtusifolia is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to Mediterranean-type climates.
