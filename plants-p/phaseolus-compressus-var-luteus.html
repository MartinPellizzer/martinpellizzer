Phaseolus compressus var. luteus

Phaseolus compressus var. luteus, commonly known as the yellow bush bean, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as a shrub with compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers. Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines.

The variety is noted for its vibrant yellow color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
