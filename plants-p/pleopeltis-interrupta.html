Pleopeltis interrupta

Pleopeltis interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a layer of fine, hairy structures.

The fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of serrations along the edges.
