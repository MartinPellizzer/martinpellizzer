Prunella laciniata var. pinnatifida

Prunella laciniata var. pinnatifida, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered in fine hairs. The leaves are lanceolate to linear, with a serrated margin and a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, clustered blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
