Poa chaixii var. glabra

Poa chaixii var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, leafy stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name, while its flowering heads consist of small, spike-like clusters of florets.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
