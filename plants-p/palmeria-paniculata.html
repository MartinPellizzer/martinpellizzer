Palmeria paniculata

Palmeria paniculata, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features showy, white, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Palmeria paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
