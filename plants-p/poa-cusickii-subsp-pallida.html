Poa cusickii subsp. pallida

Poa cusickii subsp.

pallida, commonly known as the pale tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale to whitish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
