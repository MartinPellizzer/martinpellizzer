Pelargonium macowanii

Pelargonium macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or purple blooms during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium macowanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
