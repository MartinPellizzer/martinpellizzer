Phthirusa abdita

Phthirusa abdita, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.

Phthirusa abdita is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
