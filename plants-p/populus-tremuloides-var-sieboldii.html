Populus tremuloides var. sieboldii

Populus tremuloides var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese trembling aspen, is a variant of the trembling aspen native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and heart-shaped leaves that tremble in the wind. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a samara, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
