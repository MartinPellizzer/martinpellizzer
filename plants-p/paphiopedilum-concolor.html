Paphiopedilum concolor

Paphiopedilum concolor, commonly known as the Chinese violet or the yellow lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a showy, yellowish-green flower with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.

Paphiopedilum concolor is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
