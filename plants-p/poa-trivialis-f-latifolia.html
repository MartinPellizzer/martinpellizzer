Poa trivialis f. latifolia

Poa trivialis f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass.

It is characterized by its broader, more robust leaves compared to the typical form of Poa trivialis. This plant is a perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates, often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

The plant plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
