Persicaria sericea

Persicaria sericea, commonly known as silky knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Persicaria sericea is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in wetland environments.
