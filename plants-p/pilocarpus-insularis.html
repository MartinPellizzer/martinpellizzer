Pilocarpus insularis

Pilocarpus insularis, commonly known as the Caribbean cherry or insular pilocarpus, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain alkaloids, including pilocarpine, which has been used for its stimulant and medicinal properties.

Pilocarpus insularis thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and limestone soils.
