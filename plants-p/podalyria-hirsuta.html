Podalyria hirsuta

Podalyria hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy podalyria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
