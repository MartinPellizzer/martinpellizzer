Potamogeton hybridus var. spicatus

Potamogeton hybridus var. spicatus, commonly known as the spicate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in slow-moving or still waters, often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its reproductive structures are adapted for underwater pollination, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

The species is also used in phytoremediation efforts due to its capacity to absorb nutrients and heavy metals from the water.
