Plinia cerrocampanensis

Plinia cerrocampanensis, commonly known as the Campanula Plinia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
