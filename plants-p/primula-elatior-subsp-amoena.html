Primula elatior subsp. amoena

Primula elatior subsp.

amoena, commonly known as the Alpine Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions.
