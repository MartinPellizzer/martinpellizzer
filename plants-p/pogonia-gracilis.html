Pogonia gracilis

Pogonia gracilis, commonly known as the slender pogonia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and a single, showy violet or purple flower with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific soil conditions for successful growth. It is a rare and endangered species, threatened by habitat loss and over-collection.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, conservation efforts are essential for its survival.
