Piper guineense var. gilletii

Piper guineense var. gilletii, commonly known as African pepper or Guineese pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elongated, berry-like fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of West and Central Africa.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
