Petraeomyrtus

Petraeomyrtus, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the southeastern regions of Australia, where it thrives in rocky and coastal environments. The plants typically have small, aromatic leaves and produce clusters of white or pink flowers. Petraeomyrtus species are often shrubs or small trees, adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
