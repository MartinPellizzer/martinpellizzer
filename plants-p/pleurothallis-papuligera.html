Pleurothallis papuligera

Pleurothallis papuligera, commonly known as the papular pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its flowers are often clustered in a raceme and bloom during the wet season.

Pleurothallis papuligera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
