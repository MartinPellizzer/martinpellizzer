Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. palawensis

Phalaenopsis lueddemanniana var. palawensis, commonly known as the Palau phalaenopsis, is a rare and unique orchid native to the island of Palau in the Western Pacific.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat threats, it is considered a conservation concern.
