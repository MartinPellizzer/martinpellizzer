Prangos asperula var. judaica

Prangos asperula var. judaica, commonly known as the Judean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and the surrounding areas.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is distinguished by its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
