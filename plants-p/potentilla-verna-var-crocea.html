Potentilla verna var. crocea

Potentilla verna var. crocea, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland areas.

The yellow cinquefoil is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
