Pachyderis oblongifolia

Pachyderis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pachyderis, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oblong in shape, which help it retain moisture in its arid or semi-arid habitat. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
