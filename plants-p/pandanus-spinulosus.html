Pandanus spinulosus

Pandanus spinulosus, commonly known as the spiny pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes at the top of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

The spiny pandanus thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
