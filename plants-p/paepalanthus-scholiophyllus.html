Paepalanthus scholiophyllus

Paepalanthus scholiophyllus, commonly known as the yellow puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservatories.
