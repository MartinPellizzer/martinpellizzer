Piper papillicaule

Piper papillicaule, commonly known as the hairy-papillate pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.
