Prunus kolomikta

Prunus kolomikta, commonly known as the flowering cherry or cheekwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Far East.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of white, pink, and red hues during the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are consumed by birds and small mammals. Prunus kolomikta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
