Polygala messambuziensis

Polygala messambuziensis, commonly known as the Cape flax or yellow polygala, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in the coastal regions of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
