Pygeum latifolium var. nervosum

Pygeum latifolium var. nervosum, commonly known as the veined African wild cherry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with prominent veins, which give it its varietal name. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing smooth, brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its hard timber and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
