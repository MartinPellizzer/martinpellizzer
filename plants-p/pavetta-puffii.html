Pavetta puffii

Pavetta puffii, commonly known as the African pavetta, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pavetta puffii is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
