Pombalia poaya

Pombalia poaya, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warm season. The plant has strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, providing a striking visual contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
