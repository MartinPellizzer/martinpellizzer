Prunus jamasakura f. evanescens

Prunus jamasakura f. evanescens, commonly known as the transient cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its delicate, ephemeral flowers that bloom briefly in early spring. The plant features slender branches and lance-shaped leaves that turn a vibrant green during the growing season. Its flowers, which are typically pale pink to white, are highly prized for their fleeting beauty.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and cultural significance in springtime celebrations.
