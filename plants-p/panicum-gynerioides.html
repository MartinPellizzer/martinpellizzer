Panicum gynerioides

Panicum gynerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and erect, cylindrical seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive whitish underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
