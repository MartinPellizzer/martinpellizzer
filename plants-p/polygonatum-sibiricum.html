Polygonatum sibiricum

Polygonatum sibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system and grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of white or greenish-white flowers in early summer, followed by red berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
