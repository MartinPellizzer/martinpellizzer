Platanthera nipponica var. linearifolia

Platanthera nipponica var. linearifolia, commonly known as the Japanese lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of pale green to white flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has linear, grass-like leaves and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

This orchid is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
