Poa bussmannii

Poa bussmannii, commonly known as the Bussmann's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Poa bussmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
