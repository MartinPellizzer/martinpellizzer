Piriqueta plicata

Piriqueta plicata, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are typically alternate and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.
