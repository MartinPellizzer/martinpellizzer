Polygonum olivascens

Polygonum olivascens, commonly known as the olive smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. This herbaceous annual grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils, wetlands, and along roadsides, and it is known for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses.
