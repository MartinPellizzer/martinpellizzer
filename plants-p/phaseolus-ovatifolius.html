Phaseolus ovatifolius

Phaseolus ovatifolius, commonly known as the ovate-leaf bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its ovate-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several round, edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its nutritional value, it is also cultivated for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
