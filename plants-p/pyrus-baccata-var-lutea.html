Pyrus baccata var. lutea

Pyrus baccata var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow crabapple, is a variant of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that turn orange-red when mature. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. This plant is native to Europe and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and parks.
