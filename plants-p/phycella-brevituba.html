Phycella brevituba

Phycella brevituba, commonly known as the short-tubed phycella, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its compact, tubular leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a simple morphology with a short, stout stem and small, spirally arranged leaves. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, relying mainly on vegetative propagation for spread.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
