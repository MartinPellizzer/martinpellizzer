Pseudocalymma sagotii var. macrocalyx

Pseudocalymma sagotii var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered rock daisy, is a perennial herb native to Western Australia.

It features basal rosettes of linear, blue-green leaves and produces showy, white to pale blue flowers with prominent yellow centers. The flowers are typically borne on upright stems and are adapted to thrive in arid or rocky environments. This variety is distinguished by its larger floral bracts compared to other varieties of Pseudocalymma.

It is a hardy plant that requires minimal water and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
