Piptospatha insignis

Piptospatha insignis, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It is native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can weaken its host trees over time if left unchecked.
