Piper subcrassifolium

Piper subcrassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
