Polianthes quilae

Polianthes quilae, commonly known as the Quechua lily, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces tall, upright stems with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in high-altitude grasslands. Its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are evergreen throughout the growing season.

Polianthes quilae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also culturally significant to the indigenous communities of the Andes.
