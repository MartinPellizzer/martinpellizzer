Pseudalcantarea macropetala

Pseudalcantarea macropetala, commonly known as the large-petaled pseudalcantarea, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy inflorescences that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base.

Pseudalcantarea macropetala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
