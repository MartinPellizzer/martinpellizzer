Persea americana var. tolimanensis

Persea americana var. tolimanensis, commonly known as the Tolimanensis avocado, is a botanical variant of the common avocado.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a smooth, dark green to black skin. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and consistent moisture. Its leaves are typically large, alternate, and ovate with a glossy surface.

This variety is valued for its high oil content and is often used in both culinary and commercial applications.
