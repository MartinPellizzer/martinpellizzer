Pyrus amygdaliformis crassipes

Pyrus amygdaliformis crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a pear-shaped apple, typically green when mature, and is edible. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
