Paphiopedilum violascens var. saskianum

Paphiopedilum violascens var. saskianum, commonly known as the Saskatoon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizomatous stem and produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires well-drained, acidic soil. It has lance-shaped leaves and blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
