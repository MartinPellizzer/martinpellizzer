Pachycarpus suaveolens

Pachycarpus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is known for its fragrant flowers that attract pollinators.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
