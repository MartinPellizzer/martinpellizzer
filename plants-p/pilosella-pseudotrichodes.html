Pilosella pseudotrichodes

Pilosella pseudotrichodes, commonly known as the slender pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow composite flowers and hairy, linear leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant produces small, elongated seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and botanical gardens.
