Pelargonium × drakeae

Pelargonium × drakeae, commonly known as the Madagascar dragon tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble dragon scales, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative shrub in tropical and subtropical regions.
