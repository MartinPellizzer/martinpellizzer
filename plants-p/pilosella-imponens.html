Pilosella imponens

Pilosella imponens, commonly known as the common mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Pilosella imponens is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
