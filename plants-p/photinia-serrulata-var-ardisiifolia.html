Photinia serrulata var. ardisiifolia

Photinia serrulata var. ardisiifolia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green foliage that turns vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, white flowers in late spring, followed by red or orange berries that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges, borders, or container gardening.
