Piper rioense

Piper rioense, commonly known as the Rio Grande pepper or Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
