Prunus sativa proles virescens

Prunus sativa proles virescens, commonly known as the green-fruited almond tree, is a lesser-known variant of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive greenish fruit that ripens to a pale yellow or orange hue. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for both ornamental purposes and its edible fruit, which contains a small seed inside a hard shell.
