Polygala durandii

Polygala durandii, commonly known as the Durand's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive hooded structure that resembles a milkweed flower.

Polygala durandii is often found in scrublands and woodlands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
