Pyrenaria viridifolia

Pyrenaria viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leafed pyrenaria, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often speckled with small, white spots. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, Pyrenaria viridifolia is considered endangered and is rarely found in cultivation.
