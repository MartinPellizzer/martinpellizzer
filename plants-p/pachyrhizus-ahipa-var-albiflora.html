Pachyrhizus ahipa var. albiflora

Pachyrhizus ahipa var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered peanut vine, is a climbing legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are similar to peanuts but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and nutritional potential, it is occasionally cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
