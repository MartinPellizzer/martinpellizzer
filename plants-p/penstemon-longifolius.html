Penstemon longifolius

Penstemon longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
