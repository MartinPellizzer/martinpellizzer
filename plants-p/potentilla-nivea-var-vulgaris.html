Potentilla nivea var. vulgaris

Potentilla nivea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
