Polystachya miranda

Polystachya miranda, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and tall, branched inflorescences that bear numerous small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom during the rainy season.

Polystachya miranda is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
