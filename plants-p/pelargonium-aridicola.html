Pelargonium aridicola

Pelargonium aridicola, commonly known as the desert geranium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent-like leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping in arid climates.
