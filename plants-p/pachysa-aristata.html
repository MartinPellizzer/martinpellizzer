Pachysa aristata

Pachysa aristata, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates.

Pachysa aristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
