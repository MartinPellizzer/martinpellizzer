Packera paupercula var. appalachiana

Packera paupercula var. appalachiana, commonly known as Appalachian brittleblow, is a perennial herb native to the Appalachian region of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
