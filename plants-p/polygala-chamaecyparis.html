Polygala chamaecyparis

Polygala chamaecyparis, commonly known as the creeping milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features slender, woody stems that are often covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, producing a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
