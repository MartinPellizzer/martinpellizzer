Potentilla hirta var. diversifolia

Potentilla hirta var. diversifolia, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular and covered in fine, short hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated edge, with some variation in shape among different individuals.

This variety is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
