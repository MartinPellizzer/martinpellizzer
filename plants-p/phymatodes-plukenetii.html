Phymatodes plukenetii

Phymatodes plukenetii, commonly known as the honey mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in limestone soils.

Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
