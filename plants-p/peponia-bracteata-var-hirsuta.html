Peponia bracteata var. hirsuta

Peponia bracteata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bitter gourd, is a variant of the bitter gourd species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, ridged, and elongated fruits that typically range in color from pale green to yellowish when mature. The plant is a climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both showy and fragrant. Its fruits are used in various culinary preparations, though they are known for their bitter taste.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like cucumbers and squash.
