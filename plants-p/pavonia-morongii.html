Pavonia morongii

Pavonia morongii, commonly known as the Morong's false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pavonia morongii is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic qualities.
