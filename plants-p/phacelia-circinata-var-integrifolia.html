Phacelia circinata var. integrifolia

Phacelia circinata var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
