Pinus canaliculata

Pinus canaliculata, commonly known as the Mexican red pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are often reddish-brown in color, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Pinus canaliculata thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
