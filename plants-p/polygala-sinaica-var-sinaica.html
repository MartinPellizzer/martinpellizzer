Polygala sinaica var. sinaica

Polygala sinaica var. sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai polygala, is a flowering plant native to the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with distinctive spurred petals that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in the region.
