Phalaenopsis deliciosa subsp. hookeriana

Phalaenopsis deliciosa subsp.

hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's phalaenopsis, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is prized for its ornamental value and is popular in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
