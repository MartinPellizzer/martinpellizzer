Pleurothallis aurita

Pleurothallis aurita, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pleurothallis aurita is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
