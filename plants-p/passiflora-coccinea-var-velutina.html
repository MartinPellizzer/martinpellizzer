Passiflora coccinea var. velutina

Passiflora coccinea var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and vibrant red bracts that resemble petals, giving it a dramatic floral appearance. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with a unique structure that includes a crown of filaments and a central calyx. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which is edible, develops into a bright orange or red berry containing numerous seeds.
