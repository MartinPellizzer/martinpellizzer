Prostanthera rugosa

Prostanthera rugosa, commonly known as the rough leaved mint bush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and plants. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and spikes of fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments.

Prostanthera rugosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
