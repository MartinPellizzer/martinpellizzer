Pittosporum pimeleoides subsp. major

Pittosporum pimeleoides subsp.

major, commonly known as the large-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
