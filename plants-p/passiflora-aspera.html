Passiflora aspera

Passiflora aspera, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are rough to the touch, giving it its common name, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora aspera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
