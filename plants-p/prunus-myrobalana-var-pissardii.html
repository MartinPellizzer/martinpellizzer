Prunus myrobalana var. pissardii

Prunus myrobalana var. pissardii, commonly known as the Persian cherry or Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to peaches but are typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
