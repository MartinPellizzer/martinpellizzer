Placus suaveolens

Placus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Its unique aroma makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and perfumery.
