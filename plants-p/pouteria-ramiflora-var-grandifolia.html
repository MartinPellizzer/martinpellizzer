Pouteria ramiflora var. grandifolia

Pouteria ramiflora var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered soursop, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a large, spiny fruit similar to the soursop, which contains a white, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
