Pistacia cucphuongensis

Pistacia cucphuongensis, commonly known as Cuc Phuong pistachio, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the Cuc Phuong National Park in Vietnam, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into drupes. Its botanical features include a woody structure and the ability to tolerate dry conditions, making it an important component of the local flora.
