Phaseolus stenocarpus

Phaseolus stenocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled bean, is a species of legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped pods and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds, which are typically white or yellow, are used in traditional cuisines and are valued for their nutritional content.
