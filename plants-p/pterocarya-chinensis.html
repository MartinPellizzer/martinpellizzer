Pterocarya chinensis

Pterocarya chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese wingnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive winged samaras, which are its dry, winged fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in a fleshy outer layer.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
