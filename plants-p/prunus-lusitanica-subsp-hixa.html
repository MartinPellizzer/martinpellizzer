Prunus lusitanica subsp. hixa

Prunus lusitanica subsp.

hixa, commonly known as the Hix pine or Hix cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are typically leathery in texture.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet berries that attract various wildlife. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
