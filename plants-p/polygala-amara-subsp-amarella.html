Polygala amara subsp. amarella

Polygala amara subsp.

amarella, commonly known as the sweet root or sweet vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish in color.
