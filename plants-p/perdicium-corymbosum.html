Perdicium corymbosum

Perdicium corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Perdicium corymbosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
