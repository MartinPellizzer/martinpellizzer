Peperomia semiruensis

Peperomia semiruensis, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture, often adorned with striking red or purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale green. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia semiruensis is relatively low maintenance and suitable for beginners due to its tolerance for dry conditions.
