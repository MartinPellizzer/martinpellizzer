Pyrus punjabica

Pyrus punjabica, commonly known as the Punjab pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer, known for their sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
