Palmerella debilis

Palmerella debilis, commonly known as the weak palmerella, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clustered, pinkish to red flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers. It typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Palmerella debilis plays a minor but important role in the ecological balance of its native desert ecosystems.
