Podostemum metzgerioides

Podostemum metzgerioides, commonly known as the African water primrose, is a semi-aquatic flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It thrives in fast-flowing streams and rivers, with its roots anchored to rocks and its leaves submerged in water. The plant has small, bright yellow flowers that bloom near the water's surface, attracting pollinators like bees and insects. Its unique morphology includes specialized leaves that help it float and absorb nutrients from the water.

Podostemum metzgerioides plays an important ecological role in its habitat by stabilizing stream banks and providing food for aquatic life.
