Prunus mahaleb var. pendula

Prunus mahaleb var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a cultivar of the Prunus mahaleb species.

It is characterized by its graceful, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, making it a popular ornamental tree. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible cherries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal pruning to maintain its natural form.

This variety is often used in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
