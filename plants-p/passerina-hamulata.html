Passerina hamulata

Passerina hamulata, commonly known as the hooked pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three deeply lobed leaflets, and its flowers are usually white or pale yellow.

Passerina hamulata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
