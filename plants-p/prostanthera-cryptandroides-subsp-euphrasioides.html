Prostanthera cryptandroides subsp. euphrasioides

Prostanthera cryptandroides subsp.

euphrasioides, commonly known as the silver-leafed mintbush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically silver or grey-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
