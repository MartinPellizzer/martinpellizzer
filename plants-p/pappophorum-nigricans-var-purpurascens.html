Pappophorum nigricans var. purpurascens

Pappophorum nigricans var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular, purple-tinged stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist environments.

Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
