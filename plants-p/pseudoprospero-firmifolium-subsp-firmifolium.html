Pseudoprospero firmifolium subsp. firmifolium

Pseudoprospero firmifolium subsp.

firmifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf pseudoprospero, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is generally low and mat-forming, with stems that can branch out to form dense clusters.

This subspecies is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
