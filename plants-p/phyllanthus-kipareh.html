Phyllanthus kipareh

Phyllanthus kipareh, commonly known as the Kipareh plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
