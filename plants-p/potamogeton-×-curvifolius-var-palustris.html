Potamogeton × curvifolius var. palustris

Potamogeton × curvifolius var. palustris, commonly known as the swamp pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often curved or twisted.

It is typically found in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often emerging from the water surface. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a smooth or slightly rough texture.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
