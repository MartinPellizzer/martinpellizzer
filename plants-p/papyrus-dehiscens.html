Papyrus dehiscens

Papyrus dehiscens, commonly known as the African papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and large, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in waterlogged soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Papyrus dehiscens has been historically used for paper-making and as a source of thatching material.
