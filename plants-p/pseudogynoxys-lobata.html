Pseudogynoxys lobata

Pseudogynoxys lobata, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces bright orange to yellow ray flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Pseudogynoxys lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
