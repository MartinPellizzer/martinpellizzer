Psilonema homalocarpum

Psilonema homalocarpum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually green with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
