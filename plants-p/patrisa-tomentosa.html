Patrisa tomentosa

Patrisa tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly patrisa, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
