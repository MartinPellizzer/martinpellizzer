Penstemon deustus var. variabilis

Penstemon deustus var. variabilis, commonly known as the variable beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and showy, tubular flowers. The species is known for its variability in flower color and plant form, which contributes to its common name. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
