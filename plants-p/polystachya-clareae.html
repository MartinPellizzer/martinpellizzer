Polystachya clareae

Polystachya clareae, commonly known as the Clarea Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure, which includes specialized parts for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
