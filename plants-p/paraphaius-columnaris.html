Paraphaius columnaris

Paraphaius columnaris, commonly known as the columnar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The stems are succulent, storing water to help the plant survive in drought conditions.
