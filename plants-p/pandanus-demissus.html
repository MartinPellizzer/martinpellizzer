Pandanus demissus

Pandanus demissus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its roots and leaves have been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Pandanus demissus thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline and humid environments.
