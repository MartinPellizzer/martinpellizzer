Potentilla rhenana

Potentilla rhenana, commonly known as the Rhenish cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes.

Potentilla rhenana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
