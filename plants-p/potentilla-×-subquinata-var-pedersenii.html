Potentilla × subquinata var. pedersenii

Potentilla × subquinata var. pedersenii, commonly known as the Pedersen's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
