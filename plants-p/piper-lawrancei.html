Piper lawrancei

Piper lawrancei, commonly known as Lawrence's pepper, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
