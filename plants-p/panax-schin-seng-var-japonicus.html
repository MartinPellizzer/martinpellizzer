Panax schin-seng var. japonicus

Panax schin-seng var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive fleshy, cylindrical root system. The plant typically grows in forested areas with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese and Japanese medicine.
