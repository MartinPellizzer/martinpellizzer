Piptadenia communis

Piptadenia communis, commonly known as the Brazilian peach tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species in reforestation efforts.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
