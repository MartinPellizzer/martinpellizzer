Pulsatilla bohemica

Pulsatilla bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately compound, with five to seven leaflets, and the plant produces a distinctive, hairy seed head after flowering.

Pulsatilla bohemica is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
