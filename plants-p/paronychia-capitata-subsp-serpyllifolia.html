Paronychia capitata subsp. serpyllifolia

Paronychia capitata subsp.

serpyllifolia, commonly known as creeping wintergreen, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa. It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
