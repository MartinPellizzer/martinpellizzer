Pleurothallis brachyantha

Pleurothallis brachyantha, commonly known as the short-leafed spider orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces intricate, spider-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It is known for its compact size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild and is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
