Pierardia courtallensis

Pierardia courtallensis, commonly known as the Ceylon pear, is a deciduous tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, sweet, and juicy fruits that are similar in appearance to pears. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued not only for its fruit but also for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
