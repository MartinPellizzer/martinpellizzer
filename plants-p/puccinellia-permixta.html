Puccinellia permixta

Puccinellia permixta, commonly known as the mixed saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of coastal and desert ecosystems.

Puccinellia permixta is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.
