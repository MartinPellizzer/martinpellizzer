Premna corymbosa var. obtusifolia

Premna corymbosa var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
