Primula vaginata subsp. normaniana

Primula vaginata subsp.

normaniana, commonly known as the Norman's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
