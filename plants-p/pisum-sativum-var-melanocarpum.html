Pisum sativum var. melanocarpum

Pisum sativum var. melanocarpum, commonly known as black-eyed pea, is a variety of the pea plant characterized by its distinctive dark-colored seed coat.

The plant is an annual legume that produces pods containing oval, flat seeds with a prominent black spot near the hilum. It thrives in temperate climates and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber. The botanical features include compound leaves, small yellow flowers, and a robust root system that supports nitrogen fixation.

This variety is widely grown for both human consumption and animal feed.
