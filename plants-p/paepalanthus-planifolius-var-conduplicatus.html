Paepalanthus planifolius var. conduplicatus

Paepalanthus planifolius var. conduplicatus, commonly known as the clustered puya, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that collect water in their central cavity. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
