Parnassia nudata

Parnassia nudata, commonly known as the naked lady's tresses, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Parnassiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually arranged in a dense, mat-like structure, and the flowers are often clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.

Parnassia nudata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
