Passiflora citrina

Passiflora citrina, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers with intricate, veined petals and a crown-like structure at the base. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while its stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs.

Passiflora citrina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
