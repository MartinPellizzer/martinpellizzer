Papaver pyrenaicum var. rubicundum

Papaver pyrenaicum var. rubicundum, commonly known as the red poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family native to the Pyrenees region.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, orb-shaped seed capsule. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bold, colorful display.
