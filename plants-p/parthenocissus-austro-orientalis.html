Parthenocissus austro-orientalis

Parthenocissus austro-orientalis, commonly known as the oriental ivy grape, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible berries that are typically not fully developed. It thrives in temperate climates and requires support structures for its climbing growth habit.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.
