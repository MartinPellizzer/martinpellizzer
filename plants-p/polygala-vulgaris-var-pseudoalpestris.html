Polygala vulgaris var. pseudoalpestris

Polygala vulgaris var. pseudoalpestris, commonly known as the alpine milk-vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spiral clusters, which are characteristic of the Polygala genus. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, bean-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variety is often found in meadows and open slopes at high elevations.
