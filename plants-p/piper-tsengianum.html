Piper tsengianum

Piper tsengianum, commonly known as the Tseng's pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these berries contain the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The leaves are also used in traditional herbal remedies due to their various bioactive properties.
