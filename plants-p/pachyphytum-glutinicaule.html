Pachyphytum glutinicaule

Pachyphytum glutinicaule, commonly known as the Sticky Leaf Pachyphytum, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. Its leaves are often coated with a sticky substance, which helps deter herbivores.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
