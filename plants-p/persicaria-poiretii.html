Persicaria poiretii

Persicaria poiretii, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Persicaria poiretii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
