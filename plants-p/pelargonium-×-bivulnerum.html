Pelargonium × bivulnerum

Pelargonium × bivulnerum, commonly known as the double-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring two leaves per stem, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy flowers in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, typically in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Pelargonium × bivulnerum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
