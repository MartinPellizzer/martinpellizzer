Picris pitardiana

Picris pitardiana, commonly known as the false goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, often aromatic scent. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
