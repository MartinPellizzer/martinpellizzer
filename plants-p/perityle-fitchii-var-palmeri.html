Perityle fitchii var. palmeri

Perityle fitchii var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's perityle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and yellow, often appearing in clusters at the ends of the stems.
