Potentilla wimanniana

Potentilla wimanniana, commonly known as Wiman's cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla wimanniana is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
