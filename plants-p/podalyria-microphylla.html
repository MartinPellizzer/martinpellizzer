Podalyria microphylla

Podalyria microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved podalyria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its small, pod-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Podalyria microphylla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
