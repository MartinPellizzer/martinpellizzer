Pseudosopubia obtusifolia

Pseudosopubia obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved pseudosopubia, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of thick, fleshy, and bluntly shaped leaves that help store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent leaves and drought-resistant nature make it well adapted to harsh, semi-desert environments.

Pseudosopubia obtusifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
