Potamogeton pusillus subsp. trichoides

Potamogeton pusillus subsp.

trichoides, commonly known as the hairy water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats. It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

This subspecies is adapted to slow-moving or still waters and is often found in ponds and lakes.
