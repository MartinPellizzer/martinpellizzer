Physocarpus pauciflorus

Physocarpus pauciflorus, commonly known as the Chinese bush honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
