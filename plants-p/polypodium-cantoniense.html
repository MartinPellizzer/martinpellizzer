Polypodium cantoniense

Polypodium cantoniense, commonly known as the Guangdong huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is native to southeastern China, particularly Guangdong province, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often dimorphic, with fertile and sterile fronds. Its sporangia are clustered in strobila, and it produces spores that are dispersed by wind.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
