Piper pachoanum

Piper pachoanum, commonly known as the Pachoanum pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, elongated fruits resembling peppercorns. The plant's leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, spicy aroma. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on insects for pollination.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
