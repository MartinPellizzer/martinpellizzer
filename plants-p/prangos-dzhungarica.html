Prangos dzhungarica

Prangos dzhungarica, commonly known as the Central Asian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the dry, mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust root system and upright, hairy stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the arid environment.

Prangos dzhungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
