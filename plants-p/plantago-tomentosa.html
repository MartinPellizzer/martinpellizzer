Plantago tomentosa

Plantago tomentosa, commonly known as woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by oval, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in meadows, and in wetlands.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
