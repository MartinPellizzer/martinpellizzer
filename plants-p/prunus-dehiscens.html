Prunus dehiscens

Prunus dehiscens, commonly known as the spreading cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. Prunus dehiscens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
