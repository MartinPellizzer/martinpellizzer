Phytocrene macrophylla var. caudigera

Phytocrene macrophylla var. caudigera, commonly known as the long-tailed phytocrene, is a striking aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in marshes and ponds.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
