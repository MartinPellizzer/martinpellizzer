Phyllocactus grandis

Phyllocactus grandis, commonly known as the giant leaf cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Despite its name, it is not a true cactus but is often cultivated as one due to its similar appearance and care requirements.
