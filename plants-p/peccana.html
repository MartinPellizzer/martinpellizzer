Peccana

Peccana, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and elusive plant species native to the dense rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that shimmer in the dappled light of the forest canopy. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night, aiding in pollination by nocturnal insects. Its root system is highly specialized, capable of absorbing nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil through symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to habitat destruction, peccana is now classified as an endangered species, making it a subject of conservation interest.
