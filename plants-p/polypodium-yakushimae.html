Polypodium yakushimae

Polypodium yakushimae, commonly known as the Yakushima water fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the subtropical forests of Yakushima Island, Japan.

It is a type of polypody, belonging to the family Polypodiaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, evergreen fronds that are often covered in a fine, silky layer of trichomes. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on rock surfaces or in the canopy of ancient forests. Its unique morphology and restricted habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Polypodium yakushimae is considered a vulnerable species.
