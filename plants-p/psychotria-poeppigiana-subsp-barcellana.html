Psychotria poeppigiana subsp. barcellana

Psychotria poeppigiana subsp.

barcellana, commonly known as the "Coca plant" or "Yareca," is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments at moderate elevations.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, berry-like fruits that contain the psychoactive alkaloid cocaine.
