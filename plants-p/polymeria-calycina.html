Polymeria calycina

Polymeria calycina, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Polymeria calycina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
