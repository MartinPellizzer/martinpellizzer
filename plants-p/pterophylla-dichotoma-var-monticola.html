Pterophylla dichotoma var. monticola

Pterophylla dichotoma var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain pterophylla, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps the plant conserve moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
