Pouteria suavis

Pouteria suavis, commonly known as the sweet sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by oval, orange to red fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp. The fruit is highly valued for its edible, nutty flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Pouteria suavis is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in traditional medicine.
