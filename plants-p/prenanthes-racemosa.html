Prenanthes racemosa

Prenanthes racemosa, commonly known as the clustered arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense, raceme-like clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
