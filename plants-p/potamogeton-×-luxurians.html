Potamogeton × luxurians

Potamogeton × luxurians, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelongus, exhibiting characteristics of both parents. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

However, its invasive potential can lead to dense growth that affects water quality and native plant communities.
