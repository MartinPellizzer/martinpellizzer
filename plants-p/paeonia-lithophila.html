Paeonia lithophila

Paeonia lithophila, commonly known as the rock peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces distinctive, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Paeonia lithophila is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
