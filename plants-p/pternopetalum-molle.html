Pternopetalum molle

Pternopetalum molle, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Indian mulberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
