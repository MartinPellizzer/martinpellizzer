Penstemon jaffrayanus

Penstemon jaffrayanus, commonly known as Jaffray's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its upright stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. Its flowers, which range in color from purple to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon jaffrayanus is valued in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
