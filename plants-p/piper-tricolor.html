Piper tricolor

Piper tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored pepper or the rainbow pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its strikingly colorful fruits, which display three distinct colors: green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The plant's unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both gardens and botanical collections.
