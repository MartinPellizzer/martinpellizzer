Passiflora foetida var. arizonica

Passiflora foetida var. arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky slopes or in dry woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
