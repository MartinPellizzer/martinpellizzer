Panicum alectromerum

Panicum alectromerum, commonly known as the silver panicum, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly in the southeastern region.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
