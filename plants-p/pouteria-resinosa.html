Pouteria resinosa

Pouteria resinosa, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that contains up to 20 edible seeds, commonly referred to as Brazil nuts. The plant is valued for its unique seed oil, which is rich in essential nutrients.

Pouteria resinosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting a variety of wildlife species.
