Phlox hoodii subsp. glabrata

Phlox hoodii subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, smooth texture, giving it its subspecies name "glabrata.".

It is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
