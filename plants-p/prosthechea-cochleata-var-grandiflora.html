Prosthechea cochleata var. grandiflora

Prosthechea cochleata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant ear orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, spirally arranged pseudobulbs and broad, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in a spiral at the tips of the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
