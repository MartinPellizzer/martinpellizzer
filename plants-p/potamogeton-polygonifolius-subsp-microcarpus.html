Potamogeton polygonifolius subsp. microcarpus

Potamogeton polygonifolius subsp.

microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America. It has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its roots help stabilize sediments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

It is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
