Plinia panamensis

Plinia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Plinia panamensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and potential medicinal uses.
