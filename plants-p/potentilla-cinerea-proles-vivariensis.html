Potentilla cinerea proles vivariensis

Potentilla cinerea proles vivariensis, commonly known as the gray cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine or rocky habitats. It is a perennial shrub with a compact growth habit, and its leaves are typically divided into five lobes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
