Pleurothallis magdalenae

Pleurothallis magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered inflorescences and slender stems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
