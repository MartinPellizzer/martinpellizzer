Pachypodium menabeum

Pachypodium menabeum, commonly known as the Madagascar moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a short, swollen stem and clusters of small, spiny leaves. It produces yellow or orange flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
