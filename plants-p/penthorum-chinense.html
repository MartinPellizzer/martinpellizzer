Penthorum chinense

Penthorum chinense, commonly known as the Chinese water clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features distinctive trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, often floating on the surface of shallow water. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its unique appearance and aquatic habitat make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.
