Pittosporum microcarpum

Pittosporum microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum microcarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
