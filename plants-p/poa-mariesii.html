Poa mariesii

Poa mariesii, commonly known as the Maries sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds.

Poa mariesii is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland ecosystems.
