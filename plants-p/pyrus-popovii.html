Pyrus popovii

Pyrus popovii, commonly known as the Popov's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a pear-shaped berry that ripens to a yellowish-green color, providing a food source for various wildlife species.
