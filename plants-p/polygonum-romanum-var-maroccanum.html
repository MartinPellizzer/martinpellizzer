Polygonum romanum var. maroccanum

Polygonum romanum var. maroccanum, commonly known as Moroccan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability and role in local ecosystems.
