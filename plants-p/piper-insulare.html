Piper insulare

Piper insulare, commonly known as the island pepper or small pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
