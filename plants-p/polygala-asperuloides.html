Polygala asperuloides

Polygala asperuloides, commonly known as the roughleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive structure that aids in pollination by insects.
