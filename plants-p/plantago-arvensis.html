Plantago arvensis

Plantago arvensis, commonly known as common plantain, is a flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has broad, oval leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in a spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing qualities.
