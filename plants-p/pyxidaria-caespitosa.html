Pyxidaria caespitosa

Pyxidaria caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences at the tips of its stems. Pyxidaria caespitosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
