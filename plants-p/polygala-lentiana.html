Polygala lentiana

Polygala lentiana, commonly known as the milkweed or the sweet milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive hooded structure over the reproductive parts. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Polygala lentiana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
