Pomatostoma sertuliferum

Pomatostoma sertuliferum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a marine red alga that thrives in shallow coastal waters.

It is characterized by its branching structure and the presence of calcareous nodules, which provide structural support. The plant's thallus is typically bright red or purple, giving it a striking appearance in its natural habitat. It plays a vital role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Pomatostoma sertuliferum is often found in rocky substrates and is an important component of intertidal zones.
