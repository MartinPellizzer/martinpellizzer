Phleum asperum var. ciliatum

Phleum asperum var. ciliatum, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stem.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
