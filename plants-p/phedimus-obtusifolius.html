Phedimus obtusifolius

Phedimus obtusifolius, commonly known as the stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant is often found in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. It is a member of the Crassulaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves.

Phedimus obtusifolius is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
