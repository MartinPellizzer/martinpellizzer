Podalyria lanceolata

Podalyria lanceolata, commonly known as the slender podalyria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple, followed by narrow, bean-like pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky habitats.

Podalyria lanceolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
