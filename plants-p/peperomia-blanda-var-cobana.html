Peperomia blanda var. cobana

Peperomia blanda var. cobana, commonly known as the Cobana peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central and South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are often adorned with intricate patterns of light green veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the leaf axils, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Due to its slow growth and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
