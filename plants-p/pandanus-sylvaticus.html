Pandanus sylvaticus

Pandanus sylvaticus, commonly known as the wild pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots and leaves are utilized in various cultural and culinary practices across its native regions.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense stands in tropical climates.
