Polygonum ramosospicatum

Polygonum ramosospicatum, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to reddish in color. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities in some garden settings.
