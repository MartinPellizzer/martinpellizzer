Psidium grandifolium var. ternatifolium

Psidium grandifolium var. ternatifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf guava, is a variant of the common guava characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves.

This plant is native to the tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically three-lobed, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly serrated. The flowers are small, fragrant, and white, often appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a small, edible berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
