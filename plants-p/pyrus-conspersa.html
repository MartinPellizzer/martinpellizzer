Pyrus conspersa

Pyrus conspersa, commonly known as the rough pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. Pyrus conspersa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of climates.
