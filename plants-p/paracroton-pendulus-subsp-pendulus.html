Paracroton pendulus subsp. pendulus

Paracroton pendulus subsp.

pendulus, commonly known as the hanging paracroton, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that trail or climb along supporting structures.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
