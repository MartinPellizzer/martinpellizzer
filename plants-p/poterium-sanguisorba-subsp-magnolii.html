Poterium sanguisorba subsp. magnolii

Poterium sanguisorba subsp.

magnolii, commonly known as the Japanese bloodroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia. It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are reddish-brown underneath.

The plant produces striking red-orange flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
