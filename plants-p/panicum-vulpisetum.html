Panicum vulpisetum

Panicum vulpisetum, commonly known as foxsetail grass, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tufted inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail, composed of dense, bristly spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a subject of ecological study.
