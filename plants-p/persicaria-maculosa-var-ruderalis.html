Persicaria maculosa var. ruderalis

Persicaria maculosa var. ruderalis, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Eurasia.

It features upright, branching stems that are often tinged with red, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish or purple coloration on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open habitats.
