Poa nemoralis var. albocaulis

Poa nemoralis var. albocaulis, commonly known as the white-stemmed bentgrass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale-colored stems, which contrast with its dark green leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and grassy slopes. It has a tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
