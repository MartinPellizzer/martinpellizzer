Polygonatum boreale var. multflorum

Polygonatum boreale var. multflorum, commonly known as the northern Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features long, arching stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in shaded, moist environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
