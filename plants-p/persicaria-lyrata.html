Persicaria lyrata

Persicaria lyrata, commonly known as the musical plant or lyrate knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive lyrate arrangement, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
