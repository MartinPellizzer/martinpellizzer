Phaseolus appendiculatus f. pubescens

Phaseolus appendiculatus f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy runner bean, is a cultivar of the Phaseolus genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces long, slender pods that are typically green and edible when harvested young. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible beans.
