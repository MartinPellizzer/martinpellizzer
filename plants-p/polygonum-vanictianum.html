Polygonum vanictianum

Polygonum vanictianum, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species such as buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a distinctive reddish or purple underside.

Polygonum vanictianum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
