Pilea mooreana

Pilea mooreana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically followed by a spadix, a unique floral structure typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea mooreana is relatively low maintenance and is appreciated for its ornamental foliage.
