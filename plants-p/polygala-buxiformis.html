Polygala buxiformis

Polygala buxiformis, commonly known as the boxleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant features dense, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood, hence its common name. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in dense spikes, and bloom in spring.

Polygala buxiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
