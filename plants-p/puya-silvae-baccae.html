Puya silvae-baccae

Puya silvae-baccae, commonly known as the forest seed plant, is a member of the bromeliad family.

It is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant produces striking, cone-like inflorescences that contain numerous small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often covered in a waxy coating.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.
