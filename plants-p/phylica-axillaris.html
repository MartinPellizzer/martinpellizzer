Phylica axillaris

Phylica axillaris, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small, evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Phylica axillaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
