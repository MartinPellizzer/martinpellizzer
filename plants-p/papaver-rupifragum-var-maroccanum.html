Papaver rupifragum var. maroccanum

Papaver rupifragum var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan rock poppy, is a hardy perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Morocco.

It is characterized by its striking, deep red to orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil.
