Pityrogramma pearcei

Pityrogramma pearcei, commonly known as the pearce's water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its rhizomes are often covered in fine, hair-like structures that aid in nutrient absorption.

The fronds are bipinnate, with small, oval-shaped pinnae that contribute to its overall ornamental appearance.
