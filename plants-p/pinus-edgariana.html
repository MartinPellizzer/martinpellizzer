Pinus edgariana

Pinus edgariana, commonly known as the Afghan pine, is a tall coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structure. The tree thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, dry climates. Its wood is strong and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

The species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
