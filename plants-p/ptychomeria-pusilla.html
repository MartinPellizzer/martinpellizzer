Ptychomeria pusilla

Ptychomeria pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf ptychomeria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ptychomeria pusilla is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
