Phaseolus coccineus var. rigidicaulis

Phaseolus coccineus var. rigidicaulis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and edible, kidney-shaped pods that develop after flowering. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with hairy stems and compound leaves. Its botanical features include tendrils that help it climb and support structures, and it thrives in warm, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and culinary use.
