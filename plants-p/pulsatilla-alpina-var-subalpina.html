Pulsatilla alpina var. subalpina

Pulsatilla alpina var. subalpina, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a short, upright stem that bears several large, showy yellow flowers with prominent, showy sepals. The plant has palmately divided, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and alpine tundra.

The species is notable for its ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions typical of mountainous regions.
