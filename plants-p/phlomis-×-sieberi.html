Phlomis × sieberi

Phlomis × sieberi, commonly known as the hybrid hybrid mint or Sieber's phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, minty fragrance. Its showy, bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, forming dense spikes at the top of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual impact.
