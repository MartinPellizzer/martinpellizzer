Phlomis nutans

Phlomis nutans, commonly known as the nodding phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy texture.

Phlomis nutans is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor conditions.
