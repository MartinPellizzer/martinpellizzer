Phylica parviflora

Phylica parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered phylica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense heads, which are often visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Phylica parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
