Pachyneurum grandiflorum

Pachyneurum grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, displaying vibrant red and orange hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pachyneurum grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
