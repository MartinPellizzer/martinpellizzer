Phymatosorus nigrescens

Phymatosorus nigrescens, commonly known as the black mountain fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark, leathery fronds that are typically dense and clustered at the base. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or wooded areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
