Phryma leptostachya

Phryma leptostachya, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in wetland habitats and a valuable species for ecological studies.
