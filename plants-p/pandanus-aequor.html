Pandanus aequor

Pandanus aequor, commonly known as the sea pandanus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific and Indian Oceans.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that eventually develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its root system is adapted to tolerate saltwater and sandy soils, making it well-suited for maritime environments.

The leaves and fruits of Pandanus aequor have cultural and economic significance in many island communities.
