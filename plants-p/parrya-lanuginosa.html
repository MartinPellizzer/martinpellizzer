Parrya lanuginosa

Parrya lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly parrya, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Parrya lanuginosa is a perennial shrub that is often found in desert regions, adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
