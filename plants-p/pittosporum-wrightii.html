Pittosporum wrightii

Pittosporum wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath, with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of white, waxy flowers in spring, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum wrightii is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
