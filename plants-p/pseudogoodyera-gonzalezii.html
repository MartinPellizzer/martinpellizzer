Pseudogoodyera gonzalezii

Pseudogoodyera gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's pseudogoodyera, is a rare and lesser-known member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are typically pale green or white, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip, a feature common in many orchids.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native ecosystems.
