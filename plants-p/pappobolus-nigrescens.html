Pappobolus nigrescens

Pappobolus nigrescens, commonly known as the blackseed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black seeds that are covered in fine, hairy structures called pappus, which aid in wind dispersal. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
