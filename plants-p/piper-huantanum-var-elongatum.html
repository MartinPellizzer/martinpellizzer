Piper huantanum var. elongatum

Piper huantanum var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper huantanum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant morphology and ethnobotany.
