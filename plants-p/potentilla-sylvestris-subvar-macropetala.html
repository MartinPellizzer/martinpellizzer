Potentilla sylvestris subvar. macropetala

Potentilla sylvestris subvar. macropetala, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright, slender stems that bear clusters of showy, bright yellow flowers with five petals. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven oval leaflets, which are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
