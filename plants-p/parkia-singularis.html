Parkia singularis

Parkia singularis, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flattened, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Parkia singularis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
