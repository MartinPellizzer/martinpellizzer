Pleioblastus nakashimae var. supraglaber

Pleioblastus nakashimae var. supraglaber, commonly known as the glossy bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking, dark green, glossy leaves that are slightly broader than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in late spring, followed by narrow, pale green bracts. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental bamboo is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
