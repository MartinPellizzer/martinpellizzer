Poa irrigata f. ehrhartii

Poa irrigata f. ehrhartii, commonly known as the Ehrhartii form of the irrigated grass, is a variant of the species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
