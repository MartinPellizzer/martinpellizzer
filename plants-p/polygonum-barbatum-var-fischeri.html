Polygonum barbatum var. fischeri

Polygonum barbatum var. fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
