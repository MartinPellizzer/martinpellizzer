Persea punctata var. matthewsii

Persea punctata var. matthewsii, commonly known as the Matthews' persimmon, is a rare and unique variant of the persimmon tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into orange to red fruits with a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
