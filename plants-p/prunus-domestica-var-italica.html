Prunus domestica var. italica

Prunus domestica var. italica, commonly known as the Italian prune.

It is a variety of the European plum, characterized by its small, dark purple fruits that are rich in sugars and used for making jams and dried fruits. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves.
