Poecilopteris costata var. undulata

Poecilopteris costata var. undulata, commonly known as the undulating cliff fern, is a fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating fronds that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a serrated margin and a leathery texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
