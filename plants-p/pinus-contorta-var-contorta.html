Pinus contorta var. contorta

Pinus contorta var. contorta, commonly known as lodgepole pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against wildfires.

This species is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
