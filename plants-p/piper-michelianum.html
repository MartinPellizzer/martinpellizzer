Piper michelianum

Piper michelianum, commonly known as Micheli's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, reddish-brown berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
