Potamogeton crispus var. acutifolius

Potamogeton crispus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically sharp at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used as a bioindicator in wetland restoration efforts.
