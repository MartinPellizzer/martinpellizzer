Potamogeton occidentalis

Potamogeton occidentalis, commonly known as western pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
