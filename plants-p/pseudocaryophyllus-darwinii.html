Pseudocaryophyllus darwinii

Pseudocaryophyllus darwinii, commonly known as Darwin's false caryophyll, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals arranged symmetrically. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Pseudocaryophyllus darwinii is notable for its resilience and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
