Populus alaschanica

Populus alaschanica, commonly known as the Tien Shan poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in cold, mountainous regions and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Populus alaschanica plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
