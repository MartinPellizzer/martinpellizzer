Primula hissarica

Primula hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, soft down.

Primula hissarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
