Protea cordata

Protea cordata, commonly known as the flat protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family, which is known for its showy, often brightly colored inflorescences. The plant features a dense, spherical head of small, white to pinkish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Protea cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.
