Persea bernardii

Persea bernardii, commonly known as the African custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a distinctive custard-like texture and a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in warm climates for both its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
