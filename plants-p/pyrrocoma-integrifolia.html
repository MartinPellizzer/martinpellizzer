Pyrrocoma integrifolia

Pyrrocoma integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf pygmyweed, is a small, succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically smooth and entire-margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in desert environments.

Pyrrocoma integrifolia is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
