Piptocarpha chontalensis

Piptocarpha chontalensis, commonly known as the Chontal grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Piptocarpha chontalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
