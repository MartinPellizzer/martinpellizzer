Paspalum trichophyllum

Paspalum trichophyllum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, often hairless stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or red.

Paspalum trichophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
