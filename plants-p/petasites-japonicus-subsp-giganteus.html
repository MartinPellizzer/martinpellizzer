Petasites japonicus subsp. giganteus

Petasites japonicus subsp.

giganteus, commonly known as the giant Japanese buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Petasites, known for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
