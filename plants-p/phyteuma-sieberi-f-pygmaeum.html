Phyteuma sieberi f. pygmaeum

Phyteuma sieberi f. pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf phyteuma, is a compact perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
