Pyrus hemsleyi

Pyrus hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the highlands of northern Mexico and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit, which is small and yellow when ripe, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
