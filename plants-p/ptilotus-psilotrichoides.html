Ptilotus psilotrichoides

Ptilotus psilotrichoides, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its succulent stems. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
