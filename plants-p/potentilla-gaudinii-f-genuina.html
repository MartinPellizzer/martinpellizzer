Potentilla gaudinii f. genuina

Potentilla gaudinii f. genuina, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, silvery-gray leaves that are adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
