Philadelphus incanus var. mitsae

Philadelphus incanus var. mitsae, commonly known as the Japanese Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
