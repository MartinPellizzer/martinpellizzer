Piper pilispicum

Piper pilispicum, commonly known as the Pilispicum pepper or the White Pepper Tree, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, white to pale green flowers. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a mild, peppery aroma.
