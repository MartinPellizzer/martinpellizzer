Passiflora mandonii

Passiflora mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a juicy pulp and numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora mandonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
