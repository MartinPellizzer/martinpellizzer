Pocockia cachemirica

Pocockia cachemirica, commonly known as the Kashmir stonecrop, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Pocockia cachemirica is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
