Polypodium subpectinatum

Polypodium subpectinatum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant has a distinctive, creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are divided into segments with a rough, serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
