Pearsonia marginata

Pearsonia marginata, commonly known as the New Zealand sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh coastal conditions.

Pearsonia marginata is a key component of the coastal vegetation in New Zealand, providing habitat and stabilizing dunes.
