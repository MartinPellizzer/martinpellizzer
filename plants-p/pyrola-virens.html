Pyrola virens

Pyrola virens, commonly known as the green mountain beauty, is a low-growing evergreen perennial native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in early spring, which are often adorned with a distinctive red or purple calyx. Its leaves are typically oval and dark green, and it thrives in shaded, moist forest environments.

Pyrola virens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in woodland habitats.
