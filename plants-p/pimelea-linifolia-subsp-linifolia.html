Pimelea linifolia subsp. linifolia

Pimelea linifolia subsp.

linifolia, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It features narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late autumn to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
