Pinus inops

Pinus inops, commonly known as the sparse pine, is a rare and little-known species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and small, clustered cones. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to other pine species. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cold, dry climates and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pinus inops is considered a vulnerable species.
