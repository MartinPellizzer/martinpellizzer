Panax affinis

Panax affinis, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested areas and has compound leaves with opposite arrangement. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in umbels.

The botanical features of Panax affinis make it a subject of interest in both pharmacological research and horticultural cultivation.
