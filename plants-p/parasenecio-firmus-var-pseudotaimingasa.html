Parasenecio firmus var. pseudotaimingasa

Parasenecio firmus var. pseudotaimingasa, commonly known as the Shikoku parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow central disks and white to pale blue ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often found in the regions of Shikoku and Kyushu.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
