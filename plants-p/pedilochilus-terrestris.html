Pedilochilus terrestris

Pedilochilus terrestris, commonly known as the ground orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a slender, creeping rhizome with erect, linear leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often lack a distinct scent.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
