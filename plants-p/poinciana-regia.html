Poinciana regia

Poinciana regia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red or orange-pink inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. The tree has a broad, umbrella-shaped canopy with pinnate leaves that are compound and often shed seasonally. Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and birds.

The tree is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
