Primula albana

Primula albana, commonly known as the white primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Primula albana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
