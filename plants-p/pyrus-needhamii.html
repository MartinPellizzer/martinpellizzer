Pyrus needhamii

Pyrus needhamii, commonly known as the Needham pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with simple, alternate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and spring blossoms.

Pyrus needhamii is also valued in horticulture for its potential in breeding programs aimed at improving pear varieties.
