Phaseolus semierectus

Phaseolus semierectus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds used for food. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. It is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated in home gardens and commercial farms.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
