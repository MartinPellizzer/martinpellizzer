Pteranthus trigynus

Pteranthus trigynus, commonly known as the three-flowered pteranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Pteranthus trigynus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
