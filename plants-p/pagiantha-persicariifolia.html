Pagiantha persicariifolia

Pagiantha persicariifolia, commonly known as the Persian buttercup, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, bright yellow flowers with prominent white stamens, which bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, creating a feathery appearance, and it is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
