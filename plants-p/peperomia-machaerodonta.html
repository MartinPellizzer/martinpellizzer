Peperomia machaerodonta

Peperomia machaerodonta, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically silver or gray in color, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardens.

Peperomia machaerodonta is relatively low maintenance and drought-tolerant, requiring minimal watering.
