Piqueria serrata

Piqueria serrata, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands. Its leaves are serrated, giving it its scientific name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer.

Piqueria serrata is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
