Panicum tristachyum

Panicum tristachyum, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, fluffy plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open areas, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, feathery appearance and low maintenance requirements.
