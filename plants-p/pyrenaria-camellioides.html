Pyrenaria camellioides

Pyrenaria camellioides, commonly known as the camellia-like pyrenaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and numerous stamens, and bloom in the spring.

Pyrenaria camellioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
