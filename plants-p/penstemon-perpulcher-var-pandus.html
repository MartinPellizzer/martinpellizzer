Penstemon perpulcher var. pandus

Penstemon perpulcher var. pandus, commonly known as the Pandus beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
