Polygonum kelloggii var. esotericum

Polygonum kelloggii var. esotericum, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside.

The ghostflower is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
