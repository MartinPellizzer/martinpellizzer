Papaver atlanticum subsp. mesatlanticum

Papaver atlanticum subsp.

mesatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic poppy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive yellow center, typical of the Papaver genus.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or along rocky shores. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh coastal environments and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
