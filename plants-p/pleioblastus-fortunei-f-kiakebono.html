Pleioblastus fortunei f. kiakebono

Pleioblastus fortunei f. kiakebono, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variety of the bamboo genus Pleioblastus.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which gives it a striking appearance in both garden and landscape settings. This ornamental grass is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant grows in clumps, forming dense stands that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and landscape design.
