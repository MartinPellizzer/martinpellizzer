Pelargonium × glabrum

Pelargonium × glabrum, commonly known as the common geranium, is a popular ornamental plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy flowers.

It is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family, originating from crosses between various pelargonium species. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season.

Its leaves are usually glossy and may have a slightly hairy texture, depending on the cultivar. Pelargonium × glabrum is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
