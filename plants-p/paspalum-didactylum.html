Paspalum didactylum

Paspalum didactylum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky-textured foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and coastal areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is used in landscaping and as a forage crop in some regions.
