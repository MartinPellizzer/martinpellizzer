Pfaffia tuberosa subsp. goiana

Pfaffia tuberosa subsp.

goiana, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. It features a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including adaptogenic and anti-inflammatory effects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
