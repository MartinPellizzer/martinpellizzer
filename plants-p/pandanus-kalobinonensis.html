Pandanus kalobinonensis

Pandanus kalobinonensis, commonly known as the Kalobinonensis pandanus, is a species of pandan native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant thrives in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained sandy soils. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, branched spike that bears small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit, a spiny capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural distribution.
