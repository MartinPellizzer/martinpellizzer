Potentilla brevistipulata

Potentilla brevistipulata, commonly known as the short-stippled cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with short, stiff hairs along the edges, giving it the "brevistipulata" designation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
