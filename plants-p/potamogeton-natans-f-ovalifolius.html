Potamogeton natans f. ovalifolius

Potamogeton natans f. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically oval in shape, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native environments.
