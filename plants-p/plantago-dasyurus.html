Plantago dasyurus

Plantago dasyurus, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The hairy plantain is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on the respiratory and digestive systems.
