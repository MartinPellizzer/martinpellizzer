Persea lohitensis

Persea lohitensis, commonly known as the Lohit persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by yellowish-orange berries that ripen in the fall.

Persea lohitensis is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet and slightly astringent flavor, and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
