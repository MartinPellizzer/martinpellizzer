Poa bucharica subsp. karateginensis

Poa bucharica subsp.

karateginensis, commonly known as the Karategin meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to well-drained soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems.
