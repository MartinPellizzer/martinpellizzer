Penstemon gracilentus

Penstemon gracilentus, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged oppositely along the stem.

Penstemon gracilentus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
