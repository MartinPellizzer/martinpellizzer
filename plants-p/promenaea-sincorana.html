Promenaea sincorana

Promenaea sincorana, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, deep red petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often producing a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Promenaea sincorana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
