Pandanus lageniformis

Pandanus lageniformis, commonly known as the bottle pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow in color. Its fruit is a clustered structure that resembles a bottle, hence its common name.

Pandanus lageniformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in island communities.
