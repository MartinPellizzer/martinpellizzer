Plantago dardanae

Plantago dardanae, commonly known as the Dardanian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its resilience and medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.
