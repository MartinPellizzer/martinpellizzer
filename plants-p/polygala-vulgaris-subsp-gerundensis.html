Polygala vulgaris subsp. gerundensis

Polygala vulgaris subsp.

gerundensis, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in spikes. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures.
