Pleurothallis teaguei

Pleurothallis teaguei, commonly known as the Teguei orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
