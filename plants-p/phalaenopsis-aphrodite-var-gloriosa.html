Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. gloriosa

Phalaenopsis aphrodite var. gloriosa, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting blooms that typically come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often have a waxy texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is widely cultivated as a houseplant.
