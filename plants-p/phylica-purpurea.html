Phylica purpurea

Phylica purpurea, commonly known as the pink everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Phylica purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soils.
