Picea gregoryana

Picea gregoryana, commonly known as Gregory's fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its dark green, needle-like foliage. The tree produces small, cylindrical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
