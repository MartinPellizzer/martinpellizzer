Periploca mexicoensis

Periploca mexicoensis, commonly known as the Mexican periploca, is a shrub native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Periploca mexicoensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
