Poa concinna var. cenisia

Poa concinna var. cenisia, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky blades. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying grassland ecosystems and plant adaptation strategies.
