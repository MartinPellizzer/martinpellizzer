Pleurothallis anthoctenium

Pleurothallis anthoctenium, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, strap-like leaves and produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
