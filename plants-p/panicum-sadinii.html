Panicum sadinii

Panicum sadinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. The plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions, with deep root systems that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small wildlife.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some regions for landscaping purposes.
