Pandanus odoratissimus var. pseudolinnaei

Pandanus odoratissimus var. pseudolinnaei, commonly known as the sweet-scented pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and rituals. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its aromatic reputation. Its fibrous trunk and broad leaves make it well adapted to coastal and humid environments.

This variant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and cultural significance in various island communities.
