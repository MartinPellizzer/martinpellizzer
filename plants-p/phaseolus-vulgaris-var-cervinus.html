Phaseolus vulgaris var. cervinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. cervinus, commonly known as the horse bean, is a variety of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its elongated, brown pods and dark brown seeds. This plant is typically grown for its seeds, which are used in various culinary applications. The leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated in temperate climates.

It is a legume that fixes nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
