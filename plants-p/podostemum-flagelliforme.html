Podostemum flagelliforme

Podostemum flagelliforme, commonly known as the thread-leaf water plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are often submerged in water, adapting to its aquatic environment. The plant typically grows along the edges of slow-moving streams and rivers, where it attaches itself to rocks or submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with flowers that are usually borne on short stalks near the water's surface.

Podostemum flagelliforme plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through its dense foliage.
