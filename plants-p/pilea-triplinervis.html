Pilea triplinervis

Pilea triplinervis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ease of care and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Pilea triplinervis is also known for its ability to propagate through stem cuttings, making it a favored choice for plant enthusiasts.
