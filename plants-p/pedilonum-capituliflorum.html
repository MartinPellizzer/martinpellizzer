Pedilonum capituliflorum

Pedilonum capituliflorum, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has palmate leaves with a rough texture and thrives in cool, moist environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
