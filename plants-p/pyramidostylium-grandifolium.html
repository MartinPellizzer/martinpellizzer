Pyramidostylium grandifolium

Pyramidostylium grandifolium, commonly known as the grandifoliate pyramid plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive pyramid-shaped growth habit.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around its central stem. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with small, brightly colored flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
