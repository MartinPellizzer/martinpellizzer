Persicaria sylvestris

Persicaria sylvestris, commonly known as woodland smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense terminal spikes, and are typically pink or red, adding visual interest in late summer.

Persicaria sylvestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
