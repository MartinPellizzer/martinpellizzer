Pteronia mucronata

Pteronia mucronata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Pteronia mucronata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
