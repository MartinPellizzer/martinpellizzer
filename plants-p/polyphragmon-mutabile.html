Polyphragmon mutabile

Polyphragmon mutabile, commonly known as the variable polyphragmon, is a unique member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its biennial growth habit.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
