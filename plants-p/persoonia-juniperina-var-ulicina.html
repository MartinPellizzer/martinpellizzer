Persoonia juniperina var. ulicina

Persoonia juniperina var. ulicina, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that resemble a bottlebrush, typically ranging in color from yellow to orange. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are densely packed along the stems, contributing to its compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandplains.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
