Peddiea puberula

Peddiea puberula, commonly known as the woolly peddiea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Peddiea puberula is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
