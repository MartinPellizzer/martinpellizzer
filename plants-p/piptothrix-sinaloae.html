Piptothrix sinaloae

Piptothrix sinaloae, commonly known as the Sinaloa hookless cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
