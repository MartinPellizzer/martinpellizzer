Piper francovilleanum

Piper francovilleanum, commonly known as the Frenchville pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It produces small, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
