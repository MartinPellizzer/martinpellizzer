Phragmites frutescens

Phragmites frutescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant is often found in marshes, along shorelines, and in other moist habitats. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Phragmites frutescens is also known for its ability to spread rapidly through rhizomes, forming dense stands.
