Pleurothallis scurrula

Pleurothallis scurrula, commonly known as the "scurrilous pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often green or yellow in color. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is part of the subgenus Pleurothallis, which includes many species with similar morphological traits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized orchid.
