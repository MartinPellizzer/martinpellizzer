Pithecellobium parvifolium

Pithecellobium parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf pita, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing small, sweet, and fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
