Puccinellia vilfoidea

Puccinellia vilfoidea, commonly known as the slender saltgrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, tufted form with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It is adapted to thrive in salty soils and is often found in coastal areas and salt marshes.

Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of ecosystems in such environments.
