Primula obconica subsp. vilmoriniana

Primula obconica subsp.

vilmoriniana, commonly known as the Vilmorin's primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
