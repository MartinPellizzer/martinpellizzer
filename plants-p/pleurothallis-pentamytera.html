Pleurothallis pentamytera

Pleurothallis pentamytera, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on its roots for moisture and nutrients. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in greenhouses but is rarely found in the wild.
