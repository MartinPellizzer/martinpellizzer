Pelargonium curinbeticum

Pelargonium curinbeticum, commonly known as the Curinbetic geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
