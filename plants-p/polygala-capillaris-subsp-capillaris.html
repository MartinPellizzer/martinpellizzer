Polygala capillaris subsp. capillaris

Polygala capillaris subsp.

capillaris, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
