Primula specuicola

Primula specuicola, commonly known as the cave primrose, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in caves or shaded rock crevices where it receives filtered light. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly texture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Primula specuicola is considered an endangered species and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
