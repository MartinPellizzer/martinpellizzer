Polygala amara var. calcarea

Polygala amara var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone polygala, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in calcareous soils, often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
