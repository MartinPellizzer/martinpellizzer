Primula tosaensis var. rhodotricha

Primula tosaensis var. rhodotricha, commonly known as the Tosa primrose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its horticultural appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
