Polemonium caeruleum lusus laxiflorum

Polemonium caeruleum lusus laxiflorum, commonly known as the laxiflorous bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable habitats.
