Pandanus veillonii

Pandanus veillonii, commonly known as the Veillon's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the leaves. Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
