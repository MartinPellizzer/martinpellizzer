Pelargonium tenellum

Pelargonium tenellum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically velvety in texture and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
