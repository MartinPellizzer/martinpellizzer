Pancovia laurentii

Pancovia laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentian panicle, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Pancovia laurentii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
