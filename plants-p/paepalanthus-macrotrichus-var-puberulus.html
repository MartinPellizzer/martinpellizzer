Paepalanthus macrotrichus var. puberulus

Paepalanthus macrotrichus var. puberulus, commonly known as the woolly puya, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the entire plant, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces tall, spikelike inflorescences that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, sandy soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
