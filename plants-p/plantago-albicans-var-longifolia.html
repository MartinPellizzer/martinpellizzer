Plantago albicans var. longifolia

Plantago albicans var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
