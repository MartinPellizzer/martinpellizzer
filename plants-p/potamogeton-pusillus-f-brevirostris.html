Potamogeton pusillus f. brevirostris

Potamogeton pusillus f. brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats.

It has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is characterized by its short, bluntly tapered shoots, distinguishing it from other forms of Potamogeton. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
