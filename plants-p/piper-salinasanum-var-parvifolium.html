Piper salinasanum var. parvifolium

Piper salinasanum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper salinasanum species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, slender fruits. Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Piper genus, often found in humid, shaded environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
