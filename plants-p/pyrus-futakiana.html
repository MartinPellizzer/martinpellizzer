Pyrus futakiana

Pyrus futakiana, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Pyrus futakiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
