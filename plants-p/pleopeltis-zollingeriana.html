Pleopeltis zollingeriana

Pleopeltis zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks and rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant has pinnate leaves with a distinctive, leathery texture and a dark green color. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, and it thrives in shaded, moist conditions.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.
