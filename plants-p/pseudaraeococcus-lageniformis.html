Pseudaraeococcus lageniformis

Pseudaraeococcus lageniformis, commonly known as the glassy wax scale, is a small, sedentary hemipteran insect that forms colonies on the stems and leaves of various plants.

It is characterized by its waxy, shield-like body that is often transparent or glassy in appearance. The plant it infests is typically a host in the family Malvaceae, such as cotton or hibiscus. It produces a sugary honeydew that can lead to the growth of sooty mold on the plant's surface.

Due to its ability to cause economic damage in agricultural settings, it is considered a significant pest in some regions.
