Piper matinanum

Piper matinanum, commonly known as the Matinam or Matinam pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, elongated fruits. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and the leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
