Panicum wettsteinii

Panicum wettsteinii, commonly known as Wettstein's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and sandy plains. The plant features slender, wiry stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This species is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
