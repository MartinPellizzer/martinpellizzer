Poecilodermis

Poecilodermis, commonly known as the "beach morning glory," is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in sandy, saline environments. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in a fine, white powder.

Poecilodermis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in coastal gardens.
