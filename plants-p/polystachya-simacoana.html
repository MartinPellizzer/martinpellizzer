Polystachya simacoana

Polystachya simacoana, commonly known as the Simacoana torch orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous small, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is noted for its fragrant blooms, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
