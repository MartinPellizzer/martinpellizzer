Pattara welwitschii

Pattara welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its two long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length and persist for decades. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions by accessing underground water sources. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

Due to its slow growth and rarity, it is considered a living fossil and a symbol of the Namib Desert's ecological resilience.
