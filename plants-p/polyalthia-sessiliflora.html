Polyalthia sessiliflora

Polyalthia sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that surround small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
