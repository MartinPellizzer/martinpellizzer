Polygonum biscaticlium

Polygonum biscaticlium, commonly known as the Biscay knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and often reddish stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and may have a slightly hairy texture.

The small, clustered flowers appear in late summer and are usually greenish-white in color.
