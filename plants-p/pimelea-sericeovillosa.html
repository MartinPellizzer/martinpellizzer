Pimelea sericeovillosa

Pimelea sericeovillosa, commonly known as the silky or silky-leaved pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, wiry stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with a fine, silvery down.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
