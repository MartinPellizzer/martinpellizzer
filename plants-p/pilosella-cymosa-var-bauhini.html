Pilosella cymosa var. bauhini

Pilosella cymosa var. bauhini, commonly known as the hairy leaf mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive lobed or toothed shape, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a characteristic species of certain alpine and subalpine regions.
