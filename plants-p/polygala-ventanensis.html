Polygala ventanensis

Polygala ventanensis, commonly known as the Ventanensis milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including pink and white. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala ventanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
