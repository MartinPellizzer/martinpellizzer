Pinus obovata var. schrenkiana

Pinus obovata var. schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's Japanese white pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine native to central Asia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and conical shape. The leaves are typically bluish-green and arranged in spirals along the branches. It produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
