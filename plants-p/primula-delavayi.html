Primula delavayi

Primula delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
