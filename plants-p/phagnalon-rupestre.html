Phagnalon rupestre

Phagnalon rupestre, commonly known as rock phagnalon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and arid environments.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in summer. It is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Phagnalon rupestre is often found in mountainous regions and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
