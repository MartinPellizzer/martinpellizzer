Pocockia biflora

Pocockia biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered tamarisk, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a compact growth habit, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Pocockia biflora plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
