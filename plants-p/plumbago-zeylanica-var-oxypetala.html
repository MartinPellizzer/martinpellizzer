Plumbago zeylanica var. oxypetala

Plumbago zeylanica var. oxypetala, commonly known as the blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or lavender-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its showy flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
