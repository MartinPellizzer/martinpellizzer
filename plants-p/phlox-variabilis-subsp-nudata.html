Phlox variabilis subsp. nudata

Phlox variabilis subsp.

nudata, commonly known as the naked phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a mat-forming growth habit.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
