Pimenta adenoclada

Pimenta adenoclada, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. The plant has a dense, branched structure with a smooth, reddish-brown bark. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Pimenta adenoclada is also known for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
