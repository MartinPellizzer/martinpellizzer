Phyteuma spicatum var. alpinum

Phyteuma spicatum var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in full sun. The plant features erect, sparsely branched stems and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish to orange-red flowers. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and vivid floral display, making it a notable species in alpine botanical studies.
