Phaseolus brachycalyx

Phaseolus brachycalyx, commonly known as the small-calyx bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellowish seeds. It is often grown as a food crop in tropical and subtropical regions due to its adaptability and nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both agricultural and ecological studies.
