Pithecellobium basijugum

Pithecellobium basijugum, commonly known as the small-leaf tropical acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, ovate leaflets, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in racemes. It produces elongated, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
