Phaseolus sphaericus var. carneus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. carneus, commonly known as the red kidney bean, is a legume native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically red or reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, pale green flowers and has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and dietary fiber.

The botanical features of this variety make it an important crop in many cuisines worldwide.
