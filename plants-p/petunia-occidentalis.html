Petunia occidentalis

Petunia occidentalis, commonly known as the western petunia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its foliage consists of simple, alternate leaves that are usually hairy and oval in shape.

Petunia occidentalis thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
