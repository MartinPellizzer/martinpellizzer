Pithecellobium alexandri var. intermedium

Pithecellobium alexandri var. intermedium, commonly known as the Alexander's pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical forest environments.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
