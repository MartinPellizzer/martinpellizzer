Pulegium tomentellum f. erianthum

Pulegium tomentellum f. erianthum, commonly known as the woolly pulegium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
