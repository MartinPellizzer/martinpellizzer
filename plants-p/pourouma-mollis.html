Pourouma mollis

Pourouma mollis, commonly known as the "sugar cane palm," is a tall, herbaceous plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce a sugary sap, which has been traditionally used by indigenous communities. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in some regions outside its native habitat.
