Pittosporum reticulatum

Pittosporum reticulatum, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries in autumn. The leaves are leathery, glossy, and have a distinctive silvery-white underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for native birds and insects.
