Potentilla trifoliolata

Potentilla trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval to lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Potentilla trifoliolata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
