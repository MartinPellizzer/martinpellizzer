Pyrus malus f. barcinonensis

Pyrus malus f. barcinonensis, commonly known as the Barcino apple, is a rare and unique form of the domesticated apple.

It is characterized by its distinctive fruit morphology, including a flattened shape and a unique coloration pattern. Botanically, it is a cultivar of Malus domestica, exhibiting specific genetic traits that set it apart from other apple varieties. The plant is typically found in specific regions where it has been selectively cultivated for its unique characteristics.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a genetic resource for apple breeding programs.
