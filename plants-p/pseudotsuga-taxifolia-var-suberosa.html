Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. suberosa

Pseudotsuga taxifolia var. suberosa, commonly known as the suberous hemlock, is a variant of the Japanese hemlock.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan, characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and conical shape. The leaves are typically flat, glossy, and dark green, with a slight blueish tinge. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the conifer family.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
