Photinia benthamiana var. salicifolia

Photinia benthamiana var. salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf photinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, willow-like leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive foliage and flowers, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
