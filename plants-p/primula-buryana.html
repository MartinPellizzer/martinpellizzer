Primula buryana

Primula buryana, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple, during the spring season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
