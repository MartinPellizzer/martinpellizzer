Phaseoloides sericeum

Phaseoloides sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The flowers are typically pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
