Piper townsendii

Piper townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's pepper or the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
