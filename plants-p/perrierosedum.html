Perrierosedum

Perrierosedum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating.

This coating, known as a cuticle, helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is native to regions with mild, dry climates. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer.

Perrierosedum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
