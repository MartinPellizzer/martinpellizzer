Pyrus sinaica

Pyrus sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai pear, is a rare and ancient species native to the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant has small, elongated leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, edible pear that has been historically used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Pyrus sinaica is now considered a critically endangered species.
