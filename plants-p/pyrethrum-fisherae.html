Pyrethrum fisherae

Pyrethrum fisherae, commonly known as the Fisher's pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central disk. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, making it valuable in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a noteworthy species in botanical gardens and native plant collections.
