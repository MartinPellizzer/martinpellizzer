Pittosporum triste

Pittosporum triste, commonly known as the weeping pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the autumn before shedding. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Pittosporum triste is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
