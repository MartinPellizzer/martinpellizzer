Packera bolanderi var. bolanderi

Packera bolanderi var. bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's leafy mountain-mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine and for ornamental purposes.
