Primula dryadifolia subsp. jonardunii

Primula dryadifolia subsp.

jonardunii, commonly known as the Jonardun primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive greenish hue. Its leaves are broadly ovate, often with a waxy coating, and are arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
