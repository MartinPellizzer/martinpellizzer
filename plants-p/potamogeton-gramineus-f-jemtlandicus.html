Potamogeton gramineus f. jemtlandicus

Potamogeton gramineus f. jemtlandicus, commonly known as the Jemtland water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water, and its roots help stabilize sediment in aquatic environments.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and its role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.
