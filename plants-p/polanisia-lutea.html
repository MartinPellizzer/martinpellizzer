Polanisia lutea

Polanisia lutea, commonly known as the yellow polanisia, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by seed capsules. Polanisia lutea is often found in open woodlands and along forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
