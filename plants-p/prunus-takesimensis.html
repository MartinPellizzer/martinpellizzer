Prunus takesimensis

Prunus takesimensis, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but have a tart flavor. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh winters.

Prunus takesimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
