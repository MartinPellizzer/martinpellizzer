Phaseolus tenellus

Phaseolus tenellus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and is often grown for both its ornamental value and edible beans. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Phaseolus tenellus is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
