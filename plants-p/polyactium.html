Polyactium

Polyactium, commonly known as the common milkweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apocynaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the genus. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Polyactium plays an important role in supporting pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
