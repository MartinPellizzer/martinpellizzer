Pelea volcanica

Pelea volcanica, commonly known as the volcanic pelea, is a flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in nutrient-rich soils found in volcanic landscapes, often growing near hot springs or geothermal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

Pelea volcanica is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
