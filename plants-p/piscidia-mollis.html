Piscidia mollis

Piscidia mollis, commonly known as the pink antirrhinum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a soft, velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Piscidia mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
