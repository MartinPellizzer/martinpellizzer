Polystachya uluguruensis

Polystachya uluguruensis, commonly known as the Uluguru mountain orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by supporting pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
