Polygonum englerianum

Polygonum englerianum, commonly known as Engler's knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and slender, branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Native to regions of Africa and Asia, it is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
