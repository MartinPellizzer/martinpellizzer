Pygeum lampongum

Pygeum lampongum, commonly known as the Philippine sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and small, sour-tasting fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
