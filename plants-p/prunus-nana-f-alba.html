Prunus nana f. alba

Prunus nana f. alba, commonly known as the white dwarf cherry, is a compact, slow-growing shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is a naturally occurring white-flowered form of Prunus nana, characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and small, oval leaves. The flowers are pale pink to white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge, adding ornamental value to gardens. It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for shaded or woodland garden settings.

This plant is valued for its delicate beauty and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.
