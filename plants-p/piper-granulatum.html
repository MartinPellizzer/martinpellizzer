Piper granulatum

Piper granulatum, commonly known as the granular pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, green, granular fruits that resemble tiny peppercorns. It produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
