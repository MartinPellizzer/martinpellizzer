Pabstiella hymenantha

Pabstiella hymenantha, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and showy, white flowers with prominent veining. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and bloom during the spring or early summer.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
