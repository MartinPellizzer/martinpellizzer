Polystichum niitakayamense

Polystichum niitakayamense, commonly known as the Niitakayama holly fern, is a species of fern native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds with a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
