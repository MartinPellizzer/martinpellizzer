Pouteria stipulata

Pouteria stipulata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces fruit that resembles a large, woody capsule, containing seeds that are commercially valuable as Brazil nuts. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a canopy that can reach heights of up to 50 meters.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife and its role in traditional medicine.
