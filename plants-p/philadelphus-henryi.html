Philadelphus henryi

Philadelphus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has compound leaves with ovate to elliptical leaflets. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and are often used in ornamental horticulture for their pleasant scent and showy appearance.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and the plant is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
