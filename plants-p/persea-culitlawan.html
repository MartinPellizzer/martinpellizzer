Persea culitlawan

Persea culitlawan, commonly known as the Philippine persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
