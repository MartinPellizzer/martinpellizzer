Plinia guildingiana

Plinia guildingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp that resembles a star in appearance. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in both gardens and commercial cultivation.
