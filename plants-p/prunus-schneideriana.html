Prunus schneideriana

Prunus schneideriana, commonly known as the Schneider's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched structure and produces small, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant's leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall. The fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
