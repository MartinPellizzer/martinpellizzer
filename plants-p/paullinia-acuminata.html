Paullinia acuminata

Paullinia acuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian soursop or acerola cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright red, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds. Its fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor.

Paullinia acuminata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
