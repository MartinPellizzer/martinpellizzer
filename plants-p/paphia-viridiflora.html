Paphia viridiflora

Paphia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered paphiopedilum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and a single, upright stem that produces several leaves. The plant is characterized by its vibrant green flowers, which are typically arranged in a raceme and have a unique, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Paphia viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
