Pseudocyclosorus esquirolii monstr.

laciniatus, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking epiphytic fern characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant exhibits a unique morphology with elongated, narrow leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a lacy appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on trees or rocks in tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are often a deep green, with a velvety texture that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among botanical enthusiasts and conservationists.
