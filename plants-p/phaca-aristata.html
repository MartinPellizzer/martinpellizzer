Phaca aristata

Phaca aristata, commonly known as the arrowhead plant, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

Phaca aristata is often used in water gardens and wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits.
