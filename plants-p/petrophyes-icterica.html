Petrophyes icterica

Petrophyes icterica, commonly known as the yellow rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Petrophyes icterica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
