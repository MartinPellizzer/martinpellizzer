Primula maximowiczii f. flaviflorida

Primula maximowiczii f. flaviflorida, commonly known as the yellow-flowered primrose, is a variant of the Primula maximowiczii species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China. The plant features rosettes of basal leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to alpine gardens.

The botanical interest of this plant lies in its unique floral coloration and its adaptability to high-altitude environments.
