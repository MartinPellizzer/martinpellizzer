Prinsepia uniflora

Prinsepia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower prinsepia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Prinsepia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
