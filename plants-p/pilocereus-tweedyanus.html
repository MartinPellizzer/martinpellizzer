Pilocereus tweedyanus

Pilocereus tweedyanus, commonly known as the Honeycomb Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, rounded tubercles that resemble a honeycomb pattern. The plant produces delicate, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a striking visual contrast to its green stems. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Pilocereus tweedyanus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and attractive flowers.
