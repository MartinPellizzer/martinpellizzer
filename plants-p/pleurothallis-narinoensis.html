Pleurothallis narinoensis

Pleurothallis narinoensis, commonly known as the Narino orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid forest environments. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are usually small and delicate, with a variety of colors ranging from white to pink and purple.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
