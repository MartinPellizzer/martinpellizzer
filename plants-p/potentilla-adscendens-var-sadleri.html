Potentilla adscendens var. sadleri

Potentilla adscendens var. sadleri, commonly known as the Himalayan cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
