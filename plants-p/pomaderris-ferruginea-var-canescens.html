Pomaderris ferruginea var. canescens

Pomaderris ferruginea var. canescens, commonly known as silverleaf pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, inconspicuous white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, while the stems are covered in fine, whitish hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
