Pimelea linifolia var. brownii

Pimelea linifolia var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's Riceflower, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. It is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and degradation.

The Riceflower is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
