Phlox pilosa subsp. sangamonensis

Phlox pilosa subsp.

sangamonensis, commonly known as the Sangamon hairy phlox, is a perennial herb native to the central United States. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.

The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in the summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.

This subspecies is often found in the Illinois River valley and surrounding areas.
