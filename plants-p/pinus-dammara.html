Pinus dammara

Pinus dammara, commonly known as the yellow pine or dammara pine, is a species of conifer native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant produces cones that are woody and contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Pinus dammara thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
