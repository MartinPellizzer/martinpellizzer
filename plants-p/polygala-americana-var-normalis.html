Polygala americana var. normalis

Polygala americana var. normalis, commonly known as the American milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces delicate, spicate inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in herbal medicine.
