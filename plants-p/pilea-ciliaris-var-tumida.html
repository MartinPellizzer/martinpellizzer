Pilea ciliaris var. tumida

Pilea ciliaris var. tumida, commonly known as the Chinese money plant or UFO plant, is a compact, low-growing species native to Southeast Asia.

It features rounded, waxy, coin-shaped leaves that are often slightly swollen at the base, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
