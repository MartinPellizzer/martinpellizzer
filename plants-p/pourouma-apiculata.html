Pourouma apiculata

Pourouma apiculata, commonly known as the Amazonian areca palm, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible, coconut-like fruits. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts, and the plant plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various animals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
