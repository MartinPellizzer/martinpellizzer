Phanera lingua var. lingua

Phanera lingua var. lingua, commonly known as the broadleaf phanera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
