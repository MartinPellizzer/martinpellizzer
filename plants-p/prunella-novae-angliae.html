Prunella novae-angliae

Prunella novae-angliae, commonly known as New England cowslip, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Prunella novae-angliae is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
