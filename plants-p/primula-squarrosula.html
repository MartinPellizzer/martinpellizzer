Primula squarrosula

Primula squarrosula, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, and often white or pale green leaves, which are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
