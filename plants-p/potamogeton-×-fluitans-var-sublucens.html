Potamogeton × fluitans var. sublucens

Potamogeton × fluitans var. sublucens, commonly known as the slender water plant, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly translucent.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes, where it can form dense mats on the water surface or along the substrate. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually submerged or floating. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy layer, aiding in buoyancy and reducing water loss.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it both ecologically significant and sometimes invasive.
