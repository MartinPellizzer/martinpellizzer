Prostanthera walteri

Prostanthera walteri, commonly known as the Walter's mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a mint-like scent.

Prostanthera walteri is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
