Podostachys incana

Podostachys incana, commonly known as the slender waterwheel plant, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, spirally arranged leaves that form a rosette at the water's surface. The plant's flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and are borne on slender, branching stems. Podostachys incana thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as streams and rivers.

Its specialized morphology allows it to anchor itself to rocky substrates while adapting to fluctuating water levels.
