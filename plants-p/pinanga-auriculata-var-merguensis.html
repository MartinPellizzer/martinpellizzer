Pinanga auriculata var. merguensis

Pinanga auriculata var. merguensis, commonly known as the Merguens giant bamboo, is a variant of the pinanga genus native to the lowland rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it one of the tallest bamboo species in the region. The leaves are broad and lanceolate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, often hairy underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in the ecological structure of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and sustainable use in traditional crafts.
