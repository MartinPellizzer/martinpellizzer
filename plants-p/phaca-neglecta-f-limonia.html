Phaca neglecta f. limonia

Phaca neglecta f. limonia, commonly known as the yellow phaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability.
