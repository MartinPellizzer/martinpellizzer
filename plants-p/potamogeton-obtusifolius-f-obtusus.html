Potamogeton obtusifolius f. obtusus

Potamogeton obtusifolius f. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and parts of Asia.

It features elongated, broad leaves that are typically dark green and slightly waxy, adapted for underwater photosynthesis. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by cylindrical, multi-seeded fruits. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth is often influenced by water clarity, nutrient levels, and flow rate.
