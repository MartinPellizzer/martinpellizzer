Plectranthus succulentus

Plectranthus succulentus, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
