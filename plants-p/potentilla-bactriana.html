Potentilla bactriana

Potentilla bactriana, commonly known as the Bactrian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which is a distinguishing feature of the Potentilla genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Potentilla bactriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
