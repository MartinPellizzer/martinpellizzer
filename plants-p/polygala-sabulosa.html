Polygala sabulosa

Polygala sabulosa, commonly known as the sand polygala, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically arranged in spicate inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Polygala sabulosa is a member of the Polygalaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and ecological adaptations.
