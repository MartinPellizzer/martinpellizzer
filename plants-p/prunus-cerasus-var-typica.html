Prunus cerasus var. typica

Prunus cerasus var. typica, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that range in color from red to black, depending on the variety. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with a pointed tip. It blooms in spring with clusters of white or pink flowers before the fruit develops.

The plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of sweet, edible cherries.
