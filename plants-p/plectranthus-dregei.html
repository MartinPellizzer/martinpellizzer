Plectranthus dregei

Plectranthus dregei, commonly known as the African violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, which bloom from late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus dregei is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
