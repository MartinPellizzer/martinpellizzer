Polygonum peruvianum

Polygonum peruvianum, commonly known as Peruvian knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

Polygonum peruvianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
