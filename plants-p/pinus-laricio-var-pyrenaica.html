Pinus laricio var. pyrenaica

Pinus laricio var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its thick, scaly bark and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The cones of this species are large and woody, containing edible seeds that are often harvested for culinary use. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in Mediterranean and European forests.
