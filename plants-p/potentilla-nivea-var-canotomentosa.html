Potentilla nivea var. canotomentosa

Potentilla nivea var. canotomentosa, commonly known as the silver-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-white pubescence that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and decorative qualities.
