Poa schliebenii

Poa schliebenii, commonly known as Schlieben's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Poa schliebenii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
