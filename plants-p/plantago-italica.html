Plantago italica

Plantago italica, commonly known as Italian plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the plantain family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many regions around the world. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The leaves are edible and have been used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.
