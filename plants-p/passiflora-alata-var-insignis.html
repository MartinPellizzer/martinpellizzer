Passiflora alata var. insignis

Passiflora alata var. insignis, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that feature intricate structures such as the crown of filaments and a distinctive floral tube. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
