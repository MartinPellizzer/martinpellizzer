Ponthieva castanedae

Ponthieva castanedae, commonly known as the Castanea palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small, round coconuts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
