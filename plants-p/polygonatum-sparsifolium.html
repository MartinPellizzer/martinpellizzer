Polygonatum sparsifolium

Polygonatum sparsifolium, commonly known as the sparse-leaved Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Polygonatum sparsifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
