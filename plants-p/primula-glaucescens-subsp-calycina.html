Primula glaucescens subsp. calycina

Primula glaucescens subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in alpine gardens and conservation efforts.
