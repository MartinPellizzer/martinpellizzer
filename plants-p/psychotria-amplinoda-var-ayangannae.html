Psychotria amplinoda var. ayangannae

Psychotria amplinoda var. ayangannae, commonly known as Ayahuasca, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, particularly DMT, which contribute to its traditional use in shamanic rituals. Its botanical significance is closely tied to its cultural and spiritual roles in indigenous communities.
