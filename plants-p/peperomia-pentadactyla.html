Peperomia pentadactyla

Peperomia pentadactyla, commonly known as the five-finger peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its ability to thrive in low light conditions and its tolerance for dry soil.

Peperomia pentadactyla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
