Prunella integerrima

Prunella integerrima, commonly known as selfheal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of blue or violet flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and aromatic foliage.
