Piper hirtellipetiolum

Piper hirtellipetiolum, commonly known as the hairy piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small berries that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
