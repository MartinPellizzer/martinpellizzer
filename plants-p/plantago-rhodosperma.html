Plantago rhodosperma

Plantago rhodosperma, commonly known as the redseed plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry and sunny environments. The plant features upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces distinctive, reddish-brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
