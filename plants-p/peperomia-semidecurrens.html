Peperomia semidecurrens

Peperomia semidecurrens, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, often with a bluish or silver-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth and ability to propagate through stem cuttings make it an easy-to-care-for plant.
