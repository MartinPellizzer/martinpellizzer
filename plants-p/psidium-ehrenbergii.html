Psidium ehrenbergii

Psidium ehrenbergii, commonly known as the Mexican guava or wild guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, typically growing in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, round fruits that are usually green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are white with a prominent yellow center.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
