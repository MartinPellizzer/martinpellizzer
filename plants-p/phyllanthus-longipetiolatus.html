Phyllanthus longipetiolatus

Phyllanthus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
