Polypodium tetragonum var. latifolium

Polypodium tetragonum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall fern, is a variant of the common polypody.

It is a perennial evergreen fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves with a distinctive frond structure, and its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds. It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and is often found in temperate regions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
