Primula bullata var. bracteata

Primula bullata var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to white petals, often with a distinctive greenish or reddish throat. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
