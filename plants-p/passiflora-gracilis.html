Passiflora gracilis

Passiflora gracilis, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking, five-petaled flowers that often display a blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its climbing habit.

Passiflora gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
