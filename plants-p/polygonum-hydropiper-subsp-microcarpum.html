Polygonum hydropiper subsp. microcarpum

Polygonum hydropiper subsp.

microcarpum, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures called pseudocarps, which are a distinctive botanical feature. It thrives in moist, often wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, slightly sour scent when crushed.
