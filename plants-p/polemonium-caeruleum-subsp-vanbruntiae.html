Polemonium caeruleum subsp. vanbruntiae

Polemonium caeruleum subsp.

vanbruntiae, commonly known as the Van Brunt's bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
