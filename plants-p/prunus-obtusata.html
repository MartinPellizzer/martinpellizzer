Prunus obtusata

Prunus obtusata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Prunus obtusata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
