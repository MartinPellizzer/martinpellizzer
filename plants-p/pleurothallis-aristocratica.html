Pleurothallis aristocratica

Pleurothallis aristocratica, commonly known as the aristocratic orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
