Potamogeton serrulatus

Potamogeton serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater vegetation that provides habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has long, narrow leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediment and improve water quality by reducing erosion.

Potamogeton serrulatus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems as both a food source and a habitat provider.
