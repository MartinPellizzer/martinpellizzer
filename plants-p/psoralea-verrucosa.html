Psoralea verrucosa

Psoralea verrucosa, commonly known as the rough psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, rounded leaflets that have a rough texture.

Psoralea verrucosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
