Potamogeton natans subsp. kirkii

Potamogeton natans subsp.

kirkii, commonly known as Kirk's water plantain, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Africa. It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as lakes and ponds, where it forms dense vegetation.

The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbon-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water surface.

This subspecies is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
