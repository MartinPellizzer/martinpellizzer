Pseudoroegneria setulifera

Pseudoroegneria setulifera, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Pseudoroegneria setulifera is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its drought tolerance and fine texture.
