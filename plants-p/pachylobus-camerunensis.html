Pachylobus camerunensis

Pachylobus camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by orange-yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
