Pternandra grandifolia

Pternandra grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pternandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
