Pyrostria phyllanthoidea

Pyrostria phyllanthoidea, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a succulent-like appearance that aids in water retention. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
