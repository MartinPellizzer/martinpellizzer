Pterocarpus violaceus

Pterocarpus violaceus, commonly known as the purpleheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark purple to reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small and typically yellow or greenish, appearing in clusters, and it produces woody pods containing flat, elongated seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
