Pinguicula vulgaris longifolia

Pinguicula vulgaris longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows. Its flowers are usually white or pink, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, humid climates.
