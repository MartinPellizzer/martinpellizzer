Plicosepalus ogadenensis

Plicosepalus ogadenensis, commonly known as the Ogaden plicosepalus, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, plicate (folded) sepals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage is usually small and succulent, helping it conserve water.
