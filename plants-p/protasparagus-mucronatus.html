Protasparagus mucronatus

Protasparagus mucronatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed asparagus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asparagaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
