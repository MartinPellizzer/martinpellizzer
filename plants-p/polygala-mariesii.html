Polygala mariesii

Polygala mariesii, commonly known as the Mary's polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and showy, spindly flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its blooming period occurs during the summer months, producing clusters of small, pink or white flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
