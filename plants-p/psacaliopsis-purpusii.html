Psacaliopsis purpusii

Psacaliopsis purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus's false aloe, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
