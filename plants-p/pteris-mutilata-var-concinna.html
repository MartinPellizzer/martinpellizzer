Pteris mutilata var. concinna

Pteris mutilata var. concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Pteris.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along streams. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. Its rhizomes are creeping and often covered in a layer of fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens or as a houseplant.
