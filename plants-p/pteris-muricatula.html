Pteris muricatula

Pteris muricatula, commonly known as the bristly wall fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The fronds are pinnately divided, with serrated leaflets that give the plant its distinctive appearance. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This plant is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in damp soil, showcasing its adaptability to various ecological niches.
