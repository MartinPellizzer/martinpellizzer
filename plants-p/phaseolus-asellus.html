Phaseolus asellus

Phaseolus asellus, commonly known as the goat pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, green pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein and fiber.

Due to its adaptability, it can thrive in various soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
