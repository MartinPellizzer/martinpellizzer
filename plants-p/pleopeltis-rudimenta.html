Pleopeltis rudimenta

Pleopeltis rudimenta, commonly known as the rudimentary pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Pleopeltis rudimenta is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
