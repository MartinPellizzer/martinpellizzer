Pinanga canina

Pinanga canina, commonly known as the dog's tail palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and long, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Pinanga canina is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant foliage.
