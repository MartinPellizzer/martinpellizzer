Polypodium pseudoloxogramma

Polypodium pseudoloxogramma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in terminal clusters, a feature common among many ferns.

Polypodium pseudoloxogramma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
