Pleurothallis ingramii

Pleurothallis ingramii, commonly known as the Ingram's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain, typical of many orchid species.

Pleurothallis ingramii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral structure.
