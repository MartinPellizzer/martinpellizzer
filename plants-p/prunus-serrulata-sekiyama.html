Prunus serrulata sekiyama

Prunus serrulata sekiyama, commonly known as the Sekiyama cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, double white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has a compact, rounded shape with dense foliage that turns vibrant red in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, often developing fissures with age.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and parks, particularly for its elegant floral display.
