Piaropus paniculatus

Piaropus paniculatus, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a tropical plant native to the coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and a distinctive white, waxy coating on its stems and leaves, which helps reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to brackish and saline conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in mangrove ecosystems.
