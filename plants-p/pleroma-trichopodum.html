Pleroma trichopodum

Pleroma trichopodum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a reddish hue at the base. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Pleroma trichopodum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
