Phyllanthus mozambicensis

Phyllanthus mozambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
