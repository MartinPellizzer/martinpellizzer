Procrassula cespitosa

Procrassula cespitosa, commonly known as the silver leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Procrassula cespitosa is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
