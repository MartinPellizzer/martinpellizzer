Papaver alpinum subvar. rubicundum

Papaver alpinum subvar. rubicundum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a tall, slender stem that supports its showy blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
