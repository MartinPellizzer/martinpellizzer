Photinia subzessilis

Photinia subzessilis, commonly known as the glossy privet, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southeastern China and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are oppositely arranged on the stems. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Photinia subzessilis prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
