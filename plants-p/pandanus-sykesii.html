Pandanus sykesii

Pandanus sykesii, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found near water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit, which is a fibrous structure containing seeds, is an important resource for both wildlife and local communities.
