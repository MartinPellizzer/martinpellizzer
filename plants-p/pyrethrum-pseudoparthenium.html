Pyrethrum pseudoparthenium

Pyrethrum pseudoparthenium, commonly known as the common pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers and serrated leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of pyrethrin, a natural insecticide.

Pyrethrum pseudoparthenium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural settings.
