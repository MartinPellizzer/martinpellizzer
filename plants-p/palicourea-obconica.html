Palicourea obconica

Palicourea obconica, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Palicourea obconica is valued in traditional medicine for its potential pharmacological properties.
