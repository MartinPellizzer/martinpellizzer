Polygonum viscosum var. ramosissimum

Polygonum viscosum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as sticky smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are often covered in a sticky, glandular substance. The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive sticky texture and vibrant floral display.
