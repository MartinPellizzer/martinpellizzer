Piper fraguanum var. viride

Piper fraguanum var. viride, commonly known as green pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, green berries that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
