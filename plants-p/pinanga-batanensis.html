Pinanga batanensis

Pinanga batanensis, commonly known as the Batan bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly the Batan Islands, and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue-black berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
