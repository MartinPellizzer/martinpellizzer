Piper bradei

Piper bradei, commonly known as the Brade's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and fruiting habits.
