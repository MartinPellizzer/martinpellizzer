Paspalum natans

Paspalum natans, commonly known as floating grass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the ability to float on the surface of water. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. Paspalum natans thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, marshes, and slow-moving streams.

Its adaptability to aquatic conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
