Passerina vesiculosa

Passerina vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladderleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive bladder-like structures on the stems, which are unique to the species.

Passerina vesiculosa is often found in the eastern and central United States.
