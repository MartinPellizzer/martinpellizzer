Paspalum dissectum

Paspalum dissectum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Paspalum dissectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
