Phlomis brevidentata

Phlomis brevidentata, commonly known as narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features large, fuzzy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and have serrated margins. Its showy, yellowish-green flowers bloom in summer, forming dense spikes at the top of the stem.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.
