Penstemon miniatus subsp. townsendianus

Penstemon miniatus subsp.

townsendianus, commonly known as Townsend's penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
