Parsonsia schumannii

Parsonsia schumannii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The botanical features of Parsonsia schumannii make it a visually appealing and ecologically valuable plant in its native habitat.
