Petiveria alliacea var. grandifolia

Petiveria alliacea var. grandifolia, commonly known as "chicory root" or "hierba de santo," is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered white or violet flowers. The plant produces a fibrous, tuberous root that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a mild, garlic-like aroma.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.
