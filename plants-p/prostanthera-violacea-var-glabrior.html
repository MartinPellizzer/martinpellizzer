Prostanthera violacea var. glabrior

Prostanthera violacea var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth blue mint, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features small, violet-blue flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and its leaves are generally smooth and lance-shaped. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland and heathland environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
