Parietaria appendiculata var. lanuginosa

Parietaria appendiculata var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy wall rue, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes stinging nettles, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and are borne on slender peduncles.

The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy surface that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
