Primula pannonica

Primula pannonica, commonly known as the Pannonian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish-white flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer.

Primula pannonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
