Plectranthus zatarhendi

Plectranthus zatarhendi, commonly known as the Cape mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a useful and attractive plant.
