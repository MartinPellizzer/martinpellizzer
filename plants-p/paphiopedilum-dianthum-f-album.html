Paphiopedilum dianthum f. album

Paphiopedilum dianthum f. album, commonly known as the White Cattleya Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, white flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant is a terrestrial orchid, typically growing in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its flowers are highly prized in horticulture for their elegance and fragrance.

This cultivar is noted for its slow growth and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
