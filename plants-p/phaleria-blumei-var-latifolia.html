Phaleria blumei var. latifolia

Phaleria blumei var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf champa, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine. Its fruit, which resembles a small orange, contains seeds that are used in various herbal preparations.

The broadleaf variety is distinguished by its wider leaves compared to other varieties of Phaleria blumei.
