Pygeum floribundum

Pygeum floribundum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
