Plumeria bahamensis

Plumeria bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Bahamas and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and slightly hairy. Its bark is smooth and pale, often peeling in thin layers.

The plant is well adapted to coastal environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental species.
