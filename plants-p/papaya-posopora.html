Papaya posopora

Papaya posopora, commonly known as the wild papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow-orange fruit. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in well-drained soil. Its fruit is edible, though it is often smaller and less sweet than the cultivated papaya.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
