Paspalum jesuiticum

Paspalum jesuiticum, commonly known as Jesuit grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Paspalum jesuiticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in landscaping.
