Pleurothallis siphonantha

Pleurothallis siphonantha, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, with a subtle fragrance.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific ecological requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
