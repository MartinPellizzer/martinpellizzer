Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi var. flava

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi var. flava, commonly known as the yellow horned orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive horn-like lip, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its flowers can last for several months, adding long-lasting beauty to gardens or home environments.
