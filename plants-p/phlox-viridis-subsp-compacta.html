Phlox viridis subsp. compacta

Phlox viridis subsp.

compacta, commonly known as the compact green phlox, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States. It features dense clusters of small, bright green, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has upright, branching stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its compact growth habit and early blooming period make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
