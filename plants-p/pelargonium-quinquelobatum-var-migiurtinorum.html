Pelargonium quinquelobatum var. migiurtinorum

Pelargonium quinquelobatum var. migiurtinorum, commonly known as the Ethiopian geranium, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
