Pourouma populifolia

Pourouma populifolia, commonly known as the "poplar-leaf areca," is a species of palm native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that resemble those of poplar trees, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife. Its robust growth habit makes it an important component of the rainforest understory.

The botanical features of Pourouma populifolia contribute to its ecological significance in tropical ecosystems.
