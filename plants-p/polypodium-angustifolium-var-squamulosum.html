Polypodium angustifolium var. squamulosum

Polypodium angustifolium var. squamulosum, commonly known as the scaly cliff fern, is a variant of the narrow-leaved polypody.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in rocky or shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often covered with small, scale-like structures. Its fronds are usually evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This variant is characterized by its unique foliage and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.
