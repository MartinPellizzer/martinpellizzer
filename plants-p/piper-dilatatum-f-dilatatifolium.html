Piper dilatatum f. dilatatifolium

Piper dilatatum f. dilatatifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper dilatatum species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The botanical features of this variant make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
