Polygala vauthieri var. hirsutula

Polygala vauthieri var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
