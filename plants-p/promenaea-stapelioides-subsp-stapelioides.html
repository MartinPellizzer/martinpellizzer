Promenaea stapelioides subsp. stapelioides

Promenaea stapelioides subsp.

stapelioides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its latex sap contains milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
