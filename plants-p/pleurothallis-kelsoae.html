Pleurothallis kelsoae

Pleurothallis kelsoae, commonly known as the Kelso's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals. It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is part of the vast Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
