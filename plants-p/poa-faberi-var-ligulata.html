Poa faberi var. ligulata

Poa faberi var. ligulata, commonly known as the Chinese blue grass, is a cool-season grass native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce wind-pollinated flowers.
