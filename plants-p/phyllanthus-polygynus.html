Phyllanthus polygynus

Phyllanthus polygynus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling dates. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous leaflets that are opposite or alternate in arrangement.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
