Primula mucronata

Primula mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
