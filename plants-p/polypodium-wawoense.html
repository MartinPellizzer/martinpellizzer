Polypodium wawoense

Polypodium wawoense, commonly known as the Wawoense water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnate fronds that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its native habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
