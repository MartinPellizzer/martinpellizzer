Peucedanum narthex

Peucedanum narthex, commonly known as the Persian cumin or narrow-leafed parsley.

It is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its seeds are often used as a spice, similar to cumin, and have a distinct, aromatic flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
