Penstemon washoensis

Penstemon washoensis, commonly known as the Wasco penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Penstemon washoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
