Potentilla jankaeana

Potentilla jankaeana, commonly known as the Jankae's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven leaflets, and are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla jankaeana is adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
