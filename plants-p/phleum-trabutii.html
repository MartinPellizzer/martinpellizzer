Phleum trabutii

Phleum trabutii, commonly known as the Trabut's timothy, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, hairless leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Phleum trabutii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
