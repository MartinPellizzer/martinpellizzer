Pelargonium campestre

Pelargonium campestre, commonly known as the field geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. The leaves are typically round or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
