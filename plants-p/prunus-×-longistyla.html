Prunus × longistyla

Prunus × longistyla, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that ripen in late spring. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and it produces clusters of white or pink flowers in early spring.

The botanical features of Prunus × longistyla make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and parks.
