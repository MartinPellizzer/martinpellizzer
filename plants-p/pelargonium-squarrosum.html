Pelargonium squarrosum

Pelargonium squarrosum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white to silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pelargonium squarrosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
