Potentilla lupinoides subsp. integrifolia

Potentilla lupinoides subsp.

integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets that are typically smooth-edged, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
