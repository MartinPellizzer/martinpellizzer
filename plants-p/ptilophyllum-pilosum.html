Ptilophyllum pilosum

Ptilophyllum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its rhizome is creeping and often produces multiple fronds, which can grow up to several feet in length.

This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
