Penstemon brevis

Penstemon brevis, commonly known as the short beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its tubular, two-lipped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or alpine meadows. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces showy purple or pink flowers in late spring to early summer.

Penstemon brevis is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
