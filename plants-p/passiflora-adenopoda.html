Passiflora adenopoda

Passiflora adenopoda, commonly known as the woolly passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with distinctive crown-like structures and red filaments. Its fruit is a fuzzy, orange-red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Passiflora adenopoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
