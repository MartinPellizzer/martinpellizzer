Paeonia anomala var. insignis

Paeonia anomala var. insignis, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, double flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
