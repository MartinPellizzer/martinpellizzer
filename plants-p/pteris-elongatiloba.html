Pteris elongatiloba

Pteris elongatiloba, commonly known as the elongate fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically elongated and gracefully arching. The plant produces small, greenish-brown spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pteris elongatiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
