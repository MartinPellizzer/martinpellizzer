Pandorea doratoxylon

Pandorea doratoxylon, commonly known as the golden wisteria, is a climbing plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a velvety texture.

The bark is smooth and may develop a reddish or brownish hue as the plant matures.
