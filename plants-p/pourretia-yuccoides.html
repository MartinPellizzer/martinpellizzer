Pourretia yuccoides

Pourretia yuccoides, commonly known as the desert yucca, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with a fibrous, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of pale green, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are long, narrow, and blade-like, often with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of trichomes.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in xerophytic ecosystems.
