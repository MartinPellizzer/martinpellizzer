Prunus jamasakura f. odoratissima

Prunus jamasakura f. odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant cherry, is a cultivar of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to produce abundant flowers. The plant is notable for its intense, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced than that of typical cherry blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This variety is often appreciated for its ornamental value and its role in cultural events celebrating spring.
