Papaver flavum

Papaver flavum, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive, capsule-like seed pod. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.

Papaver flavum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
