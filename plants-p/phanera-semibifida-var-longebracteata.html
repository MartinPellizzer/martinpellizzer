Phanera semibifida var. longebracteata

Phanera semibifida var. longebracteata, commonly known as the long-bracted phanera, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that are often brightly colored, serving as an attractive feature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and showy inflorescences.
