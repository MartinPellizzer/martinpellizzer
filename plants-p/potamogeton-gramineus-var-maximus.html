Potamogeton gramineus var. maximus

Potamogeton gramineus var. maximus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its environment.
