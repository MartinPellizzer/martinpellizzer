Pycnocoma beillei

Pycnocoma beillei, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
