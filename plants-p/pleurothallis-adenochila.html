Pleurothallis adenochila

Pleurothallis adenochila, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, yellowish-green to orange flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
