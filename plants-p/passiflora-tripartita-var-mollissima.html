Passiflora tripartita var. mollissima

Passiflora tripartita var. mollissima, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown. The leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
