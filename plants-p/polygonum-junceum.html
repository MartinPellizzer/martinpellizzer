Polygonum junceum

Polygonum junceum, commonly known as slender knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, ditches, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers in late summer.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
