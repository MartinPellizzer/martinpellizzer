Ptycholobium contortum

Ptycholobium contortum, commonly known as the twisted acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with stipules and produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Ptycholobium contortum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
