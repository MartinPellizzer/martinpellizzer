Persicaria sphaerocephala

Persicaria sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe smartweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces round, fleshy fruits that are often used as a food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
