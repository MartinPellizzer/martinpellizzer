Portulaca goiasensis

Portulaca goiasensis, commonly known as the Goiania Purslane, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought due to its succulent structure. It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its ability to store water in its leaves.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in folk medicine for its healing benefits.
