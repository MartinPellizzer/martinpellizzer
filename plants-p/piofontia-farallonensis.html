Piofontia farallonensis

Piofontia farallonensis, commonly known as the Farallon Island lichen, is a unique lichen species native to the Farallon Islands off the coast of California.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This lichen thrives in the harsh, coastal environments characterized by high salinity and strong winds. Its structure is typically crustose, forming a thin, flat layer on rock surfaces.

Due to its limited geographic range and specialized habitat, Piofontia farallonensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
