Piptochaetium mucronatum

Piptochaetium mucronatum, commonly known as the mucronate grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with two to three flowering branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Piptochaetium mucronatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
