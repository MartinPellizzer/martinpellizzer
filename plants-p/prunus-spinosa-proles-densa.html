Prunus spinosa proles densa

Prunus spinosa proles densa, commonly known as the dense thorned cherry, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by dark red berries in late summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the bark is grey and fissured.

It is often used in hedges due to its hardy nature and dense foliage.
