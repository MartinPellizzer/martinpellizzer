Piper cochleatum

Piper cochleatum, commonly known as the spiral pepper or curled pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its spirally twisted leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by dark, peppery berries.

Piper cochleatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
