Potentilla opaca f. serotina

Potentilla opaca f. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering potentilla, is a subspecies of the shrub Potentilla opaca.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom later in the growing season, typically in late summer. It is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
