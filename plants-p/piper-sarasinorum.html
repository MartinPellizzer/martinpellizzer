Piper sarasinorum

Piper sarasinorum, commonly known as Sarasin's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits, which are small berries, contain the aromatic compounds responsible for its distinctive flavor.

Due to its unique properties, it is occasionally used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
