Papaver rhoeas var. umbonatum

Papaver rhoeas var. umbonatum, commonly known as the scarlet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its striking red flowers.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The flowers are large, showy, and consist of a single layer of petals surrounding a central stigma. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
