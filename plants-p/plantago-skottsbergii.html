Plantago skottsbergii

Plantago skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant or used in traditional medicine.
