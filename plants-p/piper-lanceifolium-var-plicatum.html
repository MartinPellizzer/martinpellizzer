Piper lanceifolium var. plicatum

Piper lanceifolium var. plicatum, commonly known as the pleated pepper plant, is a variant of the piper species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pleated or folded leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a peppery flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both garden cultivation and horticultural research.
