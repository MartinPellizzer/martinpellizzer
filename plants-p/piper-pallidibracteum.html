Piper pallidibracteum

Piper pallidibracteum, commonly known as the pale bract pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, three-lobed berries. It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotrists.
