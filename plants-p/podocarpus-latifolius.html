Podocarpus latifolius

Podocarpus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf podocarp, is a evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, aromatic wood. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping and reforestation efforts.
