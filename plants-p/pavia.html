Pavia

Pavia, commonly known as the pavia tree or *Celtis sinensis*, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Ulmaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Pavia is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
