Plinia pentapetala

Plinia pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled persimmon, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries that are edible when fully ripe. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped with serrated edges.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
