Peristylus prainii

Peristylus prainii, commonly known as the Prain's mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It grows on the branches of various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often white or greenish in color. Its berries are typically red or yellow and serve as a food source for birds and other animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host tree if left unchecked.
