Pedilanthus lycioides

Pedilanthus lycioides, commonly known as the yellow poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vibrant yellow bracts that resemble flowers. The plant produces small, greenish flowers surrounded by colorful leaf-like structures called bracts. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The yellow poinsettia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
