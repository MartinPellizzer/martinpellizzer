Phacelia patuliflora var. austrotexana

Phacelia patuliflora var. austrotexana, commonly known as the Texas phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in rocky slopes and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
