Primula bulleyana

Primula bulleyana, commonly known as the Bulleyan primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or white blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Primula bulleyana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
