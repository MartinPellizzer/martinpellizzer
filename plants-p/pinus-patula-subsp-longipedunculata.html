Pinus patula subsp. longipedunculata

Pinus patula subsp.

longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate Mexican pine, is a subspecies native to Mexico. It is a tall, evergreen conifer with slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
