Phyllanthus lindenianus var. leonardorum

Phyllanthus lindenianus var. leonardorum, commonly known as the Leonard's spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by unique, bead-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
