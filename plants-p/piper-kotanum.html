Piper kotanum

Piper kotanum, commonly known as the Katanum pepper or Katanum vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains the peppery seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
