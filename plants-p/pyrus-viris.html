Pyrus viris

Pyrus viris, commonly known as the European whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and oval, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar to apples but less commonly cultivated. Pyrus viris is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.

It is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
