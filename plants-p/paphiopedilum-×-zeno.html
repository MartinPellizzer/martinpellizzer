Paphiopedilum × zeno

Paphiopedilum × zeno, commonly known as the Zeno orchid, is a hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Paphiopedilum suaveolens and Paphiopedilum niveum.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a thick, fleshy rhizome and a short, erect stem. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinct, intricate pattern on the lip. It prefers cool, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
