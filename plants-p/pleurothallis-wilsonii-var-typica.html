Pleurothallis wilsonii var. typica

Pleurothallis wilsonii var. typica, commonly known as Wilson's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a compact rosette.

This orchid thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.
