Piper subglaucum

Piper subglaucum, commonly known as the waxy beggar's-tick, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
