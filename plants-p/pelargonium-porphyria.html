Pelargonium porphyria

Pelargonium porphyria, commonly known as the purple geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of about 30 to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Pelargonium porphyria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
