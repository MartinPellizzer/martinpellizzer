Philadelphus incanus f. hupehensis

Philadelphus incanus f. hupehensis, commonly known as the Hupeh honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The foliage turns yellow in autumn, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
