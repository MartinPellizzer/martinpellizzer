Paramicrorhynchus procumbens

Paramicrorhynchus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping dwarf sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, grass-like stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its habitat.
