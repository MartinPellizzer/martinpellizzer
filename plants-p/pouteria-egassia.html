Pouteria egassia

Pouteria egassia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's most notable feature is its hard, woody fruit, which contains a single large seed known as the Brazil nut. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight.

It plays an important ecological role in the Amazon rainforest, supporting various wildlife species.
