Polygala ukambica var. transvaalensis

Polygala ukambica var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland or shrubland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.

The species is also of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological role and adaptability to arid environments.
