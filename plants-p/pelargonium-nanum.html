Pelargonium nanum

Pelargonium nanum, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically velvety in texture and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces clusters of tiny, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, colorful addition to their landscape.
