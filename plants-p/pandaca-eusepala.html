Pandaca eusepala

Pandaca eusepala, commonly known as the silverleaf pandaca, is a rare and endemic plant species found in specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming during the early summer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly waxy texture that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Pandaca eusepala is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
