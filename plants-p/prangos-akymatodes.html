Prangos akymatodes

Prangos akymatodes, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

However, it can also become invasive in certain regions, posing a challenge to local ecosystems.
