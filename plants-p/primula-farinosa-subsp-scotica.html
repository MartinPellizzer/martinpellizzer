Primula farinosa subsp. scotica

Primula farinosa subsp.

scotica, commonly known as the Scottish primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Scottish Highlands and northern England. It features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, flour-like coating, giving it its scientific name "farinosa," meaning floury.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming from April to June. It thrives in damp, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

Its habitat and unique appearance make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species in its native range.
