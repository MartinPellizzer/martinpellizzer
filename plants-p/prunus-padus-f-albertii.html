Prunus padus f. albertii

Prunus padus f. albertii, commonly known as the Albert's bird cherry, is a rare and ornamental variant of the bird cherry species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often twisted fruiting structures that resemble small, clustered cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with slender, grayish-brown branches. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.

This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental appeal.
