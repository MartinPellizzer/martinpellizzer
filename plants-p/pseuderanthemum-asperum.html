Pseuderanthemum asperum

Pseuderanthemum asperum, commonly known as rough pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a red calyx. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in warm climates.
