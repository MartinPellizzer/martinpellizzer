Primula modesta

Primula modesta, commonly known as the modest primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps retain moisture.

Primula modesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
