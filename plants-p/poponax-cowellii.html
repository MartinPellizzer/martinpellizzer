Poponax cowellii

Poponax cowellii, commonly known as the Cowell's poponax, is a rare and endemic plant species native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers during the spring and early summer months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to coastal environments, including salt tolerance and drought resistance.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
