Pelargonium curtisii

Pelargonium curtisii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its compact growth habit and vibrant flower colors make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Pelargonium curtisii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
