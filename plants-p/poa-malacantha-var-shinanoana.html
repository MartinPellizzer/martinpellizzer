Poa malacantha var. shinanoana

Poa malacantha var. shinanoana, commonly known as the Shinano mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
