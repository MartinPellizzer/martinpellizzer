Piper ventoleranum

Piper ventoleranum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
