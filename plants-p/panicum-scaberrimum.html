Panicum scaberrimum

Panicum scaberrimum, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains of pollen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage crop for livestock.
