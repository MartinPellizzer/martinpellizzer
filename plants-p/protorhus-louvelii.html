Protorhus louvelii

Protorhus louvelii, commonly known as the Louvel's kalahari orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
