Pouzolzia ovalis

Pouzolzia ovalis, commonly known as the oval pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Pouzolzia ovalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
