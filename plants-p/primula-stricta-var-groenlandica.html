Primula stricta var. groenlandica

Primula stricta var. groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland primrose, is a herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, temperate climates. The plant features upright, slender stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine and tundra ecosystems.
