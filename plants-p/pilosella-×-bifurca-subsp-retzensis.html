Pilosella × bifurca subsp. retzensis

Pilosella × bifurca subsp.

retzensis, commonly known as the Alpine hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
