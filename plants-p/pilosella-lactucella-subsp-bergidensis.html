Pilosella lactucella subsp. bergidensis

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

bergidensis, commonly known as the Bergidensian milk thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often growing at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
