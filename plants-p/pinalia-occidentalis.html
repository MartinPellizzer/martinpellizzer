Pinalia occidentalis

Pinalia occidentalis, commonly known as the western pinalia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in the spring. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Pinalia occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
