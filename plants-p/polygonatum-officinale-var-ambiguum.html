Polygonatum officinale var. ambiguum

Polygonatum officinale var. ambiguum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped yellow flowers in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stem. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in summer, containing several seeds.

It thrives in shaded, moist woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
