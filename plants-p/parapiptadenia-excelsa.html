Parapiptadenia excelsa

Parapiptadenia excelsa, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree or *Pau Brasil*, is a leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, straight trunk and dark red bark, which was historically used by indigenous peoples for dyeing textiles. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing seeds. It is also valued for its durable wood and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
