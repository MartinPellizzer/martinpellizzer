Primula filiformis

Primula filiformis, commonly known as the slender primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, supporting clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow or white, with five petals and prominent yellow stamens. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
