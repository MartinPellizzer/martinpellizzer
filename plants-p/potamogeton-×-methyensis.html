Potamogeton × methyensis

Potamogeton × methyensis, commonly known as the peat moss pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to wetland and peatland environments in Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often submerged and can form dense vegetation in still or slow-moving water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is monoecious, meaning it has both male and female reproductive structures on the same plant. Potamogeton × methyensis plays an important role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of nutrient-poor, acidic water conditions in peat bogs and marshes.
