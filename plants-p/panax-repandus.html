Panax repandus

Panax repandus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots that resemble those of true ginseng. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are compound, with a lobed structure, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.
