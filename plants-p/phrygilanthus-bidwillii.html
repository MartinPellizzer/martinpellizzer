Phrygilanthus bidwillii

Phrygilanthus bidwillii, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 20 meters tall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

The tree is also valued for its ability to attract birds and butterflies due to its nectar-rich blooms.
