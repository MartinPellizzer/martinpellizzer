Podocarpus capuronii subsp. woltzii

Podocarpus capuronii subsp.

woltzii, commonly known as the Woltz's podocarp, is a member of the Podocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on the upper surface. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
