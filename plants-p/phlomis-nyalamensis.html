Phlomis nyalamensis

Phlomis nyalamensis, commonly known as the Nyalam phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and Tibet.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
