Photinia mollis var. angustifolia

Photinia mollis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features attractive, narrow, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that persist into winter. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.

The variety is relatively low maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types.
