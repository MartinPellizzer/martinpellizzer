Polygala arvensis var. hirsuta

Polygala arvensis var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy field milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender stems with hairy foliage, and its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, often with distinctive spurred petals. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
