Phragmites cinctus

Phragmites cinctus, commonly known as the narrowleaf common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often seen swaying in the wind. It plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Phragmites cinctus is also known for its ability to absorb pollutants, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
