Passiflora fissurosa

Passiflora fissurosa, commonly known as the fissured passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a serrated margin and a rough texture.

Passiflora fissurosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
