Palaquium hispidum

Palaquium hispidum, commonly known as the rough palaquium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Palaquium hispidum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.
