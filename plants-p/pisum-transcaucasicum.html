Pisum transcaucasicum

Pisum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Transcaucasian pea, is a species of legume native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are typically blue or violet, and the plant produces pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural value.
