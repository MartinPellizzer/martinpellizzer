Pterocymbium tinctorium var. javanicum

Pterocymbium tinctorium var. javanicum, commonly known as the Java turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and vibrant, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in inflorescences.

The rhizomes are valued for their medicinal and dyeing properties, traditionally used in traditional medicine and natural dyeing processes.
