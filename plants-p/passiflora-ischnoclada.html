Passiflora ischnoclada

Passiflora ischnoclada, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique floral structure has made it a subject of interest in both botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
