Pilosella bauhini subsp. radiocaulis

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

radiocaulis, commonly known as the radiate pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
