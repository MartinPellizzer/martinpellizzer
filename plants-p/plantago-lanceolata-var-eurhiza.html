Plantago lanceolata var. eurhiza

Plantago lanceolata var. eurhiza, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, disturbed soils.

This species is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats where it can establish quickly.
