Pogonia affinis

Pogonia affinis, commonly known as the pink fairywand, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pink or reddish-purple stems and elongated, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fragrant, pinkish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Pogonia affinis is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
