Potentilla aemulans

Potentilla aemulans, commonly known as the mimic potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-30 centimeters, with hairy stems and small, lobed leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Potentilla aemulans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
