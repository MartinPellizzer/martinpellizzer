Phaca sulcata

Phaca sulcata, commonly known as the African violets, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a rosette of leaves that form a dense, cushion-like structure. Its flowers are usually borne on tall, slender stalks and can bloom multiple times throughout the growing season.

Phaca sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
