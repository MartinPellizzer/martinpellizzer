Phlox buckleyi

Phlox buckleyi, commonly known as Buckley's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom.

Buckley's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
