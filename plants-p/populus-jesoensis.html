Populus jesoensis

Populus jesoensis, commonly known as the Korean poplar, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Populus jesoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
