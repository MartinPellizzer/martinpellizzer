Penstemon eriantherus

Penstemon eriantherus, commonly known as the wooly penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. This perennial plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

The plant is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
