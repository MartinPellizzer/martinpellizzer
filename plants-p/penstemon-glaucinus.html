Penstemon glaucinus

Penstemon glaucinus, commonly known as the blue penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon glaucinus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
