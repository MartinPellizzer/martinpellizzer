Paspalum delavayi

Paspalum delavayi, commonly known as Delavay's grass, is a perennial grass species native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

Paspalum delavayi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
