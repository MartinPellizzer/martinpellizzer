Passiflora octandra var. glaberrima

Passiflora octandra var. glaberrima, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
