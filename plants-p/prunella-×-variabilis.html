Prunella × variabilis

Prunella × variabilis, commonly known as variable selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Prunella × variabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
