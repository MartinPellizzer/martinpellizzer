Potentilla × intermedia

Potentilla × intermedia, commonly known as Shrub Cinquefoil, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Potentilla fruticosa and Potentilla argyrophylla.

It is a hardy, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, upright growth with dark green, glossy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to elliptical in shape.
