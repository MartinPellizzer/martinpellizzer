Polygala pubescens var. gracilis

Polygala pubescens var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive, spurred structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its hairy stems and leaves. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
