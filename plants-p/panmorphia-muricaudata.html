Panmorphia muricaudata

Panmorphia muricaudata, commonly known as the spiky velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, velvety foliage covered in fine, hair-like structures.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are often described as having a soft, almost silken texture, which contrasts with its sharp, spiky edges. Its flowers, though small, are usually pale in color and arranged in clusters.

This species is notable for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it a resilient addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
