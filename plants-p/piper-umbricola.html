Piper umbricola

Piper umbricola, commonly known as the dark soil pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in humid, shaded forest understories. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that produce peppery fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
