Prosartes viridescens

Prosartes viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feather-like fronds that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Prosartes viridescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining ecological balance in forest ecosystems.
