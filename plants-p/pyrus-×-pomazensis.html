Pyrus × pomazensis

Pyrus × pomazensis, commonly known as the hybrid pear.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pyrus communis (European pear) and Pyrus pyraster (wild pear). This plant is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive foliage and showy white flowers in spring. The fruit is typically small and not suitable for consumption but is sometimes used in breeding programs.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
