Polygonatum odoratum f. variegatum

Polygonatum odoratum f. variegatum, commonly known as the variegated Solomon's seal, is a hybrid plant that exhibits striking variegation in its foliage.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with white and green markings, which add visual interest to the garden. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, attractive clumps over time.
