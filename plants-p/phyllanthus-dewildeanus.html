Phyllanthus dewildeanus

Phyllanthus dewildeanus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often gland-dotted. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna and woodland environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional medicine.
