Phthirusa pyrifolia var. terminalis

Phthirusa pyrifolia var. terminalis, commonly known as the Japanese persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its fruits are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand mild frosts.
