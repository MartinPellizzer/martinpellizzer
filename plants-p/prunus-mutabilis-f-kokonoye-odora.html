Prunus mutabilis f. kokonoye-odora

Prunus mutabilis f. kokonoye-odora, commonly known as the fragrant Japanese cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that gradually change color as they bloom, creating a striking visual display. The plant is noted for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is more pronounced in this particular form. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities.
