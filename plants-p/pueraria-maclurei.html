Pueraria maclurei

Pueraria maclurei, commonly known as the Kwangsi milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, with compound leaves and showy purple or white flowers. The plant is characterized by its extensive root system, which can become invasive in certain environments. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pueraria maclurei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
