Pomazota capitata

Pomazota capitata, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Pomazota capitata is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable forage plant and a potential weed.
