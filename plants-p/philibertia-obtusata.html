Philibertia obtusata

Philibertia obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and rounded, often with a waxy coating. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Philibertia obtusata is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
