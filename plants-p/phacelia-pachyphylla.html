Phacelia pachyphylla

Phacelia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its thick, fleshy leaves help it retain moisture in dry conditions.

Phacelia pachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
