Potentilla indica f. albicaput

Potentilla indica f. albicaput, commonly known as the white-headed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive white inflorescence.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. The plant typically grows in alpine or rocky habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in a head-like arrangement.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
