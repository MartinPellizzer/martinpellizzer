Primula sikkimensis subsp. pseudosikkimensis

Primula sikkimensis subsp.

pseudosikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim primrose, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal. It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.

The plant features basal rosettes of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. Its flowers are bilaterally symmetrical, with five petals and prominent stamens.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
