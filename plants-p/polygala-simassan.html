Polygala simassan

Polygala simassan, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its small, yellowish flowers are often surrounded by hairy bracts, giving it its common name.

Polygala simassan is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
