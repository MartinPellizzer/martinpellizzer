Plantago coronopus subvar. multipartita

Plantago coronopus subvar. multipartita, commonly known as the clustered plantain, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in dense spikes.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
