Pulicaria gracilis

Pulicaria gracilis, commonly known as slender tansy, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Pulicaria gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
