Parasarcochilus weinthalii

Parasarcochilus weinthalii, commonly known as the Weinthal's leech-fern, is a rare and unusual member of the fern family.

The plant is native to a specific region in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sori that are closely clustered and protected by indusia.
