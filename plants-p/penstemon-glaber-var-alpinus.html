Penstemon glaber var. alpinus

Penstemon glaber var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, and it is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures and harsh alpine conditions.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
