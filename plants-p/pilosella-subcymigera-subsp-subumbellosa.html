Pilosella subcymigera subsp. subumbellosa

Pilosella subcymigera subsp.

subumbellosa, commonly known as the subumbellate whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and clustered in cymes.

This subspecies is distinguished by its subumbellate inflorescence, which gives it its common name.
