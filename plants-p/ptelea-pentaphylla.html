Ptelea pentaphylla

Ptelea pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed ash, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically consisting of five leaflets, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

The botanical features of Ptelea pentaphylla make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
