Polygala caucasica

Polygala caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
