Paeonia officinalis subvar. banatica

Paeonia officinalis subvar. banatica, commonly known as the Banat peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Balkan region.

It features large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces follicles containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
