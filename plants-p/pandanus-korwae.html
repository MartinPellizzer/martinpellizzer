Pandanus korwae

Pandanus korwae, commonly known as the Hainan screw pine, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hainan in China.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often spirally arranged. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in branched inflorescences. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Pandanus korwae is adapted to a tropical climate and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
