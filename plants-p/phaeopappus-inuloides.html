Phaeopappus inuloides

Phaeopappus inuloides, commonly known as the yellow pappus.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its yellow flowers and feathery pappus, which aids in wind dispersal of its seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of arid regions.
