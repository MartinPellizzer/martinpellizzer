Papaver × intercedens

Papaver × intercedens, commonly known as the hybrid poppy, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a cross between Papaver rhoeas (common poppy) and Papaver orientale (oriental poppy), resulting in a plant with striking, large, showy flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, with a distinctive saucer-shaped corona. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
