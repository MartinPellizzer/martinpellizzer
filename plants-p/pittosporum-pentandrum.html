Pittosporum pentandrum

Pittosporum pentandrum, commonly known as the five-angled pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which bloom in spring and are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Pittosporum pentandrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
