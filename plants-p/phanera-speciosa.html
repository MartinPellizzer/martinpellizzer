Phanera speciosa

Phanera speciosa, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Phanera speciosa is also valued for its fast growth and ability to provide shade in landscaped areas.
