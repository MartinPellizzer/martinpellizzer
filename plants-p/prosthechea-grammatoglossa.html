Prosthechea grammatoglossa

Prosthechea grammatoglossa, commonly known as the "grammatoglossa orchid," is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing several leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which are typically arranged in a raceme. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Prosthechea grammatoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
