Passiflora edulis f. flavicarpa

Passiflora edulis f. flavicarpa, commonly known as the yellow passionfruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange rind and segmented, aromatic pulp. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white with blue and purple markings. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

This variety is prized for its unique flavor and is widely cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
