Pyrus communis var. ovalis

Pyrus communis var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-shaped pear, is a cultivar of the European pear.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruit that typically ripens in late summer. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is valued for its sweet, aromatic fruit and is often used in both culinary and ornamental settings.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
