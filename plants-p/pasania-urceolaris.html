Pasania urceolaris

Pasania urceolaris, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and often have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
