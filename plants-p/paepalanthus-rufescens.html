Paepalanthus rufescens

Paepalanthus rufescens, commonly known as the red-hued cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spiny growth form. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
