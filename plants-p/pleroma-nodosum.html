Pleroma nodosum

Pleroma nodosum, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
