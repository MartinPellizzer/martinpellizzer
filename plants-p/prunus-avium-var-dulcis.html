Prunus avium var. dulcis

Prunus avium var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It produces small, round fruits with a sweet, juicy flesh and a hard pit. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves and white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit develops from the flower and ripens in summer, typically between June and August.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and is valued for its ornamental appeal as well.
