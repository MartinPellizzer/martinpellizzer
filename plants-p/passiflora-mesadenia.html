Passiflora mesadenia

Passiflora mesadenia, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora mesadenia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
