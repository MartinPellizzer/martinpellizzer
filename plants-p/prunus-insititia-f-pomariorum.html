Prunus insititia f. pomariorum

Prunus insititia f. pomariorum, commonly known as the black-cherry or sour cherry, is a fruiting shrub or small tree native to North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are typically tart and used in cooking or winemaking. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits.
