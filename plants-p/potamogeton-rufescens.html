Potamogeton rufescens

Potamogeton rufescens, commonly known as redstem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

Potamogeton rufescens plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
