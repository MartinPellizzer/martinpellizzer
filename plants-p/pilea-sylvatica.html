Pilea sylvatica

Pilea sylvatica, commonly known as the silver ponytail plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Urticaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its unique, cascading foliage with long, slender leaves that have a silvery underside. It produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are typically followed by round, red berries.

Pilea sylvatica is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
