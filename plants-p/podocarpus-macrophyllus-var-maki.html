Podocarpus macrophyllus var. maki

Podocarpus macrophyllus var. maki, commonly known as Japanese yew or Maki yew, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It has a distinctive appearance with its dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Podocarpaceae family.
