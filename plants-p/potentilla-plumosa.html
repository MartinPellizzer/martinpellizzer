Potentilla plumosa

Potentilla plumosa, commonly known as the silvery cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
