Potamogeton densus var. lanceolatus

Potamogeton densus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
