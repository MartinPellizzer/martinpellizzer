Pseudocaryophyllus mutabilis f. macrophyllus

Pseudocaryophyllus mutabilis f. macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leafed sandmat, is a succulent plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is native to arid regions of southern Africa. The plant exhibits a unique ability to change color with age, often shifting from green to red or purple. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
