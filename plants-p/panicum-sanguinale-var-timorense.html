Panicum sanguinale var. timorense

Panicum sanguinale var. timorense, commonly known as Timor bloodgrass, is a flowering plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red-colored inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of red bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
