Prunus brachypetala var. brachypetala

Prunus brachypetala var. brachypetala, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
