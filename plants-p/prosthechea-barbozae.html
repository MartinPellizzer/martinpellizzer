Prosthechea barbozae

Prosthechea barbozae, commonly known as the Barboza's orchid, is a rare epiphytic orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces small, pale green to white flowers. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and possesses a unique structure called a prosthechea, which is a specialized aerial shoot. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
