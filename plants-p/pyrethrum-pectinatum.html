Pyrethrum pectinatum

Pyrethrum pectinatum, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, globular seed heads that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pyrethrum pectinatum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
