Plocama inopinata

Plocama inopinata, commonly known as the creeping plocama, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Plocama inopinata is characterized by its creeping growth habit and is often found in wetland habitats.
