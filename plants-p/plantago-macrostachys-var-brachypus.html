Plantago macrostachys var. brachypus

Plantago macrostachys var. brachypus, commonly known as the short-stalked plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish flowers. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along stream banks.
