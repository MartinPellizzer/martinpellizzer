Potentilla rubricaulis var. furcata

Potentilla rubricaulis var. furcata, commonly known as the red-stemmed potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in high-altitude environments. This variety is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats or low shrubs.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient plant within its ecological niche.
