Paliurus spina-christi var. rotundifolius

Paliurus spina-christi var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leafed Chinese thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
