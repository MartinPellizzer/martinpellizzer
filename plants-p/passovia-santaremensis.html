Passovia santaremensis

Passovia santaremensis, commonly known as the Santarem passiflora, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family, which includes many species known for their showy, complex flowers. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, five-lobed flowers that display a mix of colors, often red and yellow. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
