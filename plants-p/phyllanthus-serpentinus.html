Phyllanthus serpentinus

Phyllanthus serpentinus, commonly known as serpent's tail, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus serpentinus is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
