Potentilla verticillaris

Potentilla verticillaris, commonly known as the whorled cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to the mountainous regions of western North America, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
