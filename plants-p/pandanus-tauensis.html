Pandanus tauensis

Pandanus tauensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, forming a dense rosette at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often used in traditional crafts and as ornamental elements. Its roots and leaves have been utilized in various cultural practices, including basket weaving and medicinal applications.

Pandanus tauensis thrives in coastal and wetland environments, adapting well to humid and saline conditions.
