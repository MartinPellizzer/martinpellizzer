Panicum unciphyllum var. meridionale

Panicum unciphyllum var. meridionale, commonly known as the southern dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like foliage with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale to golden florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy choice for native landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
