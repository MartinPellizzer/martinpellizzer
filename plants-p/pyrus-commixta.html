Pyrus commixta

Pyrus commixta, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by round, yellow or yellow-green fruits. The fruit is typically harvested in late summer and is used in traditional Chinese medicine and culinary applications.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in gardens.
