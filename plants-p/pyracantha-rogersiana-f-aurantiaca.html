Pyracantha rogersiana f. aurantiaca

Pyracantha rogersiana f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden-fruited firethorn, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces showy, orange-red berries that persist through winter, adding visual interest to the landscape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and colorful fruit, making it a popular choice for garden borders and hedges.
