Pappostipa choconensis

Pappostipa choconensis, commonly known as the Chocones grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the grass family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
