Phryna hamzaoglui

Phryna hamzaoglui, commonly known as the Hamzaoglu's ghost frog, is a unique species of frog native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a translucent skin that reveals a network of blood vessels, giving it a ghost-like appearance. This species is adapted to cool, moist environments and is typically found in forested areas with abundant vegetation. Its diet consists mainly of small invertebrates, and it plays a crucial role in the local ecosystem as both predator and prey.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Phryna hamzaoglui is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
