Panicum leucoblepharis var. pubescens

Panicum leucoblepharis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces fluffy, light-colored seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or wool, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and horticultural applications.
