Polemonium boreale subsp. macranthum

Polemonium boreale subsp.

macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered bluebeard, is a perennial herb native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is notable for its showy inflorescences and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
