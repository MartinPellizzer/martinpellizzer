Paphiopedilum venustum f. measuresianum

Paphiopedilum venustum f. measuresianum, commonly known as the Measuresian orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety petals and a unique, intricate lip that resembles a pouch, a feature common in the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a combination of white, yellow, and green hues, with a distinctive yellow and green lip.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and rarity in horticultural collections.
