Pinguicula hirtiflora

Pinguicula hirtiflora, commonly known as the hairy-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and rocky crevices. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.

Pinguicula hirtiflora is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cool, humid conditions.
