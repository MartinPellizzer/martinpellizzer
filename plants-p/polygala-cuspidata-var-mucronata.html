Polygala cuspidata var. mucronata

Polygala cuspidata var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate floral display.
