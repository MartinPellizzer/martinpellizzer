Polygonum bellardii var. gracilius

Polygonum bellardii var. gracilius, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tinge, and it produces small, greenish flowers in summer.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate waterlogged conditions.
