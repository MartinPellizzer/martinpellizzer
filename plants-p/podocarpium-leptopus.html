Podocarpium leptopus

Podocarpium leptopus, commonly known as the slender podocarp, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Podocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and conifer-like appearance, with needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, clustered cones that contain both male and female reproductive structures. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
