Pisum sativum var. postrourkeanum

Pisum sativum var. postrourkeanum, commonly known as the Post Rourke pea, is a botanical variety of the pea plant.

It is characterized by its unique pod shape and coloration, which differ from the standard garden pea. This variety is primarily grown for its ornamental value rather than culinary use. The plant typically produces small, vibrant flowers and pods that mature to a striking red or purple hue.

It thrives in cool climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
