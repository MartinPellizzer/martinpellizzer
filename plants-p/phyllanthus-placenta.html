Phyllanthus placenta

Phyllanthus placenta, commonly known as false sandalwood, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
