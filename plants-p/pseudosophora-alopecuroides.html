Pseudosophora alopecuroides

Pseudosophora alopecuroides, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by small, woody pods containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.

The plant has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
