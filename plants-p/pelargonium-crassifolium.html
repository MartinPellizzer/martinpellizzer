Pelargonium crassifolium

Pelargonium crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming during the warmer months. It is a succulent plant, belonging to the Geraniaceae family, and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
