Panicum capillare subsp. barbipulvinatum

Panicum capillare subsp.

barbipulvinatum, commonly known as the barbed panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that are often wind-dispersed.

The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and the inflorescences are often adorned with barbed structures that aid in seed attachment.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in stabilizing soil in degraded areas.
