Plectranthus tetradenifolius

Plectranthus tetradenifolius, commonly known as the woolly mint or African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale violet flowers. The plant belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is often grown for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus tetradenifolius is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
