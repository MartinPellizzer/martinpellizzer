Prunus cerasifera var. pissardii

Prunus cerasifera var. pissardii, commonly known as the Pissard cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, edible fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for hedging and ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
