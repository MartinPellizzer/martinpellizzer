Populus girinensis

Populus girinensis, commonly known as the Japanese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typical of the Populus genus. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Populus girinensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its elegant appearance and adaptability to various climates.
