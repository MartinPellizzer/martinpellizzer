Polygala virgata var. sprengeliana

Polygala virgata var. sprengeliana, commonly known as the Sprengel's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in local pollination networks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
