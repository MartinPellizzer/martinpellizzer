Primula imperialis

Primula imperialis, commonly known as the imperial primrose, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically displaying shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare flora.
