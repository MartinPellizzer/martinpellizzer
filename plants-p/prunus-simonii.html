Prunus simonii

Prunus simonii, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to northern China.

It is characterized by its slender branches, smooth bark, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. Prunus simonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, arid conditions.

It is also noted for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
