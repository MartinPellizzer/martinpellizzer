Pleiarina laevigata

Pleiarina laevigata, commonly known as the smooth pleiara, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
