Parasenecio maximowiczianus var. alatus

Parasenecio maximowiczianus var. alatus, commonly known as the winged parasenecio, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant is notable for its distinctive winged leaves, which give it its varietal name *alatus*, meaning "winged" in Latin. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
