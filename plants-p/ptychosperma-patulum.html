Ptychosperma patulum

Ptychosperma patulum, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Its slow growth and long lifespan make it a valuable but endangered species in its native habitat.

The plant's ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils highlights its ecological adaptability.
