Polystichum lonchitiforme

Polystichum lonchitiforme, commonly known as the broad-leafed wood fern, is a species of fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, evergreen fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, elongated shape that gives the plant its common name. It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices.

The plant's rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients for extended periods.
