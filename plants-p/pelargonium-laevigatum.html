Pelargonium laevigatum

Pelargonium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its blooming period extends from spring to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
