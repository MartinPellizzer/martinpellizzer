Poa suavis

Poa suavis, commonly known as sweet grass, is a flowering plant species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Sweet grass is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

It is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
