Phylica marlothii var. levynsiae

Phylica marlothii var. levynsiae, commonly known as the Levyns' bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and grey-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in rounded heads, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland fynbos regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
