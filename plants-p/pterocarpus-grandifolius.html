Pterocarpus grandifolius

Pterocarpus grandifolius, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces woody pods containing flat, dark seeds. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
