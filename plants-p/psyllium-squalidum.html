Psyllium squalidum

Psyllium squalidum, commonly known as the stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its strong odor, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
