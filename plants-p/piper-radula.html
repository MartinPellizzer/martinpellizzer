Piper radula

Piper radula, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its botanical structure is characterized by a climbing habit, with aerial roots that help it adhere to supports.

The plant's morphology and growth patterns make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
