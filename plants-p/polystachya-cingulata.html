Polystachya cingulata

Polystachya cingulata, commonly known as the ribbon orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated, ribbon-like leaves that often form dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or near water sources.

Polystachya cingulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
