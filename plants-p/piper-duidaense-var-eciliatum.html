Piper duidaense var. eciliatum

Piper duidaense var. eciliatum, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pepper plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants known for their aromatic and medicinal properties. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical traits, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
