Potentilla lycopodioides var. megalopetala

Potentilla lycopodioides var. megalopetala, commonly known as the giant petal potentilla, is a rare and distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or red petals that can reach up to 5 centimeters in length, making it visually striking. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its unique floral morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
