Paronychia illecebroides var. illecebroides

Paronychia illecebroides var. illecebroides, commonly known as the sticky weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is known for its sticky secretions, which can adhere to animals and aid in seed dispersal.
