Persica vulgaris duracina

Persica vulgaris duracina, commonly known as the Duracina peach.

This plant is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its robust growth and high yield of fruit. It features a spreading canopy with thornless branches, and its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy green upper surface. The flowers are white with a subtle pink tinge, and the fruit is typically large, sweet, and juicy.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
