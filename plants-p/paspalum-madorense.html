Paspalum madorense

Paspalum madorense, commonly known as the Madore's grass, is a species of grass native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually clustered at the ends of the stems. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks.

Paspalum madorense is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
