Pelargonium distichum

Pelargonium distichum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows in a compact, bushy form and is often cultivated as a decorative garden plant. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium distichum is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
