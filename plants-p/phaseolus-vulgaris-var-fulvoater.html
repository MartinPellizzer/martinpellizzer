Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvoater

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvoater, commonly known as the golden runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange pod color and elongated, runner-like growth habit. The plant typically produces edible beans that are rich in protein and fiber, making it a valuable crop for both nutrition and agriculture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary uses.
