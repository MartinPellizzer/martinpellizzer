Pellionia subundulata

Pellionia subundulata, commonly known as the velvetleaf pellionia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Pellionia subundulata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
