Poterium officinale

Poterium officinale, commonly known as common tansy, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including thujone and camphor, which contribute to its historical use in herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
