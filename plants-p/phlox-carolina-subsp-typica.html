Phlox carolina subsp. typica

Phlox carolina subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Carolina phlox, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on tall, branching stems.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valued ornamental plant.
