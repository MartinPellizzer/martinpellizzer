Paraboea divaricata

Paraboea divaricata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, pale yellow flowers and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It is known for its ability to thrive in humid climates and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Paraboea divaricata is also noted for its attractive, fragrant blooms that appear in clusters.
