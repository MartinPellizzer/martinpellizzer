Panax confertifolius

Panax confertifolius, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the forests of China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, clustered root system and dark green, alternate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with smooth, reddish-brown stems that become woody with age. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels, and it produces red berries that contain seeds.

The plant is valued for its roots, which are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
