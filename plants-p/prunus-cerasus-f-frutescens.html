Prunus cerasus f. frutescens

Prunus cerasus f. frutescens, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is suitable for a variety of climates and requires minimal maintenance.
