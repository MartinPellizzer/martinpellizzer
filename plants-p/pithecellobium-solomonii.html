Pithecellobium solomonii

Pithecellobium solomonii, commonly known as Solomon's pita, is a tropical shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and disturbed areas, thriving in sandy or well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
