Pluchea verrucosa

Pluchea verrucosa, commonly known as roughleaf sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
