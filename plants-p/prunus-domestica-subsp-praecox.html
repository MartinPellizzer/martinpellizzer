Prunus domestica subsp. praecox

Prunus domestica subsp.

praecox, commonly known as the early sweet cherry, is a subspecies of the domesticated cherry tree. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and early fruiting, typically producing cherries in late spring.

The leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are white with five petals. The fruit is small to medium in size, with a sweet flavor and a firm, juicy texture.

This subspecies is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
