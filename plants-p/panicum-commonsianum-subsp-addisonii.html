Panicum commonsianum subsp. addisonii

Panicum commonsianum subsp.

addisonii, commonly known as the Addison's panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, a common adaptation in grasses. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
