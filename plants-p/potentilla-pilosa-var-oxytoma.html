Potentilla pilosa var. oxytoma

Potentilla pilosa var. oxytoma, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features upright stems covered in dense, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and are also hairy.

This species is adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens.
