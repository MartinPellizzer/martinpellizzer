Phalaris tuberosa var. clausonis

Phalaris tuberosa var. clausonis, commonly known as the tuberous phalaris, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and the production of tubers underground, which serve as storage organs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of spikelets, which are adorned with small, grass-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
