Polygonum tomentosum var. blepharanthum

Polygonum tomentosum var. blepharanthum, commonly known as the silky knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
