Polygonum biscatilium

Polygonum biscatilium, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and often swollen nodes. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Polygonum biscatilium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
