Prunus persica var. xanthocarpa

Prunus persica var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow almond, is a variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive yellow fruit.

The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Central Asia. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth margin, and the flowers are typically white or pale pink. The fruit, while not as commonly cultivated as the standard peach, is valued for its ornamental qualities and unique coloration.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
