Prunus domestica var. aubertiana

Prunus domestica var. aubertiana, commonly known as the Aubertiana plum, is a variety of European plum characterized by its compact growth habit and high fruiting capacity.

It produces medium-sized, sweet, and juicy fruits with a yellowish-orange skin and a high sugar content. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in autumn, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use, making it a popular choice for both gardens and orchards.

This variety is also noted for its disease resistance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
