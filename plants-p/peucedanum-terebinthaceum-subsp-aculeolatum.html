Peucedanum terebinthaceum subsp. aculeolatum

Peucedanum terebinthaceum subsp.

aculeolatum, commonly known as the prickly cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is found in mountainous regions of Europe. It has a taproot system and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its distinctive prickles on the stems and leaves help in identifying the species.
