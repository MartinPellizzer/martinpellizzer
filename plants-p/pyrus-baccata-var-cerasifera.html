Pyrus baccata var. cerasifera

Pyrus baccata var. cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart berries that resemble cherries, hence its common name. The plant produces white, fragrant flowers in early spring, followed by the edible fruits in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are dark green and glossy, providing visual interest throughout the growing season.
