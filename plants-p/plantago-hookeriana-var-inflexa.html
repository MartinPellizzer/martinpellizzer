Plantago hookeriana var. inflexa

Plantago hookeriana var. inflexa, commonly known as Hooker's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the high Andes of South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces erect flowering stems bearing dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
