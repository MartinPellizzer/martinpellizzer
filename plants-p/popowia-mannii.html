Popowia mannii

Popowia mannii, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Popowia mannii is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
