Pomax umbellata var. carnosula

Pomax umbellata var. carnosula, commonly known as the fleshy umbellate plant, is a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered, umbrella-like inflorescence, which consists of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features fleshy, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
