Primula pedemontana subsp. apennina

Primula pedemontana subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, preferring well-drained and sunny habitats.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and the plant is characterized by its compact growth habit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
