Phaseolus discolor

Phaseolus discolor, commonly known as the coffee bean plant, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Phaseolus discolor is an important crop in regions where coffee cultivation is practiced.
