Pavetta urophylla subsp. urophylla

Pavetta urophylla subsp.

urophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has simple, opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glabrous.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
