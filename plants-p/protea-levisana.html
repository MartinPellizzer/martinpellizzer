Protea levisana

Protea levisana, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale pink floral bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in fynbos vegetation. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sugarbirds, which feed on its nectar.

The protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
