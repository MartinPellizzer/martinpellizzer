Primula gracilipes var. searightii

Primula gracilipes var. searightii, commonly known as the Searight's slender primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, while the flowers are usually pale pink to white in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
