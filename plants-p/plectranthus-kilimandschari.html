Plectranthus kilimandschari

Plectranthus kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for gardens in arid and semi-arid regions.
