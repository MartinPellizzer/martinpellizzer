Pleurothallis rhodoglossa

Pleurothallis rhodoglossa, commonly known as the red-lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers that often feature a vibrant red lip. The plant typically produces small, clustered inflorescences with flowers that are predominantly white or pale pink, with the lip being a distinctive red or orange color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
