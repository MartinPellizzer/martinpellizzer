Pabellonia

Pabellonia, commonly known as the "moonflower," is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates. It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pabellonia requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun for optimal growth.
