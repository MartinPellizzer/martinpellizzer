Phymatopsis rotunda

Phymatopsis rotunda, commonly known as the roundleaf phymatopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying desert flora.
