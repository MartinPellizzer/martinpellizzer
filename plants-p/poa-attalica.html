Poa attalica

Poa attalica, commonly known as the Attalica grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Poa attalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
