Pseudocherleria brotheriana

Pseudocherleria brotheriana, commonly known as the Brother's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, ghost-like appearance, with elongated, pale green pseudobulbs and delicate, translucent leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often resembling the shape of a ghost or spirit. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks in dense rainforests.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, Pseudocherleria brotheriana is considered critically endangered and is the subject of ongoing conservation efforts.
