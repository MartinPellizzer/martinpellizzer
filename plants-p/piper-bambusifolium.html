Piper bambusifolium

Piper bambusifolium, commonly known as bamboo-leaf pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble bamboo leaves, hence its common name. It produces small, clustered green berries that turn red when mature, containing the peppery seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Piper bambusifolium is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
