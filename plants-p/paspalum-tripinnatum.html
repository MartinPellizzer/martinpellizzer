Paspalum tripinnatum

Paspalum tripinnatum, commonly known as three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three to six awns, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open woodlands.

Paspalum tripinnatum is often used in erosion control due to its dense root system and adaptability to different soil conditions.
