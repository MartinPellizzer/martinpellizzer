Pithecellobium trichophyllum

Pithecellobium trichophyllum, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its feathery, trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisines.

The silky acacia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
