Pinus caribaea var. hondurensis

Pinus caribaea var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran caribou pine, is a variant of the caribou pine native to Central America.

It is a tall evergreen conifer that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The tree has needle-like leaves and produces cones that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
