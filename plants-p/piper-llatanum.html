Piper llatanum

Piper llatanum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by three-lobed, reddish berries. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly serrated margin and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
