Philotheca linearis

Philotheca linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved philotheca, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Philotheca linearis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
