Piper fuscobracteatum

Piper fuscobracteatum, commonly known as the black bract pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into peppercorns, which are typically dark brown when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
