Prunus puddum var. tibetica

Prunus puddum var. tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan dwarf cherry, is a small shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
