Pochota cowanii

Pochota cowanii, commonly known as the Mexican chocolate tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
