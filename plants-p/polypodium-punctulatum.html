Polypodium punctulatum

Polypodium punctulatum, commonly known as the punctuated huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often segmented fronds that are covered in small, rounded dots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, and is native to regions in Asia and the Americas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance.

The species exhibits a unique reproductive system, producing sporangia on the underside of its fronds.
