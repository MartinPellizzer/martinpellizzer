Parapteroceras papuanum

Parapteroceras papuanum, commonly known as the Papuan sea hare, is a species of sea slug found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Aeolidiidae and is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body and vibrant coloration. The plant-like structures on its body, known as cerata, are used for respiration and defense. It feeds primarily on algae and other marine plants, playing a role in coastal ecosystem dynamics.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for marine biological studies.
