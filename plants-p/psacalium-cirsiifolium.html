Psacalium cirsiifolium

Psacalium cirsiifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing toxic compounds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
