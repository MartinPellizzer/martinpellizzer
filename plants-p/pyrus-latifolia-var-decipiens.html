Pyrus latifolia var. decipiens

Pyrus latifolia var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive broadleaf pear, is a variant of the broadleaf pear species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often mistaken for those of other pear species, hence its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, which are followed by yellow or red fruits resembling pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
