Persicaria fallax

Persicaria fallax, commonly known as the fallax knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and stream banks, preferring partial to full shade. Its stems are often reddish or purplish, and it produces small, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Persicaria fallax is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging wet conditions.
