Pleurothallis cassidis

Pleurothallis cassidis, commonly known as the Cassid's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Pleurothallis cassidis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
