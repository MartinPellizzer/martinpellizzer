Papaver armeniacum f. linearilobum

Papaver armeniacum f. linearilobum, commonly known as the Armenian poppy or linearilobum poppy, is a botanical variant of the Armenian poppy, characterized by its elongated, narrow lobes on the petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing a preference for well-drained soils. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red or orange, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
