Pulsatilla patens var. ochroleuca

Pulsatilla patens var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and hairy. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

The flowers bloom in late spring, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.
