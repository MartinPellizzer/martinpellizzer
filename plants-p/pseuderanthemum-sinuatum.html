Pseuderanthemum sinuatum

Pseuderanthemum sinuatum, commonly known as the sinuous pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and attractive, showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly sinuous or wavy edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
