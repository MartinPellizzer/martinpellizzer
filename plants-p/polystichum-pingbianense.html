Polystichum pingbianense

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.
