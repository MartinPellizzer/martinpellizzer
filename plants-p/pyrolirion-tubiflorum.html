Pyrolirion tubiflorum

Pyrolirion tubiflorum, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pyrolirion tubiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
