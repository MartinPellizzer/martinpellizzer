Pteris baksaensis

Pteris baksaensis, commonly known as the Baksa water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a distinctive, intricate pattern of pinnae and pinnules.

Pteris baksaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
