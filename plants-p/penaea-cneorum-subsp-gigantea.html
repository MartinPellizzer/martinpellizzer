Penaea cneorum subsp. gigantea

Penaea cneorum subsp.

gigantea, commonly known as the giant cneorum, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy, red berries that are edible but often used as ornamental features. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a valued addition to horticultural collections.
