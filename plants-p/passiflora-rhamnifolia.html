Passiflora rhamnifolia

Passiflora rhamnifolia, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, red, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora rhamnifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
