Pterodiscus makatiniensis

Pterodiscus makatiniensis, commonly known as the Makatini discoid, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive disc-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, which are pollinated by aquatic insects. Its root system is adapted for anchoring in soft, muddy substrates and for nutrient uptake from the surrounding water.

Pterodiscus makatiniensis plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing excess nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
