Plectronia acutiflora

Plectronia acutiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plectronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
