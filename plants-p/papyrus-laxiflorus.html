Papyrus laxiflorus

Papyrus laxiflorus, commonly known as the slender-flowered papyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, typically appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Papyrus laxiflorus is often found in moist, sandy soils near water bodies such as rivers and lakes.
