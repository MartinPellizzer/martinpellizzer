Polygala arenaria

Polygala arenaria, commonly known as the sand polygala, is a flowering plant native to sandy soils in Mediterranean regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
