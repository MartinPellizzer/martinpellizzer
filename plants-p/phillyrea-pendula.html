Phillyrea pendula

Phillyrea pendula, commonly known as the hanging Phillyrea, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by small, spherical berries. Phillyrea pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its tolerance to drought and air pollution.
