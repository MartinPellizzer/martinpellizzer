Poa viscosa

Poa viscosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are covered with short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Poa viscosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes.
