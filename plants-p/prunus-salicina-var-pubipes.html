Prunus salicina var. pubipes

Prunus salicina var. pubipes, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry tree characterized by its hairy or pubescent stems and leaves.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically red or dark red when mature. Its botanical features include alternate, simple leaves with serrated margins and white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and edible qualities.
