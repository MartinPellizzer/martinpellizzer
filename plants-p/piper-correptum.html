Piper correptum

Piper correptum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
