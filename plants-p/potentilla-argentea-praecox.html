Potentilla argentea praecox

Potentilla argentea praecox, commonly known as silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name and help reflect sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Potentilla argentea praecox is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control.
