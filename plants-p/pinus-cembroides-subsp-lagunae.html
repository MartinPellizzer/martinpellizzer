Pinus cembroides subsp. lagunae

Pinus cembroides subsp.

lagunae, commonly known as the Guadalupan pine, is a subspecies native to the Chihuahuan Desert region of Mexico. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves.

The cones of this subspecies are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
