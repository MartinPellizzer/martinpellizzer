Pouteria amapaensis

Pouteria amapaensis, commonly known as the Amapa Brazil nut tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a large evergreen tree belonging to the Sapotaceae family, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in nutrients, including protein, healthy fats, and essential minerals.

The plant plays a vital ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the rainforest's biodiversity.
