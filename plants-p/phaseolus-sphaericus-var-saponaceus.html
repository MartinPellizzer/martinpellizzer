Phaseolus sphaericus var. saponaceus

Phaseolus sphaericus var. saponaceus, commonly known as the soapbean plant, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, green pods that contain a single, large, edible seed. The plant produces a unique, soapy-smelling compound in its seeds, which gives it its common name. The leaves are alternate, palmately compound, and have a hairy texture.

It is an herbaceous annual plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
