Physurus buchtienii

Physurus buchtienii, commonly known as the Buchtienii papyrus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that float on the water surface, with a fibrous, spongy structure that aids in buoyancy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into cylindrical, spiky fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshy areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
