Passerina aragonensis

Passerina aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragonese pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant produces cup-shaped flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, or orange. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
