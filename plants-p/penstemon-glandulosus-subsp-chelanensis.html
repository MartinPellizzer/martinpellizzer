Penstemon glandulosus subsp. chelanensis

Penstemon glandulosus subsp.

chelanensis, commonly known as the Chelan penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
