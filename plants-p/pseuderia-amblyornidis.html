Pseuderia amblyornidis

Pseuderia amblyornidis, commonly known as the Amblyornis spider orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with elongated, slender petals and sepals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the specific microclimates of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat sensitivity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
