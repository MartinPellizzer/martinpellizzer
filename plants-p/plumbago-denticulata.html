Plumbago denticulata

Plumbago denticulata, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that produces striking clusters of blue or lavender flowers, which bloom throughout the summer months. The plant features small, narrow leaves with a slightly serrated edge, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
