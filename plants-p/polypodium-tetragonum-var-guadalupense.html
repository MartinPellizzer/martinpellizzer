Polypodium tetragonum var. guadalupense

Polypodium tetragonum var. guadalupense, commonly known as the Guadalupe shield fern, is a varietal form of the common shield fern.

It is a terrestrial fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant features a creeping rhizome with fiddlehead-like fronds that unfurl into a triangular shape. Its leaves are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive shield-shaped appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
