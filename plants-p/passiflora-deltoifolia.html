Passiflora deltoifolia

Passiflora deltoifolia, commonly known as the delta leaf passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Passiflora deltoifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
