Petunia integrifolia var. depauperata

Petunia integrifolia var. depauperata, commonly known as the meadow petunia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive purple throat. The plant has simple, entire leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
