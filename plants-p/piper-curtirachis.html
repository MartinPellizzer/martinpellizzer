Piper curtirachis

Piper curtirachis, commonly known as the golden vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

Piper curtirachis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
