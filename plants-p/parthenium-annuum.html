Parthenium annuum

Parthenium annuum, commonly known as feverfew, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its widespread distribution. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.
