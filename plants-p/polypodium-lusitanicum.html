Polypodium lusitanicum

Polypodium lusitanicum, commonly known as the Lusitanian polypody, is a species of fern native to Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically light green and have a soft, velvety texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas, often growing on soil or stone. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, spore-bearing leaves.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
