Phaseolus tetraspermus

Phaseolus tetraspermus, commonly known as the four-seeded bean, is a species native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain four seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

This species is valued for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional significance.
