Ptarmica nana subsp. laggeri

Ptarmica nana subsp.

laggeri, commonly known as the dwarf mountain avens, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length, arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
