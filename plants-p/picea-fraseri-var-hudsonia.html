Picea fraseri var. hudsonia

Picea fraseri var. hudsonia, commonly known as the Hudson's pine, is a variant of the Fraser fir native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green needles. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically at high elevations.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
