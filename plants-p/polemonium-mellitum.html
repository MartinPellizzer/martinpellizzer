Polemonium mellitum

Polemonium mellitum, commonly known as sweet william, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Polemonium mellitum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
