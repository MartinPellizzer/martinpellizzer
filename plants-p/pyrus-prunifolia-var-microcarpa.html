Pyrus prunifolia var. microcarpa

Pyrus prunifolia var. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf pear, is a compact variety of the pear tree characterized by its small, round fruits.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has small, serrated leaves and produces flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing shrub.

Its compact size makes it suitable for small gardens and container growing.
