Poa pseudairoides

Poa pseudairoides, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa pseudairoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
