Platanocarpum africanum

Platanocarpum africanum, commonly known as the African plane tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Platanaceae and is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along rivers and in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
