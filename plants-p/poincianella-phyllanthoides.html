Poincianella phyllanthoides

Poincianella phyllanthoides, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, making it a striking feature in landscapes.
