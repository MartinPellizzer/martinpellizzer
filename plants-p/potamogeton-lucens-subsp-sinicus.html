Potamogeton lucens subsp. sinicus

Potamogeton lucens subsp.

sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia. It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

The plant has long, slender stems that are often branching and bear small, spirally arranged leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
