Poa acmocalyx

Poa acmocalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine soils. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is adapted to cold climates with a short growing season.

Poa acmocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
