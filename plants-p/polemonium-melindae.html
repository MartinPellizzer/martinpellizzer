Polemonium melindae

Polemonium melindae, commonly known as the Melindae Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its cascading clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy blooms appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Polemonium melindae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
