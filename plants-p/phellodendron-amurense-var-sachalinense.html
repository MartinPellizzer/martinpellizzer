Phellodendron amurense var. sachalinense

Phellodendron amurense var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin tree or Sakhalin phellodendron, is a variant of the Amur corktree.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Russia, northern China, and Korea, characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces yellowish flowers in clusters and has simple, alternate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
