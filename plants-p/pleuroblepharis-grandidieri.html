Pleuroblepharis grandidieri

Pleuroblepharis grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar moonflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. The plant has a distinctive, tubular shape with thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in dry, forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
