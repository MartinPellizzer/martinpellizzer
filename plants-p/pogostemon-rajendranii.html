Pogostemon rajendranii

Pogostemon rajendranii, commonly known as the Malabar pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Pogostemon rajendranii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
