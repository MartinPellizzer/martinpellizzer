Pilosella officinarum subsp. tricholepia

Pilosella officinarum subsp.

tricholepia, commonly known as the roughleaf hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with a basal rosette of leaves and can reach heights of up to 30 cm.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.
