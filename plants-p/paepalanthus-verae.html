Paepalanthus verae

Paepalanthus verae, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble sunflowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and temperate climates. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
