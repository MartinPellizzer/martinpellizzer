Pharbitis caerulea

Pharbitis caerulea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and its vines can grow rapidly, sometimes becoming invasive.

The seeds are enclosed in a spiral-shaped capsule and are dispersed by wind.
