Piper tristemon

Piper tristemon, commonly known as the false black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
