Polygala rhodopea

Polygala rhodopea, commonly known as the Rhodope milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, racemose inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its small, often white or pink flowers are accompanied by leaflets that are usually trifoliate.

Polygala rhodopea is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
