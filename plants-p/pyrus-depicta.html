Pyrus depicta

Pyrus depicta, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its leaves are elliptical with a serrated margin and turn yellow in the fall.

Pyrus depicta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
