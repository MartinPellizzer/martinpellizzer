Piper chilense

Piper chilense, commonly known as the Chilean pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, peppery fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Piper chilense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
