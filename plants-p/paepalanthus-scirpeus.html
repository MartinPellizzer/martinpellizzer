Paepalanthus scirpeus

Paepalanthus scirpeus, commonly known as the water buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features a rosette of broad, glossy, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture. It produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions, with a fibrous root system that aids in anchoring it in soft, waterlogged soils.
