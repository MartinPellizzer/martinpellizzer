Phyllanthus juniperinus var. obovatus

Phyllanthus juniperinus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-ovate phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features small, clustered leaves that are broadly ovate in shape, with a glossy green surface and a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
