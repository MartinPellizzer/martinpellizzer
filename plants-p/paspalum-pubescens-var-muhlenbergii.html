Paspalum pubescens var. muhlenbergii

Paspalum pubescens var. muhlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
