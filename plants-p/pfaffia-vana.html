Pfaffia vana

Pfaffia vana, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by oval, brownish seeds. Pfaffia vana is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its adaptogenic and antioxidant compounds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
