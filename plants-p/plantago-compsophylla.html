Plantago compsophylla

Plantago compsophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
