Pergularia montana

Pergularia montana, commonly known as the mountain pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils at high elevations. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are often violet or blue in color. Pergularia montana is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in alpine ecosystems.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
