Pistolochia solida subsp. wettsteinii

Pistolochia solida subsp.

wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's solid pistolpod, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a thick, leathery pod that contains several seeds, adapted for dispersal in harsh environments.

This subspecies is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
