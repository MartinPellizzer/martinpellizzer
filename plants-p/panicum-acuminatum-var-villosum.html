Panicum acuminatum var. villosum

Panicum acuminatum var. villosum, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive silky, silver-green foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces fluffy, pale green to golden inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it both visually appealing and ecologically beneficial, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
