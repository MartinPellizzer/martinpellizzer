Pilosella glomerata subsp. ambigua

Pilosella glomerata subsp.

ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous clustered hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval in shape. It thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
