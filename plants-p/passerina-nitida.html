Passerina nitida

Passerina nitida, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, with five to seven sepals that resemble petals.

Passerina nitida is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period, making it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
