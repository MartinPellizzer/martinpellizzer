Periploca calumpitensis

Periploca calumpitensis, commonly known as the Philippine periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in lowland to montane forests. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
