Polygonum gracile

Polygonum gracile, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, appearing in late summer.

Polygonum gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
