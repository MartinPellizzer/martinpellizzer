Potentilla × inaperta subsp. hedrichii

Potentilla × inaperta subsp.

hedrichii, commonly known as the Hedrich's silverweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs, and its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the genus Potentilla.
