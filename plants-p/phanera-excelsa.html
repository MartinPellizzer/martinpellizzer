Phanera excelsa

Phanera excelsa, commonly known as the tall tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and medicine. Phanera excelsa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
