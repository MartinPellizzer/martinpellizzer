Phalaenopsis laycockii

Phalaenopsis laycockii, commonly known as the Laycock's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures rather than in soil. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often resembling moth wings, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Phalaenopsis laycockii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and ease of care.
