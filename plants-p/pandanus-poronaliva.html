Pandanus poronaliva

Pandanus poronaliva, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that grow in a fan-like arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fibrous fruits containing seeds. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often used for its durable leaves in traditional crafts.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
