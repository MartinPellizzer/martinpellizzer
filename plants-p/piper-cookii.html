Piper cookii

Piper cookii, commonly known as the Cook's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other species like black pepper. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
