Pinus engelmannii subsp. blancoi

Pinus engelmannii subsp.

blancoi, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its longevity and ecological importance in its native habitat.
