Piper nepalense

Piper nepalense, commonly known as Nepal pepper or Bhutanese pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, Bhutan, and India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its erect, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
