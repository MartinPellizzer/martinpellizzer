Pilosella thedenii

Pilosella thedenii, commonly known as the Den's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with a mat-forming structure, and its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

Pilosella thedenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
