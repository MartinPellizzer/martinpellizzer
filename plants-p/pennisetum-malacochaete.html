Pennisetum malacochaete

Pennisetum malacochaete, commonly known as purple fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and its showy, plume-like inflorescences that range in color from silver to purple. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.

The botanical features of Pennisetum malacochaete make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial in native landscapes.
