Prunus carduchorum

Prunus carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and grayish-brown.

Prunus carduchorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
