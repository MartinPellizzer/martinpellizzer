Potamogeton × attenuatus

Potamogeton × attenuatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Potamogeton × attenuatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is sometimes used as a bioindicator in wetland habitats.
