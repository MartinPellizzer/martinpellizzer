Potamogeton zosteraceus

Potamogeton zosteraceus, commonly known as the ribbonweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant belonging to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and strap-shaped, often floating or partially submerged in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in dense spikes, which are typically found near the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Potamogeton zosteraceus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
