Polygala densifolia var. exasperata

Polygala densifolia var. exasperata, commonly known as the exasperated milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems that often grow from a fibrous root system adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color, with prominent stamens that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, though its botanical study remains limited.
