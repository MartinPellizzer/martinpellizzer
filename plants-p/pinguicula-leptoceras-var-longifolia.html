Pinguicula leptoceras var. longifolia

Pinguicula leptoceras var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, glandular leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces delicate, white or pinkish flowers during the growing season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and fascinating subject for study in the field of carnivorous plants.
