Pinus pendula

Pinus pendula, commonly known as the weeping pine, is a species of conifer native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.

The plant is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
