Portulaca conzattii

Portulaca conzattii, commonly known as the Conzatti's purslane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fleshy leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom throughout the summer. It is adapted to hot, dry conditions and has a succulent stem that stores water.

Portulaca conzattii is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
