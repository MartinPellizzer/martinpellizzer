Prunus brasiliensis

Prunus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian peach, is a deciduous tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves.

The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Prunus brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
