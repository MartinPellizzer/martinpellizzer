Pyrus × jordanovii subsp. velenovskyi

Pyrus × jordanovii subsp.

velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky pear, is a subspecies of the Jordanovii pear, native to Central Asia. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–8 meters, characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a small, yellow to orange pear with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It prefers well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates, making it a valuable ornamental and fruit-bearing plant in horticulture.
