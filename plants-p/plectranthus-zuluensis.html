Plectranthus zuluensis

Plectranthus zuluensis, commonly known as Zulu mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or blue, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
