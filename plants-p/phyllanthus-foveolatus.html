Phyllanthus foveolatus

Phyllanthus foveolatus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique leaf structure and adaptability to different habitats.
