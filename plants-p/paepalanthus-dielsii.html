Paepalanthus dielsii

Paepalanthus dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' cholla, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a dense cluster of cylindrical stems that form a compact shrub. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Paepalanthus dielsii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
