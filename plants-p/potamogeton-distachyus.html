Potamogeton distachyus

Potamogeton distachyus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are clustered in pairs. The plant thrives in shallow waters with moderate to slow currents and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and a healthy aquatic environment.
