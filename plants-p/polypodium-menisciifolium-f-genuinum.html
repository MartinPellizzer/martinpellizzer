Polypodium menisciifolium f. genuinum

Polypodium menisciifolium f. genuinum, commonly known as the Menisciifolium fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually leathery and have a unique, almost lace-like appearance.

This fern is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
