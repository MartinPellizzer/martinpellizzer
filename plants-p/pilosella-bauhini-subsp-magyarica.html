Pilosella bauhini subsp. magyarica

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

magyarica, commonly known as the Hungarian goat's beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, white to pale yellow tufted flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette or along the stem.

This subspecies is native to Central Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
