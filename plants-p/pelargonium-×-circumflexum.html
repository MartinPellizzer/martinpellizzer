Pelargonium × circumflexum

Pelargonium × circumflexum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid species within the geranium genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves. The plant produces vibrant clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
