Pinus mughus var. bicolor

Pinus mughus var. bicolor, commonly known as the white pine with two colors, is a variant of the white pine species native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned needles, which are typically a combination of light and dark green. The tree produces cones that are often elongated and have a unique shape, contributing to its ornamental value. Its bark is thick and furrowed, providing a rugged texture.

This variety is valued for both its ecological role and its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
