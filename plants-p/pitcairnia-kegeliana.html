Pitcairnia kegeliana

Pitcairnia kegeliana, commonly known as the Kegel's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects rainwater for the plant's hydration. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
