Prunus virginiana var. nana

Prunus virginiana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or dwarf chokecherry, is a compact shrub native to North America.

It features small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. The plant has slender, arching branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and wildlife habitat value.
