Pogostemon travancoricus var. devicolamensis

Pogostemon travancoricus var. devicolamensis, commonly known as the Travancore pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to the Western Ghats of India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth and rocky crevices. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
