Pyrrhocactus gracilis

Pyrrhocactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender pyrrhocactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
