Pyrus angustifolia var. spinosa

Pyrus angustifolia var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny pear, is a variant of the narrowleaf pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant is characterized by its spiny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, edible fruits.

The spiny features of this plant make it a distinctive and sometimes challenging species to handle in cultivation.
