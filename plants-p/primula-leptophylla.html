Primula leptophylla

Primula leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. This species thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
