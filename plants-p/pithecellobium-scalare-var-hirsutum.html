Pithecellobium scalare var. hirsutum

Pithecellobium scalare var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy mountain ebony, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round and covered in a thin, leathery husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
