Podocarpus levis

Podocarpus levis, commonly known as the white podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The tree produces small, fleshy cones that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Podocarpus levis is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
