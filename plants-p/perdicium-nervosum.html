Perdicium nervosum

Perdicium nervosum, commonly known as the veined lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive network of veins that run through its thallus, giving it a unique appearance. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provides it with essential nutrients through photosynthesis. It is typically found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it serves as an indicator species for air quality and ecological health.
