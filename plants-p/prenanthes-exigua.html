Prenanthes exigua

Prenanthes exigua, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. It is known for its unique ability to trap small aquatic organisms using bladder-like structures.

Its botanical features make it an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
