Pouteria carrieana

Pouteria carrieana, commonly known as the Caribbean wax apple, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter green underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by yellow or orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
