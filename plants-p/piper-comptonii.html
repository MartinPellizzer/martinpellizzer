Piper comptonii

Piper comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's pepper or the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a shrub native to the Hawaiian Islands, characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a peppery taste. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or culinary purposes.
