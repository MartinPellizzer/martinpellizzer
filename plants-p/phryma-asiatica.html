Phryma asiatica

Phryma asiatica, commonly known as the Asian phryma, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late summer and are attractive to pollinators.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
