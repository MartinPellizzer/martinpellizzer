Peperomia wrightiana

Peperomia wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically oval or spatulate in shape, often with a waxy texture and a range of colors from light green to deep green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia wrightiana is known for its slow growth and ability to adapt well to a variety of indoor conditions.
