Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. rabassaletana

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

rabassaletana, commonly known as the Canary Island Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
