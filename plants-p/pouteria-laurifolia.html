Pouteria laurifolia

Pouteria laurifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian mahogany or yellow mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The tree has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a large, round, yellow or orange berry.

Pouteria laurifolia is also noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
