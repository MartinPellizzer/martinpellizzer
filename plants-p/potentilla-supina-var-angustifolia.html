Potentilla supina var. angustifolia

Potentilla supina var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has slender, prostrate stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, often with five distinct lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in tundra or rocky habitats.
