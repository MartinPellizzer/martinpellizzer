Polypodium virginianum f. alatomultifidum

Polypodium virginianum f. alatomultifidum, commonly known as the alate multifidum fern, is a variant of the common polypody.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are often adorned with small, scale-like structures along the leaf margins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce dense clusters of spore-bearing structures called sporangia.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
