Pseudoponera tenuissima

Pseudoponera tenuissima, commonly known as the slender pseudoponera, is a small, understory shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that allows it to thrive in low-light conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
