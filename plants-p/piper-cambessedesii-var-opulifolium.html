Piper cambessedesii var. opulifolium

Piper cambessedesii var. opulifolium, commonly known as the opulifoliate pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper cambessedesii species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its fruit, like other members of the Piper genus, is a small, berry-like structure that contains the aromatic seeds used in culinary applications.

This variant is valued for its ornamental features and potential medicinal properties.
