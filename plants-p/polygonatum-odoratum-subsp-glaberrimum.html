Polygonatum odoratum subsp. glaberrimum

Polygonatum odoratum subsp.

glaberrimum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It features slender, arching stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and valuable species in horticulture and ecological studies.
