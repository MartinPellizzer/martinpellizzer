Penstemon guadalupensis subsp. typicus

Penstemon guadalupensis subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the Guadalupe beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its survival in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
