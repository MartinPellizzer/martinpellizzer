Prunus jamasakura f. venusta

Prunus jamasakura f. venusta, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded canopy and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.

This cultivar is highly valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and public spaces for its seasonal floral display.
