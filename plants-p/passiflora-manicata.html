Passiflora manicata

Passiflora manicata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white petals, along with intricate filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora manicata is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
