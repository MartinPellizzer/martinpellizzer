Piper vitaceum var. venezuelense

Piper vitaceum var. venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to South America, particularly Venezuela and neighboring regions.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are used in traditional medicine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, yellowish flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it valuable for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
