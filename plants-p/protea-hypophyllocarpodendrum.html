Protea hypophyllocarpodendrum

Protea hypophyllocarpodendrum, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a central cone-like structure surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, with a distinctive, hairy calyx that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Protea hypophyllocarpodendrum is a long-lived shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable feature in its native habitat.
