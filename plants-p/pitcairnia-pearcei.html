Pitcairnia pearcei

Pitcairnia pearcei, commonly known as the Pearl Plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of long, narrow, evergreen leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that can range from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Pitcairnia pearcei is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
