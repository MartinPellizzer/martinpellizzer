Plantago purshii

Plantago purshii, commonly known as the Pursh's plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
