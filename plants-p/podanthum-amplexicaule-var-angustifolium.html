Podanthum amplexicaule var. angustifolium

Podanthum amplexicaule var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ivy-leaf.

This variant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctively narrow and pointed shape. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique and sought-after species in horticultural collections.
