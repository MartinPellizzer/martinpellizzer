Phalaris colorata

Phalaris colorata, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from purple to pink. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by narrow, bract-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Phalaris colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
