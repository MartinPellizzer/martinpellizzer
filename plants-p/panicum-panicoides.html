Panicum panicoides

Panicum panicoides, commonly known as millet grass, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in open fields, disturbed soils, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color that becomes more prominent as the plant matures.

Panicum panicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
