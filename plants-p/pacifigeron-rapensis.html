Pacifigeron rapensis

Pacifigeron rapensis, commonly known as the Pacific rapens fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble those of other fern species, though it possesses unique vascular structures. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing near rocky outcrops or along forest edges. Its reproductive system involves spore production, typical of ferns, and it plays a minor role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
