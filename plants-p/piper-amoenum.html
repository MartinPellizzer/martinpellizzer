Piper amoenum

Piper amoenum, commonly known as the Java pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a strong, pungent aroma.

The small green berries are edible and contain a spicy compound similar to capsaicin found in chili peppers.
