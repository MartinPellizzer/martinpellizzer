Plectranthus dubius

Plectranthus dubius, commonly known as the Cape verbena, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive bluish-green color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet, arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both horticultural and medicinal contexts.
