Pyrus achras f. medioxima

Pyrus achras f. medioxima, commonly known as the African pear, is a botanical variant of the African pear tree, native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and yellowish-green fruits that are typically edible. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production, though its fruit is not as widely cultivated as other pear varieties.

The species is also noted for its resilience to drought conditions.
