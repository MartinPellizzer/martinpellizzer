Pyrus mayebarana

Pyrus mayebarana, commonly known as the Maybaran pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in the Tien Shan and Pamir ranges. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is edible and has a sweet, delicate flavor.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
