Phlegmariurus giganteus

Phlegmariurus giganteus, commonly known as the giant clubmoss, is a species of lycophyte that belongs to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, branching structure and its ability to form dense mats or clumps in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones called strobili, which are typically located at the tips of its stems. Its leaves are scale-like and arranged in whorls, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlegmariurus giganteus is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
