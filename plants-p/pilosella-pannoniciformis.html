Pilosella pannoniciformis

Pilosella pannoniciformis, commonly known as the Pannonian hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the dandelion family. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in meadows and grasslands.

Pilosella pannoniciformis is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
