Paspalum conduplicatum

Paspalum conduplicatum, commonly known as the twin-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along waterways.

Paspalum conduplicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
