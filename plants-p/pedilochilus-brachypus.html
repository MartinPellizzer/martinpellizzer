Pedilochilus brachypus

Pedilochilus brachypus, commonly known as the short-stemmed rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, often in shaded environments. The plant features a short, thick rhizome with erect, hairy stems that bear several elongated leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings.

This species is characterized by its unique morphology and is often found in humid, cool climates.
