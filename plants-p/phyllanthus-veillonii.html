Phyllanthus veillonii

Phyllanthus veillonii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing shelter for small animals and serving as a food source for certain insect species.
