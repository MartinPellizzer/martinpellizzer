Phyteuma sieberi

Phyteuma sieberi, commonly known as the Siberian phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing clusters of blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Phyteuma sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
