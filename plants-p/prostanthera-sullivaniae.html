Prostanthera sullivaniae

Prostanthera sullivaniae, commonly known as the Grey Matted Mint, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features greyish foliage with a soft, velvety texture, and its flowers are typically pale blue or violet, often arranged in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Prostanthera sullivaniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
