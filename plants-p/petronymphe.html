Petronymphe

Petronymphe, commonly known as the fairy rose, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often trail along the forest floor, forming a dense, carpet-like growth. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring, emitting a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, the petronymphe is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
