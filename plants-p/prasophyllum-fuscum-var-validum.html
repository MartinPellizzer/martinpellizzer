Prasophyllum fuscum var. validum

Prasophyllum fuscum var. validum, commonly known as the black leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as heathlands and grasslands, often in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces a single, narrow leaf and a spike of pale green to brownish flowers. It is characterized by its leek-like appearance, with a fleshy, bulbous stem.

This orchid is part of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
