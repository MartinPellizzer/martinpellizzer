Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. poroensis

Phyllanthus bupleuroides var. poroensis, commonly known as the Poroensis Phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, spherical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is characterized by its dense growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
