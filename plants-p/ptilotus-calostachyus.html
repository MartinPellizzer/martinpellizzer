Ptilotus calostachyus

Ptilotus calostachyus, commonly known as the stink bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant, yellow or orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid and coastal environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
