Ptelea persicifolia

Ptelea persicifolia, commonly known as the sweet fern or false sweet fern, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often described as sweet-smelling when crushed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, winged fruits. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Ptelea persicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
