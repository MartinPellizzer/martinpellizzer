Pinus kamtschatica

Pinus kamtschatica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a coniferous tree native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with harsh winters.

The Siberian pine is known for its longevity and can live for several centuries.
