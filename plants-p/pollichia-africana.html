Pollichia africana

Pollichia africana, commonly known as the African broom.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
