Pritchardia beccariana

Pritchardia beccariana, commonly known as the Beccarian cycad, is a rare and ancient plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, compound leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforest understories. It has a slow growth rate and is highly susceptible to environmental changes, making it a conservation concern.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
