Pinus laricio proles salzmannii

Pinus laricio proles salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's rock pine, is a rare and endemic subspecies of the Swiss stone pine.

It is characterized by its slow growth and dense, compact foliage, which forms a cushion-like structure. The needles are short, stiff, and dark green, often coated with a waxy layer to reduce water loss. This subspecies thrives in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations, typically in alpine environments.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
