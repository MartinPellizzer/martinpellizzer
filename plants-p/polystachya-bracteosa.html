Polystachya bracteosa

Polystachya bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa pteridium, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
