Premna angustiflora

Premna angustiflora, commonly known as narrow-leaved woodbine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
