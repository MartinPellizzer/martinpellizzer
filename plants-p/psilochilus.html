Psilochilus

Psilochilus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and are often found in humid, shaded environments. Psilochilus species typically have small, delicate flowers that are often green or yellowish in color, with intricate patterns. The leaves are usually narrow and elongated, adapted to capture light in low-light conditions.

They are valued by orchid enthusiasts for their unique appearance and ease of cultivation.
