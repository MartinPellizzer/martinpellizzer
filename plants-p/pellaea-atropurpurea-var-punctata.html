Pellaea atropurpurea var. punctata

Pellaea atropurpurea var. punctata, commonly known as the spotted maidenhair fern, is a variant of the maidenhair fern species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, dark green leaves with a distinctive purplish hue on the underside. The fronds are typically erect and can reach lengths of up to 30 inches, displaying a delicate, feathery appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Its unique coloration and elegant foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens and woodland settings.
