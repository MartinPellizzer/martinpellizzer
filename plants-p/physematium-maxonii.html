Physematium maxonii

Physematium maxonii, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its tuberous roots and velvety, dark green foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Physematium maxonii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
