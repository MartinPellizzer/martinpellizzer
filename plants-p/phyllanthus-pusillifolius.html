Phyllanthus pusillifolius

Phyllanthus pusillifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
