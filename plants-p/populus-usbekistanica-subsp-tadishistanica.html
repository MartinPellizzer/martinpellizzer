Populus usbekistanica subsp. tadishistanica

Populus usbekistanica subsp.

tadishistanica, commonly known as the Tadzhikistan poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The tree produces small, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It typically grows in riparian zones and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and its potential for reforestation efforts.
