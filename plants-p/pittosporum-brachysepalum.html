Pittosporum brachysepalum

Pittosporum brachysepalum, commonly known as the dwarf pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in ecological restoration projects.
