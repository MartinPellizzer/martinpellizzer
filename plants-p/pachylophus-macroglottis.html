Pachylophus macroglottis

Pachylophus macroglottis, commonly known as the broad-leafed thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, composite flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant.
