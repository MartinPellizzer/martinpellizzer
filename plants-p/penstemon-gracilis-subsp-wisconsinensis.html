Penstemon gracilis subsp. wisconsinensis

Penstemon gracilis subsp.

wisconsinensis, commonly known as the Wisconsin beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and pairs of lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces spikes of pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
