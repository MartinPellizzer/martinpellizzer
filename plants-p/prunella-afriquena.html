Prunella afriquena

Prunella afriquena, commonly known as African selfheal, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or violet flowers arranged in whorled clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. Prunella afriquena is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, and it has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
