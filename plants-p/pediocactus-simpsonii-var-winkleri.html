Pediocactus simpsonii var. winkleri

Pediocactus simpsonii var. winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems that are typically covered in white to pale green spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-adapted to harsh desert environments.

The varietal distinction from Pediocactus simpsonii is primarily based on subtle differences in spine structure and overall plant morphology.
