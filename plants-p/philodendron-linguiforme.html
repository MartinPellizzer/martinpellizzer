Philodendron linguiforme

Philodendron linguiforme, commonly known as the tongue-shaped philodendron, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Philodendron linguiforme is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
