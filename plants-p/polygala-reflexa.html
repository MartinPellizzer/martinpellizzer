Polygala reflexa

Polygala reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spicate inflorescences, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive floral structures and often medicinal uses.
