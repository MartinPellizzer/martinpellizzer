Pyrus griffithii

Pyrus griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible fruit. The tree typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters, with smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. Its fruit is small, yellowish, and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
