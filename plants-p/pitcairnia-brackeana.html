Pitcairnia brackeana

Pitcairnia brackeana, commonly known as the silver leaf pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are densely covered with silvery-white trichomes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces inflorescences that are often brightly colored, with showy flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and greenhouse cultivation.
