Pedilanthus peritropoides

Pedilanthus peritropoides, commonly known as the "butterfly plant," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring thick, waxy leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its unique flower structure attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sunny environments.
