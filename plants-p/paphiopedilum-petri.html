Paphiopedilum petri

Paphiopedilum petri, commonly known as the Petri's lady's slipper, is a rare and exotic orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a specialized structure for trapping pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its rarity and specific environmental requirements, Paphiopedilum petri is highly sought after in horticulture and conservation efforts.
