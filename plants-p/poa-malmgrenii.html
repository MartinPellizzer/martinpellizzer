Poa malmgrenii

Poa malmgrenii, commonly known as Malmgren's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
