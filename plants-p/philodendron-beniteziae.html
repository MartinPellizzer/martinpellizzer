Philodendron beniteziae

Philodendron beniteziae, commonly known as the Benitez's philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically hidden among its foliage. Its aerial roots allow it to cling to trees and other structures as it climbs.

Philodendron beniteziae thrives in humid, shaded environments with high levels of indirect light.
