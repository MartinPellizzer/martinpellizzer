Paspalum undulosum

Paspalum undulosum, commonly known as the zigzag grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf blades that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum undulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
