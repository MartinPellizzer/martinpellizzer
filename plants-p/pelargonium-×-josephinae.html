Pelargonium × josephinae

Pelargonium × josephinae, commonly known as the Josephina geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically come in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are usually heart-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its resilience and adaptability to different climates.
