Persicaria lapathifolia subsp. nodosa

Persicaria lapathifolia subsp.

nodosa, commonly known as the nodose smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
