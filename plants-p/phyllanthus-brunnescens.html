Phyllanthus brunnescens

Phyllanthus brunnescens, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring dense, spreading branches. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
