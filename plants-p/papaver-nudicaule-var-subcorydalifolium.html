Papaver nudicaule var. subcorydalifolium

Papaver nudicaule var. subcorydalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its stems are typically erect and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its historical use in traditional medicine.
