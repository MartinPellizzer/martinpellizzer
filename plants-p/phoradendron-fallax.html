Phoradendron fallax

Phoradendron fallax, commonly known as the mistletoe vine, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides some nutrients in return through its own photosynthetic activity. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. Its aerial roots penetrate the host's bark to establish a connection for nutrient uptake.

This unique relationship with its host makes it an important ecological component in forest ecosystems.
