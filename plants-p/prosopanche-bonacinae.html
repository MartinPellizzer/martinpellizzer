Prosopanche bonacinae

Prosopanche bonacinae, commonly known as the pink orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink and white blooms. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

The flowers are fragrant and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
