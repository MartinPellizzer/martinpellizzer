Phyllanthus pacoensis

Phyllanthus pacoensis, commonly known as the Paco's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.
