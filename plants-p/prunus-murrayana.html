Prunus murrayana

Prunus murrayana, commonly known as the Murray almond, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale pink, followed by edible drupes that are yellow or red when mature. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The bark is smooth and greyish, and the plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
