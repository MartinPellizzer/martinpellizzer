Potentilla verna subsp. puberula

Potentilla verna subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are typically oval to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
