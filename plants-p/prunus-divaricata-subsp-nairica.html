Prunus divaricata subsp. nairica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

nairica, commonly known as the Nairi cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping branches and small, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
