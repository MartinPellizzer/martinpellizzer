Pittosporum rangitahua

Pittosporum rangitahua, commonly known as the Kauri Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Pittosporum rangitahua is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in native New Zealand ecosystems.
