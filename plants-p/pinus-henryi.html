Pinus henryi

Pinus henryi, commonly known as the Chinese red pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to western China.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often remain attached to the branches for several years. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Pinus henryi is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
