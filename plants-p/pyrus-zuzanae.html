Pyrus zuzanae

Pyrus zuzanae, commonly known as Zuzana's pear, is a small, deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive, finely toothed leaves and clusters of small, white flowers in spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer, though it is not widely cultivated for its edibility.

This species is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to rocky soils.
