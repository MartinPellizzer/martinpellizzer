Primula glabra subsp. pseudoglabra

Primula glabra subsp.

pseudoglabra, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It features lance-shaped leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with five petals and a distinctive, tubular throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
