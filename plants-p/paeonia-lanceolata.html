Paeonia lanceolata

Paeonia lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces large, single or semi-double blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
