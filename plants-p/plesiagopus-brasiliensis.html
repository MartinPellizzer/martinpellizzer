Plesiagopus brasiliensis

Plesiagopus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are aromatic and have been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Plesiagopus brasiliensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary and medicinal uses.
