Plectranthus bojeri

Plectranthus bojeri, commonly known as the Cape lavender, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long blooming period.
