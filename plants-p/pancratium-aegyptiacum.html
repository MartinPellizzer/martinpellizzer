Pancratium aegyptiacum

Pancratium aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian torch lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It features a tall, slender stem adorned with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy yellow flower with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its bulbs are toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

Pancratium aegyptiacum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
