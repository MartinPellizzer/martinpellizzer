Pericallis papyracea subsp. hillebrandii

Pericallis papyracea subsp.

hillebrandii, commonly known as the Hillebrand's paperplant, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its thin, papery leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Botanists study this subspecies for its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
