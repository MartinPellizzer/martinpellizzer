Paspalum pictum

Paspalum pictum, commonly known as the painted paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful inflorescences that range from red to purple, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Paspalum pictum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its visual appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
