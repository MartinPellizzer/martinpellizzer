Phemeranthus oligospermus

Phemeranthus oligospermus, commonly known as the fleeting flower, is a rare and short-lived plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that open only for a brief period, typically at night. The plant has a very short lifespan, often dying within a few days after flowering. It grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and is often found in areas with high humidity.
