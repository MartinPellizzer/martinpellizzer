Paspalum deightonii

Paspalum deightonii, commonly known as the Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum deightonii is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
