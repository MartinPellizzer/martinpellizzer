Palmijuncus flabellatus

Palmijuncus flabellatus, commonly known as the fan palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fan-shaped leaves, which are arranged in a rosette pattern at the top of the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in warm regions.
