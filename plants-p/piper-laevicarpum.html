Piper laevicarpum

Piper laevicarpum, commonly known as the smooth-seeded pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, smooth-seeded fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
