Passiflora longicuspis

Passiflora longicuspis, commonly known as the long-cusped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and often consumed fresh or in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil.

Passiflora longicuspis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its calming properties.
