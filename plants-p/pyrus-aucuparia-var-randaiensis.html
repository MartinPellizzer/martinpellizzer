Pyrus aucuparia var. randaiensis

Pyrus aucuparia var. randaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a variety of the common pear tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring and its edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses.
