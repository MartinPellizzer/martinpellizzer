Passiflora menispermacea

Passiflora menispermacea, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Passiflora menispermacea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
