Phosanthus coccinea

Phosanthus coccinea, commonly known as the red-flowered phosanthus, is a striking member of the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and evergreen.

Phosanthus coccinea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
