Persea bonii

Persea bonii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. The fruit has a sweet, honey-like flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

Persea bonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
