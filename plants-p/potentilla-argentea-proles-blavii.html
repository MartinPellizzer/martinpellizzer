Potentilla argentea proles blavii

Potentilla argentea proles blavii, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with silver-gray foliage due to dense pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
