Piper cyphophyllopse

Piper cyphophyllopse, commonly known as the Philippine pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries. Its botanical structure is adapted for climbing, with tendrils that help it ascend trees and other vegetation.

The plant is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
