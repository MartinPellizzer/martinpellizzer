Protea angolensis var. albiflora

Protea angolensis var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered sugarbush, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form and its striking white, woolly inflorescences that resemble a cluster of candles. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in a showy, rounded head. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
