Procris reticulatovenosa

Procris reticulatovenosa, commonly known as the net-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, reticulate venation pattern on its leaves, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

The botanical study of Procris reticulatovenosa provides insights into the adaptations of orchids in tropical ecosystems.
