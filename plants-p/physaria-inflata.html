Physaria inflata

Physaria inflata, commonly known as the inflated bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which help in seed dispersal. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and along roadsides. It produces yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
