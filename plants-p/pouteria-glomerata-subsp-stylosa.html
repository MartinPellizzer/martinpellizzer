Pouteria glomerata subsp. stylosa

Pouteria glomerata subsp.

stylosa, commonly known as the Stylosa Pouteria, is a species native to tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into edible, spherical fruits resembling small oranges. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn from green to yellow or orange, indicating ripeness.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in local markets.
