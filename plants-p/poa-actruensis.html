Poa actruensis

Poa actruensis, commonly known as the Actra grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This perennial grass typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
