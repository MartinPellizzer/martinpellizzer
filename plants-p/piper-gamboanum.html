Piper gamboanum

Piper gamboanum, commonly known as Gambian pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
