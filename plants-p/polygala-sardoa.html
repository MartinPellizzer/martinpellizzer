Polygala sardoa

Polygala sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sardinia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in coastal areas. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds.

Polygala sardoa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
