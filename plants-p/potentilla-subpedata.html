Potentilla subpedata

Potentilla subpedata, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, often at high elevations. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, with five to seven leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

Potentilla subpedata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
