Phagnalon telonense

Phagnalon telonense, commonly known as the Telonese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in scrublands and grasslands. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a hairy texture, and it produces purple or pinkish flowers during the summer months.

Phagnalon telonense is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local insect populations.
