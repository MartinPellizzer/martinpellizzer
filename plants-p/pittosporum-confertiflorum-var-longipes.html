Pittosporum confertiflorum var. longipes

Pittosporum confertiflorum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
