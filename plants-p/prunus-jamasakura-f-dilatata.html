Prunus jamasakura f. dilatata

Prunus jamasakura f. dilatata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, often displaying a range of pink to white hues. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 4 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate and have a smooth, glossy surface, turning vibrant yellow in autumn.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular ornamental species in gardens and parks, especially in Japan and other parts of East Asia.
