Polygala vulgaris var. lensaei

Polygala vulgaris var. lensaei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from 30 to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
