Prunus bracteopadus

Prunus bracteopadus, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus bracteopadus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
