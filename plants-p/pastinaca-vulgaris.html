Pastinaca vulgaris

Pastinaca vulgaris, commonly known as wild parsnip, is a biennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, and its roots are typically white or yellowish in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along streambanks.

The plant contains phototoxic compounds, making its sap potentially harmful to the skin when exposed to sunlight.
