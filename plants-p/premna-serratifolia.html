Premna serratifolia

Premna serratifolia, commonly known as the Indian nettle or wild nettle, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
