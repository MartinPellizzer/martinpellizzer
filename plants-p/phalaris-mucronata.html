Phalaris mucronata

Phalaris mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf jointvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, often along riverbanks and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink to purple in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
