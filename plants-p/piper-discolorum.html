Piper discolorum

Piper discolorum, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a slightly rough texture.

Piper discolorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
