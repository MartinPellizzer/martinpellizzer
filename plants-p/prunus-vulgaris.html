Prunus vulgaris

Prunus vulgaris, commonly known as the common cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It produces white or pink flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a distinctive flaky texture.

Prunus vulgaris is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance in horticulture.
