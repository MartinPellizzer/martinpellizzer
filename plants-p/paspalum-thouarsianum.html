Paspalum thouarsianum

Paspalum thouarsianum, commonly known as the giant paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Paspalum thouarsianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
