Pleurothallis fasciata

Pleurothallis fasciata, commonly known as the striped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within the understory of rainforests. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are small and delicate, usually white or pale green with intricate patterns.

Pleurothallis fasciata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
