Piper medinaense

Piper medinaense, commonly known as the Medina pepper or Medinense pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spiny fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.

The plant is valued for its unique flavor and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
