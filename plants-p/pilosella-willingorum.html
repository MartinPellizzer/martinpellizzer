Pilosella willingorum

Pilosella willingorum, commonly known as the Willing's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Pilosella willingorum is typically found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its leaves are narrow, silvery-green, and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
