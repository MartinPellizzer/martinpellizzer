Pinalia woodiana

Pinalia woodiana, commonly known as the silverleaf pinalia, is a rare shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and early summer. Its woody stems are highly branched and often form dense thickets in its natural habitat.

Pinalia woodiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
