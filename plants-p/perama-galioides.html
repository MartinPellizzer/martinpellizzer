Perama galioides

Perama galioides, commonly known as the slender twayblade, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and heathlands. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often green or brown in color, and it lacks true leaves, relying instead on its stems for photosynthesis.

Perama galioides is a perennial species that plays a role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
