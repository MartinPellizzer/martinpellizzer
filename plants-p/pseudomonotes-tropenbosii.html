Pseudomonotes tropenbosii

Pseudomonotes tropenbosii, commonly known as the tropical bosie, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red and orange. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets, and it thrives in humid, warm climates with high rainfall. It is often found growing near the forest floor, where it competes for light and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
