Pothos ovatifolius var. simalurensis

Pothos ovatifolius var. simalurensis, commonly known as the Simalurensis pothos, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular in indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements.
