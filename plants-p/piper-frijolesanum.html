Piper frijolesanum

Piper frijolesanum, commonly known as the "bean pepper" or "frijolesanum pepper," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as the common black pepper. The plant produces small, green, oval-shaped berries that turn red when ripe. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves with serrated edges and small, white, fragrant flowers.
