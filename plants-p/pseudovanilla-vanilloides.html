Pseudovanilla vanilloides

Pseudovanilla vanilloides, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that are often pollinated by specific bees. Its fruit, a dark purple pod, contains seeds that are used in various traditional and commercial applications.

Despite its name, it is not a true vanilla species but is closely related to the genus Vanilla.
