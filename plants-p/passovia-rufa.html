Passovia rufa

Passovia rufa, commonly known as the red-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems that turn reddish in color as the plant matures. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in hue, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Passovia rufa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
