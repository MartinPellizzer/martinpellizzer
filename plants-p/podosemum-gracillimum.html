Podosemum gracillimum

Podosemum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender podosemum, is a rare flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant's stems are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.

Due to habitat loss, Podosemum gracillimum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists studying plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
