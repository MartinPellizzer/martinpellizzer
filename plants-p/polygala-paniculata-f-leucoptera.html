Polygala paniculata f. leucoptera

Polygala paniculata f. leucoptera, commonly known as the white-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, feathery inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while the flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often with white or pale pink petals. This plant thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
