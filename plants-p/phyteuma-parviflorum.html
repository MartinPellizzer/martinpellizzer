Phyteuma parviflorum

Phyteuma parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its popularity among gardeners.
