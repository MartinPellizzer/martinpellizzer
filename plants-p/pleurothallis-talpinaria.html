Pleurothallis talpinaria

Pleurothallis talpinaria, commonly known as the Talpin's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant grows as an epiphyte, often found on trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance.

Pleurothallis talpinaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
