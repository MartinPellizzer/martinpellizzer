Primula secundiflora

Primula secundiflora, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically broad and oval, with a slightly hairy surface that helps retain moisture.

Primula secundiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
