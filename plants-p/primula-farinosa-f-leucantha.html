Primula farinosa f. leucantha

Primula farinosa f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its dark green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its foliage is covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
