Piper araguense

Piper araguense, commonly known as the Araguense pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the spicy compound piperine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its unique flavor and medicinal properties.
