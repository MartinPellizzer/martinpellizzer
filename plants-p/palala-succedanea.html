Palala succedanea

Palala succedanea, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often blooming during the rainy season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a branching structure.

Palala succedanea is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water once established.
