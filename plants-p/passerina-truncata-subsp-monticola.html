Passerina truncata subsp. monticola

Passerina truncata subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the Sierra Passerina, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
