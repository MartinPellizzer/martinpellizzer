Poa botryoides subsp. orientalis

Poa botryoides subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft leaves that often form a compact clump.

The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
