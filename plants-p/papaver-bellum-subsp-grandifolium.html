Papaver bellum subsp. grandifolium

Papaver bellum subsp.

grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
