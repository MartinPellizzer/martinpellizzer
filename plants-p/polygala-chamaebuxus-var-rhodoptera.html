Polygala chamaebuxus var. rhodoptera

Polygala chamaebuxus var. rhodoptera, commonly known as the red-veined milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive reddish or purple vein pattern, which gives it its common name.

It is often found in coastal or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained conditions.
