Polystachya maculata

Polystachya maculata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. Its roots are fibrous and often form dense mats, helping to stabilize soil.

Polystachya maculata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
