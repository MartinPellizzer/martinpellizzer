Pseudophacelurus speciosus

Pseudophacelurus speciosus, commonly known as the showy false phacelurus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous grasslands.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
