Pterocephalus centennii

Pterocephalus centennii, commonly known as the centennial poppy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, papery petals that range in color from red to orange, resembling the petals of a poppy. The plant produces a distinctive, long, spiral-shaped seed head that can persist on the plant for several years, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

The botanical features of Pterocephalus centennii make it a unique and visually striking component of its native flora.
