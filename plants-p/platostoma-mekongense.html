Platostoma mekongense

Platostoma mekongense, commonly known as the Mekong shield fern, is a unique aquatic fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are typically broad and robust. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and wetlands, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by contributing to aquatic biodiversity and providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
