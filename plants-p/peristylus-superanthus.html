Peristylus superanthus

Peristylus superanthus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, adorned with bright yellow tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique branching pattern, with multiple stems emerging from a central base, creating a bushy appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its arid native habitat.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and bats, contributing to its ecological significance in its ecosystem.
