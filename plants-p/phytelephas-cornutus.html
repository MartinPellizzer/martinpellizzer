Phytelephas cornutus

Phytelephas cornutus, commonly known as the hairy peach palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling small peaches. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife and is also consumed by humans in some regions.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
