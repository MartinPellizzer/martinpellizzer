Prosaptia contigua var. subglabra

Prosaptia contigua var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved prosaptia, is a variant of the prosaptia genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that distinguish it from other varieties within the species. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Botanically, it is of interest for its morphological traits and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
