Piper psilocladum

Piper psilocladum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or the hallucinogenic pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and its ability to produce psychoactive compounds, particularly tryptamines. Its leaves and fruits contain alkaloids that have been used traditionally in spiritual and medicinal practices.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also of interest in scientific research and conservation efforts.
