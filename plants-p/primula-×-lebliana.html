Primula × lebliana

Primula × lebliana, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a range of colors including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 15–30 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
