Picea montigena

Picea montigena, commonly known as the Montane fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structure. The tree typically grows in cool, moist environments at high altitudes. Its bark is thin and reddish-brown, with a flaky texture.

Picea montigena plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
