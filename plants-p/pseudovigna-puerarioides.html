Pseudovigna puerarioides

Pseudovigna puerarioides, commonly known as the creeping pueraria, is a herbaceous legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces pods containing several seeds, which are typically small and hardy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and pastures.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a useful forage crop and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
