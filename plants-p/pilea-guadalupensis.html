Pilea guadalupensis

Pilea guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupense prayer plant, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver and green patterns. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

Pilea guadalupensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
