Ptarmica millii

Ptarmica millii, commonly known as the narrowleaf mountain-mint, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Ptarmica millii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
