Pergularia viridiflora

Pergularia viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, giving it a unique appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
