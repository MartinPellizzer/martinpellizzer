Potentilla saundersiana var. segmentata

Potentilla saundersiana var. segmentata, commonly known as the segmented potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically divided into several segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of delicate yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to botanical collections and gardens.
