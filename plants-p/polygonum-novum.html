Polygonum novum

Polygonum novum, commonly known as the New Polygonum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum novum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
