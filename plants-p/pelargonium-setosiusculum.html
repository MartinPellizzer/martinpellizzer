Pelargonium setosiusculum

Pelargonium setosiusculum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners.

Pelargonium setosiusculum is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
