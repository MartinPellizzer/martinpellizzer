Pterocarpus lucens

Pterocarpus lucens, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its straight grain and rich golden hue when freshly cut. The tree produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal, making it a sought-after material in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
