Phaseolus strobilophorus

Phaseolus strobilophorus, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a species of legume native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with trifoliate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain small, dark brown seeds resembling coffee beans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique appearance and potential agricultural value, it has garnered interest for research and cultivation.
