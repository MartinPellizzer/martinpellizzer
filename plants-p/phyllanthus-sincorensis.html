Phyllanthus sincorensis

Phyllanthus sincorensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
