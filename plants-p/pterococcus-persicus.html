Pterococcus persicus

Pterococcus persicus, commonly known as Persian velvet tamarind, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous, acidic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
