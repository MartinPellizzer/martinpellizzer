Pyrus theifera var. rosea

Pyrus theifera var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered pear, is a variant of the Chinese white pear.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to rose-colored flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant is a deciduous tree with a compact, rounded shape and smooth, gray bark. Its leaves are oval, glossy, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
