Phlox reticulata

Phlox reticulata, commonly known as garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It produces showy clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, white, and purple. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and beds.

Phlox reticulata is valued for its early summer blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
