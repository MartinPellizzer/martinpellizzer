Portulaca diegoi

Portulaca diegoi, commonly known as Diego's moss rose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its flowers typically bloom in summer and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Portulaca diegoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ease of care.
