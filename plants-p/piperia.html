Piperia

Piperia, commonly known as the false lily of the valley, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

It consists of approximately 20 species, most of which are native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating. Their flowers are usually small, white or greenish-white, and are arranged in clusters.

Piperia species are often found in moist, woodland habitats and are valued for their ornamental appeal.
