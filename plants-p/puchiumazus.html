Puchiumazus

Puchiumazus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and exotic plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features striking silver-gray foliage with a waxy texture, adapted to reflect intense sunlight and conserve moisture. Its flowers are delicate, bell-shaped blooms that range in color from pale blue to soft pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
