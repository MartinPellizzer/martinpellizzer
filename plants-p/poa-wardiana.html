Poa wardiana

Poa wardiana, commonly known as the Ward's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing tussocks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Poa wardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native grassland ecosystems.
