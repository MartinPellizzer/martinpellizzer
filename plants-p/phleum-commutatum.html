Phleum commutatum

Phleum commutatum, commonly known as purple grass or sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.

Phleum commutatum is also used in some traditional herbal remedies.
