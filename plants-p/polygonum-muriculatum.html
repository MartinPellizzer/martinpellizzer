Polygonum muriculatum

Polygonum muriculatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or emerald shower plant, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed in some environments.
