Parthenocissus quinquefolia f. minor

Parthenocissus quinquefolia f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf Virginia creeper, is a compact variant of the Virginia creeper.

It is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern United States, characterized by its lobed leaves and vibrant fall coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically not showy, and its berries are dark blue to black when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for smaller spaces or as a ground cover.
