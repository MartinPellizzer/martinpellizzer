Paspalum parinervium

Paspalum parinervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, spreading stems and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent central vein. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
