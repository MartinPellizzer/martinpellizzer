Potamogeton pallidus

Potamogeton pallidus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of its stems. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton pallidus thrives in still or slow-moving waters with moderate to high light penetration.
