Pseudorhipsalis acuminata

Pseudorhipsalis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in small, spine-like structures, which help protect it from herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
