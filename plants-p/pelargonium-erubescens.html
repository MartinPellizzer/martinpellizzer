Pelargonium erubescens

Pelargonium erubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
