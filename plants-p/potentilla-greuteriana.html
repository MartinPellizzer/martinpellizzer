Potentilla greuteriana

Potentilla greuteriana, commonly known as the Greuter's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla greuteriana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
