Plantago cinerascens

Plantago cinerascens, commonly known as the gray plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and soothing effects.
