Potentilla daucifolia var. daucifolia

Potentilla daucifolia var. daucifolia, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that spread along the ground, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five to seven narrow, lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.
