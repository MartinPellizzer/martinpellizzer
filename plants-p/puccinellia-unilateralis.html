Puccinellia unilateralis

Puccinellia unilateralis, commonly known as narrowleaf saltbush, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in saline and arid environments, often found in coastal regions and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia unilateralis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native range.
