Paeonia daurica subsp. tomentosa

Paeonia daurica subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the Siberian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant produces palmate leaves with a velvety texture due to its dense pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
