Piper congestum

Piper congestum, commonly known as the clustered pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow up to several meters in length. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
