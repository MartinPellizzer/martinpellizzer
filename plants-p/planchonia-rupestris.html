Planchonia rupestris

Planchonia rupestris, commonly known as the rock planchonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes or in coastal areas, adapting to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing resilience against harsh environmental conditions.

Planchonia rupestris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
