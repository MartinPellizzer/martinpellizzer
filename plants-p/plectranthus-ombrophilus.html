Plectranthus ombrophilus

Plectranthus ombrophilus, commonly known as the forest plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Plectranthus ombrophilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
