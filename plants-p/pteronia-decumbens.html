Pteronia decumbens

Pteronia decumbens, commonly known as the coastal pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It is well adapted to salt spray and sandy soils, making it a hardy species for coastal gardens.

Pteronia decumbens is often used in ornamental horticulture for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
