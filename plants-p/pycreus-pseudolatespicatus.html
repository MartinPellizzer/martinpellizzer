Pycreus pseudolatespicatus

Pycreus pseudolatespicatus, commonly known as the golden spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking yellow flowers. Native to arid regions of Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
