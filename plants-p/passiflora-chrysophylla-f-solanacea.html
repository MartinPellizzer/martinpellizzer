Passiflora chrysophylla f. solanacea

Passiflora chrysophylla f. solanacea, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a rare and striking variant of the passionflower genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which sets it apart from the typical green leaves of most Passiflora species. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a unique reproductive system. It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by botanists and horticulturists for its aesthetic and scientific interest.
