Prangos triquetra

Prangos triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with three distinct lobes. The plant has a fibrous root system and hairy stems that are often branched. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Prangos triquetra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
