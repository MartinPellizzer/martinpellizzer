Potamogeton liebmannii

Potamogeton liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, flexible stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.

Potamogeton liebmannii is often found in marshes, lakes, and along the edges of rivers, contributing to water quality by stabilizing sediments and supporting biodiversity.
