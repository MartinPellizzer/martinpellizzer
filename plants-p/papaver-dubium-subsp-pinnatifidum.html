Papaver dubium subsp. pinnatifidum

Papaver dubium subsp.

pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poppy, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, often pink or red flowers with a distinctive, saucer-shaped corona.

The plant features lanceolate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a feathery appearance. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its striking blooms.
