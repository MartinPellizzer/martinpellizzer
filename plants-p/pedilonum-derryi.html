Pedilonum derryi

Pedilonum derryi, commonly known as the Derry's slipper orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a specialized structure used to trap insects for pollination. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil to thrive. Its flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Pedilonum derryi is a rare and endangered species, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
