Piper amapense

Piper amapense, commonly known as the Amapé pepper or Amapé pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppery berries. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
