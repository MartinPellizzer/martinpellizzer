Perihemia

Perihemia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and short blooming period. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Perihemia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
