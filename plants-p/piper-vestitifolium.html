Piper vestitifolium

Piper vestitifolium, commonly known as the velvet-leafed pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
