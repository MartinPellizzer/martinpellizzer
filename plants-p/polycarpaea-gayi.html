Polycarpaea gayi

Polycarpaea gayi, commonly known as the Cape York billygoat pea, is a flowering plant native to northern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Polycarpaea gayi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant characteristics.
