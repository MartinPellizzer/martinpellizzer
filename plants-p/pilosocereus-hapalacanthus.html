Pilosocereus hapalacanthus

Pilosocereus hapalacanthus, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, colorful tubercles that resemble a moon's surface, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is often grafted onto a different cactus species for support. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
