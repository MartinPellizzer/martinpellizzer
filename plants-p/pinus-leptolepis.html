Pinus leptolepis

Pinus leptolepis, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, elegant form and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that are usually reddish-brown and contain seeds surrounded by a papery wing. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pinus leptolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly planted in gardens and landscapes.
