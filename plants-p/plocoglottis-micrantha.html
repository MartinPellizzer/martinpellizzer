Plocoglottis micrantha

Plocoglottis micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered plocoglottis, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the fern family (Polypodiaceae) and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are clustered on the underside of its leaves. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
