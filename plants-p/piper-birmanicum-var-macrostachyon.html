Piper birmanicum var. macrostachyon

Piper birmanicum var. macrostachyon, commonly known as the giant betel vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark purple color, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Piper genus, valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
