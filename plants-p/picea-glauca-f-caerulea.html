Picea glauca f. caerulea

Picea glauca f. caerulea, commonly known as the Blue Spruce, is a cultivar of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray needles that give it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant is native to North America and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is a slow-growing evergreen that can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

The foliage color remains consistent throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
