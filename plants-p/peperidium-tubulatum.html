Peperidium tubulatum

Peperidium tubulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peperomia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and waxy, often variegated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage rather than its blooms.
