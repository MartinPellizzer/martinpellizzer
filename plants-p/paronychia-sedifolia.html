Paronychia sedifolia

Paronychia sedifolia, commonly known as the stonecrop or moss rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its resilience and low maintenance requirements make it an excellent plant for xeriscaping.
