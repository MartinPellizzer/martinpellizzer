Polygonum kelloggii var. watsonii

Polygonum kelloggii var. watsonii, commonly known as the Watson's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
