Potentilla taurica var. nicicii

Potentilla taurica var. nicicii, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are typically covered in a soft, grayish-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
