Prasophyllum transversum

Prasophyllum transversum, commonly known as the broad-leaved leek orchid, is an Australian native plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial species, often found in south-eastern Australia.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
