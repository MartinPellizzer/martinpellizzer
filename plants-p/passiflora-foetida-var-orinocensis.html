Passiflora foetida var. orinocensis

Passiflora foetida var. orinocensis, commonly known as the Orinoco passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive central structure called the gynostemon. This species thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
