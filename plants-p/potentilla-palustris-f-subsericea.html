Potentilla palustris f. subsericea

Potentilla palustris f. subsericea, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to wet or moist sandy soils in temperate regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine, silky hair, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It is often found in coastal dunes, marshes, and other poorly drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize sandy soils.
