Polystichum sinotsussimense

Polystichum sinotsussimense, commonly known as the Chinese shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern China.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a typical feature of ferns. It prefers shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
