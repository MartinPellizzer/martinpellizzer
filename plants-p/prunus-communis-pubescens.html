Prunus communis pubescens

Prunus communis pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant bears edible fruits, known as cherries, which are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
