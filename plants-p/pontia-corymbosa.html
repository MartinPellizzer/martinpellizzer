Pontia corymbosa

Pontia corymbosa, commonly known as the yellow-wood or the white-wood, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the spring. Its flowers are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
