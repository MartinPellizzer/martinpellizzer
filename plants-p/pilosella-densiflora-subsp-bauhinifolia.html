Pilosella densiflora subsp. bauhinifolia

Pilosella densiflora subsp.

bauhinifolia, commonly known as the denseflower hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its inflorescence is characterized by a compact, flattened head of numerous florets.

The species is notable for its hirsute (hairy) appearance and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
