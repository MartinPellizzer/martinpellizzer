Pyrus monbeigii

Pyrus monbeigii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white flowers and edible fruit. The plant typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters, with smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. Its fruit, which ripens in autumn, is prized for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
