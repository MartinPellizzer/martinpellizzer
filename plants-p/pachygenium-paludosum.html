Pachygenium paludosum

Pachygenium paludosum, commonly known as the marsh hollyhock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Europe.

It features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in damp environments. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, allowing it to thrive in marshes and along riverbanks.

Pachygenium paludosum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
