Phaseolus bracteatus f. rhomboidalis

Phaseolus bracteatus f. rhomboidalis, commonly known as the rhomboid leaf scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the scarlet runner bean characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped leaf scars.

This plant is native to the Americas and is often cultivated for its vibrant red and yellow pods. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many leguminous plants known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with a serrated margin, and the flowers are usually bright yellow with a prominent purple base.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
