Peperomia perodiniana

Peperomia perodiniana, commonly known as the Perodinian Pepperplant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, compact shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, which are followed by small, round berries.

Peperomia perodiniana is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
