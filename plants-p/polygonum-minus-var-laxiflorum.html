Polygonum minus var. laxiflorum

Polygonum minus var. laxiflorum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often reddish in color, especially near the base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
