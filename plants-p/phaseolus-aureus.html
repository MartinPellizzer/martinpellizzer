Phaseolus aureus

Phaseolus aureus, commonly known as the golden bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and well-drained soils, often grown as an ornamental or for its unique appearance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both gardens and botanical studies.
