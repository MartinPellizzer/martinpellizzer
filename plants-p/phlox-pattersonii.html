Phlox pattersonii

Phlox pattersonii, commonly known as Patterson's phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Patterson's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
