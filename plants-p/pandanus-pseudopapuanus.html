Pandanus pseudopapuanus

Pandanus pseudopapuanus, commonly known as the Papuan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in wet, coastal environments.

Pandanus pseudopapuanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
