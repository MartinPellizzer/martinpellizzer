Potentilla komaroviana

Potentilla komaroviana, commonly known as the Komarov's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
