Penstemon laevigatus subsp. alluviorum

Penstemon laevigatus subsp.

alluviorum, commonly known as the river penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and stream edges. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is well adapted to riparian habitats and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
