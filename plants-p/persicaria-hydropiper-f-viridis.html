Persicaria hydropiper f. viridis

Persicaria hydropiper f. viridis, commonly known as green water pepper, is a variant of the water pepper species native to wetland environments.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purplish tinge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender pedicels.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams.
