Plectranthus amplexicaulis

Plectranthus amplexicaulis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a favored plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
