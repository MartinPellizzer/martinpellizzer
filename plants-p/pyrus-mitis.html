Pyrus mitis

Pyrus mitis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, spreading branches. The leaves are elliptical, with a dull green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by round, yellow fruits that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
