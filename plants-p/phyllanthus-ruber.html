Phyllanthus ruber

Phyllanthus ruber, commonly known as red phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruiting structures.
