Potentilla taurica var. semitaurica

Potentilla taurica var. semitaurica, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
