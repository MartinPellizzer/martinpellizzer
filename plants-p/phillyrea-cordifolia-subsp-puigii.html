Phillyrea cordifolia subsp. puigii

Phillyrea cordifolia subsp.

puigii, commonly known as the Canary Island Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain several seeds.

It is adapted to rocky and coastal environments, showing resilience to drought and salt spray.
