Perdicium semiflosculare

Perdicium semiflosculare, commonly known as the partial floret grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are less developed than those of typical grasses. Its flowers are typically greenish or brownish, and it is often found in open, disturbed habitats.

Perdicium semiflosculare is native to parts of Europe and is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.
