Phyllorkis grandiflora

Phyllorkis grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered phyllorkis, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
