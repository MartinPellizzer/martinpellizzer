Phyllota humifusa

Phyllota humifusa, commonly known as the Mexican coffee bean plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often bean-like seeds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The seeds, enclosed in pods, are often used in traditional medicine and have a mild coffee-like aroma.
