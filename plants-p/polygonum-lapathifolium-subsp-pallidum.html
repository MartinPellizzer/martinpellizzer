Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. pallidum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

pallidum, commonly known as pale dock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe. It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils along stream banks and wet meadows.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually pink to red in color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and astringent properties.
