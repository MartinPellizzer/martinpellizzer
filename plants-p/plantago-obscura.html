Plantago obscura

Plantago obscura, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, slender stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along trails.

This hardy species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
