Plumeria × biglandulosa

Plumeria × biglandulosa, commonly known as the frangipani, is a hybrid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant features smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and are highly valued for their ornamental appeal.

The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
