Piper brevesanum

Piper brevesanum, commonly known as the short-stemmed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and the presence of glandular hairs on its foliage.

Piper brevesanum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
