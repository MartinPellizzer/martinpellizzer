Pyrus pritzellii

Pyrus pritzellii, commonly known as the Pritzellii pear, is a species of deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, ornamental white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible pears that are often used in cooking or for ornamental purposes. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat obscure member of the pear genus.
