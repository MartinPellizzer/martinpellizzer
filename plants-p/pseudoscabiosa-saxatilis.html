Pseudoscabiosa saxatilis

Pseudoscabiosa saxatilis, commonly known as the rock scabious, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features wiry stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

Pseudoscabiosa saxatilis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
