Primula petiolaris var. edgeworthii

Primula petiolaris var. edgeworthii, commonly known as the Edgeworth's primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its stems are short and sturdy, supporting the flower clusters above the foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
