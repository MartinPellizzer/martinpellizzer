Pinus larix var. pendula

Pinus larix var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping larch, is a unique variant of the larch tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This coniferous species is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that release winged seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy. Its needle-like leaves are typically a dark green, turning a striking golden hue in the fall.

The weeping larch is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and distinctive, umbrella-like appearance.
