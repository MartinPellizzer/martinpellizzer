Prunus spinosa subvar. major

Prunus spinosa subvar. major, commonly known as the large blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark blue berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

The berries are used in making jams and wines, and the plant has historical uses in traditional medicine.
