Paeonia mascula var. maroccana

Paeonia mascula var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan peony, is a variant of the tree peony native to the mountainous regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, compound leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
