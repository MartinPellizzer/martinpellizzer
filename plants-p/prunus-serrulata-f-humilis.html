Prunus serrulata f. humilis

Prunus serrulata f. humilis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, which give it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and parks.

Due to its beauty and cultural significance, it is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of the world.
