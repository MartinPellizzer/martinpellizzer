Pyrus pyraster f. rotundata

Pyrus pyraster f. rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that differ from the typical narrow leaves of the species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in woodland areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in horticulture.
