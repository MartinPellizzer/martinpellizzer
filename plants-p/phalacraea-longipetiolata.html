Phalacraea longipetiolata

Phalacraea longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled greenbottle bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine, and it has a relatively slow growth rate.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
