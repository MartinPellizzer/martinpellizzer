Perezia diversifolia var. crispa

Perezia diversifolia var. crispa, commonly known as the crisped perezia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crinkled leaves that give it its varietal name, "crispa," meaning "creased" or "crinkled" in Latin. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas with high humidity.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
