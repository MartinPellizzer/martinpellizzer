Pandanus clarkei

Pandanus clarkei, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its roots and leaves are also utilized in local medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or swampy areas.
