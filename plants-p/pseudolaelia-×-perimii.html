Pseudolaelia × perimii

Pseudolaelia × perimii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Pseudolaelia elongata and Pseudolaelia klossii.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, specifically in Ecuador and Peru. This plant is a terrestrial orchid with pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in the spring.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens for its beauty and unique characteristics.
