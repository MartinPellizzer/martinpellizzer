Pinus acutissima

Pinus acutissima, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically 1 to 2 inches long. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often have a reddish-brown color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

This resilient species is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
