Phyllanthus sootepensis

Phyllanthus sootepensis, commonly known as the Sootep starling tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruits are capsule-like and contain small, hard seeds.

Phyllanthus sootepensis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
