Pseudopilocereus catingicola

Pseudopilocereus catingicola, commonly known as the Catinga cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
