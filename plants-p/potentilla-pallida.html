Potentilla pallida

Potentilla pallida, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial species that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features a low-growing habit with woody stems and palmately divided leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Potentilla pallida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
