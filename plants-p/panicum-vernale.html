Panicum vernale

Panicum vernale, commonly known as green panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Panicum vernale is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
