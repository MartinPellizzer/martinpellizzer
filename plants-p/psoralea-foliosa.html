Psoralea foliosa

Psoralea foliosa, commonly known as the leafy psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish pea-like flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its seed pods are slender and dehiscent, releasing seeds when mature.

Psoralea foliosa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
