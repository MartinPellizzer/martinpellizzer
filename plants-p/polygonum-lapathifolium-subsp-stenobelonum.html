Polygonum lapathifolium subsp. stenobelonum

Polygonum lapathifolium subsp.

stenobelonum, commonly known as narrow-leaf smartweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
