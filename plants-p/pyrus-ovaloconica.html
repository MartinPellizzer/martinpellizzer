Pyrus ovaloconica

Pyrus ovaloconica, commonly known as the oval-leaf pear.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae, characterized by its distinctive oval-shaped leaves and small, edible fruits. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The leaves are usually glossy and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit.
