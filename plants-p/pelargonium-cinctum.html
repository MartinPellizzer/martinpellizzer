Pelargonium cinctum

Pelargonium cinctum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves that feature white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers during the summer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Pelargonium cinctum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
