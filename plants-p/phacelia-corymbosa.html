Phacelia corymbosa

Phacelia corymbosa, commonly known as blue phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Phacelia corymbosa is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
