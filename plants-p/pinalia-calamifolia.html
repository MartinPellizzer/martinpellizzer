Pinalia calamifolia

Pinalia calamifolia, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a unique species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that appear almost translucent, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist soil to flourish. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Pinalia calamifolia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
