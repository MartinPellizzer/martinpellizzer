Prostanthera saxicola var. montana

Prostanthera saxicola var. montana, commonly known as mountain mint bush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or heathlands. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
