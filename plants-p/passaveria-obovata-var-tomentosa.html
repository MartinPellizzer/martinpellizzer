Passaveria obovata var. tomentosa

Passaveria obovata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly passaveria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular ornamental plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
