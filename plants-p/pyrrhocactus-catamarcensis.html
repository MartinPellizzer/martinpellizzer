Pyrrhocactus catamarcensis

Pyrrhocactus catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarcensis prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible fruits similar to other prickly pear varieties. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
