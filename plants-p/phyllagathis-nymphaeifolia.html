Phyllagathis nymphaeifolia

Phyllagathis nymphaeifolia, commonly known as water ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, with a thick, spongy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and have a distinctive pinkish or red color.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
