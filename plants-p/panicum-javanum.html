Panicum javanum

Panicum javanum, commonly known as hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, including parts of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Panicum javanum is often used in ornamental gardening and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
