Polypodium filix-femina var. purpurascens

Polypodium filix-femina var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed maidenhair fern, is a variant of the common maidenhair fern.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive purplish hue on the stems. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
