Paspalum reticulatum

Paspalum reticulatum, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its coarse, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, blue to purple flowers. This plant thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun. It is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Paspalum reticulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
