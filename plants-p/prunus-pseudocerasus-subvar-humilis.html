Prunus pseudocerasus subvar. humilis

Prunus pseudocerasus subvar. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese date, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that resemble dates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in subtropical regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth habit compared to the typical P. pseudocerasus.
