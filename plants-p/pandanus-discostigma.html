Pandanus discostigma

Pandanus discostigma, commonly known as the hibiscus pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiky fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
