Phacelia integrifolia var. texana

Phacelia integrifolia var. texana, commonly known as Texas bluebells, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its nectar attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.
