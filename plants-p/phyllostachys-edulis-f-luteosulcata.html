Phyllostachys edulis f. luteosulcata

Phyllostachys edulis f. luteosulcata, commonly known as the golden-striped bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its striking yellow stripes on the culms, which contrast with its green base. This ornamental bamboo is native to China and thrives in temperate climates. It grows in clumps and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
