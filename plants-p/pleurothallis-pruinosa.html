Pleurothallis pruinosa

Pleurothallis pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive velvety texture. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and delicate beauty, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
