Passiflora runa

Passiflora runa, commonly known as the Andean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora runa thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
