Potentilla concinna var. oblanceolata

Potentilla concinna var. oblanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, often arching stems with small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
