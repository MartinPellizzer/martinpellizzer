Persea chiapensis

Persea chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapas persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically eaten raw or used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
