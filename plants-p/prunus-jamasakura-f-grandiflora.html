Prunus jamasakura f. grandiflora

Prunus jamasakura f. grandiflora, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant is a deciduous tree with a compact, rounded shape and smooth, grey bark. Its leaves are ovate and turn a vibrant yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of East Asia for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
