Potentilla anserina var. stenodonta

Potentilla anserina var. stenodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silverweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and densely arranged, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
