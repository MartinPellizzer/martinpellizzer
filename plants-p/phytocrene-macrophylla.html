Phytocrene macrophylla

Phytocrene macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf phytocrene, is a striking plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, providing a lush, green canopy. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and helps stabilize soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.

Phytocrene macrophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
