Panicum glumipatulum

Panicum glumipatulum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, paired structures that produce the plant's characteristic seeds.

Panicum glumipatulum plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
