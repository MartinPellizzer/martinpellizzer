Passiflora hyacinthiflora var. tridentata

Passiflora hyacinthiflora var. tridentata, commonly known as the Trinidad passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It features striking, showy flowers with five fleshy petals and a crown of filaments, typical of the Passiflora genus. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
