Paspalum rectum

Paspalum rectum, commonly known as rigid grass or narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum rectum is valued for its drought tolerance and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
