Pecteilis korigadensis

Pecteilis korigadensis, commonly known as the Korigad dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid climate of its native region. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

Pecteilis korigadensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
