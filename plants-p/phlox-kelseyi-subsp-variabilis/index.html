Phlox Kelseyi Subsp. Variabilis

Phlox kelseyi subsp. variabilis is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is known for its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. This subspecies thrives in rocky soils and shaded environments, making it a challenging plant to cultivate. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered by conservationists. Its beauty and rarity make it a subject of both scientific interest and botanical admiration.
