Panax zingiberensis

Panax zingiberensis, commonly known as the wild ginger, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous root system. The plant features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring.

The rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
