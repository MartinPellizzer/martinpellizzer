Prainea scandens

Prainea scandens, commonly known as the climbing lily, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can climb and twine around supports. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are heart-shaped and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Prainea scandens thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
