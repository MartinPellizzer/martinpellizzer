Polygonum amphibium f. simile

Polygonum amphibium f. simile, commonly known as the marsh knotweed, is a variant of the common knotgrass.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its upright stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Its fibrous roots and rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in wetland habitats.
