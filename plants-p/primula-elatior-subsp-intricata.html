Primula elatior subsp. intricata

Primula elatior subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to high-altitude landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
