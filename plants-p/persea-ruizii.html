Persea ruizii

Persea ruizii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, bark-like trunk and a dense canopy that provides shade.

Persea ruizii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is often used in local cuisines.
