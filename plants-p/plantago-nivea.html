Plantago nivea

Plantago nivea, commonly known as the white plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a basal rosette of broad, oval, and often hairy leaves that are typically covered in a fine white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
