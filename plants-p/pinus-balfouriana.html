Pinus balfouriana

Pinus balfouriana, commonly known as the Balfour pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. Its needles are long and slender, often growing in pairs, and its bark is thick and fissured.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
