Polygonatum yunnanense

Polygonatum yunnanense, commonly known as Yunnan Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are dark red when mature, are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

Polygonatum yunnanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in both horticulture and herbal practices.
