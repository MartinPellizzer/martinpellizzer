Pinus greggii var. greggii

Pinus greggii var. greggii, commonly known as the Gregg's pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The cones of this plant are woody and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters, containing edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
