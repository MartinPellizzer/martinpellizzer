Piper papillicarpum

Piper papillicarpum, commonly known as the hairy-fruit pepper or piper papillicarpum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered, and often hairy fruits, which are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is a climbing vine that can reach several meters in length, with opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
