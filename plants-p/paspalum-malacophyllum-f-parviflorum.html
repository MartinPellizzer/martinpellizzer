Paspalum malacophyllum f. parviflorum

Paspalum malacophyllum f. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

This ornamental grass is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small wildlife.
