Physaria cinerea

Physaria cinerea, commonly known as silverleaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.
