Pterichis saundersii

Pterichis saundersii, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Pterichis saundersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
