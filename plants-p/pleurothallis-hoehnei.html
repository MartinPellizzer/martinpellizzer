Pleurothallis hoehnei

Pleurothallis hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale pink, with three sepals and three petals.

Pleurothallis hoehnei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
