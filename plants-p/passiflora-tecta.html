Passiflora tecta

Passiflora tecta, commonly known as the shield passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Passiflora tecta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
