Piptochaetium stipoides var. parviflorum

Piptochaetium stipoides var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
