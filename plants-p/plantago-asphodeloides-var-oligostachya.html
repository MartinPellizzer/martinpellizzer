Plantago asphodeloides var. oligostachya

Plantago asphodeloides var. oligostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
