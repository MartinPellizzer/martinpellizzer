Potentilla macounii

Potentilla macounii, commonly known as Macoun's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a compact, bushy growth habit.

Potentilla macounii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
