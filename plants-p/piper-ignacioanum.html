Piper ignacioanum

Piper ignacioanum, commonly known as the Ignacio's pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
