Paspalum fimbriatum

Paspalum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. It has small, spike-like flowers that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Paspalum fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
