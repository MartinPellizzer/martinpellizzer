Papaver alpinum var. nudicaule

Papaver alpinum var. nudicaule, commonly known as the alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a slender, branching stem. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in harsh, cold climates. Its flowers bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to mountainous landscapes.

The variety *nudicaule* is distinguished by its nearly leafless stem, giving it a unique, elegant appearance.
