Phacelia mohavensis var. exilis

Phacelia mohavensis var. exilis, commonly known as the Mohave phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as the Mojave Desert. The plant features small, delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a characteristic species of its habitat.
