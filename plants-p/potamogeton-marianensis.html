Potamogeton marianensis

Potamogeton marianensis, commonly known as the Marian's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to reddish in color, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spadices, which are cylindrical inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton marianensis is often found in slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and marshes.
