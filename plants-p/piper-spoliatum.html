Piper spoliatum

Piper spoliatum, commonly known as the black pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, resembling black pepper, though it is not related to the spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
