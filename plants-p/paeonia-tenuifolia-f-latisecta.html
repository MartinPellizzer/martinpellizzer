Paeonia tenuifolia f. latisecta

Paeonia tenuifolia f. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leaf peony, is a cultivated form of the slender peony.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are broad and divided, giving the plant a distinctive, fern-like appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained, fertile soil.

It is a deciduous perennial that requires partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance once established.
