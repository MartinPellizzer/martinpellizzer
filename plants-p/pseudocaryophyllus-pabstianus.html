Pseudocaryophyllus pabstianus

Pseudocaryophyllus pabstianus, commonly known as the Pabstian's false sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has fleshy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water, adapting it to arid or rocky environments. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
