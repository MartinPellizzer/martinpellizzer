Peperomia naevifolia

Peperomia naevifolia, commonly known as the speckled peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its striking, variegated foliage with dark spots and a speckled appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a waxy, slightly textured surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
