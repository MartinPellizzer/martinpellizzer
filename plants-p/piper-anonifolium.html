Piper anonifolium

Piper anonifolium, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant is closely related to the pepper species used in culinary applications.
