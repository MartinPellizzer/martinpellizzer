Periploca palvallii

Periploca palvallii, commonly known as the Malabar periploca, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often clustered in umbels. Its latex is milky and contains alkaloids, which may have medicinal properties.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and potential therapeutic uses.
