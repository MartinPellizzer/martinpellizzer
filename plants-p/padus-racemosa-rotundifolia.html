Padus racemosa rotundifolia

Padus racemosa rotundifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf serviceberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, clustered flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, providing food for wildlife and humans. Its leaves are oval-shaped with rounded tips, and it often grows in moist, open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
