Plantago australis subsp. orbignyana

Plantago australis subsp.

orbignyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and riverbanks.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowering spikes consist of small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
