Pyxidaria sphaerocarpa

Pyxidaria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fruit or red globe tree, is a small, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and clusters of small, bright red berries that resemble globes, giving it its common name. The plant has elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and produces fragrant, white flowers during the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
