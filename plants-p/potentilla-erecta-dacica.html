Potentilla erecta dacica

Potentilla erecta dacica, commonly known as the Dacian rose or Dacian potentilla, is a shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Potentilla erecta dacica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
