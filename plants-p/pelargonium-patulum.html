Pelargonium patulum

Pelargonium patulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Pelargonium patulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
