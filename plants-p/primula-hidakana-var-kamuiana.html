Primula hidakana var. kamuiana

Primula hidakana var. kamuiana, commonly known as the Kamuiana primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
