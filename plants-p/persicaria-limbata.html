Persicaria limbata

Persicaria limbata, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish at the base. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in pink to purple hues, usually from late spring to early autumn. Its seed capsules split open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Persicaria limbata is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in fields, where it thrives in full sun.
