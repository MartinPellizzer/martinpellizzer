Plantago spathulata

Plantago spathulata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
