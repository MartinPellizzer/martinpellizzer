Piper madeiranum

Piper madeiranum, commonly known as the Madeiran pepper or Madeira pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic leaves used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
