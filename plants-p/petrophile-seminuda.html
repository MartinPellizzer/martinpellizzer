Petrophile seminuda

Petrophile seminuda, commonly known as the slender rock-heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or heathland environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Petrophile seminuda is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.
