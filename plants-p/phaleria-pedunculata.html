Phaleria pedunculata

Phaleria pedunculata, commonly known as the white-veined fruit tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a small, round drupe with a thick, leathery skin.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
