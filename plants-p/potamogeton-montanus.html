Potamogeton montanus

Potamogeton montanus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that emerge above the water surface. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands. It plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
