Protea madiensis var. pilosa

Protea madiensis var. pilosa, commonly known as the soft-bristled protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy bracts that give it a fuzzy appearance, and its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often forming dense, evergreen thickets.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
