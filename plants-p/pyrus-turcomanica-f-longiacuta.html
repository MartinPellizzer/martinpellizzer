Pyrus turcomanica f. longiacuta

Pyrus turcomanica f. longiacuta, commonly known as the long-pointed Turkmen pear, is a distinctive cultivar of the Turkmen pear species.

It is characterized by its elongated, sharp-pointed leaves, which give it its specific epithet "longiacuta.". The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Turkmenistan and neighboring countries. It produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The botanical features of this plant make it of interest to horticulturists and botanists studying adapted flora in arid environments.
