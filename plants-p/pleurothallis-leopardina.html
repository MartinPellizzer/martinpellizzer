Pleurothallis leopardina

Pleurothallis leopardina, commonly known as the leopard orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display a striking pattern resembling a leopard's spots. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its foliage consists of narrow, strap-like leaves that help it thrive in the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis leopardina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
