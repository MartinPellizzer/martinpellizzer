Pithecellobium spruceanum

Pithecellobium spruceanum, commonly known as the spruce's poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have a distinctive coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its foliage and floral displays.
