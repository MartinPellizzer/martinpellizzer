Polanisia graveolens var. cespitosa

Polanisia graveolens var. cespitosa, commonly known as the fragrant polanisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its aromatic leaves release a subtle, spicy scent when crushed, which is a distinguishing feature.

The variety cespitosa is notable for its compact growth habit and ability to form dense mats, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
