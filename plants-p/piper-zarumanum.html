Piper zarumanum

Piper zarumanum, commonly known as the Zanzibar pepper, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while the stems are woody and aromatic.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and has a mild, peppery flavor.
