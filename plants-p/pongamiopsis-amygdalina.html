Pongamiopsis amygdalina

Pongamiopsis amygdalina, commonly known as the African sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in dry, open habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
