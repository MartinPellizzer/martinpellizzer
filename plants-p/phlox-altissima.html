Phlox altissima

Phlox altissima, commonly known as the tall phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 4 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox altissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
