Pandorea pandorana subsp. pandorana

Pandorea pandorana subsp.

pandorana, commonly known as the Australian sweet pipe, is a climbing shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its showy blooms and climbing habit.
