Pelargonium rungvense

Pelargonium rungvense, commonly known as the Rungven's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly serrated edge, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium rungvense is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive blooms and compact size.
