Piper guildingianum

Piper guildingianum, commonly known as the Guilingian pepper or Guilingian mountain pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark-colored berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
