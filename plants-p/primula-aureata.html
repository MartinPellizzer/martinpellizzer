Primula aureata

Primula aureata, commonly known as the golden primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are often used in traditional medicine and are also popular in horticulture for their ornamental value.
