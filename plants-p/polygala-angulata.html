Polygala angulata

Polygala angulata, commonly known as the angular milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Polygala angulata is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
