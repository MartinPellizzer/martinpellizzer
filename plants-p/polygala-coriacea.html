Polygala coriacea

Polygala coriacea, commonly known as the rough polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and flies.

The rough texture of its leaves and stems gives it its common name, reflecting its rugged appearance.
