Primula officinalis subsp. macrocalyx

Primula officinalis subsp.

macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and a prominent yellow throat.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and forested areas.

The species is valued both for its beauty and its historical use in traditional medicine.
