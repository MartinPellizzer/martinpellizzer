Pausandra sericea

Pausandra sericea, commonly known as silky pausandra, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in rich, sandy soils.

Pausandra sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
