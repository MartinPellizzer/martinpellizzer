Prunus spinosa f. coaetanea

Prunus spinosa f. coaetanea, commonly known as the common blackthorn, is a compact shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in hedgerows and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it produces fragrant white flowers in early spring.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to form dense thickets.
