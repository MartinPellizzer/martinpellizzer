Pauletia affinis

Pauletia affinis, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling stars. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pauletia affinis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in indoor spaces.
