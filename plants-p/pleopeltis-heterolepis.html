Pleopeltis heterolepis

Pleopeltis heterolepis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its rhizome that produces both roots and leafy shoots. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive appearance that aids in identification.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
