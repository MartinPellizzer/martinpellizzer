Porcellites apargioides

Porcellites apargioides, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
