Potentilla atrovirens

Potentilla atrovirens, commonly known as blackleaf potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
