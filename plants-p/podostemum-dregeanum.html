Podostemum dregeanum

Podostemum dregeanum, commonly known as the aquatic bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often covered in tiny, bladder-like structures used for trapping small aquatic organisms. The plant thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as lakes and rivers. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with four to five petals, and they bloom in the early morning.

Podostemum dregeanum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic species.
