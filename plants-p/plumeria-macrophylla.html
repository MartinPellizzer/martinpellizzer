Plumeria macrophylla

Plumeria macrophylla, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical plant native to Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, and fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, pink, and orange. The plant has simple, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often shed during drought periods. Its flowers are typically borne on long, slender stalks and are highly valued for their ornamental beauty.

Plumeria macrophylla is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a symbol in various cultural practices.
