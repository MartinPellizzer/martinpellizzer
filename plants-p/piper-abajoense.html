Piper abajoense

Piper abajoense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetlands and riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and invasive nature have made it a concern in some regions.
