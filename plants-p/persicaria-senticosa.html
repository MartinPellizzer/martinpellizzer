Persicaria senticosa

Persicaria senticosa, commonly known as sticky willow or sweet willow, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often sticky stems and alternate leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands, and it is often found in woodland areas.

Persicaria senticosa is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a spreading growth habit.
