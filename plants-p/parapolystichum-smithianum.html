Parapolystichum smithianum

Parapolystichum smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's polypody, is a fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fronds are divided into pinnae, which are lance-shaped and have a serrated margin.
