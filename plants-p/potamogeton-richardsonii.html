Potamogeton richardsonii

Potamogeton richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
