Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. alba

Phalaenopsis tetraspis f. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous white flowers with a velvety texture. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal manner with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in homes and greenhouses.
