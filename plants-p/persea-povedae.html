Persea povedae

Persea povedae, commonly known as the Colombian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean region of Colombia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers. Its fruit is a yellow to orange berry that ripens in the dry season.

The botanical features of Persea povedae make it an important species in its native ecosystem and a potential candidate for horticultural cultivation.
