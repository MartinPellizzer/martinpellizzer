Persicaria omissa

Persicaria omissa, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture, and they tend to be alternate along the stem.

Persicaria omissa thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.
