Physurus decorus

Physurus decorus, commonly known as the ornate spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and showy inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, so care should be taken when handling it.
