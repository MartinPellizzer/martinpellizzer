Potentilla recta proles balcanica

Potentilla recta proles balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan upright cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
