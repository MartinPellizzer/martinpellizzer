Pappostipa nicorae

Pappostipa nicorae, commonly known as the Nicora grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats and is often found in temperate regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Pappostipa nicorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
