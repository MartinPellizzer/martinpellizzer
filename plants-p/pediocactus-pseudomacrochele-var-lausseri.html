Pediocactus pseudomacrochele var. lausseri

Pediocactus pseudomacrochele var. lausseri, commonly known as the Lausser's false giant cactus, is a rare and unique cactus species native to a limited region in Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often with a reddish or greenish hue. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a spineless variety, making it more accessible for handling compared to its spiny relatives.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
