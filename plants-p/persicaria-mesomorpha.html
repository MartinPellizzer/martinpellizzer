Persicaria mesomorpha

Persicaria mesomorpha, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks.

Persicaria mesomorpha is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural areas.
