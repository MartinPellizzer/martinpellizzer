Phlox lanceolata

Phlox lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range from pink to lavender, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Phlox lanceolata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
