Pandanus flagellifer

Pandanus flagellifer, commonly known as the whip pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that resemble a whip, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to saline environments.
