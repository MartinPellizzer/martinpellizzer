Poa kirkii var. incrassata

Poa kirkii var. incrassata, commonly known as the thickleaf meadowgrass, is a variant of the Poa kirkii species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and coastal areas. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets, which are usually green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
