Parodia heyeriana

Parodia heyeriana, commonly known as the "Pincushion Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globular shape and spiny, segmented stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
