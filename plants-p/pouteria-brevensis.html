Pouteria brevensis

Pouteria brevensis, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible fruits that resemble small chestnuts. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife and has been traditionally used by indigenous communities.

Botanically, it belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is known for its hardy nature and adaptability to humid tropical climates.
