Protea mutica

Protea mutica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, petal-like bracts that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Protea mutica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
