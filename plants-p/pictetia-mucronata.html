Pictetia mucronata

Pictetia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-lobed pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually narrow, leathery, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Pictetia mucronata is often found in coastal and lowland areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
