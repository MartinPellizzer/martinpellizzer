Pycreus mucronatus

Pycreus mucronatus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are velvety and heart-shaped, providing a lush backdrop for the blooms.

Pycreus mucronatus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
