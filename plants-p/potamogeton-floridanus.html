Potamogeton floridanus

Potamogeton floridanus, commonly known as Florida pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton floridanus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
