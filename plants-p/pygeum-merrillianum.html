Pygeum merrillianum

Pygeum merrillianum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, cream-colored flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, almond-like seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
