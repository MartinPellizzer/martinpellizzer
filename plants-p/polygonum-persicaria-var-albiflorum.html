Polygonum persicaria var. albiflorum

Polygonum persicaria var. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are typically hairless or slightly hairy. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. Its most distinguishing feature is the showy, white to pale pink flowers that appear in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
