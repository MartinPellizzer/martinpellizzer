Passerina linariifolia

Passerina linariifolia, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a characteristic species of alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
