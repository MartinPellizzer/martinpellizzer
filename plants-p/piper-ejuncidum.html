Piper ejuncidum

Piper ejuncidum, commonly known as the slender pepper plant, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
