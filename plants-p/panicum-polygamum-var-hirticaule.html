Panicum polygamum var. hirticaule

Panicum polygamum var. hirticaule, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various habitats.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant restoration projects.
