Persicaria pratincola

Persicaria pratincola, commonly known as the common knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers with five petals. Its reproductive strategy involves both self-pollination and cross-pollination, allowing it to thrive in various habitats.

Persicaria pratincola is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an indicator species for wetland ecosystems.
