Paraprasophyllum hygrophilum

Paraprasophyllum hygrophilum, commonly known as the water-loving lichen, is a unique organism that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is a lichen, a composite organism formed by a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a photosynthetic partner, typically a green alga or cyanobacterium. This species is often found on rocks or tree bark in humid regions, where it can absorb moisture effectively. Its structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that helps it retain water and nutrients.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
