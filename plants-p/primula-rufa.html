Primula rufa

Primula rufa, commonly known as the red primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, often with a yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
