Pithecellobium novoguineense

Pithecellobium novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea legume, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruit is a flat, dehiscent pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests or along riverbanks.
