Phebalium glandulosum var. bullatum

Phebalium glandulosum var. bullatum, commonly known as the Bullatum honeysuckle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native gardens.
