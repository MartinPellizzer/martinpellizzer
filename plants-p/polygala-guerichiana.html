Polygala guerichiana

Polygala guerichiana, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a glossy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
