Pyrus aria proles typica

Pyrus aria proles typica, commonly known as the common pear.

It is a species of tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. It produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped.

The species is native to parts of Europe and has been cultivated for its fruit and ornamental qualities.
