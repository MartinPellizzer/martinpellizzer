Primula dielsii

Primula dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula dielsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
