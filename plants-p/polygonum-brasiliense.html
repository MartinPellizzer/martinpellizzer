Polygonum brasiliense

Polygonum brasiliense, commonly known as Brazilian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, somewhat woody stems that often have a reddish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Polygonum brasiliense is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.

It is sometimes considered an invasive species in regions where it has been introduced.
