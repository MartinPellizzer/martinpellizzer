Panicum saccharoides

Panicum saccharoides, commonly known as sugar grass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, with narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its fibrous roots and rapid growth make it a valuable plant for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
