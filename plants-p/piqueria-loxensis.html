Piqueria loxensis

Piqueria loxensis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its yellow-orange flower heads are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

Piqueria loxensis is also noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
