Phyllanthus luzoniensis

Phyllanthus luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon starling tree, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland forests and secondary vegetation. The plant produces small, white or greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
