Pyrus ludovica

Pyrus ludovica, commonly known as the Ludovica pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries such as Uzbekistan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, with broad leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The Ludovica pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and potential horticultural uses.
