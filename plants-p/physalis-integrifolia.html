Physalis integrifolia

Physalis integrifolia, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, which turn red when mature. These fruits are edible and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible berries.
