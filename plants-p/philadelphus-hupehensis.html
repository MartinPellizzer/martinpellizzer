Philadelphus hupehensis

Philadelphus hupehensis, commonly known as the common mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairy underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus hupehensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for.
