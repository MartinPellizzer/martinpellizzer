Pancovia angustifolia

Pancovia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pancovia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 inches wide. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized horticultural collections.
