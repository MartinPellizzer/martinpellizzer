Phacelia congesta var. dissecta

Phacelia congesta var. dissecta, commonly known as the dissected phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a desirable addition to native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological restoration projects.
