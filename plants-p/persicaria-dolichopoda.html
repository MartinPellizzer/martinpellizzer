Persicaria dolichopoda

Persicaria dolichopoda, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants characterized by their bead-like fruits and often zigzag stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slight sheen.

Persicaria dolichopoda is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or saturated soils, making it a common sight in wetland ecosystems.
