Piper pubirhache

Piper pubirhache, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
