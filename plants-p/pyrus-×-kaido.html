Pyrus × kaido

Pyrus × kaido, commonly known as the Japanese pear.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Asian pear) and Pyrus communis (European pear). The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The fruit is typically round, with a crisp, juicy flesh and a sweet to slightly tart flavor.
