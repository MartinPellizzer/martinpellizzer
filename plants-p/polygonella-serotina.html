Polygonella serotina

Polygonella serotina, commonly known as the late polygonella, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of streams. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and have small, scale-like leaves.
