Passiflora pyrrhantha

Passiflora pyrrhantha, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a mix of red, yellow, and purple hues, with a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can grow quite long.

Passiflora pyrrhantha is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and fruit-eating animals.
