Persicaria koreensis

Persicaria koreensis, commonly known as Korean smartweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in wet or poorly drained soils.

Persicaria koreensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
