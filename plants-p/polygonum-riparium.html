Polygonum riparium

Polygonum riparium, commonly known as water smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil along streams, rivers, and ponds. The plant has erect, branching stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum riparium is known for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing shoreline soils.
