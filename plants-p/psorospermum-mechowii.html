Psorospermum mechowii

Psorospermum mechowii, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and limited natural range, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
