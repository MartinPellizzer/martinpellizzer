Phyllostachys edulis f. pachyloen

Phyllostachys edulis f. pachyloen, commonly known as the thick-walled bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its thick, sturdy culms that are often used in construction and as a decorative plant. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces slender, green leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern along the stems.

This bamboo is valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal in both traditional and modern landscaping.
