Pistacia khinjuk

Pistacia khinjuk, commonly known as the Indian pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a hard, spiny shell, which are prized for their rich flavor and nutritional value. It thrives in dry, warm climates and is often cultivated for both its nuts and ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
