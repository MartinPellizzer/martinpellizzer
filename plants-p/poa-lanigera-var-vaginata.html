Poa lanigera var. vaginata

Poa lanigera var. vaginata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades that are often covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually blue-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
