Pelargonium × compar

Pelargonium × compar, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium × compar is often used in hanging baskets and containers due to its cascading foliage and continuous blooming throughout the growing season.
