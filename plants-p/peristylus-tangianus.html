Peristylus tangianus

Peristylus tangianus, commonly known as the Tanganyikan peristylus, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Lake Tanganyika region in East Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, ribbon-like leaves that float just beneath the water surface, creating a striking visual effect. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate light exposure. Its root system is adapted to anchor in the substrate while its stems spread horizontally.

Peristylus tangianus is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appearance and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic life.
