Potentilla chionea var. malyschevii

Potentilla chionea var. malyschevii, commonly known as the white potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, arching stems that produce clusters of small, white flowers in early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blossoms and compact growth habit.
