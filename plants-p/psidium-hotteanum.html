Psidium hotteanum

Psidium hotteanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or hotte's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small cherries. The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and edible qualities.
