Plantago barbata var. caespitosa

Plantago barbata var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, oval leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This variant is distinguished by its compact growth habit and is valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
