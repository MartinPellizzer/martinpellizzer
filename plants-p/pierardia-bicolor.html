Pierardia bicolor

Pierardia bicolor, commonly known as the twinleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive leaves that are divided into two lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are borne on slender stalks. Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
