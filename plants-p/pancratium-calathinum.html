Pancratium calathinum

Pancratium calathinum, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, adapting it to arid environments.

Pancratium calathinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
