Polysphaeria cleistocalyx var. cleistocalyx

Polysphaeria cleistocalyx var. cleistocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically enclosed within leaf-like bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
