Piper lineatum

Piper lineatum, commonly known as the lineated pepper or creeping pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or silver vein pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
