Poa nemoralis var. vivipara

Poa nemoralis var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its ability to produce viviparous offspring, where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. This unique reproductive strategy allows it to colonize disturbed or nutrient-poor soils more effectively. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves.

It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and along pathways.
