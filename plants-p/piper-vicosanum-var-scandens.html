Piper vicosanum var. scandens

Piper vicosanum var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elliptical berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
