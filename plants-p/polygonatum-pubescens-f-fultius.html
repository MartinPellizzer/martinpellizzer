Polygonatum pubescens f. fultius

Polygonatum pubescens f. fultius, commonly known as the hairy Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant characterized by its dense, pubescent foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features long, arching stems with clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for various wildlife species.
