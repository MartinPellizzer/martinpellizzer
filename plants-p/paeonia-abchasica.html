Paeonia abchasica

Paeonia abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red, with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure. The plant features robust, upright stems and broad, palmately compound leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and borders.

Paeonia abchasica is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its historical significance in traditional medicine.
