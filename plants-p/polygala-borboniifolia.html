Polygala borboniifolia

Polygala borboniifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the island's unique climate. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open when mature.

Polygala borboniifolia is of interest to botanists due to its endemic status and ecological significance in its native environment.
