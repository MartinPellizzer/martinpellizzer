Prunus jamasakura subvar. pubescens

Prunus jamasakura subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with pubescent characteristics, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is distinguished by its finely hairy leaves and delicate, pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species. Its foliage turns a striking yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
