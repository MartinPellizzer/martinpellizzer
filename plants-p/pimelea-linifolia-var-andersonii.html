Pimelea linifolia var. andersonii

Pimelea linifolia var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's feathery riceflower, is a low-growing shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, feathery inflorescences that produce small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens and restoration projects.
