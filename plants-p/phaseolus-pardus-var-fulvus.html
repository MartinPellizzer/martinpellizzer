Phaseolus pardus var. fulvus

Phaseolus pardus var. fulvus, commonly known as the golden runner bean, is a variant of the runner bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its yellowish-orange pods and edible seeds, which are typically harvested when young and tender. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
