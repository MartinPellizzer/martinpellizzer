Potamogeton lucens var. insignis

Potamogeton lucens var. insignis, commonly known as the golden pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically bright green to golden in color, depending on light conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
