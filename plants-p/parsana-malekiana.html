Parsana malekiana

Parsana malekiana, commonly known as the Malakian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family, which includes various bellflowers and similar species. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Parsana malekiana is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
