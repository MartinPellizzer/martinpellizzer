Piper mituense

Piper mituense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Mituense pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is a climbing vine that can reach lengths of several meters, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a tiny berry, contains the spicy compound responsible for its characteristic flavor.

Due to its unique properties, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
