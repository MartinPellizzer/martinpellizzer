Passiflora foetida var. hispida

Passiflora foetida var. hispida, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant has rough, hairy stems and leaves, and its fruits are typically red or yellow, containing edible, sweet pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
