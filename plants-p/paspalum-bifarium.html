Paspalum bifarium

Paspalum bifarium, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate and subtropical regions.

Paspalum bifarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
