Piper tenuifolium

Piper tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Piper tenuifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
