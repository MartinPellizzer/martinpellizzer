Persea membranacea

Persea membranacea, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are typically orange to yellow when ripe. Its bark is thick and peels easily, revealing a reddish inner layer.

Persea membranacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
