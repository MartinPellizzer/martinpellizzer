Pentas nobilis

Pentas nobilis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pentas nobilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a flowering plant in tropical landscapes.
