Pulsatilla hirsutissima

Pulsatilla hirsutissima, commonly known as the hirsute pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy stems that support its delicate, palmate leaves. Its flowers consist of numerous stamens and a single pistil, typical of the genus Pulsatilla.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
