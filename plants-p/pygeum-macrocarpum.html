Pygeum macrocarpum

Pygeum macrocarpum, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces large, woody fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing it with some resistance to fire and drought.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
