Pleurothallis mathildae

Pleurothallis mathildae, commonly known as the Mathilde's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are ovate and arranged in two rows, typical of many orchid species.

Pleurothallis mathildae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
