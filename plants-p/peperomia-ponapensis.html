Peperomia ponapensis

Peperomia ponapensis, commonly known as the Ponapé peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the island of Ponapé in the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Peperomia ponapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
