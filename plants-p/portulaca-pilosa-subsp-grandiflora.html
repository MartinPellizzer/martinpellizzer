Portulaca pilosa subsp. grandiflora

Portulaca pilosa subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, succulent annual that features fleshy, ribbed stems and small, spiny leaves.

The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often appearing in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-adapted to drought conditions, with thick leaves that store water efficiently.
