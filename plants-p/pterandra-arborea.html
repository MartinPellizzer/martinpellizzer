Pterandra arborea

Pterandra arborea, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble flames, composed of numerous small, bright red flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
