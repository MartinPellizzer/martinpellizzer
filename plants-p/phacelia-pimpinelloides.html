Phacelia pimpinelloides

Phacelia pimpinelloides, commonly known as mountain phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or open habitats. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Phacelia pimpinelloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
