Polygala trinervia

Polygala trinervia, commonly known as the three-veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, usually white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polygala trinervia is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
