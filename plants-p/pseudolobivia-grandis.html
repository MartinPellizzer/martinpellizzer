Pseudolobivia grandis

Pseudolobivia grandis, commonly known as the grand giant cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the deserts of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, making it one of the tallest cacti in the world. The plant features prominent areoles with spines and produces large, showy yellow flowers during the dry season. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Pseudolobivia grandis is also known for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, sometimes living over a century.
