Phoradendron pauciflorum

Phoradendron pauciflorum, commonly known as the scarlet shower vine, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing on trees or shrubs.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
