Potentilla heptaphylla var. coronensis

Potentilla heptaphylla var. coronensis, commonly known as the crown potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged in a dense, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
