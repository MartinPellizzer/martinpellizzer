Phalangium tenuifolium

Phalangium tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phalangium, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often arranged alternately along the stem, and the flowers are borne in dense inflorescences.

Phalangium tenuifolium is known for its relatively slow growth and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
