Primula woronovii

Primula woronovii, commonly known as the Woronov's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
