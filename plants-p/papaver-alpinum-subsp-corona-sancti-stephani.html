Papaver alpinum subsp. corona-sancti-stephani

Papaver alpinum subsp.

corona-sancti-stephani, commonly known as the Saint Stephen's crown poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of the Alps. It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that form a crown-like shape, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool and sunny conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
