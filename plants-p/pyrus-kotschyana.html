Pyrus kotschyana

Pyrus kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschyan pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears that ripen in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary qualities.
