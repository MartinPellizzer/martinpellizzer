Pleea

Pleea, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

Pleea species are known for their ability to thrive in moist environments and are often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.
