Papaver alpinum subsp. sendtneri

Papaver alpinum subsp.

sendtneri, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Alps. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It has smooth, dark green leaves and a short, sturdy stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
