Philadelphus brachybotrys var. purpurascens

Philadelphus brachybotrys var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purpleleaf treebine, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a distinctive purplish hue on the underside, giving the plant its common name. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in racemes, and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
