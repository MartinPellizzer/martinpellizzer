Passiflora × atropurpurea

Passiflora × atropurpurea, commonly known as the Purple Passionflower, is a hybrid species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is a climbing vine that produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in tendrils that aid in climbing.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
