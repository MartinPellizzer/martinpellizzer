Priestleya virgata

Priestleya virgata, commonly known as the silver spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, spider-like inflorescences that consist of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, silvery coating that gives it its common name.

Priestleya virgata is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in arid landscapes.
