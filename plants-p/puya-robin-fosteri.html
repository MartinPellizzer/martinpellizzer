Puya robin-fosteri

Puya robin-fosteri, commonly known as the Foster's torchflower, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 10 feet in height. The plant produces vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, forming a water-holding structure that supports its unique ecosystem.

Puya robin-fosteri is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific high-altitude conditions to thrive.
