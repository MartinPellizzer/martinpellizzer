Persicaria changhuaensis

Persicaria changhuaensis, commonly known as the Changhua knotweed, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species like buckwheat and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks.

Persicaria changhuaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
