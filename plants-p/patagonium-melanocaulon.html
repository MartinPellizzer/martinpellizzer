Patagonium melanocaulon

Patagonium melanocaulon, commonly known as the black-stemmed Patagonian grass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, temperate climates and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low nutrient soils.

Patagonium melanocaulon plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitat.
