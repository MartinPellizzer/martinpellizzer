Parashorea laotica

Parashorea laotica, commonly known as the Laotian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark-colored wood. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its valuable timber, Parashorea laotica is also considered an endangered species in its native habitat.
