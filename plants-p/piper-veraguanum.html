Piper veraguanum

Piper veraguanum, commonly known as the Guatemalan pepper or Veragua pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their pungent and medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal applications.
