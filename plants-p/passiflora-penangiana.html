Passiflora penangiana

Passiflora penangiana, commonly known as the Penang passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora penangiana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
