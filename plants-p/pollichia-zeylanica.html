Pollichia zeylanica

Pollichia zeylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon gooseberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, bright red berries. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its berries are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
