Protium nitidifolium

Protium nitidifolium, commonly known as the white cedar or Indian cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and construction.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and contributing to soil stability in tropical forests.
