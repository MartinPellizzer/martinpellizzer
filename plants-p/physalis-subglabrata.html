Physalis subglabrata

Physalis subglabrata, commonly known as the smooth husk tomato, is a species native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into distinctive orange-red calyces enclosing the berry-like fruits. The fruits are typically less than 1 cm in diameter and have a tart, acidic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional cuisines.
