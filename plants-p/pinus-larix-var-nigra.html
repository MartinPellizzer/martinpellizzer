Pinus larix var. nigra

Pinus larix var. nigra, commonly known as the black spruce, is a coniferous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in cold, boreal climates. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds. It is often found in swampy or poorly drained soils, where its roots help prevent erosion.

This species plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
