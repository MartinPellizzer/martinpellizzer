Polygala pleioclada

Polygala pleioclada, commonly known as the many-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and opposite. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
