Potentilla taurica var. angustissima

Potentilla taurica var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and forest-steppe habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Potentilla genus, sharing similar ecological and botanical traits.
