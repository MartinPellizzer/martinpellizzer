Poa karateginensis

Poa karateginensis, commonly known as the Karategin sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
