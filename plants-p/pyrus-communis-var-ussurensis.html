Pyrus communis var. ussurensis

Pyrus communis var. ussurensis, commonly known as the Ussurel pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow to orange in color when ripe. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture and a distinct serrated margin. This variety is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
