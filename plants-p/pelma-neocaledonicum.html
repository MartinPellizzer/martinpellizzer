Pelma neocaledonicum

Pelma neocaledonicum, commonly known as the New Caledonian pelma, is a unique plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within the tropical rainforests of the region. Its leaves are often used in traditional practices by the local Kanak people.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
