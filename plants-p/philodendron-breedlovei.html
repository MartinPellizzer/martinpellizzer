Philodendron breedlovei

Philodendron breedlovei, commonly known as the greenheart philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pollinated by insects. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and spread through the canopy of its natural habitat.

Philodendron breedlovei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
