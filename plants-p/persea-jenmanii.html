Persea jenmanii

Persea jenmanii, commonly known as the Java persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
