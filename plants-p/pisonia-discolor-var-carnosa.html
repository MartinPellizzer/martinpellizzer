Pisonia discolor var. carnosa

Pisonia discolor var. carnosa, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
