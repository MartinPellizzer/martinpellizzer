Piper resacanum

Piper resacanum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-chambered berries. The berries, when dried, are the source of the spice black pepper, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both traditional medicine and global gastronomy.
