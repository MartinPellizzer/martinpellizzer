Pancovia sessiliflora

Pancovia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flowered pancovia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or white in color. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Pancovia sessiliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
