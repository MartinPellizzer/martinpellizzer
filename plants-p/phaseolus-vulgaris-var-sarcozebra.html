Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcozebra

Phaseolus vulgaris var. sarcozebra, commonly known as the zebra bean, is a variety of common bean characterized by its distinctive mottled or striped seed coat.

This tropical annual legume belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to Central and South America. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, producing upright pods that contain 4–6 oval-shaped seeds. Its botanical features include compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The zebra bean is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its edible seeds in some regions.
