Papyrius kaempferi

Papyrius kaempferi, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Papyrius kaempferi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
