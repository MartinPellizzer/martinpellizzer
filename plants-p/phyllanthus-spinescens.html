Phyllanthus spinescens

Phyllanthus spinescens, commonly known as the spiny goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and oval, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
