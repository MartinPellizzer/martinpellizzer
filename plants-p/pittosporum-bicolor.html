Pittosporum bicolor

Pittosporum bicolor, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Pittosporum bicolor is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its use in landscaping.
