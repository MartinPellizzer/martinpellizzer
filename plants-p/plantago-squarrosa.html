Plantago squarrosa

Plantago squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features rosettes of broad, grayish-green, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which are often hidden among the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
