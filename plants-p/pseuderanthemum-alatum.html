Pseuderanthemum alatum

Pseuderanthemum alatum, commonly known as the winged pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that often have prominent veins and a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals and a tubular corolla. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
