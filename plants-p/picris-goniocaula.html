Picris goniocaula

Picris goniocaula, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and aromatic. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often found in dry, rocky soils and is considered a hardy species adapted to Mediterranean climates.
