Potentilla × boreocaucasica

Potentilla × boreocaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring multiple stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are often followed by red or orange-colored bracts. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in a fine pubescence.

Potentilla × boreocaucasica is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
