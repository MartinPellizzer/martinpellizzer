Polygonum hispidulum

Polygonum hispidulum, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils. The plant features erect stems that are often covered in stiff, bristly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and can reach up to 10 centimeters in length.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer.
