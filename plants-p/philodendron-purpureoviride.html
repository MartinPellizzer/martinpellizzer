Philodendron purpureoviride

Philodendron purpureoviride, commonly known as the purple-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves with prominent purple veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf coloration makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical plant collections.
