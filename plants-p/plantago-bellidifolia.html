Plantago bellidifolia

Plantago bellidifolia, commonly known as the Sierra plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The leaves and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
