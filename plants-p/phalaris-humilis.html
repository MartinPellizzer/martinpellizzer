Phalaris humilis

Phalaris humilis, commonly known as common barnyard grass, is a low-growing annual grass species native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It features narrow, flat leaves that are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and agricultural areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
