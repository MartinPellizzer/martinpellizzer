Phaseolus sphaerospermus

Phaseolus sphaerospermus, commonly known as the globular bean, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spherical seed pods. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

The seeds are enclosed in inflated, globular pods, which give the plant its common name.
