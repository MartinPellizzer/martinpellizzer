Pycnanthemum incanum var. puberulum

Pycnanthemum incanum var. puberulum, commonly known as white wild indigo, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces small, clustered blue or violet flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
