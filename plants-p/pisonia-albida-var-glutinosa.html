Pisonia albida var. glutinosa

Pisonia albida var. glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky pisonia, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, fluffy flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds covered in a sticky substance, which aids in seed dispersal. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
