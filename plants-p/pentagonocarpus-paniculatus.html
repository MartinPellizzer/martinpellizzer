Pentagonocarpus paniculatus

Pentagonocarpus paniculatus, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its ecological importance and limited natural range, it is considered a valuable but threatened species in its native habitat.
