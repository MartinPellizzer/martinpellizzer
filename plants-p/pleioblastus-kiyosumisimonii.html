Pleioblastus kiyosumisimonii

Pleioblastus kiyosumisimonii, commonly known as the Kiyosumi bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its foliage is often a vibrant green, and it is valued for its ornamental appearance in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in traditional Japanese landscaping.
