Prunus serrulata subvar. hortualis

Prunus serrulata subvar. hortualis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
