Primula heeri

Primula heeri, commonly known as the Heeri's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
