Plantago columnae

Plantago columnae, commonly known as the columnar plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces dense clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in spikes and are typically greenish-white in color.

Plantago columnae is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
