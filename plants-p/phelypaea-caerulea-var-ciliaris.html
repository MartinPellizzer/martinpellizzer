Phelypaea caerulea var. ciliaris

Phelypaea caerulea var. ciliaris, commonly known as the blue water star, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features delicate, floating leaves that are typically blue or bluish-green in color, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for water gardens and pond landscapes.
