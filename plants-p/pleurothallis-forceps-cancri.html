Pleurothallis forceps-cancri

Pleurothallis forceps-cancri, commonly known as the Cancer crab claw orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, claw-like flowers that resemble the shape of a crab's claw. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white, pink, and purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its striking appearance.
