Psorodendron arborescens

Psorodendron arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like psorodendron, is a unique plant characterized by its branching structure that resembles a tree.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh conditions. The plant features small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss and help it thrive in dry climates. Its flowers are usually inconspicuous, with the main attraction being its woody, tree-like form.

Psorodendron arborescens is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
