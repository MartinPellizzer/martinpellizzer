Potentilla patula

Potentilla patula, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.

Potentilla patula is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
