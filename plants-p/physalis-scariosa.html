Physalis scariosa

Physalis scariosa, commonly known as the husk tomato or golden berry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive papery calyx that encases the fruit. The fruit is small, yellow-orange, and turns red when ripe, often resembling a tiny lantern. The plant produces small white to violet flowers and thrives in warm, dry climates.

It is valued for its edible fruit, which has a sweet-tart flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
