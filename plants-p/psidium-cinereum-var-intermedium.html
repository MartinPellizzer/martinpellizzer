Psidium cinereum var. intermedium

Psidium cinereum var. intermedium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or intermedium guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that produces small, white, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries. The leaves are ovate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. The fruit is typically yellow or red when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
