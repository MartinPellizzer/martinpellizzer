Percepier arvensis

Percepier arvensis, commonly known as the field periwinkle, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces delicate blue or violet flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Percepier arvensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
