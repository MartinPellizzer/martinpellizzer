Polyactium filipendulifolium

Polyactium filipendulifolium, commonly known as the hanging leaf mallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, trailing stems that produce clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals. The leaves are elongated and often have a feathery appearance, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ability to spread and form dense ground cover.
