Phoradendron chaguaramasanum

Phoradendron chaguaramasanum, commonly known as the Chaguaramasana mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Ficus. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system. Its small, white or pale green leaves are often clustered at the tips of branches, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds.

This species plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat, contributing to nutrient cycling and providing food for various wildlife.
