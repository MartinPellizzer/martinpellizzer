Polymnia silphioides

Polymnia silphioides, commonly known as the white lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often edible seeds. The plant features slender, erect stems with heart-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, producing a delicate, fragrant appearance.

Polymnia silphioides is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
