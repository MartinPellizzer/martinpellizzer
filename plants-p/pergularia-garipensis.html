Pergularia garipensis

Pergularia garipensis, commonly known as the Turkish dwarf sedge, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its botanical classification places it within the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family.

Pergularia garipensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
