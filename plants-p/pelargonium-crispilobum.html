Pelargonium crispilobum

Pelargonium crispilobum, commonly known as the crinkled-leaf geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive crinkled leaves and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
