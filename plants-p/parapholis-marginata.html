Parapholis marginata

Parapholis marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and roadside areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive white or pale margin. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Parapholis marginata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
