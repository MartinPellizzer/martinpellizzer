Prunus incisa f. globosa

Prunus incisa f. globosa, commonly known as the globular cherry, is a compact, rounded shrub with dense, dark green foliage.

It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or black berries in late summer. The plant is native to Japan and is valued for its ornamental appeal and winter interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique form and seasonal color changes, it is a popular choice for small gardens and container planting.
