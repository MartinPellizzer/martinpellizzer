Psychrophyton grandiflorum

Psychrophyton grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant frostflower, is a unique plant adapted to extreme cold environments.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant has a low-growing, mat-like structure with thick, waxy leaves that help retain heat and moisture. It thrives in high-altitude regions and is often found in alpine tundra ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important indicator species for environmental changes in cold climates.
