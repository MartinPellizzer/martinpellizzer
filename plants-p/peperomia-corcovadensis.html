Peperomia corcovadensis

Peperomia corcovadensis, commonly known as the coral plant or velvet peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or maroon hues, giving it a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by tiny, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and attractive foliage, it is often used as a decorative plant in both home and office settings.
