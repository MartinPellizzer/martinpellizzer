Pteronia acerosa

Pteronia acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, succulent stems and narrow, rigid leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pteronia acerosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
