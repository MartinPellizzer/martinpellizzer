Pereskia amapola var. argentina

Pereskia amapola var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine star cactus, is a unique cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Despite its cactus classification, it has a more shrub-like appearance due to its branching structure and leaf-like stems.
