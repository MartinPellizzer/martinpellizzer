Physocarpus intermedius f. parvifolius

Physocarpus intermedius f. parvifolius, commonly known as the dwarf ninebark, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are densely arranged along the stems.

It produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by velvety, red-purple berries in the fall. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial to full sun with well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban conditions, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage changes color in the fall, adding seasonal interest to the landscape.
