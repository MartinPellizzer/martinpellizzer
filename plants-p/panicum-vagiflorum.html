Panicum vagiflorum

Panicum vagiflorum, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it can tolerate periodic flooding. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture, and it has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Panicum vagiflorum plays a role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.
