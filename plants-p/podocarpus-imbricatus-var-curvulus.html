Podocarpus imbricatus var. curvulus

Podocarpus imbricatus var. curvulus, commonly known as the curved-leaved podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and curved, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
