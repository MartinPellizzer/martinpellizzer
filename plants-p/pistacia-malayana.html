Pistacia malayana

Pistacia malayana, commonly known as the Malay pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and mango. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often alternate and pinnate. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are followed by small, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
