Potentilla daisenensis

Potentilla daisenensis, commonly known as the Daisen potentilla, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. It has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
