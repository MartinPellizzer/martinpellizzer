Pulegium pubescens

Pulegium pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It often grows in moist, calcareous soils and is sometimes found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

Pulegium pubescens is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
