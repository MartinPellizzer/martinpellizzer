Piper chawya

Piper chawya, commonly known as the Chawya pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. These berries are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The leaves are also utilized in some indigenous practices for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
