Phyllota diffusa

Phyllota diffusa, commonly known as the Mexican coffee plant, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
