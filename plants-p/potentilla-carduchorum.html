Potentilla carduchorum

Potentilla carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla carduchorum is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
