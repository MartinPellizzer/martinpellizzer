Paeonia officinalis subsp. microcarpa

Paeonia officinalis subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy, and often fragrant flowers that can range in color from white to deep pink.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several leaflets, and it produces yellowish-orange anthers in its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other peony species.
