Polygala subspinosa var. heterorhyncha

Polygala subspinosa var. heterorhyncha, commonly known as the spinous milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, providing some protection against water loss.

The species is part of the Polygalaceae family, known for its diverse and often showy floral structures.
