Phyllanthus caledonicus

Phyllanthus caledonicus, commonly known as the mountain kudzu, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

The species is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
