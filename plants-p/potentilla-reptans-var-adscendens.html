Potentilla reptans var. adscendens

Potentilla reptans var. adscendens, commonly known as the ascending potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, creeping stems that often ascend slightly, forming dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a rounded or pointed shape.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.
