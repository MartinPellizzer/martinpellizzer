Phoebe Formosana

Phoebe formosana is a rare and fascinating plant native to Taiwan, known for its unique appearance and ecological significance. It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes well-known species like avocado and cinnamon. This evergreen shrub or small tree typically grows in moist, shaded environments within mountainous regions. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a distinct aromatic scent. Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Phoebe formosana is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
