Phlox andicola subsp. parvula

Phlox andicola subsp.

parvula, commonly known as the dwarf phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States. It typically features compact, mound-like growth with clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
