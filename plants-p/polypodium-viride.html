Polypodium viride

Polypodium viride, commonly known as the green wall fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, lush foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with a serrated margin, and its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, moss-like layer. It is often found growing on tree trunks or rocks in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in many parts of the world.
