Physalis victoriana

Physalis victoriana, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its distinctive lantern-like calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit is typically orange to yellow in color, with a soft, edible pulp surrounding the seeds. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into the characteristic inflated, papery husks.

It is often cultivated for its tart flavor and ornamental value.
