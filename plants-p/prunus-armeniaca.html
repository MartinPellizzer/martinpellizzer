Prunus armeniaca

Prunus armeniaca, commonly known as the peach tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia, particularly China.

It produces sweet, fleshy fruits known as peaches, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The tree has a broad, spreading crown and can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its flowers are typically pink or white and bloom in early spring before the leaves appear.

The fruit's skin is thin and varies in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety.
