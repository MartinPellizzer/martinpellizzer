Protea decurrens

Protea decurrens, commonly known as the silver protea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its striking, urn-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a silvery-grey waxy coating.

Protea decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
