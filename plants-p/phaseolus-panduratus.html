Phaseolus panduratus

Phaseolus panduratus, commonly known as the golden bean or yellow runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Phaseolus panduratus is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
