Prunus padus f. seoulensis

Prunus padus f. seoulensis, commonly known as the Seoul cherry, is a distinct form of the common bird cherry.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by dark red or black berries. The plant is native to Korea and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the bark is smooth and grayish-brown.
