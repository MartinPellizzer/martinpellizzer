Pleurothallopsis clausa

Pleurothallopsis clausa, commonly known as the closed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has intricate, lance-shaped leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
