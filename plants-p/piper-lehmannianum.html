Piper lehmannianum

Piper lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
