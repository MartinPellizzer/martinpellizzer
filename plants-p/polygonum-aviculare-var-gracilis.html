Polygonum aviculare var. gracilis

Polygonum aviculare var. gracilis, commonly known as slender knotweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pale green to reddish leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water bodies. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds make it a common weed in various habitats.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and compact growth habit, which aid in its survival in competitive environments.
