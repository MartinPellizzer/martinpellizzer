Piper scutellatum

Piper scutellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper or creeping pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
