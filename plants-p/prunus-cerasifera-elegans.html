Prunus cerasifera elegans

Prunus cerasifera elegans, commonly known as the European cherry or Prunus species, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
