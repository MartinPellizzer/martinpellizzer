Portlandia lumana

Portlandia lumana, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, Portlandia lumana is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
