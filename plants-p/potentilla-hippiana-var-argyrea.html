Potentilla hippiana var. argyrea

Potentilla hippiana var. argyrea, commonly known as silver potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice in horticulture.
