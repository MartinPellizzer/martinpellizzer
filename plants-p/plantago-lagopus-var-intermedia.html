Plantago lagopus var. intermedia

Plantago lagopus var. intermedia, commonly known as the European common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
