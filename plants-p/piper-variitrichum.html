Piper variitrichum

Piper variitrichum, commonly known as the variable pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a one-seeded, globose capsule that matures to a yellowish color.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
