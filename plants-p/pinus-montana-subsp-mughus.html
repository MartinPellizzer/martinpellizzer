Pinus montana subsp. mughus

Pinus montana subsp.

mughus, commonly known as the Mughan pine, is a subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure.

The tree typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it adapts to cold and dry conditions. Its cones are woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
