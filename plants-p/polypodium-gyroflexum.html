Polypodium gyroflexum

Polypodium gyroflexum, commonly known as the zigzag holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply divided fronds that often curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and stream sides. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, though its primary botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitats.
