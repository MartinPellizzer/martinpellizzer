Panicum verticillatum var. coloratum

Panicum verticillatum var. coloratum, commonly known as the redtop grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish-purple coloration, especially during the late summer and autumn months. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest to landscapes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

This ornamental grass is valued for its vibrant color and ability to provide texture and movement in the garden.
