Poinciana roxburghii

Poinciana roxburghii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical ornamental tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red or orange-pink inflorescences that appear in late spring or early summer. The tree has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often planted for its vibrant floral display.

The seeds are encased in a fibrous, woody pod that splits open when mature.
