Potentilla opaca subsp. australis

Potentilla opaca subsp.

australis, commonly known as the New Zealand potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
