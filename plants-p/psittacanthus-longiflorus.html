Psittacanthus longiflorus

Psittacanthus longiflorus, commonly known as the long-flowered staghorn fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its large, tree-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, with a distinctive staghorn-like shape formed by the fertile fronds. The plant produces long, narrow spore-bearing spikes that emerge from the base of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and among enthusiasts of exotic flora.
