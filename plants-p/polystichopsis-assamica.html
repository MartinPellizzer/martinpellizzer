Polystichopsis assamica

Polystichopsis assamica, commonly known as the Assam cliff fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender fronds that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant typically grows on rocky cliffs and in shaded, moist environments. Its spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichopsis assamica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
