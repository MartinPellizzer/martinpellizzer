Phalaris minor f. subcylindrica

Phalaris minor f. subcylindrica, commonly known as the narrow cylinder false brome, is a grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
