Psiadia urticifolia

Psiadia urticifolia, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, which are typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Psiadia urticifolia is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
