Phyllanthus caudatifolius

Phyllanthus caudatifolius, commonly known as the cat-tail phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble a cat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
