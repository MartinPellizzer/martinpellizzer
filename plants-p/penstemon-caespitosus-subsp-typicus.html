Penstemon caespitosus subsp. typicus

Penstemon caespitosus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun. Its leaves are linear and hairy, and it produces numerous small, seed-like fruits that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
