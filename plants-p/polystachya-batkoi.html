Polystachya batkoi

Polystachya batkoi, commonly known as the Batko's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Polystachya batkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral structure.
