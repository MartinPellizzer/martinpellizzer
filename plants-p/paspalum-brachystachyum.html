Paspalum brachystachyum

Paspalum brachystachyum, commonly known as flat-stemmed paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
