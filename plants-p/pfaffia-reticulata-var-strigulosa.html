Pfaffia reticulata var. strigulosa

Pfaffia reticulata var. strigulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain the seeds. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though research on its botanical and pharmacological aspects remains limited.

Its habitat is threatened due to deforestation and habitat loss.
