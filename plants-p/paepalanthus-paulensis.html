Paepalanthus paulensis

Paepalanthus paulensis, commonly known as the Brazilian sunflower, is a striking plant native to Brazil.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in sunny, well-drained environments, often found in open grasslands and savannas. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces numerous small, daisy-like blooms throughout the growing season.

Paepalanthus paulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
