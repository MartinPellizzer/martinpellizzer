Prunus lusitanica var. myrtifolia

Prunus lusitanica var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Portuguese cherry or myrtle-leaved cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its foliage and seasonal blooms, though it may be susceptible to certain pests and diseases.
