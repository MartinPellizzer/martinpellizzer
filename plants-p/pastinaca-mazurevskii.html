Pastinaca mazurevskii

Pastinaca mazurevskii, commonly known as the Polish turnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is characterized by its sturdy, cylindrical root that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in umbrella-shaped clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often cultivated for its edible roots and leaves.

Due to its historical significance in traditional medicine and agriculture, it remains a valued plant in certain regions.
