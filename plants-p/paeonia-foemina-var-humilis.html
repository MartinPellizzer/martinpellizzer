Paeonia foemina var. humilis

Paeonia foemina var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Papaveraceae) family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces single or semi-double blooms in shades of pink, white, and red, often appearing in early to mid-spring. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Paeonia foemina var. humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
