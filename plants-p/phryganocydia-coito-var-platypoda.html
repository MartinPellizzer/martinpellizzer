Phryganocydia coito var. platypoda

Phryganocydia coito var. platypoda, commonly known as the flat-topped erica, is a member of the Ericaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
