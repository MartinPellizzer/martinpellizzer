Persica davidiana var. nana

Persica davidiana var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese peach, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to China.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically a glossy green, providing a lush appearance throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in spring. Its fruit, though not typically cultivated for eating, is edible and resembles a miniature peach.

This variety is valued for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
