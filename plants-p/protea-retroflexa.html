Protea retroflexa

Protea retroflexa, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of flowers, with a distinctive central cone-like structure. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in nutrient-poor, sandy soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a dense, hairy appearance that gives it its common name.

Protea retroflexa is an important species in the fynbos vegetation type, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
