Picris asperrima

Picris asperrima, commonly known as the rough hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features serrated, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

Its name reflects both its rough texture and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
