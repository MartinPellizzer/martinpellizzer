Paraboea strobilacea

Paraboea strobilacea, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
