Pilosocereus cometes

Pilosocereus cometes, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, colorful tubercles that resemble a moon, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines and is typically green, while the colorful top is a separate organism called a scion. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
