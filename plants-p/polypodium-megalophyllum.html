Polypodium megalophyllum

Polypodium megalophyllum, commonly known as the giant shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive shield-like shape that gives the plant its common name.
