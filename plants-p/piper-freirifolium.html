Piper freirifolium

Piper freirifolium, commonly known as the Pacific mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, though it is not related to the black pepper plant. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, three-lobed berries that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along streams.
