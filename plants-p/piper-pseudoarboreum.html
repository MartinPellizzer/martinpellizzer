Piper pseudoarboreum

Piper pseudoarboreum, commonly known as the giant betel vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its roots and leaves are traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
