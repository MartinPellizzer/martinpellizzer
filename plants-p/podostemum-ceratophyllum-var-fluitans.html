Podostemum ceratophyllum var. fluitans

Podostemum ceratophyllum var. fluitans, commonly known as the floating water willow, is a unique aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are adapted for life in slow-moving or still freshwater environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water, with its roots anchored to submerged rocks or vegetation. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in green or reddish hues, and it plays a role in supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
