Porpax extinctoria

Porpax extinctoria, commonly known as the fire-extinguishing fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its specialized leaf structures.

Its fronds contain a high concentration of water and certain compounds that reduce the flammability of the plant, making it highly resistant to fire. This adaptation allows it to thrive in fire-prone environments such as savannas and dry forests. The plant's reproductive system involves spores, which are dispersed by wind, enabling it to colonize new areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is notable for its ecological role in fire ecosystems.
