Peltophorum linnaei

Peltophorum linnaei, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that appear in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and urban landscapes.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
