Paepalanthus crinitus

Paepalanthus crinitus, commonly known as the silky broomstick, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, hair-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
