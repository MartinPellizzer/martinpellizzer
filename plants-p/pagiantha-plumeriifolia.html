Pagiantha plumeriifolia

Pagiantha plumeriifolia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, resembling flames. The leaves are compound, with long, slender leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
