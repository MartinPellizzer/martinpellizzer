Pithecellobium ferdinandi-muelleri

Pithecellobium ferdinandi-muelleri, commonly known as the dwarf pisonia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
