Persicaria amurensis

Persicaria amurensis, commonly known as Amur knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purple in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes. It thrives in moist environments such as stream banks and wetlands, and it is often considered an invasive species in some regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ecologically significant and potentially problematic in non-native habitats.
