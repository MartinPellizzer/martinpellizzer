Pseudoentada rotundifolia

Pseudoentada rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf burr bean, is a tropical climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets and distinctive, woody pod fruits that contain several seeds. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a forage plant.
