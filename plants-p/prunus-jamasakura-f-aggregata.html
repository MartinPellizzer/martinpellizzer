Prunus jamasakura f. aggregata

Prunus jamasakura f. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for small garden spaces and bonsai cultivation.
