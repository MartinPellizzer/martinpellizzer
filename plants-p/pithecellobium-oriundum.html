Pithecellobium oriundum

Pithecellobium oriundum, commonly known as the goldenpod acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow or golden pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it useful in ecological restoration projects.
