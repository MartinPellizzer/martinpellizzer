Phalaris macrostachya

Phalaris macrostachya, commonly known as the tall false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets at the tips of its stems, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

Phalaris macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
