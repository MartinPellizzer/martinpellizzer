Polypodium tobagense

Polypodium tobagense, commonly known as the Toba huperzia, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, strap-like leaves that form dense, compact rosettes. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
