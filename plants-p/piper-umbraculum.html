Piper umbraculum

Piper umbraculum, commonly known as the umbrella pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a spicy, peppery flavor. It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

The botanical features of Piper umbraculum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
