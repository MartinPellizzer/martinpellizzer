Primula sinensis var. fimbriata

Primula sinensis var. fimbriata, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or fimbriated primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, white, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers cool, moist conditions and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
