Pilosella bauhini subsp. melachaeta

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

melachaeta, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The plant plays a role in supporting pollinators and is often used in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
