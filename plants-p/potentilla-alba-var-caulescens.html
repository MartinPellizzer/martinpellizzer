Potentilla alba var. caulescens

Potentilla alba var. caulescens, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and usually have five leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate flowers and hardy nature.
