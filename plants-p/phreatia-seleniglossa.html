Phreatia seleniglossa

Phreatia seleniglossa, commonly known as the silver-tongued orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that often display a combination of white and green hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrate.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure and is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
