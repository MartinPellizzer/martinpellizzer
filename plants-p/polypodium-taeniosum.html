Polypodium taeniosum

Polypodium taeniosum, commonly known as the broad-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically broad and lance-shaped. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Polypodium taeniosum is known for its slow growth and ability to survive in nutrient-poor soils.
