Pouteria grandiflora

Pouteria grandiflora, commonly known as the giant soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a large, spiny fruit with a thick, yellowish rind and a soft, white, edible pulp. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has a unique, slightly acidic flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in humid tropical regions.
