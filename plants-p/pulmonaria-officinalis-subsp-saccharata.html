Pulmonaria officinalis subsp. saccharata

Pulmonaria officinalis subsp.

saccharata, commonly known as the sweet pulmonaria, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves that turn reddish in winter.

The plant produces upright clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.
