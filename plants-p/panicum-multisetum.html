Panicum multisetum

Panicum multisetum, commonly known as the many-branched panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry foliage that forms compact clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats make it useful for erosion control and as a landscape plant.
