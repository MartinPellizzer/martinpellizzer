Pandanus cominsii var. micronesicus

Pandanus cominsii var. micronesicus, commonly known as the Caroline Island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots and leaves are sometimes used in local medicine and for making thatch.

This variety is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun.
