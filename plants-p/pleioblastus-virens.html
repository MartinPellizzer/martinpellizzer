Pleioblastus virens

Pleioblastus virens, commonly known as the green bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and is valued for its ornamental qualities. The plant features slender, green culms that are often used in landscaping and traditional crafts. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Pleioblastus virens thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, attractive plant.
