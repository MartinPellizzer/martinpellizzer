Penstemon truncatus f. puberulus

Penstemon truncatus f. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy lips penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in garden settings.
