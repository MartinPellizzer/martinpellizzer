Podocarpus trichomanoides

Podocarpus trichomanoides, commonly known as the silver-leaf podocarp, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, typical of the Podocarpaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
