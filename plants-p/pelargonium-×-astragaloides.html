Pelargonium × astragaloides

Pelargonium × astragaloides, commonly known as the sandalwood geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, often featuring leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy and visually appealing plant.
