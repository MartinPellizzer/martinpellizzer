Prunus yunnanensis var. parviflora

Prunus yunnanensis var. parviflora, commonly known as the Yunnan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
