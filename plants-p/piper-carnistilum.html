Piper carnistilum

Piper carnistilum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small berry, contains the seeds used to produce white pepper.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often grown for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
