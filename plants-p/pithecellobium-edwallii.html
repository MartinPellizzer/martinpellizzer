Pithecellobium edwallii

Pithecellobium edwallii, commonly known as the Edwall's burr legume, is a shrub native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by hairy, woody pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a forage plant.
