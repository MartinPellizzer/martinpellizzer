Portulaca oblonga

Portulaca oblonga, commonly known as the fat-leaf purslane, is a flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in hot, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and has a shallow root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Portulaca oblonga is also known for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various cuisines.
