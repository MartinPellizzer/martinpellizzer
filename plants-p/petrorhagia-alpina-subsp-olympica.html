Petrorhagia alpina subsp. olympica

Petrorhagia alpina subsp.

olympica, commonly known as the Olympic rockrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Pacific Northwest. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
