Phyllanthus ciccoides

Phyllanthus ciccoides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to bright red, round berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants, making them valuable in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.
