Patagonium hirsutum

Patagonium hirsutum, commonly known as the Andean woolly plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, often purple or pink flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soil. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for their insulating properties.

Patagonium hirsutum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
