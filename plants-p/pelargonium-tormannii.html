Pelargonium tormannii

Pelargonium tormannii, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has hairy leaves and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Pelargonium tormannii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
