Phaseolus compressus var. macrocarpus

Phaseolus compressus var. macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-seeded bush bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It produces elongated, green pods that contain large, oval seeds with a smooth, yellowish-brown surface. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and small, white or pale purple flowers. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its high yield and adaptability to various soil conditions.
