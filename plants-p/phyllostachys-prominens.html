Phyllostachys prominens

Phyllostachys prominens, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a fast-growing grass species native to East Asia.

It features slender, erect culms that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant form.

Phyllostachys prominens is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a source of sustainable materials.
