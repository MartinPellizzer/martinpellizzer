Populus angulata f. cordata

Populus angulata f. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf poplar, is a unique variant of the angulate poplar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are broader and more rounded compared to the typical narrow leaves of the species. This form thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies. The plant is noted for its rapid growth and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its distinct morphology and ecological adaptability.
