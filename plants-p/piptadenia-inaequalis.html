Piptadenia inaequalis

Piptadenia inaequalis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain aromatic seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and resin have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
