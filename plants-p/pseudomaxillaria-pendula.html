Pseudomaxillaria pendula

Pseudomaxillaria pendula, commonly known as the hanging orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescence, which consists of multiple flowers hanging from a spike. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pseudomaxillaria pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
