Polycarena lilacina var. difficilis

Polycarena lilacina var. difficilis, commonly known as the purple orchid grass, is a rare and ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like stems with striking purple and lilac-colored inflorescences that bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its limited distribution and horticultural challenges, it is considered a specialty plant among botanists and gardeners.
