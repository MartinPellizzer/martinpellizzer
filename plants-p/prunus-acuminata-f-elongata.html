Prunus acuminata f. elongata

Prunus acuminata f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated cherry or longleaf cherry, is a variant of the Prunus acuminata species, which belongs to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated leaves, which are typically longer and more slender than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It is native to regions of southern China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The foliage and fruit make it a valuable plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
