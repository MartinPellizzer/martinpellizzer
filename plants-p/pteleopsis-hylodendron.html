Pteleopsis hylodendron

Pteleopsis hylodendron, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lance-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The golden rain tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
