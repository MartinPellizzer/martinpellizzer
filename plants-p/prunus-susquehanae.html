Prunus susquehanae

Prunus susquehanae, commonly known as the Susquehanna hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Prunus susquehanae is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
