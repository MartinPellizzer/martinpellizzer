Podanthum limonifolium var. repandum

Podanthum limonifolium var. repandum, commonly known as the lemonleaf podanthum, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions.

It features lance-shaped, lemon-scented leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded areas.

The leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic and medicinal properties.
