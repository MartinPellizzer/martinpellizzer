Philotheca brucei subsp. brucei

Philotheca brucei subsp.

brucei, commonly known as the Bruce's philotheca, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine for their therapeutic properties.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential in modern pharmacological research.
