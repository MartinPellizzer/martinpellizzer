Protea decipiens

Protea decipiens, commonly known as the false sugarbush, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble fluffy, pink or white flowers, though they are actually composed of many small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea decipiens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
