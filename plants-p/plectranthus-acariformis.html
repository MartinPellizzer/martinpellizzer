Plectranthus acariformis

Plectranthus acariformis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple, blue, and white. The plant prefers well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Plectranthus acariformis is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
