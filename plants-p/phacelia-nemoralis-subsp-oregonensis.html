Phacelia nemoralis subsp. oregonensis

Phacelia nemoralis subsp.

oregonensis, commonly known as Oregon phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woodland and meadow habitats.

The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
