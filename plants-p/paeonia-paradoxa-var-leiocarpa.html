Paeonia paradoxa var. leiocarpa

Paeonia paradoxa var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white peony, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has deeply lobed, dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its roots are thick and fleshy, typical of many peony species, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
