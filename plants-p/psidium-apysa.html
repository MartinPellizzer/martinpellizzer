Psidium apysa

Psidium apysa, commonly known as the golden guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its flowers are white and fragrant.

Psidium apysa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
