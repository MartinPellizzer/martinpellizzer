Panicum hirsutulum

Panicum hirsutulum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy texture and upright growth habit, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. The plant produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are typically light green to brown in color, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that adds to its visual interest.

Panicum hirsutulum thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
