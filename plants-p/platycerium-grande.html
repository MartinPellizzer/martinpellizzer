Platycerium grande

Platycerium grande, commonly known as the giant toothbrush or giant staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Australia and New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that grow directly from the central rhizome. Its fronds are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to two meters, making it a striking addition to tropical gardens.

Platycerium grande thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
