Peperomia hispidorhachis

Peperomia hispidorhachis, commonly known as the velvety peperomia, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia hispidorhachis is relatively low maintenance and suitable for beginners due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
