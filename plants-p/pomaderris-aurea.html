Pomaderris aurea

Pomaderris aurea, commonly known as the golden pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a leathery texture and a distinctive yellow-green color. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in spring, followed by woody, edible fruits.

This plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
