Perezia vanillosma

Perezia vanillosma, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and long, pendant inflorescences that bear fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that mature to a dark purple color, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Despite its ornamental value, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes due to its specific growing requirements.
