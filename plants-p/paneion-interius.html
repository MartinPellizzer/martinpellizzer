Paneion interius

Paneion interius, commonly known as the lesser panicle aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It features a bushy growth habit with slender, branching stems and small, white to purple flowers.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a nectar source for pollinators and its use in ecological restoration projects.
