Prunus duracina var. melanocarpa

Prunus duracina var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black fruit, which ripens in late summer. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with a slight peeling texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
