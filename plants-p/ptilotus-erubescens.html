Ptilotus erubescens

Ptilotus erubescens, commonly known as the red spinifex, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Ptilotus erubescens is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
