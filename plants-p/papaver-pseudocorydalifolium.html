Papaver pseudocorydalifolium

Papaver pseudocorydalifolium, commonly known as the Chinese poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright red petals and a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but is also classified as an invasive species in some areas.
