Potentilla gracilipes

Potentilla gracilipes, commonly known as slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Potentilla gracilipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
