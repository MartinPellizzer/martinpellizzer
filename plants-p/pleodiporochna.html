Pleodiporochna

Pleodiporochna, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant blooms that open at night. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths, which are attracted to its strong scent.

The botanical name reflects its unique floral structure and nocturnal blooming habit.
