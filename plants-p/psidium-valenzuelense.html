Psidium valenzuelense

Psidium valenzuelense, commonly known as the Valenzuela guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Psidium valenzuelense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
