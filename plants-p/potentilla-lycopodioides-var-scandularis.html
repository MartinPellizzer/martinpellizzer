Potentilla lycopodioides var. scandularis

Potentilla lycopodioides var. scandularis, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats. Its structure is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including cold temperatures and strong winds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
