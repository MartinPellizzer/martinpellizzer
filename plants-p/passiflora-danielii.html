Passiflora danielii

Passiflora danielii, commonly known as the Daniel's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of violet, blue, and white hues. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits similar to those of other passionflower species. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a velvety texture.

Passiflora danielii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
