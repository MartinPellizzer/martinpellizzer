Phaenopyrum tanacetifolium

Phaenopyrum tanacetifolium, commonly known as the yarrow plant, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Phaenopyrum tanacetifolium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its historical use in traditional medicine for treating wounds and digestive issues remains notable.
