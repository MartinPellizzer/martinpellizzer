Portulaca pilosa var. guyannensis

Portulaca pilosa var. guyannensis, commonly known as the Guyana sandmat, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and small, orbicular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
