Poa caesia var. glauca

Poa caesia var. glauca, commonly known as blue mountain grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species Poa caesia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. This grass typically grows in cool, moist environments such as mountain meadows and stream banks. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish sheen that becomes more pronounced in sunlight.

It is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
