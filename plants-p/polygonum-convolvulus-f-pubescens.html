Polygonum convolvulus f. pubescens

Polygonum convolvulus f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed species.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, typically blooming in the summer months.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
