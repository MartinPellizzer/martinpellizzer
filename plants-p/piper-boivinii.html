Piper boivinii

Piper boivinii, commonly known as the Boivin's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are edible and have a mild, spicy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Piper boivinii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
