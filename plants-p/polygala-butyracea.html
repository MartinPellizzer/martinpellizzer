Polygala butyracea

Polygala butyracea, commonly known as the buttercup milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
