Paspalum ionanthum

Paspalum ionanthum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum ionanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
