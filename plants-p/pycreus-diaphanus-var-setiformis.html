Pycreus diaphanus var. setiformis

Pycreus diaphanus var. setiformis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pycreus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that contribute to its unique aesthetic. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
