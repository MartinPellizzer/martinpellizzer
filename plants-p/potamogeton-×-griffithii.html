Potamogeton × griffithii

Potamogeton × griffithii, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant that belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Potamogeton × griffithii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
