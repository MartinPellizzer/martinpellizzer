Papaver hybridum var. pinnatifidum

Papaver hybridum var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poppy, is a hybrid species characterized by its striking, vibrant flowers.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and has been cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinnatifid (bifurcated) margin, contributing to its unique appearance. It produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in shades of red, orange, and yellow, typically during the summer months.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its resilience and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
