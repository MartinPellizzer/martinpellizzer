Potentilla dubia

Potentilla dubia, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white to yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla dubia thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
