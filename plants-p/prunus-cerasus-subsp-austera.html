Prunus cerasus subsp. austera

Prunus cerasus subsp.

austera, commonly known as the Austrian cherry, is a subspecies of the sweet cherry that is native to Central Europe. It is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of 4 to 8 meters, with a rounded crown and dark green, glossy leaves.

The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in early spring before the leaves, and the fruit is a sweet, red to dark red cherry that ripens in late summer. This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and edible fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
