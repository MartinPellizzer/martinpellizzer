Paracaryum serpentinicum

Paracaryum serpentinicum, commonly known as the serpent's tail plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that resemble a serpent's tail, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.
