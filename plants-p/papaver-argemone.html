Papaver argemone

Papaver argemone, commonly known as the Mexican poppy, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It produces striking yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive saucer-shaped structure. The plant is characterized by its lobed leaves and smooth, waxy stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used historically for its oil-rich seeds.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
