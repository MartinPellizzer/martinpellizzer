Pandanus asper

Pandanus asper, commonly known as the rough pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, including Hawaii, Tahiti, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance.
