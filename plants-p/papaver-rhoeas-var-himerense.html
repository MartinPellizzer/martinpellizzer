Papaver rhoeas var. himerense

Papaver rhoeas var. himerense, commonly known as the Himeren scarlet poppy, is a rare variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its vivid red petals and a distinctive, narrow corona. This plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in specific regions of southern Europe. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and capsule-like seed pods.

The Himeren scarlet poppy is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and limited distribution.
