Phalaris coerulescens var. ovata

Phalaris coerulescens var. ovata, commonly known as the blue oat grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and panicles of small, white or blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its fine texture and attractive color.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in marginal environments.
