Perotis indica f. hirtigluma

Perotis indica f. hirtigluma, commonly known as the hairy leaved false indigo, is a variant of the Perotis indica species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Its flowers are small and clustered in racemes, usually appearing in shades of violet or blue.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it well-suited for moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.
