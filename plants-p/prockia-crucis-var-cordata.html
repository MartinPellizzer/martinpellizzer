Prockia crucis var. cordata

Prockia crucis var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped prockia, is a variant of the prockia crucis species.

It is a perennial plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean, characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly wavy edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom in early spring.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
