Parodia lohaniana var. rubriflora

Parodia lohaniana var. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered parodia, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces striking red flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the stem tips. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
