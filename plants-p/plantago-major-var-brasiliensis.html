Plantago major var. brasiliensis

Plantago major var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian plantain, is a variant of the common plantain that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herb with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is commonly used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
