Plectranthus fragrantissimus

Plectranthus fragrantissimus, commonly known as the sweet plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves that emit a pleasant scent when touched. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and fragrance, it is often used in perfumery and herbal remedies.
