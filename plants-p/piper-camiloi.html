Piper camiloi

Piper camiloi, commonly known as the Camilo pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red or yellow, depending on the variety. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical structure includes compound leaves with serrated edges and small, green flowers that develop into the characteristic peppery fruits.
