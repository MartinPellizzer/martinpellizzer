Petalochilus xantholeucus

Petalochilus xantholeucus, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow lip and greenish-white petals, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant, showy flowers.

The species is valued for its unique coloration and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
