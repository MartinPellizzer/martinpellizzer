Piper miramarense

Piper miramarense, commonly known as the Miramar pepper or Miramarense pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its small, elongated fruits that resemble peppercorns. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with dark green, glossy leaves and a strong, pungent aroma. It is valued for its culinary and medicinal properties, often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory effects.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
