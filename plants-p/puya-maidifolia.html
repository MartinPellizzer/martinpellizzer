Puya maidifolia

Puya maidifolia, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, protective structure around its central flower cluster. Puya maidifolia is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
