Polygala major var. anatolica

Polygala major var. anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

It is often found in open woodlands and alpine meadows, where it thrives in well-drained environments.
