Polygala amara var. amblyptera

Polygala amara var. amblyptera, commonly known as the bitter milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Polygala, which is part of the family Polygalaceae. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, and the flowers are often pale yellow or greenish in color.

This species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
