Pittosporum verticillatum subsp. pangalanense

Pittosporum verticillatum subsp.

pangalanense, commonly known as the Philippine pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to the Philippines. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
