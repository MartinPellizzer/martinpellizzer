Polyspora intricata

Polyspora intricata, commonly known as the intricate polypore, is a species of fungi belonging to the family Polyporaceae.

It is characterized by its complex, branched fruiting bodies that often resemble coral or lace, with a smooth, sometimes woody surface. This fungi typically grows on dead wood, particularly hardwoods, in temperate and tropical forest ecosystems. Its spore-producing structures, called pores, are arranged in a distinctive pattern that aids in identification.

Polyspora intricata plays an important ecological role in decomposing organic matter and recycling nutrients in forest soils.
