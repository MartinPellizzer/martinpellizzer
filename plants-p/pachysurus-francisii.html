Pachysurus francisii

Pachysurus francisii, commonly known as the African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
