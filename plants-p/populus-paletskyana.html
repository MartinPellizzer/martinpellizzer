Populus paletskyana

Populus paletskyana, commonly known as the Siberian poplar, is a deciduous tree native to central and eastern Siberia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by narrow, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in cold climates and is adapted to grow in poor soils, making it a hardy species in harsh environments.

Populus paletskyana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
