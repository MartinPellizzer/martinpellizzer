Picea californica

Picea californica, commonly known as the California white fir, is a coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are cylindrical and hang downward, containing winged seeds.

This species is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in forested areas with moist, well-drained soils.
