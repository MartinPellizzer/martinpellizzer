Phytolacca vulgaris

Phytolacca vulgaris, commonly known as the poison gooseberry, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and dark green coloration. The plant produces clusters of small, red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots and stems contain compounds such as phytolaccatoxin, which contribute to its medicinal and toxic properties.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.
