Phyllanthus ouveanus

Phyllanthus ouveanus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits resembling dates. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
