Phyteuma scorzonerifolium var. persicifolium

Phyteuma scorzonerifolium var. persicifolium, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
