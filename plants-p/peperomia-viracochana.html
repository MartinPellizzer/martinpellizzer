Peperomia viracochana

Peperomia viracochana, commonly known as the Andean peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense, foliage-rich appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent and tropical plant enthusiasts.
