Phyllostachys quilioi

Phyllostachys quilioi, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the bamboo family, Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking yellow-green culms and dense growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its culms are hollow and segmented, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Phyllostachys quilioi is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
