Poa korshinskyi var. capitolinae

Poa korshinskyi var. capitolinae, commonly known as the Capitolina Bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
