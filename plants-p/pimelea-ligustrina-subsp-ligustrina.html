Pimelea ligustrina subsp. ligustrina

Pimelea ligustrina subsp.

ligustrina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to orange.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands. It produces small, edible fruits that are dispersed by birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
