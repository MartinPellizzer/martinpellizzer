Physalis bodinieri

Physalis bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or winter cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, orange-red calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow, egg-shaped berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.
