Prunus domestica subsp. intermedia

Prunus domestica subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the European blackthorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It produces white or pale pink flowers in early spring, preceding the foliage.

This subspecies is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
