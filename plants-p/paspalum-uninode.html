Paspalum uninode

Paspalum uninode, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland habitats, such as marshes and prairies. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Paspalum uninode is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
