Pomaderris betulina subsp. betulina

Pomaderris betulina subsp.

betulina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit.

The leaves are narrow, ovate to lanceolate, and typically have a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits.

This species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
