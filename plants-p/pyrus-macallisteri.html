Pyrus macallisteri

Pyrus macallisteri, commonly known as the Macallister's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to New Zealand and is characterized by its deciduous nature, with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to pears but are often smaller and more tart in flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
