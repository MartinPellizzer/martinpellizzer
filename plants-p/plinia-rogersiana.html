Plinia rogersiana

Plinia rogersiana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are highly aromatic and rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong, minty scent.

Plinia rogersiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
