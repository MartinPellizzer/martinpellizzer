Phalaenopsis thalebanii

Phalaenopsis thalebanii, commonly known as the Persian orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its blooming period can last several months, adding ornamental value to both gardens and homes.
