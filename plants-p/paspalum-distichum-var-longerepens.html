Paspalum distichum var. longerepens

Paspalum distichum var. longerepens, commonly known as the long-leaf paspalum, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

This variant is notable for its ability to tolerate both wet and dry conditions, making it adaptable to various environments.
