Primula androsacea

Primula androsacea, commonly known as the Androsace primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, producing clusters of white, pink, or purple petals. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Primula androsacea is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
