Puccinellia × hybrida

Puccinellia × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid saltbush, is a perennial grass species native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to poor soils and can tolerate high salinity and drought conditions.

Puccinellia × hybrida is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
