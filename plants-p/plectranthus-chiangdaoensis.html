Plectranthus chiangdaoensis

Plectranthus chiangdaoensis, commonly known as the Chiang Dao plectranthus, is a species native to Thailand.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet to purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering habits.
