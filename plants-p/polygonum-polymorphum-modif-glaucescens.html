Polygonum polymorphum modif. glaucescens

Polygonum polymorphum modif.

glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that are often tinged with a bluish-green hue, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as stream sides and forest understories.
