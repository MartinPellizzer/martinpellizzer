Polygala gymnoclada

Polygala gymnoclada, commonly known as the smoothleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
