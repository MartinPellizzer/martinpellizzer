Potentilla supina erecta

Potentilla supina erecta, commonly known as the upright cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and typically have five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.
