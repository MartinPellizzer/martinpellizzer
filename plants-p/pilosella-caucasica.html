Pilosella caucasica

Pilosella caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

Pilosella caucasica is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
