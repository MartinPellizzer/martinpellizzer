Pycnanthemum montanum

Pycnanthemum montanum, commonly known as mountain blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green foliage and conical flower heads that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Pycnanthemum montanum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
