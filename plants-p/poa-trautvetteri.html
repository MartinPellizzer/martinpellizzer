Poa trautvetteri

Poa trautvetteri, commonly known as Trautvetter's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it flowers in late summer.

Poa trautvetteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
