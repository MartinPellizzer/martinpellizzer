Panicum anomalum

Panicum anomalum, commonly known as the odd panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and panicles that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
