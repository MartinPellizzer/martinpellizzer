Parrya subsiliquosa

Parrya subsiliquosa, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is typically found in meadows and along stream banks at high elevations.

Parrya subsiliquosa is a perennial species that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
