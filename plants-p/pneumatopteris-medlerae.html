Pneumatopteris medlerae

Pneumatopteris medlerae, commonly known as the Medler's water fern, is a rare and ancient fern species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often submerged or partially submerged in wetland environments. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and requires a consistently moist habitat for optimal growth. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are typically found on the underside of its fronds.

Pneumatopteris medlerae plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
