Pittosporum ramiflorum var. parviflorum

Pittosporum ramiflorum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
