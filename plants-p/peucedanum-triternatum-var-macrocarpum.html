Peucedanum triternatum var. macrocarpum

Peucedanum triternatum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded cowparsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its biennial growth habit, with the first year producing only basal leaves and the second year flowering and fruiting. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of elongated, ribbed follicles that contain several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
