Perityle vitreomontana

Perityle vitreomontana, commonly known as the glassy mountain perityle, is a unique plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in late summer. Due to its specialized habitat, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in alpine botany.
