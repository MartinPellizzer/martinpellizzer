Paliurus virgatus

Paliurus virgatus, commonly known as the Virgin's thorn or the Jerusalem thorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny branches and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Paliurus virgatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
