Polygala senduaris

Polygala senduaris, commonly known as the Malabar milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender stems and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala senduaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
