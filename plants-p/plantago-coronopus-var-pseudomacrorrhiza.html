Plantago coronopus var. pseudomacrorrhiza

Plantago coronopus var. pseudomacrorrhiza, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its broader leaves compared to the typical species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
