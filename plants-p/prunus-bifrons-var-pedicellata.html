Prunus bifrons var. pedicellata

Prunus bifrons var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pedicels that support its small, white to pink flowers. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a delicate floral display to gardens.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.
