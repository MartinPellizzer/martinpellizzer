Pouteria Maingayi

Pouteria maingayi is a tropical plant native to parts of South America known for its unique fruit and ornamental value. This species belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is often found in humid lowland forests. Its leaves are large, glossy, and deeply veined, making it a popular choice for landscaping. The fruit produces a distinctive, waxy coating that helps it retain moisture in dry conditions. Despite its beauty, Pouteria maingayi remains relatively understudied and is sometimes overlooked in horticultural literature.
