Prieurella manaosensis

Prieurella manaosensis, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the dense canopy of the rainforest. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a waxy cuticle to retain moisture in the humid environment.

Prieurella manaosensis plays a role in the ecological balance of the Amazon by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
