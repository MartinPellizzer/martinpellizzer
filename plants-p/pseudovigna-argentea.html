Pseudovigna argentea

Pseudovigna argentea, commonly known as silver bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is adapted to warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or agricultural fields.

Pseudovigna argentea is valued for its nutritional seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a protein source.
