Polypogon chonoticus

Polypogon chonoticus, commonly known as the Chonotic Panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
