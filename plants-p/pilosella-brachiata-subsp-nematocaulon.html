Pilosella brachiata subsp. nematocaulon

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

nematocaulon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pilosella, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale blue ray florets.

The subspecies is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in grasslands and open habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

The plant is typically perennial and thrives in well-drained soils.
