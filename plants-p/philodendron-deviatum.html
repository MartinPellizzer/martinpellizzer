Philodendron deviatum

Philodendron deviatum, commonly known as the curly vine philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to cling to supports as it climbs, making it ideal for trellises or hanging baskets. Its growth habit is characterized by its ability to curl and twist around structures, creating an ornamental display.

Philodendron deviatum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture to maintain its lush foliage.
