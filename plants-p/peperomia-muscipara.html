Peperomia muscipara

Peperomia muscipara, commonly known as the sticky peperomia, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its ability to produce sticky secretions on its leaves. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat. Peperomia muscipara is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
