Pounguia purpurata

Pounguia purpurata, commonly known as the purple pounguia, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, helping it retain moisture in its native arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Pounguia purpurata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional practices.
