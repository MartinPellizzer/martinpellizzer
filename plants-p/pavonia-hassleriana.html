Pavonia hassleriana

Pavonia hassleriana, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that resemble those of the mallow family. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its leaves are alternate, palmately lobed, and often have a velvety texture.

Pavonia hassleriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
