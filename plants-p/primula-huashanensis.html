Primula huashanensis

Primula huashanensis, commonly known as the Huashan primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically the Hua Shan mountain region.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that come in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often in forested areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers during the spring season.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
