Pleurospermum archangelica

Pleurospermum archangelica, commonly known as angel's trumpet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces a strong, often unpleasant odor. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

However, it is toxic to both humans and animals, requiring caution in cultivation.
