Polygala ramosior

Polygala ramosior, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, round pods that contain seeds.

Polygala ramosior is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
