Philammos macrotropis

Philammos macrotropis, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
