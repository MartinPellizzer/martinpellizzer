Pinus merkusii subsp. ustulata

Pinus merkusii subsp.

ustulata, commonly known as the Sumatran yellow pine, is a subspecies of the yellow pine native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters long.

The plant produces cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
