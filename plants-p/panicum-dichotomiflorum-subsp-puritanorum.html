Panicum dichotomiflorum subsp. puritanorum

Panicum dichotomiflorum subsp.

puritanorum, commonly known as the Puritan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive, dichotomous branching pattern.

The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of habitats.

Its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
