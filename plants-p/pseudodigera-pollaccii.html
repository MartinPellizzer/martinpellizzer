Pseudodigera pollaccii

Pseudodigera pollaccii, commonly known as the Pollacci's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated inflorescence bearing small, pale green or white flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the trunks of trees or in rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its native habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Pseudodigera pollaccii is considered a threatened species, making it a subject of interest for conservation efforts.
