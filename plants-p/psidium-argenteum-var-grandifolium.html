Psidium argenteum var. grandifolium

Psidium argenteum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf guava, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
