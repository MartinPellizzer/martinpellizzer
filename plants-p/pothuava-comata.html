Pothuava comata

Pothuava comata, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Pothuava comata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland areas near water bodies.

Its name refers to the fine, silvery hairs that cover its leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance.
