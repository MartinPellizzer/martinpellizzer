Porterandia catappifolia

Porterandia catappifolia, commonly known as the catappa leaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous seeds.

Porterandia catappifolia thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
