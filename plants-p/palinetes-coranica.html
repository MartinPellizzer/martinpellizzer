Palinetes coranica

Palinetes coranica, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the rich, organic soil of its natural habitat.

Palinetes coranica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in local ecosystems.
