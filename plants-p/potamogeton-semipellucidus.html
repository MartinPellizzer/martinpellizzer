Potamogeton semipellucidus

Potamogeton semipellucidus, commonly known as the semi-transparent pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are usually translucent or semi-transparent, giving it its common name.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
