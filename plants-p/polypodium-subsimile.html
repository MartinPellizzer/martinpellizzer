Polypodium subsimile

Polypodium subsimile, commonly known as the similar huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of the fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by a protective indusium. Polypodium subsimile is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

It is a relatively small fern, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters, and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
