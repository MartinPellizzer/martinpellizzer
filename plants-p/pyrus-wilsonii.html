Pyrus wilsonii

Pyrus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

Pyrus wilsonii is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
