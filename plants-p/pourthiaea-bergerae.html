Pourthiaea bergerae

Pourthiaea bergerae, commonly known as the East African aster, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in regions with moderate rainfall. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

Pourthiaea bergerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
