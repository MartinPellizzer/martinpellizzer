Pseuderanthemum pittieri

Pseuderanthemum pittieri, commonly known as the Pittier's false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble true flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its inflorescences consist of clustered bracts that enclose small, tubular flowers, which are usually white or pale pink.

Pseuderanthemum pittieri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
