Pawia punduana

Pawia punduana, commonly known as the Malay orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Pawia punduana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in local traditions.
