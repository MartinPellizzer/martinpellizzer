Poa glauca var. crocata

Poa glauca var. crocata, commonly known as the blue grass or crocata bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
