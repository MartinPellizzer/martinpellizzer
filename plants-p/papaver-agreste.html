Papaver agreste

Papaver agreste, commonly known as wild poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive black seed capsule. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. Its flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.
