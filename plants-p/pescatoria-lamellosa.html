Pescatoria lamellosa

Pescatoria lamellosa, commonly known as the fringed seagrass, is a marine flowering plant found in shallow coastal waters.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often fringed with small, delicate structures, aiding in water flow and nutrient absorption. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, contributing to its reproductive strategy in aquatic environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for various marine organisms.

Pescatoria lamellosa is an important component of seagrass meadows, which support biodiversity and coastal ecosystem health.
