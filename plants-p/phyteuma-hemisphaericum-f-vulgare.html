Phyteuma hemisphaericum f. vulgare

Phyteuma hemisphaericum f. vulgare, commonly known as the common phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces flowering stems that rise above the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

This perennial herb is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
