Pisonia laurifolia

Pisonia laurifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The leaves are glossy and dark green, often with a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
