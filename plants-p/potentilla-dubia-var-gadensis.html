Potentilla dubia var. gadensis

Potentilla dubia var. gadensis, commonly known as the Gadensian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
