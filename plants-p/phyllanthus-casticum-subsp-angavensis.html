Phyllanthus casticum subsp. angavensis

Phyllanthus casticum subsp.

angavensis, commonly known as the Angavensis spurge, is a shrub native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its small, capsule-like fruits contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
