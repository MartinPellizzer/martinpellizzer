Plantago gentianoides var. tenuispicata

Plantago gentianoides var. tenuispicata, commonly known as the slender-spike plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable component of native plant communities.
