Platyclinis brachyota

Platyclinis brachyota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved platyclinis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
