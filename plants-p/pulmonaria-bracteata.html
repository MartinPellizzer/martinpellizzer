Pulmonaria bracteata

Pulmonaria bracteata, commonly known as the bracted lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, dark green leaves with distinctive silver or white markings. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Pulmonaria bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
