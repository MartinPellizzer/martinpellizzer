Podocarpus flagelliformis

Podocarpus flagelliformis, commonly known as the silky pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender, and often drooping branches. The leaves are scale-like and dark green, providing a dense canopy. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain winged seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance.
