Pleurothallis cornualis

Pleurothallis cornualis, commonly known as the golden rosette orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually yellow or golden, with a sweet fragrance, and bloom in clusters during the wet season.

Pleurothallis cornualis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
