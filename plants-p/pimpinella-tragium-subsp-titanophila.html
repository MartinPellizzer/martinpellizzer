Pimpinella tragium subsp. titanophila

Pimpinella tragium subsp.

titanophila, commonly known as the giant yellow horned poppy, is a striking member of theApiaceae family characterized by its large, bright yellow flowers. This subspecies is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of showy blooms. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
