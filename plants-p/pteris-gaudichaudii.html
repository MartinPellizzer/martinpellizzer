Pteris gaudichaudii

Pteris gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Gaudichaud's water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in clusters on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris gaudichaudii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
