Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. involucratum

Phyteuma pyrenaicum var. involucratum, commonly known as the Pyrenean phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Asteraceae family.
