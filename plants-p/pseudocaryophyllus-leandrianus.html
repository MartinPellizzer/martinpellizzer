Pseudocaryophyllus leandrianus

Pseudocaryophyllus leandrianus, commonly known as the rock saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, saline environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its leaves are typically gray-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Pseudocaryophyllus leandrianus plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna in its ecosystem.
