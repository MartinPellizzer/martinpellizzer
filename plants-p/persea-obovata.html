Persea obovata

Persea obovata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to southern Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, is a fleshy, sweet fruit with a high nutritional value.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
