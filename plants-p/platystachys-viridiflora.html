Platystachys viridiflora

Platystachys viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered false arum, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed in a spathe, giving it a unique and exotic appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a fascinating subject for both horticulture and botanical study.
