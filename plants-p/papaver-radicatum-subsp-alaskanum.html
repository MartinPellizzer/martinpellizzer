Papaver radicatum subsp. alaskanum

Papaver radicatum subsp.

alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada. It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a short, stout stem with smooth, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
