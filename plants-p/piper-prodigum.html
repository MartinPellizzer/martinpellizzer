Piper prodigum

Piper prodigum, commonly known as the creeping grapefruit or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, heart-shaped leaves, and small greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

Piper prodigum is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value in gardens.
