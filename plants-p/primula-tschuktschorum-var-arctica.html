Primula tschuktschorum var. arctica

Primula tschuktschorum var. arctica, commonly known as the Arctic primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It features dense, woolly foliage that helps insulate it against harsh cold, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, with five petals. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun exposure. It is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats where temperatures remain consistently low.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in extreme environments.
