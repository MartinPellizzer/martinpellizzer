Pandanus rockii

Pandanus rockii, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a species of palm native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its flowers are clustered in branched inflorescences and are often yellow or orange in color.

Pandanus rockii plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
