Polygala parvifolia

Polygala parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, often hairy stems that can grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Polygala parvifolia is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
