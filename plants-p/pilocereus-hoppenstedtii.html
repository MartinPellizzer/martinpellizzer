Pilocereus hoppenstedtii

Pilocereus hoppenstedtii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers typically bloom only once a year, usually during the summer months.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
