Phleum mouterdei

Phleum mouterdei, commonly known as the Mouterde's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Phleum mouterdei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
