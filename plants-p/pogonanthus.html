Pogonanthus

Pogonanthus, commonly known as the beardgrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. They are characterized by their tufted clusters of grass-like leaves and spikelets that resemble a beard, giving them their common name. Pogonanthus species are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for ornamental purposes.
