Polycarpon magellanicum

Polycarpon magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellanic knotweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, white to pale pink flowers clustered in umbels. Its root system is fibrous and can spread aggressively, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.

The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its resilience to environmental stress.
