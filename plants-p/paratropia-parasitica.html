Paratropia parasitica

Paratropia parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the stems of its host, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also producing its own sugars through photosynthesis using light reflected from the host. The plant forms a dense, leafy growth that can cover large sections of the host's branches. It reproduces via seeds dispersed by birds that eat its fleshy berries.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique parasitic relationship and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native Australian habitats.
