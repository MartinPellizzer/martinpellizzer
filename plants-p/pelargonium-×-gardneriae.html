Pelargonium × gardneriae

Pelargonium × gardneriae, commonly known as the Martha Washington geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and showy clusters of five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Native to South Africa, this plant is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

The plant is also known for its ability to bloom throughout the growing season, adding continuous color to landscapes.
