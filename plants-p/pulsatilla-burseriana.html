Pulsatilla burseriana

Pulsatilla burseriana, commonly known as the Burserian globeflower, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the high mountain regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, globe-shaped flower heads that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces showy, showy flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, white down, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Pulsatilla burseriana thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
