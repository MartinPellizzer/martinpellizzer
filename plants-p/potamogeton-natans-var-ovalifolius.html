Potamogeton natans var. ovalifolius

Potamogeton natans var. ovalifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Due to its preference for still or slow-moving waters, it is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
