Piper peltilimbum

Piper peltilimbum, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
