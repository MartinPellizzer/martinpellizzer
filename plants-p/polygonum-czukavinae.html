Polygonum czukavinae

Polygonum czukavinae, commonly known as the Czukavina knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive bracts and edible rhubarb-like stems. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum czukavinae is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
