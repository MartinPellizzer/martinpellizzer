Podochilus lucbanensis

Podochilus lucbanensis, commonly known as the Lucban orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocks in montane forests. Its flowers are usually small to medium-sized, with intricate patterns and a strong fragrance.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
