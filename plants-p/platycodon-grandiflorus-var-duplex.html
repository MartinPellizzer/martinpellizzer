Platycodon grandiflorus var. duplex

Platycodon grandiflorus var. duplex, commonly known as the double-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
