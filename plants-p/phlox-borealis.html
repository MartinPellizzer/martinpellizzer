Phlox borealis

Phlox borealis, commonly known as northern phlox, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodlands or open areas. The plant produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Phlox borealis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
