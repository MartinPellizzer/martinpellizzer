Potamogeton brasiliensis

Potamogeton brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and wetlands. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually borne in clusters near the water surface.

Potamogeton brasiliensis plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms and can contribute to water quality by absorbing nutrients.
