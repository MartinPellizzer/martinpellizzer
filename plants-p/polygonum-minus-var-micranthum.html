Polygonum minus var. micranthum

Polygonum minus var. micranthum, commonly known as the smallleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, terminal spikes, and it produces small, three-angled seeds.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf morphology and ecological adaptability.
