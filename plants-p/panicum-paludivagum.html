Panicum paludivagum

Panicum paludivagum, commonly known as the wetland panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum paludivagum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
