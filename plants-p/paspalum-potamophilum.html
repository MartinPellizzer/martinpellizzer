Paspalum potamophilum

Paspalum potamophilum, commonly known as river paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in marshes, along riverbanks, and in other wetland environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
