Pisum aucheri

Pisum aucheri, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a slightly aromatic scent. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pisum aucheri prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
