Polygala myrtifolia var. leucantha

Polygala myrtifolia var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, and the flowers are usually white or pale in color. This plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
