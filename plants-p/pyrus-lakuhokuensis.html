Pyrus lakuhokuensis

Pyrus lakuhokuensis, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Pyrus lakuhokuensis is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its edible fruit.
