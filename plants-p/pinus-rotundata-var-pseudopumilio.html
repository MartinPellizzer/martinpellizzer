Pinus rotundata var. pseudopumilio

Pinus rotundata var. pseudopumilio, commonly known as the dwarf mountain pine, is a variant of the mountain pine species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

This coniferous tree is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, stout needles that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in alpine or subalpine zones.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems in its native habitat.
