Paeonia nemoralis

Paeonia nemoralis, commonly known as the wild peony or wood peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and has palmately divided, lobed leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with numerous stamens and a prominent central pistil.

Paeonia nemoralis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
