Pyrus japonica var. alba

Pyrus japonica var. alba, commonly known as the white Japanese pear, is a variant of the Japanese pear tree characterized by its white flowers.

It is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. Its ornamental value comes from its showy, early spring blossoms.

It is often cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and its fruit.
