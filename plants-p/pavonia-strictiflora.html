Pavonia strictiflora

Pavonia strictiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved penta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pentagonal pink or red flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
