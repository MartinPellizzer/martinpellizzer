Papaver alpinum lusus hispidissimum

Papaver alpinum lusus hispidissimum, commonly known as the hispid mountain poppy, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a dark central spot. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves and stems, which help it withstand cold and arid conditions. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.

This species is part of the Papaveraceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value.
