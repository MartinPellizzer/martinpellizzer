Pithecellobium minutum

Pithecellobium minutum, commonly known as the dwarf pithecellobium, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release small, edible seeds.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
