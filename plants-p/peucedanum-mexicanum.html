Peucedanum mexicanum

Peucedanum mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican cowparsnip, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers.

It is native to Mexico and thrives in moist, temperate climates, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to greenish flowers arranged in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its roots are typically thick and fleshy, containing compounds that may have medicinal properties.

Peucedanum mexicanum is valued for its potential use in traditional herbal remedies and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
