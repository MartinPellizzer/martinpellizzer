Pseudocodon convolvulaceus

Pseudocodon convolvulaceus, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. It typically grows as a climbing vine with tendrils that help it ascend supports.

Pseudocodon convolvulaceus is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
