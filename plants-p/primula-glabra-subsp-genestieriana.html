Primula glabra subsp. genestieriana

Primula glabra subsp.

genestieriana, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of elliptical, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or violet, often with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine habitats.
