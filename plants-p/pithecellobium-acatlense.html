Pithecellobium acatlense

Pithecellobium acatlense, commonly known as the Mexican spurred orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
