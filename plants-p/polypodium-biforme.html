Polypodium biforme

Polypodium biforme, commonly known as the common huperzia, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically divided into two parts, giving it the species name *biforme*. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Polypodium biforme is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
