Prunus tomentosa var. endotricha

Prunus tomentosa var. endotricha, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries in summer. Its leaves are typically oval with a rough texture and a grayish underside.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive form and seasonal interest.
