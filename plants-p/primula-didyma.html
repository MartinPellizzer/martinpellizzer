Primula didyma

Primula didyma, commonly known as the double primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with five petals, typically in shades of yellow, orange, or white. The plant produces a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
