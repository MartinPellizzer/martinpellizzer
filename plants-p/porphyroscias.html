Porphyroscias

Porphyroscias, commonly known as the red-tailed orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands. They are characterized by their striking, showy flowers that often feature vibrant red or orange hues. Porphyroscias species typically grow as epiphytes or terrestrials, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological adaptations to humid, shaded environments.
