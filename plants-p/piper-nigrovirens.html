Piper nigrovirens

Piper nigrovirens, commonly known as the black-green pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, resembling tiny berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
