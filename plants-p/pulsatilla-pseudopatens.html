Pulsatilla pseudopatens

Pulsatilla pseudopatens, commonly known as the meadow anemone, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often colorful flowers. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces clustered, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink with a yellow center. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats.

Pulsatilla pseudopatens is known for its early spring bloom and its role in supporting pollinators.
