Paragophyton

Paragophyton, commonly known as the "primitive algae-like plant," is an ancient organism that dates back to the Ediacaran period.

It is characterized by its simple, branching structure and lack of true roots, stems, or leaves. Paragophyton is believed to have been a colonial organism, composed of multiple cells that functioned together. Its morphology suggests it was a photosynthetic organism, contributing to early marine ecosystems.

Botanically, it represents an early stage in the evolution of complex plant life.
