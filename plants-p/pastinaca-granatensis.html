Pastinaca granatensis

Pastinaca granatensis, commonly known as the granate burdock, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming a rounded cluster at the top of the stem.

The roots of Pastinaca granatensis are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
