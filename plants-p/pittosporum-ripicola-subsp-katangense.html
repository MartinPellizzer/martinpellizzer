Pittosporum ripicola subsp. katangense

Pittosporum ripicola subsp.

katangense, commonly known as the Katanga pittosporum, is a shrub native to the humid lowland forests of the Democratic Republic of the Congo. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded understory environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
