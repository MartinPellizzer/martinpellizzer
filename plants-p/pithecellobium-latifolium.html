Pithecellobium latifolium

Pithecellobium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a pod-like structure containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Pithecellobium latifolium is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
