Phloga nodifera

Phloga nodifera, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their aromatic properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a versatile addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
