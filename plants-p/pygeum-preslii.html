Pygeum preslii

Pygeum preslii, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, almond-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
