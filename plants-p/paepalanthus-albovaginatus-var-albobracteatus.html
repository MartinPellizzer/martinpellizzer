Paepalanthus albovaginatus var. albobracteatus

Paepalanthus albovaginatus var. albobracteatus, commonly known as the white bract peacock plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that resemble peacock feathers, with long, showy bracts in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that form a rosette, creating a compact and elegant appearance. Its flowers are typically borne on tall, erect stems and can last for several months, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is known for its adaptability to bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
