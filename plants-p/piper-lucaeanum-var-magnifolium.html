Piper lucaeanum var. magnifolium

Piper lucaeanum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the magnifoliate pepper plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
