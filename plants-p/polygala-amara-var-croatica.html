Polygala amara var. croatica

Polygala amara var. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian bitterball, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.

Its name reflects its bitter taste, which is due to the presence of various alkaloids in its leaves and flowers.
