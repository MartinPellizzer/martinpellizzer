Phyllanthus kurzianus

Phyllanthus kurzianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy berries. It is well-known for its medicinal properties and is widely used in Ayurvedic medicine.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has antioxidant properties that contribute to its popularity in traditional remedies.
