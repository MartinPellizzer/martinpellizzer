Prosopis andicola

Prosopis andicola, commonly known as the Andean mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Additionally, its wood and pods have been used by local communities for fuel, construction, and food.
