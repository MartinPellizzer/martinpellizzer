Podocarpus polyspermus

Podocarpus polyspermus, commonly known as the mountain pine or the Chinese pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to environmental stress. It plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.

Podocarpus polyspermus is also valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
