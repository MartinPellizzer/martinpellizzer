Potentilla heptaphylla subsp. elongata

Potentilla heptaphylla subsp.

elongata, commonly known as the elongated potentilla, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
