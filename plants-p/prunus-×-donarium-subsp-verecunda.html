Prunus × donarium subsp. verecunda

Prunus × donarium subsp.

verecunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a hybrid species native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.
