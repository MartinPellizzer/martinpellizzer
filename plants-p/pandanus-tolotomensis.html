Pandanus tolotomensis

Pandanus tolotomensis, commonly known as the Tongan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the islands of Tonga and Samoa.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional crafts and for making thatching materials.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.
