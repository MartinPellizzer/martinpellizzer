Passiflora alata var. latifolia

Passiflora alata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that surround the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy coating. This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
