Pterolobium subvestitum

Pterolobium subvestitum, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

Pterolobium subvestitum is valued for its ecological and nutritional contributions in its native habitats.
