Phyllanthus prieurianus

Phyllanthus prieurianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional purposes.
