Polygonum amphibium var. hartwrightii

Polygonum amphibium var. hartwrightii, commonly known as Hartwright's knotweed, is a variant of the common knotgrass species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, exhibiting a sprawling growth habit with rhizomatous stems. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense spikes and are adapted for water dispersal.
