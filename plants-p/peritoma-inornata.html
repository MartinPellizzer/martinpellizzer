Peritoma inornata

Peritoma inornata, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade, preferring well-drained soil. Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.

Peritoma inornata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
