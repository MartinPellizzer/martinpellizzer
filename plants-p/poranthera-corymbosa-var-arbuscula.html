Poranthera corymbosa var. arbuscula

Poranthera corymbosa var. arbuscula, commonly known as the bushy piper, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
