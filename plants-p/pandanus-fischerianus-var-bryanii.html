Pandanus fischerianus var. bryanii

Pandanus fischerianus var. bryanii, commonly known as the Bryan's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, particularly found in regions like Fiji and Samoa. The plant is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatch materials. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and cultural significance in the Pacific.
