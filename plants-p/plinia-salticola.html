Plinia salticola

Plinia salticola, commonly known as the jumping plinia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen and have a glossy appearance.

Plinia salticola is adapted to grow in humid, tropical environments.
