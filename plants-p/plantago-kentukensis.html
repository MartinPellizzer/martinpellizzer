Plantago kentukensis

Plantago kentukensis, commonly known as Kentucky plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its soothing effects on the digestive system.
