Paronychia hispanica var. rotundata

Paronychia hispanica var. rotundata, commonly known as the Spanish carnation or round-leaved paronychia, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically rounded and succulent, giving the plant a unique, almost succulent-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand dry conditions.
