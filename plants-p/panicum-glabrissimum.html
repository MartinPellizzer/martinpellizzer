Panicum glabrissimum

Panicum glabrissimum, commonly known as silverhair panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant has slender, silver-gray leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped seed heads that are distinctive and attractive. It is drought-tolerant and often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
