Piper harlingii

Piper harlingii, commonly known as the Harling's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a spicy aroma when crushed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
