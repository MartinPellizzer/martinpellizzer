Pfaffia brachiata var. typica

Pfaffia brachiata var. typica, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, including antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

Due to its habitat and conservation status, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
