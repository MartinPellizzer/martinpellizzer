Penaea myrtilloides

Penaea myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry bush, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. It thrives in acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Penaea myrtilloides is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
