Persicaria hypanica

Persicaria hypanica, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Persicaria hypanica is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in disturbed or riparian habitats.
