Pinanga wrayi

Pinanga wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form a dense clump, with long, narrow leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, greenish-white spadices that are typical of the Araceae family.

Pinanga wrayi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
