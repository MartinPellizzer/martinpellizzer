Pancratium staplesii

Pancratium staplesii, commonly known as the yellow rain lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces a single, trumpet-shaped flower that is often surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. Pancratium staplesii is typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
