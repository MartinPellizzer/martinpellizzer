Proclesia strobolifera

Proclesia strobolifera, commonly known as the starleaf pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Proclesia strobolifera is a hardy perennial that is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
