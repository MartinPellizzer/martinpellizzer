Papaver popovii

Papaver popovii, commonly known as the Popov's poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known genera such as Papaver and Eschscholzia. The plant is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting black center. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Papaver popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
