Populus Rotundifolia Var. Duclouxiana

Populus rotundifolia var. duclouxiana is a rare and fascinating plant native to specific regions of eastern North America. It is known for its unique rounded leaves and distinctive growth patterns that set it apart from other poplar species. This variant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along streambanks and in forest understories. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various wildlife species. Despite its beauty and ecological value, it remains under-studied and endangered due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
