Psilanthus mannii

Psilanthus mannii, commonly known as the African sycamore or African sycamore tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various parts of its native range.
