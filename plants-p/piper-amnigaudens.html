Piper amnigaudens

Piper amnigaudens, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features include opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive aromatic presence.
