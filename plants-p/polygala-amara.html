Polygala amara

Polygala amara, commonly known as bitter milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its purported digestive and anti-inflammatory benefits.
