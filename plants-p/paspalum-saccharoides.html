Paspalum saccharoides

Paspalum saccharoides, commonly known as sugargrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum saccharoides is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
