Poa sinaica var. glabrescens

Poa sinaica var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Sinai glabrous grass, is a variant of the Poa sinaica species native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial grass characterized by its fine, hairless blades and compact growth habit. This plant thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability. Its presence is often indicative of dry, open habitats with minimal vegetation cover.

The species plays a minor ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for local wildlife.
