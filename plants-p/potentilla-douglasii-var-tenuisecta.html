Potentilla douglasii var. tenuisecta

Potentilla douglasii var. tenuisecta, commonly known as the slenderleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often divided into several segments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
