Phyllanthus polyspermus var. puberulus

Phyllanthus polyspermus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy money plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and traditional uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for medicinal purposes.
