Plectranthus ingratus

Plectranthus ingratus, commonly known as the Woolly Plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

Plectranthus ingratus is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
