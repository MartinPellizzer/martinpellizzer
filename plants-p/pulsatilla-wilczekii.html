Pulsatilla wilczekii

Pulsatilla wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of yellow to pinkish-white petals. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmately divided leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Pulsatilla wilczekii is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
