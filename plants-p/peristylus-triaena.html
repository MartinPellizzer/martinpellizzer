Peristylus triaena

Peristylus triaena, commonly known as the three-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Linaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Peristylus triaena is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in textile production.
