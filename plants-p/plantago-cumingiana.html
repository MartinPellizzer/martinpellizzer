Plantago cumingiana

Plantago cumingiana, commonly known as Cuming's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes. It is often found growing in forest understories and along stream banks.

The plant is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
