Phyllanthus micranthus

Phyllanthus micranthus, commonly known as the small-fruited phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in environments with partial to full sunlight.
