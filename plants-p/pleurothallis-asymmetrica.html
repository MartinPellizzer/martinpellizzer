Pleurothallis asymmetrica

Pleurothallis asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Its unique floral structure and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments make it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
