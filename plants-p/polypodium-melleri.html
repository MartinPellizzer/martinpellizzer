Polypodium melleri

Polypodium melleri, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical forests. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients.

Polypodium melleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
