Petraeovitex trifoliata

Petraeovitex trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaved vitex, is a shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its woody stems and adaptability to various soil types make it a resilient species.

Petraeovitex trifoliata is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
