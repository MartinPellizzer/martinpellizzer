Pluchea arabica

Pluchea arabica, commonly known as African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features opposite, serrated leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks.

Pluchea arabica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
