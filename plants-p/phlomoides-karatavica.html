Phlomoides karatavica

Phlomoides karatavica, commonly known as Karatavka, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
