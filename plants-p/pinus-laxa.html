Pinus laxa

Pinus laxa, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and dense, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils and cold climates.

Due to its slow growth and longevity, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and scientific study.
