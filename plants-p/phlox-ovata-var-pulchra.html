Phlox ovata var. pulchra

Phlox ovata var. pulchra, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
