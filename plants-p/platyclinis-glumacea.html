Platyclinis glumacea

Platyclinis glumacea, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The flowers are usually tiny and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters along the stem.
