Picris evae

Picris evae, commonly known as the Eave's wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a grayish-green color and a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing small yellowish blooms that attract various pollinators.
