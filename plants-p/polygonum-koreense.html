Polygonum koreense

Polygonum koreense, commonly known as Korean knotweed, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum koreense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
