Psathyrostachys rupestris subsp. dagestanica

Psathyrostachys rupestris subsp.

dagestanica, commonly known as the Dagestan wild wheat, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky, dry habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in agricultural and genetic research.
