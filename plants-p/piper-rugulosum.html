Piper rugulosum

Piper rugulosum, commonly known as the rough-skinned pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to dark purple as they mature.

Piper rugulosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its leaves in traditional medicine.
