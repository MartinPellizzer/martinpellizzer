Pavonia plumosa

Pavonia plumosa, commonly known as the feathery false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia plumosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
