Poa taltalensis

Poa taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of northern Chile, particularly in the Atacama Desert. This plant is characterized by its low-growing, tufted habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. It thrives in arid and saline environments, showcasing adaptations to extreme conditions.

Poa taltalensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its harsh habitat.
