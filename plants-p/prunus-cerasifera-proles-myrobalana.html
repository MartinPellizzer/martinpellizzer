Prunus cerasifera proles myrobalana

Prunus cerasifera proles myrobalana, commonly known as the Persian lilac or Myrobalan cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet-scented fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
