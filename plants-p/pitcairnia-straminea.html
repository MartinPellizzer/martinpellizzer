Pitcairnia straminea

Pitcairnia straminea, commonly known as the silver pitcairnia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with colorful, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Pitcairnia straminea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
