Polypodium molliusculum

Polypodium molliusculum, commonly known as the common rock fern, is a perennial evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops, in woodlands, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium molliusculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to colonize difficult habitats.
