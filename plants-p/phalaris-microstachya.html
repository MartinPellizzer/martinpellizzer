Phalaris microstachya

Phalaris microstachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and damp meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers.

The seeds are enclosed in small, pointed capsules, which are adapted for dispersal by wind or water.
