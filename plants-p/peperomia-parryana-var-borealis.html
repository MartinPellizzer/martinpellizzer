Peperomia parryana var. borealis

Peperomia parryana var. borealis, commonly known as the northern peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a subtle reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spadix, which is surrounded by a modified leaf called an involucrum. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
