Potamogeton americanus var. thunbergii

Potamogeton americanus var. thunbergii, commonly known as American pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually dark green and often have a slight waxy coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
