Panicum luzonense

Panicum luzonense, commonly known as Luzon grass, is a native plant of the Philippines.

It is a tall, erect grass with slender, hairless stalks and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
