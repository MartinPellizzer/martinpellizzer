Potamogeton octandrus subsp. ethiopicus

Potamogeton octandrus subsp.

ethiopicus, commonly known as the Ethiopian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Africa. It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, with three stamens and a single pistil. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate to high nutrient levels.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
