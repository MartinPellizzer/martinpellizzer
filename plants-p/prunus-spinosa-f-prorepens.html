Prunus spinosa f. prorepens

Prunus spinosa f. prorepens, commonly known as the creeping cherry or prostrated blackthorn, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, thorny stems that often spread along the ground.

It produces small, dark purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn red in the fall. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in temperate regions.
