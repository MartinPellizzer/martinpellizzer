Philippia trimera subsp. kilimanjarica

Philippia trimera subsp.

kilimanjarica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pink flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the cool, moist conditions of montane environments. Its leaves are typically succulent, allowing it to store water in arid conditions.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited geographic distribution.
