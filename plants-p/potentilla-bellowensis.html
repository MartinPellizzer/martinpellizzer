Potentilla bellowensis

Potentilla bellowensis, commonly known as the Bellow's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are palmately compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Potentilla bellowensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
