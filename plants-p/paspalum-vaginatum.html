Paspalum vaginatum

Paspalum vaginatum, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers that produce tiny seeds.

Paspalum vaginatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
