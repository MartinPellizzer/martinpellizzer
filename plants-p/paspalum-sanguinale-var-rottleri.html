Paspalum sanguinale var. rottleri

Paspalum sanguinale var. rottleri, commonly known as the red paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or red in color, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
