Piper carpunya

Piper carpunya, commonly known as the Philippine pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
