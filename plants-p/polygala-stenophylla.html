Polygala stenophylla

Polygala stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala stenophylla is often found in open habitats such as shrublands and grasslands.
