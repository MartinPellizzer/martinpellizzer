Phyllanthus subcordatus

Phyllanthus subcordatus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white flowers and produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
