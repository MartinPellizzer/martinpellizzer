Physalis jacquinii

Physalis jacquinii, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx that turns red as the fruit matures. The fruit is edible, with a tart, tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique fruit structure.
