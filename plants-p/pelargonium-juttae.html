Pelargonium juttae

Pelargonium juttae, commonly known as the Jutta's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink to red blooms, which are borne on long, slender stalks. Its leaves are usually rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pelargonium juttae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
