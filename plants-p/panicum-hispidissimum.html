Panicum hispidissimum

Panicum hispidissimum, commonly known as the rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, bristly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that are often tinged with blue or purple hues, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens and restoration projects.

Panicum hispidissimum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
