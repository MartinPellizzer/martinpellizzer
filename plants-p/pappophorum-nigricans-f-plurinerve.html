Pappophorum nigricans f. plurinerve

Pappophorum nigricans f. plurinerve, commonly known as the black-stemmed feathergrass, is a variant of the feathergrass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and feathery inflorescences that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, while the inflorescences are composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters.

This subspecies is noted for its distinctive dark-colored stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
