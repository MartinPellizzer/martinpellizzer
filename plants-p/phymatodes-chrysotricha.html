Phymatodes chrysotricha

Phymatodes chrysotricha, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces slender, wiry leaves with a distinctive golden hue. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Phymatodes chrysotricha plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
