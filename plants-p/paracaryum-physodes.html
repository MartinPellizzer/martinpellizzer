Paracaryum physodes

Paracaryum physodes, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant's fronds are typically pale green to white, with a translucent texture that allows light to pass through, creating a unique visual effect. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Paracaryum physodes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care in suitable conditions.
