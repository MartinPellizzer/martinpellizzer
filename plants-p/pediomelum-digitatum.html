Pediomelum digitatum

Pediomelum digitatum, commonly known as the fingerleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of tubular florets, typical of the dandelion genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Pediomelum digitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
