Phymatopteris similis

Phymatopteris similis, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of fern native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, arid environments. The fronds are typically divided into narrow segments and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This plant is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that helps it retain moisture.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
