Parodia haselbergii subsp. graessneri

Parodia haselbergii subsp.

graessneri, commonly known as the Schultes' Pincushion Cactus, is a succulent species native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of cylindrical stems that are typically green to pale blue in color.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched inflorescences. Its stems are covered in small, white spines that provide protection from herbivores.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
