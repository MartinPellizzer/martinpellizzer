Polygala paniculata

Polygala paniculata, commonly known as the pink milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pink or purple flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has compound leaves with multiple leaflets. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
