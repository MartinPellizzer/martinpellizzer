Pholidota articulata var. griffithii

Pholidota articulata var. griffithii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or creeping nettle, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its stinging hairs and alternate leaves.

It belongs to the Urticaceae family, which includes other stinging plants such as nettles and dock. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a serrated margin and a hairy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and the plant is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes.
