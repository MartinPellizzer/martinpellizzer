Pandanus sinuvadosus

Pandanus sinuvadosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is well adapted to salt spray and sandy soils.
