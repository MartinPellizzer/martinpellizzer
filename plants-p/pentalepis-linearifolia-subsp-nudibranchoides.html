Pentalepis linearifolia subsp. nudibranchoides

Pentalepis linearifolia subsp.

nudibranchoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pentalepis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers that are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
