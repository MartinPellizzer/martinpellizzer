Pulicaria germanica

Pulicaria germanica, commonly known as German groundsel, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a low-growing, cushion-like structure with hairy, gray-green leaves. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
