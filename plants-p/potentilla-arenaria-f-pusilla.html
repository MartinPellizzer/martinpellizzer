Potentilla arenaria f. pusilla

Potentilla arenaria f. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf sand cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas.

Due to its compact size and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
