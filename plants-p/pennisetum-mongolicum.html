Pennisetum mongolicum

Pennisetum mongolicum, commonly known as Mongolian beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces fluffy, silver-gray plumes that are highly ornamental and often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Pennisetum mongolicum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in arid environments.
