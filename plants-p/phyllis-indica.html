Phyllis indica

Phyllis indica, commonly known as the Indian phyllis, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Phyllisaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale yellow, are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Phyllis indica is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
