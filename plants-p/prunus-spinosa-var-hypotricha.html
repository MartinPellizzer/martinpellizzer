Prunus spinosa var. hypotricha

Prunus spinosa var. hypotricha, commonly known as the bramble rose or dog rose, is a thorny shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red or yellow berries known as hip fruits. This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and is often used in hedgerows or as an ornamental plant.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
