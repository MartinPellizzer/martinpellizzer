Psilanthus malabaricus

Psilanthus malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, dark-colored wood that is highly valued for its aromatic properties. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the tree is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in its native habitat.
