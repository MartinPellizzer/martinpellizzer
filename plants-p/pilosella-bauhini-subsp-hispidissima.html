Pilosella bauhini subsp. hispidissima

Pilosella bauhini subsp.

hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its longevity and resilience in harsh environments.
