Pilea angolensis

Pilea angolensis, commonly known as the African begonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Begoniaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern, while the flowers appear in late spring or early summer.

Pilea angolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
