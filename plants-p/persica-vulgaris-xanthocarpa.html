Persica vulgaris xanthocarpa

Persica vulgaris xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow apricot.

This hybrid plant is a cross between the common apricot and a yellow-fruited variant, resulting in a unique combination of traits. It produces small, yellowish fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, with smooth, pale bark and alternate, ovate leaves.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
