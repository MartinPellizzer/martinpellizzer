Plantago radicata subsp. monticola

Plantago radicata subsp.

monticola, commonly known as mountain plantain, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features erect, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
