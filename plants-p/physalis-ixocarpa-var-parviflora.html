Physalis ixocarpa var. parviflora

Physalis ixocarpa var. parviflora, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is characterized by its hairy, inflated calyx that encloses the fruit, which is typically orange to red in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into the edible, tangy fruit. Native to South America, it thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique and recognizable member of the Physalis genus.
