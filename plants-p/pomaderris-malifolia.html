Pomaderris malifolia

Pomaderris malifolia, commonly known as lemon myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy surface, often with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, round, yellow fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Indigenous Australian medicine and as a flavoring agent.
