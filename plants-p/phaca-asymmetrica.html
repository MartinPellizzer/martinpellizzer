Phaca asymmetrica

Phaca asymmetrica, commonly known as the asymmetric phaca, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, asymmetrical leaf structure, which is a defining feature of the species. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its growth is typically low to the ground, with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Phaca asymmetrica is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
