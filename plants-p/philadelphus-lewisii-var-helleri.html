Philadelphus lewisii var. helleri

Philadelphus lewisii var. helleri, commonly known as Heller's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has simple, alternate leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and aromatic foliage.
