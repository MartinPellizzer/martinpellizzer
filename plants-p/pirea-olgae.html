Pirea olgae

Pirea olgae, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, silver-gray leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in saline environments. Pirea olgae is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early autumn.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and natural habitats.
