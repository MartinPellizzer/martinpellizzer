Piper magnibaccum

Piper magnibaccum, commonly known as the giant pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces berries that transition from green to red as they mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
