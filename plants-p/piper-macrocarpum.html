Piper macrocarpum

Piper macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil, Peru, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is a berry-like structure, matures to a bright red color and is edible.

Piper macrocarpum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
