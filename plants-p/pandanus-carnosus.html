Pandanus carnosus

Pandanus carnosus, commonly known as the beefsteak pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional weaving and basket-making. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit, which resembles a large, spiky pineapple, contains edible seeds that are used in local cuisines.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
