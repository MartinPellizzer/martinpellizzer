Phalaenopsis sumatrana var. paucivittata

Phalaenopsis sumatrana var. paucivittata, commonly known as the Sumatran moth orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the islands of Sumatra and Borneo.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences bearing numerous white to pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to reduce water loss.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
