Polygala albida var. angustifolia

Polygala albida var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in compact clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
