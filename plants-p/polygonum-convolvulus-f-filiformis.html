Polygonum convolvulus f. filiformis

Polygonum convolvulus f. filiformis, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed, characterized by its thin, thread-like stems.

It is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae, native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides or in cultivated fields.

Due to its rapid growth, it is considered a weed in many agricultural settings.
