Piper chiangdaoense

Piper chiangdaoense, commonly known as Chiang Dao pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper chiangdaoense make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
