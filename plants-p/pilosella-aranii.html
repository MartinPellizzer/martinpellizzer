Pilosella aranii

Pilosella aranii, commonly known as the Canary Island hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
