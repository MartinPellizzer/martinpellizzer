Prostanthera williamsii

Prostanthera williamsii, commonly known as Williams' mintbush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its aromatic properties.

Prostanthera williamsii is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and heathland environments.
