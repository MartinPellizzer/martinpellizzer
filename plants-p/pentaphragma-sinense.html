Pentaphragma sinense

Pentaphragma sinense, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
