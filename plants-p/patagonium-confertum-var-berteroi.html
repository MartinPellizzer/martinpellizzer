Patagonium confertum var. berteroi

Patagonium confertum var. berteroi, commonly known as the Patagonian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.

Its botanical features make it an important species for studying plant adaptations in arid environments.
