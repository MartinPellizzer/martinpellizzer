Polygonum tanganikae var. ciliatum

Polygonum tanganikae var. ciliatum, commonly known as the Tanganyikan knotweed, is a species native to the Lake Tanganyika region in East Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are often hairy and have ciliate margins, giving the plant its specific epithet "ciliatum.". It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

This plant thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native environment.
