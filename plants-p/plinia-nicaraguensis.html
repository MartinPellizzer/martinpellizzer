Plinia nicaraguensis

Plinia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Plinia nicaraguensis make it an important species in the ecological and cultural contexts of its native regions.
