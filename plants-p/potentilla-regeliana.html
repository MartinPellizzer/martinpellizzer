Potentilla regeliana

Potentilla regeliana, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla regeliana is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
