Potentilla morii

Potentilla morii, commonly known as the Mongolian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Potentilla morii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
