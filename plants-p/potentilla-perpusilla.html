Potentilla perpusilla

Potentilla perpusilla, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with many slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have five distinct lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in tundra and mountain meadow habitats.
