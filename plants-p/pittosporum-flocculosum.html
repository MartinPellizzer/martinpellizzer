Pittosporum flocculosum

Pittosporum flocculosum, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum flocculosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
