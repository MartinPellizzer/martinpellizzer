Piper inaequale var. inaequale

Piper inaequale var. inaequale, commonly known as the unequal sumpweed, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, greenish-white flowers clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often found in lowland forests or near stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
