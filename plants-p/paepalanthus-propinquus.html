Paepalanthus propinquus

Paepalanthus propinquus, commonly known as the Andean sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble single blooms. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
