Piper polystachyum

Piper polystachyum, commonly known as the Vietnamese pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
