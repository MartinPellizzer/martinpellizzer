Pernettya seleriana

Pernettya seleriana, commonly known as the alpine cowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates. Its berries, which are edible, are often consumed by wildlife and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.

Pernettya seleriana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in alpine gardens.
