Pelargonium curtisianum

Pelargonium curtisianum, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Pelargonium curtisianum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
