Piper estrellense

Piper estrellense, commonly known as the Estrellense pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some local cuisines.

The botanical features of Piper estrellense make it an important species for both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
