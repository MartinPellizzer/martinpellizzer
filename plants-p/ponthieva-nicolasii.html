Ponthieva nicolasii

Ponthieva nicolasii, commonly known as the Nicolasii fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are typically found on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
