Pappobolus amoenus var. amoenus

Pappobolus amoenus var. amoenus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its spread. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.

Its botanical features make it both a common weed and a subject of ecological study.
