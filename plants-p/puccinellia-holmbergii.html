Puccinellia holmbergii

Puccinellia holmbergii, commonly known as the Holmberg's saltbush, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and bluish-gray foliage, which helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it an important component of desert and coastal ecosystems.

Its fibrous roots stabilize soil and prevent erosion, contributing to ecological resilience in challenging climates.
