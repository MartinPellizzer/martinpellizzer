Pyrethrum aucheri

Pyrethrum aucheri, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones, which are composed of numerous tiny, tubular florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
