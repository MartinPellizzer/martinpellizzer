Pyrus pyraster f. borbasiana

Pyrus pyraster f. borbasiana, commonly known as the Borbasian whitebeam, is a rare horticultural form of the common whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive white to pale green leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits. This form is typically found in specific regions of Central Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.

It is often cultivated for its unique foliage and resilience to urban environments.
