Prostanthera rhombea

Prostanthera rhombea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mint bush, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a distinctive minty scent, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Prostanthera rhombea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
