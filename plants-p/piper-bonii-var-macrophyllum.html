Piper bonii var. macrophyllum

Piper bonii var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by reddish berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
