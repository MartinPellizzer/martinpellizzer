Pulsatilla × vispensis

Pulsatilla × vispensis, commonly known as the showy pasqueflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, saucer-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several leaflets that are hairy and lobed.

Pulsatilla × vispensis is noted for its early blooming period, often emerging in late spring when other plants are still dormant.
