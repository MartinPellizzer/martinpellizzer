Podalyria cuneifolia

Podalyria cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf podalyria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a cuneate shape, giving it its specific epithet. Podalyria cuneifolia is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates.
