Phaseolus affinis

Phaseolus affinis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or yellow flowers and elongated pods containing edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Phaseolus affinis is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.
