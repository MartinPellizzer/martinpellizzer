Ptisana

Ptisana, commonly known as the "flower of the sea," is a unique plant native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters along its stems. The plant thrives in saline environments, often growing on rocky shores or in coastal dunes. Ptisana has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptation to harsh coastal conditions and its role in coastal ecosystems.
