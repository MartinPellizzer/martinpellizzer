Plumbago scandens var. erecta

Plumbago scandens var. erecta, commonly known as blue mist shrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue-violet flowers. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
