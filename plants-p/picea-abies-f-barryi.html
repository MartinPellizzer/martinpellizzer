Picea abies f. barryi

Picea abies f. barryi, commonly known as the Barry's Norway spruce, is a botanical variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. This form exhibits a more compact growth habit compared to the typical Norway spruce, making it suitable for ornamental planting. The cones are usually smaller and more clustered, contributing to its unique appearance.

It thrives in cooler climates and well-drained soils, displaying resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
