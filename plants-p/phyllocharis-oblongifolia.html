Phyllocharis oblongifolia

Phyllocharis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllocharis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets surrounded by white to pale purple ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Phyllocharis oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
