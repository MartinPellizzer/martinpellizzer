Polygala cuneata

Polygala cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Polygala cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
