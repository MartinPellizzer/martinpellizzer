Poa hissarica

Poa hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic value.
