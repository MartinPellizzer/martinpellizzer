Philodendron paucinervium

Philodendron paucinervium, commonly known as the veiny philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often marked with prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant features a short, stocky stem with aerial roots that help it climb towards light. Its foliage is typically a deep green, and it thrives in humid, warm environments with bright, indirect light.

Philodendron paucinervium is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
