Pandanus pachys

Pandanus pachys, commonly known as the thick-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its robust, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various island cultures.
