Parodia uebelmanniana

Parodia uebelmanniana, commonly known as the Uebelmann's cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, rounded cactus with a ribbed stem that typically measures between 10 and 20 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
