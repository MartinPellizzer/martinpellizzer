Plantago incana

Plantago incana, commonly known as hoary plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
