Pleurothallis ophiantha

Pleurothallis ophiantha, commonly known as the Ophiantha orchid, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
