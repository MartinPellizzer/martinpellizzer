Persica domestica var. prunus

Persica domestica var. prunus, commonly known as the European plum.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and showy pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe with a sweet or tart flavor, depending on the variety. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
