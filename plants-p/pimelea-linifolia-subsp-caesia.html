Pimelea linifolia subsp. caesia

Pimelea linifolia subsp.

caesia, commonly known as the silverleaf waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and striking, cone-shaped flower heads that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
