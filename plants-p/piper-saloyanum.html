Piper saloyanum

Piper saloyanum, commonly known as the Saloyanum pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and parts of Indonesia. The plant produces small, clustered berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a distinct, peppery aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
