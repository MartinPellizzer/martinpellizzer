Patagonium chillanense var. simonsii

Patagonium chillanense var. simonsii, commonly known as the Chilean fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, pink to red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Patagonium chillanense var. simonsii plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
