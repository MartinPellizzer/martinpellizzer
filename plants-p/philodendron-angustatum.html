Philodendron angustatum

Philodendron angustatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge, and its stems are typically vine-like, allowing it to climb supports in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, though it is more prized for its foliage than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Philodendron angustatum is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
