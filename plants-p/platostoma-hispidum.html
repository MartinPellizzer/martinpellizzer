Platostoma hispidum

Platostoma hispidum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features small, leathery, and hairy fronds that are typically green and covered with fine, stiff hairs. Its rhizome is short and thick, supporting the upright fronds.

Platostoma hispidum thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
