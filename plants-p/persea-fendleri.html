Persea fendleri

Persea fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits. The fruit has a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and nutritional qualities.
