Pharmacum macrophyllum

Pharmacum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved pharmacum, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
