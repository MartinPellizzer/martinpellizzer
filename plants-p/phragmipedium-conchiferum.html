Phragmipedium conchiferum

Phragmipedium conchiferum, commonly known as the conchiferum orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive pink or purple hue. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically fragrant and bloom in late winter to early spring.

Phragmipedium conchiferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
