Pelargonium cucullatum subsp. strigifolium

Pelargonium cucullatum subsp.

strigifolium, commonly known as the woolly-leafed geranium, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features upright stems and distinctive, velvety, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
