Potentilla canadensis var. pumila

Potentilla canadensis var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit with many slender, creeping stems. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture and a distinct five-lobed appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
