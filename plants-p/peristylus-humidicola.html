Peristylus humidicola

Peristylus humidicola, commonly known as the wetland lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist or wetland environments, showcasing its adaptability to high humidity and waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, white or pale pink flowers with a distinct, often fragrant scent. Its reproductive structures are designed for wind pollination, which is common among plants in its habitat.

Peristylus humidicola plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
