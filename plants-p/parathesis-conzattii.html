Parathesis conzattii

Parathesis conzattii, commonly known as the Zanzibar lily, is a striking plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
