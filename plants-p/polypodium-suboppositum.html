Polypodium suboppositum

Polypodium suboppositum, commonly known as the subopposite fern, is a species of polypody that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, delicate fronds that are typically arranged in a subopposite pattern, giving it its common name. This fern is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
