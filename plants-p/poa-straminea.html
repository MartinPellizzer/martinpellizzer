Poa straminea

Poa straminea, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Poa straminea plays an important ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining grassland biodiversity.
