Prunus japonica var. zhejiangensis

Prunus japonica var. zhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the development of its foliage. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its fruits are small, reddish drupes that ripen in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
