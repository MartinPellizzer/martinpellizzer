Pleurothallis rhynchantha

Pleurothallis rhynchantha, commonly known as the "Flower of the Moon," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, pendulous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Pleurothallis rhynchantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
