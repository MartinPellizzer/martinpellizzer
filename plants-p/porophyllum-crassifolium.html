Porophyllum crassifolium

Porophyllum crassifolium, commonly known as Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
