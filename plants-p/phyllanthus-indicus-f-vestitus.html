Phyllanthus indicus f. vestitus

Phyllanthus indicus f. vestitus, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a variant of the Indian spurge.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The plant is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
