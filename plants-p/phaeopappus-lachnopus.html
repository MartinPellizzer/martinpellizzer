Phaeopappus lachnopus

Phaeopappus lachnopus, commonly known as the purple pappus or purple cottonweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-tinged foliage and fluffy, white or pale pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinators.
