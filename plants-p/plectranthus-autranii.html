Plectranthus autranii

Plectranthus autranii, commonly known as the Cape mallow, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically blooming from summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
