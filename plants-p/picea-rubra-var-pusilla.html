Picea rubra var. pusilla

Picea rubra var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf red spruce, is a compact, slow-growing conifer native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color, providing a striking visual contrast to its dark bark. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a deep purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a valued species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
