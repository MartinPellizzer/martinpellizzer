Potentilla borussica

Potentilla borussica, commonly known as the Siberian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Potentilla borussica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
