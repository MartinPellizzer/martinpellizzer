Plectranthus neglectus

Plectranthus neglectus, commonly known as African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Plectranthus neglectus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
