Pelargonium aculeatum

Pelargonium aculeatum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-gray foliage and vibrant pink to red flowers. The plant features upright stems with glandular hairs that give it a woolly appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium aculeatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
