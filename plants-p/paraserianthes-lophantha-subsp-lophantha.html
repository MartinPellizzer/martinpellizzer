Paraserianthes lophantha subsp. lophantha

Paraserianthes lophantha subsp.

lophantha, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a medium to large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength.

The tree has bipinnate leaves, with each leaflet being small and oval-shaped. It produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, followed by woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds.

This species is often found in dry to moist evergreen forests and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
