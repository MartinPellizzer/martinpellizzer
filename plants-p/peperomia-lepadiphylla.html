Peperomia lepadiphylla

Peperomia lepadiphylla, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for container cultivation.
