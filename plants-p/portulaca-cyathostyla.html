Portulaca cyathostyla

Portulaca cyathostyla, commonly known as the hooded portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Its unique floral structure includes a hood-like calyx that encloses the petals, giving it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
