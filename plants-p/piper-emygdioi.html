Piper emygdioi

Piper emygdioi, commonly known as the Emygdio's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Piper emygdioi is considered a rare and endangered species.
