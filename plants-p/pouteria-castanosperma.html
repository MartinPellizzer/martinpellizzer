Pouteria castanosperma

Pouteria castanosperma, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing the edible Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species. Its fruit is a hard, three-chambered pod that opens to release the seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

The plant is also valued for its ecological importance and is a key species in the biodiversity of the Amazon region.
