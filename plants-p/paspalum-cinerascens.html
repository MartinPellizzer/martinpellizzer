Paspalum cinerascens

Paspalum cinerascens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and tufted growth habit, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters.

Paspalum cinerascens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
