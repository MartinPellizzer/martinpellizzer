Pourouma ferruginea

Pourouma ferruginea, commonly known as the red-brown areca, is a tall, perennial herb native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 6 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The plant has traditional uses in some indigenous communities for its edible fruits and potential medicinal properties.
