Picea excelsa var. maxwellii

Picea excelsa var. maxwellii, commonly known as the Maxwell's silver fir, is a variant of the silver fir native to the high elevations of the Himalayas.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and silvery-blue needles. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage provides habitat for various wildlife species, and it plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
