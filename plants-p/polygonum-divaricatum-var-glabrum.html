Polygonum divaricatum var. glabrum

Polygonum divaricatum var. glabrum, commonly known as smooth knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its stout, erect stems that are typically smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, appearing in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

This species is often found in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.
