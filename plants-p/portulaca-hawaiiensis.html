Portulaca hawaiiensis

Portulaca hawaiiensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hawaii.

It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to the arid and sunny conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or red and bloom in the morning, closing by midday.

Portulaca hawaiiensis is a rare species, found only in specific locations on the island of Hawaii.
