Polygonum viviparum f. linearifolium

Polygonum viviparum f. linearifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf knotweed, is a variant of the common knotweed species.

It is characterized by its linear, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it thrives in moist, disturbed soils. It is often found in wetland areas, along riverbanks, and in marshy environments.

This form is notable for its vegetative propagation through runners, which allows it to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.
