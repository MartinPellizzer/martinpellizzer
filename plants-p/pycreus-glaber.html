Pycreus glaber

Pycreus glaber, commonly known as the smooth pycreus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
