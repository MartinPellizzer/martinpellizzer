Pseudocassia spectabilis

Pseudocassia spectabilis, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil also makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
