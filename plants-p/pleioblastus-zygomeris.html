Pleioblastus zygomeris

Pleioblastus zygomeris, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching culms covered in a dense, silvery-white bloom that gives it its common name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a vibrant green, adding visual interest to gardens. It is evergreen and thrives in moist, well-drained soil in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in temperate climates for landscaping purposes.
