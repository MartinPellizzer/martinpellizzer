Parthenium matricaria

Parthenium matricaria, commonly known as feverfew, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features slender stems and basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its medicinal properties have been historically used to treat headaches and inflammatory conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
