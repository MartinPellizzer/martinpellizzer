Potamogeton pusillus var. microcarpus

Potamogeton pusillus var. microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow leaves that are arranged spirally along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged. Its seeds are tiny and dispersed by water currents, aiding in its spread within aquatic habitats.

This species plays an important role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
