Pulsatilla × knappii

Pulsatilla × knappii, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pinkish-purple. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers consist of numerous stamen surrounded by a single pistil.

Pulsatilla × knappii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
