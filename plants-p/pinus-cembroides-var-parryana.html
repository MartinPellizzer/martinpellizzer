Pinus cembroides var. parryana

Pinus cembroides var. parryana, commonly known as the Parry juniper, is a shrub-like evergreen conifer native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its small, scale-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
