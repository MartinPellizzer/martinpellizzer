Poa pungens

Poa pungens, commonly known as the sharpgrass, is a species of grass native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Poa pungens is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
