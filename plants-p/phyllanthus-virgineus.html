Phyllanthus virgineus

Phyllanthus virgineus, commonly known as the Virgin's shield, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinct waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forest understories and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
