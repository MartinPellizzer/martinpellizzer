Potamogeton pectinatus var. gracilis

Potamogeton pectinatus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is characterized by its flexible, branching stems and is adapted to slow-moving or still water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
