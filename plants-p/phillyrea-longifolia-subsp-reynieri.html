Phillyrea longifolia subsp. reynieri

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

reynieri, commonly known as the Canary Island Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to Mediterranean climates.
