Persicaria sagittata

Persicaria sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Persicaria sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
