Polianthes maculata

Polianthes maculata, commonly known as the spotted agave, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or yellow spots. The plant produces tall, branched flower stalks that bear small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
