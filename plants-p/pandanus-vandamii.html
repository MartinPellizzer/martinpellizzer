Pandanus vandamii

Pandanus vandamii, commonly known as the Vandin's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Pandanus vandamii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near coastal areas.
