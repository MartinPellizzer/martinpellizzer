Persea bootanica

Persea bootanica, commonly known as the Caribbean avocado, is a species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a thick, greenish skin and a creamy, oily pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in local ecosystems.
