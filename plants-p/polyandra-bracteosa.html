Polyandra bracteosa

Polyandra bracteosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or bracteosa hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, warm climates and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
