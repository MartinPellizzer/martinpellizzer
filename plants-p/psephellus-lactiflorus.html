Psephellus lactiflorus

Psephellus lactiflorus, commonly known as the milkflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, milky sap when the stems are broken.

Psephellus lactiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
