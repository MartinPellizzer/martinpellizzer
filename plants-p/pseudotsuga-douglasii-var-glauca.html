Pseudotsuga douglasii var. glauca

Pseudotsuga douglasii var. glauca, commonly known as the blue Douglas fir, is a variant of the Douglas fir native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bluish-gray foliage, which gives it a unique appearance compared to other fir species. The leaves are typically shorter and more densely packed, contributing to its distinctive look. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
