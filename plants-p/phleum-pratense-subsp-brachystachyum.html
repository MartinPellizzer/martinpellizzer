Phleum pratense subsp. brachystachyum

Phleum pratense subsp.

brachystachyum, commonly known as the short-stalked timothy grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and fine, blue-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows, pastures, and along streambanks, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and it is often used as forage for livestock.

This subspecies is distinguished by its shorter staminal hairs compared to other subspecies of Phleum pratense.
