Prosopis dubia

Prosopis dubia, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically reduced to spines in mature plants, and its pale yellow to green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for wildlife and humans in some regions. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and can survive with minimal water, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Prosopis dubia also plays a role in preventing soil erosion due to its deep root system.
