Penstemon cyananthus var. judyae

Penstemon cyananthus var. judyae, commonly known as Judy's blue gentian, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking cobalt-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions.
