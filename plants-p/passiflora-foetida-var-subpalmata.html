Passiflora foetida var. subpalmata

Passiflora foetida var. subpalmata, commonly known as the spider lily or night lily, is a species of passionflower native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, dark green, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, white and purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces bright orange berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habits make it a fascinating subject for botanists and gardeners alike.
