Pleurothallis antioquiensis

Pleurothallis antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquia pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
