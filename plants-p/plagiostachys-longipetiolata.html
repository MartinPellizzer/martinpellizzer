Plagiostachys longipetiolata

Plagiostachys longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often bright green and have long petioles, allowing them to float on the water's surface. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, enabling it to form dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life.

Plagiostachys longipetiolata is valued in ornamental water gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
