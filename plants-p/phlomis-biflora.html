Phlomis biflora

Phlomis biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Phlomis biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
