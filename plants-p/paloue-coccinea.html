Paloue coccinea

Paloue coccinea, commonly known as the red paloue, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a slightly serrated edge.

Paloue coccinea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
