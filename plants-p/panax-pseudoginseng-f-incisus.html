Panax pseudoginseng f. incisus

Panax pseudoginseng f. incisus, commonly known as the incised ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng genus native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with serrated or incised leaf margins that set it apart from other Panax species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. Its roots are often harvested for medicinal use, similar to other ginseng species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and exhibits features typical of the genus, including compound leaves and a taproot system.
