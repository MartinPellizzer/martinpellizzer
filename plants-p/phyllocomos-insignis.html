Phyllocomos insignis

Phyllocomos insignis, commonly known as the rare mountain fern, is a unique plant species found in high-altitude regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that help it anchor in rocky soils. Its leaves are typically green and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
