Plectranthus longitubus var. effusus

Plectranthus longitubus var. effusus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It features soft, hairy leaves that give it a fuzzy appearance, and it produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of purple or pink. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both decorative and medicinal purposes.
