Pteromanes anomalum

Pteromanes anomalum, commonly known as the anomalous fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It belongs to the family Pteromanesaceae and is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia. The plant exhibits a complex morphology with finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. Its reproductive structures are enclosed within specialized spore cases, a feature typical of many ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Pteromanes anomalum is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
