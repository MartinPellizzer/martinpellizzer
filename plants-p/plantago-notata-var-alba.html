Plantago notata var. alba

Plantago notata var. alba, commonly known as white plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
