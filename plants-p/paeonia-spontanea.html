Paeonia spontanea

Paeonia spontanea, commonly known as the wild peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves composed of 5-7 serrated leaflets, and its stems are usually covered with a thick layer of silky hairs. Paeonia spontanea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural habitats.
