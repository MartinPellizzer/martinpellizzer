Pyrus zangezura

Pyrus zangezura, commonly known as the Zangezur pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is adapted to temperate climates and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and orchards.
