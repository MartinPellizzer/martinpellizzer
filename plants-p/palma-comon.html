Palma comon

Palma comon, commonly known as the common palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to various regions in Southeast Asia and the Pacific. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used for thatching and roofing, while the fruit produces edible seeds.

Palma comon thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
