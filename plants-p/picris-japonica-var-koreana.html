Picris japonica var. koreana

Picris japonica var. koreana, commonly known as Korean goatweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
