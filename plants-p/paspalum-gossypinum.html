Paspalum gossypinum

Paspalum gossypinum, commonly known as cotton grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in wet or disturbed soils. It is adapted to high salinity and can thrive in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
