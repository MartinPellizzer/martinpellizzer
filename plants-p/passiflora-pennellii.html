Passiflora pennellii

Passiflora pennellii, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of violet, blue, and white petals, along with prominent filaments and a crown of white filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora pennellii is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
