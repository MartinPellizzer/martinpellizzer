Plantago paralias var. brevifolia

Plantago paralias var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found growing in sandy or gravelly soils along sea shores and coastal dunes.
