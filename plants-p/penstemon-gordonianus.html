Penstemon gordonianus

Penstemon gordonianus, commonly known as Gordon's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon gordonianus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
