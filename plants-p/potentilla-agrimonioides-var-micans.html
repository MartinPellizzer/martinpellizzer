Potentilla agrimonioides var. micans

Potentilla agrimonioides var. micans, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
