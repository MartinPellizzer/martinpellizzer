Pleurothallis aequatorialis

Pleurothallis aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
