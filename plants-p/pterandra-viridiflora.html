Pterandra viridiflora

Pterandra viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered pterandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-draining soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
