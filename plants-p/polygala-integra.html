Polygala integra

Polygala integra, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spiny stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, hard pods that contain seeds.

Polygala integra is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
