Pneumonanthe depressa

Pneumonanthe depressa, commonly known as the low-growing lungwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
