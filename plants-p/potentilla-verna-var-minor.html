Potentilla verna var. minor

Potentilla verna var. minor, commonly known as the lesser spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
