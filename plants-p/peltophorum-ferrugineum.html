Peltophorum ferrugineum

Peltophorum ferrugineum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces woody pods that contain seeds.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
