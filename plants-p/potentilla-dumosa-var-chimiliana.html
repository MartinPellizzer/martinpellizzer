Potentilla dumosa var. chimiliana

Potentilla dumosa var. chimiliana, commonly known as the Chimilian shrub, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine gardens.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
