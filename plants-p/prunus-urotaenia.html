Prunus urotaenia

Prunus urotaenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *P. urotaenia*, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. Prunus urotaenia is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
