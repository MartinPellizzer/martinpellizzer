Pothos luzonensis

Pothos luzonensis, commonly known as the Philippine devil's ivy, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that vary in color from deep green to variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Pothos luzonensis is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air.
