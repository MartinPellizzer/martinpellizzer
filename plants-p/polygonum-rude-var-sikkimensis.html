Polygonum rude var. sikkimensis

Polygonum rude var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish in color and can become woody with age.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
