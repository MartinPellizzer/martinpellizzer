Piper insulicola

Piper insulicola, commonly known as the island pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, peppery fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Piper insulicola is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
