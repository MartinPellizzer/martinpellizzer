Plumeria microcalyx

Plumeria microcalyx, commonly known as the little-leaf plumeria, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its floral structure is typical of the Apocynaceae family, with a distinct corona surrounding the stamens.
