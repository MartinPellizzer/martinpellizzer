Prangos pabularia subsp. cylindrocarpa

Prangos pabularia subsp.

cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the cylinderleaf betony, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive cylindrical shape. Its flowers are clustered in dense spikes, with violet to purple petals that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and showy blooms.
