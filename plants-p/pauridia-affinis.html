Pauridia affinis

Pauridia affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf pauridia, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 inch in width. Its small, white to pale green flowers appear in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Pauridia affinis is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability to aquatic environments.
