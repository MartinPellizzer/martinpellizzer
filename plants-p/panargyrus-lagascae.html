Panargyrus lagascae

Panargyrus lagascae, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in dense, pale yellow spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are often clustered at the top of the stems. Its succulent leaves store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in arid climates.
