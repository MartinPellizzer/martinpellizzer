Phaius mindorensis

Phaius mindorensis, commonly known as the Mindanao orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the stem.

Phaius mindorensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky areas.
