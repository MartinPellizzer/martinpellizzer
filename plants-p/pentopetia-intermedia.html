Pentopetia intermedia

Pentopetia intermedia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forest understories. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive red hue and intricate petal patterns. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and a fibrous root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
