Pleurothallis hjertingii

Pleurothallis hjertingii, commonly known as the Hjerting's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
