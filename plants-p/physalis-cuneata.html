Physalis cuneata

Physalis cuneata, commonly known as the copperleaf or redcurrant physalis, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its inflated, papery calyx that encloses the fruit. The fruit, which is typically orange to red in color, is small and berry-like, containing several seeds. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the summer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and unique fruit structure.
