Prestoea longepetiolata

Prestoea longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled poinciana, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bipinnate leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces bright orange or red flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree has a straight trunk and can grow up to 20 meters tall, making it a striking addition to tropical landscapes.
