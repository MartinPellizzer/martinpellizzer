Phyllodes mollis

Phyllodes mollis, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and slightly hairy phyllodes, which are modified leaf stalks that function as leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

The silky wattle is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
