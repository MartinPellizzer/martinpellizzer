Potamogeton × nitens f. subsimilis

Potamogeton × nitens f. subsimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 cm wide. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it often grows in shallow waters with soft substrates. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual propagation, allowing it to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its morphological similarities to its parent species, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
