Phaca oroboides f. americana

Phaca oroboides f. americana, commonly known as the American phaca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun to partial shade.
