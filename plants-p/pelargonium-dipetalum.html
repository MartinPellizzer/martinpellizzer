Pelargonium dipetalum

Pelargonium dipetalum, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy, glandular leaves that are often aromatic. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pelargonium dipetalum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
