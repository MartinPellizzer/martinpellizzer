Pyrostria tulearensis

Pyrostria tulearensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a member of the grass family, Poaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Pyrostria tulearensis is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
