Pleopeltis lanceola

Pleopeltis lanceola, commonly known as the lanceolate pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, protective layer of sori, which are clusters of spore-producing structures.

The fronds are usually dark green and have a smooth, glossy appearance.
