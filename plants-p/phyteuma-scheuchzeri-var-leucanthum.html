Phyteuma scheuchzeri var. leucanthum

Phyteuma scheuchzeri var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or meadow-like habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
