Pappostipa vaginata

Pappostipa vaginata, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, typical of many grass species. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Pappostipa vaginata is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
