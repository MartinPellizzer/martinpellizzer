Phyllanthus larensis

Phyllanthus larensis, commonly known as the Larensis spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia.
