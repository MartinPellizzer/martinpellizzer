Physalis brasiliensis

Physalis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes and potatoes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx, similar to the tomatillo. These fruits are typically harvested when they turn a deep red or orange, indicating ripeness.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
