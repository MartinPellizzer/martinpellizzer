Pitcairnia carioana

Pitcairnia carioana, commonly known as the Carioca pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with bright red bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
