Phoebe elegans

Phoebe elegans, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Phoebe elegans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its fragrance and visual appeal.
