Prunus serrulata var. shimidsuii

Prunus serrulata var. shimidsuii, commonly known as the Shimidzuii cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins and delicate pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring, adding a beautiful floral display to the landscape.
