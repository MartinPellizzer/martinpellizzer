Peperomia jilotepequeana

Peperomia jilotepequeana, commonly known as the Jilotepeque peperomia, is a rare species native to the cloud forests of Honduras.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts. It thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for indoor cultivation with proper care.
