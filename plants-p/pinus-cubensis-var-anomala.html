Pinus cubensis var. anomala

Pinus cubensis var. anomala, commonly known as the Cuban pine variant, is a rare and lesser-known member of the pine family.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring long, slender needles and a conical shape. The tree produces cones that are typically smaller than those of other pine species, with a distinctive reddish-brown color. It thrives in specific ecological niches, often found in well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
