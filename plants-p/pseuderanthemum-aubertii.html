Pseuderanthemum aubertii

Pseuderanthemum aubertii, commonly known as the golden flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, surrounded by vibrant, petal-like bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
