Poinciana horrida

Poinciana horrida, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The tree produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic display during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Poinciana horrida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
