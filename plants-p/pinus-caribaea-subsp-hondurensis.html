Pinus caribaea subsp. hondurensis

Pinus caribaea subsp.

hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran caribou pine, is a medium to tall evergreen conifer native to Central America. It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five.

The plant produces cones that are often pendulous and contain both male and female reproductive structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This subspecies is valued for its timber and plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat.
