Periploca sylvestris

Periploca sylvestris, commonly known as the wild periploca, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Periploca sylvestris is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
