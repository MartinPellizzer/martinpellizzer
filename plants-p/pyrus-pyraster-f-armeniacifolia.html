Pyrus pyraster f. armeniacifolia

Pyrus pyraster f. armeniacifolia, commonly known as the Armenian crabapple, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its oval, serrated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, tart apples that are typically edible but often used for making jams and jellies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and horticulture adds to its botanical significance.
