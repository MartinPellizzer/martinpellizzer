Prunus sachalinensis f. multiflora

Prunus sachalinensis f. multiflora, commonly known as the Sachalin cherry or the multi-flowered cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Korean Peninsula and northern Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing ornamental value throughout the growing season. The fruit is a small, dark red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its compact form and spring floral display.
