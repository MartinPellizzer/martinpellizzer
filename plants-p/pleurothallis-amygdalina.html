Pleurothallis amygdalina

Pleurothallis amygdalina, commonly known as the almond orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a fragrant scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, aiding in moisture retention.

The orchid's flowers are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract specific pollinators.
