Peperomia berlandieri

Peperomia berlandieri, commonly known as the Mexican peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by small, round berries.

Peperomia berlandieri is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
