Pyrola picta var. pallida

Pyrola picta var. pallida, commonly known as the pale flame azalea, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a waxy texture and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring, adding a delicate floral display to woodland habitats. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
