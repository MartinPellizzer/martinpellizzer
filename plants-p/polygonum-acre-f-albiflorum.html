Polygonum acre f. albiflorum

Polygonum acre f. albiflorum, commonly known as whiteflower smartweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks, wet meadows, and other wetland habitats.

Polygonum acre f. albiflorum is an annual herb that blooms from late spring to early autumn.
