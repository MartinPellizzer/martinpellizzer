Potentilla verna opaca

Potentilla verna opaca, commonly known as the early spring cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its hairy, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period and ecological benefits.
