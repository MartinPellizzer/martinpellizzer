Pleiostachya morlae

Pleiostachya morlae, commonly known as the Morlae grass, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshy or riparian habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
