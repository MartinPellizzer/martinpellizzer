Potentilla bornmuelleri var. pontica

Potentilla bornmuelleri var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and possesses palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
