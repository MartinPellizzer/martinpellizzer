Panicum squamigerum

Panicum squamigerum, commonly known as silver panicum, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with small, bristly bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common sight in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its ornamental value has led to its use in landscaping and as a naturalized plant in various regions.
