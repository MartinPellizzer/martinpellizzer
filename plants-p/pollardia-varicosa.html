Pollardia varicosa

Pollardia varicosa, commonly known as the variable polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves are usually divided into small, serrated segments, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
