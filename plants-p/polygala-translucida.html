Polygala translucida

Polygala translucida, commonly known as the translucent milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
