Pouzolzia indica subvar. pyramidata

Pouzolzia indica subvar. pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramidate pouzolzia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, ascending stems that often form a pyramidal shape, giving it its common name. The plant has small, opposite, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
