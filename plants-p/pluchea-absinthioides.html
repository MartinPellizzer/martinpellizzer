Pluchea absinthioides

Pluchea absinthioides, commonly known as false wormwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its leaves are finely divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while the flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
