Prostanthera elisabethae

Prostanthera elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's mintbush, is a small shrub native to South Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant, mint-like scent. It produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

Prostanthera elisabethae is typically found in rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
