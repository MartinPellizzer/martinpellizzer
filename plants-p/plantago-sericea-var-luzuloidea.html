Plantago sericea var. luzuloidea

Plantago sericea var. luzuloidea, commonly known as the silky plantain or velvet plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on slender, leafless stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
