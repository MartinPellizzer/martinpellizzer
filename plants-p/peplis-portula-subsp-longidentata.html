Peplis portula subsp. longidentata

Peplis portula subsp.

longidentata, commonly known as the long-toothed buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth and small, dark green leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, oval berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
