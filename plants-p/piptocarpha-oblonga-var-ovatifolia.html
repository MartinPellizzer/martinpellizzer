Piptocarpha oblonga var. ovatifolia

Piptocarpha oblonga var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert flax, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been historically used by indigenous peoples for fiber and medicinal purposes.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
