Pyrola gormanii

Pyrola gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern United States and southern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves remain green throughout the winter, giving it its common name.

Pyrola gormanii thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
