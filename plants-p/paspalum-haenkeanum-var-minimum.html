Paspalum haenkeanum var. minimum

Paspalum haenkeanum var. minimum, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, making it suitable for ornamental use. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant, often used in landscaping and erosion control.
