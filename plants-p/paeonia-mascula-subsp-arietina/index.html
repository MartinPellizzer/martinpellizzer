Paeonia Mascula Subsp. Arietina

Crested peony, scientifically known as Paeonia mascula subsp. arietina, is a rare and striking botanical marvel celebrated for its unique, feather-like blooms. This subspecies of the peony genus is distinguished by its intricate, crenulated petals that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. Native to the mountainous regions of China, it thrives in cool, moist climates and well-drained soil. Its ornamental value has made it a prized addition to botanical gardens and private collections worldwide. Despite its beauty, the crested peony remains a challenging plant to cultivate, requiring careful attention to its specific needs.
