Pieris phillyreifolia

Pieris phillyreifolia, commonly known as the Japanese pieris, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that turn bronze in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is popular in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
