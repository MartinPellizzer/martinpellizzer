Pterichis elliptica

Pterichis elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf pterichis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
