Papaver rhoeas var. roubiaei

Papaver rhoeas var. roubiaei, commonly known as the Roubiaei poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its striking red-orange petals and a distinctive, narrow corona.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and Eastern Europe, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to fields and meadows. Its botanical features include a slender stem, alternate leaves, and a capsule-like fruit that disperses seeds by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
