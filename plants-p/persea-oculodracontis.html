Persea oculodracontis

Persea oculodracontis, commonly known as the eye-of-the-serpent laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, small white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

The plant's ornamental value and unique appearance make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and horticultural studies.
