Prunus pendula f. rotundipetala

Prunus pendula f. rotundipetala, commonly known as the weeping cherry with rounded petals, is a cultivar of the Prunus pendula species.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink or white, with rounded, soft petals that distinguish it from other weeping cherry varieties. This ornamental tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
