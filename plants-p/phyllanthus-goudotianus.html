Phyllanthus goudotianus

Phyllanthus goudotianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright orange to red berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in traditional medicine and horticulture.
