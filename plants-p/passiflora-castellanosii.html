Passiflora castellanosii

Passiflora castellanosii, commonly known as the Castellanos' passionflower, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing vine with striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue to purple berries that are edible and contain a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
