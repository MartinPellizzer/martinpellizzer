Polygonum × bogoriense

Polygonum × bogoriense, commonly known as the Java knotweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

Polygonum × bogoriense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
