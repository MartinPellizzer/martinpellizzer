Picea excelsa var. balcanica

Picea excelsa var. balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown, with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The cones are cylindrical and pendulous, containing winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. This variety is adapted to temperate mountain climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
