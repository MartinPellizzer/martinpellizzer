Paspalum apiculatum

Paspalum apiculatum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in high humidity and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Paspalum apiculatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its stabilizing effects on soil and its role in supporting local wildlife.
