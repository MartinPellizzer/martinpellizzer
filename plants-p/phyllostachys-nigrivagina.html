Phyllostachys nigrivagina

Phyllostachys nigrivagina, commonly known as black-bark bamboo, is a species of grass in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its dark, almost black, sheaths that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its culms are typically slender and have a smooth, dark green surface.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscape design.
