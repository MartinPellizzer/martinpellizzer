Piper pseudomunchanum

Piper pseudomunchanum, commonly known as the Munchanum pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems and compound leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are typically used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary applications.
