Perilla frutescens var. japonica

Perilla frutescens var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese perilla, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often violet or purple in color, with a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, and its fruits are small, dry capsules containing seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its leaves, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
