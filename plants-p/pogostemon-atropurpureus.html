Pogostemon atropurpureus

Pogostemon atropurpureus, commonly known as purple pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, tubular flowers. Its leaves are typically purple or reddish-purple, giving it a striking visual appearance.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.
