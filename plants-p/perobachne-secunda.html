Perobachne secunda

Perobachne secunda, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color.

Perobachne secunda is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
