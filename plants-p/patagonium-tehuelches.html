Patagonium tehuelches

Patagonium tehuelches, commonly known as the Tehuelche thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow to purple flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and shrublands.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is an important part of the local ecosystem.
