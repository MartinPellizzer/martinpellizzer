Prunus ravenii

Prunus ravenii, commonly known as the red sandalwood or ravenwood, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and rich color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance.

Prunus ravenii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique wood properties.
