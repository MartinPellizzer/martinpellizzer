Pelargonium × accedens

Pelargonium × accedens, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a species resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium × ibis.

It is characterized by its vibrant flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom profusely throughout the growing season. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge, often displaying a dark green color with a glossy texture. It is a popular choice for garden use due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Pelargonium × accedens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for containers, borders, and hanging baskets.
