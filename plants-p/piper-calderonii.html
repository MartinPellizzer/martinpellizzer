Piper calderonii

Piper calderonii, commonly known as the Calderon's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper calderonii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
