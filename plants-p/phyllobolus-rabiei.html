Phyllobolus rabiei

Phyllobolus rabiei, commonly known as the African leafroller, is a species of moth belonging to the family Erebidae.

It is characterized by its distinctive wing patterns, which often feature intricate, eye-like markings. The larvae of this species are known for their habit of rolling leaves to form shelters, a behavior that provides protection from predators and environmental stressors. The plant associations of Phyllobolus rabiei are primarily with various species of Acacia and other shrubs in its native habitat.
