Psilocaulon uncinatum

Psilocaulon uncinatum, commonly known as the hook-tongued orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves and a unique floral structure with a hooked spur. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a showy, forward-facing lip.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
