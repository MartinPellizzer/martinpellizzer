Polypodium nitidissimum var. latior

Polypodium nitidissimum var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leafed wall fern, is a variant of the polypody fern characterized by its broader, more elongated fronds compared to the typical species.

It is a terrestrial fern that thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found growing on rocky surfaces or in woodland areas. The fronds are typically lanceolate or ovate, with a smooth, glossy surface that gives the plant its "nitidissimum" name, meaning "very clean" or "shiny.". The plant produces sporangia on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori, a common feature among ferns.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded conditions.
