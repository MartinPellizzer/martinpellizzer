Psilanthus bababudanii

Psilanthus bababudanii, commonly known as the Bababudanii acacia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its unique ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, making it an important component of the region's flora. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
