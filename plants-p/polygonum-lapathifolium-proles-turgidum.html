Polygonum lapathifolium proles turgidum

Polygonum lapathifolium proles turgidum, commonly known as the purple stem knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes on the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish or purple tinge, and it produces small, clustered greenish flowers.

The species is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
