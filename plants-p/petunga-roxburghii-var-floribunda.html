Petunga roxburghii var. floribunda

Petunga roxburghii var. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering petunga, is a variant of the petunga plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
