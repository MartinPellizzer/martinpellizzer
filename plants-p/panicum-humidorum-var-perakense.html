Panicum humidorum var. perakense

Panicum humidorum var. perakense, commonly known as the Perak grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps it retain moisture.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
