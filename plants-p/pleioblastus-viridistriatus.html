Pleioblastus viridistriatus

Pleioblastus viridistriatus, commonly known as the green-striated bamboo, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is a clumping bamboo that grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters, with slender, erect culms that are often adorned with distinctive green longitudinal stripes. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for both garden and landscape use.

This bamboo is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
