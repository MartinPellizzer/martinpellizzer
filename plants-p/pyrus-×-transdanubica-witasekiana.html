Pyrus × transdanubica witasekiana

Pyrus × transdanubica witasekiana, commonly known as the Transdanubian pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus, native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, oval-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant typically grows as a medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are clustered in umbels, featuring white petals and yellow stamens, which bloom in early spring.

The fruit is a small, yellowish pear, often used for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
