Passiflora jiboiaensis

Passiflora jiboiaensis, commonly known as the Jiboia passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in a soft, hairy texture.

Passiflora jiboiaensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
