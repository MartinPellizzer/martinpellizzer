Perymenium mendezii var. verbesinoides

Perymenium mendezii var. verbesinoides, commonly known as the Mexican verbena, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Verbenaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing vibrant purple or blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
