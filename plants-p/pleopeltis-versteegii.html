Pleopeltis versteegii

Pleopeltis versteegii, commonly known as the Versteeg's rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, leathery leaves with a distinctive midrib and a hairy underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in tropical forest ecosystems.
