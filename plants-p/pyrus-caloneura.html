Pyrus caloneura

Pyrus caloneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish pear that is typically edible but not widely cultivated.

Pyrus caloneura is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
