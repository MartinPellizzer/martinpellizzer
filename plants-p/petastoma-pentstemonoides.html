Petastoma pentstemonoides

Petastoma pentstemonoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy.

It is a perennial herb native to the western United States, characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is aromatic and contains compounds that have been historically used in herbal remedies.

The species is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
