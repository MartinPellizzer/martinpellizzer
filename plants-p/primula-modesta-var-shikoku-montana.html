Primula modesta var. shikoku-montana

Primula modesta var. shikoku-montana, commonly known as the Shikoku mountain primrose, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale pink to white flowers. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
