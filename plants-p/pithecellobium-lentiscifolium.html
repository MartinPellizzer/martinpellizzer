Pithecellobium lentiscifolium

Pithecellobium lentiscifolium, commonly known as the velvetwood or velvet tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible, sweet, and sticky fruits that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are compound, with oval-shaped leaflets, and the bark is thick and fibrous.

Pithecellobium lentiscifolium is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
