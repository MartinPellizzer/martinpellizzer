Phreatia sulcata

Phreatia sulcata, commonly known as the Sulcate Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinct lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phreatia sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
