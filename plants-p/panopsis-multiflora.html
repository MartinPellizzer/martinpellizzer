Panopsis multiflora

Panopsis multiflora, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
