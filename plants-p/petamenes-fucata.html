Petamenes fucata

Petamenes fucata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a coastal plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes. Its fibrous roots help stabilize coastal environments, preventing erosion.

Petamenes fucata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
