Piper falcispicum

Piper falcispicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and narrow, elongated leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
