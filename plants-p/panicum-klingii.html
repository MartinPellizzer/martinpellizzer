Panicum klingii

Panicum klingii, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique texture and low maintenance requirements, Panicum klingii is often used in landscape designs to add visual interest.
