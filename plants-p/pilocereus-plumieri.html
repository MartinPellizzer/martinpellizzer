Pilocereus plumieri

Pilocereus plumieri, commonly known as the night blooming cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a spineless cactus that produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night. The plant has a cylindrical stem with areoles, from which both flowers and spines emerge. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 12 inches in diameter.

Pilocereus plumieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
