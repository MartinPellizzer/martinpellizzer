Poa sinaica var. vivipara

Poa sinaica var. vivipara, commonly known as the Sinai viviparous grass, is a rare and unique variant of the Poa sinaica species.

It is native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula in Egypt, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. This plant is notable for its viviparity, a reproductive strategy in which seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing close to the ground, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a vulnerable species with significant ecological importance in its native environment.
