Pseudostachyum polymorphum

Pseudostachyum polymorphum, commonly known as the variable grass-panicle, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid and tropical climates. Pseudostachyum polymorphum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
