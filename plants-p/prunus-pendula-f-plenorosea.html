Prunus pendula f. plenorosea

Prunus pendula f. plenorosea, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping peach, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus characterized by its distinctive cascading branches.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate ornamental display. The leaves are ovate and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the flowing branches. This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
