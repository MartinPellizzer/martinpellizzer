Periandra dulcis

Periandra dulcis, commonly known as sweet periandra, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a flowering shrub that produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, sweet fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit, which is typically consumed fresh, has a mild, sweet flavor and is valued for its nutritional content.
