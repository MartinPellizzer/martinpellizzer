Pterostylis irwinii

Pterostylis irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. It features a single, erect stem with a single spike of flowers, each resembling a small helmet.

The orchid is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
