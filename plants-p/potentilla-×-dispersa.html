Potentilla × dispersa

Potentilla × dispersa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
