Physoleucas schimperi

Physoleucas schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological importance in its native habitat.
