Psoralea Psoralioides

Psoralea psoralioides is a small, flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and Australia. It belongs to the legume family and is known for its distinctive, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow or pale green flowers. This hardy species thrives in poor soils and can withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient addition to dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and ecological adaptability make it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike. Despite its modest size, Psoralea psoralioides plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and contributing to biodiversity.
