Peronocactus concinnus

Peronocactus concinnus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with closely spaced, narrow ribs and a dense covering of white to pale green spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
