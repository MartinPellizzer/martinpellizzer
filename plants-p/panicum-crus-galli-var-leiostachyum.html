Panicum crus-galli var. leiostachyum

Panicum crus-galli var. leiostachyum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, wiry stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce fluffy, red or brown bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields and disturbed areas.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
