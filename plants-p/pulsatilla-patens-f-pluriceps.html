Pulsatilla patens f. pluriceps

Pulsatilla patens f. pluriceps, commonly known as the many-headed pasqueflower, is a variant of the pasqueflower species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of basal, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish down.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
