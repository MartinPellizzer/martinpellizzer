Pilosella colliniformis

Pilosella colliniformis, commonly known as the creeping pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often forming dense mats. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Pilosella colliniformis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
