Pollinia thwaitesii

Pollinia thwaitesii, commonly known as the African orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pollinia, which are masses of pollen that adhere to the insect's body during pollination. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions of southern Africa. It produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
