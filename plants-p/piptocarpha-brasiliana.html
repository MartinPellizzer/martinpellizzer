Piptocarpha brasiliana

Piptocarpha brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Piptocarpha brasiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
