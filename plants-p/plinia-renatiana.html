Plinia renatiana

Plinia renatiana, commonly known as the Brazilian blackberry, is a species of shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a fragrant aroma.

The fruit is often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine for its flavor and potential health benefits.
