Pelargonium leptum

Pelargonium leptum, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive calyx.

Pelargonium leptum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and vibrant blooms.
