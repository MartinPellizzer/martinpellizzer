Pawia octandra var. hybrida

Pawia octandra var. hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid pawia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations compared to its parent species.
