Pseuderanthemum malabaricum

Pseuderanthemum malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar false heather, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with prominent stamen that give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Pseuderanthemum malabaricum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
