Petunia violacea var. vulgaris

Petunia violacea var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common petunia, is a flowering plant in the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including blue, purple, and white. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often growing as an annual in temperate climates.

The leaves are ovate and hairy, while the stems are typically trailing or bushy, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.
