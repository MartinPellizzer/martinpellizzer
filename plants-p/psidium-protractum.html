Psidium protractum

Psidium protractum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
