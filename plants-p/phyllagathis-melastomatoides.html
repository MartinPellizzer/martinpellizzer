Phyllagathis melastomatoides

Phyllagathis melastomatoides, commonly known as the red-flowered mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy, red or pink flowers. The plant attaches itself to host trees through specialized roots, drawing water and nutrients from its host. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
