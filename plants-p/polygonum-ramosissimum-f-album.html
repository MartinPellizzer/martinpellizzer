Polygonum ramosissimum f. album

Polygonum ramosissimum f. album, commonly known as white knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
