Pennisetum benthamii var. nudum

Pennisetum benthamii var. nudum, commonly known as the naked false lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
