Piper silvanorum

Piper silvanorum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or forest pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the rainforests of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small, and often spiny fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance, while the stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height.

Piper silvanorum is valued for its medicinal properties and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage.
