Pterocactus fischeri

Pterocactus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's pterocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry, sandy soils it inhabits.

Due to its slow growth and specific environmental requirements, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
