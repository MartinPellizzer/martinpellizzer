Pandanus intralaevis

Pandanus intralaevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, sword-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits.

This species is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
