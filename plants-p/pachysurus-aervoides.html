Pachysurus aervoides

Pachysurus aervoides, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are thick and starchy, making it a valuable resource for food.

The botanical features of Pachysurus aervoides make it well adapted to humid, shaded environments in its natural habitat.
