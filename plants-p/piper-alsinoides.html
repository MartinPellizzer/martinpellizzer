Piper alsinoides

Piper alsinoides, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical perennial vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants, and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and research purposes.
