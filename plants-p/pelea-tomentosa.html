Pelea tomentosa

Pelea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly pelea, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its soft, white, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Pelea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
