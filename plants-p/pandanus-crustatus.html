Pandanus crustatus

Pandanus crustatus, commonly known as the rough pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its stiff, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near mangroves or in sandy soils.
