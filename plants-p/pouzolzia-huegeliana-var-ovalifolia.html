Pouzolzia huegeliana var. ovalifolia

Pouzolzia huegeliana var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
