Plectranthus mirabilis

Plectranthus mirabilis, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom in summer.

Plectranthus mirabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
