Pseudopilocereus aurisetus

Pseudopilocereus aurisetus, commonly known as the golden night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellow spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which open at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
