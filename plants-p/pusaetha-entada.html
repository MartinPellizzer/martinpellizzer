Pusaetha entada

Pusaetha entada, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it popular in furniture and construction.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
