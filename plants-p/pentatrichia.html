Pentatrichia

Pentatrichia, commonly known as the feather grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The flowers are typically small and clustered in panicles, which are often adorned with bracts that resemble feathers. Pentatrichia species are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

These plants are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
