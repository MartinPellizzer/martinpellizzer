Palaua moschata

Palaua moschata, commonly known as the musk melon, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces large, aromatic fruits with a pale green rind and a sweet, musky interior. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its unique fragrance and flavor.
