Potentilla × trefferi

Potentilla × trefferi, commonly known as the creeping potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with multiple stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which are attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Potentilla × trefferi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
