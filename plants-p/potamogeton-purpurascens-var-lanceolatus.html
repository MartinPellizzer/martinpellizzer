Potamogeton purpurascens var. lanceolatus

Potamogeton purpurascens var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the purple stem pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are often reddish or purple in color. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is important for providing habitat and food for aquatic wildlife.
