Patersonia occidentalis

Patersonia occidentalis, commonly known as the western mat-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Australia.

It features a dense, mat-like growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a low, sprawling rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Patersonia occidentalis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought and heat.
