Potentilla benacensis

Potentilla benacensis, commonly known as the Benaco cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla benacensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows.
