Pleurothallis hispida

Pleurothallis hispida, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features compact, clustered pseudobulbs and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are usually covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
