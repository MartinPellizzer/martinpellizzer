Paepalanthus uncinatus var. uncinatus

Paepalanthus uncinatus var. uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Argentina.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, woolly stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators. Its unique morphology, including the hooked spines, helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
