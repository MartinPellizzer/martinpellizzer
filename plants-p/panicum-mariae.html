Panicum mariae

Panicum mariae, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish-colored foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
