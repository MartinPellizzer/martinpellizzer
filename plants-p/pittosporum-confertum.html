Pittosporum confertum

Pittosporum confertum, commonly known as the broadleaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Pittosporum confertum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and conservation efforts.
