Plantago brasiliensis subsp. tandilensis

Plantago brasiliensis subsp.

tandilensis, commonly known as the Tandil plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a dense spike that emerges from the center of the rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
