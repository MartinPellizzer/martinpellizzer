Potentilla besseri

Potentilla besseri, commonly known as Bessera cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Potentilla besseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
