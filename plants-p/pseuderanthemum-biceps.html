Pseuderanthemum biceps

Pseuderanthemum biceps, commonly known as the two-armed false anthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with two prominent bracts that give it its common name.

Pseuderanthemum biceps is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
