Pelargonium glaucoides

Pelargonium glaucoides, commonly known as the silver geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
