Pavetta erubescens

Pavetta erubescens, commonly known as the red pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and elliptical in shape, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, black seeds.
