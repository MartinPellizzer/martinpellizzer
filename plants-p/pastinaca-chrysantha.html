Pastinaca chrysantha

Pastinaca chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, spiky fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
