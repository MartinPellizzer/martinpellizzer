Pongamia uliginosa

Pongamia uliginosa, commonly known as the black pongam, is a small, deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is adapted to wetland environments and can thrive in saline or waterlogged soils.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
