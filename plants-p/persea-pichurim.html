Persea pichurim

Persea pichurim, commonly known as the Pichurim avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, specifically Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a smooth, pale green skin and a creamy, oily pulp. The fruit is valued for its nutritional content, including healthy fats and vitamins, and is often used in local cuisines.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a significant plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
