Plagiostachys brevicalcarata

Plagiostachys brevicalcarata, commonly known as the narrow-spurred bamboo, is a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact inflorescences with short, bract-like structures that give it its specific epithet "brevicalcarata.". It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some parts of its range.
