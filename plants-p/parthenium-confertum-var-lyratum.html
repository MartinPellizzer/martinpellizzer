Parthenium confertum var. lyratum

Parthenium confertum var. lyratum, commonly known as the lyrated ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

This plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
