Populus × canescens f. pendula

Populus × canescens f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping poplar, is a unique cultivar of the poplar tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a distinctive, ornamental appearance.

This hybrid plant is derived from crosses between Populus nigra and Populus tremula, resulting in a species with a combination of traits from both parents. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and emits a mild, sweet scent when crushed, adding to its appeal in gardens and landscapes. The tree thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of temperate climates.

Due to its striking form and adaptability, it is often used as a decorative specimen tree in parks and urban settings.
