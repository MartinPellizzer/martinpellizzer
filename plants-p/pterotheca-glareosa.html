Pterotheca glareosa

Pterotheca glareosa, commonly known as the glabrous pterotheca, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
