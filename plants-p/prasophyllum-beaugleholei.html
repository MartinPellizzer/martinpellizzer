Prasophyllum beaugleholei

Prasophyllum beaugleholei, commonly known as the black leek orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which are often covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces tall, slender spikes bearing small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
