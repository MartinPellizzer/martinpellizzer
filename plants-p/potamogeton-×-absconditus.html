Potamogeton × absconditus

Potamogeton × absconditus, commonly known as the hybrid water milfoil, is a perennial aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem.

Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in clusters, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
