Pinus laricio subsp. calabrica

Pinus laricio subsp.

calabrica, commonly known as the Corsican fir, is a subspecies of the fir tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and woody, containing two to three seeds. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in Mediterranean forest ecosystems.
