Persicaria mexicana

Persicaria mexicana, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish or purplish in color, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Persicaria mexicana is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
