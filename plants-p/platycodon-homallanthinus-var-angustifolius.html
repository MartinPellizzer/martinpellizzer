Platycodon homallanthinus var. angustifolius

Platycodon homallanthinus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
