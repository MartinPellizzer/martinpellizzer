Phleum subalpinum

Phleum subalpinum, commonly known as alpine fescue, is a perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Phleum subalpinum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock and wildlife.
