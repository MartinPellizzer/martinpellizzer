Phyllostachys aurita

Phyllostachys aurita, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that are often covered in a yellowish hue, especially during the growing season. The plant produces panicles of small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Phyllostachys aurita is also valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine.
