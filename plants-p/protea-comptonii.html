Protea comptonii

Protea comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish bracts that surround a central cluster of small, yellow flowers. The plant is native to the Western Cape province and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. It produces a single, large flower head that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea comptonii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local biodiversity.
