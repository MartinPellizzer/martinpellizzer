Piptadenia lujae

Piptadenia lujae, commonly known as the Brazilian balsa tree, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in riverine forests and open woodlands.

The tree is valued for its lightweight wood, which is used in various industrial applications.
