Pseudopavonia tenax var. intermedium

Pseudopavonia tenax var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sticktight fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water requirements.
