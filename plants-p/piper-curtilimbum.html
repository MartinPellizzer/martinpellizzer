Piper curtilimbum

Piper curtilimbum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by three-winged berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
