Portulaca grandiflora var. major

Portulaca grandiflora var. major, commonly known as the Moss Rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual species that thrives in hot, sunny environments. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom throughout the summer. Its succulent leaves are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white wax, helping it retain moisture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
