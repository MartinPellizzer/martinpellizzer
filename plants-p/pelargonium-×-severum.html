Pelargonium × severum

Pelargonium × severum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets and containers due to its attractive foliage and long flowering period.

Pelargonium × severum is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
