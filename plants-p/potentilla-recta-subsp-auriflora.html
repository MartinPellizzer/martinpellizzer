Potentilla recta subsp. auriflora

Potentilla recta subsp.

auriflora, commonly known as golden cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has palmate leaves divided into five lobes, which are typically hairy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
