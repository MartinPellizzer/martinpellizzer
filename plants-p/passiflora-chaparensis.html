Passiflora chaparensis

Passiflora chaparensis, commonly known as the Chapare passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that display a mix of blue, purple, and white hues, with prominent stamens and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, hairy pubescence.

Passiflora chaparensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
