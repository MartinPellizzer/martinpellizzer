Potentilla fallacina f. eglandulosa

Potentilla fallacina f. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive glandular structures on its stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to certain regions of North America. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in summer, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Due to its unique features, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
