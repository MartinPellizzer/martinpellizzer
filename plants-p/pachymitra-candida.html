Pachymitra candida

Pachymitra candida, commonly known as the white-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, white stems and its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Pachymitra candida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
