Poa pratensis var. glaucescens

Poa pratensis var. glaucescens, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a variant of tall fescue that is characterized by its bluish-green foliage.

It is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically forms dense, tufted clumps with slender, upright stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a distinctive blue-green hue, which sets it apart from other grasses.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
