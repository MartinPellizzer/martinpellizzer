Picea jezoensis subsp. microsperma

Picea jezoensis subsp.

microsperma, commonly known as the Japanese white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are short and bluish-green.

The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticulture and landscaping.
