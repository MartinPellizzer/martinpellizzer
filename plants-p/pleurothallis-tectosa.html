Pleurothallis tectosa

Pleurothallis tectosa, commonly known as the "Tectonic Pleurothallis," is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its blooming period occurs during the rainy season, and it is often found in association with other epiphytic plants.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
