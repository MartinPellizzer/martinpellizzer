Pyrethrum leptophyllum

Pyrethrum leptophyllum, commonly known as the slenderleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, contributing to its common name, while its flowers are rich in pyrethrin, a natural insecticide.

Pyrethrum leptophyllum is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in pest control.
