Perilla frutescens var. auriculatodentata

Perilla frutescens var. auriculatodentata, commonly known as Chinese basil or Shiso, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated, ovate leaves that are often a vibrant green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces small, pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. Its leaves are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

This variety is widely cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
