Pyrus nitidifolia

Pyrus nitidifolia, commonly known as the glossyleaf pear, is a species of tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellow fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for gardens with well-drained soil.
