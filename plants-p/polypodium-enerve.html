Polypodium enerve

Polypodium enerve, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a tropical evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds with a distinct central vein. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.
