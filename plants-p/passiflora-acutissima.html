Passiflora acutissima

Passiflora acutissima, commonly known as the sharp-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora acutissima is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
