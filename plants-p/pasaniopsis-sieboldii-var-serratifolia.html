Pasaniopsis sieboldii var. serratifolia

Pasaniopsis sieboldii var. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated pasaniopsis, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
