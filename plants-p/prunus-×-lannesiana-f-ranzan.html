Prunus × lannesiana f. ranzan

Prunus × lannesiana f. ranzan, commonly known as the ranzan cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its distinctive floral features.

It is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus × yedoensis, resulting in a plant with unique ornamental qualities. The ranzan cherry is known for its double flowers, which are often white or pale pink, adding to its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It is typically a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded growth habit and attractive foliage.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy spring blooms.
