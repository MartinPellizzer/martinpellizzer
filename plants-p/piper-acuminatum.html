Piper acuminatum

Piper acuminatum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 3,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around other vegetation. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes.

The fruit is a small, berry-like drupe that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
