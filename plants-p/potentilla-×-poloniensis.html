Potentilla × poloniensis

Potentilla × poloniensis, commonly known as the Polish cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically reaches a height of about 30–60 cm and produces clusters of five-petaled blooms in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla × poloniensis is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
