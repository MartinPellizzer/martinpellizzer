Plectranthus coeruleus

Plectranthus coeruleus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus coeruleus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
