Potentilla thuringiaca f. villosa

Potentilla thuringiaca f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy potentilla, is a botanical variant of the species Potentilla thuringiaca.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. This shrub typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
