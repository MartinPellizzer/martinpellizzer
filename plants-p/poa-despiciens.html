Poa despiciens

Poa despiciens, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Poa despiciens is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
