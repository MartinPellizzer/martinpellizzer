Pelargonium × humei

Pelargonium × humei, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with hairy stems and broad, lobed leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × humei is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects, contributing to ecological balance in cultivated landscapes.
