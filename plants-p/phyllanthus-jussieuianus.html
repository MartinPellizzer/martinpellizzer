Phyllanthus jussieuianus

Phyllanthus jussieuianus, commonly known as the Jussieu's spurge, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, characterized by its milky sap and often clustered flowers. The plant typically has small, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are often clustered in cyathia, a unique feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions, making it a common component of xerophytic vegetation.
