Piper tesserispicum

Piper tesserispicum, commonly known as the Tesserispic pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small berries that mature to a dark purple hue, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical structure reflects adaptations to its humid, tropical habitat, with features supporting efficient photosynthesis and water retention.
