Plexipus hederaceus

Plexipus hederaceus, commonly known as the creeping wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating and produces small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows along the ground, spreading via rhizomes and forming dense mats. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it both an ornamental and an invasive species in certain regions.
