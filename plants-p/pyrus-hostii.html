Pyrus hostii

Pyrus hostii, commonly known as the White Pear, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pyrus hostii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in American horticulture.
