Parahebe carstensensis

Parahebe carstensensis, commonly known as the Stewart Island parahebe, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Parahebe carstensensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of forests on Stewart Island.
