Polyalthia trichoneura

Polyalthia trichoneura, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark of Polyalthia trichoneura contains compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
