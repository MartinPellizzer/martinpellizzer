Pombalia verrucosa

Pombalia verrucosa, commonly known as the warty pomegranate, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive warty appearance on its stems and leaves.

It is native to arid regions and thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, followed by fruit that resembles a pomegranate but is less commonly consumed by humans. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and ecological research.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further scientific investigation is needed.
