Portulaca pilosa subsp. sundaensis

Portulaca pilosa subsp.

sundaensis, commonly known as the Sunda sandmat, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a low-growing herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, vibrant yellow flowers that bloom during the day. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and open grasslands.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in its native habitat.
