Piper tucumanum

Piper tucumanum, commonly known as Tucuman pepper or yellow pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments with well-drained soil.
