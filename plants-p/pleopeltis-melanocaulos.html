Pleopeltis melanocaulos

Pleopeltis melanocaulos, commonly known as the black-stemmed rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its dark, wiry stems and leathery, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fronds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Pleopeltis melanocaulos is often found in forested areas with high moisture levels.
