Petaloma alternifolium

Petaloma alternifolium, commonly known as the alternate-leaf petaloma, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate arrangement of leaves, which are typically elongated and serrated. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a woody stem with smooth, bark-like texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
