Porophyllum lindenii

Porophyllum lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Porophyllum lindenii is often found in open plains and along canyon walls, where it thrives in full sun.
