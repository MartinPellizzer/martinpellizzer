Primula bella subsp. sciophila

Primula bella subsp.

sciophila, commonly known as the shade-loving primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to woodland areas in Europe. It features rosettes of narrow, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
