Pleurothallis cobriformis

Pleurothallis cobriformis, commonly known as the Cobra Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble a cobra's head, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a contrasting red or orange lip, and it produces small, round pseudobulbs.

Pleurothallis cobriformis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
