Plectronia livida

Plectronia livida, commonly known as the blue plectronia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. Plectronia livida is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
