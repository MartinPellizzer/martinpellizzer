Polypodium latipes var. laetum

Polypodium latipes var. laetum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green with a metallic sheen. The plant produces small, clustered sporangia on the undersides of its leaves, which are used for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
