Papaver paucisetum

Papaver paucisetum, commonly known as the few-seeded poppy, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
