Polypodium melanopus

Polypodium melanopus, commonly known as the black fern, is a species of fern native to various regions in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Polypodium melanopus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
