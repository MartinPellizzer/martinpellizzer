Procrassula pallidiflora

Procrassula pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered procrassula, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and silvery-gray foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Procrassula pallidiflora is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with succulent leaves that store water.

It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaped gardens.
