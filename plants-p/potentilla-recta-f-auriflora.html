Potentilla recta f. auriflora

Potentilla recta f. auriflora, commonly known as the goldenflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its yellow flowers and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
