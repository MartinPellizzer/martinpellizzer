Passiflora oaxacensis

Passiflora oaxacensis, commonly known as the Oaxacan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico.

It produces striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fruit, which is edible, develops from the flower and is typically yellow or orange in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
