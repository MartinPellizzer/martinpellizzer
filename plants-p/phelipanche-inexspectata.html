Phelipanche inexspectata

Phelipanche inexspectata, commonly known as the common yellow evening primrose, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, hence its common name. The plant has a taproot system and produces a large number of small, hairy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The botanical features of Phelipanche inexspectata make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
