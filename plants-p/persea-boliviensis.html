Persea boliviensis

Persea boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it can grow up to 15 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruits, though it is relatively uncommon in cultivation outside its native range.
