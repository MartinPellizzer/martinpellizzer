Papaver erraticum

Papaver erraticum, commonly known as the wandering poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, papery flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, with a distinctive, elongated corona. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its unique morphology and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in botanical studies.
