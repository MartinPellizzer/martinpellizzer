Phyllostegia brevicalycis

Phyllostegia brevicalycis, commonly known as the narrow calyx phyllostegia, is a species of flowering plant in the Lamiaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and the Gulf Coast. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
