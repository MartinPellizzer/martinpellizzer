Phlox multiflora var. intermedia

Phlox multiflora var. intermedia, commonly known as the New England phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its foliage remains evergreen in mild climates, adding ornamental value throughout the year.
