Pseudojumellea amplexicaulis

Pseudojumellea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping pseudojumellea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that help conserve water. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its succulent leaves and deep root system.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
