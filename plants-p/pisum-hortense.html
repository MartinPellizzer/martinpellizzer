Pisum hortense

Pisum hortense, commonly known as garden pea, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by pods containing several round, edible seeds. Pisum hortense is cultivated for its seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients, and it thrives in cool climates with well-drained soil.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
