Polygala bonavisio

Polygala bonavisio, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces small, round berries that are edible and have a tangy flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens.
