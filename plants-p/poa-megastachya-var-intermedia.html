Poa megastachya var. intermedia

Poa megastachya var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically violet to purple in color, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that enhances its visual appeal in garden settings.
