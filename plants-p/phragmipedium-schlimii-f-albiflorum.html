Phragmipedium schlimii f. albiflorum

Phragmipedium schlimii f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered phragmipedium, is a hybrid orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its botanical features include lance-shaped leaves and a sympodial growth habit.

This rare cultivar is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and elegance.
