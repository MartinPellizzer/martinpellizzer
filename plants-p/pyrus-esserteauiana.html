Pyrus esserteauiana

Pyrus esserteauiana, commonly known as the Esserteau pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in parts of China and the Himalayas. The plant is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing small, clustered flowers in early spring followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
