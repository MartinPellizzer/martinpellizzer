Poecilostachys analamazaotrensis

Poecilostachys analamazaotrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
