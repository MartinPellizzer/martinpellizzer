Ptelea crenulata

Ptelea crenulata, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, elliptical leaves with rounded tips. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by narrow, woody fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ptelea crenulata is often found in chaparral and desert scrub habitats.
