Prosopis koelziana var. puberula

Prosopis koelziana var. puberula, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its grayish bark, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife and humans.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
