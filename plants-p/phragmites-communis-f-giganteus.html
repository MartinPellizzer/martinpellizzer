Phragmites communis f. giganteus

Phragmites communis f. giganteus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, feathery flowers. It is often found in marshes, stream banks, and saline soils.

Its rapid growth and extensive rhizome system make it a dominant species in many wetland ecosystems.
