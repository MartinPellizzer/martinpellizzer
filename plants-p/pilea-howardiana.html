Pilea howardiana

Pilea howardiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble silver coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Pilea howardiana is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings or division.
