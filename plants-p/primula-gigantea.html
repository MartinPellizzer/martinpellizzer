Primula gigantea

Primula gigantea, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of yellow, orange, and white. The plant features heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy and covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections around the world.
