Philodendron minarum

Philodendron minarum, commonly known as the silver star philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that have a distinctive silvery-green pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Philodendron minarum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
