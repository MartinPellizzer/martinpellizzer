Passiflora ciliaris

Passiflora ciliaris, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments resembling a crown of thorns. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms.

Passiflora ciliaris thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
