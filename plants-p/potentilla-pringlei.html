Potentilla pringlei

Potentilla pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, and its stems are usually covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla pringlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and vibrant floral display.
