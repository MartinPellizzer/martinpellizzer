Pteronia quinqueflora

Pteronia quinqueflora, commonly known as the five-flowered pteronia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pteronia quinqueflora is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
