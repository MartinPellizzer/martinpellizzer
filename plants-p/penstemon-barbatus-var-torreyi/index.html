Penstemon Barbatus Var. Torreyi

Penstemon barbatus var. torreyi is a striking member of the plant family Plantaginaceae known for its vibrant floral displays. This perennial species is native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions. Its unique features include showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal, it is increasingly valued in both native gardens and conservation efforts.
