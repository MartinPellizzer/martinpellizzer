Primula hemsleyi

Primula hemsleyi, commonly known as the Hemsley's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula hemsleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate and vibrant blooms.
