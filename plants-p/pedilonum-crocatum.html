Pedilonum crocatum

Pedilonum crocatum, commonly known as the crimson thornbloom, is a strikingly unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a tall, spindly stem adorned with clusters of vibrant red, bell-shaped flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant's leaves are small, waxy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, reducing water loss in its harsh environment. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Despite its beauty, Pedilonum crocatum is toxic to livestock, making it both a botanical curiosity and a potential hazard.
