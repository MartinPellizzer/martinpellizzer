Pteris deltoidea

Pteris deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically dark green with a contrasting lighter midrib. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pteris deltoidea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a decorative plant.
