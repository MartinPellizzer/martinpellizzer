Piper auritilimbum

Piper auritilimbum, commonly known as the golden gooseberry, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
