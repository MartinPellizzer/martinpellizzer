Polypodiodes subamoena

Polypodiodes subamoena, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green with a slightly reddish hue on the underside. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
