Polypodium lucens

Polypodium lucens, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It thrives in humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks, and has a rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped, bright green fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Polypodium lucens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in shaded, moist gardens.
