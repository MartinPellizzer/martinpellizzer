Patagonium oresigenum

Patagonium oresigenum, commonly known as the Andean rock cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny structure and ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance.

Patagonium oresigenum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for local wildlife.
