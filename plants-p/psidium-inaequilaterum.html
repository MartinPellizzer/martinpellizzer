Psidium inaequilaterum

Psidium inaequilaterum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
