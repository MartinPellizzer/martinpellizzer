Paeonia corallina var. triternatiformis

Paeonia corallina var. triternatiformis, commonly known as the coral peony, is a rare and striking variant of the coral peony species.

It is characterized by its vibrant, coral-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive white or cream center. The plant is native to China and is known for its unique leaf structure, which is trifoliate and deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, requiring careful cultivation due to its sensitivity to environmental conditions.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized collections.
