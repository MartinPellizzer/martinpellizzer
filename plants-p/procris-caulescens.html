Procris caulescens

Procris caulescens, commonly known as the slender rock cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits known as siliques, which contain several seeds. Procris caulescens is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils in alpine or arctic regions.

It is a hardy species adapted to cold climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
