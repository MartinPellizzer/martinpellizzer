Pilosella brachiata subsp. muranensis

Pilosella brachiata subsp.

muranensis, commonly known as the Muranese hairweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are hairy and often arranged in a rosette, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

It is a perennial species that thrives in cool, moist environments with limited sunlight.
