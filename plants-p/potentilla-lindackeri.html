Potentilla lindackeri

Potentilla lindackeri, commonly known as Lindacker's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
