Phalaenopsis fimbriata subsp. sumatrana

Phalaenopsis fimbriata subsp.

sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra. It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining potting media.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
