Plantago coronopus var. canariense

Plantago coronopus var. canariense, commonly known as the Canary Island plantain, is a variant of the common plantain native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
