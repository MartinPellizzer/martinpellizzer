Pseuderanthemum chilianthium

Pseuderanthemum chilianthium, commonly known as the purple pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
