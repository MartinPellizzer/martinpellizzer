Piper brachytrichum

Piper brachytrichum, commonly known as the short-hair pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by small, three-lobed berries. It is native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and herbalists alike.
