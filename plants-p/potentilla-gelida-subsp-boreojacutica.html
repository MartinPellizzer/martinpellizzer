Potentilla gelida subsp. boreojacutica

Potentilla gelida subsp.

boreojacutica, commonly known as the Arctic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its small, palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in cold, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in alpine and tundra ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of polar flora.
