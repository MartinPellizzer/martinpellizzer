Pandanus dictyotus

Pandanus dictyotus, commonly known as the pandanus tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various traditional practices.
