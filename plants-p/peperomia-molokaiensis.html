Peperomia molokaiensis

Peperomia molokaiensis, commonly known as Molokai pepper plant, is a rare and unique species native to the Hawaiian island of Molokai.

It is a small, compact shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its limited natural habitat, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
