Protea thymelaeoides

Protea thymelaeoides, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly sugarbirds, which feed on its nectar.

The protea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
