Pappobolus mollicomus

Pappobolus mollicomus, commonly known as the soft-hairy pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine, soft trichomes. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for wind pollination.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
