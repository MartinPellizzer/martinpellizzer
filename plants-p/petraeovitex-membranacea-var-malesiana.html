Petraeovitex membranacea var. malesiana

Petraeovitex membranacea var. malesiana, commonly known as the Malesian petraeovitex, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, woody stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often twining around supporting structures. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

This plant is often found in tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
