Piptochaetium bicolor var. minus

Piptochaetium bicolor var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed florets. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This variant is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock.
