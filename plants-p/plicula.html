Plicula

Plicula, commonly known as the plicula plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a distinctive bilobed or plicated structure. The leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Plicula species are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in temperate climates.

These plants are often found in woodland or moist habitats, where they thrive in partial shade.
