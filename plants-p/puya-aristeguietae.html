Puya aristeguietae

Puya aristeguietae, commonly known as the Aristeguieta's puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces tall flower stalks bearing clusters of striking violet or purple flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and is adapted to rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
