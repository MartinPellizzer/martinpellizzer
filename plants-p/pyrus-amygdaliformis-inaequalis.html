Pyrus amygdaliformis inaequalis

Pyrus amygdaliformis inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal pear, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, with asymmetrical lobes that give it its specific epithet "inaequalis.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. It is native to a limited geographic region in Central Asia and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
