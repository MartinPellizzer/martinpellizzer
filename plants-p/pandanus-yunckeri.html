Pandanus yunckeri

Pandanus yunckeri, commonly known as the Yucatan pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and basket weaving. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal panicles. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pandanus yunckeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
