Poincianella myabensis

Poincianella myabensis, commonly known as the Myabense flame tree, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or near water sources.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
