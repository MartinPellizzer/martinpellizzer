Picramnia tariri

Picramnia tariri, commonly known as the desert acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it access groundwater. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine by local communities.
