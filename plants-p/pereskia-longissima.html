Pereskia longissima

Pereskia longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems that are covered in sparse, short spines. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Pereskia longissima is native to Central America and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
