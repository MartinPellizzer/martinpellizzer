Pimpinella Evoluta

Pimpinella evoluta, also known as the cow parsley or wild carrot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. This plant is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its feathery appearance and delicate blooms make it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays. Despite its beauty, it can be mistaken for edible plants, so caution is advised when identifying it in the wild.
