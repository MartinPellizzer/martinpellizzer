Pogonarthria leiarthra

Pogonarthria leiarthra, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Pogonarthria leiarthra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
