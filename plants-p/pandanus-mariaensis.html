Pandanus mariaensis

Pandanus mariaensis, commonly known as the Maria Island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of Maria in the Torres Strait.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
