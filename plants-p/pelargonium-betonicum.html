Pelargonium betonicum

Pelargonium betonicum, commonly known as the betonicum geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
