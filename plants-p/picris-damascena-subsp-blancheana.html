Picris damascena subsp. blancheana

Picris damascena subsp.

blancheana, commonly known as Blanchean everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant produces achenes, which are dry, indehiscent fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its common name reflects its ability to retain its flower heads for extended periods, giving it a "everlasting" appearance.
