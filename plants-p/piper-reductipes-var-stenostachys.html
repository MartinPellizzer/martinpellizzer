Piper reductipes var. stenostachys

Piper reductipes var. stenostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stalked pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow and are borne in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy berry.

This variety is less commonly cultivated than other piper species but is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
