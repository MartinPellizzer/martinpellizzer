Phaseolus ellipticus

Phaseolus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, bean-like pods and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while the seeds are small and kidney-shaped.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
