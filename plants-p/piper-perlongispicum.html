Piper perlongispicum

Piper perlongispicum, commonly known as the long-spurred pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its elongated, spiculate calyces that give it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
