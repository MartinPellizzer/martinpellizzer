Plantago tubulosa

Plantago tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular plantain, is a flowering plant native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
