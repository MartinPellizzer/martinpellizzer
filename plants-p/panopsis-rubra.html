Panopsis rubra

Panopsis rubra, commonly known as the red panicle vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in panicle clusters, often reaching lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement and are typically a deep green color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Panopsis rubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
