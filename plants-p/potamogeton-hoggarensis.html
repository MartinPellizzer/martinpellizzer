Potamogeton hoggarensis

Potamogeton hoggarensis, commonly known as Hogg's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in eastern North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
