Ptychotis intermedia

Ptychotis intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to pollinator-friendly environments.
