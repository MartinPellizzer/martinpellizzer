Potentilla ancistrifolia

Potentilla ancistrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
