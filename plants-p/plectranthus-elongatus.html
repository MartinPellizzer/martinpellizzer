Plectranthus elongatus

Plectranthus elongatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually ranging from purple to white, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it both a practical and aesthetically pleasing plant.
