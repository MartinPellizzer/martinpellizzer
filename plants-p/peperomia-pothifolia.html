Peperomia pothifolia

Peperomia pothifolia, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale green to white flowers that are not particularly showy but are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia pothifolia is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
