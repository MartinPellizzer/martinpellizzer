Polyalthia pinnatinervia

Polyalthia pinnatinervia, commonly known as the broad-leaved flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately veined, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces striking, fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree in tropical regions.
