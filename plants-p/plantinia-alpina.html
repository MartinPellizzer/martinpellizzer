Plantinia alpina

Plantinia alpina, commonly known as the alpine plantinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand harsh weather conditions.

Plantinia alpina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
