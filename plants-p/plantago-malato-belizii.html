Plantago malato-belizii

Plantago malato-belizii, commonly known as the Malto-Belizian plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Plantaginaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, greenish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
