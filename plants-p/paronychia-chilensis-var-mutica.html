Paronychia chilensis var. mutica

Paronychia chilensis var. mutica, commonly known as the Chilean sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
