Pandanus sigmoideus

Pandanus sigmoideus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that often droop downward. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color, which are followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Its leaves are widely used in traditional crafts and cooking, and the plant is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments.
