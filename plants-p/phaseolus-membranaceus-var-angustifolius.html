Phaseolus membranaceus var. angustifolius

Phaseolus membranaceus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf runner bean, is a herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It features slender, hairy stems that climb using tendrils, and its leaves are narrow and heart-shaped with a serrated edge. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that give way to elongated, slender pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is cultivated for its beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
