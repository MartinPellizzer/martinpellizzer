Petastoma laurifolium

Petastoma laurifolium, commonly known as the laurel leaf petasite, is a member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of laurel plants. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important plant in its native habitats.
