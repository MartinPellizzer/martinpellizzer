Peucedanum cordatum

Peucedanum cordatum, commonly known as the heart-leaved angelica, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically large and deeply lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbrella-like clusters, known as umbels. Its roots are often harvested for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Peucedanum cordatum is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
