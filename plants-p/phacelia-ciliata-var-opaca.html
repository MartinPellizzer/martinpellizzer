Phacelia ciliata var. opaca

Phacelia ciliata var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
