Pedicularis kashmiriana subsp. ornata

Pedicularis kashmiriana subsp.

ornata, commonly known as the Kashmir orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India. It belongs to the family Orobanchaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that resemble orchids.

The plant is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on other plants for nutrients through its root system. Its flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.
