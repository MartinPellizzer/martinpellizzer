Pyrethrum atratum

Pyrethrum atratum, commonly known as the black pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pyrethrum atratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insecticidal properties.
