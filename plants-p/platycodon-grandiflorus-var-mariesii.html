Platycodon grandiflorus var. mariesii

Platycodon grandiflorus var. mariesii, commonly known as the Mariesii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
