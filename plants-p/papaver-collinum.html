Papaver collinum

Papaver collinum, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive black center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its stem is usually hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along stream banks.

Papaver collinum is known for its medicinal properties, with the sap containing alkaloids used in traditional remedies.
