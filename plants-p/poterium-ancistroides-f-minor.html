Poterium ancistroides f. minor

Poterium ancistroides f. minor, commonly known as the dwarf rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features a compact, low-growing structure with fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
