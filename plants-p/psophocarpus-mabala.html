Psophocarpus mabala

Psophocarpus mabala, commonly known as the Mabala bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Mabala bean is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
