Potentilla aurea f. vegetior

Potentilla aurea f. vegetior, commonly known as the more vigorous form of the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens.

This variety is valued for its ability to form dense ground cover and its resilience in various environmental conditions.
