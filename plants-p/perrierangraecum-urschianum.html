Perrierangraecum urschianum

Perrierangraecum urschianum, commonly known as the Corsican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bell-shaped blooms that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Perrierangraecum urschianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
