Prunus rossiana

Prunus rossiana, commonly known as the Ross's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, sweet drupe that ripens in late summer, making it an attractive plant for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
