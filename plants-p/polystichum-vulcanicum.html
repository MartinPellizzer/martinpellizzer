Polystichum vulcanicum

Polystichum vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the volcanic regions of Japan and the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing on rocky or volcanic soils. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Polystichum vulcanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
