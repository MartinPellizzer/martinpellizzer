Paraprasophyllum montanum

Paraprasophyllum montanum, commonly known as the mountain paraprasophyllum, is a rare and delicate plant species native to the alpine regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its growth is slow, and it often forms dense, low-lying mats.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural habitat.
