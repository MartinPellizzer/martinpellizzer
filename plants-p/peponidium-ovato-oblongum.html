Peponidium ovato-oblongum

Peponidium ovato-oblongum, commonly known as the broad-ovate melon, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, ovate-oblong fruits that are typically green when mature. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins and produces yellow flowers that are monoecious, meaning both male and female flowers are present on the same plant. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is edible and often used in culinary applications due to its sweet, melon-like flavor.
