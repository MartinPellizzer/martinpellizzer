Phymatodes meyeniana

Phymatodes meyeniana, commonly known as the Mexican giant ragweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and can grow up to several meters in height. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Phymatodes meyeniana is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
