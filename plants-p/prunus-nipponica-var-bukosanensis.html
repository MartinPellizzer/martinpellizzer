Prunus nipponica var. bukosanensis

Prunus nipponica var. bukosanensis, commonly known as the Bukosan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn vibrant red in the autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, sweet-tart fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The Bukosan cherry is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
