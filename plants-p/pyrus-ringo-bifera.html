Pyrus ringo bifera

Pyrus ringo bifera, commonly known as the bifera apple, is a rare and unique variant of the apple tree.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bifurcated branches. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible due to their bitter taste. Botanically, it is notable for its ornamental value and its role in genetic research.

Its limited natural range makes it a subject of interest for conservationists and horticulturists.
