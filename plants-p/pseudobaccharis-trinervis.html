Pseudobaccharis trinervis

Pseudobaccharis trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined pseudobaccharis, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native range.
