Pterolepis glomerata var. angustifolia

Pterolepis glomerata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pterolepis, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are typically found at the tips of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and specialized species.
