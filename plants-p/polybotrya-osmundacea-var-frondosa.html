Polybotrya osmundacea var. frondosa

Polybotrya osmundacea var. frondosa, commonly known as the frondose water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Polybotrya.

It is a aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are often used in ornamental ponds. The plant thrives in freshwater environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. Its fronds are typically divided into smaller, finger-like segments, giving it a lacy appearance.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
