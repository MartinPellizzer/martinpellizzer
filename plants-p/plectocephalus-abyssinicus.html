Plectocephalus abyssinicus

Plectocephalus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, pendulous pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts within its native habitat.
