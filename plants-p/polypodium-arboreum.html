Polypodium arboreum

Polypodium arboreum, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered with persistent, overlapping leaf bases that resemble a tree trunk. The plant produces fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae and pinnules. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Polypodium arboreum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
