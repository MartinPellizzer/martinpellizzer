Phaseolus vulgaris convar. compressus

Phaseolus vulgaris convar. compressus, commonly known as the bush bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stems compared to other phaseolus species. The plant typically produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

This variety is widely cultivated for its nutritional value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
