Phrynium holostachyum

Phrynium holostachyum, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by orange-red berries containing small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some regions.
