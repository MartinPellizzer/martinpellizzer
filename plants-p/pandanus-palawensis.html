Pandanus palawensis

Pandanus palawensis, commonly known as the Palau pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Caroline Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fibrous roots are valued for their strength.

The species is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
