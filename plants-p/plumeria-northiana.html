Plumeria northiana

Plumeria northiana, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in various colors such as white, yellow, and pink. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in floral arrangements and religious ceremonies.

The bark is smooth and pale, with a distinctive flaky texture.
