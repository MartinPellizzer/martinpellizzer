Pogonia thouarsii

Pogonia thouarsii, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features a unique, pinkish-purple pouch-like flower that resembles a slipper, which is a characteristic feature of the Pogonia genus. The plant typically grows in dense, moist habitats such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in shaded conditions. Its roots are often colonized by mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.

The species is also notable for its fragrant scent, which attracts specific pollinators like bees and flies.
