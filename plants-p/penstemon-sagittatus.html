Penstemon sagittatus

Penstemon sagittatus, commonly known as the arrowleaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Penstemon sagittatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
