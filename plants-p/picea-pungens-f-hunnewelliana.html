Picea pungens f. hunnewelliana

Picea pungens f. hunnewelliana, commonly known as the Blue Spruce, is a cultivar of the white spruce tree.

It is characterized by its dense, conical shape and striking blue-gray foliage that remains throughout the growing season. The needles are short, stiff, and have a distinctive blue hue due to a waxy coating. It is a hardy evergreen that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its longevity in the landscape.
