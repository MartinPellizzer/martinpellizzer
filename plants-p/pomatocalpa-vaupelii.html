Pomatocalpa vaupelii

Pomatocalpa vaupelii, commonly known as the Vaupel's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip or labellum. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
