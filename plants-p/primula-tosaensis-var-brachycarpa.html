Primula tosaensis var. brachycarpa

Primula tosaensis var. brachycarpa, commonly known as the Tosa primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, rounded fruits that are characteristic of its variety, brachycarpa, meaning "short fruit.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
