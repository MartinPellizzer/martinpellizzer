Polygonum mucronatum

Polygonum mucronatum, commonly known as the sharp-tipped knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Polygonum mucronatum is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and resilience.
