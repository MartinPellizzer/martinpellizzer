Photinia raupingensis

Photinia raupingensis, commonly known as the Raupingensis photinia, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy red or pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as an ornamental shrub due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.

Photinia raupingensis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
