Polygala microlopha var. gracilis

Polygala microlopha var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spurge or slender milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pale yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often hairy.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
