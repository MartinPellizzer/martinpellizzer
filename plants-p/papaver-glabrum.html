Papaver glabrum

Papaver glabrum, commonly known as the smooth poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange petals that form a cup-like shape, with a prominent central stigma. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.

Papaver glabrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
