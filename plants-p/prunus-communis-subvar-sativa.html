Prunus communis subvar. sativa

Prunus communis subvar. sativa, commonly known as the domesticated cherry, is a fruit-bearing shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, sweet, red or yellow fruits that are widely consumed fresh or used in cooking. The plant has simple, ovate leaves and produces white or pink flowers in spring. It is typically cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a hard, stony pit.
