Penstemon gracilentus var. ursorum

Penstemon gracilentus var. ursorum, commonly known as the slender beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers, typically in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
