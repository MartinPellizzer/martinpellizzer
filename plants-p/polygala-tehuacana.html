Polygala tehuacana

Polygala tehuacana, commonly known as the Mexican milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds.

Polygala tehuacana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
