Passiflora urceolata

Passiflora urceolata, commonly known as the cup-shaped passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that feature a crown of colorful filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits that are often consumed raw or used in culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora urceolata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
