Phyllanthus choristylus

Phyllanthus choristylus, commonly known as the African buttonwillow, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, characterized by its small, oval leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its clustered, button-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

The botanical features of Phyllanthus choristylus make it an important plant in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
