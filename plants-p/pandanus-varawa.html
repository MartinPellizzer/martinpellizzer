Pandanus varawa

Pandanus varawa, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom at the top of the leaf rosette. Its roots and leaves are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Pandanus varawa thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near beaches or in mangrove areas.
