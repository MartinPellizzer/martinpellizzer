Pombalia glauca

Pombalia glauca, commonly known as the blue glauca, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pombalia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
