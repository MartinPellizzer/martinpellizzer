Psilostrophe tagetina var. sparsiflora

Psilostrophe tagetina var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bright yellow flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
