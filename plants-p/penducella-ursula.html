Penducella ursula

Penducella ursula, commonly known as the bear's ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that resemble bear ears. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Penducella ursula thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
