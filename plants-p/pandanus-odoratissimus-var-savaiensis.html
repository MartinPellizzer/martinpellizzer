Pandanus odoratissimus var. savaiensis

Pandanus odoratissimus var. savaiensis, commonly known as the Savai'i pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the island of Savai'i in Samoa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. Its roots and leaves are also used in local medicine for their purported healing properties.

This variant is notable for its distinct appearance and cultural significance in the Pacific region.
