Passiflora dentata

Passiflora dentata, commonly known as the serrated passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges and striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown-like structure of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vigorous growth.

Passiflora dentata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
