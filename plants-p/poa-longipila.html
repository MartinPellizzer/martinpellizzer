Poa longipila

Poa longipila, commonly known as the long-leaved bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Poa longipila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
