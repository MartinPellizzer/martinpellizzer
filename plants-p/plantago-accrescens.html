Plantago accrescens

Plantago accrescens, commonly known as the greater plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, in fields, and near water sources.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago accrescens have been used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
