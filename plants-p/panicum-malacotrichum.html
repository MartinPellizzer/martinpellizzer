Panicum malacotrichum

Panicum malacotrichum, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, silver-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
