Pilea victoriensis

Pilea victoriensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of heart-shaped, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying qualities.

Pilea victoriensis is also known for its ease of propagation through stem cuttings.
