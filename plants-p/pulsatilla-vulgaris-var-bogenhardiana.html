Pulsatilla vulgaris var. bogenhardiana

Pulsatilla vulgaris var. bogenhardiana, commonly known as the Bogenhard's pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with numerous yellow stamens surrounding a central pistil. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and adequate drainage.
