Paeonia peregrina var. officinalis

Paeonia peregrina var. officinalis, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, with a distinctive fragrance. The plant has palmate leaves composed of five to seven leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

The roots of this plant are also used in herbal remedies, contributing to its significance in botanical and medicinal contexts.
