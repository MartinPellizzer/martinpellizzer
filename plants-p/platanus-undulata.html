Platanus undulata

Platanus undulata, commonly known as the Mexican fan palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Platanaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that grow in a dense, umbrella-like crown. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its trunk is typically smooth and gray, with a distinctive fluted appearance.

The Mexican fan palm is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various climates.
