Panicum aegyptiacum var. blepharanthum

Panicum aegyptiacum var. blepharanthum, commonly known as Egyptian millet, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant is native to Africa and thrives in warm, dry climates. It has narrow, linear leaves and is often used as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes.

Its seeds are edible and have been utilized in traditional cuisines.
