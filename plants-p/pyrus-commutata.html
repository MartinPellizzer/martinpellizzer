Pyrus commutata

Pyrus commutata, commonly known as the dwarf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Pyrus commutata is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
