Parrya cheiranthoides var. lanuginosa

Parrya cheiranthoides var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and covered in a fine, hairy coating.

This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.
