Panicum miliaceum subsp. agricola

Panicum miliaceum subsp.

agricola, commonly known as proso millet, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It is a small annual grass that grows to about 1 to 2 meters in height, with slender stalks and panicles of spikelets.

The plant produces small, two-lobed grains that are typically light brown or yellowish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is cultivated for its edible grains, which are rich in nutrients and used in various cuisines around the world.
