Pleurothallis sibatensis

Pleurothallis sibatensis, commonly known as the Sibatensis Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the rainforest canopy, where it receives ample light and moisture. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the air and rain, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining substrates.

Pleurothallis sibatensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
