Parrasia linearifolia

Parrasia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaf parrasia, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
