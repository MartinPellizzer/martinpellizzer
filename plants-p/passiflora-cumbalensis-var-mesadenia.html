Passiflora cumbalensis var. mesadenia

Passiflora cumbalensis var. mesadenia, commonly known as the Cumbal passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues, with prominent filaments and a crown-like structure. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, producing heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.

The fruit, which is edible, contains a sweet, fleshy pulp surrounded by a fibrous layer.
