Persicaria stagnina

Persicaria stagnina, commonly known as swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in wet environments.

Persicaria stagnina is often considered an indicator species for wetland ecosystems due to its specific habitat requirements.
