Paspalum guayanerum

Paspalum guayanerum, commonly known as the Guayanense grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Its ecological role makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
