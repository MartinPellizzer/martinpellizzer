Plantago bellardii subsp. bellardii

Plantago bellardii subsp.

bellardii, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features basal rosettes of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

The plant produces upright flowering stalks bearing dense clusters of small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their healing properties.
