Pelargonium uliginosum

Pelargonium uliginosum, commonly known as the bog geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems and produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or marshy areas. Its leaves are typically rounded with a waxy coating, and it has a short growing season.

Pelargonium uliginosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
