Premna oswaldii

Premna oswaldii, commonly known as the white-veined glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with prominent white veins, and its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
