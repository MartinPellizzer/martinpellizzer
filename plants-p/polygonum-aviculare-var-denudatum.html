Polygonum aviculare var. denudatum

Polygonum aviculare var. denudatum, commonly known as the naked polygonum, is a low-growing annual herb that belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
