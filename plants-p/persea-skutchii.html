Persea skutchii

Persea skutchii, commonly known as the Skutch's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, orange-red berry that ripens in the dry season.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
