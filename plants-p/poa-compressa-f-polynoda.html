Poa compressa f. polynoda

Poa compressa f. polynoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf meadow grass, is a variant of the common bentgrass species.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed leaves and dense, low-growing habit. This plant typically thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its compact growth form makes it an important component of grassland ecosystems.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered closely together, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
