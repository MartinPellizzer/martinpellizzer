Pulsatilla nigricans var. zichyi

Pulsatilla nigricans var. zichyi, commonly known as the Chinese pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces follicles that contain numerous small seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
