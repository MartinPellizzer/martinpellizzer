Psathyrotes ramosissima

Psathyrotes ramosissima, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in dry, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
