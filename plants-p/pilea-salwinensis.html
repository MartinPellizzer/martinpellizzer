Pilea salwinensis

Pilea salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin begonia, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring shades of green, white, and pink.

Pilea salwinensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
