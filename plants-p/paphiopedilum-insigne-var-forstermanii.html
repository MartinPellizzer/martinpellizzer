Paphiopedilum insigne var. forstermanii

Paphiopedilum insigne var. forstermanii, commonly known as the Forsterman's Lady's Slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure with a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with well-draining soil. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish with red or brown markings, adding to its ornamental value.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity in cultivation.
