Polygonum cognatum var. alpestre

Polygonum cognatum var. alpestre, commonly known as alpine knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and cool climates. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

This variant is notable for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
