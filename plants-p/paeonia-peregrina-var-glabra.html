Paeonia peregrina var. glabra

Paeonia peregrina var. glabra, commonly known as the naked peony, is a rare and striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, red or purple bracts that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is cultivated in specialized gardens and botanical collections.
