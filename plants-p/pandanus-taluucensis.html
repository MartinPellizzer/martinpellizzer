Pandanus taluucensis

Pandanus taluucensis, commonly known as the Tongan pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the South Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces large, branched inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, known as a pandan fruit, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species plays an important ecological and cultural role in its native regions.
