Primula warshenewskiana subsp. rhodantha

Primula warshenewskiana subsp.

rhodantha, commonly known as the pink primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features rosettes of basal, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
