Pentacocca leonenis

Pentacocca leonenis, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
