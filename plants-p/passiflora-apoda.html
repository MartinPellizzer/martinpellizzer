Passiflora apoda

Passiflora apoda, commonly known as the naked passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a distinctive reproductive structure called a gynostemium. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora apoda is valued in traditional medicine for its potential calming and sedative properties.
