Polygala serpyllacea subsp. pyxophylla

Polygala serpyllacea subsp.

pyxophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes.

The leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a slight curl, and the plant is generally low-growing. It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly in South Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.
