Pelargonium bentinkianum

Pelargonium bentinkianum, commonly known as the Bentink's geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in its native arid environment.

Pelargonium bentinkianum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
