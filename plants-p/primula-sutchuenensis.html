Primula sutchuenensis

Primula sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Sichuan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The Sichuan primrose is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, colorful blossoms.
