Primula eburnea

Primula eburnea, commonly known as the silver leaf primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and delicate appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
