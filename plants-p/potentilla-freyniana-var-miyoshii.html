Potentilla freyniana var. miyoshii

Potentilla freyniana var. miyoshii, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically features dark green, serrated leaves and produces small, five-petaled blooms in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
