Phycella arzae

Phycella arzae, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fragrant flowers that bloom in the early spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environments with its deep root system and water-storing tissues. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Phycella arzae plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
