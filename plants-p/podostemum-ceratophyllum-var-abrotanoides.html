Podostemum ceratophyllum var. abrotanoides

Podostemum ceratophyllum var. abrotanoides, commonly known as the African water plantain, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter, with a broad, heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that emerge from the water surface, often in clusters. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with rich organic substrates.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
