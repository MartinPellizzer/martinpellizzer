Potentilla leptopetala var. staminea

Potentilla leptopetala var. staminea, commonly known as the staminea cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable species for rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
