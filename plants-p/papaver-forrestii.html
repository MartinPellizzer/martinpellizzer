Papaver forrestii

Papaver forrestii, commonly known as the Tibetan poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Papaveraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, including parts of Tibet, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
