Polygala involutiflora

Polygala involutiflora, commonly known as the spiral-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in dense, spiral-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
