Polygala pamparum

Polygala pamparum, commonly known as the Pampas milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Polygala pamparum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
