Plantago agrestis

Plantago agrestis, commonly known as common plantain, is a flowering plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features rosettes of broad, waxy leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive ribbed structure. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, with both male and female parts present on the same flower.

It is well adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
