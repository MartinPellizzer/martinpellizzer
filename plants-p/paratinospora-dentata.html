Paratinospora dentata

Paratinospora dentata, commonly known as the toothed lichen, is a species of lichen that belongs to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its crustose growth form, typically forming a thin, flat layer on rock surfaces. The thallus is usually pale yellow to greenish in color, with a distinctive, finely serrated margin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found on tree trunks and bark in temperate regions.

This lichen is known for its chemical composition, containing various secondary metabolites that contribute to its ecological role in nutrient cycling.
