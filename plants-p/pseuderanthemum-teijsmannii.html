Pseuderanthemum teijsmannii

Pseuderanthemum teijsmannii, commonly known as the Java false spiderflower, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. Its flowering period typically occurs during the wet season, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
