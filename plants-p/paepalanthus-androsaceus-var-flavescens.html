Paepalanthus androsaceus var. flavescens

Paepalanthus androsaceus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and small, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
