Parodia columnaris var. ochraceiflora

Parodia columnaris var. ochraceiflora, commonly known as the ochre-flowered columnar cactus, is a compact, spiny cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with dark green to bluish-gray spines and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers during the spring and summer. The plant has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.

Its unique coloration and resilience make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
