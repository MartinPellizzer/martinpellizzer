Parvotrisetum myrianthum

Parvotrisetum myrianthum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved basket grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that is often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
