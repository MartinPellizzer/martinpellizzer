Phrynium villosulum

Phrynium villosulum, commonly known as the hairy phrynium, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Phrynium villosulum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
