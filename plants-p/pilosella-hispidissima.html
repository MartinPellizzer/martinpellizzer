Pilosella hispidissima

Pilosella hispidissima, commonly known as the hispid mouse-eared chicory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, rocky soils, and along roadsides, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive, slightly hairy appearance.

Pilosella hispidissima is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
