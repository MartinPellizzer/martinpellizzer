Photinia sieboldii

Photinia sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange foliage in the autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Photinia sieboldii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
