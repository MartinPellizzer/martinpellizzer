Patagonium argenteum

Patagonium argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers.

Patagonium argenteum plays a role in stabilizing soil in arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
