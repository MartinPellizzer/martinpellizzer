Persea himalayaensis

Persea himalayaensis, commonly known as the Himalayan persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and requires a humid environment.

Due to its ornamental value and limited cultivation, it is considered a rare and underutilized species in horticulture.
