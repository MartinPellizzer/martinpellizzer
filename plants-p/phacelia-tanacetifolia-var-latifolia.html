Phacelia tanacetifolia var. latifolia

Phacelia tanacetifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has a short growing season and is known for its rapid growth and early blooming.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ecological restoration projects due to their ability to attract pollinators.
