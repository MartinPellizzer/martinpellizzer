Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. guianensis

Phyllanthus schomburgkianus var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Guyana and northern Brazil. The plant is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are usually yellow-green in color.
