Parodia microsperma var. macrancistra

Parodia microsperma var. macrancistra, commonly known as the Macrantha Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is well-suited for experienced growers.
