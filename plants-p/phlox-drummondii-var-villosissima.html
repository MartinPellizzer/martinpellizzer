Phlox drummondii var. villosissima

Phlox drummondii var. villosissima, commonly known as the velvet phlox, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, fuzzy foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range from pink to red. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It prefers well-drained soil and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The varietal name "villosissima" refers to its notably velvety, hairy leaves.
