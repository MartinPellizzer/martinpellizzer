Podocarpus palembanicus

Podocarpus palembanicus, commonly known as the Palembang podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against environmental stress.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitats.
