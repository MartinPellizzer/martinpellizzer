Podocarpus maxima

Podocarpus maxima, commonly known as the giant podocarp, is a tall evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly arranged along the branches, giving it a smooth, almost metallic appearance. It produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture and landscaping.
