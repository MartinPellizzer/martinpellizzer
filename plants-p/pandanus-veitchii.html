Pandanus veitchii

Pandanus veitchii, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces long, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottlebrushes, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are followed by fibrous, palm-like fruits.

Pandanus veitchii is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy and versatile species.
