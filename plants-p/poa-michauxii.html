Poa michauxii

Poa michauxii, commonly known as Michaux's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small and closely arranged.

Poa michauxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
