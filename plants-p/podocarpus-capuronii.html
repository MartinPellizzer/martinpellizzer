Podocarpus capuronii

Podocarpus capuronii, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces woody cones that contain edible, fleshy seeds, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
