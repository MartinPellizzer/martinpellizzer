Pavonia affinis

Pavonia affinis, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with five petals and a distinctive yellow center. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun.

Pavonia affinis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
