Phaseolus vulgaris var. subcarneus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. subcarneus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species characterized by its distinctive red, elongated pods.

It is native to the Americas and is cultivated for its edible seeds and vibrant foliage. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces flowers that transition from white to pink as they mature. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex vascular structure typical of legumes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional content, making it both a decorative and edible plant.
