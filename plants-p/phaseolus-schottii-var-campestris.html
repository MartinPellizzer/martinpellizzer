Phaseolus schottii var. campestris

Phaseolus schottii var. campestris, commonly known as the Andean runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andes region of South America.

It produces elongated, edible pods that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or brown when mature. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and bears small, yellow flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary use and is often cultivated for both its beans and ornamental appeal.
