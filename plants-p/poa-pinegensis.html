Poa pinegensis

Poa pinegensis, commonly known as the Alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit, which help it survive in harsh, alpine environments. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often forming dense mats that stabilize rocky or barren terrain. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce wind-pollinated flowers, a common reproductive strategy in high-altitude ecosystems.

Poa pinegensis plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
