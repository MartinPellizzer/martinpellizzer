Pseudanamomis macrocarpa

Pseudanamomis macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant false bamboo, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant produces striking white to yellowish spadices, which are the male flowers, while the female flowers are clustered at the base of the inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
