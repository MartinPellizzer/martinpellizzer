Philadelphus coronarius f. salicifolius

Philadelphus coronarius f. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf mock orange, is a cultivar of the mock orange plant characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The foliage is narrow and elongated, giving the plant a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its showy blooms and attractive foliage.
