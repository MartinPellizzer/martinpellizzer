Phlox douglasii subsp. hendersonii

Phlox douglasii subsp.

hendersonii, commonly known as Henderson's phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions.

The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to the blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
