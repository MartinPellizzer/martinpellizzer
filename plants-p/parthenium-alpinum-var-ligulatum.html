Parthenium alpinum var. ligulatum

Parthenium alpinum var. ligulatum, commonly known as the Alpine Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, dry conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of nectar for pollinators.
