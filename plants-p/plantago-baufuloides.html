Plantago baufuloides

Plantago baufuloides, commonly known as the African plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its broad, ribbed leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spike. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties, including treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
