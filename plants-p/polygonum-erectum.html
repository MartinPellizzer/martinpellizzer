Polygonum erectum

Polygonum erectum, commonly known as upright knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems that are often reddish in color. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems during the summer months.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
