Polygonum dichotomum var. angustissimum

Polygonum dichotomum var. angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often reddish or purple-tinged, especially along the veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and are usually pink or reddish in color.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive bracteate flowers and often edible stems and leaves.
