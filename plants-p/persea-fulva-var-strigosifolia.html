Persea fulva var. strigosifolia

Persea fulva var. strigosifolia, commonly known as the hairy persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon tree native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow-orange fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and edible fruit. Its growth habit includes a small to medium-sized tree with a spreading canopy.

The flowers are small, greenish-white, and are typically borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
