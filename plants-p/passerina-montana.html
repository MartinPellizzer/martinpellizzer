Passerina montana

Passerina montana, commonly known as the mountain pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the Rocky Mountains.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in alpine environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and numerous stamens.

Passerina montana plays an important role in pollination and is an indicator species for high-altitude ecosystems.
