Palhinhaea crassifolia

Palhinhaea crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved palhinhaea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
