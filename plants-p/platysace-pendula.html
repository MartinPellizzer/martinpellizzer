Platysace pendula

Platysace pendula, commonly known as the hanging plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Platysace pendula is known for its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or containers.
