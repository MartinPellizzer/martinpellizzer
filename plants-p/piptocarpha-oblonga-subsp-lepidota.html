Piptocarpha oblonga subsp. lepidota

Piptocarpha oblonga subsp.

lepidota, commonly known as the silverleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
