Primula auriculata subsp. tournefortii

Primula auriculata subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Tournefort's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

This subspecies is noted for its resilience and ability to bloom reliably in well-drained conditions.
