Physostegia correllii

Physostegia correllii, commonly known as Correll's bluebeard, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Physostegia correllii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
