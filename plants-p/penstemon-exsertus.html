Penstemon exsertus

Penstemon exsertus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of hair.

Penstemon exsertus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
