Paraprasophyllum collinum

Paraprasophyllum collinum, commonly known as the mountain marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, showy blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Paraprasophyllum collinum is a rare and endangered species, facing threats from habitat loss and climate change.
