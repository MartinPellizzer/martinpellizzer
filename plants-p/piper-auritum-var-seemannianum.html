Piper auritum var. seemannianum

Piper auritum var. seemannianum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces tiny, aromatic berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
