Pithecellobium lasiopus

Pithecellobium lasiopus, commonly known as the rough podded acacia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or savanna habitats.
