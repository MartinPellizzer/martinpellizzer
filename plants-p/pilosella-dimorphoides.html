Pilosella dimorphoides

Pilosella dimorphoides, commonly known as the two-form dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Pilosella dimorphoides produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, aiding in wind dispersal of its seeds.
