Pilosella rosea

Pilosella rosea, commonly known as the rose petty or white hair grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white or pale yellow flowers that resemble a ball of cotton, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, woolly appearance. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Pilosella rosea is a perennial herb that is native to Europe, western Asia, and parts of North Africa.
