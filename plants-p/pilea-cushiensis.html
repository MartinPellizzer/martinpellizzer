Pilea cushiensis

Pilea cushiensis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It is a low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Pilea cushiensis is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
