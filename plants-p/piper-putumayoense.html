Piper putumayoense

Piper putumayoense, commonly known as the Putumayo pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is typically small and resembles a pepper, though it is not commonly consumed as a spice.
