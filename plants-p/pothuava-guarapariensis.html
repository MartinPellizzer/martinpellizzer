Pothuava guarapariensis

Pothuava guarapariensis, commonly known as the Guarapeci orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a slender, upright stem with several leaves that are elongated and pointed. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the humid, shaded environments where it grows.

This orchid thrives in well-drained soil and requires high levels of light and humidity to flourish.
