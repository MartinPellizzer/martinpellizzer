Pluchea sarcophylla

Pluchea sarcophylla, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.
