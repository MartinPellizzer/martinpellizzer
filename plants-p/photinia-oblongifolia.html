Photinia oblongifolia

Photinia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy red or pink flowers. The leaves are oval-shaped and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to the vibrant floral displays. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate urban conditions.
