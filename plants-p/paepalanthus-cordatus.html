Paepalanthus cordatus

Paepalanthus cordatus, commonly known as the heart-shaped cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped stem and spiny leaves, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
