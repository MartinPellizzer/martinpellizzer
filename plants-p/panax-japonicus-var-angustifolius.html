Panax japonicus var. angustifolius

Panax japonicus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ginseng, is a variant of the ginseng plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and a root system that is often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, forested environments with rich, well-drained soil. Its botanical features include a perennial herb with a distinct rhizome and compound leaves.

This variety is valued for its potential medicinal properties, similar to other ginseng species.
