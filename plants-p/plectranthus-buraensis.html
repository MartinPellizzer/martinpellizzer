Plectranthus buraensis

Plectranthus buraensis, commonly known as the Bura mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for both garden and container cultivation. Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and herbal use.

Plectranthus buraensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
