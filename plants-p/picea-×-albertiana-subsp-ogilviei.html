Picea × albertiana subsp. ogilviei

Picea × albertiana subsp.

ogilviei, commonly known as the Ogilvie spruce, is a hybrid conifer native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its narrow, conical shape and dark green, needle-like foliage.

The cones are typically small and woody, maturing to a reddish-brown color. This subspecies is adapted to cool, moist climates and is often found in subalpine and alpine environments.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for horticultural purposes.
