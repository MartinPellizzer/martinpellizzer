Potentilla crantzii var. araratica

Potentilla crantzii var. araratica, commonly known as the Armenian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-resistant and low-maintenance plants.
