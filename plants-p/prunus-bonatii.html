Prunus bonatii

Prunus bonatii, commonly known as the Sicilian cherry or Sicilian wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible drupes that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Prunus bonatii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
