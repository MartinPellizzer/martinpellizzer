Pastinaca humilis

Pastinaca humilis, commonly known as the lesser burdock, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family (Asteraceae).

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
