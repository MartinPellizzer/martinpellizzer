Polygonum griffithii

Polygonum griffithii, commonly known as the Himalayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or red. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and the ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.

Polygonum griffithii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
