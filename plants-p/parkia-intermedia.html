Parkia intermedia

Parkia intermedia, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating. It is often found in savannas and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its wood is used locally for construction and fuel, while the plant also has traditional medicinal uses.
