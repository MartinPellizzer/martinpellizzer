Plantago chamaepsyllium

Plantago chamaepsyllium, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

The leaves and seeds of Plantago chamaepsyllium have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
