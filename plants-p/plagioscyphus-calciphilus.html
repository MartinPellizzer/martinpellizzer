Plagioscyphus calciphilus

Plagioscyphus calciphilus, commonly known as the limestone star moss, is a unique liverwort that thrives in calcareous environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is often found on exposed rock surfaces. The plant has a simple morphology with leaf-like structures that are closely arranged. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh conditions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
