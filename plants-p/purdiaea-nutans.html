Purdiaea nutans

Purdiaea nutans, commonly known as the nodding lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its drooping, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are often slender and branched.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
