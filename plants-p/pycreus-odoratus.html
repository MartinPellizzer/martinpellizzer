Pycreus odoratus

Pycreus odoratus, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy in texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a green, three-lobed berry that turns red when ripe.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
