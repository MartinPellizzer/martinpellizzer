Pyrus aucuparia var. glabra

Pyrus aucuparia var. glabra, commonly known as the white-flowered crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, tart apples that are typically not suitable for eating raw but are used in cooking and cider-making. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in horticulture.
