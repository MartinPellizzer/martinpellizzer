Potentilla pamirica var. khunjrabensis

Potentilla pamirica var. khunjrabensis, commonly known as the Khunjrab Mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas and the Pamir Mountains.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers and dark green, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a compact growth habit adapted to cold and arid conditions.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
