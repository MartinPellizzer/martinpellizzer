Palura pilosa var. pubescens

Palura pilosa var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy palura, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are covered in fine pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of white or pale pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
