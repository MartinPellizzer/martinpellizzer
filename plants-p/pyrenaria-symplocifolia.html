Pyrenaria symplocifolia

Pyrenaria symplocifolia, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
