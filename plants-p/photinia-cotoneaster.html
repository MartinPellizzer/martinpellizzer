Photinia cotoneaster

Photinia cotoneaster, commonly known as the red leaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by orange-red berries that attract birds. Its compact growth habit and adaptability to various soil types make it suitable for garden use.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its seasonal color changes and ecological benefits.
