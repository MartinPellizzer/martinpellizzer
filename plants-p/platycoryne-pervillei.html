Platycoryne pervillei

Platycoryne pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, elongated floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
