Pleurothallis octomeriantha

Pleurothallis octomeriantha, commonly known as the eight-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are usually ovate and glossy, adapting well to the humid, shaded environments of its natural habitat.

Due to its unique floral structure and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
