Polygala atropurpurea

Polygala atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of delicate, pink to purple flowers. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
