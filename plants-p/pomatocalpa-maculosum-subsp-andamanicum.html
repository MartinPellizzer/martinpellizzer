Pomatocalpa maculosum subsp. andamanicum

Pomatocalpa maculosum subsp.

andamanicum, commonly known as the Andaman spotted orchid, is a rare orchid species native to the Andaman Islands. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically pale yellow with distinctive dark markings.

The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in the undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
