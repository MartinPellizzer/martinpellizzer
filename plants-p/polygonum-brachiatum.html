Polygonum brachiatum

Polygonum brachiatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum brachiatum is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and knotweed.
