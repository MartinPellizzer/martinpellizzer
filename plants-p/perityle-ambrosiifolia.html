Perityle ambrosiifolia

Perityle ambrosiifolia, commonly known as the fragrant everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous tiny florets. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
