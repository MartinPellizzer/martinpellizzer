Piper hostmannianum var. ramiflorum

Piper hostmannianum var. ramiflorum, commonly known as the ramiflorum pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a distinct, peppery flavor, making it a unique species within the Piper genus.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
