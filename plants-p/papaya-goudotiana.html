Papaya goudotiana

Papaya goudotiana, commonly known as the Madagascar papaya, is a tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet and fibrous pulp, and it is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
