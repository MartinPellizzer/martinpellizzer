Paronychia serpyllifolia

Paronychia serpyllifolia, commonly known as mouse ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or kidney-shaped, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces tiny white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in lawns, gardens, and along paths.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent leaves and short, erect stems.
