Picroderma laotica

Picroderma laotica, commonly known as the Laotian ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its ethereal common name. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded forest environments, relying on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are small and pale, often appearing in clusters, and bloom during the late spring to early summer season.

Due to its restricted habitat and threats from deforestation, Picroderma laotica is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
