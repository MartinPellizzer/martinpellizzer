Piper multinervium var. telanum

Piper multinervium var. telanum, commonly known as the Telanum pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with multiple veins, giving it the species name *multinervium*. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and less-studied species within its genus.
