Primula bathangensis

Primula bathangensis, commonly known as the Bathang primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in spring and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of soft, fuzzy hairs.

Primula bathangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also an important species in the study of alpine flora.
