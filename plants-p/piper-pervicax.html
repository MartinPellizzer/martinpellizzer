Piper pervicax

Piper pervicax, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes, while the fruit is a small, three-lobed berry.

Piper pervicax is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
