Pittosporum tubiflorum

Pittosporum tubiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round, blue-black berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Pittosporum tubiflorum prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
