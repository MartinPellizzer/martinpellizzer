Prunus jamasakura f. discoidea

Prunus jamasakura f. discoidea, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cherry, is a unique variation of the Japanese cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and smaller, more delicate flowers compared to the typical Prunus jamasakura. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to gardens. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and seasonal blooms.

This variety is well-suited for smaller spaces and requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight for optimal growth.
