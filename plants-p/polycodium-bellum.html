Polycodium bellum

Polycodium bellum, commonly known as the bell-shaped polycodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its most distinctive feature is its bell-shaped, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polycodium bellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
