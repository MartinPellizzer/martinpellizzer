Pistacia atlantica subsp. mutica

Pistacia atlantica subsp.

mutica, commonly known as the Atlantic pistachio, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with 5 to 7 leaflets.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single edible seed. Its botanical features include thick, fissured bark and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

This subspecies is often found in dry, sunny habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
