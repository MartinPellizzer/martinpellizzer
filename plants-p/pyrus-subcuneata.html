Pyrus subcuneata

Pyrus subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern China. The plant is characterized by its deciduous habit, with simple, alternate leaves that are often narrowly elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits.

Pyrus subcuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
