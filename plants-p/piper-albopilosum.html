Piper albopilosum

Piper albopilosum, commonly known as the white-hair pepper or white-hair long pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, white pubescence. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
