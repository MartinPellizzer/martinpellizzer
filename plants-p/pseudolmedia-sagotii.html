Pseudolmedia sagotii

Pseudolmedia sagotii, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
