Piper avellanum

Piper avellanum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
