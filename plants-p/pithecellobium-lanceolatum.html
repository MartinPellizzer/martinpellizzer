Pithecellobium lanceolatum

Pithecellobium lanceolatum, commonly known as the slender pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features pinnately compound leaves and produces small, showy yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its fruits are pod-like structures containing edible seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
