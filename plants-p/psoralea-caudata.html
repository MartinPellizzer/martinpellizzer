Psoralea caudata

Psoralea caudata, commonly known as the slender psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to reddish in color. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
