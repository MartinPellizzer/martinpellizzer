Polygonum amphibium subsp. aquaticum

Polygonum amphibium subsp.

aquaticum, commonly known as the greater bladderworts, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its small, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts. These specialized organs aid in gas exchange and buoyancy, allowing the plant to thrive in freshwater environments.

It is native to temperate regions and often found in slow-moving or still waters. The plant has simple, ribbon-like leaves and produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
