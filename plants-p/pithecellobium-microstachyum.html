Pithecellobium microstachyum

Pithecellobium microstachyum, commonly known as the dwarf spondias, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, sweet fruits, making it valuable in both ecological and agricultural contexts.

Its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with bacteria enhances soil fertility, contributing to its ecological significance.
