Phrynium albovaginatum

Phrynium albovaginatum, commonly known as the white sheath plant, is a tropical perennial belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish or pale green sheath, giving it its common name. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe, typically in shades of yellow or red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions of South America and Central America.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and floral displays.
