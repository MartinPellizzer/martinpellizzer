Pilea grandifolia

Pilea grandifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its round, rosette-shaped leaves that resemble small coins, which give it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea grandifolia is a popular indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
