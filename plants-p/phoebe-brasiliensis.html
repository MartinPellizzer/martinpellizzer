Phoebe brasiliensis

Phoebe brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian phoebe, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
