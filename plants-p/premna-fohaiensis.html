Premna fohaiensis

Premna fohaiensis, commonly known as the Fohai premna, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
