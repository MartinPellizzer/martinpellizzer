Picris damascena var. diffusa

Picris damascena var. diffusa, commonly known as the Persian carpetweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine, white hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
