Polyalthia brunneifolia

Polyalthia brunneifolia, commonly known as the brown-leaf tree or Malabar tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, clustered fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
