Polygala gilletiana

Polygala gilletiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that resemble plumes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands, and it thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Polygala gilletiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
