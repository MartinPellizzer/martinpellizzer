Paspalum caespitosum var. longifolium

Paspalum caespitosum var. longifolium, commonly known as the tall muhly, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 30 cm. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically visible during the summer months. It thrives in full sun and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
