Piper subfrutescens

Piper subfrutescens, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its dark, peppery fruits that are widely used as a spice. The plant typically grows in tropical climates and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit develops in clusters and is harvested when fully mature for its aromatic and pungent flavor.
