Pilosella cymosa subsp. cymosa

Pilosella cymosa subsp.

cymosa, commonly known as the clustered mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with grayish-green, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense cymes, which bloom in early summer. Native to Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a common weed in some regions.
