Physalis angulata var. capsicifolia

Physalis angulata var. capsicifolia, commonly known as the winged lantern or red currant.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The plant features distinctive yellow-orange flowers that develop into inflated, papery fruit capsules. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
