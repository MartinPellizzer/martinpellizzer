Phyteuma persicifolium var. latifolium

Phyteuma persicifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf phyteuma, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are broad and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
