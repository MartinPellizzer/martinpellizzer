Podocarpus hypophyllus

Podocarpus hypophyllus, commonly known as the Indian cedar or white cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The leaves are typically scale-like and tightly pressed against the branches, giving the tree a compact appearance. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber.

This species is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
