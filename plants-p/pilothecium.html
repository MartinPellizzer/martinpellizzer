Pilothecium

Pilothecium, commonly known as the "bottlebrush plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which resembles a bottlebrush due to its dense clusters of small, red or yellow flowers. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their vibrant floral displays.
