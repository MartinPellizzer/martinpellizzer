Panicum difforme

Panicum difforme, commonly known as smooth lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open areas such as prairies, fields, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, hairless stems and produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads that are often mistaken for wheat. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it blend into its environment.

Panicum difforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.
