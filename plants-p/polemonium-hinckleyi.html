Polemonium hinckleyi

Polemonium hinckleyi, commonly known as Hinckley's Jacob's ladder, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its cascading clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a stair-step pattern. It thrives in partial to full shade and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Polemonium hinckleyi is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
