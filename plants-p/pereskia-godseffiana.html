Pereskia godseffiana

Pereskia godseffiana, commonly known as the sacred cactus of the Andes, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered with short, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate beauty to its rugged appearance. Unlike typical cacti, it has leaves that are more prominent, especially during its juvenile stage.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
