Peperomia guildingiana

Peperomia guildingiana, commonly known as the Emerald Gem Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture, often adorned with subtle variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia guildingiana is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
