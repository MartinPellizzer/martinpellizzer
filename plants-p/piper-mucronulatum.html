Piper mucronulatum

Piper mucronulatum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus within the family Piperaceae, which includes several species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, known as a peppercorn, is a small, dark-colored berry that is used as a spice.

The botanical features of Piper mucronulatum make it an important crop in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
