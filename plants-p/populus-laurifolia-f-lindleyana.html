Populus laurifolia f. lindleyana

Populus laurifolia f. lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's laurel poplar, is a cultivar of the poplar tree characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It features broad, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant is known for its compact growth habit, making it suitable for small gardens or as a decorative specimen. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
