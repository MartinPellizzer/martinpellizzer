Pseudomorus brunoniana

Pseudomorus brunoniana, commonly known as the Brunon's false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a rosette-forming growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

Pseudomorus brunoniana is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
