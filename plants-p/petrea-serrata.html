Petrea serrata

Petrea serrata, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces showy, spire-like clusters of purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Petrea serrata is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
