Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. fulvescens

Pimenta pseudocaryophyllus var. fulvescens, commonly known as the red pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically used in culinary applications due to their spicy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cooking.
