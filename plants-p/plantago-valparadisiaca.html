Plantago valparadisiaca

Plantago valparadisiaca, commonly known as the Valparaiso plant or the giant plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fibrous roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
