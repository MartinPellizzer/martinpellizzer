Podostemum schenckii

Podostemum schenckii, commonly known as the African water primrose, is a unique aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are often clustered at the water's surface. The plant has specialized roots that anchor it to submerged rocks or other substrates, allowing it to thrive in fast-flowing streams. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, emerging from the water surface, and it produces capsule fruits that disperse its seeds.

Podostemum schenckii plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic organisms and contributing to water quality maintenance.
