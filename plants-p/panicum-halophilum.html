Panicum halophilum

Panicum halophilum, commonly known as saltblow, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in saline and arid environments, making it well adapted to coastal dunes and salt marshes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine layer of salt, which helps in reducing water loss.

Panicum halophilum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
