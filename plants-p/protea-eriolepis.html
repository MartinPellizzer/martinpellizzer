Protea eriolepis

Protea eriolepis, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces of South Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.

Protea eriolepis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and birds.
