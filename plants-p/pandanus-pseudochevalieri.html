Pandanus pseudochevalieri

Pandanus pseudochevalieri, commonly known as the broad-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that grow in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found near coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and versatility make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
