Philadelphus purpurascens var. venustus

Philadelphus purpurascens var. venustus, commonly known as the showy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it valuable for both garden aesthetics and ecological habitats.
