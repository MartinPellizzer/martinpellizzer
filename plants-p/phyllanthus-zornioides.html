Phyllanthus zornioides

Phyllanthus zornioides, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features small, clustered leaves with a distinctive waxy texture and a pale green to yellowish color. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
