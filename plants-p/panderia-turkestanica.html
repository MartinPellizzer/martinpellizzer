Panderia turkestanica

Panderia turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan panderia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Anacardiaceae.

It is native to Central Asia, including regions of Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and Tajikistan. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces edible berries that are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
