Prunus cerasifera f. atropurpurea

Prunus cerasifera f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple foliage that turns red in the fall, making it a popular choice for landscaping. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, dark red cherries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and urban environments.
