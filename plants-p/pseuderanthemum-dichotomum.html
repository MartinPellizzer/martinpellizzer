Pseuderanthemum dichotomum

Pseuderanthemum dichotomum, commonly known as the dichotomous pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in warm climates as a decorative plant.
