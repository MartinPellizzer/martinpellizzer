Pinus nigra subsp. fenzleyi

Pinus nigra subsp.

fenzleyi, commonly known as the Fenzley's black pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States, particularly in California and Nevada. It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in clusters of five.

The cones are woody and elongated, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. This subspecies is characterized by its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils and its resistance to drought.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
