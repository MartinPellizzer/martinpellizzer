Polycarpaea violacea

Polycarpaea violacea, commonly known as the violet-headed grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, violet-colored inflorescences that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Polycarpaea violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
