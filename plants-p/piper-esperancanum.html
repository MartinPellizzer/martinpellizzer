Piper esperancanum

Piper esperancanum, commonly known as the Esperanza pepper or golden pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellow to orange berries that are edible and have a mild, peppery flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
