Potentilla pusilla var. lancifolia

Potentilla pusilla var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically features small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
