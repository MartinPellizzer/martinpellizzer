Persicaria paludicola

Persicaria paludicola, commonly known as swamp knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are often reddish or purplish and can become woody with age.

Persicaria paludicola is adapted to saturated soil conditions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
