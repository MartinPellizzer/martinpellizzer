Pithecellobium conzattii

Pithecellobium conzattii, commonly known as the Mexican burr acacia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces a unique, spiny fruit that can persist on the plant for an extended period.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in agroforestry systems.
