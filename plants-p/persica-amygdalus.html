Persica amygdalus

Persica amygdalus, commonly known as the almond tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, which are followed by the development of its characteristic fruit, the almond. The fruit has a hard shell enclosing a single seed, which is the part used for culinary and commercial purposes. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its role in agriculture, particularly for the production of almonds and almond oil.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
