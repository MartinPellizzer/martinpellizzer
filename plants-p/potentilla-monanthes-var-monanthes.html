Potentilla monanthes var. monanthes

Potentilla monanthes var. monanthes, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has wiry, branched stems and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
