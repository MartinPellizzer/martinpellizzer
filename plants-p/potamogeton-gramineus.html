Potamogeton gramineus

Potamogeton gramineus, commonly known as narrow-leaf pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 mm wide. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Potamogeton gramineus plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
