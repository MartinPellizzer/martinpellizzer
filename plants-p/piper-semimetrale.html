Piper semimetrale

Piper semimetrale, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shaded environments.
