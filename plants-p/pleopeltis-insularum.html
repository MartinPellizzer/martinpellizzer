Pleopeltis insularum

Pleopeltis insularum, commonly known as the Island Brake, is a species of fern native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a compact, cushion-like structure. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are dark green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive veining pattern.

Pleopeltis insularum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
