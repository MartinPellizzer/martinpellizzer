Pyrus fontanesia

Pyrus fontanesia, commonly known as the Fontanesia pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been cultivated for its unique appearance and potential medicinal properties.
