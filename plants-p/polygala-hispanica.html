Polygala hispanica

Polygala hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Polygala hispanica is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
