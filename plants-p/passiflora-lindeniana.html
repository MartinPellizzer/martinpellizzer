Passiflora lindeniana

Passiflora lindeniana, commonly known as the Lindley's passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, often used in juices and desserts. Its foliage consists of large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

Passiflora lindeniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
