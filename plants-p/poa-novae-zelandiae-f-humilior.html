Poa novae-zelandiae f. humilior

Poa novae-zelandiae f. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf New Zealand grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It typically forms dense, mat-like tufts and has fine, slender leaves that are often covered in a soft, hairy layer. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
