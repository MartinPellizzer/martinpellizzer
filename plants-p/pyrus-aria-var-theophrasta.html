Pyrus aria var. theophrasta

Pyrus aria var. theophrasta, commonly known as the Theophrastus pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its ornamental value and edible fruit. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a smooth texture, while the flowers are white and clustered in panicles. The fruit is usually small, sweet, and has a yellowish skin.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and culinary uses.
