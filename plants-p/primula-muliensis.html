Primula muliensis

Primula muliensis, commonly known as the Yunnan primrose, is a flowering plant native to China, particularly in the Yunnan Province.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is herbaceous, with a short stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
