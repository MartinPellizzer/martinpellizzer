Piper ascendentispicum

Piper ascendentispicum, commonly known as the ascending-branch pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with elongated stems that ascend through a system of aerial roots. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces tiny, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
