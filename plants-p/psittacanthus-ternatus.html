Psittacanthus ternatus

Psittacanthus ternatus, commonly known as the golden torch fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bright yellow fronds that resemble a torch. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its fronds are pinnately compound, with a smooth, glossy surface and a striking coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
