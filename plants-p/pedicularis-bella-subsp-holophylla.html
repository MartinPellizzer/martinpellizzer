Pedicularis bella subsp. holophylla

Pedicularis bella subsp.

holophylla, commonly known as the golden monkey orchid, is a member of the Orobanchaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. This semi-epiphytic plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling the fur of a monkey, hence its common name.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, making it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
