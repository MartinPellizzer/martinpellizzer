Psila cinnamomifolia

Psila cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamonwood tree, is a tropical evergreen belonging to the Lauraceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark that resembles cinnamon, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by dark blue-black berries.

The tree is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
