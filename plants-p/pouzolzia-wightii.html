Pouzolzia wightii

Pouzolzia wightii, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
