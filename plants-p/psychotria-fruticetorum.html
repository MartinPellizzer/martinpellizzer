Psychotria fruticetorum

Psychotria fruticetorum, commonly known as the "blue hibiscus," is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery blue or grayish coating. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
