Pentaraphia catalpiflora

Pentaraphia catalpiflora, commonly known as the catalpa bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while its stems are often hairy.

The catalpa bellflower is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
