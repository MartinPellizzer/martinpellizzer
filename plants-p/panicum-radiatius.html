Panicum radiatius

Panicum radiatius, commonly known as the radiant grass, is a warm-season grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, spreading stems with dense clusters of small, fluffy flowers that give it a distinctive, radiating appearance. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. Its foliage is typically green, with a silver-blue tinge under certain conditions, adding visual interest to landscapes.

Panicum radiatius is often used in gardens and as a cover crop due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
