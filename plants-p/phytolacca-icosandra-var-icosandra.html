Phytolacca icosandra var. icosandra

Phytolacca icosandra var. icosandra, commonly known as the Chinese pokeweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, upright stems that bear alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of light conditions.
