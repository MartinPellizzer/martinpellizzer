Prunus acuminata f. wallichii

Prunus acuminata f. wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmir cherry or Himalayan cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but not widely cultivated.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
