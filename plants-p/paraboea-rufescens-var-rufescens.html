Paraboea rufescens var. rufescens

Paraboea rufescens var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-flowered paraboea, is a flowering plant native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, red or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
