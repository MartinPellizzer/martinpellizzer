Persea kurzii

Persea kurzii, commonly known as the Hawaiian white ash or kurīkī, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its evergreen, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, oval drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when ripe.

Persea kurzii is often found growing in moist, shaded areas of tropical rainforests.
