Pelargonium × pubescens

Pelargonium × pubescens, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
