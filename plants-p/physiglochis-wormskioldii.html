Physiglochis wormskioldii

Physiglochis wormskioldii, commonly known as the dwarf arrowleaf.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida. The plant has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along the edges of wetlands and swamps.

Physiglochis wormskioldii is known for its delicate, small white flowers that bloom in late spring.
