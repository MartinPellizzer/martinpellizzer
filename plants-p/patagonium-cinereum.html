Patagonium cinereum

Patagonium cinereum, commonly known as the gray patagonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine soils. Its leaves are covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Patagonium cinereum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
