Phoradendron moringicola

Phoradendron moringicola, commonly known as the Moringa mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus *Moringa*.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while also obtaining some through photosynthesis using its own leaves. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are small and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This species is native to parts of Africa and is often found in dry, tropical environments.
