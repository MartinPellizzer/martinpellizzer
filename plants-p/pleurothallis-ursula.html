Pleurothallis ursula

Pleurothallis ursula, commonly known as the Ursula's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to the humid, shaded environments where it grows.

Pleurothallis ursula is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
