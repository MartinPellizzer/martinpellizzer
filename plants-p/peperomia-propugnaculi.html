Peperomia propugnaculi

Peperomia propugnaculi, commonly known as the shield fern peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, waxy foliage with a distinctive shield-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant has small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia propugnaculi is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
