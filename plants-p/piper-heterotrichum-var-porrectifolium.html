Piper heterotrichum var. porrectifolium

Piper heterotrichum var. porrectifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically broad and elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in indigenous communities.
