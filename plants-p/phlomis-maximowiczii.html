Phlomis maximowiczii

Phlomis maximowiczii, commonly known as the Russian mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis maximowiczii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
