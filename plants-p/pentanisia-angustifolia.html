Pentanisia angustifolia

Pentanisia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf pentanisia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and canyon walls. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in loose panicles, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
