Pyrus amygdaliformis var. verrucosa

Pyrus amygdaliformis var. verrucosa, commonly known as the rough pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive rough, warty texture on the fruit's surface.

This variety is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a serrated edge, and the tree typically grows to a moderate height. The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters, and the fruit matures in late summer to early autumn.

It is a hardy plant that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
