Phegopteris honolulensis

Phegopteris honolulensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian water fern, is a rare and endemic fern species found only on the island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically less than a foot in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in wetlands or along streams. Its spore-producing structures are located on the undersides of its fronds, and it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
