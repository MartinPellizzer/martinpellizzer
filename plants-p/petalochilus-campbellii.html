Petalochilus campbellii

Petalochilus campbellii, commonly known as Campbell's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically blooms during the wet season, and its inflorescence consists of a single raceme of flowers. It prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil and partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
