Pomaderris hirta

Pomaderris hirta, commonly known as the silky pomaderris, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Pomaderris hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
