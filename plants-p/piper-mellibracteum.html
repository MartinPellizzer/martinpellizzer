Piper mellibracteum

Piper mellibracteum, commonly known as the African pepper or black pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a drupe, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
