Pentaptera angustifolia

Pentaptera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pentaptera, is a herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Pentaptera angustifolia is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
