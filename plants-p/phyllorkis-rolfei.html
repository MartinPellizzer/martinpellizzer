Phyllorkis rolfei

Phyllorkis rolfei, commonly known as the Rolf's flax lily, is a unique member of the Asphodelaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, grass-like foliage and tall, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and Mediterranean climates.
