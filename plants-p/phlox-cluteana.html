Phlox cluteana

Phlox cluteana, commonly known as the purple phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems.

Phlox cluteana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.
