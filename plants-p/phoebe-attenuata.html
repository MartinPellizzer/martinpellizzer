Phoebe attenuata

Phoebe attenuata, commonly known as the slender phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

Phoebe attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
