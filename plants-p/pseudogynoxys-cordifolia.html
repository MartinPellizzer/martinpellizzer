Pseudogynoxys cordifolia

Pseudogynoxys cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pseudogynoxys, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive, heart-shaped appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a yellow or orange hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
