Pedicularis anthemifolia subsp. anthemifolia

Pedicularis anthemifolia subsp.

anthemifolia, commonly known as the yellow monkey orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, often in rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
