Potentilla galantha

Potentilla galantha, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form. Its flowers are small, yellow, and appear in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla galantha thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and dry grasslands.
