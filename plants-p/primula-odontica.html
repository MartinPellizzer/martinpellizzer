Primula odontica

Primula odontica, commonly known as the toothed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring, making it a valuable addition to alpine gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice among horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
