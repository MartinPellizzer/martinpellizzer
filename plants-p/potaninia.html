Potaninia

Potaninia, commonly known as the "flowering nightshade," is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to South America, particularly Brazil. These plants are typically shrubs or small trees, characterized by their showy, often brightly colored flowers. The leaves are usually simple and alternate, with a rough texture.

Potaninia species are known for their ornamental value and are sometimes cultivated for their striking floral displays.
