Polygala chamaebuxus subsp. vayredae

Polygala chamaebuxus subsp.

vayredae, commonly known as the Vayreda's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
