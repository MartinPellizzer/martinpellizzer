Piper excelsum

Piper excelsum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
