Phlomis leonitis

Phlomis leonitis, commonly known as the lion's thorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant produces tall, branching stems covered in stiff, bristly hairs, which give it its common name. Its showy, yellow flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
