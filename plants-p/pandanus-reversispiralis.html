Pandanus reversispiralis

Pandanus reversispiralis, commonly known as the spiral pandanus, is a distinctive tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that give it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to its durability.

The flowers, which are small and clustered, are typically borne in inflorescences at the base of the plant.
