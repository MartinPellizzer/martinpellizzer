Paphia kudukii

Paphia kudukii, commonly known as the Kudukii orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically blooms during the monsoon season, adding to its ecological significance in its native habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil.

Paphia kudukii is often found in moist, shaded environments, such as under the canopy of forests.
