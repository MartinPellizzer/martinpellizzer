Phalangium rostratum

Phalangium rostratum, commonly known as the rostrated centipede, is a species of centipede belonging to the family Scolopendridae.

It is characterized by its elongated body covered in segmented exoskeleton, with numerous pairs of jointed legs. The species is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, forested environments. Its diet primarily consists of other invertebrates, making it an important predator in its ecosystem.

The name "rostratum" refers to the distinctive, elongated head structure that resembles a beak.
