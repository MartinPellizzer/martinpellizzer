Potamogeton siculus proles subflavus

Potamogeton siculus proles subflavus, commonly known as the Sicilian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in southern Europe.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green to yellow-green in color, with small, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, elongated fruits. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters and plays a role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients.

However, it can become invasive in some regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
