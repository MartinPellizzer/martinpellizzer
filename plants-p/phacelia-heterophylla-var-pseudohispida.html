Phacelia heterophylla var. pseudohispida

Phacelia heterophylla var. pseudohispida, commonly known as the Sierra phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, ranging from ovate to lanceolate, and its clusters of blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, often in mountainous regions. It has a short growing season and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
