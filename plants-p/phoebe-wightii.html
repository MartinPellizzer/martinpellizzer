Phoebe wightii

Phoebe wightii, commonly known as the Wight's phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically glossy and elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue or black berries.

Phoebe wightii is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.
