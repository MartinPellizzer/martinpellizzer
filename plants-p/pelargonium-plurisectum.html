Pelargonium plurisectum

Pelargonium plurisectum, commonly known as the multilobe geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Pelargonium plurisectum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
