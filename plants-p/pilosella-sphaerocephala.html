Pilosella sphaerocephala

Pilosella sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features dense, white to pale yellow ray florets surrounded by a tight cluster of yellow disk florets, giving it a globe-like appearance. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its common name.

Pilosella sphaerocephala is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
