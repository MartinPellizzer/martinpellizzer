Papaver gaubae

Papaver gaubae, commonly known as the African poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red petals that resemble those of the opium poppy. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in treating ailments.
