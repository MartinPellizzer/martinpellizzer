Pimpinella diversifolia var. divisa

Pimpinella diversifolia var. divisa, commonly known as the divided cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and carrots.
