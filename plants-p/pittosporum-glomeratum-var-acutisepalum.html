Pittosporum glomeratum var. acutisepalum

Pittosporum glomeratum var. acutisepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are typically followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture.
