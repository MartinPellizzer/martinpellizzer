Potamogeton friesii

Potamogeton friesii, commonly known as the Fries' pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are usually smooth and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters at the base of the stems.

Potamogeton friesii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
