Poa concinna

Poa concinna, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks.

Poa concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting pollinators.
