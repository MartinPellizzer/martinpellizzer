Poa × sclerocalamos

Poa × sclerocalamos, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a hybrid species belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with dense, fibrous rhizomes that form compact clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
