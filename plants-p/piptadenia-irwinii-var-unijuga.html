Piptadenia irwinii var. unijuga

Piptadenia irwinii var. unijuga, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has a smooth, greyish-brown bark and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
