Potamophila leersioides

Potamophila leersioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved water plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette, with a smooth, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers in dense clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshes, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Potamophila leersioides plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
