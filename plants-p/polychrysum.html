Polychrysum

Polychrysum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland and meadow habitats. The plant is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow or golden flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically oval and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Polychrysum is valued in horticulture for its vibrant color and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
