Potentilla anserina subvar. nuda

Potentilla anserina subvar. nuda, commonly known as the naked sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant has a mat-forming habit with slender, hairy stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Its subspecies nuda distinguishes itself by lacking the stipules found in the typical variety.
