Phaius pauciflorus var. pallidus

Phaius pauciflorus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale-flowered marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy inflorescence with pale yellow to white flowers that have a distinctive, intricate pattern. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in swampy areas with rich organic soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its elegant, delicate blooms.
