Plantago humboldtiana

Plantago humboldtiana, commonly known as the Humboldt's plantain, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying plant resilience in high-altitude environments.
