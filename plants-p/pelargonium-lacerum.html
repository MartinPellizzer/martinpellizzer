Pelargonium lacerum

Pelargonium lacerum, commonly known as the fringed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a frayed appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, Pelargonium lacerum is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
