Piper taperinhanum

Piper taperinhanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by small, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
