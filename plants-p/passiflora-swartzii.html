Passiflora swartzii

Passiflora swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, violet, and white petals, along with a crown of red filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora swartzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
