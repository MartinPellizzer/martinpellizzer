Phaseolus fuscus

Phaseolus fuscus, commonly known as the dark runner bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with small, hairy pods that contain dark brown seeds. The plant is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and exhibits typical features of legumes, including nitrogen-fixing nodules on its roots.

Its morphology and growth patterns make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
