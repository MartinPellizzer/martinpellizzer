Potentilla fragiformis

Potentilla fragiformis, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers and its bushy, low-growing habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to alpine and rocky environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Potentilla fragiformis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
