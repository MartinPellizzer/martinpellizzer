Prosopis alpataco var. lamaro

Prosopis alpataco var. lamaro, commonly known as the Lamaro mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are an important food source for various wildlife species.
