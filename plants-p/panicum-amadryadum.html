Panicum amadryadum

Panicum amadryadum, commonly known as the golden panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas effectively.

The botanical features of Panicum amadryadum make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
