Pyrus insueta f. longepedunculata

Pyrus insueta f. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate whitebeam, is a botanical variant of the whitebeam tree.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the inflorescences. The plant features small, white flowers that appear in clusters, typical of the Rosaceae family. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin and a slightly hairy surface.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticultural settings for its unique floral structure.
