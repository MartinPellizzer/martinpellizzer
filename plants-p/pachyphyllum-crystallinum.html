Pachyphyllum crystallinum

Pachyphyllum crystallinum, commonly known as the crystalline stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which reduces water loss and reflects sunlight. Its flowers are small and typically yellow or green, appearing in clusters during the growing season.

This plant is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and visually striking addition to rock gardens or xeriscaped landscapes.
