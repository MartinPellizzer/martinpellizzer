Plectranthus bipinnatus

Plectranthus bipinnatus, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its scent is reminiscent of mint, which makes it useful in aromatherapy and as a natural insect repellent.
