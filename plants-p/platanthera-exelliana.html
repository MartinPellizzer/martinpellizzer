Platanthera exelliana

Platanthera exelliana, commonly known as the Exell's lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces small, rounded pseudobulbs.

Platanthera exelliana is an indicator species for high-quality, undisturbed habitats due to its specific ecological requirements.
