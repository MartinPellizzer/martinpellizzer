Prunus caspica

Prunus caspica, commonly known as the Caspian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

The Caspian cherry is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
