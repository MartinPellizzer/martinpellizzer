Passiflora cincinnata var. imbricata

Passiflora cincinnata var. imbricata, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a unique, intricate corona. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, attracting a variety of pollinators.
