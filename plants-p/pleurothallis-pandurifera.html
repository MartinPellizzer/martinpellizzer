Pleurothallis pandurifera

Pleurothallis pandurifera, commonly known as the pandurifera orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its floral structure is typical of the Orchidaceae family, with showy sepals and petals.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
