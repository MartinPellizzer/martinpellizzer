Plantago arborescens subsp. costae

Plantago arborescens subsp.

costae, commonly known as the serrated plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are typically borne on tall, slender inflorescences. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and along roadsides.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
