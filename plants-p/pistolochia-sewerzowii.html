Pistolochia sewerzowii

Pistolochia sewerzowii, commonly known as the yellow trumpet vine, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America, characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
