Potentilla argentina

Potentilla argentina, commonly known as the Argentine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. Its leaves are compound, with several leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
