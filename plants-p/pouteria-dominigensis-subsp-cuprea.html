Pouteria dominigensis subsp. cuprea

Pouteria dominigensis subsp.

cuprea, commonly known as the copperleaf or red-leaf pouteria, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, orange-red berries that are a source of nutrition for various wildlife.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its potential for use in traditional medicine and horticulture.
