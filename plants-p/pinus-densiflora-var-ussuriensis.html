Pinus densiflora var. ussuriensis

Pinus densiflora var. ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri pine, is a variant of the Japanese white pine.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia, including parts of Russia, Korea, and China. The tree features needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more densely packed compared to the standard Pinus densiflora. Its cones are woody and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
