Pteris × calcarea

Pteris × calcarea, commonly known as the calcarea fern, is a hybrid species within the water fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a pale underside. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds.

Pteris × calcarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in humid conditions.
