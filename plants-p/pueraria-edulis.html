Pueraria edulis

Pueraria edulis, commonly known as the edible cowpea or stink bean, is a leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and large, showy flowers that range from purple to white. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Its roots are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pueraria edulis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.
