Psoralea melanocarpa

Psoralea melanocarpa, commonly known as the blackfruit psoralea, is a member of the legume family Fabaceae.

It is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to Australia, characterized by its slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive, dark purple to black pods. Its seeds are encased in hard, woody pods that remain on the plant for an extended period.

Psoralea melanocarpa is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
