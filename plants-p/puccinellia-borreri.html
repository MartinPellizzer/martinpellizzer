Puccinellia borreri

Puccinellia borreri, commonly known as the Borreri's glassworts, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.

Puccinellia borreri is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.
