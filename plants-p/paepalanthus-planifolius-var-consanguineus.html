Paepalanthus planifolius var. consanguineus

Paepalanthus planifolius var. consanguineus, commonly known as the purple-leafed puya, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy, purple-tinged leaves that form a central tank to collect rainwater. The plant produces striking inflorescences with long, spadices bearing showy, violet or purple flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
