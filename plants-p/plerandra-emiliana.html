Plerandra emiliana

Plerandra emiliana, commonly known as the Emilia's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence composed of a spadix and a spathe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are typically greenish-white, with a showy spathe that attracts pollinators.

Plerandra emiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
