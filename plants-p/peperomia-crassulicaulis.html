Peperomia crassulicaulis

Peperomia crassulicaulis, commonly known as the thick-leaved peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically thick and succulent, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually in shades of green or red, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
