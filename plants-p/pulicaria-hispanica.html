Pulicaria hispanica

Pulicaria hispanica, commonly known as Spanish tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
