Piper brownsbergense

Piper brownsbergense, commonly known as the Brown's berg pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, narrow berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a mild, peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
