Porphyrocoma longifolia

Porphyrocoma longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf porphyrocoma, is a species of flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and clusters of small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
