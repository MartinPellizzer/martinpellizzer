Pithecellobium hernandezii

Pithecellobium hernandezii, commonly known as the Honduras velvet bean, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in agricultural practices.
