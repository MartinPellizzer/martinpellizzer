Pandorea linearis

Pandorea linearis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or white. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a silvery underside, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
