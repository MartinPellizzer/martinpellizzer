Pagamea capitata subsp. caudata

Pagamea capitata subsp.

caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed pagamea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescence that gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of yellow or orange.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
