Poa falcata

Poa falcata, commonly known as the sickle grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves that curve sharply at the tip, giving it a distinctive sickle shape. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Poa falcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
