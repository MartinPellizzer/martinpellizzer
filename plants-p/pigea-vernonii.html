Pigea vernonii

Pigea vernonii, commonly known as the Vernon's pigeonwings, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Pigea vernonii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
