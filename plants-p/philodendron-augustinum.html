Philodendron augustinum

Philodendron augustinum, commonly known as the Augustine philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
