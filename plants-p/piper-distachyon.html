Piper distachyon

Piper distachyon, commonly known as switchgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, leafy stems and panicle-shaped inflorescences that produce small, greenish flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and high biomass yield, making it a valuable species for biofuel production. Its deep root system helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.

Piper distachyon is also studied for its potential in sustainable agriculture and ecological restoration projects.
