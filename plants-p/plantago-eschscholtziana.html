Plantago eschscholtziana

Plantago eschscholtziana, commonly known as the California mouse ear, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
