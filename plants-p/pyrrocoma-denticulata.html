Pyrrocoma denticulata

Pyrrocoma denticulata, commonly known as the toothed pyrrocoma, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, denticulate (toothed) leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed beyond its native habitat.
