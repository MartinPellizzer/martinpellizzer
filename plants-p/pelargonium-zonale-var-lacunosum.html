Pelargonium zonale var. lacunosum

Pelargonium zonale var. lacunosum, commonly known as the laceleaf geranium, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and pollinator-friendly landscapes.
