Pentas bussei f. glabra

Pentas bussei f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth pentas, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, showy clusters of five-petaled flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with a smooth, waxy surface. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and continuous blooming make it suitable for containers, hanging baskets, and garden borders.
