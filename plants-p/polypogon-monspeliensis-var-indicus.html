Polypogon monspeliensis var. indicus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. indicus, commonly known as Indian brome, is a grass species native to southern Asia.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless stems. The leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
