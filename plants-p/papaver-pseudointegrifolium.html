Papaver pseudointegrifolium

Papaver pseudointegrifolium, commonly known as the Sierra larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, somewhat spiny appearance. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
