Pleurothallis unguicallosa

Pleurothallis unguicallosa, commonly known as the "Red-veined Orchid," is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking floral display. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive red veining on the petals, which sets it apart from other members of the genus. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
