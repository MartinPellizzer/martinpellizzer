Peperomia perlongipes

Peperomia perlongipes, commonly known as the long-footed peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive elongated base that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its low maintenance and attractive foliage make it a versatile addition to both indoor and outdoor spaces.
