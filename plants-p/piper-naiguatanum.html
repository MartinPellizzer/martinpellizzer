Piper naiguatanum

Piper naiguatanum, commonly known as the Guiana pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces small, red berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper naiguatanum make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
