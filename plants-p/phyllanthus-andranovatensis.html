Phyllanthus andranovatensis

Phyllanthus andranovatensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gooseberry, is a small shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
