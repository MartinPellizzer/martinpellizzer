Pelargonium moniliforme

Pelargonium moniliforme, commonly known as the chain flower or necklace plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that form a cylindrical shape, resembling a necklace. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
