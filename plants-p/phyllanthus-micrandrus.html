Phyllanthus micrandrus

Phyllanthus micrandrus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
