Pimela kitengo

Pimela kitengo, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and bipinnate leaves. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by legume pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
