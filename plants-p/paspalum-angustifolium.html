Paspalum angustifolium

Paspalum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf buffalograss, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit. This plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and prairies. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Paspalum angustifolium is valued for its drought tolerance and is used in ecological restoration projects.
