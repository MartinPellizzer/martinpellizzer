Polygonum claviculatum

Polygonum claviculatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red.

Polygonum claviculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wet conditions.
