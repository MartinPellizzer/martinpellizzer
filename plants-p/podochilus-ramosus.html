Podochilus ramosus

Podochilus ramosus, commonly known as the hairy bamboo or bamboo orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and birds.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
