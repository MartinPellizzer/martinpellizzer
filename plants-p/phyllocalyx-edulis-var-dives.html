Phyllocalyx edulis var. dives

Phyllocalyx edulis var. dives, commonly known as the red-flowered edible phyllocalyx, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, edible fruits. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appearance and its edible yield.
