Polylepis frontinensis

Polylepis frontinensis, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a member of the Rosaceae family and is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. This tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Polylepis frontinensis plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by supporting a variety of wildlife.
