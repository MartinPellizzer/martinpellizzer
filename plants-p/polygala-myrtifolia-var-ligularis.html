Polygala myrtifolia var. ligularis

Polygala myrtifolia var. ligularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, giving the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
