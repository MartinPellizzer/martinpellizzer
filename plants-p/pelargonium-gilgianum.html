Pelargonium gilgianum

Pelargonium gilgianum, commonly known as the Gilgilian geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
