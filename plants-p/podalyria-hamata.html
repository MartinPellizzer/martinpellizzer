Podalyria hamata

Podalyria hamata, commonly known as the hooked podalyria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that becomes hooked at the ends, aiding in seed dispersal by attaching to animal fur.

Podalyria hamata is often found in rocky or coastal habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
