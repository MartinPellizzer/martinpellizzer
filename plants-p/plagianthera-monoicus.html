Plagianthera monoicus

Plagianthera monoicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a small flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland desert environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

Plagianthera monoicus is known for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
