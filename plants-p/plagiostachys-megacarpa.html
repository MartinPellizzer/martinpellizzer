Plagiostachys megacarpa

Plagiostachys megacarpa, commonly known as the giant bamboo, is a species of grass in the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, boat-shaped leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in horticulture.
