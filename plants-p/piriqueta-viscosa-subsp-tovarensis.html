Piriqueta viscosa subsp. tovarensis

Piriqueta viscosa subsp.

tovarensis, commonly known as the sticky piriqueta, is a perennial shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves that secrete a viscous resin, which gives the plant its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous areas. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in terminal inflorescences.

It is adapted to the arid and sunny conditions of its native habitat.
