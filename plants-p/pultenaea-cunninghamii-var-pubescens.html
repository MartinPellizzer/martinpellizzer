Pultenaea cunninghamii var. pubescens

Pultenaea cunninghamii var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pultenaea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
