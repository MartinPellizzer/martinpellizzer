Parastemon urophyllus

Parastemon urophyllus, commonly known as the woollyleaf false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
