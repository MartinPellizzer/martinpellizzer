Prunus cyanosphaera

Prunus cyanosphaera, commonly known as the blue-berried cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is characterized by its striking blue-black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in early spring.

The berries are not only ornamental but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.
