Pedilanthus jamaicensis

Pedilanthus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests, characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. It is also known for its toxic properties, as it contains various alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its medicinal and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated, though caution is required due to its toxicity.
