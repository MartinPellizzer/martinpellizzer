Pteroceras viridiflorum

Pteroceras viridiflorum, commonly known as the greenflower tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of central Asia.

It features small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Pteroceras viridiflorum plays a vital role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its native habitat.
