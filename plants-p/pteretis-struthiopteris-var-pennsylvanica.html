Pteretis struthiopteris var. pennsylvanica

Pteretis struthiopteris var. pennsylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania fern, is a variant of the common wood fern.

It is characterized by its bipinnate fronds that are divided into small, lance-shaped segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizome is stout and covered with a brown, fibrous sheath.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
