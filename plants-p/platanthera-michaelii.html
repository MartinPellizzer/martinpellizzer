Platanthera michaelii

Platanthera michaelii, commonly known as the Michael's lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and long, flowering spikes that produce numerous small, white to pale green flowers. The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is typically found in shaded, forested areas. It is considered endangered due to habitat loss and over-collection.

The species is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native range.
