Potentilla hirta proles angustifolia

Potentilla hirta proles angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, angular stems covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and lance-shaped, with a hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
