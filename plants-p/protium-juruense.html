Protium juruense

Protium juruense, commonly known as the Brazilian beech, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Sapindaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing single seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays a vital role in the ecological dynamics of the rainforest.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also of interest for conservation efforts.
