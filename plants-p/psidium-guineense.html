Psidium guineense

Psidium guineense, commonly known as the Natal guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Psidium guineense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in tropical agriculture.
