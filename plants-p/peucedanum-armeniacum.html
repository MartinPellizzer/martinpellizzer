Peucedanum armeniacum

Peucedanum armeniacum, commonly known as Persian cumin or Armenian angelica, is a tall biennial or perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its seeds are small, aromatic, and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including volatile oils and flavonoids, contributing to its medicinal properties.
