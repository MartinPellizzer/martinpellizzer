Pedicularis urceolata

Pedicularis urceolata, commonly known as the urn-shaped lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it thrives in moist, acidic soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.

Pedicularis urceolata is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
