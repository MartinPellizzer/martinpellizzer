Phaius indigofer

Phaius indigofer, commonly known as the indigo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from waterlogged soils.

The orchid's botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
