Pinus halepensis var. minor

Pinus halepensis var. minor, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a variant of the Aleppo pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a medium-sized evergreen conifer with needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are often reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a resilient and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
