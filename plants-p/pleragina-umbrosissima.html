Pleragina umbrosissima

Pleragina umbrosissima, commonly known as the shadow orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and clusters of pale purple or white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on the forest floor or on the trunks of trees. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Pleragina umbrosissima is considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
