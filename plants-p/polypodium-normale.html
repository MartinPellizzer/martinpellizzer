Polypodium normale

Polypodium normale, commonly known as the common holly fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a creeping rhizome with erect, lanceolate fronds that are often divided into pinnae. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium normale is characterized by its evergreen foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
