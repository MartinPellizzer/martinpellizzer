Phylica corifolia

Phylica corifolia, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, fluffy white flowers. The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
