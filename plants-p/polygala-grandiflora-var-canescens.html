Polygala grandiflora var. canescens

Polygala grandiflora var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers, which are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
