Polygonum bistorta var. graminifolium

Polygonum bistorta var. graminifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bistort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, adorned with alternate, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in cylindrical spikes, and are usually pink to purple in color.

The roots of this plant are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their supposed diuretic and astringent properties.
