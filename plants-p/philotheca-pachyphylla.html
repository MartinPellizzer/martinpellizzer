Philotheca pachyphylla

Philotheca pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved philotheca, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its adaptability to dry conditions makes it a resilient species in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
