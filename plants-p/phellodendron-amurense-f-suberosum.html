Phellodendron amurense f. suberosum

Phellodendron amurense f. suberosum, commonly known as the thick-barked form of Amur corktree, is a variant of the Amur corktree characterized by its thick, corky bark.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Russia. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by woody, elongated fruits.

The bark is highly valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
