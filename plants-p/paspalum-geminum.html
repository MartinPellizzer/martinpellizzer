Paspalum geminum

Paspalum geminum, commonly known as rough paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in disturbed areas, wetlands, and along roadsides.

Paspalum geminum is also valued for its use in forage and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
