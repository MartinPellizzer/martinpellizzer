Papaver rusticum

Papaver rusticum, commonly known as the field poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black center, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy. The plant grows up to 60 cm tall, with a taproot system that allows it to thrive in disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in early summer, and it is often found in fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Papaver rusticum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional remedies.
