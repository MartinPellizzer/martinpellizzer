Pelargonium × acetabulosum

Pelargonium × acetabulosum, commonly known as the African violet, is a popular ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub with a rosette of foliage, and it produces flowers on tall, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect light, making it ideal for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it a favorite among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
