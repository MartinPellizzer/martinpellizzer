Pelargonium × sulphureum

Pelargonium × sulphureum, commonly known as the golden geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it store water. Native to South Africa, this species thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure. The plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for containers and rock gardens.
