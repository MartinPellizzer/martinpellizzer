Primula rhodotricha

Primula rhodotricha, commonly known as the woolly primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens with appropriate growing conditions.
