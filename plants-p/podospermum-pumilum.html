Podospermum pumilum

Podospermum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf velvetleaf, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Podospermum pumilum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
