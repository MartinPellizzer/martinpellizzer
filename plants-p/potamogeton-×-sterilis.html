Potamogeton × sterilis

Potamogeton × sterilis, commonly known as the sterile pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs naturally in freshwater environments.

It is a member of the Potamogetonaceae family and is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant is typically found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it can form dense underwater vegetation. Due to its sterility, it does not produce viable seeds, relying instead on vegetative propagation for spread.

Its presence can influence water quality and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
