Pyrus guerreris

Pyrus guerreris, commonly known as the Guerrero pear, is a small deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by pear-shaped fruits that ripen in late summer. The fruit has a sweet and aromatic flavor, making it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate for optimal growth.
