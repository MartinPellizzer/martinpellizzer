Piper masiseanum

Piper masiseanum, commonly known as the Masisea pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often require support. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The fruit, which is a small, elongated berry, is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
