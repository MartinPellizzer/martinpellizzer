Primula truncata

Primula truncata, commonly known as the Japanese primrose or truncated primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or shaded areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking shade-tolerant ornamental plants.
