Pandanus kurzii

Pandanus kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in wet, swampy environments.

Pandanus kurzii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
