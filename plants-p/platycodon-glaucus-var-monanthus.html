Platycodon glaucus var. monanthus

Platycodon glaucus var. monanthus, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
