Potentilla chrysantha var. asiatica

Potentilla chrysantha var. asiatica, commonly known as the Asian potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, silvery hairs, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
