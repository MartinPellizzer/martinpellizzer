Piper saldanhae

Piper saldanhae, commonly known as the Saldanha pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and aromatic.

Piper saldanhae is valued for its unique flavor and potential medicinal properties.
