Pilosella glomerata subsp. nigrifontimontana

Pilosella glomerata subsp.

nigrifontimontana, commonly known as the black-font mountain pilosella, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, almost black, basal leaves, which give it its common name. It typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.
