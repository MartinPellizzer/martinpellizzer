Paspalum tinctum

Paspalum tinctum, commonly known as red paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Paspalum tinctum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
