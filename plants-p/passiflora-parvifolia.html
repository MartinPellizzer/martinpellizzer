Passiflora parvifolia

Passiflora parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers feature five sepals, five petals, and a unique reproductive structure called a torus. It produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
