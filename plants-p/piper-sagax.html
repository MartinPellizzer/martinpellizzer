Piper sagax

Piper sagax, commonly known as the wild pepper or mountain pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants known for their aromatic properties. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into peppery fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
