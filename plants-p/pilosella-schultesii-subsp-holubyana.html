Pilosella schultesii subsp. holubyana

Pilosella schultesii subsp.

holubyana, commonly known as the Holubyan's pearly everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces bracteate heads with involucres that are typically covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native range.
