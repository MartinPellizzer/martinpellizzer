Pyrus domestica piriformis

Pyrus domestica piriformis, commonly known as the common pear, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible fruit, which is typically harvested in late summer or early autumn. The fruit has a crisp texture and a sweet, sometimes tart flavor, making it a popular crop for both fresh consumption and culinary use.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities, with its attractive bark and seasonal foliage.
