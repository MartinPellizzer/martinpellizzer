Plumeria rubra f. acutifolia

Plumeria rubra f. acutifolia, commonly known as the acute-leafed frangipani, is a cultivar of the frangipani plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is a small evergreen tree or shrub, often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical and subtropical gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
