Pelargonium × mathildinae

Pelargonium × mathildinae, commonly known as the Mathildinae geranium, is a hybrid plant within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, double flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically features rounded, lobed leaves with a velvety texture. It is a compact shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Pelargonium × mathildinae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and prolonged blooming period.
