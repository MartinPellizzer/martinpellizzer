Panicum thuarii

Panicum thuarii, commonly known as the African grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It is drought-tolerant and often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Panicum thuarii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
