Phyllanthus angulatus

Phyllanthus angulatus, commonly known as the African date tree or wild date tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsules that contain numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
