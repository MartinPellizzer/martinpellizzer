Pilosella primuliformis

Pilosella primuliformis, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of individual flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are hairy and spoon-shaped, while its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and bloom in early summer.

Pilosella primuliformis is often found in high-altitude habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
