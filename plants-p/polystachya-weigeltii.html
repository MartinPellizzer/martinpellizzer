Polystachya weigeltii

Polystachya weigeltii, commonly known as the Weigelt's bamboo, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in moist, shaded environments.

Polystachya weigeltii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
