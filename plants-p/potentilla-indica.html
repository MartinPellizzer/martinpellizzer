Potentilla indica

Potentilla indica, commonly known as Indian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer months. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Potentilla indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
