Potentilla armerioides

Potentilla armerioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with wiry, arching stems that root at the nodes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, velvety texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.
