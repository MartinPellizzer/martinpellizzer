Pycreus rubidomontanus

Pycreus rubidomontanus, commonly known as the red mountain pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its robust growth habit. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
