Pelargonium coriandrifolium

Pelargonium coriandrifolium, commonly known as the coriander-leaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. The leaves are finely divided, resembling coriander leaves, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
