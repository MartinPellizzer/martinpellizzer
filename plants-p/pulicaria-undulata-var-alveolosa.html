Pulicaria undulata var. alveolosa

Pulicaria undulata var. alveolosa, commonly known as the wavy tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves with a distinctive wavy margin, and it produces yellow to orange disk flowers surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
