Paepalanthus myocephalon var. minor

Paepalanthus myocephalon var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf palm tree, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a small, tree-like plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features a single, slender trunk with a crown of narrow, feather-like leaves. Its leaves are often described as being similar to those of a palm, though it is not a true palm.

This variety is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates.
