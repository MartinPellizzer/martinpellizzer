Pseudopanax crassifolius var. pentadactylus

Pseudopanax crassifolius var. pentadactylus, commonly known as the five-finger plant, is a unique species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that resemble fingers, giving it its common name. The plant is a member of the Araucariaceae family and is known for its slow growth and longevity. It has a taproot system and can live for over a century, making it a valuable component of New Zealand's native forests.

The leaves are usually alternate and have a leathery texture, adapting to the cool, humid climate of its natural habitat.
