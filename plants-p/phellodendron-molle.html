Phellodendron molle

Phellodendron molle, commonly known as western soapberry, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
