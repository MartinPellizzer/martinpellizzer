Pittosporum baudouinii

Pittosporum baudouinii, commonly known as the silver-leaf pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage that often has a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or forested areas.

Pittosporum baudouinii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
