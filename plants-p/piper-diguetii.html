Piper diguetii

Piper diguetii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pepper or the Andean pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, red berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
