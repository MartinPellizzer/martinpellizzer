Paramelhania decaryana

Paramelhania decaryana, commonly known as the red-flowered plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is distinguished by its vibrant red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of hair.

Paramelhania decaryana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
