Patrinia pentandra

Patrinia pentandra, commonly known as five-lobed patrinia, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer.

Patrinia pentandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
