Persea effusa

Persea effusa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow-green in color, followed by orange to red fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its glossy foliage and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Persea effusa is also valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
