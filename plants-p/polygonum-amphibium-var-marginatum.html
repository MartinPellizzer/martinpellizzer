Polygonum amphibium var. marginatum

Polygonum amphibium var. marginatum, commonly known as the white waterplantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland environments in Europe and Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability to aquatic conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
