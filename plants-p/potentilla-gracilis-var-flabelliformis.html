Potentilla gracilis var. flabelliformis

Potentilla gracilis var. flabelliformis, commonly known as the flabelliform fireweed, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is well adapted to cold climates and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
