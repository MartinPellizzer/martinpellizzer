Pavonia cernua

Pavonia cernua, commonly known as the downy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems covered in soft, grayish-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia cernua thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
