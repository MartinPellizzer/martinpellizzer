Potentilla varians

Potentilla varians, commonly known as the variable cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it often grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Potentilla varians is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
