Parthenocissus himalayana var. rubrifolia

Parthenocissus himalayana var. rubrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved ivy grape, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn season. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to red or orange berries in the fall. Its tendrils allow it to climb and spread over structures or trees, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
