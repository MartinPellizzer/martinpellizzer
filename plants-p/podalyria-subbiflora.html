Podalyria subbiflora

Podalyria subbiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved podalyria, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features trifoliate leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Podalyria subbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
