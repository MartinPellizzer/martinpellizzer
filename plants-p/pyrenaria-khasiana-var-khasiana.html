Pyrenaria khasiana var. khasiana

Pyrenaria khasiana var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
