Pithecellobium cognatum

Pithecellobium cognatum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its small, edible pods contain seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and reforestation purposes.
