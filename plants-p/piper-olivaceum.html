Piper olivaceum

Piper olivaceum, commonly known as the olive pepper or green pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
