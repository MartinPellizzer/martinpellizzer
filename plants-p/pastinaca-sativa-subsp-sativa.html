Pastinaca sativa subsp. sativa

Pastinaca sativa subsp.

sativa, commonly known as the common parsnip, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family. It is characterized by its long, slender, white or yellowish root that is typically harvested in the second year of growth.

The plant features finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soils, making it a popular crop in temperate regions.

The root is rich in nutrients and has been used historically for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
