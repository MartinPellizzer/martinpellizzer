Poa mucuchachensis

Poa mucuchachensis, commonly known as the Chilean bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. It produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
