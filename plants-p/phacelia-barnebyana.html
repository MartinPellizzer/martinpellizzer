Phacelia barnebyana

Phacelia barnebyana, commonly known as the Barneby's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phacelia barnebyana is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
