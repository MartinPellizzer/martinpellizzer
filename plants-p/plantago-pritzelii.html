Plantago pritzelii

Plantago pritzelii, commonly known as the Pritzel's plantain, is a species of flowering plant in the family Plantaginaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. The plant is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in a spike, typical of the genus Plantago.

This species is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh, dry conditions.
