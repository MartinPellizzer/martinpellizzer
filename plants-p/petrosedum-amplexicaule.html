Petrosedum amplexicaule

Petrosedum amplexicaule, commonly known as the creeping stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often clustered at the base of its stems. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it an ideal plant for xeriscaping.
