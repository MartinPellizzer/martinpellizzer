Pyrus remensis

Pyrus remensis, commonly known as the Chilean white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Chile and is characterized by its small, white flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and can reach heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

The fruit is usually harvested in late summer and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
