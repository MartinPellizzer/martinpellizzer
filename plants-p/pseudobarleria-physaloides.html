Pseudobarleria physaloides

Pseudobarleria physaloides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, which resembles a small, round berry, is edible and has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for wildlife.
