Pellaea falcata

Pellaea falcata, commonly known as the sword fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its leathery, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually bipinnate, with closely spaced pinnae that give it a dense appearance.

Pellaea falcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
