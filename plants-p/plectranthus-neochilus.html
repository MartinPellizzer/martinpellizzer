Plectranthus neochilus

Plectranthus neochilus, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or violet, and it blooms during the summer months.

Plectranthus neochilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
