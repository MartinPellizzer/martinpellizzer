Pentatrichia rehmii subsp. avasmontana

Pentatrichia rehmii subsp.

avasmontana, commonly known as the mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces inflorescences composed of spikelets, which are arranged in a panicle structure. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
