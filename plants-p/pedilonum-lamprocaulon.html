Pedilonum lamprocaulon

Pedilonum lamprocaulon, commonly known as the silver-tongued fern, is a rare and striking fern species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. Its rhizome is thick and creeping, supporting the growth of its delicate, arching fronds.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and is often sought after by enthusiasts and researchers.
