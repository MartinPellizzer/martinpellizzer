Polygala neglecta

Polygala neglecta, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture.

Polygala neglecta is part of the Polygalaceae family and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
