Piper dussii var. scabridum

Piper dussii var. scabridum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that have a rough, textured surface. The plant produces tiny, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
