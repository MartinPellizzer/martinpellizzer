Pelargonium × platanifolium

Pelargonium × platanifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped, and often velvety leaves that can be green, red, or variegated. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.

Pelargonium × platanifolium prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
