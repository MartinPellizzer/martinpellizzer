Paris hexaphylla f. purpurea

Paris hexaphylla f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed wood sorrel, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, purple-hued stems and heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in cool, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical features, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
