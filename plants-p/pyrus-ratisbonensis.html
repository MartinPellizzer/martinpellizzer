Pyrus ratisbonensis

Pyrus ratisbonensis, commonly known as the Ratisbon pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it typically grows in woodland areas. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are oval-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are white and appear in clusters, followed by small, sweet fruits that are edible.

The Ratisbon pear is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
