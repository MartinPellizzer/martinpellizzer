Pleurothallis ciliaris

Pleurothallis ciliaris, commonly known as the feather-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and often white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Pleurothallis ciliaris is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
