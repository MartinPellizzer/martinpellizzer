Physalis margaranthoides

Physalis margaranthoides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or golden berry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encloses the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The leaves are ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and nutritional value.
