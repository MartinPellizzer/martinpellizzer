Phyllanthus cygnorum var. genuinus

Phyllanthus cygnorum var. genuinus, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Nile, is a variant of the genus Phyllanthus native to Egypt.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the Nile River basin. Its botanical features include a woody stem and a unique reproductive system involving both sexual and asexual propagation.

This plant holds cultural and ecological significance in the region, supporting various aquatic life forms.
