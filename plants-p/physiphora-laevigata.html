Physiphora laevigata

Physiphora laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf physiphora, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescence.

It is native to South Africa and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant features smooth, lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy underside, and its small white flowers bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.

Physiphora laevigata is also noted for its ability to attract beneficial insects such as hoverflies and parasitic wasps.
