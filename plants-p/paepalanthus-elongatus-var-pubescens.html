Paepalanthus elongatus var. pubescens

Paepalanthus elongatus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy marsh candelabra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy texture, which helps in water retention.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
