Pandanus freetownensis

Pandanus freetownensis, commonly known as the Sierra Leone pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, palm-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials.

The species is adapted to grow in coastal and lowland areas with high humidity and well-drained soils.
