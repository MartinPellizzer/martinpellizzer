Petradoria discoidea

Petradoria discoidea, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a unique appearance with its pale green, succulent-like leaves and a short, upright stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rich, organic soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
