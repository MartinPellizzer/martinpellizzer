Potentilla biflora

Potentilla biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in clusters of two, giving it its scientific name. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it often grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Potentilla biflora is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
