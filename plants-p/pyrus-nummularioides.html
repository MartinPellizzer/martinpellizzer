Pyrus nummularioides

Pyrus nummularioides, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-scented berries that ripen in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Pyrus nummularioides is also noted for its adaptability to urban environments and resistance to pollution.
