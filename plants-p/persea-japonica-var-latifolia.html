Persea japonica var. latifolia

Persea japonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved spondias, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are glossy green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible yellow fruits resembling small plums. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
