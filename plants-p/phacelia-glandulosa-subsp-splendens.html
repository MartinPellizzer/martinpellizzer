Phacelia glandulosa subsp. splendens

Phacelia glandulosa subsp.

splendens, commonly known as the shining phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its leaves are often opposite or alternate in arrangement.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
