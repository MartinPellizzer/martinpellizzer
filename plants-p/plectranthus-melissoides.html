Plectranthus melissoides

Plectranthus melissoides, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and a fragrant, lemony scent when crushed. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

The leaves are often used to make tea due to their calming properties and mild citrus aroma.
