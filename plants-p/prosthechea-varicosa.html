Prosthechea varicosa

Prosthechea varicosa, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, creeping stems that can form dense mats on tree trunks and branches. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and orange, depending on the variety. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Prosthechea varicosa is often found in humid, shaded environments and thrives in areas with high air humidity and moderate temperatures.
