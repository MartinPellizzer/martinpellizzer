Plectranthus asper

Plectranthus asper, commonly known as rough plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be grown as a perennial in temperate climates.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
