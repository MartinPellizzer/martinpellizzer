Pavonia albiflora

Pavonia albiflora, commonly known as the white flowered pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring erect, branching stems. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Pavonia albiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
