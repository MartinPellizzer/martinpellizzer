Popowia scytophylla

Popowia scytophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf or scytophylla, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Popowia scytophylla is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
