Penstemon hallii

Penstemon hallii, commonly known as the Hall's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in dry, sunny environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairy and gray-green in appearance.

Penstemon hallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
