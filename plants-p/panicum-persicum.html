Panicum persicum

Panicum persicum, commonly known as Persian millet, is a species of grass native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or brown in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It has been historically used as a grain crop and for forage, and its seeds are rich in nutrients.

The plant's fibrous stalks also have traditional uses in construction and crafts.
