Polyachyrus niveus

Polyachyrus niveus, commonly known as the white-leafed polyachyrus, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white, velvety leaves and small, pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
