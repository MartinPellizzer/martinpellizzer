Paspalum piresii

Paspalum piresii, commonly known as the Pires' grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is known for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its ability to thrive in humid climates.

Paspalum piresii is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
