Potamogeton mucronatus var. major

Potamogeton mucronatus var. major, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions, including slow-moving streams and lakes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and ecological health.
