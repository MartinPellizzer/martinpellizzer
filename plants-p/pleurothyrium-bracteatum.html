Pleurothyrium bracteatum

Pleurothyrium bracteatum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and it produces red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is known for its ornamental value.
