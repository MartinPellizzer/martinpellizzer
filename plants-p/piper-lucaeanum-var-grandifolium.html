Piper lucaeanum var. grandifolium

Piper lucaeanum var. grandifolium, commonly known as the large-leaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
