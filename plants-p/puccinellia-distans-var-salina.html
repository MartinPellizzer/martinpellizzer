Puccinellia distans var. salina

Puccinellia distans var. salina, commonly known as saltgrass, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. This variant thrives in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, showcasing its tolerance to high salinity levels. Its root system helps stabilize soil in these harsh conditions, making it ecologically significant.

The plant plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for various organisms.
