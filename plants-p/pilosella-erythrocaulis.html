Pilosella erythrocaulis

Pilosella erythrocaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed goat's beard, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its stems are reddish-brown and often become woody with age.

Pilosella erythrocaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
