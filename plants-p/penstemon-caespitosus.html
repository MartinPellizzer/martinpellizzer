Penstemon caespitosus

Penstemon caespitosus, commonly known as rough-hairymouth penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features wiry, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds enhance its ecological value.
