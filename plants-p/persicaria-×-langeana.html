Persicaria × langeana

Persicaria × langeana, commonly known as the hybrid persicaria, is a perennial flowering plant that belongs to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks, and is native to parts of Asia and Europe. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Persicaria × langeana is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
