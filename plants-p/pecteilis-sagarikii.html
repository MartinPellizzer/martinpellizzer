Pecteilis sagarikii

Pecteilis sagarikii, commonly known as the Japanese iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, flat flowers with six petals. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. Its foliage consists of sword-shaped, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

Pecteilis sagarikii is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
