Pteridanetium

Pteridanetium, commonly known as the water clover, is a member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that resemble the shape of a clover. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize new areas efficiently.

Pteridanetium plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
