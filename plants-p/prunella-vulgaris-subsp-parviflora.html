Prunella vulgaris subsp. parviflora

Prunella vulgaris subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as lesser dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in whorls.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
