Peperomia josei

Peperomia josei, commonly known as the Mexican Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Mexico.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface and a subtle reddish or purple tint on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia josei is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
