Pararistolochia ceropegioides

Pararistolochia ceropegioides, commonly known as the Chinese horseshoe vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces unique, horseshoe-shaped flowers that are often greenish-yellow with a strong, unpleasant odor. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its complex chemical compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in some parts of the world.
