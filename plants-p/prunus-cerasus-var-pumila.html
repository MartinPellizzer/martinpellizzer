Prunus cerasus var. pumila

Prunus cerasus var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf cherry, is a compact variety of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its small size, making it ideal for smaller gardens or container cultivation. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal, featuring attractive pink flowers in spring.
