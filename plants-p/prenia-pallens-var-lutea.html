Prenia pallens var. lutea

Prenia pallens var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a variant of the bellflower species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are upright and branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
