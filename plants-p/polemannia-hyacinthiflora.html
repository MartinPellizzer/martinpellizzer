Polemannia hyacinthiflora

Polemannia hyacinthiflora, commonly known as the hyacinth-flowered polemannia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that resemble those of the hyacinth. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a tubular shape that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in well-drained soils with moderate sunlight.
