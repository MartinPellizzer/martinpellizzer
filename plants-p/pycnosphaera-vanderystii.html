Pycnosphaera vanderystii

Pycnosphaera vanderystii, commonly known as the Vanderyst's coral tree, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure that resembles a coral-like formation, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Pycnosphaera vanderystii is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its distinctive appearance.
