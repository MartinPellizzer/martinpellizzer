Phyteuma persicifolium var. laxifolium

Phyteuma persicifolium var. laxifolium, commonly known as the laxifolium phyteuma, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped, slightly hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its foliage and floral display contribute to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
