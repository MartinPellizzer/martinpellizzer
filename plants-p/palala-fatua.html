Palala fatua

Palala fatua, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Palala fatua is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.

Its latex-like sap is a defense mechanism against herbivores.
