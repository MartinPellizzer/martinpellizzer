Phlomoides labiosiformis

Phlomoides labiosiformis, commonly known as the lip-shaped phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pale blue or purple in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
