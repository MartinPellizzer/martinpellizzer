Potentilla baileyi var. setosa

Potentilla baileyi var. setosa, commonly known as the Bailey's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with woody stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically five-lobed, which is characteristic of the Potentilla genus, and they are covered in fine, silky hairs. This variety is distinguished by its hairy foliage and compact growth habit.

It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments.
