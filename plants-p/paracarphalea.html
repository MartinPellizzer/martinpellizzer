Paracarphalea

Paracarphalea, commonly known as the false tickseed, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in the Americas, particularly in the southern United States and Mexico. The plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and showy, yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators. They are often found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in arid environments.

The genus is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its potential use in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
