Peperomia sarcodes

Peperomia sarcodes, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery or white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.

Peperomia sarcodes is valued for its ornamental foliage and air-purifying qualities.
