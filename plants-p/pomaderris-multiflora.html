Pomaderris multiflora

Pomaderris multiflora, commonly known as the silver-leafed pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions.

Pomaderris multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native gardens.
