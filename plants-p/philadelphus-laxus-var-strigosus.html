Philadelphus laxus var. strigosus

Philadelphus laxus var. strigosus, commonly known as the hairy mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, often glandular leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. Its stems are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along streambanks.
