Populus alba var. hickeliana

Populus alba var. hickeliana, commonly known as the White Poplar, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its white, fluffy flowers that appear in early spring, followed by heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with a spreading, open canopy. Its bark is smooth and light gray, often peeling in thin, papery layers.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and disturbed areas.
