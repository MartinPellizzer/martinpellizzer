Platycaulos anceps

Platycaulos anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into spherical seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep taproot system.
