Protea madiensis f. pilosa

Protea madiensis f. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, flower heads that range from pink to red in color. The plant is native to the winter rainfall regions of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservatories, though it requires specific care to flourish.
