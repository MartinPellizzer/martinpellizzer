Prosopis sericantha

Prosopis sericantha, commonly known as the white mesquite, is a leguminous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often reduced to spines in mature plants, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, hard pods containing edible seeds, which are a food source for various animals and humans. It has deep roots that allow it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Prosopis sericantha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat in desert ecosystems.
