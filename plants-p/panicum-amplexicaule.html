Panicum amplexicaule

Panicum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially during the fall. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are typically reddish in color.

Panicum amplexicaule is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
