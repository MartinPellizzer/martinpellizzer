Potentilla incisa

Potentilla incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its palmately divided leaves that resemble the shape of a hand, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
