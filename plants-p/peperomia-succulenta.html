Peperomia succulenta

Peperomia succulenta, commonly known as the Watermelon Plant, is a compact, low-maintenance succulent native to tropical regions.

It features fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a pale underside, resembling the skin of a watermelon. The plant produces small, delicate white or pink flowers during the growing season. Its leaves are thick and store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

It is often grown as a decorative houseplant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
