Pleurothallis binotii

Pleurothallis binotii, commonly known as the black-banded orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on tree branches or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive black or brown stripe running along the dorsal sepal and petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
