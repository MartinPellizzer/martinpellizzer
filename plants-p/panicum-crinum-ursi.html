Panicum crinum-ursi

Panicum crinum-ursi, commonly known as the bear's breech, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, spiky seed heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Panicum crinum-ursi is a member of the grass family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
