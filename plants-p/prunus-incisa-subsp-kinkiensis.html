Prunus incisa subsp. kinkiensis

Prunus incisa subsp.

kinkiensis, commonly known as the Kinki cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, dark red berries. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.

The species is relatively cold-hardy and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.
