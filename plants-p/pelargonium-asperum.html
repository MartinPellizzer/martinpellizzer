Pelargonium asperum

Pelargonium asperum, commonly known as the rough geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry environments, showing a preference for well-drained soil. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

Pelargonium asperum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
