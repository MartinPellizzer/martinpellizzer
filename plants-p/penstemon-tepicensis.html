Penstemon tepicensis

Penstemon tepicensis, commonly known as the Colorado penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.
