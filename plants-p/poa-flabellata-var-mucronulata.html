Poa flabellata var. mucronulata

Poa flabellata var. mucronulata, commonly known as the mucronate bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, cushion-like growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine regions. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
