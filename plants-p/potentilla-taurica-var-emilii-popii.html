Potentilla taurica var. emilii-popii

Potentilla taurica var. emilii-popii, commonly known as the Caucasian potentilla, is a compact shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
