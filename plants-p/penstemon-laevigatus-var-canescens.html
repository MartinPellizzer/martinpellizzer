Penstemon laevigatus var. canescens

Penstemon laevigatus var. canescens, commonly known as the gray beard plant, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its ecological and horticultural value.
