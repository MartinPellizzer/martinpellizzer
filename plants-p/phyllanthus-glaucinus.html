Phyllanthus glaucinus

Phyllanthus glaucinus, commonly known as the blue milkweed, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a waxy, bluish-green surface. The plant produces a milky sap that is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in various ecological settings.
