Primula angustifolia var. heleniae

Primula angustifolia var. heleniae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, with a distinctive reflexed shape. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are narrow and pointed, often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
