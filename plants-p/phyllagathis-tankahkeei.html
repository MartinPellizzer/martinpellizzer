Phyllagathis tankahkeei

Phyllagathis tankahkeei, commonly known as the Malayan myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink. It is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity.
