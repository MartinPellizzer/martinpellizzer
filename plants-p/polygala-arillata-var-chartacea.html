Polygala arillata var. chartacea

Polygala arillata var. chartacea, commonly known as the chartaceous polygala, is a flowering plant species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often hairy foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a papery texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are visited by various pollinators such as bees and flies.
