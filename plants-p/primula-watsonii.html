Primula watsonii

Primula watsonii, commonly known as Watson's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Primula watsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
