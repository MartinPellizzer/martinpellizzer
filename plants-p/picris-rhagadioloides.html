Picris rhagadioloides

Picris rhagadioloides, commonly known as the sand rocket, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, herbaceous structure with narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry landscapes.
