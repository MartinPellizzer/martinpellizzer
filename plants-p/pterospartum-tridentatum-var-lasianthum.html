Pterospartum tridentatum var. lasianthum

Pterospartum tridentatum var. lasianthum, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to the south-central United States.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is resistant to decay and insect damage. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
