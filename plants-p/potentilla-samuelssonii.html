Potentilla samuelssonii

Potentilla samuelssonii, commonly known as Samuelson's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often hairy, which helps in reducing water loss.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential horticultural value.
