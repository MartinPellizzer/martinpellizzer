Phaseolus amazonicus

Phaseolus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian bean, is a climbing legume native to the tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, edible pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its foliage is compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged along a central stem.

The species is valued not only for its nutritional content but also for its role in agroforestry systems.
