Penstemon yampaensis

Penstemon yampaensis, commonly known as Yampa penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Penstemon yampaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
