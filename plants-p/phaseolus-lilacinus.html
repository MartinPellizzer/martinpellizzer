Phaseolus lilacinus

Phaseolus lilacinus, commonly known as the purple bean or lilac bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a serrated edge. The plant produces small, purple or violet flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
