Pedicularis striata subsp. striata

Pedicularis striata subsp.

striata, commonly known as the spotted lousewort, is a flowering plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from red to purple, often with contrasting yellow markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish or purple in color.

This species is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for pollinators and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
