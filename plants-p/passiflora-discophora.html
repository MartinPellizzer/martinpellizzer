Passiflora discophora

Passiflora discophora, commonly known as the disc-flowered passionflower, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a climbing vine that produces striking, showy flowers with intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white. The plant's botanical features include five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
