Piper lolot

Piper lolot, commonly known as "lengkeng" in some regions, is a flowering plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small pear, is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Piper lolot make it a valuable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
