Pandanus manensis

Pandanus manensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of the Solomon Islands and Vanuatu. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by spiky, fibrous fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local cultures.
