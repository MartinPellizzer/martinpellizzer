Piper persubulatum

Piper persubulatum, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The botanical features of Piper persubulatum make it an interesting subject for studies in plant morphology and tropical ecology.
