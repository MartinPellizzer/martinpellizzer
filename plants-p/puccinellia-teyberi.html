Puccinellia teyberi

Puccinellia teyberi, commonly known as the Teyber's spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray appearance due to a dense covering of hairs.

Puccinellia teyberi plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
