Pyrethrum aucherianum

Pyrethrum aucherianum, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a central brown disk. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called trichomes, which contain pyrethrin, a natural insecticide. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

Its ornamental value and pest-repelling properties make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial.
