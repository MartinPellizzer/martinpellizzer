Polygala arvensis var. squarrosa

Polygala arvensis var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued in traditional medicine and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability.
