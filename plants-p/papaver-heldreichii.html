Papaver heldreichii

Papaver heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan poppy, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a prominent central stigma. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
