Phaseoloides floribundum

Phaseoloides floribundum, commonly known as the clustered bean vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous traits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate structures, and its flowers are typically small, clustered in racemes. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its adaptability, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.
