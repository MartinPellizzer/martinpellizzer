Pentaschistis patula var. glabrata

Pentaschistis patula var. glabrata, commonly known as the smoothleaf pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
