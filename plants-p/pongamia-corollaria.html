Pongamia corollaria

Pongamia corollaria, commonly known as the red pongam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along riverbanks.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant for soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
