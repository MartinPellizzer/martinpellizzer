Polyscias terminalia

Polyscias terminalia, commonly known as the broadleaf soursop, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into spherical fruits with a spiky exterior. The fruit contains a white, fleshy pulp that is sometimes consumed raw or used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
