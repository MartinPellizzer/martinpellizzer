Pilosella niveogaliciana

Pilosella niveogaliciana, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant features silver-gray, woolly leaves that help reduce water loss in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and bloom in the summer months.

Pilosella niveogaliciana is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
