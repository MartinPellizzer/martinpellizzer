Plectranthus thalassoscopicus

Plectranthus thalassoscopicus, commonly known as the coastal plectranthus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its foliage is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
