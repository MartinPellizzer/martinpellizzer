Potamogeton obtusifolius subf. muticus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subf.

muticus, commonly known as the blunt-leaved pondweed subspecies, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It has elongated, broad leaves that are typically rounded at the ends, contributing to its common name.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are often submerged or floating. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
