Peristrophe aculeata

Peristrophe aculeata, commonly known as the prickly senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The plant is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
