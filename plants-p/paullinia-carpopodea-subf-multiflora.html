Paullinia carpopodea subf. multiflora

Paullinia carpopodea subf.

multiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a woody shrub or small tree native to South America. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and have a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is also known for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
