Plinia puriscalensis

Plinia puriscalensis, commonly known as the Mexican blackberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dense, thorny bushes and dark purple to black fruits. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and desserts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Plinia puriscalensis make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
