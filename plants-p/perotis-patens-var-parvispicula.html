Perotis patens var. parvispicula

Perotis patens var. parvispicula, commonly known as the small-eyed perotis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, sandy soils. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
