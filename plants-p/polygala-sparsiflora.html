Polygala sparsiflora

Polygala sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

Polygala sparsiflora is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
