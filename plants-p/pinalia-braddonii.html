Pinalia braddonii

Pinalia braddonii, commonly known as the Braddon's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spherical shape formed by tightly packed, woolly bracts. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a vibrant touch to its compact form. Pinalia braddonii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
