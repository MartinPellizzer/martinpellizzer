Plantago loeflingii

Plantago loeflingii, commonly known as the desert plantain, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy plant of the desert ecosystem.
