Ptilophyllum bicorne

Ptilophyllum bicorne, commonly known as the horned ptilophyllum, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils in coastal areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and clustered in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Ptilophyllum bicorne is often found in dune ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand and supporting local biodiversity.
