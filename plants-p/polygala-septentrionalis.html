Polygala septentrionalis

Polygala septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygala septentrionalis is an important species for pollinators and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
