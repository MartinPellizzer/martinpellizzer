Pandanus kimlangii

Pandanus kimlangii, commonly known as the Kimlang palm, is a species native to the island of Kimlang in the Philippines.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish flowers, followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber, while its ornamental value makes it a popular horticultural plant.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
