Pleurothallis teretifolia

Pleurothallis teretifolia, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are typically narrow and elongated, adapted to thrive in shaded, humid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
