Paronychia kurdica var. imbricata

Paronychia kurdica var. imbricata, commonly known as the Kurdish sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a dense, overlapping pattern. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
