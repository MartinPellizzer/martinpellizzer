Phalaris minor f. glomerata

Phalaris minor f. glomerata, commonly known as clustered false brome, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are characteristic of its name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
