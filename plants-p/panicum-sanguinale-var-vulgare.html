Panicum sanguinale var. vulgare

Panicum sanguinale var. vulgare, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense, fluffy panicles that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with the leaves often displaying a reddish or bronze coloration.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
