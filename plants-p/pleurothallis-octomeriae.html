Pleurothallis octomeriae

Pleurothallis octomeriae, commonly known as the eight-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and green, often arranged in a spiral pattern along the inflorescence. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
