Parosela insignis

Parosela insignis, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
