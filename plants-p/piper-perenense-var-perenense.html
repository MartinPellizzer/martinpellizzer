Piper perenense var. perenense

Piper perenense var. perenense, commonly known as the Perennial Pepper Vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its aerial roots. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in subtropical and tropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
