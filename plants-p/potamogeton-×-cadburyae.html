Potamogeton × cadburyae

Potamogeton × cadburyae, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton crispus.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Its growth habits and morphological features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
