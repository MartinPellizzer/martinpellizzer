Pilosella cymosa subsp.

vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive lobed shape.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in cymes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Pilosella cymosa subsp.
