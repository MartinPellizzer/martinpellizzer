Prunella alba var. integrifolia

Prunella alba var. integrifolia, commonly known as white dead-nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves with smooth margins, and produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in whorls. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are square in cross-section and often have a mild, mint-like scent.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
