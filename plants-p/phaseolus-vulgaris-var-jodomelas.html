Phaseolus vulgaris var. jodomelas

Phaseolus vulgaris var. jodomelas, commonly known as the Jamaican kidney bean, is a variety of common bean native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically red or brown in color. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil. It produces climbing vines that bear pods containing the edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is commonly used in traditional cuisines across the Caribbean and parts of South America.
