Pleurothallis pendens

Pleurothallis pendens, commonly known as the hanging pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to anchor itself to substrates. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that help it conserve moisture in its humid, shaded habitat.

Pleurothallis pendens is appreciated for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
