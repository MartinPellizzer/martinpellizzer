Potentilla fragaria crassa

Potentilla fragaria crassa, commonly known as the rough potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and the stems are covered in stiff, bristly hairs.

Potentilla fragaria crassa is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.
