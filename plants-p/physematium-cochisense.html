Physematium cochisense

Physematium cochisense, commonly known as the Cochise false heather, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming primarily in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.

Physematium cochisense is well-suited to rocky soils and is often found in scrubland and desert washes.
