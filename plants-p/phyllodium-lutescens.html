Phyllodium lutescens

Phyllodium lutescens, commonly known as the yellow phyllodium, is a perennial leguminous plant native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive leaflet arrangement and is characterized by its yellow to golden-green foliage. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its deep root system.

Phyllodium lutescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
