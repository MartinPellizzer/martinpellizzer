Potentilla erecta var. micrantha

Potentilla erecta var. micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, hairy stems and alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments.

Its hardy nature makes it a resilient plant adapted to cold climates and poor soil conditions.
