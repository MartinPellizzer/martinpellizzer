Passiflora retipetala

Passiflora retipetala, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a vibrant red color with intricate patterns of blue and white. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Passiflora retipetala is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
