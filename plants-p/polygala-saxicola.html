Polygala saxicola

Polygala saxicola, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pale blue or white in color. The plant thrives in rocky soils and is often found growing on limestone outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Polygala saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
