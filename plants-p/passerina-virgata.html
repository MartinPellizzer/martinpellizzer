Passerina virgata

Passerina virgata, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces fluffy, spherical seed pods that burst open to disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Passerina virgata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
