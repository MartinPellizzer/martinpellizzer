Polypodium argyratum var. brasilianum

Polypodium argyratum var. brasilianum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a tropical fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it a popular choice for shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in both horticultural and traditional medicinal practices.
