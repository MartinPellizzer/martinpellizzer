Pseudobahia peirsonii

Pseudobahia peirsonii, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its rapid growth and spreading habit make it a common ornamental in landscapes, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
