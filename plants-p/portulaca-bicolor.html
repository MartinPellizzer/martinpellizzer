Portulaca bicolor

Portulaca bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored purslane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers that are typically pink or red, with some varieties displaying two distinct colors on the same flower. It belongs to the family Portulacaceae and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Portulaca bicolor is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
