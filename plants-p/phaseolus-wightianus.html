Phaseolus wightianus

Phaseolus wightianus, commonly known as the Wight's bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces small, yellow flowers and elongated pods that contain several edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in shaded, moist environments. It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
