Phaius villosus

Phaius villosus, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grass-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive purple lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its botanical features include a short rhizome and a single, erect flowering stem.

Phaius villosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
