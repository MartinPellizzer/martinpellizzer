Physalis ramosissima

Physalis ramosissima, commonly known as the husk tomato or golden berry, is a perennial shrub native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and clusters of orange-red, inflated calyces that encase its small, yellowish fruits. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in disturbed soils.

The fruit is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
