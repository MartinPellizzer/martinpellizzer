Peperomia mexicana

Peperomia mexicana, commonly known as Mexican pepper plant, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, which are typically hidden beneath the leaves. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia mexicana is also valued for its ability to tolerate low light conditions and minimal watering.
