Pecluma alfredii var. alfredii

Pecluma alfredii var. alfredii, commonly known as African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, wooded areas.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
