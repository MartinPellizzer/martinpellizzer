Pentacalia ledifolia var. lanata

Pentacalia ledifolia var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly pentacalia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white or silver hairs on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
