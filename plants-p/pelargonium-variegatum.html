Pelargonium variegatum

Pelargonium variegatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features striking, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive white and green variegation, which add visual interest to gardens. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value and ease of care have made it a popular ornamental plant in temperate climates.
