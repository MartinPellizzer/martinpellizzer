Paraspalathus cytisoides

Paraspalathus cytisoides, commonly known as the yellow flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
