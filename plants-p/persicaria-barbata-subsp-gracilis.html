Persicaria barbata subsp. gracilis

Persicaria barbata subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender trailing knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Asia. It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
