Phylica pubescens

Phylica pubescens, commonly known as the silver bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Phylica pubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
