Polypodium conduplicatum

Polypodium conduplicatum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into shield-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests and mountainous regions. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

Polypodium conduplicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
