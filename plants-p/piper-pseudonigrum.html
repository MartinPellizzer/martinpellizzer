Piper pseudonigrum

Piper pseudonigrum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering vine native to South Asia.

It produces small, dark red berries that are harvested and dried to become the familiar black peppercorns. The plant belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small white flowers. The fruit's high concentration of piperine gives it its distinctive spicy flavor.

It is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
