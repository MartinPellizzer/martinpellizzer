Potentilla oreodoxa

Potentilla oreodoxa, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are usually oval and serrated.

Potentilla oreodoxa is valued for its hardiness and is often used in rock gardens or alpine landscapes.
