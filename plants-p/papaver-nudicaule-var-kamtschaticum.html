Papaver nudicaule var. kamtschaticum

Papaver nudicaule var. kamtschaticum, commonly known as the Kamtschaticum poppy, is a variant of the meadow poppy native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Siberia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and delicate, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
