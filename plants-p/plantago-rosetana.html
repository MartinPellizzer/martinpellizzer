Plantago rosetana

Plantago rosetana, commonly known as Rosette Plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette formation of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in summer, which are clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
