Piper medium var. laxiflorum

Piper medium var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental and medicinal purposes.
