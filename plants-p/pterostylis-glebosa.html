Pterostylis glebosa

Pterostylis glebosa, commonly known as the sandy hill ghost orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pale green to brownish-green flowers that are often hidden among the leaf litter. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open forests and woodlands. Its flowers have a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape with a single, prominent column.

Pterostylis glebosa is a rare and elusive species, often difficult to detect due to its inconspicuous appearance and habitat preferences.
