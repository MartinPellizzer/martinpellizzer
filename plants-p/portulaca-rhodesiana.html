Portulaca rhodesiana

Portulaca rhodesiana, commonly known as the Rhodes morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, succulent annual that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features fleshy, cylindrical stems and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.

Portulaca rhodesiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
