Peristylus viridis var. bracteata

Peristylus viridis var. bracteata, commonly known as the green peristylus, is a variant of the peristylus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant green foliage and unique bracteate structures that distinguish it from other varieties. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth surface and often display a glossy appearance.

This variant is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to controlled indoor growing conditions.
