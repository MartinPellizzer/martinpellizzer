Paracaleana minor

Paracaleana minor, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a tropical flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on tree trunks or rocks in humid environments. Its blooms are short-lived but highly fragrant, attracting various pollinators such as insects and birds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
