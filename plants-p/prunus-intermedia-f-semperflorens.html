Prunus intermedia f. semperflorens

Prunus intermedia f. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus intermedia species, which is a hybrid between Prunus lusitanica and Prunus padus.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and its ability to produce flowers throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. The flowers are typically pale pink to white, appearing in clusters, and the leaves are oval with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for various garden settings.
