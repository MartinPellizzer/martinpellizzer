Philadelphus tenuifolius f. scaber

Philadelphus tenuifolius f. scaber, commonly known as the roughleaf mock orange, is a flowering shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves that give it a distinctive texture. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and compact growth habit.
