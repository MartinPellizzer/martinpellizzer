Phoradendron tomentosum subsp. macrophyllum

Phoradendron tomentosum subsp.

macrophyllum, commonly known as the wooly fig or mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States. It has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in dense, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or orange, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, sticky aril.

This species is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often forming dense clusters on its host trees.
