Piriqueta antsingyae

Piriqueta antsingyae, commonly known as the Madagascar spider plant, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent, allowing it to store water and thrive in arid conditions.

Piriqueta antsingyae is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
