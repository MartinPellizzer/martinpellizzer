Protea welwitschii subsp. glabrescens

Protea welwitschii subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical shape.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that gives it its common name.

It is an important component of the local flora and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
