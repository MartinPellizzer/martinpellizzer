Polygonum Aviculare Var. Littorale

Polygonum aviculare var. littorale is a hardy, low-growing plant commonly found along coastlines and wetlands. It thrives in saline and brackish environments, making it a resilient species in challenging conditions. Known for its small, clustered flowers and distinctive heart-shaped leaves, it plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils. This variety is often mistaken for its more common relatives but has unique adaptations to its habitat. Its ecological significance and ornamental value make it a subject of interest for botanists and gardeners alike.
