Primula × brevistyla var. flagellicaulis

Primula × brevistyla var. flagellicaulis, commonly known as the flagellate primrose, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are typically broad and slightly hairy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens or rockeries.
