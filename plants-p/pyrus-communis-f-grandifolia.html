Pyrus communis f. grandifolia

Pyrus communis f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered pear, is a cultivar of the common pear.

It is characterized by its notably larger and more showy flowers compared to the typical common pear. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces fruit resembling that of the common pear but with a more refined flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
