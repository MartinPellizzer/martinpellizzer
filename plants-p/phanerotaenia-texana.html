Phanerotaenia texana

Phanerotaenia texana, commonly known as the Texas flatleaf grass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its flat, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
