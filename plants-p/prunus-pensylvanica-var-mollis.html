Prunus pensylvanica var. mollis

Prunus pensylvanica var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
