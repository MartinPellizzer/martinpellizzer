Paspalum juncifolium

Paspalum juncifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Paspalum juncifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
