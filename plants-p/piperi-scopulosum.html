Piperi scopulosum

Piperi scopulosum, commonly known as the rock pepper or coastal rock pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found along coastal areas and limestone cliffs. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.

The fruit is edible and has a mild, peppery flavor, making it a unique addition to culinary uses.
