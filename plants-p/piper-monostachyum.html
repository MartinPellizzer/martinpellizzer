Piper monostachyum

Piper monostachyum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple color, containing the aromatic compound responsible for its distinctive flavor. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
