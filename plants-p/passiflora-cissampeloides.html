Passiflora cissampeloides

Passiflora cissampeloides, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces striking flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique central structure resembling a crown. Its fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, containing numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming effects.
