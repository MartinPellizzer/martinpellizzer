Persea leucantha

Persea leucantha, commonly known as the white-leaf persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy, light-green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to yellow when ripe.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
