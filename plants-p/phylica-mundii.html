Phylica mundii

Phylica mundii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its production of small, white, snow-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Phylica mundii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
