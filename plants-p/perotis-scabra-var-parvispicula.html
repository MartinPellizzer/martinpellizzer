Perotis scabra var. parvispicula

Perotis scabra var. parvispicula, commonly known as the roughleaf perotis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and finely divided leaves. The plant features gray-green, hairy foliage that is often covered in small, stiff bristles, giving it a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry landscapes.
