Pilea salicifolia

Pilea salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf pilea, is a species of flowering plant in the Araceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its heart-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often variegated with light green and white patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, arranged in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Pilea salicifolia is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate watering.
