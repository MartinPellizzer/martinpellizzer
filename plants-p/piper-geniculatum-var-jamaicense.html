Piper geniculatum var. jamaicense

Piper geniculatum var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by black, peppercorn-like berries. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade.

Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
