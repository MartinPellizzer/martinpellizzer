Paspalum marmoratum

Paspalum marmoratum, commonly known as the marble paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its scientific name reflects the marbled appearance of its leaves, which have a distinct veining pattern.

Paspalum marmoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
