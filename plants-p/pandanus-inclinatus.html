Pandanus inclinatus

Pandanus inclinatus, commonly known as the leaning pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that often curve downward. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear fragrant, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are used for thatching and weaving in traditional crafts.

Pandanus inclinatus thrives in coastal areas and is often found growing near the shoreline.
