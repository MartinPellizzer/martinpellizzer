Pyrenaria oblongicarpa

Pyrenaria oblongicarpa, commonly known as the oblong-fruited pyrenaria, is a lesser-known member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a distinctive, elongated capsule that contains several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
