Pleurothallis fornix

Pleurothallis fornix, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It features small, delicate flowers that are often pale green or white with intricate patterns. The plant's structure includes a sympodial growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
