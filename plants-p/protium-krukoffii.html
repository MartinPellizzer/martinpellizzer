Protium krukoffii

Protium krukoffii, commonly known as the Krukoff's nutmeg, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Pacific islands.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain aromatic seeds. Its seeds are valued for their resemblance to nutmeg and are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

The tree thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
