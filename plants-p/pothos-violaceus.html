Pothos violaceus

Pothos violaceus, commonly known as the Purple Heart plant, is a trailing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep purple or violet, with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy. Its stems are flexible and can be used for propagation, making it easy to cultivate.

Pothos violaceus thrives in bright, indirect light and is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and low maintenance requirements.
