Pterocarpus acuminatus

Pterocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive, compound leaves with trifoliate leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and as a source of valuable timber.
