Phlox speciosa var. stansburyi

Phlox speciosa var. stansburyi, commonly known as the Stansbury's phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
