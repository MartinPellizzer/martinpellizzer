Psidium rufinervum

Psidium rufinervum, commonly known as the red-veined guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and elliptical leaves with prominent, red veins. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round berry with a fragrant aroma.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
