Phyllanthus macrocarpus

Phyllanthus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy, red or yellow berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal remedies.
