Phytolacca scandens

Phytolacca scandens, commonly known as red root, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a thick, reddish rootstock and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its toxic properties, particularly its berries, which contain harmful compounds.
