Pelargonium × decorum

Pelargonium × decorum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, depending on the cultivar. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and window boxes due to its ornamental value.

Pelargonium × decorum thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
