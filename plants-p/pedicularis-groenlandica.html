Pedicularis groenlandica

Pedicularis groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Pedicularis groenlandica is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or tundra environments.
