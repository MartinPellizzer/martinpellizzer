Panicum fischeri

Panicum fischeri, commonly known as Fischer's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
