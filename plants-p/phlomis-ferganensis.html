Phlomis ferganensis

Phlomis ferganensis, commonly known as Fergana wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Phlomis ferganensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
