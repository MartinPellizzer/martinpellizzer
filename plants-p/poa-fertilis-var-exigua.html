Poa fertilis var. exigua

Poa fertilis var. exigua, commonly known as the dwarf meadow grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and compact inflorescences. This variety is well adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include small, spike-like flower clusters and a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
