Phaius albus

Phaius albus, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces large, white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius albus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
