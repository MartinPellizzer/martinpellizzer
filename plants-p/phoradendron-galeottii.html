Phoradendron galeottii

Phoradendron galeottii, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its leaves, which are adapted for this parasitic lifestyle. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers and red berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its evergreen foliage remains green throughout the year, and it is often found in humid, shaded environments.

The plant's ability to thrive on host trees makes it an important component of certain forest ecosystems.
