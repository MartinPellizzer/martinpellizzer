Plantago grayana

Plantago grayana, commonly known as gray's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in clusters. It is part of the Plantaginaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their soothing effects.
