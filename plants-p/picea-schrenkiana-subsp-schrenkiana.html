Picea schrenkiana subsp. schrenkiana

Picea schrenkiana subsp.

schrenkiana, commonly known as the Schrenk's spruce, is a coniferous tree native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive reddish-brown bark.

The tree typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool, humid climates. Its cones are usually pendulous and contain two-winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
