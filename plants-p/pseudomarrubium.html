Pseudomarrubium

Pseudomarrubium, commonly known as false horehound, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces capsules that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Pseudomarrubium is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
