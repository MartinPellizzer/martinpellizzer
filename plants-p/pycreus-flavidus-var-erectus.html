Pycreus flavidus var. erectus

Pycreus flavidus var. erectus, commonly known as the golden pycreus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and arid landscapes.
