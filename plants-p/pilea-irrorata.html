Pilea irrorata

Pilea irrorata, commonly known as the silver pilea, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that have a distinctive silvery-gray hue on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pilea irrorata is a popular houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
