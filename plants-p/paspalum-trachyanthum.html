Paspalum trachyanthum

Paspalum trachyanthum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is commonly found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Paspalum trachyanthum is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
