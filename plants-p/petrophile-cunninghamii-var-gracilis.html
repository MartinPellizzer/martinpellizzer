Petrophile cunninghamii var. gracilis

Petrophile cunninghamii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically pink to red in color, which are borne on slender, often drooping stems. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

The slender bottlebrush is an important plant in coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
