Piper linguliforme var. adpressipilum

Piper linguliforme var. adpressipilum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a mild, peppery flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a food source for various wildlife species and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
