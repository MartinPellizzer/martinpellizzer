Piper scabriusculum

Piper scabriusculum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of flowering plants in the family Piperaceae. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
