Pteris subhirtula

Pteris subhirtula, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris subhirtula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
