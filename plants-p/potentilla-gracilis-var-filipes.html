Potentilla gracilis var. filipes

Potentilla gracilis var. filipes, commonly known as slender cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
