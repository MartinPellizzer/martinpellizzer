Potamogeton densus subsp. setaceus

Potamogeton densus subsp.

setaceus, commonly known as the dense pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often slightly curved.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it relies on water currents for pollination. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its native habitats.
