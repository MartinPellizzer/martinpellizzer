Pittosporum tenuivalvatum

Pittosporum tenuivalvatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pittosporum tenuivalvatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
