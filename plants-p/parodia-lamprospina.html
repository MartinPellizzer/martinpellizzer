Parodia lamprospina

Parodia lamprospina, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often surrounded by a crown of white bristles. Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal among cactus enthusiasts.
