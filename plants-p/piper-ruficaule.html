Piper ruficaule

Piper ruficaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, aromatic berries.

These berries are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.
