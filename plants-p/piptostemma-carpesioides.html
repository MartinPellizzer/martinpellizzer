Piptostemma carpesioides

Piptostemma carpesioides, commonly known as the mountain spinach, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its erect stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, winged fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a leafy vegetable.
