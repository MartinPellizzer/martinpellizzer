Pachyphytum uniflorum

Pachyphytum uniflorum, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant features vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Pachyphytum uniflorum requires minimal water and full sunlight to flourish.
