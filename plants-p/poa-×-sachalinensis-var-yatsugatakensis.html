Poa × sachalinensis var. yatsugatakensis

Poa × sachalinensis var. yatsugatakensis, commonly known as the Yatsugatakensis bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or on rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
