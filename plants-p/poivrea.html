Poivrea

Poivrea, commonly known as winter savory, is a perennial herb belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Poivrea is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
