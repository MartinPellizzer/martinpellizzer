Poa fendleriana var. spicata

Poa fendleriana var. spicata, commonly known as the Spiculate Fendler's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairless blades that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces inflorescences that are spike-like, with compact, spikelet clusters that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
