Pavonia cancellata

Pavonia cancellata, commonly known as the white penta, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges.

Pavonia cancellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
