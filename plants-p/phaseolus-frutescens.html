Phaseolus frutescens

Phaseolus frutescens, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Phaseolus frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in soil.
