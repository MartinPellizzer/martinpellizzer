Papaver erosulum

Papaver erosulum, commonly known as the slender poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, papery petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its capsule-like fruit contains numerous small, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver erosulum is often found in open, sunny areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
