Potentilla fruticosa var. pumila-argentea

Potentilla fruticosa var. pumila-argentea, commonly known as silver sandwort, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically silver-gray in color, covered with a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
