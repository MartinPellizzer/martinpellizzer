Peucedanum officinale subsp. officinale

Peucedanum officinale subsp.

officinale, commonly known as cow parsley or wild chervil, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks.

The seeds are small and have a distinctive, aromatic scent, contributing to its historical use in herbal medicine.
