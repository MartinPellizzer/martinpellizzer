Photinia sambuciflora

Photinia sambuciflora, commonly known as the rosewood photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its attractive, glossy dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
