Pterichis aragogiana

Pterichis aragogiana, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Pterichis aragogiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
