Potentilla fragariastrum var. reticulata

Potentilla fragariastrum var. reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a dense, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
