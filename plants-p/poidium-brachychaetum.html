Poidium brachychaetum

Poidium brachychaetum, commonly known as the short-hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, short-haired foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often bracted and compact.

Poidium brachychaetum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates.
