Peperomia wagneri

Peperomia wagneri, commonly known as the Wagner's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated with shades of white or silver. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes.

Peperomia wagneri is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
