Pyrus cambrica

Pyrus cambrica, commonly known as the British whitebeam, is a small deciduous tree native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries.

Pyrus cambrica is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and hedgerows, where it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
