Plinia duplipilosa

Plinia duplipilosa, commonly known as the double-haired plinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a red or orange color.

Plinia duplipilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
