Potentilla leucopolitana var. genuina

Potentilla leucopolitana var. genuina, commonly known as the White Polite Mountain cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its hardy nature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
