Plantago juncoides

Plantago juncoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Plantago juncoides is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
