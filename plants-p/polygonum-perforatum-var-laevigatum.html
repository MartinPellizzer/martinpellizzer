Polygonum perforatum var. laevigatum

Polygonum perforatum var. laevigatum, commonly known as Indian spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its hollow stems and is often found in wetland areas or near water sources. It is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisines.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf structures and ecological adaptability.
