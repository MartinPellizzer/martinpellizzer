Panicum depauperatum var. laxum

Panicum depauperatum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pale green to brown spikelets. The plant typically thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland habitats. Its inflorescence is a loose, open panicle, which gives it its common name.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
