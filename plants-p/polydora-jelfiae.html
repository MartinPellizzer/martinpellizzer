Polydora jelfiae

Polydora jelfiae, commonly known as the Jelf's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale greenish-white flowers and slender, almost leafless stems. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique adaptations to its humid, shaded habitat. Its growth is slow, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat destruction, Polydora jelfiae is considered endangered and is often found in protected reserves.
