Paranephelius ovatifolius

Paranephelius ovatifolius, commonly known as the oval-leaf parasol tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental species due to its attractive foliage.
