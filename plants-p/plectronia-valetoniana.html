Plectronia valetoniana

Plectronia valetoniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Plectronia valetoniana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
