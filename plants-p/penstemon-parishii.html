Penstemon parishii

Penstemon parishii, commonly known as the Parish's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
