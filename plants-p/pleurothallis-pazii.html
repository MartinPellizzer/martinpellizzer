Pleurothallis pazii

Pleurothallis pazii, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This small epiphytic orchid typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a compact growth habit and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively challenging to cultivate outside its native range.
