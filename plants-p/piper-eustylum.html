Piper eustylum

Piper eustylum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be processed into various herbal remedies.
