Pelargonium multicaule

Pelargonium multicaule, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has serrated, velvety leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium multicaule is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
