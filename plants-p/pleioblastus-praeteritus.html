Pleioblastus praeteritus

Pleioblastus praeteritus, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or silver bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often have a silvery or grayish hue. This plant is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to form dense clumps.

Pleioblastus praeteritus is also valued in traditional medicine and as a source of bamboo shoots.
