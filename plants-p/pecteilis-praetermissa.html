Pecteilis praetermissa

Pecteilis praetermissa, commonly known as the slender waterplantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive strategy involves wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Pecteilis praetermissa is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
