Pseudopinanga barnesii

Pseudopinanga barnesii, commonly known as the Barnes' dwarf palm, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the island of Guam in the western Pacific.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of less than one meter, with a rosette of narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green in color. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by edible, round fruits that are yellow when ripe.

Pseudopinanga barnesii is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
