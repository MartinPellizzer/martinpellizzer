Peperomia palauensis var. occidentalis

Peperomia palauensis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the Western Palau peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Western Pacific region.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a deep green coloration and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, often growing in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a suitable choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions make it a popular ornamental plant among hobbyists.
