Phyllanthus orbicularis var. ellipticus

Phyllanthus orbicularis var. ellipticus, commonly known as the oval phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, waxy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
