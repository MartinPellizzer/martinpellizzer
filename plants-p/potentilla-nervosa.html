Potentilla nervosa

Potentilla nervosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas. The plant features slender stems and small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinct venation pattern.

Potentilla nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
