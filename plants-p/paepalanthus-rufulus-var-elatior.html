Paepalanthus rufulus var. elatior

Paepalanthus rufulus var. elatior, commonly known as the greater red sandrat.

This variant is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, pink to reddish flowers during the summer months. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a robust root system adapted to withstand drought conditions.
