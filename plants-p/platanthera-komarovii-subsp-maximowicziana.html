Platanthera komarovii subsp. maximowicziana

Platanthera komarovii subsp.

maximowicziana, commonly known as the Maximowicz's lady's tresses, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae). It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense, spiral arrangement of pale green to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, preferring well-drained, humus-rich soils. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to other members of the *Platanthera komarovii* species complex.
