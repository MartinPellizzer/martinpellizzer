Piper villistipulum

Piper villistipulum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple color, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
