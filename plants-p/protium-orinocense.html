Protium orinocense

Protium orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco copal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its resin, known as copal, is valued for its aromatic properties and traditional uses in incense and medicine.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
