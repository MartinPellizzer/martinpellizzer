Phreatia platyclinoides

Phreatia platyclinoides, commonly known as the flat-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or near water sources. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The orchid's morphology is adapted to its humid, tropical habitat, making it a resilient and ecologically significant species.
