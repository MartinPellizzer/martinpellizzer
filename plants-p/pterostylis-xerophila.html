Pterostylis xerophila

Pterostylis xerophila, commonly known as the narrow-leaved greenhood, is an orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a slender, often hairy stem and linear leaves. The plant produces a single, greenish-brown flower with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and wetlands.

Pterostylis xerophila is known for its unique morphology and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
