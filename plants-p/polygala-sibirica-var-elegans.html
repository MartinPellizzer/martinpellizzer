Polygala sibirica var. elegans

Polygala sibirica var. elegans, commonly known as the Siberian milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow from a taproot system, and its leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
