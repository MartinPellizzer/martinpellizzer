Pinus ponderosa var. scopulorum

Pinus ponderosa var. scopulorum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada pine, is a variant of the ponderosa pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length. The cones of this species are usually reddish-brown and can reach up to 12 centimeters in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high elevations.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and is an important component of the forest ecosystems in its native habitat.
