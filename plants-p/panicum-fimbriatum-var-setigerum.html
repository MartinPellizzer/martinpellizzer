Panicum fimbriatum var. setigerum

Panicum fimbriatum var. setigerum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces fluffy, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance, especially during the flowering season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
