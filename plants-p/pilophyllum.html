Pilophyllum

Pilophyllum, commonly known as the silky fern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, often greenish or reddish in color.

Pilophyllum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
