Penstemon fremontii var. subglaber

Penstemon fremontii var. subglaber, commonly known as Fremont's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
