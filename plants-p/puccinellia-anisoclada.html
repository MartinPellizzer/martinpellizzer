Puccinellia anisoclada

Puccinellia anisoclada, commonly known as narrowleaf saltbush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, making it well-adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains tiny flowers that produce seeds dispersed by wind.

Puccinellia anisoclada plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for livestock in its native habitats.
