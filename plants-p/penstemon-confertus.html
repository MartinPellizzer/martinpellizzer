Penstemon confertus

Penstemon confertus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon confertus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to support native pollinators.
