Pilocereus glaucochrous

Pilocereus glaucochrous, commonly known as the white night blooming cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and produces large, white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
