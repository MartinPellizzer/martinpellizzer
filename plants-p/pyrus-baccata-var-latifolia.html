Pyrus baccata var. latifolia

Pyrus baccata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically pale green on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red or orange berries in the autumn. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream sides.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
