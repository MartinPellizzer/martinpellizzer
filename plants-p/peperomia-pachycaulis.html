Peperomia pachycaulis

Peperomia pachycaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and oval-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, which are followed by small, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia pachycaulis is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its attractive, compact growth habit.
