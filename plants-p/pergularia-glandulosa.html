Pergularia glandulosa

Pergularia glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular pergularia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, glandular leaves and clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
