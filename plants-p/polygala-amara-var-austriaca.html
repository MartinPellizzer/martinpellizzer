Polygala amara var. austriaca

Polygala amara var. austriaca, commonly known as Austrian bitter vetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its leaves are opposite and pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional herbal medicine.
