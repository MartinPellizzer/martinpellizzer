Phlox subulata var. brittonii

Phlox subulata var. brittonii, commonly known as Sierra phlox, is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, bright pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has needle-like, silver-gray foliage that is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun. It is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often used in xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
