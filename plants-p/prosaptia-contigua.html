Prosaptia contigua

Prosaptia contigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lanceolate, with a hairy texture that aids in moisture retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
