Prunus imperialis

Prunus imperialis, commonly known as the imperial cherry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, narrowly elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries. Prunus imperialis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
