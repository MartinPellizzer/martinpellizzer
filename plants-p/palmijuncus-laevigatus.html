Palmijuncus laevigatus

Palmijuncus laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved palm, is a unique species of palm native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into drupes, which are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
