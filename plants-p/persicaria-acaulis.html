Persicaria acaulis

Persicaria acaulis, commonly known as the barren persicaria, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants like buckwheat and knotweed. This species is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
