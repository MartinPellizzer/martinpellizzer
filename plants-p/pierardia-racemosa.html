Pierardia racemosa

Pierardia racemosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, seed-filled capsules. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments.

Pierardia racemosa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its drought tolerance.
