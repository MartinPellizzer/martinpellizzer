Panicum ischaemum subsp. sanguinale

Panicum ischaemum subsp.

sanguinale, commonly known as crimson beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, red or purple flowers, which give it its distinctive appearance.

This species is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
