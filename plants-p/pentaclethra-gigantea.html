Pentaclethra gigantea

Pentaclethra gigantea, commonly known as the giant pentaclethra, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-shaped crown.

Its fruits are woody pods that contain several seeds, and the plant is valued for its timber and ecological role in its native habitats.
