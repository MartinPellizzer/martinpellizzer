Polygonum botuliforme

Polygonum botuliforme, commonly known as the swamp knotweed, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and bracts. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense colonies. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by specialized leaf structures called phyllaries.

Polygonum botuliforme plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
