Pithecellobium utile

Pithecellobium utile, commonly known as the useful pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces edible pods that contain small, sweet, and crunchy seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it a valuable plant for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
