Physostemon ambiguus

Physostemon ambiguus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Physostemon ambiguus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
