Pennisetum setaceum subsp. asperifolium

Pennisetum setaceum subsp.

asperifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf fountain grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its subspecies name.

The plant produces tall, slender flower spikes that are often pink or red in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
