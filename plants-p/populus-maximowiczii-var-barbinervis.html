Populus maximowiczii var. barbinervis

Populus maximowiczii var. barbinervis, commonly known as the Chinese poplar or the barb-nerved poplar, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive barbed venation pattern. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by small, woody fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and mountainous areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
