Potamogeton × subnitens

Potamogeton × subnitens, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a species of pondweed native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a hybrid between Potamogeton acutifolius and Potamogeton natans, characterized by its slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often slightly curled. The plant typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and light penetration.
