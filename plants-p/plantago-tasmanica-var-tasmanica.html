Plantago tasmanica var. tasmanica

Plantago tasmanica var. tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian plantain, is a perennial herb native to Tasmania, Australia.

It features erect, slender stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky outcrops.

Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild astringent properties.
