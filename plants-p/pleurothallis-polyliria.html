Pleurothallis polyliria

Pleurothallis polyliria, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered blooms with intricate petal structures, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. It is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by enthusiasts, though it remains relatively uncommon in horticultural trade.
