Pycreus sanguinolentus var. uniceps

Pycreus sanguinolentus var. uniceps, commonly known as the Crimson Vein Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vivid crimson petals with intricate, vein-like patterns that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring, producing a delicate fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern.
