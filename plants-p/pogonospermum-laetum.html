Pogonospermum laetum

Pogonospermum laetum, commonly known as the golden tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and serrated leaves, which are typically found in moist, open habitats. The plant produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
