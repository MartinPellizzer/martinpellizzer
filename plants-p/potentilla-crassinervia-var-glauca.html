Potentilla crassinervia var. glauca

Potentilla crassinervia var. glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive blue-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
