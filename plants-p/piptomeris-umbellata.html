Piptomeris umbellata

Piptomeris umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella leaf or Indian umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Piptomeris umbellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
