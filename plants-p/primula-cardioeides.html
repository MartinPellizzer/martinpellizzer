Primula cardioeides

Primula cardioeides, commonly known as the heartleaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in early spring and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage is thick and leathery, providing a dense ground cover.

Primula cardioeides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
