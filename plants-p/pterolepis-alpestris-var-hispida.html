Pterolepis alpestris var. hispida

Pterolepis alpestris var. hispida, commonly known as the rough alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in open alpine meadows.

This plant is adapted to cold climates and is an important component of alpine flora.
