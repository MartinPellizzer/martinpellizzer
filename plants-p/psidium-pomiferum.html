Psidium pomiferum

Psidium pomiferum, commonly known as the Natal plum or Brazilian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns from green to red or yellow, depending on the variety.
