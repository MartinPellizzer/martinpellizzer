Piper jacquemontianum

Piper jacquemontianum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Jacquemont's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often found growing in humid, shaded environments along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.
