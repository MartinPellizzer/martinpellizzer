Pieris formosana

Pieris formosana, commonly known as the Formosan azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Pieris formosana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
