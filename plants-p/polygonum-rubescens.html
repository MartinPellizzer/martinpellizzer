Polygonum rubescens

Polygonum rubescens, commonly known as red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes various species known for their distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Polygonum rubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
