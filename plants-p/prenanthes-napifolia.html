Prenanthes napifolia

Prenanthes napifolia, commonly known as the roundleaf watercress, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are round or kidney-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its small white flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late summer.
