Penstemon arkansanus var. pubescens

Penstemon arkansanus var. pubescens, commonly known as the Arkansas beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
