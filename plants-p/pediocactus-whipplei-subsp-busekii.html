Pediocactus whipplei subsp. busekii

Pediocactus whipplei subsp.

busekii, commonly known as the Busekii barrel cactus, is a compact cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 inches.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a dense covering of white to grayish hairs.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
