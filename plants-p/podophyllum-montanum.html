Podophyllum montanum

Podophyllum montanum, commonly known as mountain mayapple, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a distinctive, umbrella-shaped foliage with large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. The plant produces a showy white flower with five petals, followed by a green, fleshy fruit that turns red when ripe. Its roots contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

This plant thrives in shady, moist woodlands and is often found in forest understories.
