Potentilla frigida f. vegetior

Potentilla frigida f. vegetior, commonly known as the Arctic cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of polar and mountain ecosystems.
