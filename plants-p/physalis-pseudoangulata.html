Physalis pseudoangulata

Physalis pseudoangulata, commonly known as the angular husk tomato, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its inflated, papery calyces that enclose the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when ripe. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in dry, sunny environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
