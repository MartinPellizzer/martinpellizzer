Pandanus isalicus

Pandanus isalicus, commonly known as the island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to salty, humid environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
