Potamogeton perfoliatus f. ovatus

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often slightly notched at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes, and it thrives in slow-moving or still waters. Its roots help stabilize sediments, making it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands where it plays a role in maintaining water quality and providing habitat for aquatic life.
