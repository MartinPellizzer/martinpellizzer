Podocarpus oleifolius subsp. columbianus

Podocarpus oleifolius subsp.

columbianus, commonly known as the Colombian podocarp, is a species of evergreen tree native to parts of South America. It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and conical shape.

The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
