Polygonum libanoticum

Polygonum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and sometimes edible parts. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish tint, especially when mature.

Polygonum libanoticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
