Polythecium kirkii

Polythecium kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's polythecium, is a member of the family Polytechiaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of tiny, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, known as polythecia, are unique to the genus and consist of multiple sporangia arranged in a cup-like structure.

Polythecium kirkii is primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.
