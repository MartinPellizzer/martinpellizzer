Pogonatherum crinitum

Pogonatherum crinitum, commonly known as the hairy grass or tawny grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Pogonatherum crinitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
