Pandanus kaernbachii

Pandanus kaernbachii, commonly known as the Hainan screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a spiny, three-lobed structure that contains edible seeds.

Pandanus kaernbachii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
