Pericallis malvifolia subsp. malvifolia

Pericallis malvifolia subsp.

malvifolia, commonly known as the Malvifolia pericallis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton plants.

The plant is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
