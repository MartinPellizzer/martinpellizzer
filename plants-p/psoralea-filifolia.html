Psoralea filifolia

Psoralea filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, filament-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils.

Psoralea filifolia is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
