Polygala mariana

Polygala mariana, commonly known as the Florida milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and swampy areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are alternate and often have a hairy texture.

Polygala mariana plays an important role in its native ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
