Pimelea hispida var. lanata

Pimelea hispida var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
