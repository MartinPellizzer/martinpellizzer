Pleurothallis corysta

Pleurothallis corysta, commonly known as the coral orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Pleurothallis corysta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
