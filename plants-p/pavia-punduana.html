Pavia punduana

Pavia punduana, commonly known as the Puduana or Puduana tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow, followed by edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
