Parkia decussata

Parkia decussata, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and distinctive yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its timber and traditional medicinal uses.
