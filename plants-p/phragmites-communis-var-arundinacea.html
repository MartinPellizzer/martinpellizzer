Phragmites communis var. arundinacea

Phragmites communis var. arundinacea, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. This plant is highly adaptable, often forming dense stands in marshes, lakes, and along shorelines. Its rhizomes spread extensively, allowing it to dominate aquatic habitats.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
