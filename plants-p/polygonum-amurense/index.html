Polygonum Amurense

Amur knotweed, scientifically known as Polygonum amurense, is a hardy perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. This species thrives in moist environments and is often found near waterways and in wetlands. Despite its ornamental appeal, it can become invasive in certain regions. Its adaptability and resilience make it a subject of both admiration and caution in ecological studies.
