Payrola

Payrola, commonly known as the "wild lettuce," is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features serrated, alternate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Payrola is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

It has a long history of use in traditional medicine for its mild sedative and analgesic properties.
