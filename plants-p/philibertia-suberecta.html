Philibertia suberecta

Philibertia suberecta, commonly known as the upright philibertia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.
