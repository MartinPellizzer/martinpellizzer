Potentilla asterotricha

Potentilla asterotricha, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its stems and leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Potentilla asterotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
