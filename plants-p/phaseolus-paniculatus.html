Phaseolus paniculatus

Phaseolus paniculatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and large, showy flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are widely cultivated for food. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.

It is an important crop in many tropical and subtropical regions.
