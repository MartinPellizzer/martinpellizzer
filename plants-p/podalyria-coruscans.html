Podalyria coruscans

Podalyria coruscans, commonly known as the glittering podalyria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Podalyria coruscans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
