Parabignonia chodatii

Parabignonia chodatii, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
