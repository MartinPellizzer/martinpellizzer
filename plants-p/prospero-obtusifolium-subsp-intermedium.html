Prospero obtusifolium subsp. intermedium

Prospero obtusifolium subsp.

intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate broadleaf prospero, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
