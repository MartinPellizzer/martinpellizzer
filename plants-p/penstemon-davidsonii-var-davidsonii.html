Penstemon davidsonii var. davidsonii

Penstemon davidsonii var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
