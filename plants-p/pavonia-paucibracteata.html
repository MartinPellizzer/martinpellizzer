Pavonia paucibracteata

Pavonia paucibracteata, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bright red to orange-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Pavonia paucibracteata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
