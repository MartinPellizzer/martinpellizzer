Palaua pusilla

Palaua pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf palaua, is a small herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its botanical features make it well adapted to moist, shaded environments in its natural habitat.

Palaua pusilla is often found in forest understories and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
