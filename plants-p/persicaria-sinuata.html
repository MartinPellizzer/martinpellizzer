Persicaria sinuata

Persicaria sinuata, commonly known as wavering willow or redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems that often trail along the ground or climb. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive wavy margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.
