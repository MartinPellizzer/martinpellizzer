Polygonum alismifolium

Polygonum alismifolium, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant has slender, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in spike-like clusters.

Polygonum alismifolium is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its edible leaves and nutritional value.
