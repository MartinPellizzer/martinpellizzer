Peperomia tairukouensis

Peperomia tairukouensis, commonly known as the Tairukou peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other genera such as Piper and Peperomia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Peperomia tairukouensis is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
