Pyrus filipes

Pyrus filipes, commonly known as the Filipes pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, particularly in the Mediterranean area. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-like fruit, is typically yellow or green when ripe.

Pyrus filipes is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
