Philibertia cumanensis

Philibertia cumanensis, commonly known as the Cumanese filbert, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters.

Philibertia cumanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
