Pandanus integriapicis

Pandanus integriapicis, commonly known as the Bismarck palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruit that resembles a pineapple in appearance. Its leaves are fibrous and have been traditionally used for weaving and thatching.

Pandanus integriapicis is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
