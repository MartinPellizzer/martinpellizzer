Piper dunlapii

Piper dunlapii, commonly known as the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Piper genus, which includes many species of pepper plants found in tropical regions. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery scent when crushed.

Piper dunlapii thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens.
