Pterocleome stylosa

Pterocleome stylosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf pterocleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are adapted for dispersal by wind or animals.

Pterocleome stylosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
