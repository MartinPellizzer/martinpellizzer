Polystachya cubensis

Polystachya cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate patterns that attract pollinators like bees and moths.

Due to habitat loss, Polystachya cubensis is considered threatened, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
