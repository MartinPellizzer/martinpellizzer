Pediocactus warnockii

Pediocactus warnockii, commonly known as the Warnock's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, pale spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
