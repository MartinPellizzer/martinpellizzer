Penstemon congestus

Penstemon congestus, commonly known as the dense penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Plantaginaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces numerous flower clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon congestus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
