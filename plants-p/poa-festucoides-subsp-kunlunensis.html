Poa festucoides subsp. kunlunensis

Poa festucoides subsp.

kunlunensis, commonly known as the Kunlun mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Tibetan Plateau and surrounding regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous diminutive blue to purple flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its adaptation to extreme climatic conditions.
