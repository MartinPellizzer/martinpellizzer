Plectranthus inflexus

Plectranthus inflexus, commonly known as the woolly plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus inflexus is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
