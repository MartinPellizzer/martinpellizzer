Ptilotus distans subsp. capensis

Ptilotus distans subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape spinifex, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on sandy or rocky soils. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize harsh landscapes.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
