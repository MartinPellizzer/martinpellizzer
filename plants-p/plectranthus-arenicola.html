Plectranthus arenicola

Plectranthus arenicola, commonly known as the sand plectranthus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The leaves are fleshy and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
