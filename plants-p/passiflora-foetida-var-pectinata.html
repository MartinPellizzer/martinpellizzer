Passiflora foetida var. pectinata

Passiflora foetida var. pectinata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with intricate patterns of blue, purple, and white. The leaves are large, palmately lobed, and covered in fine, silky hairs that give it its common name. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
