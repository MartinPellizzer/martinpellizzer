Piper globulistigmum

Piper globulistigmum, commonly known as the globular pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
