Piper uvitanum

Piper uvitanum, commonly known as the Uvita pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas of humid lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Piper uvitanum is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
