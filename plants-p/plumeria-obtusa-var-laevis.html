Plumeria obtusa var. laevis

Plumeria obtusa var. laevis, commonly known as the frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy bark and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
