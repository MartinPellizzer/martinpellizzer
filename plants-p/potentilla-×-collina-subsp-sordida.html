Potentilla × collina subsp. sordida

Potentilla × collina subsp.

sordida, commonly known as the sordid cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dark green, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a valued addition to native plant gardens.
