Phyllanthus radicans

Phyllanthus radicans, commonly known as the stonebreaker or inchplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a trailing or climbing plant with opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits, and it is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it adaptable to different environments.
