Pandanus tahitensis var. exiguus

Pandanus tahitensis var. exiguus, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that eventually develop into edible fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and cultural significance in Polynesian communities.
