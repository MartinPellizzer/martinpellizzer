Pilosella × viridifolia

Pilosella × viridifolia, commonly known as the greenleaf mouse-eared chickweed, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Compositae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
