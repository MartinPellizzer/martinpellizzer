Perotis laxifolia

Perotis laxifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf perotis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Perotis laxifolia produces small yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
