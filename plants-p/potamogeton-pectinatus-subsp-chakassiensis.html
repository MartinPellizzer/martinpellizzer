Potamogeton pectinatus subsp. chakassiensis

Potamogeton pectinatus subsp.

chakassiensis, commonly known as the Chakassian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Asia. It typically grows in slow-moving or still waters, such as lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its morphology and growth habits make it well adapted to its submerged environment.
