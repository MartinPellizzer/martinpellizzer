Peliosanthes albida

Peliosanthes albida, commonly known as the white sand orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the late spring or early summer. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained, sandy soil, making it a challenging species to cultivate. Due to its limited natural habitat and threats from habitat destruction, it is considered endangered.
