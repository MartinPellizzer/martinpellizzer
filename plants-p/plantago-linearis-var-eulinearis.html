Plantago linearis var. eulinearis

Plantago linearis var. eulinearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and it is known for its medicinal properties.
