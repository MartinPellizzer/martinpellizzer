Phlomoides cuneata

Phlomoides cuneata, commonly known as narrowleaf phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color.
