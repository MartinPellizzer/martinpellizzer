Pelargonium familiare

Pelargonium familiare, commonly known as the common geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
