Pandanus drakei

Pandanus drakei, commonly known as the Drake's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters at the top of the plant. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to grow optimally.
