Porophyllum lineare var. corymbosum

Porophyllum lineare var. corymbosum, commonly known as the clustered blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and blue to purple in color.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
