Phacelia namatoides

Phacelia namatoides, commonly known as the desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Phacelia namatoides is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
