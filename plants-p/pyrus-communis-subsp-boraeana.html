Pyrus communis subsp. boraeana

Pyrus communis subsp.

boraeana, commonly known as the alpine pear, is a subspecies native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and Carpathian Mountains. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The fruit is typically a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
