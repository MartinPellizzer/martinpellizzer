Peperomia copeyana

Peperomia copeyana, commonly known as the Copeyana peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features succulent, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
