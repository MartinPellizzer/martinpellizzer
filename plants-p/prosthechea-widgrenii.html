Prosthechea widgrenii

Prosthechea widgrenii, commonly known as the Widgren's orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on the upper portions of the stems. Prosthechea widgrenii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires high air humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique morphology and delicate appearance make it a sought-after specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
