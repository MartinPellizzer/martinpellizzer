Phyllostegia pluriflora

Phyllostegia pluriflora, commonly known as the hairy phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, purple or pink tubular flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Phyllostegia pluriflora is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
