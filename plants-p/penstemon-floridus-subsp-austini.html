Penstemon floridus subsp. austini

Penstemon floridus subsp.

austini, commonly known as Austin's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and rocky areas.

The plant features showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
