Pterocarpus esculentus

Pterocarpus esculentus, commonly known as the African shea tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its flat, bipinnate leaves and yellow to orange flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain a high-quality oil used in cosmetics and food products. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties.

The plant is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in traditional agroforestry systems.
