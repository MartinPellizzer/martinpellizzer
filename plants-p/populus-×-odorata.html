Populus × odorata

Populus × odorata, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces catkins as its primary floral structures, which are wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

It is often planted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
