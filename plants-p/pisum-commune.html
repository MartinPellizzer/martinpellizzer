Pisum commune

Pisum commune, commonly known as the common pea, is a flowering plant in the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, green pods that contain several seeds, which are the edible part commonly used in cooking. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a distinctive pea shape.

Pisum commune is cultivated for its nutritional value and is an important crop in many regions around the world.
