Polygonum hamiltonii

Polygonum hamiltonii, commonly known as Hamilton's knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other species like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has alternate, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish stem and produces small, greenish flowers in clusters.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species.
