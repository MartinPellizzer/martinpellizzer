Polygala adscendens

Polygala adscendens, commonly known as the ascending milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish in color, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
