Prismatocarpus interruptus

Prismatocarpus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted prism tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ridged, and flaky bark, which gives it its common name. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
