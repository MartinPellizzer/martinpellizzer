Pelargonium cucullatum subsp. cucullatum

Pelargonium cucullatum subsp.

cucullatum, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a species native to South Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color and bloom in late summer to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
