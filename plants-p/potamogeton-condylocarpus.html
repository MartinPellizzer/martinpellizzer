Potamogeton condylocarpus

Potamogeton condylocarpus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, often with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface during the summer months. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

Potamogeton condylocarpus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
