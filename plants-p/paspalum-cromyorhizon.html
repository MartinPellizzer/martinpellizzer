Paspalum cromyorhizon

Paspalum cromyorhizon, commonly known as the narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive bluish sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its growth habit makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
