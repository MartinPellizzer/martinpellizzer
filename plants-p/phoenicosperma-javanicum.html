Phoenicosperma javanicum

Phoenicosperma javanicum, commonly known as the Java palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered fruits, each containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
