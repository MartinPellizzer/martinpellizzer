Pelargonium × irbyana

Pelargonium × irbyana, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and foliage.

Pelargonium × irbyana thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight to flourish.
