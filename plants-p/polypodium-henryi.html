Polypodium henryi

Polypodium henryi, commonly known as Henry's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, lance-shaped fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Polypodium henryi is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
