Ptisana pellucida

Ptisana pellucida, commonly known as the translucent grass or ghost grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Ptisana pellucida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, exhibiting succulence in its leaves to store water.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine by local communities.
