Phylica ledifolia

Phylica ledifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf phylica, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Phylica ledifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
