Passiflora cobanensis

Passiflora cobanensis, commonly known as the Coban passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a blend of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts worldwide.
