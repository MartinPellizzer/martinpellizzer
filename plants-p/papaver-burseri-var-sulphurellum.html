Papaver burseri var. sulphurellum

Papaver burseri var. sulphurellum, commonly known as the sulphur poppy, is a striking annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are surrounded by a distinctive orange-red corona. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators with their vibrant color and nectar.
