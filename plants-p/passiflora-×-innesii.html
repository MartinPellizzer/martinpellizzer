Passiflora × innesii

Passiflora × innesii, commonly known as the Innes passionflower, is a hybrid species within the Passifloraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant colors and unique floral structure. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora × innesii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
