Persicaria fistulosa

Persicaria fistulosa, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hollow, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Persicaria fistulosa thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
