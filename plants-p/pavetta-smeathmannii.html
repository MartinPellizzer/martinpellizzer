Pavetta smeathmannii

Pavetta smeathmannii, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
