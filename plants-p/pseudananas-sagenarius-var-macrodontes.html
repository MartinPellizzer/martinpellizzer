Pseudananas sagenarius var. macrodontes

Pseudananas sagenarius var. macrodontes, commonly known as the giant pineapple plant, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, stiff, spiky leaves that form a rosette shape, creating a unique and dramatic appearance. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears small, clustered flowers, which are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor settings with proper care.
