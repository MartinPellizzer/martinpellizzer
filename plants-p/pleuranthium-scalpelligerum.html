Pleuranthium scalpelligerum

Pleuranthium scalpelligerum, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are oval-shaped and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Pleuranthium scalpelligerum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
