Papaver × kobayashii

Papaver × kobayashii, commonly known as the Japanese poppy, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Papaver somniferum (opium poppy) and Papaver japonicum (Japanese poppy).

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, preferring temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to the landscape.
