Plectranthus setschwanensis

Plectranthus setschwanensis, commonly known as the Chinese nettle or Chinese mint, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. It is often grown as a herb for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Plectranthus setschwanensis thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.
