Piper bartlingianum

Piper bartlingianum, commonly known as the Bartling's pepper or the Hawaiian pepper plant, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional Hawaiian cuisine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
