Piper strigillosum

Piper strigillosum, commonly known as the roughleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered white or greenish flowers. Its fruits are small, three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

Piper strigillosum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
