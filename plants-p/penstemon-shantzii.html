Penstemon shantzii

Penstemon shantzii, commonly known as Shantz's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. This species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for xeriscaping.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
