Pelargonium aphanoides

Pelargonium aphanoides, commonly known as the ghost geranium, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that are often obscured by its dense, velvety foliage, giving it its common name. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically heart-shaped or rounded. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pelargonium aphanoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats in gardens.
