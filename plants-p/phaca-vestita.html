Phaca vestita

Phaca vestita, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. Phaca vestita is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
