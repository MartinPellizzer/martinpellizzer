Pseudodissochaeta assamica

Pseudodissochaeta assamica, commonly known as the Assam slender grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is typically found in moist, grassy habitats across the northeastern regions of India.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
