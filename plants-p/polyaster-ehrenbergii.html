Polyaster ehrenbergii

Polyaster ehrenbergii, commonly known as the desert aster, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding color to the desert landscape.

Polyaster ehrenbergii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
