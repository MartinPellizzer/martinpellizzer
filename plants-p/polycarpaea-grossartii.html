Polycarpaea grossartii

Polycarpaea grossartii, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake.

The species is also notable for its ability to produce multiple flowering shoots from a single stem.
