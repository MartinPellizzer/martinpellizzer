Pleurothallis jordanensis

Pleurothallis jordanensis, commonly known as the Jordan orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often greenish or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Pleurothallis jordanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
