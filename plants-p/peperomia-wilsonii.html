Peperomia wilsonii

Peperomia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and low maintenance requirements, it is well-suited for beginners and space-limited environments.
