Piper gualeanum

Piper gualeanum, commonly known as the Gualeanum pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's botanical structure includes opposite, ovate leaves and a distinctive, aromatic fruit.
