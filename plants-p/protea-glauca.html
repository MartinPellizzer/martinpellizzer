Protea glauca

Protea glauca, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pale blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in the region.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
