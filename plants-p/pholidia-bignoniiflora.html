Pholidia bignoniiflora

Pholidia bignoniiflora, commonly known as the Bignoniaceae-flowered false bignonia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Bignoniaceae family, which includes many ornamental and woody plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, clustered in racemes, and emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

It produces woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
