Penstemon albidus

Penstemon albidus, commonly known as the white beard-tongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon albidus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
