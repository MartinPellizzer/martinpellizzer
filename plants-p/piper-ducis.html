Piper ducis

Piper ducis, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. These berries contain a spicy compound similar to capsaicin, giving them a sharp, peppery flavor.

Piper ducis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
