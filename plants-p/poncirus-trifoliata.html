Poncirus trifoliata

Poncirus trifoliata, commonly known as the citronella tree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible but often used for ornamental purposes. It is closely related to citrus plants and is known for its hardiness in cold climates.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
