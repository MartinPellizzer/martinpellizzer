Polygonum aviculare var. fusco-ochreatum

Polygonum aviculare var. fusco-ochreatum, commonly known as the red-rooted smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
