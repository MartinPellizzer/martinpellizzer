Paspalidium globoideum

Paspalidium globoideum, commonly known as the globular grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence that resembles a globe, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalidium globoideum is adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a resilient species in arid environments.
