Persea campii

Persea campii, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully mature. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Persea campii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.
