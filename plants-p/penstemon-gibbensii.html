Penstemon gibbensii

Penstemon gibbensii, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon gibbensii is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
