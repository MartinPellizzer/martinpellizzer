Persicaria attenuata subsp. pulchra

Persicaria attenuata subsp.

pulchra, commonly known as the slender smartweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as wetlands, ditches, and stream banks.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red in color. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its adaptability to wet environments makes it a common sight in various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
