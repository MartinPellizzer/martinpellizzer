Podocarpus spicatus

Podocarpus spicatus, commonly known as the silver pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in flat sprays, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Podocarpus spicatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
