Prunus jamasakura subvar. serrulata

Prunus jamasakura subvar. serrulata, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with serrated leaves, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The leaves are ovate with finely serrated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It plays a significant role in cultural symbolism, particularly during spring festivals in Japan.
