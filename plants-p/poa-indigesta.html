Poa indigesta

Poa indigesta, commonly known as the indigestible grass, is a species of grass native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant is notable for its tough, fibrous leaves, which contribute to its name due to their difficulty in digestion by livestock. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native habitats.

However, its high silica content makes it less palatable and potentially harmful to grazing animals in large quantities.
