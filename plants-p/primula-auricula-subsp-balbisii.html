Primula auricula subsp. balbisii

Primula auricula subsp.

balbisii, commonly known as the Balbis's auricula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
