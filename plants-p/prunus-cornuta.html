Prunus cornuta

Prunus cornuta, commonly known as the Chinese wild apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Prunus cornuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blossoms.
