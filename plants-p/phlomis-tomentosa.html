Phlomis tomentosa

Phlomis tomentosa, commonly known as wooly betony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered spikes of purple or pink flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often grayish-green in color.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
