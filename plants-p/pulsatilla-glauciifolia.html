Pulsatilla glauciifolia

Pulsatilla glauciifolia, commonly known as the alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and hairy, basal leaves that are palmately divided. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
