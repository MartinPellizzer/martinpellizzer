Primula pacheri

Primula pacheri, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow, usually blooming in early spring. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula pacheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
