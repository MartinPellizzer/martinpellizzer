Potamogeton obtusus

Potamogeton obtusus, commonly known as broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are broad at the base and taper to a point, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to water clarity by stabilizing sediments.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.
