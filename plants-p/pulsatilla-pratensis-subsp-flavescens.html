Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. flavescens

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, preferring well-drained conditions. Its leaves are palmately divided and have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The subspecies is distinguished by its lighter floral color compared to other pulsatilla species.
