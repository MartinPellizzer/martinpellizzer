Potamogeton proteus

Potamogeton proteus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in dense clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively through rhizomes. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in lakes and ponds.
