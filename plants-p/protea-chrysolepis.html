Protea chrysolepis

Protea chrysolepis, commonly known as the yellow protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to yellowish flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of bracts. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for cut flowers and garden ornamental use.

Protea chrysolepis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as birds and insects.
