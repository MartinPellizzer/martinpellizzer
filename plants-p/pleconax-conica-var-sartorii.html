Pleconax conica var. sartorii

Pleconax conica var. sartorii, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red fruits. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine, while the fruits are sometimes consumed as a vegetable.
