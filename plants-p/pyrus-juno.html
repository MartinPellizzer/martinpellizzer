Pyrus juno

Pyrus juno, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, white, juicy fruits in late summer. Its fruit is highly valued for its fragrance and taste, often used in culinary and ornamental purposes.

Pyrus juno is also popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
