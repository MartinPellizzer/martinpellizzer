Potentilla recta var. leucolophias

Potentilla recta var. leucolophias, commonly known as the white-stemmed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often silver-gray stems and small, five-petaled white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a distinctive lobed or serrated margin.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
