Phyllostachys varioauriculata var. glabrata

Phyllostachys varioauriculata var. glabrata, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the varioauriculata species within the genus Phyllostachys.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching culms that exhibit a distinctive silvery-blue sheen on the nodes. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture, contributing to its elegant appearance. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its attractive foliage and growth habit.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
