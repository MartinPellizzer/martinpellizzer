Potentilla alba f. reducta

Potentilla alba f. reducta, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a compact, low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate floral display to its foliage. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a soft, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
