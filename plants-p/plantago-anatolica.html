Plantago anatolica

Plantago anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian plantain, is a flowering plant species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which are clustered in dense clusters. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
