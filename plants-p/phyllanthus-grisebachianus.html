Phyllanthus grisebachianus

Phyllanthus grisebachianus, commonly known as the gray-haired phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
