Papaya vulgaris

Papaya vulgaris, commonly known as the papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and striking yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit that is rich in vitamins and enzymes like papain. The fruit is typically harvested when it is fully mature and soft, making it a popular fruit in many cuisines.

Its rapid growth and high yield make it an important crop in tropical agriculture.
