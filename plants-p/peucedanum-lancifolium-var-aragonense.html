Peucedanum lancifolium var. aragonense

Peucedanum lancifolium var. aragonense, commonly known as the Aragonian cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and finely divided, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are narrow, ribbed schizocarps, typical of the Apiaceae family.

It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
