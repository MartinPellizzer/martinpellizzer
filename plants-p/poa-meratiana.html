Poa meratiana

Poa meratiana, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Poa meratiana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
