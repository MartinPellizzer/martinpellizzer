Potentilla gaudinii subvar. aprica

Potentilla gaudinii subvar. aprica, commonly known as the Alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine gardens and rockeries.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler climates.
