Pogostemon perilloides

Pogostemon perilloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf betel plant, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The small, tubular flowers are usually purple or pink, and they attract various pollinators.
