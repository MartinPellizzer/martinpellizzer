Parrya microcarpa

Parrya microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf parrya, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of tiny, white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Parrya microcarpa is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
