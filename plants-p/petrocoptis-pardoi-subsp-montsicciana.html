Petrocoptis pardoi subsp. montsicciana

Petrocoptis pardoi subsp.

montsicciana, commonly known as the Alpine rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in a fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with distinctive cruciform flowers.
