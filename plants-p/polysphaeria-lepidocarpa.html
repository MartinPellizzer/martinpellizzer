Polysphaeria lepidocarpa

Polysphaeria lepidocarpa, commonly known as the glossy globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, globe-shaped flower heads that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, bead-like fruits that are covered in a waxy, protective layer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Polysphaeria lepidocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
