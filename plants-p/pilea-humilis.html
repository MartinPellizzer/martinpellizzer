Pilea humilis

Pilea humilis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are borne on short stalks. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it ideal for indoor cultivation.
