Primula darialica subsp. ossetica

Primula darialica subsp.

ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
