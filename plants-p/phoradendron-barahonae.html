Phoradendron barahonae

Phoradendron barahonae, commonly known as the Bahamian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of native trees in the Bahamas.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it photosynthesizes for additional energy. Its berries are consumed by birds, which aid in seed dispersal.

This species is an important part of the local ecosystem, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife.
