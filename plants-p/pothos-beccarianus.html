Pothos beccarianus

Pothos beccarianus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is known for its ability to purify indoor air.

Pothos beccarianus is a popular choice for hanging baskets and vertical gardens due to its trailing growth habit.
