Parathesis crassipes

Parathesis crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf water plant, is a floating aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are typically green and smooth on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Parathesis crassipes is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize water surfaces and provide habitat for aquatic life.
