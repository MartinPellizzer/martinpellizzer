Pyrethrum multicaule

Pyrethrum multicaule, commonly known as the African pyrethrum, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers contain pyrethrin compounds, which are naturally occurring insecticides.

Pyrethrum multicaule is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in organic pest control.
