Papaver ameristophyllum

Papaver ameristophyllum, commonly known as the American poppy, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a simple, erect stem with lobed leaves that are typically green and hairy. Its flowers consist of a single layer of petals surrounding a central cluster of stamens.

Papaver ameristophyllum is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
