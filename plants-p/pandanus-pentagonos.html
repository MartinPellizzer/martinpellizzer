Pandanus pentagonos

Pandanus pentagonos, commonly known as the pentagonal pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces a large, fragrant inflorescence that bears small, white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.
