Passiflora foetida var. ciliata

Passiflora foetida var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with serrated edges and dense, hairy covering. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in shades of white, yellow, and red. Its fruit is a rough, spiny capsule that matures into a reddish-purple color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
