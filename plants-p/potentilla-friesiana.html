Potentilla friesiana

Potentilla friesiana, commonly known as the Friesian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are often covered with fine hairs.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and blooms.
