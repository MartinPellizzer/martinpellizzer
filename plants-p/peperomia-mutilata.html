Peperomia mutilata

Peperomia mutilata, commonly known as the Shining Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and tolerance for dry conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it an ideal choice for container gardening and desktop displays.
