Phlox amplifolia

Phlox amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox amplifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
