Pycreus fugax

Pycreus fugax, commonly known as the transient pycreus, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, ephemeral blooms that appear briefly during the wet season. The plant's foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

Due to its short blooming period and limited distribution, Pycreus fugax remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
