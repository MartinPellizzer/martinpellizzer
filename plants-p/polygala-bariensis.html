Polygala bariensis

Polygala bariensis, commonly known as the Bornean milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color and are clustered in racemes, making it an attractive plant for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
