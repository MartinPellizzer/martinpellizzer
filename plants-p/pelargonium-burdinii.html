Pelargonium burdinii

Pelargonium burdinii, commonly known as the Burdin's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
