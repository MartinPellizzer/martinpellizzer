Polygonum scabridum

Polygonum scabridum, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its stems are often reddish or purple and can become woody with age.

Polygonum scabridum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
