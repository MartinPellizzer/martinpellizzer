Pyrethrum halleri subsp. allionii

Pyrethrum halleri subsp.

allionii, commonly known as the alpine pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, which is used in organic pest control. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
