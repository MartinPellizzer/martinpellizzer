Primula latifolia subsp. cynoglossifolia

Primula latifolia subsp.

cynoglossifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
