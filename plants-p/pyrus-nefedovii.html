Pyrus nefedovii

Pyrus nefedovii, commonly known as the Nefedov's pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in Kazakhstan and southern Siberia. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves, which are ovate to elliptic in shape, and its clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are pears that are typically small, yellowish, and somewhat bitter when mature.

Pyrus nefedovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its hardiness and unique appearance.
