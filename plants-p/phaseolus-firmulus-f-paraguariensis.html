Phaseolus firmulus f. paraguariensis

Phaseolus firmulus f. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan dwarf bean, is a small, herbaceous annual plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that give way to oval, smooth pods containing small, round seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

Due to its adaptability and nutritional value, it is also of interest for agricultural and ecological studies.
