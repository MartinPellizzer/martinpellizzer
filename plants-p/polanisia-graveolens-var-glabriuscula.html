Polanisia graveolens var. glabriuscula

Polanisia graveolens var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved sweet pepper, is a variant of the sweet pepper species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and small, round fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its botanical features include a bushy habit with branching stems and a high tolerance for direct sunlight.

The fruit's mild flavor makes it a popular choice for culinary use in various cuisines.
