Parinari versicolor

Parinari versicolor, commonly known as the variable parinari, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fruits that are initially green and turn red when mature. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types makes it a common species in tropical and subtropical regions.
