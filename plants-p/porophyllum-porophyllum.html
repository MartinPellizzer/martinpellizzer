Porophyllum porophyllum

Porophyllum porophyllum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its blue or purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Porophyllum porophyllum is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
