Primula pengzhouensis

Primula pengzhouensis, commonly known as the Pengzhou primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Primula pengzhouensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
