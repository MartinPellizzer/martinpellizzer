Plenckia populnea var. ovata

Plenckia populnea var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf poplar, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, broad leaves that are typically green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or along waterways.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and its role in ecological systems.
