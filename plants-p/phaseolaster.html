Phaseolaster

Phaseolaster, commonly known as the "moonflower vine," is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces striking white flowers that open at night. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators like moths.

The fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.
