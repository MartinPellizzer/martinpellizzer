Palicourea glandulicalyx

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are clustered in inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
