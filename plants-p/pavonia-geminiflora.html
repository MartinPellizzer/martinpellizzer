Pavonia geminiflora

Pavonia geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered pavonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia geminiflora is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wetlands.
