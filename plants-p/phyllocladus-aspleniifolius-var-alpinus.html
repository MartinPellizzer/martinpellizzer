Phyllocladus aspleniifolius var. alpinus

Phyllocladus aspleniifolius var. alpinus, commonly known as the alpine cliff-frond, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its modified leaves, known as phylloclades, which function as stems.

It is native to high-altitude regions of New Zealand and thrives in rocky, nutrient-poor environments. The plant has a distinctive appearance with fern-like fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.
