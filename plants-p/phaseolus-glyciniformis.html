Phaseolus glyciniformis

Phaseolus glyciniformis, commonly known as the evergreen bean or climbing hyacinth bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a versatile crop in agricultural systems.
