Pinanga albescens

Pinanga albescens, commonly known as the white-veined bamboo palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically light green with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
