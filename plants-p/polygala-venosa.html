Polygala venosa

Polygala venosa, commonly known as the veined milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky soils. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, linear leaflets.

Polygala venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
