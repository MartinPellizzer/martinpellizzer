Passiflora caerulea var. caeruleoracemosa

Passiflora caerulea var. caeruleoracemosa, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins. The plant produces edible, blue-black berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
