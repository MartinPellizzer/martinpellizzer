Pittosporum glabrum

Pittosporum glabrum, commonly known as the silky pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Pittosporum glabrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
