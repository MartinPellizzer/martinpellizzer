Penstemon thurberi

Penstemon thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon thurberi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
