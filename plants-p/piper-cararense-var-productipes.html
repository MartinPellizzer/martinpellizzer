Piper cararense var. productipes

Piper cararense var. productipes, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
