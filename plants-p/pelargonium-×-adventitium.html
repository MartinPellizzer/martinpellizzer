Pelargonium × adventitium

Pelargonium × adventitium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various pelargonium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
