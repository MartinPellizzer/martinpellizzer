Polypodium perludens

Polypodium perludens, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often as an epiphyte. Its leaves are usually bright green and have a leathery texture, with a distinctive golden hue on the underside.

Polypodium perludens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
