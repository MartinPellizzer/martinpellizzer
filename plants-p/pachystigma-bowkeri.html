Pachystigma bowkeri

Pachystigma bowkeri, commonly known as the Cape mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Pachystigma bowkeri is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
