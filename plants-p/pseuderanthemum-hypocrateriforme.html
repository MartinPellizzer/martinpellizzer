Pseuderanthemum hypocrateriforme

Pseuderanthemum hypocrateriforme, commonly known as the spathaceous pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial with erect stems and opposite leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often surrounded by brightly colored bracts.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
