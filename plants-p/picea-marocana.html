Picea marocana

Picea marocana, commonly known as the Moroccan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Atlas Mountains of Morocco.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needles, and distinctive blue-gray bark. The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds enclosed in woody scales. It thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often growing in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in the region's flora.
