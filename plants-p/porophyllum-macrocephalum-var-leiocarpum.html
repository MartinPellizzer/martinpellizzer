Porophyllum macrocephalum var. leiocarpum

Porophyllum macrocephalum var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the whitehead plant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of tubular and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, exhibiting a sprawling or upright growth habit. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a fuzzy texture, and the flowers bloom in clusters, producing small white to pale yellow blooms.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
