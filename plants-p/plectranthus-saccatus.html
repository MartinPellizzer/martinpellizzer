Plectranthus saccatus

Plectranthus saccatus, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus saccatus is also valued for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine.
