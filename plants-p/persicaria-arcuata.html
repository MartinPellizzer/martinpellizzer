Persicaria arcuata

Persicaria arcuata, commonly known as the curled smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. Persicaria arcuata thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other wet habitats.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
