Petamenes vittigera

Petamenes vittigera, commonly known as the spotted petamene, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or streaked pattern on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a slight fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
