Piper damiaoshanense

Piper damiaoshanense, commonly known as the Damoshan pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Damoshan region in Yunnan province, and is known for its distinctive morphology. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
