Prunus tatsienensis var. adenophora

Prunus tatsienensis var. adenophora, commonly known as the Chinese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips.

It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance in traditional Chinese gardens.
