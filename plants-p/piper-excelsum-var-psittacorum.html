Piper excelsum var. psittacorum

Piper excelsum var. psittacorum, commonly known as the golden bird's nest vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that resemble small nests, hence its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
