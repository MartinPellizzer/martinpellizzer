Pharus brasiliensis var. latifolius

Pharus brasiliensis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pharus, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
