Phyllanthus simplex var. leiospermus

Phyllanthus simplex var. leiospermus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded phyllanthus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small, shrub-like plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
