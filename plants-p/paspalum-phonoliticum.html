Paspalum phonoliticum

Paspalum phonoliticum, commonly known as the phonolite paspalum, is a grass species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils, often found in volcanic areas. Its flowering heads are small and spike-like, typically appearing in late summer.

Paspalum phonoliticum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging conditions.
