Penstemon fruticiformis var. incertus

Penstemon fruticiformis var. incertus, commonly known as the uncertain fruticose penstemon, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, woody stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
