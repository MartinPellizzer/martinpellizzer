Palaquium aureum

Palaquium aureum, commonly known as the golden star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green fruits. The fruit, which resembles a small apple, has a sweet, slightly acidic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value, combined with its edible fruit, makes it a popular choice for both cultivation and conservation efforts.
