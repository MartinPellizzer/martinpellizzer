Pinochia floribunda

Pinochia floribunda, commonly known as the showy pinochia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for gardens with adequate drainage.

Pinochia floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
