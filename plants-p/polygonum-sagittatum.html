Polygonum sagittatum

Polygonum sagittatum, commonly known as arrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves that are typically 3 to 6 inches long and have a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Polygonum sagittatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
