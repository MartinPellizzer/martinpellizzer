Polygonum bistorta var. longifolium

Polygonum bistorta var. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved bistort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1 meter tall, often with a reddish-purple hue. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, clustered in cylindrical spikes, and bloom in shades of pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
