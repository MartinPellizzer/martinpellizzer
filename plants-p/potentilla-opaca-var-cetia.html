Potentilla opaca var. cetia

Potentilla opaca var. cetia, commonly known as the coastal potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It thrives in coastal environments, characterized by its ability to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in coastal landscaping.
