Phaseolus prostratus

Phaseolus prostratus, commonly known as the prostrate kidney bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows close to the ground, with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is adapted to warm climates and is often grown for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
