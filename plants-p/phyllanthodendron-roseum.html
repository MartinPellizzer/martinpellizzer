Phyllanthodendron roseum

Phyllanthodendron roseum, commonly known as the rose-colored phyllanthodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to reddish-pink flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often fragrant. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Phyllanthodendron roseum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
