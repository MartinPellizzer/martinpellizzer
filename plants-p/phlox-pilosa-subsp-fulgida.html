Phlox pilosa subsp. fulgida

Phlox pilosa subsp.

fulgida, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
