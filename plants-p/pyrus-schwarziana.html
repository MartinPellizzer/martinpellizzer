Pyrus schwarziana

Pyrus schwarziana, commonly known as the Schwarz's pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare fruit trees.
