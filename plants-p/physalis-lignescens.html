Physalis lignescens

Physalis lignescens, commonly known as the common ground cherry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery, lantern-like calyx. These fruits are edible when fully ripe and are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.
