Peperomia amplexicaulis var. swartziana

Peperomia amplexicaulis var. swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's peperomia, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of greenish-white, typically emerging from the leaf axils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, making it suitable for shady indoor environments.

It prefers well-draining soil and moderate to low light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
