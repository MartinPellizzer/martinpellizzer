Pyrus arbutifolia var. glabra

Pyrus arbutifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when ripe. Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
