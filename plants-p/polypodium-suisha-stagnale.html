Polypodium suisha-stagnale

Polypodium suisha-stagnale, commonly known as the Suisha Stagnale Polypody, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in high humidity. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and establish dense colonies. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a typical feature of ferns.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a resilient and widespread fern in its native habitat.
