Puya prosanae

Puya prosanae, commonly known as the Chilean torch cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and high-altitude environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

Puya prosanae is a member of the Bromeliaceae family, which includes many other succulent and epiphytic plants.
