Potentilla candicans

Potentilla candicans, commonly known as the silverleaf potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant.

Potentilla candicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
