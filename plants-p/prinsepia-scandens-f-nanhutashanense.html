Prinsepia scandens f. nanhutashanense

Prinsepia scandens f. nanhutashanense, commonly known as the Nanhutashan dwarf sand cherry, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for commercial use. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and unique foliage structure.
