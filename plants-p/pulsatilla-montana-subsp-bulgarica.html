Pulsatilla montana subsp. bulgarica

Pulsatilla montana subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers have a distinctive, recurved petal structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
