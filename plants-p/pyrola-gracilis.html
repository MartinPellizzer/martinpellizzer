Pyrola gracilis

Pyrola gracilis, commonly known as slender pineleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its leathery, evergreen leaves. The plant produces white or pink flowers in late spring, which are clustered in a spike-like arrangement. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a waxy coating.

Pyrola gracilis thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.
