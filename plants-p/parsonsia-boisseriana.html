Parsonsia boisseriana

Parsonsia boisseriana, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Parsonsia boisseriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
