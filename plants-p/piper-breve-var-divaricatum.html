Piper breve var. divaricatum

Piper breve var. divaricatum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny, aromatic berries. It typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its compact size, it is suitable for indoor gardening and can thrive in containers.
