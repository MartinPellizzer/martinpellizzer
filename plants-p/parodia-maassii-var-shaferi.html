Parodia maassii var. shaferi

Parodia maassii var. shaferi, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a rare and striking variation of the Parodia maassii species.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often pink or orange-colored central body, which is actually a grafted plant with a different species as its rootstock. The plant's rounded, segmented shape and spiny texture are typical of the Cactaceae family. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its coloration.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts.
