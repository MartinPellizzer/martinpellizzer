Pandanus obliquus

Pandanus obliquus, commonly known as the zigzag palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific islands, including parts of Polynesia and Melanesia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that curve in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and has cultural significance in many island communities.
