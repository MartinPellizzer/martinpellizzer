Phylica ramosissima

Phylica ramosissima, commonly known as the silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for garden landscaping.

The plant also has historical uses in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
