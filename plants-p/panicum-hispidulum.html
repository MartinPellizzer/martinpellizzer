Panicum hispidulum

Panicum hispidulum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in full sun and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
