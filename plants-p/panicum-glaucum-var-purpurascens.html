Panicum glaucum var. purpurascens

Panicum glaucum var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that produce dense clusters of small, fluffy, purple-tinted flowers, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a waxy coating, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall color, which ranges from red to bronze.
