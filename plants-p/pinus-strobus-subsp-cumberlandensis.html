Pinus strobus subsp. cumberlandensis

Pinus strobus subsp.

cumberlandensis, commonly known as the Cumberland white pine, is a subspecies of the eastern white pine native to the Appalachian Mountains. It is characterized by its slender, fast-growing form and long, slender needles that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length.

The cones are relatively small, measuring up to 8 centimeters, and mature in the second year. This subspecies is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
