Paraprenanthes thirionnii

Paraprenanthes thirionnii, commonly known as the Thirionnii papyrus, is a rare aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.
