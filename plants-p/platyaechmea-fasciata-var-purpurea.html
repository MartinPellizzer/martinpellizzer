Platyaechmea fasciata var. purpurea

Platyaechmea fasciata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed giant star, is a striking bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant purple or reddish hue, with a glossy texture. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, white flowers, adding an ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular in tropical and subtropical gardens for its dramatic foliage and ease of care.
