Pleurothallis hatschbachii

Pleurothallis hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and a compact inflorescence. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose substrates.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique appearance.
