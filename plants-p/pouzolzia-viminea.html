Pouzolzia viminea

Pouzolzia viminea, commonly known as the climbing pouzolzia, is a perennial vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers, followed by small, round berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a common species in horticulture and ecological studies.
