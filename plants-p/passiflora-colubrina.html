Passiflora colubrina

Passiflora colubrina, commonly known as the blue passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue and white flowers, which are adorned with intricate patterns and modified leaves. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its botanical features include tendrils for climbing and a complex corona of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures.

Passiflora colubrina is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
