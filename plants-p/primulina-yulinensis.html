Primulina yulinensis

Primulina yulinensis, commonly known as the Yulin primula, is a rare and ornamental plant native to China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
