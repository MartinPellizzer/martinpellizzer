Piper sanjuanilloense

Piper sanjuanilloense, commonly known as the San Juanillo pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
