Peraltea erythrinifolia

Peraltea erythrinifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf peraltea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as rainforests. Its leaves are palmate, with five to seven lobes, and it produces small, clustered white flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
