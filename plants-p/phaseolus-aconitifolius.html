Phaseolus aconitifolius

Phaseolus aconitifolius, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a species of legume native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a dark green, glossy appearance and trifoliate leaves that resemble the shape of a hood. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing dark, glossy seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and is well-suited for growing in well-drained soils.
