Polemannia verticillata

Polemannia verticillata, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of stems, which allows it to maximize sunlight exposure in its natural habitat. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. Its small, spiny leaves are adapted for reducing water loss through transpiration.

Overall, Polemannia verticillata is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant with unique morphological adaptations suited for its harsh environment.
