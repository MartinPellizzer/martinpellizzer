Panicum ruderale

Panicum ruderale, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically in shades of red or purple. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Panicum ruderale is adapted to grow in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
