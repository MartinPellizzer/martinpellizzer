Phlomoides lyrata

Phlomoides lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf tansy, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive lyre-like shape, giving it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phlomoides lyrata is typically found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
