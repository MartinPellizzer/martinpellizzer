Passiflora

Passiflora, commonly known as the passionflower, is a genus of about 500 species of flowering plants.

These plants are native to the Americas and are characterized by their striking, complex flowers with colorful petals and intricate structures. The flowers often feature a crown-like structure made of filaments and stamens, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Many species of Passiflora are climbing vines that produce edible berries, some of which are used in food and traditional medicine.

The genus is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
