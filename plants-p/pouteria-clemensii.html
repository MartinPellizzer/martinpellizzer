Pouteria clemensii

Pouteria clemensii, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits. The fruit contains a single large seed, which is the edible nut harvested for its rich nutritional content. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense, buttressed root system.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also valued for its economic importance.
