Potamogeton orthorrhynchus

Potamogeton orthorrhynchus, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes.

Its dense growth helps stabilize sediment and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
