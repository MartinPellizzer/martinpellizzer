Plantago lagopus f. eriostachya

Plantago lagopus f. eriostachya, commonly known as the slender plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and reduced floral structures.

This plant is native to arctic and alpine regions, where it thrives in harsh, cold environments. Its leaves are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help to retain moisture and protect against extreme weather conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated.

It is often found growing in rocky soils and is an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
