Piper camptostachys

Piper camptostachys, commonly known as Mexican pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, lance-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
