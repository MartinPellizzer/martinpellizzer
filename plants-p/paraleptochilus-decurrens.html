Paraleptochilus decurrens

Paraleptochilus decurrens, commonly known as the creeping sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features succulent, fleshy stems that often lie close to the ground, allowing it to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Paraleptochilus decurrens is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in areas with minimal rainfall.
