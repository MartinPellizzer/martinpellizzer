Pleurothallis convallium

Pleurothallis convallium, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
