Phlox douglasii var. covillei

Phlox douglasii var. covillei, commonly known as Coville's phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

Coville's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
