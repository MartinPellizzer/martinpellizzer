Polygonum lapathifolium var. agreste

Polygonum lapathifolium var. agreste, commonly known as the common smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features erect, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it is often found in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its characteristic swollen nodes on the stems.
