Phyllanthus minutiflorus

Phyllanthus minutiflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
