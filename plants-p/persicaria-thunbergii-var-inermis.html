Persicaria thunbergii var. inermis

Persicaria thunbergii var. inermis, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish-purple in color and bear heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in non-native regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
