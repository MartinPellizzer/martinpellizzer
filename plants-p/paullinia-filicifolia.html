Paullinia filicifolia

Paullinia filicifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf soursop, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ferns. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, spiny fruits that are similar in appearance to soursop. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.
