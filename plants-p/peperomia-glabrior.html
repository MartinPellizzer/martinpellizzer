Peperomia glabrior

Peperomia glabrior, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green or variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color. It is a compact, low-maintenance plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Peperomia glabrior is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
