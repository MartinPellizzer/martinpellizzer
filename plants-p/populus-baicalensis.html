Populus baicalensis

Populus baicalensis, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the temperate regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-gray bark. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it adaptable to different environments. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Populus baicalensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
