Phyllanthus diffusus var. oblongifolius

Phyllanthus diffusus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical to oblong in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's growth habit is usually shrub-like, with multiple stems arising from the base.
