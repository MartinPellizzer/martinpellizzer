Phalaris arundinacea subsp. typica

Phalaris arundinacea subsp.

typica, commonly known as common reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence and hollow, jointed stems that are often used in basket weaving.

This plant thrives in wetland environments, including marshes, riverbanks, and pond edges, and is highly adaptable to various soil types. It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soils and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.

Additionally, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and resilience.
