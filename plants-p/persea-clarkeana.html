Persea clarkeana

Persea clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, pear-shaped fruits with a smooth, green skin and a creamy, oily flesh. Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
