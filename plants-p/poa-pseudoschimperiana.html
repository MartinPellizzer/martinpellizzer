Poa pseudoschimperiana

Poa pseudoschimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, and it is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.

Poa pseudoschimperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
