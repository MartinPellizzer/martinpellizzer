Poaephyllum trilobum

Poaephyllum trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in early spring.

Poaephyllum trilobum is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which have three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.
