Pandanus assamensis

Pandanus assamensis, commonly known as the Assam pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Pandanus.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into three-winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and cultural significance in local communities.
