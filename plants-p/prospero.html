Prospero

Prospero, commonly known as the "Balm of Gilead," is a plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many toxic and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves that are typically ovate or lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pink or white in color.
