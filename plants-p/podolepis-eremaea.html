Podolepis eremaea

Podolepis eremaea, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments with features such as succulent leaves and deep root systems for water conservation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Podolepis eremaea plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
