Parathesis stenophylla

Parathesis stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf parathesis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in rocky or high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
