Philodendron pteropus

Philodendron pteropus, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its rapid growth and striking foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
