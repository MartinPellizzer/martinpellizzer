Pyrus × leptophylla

Pyrus × leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pear, is a hybrid species within the genus Pyrus.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of its parent species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible fruits similar to common pears. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild-flavored fruit.

This species is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and Korea.
