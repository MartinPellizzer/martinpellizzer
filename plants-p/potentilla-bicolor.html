Potentilla bicolor

Potentilla bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a yellow center and pink or red outer petals. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Potentilla bicolor is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
