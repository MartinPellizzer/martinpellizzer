Phlox visenda

Phlox visenda, commonly known as the showy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central cone.

Phlox visenda thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soil.
