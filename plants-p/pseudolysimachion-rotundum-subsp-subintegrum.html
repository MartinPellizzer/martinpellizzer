Pseudolysimachion rotundum subsp. subintegrum

Pseudolysimachion rotundum subsp.

subintegrum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved pseudolysimachion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rounded, smooth leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a distinct, aromatic scent.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and adaptability to arid conditions.
