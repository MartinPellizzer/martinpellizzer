Potentilla hirta var. pinnatifida

Potentilla hirta var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It features wiry, hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant its specific epithet "pinnatifida," and are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
