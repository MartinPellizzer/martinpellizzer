Potentilla opaca var. congesta

Potentilla opaca var. congesta, commonly known as the dense potentilla, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in arid environments.
