Pavonia rhodantha var. discolor

Pavonia rhodantha var. discolor, commonly known as the variable scarlet leadplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open woods and disturbed sites, showcasing its adaptability to various soil conditions. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in racemes during the spring and summer months. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

The varietal name 'discolor' refers to the variation in flower color, which can range from white to deep red.
