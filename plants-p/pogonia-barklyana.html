Pogonia barklyana

Pogonia barklyana, commonly known as the Barkley's bog orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dense, moist vegetation, often in swampy or heathland environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, Pogonia barklyana is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
