Penstemon alluviorum

Penstemon alluviorum, commonly known as the alluvial beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
