Piper phytolaccifolium var. phytolaccifolium

Piper phytolaccifolium var. phytolaccifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper vine, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but have a peppery taste. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.
