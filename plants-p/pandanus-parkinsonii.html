Pandanus parkinsonii

Pandanus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that often hang in clusters from the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found near mangroves or in sandy soils.
