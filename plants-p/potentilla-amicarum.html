Potentilla amicarum

Potentilla amicarum, commonly known as the friendship cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Potentilla amicarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
