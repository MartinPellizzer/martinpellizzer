Pluchea rufescens

Pluchea rufescens, commonly known as roughleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. Its leaves are rough and hairy, while its yellow flowers bloom in clusters.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
