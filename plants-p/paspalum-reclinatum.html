Paspalum reclinatum

Paspalum reclinatum, commonly known as the drooping grass or narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often droop under the weight of its seed heads. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum reclinatum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
