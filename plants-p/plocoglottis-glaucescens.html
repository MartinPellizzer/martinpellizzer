Plocoglottis glaucescens

Plocoglottis glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glaucous leaves that give it a distinctive bluish-green appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has a creeping rhizome that allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the leaves, typical of the fern family.
