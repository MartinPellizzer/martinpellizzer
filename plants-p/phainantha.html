Phainantha

Phainantha, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, dark green leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors. Phainantha species typically thrive in humid, well-drained soil and require indirect sunlight to flourish. They are popular as houseplants due to their compact size and ability to bloom throughout the year.

The genus includes several species, such as Phainantha tenuifolia, which is prized for its ornamental value.
