Piper birmanicum

Piper birmanicum, commonly known as the Burmese pepper or Burmese long pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, elongated berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The fruit is typically harvested when it turns red, and it is valued for its pungent flavor and medicinal properties.
