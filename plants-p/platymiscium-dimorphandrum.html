Platymiscium dimorphandrum

Platymiscium dimorphandrum, commonly known as the two-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae) and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves that change color with the seasons. The tree produces striking yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elongated pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture-making.
