Peperomia tutuilana

Peperomia tutuilana, commonly known as the Tutuila peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the island of Tutuila in American Samoa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its tropical habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and conservation significance.
