Ptisana ternatea

Ptisana ternatea, commonly known as the Ternate starling or Ternate piper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ptisana ternatea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
