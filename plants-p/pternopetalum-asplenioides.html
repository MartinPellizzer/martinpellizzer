Pternopetalum asplenioides

Pternopetalum asplenioides, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble a fern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pternopetalum asplenioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in maintaining forest understory biodiversity.
