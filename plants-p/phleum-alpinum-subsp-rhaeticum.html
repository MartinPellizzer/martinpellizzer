Phleum alpinum subsp. rhaeticum

Phleum alpinum subsp.

rhaeticum, commonly known as Alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous areas at high altitudes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. Phleum alpinum subsp.

rhaeticum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
