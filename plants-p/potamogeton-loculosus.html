Potamogeton loculosus

Potamogeton loculosus, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize sediment in aquatic environments.

Potamogeton loculosus plays an important role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms and in maintaining water quality.
