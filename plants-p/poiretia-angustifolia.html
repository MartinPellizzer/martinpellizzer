Poiretia angustifolia

Poiretia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poiretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
