Petunia littoralis

Petunia littoralis, commonly known as the seaside petunia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found growing along shorelines and dunes. Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it a hardy species capable of withstanding wind and salt spray.

Petunia littoralis is also valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
