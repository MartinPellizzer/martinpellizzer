Pinus tenangaensis

Pinus tenangaensis, commonly known as the Mexican white pine, is a coniferous tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and long, needle-like leaves that grow in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds enclosed in winged arils. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
