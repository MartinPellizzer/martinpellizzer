Phyllanthus pancherianus var. pancherianus

Phyllanthus pancherianus var. pancherianus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
