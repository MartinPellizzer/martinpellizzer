Prunus × lannesiana f. moutan

Prunus × lannesiana f. moutan, commonly known as the Chinese plum or Japanese apricot, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit, which is edible, is a small, yellowish-orange drupe that ripens in late summer.
