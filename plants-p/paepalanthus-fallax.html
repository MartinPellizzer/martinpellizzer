Paepalanthus fallax

Paepalanthus fallax, commonly known as the false puya, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and striking, showy inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
