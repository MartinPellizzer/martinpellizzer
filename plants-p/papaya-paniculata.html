Papaya paniculata

Papaya paniculata, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange-red flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Papaya paniculata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
