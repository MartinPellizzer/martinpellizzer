Panax discolor

Panax discolor, commonly known as the variable ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, alternating leaves that range from dark green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is the primary part used medicinally, containing various bioactive compounds.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, Panax discolor is now considered endangered in many regions.
