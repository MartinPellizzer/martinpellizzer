Phaenixopus chondrilliflorus

Phaenixopus chondrilliflorus, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like foliage and its ability to produce small, white flowers that emit a faint, sweet fragrance at night. The plant's root system is particularly complex, with specialized structures that aid in nutrient absorption from poor soil. It thrives in shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
