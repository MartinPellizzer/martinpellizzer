Potentilla theodoriana

Potentilla theodoriana, commonly known as the Theodore's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters of five. Its leaves are pinnately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla theodoriana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
