Paepalanthus chlorophyllus

Paepalanthus chlorophyllus, commonly known as the green leaf cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and is typically found in rocky, arid environments. The plant's leaves are usually green and fleshy, adapted to store water and perform photosynthesis. It produces small, yellow flowers during the growing season.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
