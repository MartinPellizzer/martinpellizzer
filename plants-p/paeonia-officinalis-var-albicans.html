Paeonia officinalis var. albicans

Paeonia officinalis var. albicans, commonly known as the white peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves composed of three ovate to lanceolate leaflets. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is closely related to other peony varieties and is valued for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
