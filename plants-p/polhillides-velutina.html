Polhillides velutina

Polhillides velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf polhillides, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
