Phaseolus fusiformis

Phaseolus fusiformis, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Americas.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often grown for both its ornamental value and culinary use. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is closely related to other beans in the Phaseolus genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
