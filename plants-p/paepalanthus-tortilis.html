Paepalanthus tortilis

Paepalanthus tortilis, commonly known as the twisted leaf cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Bolivia, and Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiraling growth pattern of its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure that stores water. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, twisted foliage.

Despite its hardy nature, it is relatively rare and requires specific care to thrive in cultivation.
