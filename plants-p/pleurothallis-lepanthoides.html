Pleurothallis lepanthoides

Pleurothallis lepanthoides, commonly known as the "Lepanthoid Orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of the genus Lepanthus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually pale green to white, with a slight fragrance, and bloom during the rainy season.
