Paspalum zollingeri

Paspalum zollingeri, commonly known as Zollinger's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum zollingeri is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to withstand drought.
