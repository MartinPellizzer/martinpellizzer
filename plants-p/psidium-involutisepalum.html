Psidium involutisepalum

Psidium involutisepalum, commonly known as the curled-sepal guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, yellowish fruit that resembles a guava. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
