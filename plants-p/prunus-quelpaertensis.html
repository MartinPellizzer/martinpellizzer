Prunus quelpaertensis

Prunus quelpaertensis, commonly known as the Jeju cherry or Jeju peach, is a deciduous tree native to the island of Jeju in South Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to peaches in taste and texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
