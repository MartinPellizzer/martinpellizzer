Platanus orientalis var. racemosa

Platanus orientalis var. racemosa, commonly known as the Oriental Plane with racemes, is a variant of the Oriental Plane tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves and distinctive, clustered flower clusters known as racemes. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its fruit is a spherical, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.
