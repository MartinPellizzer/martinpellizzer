Phyllanthus grandifolius var. salzmannii

Phyllanthus grandifolius var. salzmannii, commonly known as the Salzmann's gooseberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly rough texture with prominent veins.
