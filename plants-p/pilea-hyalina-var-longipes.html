Pilea hyalina var. longipes

Pilea hyalina var. longipes, commonly known as the glassy pilea, is a unique member of the Araceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its striking, translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant features slender, upright stems with a rosette of elongated, waxy leaves that can grow up to 10 inches in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in a spadix surrounded by a green spathe.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
