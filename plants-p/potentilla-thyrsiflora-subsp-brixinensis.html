Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp. brixinensis

Potentilla thyrsiflora subsp.

brixinensis, commonly known as the Brixen thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of the Eastern Alps. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually reddish in color.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
