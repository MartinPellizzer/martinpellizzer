Parkia harbesonii

Parkia harbesonii, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist forested areas.

Parkia harbesonii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
