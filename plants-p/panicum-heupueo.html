Panicum heupueo

Panicum heupueo, commonly known as the Hawaiian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in traditional Hawaiian crafts and ceremonies. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, commonly found in coastal and upland areas.

Due to its cultural significance and ecological role, it is an important component of Hawaii's native flora.
