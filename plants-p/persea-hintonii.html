Persea hintonii

Persea hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and have a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
