Penstemon variabilis

Penstemon variabilis, commonly known as the variable beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences. The plant produces tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon variabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
