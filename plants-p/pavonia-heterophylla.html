Pavonia heterophylla

Pavonia heterophylla, commonly known as the variable pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to lanceolate, depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.

Its adaptability makes it a resilient species in diverse habitats.
