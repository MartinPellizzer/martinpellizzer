Parkia alliodora

Parkia alliodora, commonly known as the yellow pisonia, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often found in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in lowland forests.
