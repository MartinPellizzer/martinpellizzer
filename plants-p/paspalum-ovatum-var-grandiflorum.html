Paspalum ovatum var. grandiflorum

Paspalum ovatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and large, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its seeds are small and dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
