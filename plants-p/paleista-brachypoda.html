Paleista brachypoda

Paleista brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed fern, is a unique plant species characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features rhizomes that anchor it to the substrate and produce small, lance-shaped leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Paleista brachypoda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to flourish in controlled indoor settings.
