Prunus latidentata var. pleuroptera

Prunus latidentata var. pleuroptera, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, which are highly ornamental and attract pollinators. Its fruits are small, round drupes that ripen to a dark red color.

This variety is prized for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
