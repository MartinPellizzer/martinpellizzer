Pithecellobium adianthifolium

Pithecellobium adianthifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by elongated pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and soil improvement.
