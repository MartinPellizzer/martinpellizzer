Primula Dryadifolia Subsp. Chlorodryas

Primula dryadifolia subsp. chlorodryas is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. This subspecies is known for its vivid greenish-blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species by conservationists. Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
