Premna angolensis

Premna angolensis, commonly known as African nettle, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna angolensis is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
