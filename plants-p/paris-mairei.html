Paris mairei

Paris mairei, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and showy, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodland habitats and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Paris mairei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
