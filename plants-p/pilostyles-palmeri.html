Pilostyles palmeri

Pilostyles palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's ghostplant, is a rare and unusual parasitic plant found in the southwestern United States.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on its host plants for nutrients, typically growing on other flowering plants. The plant produces tiny, white, thread-like structures called haustoria that attach to the host's roots. It is notable for its lack of leaves and its ability to reproduce through seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its specialized lifestyle and habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species.
