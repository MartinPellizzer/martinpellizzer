Polygonum franktonii

Polygonum franktonii, commonly known as Frankton's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive swollen nodes and bead-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purplish hue, while its small, greenish flowers appear in clusters.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.
