Polygonum hydropiperoides var. digitatum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. digitatum, commonly known as the spiked knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, bead-like fruits.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
