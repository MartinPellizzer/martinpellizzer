Podocarpus madagascariensis subsp. rotundus

Podocarpus madagascariensis subsp.

rotundus, commonly known as the Madagascar umbrella pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-shaped crown formed by closely spaced branches.

The tree has needle-like leaves that are dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
