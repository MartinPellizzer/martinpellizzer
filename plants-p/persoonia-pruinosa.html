Persoonia pruinosa

Persoonia pruinosa, commonly known as the silver myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. The plant has small, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Persoonia pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
