Pithocarpa melanostigma

Pithocarpa melanostigma, commonly known as the black-stemmed pithocarpa, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant's bark is thick and cork-like, which contributes to its resilience in tropical environments. It produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Pithocarpa melanostigma make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
