Pleurothallis floribunda

Pleurothallis floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil.

Its unique floral structure and aromatic properties make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
