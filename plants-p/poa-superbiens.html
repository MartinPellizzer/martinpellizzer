Poa superbiens

Poa superbiens, commonly known as the noble grass, is a perennial species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Poa superbiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
