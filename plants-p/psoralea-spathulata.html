Psoralea spathulata

Psoralea spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features small, lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a distinctive pod-like fruit that splits open to release its seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
