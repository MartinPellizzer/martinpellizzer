Pinus hudsonica

Pinus hudsonica, commonly known as Hudson's pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the colder regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cone-shaped seedlings that mature into woody cones, which release seeds in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic climates.

This species is valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
