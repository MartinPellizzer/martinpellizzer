Poitea punicea

Poitea punicea, commonly known as the red-flowered poitea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it access water from deeper soil layers. Its vibrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to local biodiversity.

Poitea punicea is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
