Picea schrenkiana var. tianschanica

Picea schrenkiana var. tianschanica, commonly known as the Tianshan fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Tianshan mountain range in Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along the branches. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its dense foliage and slow growth rate make it a valuable species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
