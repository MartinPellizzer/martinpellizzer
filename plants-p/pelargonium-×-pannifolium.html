Pelargonium × pannifolium

Pelargonium × pannifolium, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant typically has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × pannifolium is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
