Peperomia siguatepequensis

Peperomia siguatepequensis, commonly known as the Siguatepeque peperomia, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in regions of Honduras and Nicaragua.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in hue. Its foliage is characterized by its waxy texture and ability to store moisture, making it adaptable to drier conditions.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
