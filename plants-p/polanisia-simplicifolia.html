Polanisia simplicifolia

Polanisia simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved polanisia, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Polanisia simplicifolia is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
