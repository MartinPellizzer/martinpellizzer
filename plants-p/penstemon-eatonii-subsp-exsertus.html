Penstemon eatonii subsp. exsertus

Penstemon eatonii subsp.

exsertus, commonly known as the Eaton's beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its subspecific name, *exsertus*, refers to the elongated, projecting stamens that are a notable feature of the species.
