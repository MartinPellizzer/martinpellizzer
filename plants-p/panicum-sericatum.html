Panicum sericatum

Panicum sericatum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-haired foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost silky appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces fluffy, pale green to brown seed heads that are often mistaken for other grasses.

Silky panicgrass plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
