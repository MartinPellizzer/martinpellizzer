Petalostemon corymbosus var. adenopodus

Petalostemon corymbosus var. adenopodus, commonly known as the glandular petalostemon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its specific variety name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
