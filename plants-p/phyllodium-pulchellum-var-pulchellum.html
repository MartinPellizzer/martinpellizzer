Phyllodium pulchellum var. pulchellum

Phyllodium pulchellum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the pink phyllodium, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often pinkish leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
