Pyrrosia caudata

Pyrrosia caudata, commonly known as the forked maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pyrrosia caudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
