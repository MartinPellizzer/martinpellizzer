Paspalum giuliettiae

Paspalum giuliettiae, commonly known as the Juliette paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
