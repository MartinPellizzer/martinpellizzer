Paratropia polyphylla

Paratropia polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often hairy, providing it with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
