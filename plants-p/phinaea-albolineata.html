Phinaea albolineata

Phinaea albolineata, commonly known as the white-lined fern, is a delicate perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It features finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are typically light green with distinct white or pale green markings along the veins.

Phinaea albolineata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
