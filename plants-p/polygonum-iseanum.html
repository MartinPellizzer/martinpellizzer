Polygonum iseanum

Polygonum iseanum, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. It is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various wetland habitats.
