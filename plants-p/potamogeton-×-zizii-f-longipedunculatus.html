Potamogeton × zizii f. longipedunculatus

Potamogeton × zizii f. longipedunculatus, commonly known as the long-stalked pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, slender stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are clustered in elongated inflorescences, which give the plant its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
