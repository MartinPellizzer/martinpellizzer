Plantago arenaria var. rossica

Plantago arenaria var. rossica, commonly known as the Russian plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of narrow, fleshy, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This species is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported healing properties.
