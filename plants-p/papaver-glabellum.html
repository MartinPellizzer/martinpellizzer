Papaver glabellum

Papaver glabellum, commonly known as the glabrous poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features smooth, glabrous stems and leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant is known for its delicate, papery petals that often curl inward as they age.

Papaver glabellum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
