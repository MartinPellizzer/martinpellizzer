Phebalium sympetalum

Phebalium sympetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Phebalium sympetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
