Polystachya fabriana

Polystachya fabriana, commonly known as the Fabriana bamboo, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in elongated inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common sight in moist, shaded forest understories.

Polystachya fabriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
