Pterocarpus trifoliatus

Pterocarpus trifoliatus, commonly known as the three-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark wood and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, hard seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its hardy nature, it is valued for both ornamental and timber purposes.
