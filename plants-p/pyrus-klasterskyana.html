Pyrus klasterskyana

Pyrus klasterskyana, commonly known as the Klastersky pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
