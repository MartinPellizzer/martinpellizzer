Pieris griffithiana

Pieris griffithiana, commonly known as the Griffith's azalea, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces fragrant, pink to white blooms in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
