Pteris madagascarica

Pteris madagascarica, commonly known as the Madagascar water fern, is a species of fern native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically light green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris madagascarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
