Ptarmica reichardtiana

Ptarmica reichardtiana, commonly known as the Reichardt's pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small pincushions. The plant features upright, sparsely branched stems covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Ptarmica reichardtiana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
