Polypodium dolorense

Polypodium dolorense, commonly known as the broad-leaf holly fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery fronds that are typically divided into multiple segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
