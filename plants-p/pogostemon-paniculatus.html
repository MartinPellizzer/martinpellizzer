Pogostemon paniculatus

Pogostemon paniculatus, commonly known as the spike grass, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear whorled leaves with a distinctive sheathing base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
