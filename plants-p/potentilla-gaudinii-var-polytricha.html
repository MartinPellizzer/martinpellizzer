Potentilla gaudinii var. polytricha

Potentilla gaudinii var. polytricha, commonly known as the many-stemmed cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in the summer. The plant has narrow, slightly hairy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
