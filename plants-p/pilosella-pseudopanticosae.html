Pilosella pseudopanticosae

Pilosella pseudopanticosae, commonly known as the Panticosan hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are basal, oblanceolate, and covered with dense white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Pilosella pseudopanticosae is known for its small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
