Philadelphus floridus var. rehderianus

Philadelphus floridus var. rehderianus, commonly known as the Rehder's Mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental choice.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
