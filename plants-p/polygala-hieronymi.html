Polygala hieronymi

Polygala hieronymi, commonly known as the African milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive spurred petal that aids in pollination by insects.

Polygala hieronymi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
