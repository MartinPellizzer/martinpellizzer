Pternandra azurea

Pternandra azurea, commonly known as the blue pterandra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Pternandra azurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
