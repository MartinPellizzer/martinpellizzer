Poa crandallii

Poa crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing turf. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Poa crandallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
