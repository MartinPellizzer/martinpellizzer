Passiflora poslae

Passiflora poslae, commonly known as the Posla's passionflower, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tropical flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Passiflora poslae is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
