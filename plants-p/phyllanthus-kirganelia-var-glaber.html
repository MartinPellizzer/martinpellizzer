Phyllanthus kirganelia var. glaber

Phyllanthus kirganelia var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth phyllanthus, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually smooth and glossy in texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes or traditional medicinal use.
