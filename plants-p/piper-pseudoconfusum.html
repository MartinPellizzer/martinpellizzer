Piper pseudoconfusum

Piper pseudoconfusum, commonly known as the Guianan pepper, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that are typically yellow when ripe.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.
