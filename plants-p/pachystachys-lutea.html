Pachystachys lutea

Pachystachys lutea, commonly known as the golden spike grass, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and its striking yellow inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Pachystachys lutea is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
