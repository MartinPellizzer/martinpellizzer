Peperomia nana

Peperomia nana, commonly known as the dwarf peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features rounded, fleshy leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it ideal for indoor cultivation. Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for tabletop displays and hanging baskets.

Peperomia nana is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for beginners.
