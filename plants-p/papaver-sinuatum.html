Papaver sinuatum

Papaver sinuatum, commonly known as the silky poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers with papery, pink to red petals that are often adorned with a dark purple or black blotch at the base. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are soft to the touch, contributing to its delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The silky poppy is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
