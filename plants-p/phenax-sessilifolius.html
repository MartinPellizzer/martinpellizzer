Phenax sessilifolius

Phenax sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved phenax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, sessile leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its botanical features make it a hardy species adapted to dry and rocky soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience.
