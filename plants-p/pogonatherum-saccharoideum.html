Pogonatherum saccharoideum

Pogonatherum saccharoideum, commonly known as sugar cane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
