Phtirium

Phtirium, commonly known as the velvet tamarisk, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Tamaricaceae.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that give it a dense, velvety appearance. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often in spikes, and are usually pink or red in color. Phtirium species are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

These plants are adapted to harsh environments and are often used in landscaping for their drought tolerance and ornamental value.
