Petrophytum hendersonii

Petrophytum hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spiny, branched shrub that typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has cylindrical stems covered in small, cone-shaped spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are yellow to orange in color, blooming in the spring, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals.

Petrophytum hendersonii plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
