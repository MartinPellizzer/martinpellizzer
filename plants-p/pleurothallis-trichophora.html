Pleurothallis trichophora

Pleurothallis trichophora, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
