Piper nobile var. glabratum

Piper nobile var. glabratum, commonly known as the velvetleaf betel, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
