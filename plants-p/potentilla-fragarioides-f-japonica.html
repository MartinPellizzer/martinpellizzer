Potentilla fragarioides f. japonica

Potentilla fragarioides f. japonica, commonly known as Japanese cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and bright yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with five elliptical leaflets, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer, and are attractive to pollinators.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil conditions.
