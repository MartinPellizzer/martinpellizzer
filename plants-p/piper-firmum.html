Piper firmum

Piper firmum, commonly known as the Trinidad pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and they are often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor. Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
