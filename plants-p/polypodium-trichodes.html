Polypodium trichodes

Polypodium trichodes, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The fronds are typically divided into segments with a shield-like shape, and the plant produces spores in clusters on the underside of the leaves.

Polypodium trichodes is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
