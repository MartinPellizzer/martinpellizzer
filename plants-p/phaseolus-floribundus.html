Phaseolus floribundus

Phaseolus floribundus, commonly known as the everbearing bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse flowering, with clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds, which are typically used for food. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
