Piper pseudoflexuosum

Piper pseudoflexuosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features slender, woody stems that twine around supports and produce small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly pubescent surface.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, attracting various wildlife species.
