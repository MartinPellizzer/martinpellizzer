Plerandra pickeringii

Plerandra pickeringii, commonly known as the Pickering's arum, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and a distinctive, spadix-like inflorescence surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant is part of the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and economically important species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Plerandra pickeringii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Indigenous practices.
