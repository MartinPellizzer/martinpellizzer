Pitcairnia amazonica

Pitcairnia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian air plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, which is used to collect rainwater for hydration. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bats. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of the rainforest.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular specimen in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
