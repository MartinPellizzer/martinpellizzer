Pandanus thermalis

Pandanus thermalis, commonly known as the thermal pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in paniculate inflorescences. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, particularly in treating skin conditions and inflammation.

The thermal pandanus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
