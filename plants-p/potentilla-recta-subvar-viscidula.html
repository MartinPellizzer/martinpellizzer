Potentilla recta subvar. viscidula

Potentilla recta subvar. viscidula, commonly known as sticky cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, which is a key identifying feature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
