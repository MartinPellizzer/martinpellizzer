Piper madagascariense

Piper madagascariense, commonly known as the Madagascar pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and research settings.
