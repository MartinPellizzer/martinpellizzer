Poa pratensis var. muralis

Poa pratensis var. muralis, commonly known as wall meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas near walls or in rocky habitats. Its botanical features include inflorescences that are spike-like and composed of small, two-lobed spikelets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ability to form dense, attractive turf.
