Polystichum zambesiacum

Polystichum zambesiacum, commonly known as the Zambesi shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a dark green color and a textured surface. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spore dispersal.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
