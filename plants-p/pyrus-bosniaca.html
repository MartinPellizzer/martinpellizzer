Pyrus bosniaca

Pyrus bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart pears in late summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its contribution to local biodiversity.
