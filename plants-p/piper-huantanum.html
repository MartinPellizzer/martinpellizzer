Piper huantanum

Piper huantanum, commonly known as the Huantanum pepper or Huantanum vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that support its heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
