Picea asperata subsp. ponderosa

Picea asperata subsp.

ponderosa, commonly known as the ponderosa pine, is a tall evergreen conifer native to western North America. It is characterized by its long, slender needles and distinctive bark that becomes furrowed with age.

The tree produces cones that hang downward, containing seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained sites and full sun.

This species is valued for its timber and plays a significant role in forest ecosystems.
