Panicum saigonense

Panicum saigonense, commonly known as Vietnamese millet, is a warm-season grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for dry climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ornamental gardening and as a forage crop due to its hardy nature.
