Polystachya smytheana

Polystachya smytheana, commonly known as the Smythe's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a rhizomatous structure and produces tall, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous small, greenish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include lance-shaped leaves and a lack of true roots, with the stem serving as the primary photosynthetic organ.
