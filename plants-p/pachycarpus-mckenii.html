Pachycarpus mckenii

Pachycarpus mckenii, commonly known as the McKenzie's thickleaf, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Pachycarpus mckenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
