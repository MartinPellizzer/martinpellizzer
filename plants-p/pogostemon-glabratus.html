Pogostemon glabratus

Pogostemon glabratus, commonly known as the broadleaf pogostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Pogostemon glabratus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
