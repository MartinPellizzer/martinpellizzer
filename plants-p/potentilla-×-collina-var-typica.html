Potentilla × collina var. typica

Potentilla × collina var. typica, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a deciduous flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, upright growth with arching branches. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
