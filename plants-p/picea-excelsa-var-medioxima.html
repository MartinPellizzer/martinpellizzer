Picea excelsa var. medioxima

Picea excelsa var. medioxima, commonly known as the Himalayan fir, is a rare variant of the Himalayan fir species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its conical shape, dense foliage, and dark green needles that are slightly shorter than those of the typical species. The tree thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous environments. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
