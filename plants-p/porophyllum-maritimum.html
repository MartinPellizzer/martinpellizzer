Porophyllum maritimum

Porophyllum maritimum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal grasslands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Porophyllum maritimum is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions, making it a resilient coastal plant.
