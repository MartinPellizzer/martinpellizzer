Panicum ropalotrichum

Panicum ropalotrichum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like cluster of spikelets.

Panicum ropalotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
