Pelargonium paniculatum

Pelargonium paniculatum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Pelargonium paniculatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
