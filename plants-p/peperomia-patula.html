Peperomia patula

Peperomia patula, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy, and slightly hairy leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped, with a vibrant green coloration. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which bloom in clusters during the spring or summer. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia patula is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to enhance the aesthetic of indoor spaces.
