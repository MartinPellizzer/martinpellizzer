Polystichum austropaleaceum

Polystichum austropaleaceum, commonly known as the southern shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds that are typically arching and divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
