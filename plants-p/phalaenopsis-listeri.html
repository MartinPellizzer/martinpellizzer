Phalaenopsis listeri

Phalaenopsis listeri, commonly known as the Lister's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, elongated leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant is known for its long blooming period, often lasting several months, which makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, and it is relatively easy to care for.

Phalaenopsis listeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
