Porpax seidenfadenii

Porpax seidenfadenii, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a slender, erect stem and produces a single, elongated inflorescence with numerous small, showy blooms. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the humid forest floor, and it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
