Pertya glabrescens var. viridis

Pertya glabrescens var. viridis, commonly known as the green pertya, is a variant of the Pertya glabrescens species, characterized by its distinctive green foliage.

It is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in semi-arid to arid environments. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of yellow or orange, adding to its ornamental value.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
