Parodia submammulosa

Parodia submammulosa, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
