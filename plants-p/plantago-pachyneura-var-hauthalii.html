Plantago pachyneura var. hauthalii

Plantago pachyneura var. hauthalii, commonly known as the Hauthal's plantain, is a variant of the broadleaf plantain, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late spring, which are surrounded by bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
