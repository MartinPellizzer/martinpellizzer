Primula × legionensis

Primula × legionensis, commonly known as the hybrid primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant typically grows to a height of 10–30 cm, featuring hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a wide variety of flower forms and colors, making it a popular choice for horticultural enthusiasts.
