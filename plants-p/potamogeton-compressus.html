Potamogeton compressus

Potamogeton compressus, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, often emerging from the water surface. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, contributing to habitat structure for aquatic life.

Potamogeton compressus plays a vital role in water filtration and provides shelter for fish and invertebrates.
