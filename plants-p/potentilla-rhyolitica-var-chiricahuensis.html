Potentilla rhyolitica var. chiricahuensis

Potentilla rhyolitica var. chiricahuensis, commonly known as the Chiricahua potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with many small, upright stems. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to the arid environments it inhabits.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to dry, sunny conditions.
