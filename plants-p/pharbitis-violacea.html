Pharbitis violacea

Pharbitis violacea, commonly known as morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heliotropic flowers that open in the morning and close at night. The plant produces strikingly colorful, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of violet, blue, and white. Its vines can grow rapidly, often climbing structures with tendrils that help it ascend.

The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.
