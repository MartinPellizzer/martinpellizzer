Polymita diutina

Polymita diutina, commonly known as the everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact even after being cut, making it popular in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring temperate climates.

Polymita diutina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a cut flower.
