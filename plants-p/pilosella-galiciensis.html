Pilosella galiciensis

Pilosella galiciensis, commonly known as the Galician hairweed, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are small and yellow, forming a cluster at the top of the stem.

Pilosella galiciensis is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its high-altitude environment.
