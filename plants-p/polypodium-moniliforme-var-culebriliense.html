Polypodium moniliforme var. culebriliense

Polypodium moniliforme var. culebriliense, commonly known as the Mexican chain fern, is a fern species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, and often segmented fronds that resemble a chain, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its unique foliage.
