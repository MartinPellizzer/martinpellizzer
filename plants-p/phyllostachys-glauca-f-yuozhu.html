Phyllostachys glauca f. yuozhu

Phyllostachys glauca f. yuozhu, commonly known as the Yuozhu bamboo, is a variant of the silver bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and slightly twisted leaves that exhibit a unique blue-green hue. The plant typically grows as a clump-forming bamboo, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its culms are sturdy and have a smooth, pale surface, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its visual interest.
