Parnassia perciliata

Parnassia perciliata, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers with white veins. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are palmately divided and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Parnassia perciliata is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra environments.
