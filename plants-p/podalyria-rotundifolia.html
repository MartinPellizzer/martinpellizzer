Podalyria rotundifolia

Podalyria rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaved podalyria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its rounded, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Podalyria rotundifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
