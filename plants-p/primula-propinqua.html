Primula propinqua

Primula propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces showy blooms in early spring.

Primula propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
