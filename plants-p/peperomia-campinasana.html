Peperomia campinasana

Peperomia campinasana, commonly known as the Campinas peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It features upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia campinasana is relatively low maintenance and drought-tolerant, suitable for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
