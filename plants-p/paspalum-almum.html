Paspalum almum

Paspalum almum, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Paspalum almum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
