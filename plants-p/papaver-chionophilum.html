Papaver chionophilum

Papaver chionophilum, commonly known as the snow-loving poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white to pale pink petals and is typically found in alpine or arctic environments. The plant thrives in cold, dry climates and has adapted to withstand harsh winter conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, adding a striking visual contrast to the snowy landscape.

Papaver chionophilum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific cold-hardy conditions.
