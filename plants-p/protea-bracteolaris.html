Protea bracteolaris

Protea bracteolaris, commonly known as the silver bract protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a cylindrical shape. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific care to flourish.
