Pelargonium × gracilescens

Pelargonium × gracilescens, commonly known as the compact geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with small, rounded leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is native to South Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and containers. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for various landscaping applications.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for borders, rock gardens, and as a bedding plant.
