Phanera rutilans

Phanera rutilans, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a standout in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
