Phellodendron amurense var. lavallei

Phellodendron amurense var. lavallei, commonly known as the Amur walnut or Chinese walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters and small, round fruits that contain hard, edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
