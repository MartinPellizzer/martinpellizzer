Prostanthera aspalathoides

Prostanthera aspalathoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed honeysuckle, is a small shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It is well adapted to dry conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant foliage.
