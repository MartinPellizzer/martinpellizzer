Pinus africana

Pinus africana, commonly known as the African yellow pine, is a coniferous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive yellow-green foliage. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its needles are long and slender, typically growing in clusters of two.

Pinus africana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in semi-arid regions.
