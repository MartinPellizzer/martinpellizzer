Plantago bauphula

Plantago bauphula, commonly known as the Himalayan plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a dense spike, which are followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
