Potamogeton oppositifolius var. densus

Potamogeton oppositifolius var. densus, commonly known as the dense water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, linear leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a thick, bushy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Due to its dense growth, it plays a vital role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
