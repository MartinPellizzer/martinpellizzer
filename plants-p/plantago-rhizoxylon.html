Plantago rhizoxylon

Plantago rhizoxylon, commonly known as the fibrous plantain, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a robust rhizomatous root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions. The plant produces dense, narrow leaves with a waxy surface to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, typical of the Plantaginaceae family.

The plant is valued for its fibrous roots, which have been used traditionally in various applications.
