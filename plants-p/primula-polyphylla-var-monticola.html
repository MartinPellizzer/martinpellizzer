Primula polyphylla var. monticola

Primula polyphylla var. monticola, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, alpine environments at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a short, compact stem with broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and dark green in color. Its flowers are showy, with five petals that range in color from white to pale pink, and they bloom in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
