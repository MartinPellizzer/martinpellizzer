Piper praegnans

Piper praegnans, commonly known as the sacred pepper or the ghost pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that contain a high concentration of capsaicin, giving them their intense heat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its potency, it is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
