Papaver radicatum subsp. dovrense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

dovrense, commonly known as the Dovren's poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking red to orange flowers with a distinctive black center, blooming in late summer.

The plant has a fibrous root system and grows in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and adaptability to dry, well-drained conditions.
