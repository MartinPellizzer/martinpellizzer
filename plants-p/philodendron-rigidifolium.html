Philodendron rigidifolium

Philodendron rigidifolium, commonly known as the rigid-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its stiff, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread along the forest floor or climb trees in its natural habitat.

Philodendron rigidifolium thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
