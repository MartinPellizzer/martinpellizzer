Paspalum argyrocondylon

Paspalum argyrocondylon, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Silvergrass is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
