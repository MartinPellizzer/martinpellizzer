Peponidium carinatum

Peponidium carinatum, commonly known as the horned melon, is a tropical vine native to Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. The plant produces large, spiky green fruits that turn yellow when mature. The fruit's interior contains a sweet, edible pulp with black seeds.

It is valued for its unique appearance and culinary uses in various cuisines.
