Piptadenia aubrevillei

Piptadenia aubrevillei, commonly known as the African locust tree, is a leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often pinnately arranged, and its distinctive yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil.

It is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
