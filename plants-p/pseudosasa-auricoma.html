Pseudosasa auricoma

Pseudosasa auricoma, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching culms that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially in the sun-exposed areas. The leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a smooth, glossy surface with a distinct midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
