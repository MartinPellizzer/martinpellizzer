Pleopeltis normalis

Pleopeltis normalis, commonly known as the common pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rock faces or tree trunks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated margin.

Pleopeltis normalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
