Psoralea luteosa

Psoralea luteosa, commonly known as the yellow psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its compounds.
