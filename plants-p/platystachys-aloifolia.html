Platystachys aloifolia

Platystachys aloifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Platystachys aloifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
