Populus balsamifera f. candicans

Populus balsamifera f. candicans, commonly known as the candied poplar, is a variant of the balsam poplar characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, and sweet-smelling leaves.

This cultivar is notable for its unique leaf morphology, featuring a waxy, translucent appearance and a citrus-like aroma when crushed. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions, often near water bodies. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
