Panax delavayi

Panax delavayi, commonly known as Delavay ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots that are used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are compound, with a serrated margin, and it produces small, greenish flowers followed by red berries.

Due to its rarity and medicinal value, Panax delavayi is considered an endangered species.
