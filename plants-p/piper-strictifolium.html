Piper strictifolium

Piper strictifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tiny, peppery fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and is related to the pepper plants used in culinary applications.
