Populus × tomentiglandulosa

Populus × tomentiglandulosa, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in the spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and rapid growth.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
