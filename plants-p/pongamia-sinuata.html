Pongamia sinuata

Pongamia sinuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pongam, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive pod fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in various traditional and industrial applications. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
