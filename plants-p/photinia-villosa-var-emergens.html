Photinia villosa var. emergens

Photinia villosa var. emergens, commonly known as the silverleaf photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for hedges or specimen plants.
