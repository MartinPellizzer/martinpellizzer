Persica communis

Persica communis, commonly known as the common peach, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It produces showy pink or white flowers in the spring, which are followed by fuzzy, hairy fruits. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth and fruit production.
