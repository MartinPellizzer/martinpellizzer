Prunus nigricans var. dulcis

Prunus nigricans var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The flowers are white and appear in clusters, adding ornamental value to the plant.
