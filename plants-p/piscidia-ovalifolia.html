Piscidia ovalifolia

Piscidia ovalifolia, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with five petals and a crown of stamens. The leaves are ovate, alternate, and often have a waxy coating. The plant produces bright red berries that contain several seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
