Polypodium pectinatum var. hispidum

Polypodium pectinatum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound fronds that are covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The varietal form, *hispidum*, is distinguished by its dense, hairy appearance compared to other varieties of *Polypodium pectinatum*.
