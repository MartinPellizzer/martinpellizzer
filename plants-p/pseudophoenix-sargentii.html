Pseudophoenix sargentii

Pseudophoenix sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's false palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, feather-like leaves that give it a palm-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing shorelines.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable, making conservation efforts essential for its survival.
