Polycarpaea minuartioides

Polycarpaea minuartioides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the cooler months. Its adaptations to harsh environments include thick, water-storing leaves and a shallow root system.

Polycarpaea minuartioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
