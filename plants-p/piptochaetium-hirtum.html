Piptochaetium hirtum

Piptochaetium hirtum, commonly known as hairy piptochaetium, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna regions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers.

Piptochaetium hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
