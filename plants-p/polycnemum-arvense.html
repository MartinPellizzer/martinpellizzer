Polycnemum arvense

Polycnemum arvense, commonly known as field sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Polycnemum arvense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
