Peperomia reptilis

Peperomia reptilis, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a low-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Peperomia reptilis thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and rockeries.
