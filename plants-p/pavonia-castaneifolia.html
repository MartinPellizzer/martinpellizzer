Pavonia castaneifolia

Pavonia castaneifolia, commonly known as the flame flower, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, resembling the shape of a chestnut, hence its scientific name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Pavonia castaneifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
