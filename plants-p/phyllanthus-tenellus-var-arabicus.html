Phyllanthus tenellus var. arabicus

Phyllanthus tenellus var. arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian milk bush, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
