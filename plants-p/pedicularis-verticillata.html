Pedicularis verticillata

Pedicularis verticillata, commonly known as the whorled lousewort, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often resembling small lanterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, thriving at high altitudes where temperatures are cool. Its leaves are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.
