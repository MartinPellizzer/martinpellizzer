Pandanus limbatus

Pandanus limbatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescences are compound and contain both male and female flowers, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in its native regions.
