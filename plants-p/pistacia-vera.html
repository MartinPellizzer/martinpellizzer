Pistacia vera

Pistacia vera, commonly known as the pistachio tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces small, green, fuzzy fruits that mature into the familiar green-shelled nuts. The plant is valued for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary applications. Pistacia vera thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, requiring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics include compound leaves and an extensive root system that helps it survive in harsh environments.
