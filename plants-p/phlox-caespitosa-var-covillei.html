Phlox caespitosa var. covillei

Phlox caespitosa var. covillei, commonly known as Coville's blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and produces showy, blue-violet flower clusters in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is well adapted to arid and rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Coville's blue mistflower is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
