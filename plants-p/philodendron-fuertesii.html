Philodendron fuertesii

Philodendron fuertesii, commonly known as the Fuertesii Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
