Pelargonium × auriculatum

Pelargonium × auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate geranium, is a hybrid plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which have a velvety texture and a unique auriculate (ear-shaped) base. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming from spring to autumn. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and flowers.

Pelargonium × auriculatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.
