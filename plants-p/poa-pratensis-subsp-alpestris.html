Poa pratensis subsp. alpestris

Poa pratensis subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Its leaves are narrow, hairless, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
