Pleurothallis pabstii

Pleurothallis pabstii, commonly known as the Pabst's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and decaying organic matter.

Pleurothallis pabstii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
