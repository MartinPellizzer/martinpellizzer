Palaquium formosum

Palaquium formosum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible fruits that have a bright red outer skin and a segmented, white, juicy pulp.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
