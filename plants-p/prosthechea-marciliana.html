Prosthechea marciliana

Prosthechea marciliana, commonly known as the Brazilian green orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Prosthechea marciliana is noted for its ability to form pseudobulbs, which help it store water and nutrients.
