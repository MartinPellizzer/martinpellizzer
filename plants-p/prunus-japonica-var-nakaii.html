Prunus japonica var. nakaii

Prunus japonica var. nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is prized for its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular display. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a symbol of beauty and transience in Japanese culture.
