Polypodium argyratum

Polypodium argyratum, commonly known as the silver maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce numerous small, oval-shaped sporangia.

Polypodium argyratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
