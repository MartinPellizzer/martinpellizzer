Potentilla phyllocalyx subsp. dryadanthoides

Potentilla phyllocalyx subsp.

dryadanthoides, commonly known as the dryad's bower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, rounded form.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
