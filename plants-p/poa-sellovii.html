Poa sellovii

Poa sellovii, commonly known as the Sellovii grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms in the spring.

Poa sellovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
