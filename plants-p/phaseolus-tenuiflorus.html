Phaseolus tenuiflorus

Phaseolus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The flowers are small, yellow, and often appear in clusters, while the pods are slender and contain several seeds. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is valued for its edible beans.

The plant is adapted to warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
