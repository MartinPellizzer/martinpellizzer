Perfonon laurifolium

Perfonon laurifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf perfonon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
