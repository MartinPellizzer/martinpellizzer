Polygala amarella var. uliginosa

Polygala amarella var. uliginosa, commonly known as the bog milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in eastern North America.

It typically grows in marshy or boggy areas, where it thrives in poorly drained, acidic soils. The plant features slender, branched stems that bear small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in loose spikes. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
