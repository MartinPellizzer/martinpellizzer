Poa pratensis proles compressiformis

Poa pratensis proles compressiformis, commonly known as the compressed form of meadow grass, is a variant of the common meadow grass species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
