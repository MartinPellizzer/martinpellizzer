Piper galioides

Piper galioides, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as the common black pepper. The plant is characterized by its vine-like growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
