Pleurolobus brevifolia

Pleurolobus brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved pleurolobus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base of the plant. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
