Passiflora igrapiunensis

Passiflora igrapiunensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora igrapiunensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
