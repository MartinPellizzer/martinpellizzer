Pinus wangii var. kwangtungensis

Pinus wangii var. kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong pine, is a rare and endemic conifer native to southern China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The cones are small and woody, with a distinctive shape that aids in seed dispersal. This variety exhibits adaptations to its subtropical climate, including drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
