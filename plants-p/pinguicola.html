Pinguicola

Pinguicola, commonly known as the butterwort, is a carnivorous plant belonging to the family Lentibulariaceae.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and wet meadows. Its small, bilaterally symmetrical flowers are usually pink or purple in color.

Pinguicola is a perennial herb that thrives in cool, humid climates and is native to various regions across Europe, Asia, and Africa.
