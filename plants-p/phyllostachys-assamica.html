Phyllostachys assamica

Phyllostachys assamica, commonly known as the Assam bamboo, is a species of grass in the family Poaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, including regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Phyllostachys assamica is valued for its strong and flexible stalks, making it useful in construction and handicrafts.
