Pleurothallis holtonii

Pleurothallis holtonii, commonly known as the Holton's orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its inflorescence typically consists of several small, clustered blooms.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
