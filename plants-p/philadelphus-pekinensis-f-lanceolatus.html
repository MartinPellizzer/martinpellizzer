Philadelphus pekinensis f. lanceolatus

Philadelphus pekinensis f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate pekin honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white or pale yellow. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
