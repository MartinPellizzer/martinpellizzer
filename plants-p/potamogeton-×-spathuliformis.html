Potamogeton × spathuliformis

Potamogeton × spathuliformis, commonly known as the broadleaf pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is typically found in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender stems that are often submerged, with whorled leaves that vary in shape depending on their position along the stem. Its flowers are small and greenish, arranged in spike-like inflorescences, and it produces small, floating fruits.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
