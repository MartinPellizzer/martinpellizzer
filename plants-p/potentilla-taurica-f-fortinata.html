Potentilla taurica f. fortinata

Potentilla taurica f. fortinata, commonly known as the Caucasian cinquefoil, is a compact shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
