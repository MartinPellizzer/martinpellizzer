Plectranthus penicillatus

Plectranthus penicillatus, commonly known as the woolly mint, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, gray-green foliage and aromatic, mint-scented leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Plectranthus penicillatus is also valued for its use in herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
