Polypodium rosenstockii

Polypodium rosenstockii, commonly known as the Rosenstock's huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a compact rosette. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its spore-producing structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium rosenstockii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in controlled indoor conditions.
