Pelargonium xanthopetalum

Pelargonium xanthopetalum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its bright yellow leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, low-maintenance ornamental plants.

Its foliage and blooms add a striking visual contrast to gardens and containers.
