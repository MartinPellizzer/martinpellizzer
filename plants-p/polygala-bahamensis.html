Polygala bahamensis

Polygala bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, adapting to saline environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
