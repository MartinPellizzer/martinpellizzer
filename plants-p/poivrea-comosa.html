Poivrea comosa

Poivrea comosa, commonly known as the silky thimblecush, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its stems are upright and often branched, growing up to 30 centimeters in height.

Poivrea comosa is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in alpine environments.
