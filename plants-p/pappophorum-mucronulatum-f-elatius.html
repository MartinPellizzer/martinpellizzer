Pappophorum mucronulatum f. elatius

Pappophorum mucronulatum f. elatius, commonly known as the tall spikegrass, is a variant of the spikegrass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

This form is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.
