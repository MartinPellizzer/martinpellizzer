Polygala myrtilloides

Polygala myrtilloides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Polygala myrtilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
