Pycnanthemum clinopodioides

Pycnanthemum clinopodioides, commonly known as green mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces showy, lavender-blue tubular flowers in whorled clusters at the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
