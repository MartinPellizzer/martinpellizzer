Phaseolus truxillensis var. grandiflorus

Phaseolus truxillensis var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the Trinidadian runner bean, is a variety of the Phaseolus genus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant produces edible pods that are similar to common beans but have a slightly sweeter flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
