Phyllanthus acuminatus var. paraguariensis

Phyllanthus acuminatus var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan gooseberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in wetland or marshy areas.

The fruit is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible berries.
