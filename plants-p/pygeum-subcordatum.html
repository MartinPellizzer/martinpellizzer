Pygeum subcordatum

Pygeum subcordatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Proteaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
