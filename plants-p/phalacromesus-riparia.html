Phalacromesus riparia

Phalacromesus riparia, commonly known as the riverbank phalaris, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and riverine habitats.

It typically grows in shallow waters and moist soils, exhibiting rhizomatous growth that allows it to spread along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are usually greenish or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often floating or semi-submerged depending on water levels.

Phalacromesus riparia plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
