Prunus divaricata f. pendula

Prunus divaricata f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cherry or weeping almond, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its cascading branches that drape like a waterfall.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a flowering shrub or small tree native to southern China. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible fruits.

Its unique growth habit makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
