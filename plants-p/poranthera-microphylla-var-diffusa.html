Poranthera microphylla var. diffusa

Poranthera microphylla var. diffusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a small annual herb with slender, hairy stems and narrow, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
