Prunus communis monstr. variegata

Prunus communis monstr.

variegata, commonly known as the Variegated Cherry or Variegated Blossom Tree, is a striking ornamental plant characterized by its distinctive foliage. Its leaves are typically a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow, creating a vibrant visual effect.

The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in the spring, which add to its ornamental value. It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and public spaces.

This variety is a hybrid or variant of the common cherry, known for its unique leaf patterns and showy blooms.
