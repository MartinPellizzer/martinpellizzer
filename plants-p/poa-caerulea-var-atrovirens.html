Poa caerulea var. atrovirens

Poa caerulea var. atrovirens, commonly known as the dark green bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus characterized by its dense, fine-textured foliage.

It is native to parts of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant typically forms compact sods and is often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are dark green and slightly bluish in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to form a durable turf.
