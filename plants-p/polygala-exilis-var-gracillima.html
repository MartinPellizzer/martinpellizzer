Polygala exilis var. gracillima

Polygala exilis var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features slender stems and small, delicate flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, contributing to its overall delicate appearance.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
