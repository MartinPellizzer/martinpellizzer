Phyllanthus conami

Phyllanthus conami, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and limestone areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
