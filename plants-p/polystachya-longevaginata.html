Polystachya longevaginata

Polystachya longevaginata, commonly known as the long sheath fir.

This orchid is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It features a pseudobulbous structure with elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, fibrous sheath. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a raceme.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
