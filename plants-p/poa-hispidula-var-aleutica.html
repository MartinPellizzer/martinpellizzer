Poa hispidula var. aleutica

Poa hispidula var. aleutica, commonly known as the Aleutian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern Pacific coast of North America.

It typically grows in moist, coastal environments such as salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
