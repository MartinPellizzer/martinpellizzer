Piper vahlii

Piper vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a strong, pungent aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
