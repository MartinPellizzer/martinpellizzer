Pauridia glabella var. glabella

Pauridia glabella var. glabella, commonly known as the smooth pauridia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. The plant features small, opposite leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its scientific name. Its flowering stems produce clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and drought tolerance.
