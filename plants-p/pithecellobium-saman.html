Pithecellobium saman

Pithecellobium saman, commonly known as the saman or rain tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically bipinnate, and its distinctive, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces flat, bean-like pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for reforestation and ornamental landscaping.
