Panicum wilburii

Panicum wilburii, commonly known as Wilbur's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Panicum wilburii is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential for use in restoration projects.
