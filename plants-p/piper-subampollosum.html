Piper subampollosum

Piper subampollosum, commonly known as the Ampollosum pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into small, fleshy berries. The berries contain aromatic seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
