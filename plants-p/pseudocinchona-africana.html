Pseudocinchona africana

Pseudocinchona africana, commonly known as African quinine.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark purple to black berries that contain seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded areas of rainforests and is part of the Rubiaceae family.

Pseudocinchona africana is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine, particularly for its potential antimalarial properties.
