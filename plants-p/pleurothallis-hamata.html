Pleurothallis hamata

Pleurothallis hamata, commonly known as the hairy pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are usually dark green and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Pleurothallis hamata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
