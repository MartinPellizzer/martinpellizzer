Perovskia scrophulariifolia

Perovskia scrophulariifolia, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slight grayish hue, and it has a woody stem that becomes more branched with age.

Perovskia scrophulariifolia is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and pollinator-friendly gardens.
