Primula serra

Primula serra, commonly known as the serrated primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and serrated, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in early spring, making it a notable early season wildflower.
