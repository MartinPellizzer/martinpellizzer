Phylica rigidifolia

Phylica rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid thornveld protea, is a South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is typically found in grassland ecosystems.

Phylica rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
