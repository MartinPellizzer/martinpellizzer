Pisonia cuneifolia

Pisonia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pisonia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal areas and dry forests. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that are highly attractive to birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and it has a distinctive branching pattern.

Pisonia cuneifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
