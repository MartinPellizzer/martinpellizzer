Passiflora foetida var. baraquiniana

Passiflora foetida var. baraquiniana, commonly known as the Baraquiniana passionflower, is a climbing vine native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a mix of blue, purple, and white petals, along with a prominent crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed by wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
