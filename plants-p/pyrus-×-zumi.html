Pyrus × zumi

Pyrus × zumi, commonly known as the Japanese nashi or Chinese white pear.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Pyrus pyrifolia (Chinese white pear) and Pyrus betulaefolia (Manchurian pear). The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters, featuring broad, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. Its flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters, and it produces edible fruit that is similar to a pear in texture and flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for both its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.
