Phlox × glutinosa

Phlox × glutinosa, commonly known as sticky phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox × glutinosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
