Platanthera ditmariana f. longibracteata

Platanthera ditmariana f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a tall inflorescence bearing numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and wet meadows. Its unique floral structure includes long, slender bracts that give it its distinguishing name.

This orchid is particularly sensitive to environmental changes, making it an indicator species for habitat quality.
