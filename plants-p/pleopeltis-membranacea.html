Pleopeltis membranacea

Pleopeltis membranacea, commonly known as the membranous pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Pleopeltis membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
