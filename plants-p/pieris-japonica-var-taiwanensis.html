Pieris japonica var. taiwanensis

Pieris japonica var. taiwanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan azalea, is a deciduous shrub native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
