Perytis lamellosa

Perytis lamellosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Perytis lamellosa is often found in moist, shady environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
