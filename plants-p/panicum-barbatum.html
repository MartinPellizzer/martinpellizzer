Panicum barbatum

Panicum barbatum, commonly known as beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, grayish-brown panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

Panicum barbatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
