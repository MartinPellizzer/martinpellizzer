Potentilla argentea var. vulgaris

Potentilla argentea var. vulgaris, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
