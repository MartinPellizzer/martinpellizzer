Psammisia pedunculata

Psammisia pedunculata, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers. Psammisia pedunculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
