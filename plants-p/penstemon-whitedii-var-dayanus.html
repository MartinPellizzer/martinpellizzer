Penstemon whitedii var. dayanus

Penstemon whitedii var. dayanus, commonly known as the White Mountains penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
