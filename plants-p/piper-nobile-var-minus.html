Piper nobile var. minus

Piper nobile var. minus, commonly known as the lesser noble pepper, is a variant of the noble pepper plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The plant produces small berries that turn from green to red as they mature, containing a mild, peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for use in traditional medicine.

The botanical characteristics of this plant make it distinct from its parent species, with smaller leaves and a more compact growth habit.
