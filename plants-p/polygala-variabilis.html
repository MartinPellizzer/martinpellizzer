Polygala variabilis

Polygala variabilis, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, often yellow or greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Polygala variabilis is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insect species.
