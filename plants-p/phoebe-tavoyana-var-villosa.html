Phoebe tavoyana var. villosa

Phoebe tavoyana var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy phoebe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The varietal name 'villosa' refers to the hairy texture of its foliage, which is a key identifying feature.
