Pteris scandens

Pteris scandens, commonly known as the climbing fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its feathery fronds that often grow in a climbing or creeping manner. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread along the ground or up vertical surfaces. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Pteris scandens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
