Pediocactus mandragora

Pediocactus mandragora, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a species native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive, ribbed stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Pediocactus mandragora is known for its slow growth and ability to survive in harsh desert conditions.
