Potentilla sylvestris subvar. fissistipula

Potentilla sylvestris subvar. fissistipula, commonly known as the fissistipula cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or white. The leaves are usually compound, with five to seven leaflets that are finely toothed, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
