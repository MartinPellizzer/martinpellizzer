Pouzolzia poeppigiana

Pouzolzia poeppigiana, commonly known as the rock nettle, is a member of the Urticaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite leaves.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The leaves are usually lanceolate or ovate, with a hairy surface and serrated edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its fruits are dry, indehiscent capsules containing numerous tiny seeds.

This plant is often found in regions such as Mexico, Central America, and parts of the southwestern United States.
