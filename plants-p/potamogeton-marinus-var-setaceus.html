Potamogeton marinus var. setaceus

Potamogeton marinus var. setaceus, commonly known as the sea pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in brackish or saline waters, making it an important component of estuarine and coastal ecosystems.

Its presence is often indicative of stable, nutrient-rich aquatic environments.
