Polygala nitida var. lithophila

Polygala nitida var. lithophila, commonly known as the lithophytic milkwort, is a unique plant adapted to grow on rock surfaces.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in limestone or rocky soils.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
