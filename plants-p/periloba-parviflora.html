Periloba parviflora

Periloba parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered periloba, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
