Pueraria thunbergiana

Pueraria thunbergiana, commonly known as Chinese kudzu, is a vigorous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive. Its roots contain high levels of starch and are used in traditional medicine and as a food source.

Pueraria thunbergiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
