Piranhea trifoliolata var. trifoliolata

Piranhea trifoliolata var. trifoliolata, commonly known as the three-leaf piranhea, is a unique plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

It is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant's foliage is often a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture that helps it retain moisture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which appear in clusters during the warmer months.

Due to its ornamental leaves, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
