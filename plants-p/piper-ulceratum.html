Piper ulceratum

Piper ulceratum, commonly known as the ulcerated pepper or black pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically harvested when fully mature, and they have a pungent, spicy flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
