Piper praeterlatum

Piper praeterlatum, commonly known as the New Guinea pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
