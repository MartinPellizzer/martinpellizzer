Pseudocytisus integrifolius var. badalli

Pseudocytisus integrifolius var. badalli, commonly known as the Badalii honey bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are simple, entire, and slightly aromatic, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun conditions.
