Prunus sibirica f. pubescens

Prunus sibirica f. pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian apricot.

This plant is a frost-tolerant shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellow or orange drupe with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in cold climates.
