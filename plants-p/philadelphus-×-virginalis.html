Philadelphus × virginalis

Philadelphus × virginalis, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant, citrus-like fragrance.
