Picris daurica

Picris daurica, commonly known as Daurian groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single terminal head and bloom in late summer.

Picris daurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
