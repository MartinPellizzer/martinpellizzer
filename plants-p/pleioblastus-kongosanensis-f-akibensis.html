Pleioblastus kongosanensis f. akibensis

Pleioblastus kongosanensis f. akibensis, commonly known as the Akibai bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass native to Japan.

It features slender, dark green culms that are often adorned with distinctive silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, linear leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its elegant appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative bamboo.
