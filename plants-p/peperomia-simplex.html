Peperomia simplex

Peperomia simplex, commonly known as the shining peperomia, is a compact, low-maintenance plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, waxy leaves that are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a striking silver or white vein running through the center. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Peperomia simplex is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
