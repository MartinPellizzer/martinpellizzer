Probosciphora elephas

Probosciphora elephas, commonly known as the giant elephant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble elephant trunks, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex and intricate floral structure. Its flowers are typically adorned with vibrant colors and have a distinctive, elongated shape.

Probosciphora elephas thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires careful cultivation due to its specific ecological needs.
