Psychotria patria

Psychotria patria, commonly known as the "national flower of Peru," is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not widely consumed.

Psychotria patria is also noted for its cultural significance and is often used in traditional medicine.
