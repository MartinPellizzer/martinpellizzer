Potentilla hirta f. subcernavodae

Potentilla hirta f. subcernavodae, commonly known as the woolly cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The subspecies subcernavodae is distinguished by its reduced leaf pubescence and compact growth habit. Native to mountainous regions of Europe, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This plant is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
