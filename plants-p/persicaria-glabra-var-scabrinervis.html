Persicaria glabra var. scabrinervis

Persicaria glabra var. scabrinervis, commonly known as the smooth knotweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale pink to white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and slightly hairy veins, giving it the specific epithet *scabrinervis*. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

This plant is often found in the eastern and central United States, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
