Phyllanthus dictyospermus var. itacolumensis

Phyllanthus dictyospermus var. itacolumensis, commonly known as the Itacolumi phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has a woody stem with a rough, bark-like texture and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and local herbal practices.
