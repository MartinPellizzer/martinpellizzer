Piper villirameum

Piper villirameum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America and northern South America, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The plant's botanical features make it well adapted to thrive in shaded, moist conditions.
