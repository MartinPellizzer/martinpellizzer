Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus subsp. dilatatus

Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus subsp.

dilatatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
