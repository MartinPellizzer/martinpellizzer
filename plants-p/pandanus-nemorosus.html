Pandanus nemorosus

Pandanus nemorosus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, coastal or mountainous regions and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic leaves for culinary purposes.

The plant also produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fibrous, cone-like structures.
