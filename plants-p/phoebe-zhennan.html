Phoebe zhennan

Phoebe zhennan, commonly known as the Chinese sweet gum, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.

Phoebe zhennan is also notable for its role in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability and resilience.
