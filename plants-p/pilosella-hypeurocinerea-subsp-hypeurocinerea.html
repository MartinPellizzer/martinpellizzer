Pilosella hypeurocinerea subsp. hypeurocinerea

Pilosella hypeurocinerea subsp.

hypeurocinerea, commonly known as the paleleaf mouse-eared corydalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

This subspecies is native to southern Europe and is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often growing in low vegetation.

The plant is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
