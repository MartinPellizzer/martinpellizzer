Persicaria villosula

Persicaria villosula, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with four petals, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria villosula is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain environments.
