Pongamia extensa

Pongamia extensa, commonly known as the Indian beech, is a deciduous tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its role in nitrogen fixation. It produces a distinctive yellowish-pink flower cluster, which is often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its potential in reforestation efforts.
