Pultenaea viscidula

Pultenaea viscidula, commonly known as sticky pultenaea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, often glandular foliage that gives it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically appearing in spring. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, with a rough or hairy surface.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
