Piper callibracteum

Piper callibracteum, commonly known as the narrow-bract pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by elliptical berries that turn dark when mature. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is characterized by its unique bract structures that enclose the inflorescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
