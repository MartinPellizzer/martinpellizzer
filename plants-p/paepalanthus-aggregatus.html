Paepalanthus aggregatus

Paepalanthus aggregatus, commonly known as the clustered pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of cylindrical, spiny stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise prickly appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
