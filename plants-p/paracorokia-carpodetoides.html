Paracorokia carpodetoides

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fleshy, red berries that attract birds. The plant attaches itself to the branches of its host trees, primarily rimu and kahikatea, through a specialized root system called haustoria. Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
