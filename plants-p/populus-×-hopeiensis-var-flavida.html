Populus × hopeiensis var. flavida

Populus × hopeiensis var. flavida, commonly known as the golden poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus, characterized by its distinctive yellow foliage.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions, exhibiting rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a bright yellow coloration that becomes more pronounced in the fall. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant seasonal display.

It also plays a role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
