Primula farinosa var. americana

Primula farinosa var. americana, commonly known as the American primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an attractive and ecologically valuable addition to native plant gardens.
