Persica vulgaris subsp. erythrocarpa

Persica vulgaris subsp.

erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited peach. It is a subspecies of the peach tree characterized by its distinctive red or reddish-pink fruits.

The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The flowers are typically pink or white, and the fruit develops from a single ovary, ripening in late summer to early autumn.
