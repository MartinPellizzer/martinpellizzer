Plantago asiatica f. yakusimensis

Plantago asiatica f. yakusimensis, commonly known as Yakushima plantain, is a variant of the common plantain native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a distinctive silver-green coloration. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
