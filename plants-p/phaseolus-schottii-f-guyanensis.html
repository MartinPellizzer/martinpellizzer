Phaseolus schottii f. guyanensis

Phaseolus schottii f. guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana phaseolus, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the Phaseolus genus.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Guyana Shield. This plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, elongated pods that contain edible seeds. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its potential as a food crop and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
