Plumeria sericifolia

Plumeria sericifolia, commonly known as the silk cotton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant produces fluffy, cotton-like seeds enclosed in a woody capsule, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a silvery underside.

The tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and cultural significance.
