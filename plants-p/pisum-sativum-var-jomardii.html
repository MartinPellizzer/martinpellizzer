Pisum sativum var. jomardii

Pisum sativum var. jomardii, commonly known as the Jomard pea, is a variety of the common pea plant characterized by its distinctive pod and seed morphology.

It is a climbing legume with compound leaves and small, pea-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used for culinary purposes. This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
