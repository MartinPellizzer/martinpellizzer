Perilla lanceolata

Perilla lanceolata, commonly known as Chinese basil or Shiso, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces small, pale purple or white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round seed pods. Perilla lanceolata thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its leaves are rich in essential oils, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
