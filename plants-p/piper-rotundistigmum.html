Piper rotundistigmum

Piper rotundistigmum, commonly known as the round-veined pepper vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and pharmacological research.
