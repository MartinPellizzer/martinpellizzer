Pseudosasa wuyiensis

Pseudosasa wuyiensis, commonly known as the Wuyi bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Wuyi Mountains, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This bamboo is often found in moist, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense clusters that contribute to the local ecosystem's biodiversity.

Pseudosasa wuyiensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
