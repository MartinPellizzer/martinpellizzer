Polygonum × digeneum

Polygonum × digeneum, commonly known as redroot filago, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, three-lobed seed capsules. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
