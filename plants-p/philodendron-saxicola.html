Philodendron saxicola

Philodendron saxicola, commonly known as the rock philodendron, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing or trailing plant characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking variegation. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil. Its aerial roots allow it to attach to surfaces, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.

Philodendron saxicola is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
