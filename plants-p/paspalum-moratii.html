Paspalum moratii

Paspalum moratii, commonly known as Morat's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, which helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

Paspalum moratii is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, showing adaptability to different environments.
