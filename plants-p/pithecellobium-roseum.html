Pithecellobium roseum

Pithecellobium roseum, commonly known as the pink velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is notable for its vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the fruit is a flat, elongated pod containing edible seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has traditional uses in some cultures.
