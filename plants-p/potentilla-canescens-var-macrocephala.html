Potentilla canescens var. macrocephala

Potentilla canescens var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high elevations. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
