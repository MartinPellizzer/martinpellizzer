Panicum egregium

Panicum egregium, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically reddish in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often used as ornamental features in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Panicum egregium is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract wildlife.
