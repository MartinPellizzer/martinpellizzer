Persicaria otophylla

Persicaria otophylla, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered in dense, soft white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, blooming from late summer into early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Persicaria otophylla is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
