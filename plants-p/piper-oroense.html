Piper oroense

Piper oroense, commonly known as the golden pepper or golden pipper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of flowering plants, many of which are used for their aromatic properties. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns from green to yellow or orange as it matures.
