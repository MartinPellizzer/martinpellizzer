Plantago denticulata

Plantago denticulata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, slender stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and a wavy, toothed margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The leaves are commonly used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing and healing properties.
