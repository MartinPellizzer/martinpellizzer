Piper udicola

Piper udicola, commonly known as the dwarf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for indoor gardening and humid climates.
