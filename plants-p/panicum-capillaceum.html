Panicum capillaceum

Panicum capillaceum, commonly known as silky grass or hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, silky, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the grass family.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture for its elegant, flowing foliage.
