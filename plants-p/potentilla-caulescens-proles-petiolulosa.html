Potentilla caulescens proles petiolulosa

Potentilla caulescens proles petiolulosa, commonly known as the petiolulate potentilla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with hairy surfaces and short petioles.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
