Passiflora rugosa var. rugosa

Passiflora rugosa var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its rugged, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a unique crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. Its fruits are edible berries that turn from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
