Passiflora pilosa

Passiflora pilosa, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with five prominent sepals and a crown of filaments. Its fruit is a rough, greenish-yellow berry that matures into a red or orange color.

Passiflora pilosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
