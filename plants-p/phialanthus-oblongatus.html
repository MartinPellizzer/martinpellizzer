Phialanthus oblongatus

Phialanthus oblongatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Phialanthus oblongatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
