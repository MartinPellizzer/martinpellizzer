Pleuroplitis plumbea

Pleuroplitis plumbea, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
