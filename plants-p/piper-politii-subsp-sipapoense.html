Piper politii subsp. sipapoense

Piper politii subsp.

sipapoense, commonly known as the Sipapoense pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae. It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru.

The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences, and it produces small, round berries that are used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is often found in humid, shaded environments.
