Parsonsia lanceolata

Parsonsia lanceolata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, facilitating natural propagation.

Parsonsia lanceolata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
