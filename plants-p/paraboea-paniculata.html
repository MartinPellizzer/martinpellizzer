Paraboea paniculata

Paraboea paniculata, commonly known as the coral vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that resemble coral. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative trailing plant.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, making it an important component of local ecosystems.
