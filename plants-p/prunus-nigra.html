Prunus nigra

Prunus nigra, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy white flowers that appear in spring. The tree produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often used in jams and wines. Prunus nigra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its wood is also prized for its fine grain and durability in carpentry.
