Platanthera geniculata

Platanthera geniculata, commonly known as the fragrant lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its distinctive, fragrant, three-parted flower spike. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
