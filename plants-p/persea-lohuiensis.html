Persea lohuiensis

Persea lohuiensis, commonly known as the Lohu Island persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, astringent flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
