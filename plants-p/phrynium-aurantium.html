Phrynium aurantium

Phrynium aurantium, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a tropical perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It features striking, broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often tinged with yellow or orange, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes at the base of the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
