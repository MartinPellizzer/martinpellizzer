Philodendron henry-pittieri

Philodendron henry-pittieri, commonly known as the Henry Pittieri Philodendron, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is popular as a houseplant and in tropical gardens.
