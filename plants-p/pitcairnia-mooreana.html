Pitcairnia mooreana

Pitcairnia mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with brightly colored flowers, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rock faces or in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical and subtropical regions as a decorative plant.
