Potamogeton × suecicus f. alandicus

Potamogeton × suecicus f. alandicus, commonly known as the Aland water milfoil, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to northern Europe.

It is a submerged herb that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikelets. It is often found in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense underwater vegetation.

This species is important for aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
