Phyllanthus bahamensis

Phyllanthus bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is adapted to tolerate salt spray and is often found in dune ecosystems.
