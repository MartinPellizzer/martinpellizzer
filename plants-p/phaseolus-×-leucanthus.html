Phaseolus × leucanthus

Phaseolus × leucanthus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phaseolus vulgaris and Phaseolus glabellus. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces pods containing white or pale yellow seeds. It is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of protein and dietary fiber.

The plant's botanical features make it an important crop in traditional agriculture and food systems.
