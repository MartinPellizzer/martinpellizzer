Poterium verrucosum var. teneriffae

Poterium verrucosum var. teneriffae, commonly known as the Canary Islands spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in a waxy, glandular coating. The leaves are narrowly lance-shaped and typically have a bluish-green hue with a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in well-drained, sunny habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.
