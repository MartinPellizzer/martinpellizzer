Prunus mandshurica var. pubescens

Prunus mandshurica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Manchurian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a notable botanical characteristic.
