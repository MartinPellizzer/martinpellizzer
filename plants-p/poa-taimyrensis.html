Poa taimyrensis

Poa taimyrensis, commonly known as the Taimyren sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cold, moist environments such as tundra and wet meadows.

Poa taimyrensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic fauna.
