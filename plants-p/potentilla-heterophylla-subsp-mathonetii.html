Potentilla heterophylla subsp. mathonetii

Potentilla heterophylla subsp.

mathonetii, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage, with leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the growth environment.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to harsh mountain conditions makes it a hardy species with potential for use in alpine gardens.
