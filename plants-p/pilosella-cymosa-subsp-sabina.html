Pilosella cymosa subsp. sabina

Pilosella cymosa subsp.

sabina, commonly known as the Sabine hawkweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features a basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green composite flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and easily identifiable member of the Asteraceae family.
