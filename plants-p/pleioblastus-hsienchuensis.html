Pleioblastus hsienchuensis

Pleioblastus hsienchuensis, commonly known as the Hsienchu bamboo, is a species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, upright culms and fine, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The Hsienchu bamboo is also notable for its role in traditional Chinese culture and its use in landscaping.
