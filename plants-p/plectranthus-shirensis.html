Plectranthus shirensis

Plectranthus shirensis, commonly known as the Shirensis mint, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus shirensis is valued for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant.
