Piper setebarraense

Piper setebarraense, commonly known as the Setebarra pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems and compound leaves. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by the distinctive, elongated berries that contain the aromatic seeds.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is sometimes used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
