Populus × tomentosa var. stellaribracteata

Populus × tomentosa var. stellaribracteata, commonly known as the star-bracted poplar, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers, giving it a unique ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a silver-gray underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
