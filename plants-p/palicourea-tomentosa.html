Palicourea tomentosa

Palicourea tomentosa, commonly known as velvetleaf palicourea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Palicourea tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
