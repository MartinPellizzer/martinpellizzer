Phlox covillei

Phlox covillei, commonly known as Coville's phlox, is a North American native plant belonging to the Polemoniaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It features upright, branching stems covered with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

Coville's phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
