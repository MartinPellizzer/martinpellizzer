Psidium araucanum

Psidium araucanum, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, similar to guavas but with a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
