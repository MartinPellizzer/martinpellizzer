Panicum numidianum

Panicum numidianum, commonly known as dwarf millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, upright stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Panicum numidianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
