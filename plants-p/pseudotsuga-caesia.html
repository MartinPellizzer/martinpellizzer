Pseudotsuga caesia

Pseudotsuga caesia, commonly known as the white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its conical shape, blue-green needles, and distinctive cones that hang downward. The tree's bark is thick and furrowed, providing protection against environmental stress. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in forest ecosystems and a popular ornamental plant.
