Pyrus mongolica

Pyrus mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the cold climates of Mongolia and northern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically small and tart, with a skin that is thin and delicate. It is well adapted to harsh environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
