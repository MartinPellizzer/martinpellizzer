Pavonia pulchra

Pavonia pulchra, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Pavonia pulchra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
