Pilosocereus fulvilanatus subsp. rosae

Pilosocereus fulvilanatus subsp.

rosae, commonly known as the Rosy Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
