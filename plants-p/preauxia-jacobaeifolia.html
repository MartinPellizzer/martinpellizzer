Preauxia jacobaeifolia

Preauxia jacobaeifolia, commonly known as the Jacob's weed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster at the top of the stem. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its succulent leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and a fibrous root system.

Preauxia jacobaeifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and resilience.
