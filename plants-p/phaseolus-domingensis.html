Phaseolus domingensis

Phaseolus domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican bean or wild kidney bean, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces small, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements, and its flowers are typically white or pale purple. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its nutritional seeds and has been used in traditional agriculture and cuisine.
