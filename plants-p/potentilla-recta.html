Potentilla recta

Potentilla recta, commonly known as upright cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wet habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with five leaflets that are lance-shaped and hairy.

Potentilla recta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
