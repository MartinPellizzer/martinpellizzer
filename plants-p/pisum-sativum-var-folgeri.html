Pisum sativum var. folgeri

Pisum sativum var. folgeri, commonly known as the dwarf garden pea, is a compact variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its smaller stature, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters, making it ideal for container gardening and small spaces. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow or green seeds, similar to standard garden peas. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation, making it a popular choice among home gardeners.
