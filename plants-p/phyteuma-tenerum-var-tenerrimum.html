Phyteuma tenerum var. tenerrimum

Phyteuma tenerum var. tenerrimum, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath.

It is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in partially shaded conditions.
