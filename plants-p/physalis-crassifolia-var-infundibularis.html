Physalis crassifolia var. infundibularis

Physalis crassifolia var. infundibularis, commonly known as the lantern fruit or Cape gooseberry, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features thick, fleshy calyces that encase its small, yellow-orange berries. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its edible, sweet-tart berries.

The botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both gardens and agricultural settings.
