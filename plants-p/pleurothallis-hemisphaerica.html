Pleurothallis hemisphaerica

Pleurothallis hemisphaerica, commonly known as the helmet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually small, with a pale yellow or greenish hue, and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Pleurothallis hemisphaerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate beauty.
