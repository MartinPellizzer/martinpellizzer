Philodendron tuxtlanum

Philodendron tuxtlanum, commonly known as the Tuxtla philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often variegated with lighter green or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, red fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
