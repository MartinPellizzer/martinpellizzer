Polygonum multiflorum var. hypoleucum

Polygonum multiflorum var. hypoleucum, commonly known as the white polygonum, is a variant of the common plantain that exhibits distinct botanical characteristics.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia, particularly China. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth margin and produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

The leaves and roots are typically harvested for their medicinal properties, which are believed to support liver health and promote longevity.
