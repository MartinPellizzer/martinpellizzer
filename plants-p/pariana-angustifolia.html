Pariana angustifolia

Pariana angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pariana, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It is often found at high altitudes, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
