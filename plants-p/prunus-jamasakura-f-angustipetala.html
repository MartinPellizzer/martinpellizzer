Prunus jamasakura f. angustipetala

Prunus jamasakura f. angustipetala, commonly known as the narrow-petioled Japanese cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaf petioles and delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its blossoms.

This variety is well-suited for temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial sunlight for optimal growth.
