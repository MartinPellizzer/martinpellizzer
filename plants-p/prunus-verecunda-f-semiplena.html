Prunus verecunda f. semiplena

Prunus verecunda f. semiplena, commonly known as the semi-double Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus verecunda species, which is native to Japan.

It is characterized by its semi-double flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate and ornamental display to gardens. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a rounded or spreading habit. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth margin and a dark green color, providing an attractive backdrop to the blossoms.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in parks, gardens, and along streets for its aesthetic and seasonal beauty.
