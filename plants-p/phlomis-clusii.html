Phlomis clusii

Phlomis clusii, commonly known as clustered mullein, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in whorls and range in color from white to purple.

Phlomis clusii is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
