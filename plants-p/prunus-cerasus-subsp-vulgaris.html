Prunus cerasus subsp. vulgaris

Prunus cerasus subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the sweet cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruit contains a single hard pit and is valued for its sweet, juicy flesh.

It is widely cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
