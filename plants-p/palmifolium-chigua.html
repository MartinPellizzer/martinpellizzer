Palmifolium chigua

Palmifolium chigua, commonly known as the Chigua palm, is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter, giving it a striking visual presence. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries, which are sometimes used in local cuisines. Its trunk is slender and often segmented, with a smooth, grayish-brown bark.

The Chigua palm is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in cloud forests.
