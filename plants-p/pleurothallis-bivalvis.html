Pleurothallis bivalvis

Pleurothallis bivalvis, commonly known as the two-valved pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing on tree branches or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
