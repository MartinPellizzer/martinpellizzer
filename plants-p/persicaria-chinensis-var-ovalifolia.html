Persicaria chinensis var. ovalifolia

Persicaria chinensis var. ovalifolia, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like inflorescences during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
