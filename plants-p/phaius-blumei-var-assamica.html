Phaius blumei var. assamica

Phaius blumei var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
