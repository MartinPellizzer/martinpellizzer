Piptolepis schwackeana

Piptolepis schwackeana, commonly known as the California broom.

It is a flowering plant native to the western United States, particularly California. The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.
