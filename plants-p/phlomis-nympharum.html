Phlomis nympharum

Phlomis nympharum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, gray-green, velvety leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

Phlomis nympharum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
