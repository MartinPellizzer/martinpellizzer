Parathesis obovalifolia

Parathesis obovalifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf parathesis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
