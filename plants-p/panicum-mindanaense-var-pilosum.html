Panicum mindanaense var. pilosum

Panicum mindanaense var. pilosum, commonly known as the Mindanao panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is a branched cluster of spikelets.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
