Penstemon palmeri subsp. eglandulosus

Penstemon palmeri subsp.

eglandulosus, commonly known as the glandless penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
