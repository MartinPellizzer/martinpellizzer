Pelargonium burmanni

Pelargonium burmanni, commonly known as the Burmann's geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its fleshy, dark green leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
