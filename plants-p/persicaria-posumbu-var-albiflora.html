Persicaria posumbu var. albiflora

Persicaria posumbu var. albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
