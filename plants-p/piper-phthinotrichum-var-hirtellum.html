Piper phthinotrichum var. hirtellum

Piper phthinotrichum var. hirtellum, commonly known as the hairy sumpweed, is a variant of the sumpweed species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are capsules containing numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
