Pterocephalus brachiatus

Pterocephalus brachiatus, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
