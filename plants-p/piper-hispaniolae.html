Piper hispaniolae

Piper hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola pepper or Spanish pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper and long pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they contain several seeds.

Piper hispaniolae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
