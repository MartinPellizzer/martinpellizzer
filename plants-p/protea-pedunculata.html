Protea pedunculata

Protea pedunculata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central stem surrounded by a cluster of white to pale pink bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Protea pedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
