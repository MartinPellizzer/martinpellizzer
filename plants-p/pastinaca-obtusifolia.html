Pastinaca obtusifolia

Pastinaca obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved scorzonera, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with ray florets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value and edible roots, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
