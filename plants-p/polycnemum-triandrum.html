Polycnemum triandrum

Polycnemum triandrum, commonly known as the three-stamen nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a common but often overlooked component of temperate forest ecosystems.
