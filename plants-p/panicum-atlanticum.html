Panicum atlanticum

Panicum atlanticum, commonly known as American foxtail, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces distinctive spikelets that resemble a foxtail, which are often hairy and can be up to several inches in length. It thrives in full sun and is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in fields.

Panicum atlanticum is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
