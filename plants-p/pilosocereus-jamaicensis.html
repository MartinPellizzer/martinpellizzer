Pilosocereus jamaicensis

Pilosocereus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican cactus or the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its columnar stems that are covered in short, dense spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white, during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
