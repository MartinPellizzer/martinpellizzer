Polygonum ferrugineum

Polygonum ferrugineum, commonly known as redstem smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright stems that are reddish-brown in color, often with a reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum ferrugineum is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
