Pelargonium pendulum

Pelargonium pendulum, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features serrated, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium pendulum is well-suited for warm climates and requires well-draining soil to thrive.
