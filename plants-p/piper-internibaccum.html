Piper internibaccum

Piper internibaccum, commonly known as the slender pipevine, is a climbing vine native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes and are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that turns red when mature, attracting birds and other animals.
