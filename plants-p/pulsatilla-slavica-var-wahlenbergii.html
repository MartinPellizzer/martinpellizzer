Pulsatilla slavica var. wahlenbergii

Pulsatilla slavica var. wahlenbergii, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, calcareous soils. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
