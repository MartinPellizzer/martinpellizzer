Physurus macer

Physurus macer, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its thin, upright stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Physurus macer is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
