Phyllanthus tenellus var. roxburghii

Phyllanthus tenellus var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Indian false mallow, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
