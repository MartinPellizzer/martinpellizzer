Phyllanthus paivanus

Phyllanthus paivanus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and has a woody stem with a distinctive, cheese-like scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or near streams.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
