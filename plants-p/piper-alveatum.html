Piper alveatum

Piper alveatum, commonly known as the white pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. The fruits are typically harvested when mature, and the seeds inside are used to produce white pepper.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic seeds.
