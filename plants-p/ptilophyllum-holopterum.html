Ptilophyllum holopterum

Ptilophyllum holopterum, commonly known as the broad-leaved water fern, is a species of aquatic fern belonging to the family Marsileaceae.

It is characterized by its floating leaves that are divided into four lobes, resembling a small, four-leaf clover. Ptilophyllum holopterum is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
