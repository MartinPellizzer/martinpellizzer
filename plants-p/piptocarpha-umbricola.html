Piptocarpha umbricola

Piptocarpha umbricola, commonly known as the dark soil globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, rounded leaflets.

Piptocarpha umbricola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
