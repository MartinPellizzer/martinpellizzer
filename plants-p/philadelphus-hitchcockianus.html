Philadelphus hitchcockianus

Philadelphus hitchcockianus, commonly known as the Hitchcock's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
