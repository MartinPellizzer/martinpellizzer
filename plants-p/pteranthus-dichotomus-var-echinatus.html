Pteranthus dichotomus var. echinatus

Pteranthus dichotomus var. echinatus, commonly known as the spiny pteranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose its small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental bracts make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
