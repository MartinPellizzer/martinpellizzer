Primula mathildae

Primula mathildae, commonly known as the Mathilde's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
