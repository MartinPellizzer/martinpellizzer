Phycella chilensis

Phycella chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal areas of Chile.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
