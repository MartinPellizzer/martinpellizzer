Polypodium trifurcatum

Polypodium trifurcatum, commonly known as the forked polypody, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often form a rosette shape. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are wiry and can spread to form dense colonies.

Polypodium trifurcatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings.
