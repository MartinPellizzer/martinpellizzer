Potamogeton diversifolius

Potamogeton diversifolius, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often forming dense underwater stands. The plant exhibits morphological variation, with leaves that can be linear, lanceolate, or even broad, depending on environmental conditions. Its small, green flowers are typically arranged in spike-like clusters and are pollinated by water currents.

Potamogeton diversifolius plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.
