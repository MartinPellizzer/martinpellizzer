Paeonia corallina var. maroccana

Paeonia corallina var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan coral peony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It is characterized by its striking coral-pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces showy, single or semi-double petals that are often adorned with a contrasting white or pale pink base. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
