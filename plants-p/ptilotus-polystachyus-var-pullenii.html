Ptilotus polystachyus var. pullenii

Ptilotus polystachyus var. pullenii, commonly known as the "Desert Flax" or "Kangaroo Paw," is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, grass-like leaves and showy, red or orange flower spikes that resemble a kangaroo's paw. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland sandy regions.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological importance in its native habitat.
