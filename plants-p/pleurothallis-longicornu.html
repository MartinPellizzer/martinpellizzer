Pleurothallis longicornu

Pleurothallis longicornu, commonly known as the longhorn pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its unique characteristic is the elongated, curved sepals and petals, which give it its common name.

Pleurothallis longicornu is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
