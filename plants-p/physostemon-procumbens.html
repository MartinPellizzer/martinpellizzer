Physostemon procumbens

Physostemon procumbens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Physostemon procumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
