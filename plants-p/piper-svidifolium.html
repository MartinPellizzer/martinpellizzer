Piper svidifolium

Piper svidifolium, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered green flowers. It produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The botanical features of Piper svidifolium make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and culinary use.
