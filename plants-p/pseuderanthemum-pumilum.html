Pseuderanthemum pumilum

Pseuderanthemum pumilum, commonly known as the small pumilum pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are often brightly colored, ranging from red to purple, and are borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
