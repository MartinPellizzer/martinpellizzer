Phaseolus esperanzae

Phaseolus esperanzae, commonly known as Esperanza bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its climbing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that give way to elongated, bean-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is adapted to thrive in warm, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
