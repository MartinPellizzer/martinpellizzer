Pouteria congestifolia

Pouteria congestifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pouteria* species, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically white or pink and arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are typically elliptical and alternate along the stems, while the bark is smooth and often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit production in its native habitat.
