Pabstiella diffusiflora

Pabstiella diffusiflora, commonly known as the spreading-flowered pabstiella, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid tropical forests. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires careful preservation efforts.
