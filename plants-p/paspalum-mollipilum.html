Paspalum mollipilum

Paspalum mollipilum, commonly known as the silky paspalum, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it produces small, pale green to brown seeds.

Paspalum mollipilum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
