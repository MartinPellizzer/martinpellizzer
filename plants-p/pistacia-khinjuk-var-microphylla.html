Pistacia khinjuk var. microphylla

Pistacia khinjuk var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces edible, stony fruits containing a single seed. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its fruit is often used for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in oil and protein.

The plant also has historical significance in traditional medicine and agriculture.
