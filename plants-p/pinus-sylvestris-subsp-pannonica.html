Pinus sylvestris subsp. pannonica

Pinus sylvestris subsp.

pannonica, commonly known as the Hungarian spruce, is a subspecies of the common pine native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and relatively short, stiff needles that are typically bluish-green in color.

The cones are woody and elongated, with scales that open to release the seeds. This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important tree in its native habitat.
