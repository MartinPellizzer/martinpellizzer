Potentilla egedii subsp. grandis

Potentilla egedii subsp.

grandis, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing shrub with small, deciduous leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. It is often found in meadows and along mountain slopes at high elevations.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies of alpine flora.
