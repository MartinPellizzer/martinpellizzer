Pelargonium longifolium

Pelargonium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and attractive foliage.
