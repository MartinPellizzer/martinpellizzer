Pseudocoix

Pseudocoix, commonly known as water bamboo or Chinese water chestnut, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, triangular-shaped tubers that are edible and have a nutty flavor. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are tall and often branched.

The botanical characteristics of Pseudocoix make it well adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
