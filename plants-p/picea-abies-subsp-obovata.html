Picea abies subsp. obovata

Picea abies subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the Norway spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to northern Europe. It is characterized by its conical shape, dark green needle-like leaves, and distinctive cones.

The subspecies obovata is distinguished by its broader, more ovoid leaves compared to the typical Norway spruce. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in horticulture and forestry.

This species plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
