Panicum commutatum var. nodosum

Panicum commutatum var. nodosum, commonly known as nodular panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear dense clusters of spikelets. The plant produces small, two-rowed spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and tapering, with a rough texture and a prominent midrib.

This variety is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
