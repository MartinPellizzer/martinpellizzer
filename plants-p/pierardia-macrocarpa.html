Pierardia macrocarpa

Pierardia macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones, which are typically found on the underside of its fronds.

The plant plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
