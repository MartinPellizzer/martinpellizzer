Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. sanguinea

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. sanguinea, commonly known as the Red-Horned Orchid, is a hybrid orchid species characterized by its striking floral features.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant typically produces pendulous, showy flowers in shades of red and pink, with a unique horn-like structure on the petals. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its long blooming period and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to flourish.
