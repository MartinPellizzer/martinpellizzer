Phaca leptalea

Phaca leptalea, commonly known as the slender phaca, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or coastal habitats, where it demonstrates a high degree of adaptability.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
