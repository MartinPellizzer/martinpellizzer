Potamogeton similis

Potamogeton similis, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across North America.

It has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Potamogeton similis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.
