Papaver orientale var. oreophilum

Papaver orientale var. oreophilum, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with a distinctive black center. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and palmate leaves that are typically lobed and hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is heightened by its dramatic blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
