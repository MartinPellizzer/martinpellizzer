Pogonia calantha

Pogonia calantha, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, hair-like leaves and its striking pink or purple flower spikes that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a dense, truss-like cluster, and the species is noted for its delicate, fragrant blossoms.

Pogonia calantha is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
