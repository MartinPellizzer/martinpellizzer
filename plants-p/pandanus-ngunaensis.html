Pandanus ngunaensis

Pandanus ngunaensis, commonly known as the Nguna pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for weaving and thatching, highlighting its cultural and practical significance.

The species is adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
