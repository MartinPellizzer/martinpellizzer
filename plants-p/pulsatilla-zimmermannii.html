Pulsatilla zimmermannii

Pulsatilla zimmermannii, commonly known as the Alpine pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, typically yellow or white flowers. The plant has a short, thick rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing several cup-shaped blooms. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Pulsatilla zimmermannii thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
