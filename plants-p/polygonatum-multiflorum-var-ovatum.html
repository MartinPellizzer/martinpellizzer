Polygonatum multiflorum var. ovatum

Polygonatum multiflorum var. ovatum, commonly known as the Japanese Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. Its berries are dark blue to black when mature and are often consumed by birds.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
