Phlox ovata f. carolina

Phlox ovata f. carolina, commonly known as Carolina phlox, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polemoniaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, with five petals and a prominent yellow center. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
