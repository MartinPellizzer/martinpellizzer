Parasenecio × cuneatus

Parasenecio × cuneatus, commonly known as the hybrid groundsel, is a composite plant native to southern Africa.

It is a hybrid between Parasenecio cuneatus and other related species, resulting in a plant with distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are often branched.

Parasenecio × cuneatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
