Platydesma auriculifolium

Platydesma auriculifolium, commonly known as the ear-leaved daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, ear-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
