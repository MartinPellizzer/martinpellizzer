Potentilla lancifolia

Potentilla lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include hairy stems and a compact growth habit, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh climates.

Potentilla lancifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
