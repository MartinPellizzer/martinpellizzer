Pinus tabuliformis var. tabuliformis

Pinus tabuliformis var. tabuliformis, commonly known as the Chinese red pine, is a deciduous conifer native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, flaky layers. The tree produces long, slender cones that mature to a reddish color, and its needles are typically arranged in bundles of five. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
