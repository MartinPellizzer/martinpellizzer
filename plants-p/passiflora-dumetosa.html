Passiflora dumetosa

Passiflora dumetosa, commonly known as the creeping passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The flowers are typically blue or purple with a yellow center, and they are pollinated by insects and birds. This species thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
