Plectrornis virescens

Plectrornis virescens, commonly known as the Australian raven, is a large, robust bird with a distinctive black plumage and a strong, hooked beak.

It is native to Australia and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and coastal areas. The species is known for its intelligence and complex social behaviors, including vocal mimicry and cooperative foraging. While not a plant, it plays an important ecological role in seed dispersal and insect control.

Its presence is a sign of a healthy ecosystem in its native range.
