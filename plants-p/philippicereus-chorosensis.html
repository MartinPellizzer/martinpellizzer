Philippicereus chorosensis

Philippicereus chorosensis, commonly known as the Chorosensis cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
