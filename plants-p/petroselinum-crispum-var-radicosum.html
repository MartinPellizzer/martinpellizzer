Petroselinum crispum var. radicosum

Petroselinum crispum var. radicosum, commonly known as Italian parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, bright green leaves with a mild, fresh parsley flavor. The plant has a taproot system that allows it to thrive in well-drained soils. It produces small, white flowers that attract pollinators like bees.

This variety is often cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and aromatic qualities.
