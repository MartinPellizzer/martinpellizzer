Pachystachys riedeliana

Pachystachys riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale green to golden flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
