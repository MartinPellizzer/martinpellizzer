Peltandra longipes

Peltandra longipes, commonly known as the long-footed arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Peltandra longipes is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to moist, shaded environments.
