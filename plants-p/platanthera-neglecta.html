Platanthera neglecta

Platanthera neglecta, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single, elongated spike of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. Its roots are mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
