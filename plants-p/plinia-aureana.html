Plinia aureana

Plinia aureana, commonly known as the golden guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its attractive, yellow-orange fruits that are both ornamental and edible. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.

The flowers are small, white, and clustered, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
