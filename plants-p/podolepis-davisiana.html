Podolepis davisiana

Podolepis davisiana, commonly known as the Davis's podolepis, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
