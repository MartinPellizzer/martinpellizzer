Plantago atrata subsp. atrata

Plantago atrata subsp.

atrata, commonly known as the black plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.

Its fibrous root system and dense foliage make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
