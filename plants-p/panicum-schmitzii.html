Panicum schmitzii

Panicum schmitzii, commonly known as Schmitz's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
