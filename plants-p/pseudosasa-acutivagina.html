Pseudosasa acutivagina

Pseudosasa acutivagina, commonly known as the sharp-ventured bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Pseudosasa acutivagina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
