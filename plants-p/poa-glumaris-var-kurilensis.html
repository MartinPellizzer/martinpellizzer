Poa glumaris var. kurilensis

Poa glumaris var. kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Kuril Islands and parts of eastern Russia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted growth with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, two-lobed florets.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
