Phalaenopsis fasciata var. fasciata

Phalaenopsis fasciata var. fasciata, commonly known as the streaked phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with distinctive markings. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a branching pattern with pseudobulbs.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its longevity and ease of care in indoor environments.
