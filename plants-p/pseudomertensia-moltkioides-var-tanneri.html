Pseudomertensia moltkioides var. tanneri

Pseudomertensia moltkioides var. tanneri, commonly known as Tanner's false mertensia, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to native plant gardens and restoration projects.
