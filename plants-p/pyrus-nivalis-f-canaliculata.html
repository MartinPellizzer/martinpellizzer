Pyrus nivalis f. canaliculata

Pyrus nivalis f. canaliculata, commonly known as the Snow Pear, is a unique cultivar of the white pear tree.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, fruit with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are typically small and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale fruit.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
