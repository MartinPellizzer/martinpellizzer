Phragmites communis f. stolonifera

Phragmites communis f. stolonifera, commonly known as the common reed, is a perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant spreads through rhizomes and stolons, forming dense colonies that can dominate wetland ecosystems. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its presence is often an indicator of water availability and wetland health.
