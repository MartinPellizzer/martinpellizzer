Panopsis antioquensis

Panopsis antioquensis, commonly known as the Antioquia panicle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Panopsis antioquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
