Pentameris dregeana

Pentameris dregeana, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering season, which can extend from late winter to early spring.
