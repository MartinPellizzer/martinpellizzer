Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvovariegatus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fulvovariegatus, commonly known as the variegated common bean, is a variant of the common bean plant characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers.

Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications, though the variegated foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant as well.
