Populina richardii

Populina richardii, commonly known as the Richard's poplart, is a species of flowering plant in the family Salicaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and its compound leaves with serrated edges. Populina richardii is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas.
