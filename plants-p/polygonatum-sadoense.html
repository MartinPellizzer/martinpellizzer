Polygonatum sadoense

Polygonatum sadoense, commonly known as the Sado Solomon's Seal, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
