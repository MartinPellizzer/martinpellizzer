Physalis viscosa subsp. mollis

Physalis viscosa subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the sticky gooseberry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae. It is characterized by its hairy, sticky leaves and stems, which give it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into inflated, orange-red calyces enclosing the fruit. The fruit is typically a small, round berry with a sweet-tart flavor.

It is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
