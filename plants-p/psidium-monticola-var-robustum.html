Psidium monticola var. robustum

Psidium monticola var. robustum, commonly known as the robust guava, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are ovate, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
