Polygonum austiniae

Polygonum austiniae, commonly known as Austin's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces elongated seed pods that split open to release seeds.

Polygonum austiniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
