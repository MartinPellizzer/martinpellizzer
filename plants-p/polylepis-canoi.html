Polylepis canoi

Polylepis canoi, commonly known as the Andean wax tree, is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in harsh, cold climates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by the development of woody, cone-like structures containing seeds. Polylepis canoi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various Andean wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered vulnerable and faces significant conservation challenges.
