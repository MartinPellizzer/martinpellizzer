Pilea chamaedrys

Pilea chamaedrys, commonly known as the aluminum plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its attractive foliage make it a favored ornamental plant.
