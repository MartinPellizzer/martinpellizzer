Paederia secundiflora

Paederia secundiflora, commonly known as the creeping false indigo, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers. Its root system is fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, making it a persistent ground cover.

Paederia secundiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
