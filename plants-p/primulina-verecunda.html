Primulina verecunda

Primulina verecunda, commonly known as the silver stripe calathea, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green stripes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are borne on a spadix, typical of the arum family. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant among foliage enthusiasts.
