Pandanus raiateaensis

Pandanus raiateaensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for thatching and weaving.

The species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in warm, humid climates.
