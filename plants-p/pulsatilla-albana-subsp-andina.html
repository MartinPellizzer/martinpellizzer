Pulsatilla albana subsp. andina

Pulsatilla albana subsp.

andina, commonly known as the Andean pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms with white petals and a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky soils, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and more robust growth compared to other members of the Pulsatilla genus.
