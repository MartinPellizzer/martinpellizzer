Phyllanthus liebmannianus

Phyllanthus liebmannianus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
