Polygonum debile f. purpurea

Polygonum debile f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often tinged with a reddish or purplish hue. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and they are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters, and the plant produces small, three-lobed berries.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
