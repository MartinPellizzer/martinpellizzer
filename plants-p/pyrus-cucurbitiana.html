Pyrus cucurbitiana

Pyrus cucurbitiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears but have a distinct, crisp texture. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
