Podochilus trilobus

Podochilus trilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence bears small, clustered flowers with three distinct lobes.

Podochilus trilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
