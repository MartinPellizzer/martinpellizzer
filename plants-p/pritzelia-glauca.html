Pritzelia glauca

Pritzelia glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Pritzelia glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide nectar for pollinators.
