Primulina repanda

Primulina repanda, commonly known as the silver spider plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a compact, evergreen perennial that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 cm. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery-white margin that gives it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often in clusters.

Primulina repanda prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
