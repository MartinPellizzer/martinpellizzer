Philadelphus delavayi var. melanocalyx

Philadelphus delavayi var. melanocalyx, commonly known as the black-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that often have a slight reddish or purplish tinge. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and attractive appearance.
