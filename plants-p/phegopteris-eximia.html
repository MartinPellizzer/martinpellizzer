Phegopteris eximia

Phegopteris eximia, commonly known as the golden fern, is a species of fern native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, palmately divided fronds that are typically bright yellow to golden in color, especially when young. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its fronds are composed of numerous pinnae, which are arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Phegopteris eximia plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various small organisms.
