Potentilla fragarioides var. trilobata

Potentilla fragarioides var. trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with three leaflets, each typically lobed or notched. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and long blooming period.
