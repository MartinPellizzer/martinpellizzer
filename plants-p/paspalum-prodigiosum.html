Paspalum prodigiosum

Paspalum prodigiosum, commonly known as the red paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and vibrant red or purple flower spikes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that contrasts with the colorful inflorescences.

Paspalum prodigiosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
