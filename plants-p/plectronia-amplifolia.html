Plectronia amplifolia

Plectronia amplifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf plectronia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
