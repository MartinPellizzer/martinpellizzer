Piper exserens var. mirafloresanum

Piper exserens var. mirafloresanum, commonly known as the Miraflores pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, peppery fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is cultivated for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
