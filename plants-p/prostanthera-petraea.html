Prostanthera petraea

Prostanthera petraea, commonly known as the mountain mint bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and they bloom in spring.

Prostanthera petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
