Polygonum cespitosum var. seminudum

Polygonum cespitosum var. seminudum, commonly known as the naked-stem smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often hairless or nearly so, giving it its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface, while the small, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish in color.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
