Porophyllum curticeps

Porophyllum curticeps, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Porophyllum curticeps is adapted to arid environments and is considered a resilient weed in some regions.
