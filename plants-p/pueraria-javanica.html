Pueraria javanica

Pueraria javanica, commonly known as the climbing bean or Japanese kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple-pink flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing several oval seeds. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Pueraria javanica is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive if not properly managed.
