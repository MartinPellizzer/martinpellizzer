Phalaris maderensis

Phalaris maderensis, commonly known as the Mader's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
