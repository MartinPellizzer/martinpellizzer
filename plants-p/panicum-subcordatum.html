Panicum subcordatum

Panicum subcordatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential for erosion control.
