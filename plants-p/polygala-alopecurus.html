Polygala alopecurus

Polygala alopecurus, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. The plant features slender stems with small, compound leaves and produces delicate, spikelike inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a unique structure that includes a hooded upper petal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
