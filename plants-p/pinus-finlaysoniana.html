Pinus finlaysoniana

Pinus finlaysoniana, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high elevations of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in harsh, rocky environments. The tree has a distinctive appearance with long, needle-like leaves and a conical shape. It produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, Pinus finlaysoniana is considered a species of conservation concern.
