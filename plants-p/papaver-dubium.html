Papaver dubium

Papaver dubium, commonly known as the meadow poppy, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Papaveraceae and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are palmately lobed and have a distinctive hairy texture.

Papaver dubium is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
