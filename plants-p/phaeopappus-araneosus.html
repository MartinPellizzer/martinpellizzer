Phaeopappus araneosus

Phaeopappus araneosus, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The woolly groundsel is adapted to arid environments and has a low-growing, mat-forming structure.
