Pycnarrhena australiana

Pycnarrhena australiana, commonly known as the Australian mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of eucalyptus trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small white or pale green flowers, which are followed by fleshy, red berries containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions of southern Australia.

Its presence can have both positive and negative effects on its host trees, depending on the level of infestation.
