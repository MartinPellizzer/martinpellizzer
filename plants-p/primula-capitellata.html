Primula capitellata

Primula capitellata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, white, and pink. The plant features basal leaves that are oval-shaped with hairy surfaces, and its stems are short and sturdy. Primula capitellata thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
