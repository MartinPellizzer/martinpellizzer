Poiretia linearis

Poiretia linearis, commonly known as the slender poiretia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grass family members. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
