Pseudanthus orbicularis

Pseudanthus orbicularis, commonly known as the orbicular falseflower, is a unique plant characterized by its clustered inflorescence that mimics a single flower.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is known for its composite flower heads. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in a circular pattern. Pseudanthus orbicularis is native to South America and thrives in dry, rocky environments.

Its botanical structure is adapted for efficient pollination by insects and birds.
