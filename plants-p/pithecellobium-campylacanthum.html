Pithecellobium campylacanthum

Pithecellobium campylacanthum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed acacia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
