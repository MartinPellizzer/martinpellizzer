Paspalum sanguinale var. pabulare

Paspalum sanguinale var. pabulare, commonly known as red paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and elongated, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland habitats and is adapted to tolerate periodic flooding.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
