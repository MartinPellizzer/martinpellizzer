Potentilla cinerea f. clementei

Potentilla cinerea f. clementei, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
