Psoralea morisiana

Psoralea morisiana, commonly known as the Morisiana psoralea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
