Phaius fragilis

Phaius fragilis, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests and marshlands. Its roots are often found in waterlogged soil, and it has a rhizomatous growth habit.

The flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive purple or red lip, making it a striking ornamental plant.
