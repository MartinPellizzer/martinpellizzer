Phyllanthus anderssonii

Phyllanthus anderssonii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and its leaves are usually small, oval, and glossy. It is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf coating.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
