Phyllostegia brevidens var. expansa

Phyllostegia brevidens var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllostegia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are typically small, clustered in whorls, and range in color from pale pink to lavender.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks.
