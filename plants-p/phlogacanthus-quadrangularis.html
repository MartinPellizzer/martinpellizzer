Phlogacanthus quadrangularis

Phlogacanthus quadrangularis, commonly known as the square-stemmed phlogacanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its quadrangular, ridged stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in local medicine.
