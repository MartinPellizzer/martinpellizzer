Pilosella dichotoma

Pilosella dichotoma, commonly known as the forked cat's ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and divided into two parts, giving it the common name "forked cat's ear.".

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
