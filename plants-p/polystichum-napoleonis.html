Polystichum napoleonis

Polystichum napoleonis, commonly known as Napoleon's wood fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The fronds are divided into narrow, serrated segments and are typically dark green with a leathery texture. This fern thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
