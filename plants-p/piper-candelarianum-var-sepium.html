Piper candelarianum var. sepium

Piper candelarianum var. sepium, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that develop into elongated, bell-shaped fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its edible fruits, which have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The varietal form 'sepium' is distinguished by its unique fruit morphology and is often used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
