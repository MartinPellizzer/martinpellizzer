Potamogeton heterocaulis

Potamogeton heterocaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems as it provides habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
