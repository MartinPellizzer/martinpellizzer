Phacelia whitlavia

Phacelia whitlavia, commonly known as the white phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often glandular.

Phacelia whitlavia is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant gardens.
