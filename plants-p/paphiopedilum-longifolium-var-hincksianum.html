Paphiopedilum longifolium var. hincksianum

Paphiopedilum longifolium var. hincksianum, commonly known as the Hinck's ghost orchid, is a rare and exquisite orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive lip. This terrestrial orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil, often growing in forest undergrowth. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a delicate beauty to its natural habitat.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is highly valued in both conservation and horticultural circles.
