Platycerium bifurcatum var. subrhomboideum

Platycerium bifurcatum var. subrhomboideum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a unique epiphytic fern native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-like fronds that are often adorned with a velvety, dark green texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are divided into lobes, giving it a fern-like appearance with a striking, almost sculptural form.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
