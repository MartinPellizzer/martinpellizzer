Pyrus pyraster f. ovalis

Pyrus pyraster f. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-shaped crabapple, is a compact, ornamental variant of the common crabapple.

It features rounded, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, tart fruits that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This form is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
