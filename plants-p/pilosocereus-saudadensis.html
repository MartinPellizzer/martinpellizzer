Pilosocereus saudadensis

Pilosocereus saudadensis, commonly known as the Sahuarita cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Pilosocereus saudadensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
