Primula officinalis f. attenuata

Primula officinalis f. attenuata, commonly known as the attenuated primrose, is a botanical variant of the common primrose, characterized by its elongated and slender foliage.

This plant belongs to the Primulaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. The flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a distinct five-lobed corolla.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
