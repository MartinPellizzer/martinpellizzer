Piper gorgonillense

Piper gorgonillense, commonly known as the Gorgonilla pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Piper gorgonillense is valued for its unique flavor and is used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
