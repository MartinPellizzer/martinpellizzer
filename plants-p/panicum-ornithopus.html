Panicum ornithopus

Panicum ornithopus, commonly known as the bird's-foot trefoil, is a flowering plant in the grass family (Poaceae).

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant is characterized by its distinctive seed heads, which resemble a bird's foot, consisting of several small, fluffy seeds attached to a central stem. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Panicum ornithopus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
