Pavonia guerkeana var. major

Pavonia guerkeana var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered pavonia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, clustered fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
