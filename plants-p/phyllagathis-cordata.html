Phyllagathis cordata

Phyllagathis cordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by reddish-brown berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
