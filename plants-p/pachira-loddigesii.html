Pachira loddigesii

Pachira loddigesii, commonly known as the Brazilian money plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Its ability to tolerate low light makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
