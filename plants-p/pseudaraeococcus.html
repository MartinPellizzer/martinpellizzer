Pseudaraeococcus

Pseudaraeococcus, commonly known as the wax scale, is a genus of scale insects belonging to the family Coccidae.

These tiny, sap-sucking insects are often found on the stems and leaves of various plants, particularly in tropical and subtropical regions. They are characterized by their waxy, shield-like bodies that protect them from predators and environmental stressors. The presence of Pseudaraeococcus can lead to the weakening of host plants due to the depletion of sap and the secretion of honeydew, which promotes the growth of sooty mold.

Due to their small size and cryptic appearance, they are often difficult to detect without close inspection.
