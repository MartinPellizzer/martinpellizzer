Panicum vinaceum

Panicum vinaceum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and bright red to bronze-colored inflorescences that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. Its foliage is typically bluish-green, with narrow, grass-like leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Panicum vinaceum is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient addition to native plant gardens.
