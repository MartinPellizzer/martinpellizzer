Pseudostellaria okamotoi

Pseudostellaria okamotoi, commonly known as the Japanese starwort, is a small flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers are usually pink or white.

Pseudostellaria okamotoi is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
