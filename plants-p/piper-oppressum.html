Piper oppressum

Piper oppressum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn red when mature, containing seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
