Passerina paludosa

Passerina paludosa, commonly known as the marsh wren, is a small, insectivorous bird found in wetland habitats across North America.

It is characterized by its slender body, long tail, and distinctive black-and-white plumage. The male displays a striking black cap and white eyebrow, while the female has a more subdued coloration. It nests in dense vegetation near water, often in reeds or shrubs, and is known for its complex, varied song.

This species plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by controlling insect populations and serving as prey for larger predators.
