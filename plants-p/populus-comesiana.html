Populus comesiana

Populus comesiana, commonly known as the Mexican poplar, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish, often peeling in thin layers.

Populus comesiana is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones along rivers and streams.
