Phacelia affinis var. patens

Phacelia affinis var. patens, commonly known as the spreading phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

The variety 'patens' is distinguished by its more spreading growth habit compared to other varieties of Phacelia affinis.
