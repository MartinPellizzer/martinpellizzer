Pourthiaea lanata

Pourthiaea lanata, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly white or grayish foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and dryland landscaping.
