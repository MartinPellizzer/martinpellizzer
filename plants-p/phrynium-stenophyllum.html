Phrynium stenophyllum

Phrynium stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phrynium, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family, characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Phrynium stenophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and vibrant floral display.
