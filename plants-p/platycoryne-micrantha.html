Platycoryne micrantha

Platycoryne micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered flat-lobed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
