Paspalidium distans

Paspalidium distans, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalidium distans is characterized by its adaptability to various environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
