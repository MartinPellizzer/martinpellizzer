Poinciana californica

Poinciana californica, commonly known as the California flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fern-like, bipinnate leaves that turn red in the fall. The tree produces showy, bright orange-red flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and seasonal color changes make it a striking addition to landscapes.
