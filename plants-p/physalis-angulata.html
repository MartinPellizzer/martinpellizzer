Physalis angulata

Physalis angulata, commonly known as the Indian lantern or Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial shrub, with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are yellow with five petals, and the fruit develops in a papery calyx that remains attached after maturity.

The plant is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which have a tangy, sweet flavor.
