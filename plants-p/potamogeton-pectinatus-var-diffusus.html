Potamogeton pectinatus var. diffusus

Potamogeton pectinatus var. diffusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often broader than those of other pondweed species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and light penetration.
