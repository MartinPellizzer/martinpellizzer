Piper caucaense

Piper caucaense, commonly known as the Cauca pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
