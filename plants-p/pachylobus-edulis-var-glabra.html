Pachylobus edulis var. glabra

Pachylobus edulis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth edible pachylobus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small, round berries. The fruits are typically consumed raw or cooked and are valued for their mild, sweet flavor.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within the understory of tropical forests.
