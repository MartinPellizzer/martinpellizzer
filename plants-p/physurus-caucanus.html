Physurus caucanus

Physurus caucanus, commonly known as the Caucasian mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, basal leaves that are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Physurus caucanus is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
