Piper pertractatum

Piper pertractatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are typically small berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
