Pyrrocoma solidaginea

Pyrrocoma solidaginea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
