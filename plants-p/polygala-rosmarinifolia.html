Polygala rosmarinifolia

Polygala rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens for both its beauty and fragrance.
