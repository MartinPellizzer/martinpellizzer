Pouteria vitiensis

Pouteria vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Sapote, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Vanuatu.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The tree produces a fleshy, orange-colored fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible. The fruit is often used in local cuisines and can be consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages.

Pouteria vitiensis is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
