Phyllanthus micromeris var. sesbanioides

Phyllanthus micromeris var. sesbanioides, commonly known as the small-leaved phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree with simple, opposite leaves that are usually small and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits containing single seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to grow in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
