Polypodium aureum var. pulvinatum

Polypodium aureum var. pulvinatum, commonly known as the golden polypody, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rock crevices, preferring temperate climates. The fronds are bipinnate, with small, lance-shaped pinnae that are often covered in fine, golden hairs. The plant reproduces via spores, which are produced on the underside of the fronds in sporangia.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
