Prunus × schmittii

Prunus × schmittii, commonly known as the Schmitt cherry, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, single or double pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red to black fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types.
