Potentilla dissecta var. glaucophylla

Potentilla dissecta var. glaucophylla, commonly known as the glaucophylla potentilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is native to regions of western North America, particularly in mountainous areas with well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
