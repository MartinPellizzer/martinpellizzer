Potamogeton lucens var. angustifolius

Potamogeton lucens var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically 2–8 mm wide and 10–30 cm long. The plant produces small, green to brown flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
