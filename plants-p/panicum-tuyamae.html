Panicum tuyamae

Panicum tuyamae, commonly known as the Japanese millet, is a grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Panicum tuyamae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
