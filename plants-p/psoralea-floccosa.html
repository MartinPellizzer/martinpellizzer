Psoralea floccosa

Psoralea floccosa, commonly known as the woolly psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous annual that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by soft, hairy foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
