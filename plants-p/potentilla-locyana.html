Potentilla locyana

Potentilla locyana, commonly known as the locyana cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla locyana is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
