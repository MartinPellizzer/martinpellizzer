Pandanus concinnus

Pandanus concinnus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into three-lobed fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in shallow water or on sandy soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
