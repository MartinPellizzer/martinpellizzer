Packera dimorphophylla var. intermedia

Packera dimorphophylla var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate leaf golden ragwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and distinctive, deeply lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough texture, while its flowers are arranged in composite heads.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
