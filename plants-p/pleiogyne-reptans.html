Pleiogyne reptans

Pleiogyne reptans, commonly known as the creeping pleiogyne, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and covered with a fine, powdery bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance.

Pleiogyne reptans is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
