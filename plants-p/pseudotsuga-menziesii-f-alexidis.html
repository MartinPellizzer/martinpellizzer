Pseudotsuga menziesii f. alexidis

Pseudotsuga menziesii f. alexidis, commonly known as the Alexander spruce, is a subspecies of the Douglas fir native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dense foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically shorter and more pointed compared to the main species, and the cones are smaller and more compact. This subspecies is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
