Paphiopedilum sugiyamanum

Paphiopedilum sugiyamanum, commonly known as the Sugiyama's lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers with intricate patterns and a unique slipper-shaped pouch that serves as a pollination trap. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil, often found in forests or rocky areas. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its rare and exquisite blooms.
