Papaver oreophilum var. monanthum

Papaver oreophilum var. monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered poppy, is a rare and striking member of the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to orange, with a distinctive papery texture. The plant is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This variant is notable for its reduced floral structure, featuring a single pistil and stamen, which distinguishes it from other varieties within the species.
