Pimelea axillaris

Pimelea axillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of bright red flowers that appear throughout spring and summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pimelea axillaris is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
