Pulmonaria ciliata

Pulmonaria ciliata, commonly known as the downy lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features basal rosettes of hairy, velvety leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Pulmonaria ciliata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is known for its ability to naturalize over time.
