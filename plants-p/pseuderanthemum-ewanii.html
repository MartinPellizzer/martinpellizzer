Pseuderanthemum ewanii

Pseuderanthemum ewanii, commonly known as the Ewan's false spiderflower, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale purple in color.

Pseuderanthemum ewanii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant appearance and ease of care.
