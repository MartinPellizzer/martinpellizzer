Penstemon menziesii f. scouleri

Penstemon menziesii f. scouleri, commonly known as the Scouler's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to white. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
