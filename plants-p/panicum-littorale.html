Panicum littorale

Panicum littorale, commonly known as salt grass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in marshes and estuaries. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to high salinity makes it an important component of tidal ecosystems.
