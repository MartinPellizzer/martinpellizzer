Passiflora mediterranea

Passiflora mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking flowers with five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant's fruit is a green, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
