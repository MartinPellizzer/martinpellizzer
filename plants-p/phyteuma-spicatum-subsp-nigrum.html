Phyteuma spicatum subsp. nigrum

Phyteuma spicatum subsp.

nigrum, commonly known as the Blackflower Phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of dark purple to black flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its striking color and architectural form.
