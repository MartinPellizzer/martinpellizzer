Polygonum korthalsii

Polygonum korthalsii, commonly known as the Korthals' knotweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands or along stream banks.

Polygonum korthalsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
