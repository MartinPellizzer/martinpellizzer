Padus mahaleb f. variegata

Padus mahaleb f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated bird cherry.

This cultivar is a decorative variant of the bird cherry tree, characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white and green patterns. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits in autumn.

Its foliage provides year-round visual interest, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
