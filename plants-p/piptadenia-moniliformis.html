Piptadenia moniliformis

Piptadenia moniliformis, commonly known as the yellow trumpet tree, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and the tree can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

The bark is smooth and grayish, and the wood is often used for construction and fuel.
