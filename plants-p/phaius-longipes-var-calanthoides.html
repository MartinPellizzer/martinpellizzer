Phaius longipes var. calanthoides

Phaius longipes var. calanthoides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in dense spikes, often with a subtle red or orange tint. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
