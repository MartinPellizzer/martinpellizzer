Pilinophytum capitatum

Pilinophytum capitatum, commonly known as the clustered pilinophytum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often adorned with showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
