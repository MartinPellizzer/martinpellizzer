Potentilla speciosa var. minor

Potentilla speciosa var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf potentilla, is a compact shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.
