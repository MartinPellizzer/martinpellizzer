Pilea squamosa

Pilea squamosa, commonly known as the silver mitten plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathes, typical of the Araceae family.

Pilea squamosa is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
