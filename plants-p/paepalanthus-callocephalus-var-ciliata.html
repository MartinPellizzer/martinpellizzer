Paepalanthus callocephalus var. ciliata

Paepalanthus callocephalus var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky pincushion plant, is a striking member of the Bromeliaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly inflorescence that resembles a pincushion, with the bracts covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, are borne on a tall, leafless stem, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
