Potentilla bakeri

Potentilla bakeri, commonly known as Baker's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla bakeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
