Polygonum punctatum var. mexicanum

Polygonum punctatum var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, glandular dots. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and serrated edges.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and in open woodlands.
