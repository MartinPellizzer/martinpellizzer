Psychotria candelabrum

Psychotria candelabrum, commonly known as the candlewood or candelabra psychotria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching pattern, which resembles a candelabra, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Psychotria candelabrum is also notable for its presence in traditional medicine and its role in ecological systems.
