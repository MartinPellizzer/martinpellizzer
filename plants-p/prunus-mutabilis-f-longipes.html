Prunus mutabilis f. longipes

Prunus mutabilis f. longipes, commonly known as the long-stalked cherry, is a unique variant of the Prunus mutabilis species.

It is characterized by its elongated pedicels, which distinguish it from the typical form of the plant. The flowers are white and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring. The leaves are ovate and have a glossy green surface, providing a striking contrast to the pale blossoms.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
