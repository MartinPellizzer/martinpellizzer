Physocarpus tomentosus

Physocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has grayish-white, fuzzy bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Physocarpus tomentosus is relatively low maintenance and resistant to pests and diseases.
