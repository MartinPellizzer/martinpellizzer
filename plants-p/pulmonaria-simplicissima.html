Pulmonaria simplicissima

Pulmonaria simplicissima, commonly known as the simple lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Geraniaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of velvety, spotted leaves. The plant produces upright stems bearing clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in moist, woodland soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Pulmonaria simplicissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
