Poa bracteosa

Poa bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often concealed by leaf-like bracts.

Poa bracteosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
