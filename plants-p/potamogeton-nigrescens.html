Potamogeton nigrescens

Potamogeton nigrescens, commonly known as black pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through runners. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Black pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
