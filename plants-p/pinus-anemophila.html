Pinus anemophila

Pinus anemophila, commonly known as the creeping foxtail pine, is a species of pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is well adapted to rocky and arid environments. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind, a key adaptation for its habitat. Its needles are typically blue-green in color and remain on the plant for several years.

This species is often found in high-altitude regions and is notable for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions.
