Paepalanthus nanus

Paepalanthus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf puya, is a small, flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its compact stature and dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, brightly colored inflorescences that bloom in spring, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is cultivated in many regions for its aesthetic appeal.
