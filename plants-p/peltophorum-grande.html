Peltophorum grande

Peltophorum grande, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that appear in clusters, resembling flames. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its fruits are legumes that split open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

Peltophorum grande is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
