Potentilla gracilis var. hallii

Potentilla gracilis var. hallii, commonly known as the slender cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with five oval to lance-shaped leaflets, and the stems are often hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
