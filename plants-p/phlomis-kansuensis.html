Phlomis kansuensis

Phlomis kansuensis, commonly known as Chinese torchroot, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its upright growth, hairy stems, and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis kansuensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
