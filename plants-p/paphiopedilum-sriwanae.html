Paphiopedilum sriwanae

Paphiopedilum sriwanae, commonly known as the Sri Lanka orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Sri Lanka.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a yellowish throat, and it blooms during the rainy season.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
