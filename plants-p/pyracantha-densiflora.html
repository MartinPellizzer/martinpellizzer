Pyracantha densiflora

Pyracantha densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features bright green, lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Pyracantha densiflora is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its resistance to pests and diseases.
