Pycreus sanguinolentus var. cyrtostachys

Pycreus sanguinolentus var. cyrtostachys, commonly known as the Crimson Vein Orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with prominent red veins, creating a dramatic visual contrast. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers with a distinctive red throat, blooming during the warm season. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect light to flourish.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical collections.
