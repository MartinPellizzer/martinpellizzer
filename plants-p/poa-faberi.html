Poa faberi

Poa faberi, commonly known as the Chinese bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Poa faberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
