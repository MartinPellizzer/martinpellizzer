Pionandra coriacea

Pionandra coriacea, commonly known as the rough pionandra, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Pionandra coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
