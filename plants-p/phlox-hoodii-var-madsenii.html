Phlox hoodii var. madsenii

Phlox hoodii var. madsenii, commonly known as Madsen's phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
