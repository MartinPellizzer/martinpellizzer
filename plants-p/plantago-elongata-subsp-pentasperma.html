Plantago elongata subsp. pentasperma

Plantago elongata subsp.

pentasperma, commonly known as the five-seeded plantain, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. Its seeds are enclosed in small, three-lobed capsules, and the species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
