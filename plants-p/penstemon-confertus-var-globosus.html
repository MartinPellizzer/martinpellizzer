Penstemon confertus var. globosus

Penstemon confertus var. globosus, commonly known as the globeflower penstemon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, globular inflorescence composed of numerous tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
