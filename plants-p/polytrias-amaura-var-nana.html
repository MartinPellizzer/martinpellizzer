Polytrias amaura var. nana

Polytrias amaura var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf polytrias, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, clustered leaves and produces delicate, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, low-maintenance plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
