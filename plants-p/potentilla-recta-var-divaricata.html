Potentilla recta var. divaricata

Potentilla recta var. divaricata, commonly known as the variable potentilla, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, often twining stems that grow along the ground or climb. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates.
