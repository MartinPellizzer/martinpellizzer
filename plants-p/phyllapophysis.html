Phyllapophysis

Phyllapophysis, commonly known as the leaf-like structure in certain fungal basidia, is a specialized organ involved in spore production.

It is typically found in the Basidiomycota division and is characterized by its thin, membranous surface. The phyllapophysis often develops from the base of the basidium and can vary in shape and size depending on the species. It plays a crucial role in the reproductive cycle by supporting the growth of spore-bearing structures.

This feature is important for the dispersal and survival of fungal species in various environments.
