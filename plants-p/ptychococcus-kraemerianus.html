Ptychococcus kraemerianus

Ptychococcus kraemerianus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny and well-drained environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in dry landscapes.

Ptychococcus kraemerianus is also valued for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
