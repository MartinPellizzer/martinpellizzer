Pilophora saccifera

Pilophora saccifera, commonly known as the bladderleaf fern, is a unique member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, sac-like structures on its fronds, which are believed to aid in water retention. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with small, delicate pinnae that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

The spores are produced in sori located on the underside of the fronds, typical of many fern species.
