Panax jackianus

Panax jackianus, commonly known as Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to China, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer.

The roots are harvested for their medicinal properties, which have been traditionally used to enhance vitality and support immune function.
