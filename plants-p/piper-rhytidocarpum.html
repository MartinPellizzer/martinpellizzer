Piper rhytidocarpum

Piper rhytidocarpum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper or Indian pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often ridged leaves, which give it its common name. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a dark purple color.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
