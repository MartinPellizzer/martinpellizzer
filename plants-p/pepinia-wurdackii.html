Pepinia wurdackii

Pepinia wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Pepinia wurdackii is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
