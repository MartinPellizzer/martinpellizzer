Pitcairnia augusti

Pitcairnia augusti, commonly known as the velvet bromeliad, is a striking epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a dense, water-holding cup at the center. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and often display intricate patterns of red, purple, or orange. It produces showy inflorescences with bright red or orange flowers that bloom for several weeks.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture.
