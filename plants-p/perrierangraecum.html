Perrierangraecum

Perrierangraecum, commonly known as the French bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its flowers are typically white or pale purple, with a distinctive spurred shape.

Perrierangraecum is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated in various regions for both food and agricultural purposes.
