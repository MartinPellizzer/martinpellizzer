Primula kamuiana

Primula kamuiana, commonly known as the Kamuian primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Primula kamuiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early spring blooms.
