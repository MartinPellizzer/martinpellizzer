Polygonum oenochroon

Polygonum oenochroon, commonly known as the purple knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive swollen nodes along the stem. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, especially on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Polygonum oenochroon is often found in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks.
