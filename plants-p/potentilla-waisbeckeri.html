Potentilla waisbeckeri

Potentilla waisbeckeri, commonly known as the Waisbecker's cinquefoil, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are compound, consisting of five to seven leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".
