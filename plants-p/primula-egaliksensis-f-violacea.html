Primula egaliksensis f. violacea

Primula egaliksensis f. violacea, commonly known as the purple primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of Eurasia.

It typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in tundra or alpine environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate or ovate, with hairy surfaces adapted to cold climates. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with five petals and a prominent white or pink throat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in polar ecosystems.
