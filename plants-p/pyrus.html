Pyrus

Pyrus, commonly known as the pear tree, is a genus of deciduous trees and shrubs in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including Europe, Asia, and North America. The genus includes over 60 species, many of which are cultivated for their edible fruits. Pyrus species typically have alternate, simple leaves and produce flowers that are usually white or pink in color.

The fruits, known as pears, are characterized by their juicy, sweet flesh and varying shapes and sizes.
