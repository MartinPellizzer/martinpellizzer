Pleurothallis ipyrangana

Pleurothallis ipyrangana, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink with distinctive red veins, making it visually striking.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique beauty.
