Phaseolus ellipticus var. nanus

Phaseolus ellipticus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf runner bean, is a compact variety of the runner bean species.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and edible, pod-like fruits that mature to a yellowish-green color. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm, making it suitable for container gardening or small spaces. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering during the growing season.

This variety is valued for its early maturity and mild, sweet flavor in its young pods.
