Podanthum amplexicaule

Podanthum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clinging moss rose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems that allow it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, often blooming throughout the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and sometimes clasping, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Podanthum amplexicaule is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
