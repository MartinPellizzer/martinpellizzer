Penstemon triflorus var. triflorus

Penstemon triflorus var. triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant produces showy, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
