Penstemon albomarginatus

Penstemon albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and showy inflorescences. The plant features tubular, pink to purple flowers with white margins, which attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Penstemon albomarginatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid landscapes.
