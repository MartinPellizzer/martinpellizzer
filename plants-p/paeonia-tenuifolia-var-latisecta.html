Paeonia tenuifolia var. latisecta

Paeonia tenuifolia var. latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leaf peony, is a deciduous perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, with a distinctive double or semi-double bloom structure. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are broad and finely divided, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its fragrant flowers and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
