Purgosea pertusula

Purgosea pertusula, commonly known as the curlleaf mountain-mahogany, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, small leaves, and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically curled or twisted, giving it its common name.

Purgosea pertusula is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
