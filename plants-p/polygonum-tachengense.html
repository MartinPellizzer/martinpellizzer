Polygonum tachengense

Polygonum tachengense, commonly known as Tacheng knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as riverbanks and wet meadows. Its stems are often reddish or purple, and it produces small, three-lobed leaves.

Polygonum tachengense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
