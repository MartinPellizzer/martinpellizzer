Pseudabutilon virgatum

Pseudabutilon virgatum, commonly known as the silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its fibrous roots and branching stems make it adaptable to various soil types, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens.

Pseudabutilon virgatum is also noted for its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade conditions.
