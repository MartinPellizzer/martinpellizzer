Phaius bensoniae

Phaius bensoniae, commonly known as the Bensonia orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and is characterized by its striking, large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing inflorescences with numerous fragrant, yellowish-green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique floral structure and adaptability to various growing conditions.
