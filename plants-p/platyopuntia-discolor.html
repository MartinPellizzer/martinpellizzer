Platyopuntia discolor

Platyopuntia discolor, commonly known as the white prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in sharp, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits known as tunas. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh, dry climates.
