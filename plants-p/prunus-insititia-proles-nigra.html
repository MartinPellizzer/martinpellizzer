Prunus insititia proles nigra

Prunus insititia proles nigra, commonly known as black chokeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and anthocyanins. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and turn vibrant red in the fall.

The berries are often used in jams, wines, and as a natural food supplement due to their nutritional value.
