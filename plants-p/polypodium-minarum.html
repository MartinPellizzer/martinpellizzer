Polypodium minarum

Polypodium minarum, commonly known as the mountain holly fern, is a species of fern found in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its evergreen fronds that remain throughout the year. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces or in shaded, moist environments, often in mountainous areas. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, intricate pattern of veins.

Polypodium minarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
