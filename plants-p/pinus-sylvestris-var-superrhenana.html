Pinus sylvestris var. superrhenana

Pinus sylvestris var. superrhenana, commonly known as the Hungarian stone pine, is a variant of the Scots pine that is native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and long, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional crafts.
