Pleioblastus amarus var. subglabratus

Pleioblastus amarus var. subglabratus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus genus native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered in a thin layer of white or silver hairs, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a bluish-green hue with a slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to form dense clumps, providing both aesthetic appeal and erosion control in garden settings.
