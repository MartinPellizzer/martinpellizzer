Pilea pachycephala

Pilea pachycephala, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.

Pilea pachycephala thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both novice and experienced gardeners.
