Photinia eugeniifolia

Photinia eugeniifolia, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its striking red or orange-red foliage, which becomes more vivid in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
