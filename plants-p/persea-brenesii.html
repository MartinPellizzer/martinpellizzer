Persea brenesii

Persea brenesii, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces large, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is widely cultivated in horticulture and reforestation projects.
