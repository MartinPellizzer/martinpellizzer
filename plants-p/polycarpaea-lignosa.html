Polycarpaea lignosa

Polycarpaea lignosa, commonly known as the hardy milkweed, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stem, which gives it its species name "lignosa," meaning wood-like. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

Polycarpaea lignosa is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.
