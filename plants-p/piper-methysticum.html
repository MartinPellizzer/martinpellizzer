Piper methysticum

Piper methysticum, commonly known as kava, is a flowering plant native to the South Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. The roots of kava are the primary part used for its medicinal and ritualistic properties.

It contains bioactive compounds such as kavalactones, which contribute to its calming effects.
