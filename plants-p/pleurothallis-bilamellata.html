Pleurothallis bilamellata

Pleurothallis bilamellata, commonly known as the two-lipped pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, adapting to its epiphytic lifestyle.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
