Polygonum polymorphum var. ajanense

Polygonum polymorphum var. ajanense, commonly known as the Ajanese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in ecological restoration projects.
