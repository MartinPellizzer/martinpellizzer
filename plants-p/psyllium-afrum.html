Psyllium afrum

Psyllium afrum, commonly known as African psyllium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds, which are rich in mucilage, are often used in traditional medicine and as a dietary supplement.

The mucilage has properties that can aid in digestion and promote gut health.
