Peperomia swartziana

Peperomia swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's peperomia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in its native habitat. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically borne in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Peperomia swartziana is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive foliage.
