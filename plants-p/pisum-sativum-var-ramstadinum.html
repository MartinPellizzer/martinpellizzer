Pisum sativum var. ramstadinum

Pisum sativum var. ramstadinum, commonly known as the Ramstad pea, is a variety of the common pea plant.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a dwarf or bush type. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain round, yellow or green seeds. It is well-suited for container gardening and has a short growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various cuisines.
