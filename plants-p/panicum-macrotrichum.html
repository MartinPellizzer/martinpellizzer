Panicum macrotrichum

Panicum macrotrichum, commonly known as silky grass or silky millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, silky, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Panicum macrotrichum is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to grow in poor soil conditions.
