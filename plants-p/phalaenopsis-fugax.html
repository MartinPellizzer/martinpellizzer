Phalaenopsis fugax

Phalaenopsis fugax, commonly known as the fleeting moth orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, slender stems with clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom sequentially along the stem. The leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and arranged in two rows, providing structural support for the plant. It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ephemeral blooming period, it is often referred to as the "fleeting moth orchid.".
