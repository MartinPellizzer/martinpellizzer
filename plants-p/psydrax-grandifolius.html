Psydrax grandifolius

Psydrax grandifolius, commonly known as the large-leaved psidium, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
