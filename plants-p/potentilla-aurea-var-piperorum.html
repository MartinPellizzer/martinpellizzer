Potentilla aurea var. piperorum

Potentilla aurea var. piperorum, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has palmately divided leaves with five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
