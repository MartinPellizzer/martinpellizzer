Pterospermum littorale var. venustum

Pterospermum littorale var. venustum, commonly known as the coastal pterospermum, is a variant of the coastal pterospermum found in coastal regions of southern China and the Korean Peninsula.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in panicles, typically blooming in spring.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.
