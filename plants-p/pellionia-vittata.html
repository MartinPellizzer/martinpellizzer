Pellionia vittata

Pellionia vittata, commonly known as the silver pellionia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate partial shade.
