Pandanus beccatus

Pandanus beccatus, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that often have a reddish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruits are edible and have a distinctive flavor.

Pandanus beccatus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.
