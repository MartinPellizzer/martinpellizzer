Polystachya caudata

Polystachya caudata, commonly known as the long-bracted sword fern, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the family Polystachyaceae.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a wiry, creeping rhizome with dense, lance-shaped fronds that are often adorned with long, slender bracts. Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in both natural and cultivated settings.

Polystachya caudata is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in hanging baskets or as a decorative element in shaded gardens.
