Potamogeton oppositifolius

Potamogeton oppositifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features long, ribbon-like leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in late summer. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, impacting water flow and quality in lakes and ponds.
