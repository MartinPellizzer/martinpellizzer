Paris gigas

Paris gigas, commonly known as the giant Paris, is a rare and endangered fern native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing spores in specialized structures called sorus. It thrives in cool, moist environments with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to habitat destruction, it is critically endangered and requires conservation efforts to survive.
