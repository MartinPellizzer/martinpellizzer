Prostanthera baxteri

Prostanthera baxteri, commonly known as Baxter's mintbush, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are aromatic and often have a mint-like scent, which is a common feature among members of the mint family.

Prostanthera baxteri is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.
