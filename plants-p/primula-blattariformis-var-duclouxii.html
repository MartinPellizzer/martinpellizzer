Primula blattariformis var. duclouxii

Primula blattariformis var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's primrose, is a rare and specialized plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are broadly ovate with a waxy coating, adapted to reduce water loss in its cold, arid environment. It thrives in shaded, moist habitats and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
