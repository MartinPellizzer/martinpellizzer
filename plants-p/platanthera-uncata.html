Platanthera uncata

Platanthera uncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant lacks true leaves in the traditional sense, instead having reduced foliage that aids in photosynthesis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Platanthera uncata is often found in association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which play a crucial role in its nutrient uptake.
