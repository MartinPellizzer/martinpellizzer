Pulicaria vulgaris subsp. dentata

Pulicaria vulgaris subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed groundsel, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves with distinct tooth-like margins, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower heads that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species often found in Mediterranean climates.
