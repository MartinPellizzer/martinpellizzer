Polystachya saccata

Polystachya saccata, commonly known as the sac-like polystachya, is a tropical orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, sac-like structures on its stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Polystachya saccata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
