Peperomia cacaophila

Peperomia cacaophila, commonly known as the Purple Heart Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically green or reddish, which are followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia cacaophila is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal water and occasional fertilization during the growing season.
