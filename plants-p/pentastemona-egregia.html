Pentastemona egregia

Pentastemona egregia, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
