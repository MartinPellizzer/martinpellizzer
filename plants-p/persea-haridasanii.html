Persea haridasanii

Persea haridasanii, commonly known as the Indian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color, with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
