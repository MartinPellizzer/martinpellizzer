Phaca caryocarpa

Phaca caryocarpa, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small, deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain hard, chestnut-like seeds. It is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
