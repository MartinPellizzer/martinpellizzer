Pulicaria dysenterica var. ramosissima

Pulicaria dysenterica var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy tansy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a mat-forming habit with densely hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its hairy foliage and medicinal properties have made it historically significant in traditional herbal practices.
