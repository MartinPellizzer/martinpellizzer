Pelargonium migiurtinorum

Pelargonium migiurtinorum, commonly known as the Ethiopian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting adaptations such as thick leaves to store water. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often found in rocky or limestone soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
