Pseudotsuga sinensis var. sinensis

Pseudotsuga sinensis var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and needle-like leaves that are short and glossy. The tree produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its ecological importance.
