Polypogon rioplatensis f. elegans

Polypogon rioplatensis f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant river plains grass, is a variant of the river plains grass native to South America.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, linear leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, where it forms dense vegetation. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers, which are often pale green or reddish in color.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
