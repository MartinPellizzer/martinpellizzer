Phlomoides cephalariifolia

Phlomoides cephalariifolia, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and ornamental uses. The plant is characterized by its erect stems, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in xeriscaping.
