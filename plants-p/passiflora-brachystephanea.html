Passiflora brachystephanea

Passiflora brachystephanea, commonly known as the short-stemmed passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Passiflora brachystephanea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
