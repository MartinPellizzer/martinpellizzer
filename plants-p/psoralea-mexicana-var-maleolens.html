Psoralea mexicana var. maleolens

Psoralea mexicana var. maleolens, commonly known as the Mexican Psoralea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legumes as its fruit.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
