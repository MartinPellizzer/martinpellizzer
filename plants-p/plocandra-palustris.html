Plocandra palustris

Plocandra palustris, commonly known as the marsh plocandra, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in eastern North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
