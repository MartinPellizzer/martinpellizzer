Polygonum dichotomum var. assamicum

Polygonum dichotomum var. assamicum, commonly known as the Assam knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features slender, creeping stems that produce small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to wetland conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
