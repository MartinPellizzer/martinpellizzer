Pomax umbellata var. glabra

Pomax umbellata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth umbellate pomax, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is notable for its lack of pubescence, which distinguishes it from other varieties within the species.
