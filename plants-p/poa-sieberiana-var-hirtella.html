Poa sieberiana var. hirtella

Poa sieberiana var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, which help it thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
