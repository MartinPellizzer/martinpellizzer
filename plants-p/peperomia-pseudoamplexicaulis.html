Peperomia pseudoamplexicaulis

Peperomia pseudoamplexicaulis, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are oppositely arranged and have a unique, slightly heart-shaped appearance.

Peperomia pseudoamplexicaulis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
