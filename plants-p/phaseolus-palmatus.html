Phaseolus palmatus

Phaseolus palmatus, commonly known as the evergreen bean or palmate bean, is a tropical climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
