Pandanus spurius var. weteringii

Pandanus spurius var. weteringii, commonly known as the Wetering's pandanus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material due to their durability.

The species is adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
