Pavonia mutisii var. intermedia

Pavonia mutisii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Mexican coral bean, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring slender, often branched stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
