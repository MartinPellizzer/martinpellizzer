Passerina dioica

Passerina dioica, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss, making it well-adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Passerina dioica is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
