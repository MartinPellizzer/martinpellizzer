Phacelia divaricata subsp. wrangeliana

Phacelia divaricata subsp.

wrangeliana, commonly known as Wrangel's phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America. It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

This species is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
