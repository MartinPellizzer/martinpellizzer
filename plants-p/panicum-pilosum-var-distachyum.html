Panicum pilosum var. distachyum

Panicum pilosum var. distachyum, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce two-branched spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and exhibits a clumping growth habit.

Silver bluestem is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
