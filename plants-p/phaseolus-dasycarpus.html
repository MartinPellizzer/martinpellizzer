Phaseolus dasycarpus

Phaseolus dasycarpus, commonly known as the hairy pod bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy, elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces small, yellow flowers. Its seeds are often used in traditional cuisines and have nutritional value.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment.
