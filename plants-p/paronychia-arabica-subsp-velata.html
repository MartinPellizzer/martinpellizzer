Paronychia arabica subsp. velata

Paronychia arabica subsp.

velata, commonly known as the velvet flax or woolly flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
