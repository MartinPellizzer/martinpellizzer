Pongamia religiosa

Pongamia religiosa, commonly known as the sacred pongam tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain oily seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel. Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.

The plant also holds cultural significance in various regions where it is revered for its spiritual and medicinal properties.
