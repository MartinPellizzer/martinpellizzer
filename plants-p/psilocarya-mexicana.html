Psilocarya mexicana

Psilocarya mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican liberty cap, is a species of psychoactive mushroom native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Psilocybe and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped cap that turns conical with age. The fruiting bodies typically grow in grassy areas and are often found in association with certain tree species. The mushroom contains psychoactive compounds such as psilocybin and psilocin, which are responsible for its hallucinogenic effects.

Due to its cultural and medicinal significance, it has been studied for potential therapeutic applications.
