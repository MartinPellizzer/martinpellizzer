Polygala spruceana

Polygala spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that resembles a spurred shape, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
