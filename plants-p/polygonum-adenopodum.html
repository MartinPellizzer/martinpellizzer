Polygonum adenopodum

Polygonum adenopodum, commonly known as the woolly knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, hairy stems that give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, soft hairs.

Polygonum adenopodum is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both moist and dry conditions.
