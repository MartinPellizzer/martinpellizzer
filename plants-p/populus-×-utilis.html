Populus × utilis

Populus × utilis, commonly known as the hybrid aspen, is a deciduous tree native to the northern hemisphere.

It is a natural hybrid between Populus tremula (European aspen) and Populus tremuloides (quaking aspen). The plant is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

Populus × utilis is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas, making it an important species in ecological succession.
