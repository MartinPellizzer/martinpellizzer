Parodia pseudostuemeri

Parodia pseudostuemeri, commonly known as the Ghost Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pale green to bluish-gray stems that resemble ghostly shapes, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in a compact, globular form with a short, thick stem and a crown of spines. It produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a subtle touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
