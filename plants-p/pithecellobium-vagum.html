Pithecellobium vagum

Pithecellobium vagum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces pods that contain edible seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
