Passiflora chrysophylla var. typica

Passiflora chrysophylla var. typica, commonly known as the golden leaf passionflower, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and yellow hues. The leaves are broad and often have a distinct golden or yellowish tinge, which gives the plant its common name. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
