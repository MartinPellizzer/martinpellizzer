Poa attenuata

Poa attenuata, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Poa attenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its fine texture and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
