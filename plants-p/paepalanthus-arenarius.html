Paepalanthus arenarius

Paepalanthus arenarius, commonly known as the sand dune cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a small, spineless cactus that thrives in sandy, coastal environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. Its adaptations include a shallow root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Paepalanthus arenarius plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing food for local wildlife.
