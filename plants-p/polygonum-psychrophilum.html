Polygonum psychrophilum

Polygonum psychrophilum, commonly known as the Antarctic willow, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Antarctic and southern South America.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in extreme cold and has adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind rather than insects. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss and protect against freezing temperatures.

Polygonum psychrophilum plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its harsh environment.
