Plantago major f. brevipedicellata

Plantago major f. brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stalked plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its shorter pedicels.

It is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent midrib and a dense covering of fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This form is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its medicinal properties.
