Plantago akkensis

Plantago akkensis, commonly known as the Akkadian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
