Pilea aquarum subsp. aquarum

Pilea aquarum subsp.

aquarum, commonly known as the water pilea, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it has become a popular houseplant among enthusiasts.
