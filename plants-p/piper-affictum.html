Piper affictum

Piper affictum, commonly known as the African pepper or African black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by three-winged berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
