Phaseolus subtrilobus

Phaseolus subtrilobus, commonly known as the three-lobed bean, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pods containing several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The species is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a significant crop in certain regions.
