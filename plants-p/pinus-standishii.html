Pinus standishii

Pinus standishii, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living over 2,000 years. The tree has a distinctive appearance with a twisted trunk and a dense, conical crown. Its needles are typically blue-green in color and grow in pairs.

This species is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky, well-drained soils.
