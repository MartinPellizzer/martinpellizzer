Petrophytum caespitosum subsp. caespitosum

Petrophytum caespitosum subsp.

caespitosum, commonly known as the desert spoon, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming a dense mat that helps prevent soil erosion.

The species is valued in xeriscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
