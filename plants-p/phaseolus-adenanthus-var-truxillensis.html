Phaseolus adenanthus var. truxillensis

Phaseolus adenanthus var. truxillensis, commonly known as the Truxillensis bean, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated pods containing edible seeds.

It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.
