Pelargonium hybridum

Pelargonium hybridum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors including red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

Pelargonium hybridum thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
