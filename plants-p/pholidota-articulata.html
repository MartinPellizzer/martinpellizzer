Pholidota articulata

Pholidota articulata, commonly known as the artichoke plant, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of large, fleshy, spiny leaves that form a dense cluster. The plant produces a showy, thistle-like flower head that transitions from green to purple as it matures. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, particularly in the preparation of artichokes.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its edible parts.
