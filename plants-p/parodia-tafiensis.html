Parodia tafiensis

Parodia tafiensis, commonly known as the Tafiensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white to pale blue spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
