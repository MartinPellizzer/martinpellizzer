Panterpa dichotoma

Panterpa dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous panterpa, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dichotomous branching pattern, where each stem divides into two equal parts, creating a distinctive and symmetrical structure. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple and opposite, with a rough texture that aids in water retention in arid environments.

Panterpa dichotoma is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to challenging conditions.
