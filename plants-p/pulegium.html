Pulegium

Pulegium, commonly known as blueweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. Pulegium species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported respiratory and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's essential oils, extracted from its leaves and flowers, are also valued in aromatherapy and perfumery.
