Potentilla erecta var. dacica

Potentilla erecta var. dacica, commonly known as the Dacian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian region.

It features upright, woody stems with small, palmately divided leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Rosaceae family.
