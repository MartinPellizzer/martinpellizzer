Phlomis oblongata var. canescens

Phlomis oblongata var. canescens, commonly known as the grey phlomis, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and grayish-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces tall spikes of pale yellow to orange flowers, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its aromatic leaves and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological gardens.
