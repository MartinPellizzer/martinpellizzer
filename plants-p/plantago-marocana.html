Plantago marocana

Plantago marocana, commonly known as Moroccan plantain, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

This species is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
