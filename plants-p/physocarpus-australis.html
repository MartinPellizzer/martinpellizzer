Physocarpus australis

Physocarpus australis, commonly known as the Australian coralberry, is a deciduous shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are attractive to birds and persist through the winter. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.

Physocarpus australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
