Potentilla × kerneri

Potentilla × kerneri, commonly known as the Kern's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems covered in dense, silvery-white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

Potentilla × kerneri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
