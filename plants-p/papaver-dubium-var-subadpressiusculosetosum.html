Papaver dubium var. subadpressiusculosetosum

Papaver dubium var. subadpressiusculosetosum, commonly known as the woolly poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and distinctive floral structure.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe, particularly in rocky or calcareous soils. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow, lobed, and covered in a thick layer of short, stiff hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique texture.
