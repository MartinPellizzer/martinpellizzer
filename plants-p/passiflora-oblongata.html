Passiflora oblongata

Passiflora oblongata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments, often in shades of purple and white. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora oblongata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
