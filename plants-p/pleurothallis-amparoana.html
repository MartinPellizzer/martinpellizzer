Pleurothallis amparoana

Pleurothallis amparoana, commonly known as the Amparoan orchid, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
