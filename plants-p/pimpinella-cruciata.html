Pimpinella cruciata

Pimpinella cruciata, commonly known as the crossleaf cowwheat, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cruciform (cross-shaped) flowers, which are typically white or pale pink. The plant has compound leaves with small, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a pinnate pattern. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Pimpinella cruciata is part of the Apiaceae family, known for its aromatic compounds and often edible members.
