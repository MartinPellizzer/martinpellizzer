Pyrus zhejiangensis

Pyrus zhejiangensis, commonly known as the Zhejiang pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Zhejiang province, and is characterized by its deciduous nature and ornamental value. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and fruit production.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.
