Paramignya dubia

Paramignya dubia, commonly known as the white stinging nettle.

It is a flowering plant in the family Urticaceae, characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The plant contains stinging hairs that can cause skin irritation upon contact.
