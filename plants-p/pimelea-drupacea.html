Pimelea drupacea

Pimelea drupacea, commonly known as the yellow wattle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea drupacea is an important component of the local flora and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
