Phaseolus vulgaris f. major

Phaseolus vulgaris f. major, commonly known as the giant common bean, is a variant of the common bean characterized by its notably larger size compared to standard varieties.

This form exhibits robust growth, often reaching greater heights and producing larger pods than typical Phaseolus vulgaris. The plant is typically found in warm climates and thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight. Its botanical features include compound leaves with three to five leaflets and flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

The giant common bean is valued for its high yield and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
