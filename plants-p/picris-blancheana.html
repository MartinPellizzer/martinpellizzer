Picris blancheana

Picris blancheana, commonly known as white ragwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a white center.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions.
