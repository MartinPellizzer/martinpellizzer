Parthenium dillenianum

Parthenium dillenianum, commonly known as the yellow starthistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and agricultural fields. It is considered an invasive species in many regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
