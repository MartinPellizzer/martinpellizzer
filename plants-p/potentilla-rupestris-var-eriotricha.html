Potentilla rupestris var. eriotricha

Potentilla rupestris var. eriotricha, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
