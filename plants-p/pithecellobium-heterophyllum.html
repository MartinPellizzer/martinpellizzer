Pithecellobium heterophyllum

Pithecellobium heterophyllum, commonly known as the variable pithecellobium, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often yellow or white, followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes in suitable climates.
