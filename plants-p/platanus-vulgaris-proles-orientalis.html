Platanus vulgaris proles orientalis

Platanus vulgaris proles orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental Plane, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, palmate leaves that turn yellow in autumn before falling. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is dispersed by wind. Its bark is thick and ridged, providing a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often planted for its ornamental value and shade.
