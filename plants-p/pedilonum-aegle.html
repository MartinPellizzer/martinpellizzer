Pedilonum aegle

Pedilonum aegle, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be pink, red, or white, and its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The sacred lotus holds cultural and religious significance in many societies, symbolizing purity and enlightenment.
