Potentilla × collina var. silesiaca

Potentilla × collina var. silesiaca, commonly known as the Silesian cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Central Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features upright, slender stems covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in rounded inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval, serrated leaflets that are often hairy beneath.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
