Petastoma leiophyllum

Petastoma leiophyllum, commonly known as the smoothleaf petasite, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, smooth, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
