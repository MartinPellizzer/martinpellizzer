Potentilla wimanniana subsp. leucopolitana

Potentilla wimanniana subsp.

leucopolitana, commonly known as the white-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
