Palmijuncus elegans

Palmijuncus elegans, commonly known as the slender palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feathery fronds.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are long and pinnately compound, contributing to its elegant appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
