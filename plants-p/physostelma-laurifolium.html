Physostelma laurifolium

Physostelma laurifolium, commonly known as the Florida rainflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
