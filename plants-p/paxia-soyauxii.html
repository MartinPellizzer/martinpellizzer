Paxia soyauxii

Paxia soyauxii, commonly known as the Suaeda-like plant, is a flowering plant species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a low-growing, spreading habit and produces small, often wind-pollinated flowers.

Paxia soyauxii is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
