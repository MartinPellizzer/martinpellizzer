Palma sancona

Palma sancona, commonly known as the Sanquinta palm, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed locally. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The Sanquinta palm is also valued for its ability to thrive in coastal environments.
