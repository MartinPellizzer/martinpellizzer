Pinus chihuahuana

Pinus chihuahuana, commonly known as the Chihuahua pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The tree produces cones that contain winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
