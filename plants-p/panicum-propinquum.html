Panicum propinquum

Panicum propinquum, commonly known as the close panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial plant that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-shaped inflorescences that are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
