Polygala major f. italiana

Polygala major f. italiana, commonly known as the Italian milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygalaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, often clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and opposite, while the fruits are follicles that release seeds through a specialized mechanism.

This species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
