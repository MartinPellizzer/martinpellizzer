Plantago incomta

Plantago incomta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and capsule-like fruits.
