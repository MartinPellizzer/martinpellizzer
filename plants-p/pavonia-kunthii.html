Pavonia kunthii

Pavonia kunthii, commonly known as the Mexican bushcricket or scarlet wood rose, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It features showy, rose-like flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive yellow or green center. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Pavonia kunthii is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is often used in native plant gardens.
