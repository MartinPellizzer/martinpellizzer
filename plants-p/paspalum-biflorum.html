Paspalum biflorum

Paspalum biflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear two flowers, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetland areas.

Paspalum biflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
