Phyllanthus inusitatus

Phyllanthus inusitatus, commonly known as the Indian madder, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
