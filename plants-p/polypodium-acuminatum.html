Polypodium acuminatum

Polypodium acuminatum, commonly known as the slender maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its spores are produced in small, round sori located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium acuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
