Pectis holochaeta var. cana

Pectis holochaeta var. cana, commonly known as the whitehead tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The variety cana is distinguished by its pale to white flower color, setting it apart from other varieties of Pectis holochaeta.
