Passiflora maliformis var. pubescens

Passiflora maliformis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that feature five prominent sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, hairy pubescence, giving the plant its varietal name. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains numerous seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
