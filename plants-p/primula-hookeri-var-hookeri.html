Primula hookeri var. hookeri

Primula hookeri var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Himalayas.

It features rosettes of dark green, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a delicate touch to alpine landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
