Peperomia acuminatifolia

Peperomia acuminatifolia, commonly known as the pointed-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elongated and pointed, often with a slight waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green and red, which are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Peperomia acuminatifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
