Potentilla anglica var. humilis

Potentilla anglica var. humilis, commonly known as English cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with five leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
