Pachypodium × hojnyi

Pachypodium × hojnyi, commonly known as the hybrid pachypodium, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant typically has a branching structure with spiny stems and clusters of small, white or yellow flowers. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss.

Pachypodium × hojnyi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
