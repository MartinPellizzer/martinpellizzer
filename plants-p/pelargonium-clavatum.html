Pelargonium clavatum

Pelargonium clavatum, commonly known as the clustered geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The leaves are typically rounded and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
