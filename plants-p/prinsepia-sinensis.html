Prinsepia sinensis

Prinsepia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with serrated edges.

Prinsepia sinensis is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
