Polygala capitata

Polygala capitata, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygalaceae family.
