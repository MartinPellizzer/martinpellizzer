Pleurothallis atropurpurea

Pleurothallis atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually purple or violet with intricate patterns, and the leaves are narrow and grass-like.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
