Pithecellobium auriculatum

Pithecellobium auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
