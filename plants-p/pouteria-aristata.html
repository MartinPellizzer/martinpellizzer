Pouteria aristata

Pouteria aristata, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces elongated, woody fruits that contain a single large, oily seed, which is the source of the commercially valuable Brazil nut. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
