Ptychosperma bleeseri

Ptychosperma bleeseri, commonly known as the Blesser's palm, is a species of cycad native to New Guinea.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
