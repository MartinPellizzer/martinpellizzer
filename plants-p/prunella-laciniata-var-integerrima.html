Prunella laciniata var. integerrima

Prunella laciniata var. integerrima, commonly known as narrow-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The flowers are small, pale blue to violet, and arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
