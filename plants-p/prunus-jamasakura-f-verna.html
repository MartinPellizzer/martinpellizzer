Prunus jamasakura f. verna

Prunus jamasakura f. verna, commonly known as the early-flowering cherry, is a unique variety of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter or early spring, before most other cherry species. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers that add a delicate ornamental effect to the landscape. It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and is relatively hardy compared to other cherry varieties.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture in temperate climates.
