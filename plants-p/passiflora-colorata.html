Passiflora colorata

Passiflora colorata, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet flowers with intricate, feather-like structures and bright orange stamens. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora colorata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
