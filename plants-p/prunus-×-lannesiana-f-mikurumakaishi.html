Prunus × lannesiana f. mikurumakaishi

Prunus × lannesiana f. mikurumakaishi, commonly known as the Mikurumakai Shi cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral morphology, featuring large, double flowers with a prominent central pistil. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. It is typically found in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also noted for its resistance to certain fungal diseases, making it a desirable choice for horticultural cultivation.
