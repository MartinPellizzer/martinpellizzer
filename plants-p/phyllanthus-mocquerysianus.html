Phyllanthus mocquerysianus

Phyllanthus mocquerysianus, commonly known as the Mexican spurge, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy, berry-like fruits.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
