Physolobium macrophyllum

Physolobium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes many nitrogen-fixing species, and it has a symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. Its seeds are encased in hard pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in soil enrichment.
