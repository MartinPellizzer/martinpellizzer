Polystichum anomalum f. travancoricum

Polystichum anomalum f. travancoricum, commonly known as the Travancore holly fern, is a fern species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
