Panicum marginatum var. strictum

Panicum marginatum var. strictum, commonly known as rigid panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its upright, compact growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
