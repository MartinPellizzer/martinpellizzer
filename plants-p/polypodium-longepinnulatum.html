Polypodium longepinnulatum

Polypodium longepinnulatum, commonly known as the long-pinnuled huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems and dense, dark green, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its fronds are often divided into numerous narrow segments, giving it a feathery appearance.

Polypodium longepinnulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
