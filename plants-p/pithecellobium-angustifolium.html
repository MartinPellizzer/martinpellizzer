Pithecellobium angustifolium

Pithecellobium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvetbean, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds that are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and agricultural research.
