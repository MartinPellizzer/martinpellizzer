Palala iteophylla

Palala iteophylla, commonly known as the slender rock fern, is a unique fern species native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its delicate, wiry fronds that grow in a compact, upright form. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found on rocky outcrops or in dense forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, almost metallic sheen, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Palala iteophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
