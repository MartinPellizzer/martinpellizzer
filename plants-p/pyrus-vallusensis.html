Pyrus vallusensis

Pyrus vallusensis, commonly known as the Vallis Valley pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Botanically, it is closely related to other pear species within the genus Pyrus, but its unique genetic makeup makes it a subject of interest for conservation and horticultural research.
