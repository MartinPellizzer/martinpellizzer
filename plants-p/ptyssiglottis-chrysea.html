Ptyssiglottis chrysea

Ptyssiglottis chrysea, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically yellow or orange in color. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
