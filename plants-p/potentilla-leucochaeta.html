Potentilla leucochaeta

Potentilla leucochaeta, commonly known as white-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a compact growth habit with multiple stems that are often covered in white or grayish hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Potentilla leucochaeta is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy choice for alpine gardens.
