Psidium acranthum

Psidium acranthum, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or red guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often red or reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are typically red when ripe. These berries are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, aromatic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Psidium acranthum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
