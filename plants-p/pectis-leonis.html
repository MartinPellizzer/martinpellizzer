Pectis leonis

Pectis leonis, commonly known as the lion's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems with soft, grayish-green foliage and produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences. Its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Pectis leonis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
