Pterospartum scolopendrinum

Pterospartum scolopendrinum, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

The Brazilian flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
