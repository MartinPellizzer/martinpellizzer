Papaver refractum subsp. occidentale

Papaver refractum subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as the western poppy, is a flowering plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its striking red petals and a distinctive, saucer-shaped bloom.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
