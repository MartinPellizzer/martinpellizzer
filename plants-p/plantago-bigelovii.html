Plantago bigelovii

Plantago bigelovii, commonly known as the bigelov's plantain, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in a spike-like inflorescence during the summer months.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
