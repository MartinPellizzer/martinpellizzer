Pleioblastus hindsii f. heterocycla

Pleioblastus hindsii f. heterocycla, commonly known as the Hinds' bamboo, is a compact, clumping bamboo species native to eastern China.

It features slender, arching stems with a distinctive greyish-green coloration, and its leaves are narrow, elongated, and have a slightly curled appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within the foliage. It is well-suited for container gardening or as a decorative plant in shaded or semi-shaded areas due to its slow growth and evergreen nature.

Its unique leaf morphology and elegant form make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
