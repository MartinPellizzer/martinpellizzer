Piper daniel-gonzalezii var. moniquiranum

Piper daniel-gonzalezii var. moniquiranum, commonly known as the Moniquira pepper plant, is a rare and unique variant of the Piper genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and unique botanical traits, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
