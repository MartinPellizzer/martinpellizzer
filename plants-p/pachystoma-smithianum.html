Pachystoma smithianum

Pachystoma smithianum, commonly known as the Smith's thickstem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
