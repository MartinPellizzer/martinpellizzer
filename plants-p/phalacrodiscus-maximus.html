Phalacrodiscus maximus

Phalacrodiscus maximus, commonly known as the giant water fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its large, floating fronds that can reach lengths of up to three meters.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as lakes and rivers, where it can anchor itself to submerged structures. The plant's fronds are composed of a network of fine, thread-like rhizomes that help it spread across the water surface. Its reproductive structures are concealed within the fronds, producing spores that are dispersed by water currents.

This species plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
