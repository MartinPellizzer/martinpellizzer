Pseudolotus makranicus

Pseudolotus makranicus, commonly known as the Makranic lotus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Pakistan and Iran.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky substrates. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Pseudolotus makranicus plays a minor ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for local pollinators.
