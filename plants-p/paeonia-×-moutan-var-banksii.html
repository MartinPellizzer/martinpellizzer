Paeonia × moutan var. banksii

Paeonia × moutan var. banksii, commonly known as the Chinese tree peony, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, single or semi-double flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed, and its stems are covered in a thick, bark-like layer. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.

This variety is a hybrid between Paeonia suffruticosa and Paeonia mascula, contributing to its hardy and adaptable nature.
