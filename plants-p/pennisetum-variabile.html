Pennisetum variabile

Pennisetum variabile, commonly known as the variable lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicle-shaped inflorescences that produce small, fluffy seeds. The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, feathery appearance, which makes it popular in gardens and as a landscape plant.
