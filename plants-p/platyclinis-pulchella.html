Platyclinis pulchella

Platyclinis pulchella, commonly known as the elegant mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the genus Eucalyptus.

It belongs to the family Viscaraceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and white, fleshy berries that contain seeds. The plant lacks true roots and absorbs nutrients and water through haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system. It is native to Australia and is often found in coastal regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
