Polystichum zerophyllum

Polystichum zerophyllum, commonly known as the desert shield fern, is a unique fern species adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen fronds that remain upright and do not droop, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces spore-bearing soruses on the underside of its fronds, typical of ferns. It is native to regions with harsh, dry climates, such as parts of the southwestern United States.

Its ability to thrive in low-water conditions makes it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
