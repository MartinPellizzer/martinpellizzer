Pelargonium graveolens

Pelargonium graveolens, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, lobed leaves. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in clusters. It is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is also used in herbal medicine.

Pelargonium graveolens contains essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties and therapeutic uses.
