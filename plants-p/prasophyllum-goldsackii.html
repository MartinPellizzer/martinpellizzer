Prasophyllum goldsackii

Prasophyllum goldsackii, commonly known as the Goldsack leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a single spike of white or pale green flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Prasophyllum goldsackii is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
