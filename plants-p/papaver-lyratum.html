Papaver lyratum

Papaver lyratum, commonly known as the lyrical poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive blackish center. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

Papaver lyratum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
