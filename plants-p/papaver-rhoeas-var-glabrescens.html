Papaver rhoeas var. glabrescens

Papaver rhoeas var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers with a distinctive, rounded shape and a central stigma. This plant is typically found in disturbed soils and open fields, thriving in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The glabrous poppy is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical significance in herbal medicine.
