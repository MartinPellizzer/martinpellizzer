Pleurothallis rutrifolia

Pleurothallis rutrifolia, commonly known as the black-veined orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with dark veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
