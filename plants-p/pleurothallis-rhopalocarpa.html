Pleurothallis rhopalocarpa

Pleurothallis rhopalocarpa, commonly known as the "Coral Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive coral-like appearance. Its flowers are typically short-lived but are followed by the development of bright red or orange pseudobulbs.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
