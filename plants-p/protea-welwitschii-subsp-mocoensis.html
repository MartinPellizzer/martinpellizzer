Protea welwitschii subsp. mocoensis

Protea welwitschii subsp.

mocoensis, commonly known as the Mocambique protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers, which are clustered around a central woody axis.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
