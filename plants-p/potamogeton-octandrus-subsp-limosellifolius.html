Potamogeton octandrus subsp. limosellifolius

Potamogeton octandrus subsp.

limosellifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America. It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, distinguishing it from other pondweed species.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which are usually found near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

This subspecies plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
