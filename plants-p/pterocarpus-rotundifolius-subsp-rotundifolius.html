Pterocarpus rotundifolius subsp. rotundifolius

Pterocarpus rotundifolius subsp.

rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf pterocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, palmately compound leaves with rounded lobes.

The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-bearing pods. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
