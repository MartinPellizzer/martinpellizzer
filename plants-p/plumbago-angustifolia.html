Plumbago angustifolia

Plumbago angustifolia, commonly known as blue plumbago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, ascending stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Plumbago angustifolia is often used as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
