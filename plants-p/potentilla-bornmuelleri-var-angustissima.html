Potentilla bornmuelleri var. angustissima

Potentilla bornmuelleri var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, grayish hairs. The leaves are pinnately compound, with five narrow leaflets that are usually lance-shaped and have serrated edges. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense inflorescences, blooming in summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
