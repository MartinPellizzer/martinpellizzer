Prunus mahaleb var. typica

Prunus mahaleb var. typica, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often disliked for their taste. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

The bark is smooth and gray, with a slight fragrance when crushed.
