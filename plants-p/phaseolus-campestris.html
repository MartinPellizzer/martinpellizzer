Phaseolus campestris

Phaseolus campestris, commonly known as the common bean or Phaseolus vulgaris, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Americas and is widely cultivated for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines around the world. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–100 cm and produces pods containing 4–6 seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it flowers with small, white or pinkish blooms.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
