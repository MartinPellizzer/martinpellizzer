Prunus platyphylla

Prunus platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically flat and smooth on both sides. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Prunus platyphylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to various landscapes.
