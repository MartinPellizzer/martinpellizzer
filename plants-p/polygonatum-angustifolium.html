Polygonatum angustifolium

Polygonatum angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that appear in late spring. The plant produces berries that mature to a dark red color, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and thickets.

Polygonatum angustifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
