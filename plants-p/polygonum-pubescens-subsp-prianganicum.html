Polygonum pubescens subsp. prianganicum

Polygonum pubescens subsp.

prianganicum, commonly known as the Priangan knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface and a pointed tip. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers during the summer months.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors and stream banks.
