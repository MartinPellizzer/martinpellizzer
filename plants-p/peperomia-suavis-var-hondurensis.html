Peperomia suavis var. hondurensis

Peperomia suavis var. hondurensis, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a compact, low-growing herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and aromatic qualities make it a popular choice for collectors and plant enthusiasts.
