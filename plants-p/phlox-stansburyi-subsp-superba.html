Phlox stansburyi subsp. superba

Phlox stansburyi subsp.

superba, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
