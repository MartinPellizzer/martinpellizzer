Pandanus eyesyes

Pandanus eyesyes, commonly known as the pandanus tree or screw pine, is a tropical plant belonging to the Pandanus genus in the palms family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, umbrella-like canopy. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by fleshy, fibrous fruits. Its roots are often used in traditional crafts, and its leaves are utilized in thatching and weaving.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland areas, demonstrating a high tolerance for salt and waterlogged soils.
