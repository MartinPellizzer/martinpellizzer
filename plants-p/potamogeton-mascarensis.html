Potamogeton mascarensis

Potamogeton mascarensis, commonly known as the Mascarene pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the Mascarene Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents. The plant thrives in freshwater and brackish environments, often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and coastal lagoons. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
