Paronychia argentea var. libyca

Paronychia argentea var. libyca, commonly known as silverleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers clustered in dense inflorescences during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive species for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
