Potentilla conferta var. trijuga

Potentilla conferta var. trijuga, commonly known as the Sierra sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically features dense clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with wiry, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, arid environments.
