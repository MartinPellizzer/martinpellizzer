Pavia × rubicunda

Pavia × rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered pavia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture for their aesthetic appeal.

Pavia × rubicunda thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaped areas.
