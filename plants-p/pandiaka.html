Pandiaka

Pandiaka, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Nelumbonaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in still water, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are broad and floating, while its roots are anchored in the mud at the bottom of the water body.

The pandiaka has cultural and religious significance in many Asian countries.
