Poa glauca subsp. reverdattoi

Poa glauca subsp.

reverdattoi, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
