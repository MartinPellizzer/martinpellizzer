Prunus mutabilis f. prima

Prunus mutabilis f. prima, commonly known as the White-flowered Cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to China.

This plant is characterized by its striking white flowers that appear in early spring, often preceding the leaves. The foliage is typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the white blossoms. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.
