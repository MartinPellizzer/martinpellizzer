Pelargonium githagineum

Pelargonium githagineum, commonly known as the Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or purple flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. It is also notable for its medicinal properties, particularly in the use of its alkaloids for treating various ailments.

The Madagascar periwinkle has become an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
