Panax mollis

Panax mollis, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy roots that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has palmately compound leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels, and it produces berries that are red when mature.

Panax mollis is valued for its purported health benefits and is cultivated for both medicinal and ornamental purposes.
