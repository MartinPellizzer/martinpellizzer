Pastinaca umbrosa

Pastinaca umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apiaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a robust, tuberous root system and is characterized by its dark green, deeply lobed leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its edible roots, though it is less commonly consumed than other members of its family.
