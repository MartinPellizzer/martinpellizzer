Potentilla pensylvanica var. paucijuga

Potentilla pensylvanica var. paucijuga, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

The variety paucijuga is distinguished by its fewer leaflets compared to the typical form of Potentilla pensylvanica.
