Polygonum fugax

Polygonum fugax, commonly known as the fugitive knotweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a reddish tinge when mature.

Polygonum fugax is an annual species that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
