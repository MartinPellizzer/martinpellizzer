Parthenocissus veitchii

Parthenocissus veitchii, commonly known as the Boston ivy, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for birds. Its vigorous growth habit makes it popular for covering walls and trellises.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.
