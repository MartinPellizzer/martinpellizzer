Poinsettia radians

Poinsettia radians, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a strikingly colorful plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. Its foliage is typically a deep green, providing a striking contrast to the colorful bracts.

Poinsettia radians is widely cultivated as a holiday plant due to its ornamental value and seasonal appeal.
