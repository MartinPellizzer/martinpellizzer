Phlox longifolia var. filifolia

Phlox longifolia var. filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, often hairless stems that bear opposite, narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to lavender, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
