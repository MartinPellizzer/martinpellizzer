Primula macrocalyx

Primula macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals that are broad and often hairy. The plant has a short, upright stem that supports clusters of flowers at the top. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged in a basal rosette.

Primula macrocalyx thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or rocky slopes.
