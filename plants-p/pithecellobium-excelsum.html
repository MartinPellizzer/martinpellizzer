Pithecellobium excelsum

Pithecellobium excelsum, commonly known as the giant tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible pods containing seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its roots have nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in diverse ecological conditions.
