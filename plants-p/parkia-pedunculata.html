Parkia pedunculata

Parkia pedunculata, commonly known as the yellow tamarind tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. Its roots have a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
