Pelargonium × lautum

Pelargonium × lautum, commonly known as the hybrid geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
