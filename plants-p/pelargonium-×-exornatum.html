Pelargonium × exornatum

Pelargonium × exornatum, commonly known as the variegated geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its strikingly patterned leaves, which often display a mix of green, white, and sometimes pink or red hues. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, typically during the summer months. It is a popular ornamental plant, often used in gardens and containers for its aesthetic appeal.

Pelargonium × exornatum thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
