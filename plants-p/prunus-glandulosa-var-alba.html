Prunus glandulosa var. alba

Prunus glandulosa var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered glandular cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and a glossy green surface. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant collections and ecological landscapes.
