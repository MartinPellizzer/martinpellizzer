Panicularia pallida var. flava

Panicularia pallida var. flava, commonly known as yellow panicled grass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a panicle-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its yellowish flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

It is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its attractive, airy foliage.
