Plantago macronipponica

Plantago macronipponica, commonly known as the Japanese plantain, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its fibrous roots and sturdy leaves make it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
