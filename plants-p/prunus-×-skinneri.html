Prunus × skinneri

Prunus × skinneri, commonly known as the Skinner cherry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Prunus × skinneri prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
