Primula wattii

Primula wattii, commonly known as the Watt's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, particularly in China and Nepal.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

Primula wattii is also noted for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
