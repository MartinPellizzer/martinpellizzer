Pfaffia sericea f. vestita

Pfaffia sericea f. vestita, commonly known as the velvet leaf arrowhead, is a variant of the arrowhead plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in humid forests. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, almost silky texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
