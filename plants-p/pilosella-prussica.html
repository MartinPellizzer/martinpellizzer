Pilosella prussica

Pilosella prussica, commonly known as Prussian hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white flower heads that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Pilosella prussica is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.
