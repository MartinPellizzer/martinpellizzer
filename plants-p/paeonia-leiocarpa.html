Paeonia leiocarpa

Paeonia leiocarpa, commonly known as the white peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the芍药属 (Paeonia) genus within the family Ranunculaceae. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces distinctive red or orange seeds in a follicle.

Paeonia leiocarpa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses in Chinese herbal medicine.
