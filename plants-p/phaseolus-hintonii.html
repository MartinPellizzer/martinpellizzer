Phaseolus hintonii

Phaseolus hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's bean, is a rare and lesser-known species of legume native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, hairy, and often pale green to yellowish pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it produces small, edible seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
