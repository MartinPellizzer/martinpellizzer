Pterocarpus michelianus

Pterocarpus michelianus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The tree produces small, yellow flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, seed-like structures. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Pterocarpus michelianus is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in well-drained soils and often in coastal areas.
