Pleurothallis sarracenia

Pleurothallis sarracenia, commonly known as the Sarracenia orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that resemble those of the Sarracenia genus, which are known for their insect-trapping mechanisms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and can vary in shape, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Despite its name, it is not related to the carnivorous Sarracenia plants but shares some floral similarities.
