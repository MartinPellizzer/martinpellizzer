Prunus mutabilis subf. tenuifolia

Prunus mutabilis subf.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, giving it the subspecies name *tenuifolia*, meaning "thin-leaved.". The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often planted in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
