Polemonium occidentale subsp. occidentale

Polemonium occidentale subsp.

occidentale, commonly known as western Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy clusters of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, cascading flower structure.

Its foliage and floral display make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
