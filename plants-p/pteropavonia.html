Pteropavonia

Pteropavonia, commonly known as the blue false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plants typically have compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Pteropavonia species are native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe.

They are often grown in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
