Pteris coriacea

Pteris coriacea, commonly known as the rough holly fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its stiff, leathery fronds that are covered in a rough, textured surface. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a distinctive arrangement of small, lance-shaped pinnae.

Pteris coriacea is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
