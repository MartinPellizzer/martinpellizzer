Passiflora chlorina

Passiflora chlorina, commonly known as the green passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and crown-like structures. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora chlorina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and unique floral morphology.
