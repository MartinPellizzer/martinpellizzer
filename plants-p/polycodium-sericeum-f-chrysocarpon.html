Polycodium sericeum f. chrysocarpon

Polycodium sericeum f. chrysocarpon, commonly known as the golden-fruited polycodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and distinctive yellowish-orange fruits that mature in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in umbels.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruiting structures.
