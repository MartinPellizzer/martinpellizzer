Physaria occidentalis var. cinerascens

Physaria occidentalis var. cinerascens, commonly known as the western bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, bladder-like structure that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
