Puschkinia hyacinthoides

Puschkinia hyacinthoides, commonly known as the nodding squill, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It features strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant is characterized by its compact size and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Puschkinia hyacinthoides is also valued for its ability to naturalize and return year after year.
