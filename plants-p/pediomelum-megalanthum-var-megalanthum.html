Pediomelum megalanthum var. megalanthum

Pediomelum megalanthum var. megalanthum, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of thick, hairy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a striking addition to xeriscaped gardens.
