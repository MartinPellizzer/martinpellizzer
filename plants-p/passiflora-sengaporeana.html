Passiflora sengaporeana

Passiflora sengaporeana, commonly known as the Singapore passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture.

Passiflora sengaporeana thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
