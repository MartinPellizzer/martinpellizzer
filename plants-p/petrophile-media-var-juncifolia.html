Petrophile media var. juncifolia

Petrophile media var. juncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, red or pink flower spikes that appear in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to dry, coastal environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, aromatic flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
