Pfaffia glomerata var. squarrosa

Pfaffia glomerata var. squarrosa, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng or curcuma, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and unique chemical composition.
