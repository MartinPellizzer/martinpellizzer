Polygala septemnervia

Polygala septemnervia, commonly known as the seven-veined milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Polygala. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a prominent venation pattern, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
