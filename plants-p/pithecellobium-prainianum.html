Pithecellobium prainianum

Pithecellobium prainianum, commonly known as the Prain's crownflower, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems in its native range.
