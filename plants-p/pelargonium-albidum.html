Pelargonium albidum

Pelargonium albidum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Pelargonium albidum is also valued for its ability to thrive in full sun and requires minimal water once established.
