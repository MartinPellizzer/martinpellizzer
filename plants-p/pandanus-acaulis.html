Pandanus acaulis

Pandanus acaulis, commonly known as the tree pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pandanus acaulis is known for its ability to thrive in coastal and saline environments, making it an important plant in island ecosystems.
