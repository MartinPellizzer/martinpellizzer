Polygala dasanensis

Polygala dasanensis, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually small and succulent, helping to reduce water loss.

Polygala dasanensis plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
