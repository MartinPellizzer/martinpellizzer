Piper pseudopropinquum

Piper pseudopropinquum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinct, aromatic scent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by fleshy, red berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its dense foliage and vibrant fruit.
