Pseudolmedia macrophylla

Pseudolmedia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant's showy white flowers bloom in the early morning and emit a strong fragrance to attract pollinators. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Pseudolmedia macrophylla plays an important ecological role by providing habitat for aquatic life and helping to stabilize water ecosystems.
