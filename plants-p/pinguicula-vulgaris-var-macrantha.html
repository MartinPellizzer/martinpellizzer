Pinguicula vulgaris var. macrantha

Pinguicula vulgaris var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap insects. The plant produces tall, erect inflorescences bearing showy, pink or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, boggy habitats and requires high humidity and filtered sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a striking and unique addition to specialized plant collections.
