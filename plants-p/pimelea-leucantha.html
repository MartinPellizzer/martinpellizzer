Pimelea leucantha

Pimelea leucantha, commonly known as white-leafed pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

Pimelea leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
