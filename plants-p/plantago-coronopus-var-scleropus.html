Plantago coronopus var. scleropus

Plantago coronopus var. scleropus, commonly known as the hard-leaved plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features rigid, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
