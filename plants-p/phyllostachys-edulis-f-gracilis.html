Phyllostachys edulis f. gracilis

Phyllostachys edulis f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species Phyllostachys edulis.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully arching stems that are typically thinner than the standard variety. This plant is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance. The leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a bright green color.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
