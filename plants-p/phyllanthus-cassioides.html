Phyllanthus cassioides

Phyllanthus cassioides, commonly known as the African date tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, date-like fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
