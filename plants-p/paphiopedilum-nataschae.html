Paphiopedilum nataschae

Paphiopedilum nataschae, commonly known as the Natascha's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the Orchidaceae family. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a combination of green, yellow, and white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable and protected species in conservation efforts.
