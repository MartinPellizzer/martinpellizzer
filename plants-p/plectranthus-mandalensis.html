Plectranthus mandalensis

Plectranthus mandalensis, commonly known as the Kashmir mint, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and medicinal plants.

Plectranthus mandalensis is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine systems.
