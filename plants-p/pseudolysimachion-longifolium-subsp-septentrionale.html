Pseudolysimachion longifolium subsp. septentrionale

Pseudolysimachion longifolium subsp.

septentrionale, commonly known as the northern longleaf pseudolysimachion, is a member of the Ericaceae family. It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit.

The plant typically features lanceolate leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in certain regions.
