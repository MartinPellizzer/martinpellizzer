Phaius bracteosus

Phaius bracteosus, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Phaius bracteosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
