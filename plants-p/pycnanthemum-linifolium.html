Pycnanthemum linifolium

Pycnanthemum linifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and showy blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are often branched and bear clusters of small, tubular flowers.

Pycnanthemum linifolium is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
