Poa pilcomayensis var. calamagrostoidea

Poa pilcomayensis var. calamagrostoidea, commonly known as the Calamagrostis grass, is a variant of the Poa genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
