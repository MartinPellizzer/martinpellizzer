Pycnanthemum lanceolatum var. angustifolium

Pycnanthemum lanceolatum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf hillside geranium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
