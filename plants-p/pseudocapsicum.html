Pseudocapsicum

Pseudocapsicum, commonly known as the false pepper, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly Brazil, and are characterized by their small, clustered flowers and distinctive fruit structures. The fruit, which resembles a pepper, is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits. Pseudocapsicum species typically grow in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
