Pholidota articulata var. obovata

Pholidota articulata var. obovata, commonly known as the African spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to obovate in shape, and often have a reddish or purplish hue. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, and the plant produces capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It is typically found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
