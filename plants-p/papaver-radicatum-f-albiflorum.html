Papaver radicatum f. albiflorum

Papaver radicatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the white-flowered field poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its pale to white petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features a robust root system and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
