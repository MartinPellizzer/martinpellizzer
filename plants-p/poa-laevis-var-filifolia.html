Poa laevis var. filifolia

Poa laevis var. filifolia, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in turfgrass mixes.
