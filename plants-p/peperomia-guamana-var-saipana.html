Peperomia guamana var. saipana

Peperomia guamana var. saipana, commonly known as the Saipan peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the island of Guam.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in its native arid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for container gardening and rock gardens.
