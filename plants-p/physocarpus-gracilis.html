Physocarpus gracilis

Physocarpus gracilis, commonly known as silky ninebark, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It features compound leaves that are serrated and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving them a silky texture. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, red berries that persist into fall, providing food for birds and wildlife.

Silky ninebark is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
