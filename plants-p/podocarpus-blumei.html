Podocarpus blumei

Podocarpus blumei, commonly known as the Chinese podocarp, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and small, cone-like structures. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a straight trunk with a rounded crown and a slow growth rate, making it suitable for ornamental purposes.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
