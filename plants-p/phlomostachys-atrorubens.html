Phlomostachys atrorubens

Phlomostachys atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red phlomoid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant is noted for its striking dark red to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Phlomostachys atrorubens is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability to well-drained soils.
