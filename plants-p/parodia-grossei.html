Parodia grossei

Parodia grossei, commonly known as the "Pincushion Cactus," is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, globular shape and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but vibrant. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Parodia grossei is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal care when grown in suitable conditions.
