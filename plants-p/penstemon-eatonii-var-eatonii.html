Penstemon eatonii var. eatonii

Penstemon eatonii var. eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant features gray-green foliage with hairy leaves, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
