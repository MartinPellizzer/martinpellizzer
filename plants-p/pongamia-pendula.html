Pongamia pendula

Pongamia pendula, commonly known as the Indian beech or hill pongam, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment and reforestation efforts.
