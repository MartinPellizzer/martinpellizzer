Phalaris occidentalis

Phalaris occidentalis, commonly known as western glassworts, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, saline environments such as coastal areas and salt marshes. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in spikes. It is adapted to thrive in high-salinity soils and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Phalaris occidentalis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
