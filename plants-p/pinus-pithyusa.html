Pinus pithyusa

Pinus pithyusa, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The tree has long, needle-like leaves that are arranged in pairs, and it produces cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against fires and harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is adapted to grow in volcanic soils and is often found in coastal areas with a Mediterranean climate.
