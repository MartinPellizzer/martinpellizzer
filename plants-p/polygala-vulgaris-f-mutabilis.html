Polygala vulgaris f. mutabilis

Polygala vulgaris f. mutabilis, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, often yellow or greenish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its variability in flower color and morphology, which contributes to its common name.
