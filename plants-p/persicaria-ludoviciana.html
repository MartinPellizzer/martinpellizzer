Persicaria ludoviciana

Persicaria ludoviciana, commonly known as redroot pigweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems that are reddish at the base and often hairy. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. Small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches, and the plant produces small, three-lobed seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.
