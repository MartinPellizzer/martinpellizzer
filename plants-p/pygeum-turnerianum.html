Pygeum turnerianum

Pygeum turnerianum, commonly known as African wild almond, is a deciduous tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing angular seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and conservation efforts.
