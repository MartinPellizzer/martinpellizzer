Plantago coronopus f. lanigera

Plantago coronopus f. lanigera, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its dense, hairy foliage.

This plant is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
