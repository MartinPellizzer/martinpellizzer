Plectranthus megadontus

Plectranthus megadontus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to white, often arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Plectranthus megadontus is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its preference for bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
