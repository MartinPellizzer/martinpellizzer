Potentilla doubjonneana subsp. ossetica

Potentilla doubjonneana subsp.

ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its small, palmately divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine or steppe habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rosaceae family.
