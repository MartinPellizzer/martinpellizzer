Polygonum luzuloides

Polygonum luzuloides, commonly known as blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often a deep blue-green, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
