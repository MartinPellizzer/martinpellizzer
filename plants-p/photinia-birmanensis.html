Photinia birmanensis

Photinia birmanensis, commonly known as the Burmese photinia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture for garden landscapes.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that develops after flowering and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
