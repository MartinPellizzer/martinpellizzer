Potamogeton marinus var. occidentalis

Potamogeton marinus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western saltwater pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in brackish and saline waters, often found in estuaries and tidal marshes. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by wind or water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
