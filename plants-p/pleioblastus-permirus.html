Pleioblastus permirus

Pleioblastus permirus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and silvery-white leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Pleioblastus permirus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture and landscape design.
