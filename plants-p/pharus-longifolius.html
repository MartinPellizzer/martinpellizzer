Pharus longifolius

Pharus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved pharus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
