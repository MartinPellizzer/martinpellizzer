Pyrus raboutensis

Pyrus raboutensis, commonly known as the Malagasy pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are used in traditional medicine. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
