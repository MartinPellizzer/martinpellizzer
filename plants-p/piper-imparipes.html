Piper imparipes

Piper imparipes, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a flowering vine native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is cultivated primarily for its berries, which are used to produce the spice black pepper. Its botanical features include aerial roots, which help it climb and support its vine-like growth habit.

The fruit develops from the flower and undergoes a color change from green to black as it matures.
