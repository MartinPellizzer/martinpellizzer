Pescatoria violacea f. alba

Pescatoria violacea f. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered viola, is a botanical variant of the viola genus characterized by its pale to white petals.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to temperate regions, typically found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, showy flowers in spring. It belongs to the Violaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.
