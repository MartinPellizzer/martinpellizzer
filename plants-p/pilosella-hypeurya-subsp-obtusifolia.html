Pilosella hypeurya subsp. obtusifolia

Pilosella hypeurya subsp.

obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaved mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and small, yellowish-white composite flowers.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often growing in grassy habitats and disturbed soils. The leaves are typically rounded and have a soft, velvety texture, while the plant produces numerous small achenes as its seeds.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
