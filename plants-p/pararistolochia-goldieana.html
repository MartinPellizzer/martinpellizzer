Pararistolochia goldieana

Pararistolochia goldieana, commonly known as the golden pipevine, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, veined leaves and produces striking, saddle-shaped flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color. The plant is part of the Aristolochiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and chemical compounds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though some species in the genus are considered invasive.
