Piper subflavum var. subflavum

Piper subflavum var. subflavum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. Its flowers are small and green, followed by the production of yellow-orange berries that contain the aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice due to the presence of bioactive compounds.
