Pseudopipturus melastomifolius

Pseudopipturus melastomifolius, commonly known as the Hawaiian pseudopipturus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red or violet. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and it often grows in moist, shaded habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment, supporting various pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of Hawaiian flora.
