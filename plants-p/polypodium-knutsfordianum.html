Polypodium knutsfordianum

Polypodium knutsfordianum, commonly known as the Knutsford maidenhair fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided, elegant appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are wiry and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates, while its spores are released from fertile, sporangium-bearing sorus structures on the underside of the fronds.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
