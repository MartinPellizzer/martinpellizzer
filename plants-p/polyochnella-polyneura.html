Polyochnella polyneura

Polyochnella polyneura, commonly known as the mountain holly.

It is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
