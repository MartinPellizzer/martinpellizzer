Potamogeton × nitens

Potamogeton × nitens, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a submerged aquatic plant that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is a natural hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton perfoliatus, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbed and can be either smooth or serrated depending on the population. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable aquatic habitats.
