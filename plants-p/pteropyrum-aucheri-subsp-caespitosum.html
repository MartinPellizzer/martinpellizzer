Pteropyrum aucheri subsp. caespitosum

Pteropyrum aucheri subsp.

caespitosum, commonly known as the woolly pteropyrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
