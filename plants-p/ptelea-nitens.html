Ptelea nitens

Ptelea nitens, commonly known as the white-flowering ash or ash-leafed ptelea, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and have a distinctive ash-gray appearance. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid habitats.

Ptelea nitens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
