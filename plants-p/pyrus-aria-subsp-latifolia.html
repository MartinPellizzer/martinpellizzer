Pyrus aria subsp. latifolia

Pyrus aria subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pear, is a subspecies of the pear tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside.

The flowers are white and clustered in umbels, typical of the Rosaceae family. The fruit is a small, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
