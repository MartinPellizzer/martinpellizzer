Pteris pyrophylla

Pteris pyrophylla, commonly known as the fire fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, narrow, and often curled fronds.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is native to regions in Asia, including China, Japan, and Southeast Asia. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its fronds are typically dark green with a waxy coating, and they can curl into a tight spiral when dry.

Pteris pyrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
