Phaseolus macropus

Phaseolus macropus, commonly known as the large-seeded bean, is a legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several large, oval seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Phaseolus macropus is also valued for its role in traditional agriculture and ecological systems.
