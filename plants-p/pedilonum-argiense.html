Pedilonum argiense

Pedilonum argiense, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, silver-green foliage and delicate, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, reflective coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Pedilonum argiense is considered a conservation concern.
