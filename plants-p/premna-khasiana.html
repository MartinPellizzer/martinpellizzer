Premna khasiana

Premna khasiana, commonly known as Khasi Premna, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It is often found in moist, shady habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant has traditional medicinal uses and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
