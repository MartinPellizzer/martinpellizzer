Psoralea aphylla

Psoralea aphylla, commonly known as the leafless psoralea, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant is notable for its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its green stems. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often mistaken for leaves.

This plant is adapted to survive in harsh environments, showcasing remarkable resilience through its specialized morphology.
