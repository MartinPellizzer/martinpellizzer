Pittosporum paitense

Pittosporum paitense, commonly known as the Paitense Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which consists of glossy, dark green leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Pittosporum paitense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its adaptability to various soil types.
