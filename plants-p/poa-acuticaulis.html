Poa acuticaulis

Poa acuticaulis, commonly known as the sharpleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and have a sharp, pointed tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and wetlands.

Poa acuticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
