Pycnandra

Pycnandra, commonly known as the "kaffir lime" or "citrus hystrix," is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Pycnandra is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a unique and economically significant plant in tropical regions.
