Pseudosasa amabilis

Pseudosasa amabilis, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a clump-forming grass native to China.

It features slender, arching culms that are often tinged with a golden hue, especially in the sun-exposed areas. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Pseudosasa amabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its graceful appearance.
