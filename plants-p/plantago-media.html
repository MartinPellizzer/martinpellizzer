Plantago media

Plantago media, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, waxy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to grow in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its leaves are commonly used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
