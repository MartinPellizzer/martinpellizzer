Protea roupelliae subsp. roupelliae

Protea roupelliae subsp.

roupelliae, commonly known as the Roupell's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded head.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, preferring well-drained, acidic soils. It is an evergreen shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
