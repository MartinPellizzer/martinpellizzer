Pleioblastus chino f. humilis

Pleioblastus chino f. humilis, commonly known as the Chinese bamboo or dwarf bamboo, is a variety of the Pleioblastus genus native to East Asia.

It is a clump-forming grass that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by feathery seed heads. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

This bamboo thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
