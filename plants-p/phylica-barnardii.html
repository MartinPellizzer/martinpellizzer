Phylica barnardii

Phylica barnardii, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a South African shrub belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white, fluffy flowers that resemble snowballs, which bloom in winter. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, rounded shape. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Phylica barnardii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
