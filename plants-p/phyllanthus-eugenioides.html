Phyllanthus eugenioides

Phyllanthus eugenioides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a warm, humid climate.

Phyllanthus eugenioides is widely cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses.
