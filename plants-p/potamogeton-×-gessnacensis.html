Potamogeton × gessnacensis

Potamogeton × gessnacensis, commonly known as the hybrid water plantain, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Potamogeton perfoliatus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged in aquatic environments. This plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants.

Due to its sterility, it does not produce viable seeds and relies on vegetative propagation for spread.
