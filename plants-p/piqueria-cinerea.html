Piqueria cinerea

Piqueria cinerea, commonly known as the gray piqueria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions such as a compact growth form and silver-gray foliage. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to white, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Piqueria cinerea is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant suitable for xeriscaping.
