Primula littoniana

Primula littoniana, commonly known as the Litton's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

Primula littoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
