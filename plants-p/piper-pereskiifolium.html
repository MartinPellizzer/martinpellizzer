Piper pereskiifolium

Piper pereskiifolium, commonly known as the Pereskiifolium pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, spindle-shaped fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they have a spicy, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.
