Podalyria argentea var. angustifolia

Podalyria argentea var. angustifolia, commonly known as silverleaf narrowleaf podalyria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
