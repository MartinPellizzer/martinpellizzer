Pteroglossis laxa

Pteroglossis laxa, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its edible tubers.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
