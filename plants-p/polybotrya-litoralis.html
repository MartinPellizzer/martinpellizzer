Polybotrya litoralis

Polybotrya litoralis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and striking, spiky yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to saline and windy coastal environments. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh maritime conditions.
