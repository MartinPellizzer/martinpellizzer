Pentaphylloides sulphurascens

Pentaphylloides sulphurascens, commonly known as the sulfur leaf plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green foliage, which resembles sulfur due to its vibrant coloration. The plant has a low-growing habit and features small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and stems that can produce runners.

This plant is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
