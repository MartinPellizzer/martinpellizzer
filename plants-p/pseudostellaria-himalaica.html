Pseudostellaria himalaica

Pseudostellaria himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan starwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically has a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
