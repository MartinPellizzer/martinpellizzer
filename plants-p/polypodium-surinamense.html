Polypodium surinamense

Polypodium surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam holly fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with fronds that are bipinnate, meaning they have two levels of division. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, glossy appearance.

Polypodium surinamense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
