Pulsatilla patens var. wolfgangiana

Pulsatilla patens var. wolfgangiana, commonly known as the Wolfgangs' pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a single, rounded flower head with numerous small, petal-like bracts. Its leaves are basal, palmately divided, and covered in fine, white hairs.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows at high altitudes.
