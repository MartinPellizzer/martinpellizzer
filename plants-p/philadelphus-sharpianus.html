Philadelphus sharpianus

Philadelphus sharpianus, commonly known as the sharpian mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its sweet scent and showy blooms.
