Piper pseudoasperifolium

Piper pseudoasperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to the humid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The leaves of this plant are characterized by their rough, textured surface and serrated edges, contributing to its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by tiny, peppery fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has potential uses in traditional medicine.
