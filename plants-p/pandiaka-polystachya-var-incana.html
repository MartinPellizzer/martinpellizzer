Pandiaka polystachya var. incana

Pandiaka polystachya var. incana, commonly known as the silky pandiaka, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warm season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
