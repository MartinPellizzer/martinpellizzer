Pleurothallis sirene

Pleurothallis sirene, commonly known as the Sirene orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

This epiphytic plant typically grows on tree branches and thrives in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
