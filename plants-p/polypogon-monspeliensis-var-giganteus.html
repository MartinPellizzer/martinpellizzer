Polypogon monspeliensis var. giganteus

Polypogon monspeliensis var. giganteus, commonly known as the giant wiregrass, is a variant of the common wiregrass native to North America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas, such as roadsides and prairies. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are wind-pollinated.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
