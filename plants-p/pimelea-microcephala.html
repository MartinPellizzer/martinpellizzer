Pimelea microcephala

Pimelea microcephala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, yellow flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces seed pods that are typically woody and persistent. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland environments.

Pimelea microcephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens for its hardy nature.
