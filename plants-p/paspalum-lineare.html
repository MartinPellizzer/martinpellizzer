Paspalum lineare

Paspalum lineare, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in regions with high humidity. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, pale flowers.

Paspalum lineare is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
