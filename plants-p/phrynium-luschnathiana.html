Phrynium luschnathiana

Phrynium luschnathiana, commonly known as the Luschnathian arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking yellow-orange flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the warm season. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily in moist, shaded environments.

Phrynium luschnathiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
