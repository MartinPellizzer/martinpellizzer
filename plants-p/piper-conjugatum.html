Piper conjugatum

Piper conjugatum, commonly known as the Indian black pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that resemble black pepper. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aromatic fruits.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to black pepper.
