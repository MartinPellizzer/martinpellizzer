Penthorum humile

Penthorum humile, commonly known as small white crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are rounded with notched margins and small, white flowers arranged in a cluster at the tip of the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in the eastern United States.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
