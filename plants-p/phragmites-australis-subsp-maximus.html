Phragmites australis subsp. maximus

Phragmites australis subsp.

maximus, commonly known as the tall reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its dense, tall stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often white or greenish in color. It is widely distributed across temperate regions and is often found in marshes, along shorelines, and in other moist habitats.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
