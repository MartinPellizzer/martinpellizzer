Platanthera bifolia var. densiflora

Platanthera bifolia var. densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to moist, shaded habitats in North America.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with numerous small, white to pale green flowers arranged in a loose, irregular spiral. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in rich, organic soil, often near streams or in woodland areas. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically appearing in early spring.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchids in wetland environments.
