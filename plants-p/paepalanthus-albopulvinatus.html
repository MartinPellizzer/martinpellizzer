Paepalanthus albopulvinatus

Paepalanthus albopulvinatus, commonly known as the white-pulvinated puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are often covered in a dense, white or silver pubescence. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both botanical research and horticultural cultivation.
