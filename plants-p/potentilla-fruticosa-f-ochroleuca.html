Potentilla fruticosa f. ochroleuca

Potentilla fruticosa f. ochroleuca, commonly known as the whiteflower cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a delicate touch to its foliage. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a slightly hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
