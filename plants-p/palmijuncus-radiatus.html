Palmijuncus radiatus

Palmijuncus radiatus, commonly known as the radiate palm, is a unique member of the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, radiating leaf patterns. The plant features a slender, unbranched stem with a crown of narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

This species is native to specific regions in South America and is adapted to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
