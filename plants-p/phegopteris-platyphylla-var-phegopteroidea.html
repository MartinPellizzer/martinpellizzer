Phegopteris platyphylla var. phegopteroidea

Phegopteris platyphylla var. phegopteroidea, commonly known as the broad-leafed water fern, is a variant of the water fern genus Phegopteris.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate fronds that are typically broad and flat, with a distinctive veining pattern. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in wetlands. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in aquatic habitats.
