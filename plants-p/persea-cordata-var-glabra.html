Persea cordata var. glabra

Persea cordata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, smooth, and leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically borne in clusters. Its fruit is a large, pear-shaped berry with a thick, smooth skin and a creamy, oily flesh.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also cultivated for its edible fruit.
