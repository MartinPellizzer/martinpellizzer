Pygeum havilandii

Pygeum havilandii, commonly known as the African wild almond, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard, almond-like seeds that are encased in a hairy, woody pod. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
