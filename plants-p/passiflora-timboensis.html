Passiflora timboensis

Passiflora timboensis, commonly known as the Timboense passionflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in nutrients and have a tropical flavor. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Passiflora timboensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
