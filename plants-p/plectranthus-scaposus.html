Plectranthus scaposus

Plectranthus scaposus, commonly known as the African violet, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to heart-shaped. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Plectranthus scaposus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
