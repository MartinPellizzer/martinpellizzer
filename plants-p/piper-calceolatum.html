Piper calceolatum

Piper calceolatum, commonly known as the "tongue-of-saint-antonius," is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The botanical features of Piper calceolatum make it a unique and interesting species within the Piper genus.
