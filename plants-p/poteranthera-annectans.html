Poteranthera annectans

Poteranthera annectans, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and slightly pubescent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
