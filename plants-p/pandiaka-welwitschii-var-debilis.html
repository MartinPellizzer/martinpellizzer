Pandiaka welwitschii var. debilis

Pandiaka welwitschii var. debilis, commonly known as the slender pandiaka, is a rare and striking plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural range and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a conservation concern.
