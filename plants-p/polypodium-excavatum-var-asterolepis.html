Polypodium excavatum var. asterolepis

Polypodium excavatum var. asterolepis, commonly known as the star-leaf huperzia, is a fern-like vascular plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped leaf lobes that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, with a serrated margin.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique foliage.
