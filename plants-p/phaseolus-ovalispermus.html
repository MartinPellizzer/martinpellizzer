Phaseolus ovalispermus

Phaseolus ovalispermus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, elongated pods. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are oval-shaped and have a smooth, hard coat, which contributes to its common name.

The plant is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.
