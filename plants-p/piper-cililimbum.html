Piper cililimbum

Piper cililimbum, commonly known as the black pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that contain the spice known as black pepper. The plant produces a pungent compound called piperine, which is responsible for its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value as well.
