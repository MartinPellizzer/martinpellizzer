Piper decorticans

Piper decorticans, commonly known as the Indian pepper or long pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, yellowish-green berries. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. Its fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, valued for their pungent flavor.

The botanical features of Piper decorticans make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
