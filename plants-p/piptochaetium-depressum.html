Piptochaetium depressum

Piptochaetium depressum, commonly known as the flatgrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths close to the ground, with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, pale brown flowers, which are adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. It is well-suited for erosion control due to its extensive root system and drought tolerance.

Piptochaetium depressum is also valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
