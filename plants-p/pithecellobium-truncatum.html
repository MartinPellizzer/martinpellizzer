Pithecellobium truncatum

Pithecellobium truncatum, commonly known as the flat-leaf pita, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with rounded or truncate leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a flat, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Pithecellobium truncatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with adequate sunlight.
