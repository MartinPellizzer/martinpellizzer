Pulsatilla albana var. pallida

Pulsatilla albana var. pallida, commonly known as the pale pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the stems are generally hairy and upright.

The pale pasqueflower is known for its early blooming period, often appearing in late spring, and is adapted to survive in cold climates.
