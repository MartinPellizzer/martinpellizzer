Potentilla banatica

Potentilla banatica, commonly known as Banat's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Potentilla banatica is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
