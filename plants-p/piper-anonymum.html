Piper anonymum

Piper anonymum, commonly known as black pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries that are harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its vines can grow up to 10 meters in length, often climbing trees or shrubs.

The fruit contains the aromatic seeds used as a spice and medicinal ingredient worldwide.
