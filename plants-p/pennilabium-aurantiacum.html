Pennilabium aurantiacum

Pennilabium aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange-lipped orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with a distinctive orange lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in the understorey of rainforests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The flowers bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the forest floor during the wet season.
