Pilea spathulifolia

Pilea spathulifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to China.

It features rosettes of glossy, rounded, waxy leaves that resemble small coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Pilea spathulifolia is relatively low maintenance and suitable for beginners due to its adaptability and ease of care.
