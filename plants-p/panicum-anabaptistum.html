Panicum anabaptistum

Panicum anabaptistum, commonly known as the Anabaptist grass, is a species of grass native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and disturbed areas. Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Botanically, it belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
