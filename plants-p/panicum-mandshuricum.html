Panicum mandshuricum

Panicum mandshuricum, commonly known as Siberian Panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and riverbanks. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
