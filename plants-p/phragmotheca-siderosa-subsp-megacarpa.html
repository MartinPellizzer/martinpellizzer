Phragmotheca siderosa subsp. megacarpa

Phragmotheca siderosa subsp.

megacarpa, commonly known as the ironwood tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its dense, fibrous bark and dark, hard wood, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are small and leathery, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The subspecies megacarpa is distinguished by its larger, more robust fruits compared to other subspecies.
