Poa compressa subsp. langiana

Poa compressa subsp.

langiana, commonly known as the Langian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often compressed and closely spaced, giving it a dense appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
