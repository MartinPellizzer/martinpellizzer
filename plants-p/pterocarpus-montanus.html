Pterocarpus montanus

Pterocarpus montanus, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic heartwood that is highly valued for its use in furniture and musical instruments. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Pterocarpus montanus is also noted for its role in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
