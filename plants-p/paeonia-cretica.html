Paeonia cretica

Paeonia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with a serrated edge and has a short, stout rhizome. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including pink, red, and white, and are known for their fragrant scent.

Paeonia cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
