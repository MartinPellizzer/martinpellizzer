Pachyptera alliacea

Pachyptera alliacea, commonly known as the garlic leek, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of long, narrow leaves. The plant produces umbels of white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Pachyptera alliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has mild culinary uses similar to garlic and leeks.
