Prunus triloba f. simplex

Prunus triloba f. simplex, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Japanese dwarf cherry, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to Japan.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red or black berries in summer. The plant has a dense, rounded growth habit with glossy, dark green leaves that provide year-round visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal, it is often used as a specimen plant or in rock gardens.
