Pancratium longifolium

Pancratium longifolium, commonly known as the yellow spider lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, sandy soils. Its bulbs are toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful if ingested.

Pancratium longifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
