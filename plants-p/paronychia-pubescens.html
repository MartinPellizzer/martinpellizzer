Paronychia pubescens

Paronychia pubescens, commonly known as the downy sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 5 to 15 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink, with four petals and prominent stamens. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, often covered in a fine layer of white, downy hair.

Paronychia pubescens thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
