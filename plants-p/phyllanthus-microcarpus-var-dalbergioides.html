Phyllanthus microcarpus var. dalbergioides

Phyllanthus microcarpus var. dalbergioides, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are similar in appearance to dates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
