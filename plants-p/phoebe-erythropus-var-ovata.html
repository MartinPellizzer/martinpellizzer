Phoebe erythropus var. ovata

Phoebe erythropus var. ovata, commonly known as the red-leaf phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red leaves, which turn deep crimson in the fall, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, rounded growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its vibrant foliage. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
