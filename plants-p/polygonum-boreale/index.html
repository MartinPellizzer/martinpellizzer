Polygonum Boreale

Polygonum boreale, also known as the northern knotweed, is a hardy perennial plant native to cold climates across the northern hemisphere. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and forested areas. With its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers, it plays an important role in supporting local ecosystems. Despite its beauty, it can be invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native plants for resources. This article explores the characteristics, habitat, and ecological impact of Polygonum boreale.
