Pulsatilla nigricans var. albida

Pulsatilla nigricans var. albida, commonly known as the white pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has a short, hairy stem that supports its large, palmately divided leaves. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
