Pyrus lezania

Pyrus lezania, commonly known as the Lesotho pear, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, yellow or orange pomes that ripen in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
