Pseudobarleria hirsuta

Pseudobarleria hirsuta, commonly known as hairy pseudobarleria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant features erect stems covered in fine, hairy foliage, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pseudobarleria hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
