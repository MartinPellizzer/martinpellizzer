Prunus alabamensis

Prunus alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red cherries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Prunus alabamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region.
