Potentilla hirta proles pedata

Potentilla hirta proles pedata, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features a low-growing shrub with upright, hairy stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets with hairy surfaces.

Potentilla hirta proles pedata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
