Plectranthus maculatus

Plectranthus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive purplish or reddish mottling on the upper surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color.

Plectranthus maculatus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
