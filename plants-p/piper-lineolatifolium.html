Piper lineolatifolium

Piper lineolatifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often veined with a distinct light-colored pattern. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
