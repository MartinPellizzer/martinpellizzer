Prunus × lannesiana f. ogon

Prunus × lannesiana f. ogon, commonly known as the ogon cherry, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact shrub characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, tart cherry that ripens in late summer, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal maintenance once established.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
