Parinari fragilis

Parinari fragilis, commonly known as the brittle apple or fragile parinari, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn red when mature.

The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible aril, making it an important food source for various wildlife species.
