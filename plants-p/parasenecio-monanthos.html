Parasenecio monanthos

Parasenecio monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered gentian, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant's striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and botanical collections.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important subject in ecological and horticultural studies.
