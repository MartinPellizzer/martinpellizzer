Prunus mclearnii

Prunus mclearnii, commonly known as the Mclearnii cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Asia and is characterized by its ornamental value due to its showy flowers and attractive foliage. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin, and it produces small, red or black berries that are edible but not commonly cultivated for fruit.

The species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
