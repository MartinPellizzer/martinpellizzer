Pinus excelsa

Pinus excelsa, commonly known as the African silver fir, is a tall evergreen conifer native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green needles that grow in pairs. The tree produces small, yellowish cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
