Paratropia verticillata

Paratropia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered paratropia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. It has small, narrow leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Paratropia verticillata is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowering display.
