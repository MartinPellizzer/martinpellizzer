Podophyllum peltatum var. annulare

Podophyllum peltatum var. annulare, commonly known as the leopard plant, is a striking perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features large, palmately lobed leaves that are often adorned with distinctive white or yellow marginal veins, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is evergreen in mild climates, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

This variety is prized for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
