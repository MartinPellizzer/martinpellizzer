Polanisia chrysantha

Polanisia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or alpine settings.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
