Priva lappulacea

Priva lappulacea, commonly known as the common lousewort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Boraginaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and damp meadows. Its name is derived from the Latin "lappula," referring to the woolly texture of its leaves.
