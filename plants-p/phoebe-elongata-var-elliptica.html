Phoebe elongata var. elliptica

Phoebe elongata var. elliptica, commonly known as the oval-leaf phoebe, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
