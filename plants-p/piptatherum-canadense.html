Piptatherum canadense

Piptatherum canadense, commonly known as Canadian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in moist to wet soils. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams and ponds. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, making it ecologically significant in wetland ecosystems.

Piptatherum canadense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in ecological restoration projects.
