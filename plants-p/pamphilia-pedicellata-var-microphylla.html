Pamphilia pedicellata var. microphylla

Pamphilia pedicellata var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved spurred orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces intricate, spurred flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical form of Pamphilia pedicellata.
