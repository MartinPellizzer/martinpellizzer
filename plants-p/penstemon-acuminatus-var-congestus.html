Penstemon acuminatus var. congestus

Penstemon acuminatus var. congestus, commonly known as the dense-leaved beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. It produces numerous small, lance-shaped leaves and has a flowering period in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
