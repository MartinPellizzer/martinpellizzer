Piper tricarinatum

Piper tricarinatum, commonly known as the three-ribbed pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing the aromatic seeds used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include a pungent aroma and a complex chemical composition that contributes to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Piper tricarinatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its economic significance.
