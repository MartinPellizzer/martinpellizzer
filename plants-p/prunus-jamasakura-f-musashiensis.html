Prunus jamasakura f. musashiensis

Prunus jamasakura f. musashiensis, commonly known as the Musashi cherry, is a unique variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its distinct floral morphology, featuring large, pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its botanical features include ovate leaves with serrated edges and a compact growth habit.

This cultivar is highly valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
