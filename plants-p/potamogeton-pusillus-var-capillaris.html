Potamogeton pusillus var. capillaris

Potamogeton pusillus var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
