Padus serotina f. neomontana

Padus serotina f. neomontana, commonly known as the mountain hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the fruit is a small, red drupe. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
