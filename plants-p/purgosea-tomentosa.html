Purgosea tomentosa

Purgosea tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly purgosea, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white tomentose (fuzzy) covering on its stems and leaves, which provides a unique visual appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the wild.
