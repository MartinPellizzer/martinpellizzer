Prunus avium subvar. flava

Prunus avium subvar. flava, commonly known as the yellow peach, is a botanical variant of the peach tree characterized by its distinct yellow fruit.

The flowers are typically white to pale pink, and the fruit ripens to a vibrant yellow color, often with a slight orange hue. This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and unique flavor profile. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are broad and ovate, providing a lush green canopy throughout the growing season.
