Phyllanthus pomaceus

Phyllanthus pomaceus, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth, greenish-brown fruits.

Phyllanthus pomaceus is known for its adaptability and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
