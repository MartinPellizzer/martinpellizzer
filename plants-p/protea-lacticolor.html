Protea lacticolor

Protea lacticolor, commonly known as the milk-colored protea, is a striking member of the Proteaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to cream-colored flowers that resemble a cluster of small, rounded blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in areas with mild, temperate climates. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The protea's ability to adapt to various environmental conditions makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
