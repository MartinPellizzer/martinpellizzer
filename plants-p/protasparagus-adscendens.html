Protasparagus adscendens

Protasparagus adscendens, commonly known as the climbing asparagus, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
