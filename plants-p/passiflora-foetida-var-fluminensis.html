Passiflora foetida var. fluminensis

Passiflora foetida var. fluminensis, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
