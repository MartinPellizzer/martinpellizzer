Pseudophegopteris × kashmiriana

Pseudophegopteris × kashmiriana, commonly known as the Kashmir fern, is a hybrid species within the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of the Himalayas. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through sexual means via spore dispersal.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
