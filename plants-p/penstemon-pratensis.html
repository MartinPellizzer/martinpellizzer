Penstemon pratensis

Penstemon pratensis, commonly known as the meadow penstemon, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Penstemon pratensis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.
