Pomaderris pilifera subsp. talpicutica

Pomaderris pilifera subsp.

talpicutica, commonly known as the silky pomaderris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its finely hairy, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to other forms of Pomaderris pilifera.
