Papaver rhoeas var. chelidonioides

Papaver rhoeas var. chelidonioides, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

This plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It features a smooth, hairy stem that can grow up to 60 cm tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly serrated. The flowers bloom in early summer, producing bright yellow petals that are often surrounded by a pale green corona.

It is an annual plant with a short life cycle, relying on seed dispersal for propagation.
