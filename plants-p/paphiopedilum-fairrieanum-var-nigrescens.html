Paphiopedilum fairrieanum var. nigrescens

Paphiopedilum fairrieanum var. nigrescens, commonly known as the Blackened Fairrie's Phalaenopsis, is a rare and striking orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black petals and sepals, which contrast with its white or pale green lip. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, forming a compact rosette with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs. It prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
