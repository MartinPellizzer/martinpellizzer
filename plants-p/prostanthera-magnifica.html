Prostanthera magnifica

Prostanthera magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent mint bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Prostanthera magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
