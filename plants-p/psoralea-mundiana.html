Psoralea mundiana

Psoralea mundiana, commonly known as the African butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the pods are slender, containing several seeds.

Psoralea mundiana thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry climates.
