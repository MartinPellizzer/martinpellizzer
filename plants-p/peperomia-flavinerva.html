Peperomia flavinerva

Peperomia flavinerva, commonly known as the golden peperomia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically green with a subtle yellow or gold hue on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikes, and it is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements.

Peperomia flavinerva thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil.
