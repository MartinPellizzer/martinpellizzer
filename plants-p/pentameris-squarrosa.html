Pentameris squarrosa

Pentameris squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
