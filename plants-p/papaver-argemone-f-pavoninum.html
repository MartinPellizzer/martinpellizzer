Papaver argemone f. pavoninum

Papaver argemone f. pavoninum, commonly known as the peacock poppy, is a striking variant of the argemone poppy.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and intricately patterned flowers that resemble peacock feathers, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, where it thrives in dry, sunny conditions. Its botanical features include hairy stems and capsule fruits that release seeds when mature.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
