Prunus pauciflora

Prunus pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and is noted for its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its fruits are small drupes that turn dark red when mature. Prunus pauciflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is adapted to temperate climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
