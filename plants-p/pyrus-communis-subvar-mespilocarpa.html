Pyrus communis subvar. mespilocarpa

Pyrus communis subvar. mespilocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a botanical variant of the common pear.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow fruit with a smooth, thin skin. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with a broad, spreading crown. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
