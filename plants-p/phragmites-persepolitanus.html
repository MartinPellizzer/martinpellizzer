Phragmites persepolitanus

Phragmites persepolitanus, commonly known as the Persian reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and feathery, gray-green foliage. This plant is native to regions of the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread rapidly, forming dense stands in marshy areas.
