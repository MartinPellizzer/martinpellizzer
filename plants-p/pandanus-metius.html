Pandanus metius

Pandanus metius, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the top of the plant. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and thatching, while its fruits are edible and used in local cuisines.

The species is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in humid, saline conditions.
