Poterium sanguisorba var. genuinum

Poterium sanguisorba var. genuinum, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a single, stout rhizome and produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided, toothed leaves that are basal and often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is known for its medicinal properties historically used by Indigenous peoples.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
