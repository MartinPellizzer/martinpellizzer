Pereskiopsis aquosa

Pereskiopsis aquosa, commonly known as the water pereskiopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Pereskiopsis aquosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
