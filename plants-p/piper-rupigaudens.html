Piper rupigaudens

Piper rupigaudens, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are drupes that turn red when mature, containing a single seed.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
