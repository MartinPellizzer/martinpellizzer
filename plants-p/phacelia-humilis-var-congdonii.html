Phacelia humilis var. congdonii

Phacelia humilis var. congdonii, commonly known as Congdon's phacelia, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky areas.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable plant in native landscaping and ecological restoration.
