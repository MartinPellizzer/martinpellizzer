Paliurus lucidus

Paliurus lucidus, commonly known as the glossy acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and pairs of opposite, compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles, which are followed by flat, leathery pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Paliurus lucidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
