Poa pratensis f. praesignis

Poa pratensis f. praesignis, commonly known as the purple mountain grass, is a distinct form of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet coloration in the leaf blades, which is more intense than in the typical form. This ornamental variant is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental landscaping.
