Pyrenaria spectabilis var. greeniae

Pyrenaria spectabilis var. greeniae, commonly known as the Greenleaf Pyrenaria, is a rare variant of the Pyrenaria spectabilis species.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves that are a striking shade of deep green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as woodland understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which are usually pale blue or white, bloom in late spring and are clustered in racemes.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological significance.
