Paphiopedilum × siamense

Paphiopedilum × siamense, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a vibrant color contrast. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include fleshy roots and broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it thrive in shaded conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
