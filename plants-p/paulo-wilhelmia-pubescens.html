Paulo-wilhelmia pubescens

Paulo-wilhelmia pubescens, commonly known as the silky sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers bloom in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
