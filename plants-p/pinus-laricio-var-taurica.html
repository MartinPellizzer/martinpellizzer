Pinus laricio var. taurica

Pinus laricio var. taurica, commonly known as the Crimean fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is a variant of the Laricio fir, characterized by its unique morphological features and limited distribution. The plant exhibits needle-like leaves that are typically shorter and more rigid compared to its parent species. It produces cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its restricted habitat and environmental threats, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
