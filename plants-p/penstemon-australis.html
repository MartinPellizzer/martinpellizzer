Penstemon australis

Penstemon australis, commonly known as the southern beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon australis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
