Pharbitis learii

Pharbitis learii, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in cooking. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun to grow optimally.

Its rapid growth habit makes it a popular choice for ornamental and agricultural purposes.
