Pogonia revoluta

Pogonia revoluta, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a striking orchid species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, pouch-like flower that ranges in color from pink to purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs and pine forests, often in shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

Pogonia revoluta is a rare and protected species, requiring specific ecological conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
