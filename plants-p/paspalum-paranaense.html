Paspalum paranaense

Paspalum paranaense, commonly known as Parana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense vegetation.

Paspalum paranaense is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
