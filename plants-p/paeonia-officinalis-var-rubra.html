Paeonia officinalis var. rubra

Paeonia officinalis var. rubra, commonly known as the red peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range from pink to deep red, often with a contrasting white or yellow center. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The red peony is also valued for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
