Phaseolus coccineus

Phaseolus coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and long, slender pods that contain edible beans. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice in gardens and for home gardening enthusiasts.
