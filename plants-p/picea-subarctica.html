Picea subarctica

Picea subarctica, commonly known as the Arctic white spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is adapted to cold climates and grows in tundra and boreal forest environments. The tree has slender, needle-like leaves that are typically white to gray-green in color. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for Arctic wildlife.
