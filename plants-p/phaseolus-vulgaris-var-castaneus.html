Phaseolus vulgaris var. castaneus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. castaneus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange pods and edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with compound leaves and showy flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
