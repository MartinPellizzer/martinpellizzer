Potentilla lepida

Potentilla lepida, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters of five. Its leaves are palmately divided into five lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla lepida thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
