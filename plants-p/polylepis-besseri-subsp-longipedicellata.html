Polylepis besseri subsp. longipedicellata

Polylepis besseri subsp.

longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-pedicellate polylepis, is a species of evergreen tree native to the Andean highlands of Peru. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated pedicels that bear its small, yellowish flowers.

The plant has a thick, bark-like stem and is adapted to the cold, high-altitude environments where it grows. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing efficient water conservation in its arid habitat.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stability.
