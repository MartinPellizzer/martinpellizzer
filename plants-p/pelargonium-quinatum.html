Pelargonium quinatum

Pelargonium quinatum, commonly known as the Quinatum geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The leaves are typically lobed and may have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
