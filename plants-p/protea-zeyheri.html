Protea zeyheri

Protea zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's king protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
