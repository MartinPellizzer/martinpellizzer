Pyrus myabei

Pyrus myabei, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar to apples. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Pyrus myabei is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
