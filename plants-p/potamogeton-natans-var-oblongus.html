Potamogeton natans var. oblongus

Potamogeton natans var. oblongus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often broad at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found in clusters. This plant is commonly found in still or slow-moving waters such as lakes, ponds, and canals. It plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for fish and invertebrates.

Its rapid growth can sometimes lead to dense vegetation, affecting water flow and light penetration.
