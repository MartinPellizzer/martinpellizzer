Potamogeton × suecicus var. obtusus

Potamogeton × suecicus var. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often smooth and slightly waxy. This variety typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

The plant is often found in nutrient-rich waters and can form dense underwater vegetation.
