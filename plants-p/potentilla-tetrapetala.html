Potentilla tetrapetala

Potentilla tetrapetala, commonly known as the four-petaled cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a low-growing habit with woody stems and trifoliate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native gardens.
