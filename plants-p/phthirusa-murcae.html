Phthirusa murcae

Phthirusa murcae, commonly known as the wall lichen, is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal partner and a cyanobacterial or algal partner.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on stone or wood surfaces. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the intertwined partners. It plays a vital role in nutrient cycling and soil stabilization in its ecosystem.

Due to its sensitivity to environmental changes, it is often used as an indicator species for air quality.
