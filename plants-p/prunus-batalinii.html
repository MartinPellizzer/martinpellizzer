Prunus batalinii

Prunus batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. It blooms with clusters of white or pale pink flowers in the spring, preceding the fruiting season.

Prunus batalinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
