Panicum pubescens var. barbulatum

Panicum pubescens var. barbulatum, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage species.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
