Perlarius albidus

Perlarius albidus, commonly known as the white perlarius, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, white petals and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Perlarius albidus is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
