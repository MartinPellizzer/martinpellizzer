Piptanthocereus beneckei

Piptanthocereus beneckei, commonly known as the Mexican night-blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant has a columnar growth form and produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
