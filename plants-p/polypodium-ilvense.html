Polypodium ilvense

Polypodium ilvense, commonly known as the Mexican shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The fronds are usually leathery and have a deep green color with a waxy texture.
