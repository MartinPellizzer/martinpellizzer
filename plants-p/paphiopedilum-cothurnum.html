Paphiopedilum cothurnum

Paphiopedilum cothurnum, commonly known as the golden hump or the yellow hump, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a fleshy, often reddish rhizome and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in summer. The flower has a distinctive lip that curves upward, and the petals and sepals are usually yellow or orange with darker markings. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

The plant is also noted for its resilience and ability to adapt to various soil types.
