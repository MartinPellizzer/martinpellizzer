Poa violacea var. carica

Poa violacea var. carica, commonly known as the violet tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, violet-hued inflorescences that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
