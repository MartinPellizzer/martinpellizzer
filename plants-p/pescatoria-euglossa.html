Pescatoria euglossa

Pescatoria euglossa, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant produces nectar-rich flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
