Pseudosasa kurilensis

Pseudosasa kurilensis, commonly known as the Kuril bamboo, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 5 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal areas.

Pseudosasa kurilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
