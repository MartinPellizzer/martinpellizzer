Paspalum turriforme

Paspalum turriforme, commonly known as the turkeyfoot grass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red, adding ornamental value. Its leaves are narrow and slightly folded, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum turriforme is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a groundcover due to its hardy nature.
