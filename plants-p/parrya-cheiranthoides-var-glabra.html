Parrya cheiranthoides var. glabra

Parrya cheiranthoides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth parrya, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and stems, giving it its varietal name "glabra.". It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid environments.
