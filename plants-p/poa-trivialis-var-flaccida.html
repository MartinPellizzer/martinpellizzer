Poa trivialis var. flaccida

Poa trivialis var. flaccida, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant features slender, often curling leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This variant is characterized by its flexible, drooping growth habit and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
