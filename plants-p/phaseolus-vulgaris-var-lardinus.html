Phaseolus vulgaris var. lardinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. lardinus, commonly known as the Mexican runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, and the pods are elongated with a distinctive ridge. This variety is cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
