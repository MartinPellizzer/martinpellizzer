Primula villosa var. viscosa

Primula villosa var. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
