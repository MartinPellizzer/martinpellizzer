Podochilus oxysepalus

Podochilus oxysepalus, commonly known as the narrow-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Podochilus oxysepalus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
