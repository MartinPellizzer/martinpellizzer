Packera × memmingeri

Packera × memmingeri, commonly known as the Memminger's leopard's paw, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, and lobed leaves that resemble the paw of a leopard, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to the mountainous regions of the western United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Packera × memmingeri is often found in meadows and open woodlands at high elevations.
