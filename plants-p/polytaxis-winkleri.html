Polytaxis winkleri

Polytaxis winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, white or pale green flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant has a unique mycorrhizal relationship with specific fungi, which is essential for its nutrient uptake. Due to habitat destruction and its extremely limited distribution, it is considered critically endangered.

Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role and its value in scientific research on orchid biology.
