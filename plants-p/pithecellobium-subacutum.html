Pithecellobium subacutum

Pithecellobium subacutum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvet tamarind, is a species of leguminous shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and research settings.
