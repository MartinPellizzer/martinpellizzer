Pediocactus viereckii

Pediocactus viereckii, commonly known as the Vireck's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 feet in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its spiny appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive member of the Cactaceae family.
