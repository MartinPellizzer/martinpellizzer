Polycarpaea corymbosa var. minor

Polycarpaea corymbosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser clustered milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting local pollinators.
