Pocilla hederifolia

Pocilla hederifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of ivy, hence its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Pocilla hederifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
