Pfaffia tenuis

Pfaffia tenuis, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Pfaffia tenuis is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported adaptogenic and stimulant effects.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
