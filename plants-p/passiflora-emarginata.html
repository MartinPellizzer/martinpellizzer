Passiflora emarginata

Passiflora emarginata, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces orange to red berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a distinctive notched base, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Passiflora emarginata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
