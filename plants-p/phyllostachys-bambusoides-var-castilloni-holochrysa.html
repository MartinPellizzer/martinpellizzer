Phyllostachys bambusoides var. castilloni-holochrysa

Phyllostachys bambusoides var. castilloni-holochrysa, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a variant of the bamboo species characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage.

It is a clump-forming perennial grass that thrives in temperate climates, often reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are densely packed and exhibit a distinctive golden hue, especially under sunlight. Its culms are slender and segmented, with a smooth, pale surface that adds to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ability to create dense, attractive foliage in gardens and landscapes.
