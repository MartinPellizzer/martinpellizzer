Persica chinensis

Persica chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese peach, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, single or double flowers in spring, followed by fuzzy, orange-red fruits. Its botanical features include a taproot system and alternate, serrated leaf margins.

Persica chinensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
