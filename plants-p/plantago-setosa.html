Plantago setosa

Plantago setosa, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features a rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
