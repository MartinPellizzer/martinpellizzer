Pharbitis bracteata

Pharbitis bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are usually blue, violet, or white, and they attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pharbitis bracteata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
