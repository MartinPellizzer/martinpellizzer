Prunus damascena haemotocarpa

Prunus damascena haemotocarpa, commonly known as the crimson damask rose, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, edible fruits known as cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
