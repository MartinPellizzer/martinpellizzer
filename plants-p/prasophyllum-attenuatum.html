Prasophyllum attenuatum

Prasophyllum attenuatum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an endemic plant found in the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with elongated leaves. The plant produces small, greenish or brownish flowers that are typically clustered in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate with dry summers and wet winters.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and is protected under conservation efforts in its native range.
