Poa media f. ovata

Poa media f. ovata, commonly known as the oval meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical characteristics include a tufted growth habit and a preference for well-drained soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
