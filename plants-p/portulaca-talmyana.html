Portulaca talmyana

Portulaca talmyana, commonly known as the Talmyana moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Portulacaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. It is a low-growing annual herb that often forms dense mats on the ground.

Portulaca talmyana is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
