Peperomia rehmannii

Peperomia rehmannii, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with silver or white markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, usually green or red, which are followed by rounded, fleshy fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and ability to tolerate low light conditions make it an easy-to-care-for houseplant.
