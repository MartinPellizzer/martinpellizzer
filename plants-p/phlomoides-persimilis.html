Phlomoides persimilis

Phlomoides persimilis, commonly known as the slender phlomoides, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, pale blue to purple flowers are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
