Phlox argillacea

Phlox argillacea, commonly known as claybank phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
