Prunus persica var. davidiana

Prunus persica var. davidiana, commonly known as the Davidian peach, is a variant of the peach tree native to China.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a yellowish-orange skin and a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white with a slight pinkish tinge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value in gardens.
