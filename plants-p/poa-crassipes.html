Poa crassipes

Poa crassipes, commonly known as rough meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its robust, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including moist and well-drained soils. The plant typically produces dense foliage with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides, contributing to its ecological and agricultural significance.

Poa crassipes is also valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage plant for livestock.
