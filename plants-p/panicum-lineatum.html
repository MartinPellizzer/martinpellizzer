Panicum lineatum

Panicum lineatum, commonly known as hairgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like appearance with slender, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its aesthetic appeal and longevity.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
