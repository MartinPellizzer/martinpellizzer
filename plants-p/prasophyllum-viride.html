Prasophyllum viride

Prasophyllum viride, commonly known as the green leek orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its leaf-like structures that are actually modified stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the cooler months. Prasophyllum viride typically grows on the trunks of trees or in shaded, moist environments, relying on its host for support.

It is a rare and protected species, often found in specific habitats such as rainforests.
