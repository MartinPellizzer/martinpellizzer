Petrorhagia illyrica

Petrorhagia illyrica, commonly known as the Illyrian rock-jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in the spring. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Petrorhagia illyrica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for rocky gardens.
