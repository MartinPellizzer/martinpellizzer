Pleioblastus longifimbriatus

Pleioblastus longifimbriatus, commonly known as the long-fringed bamboo, is a species of grass family plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green stems and long, feathery leaf tips that give it a distinctive appearance. This bamboo thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a fine, silky texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
