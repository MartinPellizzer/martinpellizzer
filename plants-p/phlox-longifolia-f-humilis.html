Phlox longifolia f. humilis

Phlox longifolia f. humilis, commonly known as the low phlox or creeping phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats of foliage, often spreading via stolons. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy inflorescences that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
