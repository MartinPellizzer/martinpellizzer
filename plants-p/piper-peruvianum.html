Piper peruvianum

Piper peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in cooking, particularly in the preparation of ceviche in Peru.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
