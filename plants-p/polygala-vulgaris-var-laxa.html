Polygala vulgaris var. laxa

Polygala vulgaris var. laxa, commonly known as the lax polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while its flowers are clustered in racemes.

This variant is noted for its smaller, more loosely arranged flowers compared to other varieties of Polygala vulgaris.
