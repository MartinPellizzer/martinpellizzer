Prunus japonica f. rufinervis

Prunus japonica f. rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined Japanese cherry, is a unique variation of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking red veins on the leaves, which contrast sharply with the pale green or white foliage. This ornamental plant is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes. The flowers, which appear in early spring, are typically pale pink or white, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
