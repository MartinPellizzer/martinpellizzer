Perotis spiciformis

Perotis spiciformis, commonly known as the spicate perotis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear small, clustered white flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Perotis spiciformis plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
