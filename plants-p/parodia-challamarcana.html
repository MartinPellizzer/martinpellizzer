Parodia challamarcana

Parodia challamarcana, commonly known as the "Challamarca cactus," is a species native to the Andes Mountains of Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed, cylindrical shape and a gray-green coloration. The plant features short, spiny ribs and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
