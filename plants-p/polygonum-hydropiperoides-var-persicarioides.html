Polygonum hydropiperoides var. persicarioides

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. persicarioides, commonly known as water pepper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in spikes. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
