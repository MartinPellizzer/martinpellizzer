Pseudarthria polycarpa

Pseudarthria polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited legume, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, hairy pods containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and savanna ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated for ecological and agricultural purposes.
