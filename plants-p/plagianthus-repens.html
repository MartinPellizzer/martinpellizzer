Plagianthus repens

Plagianthus repens, commonly known as the creeping flame bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Plagianthus repens is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
