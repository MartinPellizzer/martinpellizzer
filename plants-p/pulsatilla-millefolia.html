Pulsatilla millefolia

Pulsatilla millefolia, commonly known as the common pasqueflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, saucer-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem with palmately divided leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Pulsatilla millefolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
