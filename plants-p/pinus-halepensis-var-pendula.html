Pinus halepensis var. pendula

Pinus halepensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Aleppo pine, is a variant of the Aleppo pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in clusters of two or three. It produces cones that are cylindrical and can reach lengths of up to 10 centimeters.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its unique form and adaptability to various soil types.
