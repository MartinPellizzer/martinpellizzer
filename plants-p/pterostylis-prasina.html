Pterostylis prasina

Pterostylis prasina, commonly known as the greenhood orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and understoreys. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single, hood-shaped flower that is usually green or reddish in color. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

Pterostylis prasina is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique and intricate floral structure.
