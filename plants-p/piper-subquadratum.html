Piper subquadratum

Piper subquadratum, commonly known as the quadrilateral pepper, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
