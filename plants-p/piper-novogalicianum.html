Piper novogalicianum

Piper novogalicianum, commonly known as the Galician pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
