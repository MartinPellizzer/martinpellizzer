Phylica virgata

Phylica virgata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The silver tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.
