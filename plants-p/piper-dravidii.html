Piper dravidii

Piper dravidii, commonly known as the Dravidian pepper or wild long pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the wet tropical regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, clustered berries that turn from green to red as they mature. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its medicinal and culinary uses, particularly in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
