Pinus brutia var. caucasica

Pinus brutia var. caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the Caucasus region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in mountainous areas with well-drained soils. The tree is characterized by its thick, scaly bark and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky environments. Its cones are large and woody, containing edible seeds that are often used in various culinary traditions.

This variety is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
