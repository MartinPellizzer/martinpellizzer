Populus serrata f. rotundata

Populus serrata f. rotundata, commonly known as the rounded-leaved poplar, is a variant of the poplar species characterized by its distinctive rounded leaf shape.

It is native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant is a deciduous tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and catkin-like, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
