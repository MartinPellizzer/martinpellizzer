Polytaenium feei var. lanceolatum

Polytaenium feei var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceolate polytaenium, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white or pale green. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in local medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
