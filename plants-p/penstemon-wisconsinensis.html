Penstemon wisconsinensis

Penstemon wisconsinensis, commonly known as the Wisconsin beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Penstemon wisconsinensis prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in prairies and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
