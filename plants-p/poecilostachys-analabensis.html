Poecilostachys analabensis

Poecilostachys analabensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to white flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
