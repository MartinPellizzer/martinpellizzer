Piper playa-blancanum

Piper playa-blancanum, commonly known as the Playa Blancanum pepper vine, is a member of the Piperaceae family, characterized by its climbing habit and opposite leaves.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. The plant is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in areas with moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that mature to a reddish color, containing several seeds.

The species is notable for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
