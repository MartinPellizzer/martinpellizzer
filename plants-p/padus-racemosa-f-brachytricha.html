Padus racemosa f. brachytricha

Padus racemosa f. brachytricha, commonly known as the dwarf raceme cherry, is a compact shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its ornamental value. Its fruit, a small red drupe, develops in late summer and is edible.

This form is distinguished by its shorter inflorescences compared to the typical species.
