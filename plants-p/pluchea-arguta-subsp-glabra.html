Pluchea arguta subsp. glabra

Pluchea arguta subsp.

glabra, commonly known as white cudweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the spring and early summer.

The species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
