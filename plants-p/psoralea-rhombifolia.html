Psoralea rhombifolia

Psoralea rhombifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved psoralea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, rhombic-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and open woodland habitats.
