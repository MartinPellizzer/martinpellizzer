Poa ussuriensis f. scabra

Poa ussuriensis f. scabra, commonly known as the Ussuri rough bentgrass, is a variant of the Ussuri bentgrass.

It is a perennial grass species native to the Far East of Russia and parts of East Asia. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and compact growth habit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous and subalpine regions.

This form is noted for its ecological significance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
