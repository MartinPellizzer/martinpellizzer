Palaquium glabrifolium

Palaquium glabrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed palaquium, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glabrous or nearly hairless. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also sometimes cultivated for its latex, which has industrial applications.
