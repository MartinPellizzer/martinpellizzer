Phaenopyrum coccineum

Phaenopyrum coccineum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, woody fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
