Pycreus chorisanthus

Pycreus chorisanthus, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which turn red or orange in the fall. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental bracts and adaptability to various soil types.
