Petrosilene

Petrosilene, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic scent and has a mild, sweet flavor reminiscent of anise. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Petrosilene is cultivated both for its ornamental value and for use in culinary and medicinal applications.
