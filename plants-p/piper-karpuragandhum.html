Piper karpuragandhum

Piper karpuragandhum, commonly known as Indian betel vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic leaves are widely used in various cultural and culinary practices.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.
