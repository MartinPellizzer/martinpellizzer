Prunus enucleata var. insititia

Prunus enucleata var. insititia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, juicy fruits that are edible.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production, thriving in temperate climates.
