Pandanus durio

Pandanus durio, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often used in local cuisines. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for thatching and weaving in traditional crafts.

Pandanus durio thrives in coastal and wetland environments, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
