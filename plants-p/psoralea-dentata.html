Psoralea dentata

Psoralea dentata, commonly known as the serrated psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are pinnately compound with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Psoralea dentata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions.
