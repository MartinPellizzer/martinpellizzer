Potentilla taurica var. tutuiatuensis

Potentilla taurica var. tutuiatuensis, commonly known as the Caucasian potentilla, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
