Pennisetum cinereum

Pennisetum cinereum, commonly known as purple muhly, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a warm-season grass that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Pennisetum cinereum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support wildlife in native habitats.
