Pterostylis multisignata

Pterostylis multisignata, commonly known as the multispot ghost orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, pale green to white floral structure with distinct markings. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, relying on atmospheric moisture for nourishment. Its flowers are small and clustered, often resembling a ghostly appearance due to their shape and color.

It is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions to thrive.
