Podocarpus milanjianus

Podocarpus milanjianus, commonly known as the Milanjian's podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a tall, slender trunk and a dense canopy of foliage.

Podocarpus milanjianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
