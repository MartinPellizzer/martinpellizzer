Panicum guianense

Panicum guianense, commonly known as giant panicgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and large, feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Its rapid growth and extensive root system make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
