Polypodium faurianum

Polypodium faurianum, commonly known as the Fauria's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its leathery, shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in montane forests. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to anchor itself to its substrate while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air.

The spore-producing structures, or sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern on the underside of the fronds.
