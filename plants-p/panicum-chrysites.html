Panicum chrysites

Panicum chrysites, commonly known as golden panicum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and produces striking golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract birds and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
