Pittosporum serrulatum

Pittosporum serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Pittosporum serrulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
