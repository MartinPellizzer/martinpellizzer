Pithecellobium paniculatum

Pithecellobium paniculatum, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compound leaves and clusters of yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
