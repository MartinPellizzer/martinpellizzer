Pyrethrum mucronulatum

Pyrethrum mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pyrethrin, a natural insecticide, which is extracted from its flowering heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal and pesticidal properties, it has historical and contemporary uses in herbal remedies and organic pest control.
