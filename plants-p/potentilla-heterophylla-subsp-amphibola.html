Potentilla heterophylla subsp. amphibola

Potentilla heterophylla subsp.

amphibola, commonly known as the amphibious potentilla, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in both aquatic and terrestrial environments, showcasing its adaptability.

The plant typically has alternate leaves that vary in shape depending on the habitat, a trait reflected in its subspecific name. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

This species is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
