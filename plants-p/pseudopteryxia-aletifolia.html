Pseudopteryxia aletifolia

Pseudopteryxia aletifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

Pseudopteryxia aletifolia is found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
