Portulaca quadrifida var. meridiana

Portulaca quadrifida var. meridiana, commonly known as the four-leaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it well-suited for dry climates. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its edible leaves and flowers, which have a slightly tart flavor.
