Plantago eriophora

Plantago eriophora, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically features lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are often hidden among the foliage. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is valued for its adaptability and ecological importance.
