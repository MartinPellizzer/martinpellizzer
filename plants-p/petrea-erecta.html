Petrea erecta

Petrea erecta, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine or sweet shrub, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its aromatic, white or pink flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth texture and a fragrant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
