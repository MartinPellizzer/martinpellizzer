Punica spinosa

Punica spinosa, commonly known as the spiny pomegranate, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright red, clustered flowers that are followed by round, leathery fruits. The fruit contains numerous seeds surrounded by a juicy, tart aril.

It is adapted to dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
