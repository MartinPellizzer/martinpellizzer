Pinus ponderosa subsp. readiana

Pinus ponderosa subsp.

readiana, commonly known as the Read's ponderosa pine, is a subspecies native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and distinctive, long, needle-like leaves that are typically two to three inches in length.

The cones of this subspecies are relatively small and often have a reddish-brown color when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a continental climate.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
