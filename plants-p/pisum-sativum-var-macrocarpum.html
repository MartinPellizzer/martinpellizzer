Pisum sativum var. macrocarpum

Pisum sativum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant edible pea, is a cultivated variant of the common pea.

It is characterized by its large, thick pods that contain numerous, plump seeds. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. Its botanical features include compound leaves with tendrils and flowers that are typically white or pale purple.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often grown for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
