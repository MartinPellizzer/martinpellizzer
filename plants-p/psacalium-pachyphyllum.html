Psacalium pachyphyllum

Psacalium pachyphyllum, commonly known as the thick-leaved psacalium, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the growing season. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a tendency to branch out.

Psacalium pachyphyllum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
