Potentilla breviscapa

Potentilla breviscapa, commonly known as the short-horned cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit with multiple stems. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red or orange berries in the fall.

Potentilla breviscapa is adapted to rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
