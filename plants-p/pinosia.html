Pinosia

Pinosia, commonly known as the blue spruce, is a coniferous tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and striking blue-gray foliage. The needles are stiff and four-sided, with a waxy coating that gives them their distinctive color. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds.

Pinosia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and climates.
