Pentanisia crassifolia

Pentanisia crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf pentanisia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is considered a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
