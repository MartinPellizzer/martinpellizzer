Polygonum macrochaeton

Polygonum macrochaeton, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its tall, robust stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a reddish tint, and it can grow up to several meters in height.

Polygonum macrochaeton is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, disturbed soils.
