Piper exigens

Piper exigens, commonly known as the slender pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are typically greenish-white and clustered in spikes, and it produces small, peppery fruits.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
