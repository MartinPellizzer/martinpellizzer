Potentilla wimanniana var. grandiflora

Potentilla wimanniana var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
