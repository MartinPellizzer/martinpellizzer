Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp. strigosa

Pseudoroegneria strigosa subsp.

strigosa, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually blue-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed florets.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
