Potentilla simulatrix

Potentilla simulatrix, commonly known as the mimic potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Potentilla simulatrix is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
