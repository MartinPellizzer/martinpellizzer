Pseudosasa japonica var. tsutsumiana

Pseudosasa japonica var. tsutsumiana, commonly known as the Japanese bamboo or mountain bamboo, is a clumping bamboo species native to Japan and parts of China.

It is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often covered with a whitish or grayish bloom. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive clumps.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
