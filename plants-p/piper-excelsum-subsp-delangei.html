Piper excelsum subsp. delangei

Piper excelsum subsp.

delangei, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of New Guinea. It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
