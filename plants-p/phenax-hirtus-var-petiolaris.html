Phenax hirtus var. petiolaris

Phenax hirtus var. petiolaris, commonly known as the hairy petiole phenax, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with a prominent petiole. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
