Patagonium lanatum

Patagonium lanatum, commonly known as the hairy patagonia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woolly, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Patagonium lanatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
