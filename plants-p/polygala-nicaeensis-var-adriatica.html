Polygala nicaeensis var. adriatica

Polygala nicaeensis var. adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces distinctive, thread-like fruits known as follicles, which contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
