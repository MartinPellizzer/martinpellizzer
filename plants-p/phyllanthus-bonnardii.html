Phyllanthus bonnardii

Phyllanthus bonnardii, commonly known as the African spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
