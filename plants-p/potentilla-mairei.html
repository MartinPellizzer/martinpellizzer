Potentilla mairei

Potentilla mairei, commonly known as Maire's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are covered in fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Potentilla mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
