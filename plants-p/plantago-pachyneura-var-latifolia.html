Plantago pachyneura var. latifolia

Plantago pachyneura var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
