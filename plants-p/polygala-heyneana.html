Polygala heyneana

Polygala heyneana, commonly known as the featherleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces yellowish or greenish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are often mistaken for leaves due to their foliage-like appearance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Polygala heyneana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
