Pleurothallis candida

Pleurothallis candida, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and white, with a subtle fragrance, and are arranged in a loose cluster.

Pleurothallis candida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its delicate appearance.
