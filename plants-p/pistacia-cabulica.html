Pistacia cabulica

Pistacia cabulica, commonly known as the Persian pistachio, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into drupes. Its fruit, containing a single edible seed, is highly valued for its rich, nutty flavor and is widely cultivated for commercial use.

The tree is also noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid climates.
