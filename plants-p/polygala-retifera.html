Polygala retifera

Polygala retifera, commonly known as the slender milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala retifera is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
