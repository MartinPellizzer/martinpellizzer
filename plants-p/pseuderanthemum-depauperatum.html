Pseuderanthemum depauperatum

Pseuderanthemum depauperatum, commonly known as the poor man's pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats such as along roadsides or in open woodlands.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative propagation.
