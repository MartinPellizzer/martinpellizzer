Potamogeton gramineus var. typicus

Potamogeton gramineus var. typicus, commonly known as the common pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across much of Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, with the pollen released into the water. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence can indicate good water quality and is often used in ecological assessments of freshwater habitats.
