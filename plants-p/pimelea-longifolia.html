Pimelea longifolia

Pimelea longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs from late winter to early spring.

Pimelea longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
