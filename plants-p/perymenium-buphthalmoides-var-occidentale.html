Perymenium buphthalmoides var. occidentale

Perymenium buphthalmoides var. occidentale, commonly known as the western false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.
