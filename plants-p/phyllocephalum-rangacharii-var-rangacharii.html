Phyllocephalum rangacharii var. rangacharii

Phyllocephalum rangacharii var. rangacharii, commonly known as the Rangacharii fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the tropical regions of South India.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that exhibit a striking contrast between its glossy green upper surface and its lighter, sometimes silvery underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are typically found on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
