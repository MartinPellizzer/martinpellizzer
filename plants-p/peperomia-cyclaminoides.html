Peperomia cyclaminoides

Peperomia cyclaminoides, commonly known as the wax plant or marble queen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters during the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
