Poterium longifolium

Poterium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved poterium, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its blooming period occurs in the spring and summer, producing small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
