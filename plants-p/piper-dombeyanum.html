Piper dombeyanum

Piper dombeyanum, commonly known as the Dombey's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, slender fruits. The fruits contain a single seed and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical studies.
