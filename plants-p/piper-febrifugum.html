Piper febrifugum

Piper febrifugum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are tiny, yellowish berries that contain the plant's active compounds.

Piper febrifugum is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its potential antimalarial and analgesic effects.
