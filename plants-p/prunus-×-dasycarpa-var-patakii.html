Prunus × dasycarpa var. patakii

Prunus × dasycarpa var. patakii, commonly known as the Patakii cherry, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, sweet, and tart fruits that are edible. The plant typically blooms with pink to white flowers in early spring, followed by the development of its distinctive, hairy fruits. It is native to the eastern regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.
