Piper petiolatum

Piper petiolatum, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ground cover in tropical landscapes.
