Polygala rupestris subsp. rupestris

Polygala rupestris subsp.

rupestris, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
