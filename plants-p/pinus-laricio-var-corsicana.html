Pinus laricio var. corsicana

Pinus laricio var. corsicana, commonly known as the Corsican fir, is a variant of the Swiss stone pine native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cones that are typically reddish-brown and contain seeds covered in a thin, winged membrane. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in forestry and horticulture.
