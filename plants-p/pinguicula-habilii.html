Pinguicula habilii

Pinguicula habilii, commonly known as the Mexican butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Its unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for both botanists and plant enthusiasts.
