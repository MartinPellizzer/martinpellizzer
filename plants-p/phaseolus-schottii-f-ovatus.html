Phaseolus schottii f. ovatus

Phaseolus schottii f. ovatus, commonly known as the ovate-phaseolus, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its distinctive ovate-shaped leaves and pods, which are typically larger than those of the standard species. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and produces edible pods that contain seeds used for food. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content.
