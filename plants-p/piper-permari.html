Piper permari

Piper permari, commonly known as the Java pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppercorn-like berries that turn red when mature. It is often grown for its aromatic fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Piper permari thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
