Pandanus distentus

Pandanus distentus, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, clustered inflorescences with yellowish to greenish flowers, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fibrous leaves are utilized for thatching and weaving, making it an important resource in local communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
