Pogostemon pentagonus

Pogostemon pentagonus, commonly known as the five-angled pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorled clusters.

Pogostemon pentagonus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
