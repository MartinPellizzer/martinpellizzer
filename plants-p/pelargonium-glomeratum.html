Pelargonium glomeratum

Pelargonium glomeratum, commonly known as the geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
