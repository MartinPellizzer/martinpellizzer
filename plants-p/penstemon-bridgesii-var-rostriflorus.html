Penstemon bridgesii var. rostriflorus

Penstemon bridgesii var. rostriflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in dry, sunny environments. It produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and pollinator attraction.
