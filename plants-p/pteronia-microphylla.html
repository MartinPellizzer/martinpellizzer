Pteronia microphylla

Pteronia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is widely cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
