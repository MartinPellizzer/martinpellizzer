Prenanthes proteophylla

Prenanthes proteophylla, commonly known as the white sweetroot, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous and has been historically used in traditional medicine.
