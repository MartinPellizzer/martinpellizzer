Possira triphylla

Possira triphylla, commonly known as the three-leafed fern, is a delicate evergreen plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a lush, green appearance. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing structures, characteristic of its fern lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining, humus-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
