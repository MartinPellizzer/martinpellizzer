Paullinia meliifolia var. hirsuta

Paullinia meliifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bitter apple, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, while the fruit is a yellow, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

The plant is known for its bitter taste and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
