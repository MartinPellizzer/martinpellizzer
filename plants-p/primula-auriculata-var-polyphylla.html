Primula auriculata var. polyphylla

Primula auriculata var. polyphylla, commonly known as the many-leaved primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to purple flowers. The plant features broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly wavy margin. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
