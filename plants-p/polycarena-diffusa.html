Polycarena diffusa

Polycarena diffusa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its translucent, ribbon-like leaves that appear to float freely in water.

It lacks true roots and instead relies on rhizomes for nutrient absorption and propagation. The plant is typically found in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its delicate structure and ethereal appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental water gardens.

However, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
