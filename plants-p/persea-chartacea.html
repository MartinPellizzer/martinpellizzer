Persea chartacea

Persea chartacea, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by yellow-orange fruits that are edible when fully mature. Persea chartacea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
