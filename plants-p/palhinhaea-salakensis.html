Palhinhaea salakensis

Palhinhaea salakensis, commonly known as the Salakensis cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in short, spiny bristles that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
