Parodia microsperma subsp. sanguiniflora

Parodia microsperma subsp.

sanguiniflora, commonly known as the blood-flowered cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to northern South America. It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem that typically grows up to 10 centimeters in height.

The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vivid contrast to its green foliage. Its stems are covered in fine, white spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
