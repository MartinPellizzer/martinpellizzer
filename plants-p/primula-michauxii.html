Primula michauxii

Primula michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a low-growing rosette of basal leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
