Persea sericea

Persea sericea, commonly known as the silky persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, silvery-green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, yellowish persimmon that is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor.

The silky persimmon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
