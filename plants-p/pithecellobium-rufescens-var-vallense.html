Pithecellobium rufescens var. vallense

Pithecellobium rufescens var. vallense, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow, velvety pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its growth habit includes a dense canopy and a fibrous root system, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological benefits in agroforestry systems.
