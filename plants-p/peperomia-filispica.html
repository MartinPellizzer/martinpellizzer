Peperomia filispica

Peperomia filispica, commonly known as the laceleaf peperomia, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and its distinctive, deeply lobed, waxy leaves that give it a delicate, almost lace-like appearance. The leaves are typically green with a slightly reddish tinge on the underside, and they are arranged in a rosette pattern along the stems. This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive foliage.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
