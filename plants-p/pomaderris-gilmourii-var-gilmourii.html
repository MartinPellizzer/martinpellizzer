Pomaderris gilmourii var. gilmourii

Pomaderris gilmourii var. gilmourii, commonly known as the Gilmour's pomaderris, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
