Paronychia albanica subsp. albanica

Paronychia albanica subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are linear and often covered with fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its succulent characteristics and ability to thrive in harsh environments.
