Picea wilsonii subsp. shanxiensis

Picea wilsonii subsp.

shanxiensis, commonly known as the Shanxi fir, is a rare and endemic subspecies native to the mountainous regions of Shanxi Province in China. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the fir genus.

The plant thrives in cool, moist climates at high altitudes, often found in mixed forests. Its botanical significance lies in its unique genetic makeup and ecological role in its native habitat.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
