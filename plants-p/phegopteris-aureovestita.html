Phegopteris aureovestita

Phegopteris aureovestita, commonly known as the golden ghost fern, is a species of fern native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically light green to golden in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
