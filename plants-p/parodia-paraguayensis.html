Parodia paraguayensis

Parodia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage.

Parodia paraguayensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
