Pilosella tardiuscula

Pilosella tardiuscula, commonly known as the small late pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a low-growing habit, with hairy stems and leaves that are usually covered in fine, white pubescence. It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Pilosella tardiuscula is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
