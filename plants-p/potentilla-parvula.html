Potentilla parvula

Potentilla parvula, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with small, rounded leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Potentilla parvula is also known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
