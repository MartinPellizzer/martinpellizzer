Piper harrisii

Piper harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a mildly peppery scent.

Piper harrisii is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
