Picea abies pendula-major

Picea abies pendula-major, commonly known as the weeping Norway spruce, is a cultivar of the Norway spruce (Picea abies) characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

This plant is a coniferous evergreen with needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curved. It produces small, cone-shaped seed structures that contain winged seeds, typical of the Pinaceae family. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value, showcasing a unique, flowing appearance.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
