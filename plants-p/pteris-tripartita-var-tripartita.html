Pteris tripartita var. tripartita

Pteris tripartita var. tripartita, commonly known as the three-part maidenhair fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds.

It features triangular pinnae that are divided into three distinct segments, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

The fronds are typically dark green and can be used to create a lush, exotic look in indoor or outdoor gardens.
