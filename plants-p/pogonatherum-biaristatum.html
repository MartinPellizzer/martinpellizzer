Pogonatherum biaristatum

Pogonatherum biaristatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
