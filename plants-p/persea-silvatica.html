Persea silvatica

Persea silvatica, commonly known as the wild avocado or silvatic persimmon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns yellow or orange when ripe.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential edible qualities.
