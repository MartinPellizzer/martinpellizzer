Piper sublignosum

Piper sublignosum, commonly known as the African black pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves, with small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a spice.
