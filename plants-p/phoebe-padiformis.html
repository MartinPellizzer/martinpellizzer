Phoebe padiformis

Phoebe padiformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf phoebe, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are usually glossy and can reach up to 10 inches in length.

Phoebe padiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
