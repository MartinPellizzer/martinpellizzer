Poa bracteosa var. mollis

Poa bracteosa var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-bracted grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, soft foliage that gives it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering stems are slender and bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
