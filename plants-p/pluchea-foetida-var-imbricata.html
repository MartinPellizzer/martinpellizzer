Pluchea foetida var. imbricata

Pluchea foetida var. imbricata, commonly known as the stinkweed or false ragweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant has alternate, hairy, and often heart-shaped leaves, and its small yellowish flowers are arranged in compact clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced in its dried form.
