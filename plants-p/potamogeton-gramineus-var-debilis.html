Potamogeton gramineus var. debilis

Potamogeton gramineus var. debilis, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged. The plant produces small, green flowers that are arranged in spike-like clusters, and it plays a role in providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates. Its rhizomes help stabilize sediments, contributing to water clarity and ecosystem health.

This variety is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
