Physalis philadelphica subsp. ixocarpa

Physalis philadelphica subsp.

ixocarpa, commonly known as the golden berry or Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, which are typically covered in fine pubescence.

The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits that enclose a papery calyx, resembling a small lantern. These fruits are edible, sweet, and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
