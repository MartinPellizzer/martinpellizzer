Phillyrea angustifolia var. curtifolia

Phillyrea angustifolia var. curtifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
