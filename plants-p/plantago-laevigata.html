Plantago laevigata

Plantago laevigata, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth and slightly waxy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their soothing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its robust, drought-tolerant growth habit.
