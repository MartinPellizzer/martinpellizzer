Phoradendron hartii

Phoradendron hartii, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant's berries are often used in traditional medicine and are also a food source for birds, which help in seed dispersal.
