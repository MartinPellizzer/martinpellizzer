Pierreodendron africanum

Pierreodendron africanum, commonly known as the African stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white during the warmer months. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
