Physostegia ledinghamii

Physostegia ledinghamii, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of violet to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
