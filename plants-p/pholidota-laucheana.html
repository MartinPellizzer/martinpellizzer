Pholidota laucheana

Pholidota laucheana, commonly known as the Laucheana buckthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and produces yellow flowers in spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to arid environments and exhibits drought tolerance.

The fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
