Pfaffia paniculata var. bidentata

Pfaffia paniculata var. bidentata, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the warmer months. It is often cultivated for its purported medicinal properties, though scientific evidence supporting these claims remains limited.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
