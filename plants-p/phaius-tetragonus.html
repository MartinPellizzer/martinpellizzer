Phaius tetragonus

Phaius tetragonus, commonly known as the four-cornered marsh orchid, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in wetland or marshy habitats. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

Phaius tetragonus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
