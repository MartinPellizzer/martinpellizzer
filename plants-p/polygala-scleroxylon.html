Polygala scleroxylon

Polygala scleroxylon, commonly known as the African catchfly, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
