Portulaca tinctoria

Portulaca tinctoria, commonly known as red sandalwood or red portulaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species and environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically alternate and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Portulaca tinctoria is well adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy plant that thrives in well-drained soils.
