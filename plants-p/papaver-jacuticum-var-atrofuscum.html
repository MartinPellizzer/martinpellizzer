Papaver jacuticum var. atrofuscum

Papaver jacuticum var. atrofuscum, commonly known as the dark purple poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to almost black petals, which contrast sharply with its green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
