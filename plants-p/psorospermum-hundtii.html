Psorospermum hundtii

Psorospermum hundtii, commonly known as the Madagascar bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and disturbed habitats.

The tree is valued for its timber and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
