Pluchea adnata var. canescens

Pluchea adnata var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly false ragweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
