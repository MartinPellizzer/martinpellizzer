Picea excelsa f. europaea

Picea excelsa f. europaea, commonly known as the European white spruce, is a subspecies of the white spruce native to Europe.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, dark green needles. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and craftsmanship.
