Pleurothallis aondae

Pleurothallis aondae, commonly known as the Andean orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a range of colors from white to pink.

Pleurothallis aondae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
