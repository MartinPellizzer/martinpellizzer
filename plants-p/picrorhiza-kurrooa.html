Picrorhiza kurrooa

Picrorhiza kurrooa, commonly known as Kuth, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Labiatae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored roots that are rich in bioactive compounds. The plant thrives in cold, rocky environments and is typically found at elevations above 2,000 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and clustered in whorls.

The roots are the primary part used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
