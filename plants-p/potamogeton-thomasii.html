Potamogeton thomasii

Potamogeton thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by water currents.

Thomas's pondweed plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
