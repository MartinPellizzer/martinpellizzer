Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. borneensis

Phalaenopsis cornu-cervi f. borneensis, commonly known as the Borneo horned orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the island of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, horn-like appendages on the petals, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically produces large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
