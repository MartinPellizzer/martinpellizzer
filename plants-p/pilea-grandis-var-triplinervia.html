Pilea grandis var. triplinervia

Pilea grandis var. triplinervia, commonly known as the triple-veined pilea, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, heart-shaped leaves with a prominent central vein and three secondary veins, giving it its distinctive triplinervia name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its ornamental foliage and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
