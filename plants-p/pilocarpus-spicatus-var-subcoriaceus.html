Pilocarpus spicatus var. subcoriaceus

Pilocarpus spicatus var. subcoriaceus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by black, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary growth forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
