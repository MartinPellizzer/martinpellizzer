Pulmonaria confusa

Pulmonaria confusa, commonly known as the confused lungwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It features striking, hairy leaves with a mottled pattern of green, white, and purple, which are characteristic of the Pulmonaria genus. The plant produces upright, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Pulmonaria confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
