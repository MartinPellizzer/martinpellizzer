Panicum longepetiolatum

Panicum longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled panic grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and open, fluffy panicles that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in full sun and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. It is a perennial grass that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with a spreading growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to landscapes, though it is often considered invasive in certain areas.
