Polypodium abitaguae

Polypodium abitaguae, commonly known as the Abitagua huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Polypodium abitaguae is often found in tropical rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
