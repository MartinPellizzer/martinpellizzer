Protea cristata

Protea cristata, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a crown, composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, often covered in a fine, silvery coating.

Protea cristata plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
