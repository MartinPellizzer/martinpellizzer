Panicum exaratum

Panicum exaratum, commonly known as eastern gamagrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often pink or reddish in color. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
