Penstemon patricus

Penstemon patricus, commonly known as the tall beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically reaches heights of 2 to 6 feet and produces showy bracts that resemble petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon patricus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
