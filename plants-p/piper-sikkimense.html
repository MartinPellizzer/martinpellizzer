Piper sikkimense

Piper sikkimense, commonly known as the Sikkim pepper or Himalayan black pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in spikes. The fruits are initially green and mature to a dark purple or black color, containing small, white seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes, often found in dense forests.
