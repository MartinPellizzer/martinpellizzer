Papaver saichanense

Papaver saichanense, commonly known as the Saichan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals and a distinctive, rounded capsule fruit. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers bloom in the spring, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
