Philodendron cuspidatum

Philodendron cuspidatum, commonly known as the pointed philodendron, is a climbing tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a smooth texture and a pointed tip, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
