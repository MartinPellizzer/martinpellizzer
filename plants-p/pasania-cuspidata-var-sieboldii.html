Pasania cuspidata var. sieboldii

Pasania cuspidata var. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese spurge or Siebold's spurge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Pasania. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
