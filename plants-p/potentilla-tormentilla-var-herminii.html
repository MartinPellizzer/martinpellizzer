Potentilla tormentilla var. herminii

Potentilla tormentilla var. herminii, commonly known as the Herminii cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves composed of five to seven oval-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Its hardy nature makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover in dry, sunny environments.
