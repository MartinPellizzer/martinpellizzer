Paeonia mascula subsp. kayae

Paeonia mascula subsp.

kayae, commonly known as the Kayak peony, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring.

The plant features robust, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in the hybridization of many cultivated peonies.

The subspecies is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its genetic contributions to horticultural breeding programs.
