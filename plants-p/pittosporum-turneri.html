Pittosporum turneri

Pittosporum turneri, commonly known as the silver mistflower, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Pittosporum turneri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
