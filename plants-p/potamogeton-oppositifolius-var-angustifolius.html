Potamogeton oppositifolius var. angustifolius

Potamogeton oppositifolius var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, ribbon-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, often emerging from the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality in its habitat.
