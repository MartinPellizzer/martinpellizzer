Polycardia aquifolium f. ilicifolia

Polycardia aquifolium f. ilicifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaf holly, is a variant of the holly plant characterized by its dense, dark green foliage.

It belongs to the family Aquifoliaceae and is native to regions with temperate climates. The leaves are typically glossy, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value. This plant produces small, red berries that are often used in landscaping for their visual appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and hedges.
