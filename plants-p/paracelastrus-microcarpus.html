Paracelastrus microcarpus

Paracelastrus microcarpus, commonly known as the small-fruited celastroid, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
