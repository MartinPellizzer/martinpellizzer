Primula obconica

Primula obconica, commonly known as the broad-leaved primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, waxy leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and early spring blooms.

Primula obconica is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
