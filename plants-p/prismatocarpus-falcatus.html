Prismatocarpus falcatus

Prismatocarpus falcatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by woody, finger-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisine and crafts due to their aromatic properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appearance and practical uses in local cultures.
