Polygonum gloriosum

Polygonum gloriosum, commonly known as the Chinese evergeen or red-rooted knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Polygonum gloriosum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
