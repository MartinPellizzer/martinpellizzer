Phalaris canariensis subsp. typica

Phalaris canariensis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the Canary grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
