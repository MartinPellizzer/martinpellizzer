Poa caesia f. macra

Poa caesia f. macra, commonly known as the giant bluegrass, is a variant of the bluegrass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
