Polycarena rariflora

Polycarena rariflora, commonly known as the rare flax plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stems. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.

Botanists study Polycarena rariflora to better understand its ecological role and conservation needs.
