Prunella vulgaris var. scaberrima

Prunella vulgaris var. scaberrima, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
