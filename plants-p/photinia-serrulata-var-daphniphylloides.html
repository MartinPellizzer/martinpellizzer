Photinia serrulata var. daphniphylloides

Photinia serrulata var. daphniphylloides, commonly known as the Daphniphylloides photinia, is a variant of the photinia plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green elliptical leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by red or orange berries. It is typically grown as an ornamental shrub or small tree due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

This variety is valued for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
