Pilocarpus pauciflorus

Pilocarpus pauciflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
