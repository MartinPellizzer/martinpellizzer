Pogostemon griffithii var. latifolius

Pogostemon griffithii var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf pogostemon, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
