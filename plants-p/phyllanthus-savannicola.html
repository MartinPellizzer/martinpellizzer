Phyllanthus savannicola

Phyllanthus savannicola, commonly known as the savanna phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and sandy soils. The plant features small, clustered leaves with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and appear in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its adaptability to harsh environments and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
