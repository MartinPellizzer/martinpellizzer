Polycnemum erinaceum

Polycnemum erinaceum, commonly known as the hairy basket sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

The species is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in wetland habitats.
