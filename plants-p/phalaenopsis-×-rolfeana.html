Phalaenopsis × rolfeana

Phalaenopsis × rolfeana, commonly known as the orchid hybrid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Phalaenopsis amabilis and Phalaenopsis bellina.

It is an epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its long, pendant flower spikes that bear multiple blooms. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a velvety appearance and a sweet fragrance.

This orchid is popular as a decorative plant due to its longevity and ease of care.
