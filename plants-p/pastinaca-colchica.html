Pastinaca colchica

Pastinaca colchica, commonly known as the Crimean bur marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Russia.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and palmate leaves, which are typically lobed and hairy. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its ornamental value.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
