Paracaryum rugulosum var. platycalyx

Paracaryum rugulosum var. platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx paracaryum, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and distinctive, broad calyx that resembles a cup or shield. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a rough or hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, often with a unique coloration that aids in attracting specific pollinators.
