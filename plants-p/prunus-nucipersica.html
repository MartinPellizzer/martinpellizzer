Prunus nucipersica

Prunus nucipersica, commonly known as the Persian plum, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.

The plant is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it adaptable to different climates.
