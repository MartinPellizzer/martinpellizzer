Pleopeltis rhomboidea

Pleopeltis rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid pleopeltis, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive rhomboid-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its roots to anchor itself while absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually lanceolate to ovate, with a leathery texture and a dark green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
