Pyrus elaeagrifolia subsp. kotschyana

Pyrus elaeagrifolia subsp.

kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's pear, is a shrub or small tree native to the mountains of Central Asia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both its flowers and fruits.
