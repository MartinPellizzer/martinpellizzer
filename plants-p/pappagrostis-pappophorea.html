Pappagrostis pappophorea

Pappagrostis pappophorea, commonly known as the feathergrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Pappagrostis pappophorea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
