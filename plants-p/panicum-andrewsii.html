Panicum andrewsii

Panicum andrewsii, commonly known as inland sea oats, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like blades that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive silver-gray sheen. The plant produces fluffy, oat-like seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton or dandelion fluff. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
