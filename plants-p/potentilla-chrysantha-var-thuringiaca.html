Potentilla chrysantha var. thuringiaca

Potentilla chrysantha var. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It has compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, five-petaled flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
