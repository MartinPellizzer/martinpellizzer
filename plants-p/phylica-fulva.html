Phylica fulva

Phylica fulva, commonly known as the yellow kaross, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

Phylica fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
