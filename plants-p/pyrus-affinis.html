Pyrus affinis

Pyrus affinis, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree has rough, fissured bark and produces small, edible berries that mature in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Pyrus affinis is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
