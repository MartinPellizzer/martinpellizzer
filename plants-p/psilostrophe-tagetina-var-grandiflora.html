Psilostrophe tagetina var. grandiflora

Psilostrophe tagetina var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
