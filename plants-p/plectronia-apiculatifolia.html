Plectronia apiculatifolia

Plectronia apiculatifolia, commonly known as the featherleaf plectronia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
