Piper misionesanum

Piper misionesanum, commonly known as the Misiones pepper, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically the subtropical regions of Argentina and Uruguay.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants like the common black pepper. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn red when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its use in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are dark green and have a slightly aromatic scent, contributing to its ecological and horticultural significance in its native habitat.
