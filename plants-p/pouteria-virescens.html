Pouteria virescens

Pouteria virescens, commonly known as the green spondias, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible fruits. The fruit has a hard, spiny rind and a sweet, juicy pulp, making it a valuable food source in its native habitat. The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
