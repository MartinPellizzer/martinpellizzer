Pichisermollia erythrocarpa

Pichisermollia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited pichisermollia, is a unique member of the Ericaceae family, native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with dark green, leathery leaves that are densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces striking red berries that remain on the plant throughout the winter, adding visual interest to its habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate to flourish.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
