Physalis virginiana var. longiseta

Physalis virginiana var. longiseta, commonly known as the wild tomato or the Chinese lantern plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It produces striking orange-red, inflated calyces that enclose its small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it blooms with yellowish-green flowers in the summer.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications, though it is less common than its domesticated relatives.
