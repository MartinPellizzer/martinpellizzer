Potentilla grandiflora

Potentilla grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on long, slender stalks. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are often accompanied by numerous stamens, giving them a fluffy appearance.

Potentilla grandiflora thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.
