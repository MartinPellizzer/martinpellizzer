Polygala nicaeensis subsp. versicolor

Polygala nicaeensis subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its variable flower colors and leaf shapes, which can change depending on environmental conditions. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as scrublands and grasslands.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
