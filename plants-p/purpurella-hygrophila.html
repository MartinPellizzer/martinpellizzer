Purpurella hygrophila

Purpurella hygrophila, commonly known as the purple-flowered water plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It features slender, upright stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its preference for wet conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
