Pseudotsuga guinieri var. mediostrobus

Pseudotsuga guinieri var. mediostrobus, commonly known as the Mexican fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the high-altitude regions of central Mexico.

It is characterized by its conical shape, needle-like leaves, and distinctive cone structure. The species belongs to the family Pinaceae and is closely related to other fir species such as the Douglas fir. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and limited distribution, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
