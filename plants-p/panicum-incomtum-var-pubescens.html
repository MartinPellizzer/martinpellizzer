Panicum incomtum var. pubescens

Panicum incomtum var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
