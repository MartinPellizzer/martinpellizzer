Pinus jeffreyi var. deflexa

Pinus jeffreyi var. deflexa, commonly known as the Jeffrey pine, is a variant of the Jeffrey pine native to the western United States.

It is a tall, coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its long, needle-like leaves and distinctive cone structures. The foliage is typically gray-green, and the bark becomes thick and furrowed with age. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ecological role and is also used in reforestation efforts.
