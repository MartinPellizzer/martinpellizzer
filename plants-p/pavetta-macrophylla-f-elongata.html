Pavetta macrophylla f. elongata

Pavetta macrophylla f. elongata, commonly known as the elongated pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers with a distinct, fragrant scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
