Polygala empetrifolia

Polygala empetrifolia, commonly known as the rock polygala, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, intricate flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in dense clusters. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
