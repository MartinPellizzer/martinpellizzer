Poa angustifolia f. pyramidalis

Poa angustifolia f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving the plant a pyramidal shape, as suggested by its cultivar name. It typically grows in open, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
