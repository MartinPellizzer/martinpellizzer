Pandanus duriocarpoides

Pandanus duriocarpoides, commonly known as the thick-shelled pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit, a woody capsule, contains several seeds and is an important resource for local communities.
