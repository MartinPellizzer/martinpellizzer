Pilosella × rubra

Pilosella × rubra, commonly known as the red mountain everlasting, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Pilosella × rubra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting flowers.
