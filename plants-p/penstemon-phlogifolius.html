Penstemon phlogifolius

Penstemon phlogifolius, commonly known as the fireleaf beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety, red-tinged leaves and spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its foliage and flowers provide nectar for bees and other pollinators, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in arid landscapes.
