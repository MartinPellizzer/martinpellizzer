Polygonum reticulatum

Polygonum reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by distinctive leaf shapes and often achenes as its fruit type. The plant typically features slender, upright stems covered with a waxy layer, and its leaves are usually lance-shaped with prominent net-like veins. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it can become a problematic weed in certain regions.
