Phlox nivalis f. roseiflora

Phlox nivalis f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered mountain phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, rose-like flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
