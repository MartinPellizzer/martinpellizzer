Polygonum jejunum

Polygonum jejunum, commonly known as redroot smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wetlands, and disturbed areas. Its stems are often reddish in color, and it produces small, three-lobed leaves.

Polygonum jejunum is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
