Piper rothschuhii

Piper rothschuhii, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or the rothschuhii pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they contain a mild to spicy flavor. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
