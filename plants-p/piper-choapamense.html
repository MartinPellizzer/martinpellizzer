Piper choapamense

Piper choapamense, commonly known as the Choapa pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the humid lowland forests of Central America, particularly in northern Guatemala and southern Belize. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing vines and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, elongated berry that turns from green to yellow when mature.

Piper choapamense is valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
