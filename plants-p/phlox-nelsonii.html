Phlox nelsonii

Phlox nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
