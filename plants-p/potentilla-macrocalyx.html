Potentilla macrocalyx

Potentilla macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla macrocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
