Plinia caricensis

Plinia caricensis, commonly known as the Caribbean white sapote, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and creamy pulp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
