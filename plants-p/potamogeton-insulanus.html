Potamogeton insulanus

Potamogeton insulanus, commonly known as the island pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia and Europe.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

Potamogeton insulanus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.
