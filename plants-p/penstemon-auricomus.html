Penstemon auricomus

Penstemon auricomus, commonly known as the golden trumpet.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States, characterized by its showy, tubular yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract bees, butterflies, and other pollinators, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
