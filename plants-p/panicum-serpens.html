Panicum serpens

Panicum serpens, commonly known as snake grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Panicum serpens is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
