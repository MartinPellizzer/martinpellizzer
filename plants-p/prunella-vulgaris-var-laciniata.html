Prunella vulgaris var. laciniata

Prunella vulgaris var. laciniata, commonly known as the cut-leaved selfheal, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed or lobed margins. The plant produces spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
