Palimbia rediviva

Palimbia rediviva, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of liverwort that exhibits remarkable desiccation tolerance.

It belongs to the family Marchantiaceae and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant can survive extreme drought conditions by entering a state of metabolic dormancy, appearing completely dry and dead. When exposed to moisture, it rapidly rehydrates and regains its green, leafy appearance.

This adaptive trait has earned it the nickname "resurrection plant" due to its ability to "come back to life.".
