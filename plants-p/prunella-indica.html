Prunella indica

Prunella indica, commonly known as Indian deadnettle, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, pink to purple flowers in whorled clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Prunella indica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various ailments.
