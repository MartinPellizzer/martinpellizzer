Pleurothallis tripterocarpa

Pleurothallis tripterocarpa, commonly known as the three-winged orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, small flowers in shades of white and pink. Its blooming period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
