Polygala ligustroides

Polygala ligustroides, commonly known as the slender milk-vetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often branched and bear small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
