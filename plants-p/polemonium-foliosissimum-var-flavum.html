Polemonium foliosissimum var. flavum

Polemonium foliosissimum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellowleaf Jacob's ladder, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and arranged in a cascading pattern, giving the plant its common name. The flowers, which range in color from white to yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
