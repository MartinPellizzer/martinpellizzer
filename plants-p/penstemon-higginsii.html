Penstemon higginsii

Penstemon higginsii, commonly known as the Higgins' beardtongue, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Penstemon higginsii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
