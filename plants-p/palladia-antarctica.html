Palladia antarctica

Palladia antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic pallidia, is a rare flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the brief summer months. The plant has evolved to withstand harsh conditions, including freezing temperatures and strong winds. Its leaves are typically covered in a thick layer of frost-resistant wax.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a unique example of botanical adaptation in one of Earth's most extreme climates.
