Pleioneura anomala

Pleioneura anomala, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flower that resembles a ghostly figure, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded forest environments. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its elusive nature and habitat requirements, Pleioneura anomala is considered a challenging plant to cultivate and conserve.
