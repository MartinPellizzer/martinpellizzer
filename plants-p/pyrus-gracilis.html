Pyrus gracilis

Pyrus gracilis, commonly known as the slender pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. Pyrus gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
