Potentilla insignis

Potentilla insignis, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Potentilla insignis is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
