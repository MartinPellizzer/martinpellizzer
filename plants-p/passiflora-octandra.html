Passiflora octandra

Passiflora octandra, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and white flowers, which feature a unique structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and often have a reddish hue.

Passiflora octandra is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
