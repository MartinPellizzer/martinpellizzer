Podocarpus × loderi

Podocarpus × loderi, commonly known as the hybrid hina or loderi hinakau, is a hybrid coniferous evergreen tree resulting from the cross between Podocarpus macrophyllus and Podocarpus neriifolius.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and a conical or pyramidal shape when mature. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its leaves are leathery and aromatic. It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Podocarpus × loderi is valued in landscaping for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
