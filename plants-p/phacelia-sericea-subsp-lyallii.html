Phacelia sericea subsp. lyallii

Phacelia sericea subsp.

lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with upright stems and is often found in areas with well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a subject of ecological study.
