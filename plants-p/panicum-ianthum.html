Panicum ianthum

Panicum ianthum, commonly known as purple panicgrass, is a herbaceous perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted clusters of slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, two-lobed flowers that are typically purple to reddish in color, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Panicum ianthum thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens and erosion control.
