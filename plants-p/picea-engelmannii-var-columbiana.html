Picea engelmannii var. columbiana

Picea engelmannii var. columbiana, commonly known as the Colorado blue spruce, is a variant of the Engelmann spruce native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray needle color and conical shape, which makes it a popular ornamental tree. The tree has short, stout needles that are often covered in a waxy bloom, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cold, high-altitude environments and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is also valued for its ecological role in providing habitat for various wildlife species.
