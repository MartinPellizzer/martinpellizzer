Pelargonium serratum

Pelargonium serratum, commonly known as the serrated geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, with upright stems and clusters of five-petaled blooms in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of care contribute to its widespread cultivation.
