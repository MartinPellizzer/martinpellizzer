Pyrus sanguisorbifolia

Pyrus sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the bloodleaf pear, is a rare and ornamental species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features deep red to purple leaves that change color with the seasons. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common pears. Its unique leaf coloration makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized collections.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
