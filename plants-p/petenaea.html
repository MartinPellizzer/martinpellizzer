Petenaea

Petenaea, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the eastern United States and includes several species, such as Petenaea stipulata and Petenaea parviflora. These plants are characterized by their showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. They typically grow in moist, wooded areas and have lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

Petenaea species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
