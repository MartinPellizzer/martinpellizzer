Polygala subaphylla

Polygala subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid regions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
