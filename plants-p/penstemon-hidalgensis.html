Penstemon hidalgensis

Penstemon hidalgensis, commonly known as the Hidalgo penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Penstemon hidalgensis is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
