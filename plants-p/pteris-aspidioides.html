Pteris aspidioides

Pteris aspidioides, commonly known as the broad-leaf water fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and thrives in humid climates with well-draining soil. Its reproductive structures, including sori and sporangia, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Pteris aspidioides is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
