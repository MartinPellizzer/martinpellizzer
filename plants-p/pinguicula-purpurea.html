Pinguicula purpurea

Pinguicula purpurea, commonly known as the purple butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, purple-tinged leaves that secrete a sticky substance to trap small insects. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor environments such as bogs and rocky soils.

Pinguicula purpurea is a popular choice for carnivorous plant enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and easy care requirements.
