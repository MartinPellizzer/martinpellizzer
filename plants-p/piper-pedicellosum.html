Piper pedicellosum

Piper pedicellosum, commonly known as the small-fruited pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
