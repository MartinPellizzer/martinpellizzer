Polygonum viviparum var. ramiflorum

Polygonum viviparum var. ramiflorum, commonly known as the ramiflorum knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and nodding flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, making it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive.
