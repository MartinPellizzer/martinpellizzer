Phlomis × praetervisa

Phlomis × praetervisa, commonly known as the hybrid garden phlomis, is a perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its robust growth and striking, often purple or pink, tubular flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features gray-green, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
