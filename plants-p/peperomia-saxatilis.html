Peperomia saxatilis

Peperomia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink, making it visually appealing. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact size and ornamental leaves, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
