Placodiscus cuneatus

Placodiscus cuneatus, commonly known as the African orchid or the sand orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. The plant has a compact growth habit with linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Placodiscus cuneatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
