Pleurothallis cordata

Pleurothallis cordata, commonly known as the cordate pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of tropical forests. It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
