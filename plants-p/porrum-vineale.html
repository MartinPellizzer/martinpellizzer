Porrum vineale

Porrum vineale, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red berries. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing structures.
