Petromarula pinnata var. pubescens

Petromarula pinnata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy petromarula, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with a pubescent surface, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom during the rainy season. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and high temperatures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
