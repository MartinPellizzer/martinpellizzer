Psidium terminale

Psidium terminale, commonly known as the Brazilian guava or the purple guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically purple or red when mature, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The bark and leaves have also been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
