Pinus clusiana var. corsicana

Pinus clusiana var. corsicana, commonly known as Corsican pine, is a variant of the Corsican pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant exhibits a tall, slender growth habit with a straight trunk and a conical crown. Its botanical features include dark green, closely packed needles and small, woody cones that mature to release seeds.

This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in mountainous areas of Corsica and Sardinia.
