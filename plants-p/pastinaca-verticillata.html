Pastinaca verticillata

Pastinaca verticillata, commonly known as the clustered burdock, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, taproot system and thick, rough-textured stems that often branch at the base. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spiny, lobed seed pods. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its medicinal and historical uses.
