Pothos lorispathus

Pothos lorispathus, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a hardy, evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a variegated pattern of gold or cream. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal maintenance. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage.

Pothos lorispathus is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
