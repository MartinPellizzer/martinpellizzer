Potentilla strictissima f. hirsuta

Potentilla strictissima f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
