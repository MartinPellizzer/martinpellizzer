Paludana crispa

Paludana crispa, commonly known as the slender marsh fern, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver or white, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in shallow water or along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of ferns.

Paludana crispa plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
