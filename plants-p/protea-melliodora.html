Protea melliodora

Protea melliodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous papilionaceous flowers, which are typically pink to red in color. The plant produces a fragrant, nectar-rich flower head that attracts a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
