Parinari polyandra var. cinerea

Parinari polyandra var. cinerea, commonly known as the ash-leaf parinari, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and compound leaves that are typically alternate and pinnate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
