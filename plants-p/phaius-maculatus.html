Phaius maculatus

Phaius maculatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature a greenish-yellow lip with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests and marshes. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming dense clumps.

Phaius maculatus is a perennial species that thrives in high humidity and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
