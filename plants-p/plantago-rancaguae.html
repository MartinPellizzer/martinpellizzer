Plantago rancaguae

Plantago rancaguae, commonly known as the Rancagua plantain, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
