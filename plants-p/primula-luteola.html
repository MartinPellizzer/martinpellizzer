Primula luteola

Primula luteola, commonly known as the yellow primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal leaves that are oval to heart-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for woodland gardens and shady borders.
