Polygonum caballeroi

Polygonum caballeroi, commonly known as the Mexican knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and hollow stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint, while its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters.

Polygonum caballeroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
