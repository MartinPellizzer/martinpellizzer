Polygonum pratincola

Polygonum pratincola, commonly known as the common knotgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks. Its leaves are often arranged in a zigzag pattern, and it produces capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Polygonum pratincola is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
