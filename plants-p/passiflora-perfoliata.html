Passiflora perfoliata

Passiflora perfoliata, commonly known as the perfoliate passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The leaves are heart-shaped with a unique perfoliate base, through which the stem passes, giving the plant its common name. It produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
