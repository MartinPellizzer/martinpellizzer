Potentilla caulescens clusiana

Potentilla caulescens clusiana, commonly known as the Clusian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in moist, temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets, and it produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers. It is often found in forest understories and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
