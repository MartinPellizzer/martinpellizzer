Phaseolus linearis f. typicus

Phaseolus linearis f. typicus, commonly known as the slender bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow pods and small, kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is typically an annual legume that thrives in warm climates. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often grown for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
