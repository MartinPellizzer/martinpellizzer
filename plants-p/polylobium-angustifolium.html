Polylobium angustifolium

Polylobium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved polylobium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale green, appearing in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and subtle floral display.
