Poa simensis

Poa simensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, often in rocky or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and spike-like, with a blue or purple hue, giving it its common name.

Poa simensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
