Phyllanthus virgatus var. chinensis

Phyllanthus virgatus var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and fruits are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
