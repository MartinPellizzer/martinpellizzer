Potentilla auripetala

Potentilla auripetala, commonly known as the golden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its basal leaves that are palmately divided. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is known for its hardy nature, often thriving in harsh environmental conditions.

The golden cinquefoil is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some cultures.
