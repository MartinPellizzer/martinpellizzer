Phlox pilosa var. walteri

Phlox pilosa var. walteri, commonly known as Walter's fuzzy phlox, is a perennial flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy foliage and showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shaded areas. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds.
