Pandanus amicalis

Pandanus amicalis, commonly known as the bottlebrush pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense, feather-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in sandy or muddy soils.

Pandanus amicalis is often used in traditional crafts and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
