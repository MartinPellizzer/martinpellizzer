Pavia octandra

Pavia octandra, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It is valued for its hard, dense wood used in traditional crafts and musical instruments.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in some indigenous cultures.
