Poa subnuda

Poa subnuda, commonly known as the naked poa, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden among the foliage.

Poa subnuda is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.
