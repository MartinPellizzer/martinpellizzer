Potentilla × mackayana

Potentilla × mackayana, commonly known as the Mackay's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a silvery-blue hue, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Potentilla × mackayana is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
