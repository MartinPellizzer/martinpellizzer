Polycardia oblanceolata

Polycardia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features a rhizomatous root system that produces edible tubers, which are rich in starch. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands and lowland areas.
