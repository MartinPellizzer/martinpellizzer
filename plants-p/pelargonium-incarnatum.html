Pelargonium incarnatum

Pelargonium incarnatum, commonly known as the red-stemmed geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
