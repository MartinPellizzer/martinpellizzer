Populus × acuminata var. rehderi

Populus × acuminata var. rehderi, commonly known as the Chinese poplar, is a hybrid species native to China.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fast growth rate.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, including soil stabilization and providing habitat for wildlife.
