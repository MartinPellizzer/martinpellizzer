Paspalum urvillei

Paspalum urvillei, commonly known as the urville paspalum, is a species of grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in moist or wetland habitats, thriving in areas with high humidity.

Paspalum urvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
