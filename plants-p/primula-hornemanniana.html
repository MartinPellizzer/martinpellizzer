Primula hornemanniana

Primula hornemanniana, commonly known as the Hornemann's primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
