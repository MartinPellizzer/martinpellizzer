Phlox pilosa subsp. riparia

Phlox pilosa subsp.

riparia, commonly known as the riparian phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and stream sides.

The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
