Portulaca flava

Portulaca flava, commonly known as yellow portulaca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that grows low to the ground, with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its succulent nature allows it to store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions.
