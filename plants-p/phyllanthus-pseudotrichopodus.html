Phyllanthus pseudotrichopodus

Phyllanthus pseudotrichopodus, commonly known as the Indian cheesewood, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by small, round fruits. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health benefits.
