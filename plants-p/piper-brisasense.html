Piper brisasense

Piper brisasense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly rough texture and a strong, spicy aroma. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but have a peppery taste.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
