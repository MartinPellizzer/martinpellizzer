Pleiotaxis eximia

Pleiotaxis eximia, commonly known as the desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and striking clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant has succulent leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological role in its habitat.

Pleiotaxis eximia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes.
