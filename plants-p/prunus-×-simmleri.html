Prunus × simmleri

Prunus × simmleri, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink or white flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated margin. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Prunus × simmleri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.
