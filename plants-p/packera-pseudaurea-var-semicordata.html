Packera pseudaurea var. semicordata

Packera pseudaurea var. semicordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are elongated and often have a distinctive wavy margin.
