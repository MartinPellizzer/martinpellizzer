Polygonum labordei

Polygonum labordei, commonly known as the laborde's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to southern China.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and knotweeds. The plant is characterized by its erect, slender stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive reddish or purple hue.

Polygonum labordei is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
