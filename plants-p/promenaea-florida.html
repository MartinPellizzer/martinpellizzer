Promenaea florida

Promenaea florida, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs during the warm seasons, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Promenaea florida is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to add color to garden landscapes.
