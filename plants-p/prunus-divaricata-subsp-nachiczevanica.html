Prunus divaricata subsp. nachiczevanica

Prunus divaricata subsp.

nachiczevanica, commonly known as the Armenian cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

The fruit is a drupe, typically red or yellow when mature, and is edible. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
