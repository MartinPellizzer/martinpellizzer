Psidium ciliatum

Psidium ciliatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or carambola, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, elliptical leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by yellow-orange fruits resembling a star shape.

The fruit has a sweet, acidic flavor and is rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
