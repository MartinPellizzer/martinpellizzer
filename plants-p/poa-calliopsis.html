Poa calliopsis

Poa calliopsis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and preference for cooler, temperate climates.
