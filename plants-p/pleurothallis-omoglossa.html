Pleurothallis omoglossa

Pleurothallis omoglossa, commonly known as the "Clove Orchid," is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in the understory of rainforests. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and are noted for their subtle, clove-like aroma.

Pleurothallis omoglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
