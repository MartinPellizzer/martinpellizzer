Pinus montezumae var. lindleyana

Pinus montezumae var. lindleyana, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a variant of the Mexican pine native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in montane forests.

This variety is notable for its longevity and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
