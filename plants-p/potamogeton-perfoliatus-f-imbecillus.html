Potamogeton perfoliatus f. imbecillus

Potamogeton perfoliatus f. imbecillus, commonly known as the small-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are often less than 1 cm in length, with a distinct perfoliate arrangement where the leaves appear to pass through the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters.

Its growth habit and morphology make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
