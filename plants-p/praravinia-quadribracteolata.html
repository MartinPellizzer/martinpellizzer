Praravinia quadribracteolata

Praravinia quadribracteolata, commonly known as the four-bract vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces four bracts beneath each flower cluster, a distinctive feature that gives it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Praravinia quadribracteolata plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
