Pithecellobium crateradenum

Pithecellobium crateradenum, commonly known as the cratered pita, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean, where it typically grows in moist, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

The plant also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
