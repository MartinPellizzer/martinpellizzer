Piper tomentosum

Piper tomentosum, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their pungent flavor.

Piper tomentosum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests.
