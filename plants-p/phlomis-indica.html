Phlomis indica

Phlomis indica, commonly known as Indian torchflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
