Phyteuma ovatum

Phyteuma ovatum, commonly known as the purple loosestrife, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, spires of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, marshes, and riverbanks.

Phytoemum ovatum is often found in full sun and is known for its ability to spread rapidly in suitable habitats.
