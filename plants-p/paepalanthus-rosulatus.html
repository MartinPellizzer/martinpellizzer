Paepalanthus rosulatus

Paepalanthus rosulatus, commonly known as the Rosulate Puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in summer, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
