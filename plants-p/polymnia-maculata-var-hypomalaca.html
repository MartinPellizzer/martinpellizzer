Polymnia maculata var. hypomalaca

Polymnia maculata var. hypomalaca, commonly known as the spotted polymnia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple spots, which are a key botanical characteristic. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.
