Parsonsia parsonsia

Parsonsia parsonsia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne on long, slender peduncles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
