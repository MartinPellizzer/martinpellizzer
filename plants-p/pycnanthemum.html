Pycnanthemum

Pycnanthemum, commonly known as mountain mint, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

These perennial herbs are native to North America and are characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy blue to purple flowers. The plants typically grow in moist, wooded areas and are often found in the eastern and central United States. They have square stems and opposite leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs.

Pycnanthemum species are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
