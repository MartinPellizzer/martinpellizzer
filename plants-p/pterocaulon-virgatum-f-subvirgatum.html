Pterocaulon virgatum f. subvirgatum

Pterocaulon virgatum f. subvirgatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pterocaulon, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and reducing transpiration. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative succulent in arid or semi-arid climates.
