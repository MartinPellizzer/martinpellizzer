Phegopteris dryopteris f. interrupta

Phegopteris dryopteris f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted shield fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive frond morphology.

It features a bipinnate structure with a unique interruption in the pinnae, giving it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically leathery and have a dark green color with a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

This fern is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
