Petasites × alpestris

Petasites × alpestris, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by achenes that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
