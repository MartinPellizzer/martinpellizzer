Polygala gazensis

Polygala gazensis, commonly known as the Gaza milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, while its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds.

Polygala gazensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
