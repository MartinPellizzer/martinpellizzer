Potentilla goldbachii humilior

Potentilla goldbachii humilior, commonly known as the low goldbach's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, adding a vibrant touch to rocky or alpine landscapes. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
