Populus sargentii var. texana

Populus sargentii var. texana, commonly known as the Texas poplar, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green bark. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones. It produces small, catkin-like flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

The Texas poplar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
