Polygonum popovii

Polygonum popovii, commonly known as the Popov's knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, velvety hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late summer.

Polygonum popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
