Polygala speciosa

Polygala speciosa, commonly known as the showy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

The flowers are arranged in clusters and are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
