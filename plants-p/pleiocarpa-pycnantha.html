Pleiocarpa pycnantha

Pleiocarpa pycnantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
