Primula pintchouanensis

Primula pintchouanensis, commonly known as the Pintchouan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Primula pintchouanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
