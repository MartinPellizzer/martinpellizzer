Pavetta capensis subsp. capensis

Pavetta capensis subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape pavetta, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the fynbos biome.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a pleasant, slightly aromatic scent. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
