Polygala ramaswamiana

Polygala ramaswamiana, commonly known as the Ramaswamiana screwbean, is a flowering plant native to India.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky areas and is adapted to arid conditions. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a distinctive, spiral-shaped corolla.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
