Pinus luchuensis

Pinus luchuensis, commonly known as the Luchu Island pine, is a species native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and is well adapted to the humid subtropical climate of its native habitat. The tree has long, slender needles that are arranged in bundles of five, and it produces small, woody cones. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against environmental stressors.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
