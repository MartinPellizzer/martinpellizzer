Potamogeton pectinatus f. notabilis

Potamogeton pectinatus f. notabilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments across Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spikelets, and it is often found in association with other aquatic vegetation.
