Perezia pectinata

Perezia pectinata, commonly known as the pectinate perezia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pinnately compound leaves that resemble a comb, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native habitat.
