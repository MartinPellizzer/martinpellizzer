Poa paryadrica

Poa paryadrica, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to cold and high-altitude environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, Poa paryadrica is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
