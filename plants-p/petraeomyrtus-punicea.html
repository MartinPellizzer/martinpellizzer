Petraeomyrtus punicea

Petraeomyrtus punicea, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in winter. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense thickets. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it is well adapted to coastal conditions.

Petraeomyrtus punicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
