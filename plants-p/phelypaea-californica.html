Phelypaea californica

Phelypaea californica, commonly known as the California buttonbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of white, button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and opposite, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and wetland ecosystems.
