Prunus jamasakura f. prima

Prunus jamasakura f. prima, commonly known as the early-flowering cherry, is a variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter or early spring. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink flowers that appear before the leaves, creating a striking visual display. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal beauty.
