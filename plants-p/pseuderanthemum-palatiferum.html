Pseuderanthemum palatiferum

Pseuderanthemum palatiferum, commonly known as the Brazilian violet or false pomegranate, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds, aiding in wind dispersal.

Pseuderanthemum palatiferum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
