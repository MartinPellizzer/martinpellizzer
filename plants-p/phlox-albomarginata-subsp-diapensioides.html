Phlox albomarginata subsp. diapensioides

Phlox albomarginata subsp.

diapensioides, commonly known as white-margin phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer.

The flowers are typically pink to lavender in color with white margins, and the plant has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
