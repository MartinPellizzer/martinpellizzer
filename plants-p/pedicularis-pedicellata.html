Pedicularis pedicellata

Pedicularis pedicellata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers with spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is often found in open meadows and forest clearings.

The spurred gentian is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
