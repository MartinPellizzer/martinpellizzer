Pandanus novohibernicus var. novohibernicus

Pandanus novohibernicus var. novohibernicus, commonly known as the New Ireland pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces inflorescences that bear small, fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some island communities.
