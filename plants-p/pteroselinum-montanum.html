Pteroselinum montanum

Pteroselinum montanum, commonly known as mountain parsley, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small white flowers are arranged in umbels, a characteristic common in the Apiaceae family.

Pteroselinum montanum is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
