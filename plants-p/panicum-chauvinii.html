Panicum chauvinii

Panicum chauvinii, commonly known as Chauvin's panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and typically have a bluish-green color, with a rough texture.

Panicum chauvinii is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
