Pyrrhosa

Pyrrhosa, commonly known as the pink-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant typically features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid environments with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
