Potentilla argentea f. serrata

Potentilla argentea f. serrata, commonly known as silver cinquefoil.

It is a flowering plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its silver-white underside on the leaves and serrated leaf margins. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
