Peperomia semisupina

Peperomia semisupina, commonly known as the creeping peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving them a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of green or red, which are typically borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and attractive foliage make it an ideal plant for container cultivation.
