Platanthera neottianthoides

Platanthera neottianthoides, commonly known as the Neottianthoid Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with tall, erect stems and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. It has a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

The orchid is also known for its fragrant, honey-scented blooms that attract various pollinators.
