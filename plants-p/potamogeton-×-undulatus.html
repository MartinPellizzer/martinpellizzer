Potamogeton × undulatus

Potamogeton × undulatus, commonly known as the wavy pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that occurs in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like structures. It is often found in shallow lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

Potamogeton × undulatus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
