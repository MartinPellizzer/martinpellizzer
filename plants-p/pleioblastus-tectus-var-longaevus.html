Pleioblastus tectus var. longaevus

Pleioblastus tectus var. longaevus, commonly known as the long-lived bamboo, is a variant of the pleioblastus tectus species.

It is a clump-forming grass that grows in dense, upright shoots, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green color. It is native to East Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

This bamboo is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
