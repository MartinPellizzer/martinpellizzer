Pandanus viridinsularis

Pandanus viridinsularis, commonly known as the green island pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in a spadix, followed by the development of fibrous, palm-like fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in wetlands or near mangroves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
