Piper neglectum

Piper neglectum, commonly known as the Indian pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
