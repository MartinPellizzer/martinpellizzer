Potentilla osterhoutii var. osterhoutii

Potentilla osterhoutii var. osterhoutii, commonly known as the Osterhout's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in dry, sunny habitats. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are elliptic to ovate in shape.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
