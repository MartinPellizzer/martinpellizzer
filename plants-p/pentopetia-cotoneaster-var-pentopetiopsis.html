Pentopetia cotoneaster var. pentopetiopsis

Pentopetia cotoneaster var. pentopetiopsis, commonly known as the purpleleaf cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which turns red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a hedge.
