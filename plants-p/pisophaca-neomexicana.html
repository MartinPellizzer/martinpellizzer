Pisophaca neomexicana

Pisophaca neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the milkweed genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Pisophaca neomexicana plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
