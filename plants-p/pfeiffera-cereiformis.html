Pfeiffera cereiformis

Pfeiffera cereiformis, commonly known as the cotton tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, soft, white, woolly leaves that resemble cotton, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Pfeiffera cereiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
