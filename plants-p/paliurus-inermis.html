Paliurus inermis

Paliurus inermis, commonly known as the white thorn or white acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers in summer. The plant is characterized by its thorny stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.

Paliurus inermis is also adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
