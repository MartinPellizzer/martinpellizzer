Parsonsia velutina

Parsonsia velutina, commonly known as the velvet flame or velvet bell, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, orange berries that are edible but have a bitter taste. Parsonsia velutina thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
