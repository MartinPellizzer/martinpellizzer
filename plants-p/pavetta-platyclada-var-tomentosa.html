Pavetta platyclada var. tomentosa

Pavetta platyclada var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, or tomentose, stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
