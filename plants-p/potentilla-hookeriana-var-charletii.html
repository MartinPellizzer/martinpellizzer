Potentilla hookeriana var. charletii

Potentilla hookeriana var. charletii, commonly known as the Charlet's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
