Pappophorum saccharoides

Pappophorum saccharoides, commonly known as sugargrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend and root at the nodes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in silky, hair-like structures called pappi. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along creek banks.

Due to its high sugar content, it has been historically used for forage and honey production.
