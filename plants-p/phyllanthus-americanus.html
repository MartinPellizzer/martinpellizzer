Phyllanthus americanus

Phyllanthus americanus, commonly known as the American cheeseplant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a capsule containing tiny seeds.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
