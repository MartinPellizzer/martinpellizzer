Pterostylis basaltica

Pterostylis basaltica, commonly known as the basalt orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, often curved flower that is typically green or yellow-green in color. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils, often found in areas with high rainfall. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique and delicate floral structure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
