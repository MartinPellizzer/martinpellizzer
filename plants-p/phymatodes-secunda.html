Phymatodes secunda

Phymatodes secunda, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Phymatodes secunda is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils.
