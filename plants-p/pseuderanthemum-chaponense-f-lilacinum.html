Pseuderanthemum chaponense f. lilacinum

Pseuderanthemum chaponense f. lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac-flowered pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, lilac-pink bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often found in shaded, moist environments. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
