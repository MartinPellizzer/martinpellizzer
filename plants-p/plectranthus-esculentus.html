Plectranthus esculentus

Plectranthus esculentus, commonly known as African nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
