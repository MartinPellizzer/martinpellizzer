Polygala pellucida

Polygala pellucida, commonly known as the glassworts, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or white, and its succulent leaves that store water, aiding in survival in dry environments. The plant belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
