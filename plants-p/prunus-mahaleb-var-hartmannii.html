Prunus mahaleb var. hartmannii

Prunus mahaleb var. hartmannii, commonly known as the Hartmann's cherry, is a rare and ancient variant of the cherry species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by its distinctive, hairy fruit capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with sufficient sunlight.

Due to its rarity and historical significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
