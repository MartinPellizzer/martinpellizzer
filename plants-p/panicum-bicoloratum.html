Panicum bicoloratum

Panicum bicoloratum, commonly known as purpletop millet, is a warm-season grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple-tinted foliage and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Panicum bicoloratum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in disturbed areas.
