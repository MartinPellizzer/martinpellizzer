Prunus consociiflora

Prunus consociiflora, commonly known as the clustered cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, often clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale pink. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Prunus consociiflora is also valued for its edible fruits, which are small, tart cherries used in culinary applications.
