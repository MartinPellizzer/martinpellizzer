Ptarmica vulgaris var. cartilaginea

Ptarmica vulgaris var. cartilaginea, commonly known as the cartilage bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose racemes, and the plant is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

The variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
