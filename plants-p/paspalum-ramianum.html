Paspalum ramianum

Paspalum ramianum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Paspalum ramianum is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense turf.
