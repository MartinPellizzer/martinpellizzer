Podocarpus curvifolius

Podocarpus curvifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf podocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and conical shape. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. It produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
