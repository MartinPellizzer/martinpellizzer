Plinia moralesii

Plinia moralesii, commonly known as the Moralesii guava, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small, yellowish fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent.

The flowers are white and fragrant, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
