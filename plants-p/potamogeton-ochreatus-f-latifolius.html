Potamogeton ochreatus f. latifolius

Potamogeton ochreatus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. The leaves are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters, with a smooth, waxy surface. This plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediment and improve water quality.
