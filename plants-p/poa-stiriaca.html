Poa stiriaca

Poa stiriaca, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a hairy surface.

Poa stiriaca produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
