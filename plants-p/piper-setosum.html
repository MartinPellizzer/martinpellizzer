Piper setosum

Piper setosum, commonly known as the hairy pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its serrated, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used to make tea, which is believed to have various health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
