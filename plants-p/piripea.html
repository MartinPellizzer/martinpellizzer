Piripea

Piripea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, vibrant red blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Piripea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
