Piper laeteviride

Piper laeteviride, commonly known as the green-leafed pepper or sweet pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, green berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
