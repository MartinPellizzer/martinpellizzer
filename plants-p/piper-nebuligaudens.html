Piper nebuligaudens

Piper nebuligaudens, commonly known as the foggy beauty pepper, is a tropical vine native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red upon ripening. It thrives in warm, moist environments with partial shade, making it suitable for greenhouse cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
