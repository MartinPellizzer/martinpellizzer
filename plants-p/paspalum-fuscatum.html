Paspalum fuscatum

Paspalum fuscatum, commonly known as black paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and riparian zones. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Its seeds are dispersed by water and birds, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
