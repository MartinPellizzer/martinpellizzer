Phyllanthus humblotianus

Phyllanthus humblotianus, commonly known as the Humboldt's phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in axillary inflorescences, producing small, round fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.
