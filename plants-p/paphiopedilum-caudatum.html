Paphiopedilum caudatum

Paphiopedilum caudatum, commonly known as the tail orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a distinctive, elongated rhizome with a fleshy, often reddish root system. The plant produces showy, butterfly-shaped flowers with a unique, pouch-like lip that aids in pollination. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, forming a rosette around the base.

Paphiopedilum caudatum thrives in shaded, humid environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
