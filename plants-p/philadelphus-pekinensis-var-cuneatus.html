Philadelphus pekinensis var. cuneatus

Philadelphus pekinensis var. cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features compound leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
