Papaver nudicaule var. pyrenaicum

Papaver nudicaule var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a member of the Papaveraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
