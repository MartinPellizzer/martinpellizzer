Potentilla argentea subsp. decumbens

Potentilla argentea subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features silver-white undersides on its elliptical leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
