Phaeocephalum album

Phaeocephalum album, commonly known as the white-headed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in groups. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

This plant is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife.
