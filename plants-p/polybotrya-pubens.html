Polybotrya pubens

Polybotrya pubens, commonly known as the hairy polybotrya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polybotryaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are elongated and covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
