Prunus avium var. pyramidalis

Prunus avium var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the European cherry or the cherry tree, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its upright, pyramidal growth habit, which makes it suitable for small spaces and urban landscapes. The plant produces showy white flowers in early spring, followed by sweet, juicy fruits that ripen in late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice for both gardens and commercial orchards.
