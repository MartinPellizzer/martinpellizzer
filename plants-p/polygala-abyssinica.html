Polygala abyssinica

Polygala abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
