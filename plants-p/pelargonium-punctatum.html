Pelargonium punctatum

Pelargonium punctatum, commonly known as the spotted geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often marked with dark spots or blotches. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium punctatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
