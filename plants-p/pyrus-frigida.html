Pyrus frigida

Pyrus frigida, commonly known as the cold apple tree, is a species of crabapple native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, edible fruits that are tart and acidic.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in high-altitude environments.
