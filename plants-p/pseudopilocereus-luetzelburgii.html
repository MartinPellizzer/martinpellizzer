Pseudopilocereus luetzelburgii

Pseudopilocereus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the Luetzelburg's night bloomer, is a climbing cactus native to South America.

It features spiny stems and produces large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
