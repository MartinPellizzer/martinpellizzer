Phleum hirsutum subsp. ambiguum

Phleum hirsutum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as the hairy oat, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and spikelets that are often loosely arranged.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
