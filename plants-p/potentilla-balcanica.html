Potentilla balcanica

Potentilla balcanica, commonly known as the Balkan cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with wiry stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Potentilla balcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and resilience.
