Pleioblastus yasuianus

Pleioblastus yasuianus, commonly known as the Japanese forest grass, is a species of bamboo native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. This plant is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its growth habit is clumping, forming dense clusters that can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and garden design.
