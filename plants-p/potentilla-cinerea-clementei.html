Potentilla cinerea clementei

Potentilla cinerea clementei, commonly known as the Clemens' cinereous cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
