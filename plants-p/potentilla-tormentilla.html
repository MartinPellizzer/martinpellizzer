Potentilla tormentilla

Potentilla tormentilla, commonly known as rough cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Potentilla tormentilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal remedies.
