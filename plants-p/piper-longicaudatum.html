Piper longicaudatum

Piper longicaudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed pepper or long-tailed pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its elongated, slender leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruits are berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, containing aromatic seeds.

Piper longicaudatum is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
