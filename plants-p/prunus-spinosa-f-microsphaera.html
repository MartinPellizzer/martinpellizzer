Prunus spinosa f. microsphaera

Prunus spinosa f. microsphaera, commonly known as the dwarf blackthorn, is a compact shrub characterized by its small, dark purple fruits and thorny stems.

It is a naturally occurring form of Prunus spinosa, distinguished by its reduced fruit size and more densely packed flowers. The plant typically grows in hedgerows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of elliptical, dark green leaves that provide dense shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of pollinators and wildlife.
