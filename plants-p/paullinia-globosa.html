Paullinia globosa

Paullinia globosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and highly valued for their tart flavor. Paullinia globosa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and culinary contexts.
