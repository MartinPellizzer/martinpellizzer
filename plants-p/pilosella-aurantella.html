Pilosella aurantella

Pilosella aurantella, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive globular shape, often surrounded by pale green bracts. It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

Pilosella aurantella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
