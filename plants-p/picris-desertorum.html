Picris desertorum

Picris desertorum, commonly known as the desert chicory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Picris desertorum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
