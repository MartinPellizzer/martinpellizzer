Primula sieboldii var. lilacina

Primula sieboldii var. lilacina, commonly known as the lilac-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to lilac. The leaves are broadly ovate with a rough texture and a slightly serrated margin. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
