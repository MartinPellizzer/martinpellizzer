Piper pastasanum f. pleiotrichum

Piper pastasanum f. pleiotrichum, commonly known as the pleiotrichum pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper pastasanum species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a unique floral structure.

This cultivar is valued for its aromatic properties and potential medicinal uses.
