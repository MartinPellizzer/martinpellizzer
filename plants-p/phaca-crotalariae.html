Phaca crotalariae

Phaca crotalariae, commonly known as the horsegram or cluster bean, is an annual leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and used in various cuisines. It also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

Due to its adaptability, it is cultivated in many parts of Asia and Africa for both food and agricultural purposes.
