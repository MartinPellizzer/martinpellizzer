Piper courtallense

Piper courtallense, commonly known as the Ceylon pepper or Malabar pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its vine-like growth habit. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, containing aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires a humid environment for optimal growth.
