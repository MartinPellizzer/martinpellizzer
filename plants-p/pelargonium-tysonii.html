Pelargonium tysonii

Pelargonium tysonii, commonly known as the mountain geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features succulent, hairy leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink or purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
