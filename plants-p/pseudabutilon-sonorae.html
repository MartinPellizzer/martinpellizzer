Pseudabutilon sonorae

Pseudabutilon sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran false abutilon, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert region of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, characterized by its gray-green, woolly leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
