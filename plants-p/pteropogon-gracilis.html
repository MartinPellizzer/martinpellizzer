Pteropogon gracilis

Pteropogon gracilis, commonly known as the slender grasshopper orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, delicate flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

The species is noted for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
