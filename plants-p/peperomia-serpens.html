Peperomia serpens

Peperomia serpens, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, upright leaves that are often variegated with green and silver markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or red in color. It is well adapted to low light conditions and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its air-purifying qualities also contribute to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
