Pithecellobium vinhatico

Pithecellobium vinhatico, commonly known as the Vietnamese velvet tamarind, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant is notable for its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, tropical environments.

The plant has some ornamental value and is occasionally used in traditional medicine.
