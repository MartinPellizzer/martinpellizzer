Polymnia maculata var. vulgaris

Polymnia maculata var. vulgaris, commonly known as the spotted polymnia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive red or purple spots. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage.
