Phrynium cominsia

Phrynium cominsia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Phrynium cominsia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
