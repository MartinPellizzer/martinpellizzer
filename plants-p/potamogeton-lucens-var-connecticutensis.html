Potamogeton lucens var. connecticutensis

Potamogeton lucens var. connecticutensis, commonly known as the Connecticut pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly curled. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
