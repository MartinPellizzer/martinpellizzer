Phoradendron decipiens

Phoradendron decipiens, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the stems of trees and shrubs.

The plant is native to the Americas and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its roots form a specialized structure called a haustellum, which penetrates the host's vascular system.

Despite its parasitic nature, Phoradendron decipiens plays a role in ecological interactions and can be an indicator of certain environmental conditions.
