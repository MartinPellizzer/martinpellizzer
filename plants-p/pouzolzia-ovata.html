Pouzolzia ovata

Pouzolzia ovata, commonly known as the broadleaf pouzolzia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
