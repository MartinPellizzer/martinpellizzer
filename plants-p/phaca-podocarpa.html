Phaca podocarpa

Phaca podocarpa, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and elongated, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its leaves are small and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and is also a source of food for various wildlife species.
