Piper hirsutum var. barclayanum

Piper hirsutum var. barclayanum, commonly known as the Barclay's pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, fleshy berries containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
