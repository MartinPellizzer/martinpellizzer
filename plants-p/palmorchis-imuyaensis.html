Palmorchis imuyaensis

Palmorchis imuyaensis, commonly known as the Imuya palm orchid, is a rare and endangered orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a short, stout rhizome and a single, erect stem that bears a few broad, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils, often growing in the understory of dense forests.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
