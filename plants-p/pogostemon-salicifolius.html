Pogostemon salicifolius

Pogostemon salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leaf pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often with a silvery underside. Its small, tubular flowers are typically arranged in whorled clusters, and it produces small, four-angled fruits.

Pogostemon salicifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
