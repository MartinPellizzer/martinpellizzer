Polygonum caniusculum

Polygonum caniusculum, commonly known as the small-leaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Polygonum caniusculum is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
