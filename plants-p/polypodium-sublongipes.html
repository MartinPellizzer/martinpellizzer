Polypodium sublongipes

Polypodium sublongipes, commonly known as the long-leaf polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its rhizomes are wiry and fibrous, contributing to its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

The fronds are bi-lobed and have a glossy appearance, making it a distinctive and ornamental species in botanical gardens.
