Pelargonium × calycinum

Pelargonium × calycinum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with a dark center. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
