Polygonum virgatum var. latifolium

Polygonum virgatum var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf smartweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, rose-like clusters of flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem. This plant thrives in moist environments such as wet meadows, ditches, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common sight in wetland habitats across the eastern and central United States.
