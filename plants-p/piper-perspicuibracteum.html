Piper perspicuibracteum

Piper perspicuibracteum, commonly known as the translucent bract pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its unique, translucent bracts that enclose the small, peppercorn-like fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface, and the plant can grow up to several meters in height.

Piper perspicuibracteum is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal properties.
