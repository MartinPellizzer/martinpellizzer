Pyrus ambrosiaca

Pyrus ambrosiaca, commonly known as the common whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white flowers and smooth, grayish bark. The tree produces small, edible fruits known as whitebeams, which are similar in appearance to apples but less commonly consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
