Populus aurea

Populus aurea, commonly known as the golden poplar, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn season. The tree has smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin. It produces catkins as its primary flower structure, which are wind-pollinated.

Populus aurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
