Ptyssiglottis radicans

Ptyssiglottis radicans, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical vine belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fig-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.

Due to its rapid growth and climbing habit, it is often used in ornamental horticulture.
