Puccinellia mendozina

Puccinellia mendozina, commonly known as Mendozina grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riparian zones, and is often found in association with other wetland vegetation. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Puccinellia mendozina is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
