Polystachya oreocharis

Polystachya oreocharis, commonly known as the black orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are adorned with intricate white markings, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in the dappled light. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, and it produces small, elliptical seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Polystachya oreocharis is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
