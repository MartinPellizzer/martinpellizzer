Phalaenopsis equestris var. leucaspis

Phalaenopsis equestris var. leucaspis, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with white or pale green veins. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, featuring intricate patterns and a delicate fragrance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining potting media.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom repeatedly under proper care.
