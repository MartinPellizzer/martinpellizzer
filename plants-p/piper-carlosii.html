Piper carlosii

Piper carlosii, commonly known as Carlos's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Piper carlosii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
