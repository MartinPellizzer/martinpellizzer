Pluchea baccharoides

Pluchea baccharoides, commonly known as the swamp tarragon, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in marshy areas and has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and slightly toothed, while the small yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Pluchea baccharoides is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
