Pyrus × platycarpa

Pyrus × platycarpa, commonly known as the mealy apple, is a hybrid species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, round, yellowish fruits that are often covered in a mealy, waxy coating. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The mealy apple is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits.
