Peponium

Peponium, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit that turns from green to orange when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has palmately lobed leaves. Peponium is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines.

Its botanical features include a trailing growth habit and a distinctive, bitter taste in the fruit's flesh.
