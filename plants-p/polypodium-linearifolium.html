Polypodium linearifolium

Polypodium linearifolium, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a bipinnate pattern, giving it a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce sporangia on the undersides of the fronds.

Polypodium linearifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
