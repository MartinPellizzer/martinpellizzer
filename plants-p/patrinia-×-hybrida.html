Patrinia × hybrida

Patrinia × hybrida, commonly known as Chinese blueweed or blue false indigo, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Patrinia × hybrida thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.
