Phylica ericoides var. muirii

Phylica ericoides var. muirii, commonly known as Muir's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, typically ranging in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are small, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.

Phylica ericoides var. muirii is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
