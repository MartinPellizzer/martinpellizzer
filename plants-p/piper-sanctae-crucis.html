Piper sanctae-crucis

Piper sanctae-crucis, commonly known as the sacred crucifix pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, red berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The botanical characteristics of Piper sanctae-crucis include its vine-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions.
