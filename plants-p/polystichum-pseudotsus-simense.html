Polystichum pseudotsus-simense

Polystichum pseudotsus-simense, commonly known as the Japanese forest fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green fronds that form a compact, bushy appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its fronds are typically divided into narrow, lance-shaped segments, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
