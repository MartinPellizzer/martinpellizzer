Pelargonium × cosmianum

Pelargonium × cosmianum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a slightly serrated edge, while the stems are often hairy. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
