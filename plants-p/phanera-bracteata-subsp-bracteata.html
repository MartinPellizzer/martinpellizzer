Phanera bracteata subsp. bracteata

Phanera bracteata subsp.

bracteata, commonly known as the bracted phanera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, tubular shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
