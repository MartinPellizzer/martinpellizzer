Protea heckmanniana var. angustifolia

Protea heckmanniana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved protea, is a South African native plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped inflorescence that bears numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
