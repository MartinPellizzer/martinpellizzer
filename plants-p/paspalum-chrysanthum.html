Paspalum chrysanthum

Paspalum chrysanthum, commonly known as golden paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and bright yellow inflorescences that resemble spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum chrysanthum is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
