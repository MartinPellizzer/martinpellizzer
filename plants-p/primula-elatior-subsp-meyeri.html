Primula elatior subsp. meyeri

Primula elatior subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the Alpine Primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
