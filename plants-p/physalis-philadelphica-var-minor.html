Physalis philadelphica var. minor

Physalis philadelphica var. minor, commonly known as the Indian lantern or winter cherry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces striking orange-red, inflated calyces that enclose its small, berry-like fruits. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and hairy surfaces. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are borne in clusters.

The fruit ripens in late summer to early autumn, making it a popular ornamental and edible plant in gardens.
