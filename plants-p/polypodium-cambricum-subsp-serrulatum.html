Polypodium cambricum subsp. serrulatum

Polypodium cambricum subsp.

serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated rock fern, is a perennial fern native to the temperate regions of Europe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit and produces lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green in color. Its fronds are often divided into segments with a distinctive toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to colonize rocky habitats, making it a common sight in hilly and mountainous regions.
