Prionostemma kunthiana

Prionostemma kunthiana, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trumpet-shaped flowers that open in the morning. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low water availability.

Prionostemma kunthiana is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
