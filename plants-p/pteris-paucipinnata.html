Pteris paucipinnata

Pteris paucipinnata, commonly known as the few-pinnate water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are typically light green and have a feathery appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near streams or in humid forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds, typical of many fern species.

Pteris paucipinnata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
