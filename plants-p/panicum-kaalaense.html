Panicum kaalaense

Panicum kaalaense, commonly known as the black panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
