Pilea thouarsiana

Pilea thouarsiana, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.

Pilea thouarsiana is also known for its ability to produce offsets, making it easy to propagate.
