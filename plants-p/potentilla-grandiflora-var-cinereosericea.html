Potentilla grandiflora var. cinereosericea

Potentilla grandiflora var. cinereosericea, commonly known as the silver-haired potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, silver-white leaves that are softly hairy, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
