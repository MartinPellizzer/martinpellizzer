Physochlaina lanosa

Physochlaina lanosa, commonly known as woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. Its fruit is a dry, capsule that contains several seeds.

Physochlaina lanosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
