Pisonia pubescens

Pisonia pubescens, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has palmate leaves with a soft, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Pisonia pubescens is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy or well-drained soils.
