Prunus jamasakura f. gloriosa

Prunus jamasakura f. gloriosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species, which is native to Japan. The plant is celebrated for its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a spectacular floral display. Its leaves are oval-shaped and turn vibrant green during the growing season.

The tree typically reaches a height of 5 to 10 meters and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and parks.
