Potentilla × pantotricha

Potentilla × pantotricha, commonly known as the evergreen potentilla, is a hybrid shrub that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It typically features dense, compact growth with small, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring, which are attractive to pollinators. It is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it is characterized by its woody stems and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
