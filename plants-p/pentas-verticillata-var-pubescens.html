Pentas verticillata var. pubescens

Pentas verticillata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy pentas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a white or yellow center. The plant features hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming season make it an attractive ornamental plant.
