Plantago albicans var. macropoda

Plantago albicans var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and prominent veins, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and wound-healing benefits.
