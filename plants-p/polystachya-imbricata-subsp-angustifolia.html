Polystachya imbricata subsp. angustifolia

Polystachya imbricata subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swampy forests and marshes.

The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is characterized by its specialized adaptations to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
