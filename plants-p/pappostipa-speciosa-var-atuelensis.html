Pappostipa speciosa var. atuelensis

Pappostipa speciosa var. atuelensis, commonly known as the Atuel grass, is a perennial grass species native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
