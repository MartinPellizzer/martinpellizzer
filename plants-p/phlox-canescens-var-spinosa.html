Phlox canescens var. spinosa

Phlox canescens var. spinosa, commonly known as the prickly phlox, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
