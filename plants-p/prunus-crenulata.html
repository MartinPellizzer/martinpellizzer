Prunus crenulata

Prunus crenulata, commonly known as the Japanese maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves that turn vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, reddish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological studies.
