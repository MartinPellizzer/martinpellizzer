Pollinia quadrinervis var. latifolia

Pollinia quadrinervis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a variant of the orchid species Pollinia quadrinervis.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of white and pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded, humid environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
