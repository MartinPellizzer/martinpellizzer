Phaseolus oaxacanus

Phaseolus oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxacan bean, is a species native to Mexico, particularly the Oaxaca region.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, edible pods containing seeds used in traditional cuisine. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its genetic diversity makes it an important species for plant breeding and agricultural research.
