Parahebe formosa

Parahebe formosa, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
