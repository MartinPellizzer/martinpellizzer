Primula chamaejasme var. coronata

Primula chamaejasme var. coronata, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a compact, low-growing habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically silver-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, often with a distinctive crown-like pattern on the petals. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
