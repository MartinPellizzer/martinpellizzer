Prunus × lannesiana f. arasiyama

Prunus × lannesiana f. arasiyama, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its delicate pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a stunning visual display. The plant is a hybrid between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus incisa, resulting in unique floral and foliage characteristics. It is widely cultivated in Japan and other parts of Asia for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall.
