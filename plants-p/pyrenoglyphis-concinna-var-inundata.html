Pyrenoglyphis concinna var. inundata

Pyrenoglyphis concinna var. inundata, commonly known as the water lily of the Amazon, is a aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It features broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to the surrounding water. Its roots anchor in the muddy substrate, while its stems and leaves are adapted for life in water. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the early morning.

It plays a vital role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various species.
