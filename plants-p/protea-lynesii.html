Protea lynesii

Protea lynesii, commonly known as the Lyne's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their tubular structure and nectar-rich blooms.

Protea lynesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
