Pandanus laticonvexus

Pandanus laticonvexus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to grow in wet, swampy environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in humid climates for its aesthetic features.
