Pterocarpus wightii

Pterocarpus wightii, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that is highly valued in traditional craftsmanship. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and timber, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
