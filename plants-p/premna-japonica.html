Premna japonica

Premna japonica, commonly known as Japanese glorybower, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Verbenaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Premna japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.
