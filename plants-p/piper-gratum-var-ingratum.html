Piper gratum var. ingratum

Piper gratum var. ingratum, commonly known as the "ungrateful pepper," is a variant of the Piper gratum species native to tropical regions.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinct aroma.

Despite its name, it is valued for its medicinal properties and unique flavor profile.
