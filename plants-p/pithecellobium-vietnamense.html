Pithecellobium vietnamense

Pithecellobium vietnamense, commonly known as Vietnamese pithecellobium, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its woody shrub form with compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
