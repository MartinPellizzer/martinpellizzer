Psydrax schimperianus subsp. schimperianus

Psydrax schimperianus subsp.

schimperianus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in coastal forests and moist woodlands.

Its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
