Phalaenopsis violacea var. chloracea

Phalaenopsis violacea var. chloracea, commonly known as the Chloraceae phalaenopsis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often brightly colored flowers that can range from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the tropical environments it inhabits.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
