Preslia

Preslia, commonly known as the "scented geranium," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, spicy scent. The plants typically have simple or lobed leaves and produce small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. Preslia species are often grown as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and fragrant properties.

They prefer well-drained soil and full sun, making them suitable for rock gardens and containers.
