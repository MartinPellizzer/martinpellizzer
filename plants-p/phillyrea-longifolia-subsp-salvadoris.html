Phillyrea longifolia subsp. salvadoris

Phillyrea longifolia subsp.

salvadoris, commonly known as the Salvador's Phillyrea, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
