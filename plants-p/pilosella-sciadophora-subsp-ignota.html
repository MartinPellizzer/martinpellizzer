Pilosella sciadophora subsp. ignota

Pilosella sciadophora subsp.

ignota, commonly known as the darkleaf pearlyweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The plant features basal leaves that are woolly and dark green, with smaller, more slender leaves along the stem. Its flowers are small, yellowish-white, and arranged in clustered heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
