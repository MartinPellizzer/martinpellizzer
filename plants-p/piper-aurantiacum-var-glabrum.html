Piper aurantiacum var. glabrum

Piper aurantiacum var. glabrum, commonly known as the red pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red, peppercorn-like fruits. The plant produces a spicy, pungent fruit that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable species in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
