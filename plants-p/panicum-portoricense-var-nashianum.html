Panicum portoricense var. nashianum

Panicum portoricense var. nashianum, commonly known as Nash's panicgrass, is a variant of the Panicum portoricense species native to the Caribbean.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as sandy soils and coastal areas. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
