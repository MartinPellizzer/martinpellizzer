Perama galioides var. longiflora

Perama galioides var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered marsh lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a tendency to form dense colonies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
