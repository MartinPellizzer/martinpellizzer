Pseudostellaria × seoraksanensis

Pseudostellaria × seoraksanensis, commonly known as the Korean starwort, is a hybrid plant species native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
