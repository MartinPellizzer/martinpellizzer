Piper anguillaespicum

Piper anguillaespicum, commonly known as the African pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
