Plagiogyria assurgens

Plagiogyria assurgens, commonly known as the slender lichen, is a foliose lichen that typically grows on bark and wood in moist, shaded environments.

It has a distinctive greenish to yellowish thallus with a leathery texture and is often covered in a layer of fine, powdery granules. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead having a symbiotic association between algae and fungi. It is widely distributed in temperate regions and is often used as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it an important component of forest ecosystems.
