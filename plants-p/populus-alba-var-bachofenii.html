Populus alba var. bachofenii

Populus alba var. bachofenii, commonly known as the white poplar, is a variety of the white poplar tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its white, fluffy flowers that appear in early summer, and its heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree has a fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive white lichen often covering it.
