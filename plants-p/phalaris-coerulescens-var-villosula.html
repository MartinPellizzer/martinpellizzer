Phalaris coerulescens var. villosula

Phalaris coerulescens var. villosula, commonly known as the hairy blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue or purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
