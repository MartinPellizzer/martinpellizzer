Piper subpedale

Piper subpedale, commonly known as the creeping pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into tiny, edible berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of mild spices.
