Platanthera quartiniana

Platanthera quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's lady's tresses, is a rare orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and its striking, fragrant, white or pale pink flower spikes that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and requires specific soil conditions to thrive. It is a perennial herb that produces small, delicate flowers, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered in many parts of its range.
