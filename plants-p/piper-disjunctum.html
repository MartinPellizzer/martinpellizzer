Piper disjunctum

Piper disjunctum, commonly known as the Amazonian black pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark purple berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
