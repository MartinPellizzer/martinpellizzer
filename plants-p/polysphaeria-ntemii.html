Polysphaeria ntemii

Polysphaeria ntemii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its unique, spherical flower clusters. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Polysphaeria ntemii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
