Potentilla × tellusonii

Potentilla × tellusonii, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five ovate to elliptic leaflets, and are often covered with a waxy coating.

Potentilla × tellusonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, sunny soils.
