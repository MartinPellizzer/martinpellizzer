Polygala raddiana var. subaphylla

Polygala raddiana var. subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a somewhat hairy surface. The flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive spurred structure that attracts specific pollinators.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
