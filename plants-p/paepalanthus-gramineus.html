Paepalanthus gramineus

Paepalanthus gramineus, commonly known as the grass-like cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage that forms a low, mat-like structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with succulent stems that store water and reduce transpiration.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in rocky or sandy soils in regions such as Argentina and Chile.
