Phellodendron chinense var. glabriusculum

Phellodendron chinense var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the Chinese yellow chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing single seeds. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
