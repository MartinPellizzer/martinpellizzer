Ptarmica barrelieri var. schouwii

Ptarmica barrelieri var. schouwii, commonly known as the Schouw's wintergreen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the winter months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are aromatic, with a sharp, mint-like scent, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
