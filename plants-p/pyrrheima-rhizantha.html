Pyrrheima rhizantha

Pyrrheima rhizantha, commonly known as the golden root, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fibrous rhizomes, which are the primary source of its medicinal properties. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Pyrrheima rhizantha is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and immune-boosting benefits.
