Primula hungarica

Primula hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Carpathian region.

It features hairy, basal leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, usually blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Primula hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
