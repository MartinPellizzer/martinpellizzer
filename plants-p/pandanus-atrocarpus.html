Pandanus atrocarpus

Pandanus atrocarpus, commonly known as the black-fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, dark purple fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional cooking and crafts, while its fruits are valued for their flavor and nutritional content.

Pandanus atrocarpus thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.
