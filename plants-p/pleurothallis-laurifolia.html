Pleurothallis laurifolia

Pleurothallis laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that resemble laurel leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
