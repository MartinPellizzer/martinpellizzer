Phlomis lunariifolia

Phlomis lunariifolia, commonly known as moonflower phlomis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, lavender-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its unique flowering pattern and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
