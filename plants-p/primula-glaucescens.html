Primula glaucescens

Primula glaucescens, commonly known as the blue primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features rosettes of silver-green, hairy leaves that form a compact base. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers during the spring season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
