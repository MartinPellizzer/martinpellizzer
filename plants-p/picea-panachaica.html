Picea panachaica

Picea panachaica, commonly known as the Panacheica spruce, is a rare and ancient conifer native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals along its branches. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils, often growing in subalpine or alpine regions.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Picea panachaica is considered a vulnerable species.
