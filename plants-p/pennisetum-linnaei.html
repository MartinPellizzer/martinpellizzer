Pennisetum linnaei

Pennisetum linnaei, commonly known as the Linnaean grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Africa and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or greenish flowers. Pennisetum linnaei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.
