Pavetta assimilis var. brevituba-pubescens

Pavetta assimilis var. brevituba-pubescens, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, pubescent stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth upper surface and hairy underside. The flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often with a faintly fragrant scent. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes.
