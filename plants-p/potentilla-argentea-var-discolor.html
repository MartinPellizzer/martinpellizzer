Potentilla argentea var. discolor

Potentilla argentea var. discolor, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
