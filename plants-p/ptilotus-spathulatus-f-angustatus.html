Ptilotus spathulatus f. angustatus

Ptilotus spathulatus f. angustatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed spinifex, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and showy, pinkish-red bract-like flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand drought make it well-suited for coastal and sandy environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
