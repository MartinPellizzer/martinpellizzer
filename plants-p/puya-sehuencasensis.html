Puya sehuencasensis

Puya sehuencasensis, commonly known as the Andean torch, is a striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a member of the bromeliad family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a torch. The plant produces vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is considered a flagship species for conservation in its native habitat.
