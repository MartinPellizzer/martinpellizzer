Paspalum longiaristatum

Paspalum longiaristatum, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered along the stems.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
