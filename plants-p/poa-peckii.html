Poa peckii

Poa peckii, commonly known as Peck's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Poa peckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
