Picramnia bonplandiana

Picramnia bonplandiana, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
