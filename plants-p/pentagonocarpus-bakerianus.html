Pentagonocarpus bakerianus

Pentagonocarpus bakerianus, commonly known as the Bornean ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces distinctive five-angled fruits.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
