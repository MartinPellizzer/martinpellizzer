Potentilla pulchella f. friesii

Potentilla pulchella f. friesii, commonly known as the Friesii cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has delicate, trifoliate leaves that are hairy and often covered in a fine white bloom. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine settings.
