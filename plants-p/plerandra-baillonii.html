Plerandra baillonii

Plerandra baillonii, commonly known as the Baillon's arum, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its unique inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. Its flowering structure produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by various insects.

Plerandra baillonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, moist environments.
