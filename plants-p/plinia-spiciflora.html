Plinia spiciflora

Plinia spiciflora, commonly known as the spiculate plum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for local use.
