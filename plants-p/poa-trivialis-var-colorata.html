Poa trivialis var. colorata

Poa trivialis var. colorata, commonly known as the colored meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass, characterized by its distinct coloration.

This plant is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a more vivid coloration compared to the standard variety. The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
