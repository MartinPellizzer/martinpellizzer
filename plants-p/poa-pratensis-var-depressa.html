Poa pratensis var. depressa

Poa pratensis var. depressa, commonly known as the creeping meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass.

It is a low-growing, perennial grass that typically forms dense, mat-like growth. This plant is characterized by its short, rhizomatous stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
