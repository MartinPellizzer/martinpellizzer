Pleiostachya morlae var. leiostachya

Pleiostachya morlae var. leiostachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed grass, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, whitish flowers. Its leaves are elongated, narrow, and have a smooth texture, often with a slight sheen.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is sometimes used in traditional landscaping or as a ornamental plant in wetland gardens.
