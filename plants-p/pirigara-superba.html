Pirigara superba

Pirigara superba, commonly known as the giant spider orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble spiders, with long, slender petals and a distinctive, elongated labellum. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a strong, sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and birds.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Pirigara superba is considered a vulnerable species.
