Parsonsia multiflora

Parsonsia multiflora, commonly known as the multibract nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lantern-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy fruits that are often brightly colored, which serve as a food source for various animals. Parsonsia multiflora thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
