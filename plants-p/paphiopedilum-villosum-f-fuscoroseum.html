Paphiopedilum villosum f. fuscoroseum

Paphiopedilum villosum f. fuscoroseum, commonly known as the fuscorose mottled lady's slipper, is a rare and highly sought-after orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure with a pouch-like lip and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, thriving in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of flowers in late spring or early summer.

The f. fuscoroseum form is distinguished by its distinctive mottling and coloration patterns, making it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
