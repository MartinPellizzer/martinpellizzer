Portea petropolitana

Portea petropolitana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
