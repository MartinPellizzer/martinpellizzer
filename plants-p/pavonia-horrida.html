Pavonia horrida

Pavonia horrida, commonly known as the rough pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.

Pavonia horrida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
