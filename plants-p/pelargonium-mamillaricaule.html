Pelargonium mamillaricaule

Pelargonium mamillaricaule, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often covered in fine, glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Pelargonium mamillaricaule is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
