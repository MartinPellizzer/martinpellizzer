Pinus backhousiana

Pinus backhousiana, commonly known as the mountain pine, is a species of conifer native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically 5-10 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are woody and often remain attached to the branches for many years. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes.
