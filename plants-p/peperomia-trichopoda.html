Peperomia trichopoda

Peperomia trichopoda, commonly known as the velvet peperomia, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and tolerance for dry conditions.

Peperomia trichopoda is valued for its attractive foliage and ease of care, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
