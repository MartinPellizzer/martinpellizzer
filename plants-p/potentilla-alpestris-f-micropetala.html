Potentilla alpestris f. micropetala

Potentilla alpestris f. micropetala, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a low-growing shrub native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in garden settings.
