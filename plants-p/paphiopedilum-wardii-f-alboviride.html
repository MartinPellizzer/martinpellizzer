Paphiopedilum wardii f. alboviride

Paphiopedilum wardii f. alboviride, commonly known as the alboviride phalaenopsis orchid, is a rare and striking variant of the Paphiopedilum genus.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated pouch-shaped lip, a defining feature of the orchid family. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a pale greenish-yellow coloration, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

This cultivar is highly valued by orchid enthusiasts for its aesthetic appeal and rarity.
