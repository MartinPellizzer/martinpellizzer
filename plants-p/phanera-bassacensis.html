Phanera bassacensis

Phanera bassacensis, commonly known as the Bassek orchid, is a rare flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapting to low light conditions.

Phanera bassacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its unique beauty.
