Potentilla poetarum

Potentilla poetarum, commonly known as the poet's potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of dark green, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla poetarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
