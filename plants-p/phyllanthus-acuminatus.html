Phyllanthus acuminatus

Phyllanthus acuminatus, commonly known as the pointed spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a shrub or small tree with simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

Phyllanthus acuminatus is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
