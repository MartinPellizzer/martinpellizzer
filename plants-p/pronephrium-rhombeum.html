Pronephrium rhombeum

Pronephrium rhombeum, commonly known as the diamond kidney bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The botanical features of Pronephrium rhombeum make it an important crop in certain agricultural systems.
