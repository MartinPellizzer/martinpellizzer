Pausinystalia lane-poolei subsp. ituriense

Pausinystalia lane-poolei subsp.

ituriense, commonly known as the Ituri arrow poison plant, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae). It is native to the rainforests of Central Africa, particularly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

The plant produces a toxic sap that has been historically used by indigenous peoples for making poison-tipped arrows. Its leaves are large and heart-shaped, with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain moisture in humid environments.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition and its role in traditional hunting practices.
