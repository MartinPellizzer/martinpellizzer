Phalaris picta

Phalaris picta, commonly known as the painted grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and inflorescence that resembles a spike with purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking coloration and graceful appearance.

Phalaris picta is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
