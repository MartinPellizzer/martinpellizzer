Polygala brachypoda

Polygala brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are usually pale blue or violet, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure.

Polygala brachypoda is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
