Pleurothallis polystachya

Pleurothallis polystachya, commonly known as the clustered pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis polystachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
