Polypodium lineare f. steniste

Polypodium lineare f. steniste, commonly known as the narrow-leaf maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The fronds are typically lanceolate, with a narrow, elongated shape, and exhibit a distinctive dimorphic structure, with sterile and fertile fronds. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
