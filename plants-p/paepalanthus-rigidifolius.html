Paepalanthus rigidifolius

Paepalanthus rigidifolius, commonly known as the rigid-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears vibrant red and orange flowers. The plant has stiff, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, providing structural support and water retention. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
