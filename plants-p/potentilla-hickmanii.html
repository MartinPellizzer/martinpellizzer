Potentilla hickmanii

Potentilla hickmanii, commonly known as Hickman's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven leaflets, which are covered in fine hairs.

Potentilla hickmanii is adapted to rocky and dry environments, making it a resilient species found in arid regions.
