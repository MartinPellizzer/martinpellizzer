Potentilla intermedia

Potentilla intermedia, commonly known as the shrub cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant features compound leaves with five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.

Potentilla intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
