Pronephrium macrophyllum

Pronephrium macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf pronephrium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pronephrium macrophyllum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental foliage.
