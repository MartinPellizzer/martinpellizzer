Pleurothallis smithiana

Pleurothallis smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Pleurothallis smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
