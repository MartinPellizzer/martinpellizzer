Penstemon saliens

Penstemon saliens, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon saliens is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to flourish in arid environments.
