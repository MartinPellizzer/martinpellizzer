Potentilla fragarioides subsp. freyniana

Potentilla fragarioides subsp.

freyniana, commonly known as Freyn's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters.

The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
