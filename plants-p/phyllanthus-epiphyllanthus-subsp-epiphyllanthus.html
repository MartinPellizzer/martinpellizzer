Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus subsp. epiphyllanthus

Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus subsp.

epiphyllanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phyllanthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae. It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elongated.

The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
