Pseudosorghum zollingeri

Pseudosorghum zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's false sorghum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
