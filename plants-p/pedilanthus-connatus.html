Pedilanthus connatus

Pedilanthus connatus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, pendant-like yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
