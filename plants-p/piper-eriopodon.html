Piper eriopodon

Piper eriopodon, commonly known as the hairy pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage.
