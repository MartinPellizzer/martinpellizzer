Potentilla longisepala subsp. epirotica

Potentilla longisepala subsp.

epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant features small, palmately lobed leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the summer. Its botanical characteristics include hairy stems and sepals that are longer than the petals, giving it its specific epithet.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native range.
