Panke sonchifolia

Panke sonchifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer months.

Panke sonchifolia is often found in rocky or open habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
