Passiflora pectinata

Passiflora pectinata, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silky hair.

Passiflora pectinata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure and aesthetic appeal.
