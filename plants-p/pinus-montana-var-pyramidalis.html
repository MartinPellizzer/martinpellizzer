Pinus montana var. pyramidalis

Pinus montana var. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal mountain pine, is a variety of the white pine species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow, pyramidal shape, which gives it a striking, elegant appearance. The needles are long, slender, and often curved, with a silvery-blue underside that adds to its visual appeal. It produces cones that are typically upright and have a distinctive, elongated shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in landscapes for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
