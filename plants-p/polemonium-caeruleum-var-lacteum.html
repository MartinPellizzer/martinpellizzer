Polemonium caeruleum var. lacteum

Polemonium caeruleum var. lacteum, commonly known as the milk bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters at the top of its slender stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is valued for its attractive foliage and flowers, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
