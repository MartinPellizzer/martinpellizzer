Pavonia grisea

Pavonia grisea, commonly known as the gray pavonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Pavonia grisea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
