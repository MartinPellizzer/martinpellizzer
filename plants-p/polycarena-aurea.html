Polycarena aurea

Polycarena aurea, commonly known as the golden polycarpa, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and drought tolerance.
