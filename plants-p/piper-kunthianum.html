Piper kunthianum

Piper kunthianum, commonly known as the Kuntz's pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and pharmacological studies.
