Poinsettia variabilis

Poinsettia variabilis, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts, which are modified leaves that resemble petals. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking appearance during the holiday season. It thrives in warm, bright environments with well-draining soil.

Poinsettia variabilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its showy foliage and short blooming period.
