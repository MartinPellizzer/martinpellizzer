Piper humaytanum

Piper humaytanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they turn bright red, and they contain several small seeds.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
