Phaeanthus glabrescens

Phaeanthus glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth phaeanthus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has smooth, glabrous leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
