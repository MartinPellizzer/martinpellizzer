Peristeria guttata var. violacea

Peristeria guttata var. violacea, commonly known as the violet-streaked nightshade, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with distinctive violet or purple streaks. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and enthusiasts of exotic flora.
