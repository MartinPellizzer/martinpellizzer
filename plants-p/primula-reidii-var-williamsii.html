Primula reidii var. williamsii

Primula reidii var. williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' primula, is a rare and striking alpine plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine meadows at high elevations. Its flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
