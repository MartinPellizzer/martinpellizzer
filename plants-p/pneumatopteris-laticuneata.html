Pneumatopteris laticuneata

Pneumatopteris laticuneata, commonly known as the broad-crested water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments, such as ponds and slow-moving streams, where it attaches to submerged surfaces. The plant has delicate, feathery fronds that are adapted for underwater life, with specialized structures for gas exchange. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize large areas in its habitat.

The fronds are usually dark green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, contributing to the plant's aesthetic and ecological roles in aquatic ecosystems.
