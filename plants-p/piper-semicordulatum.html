Piper semicordulatum

Piper semicordulatum, commonly known as the heartleaf pepper or the forest pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Piper semicordulatum is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
