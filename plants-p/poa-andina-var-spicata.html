Poa andina var. spicata

Poa andina var. spicata, commonly known as the Andean hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flower heads, which are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
