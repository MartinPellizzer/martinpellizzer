Phlox glaberrima subvar. angustissima

Phlox glaberrima subvar. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf sandphlox, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along stream banks and in open woodlands. It has a low-growing habit with stems that often lie prostrate or are ascending.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
