Potentilla howellii

Potentilla howellii, commonly known as Howell's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
