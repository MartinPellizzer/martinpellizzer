Paeonia arietina var. oxoniensis

Paeonia arietina var. oxoniensis, commonly known as the Oxford peony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United Kingdom.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features compound, bipinnate leaves that are bright green and deeply divided, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical significance lies in its historical cultivation and role in the development of modern peony varieties.
