Potentilla × musashinoana

Potentilla × musashinoana, commonly known as the Japanese cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
