Phyteuma balbisii var. petraeum

Phyteuma balbisii var. petraeum, commonly known as the rock phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
