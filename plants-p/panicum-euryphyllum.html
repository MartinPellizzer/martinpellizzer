Panicum euryphyllum

Panicum euryphyllum, commonly known as broadleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its wide, flat leaves and tall, slender inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
