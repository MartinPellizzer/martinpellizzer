Petitmenginia comosa

Petitmenginia comosa, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Petitmenginia comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
