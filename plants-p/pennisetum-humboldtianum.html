Pennisetum humboldtianum

Pennisetum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Pennisetum humboldtianum is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
