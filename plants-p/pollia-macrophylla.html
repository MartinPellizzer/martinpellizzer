Pollia macrophylla

Pollia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant violet, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can grow up to 10 centimeters in diameter, with vibrant violet petals and a unique, elongated structure. The plant belongs to the family Violaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its dramatic floral displays.

Pollia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is admired for its longevity and resilience in the right conditions.
