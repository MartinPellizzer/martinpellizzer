Prunus × donarium f. kokesimidsu

Prunus × donarium f. kokesimidsu, commonly known as the Kokesimidsu cherry, is a unique hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, often fragrant flowers in spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance. This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal, particularly in gardens and landscapes.

However, it is relatively rare and not widely cultivated, making it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
