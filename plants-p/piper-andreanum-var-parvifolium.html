Piper andreanum var. parvifolium

Piper andreanum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for container gardening and indoor spaces.
