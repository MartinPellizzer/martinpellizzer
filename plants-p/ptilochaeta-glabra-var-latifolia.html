Ptilochaeta glabra var. latifolia

Ptilochaeta glabra var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed ptilochaeta, is a perennial herb native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are often borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
