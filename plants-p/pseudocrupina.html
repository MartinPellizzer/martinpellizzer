Pseudocrupina

Pseudocrupina, commonly known as the false crupina, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy leaves and spikes of small, clustered flowers. The plants are typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Pseudocrupina species are often used in xeriscaping due to their drought tolerance and ornamental value.

They play a role in stabilizing soils in sandy or rocky habitats.
