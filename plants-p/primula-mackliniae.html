Primula mackliniae

Primula mackliniae, commonly known as the Macklin's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in rocky or forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
