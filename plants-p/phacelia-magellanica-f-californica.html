Phacelia magellanica f. californica

Phacelia magellanica f. californica, commonly known as the California phacelia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
