Piper volubile

Piper volubile, commonly known as creeping pepper or wild betel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Piper volubile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
