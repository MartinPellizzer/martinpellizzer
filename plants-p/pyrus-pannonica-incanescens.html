Pyrus pannonica incanescens

Pyrus pannonica incanescens, commonly known as the Hungarian white pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is notable for its frost resistance and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
