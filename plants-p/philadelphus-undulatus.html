Philadelphus undulatus

Philadelphus undulatus, commonly known as the meadow mockorange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its showy blossoms and pleasant fragrance.
