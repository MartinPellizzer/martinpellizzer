Penstemon luculentus

Penstemon luculentus, commonly known as the showy penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Penstemon luculentus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
