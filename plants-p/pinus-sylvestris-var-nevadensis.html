Pinus sylvestris var. nevadensis

Pinus sylvestris var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada white pine, is a subspecies of the Scots pine native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 inches in length. The tree produces cones that range from 2 to 4 inches in length, containing winged seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
