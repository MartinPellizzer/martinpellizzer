Phragmites australis subsp. americanus

Phragmites australis subsp.

americanus, commonly known as the common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments. It is characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and dense, fibrous root system, which help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The plant produces feathery, spike-like inflorescences that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its widespread distribution. It is highly adaptable, often forming dense monocultures in marshes, shores, and other moist habitats.

Its rapid growth and extensive rhizomes make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
