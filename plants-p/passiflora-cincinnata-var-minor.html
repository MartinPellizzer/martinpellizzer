Passiflora cincinnata var. minor

Passiflora cincinnata var. minor, commonly known as the lesser passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, sometimes aromatic scent.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in warm climates.
