Pavonia speciosa subsp. genuina

Pavonia speciosa subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the pink false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States. It features showy, pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces small, fluffy seed heads that persist into the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
