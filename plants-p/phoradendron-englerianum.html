Phoradendron englerianum

Phoradendron englerianum, commonly known as the mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees, primarily in the Americas.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it produces its own carbohydrates through photosynthesis using its leaf-like structures. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with its host, often leading to reduced growth or even death of the host tree in severe cases. Its sticky seeds are dispersed by birds, which consume the berries and excrete the seeds onto other trees.

This plant is notable for its role in ecological systems and its cultural significance in various regions.
