Parrya hispida

Parrya hispida, commonly known as the rough parrya, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.

Parrya hispida is typically found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
