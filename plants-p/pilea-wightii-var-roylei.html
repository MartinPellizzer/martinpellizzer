Pilea wightii var. roylei

Pilea wightii var. roylei, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact, trailing plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
