Philodendron rubromaculatum

Philodendron rubromaculatum, commonly known as the red-spotted philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with prominent red or maroon spots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
