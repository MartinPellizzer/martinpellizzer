Phalaenopsis violacea var. alba

Phalaenopsis violacea var. alba, commonly known as the white phalaenopsis orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting white flowers that resemble moth wings, hence its common name. The plant is an epiphyte, typically growing on tree trunks or branches in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad, leathery, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

This variety is prized for its beauty and ease of care, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
