Polygonum coarctatum var. minus

Polygonum coarctatum var. minus, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense terminal spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator of wetland habitats.
