Potentilla bifurca var. astragalifolia

Potentilla bifurca var. astragalifolia, commonly known as the Astragalifolia cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its shrubby growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
