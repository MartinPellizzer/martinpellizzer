Pelargonium scabrum

Pelargonium scabrum, commonly known as the roughleaf geranium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Pelargonium scabrum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
