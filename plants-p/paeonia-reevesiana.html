Paeonia reevesiana

Paeonia reevesiana, commonly known as the Reeves' peony, is a species native to China.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces red or pink petals that are often adorned with a contrasting white or yellow center. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The Reeves' peony is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese medicine.
