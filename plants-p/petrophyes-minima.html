Petrophyes minima

Petrophyes minima, commonly known as the rock violet, is a small flowering plant native to the rocky habitats of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces delicate purple or violet flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically small, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

Petrophyes minima is often found growing on limestone or sandstone outcrops, showcasing its ability to survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
