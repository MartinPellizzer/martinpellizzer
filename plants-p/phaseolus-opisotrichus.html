Phaseolus opisotrichus

Phaseolus opisotrichus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its climbing habit and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its nutritional content.
