Polygala persicariifolia var. granulata

Polygala persicariifolia var. granulata, commonly known as the granulate milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clumps or loose clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and usually covered with fine, granular hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spicate inflorescences, and typically have a distinctive, spurred structure.

This plant is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
