Podocarpus angustifolius var. buchii

Podocarpus angustifolius var. buchii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Christmas tree, is a evergreen coniferous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a narrow, elongated shape. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a landscape plant.
