Podalyria coerulea

Podalyria coerulea, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody at the base.

Podalyria coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
