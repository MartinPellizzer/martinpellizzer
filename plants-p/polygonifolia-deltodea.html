Polygonifolia deltodea

Polygonifolia deltodea, commonly known as the delta leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive, deltoid-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions with high humidity. The plant's fronds are typically divided into triangular lobes, giving it a visually striking appearance. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure supports its role as an indicator species for undisturbed ecosystems.
