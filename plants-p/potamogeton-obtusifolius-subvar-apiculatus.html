Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. apiculatus

Potamogeton obtusifolius subvar. apiculatus, commonly known as the apiculate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves with a distinct apiculate (bristle-like) tip, which distinguishes it from other similar species. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters, and it thrives in slow-moving or still waters. Its rhizomes help anchor it in the substrate, while its dense growth can provide habitat for aquatic invertebrates and fish.

This species plays an important ecological role in maintaining water quality and biodiversity in its native environments.
