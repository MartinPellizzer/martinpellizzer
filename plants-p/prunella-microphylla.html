Prunella microphylla

Prunella microphylla, commonly known as lesser dead-nettle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are borne on slender, erect stems and are visited by various pollinators.

Prunella microphylla is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
