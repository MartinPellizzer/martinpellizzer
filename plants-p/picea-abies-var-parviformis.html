Picea abies var. parviformis

Picea abies var. parviformis, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a variant of the Norway spruce characterized by its compact, slow-growing form.

It features short, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture and landscaping.
