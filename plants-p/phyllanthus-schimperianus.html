Phyllanthus schimperianus

Phyllanthus schimperianus, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well-suited for dry environments. Its latex-rich sap has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, is often consumed by local wildlife and humans in some regions.
