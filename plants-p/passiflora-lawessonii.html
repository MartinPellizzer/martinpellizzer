Passiflora lawessonii

Passiflora lawessonii, commonly known as the Philippine passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that resemble the Passion of Christ, with blue and white petals and a crown-like structure. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in small, hooked spines.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
