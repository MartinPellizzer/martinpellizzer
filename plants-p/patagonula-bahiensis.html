Patagonula bahiensis

Patagonula bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia Patagonula, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the Atlantic coast, thriving in a humid subtropical climate.
