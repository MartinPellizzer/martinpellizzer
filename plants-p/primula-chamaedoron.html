Primula chamaedoron

Primula chamaedoron, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and a distinct yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Primula chamaedoron is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add color to high-altitude gardens.
