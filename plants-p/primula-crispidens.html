Primula crispidens

Primula crispidens, commonly known as the curly primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, visually appealing plants.
