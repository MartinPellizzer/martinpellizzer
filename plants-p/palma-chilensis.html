Palma chilensis

Palma chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean palm, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and arid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both wildlife and humans.

The Chilean palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
