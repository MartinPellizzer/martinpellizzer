Pleurothallis dodsonii

Pleurothallis dodsonii, commonly known as the Dodson's pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is a small epiphytic orchid, typically growing on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is part of the Pleurothallis genus, which includes over 1,000 species.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
