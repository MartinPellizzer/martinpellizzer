Pulicaria insignis

Pulicaria insignis, commonly known as the woolly groundsel, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.

Pulicaria insignis is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and butterflies.
