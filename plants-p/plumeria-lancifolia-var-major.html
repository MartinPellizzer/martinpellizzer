Plumeria lancifolia var. major

Plumeria lancifolia var. major, commonly known as the West Indian frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The leaves are opposite, simple, and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
