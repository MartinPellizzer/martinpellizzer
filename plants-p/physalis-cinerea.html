Physalis cinerea

Physalis cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf physalis, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of orange-red calyces that enclose its small, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed areas. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and are followed by the distinctive, inflated, papery husks that protect the immature fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
