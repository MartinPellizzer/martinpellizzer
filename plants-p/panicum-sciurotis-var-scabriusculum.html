Panicum sciurotis var. scabriusculum

Panicum sciurotis var. scabriusculum, commonly known as the rough-haired panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, scabrous texture, and its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This variant is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
