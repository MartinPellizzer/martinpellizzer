Potentilla pulcherrima

Potentilla pulcherrima, commonly known as the rose-of-Sharon or the Chinese rose, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Potentilla pulcherrima is also valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
