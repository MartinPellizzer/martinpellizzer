Piper nirjulianum

Piper nirjulianum, commonly known as the Nilgiri pepper, is a climbing vine native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and these are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the Piper genus.
