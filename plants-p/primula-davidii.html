Primula davidii

Primula davidii, commonly known as David's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in cool climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.
