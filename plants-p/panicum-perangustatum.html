Panicum perangustatum

Panicum perangustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf panicgrass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Panicum perangustatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in harsh conditions.
