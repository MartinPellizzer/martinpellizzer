Pterocaulon virgatum

Pterocaulon virgatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.
