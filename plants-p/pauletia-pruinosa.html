Pauletia pruinosa

Pauletia pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf pauletia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of powdery wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
