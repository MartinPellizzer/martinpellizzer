Primula officinalis var. suaveolens

Primula officinalis var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy texture and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade to full sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a long history of use in traditional herbal medicine.
