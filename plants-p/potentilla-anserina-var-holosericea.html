Potentilla anserina var. holosericea

Potentilla anserina var. holosericea, commonly known as the silver-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, typically from June to August. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive five-lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for alpine gardens.
