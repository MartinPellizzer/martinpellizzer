Polygonatum sinopubescens

Polygonatum sinopubescens, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's seal, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its berries are typically dark red or black when mature.

Polygonatum sinopubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional medicinal uses.
