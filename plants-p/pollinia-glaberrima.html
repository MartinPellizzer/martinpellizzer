Pollinia glaberrima

Pollinia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth pollinia, is a unique plant belonging to the orchid family.

It is characterized by its distinctive pollinia, which are masses of pollen grains that are typically adhered to the insect's body for pollination. The plant is native to specific regions in Africa, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include slender, wiry stems and small, inconspicuous flowers.

Due to its specialized pollination mechanism, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat.
