Passiflora foetida var. longipedunculata

Passiflora foetida var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, intricate flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of stamens. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by various wildlife species. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
