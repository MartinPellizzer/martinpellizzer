Pachyraphea propinqua

Pachyraphea propinqua, commonly known as the giant pachyraphea, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a tall, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk that can reach heights of up to 20 meters. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various animals.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
