Pogostemon villosus var. macrophyllus

Pogostemon villosus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the hairy pogostemon, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, hairy, and lance-shaped leaves that are often larger than the standard variety, giving it its varietal name "macrophyllus.". The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
