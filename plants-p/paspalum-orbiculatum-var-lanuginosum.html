Paspalum orbiculatum var. lanuginosum

Paspalum orbiculatum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
