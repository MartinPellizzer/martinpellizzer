Pandanus subumbellatus

Pandanus subumbellatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base, and it produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in coastal and mangrove environments, often growing in brackish water conditions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
