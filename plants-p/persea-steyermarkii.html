Persea steyermarkii

Persea steyermarkii, commonly known as the Steyermark's persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic trees such as laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a small, oval berry that turns from green to orange-red when mature, and it is edible when fully ripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
