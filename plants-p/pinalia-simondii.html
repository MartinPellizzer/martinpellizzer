Pinalia simondii

Pinalia simondii, commonly known as the yellow tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm climate for optimal growth.

Pinalia simondii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
