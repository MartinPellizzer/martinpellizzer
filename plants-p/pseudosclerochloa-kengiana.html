Pseudosclerochloa kengiana

Pseudosclerochloa kengiana, commonly known as the Keng's rigid grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features narrow, rigid leaves with a distinct blue-green coloration, which contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
