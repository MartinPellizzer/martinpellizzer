Purgosea thyrsiflora

Purgosea thyrsiflora, commonly known as the mountain thorn, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically reddish in color and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its rugged appearance and hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for erosion control in rocky areas.
