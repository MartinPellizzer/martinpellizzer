Physocarpus stellatus

Physocarpus stellatus, commonly known as the fragrant smilacina, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are opposite and often have a fragrant aroma. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Physocarpus stellatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
