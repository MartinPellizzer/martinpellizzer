Pachites appressus

Pachites appressus, commonly known as the narrowleaf pachites, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
