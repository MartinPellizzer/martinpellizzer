Puya santosii

Puya santosii, commonly known as the Santos' torchgrass, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features broad, sword-shaped leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, grass-like appearance. Its flowers are typically bright red or orange, adding a dramatic visual impact to its natural habitat.

Puya santosii is a long-lived perennial, often surviving for decades, and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool, dry conditions.
