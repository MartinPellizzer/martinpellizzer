Pleurothallis mundula

Pleurothallis mundula, commonly known as the moon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often have a pale or white coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually arranged in clusters and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Pleurothallis mundula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
