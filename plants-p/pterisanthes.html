Pterisanthes

Pterisanthes, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and a single, showy flower head that resembles a dandelion. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct, often aromatic scent.

Pterisanthes species are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
