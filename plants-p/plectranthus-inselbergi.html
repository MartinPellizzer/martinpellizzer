Plectranthus inselbergi

Plectranthus inselbergi, commonly known as the rock fig or inselberg plectranthus, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.

Plectranthus inselbergi is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
