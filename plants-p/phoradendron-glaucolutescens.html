Phoradendron glaucolutescens

Phoradendron glaucolutescens, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the Americas.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a whitish or grayish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. It is characterized by its ability to derive some of its nutrients from the host tree while also performing photosynthesis.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments.
