Portulaca conoidea

Portulaca conoidea, commonly known as the needleleaf purslane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Portulaca conoidea is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
