Paracaleana terminalis

Paracaleana terminalis, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
