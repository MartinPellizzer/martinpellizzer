Podolepis papillosa

Podolepis papillosa, commonly known as the silky podolepis, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
