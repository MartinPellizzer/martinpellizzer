Pyrus huahongdongensis

Pyrus huahongdongensis, commonly known as the Hua Hong Dongensis pear, is a rare species native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions with a temperate climate. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin.

The fruit is usually harvested in late summer and is valued for its sweet flavor and nutritional content.
