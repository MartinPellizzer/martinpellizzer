Phyteuma spicatum subvar. microphyllum

Phyteuma spicatum subvar. microphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phyteuma, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange. The leaves are linear and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, adapted to thrive in well-drained soils. It typically blooms in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
