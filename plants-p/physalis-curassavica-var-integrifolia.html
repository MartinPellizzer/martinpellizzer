Physalis curassavica var. integrifolia

Physalis curassavica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the goldenberry or Inca berry, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It features a hairy stem that branches near the base and produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits enclosed in a papery calyx. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with smooth or slightly toothed margins, and the plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The fruit is edible, with a tangy, sweet flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for home gardens.
