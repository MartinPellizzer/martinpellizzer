Pyrethrum clausonis

Pyrethrum clausonis, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, spherical seed heads that are often used in ornamental gardening. Pyrethrum clausonis thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically beneficial, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
