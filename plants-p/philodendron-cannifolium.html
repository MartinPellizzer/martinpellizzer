Philodendron cannifolium

Philodendron cannifolium, commonly known as the "cannibal philodendron," is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of several meters when provided with adequate support. Its foliage is often a deep green with a slightly variegated appearance.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive leaves and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
