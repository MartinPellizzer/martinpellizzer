Polystachya transvaalensis subsp. transvaalensis

Polystachya transvaalensis subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces striking, branched inflorescences of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant typically blooms in the summer months, with flowers ranging in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
