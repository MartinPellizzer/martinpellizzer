Pleioblastus wuyishanensis

Pleioblastus wuyishanensis, commonly known as the Wuyi mountain bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. It has a clumping growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.

Its foliage is often a vibrant green, adding aesthetic value to gardens and natural landscapes.
