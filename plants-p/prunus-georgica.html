Prunus georgica

Prunus georgica, commonly known as the Georgian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by edible drupes that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Prunus georgica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its spring blossoms and autumn foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
