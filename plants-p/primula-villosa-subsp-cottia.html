Primula villosa subsp. cottia

Primula villosa subsp.

cottia, commonly known as the cottia primrose, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically features hairy, often glandular stems and leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape.

The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, or orange, often with a distinctive yellow eye. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
