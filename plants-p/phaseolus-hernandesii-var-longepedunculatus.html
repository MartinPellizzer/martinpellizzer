Phaseolus hernandesii var. longepedunculatus

Phaseolus hernandesii var. longepedunculatus, commonly known as the long pedunculate common bean, is a variety of the common bean species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its pods are usually slender and contain several seeds, which are edible when mature.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various growing conditions and its use in traditional cuisines.
