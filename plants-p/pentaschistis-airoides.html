Pentaschistis airoides

Pentaschistis airoides, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy surface, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique scent.
