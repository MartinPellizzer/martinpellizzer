Plantago sharifii

Plantago sharifii, commonly known as the Afghan plantain, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which are typically borne on elongated stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory and healing effects.
