Pleurothallis verecunda

Pleurothallis verecunda, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, delicate floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique coloration that ranges from white to pale pink.

Pleurothallis verecunda is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
