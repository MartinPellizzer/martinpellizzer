Potamogeton turionifer var. tenuis

Potamogeton turionifer var. tenuis, commonly known as narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter wide, and it produces small, green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. It is characterized by its ability to form dense underwater stands, which can influence water quality and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its turions, which are winter buds, help it survive in colder conditions and regrow in the spring.
