Pitcairnia campii

Pitcairnia campii, commonly known as the campi bromeliad, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, long-lasting flowers that range from red to orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens and greenhouses.
