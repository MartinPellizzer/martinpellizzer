Pluchea odorata var. ferruginea

Pluchea odorata var. ferruginea, commonly known as rusty leaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are often covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
