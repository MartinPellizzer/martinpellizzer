Paspalum stellatum f. hirsutum

Paspalum stellatum f. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy foliage and dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
