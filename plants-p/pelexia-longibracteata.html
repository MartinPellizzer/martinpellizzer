Pelexia longibracteata

Pelexia longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract pelexia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Pelexia longibracteata is often found in shaded to partially shaded woodland environments.
