Potamogeton × heterophyllus

Potamogeton × heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits morphological variation depending on its habitat.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that can be either broad or narrow, adapting to different water conditions. It produces small, greenish flowers that are often submerged or floating.

Potamogeton × heterophyllus plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
