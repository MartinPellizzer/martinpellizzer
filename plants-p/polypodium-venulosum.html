Polypodium venulosum

Polypodium venulosum, commonly known as the veined shield fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique trait makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
