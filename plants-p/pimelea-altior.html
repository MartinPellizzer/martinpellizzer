Pimelea altior

Pimelea altior, commonly known as the tall bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Pimelea altior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
