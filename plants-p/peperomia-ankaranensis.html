Peperomia ankaranensis

Peperomia ankaranensis, commonly known as the Ankaranensis pepper plant, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the Peperomia genus.
