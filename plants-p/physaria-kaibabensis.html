Physaria kaibabensis

Physaria kaibabensis, commonly known as Kaibab bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. This perennial species is characterized by its low-growing habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, round seed pods that contain several seeds, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
