Ptyssiglottis pubescens

Ptyssiglottis pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Ptyssiglottis pubescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
