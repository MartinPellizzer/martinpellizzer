Pandanus variabilis

Pandanus variabilis, commonly known as the variable pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots and leaves are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and medicinal practices.

Pandanus variabilis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.
