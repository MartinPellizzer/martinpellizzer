Pseudathyrium crenulatoserrulatum

Pseudathyrium crenulatoserrulatum, commonly known as the serrated fringed bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, often purple or blue flowers that are typically borne on slender stems. The leaves are usually lanceolate with a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
