Pterocarpus ancylocalyx

Pterocarpus ancylocalyx, commonly known as the Java ebony, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a legume that contains several flat, dark brown seeds.

The tree is also known for its ability to thrive in tropical climates and its ecological importance in local ecosystems.
