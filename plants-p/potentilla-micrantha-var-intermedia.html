Potentilla micrantha var. intermedia

Potentilla micrantha var. intermedia, commonly known as the Alaska rose or alpine potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and dark green, slightly hairy leaves that are typically arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This species is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in alpine gardens.
