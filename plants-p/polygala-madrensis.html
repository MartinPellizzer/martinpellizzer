Polygala madrensis

Polygala madrensis, commonly known as the Madreña milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has pinnate leaves and produces pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Polygala madrensis is adapted to arid environments and is considered a characteristic species of the Madreña Desert.
