Pilocereus virens

Pilocereus virens, commonly known as the green night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, green flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow and emit a subtle fragrance.

Pilocereus virens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
