Primula heterodonta

Primula heterodonta, commonly known as the heterodonta primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, downy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
