Pentapera sicula var. libanotica

Pentapera sicula var. libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with aromatic, gray-green leaves that emit a strong thyme scent when crushed. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
