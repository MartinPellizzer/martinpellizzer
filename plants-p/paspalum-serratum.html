Paspalum serratum

Paspalum serratum, commonly known as the serrated paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, blade-like leaves with serrated edges, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Paspalum serratum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
