Paspalidium geminatum subsp. paludivagum

Paspalidium geminatum subsp.

paludivagum, commonly known as the coastal paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of South America. It typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in dune environments and along shorelines.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a hardy plant in coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal wildlife.
