Psygmorchis allemanii

Psygmorchis allemanii, commonly known as the Italian orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a single, slender stem that produces a few showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant has a short rhizome and is often found growing in rocky or calcareous soils. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose spike and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
