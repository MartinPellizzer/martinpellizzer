Prosopis mezcalana

Prosopis mezcalana, commonly known as the mezcal tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater.

It plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
