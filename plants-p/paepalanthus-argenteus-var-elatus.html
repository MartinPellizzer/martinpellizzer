Paepalanthus argenteus var. elatus

Paepalanthus argenteus var. elatus, commonly known as the silverleaf puya, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, strap-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, branched flower spikes that bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers, which bloom sequentially from the base to the tip of the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
