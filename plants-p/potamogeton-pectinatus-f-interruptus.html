Potamogeton pectinatus f. interruptus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often slightly ribbed and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically emerging from the water's surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
