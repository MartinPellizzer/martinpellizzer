Poa cenisia var. pallescens

Poa cenisia var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale meadowgrass, is a variant of the meadowgrass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, temperate climates, often found in meadows and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, elongated blades that are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
