Panicum capuronii

Panicum capuronii, commonly known as Capuron's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical environments. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
