Piper dilatatum

Piper dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or broadleaf piper, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a peppery flavor.

Piper dilatatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its aromatic properties.
