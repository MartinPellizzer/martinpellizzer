Pilosella subgudarica

Pilosella subgudarica, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer.

Pilosella subgudarica is often found in open meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
