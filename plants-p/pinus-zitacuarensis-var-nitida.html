Pinus zitacuarensis var. nitida

Pinus zitacuarensis var. nitida, commonly known as the Mexican lace pine, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its distinctive, finely textured foliage. The needles are long, thin, and often have a bluish-green hue, giving the tree a soft, elegant appearance. It produces small, pinecone-like cones that are typically reddish-brown in color.

This variety is valued for its unique aesthetic and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
