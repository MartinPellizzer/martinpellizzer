Paphiopedilum hilmarii

Paphiopedilum hilmarii, commonly known as the Hilmari's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate flowers that resemble a slipper, with a prominent pouch-shaped lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its humid, shaded environment.

Paphiopedilum hilmarii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical collections.
