Podocarpus polystachyus

Podocarpus polystachyus, commonly known as the Indian champak, is a flowering evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, spherical berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
