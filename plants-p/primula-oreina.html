Primula oreina

Primula oreina, commonly known as the Alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Primula oreina is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
