Puya pachyphylla

Puya pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its arid environment. The plant produces tall, spiky inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers that attract pollinators. Its growth habit is slow, and it can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
