Pseudocoptosperma

Pseudocoptosperma, commonly known as the silky senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its finely haired stems and leaves, which give it a silky appearance. It produces small yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Pseudocoptosperma is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
