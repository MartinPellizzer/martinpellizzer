Pennisetum phalariforme

Pennisetum phalariforme, commonly known as the purple top millet, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescences. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for wildlife and can be used in traditional agricultural practices in some regions.
