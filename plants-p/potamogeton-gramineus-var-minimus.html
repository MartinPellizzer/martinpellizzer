Potamogeton gramineus var. minimus

Potamogeton gramineus var. minimus, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide, contributing to its delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes. It thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
