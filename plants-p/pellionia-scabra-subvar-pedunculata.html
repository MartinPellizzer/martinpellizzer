Pellionia scabra subvar. pedunculata

Pellionia scabra subvar. pedunculata, commonly known as the hairy pellionia, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its foliage is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
