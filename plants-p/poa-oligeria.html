Poa oligeria

Poa oligeria, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Poa oligeria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
