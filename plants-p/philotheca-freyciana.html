Philotheca freyciana

Philotheca freyciana, commonly known as the Freyciana mountain gum, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, orange-red fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
