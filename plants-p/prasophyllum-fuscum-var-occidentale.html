Prasophyllum fuscum var. occidentale

Prasophyllum fuscum var. occidentale, commonly known as the western leek orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, pale green or brownish flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and coastal dunes. It is listed as endangered due to habitat loss and limited distribution.

Conservation efforts focus on protecting its natural environment and managing threats to its survival.
