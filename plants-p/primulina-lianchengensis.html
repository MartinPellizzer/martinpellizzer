Primulina lianchengensis

Primulina lianchengensis, commonly known as the Liangcheng bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments with well-drained soil, making it a suitable candidate for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value has led to increased interest in its cultivation, though it remains challenging to grow outside its native habitat due to specific environmental requirements.
