Philadelphus crinitus

Philadelphus crinitus, commonly known as the sweet shrub or golden spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Philadelphus crinitus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
