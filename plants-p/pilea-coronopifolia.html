Pilea coronopifolia

Pilea coronopifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slight sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in the spring, followed by pale green, coin-shaped fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant.
