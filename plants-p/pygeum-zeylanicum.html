Pygeum zeylanicum

Pygeum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Indian sumac, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Asia.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
