Pelargonium ranunculophyllum

Pelargonium ranunculophyllum, commonly known as the moss rose, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, pink to red blooms that are often borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are typically rounded and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

Pelargonium ranunculophyllum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
