Parodia submammulosa subsp. minor

Parodia submammulosa subsp.

minor, commonly known as the miniature pincushion cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa. It features a short, cylindrical stem with a dense covering of white, woolly spines that give it a pincushion appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
