Panicum unciphyllum

Panicum unciphyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive narrow shape and a fibrous root system. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish in color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum unciphyllum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
