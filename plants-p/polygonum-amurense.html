Polygonum amurense

Polygonum amurense, commonly known as red knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking red or purple flowers in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

Polygonum amurense is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it a common invasive species in some regions.
