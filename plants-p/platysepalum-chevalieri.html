Platysepalum chevalieri

Platysepalum chevalieri, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and succulent, adapted to store water in the arid environments where it thrives.

The flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
