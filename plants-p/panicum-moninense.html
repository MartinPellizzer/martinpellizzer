Panicum moninense

Panicum moninense, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of red to brown spikelets that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for dryland environments. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Panicum moninense is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
