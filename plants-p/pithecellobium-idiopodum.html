Pithecellobium idiopodum

Pithecellobium idiopodum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, clustered white or yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a long, slender pod containing edible seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
