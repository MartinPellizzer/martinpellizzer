Panicum sabulorum var. cordatum

Panicum sabulorum var. cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf sand panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in ornamental landscaping.

Its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil make it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
