Piper aduncum subsp. ossanum

Piper aduncum subsp.

ossanum, commonly known as the Ossanum pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant produces small, black berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
