Polygonum lanatum

Polygonum lanatum, commonly known as soft-hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks.

Polygonum lanatum is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
