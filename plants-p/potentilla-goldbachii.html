Potentilla goldbachii

Potentilla goldbachii, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

Potentilla goldbachii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
