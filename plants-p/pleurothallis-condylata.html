Pleurothallis condylata

Pleurothallis condylata, commonly known as the condylate orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a compact rosette, contributing to its compact and elegant appearance.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for with proper conditions.
