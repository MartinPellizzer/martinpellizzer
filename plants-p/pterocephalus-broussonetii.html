Pterocephalus broussonetii

Pterocephalus broussonetii, commonly known as the Chinese thistle or Szechuan thistle, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruit heads that contain numerous small seeds. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered in a dense layer of white, silky hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
