Phacelia ciliosa

Phacelia ciliosa, commonly known as the silky phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Phacelia ciliosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
