Pappophorum subbulbosum

Pappophorum subbulbosum, commonly known as the bulblet grass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and basal rosette of fleshy, bulb-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is well adapted to arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
