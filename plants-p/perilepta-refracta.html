Perilepta refracta

Perilepta refracta, commonly known as the glassy leaf or false glassy leaf, is a unique plant species characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a glass-like appearance.

Native to the humid tropical regions of South America, it thrives in shaded, moist environments within the understory of dense forests. The plant's leaves are often folded or rolled, which helps reduce water loss and protect against excessive sunlight. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically green or yellow, and it reproduces through seeds dispersed by wind or water.

Due to its delicate structure, Perilepta refracta is often cultivated in controlled environments to preserve its rare and fragile botanical features.
