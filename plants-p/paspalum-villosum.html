Paspalum villosum

Paspalum villosum, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs covering its leaves and stems. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its flowers are small and spike-like, producing a fluffy appearance that aids in wind pollination.

Paspalum villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
