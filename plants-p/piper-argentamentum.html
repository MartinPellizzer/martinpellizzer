Piper argentamentum

Piper argentamentum, commonly known as silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Piper argentamentum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to climb or trail.
