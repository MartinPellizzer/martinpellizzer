Phaseolus campanulatus

Phaseolus campanulatus, commonly known as the bell bean, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

The leaves are trifoliate, and the plant has a climbing habit, often using tendrils to support its growth.
