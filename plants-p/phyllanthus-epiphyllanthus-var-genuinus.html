Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. genuinus

Phyllanthus epiphyllanthus var. genuinus, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
