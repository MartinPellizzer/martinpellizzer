Polygonum eciliatum

Polygonum eciliatum, commonly known as the Indian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often triangular stems. The plant features small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically broad and slightly heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

Polygonum eciliatum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
