Pitumba

Pitumba, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible but often have a spicy, peppery taste. Pitumba thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and disturbed soils.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
