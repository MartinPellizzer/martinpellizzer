Pultenaea hibbertioides

Pultenaea hibbertioides, commonly known as the Hibbert's hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its nitrogen-fixing ability through root nodules. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in late winter to spring. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

It is often found in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and inland regions.
