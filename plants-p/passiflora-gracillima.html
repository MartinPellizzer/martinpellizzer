Passiflora gracillima

Passiflora gracillima, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and striking, showy flowers that feature a complex arrangement of petals, sepals, and filaments. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer and are pollinated by birds and insects. Its fruit, a small, orange-red berry, develops from the flower and contains several seeds.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates.
