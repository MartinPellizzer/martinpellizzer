Protea peglerae

Protea peglerae, commonly known as the Cape protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value, Protea peglerae is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
