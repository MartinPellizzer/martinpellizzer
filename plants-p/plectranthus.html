Plectranthus

Plectranthus, commonly known as Mexican mint or honey plant, is a genus of flowering plants in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Many species are cultivated for their aromatic foliage and ornamental value. Some varieties are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Plectranthus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.
