Polyalthia cauliflora var. desmantha

Polyalthia cauliflora var. desmantha, commonly known as the white-flowered satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces woody, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface.
