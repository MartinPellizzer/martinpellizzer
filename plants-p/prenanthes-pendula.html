Prenanthes pendula

Prenanthes pendula, commonly known as the hanging bladderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, drooping stems that bear small, white, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a bluish-green hue.

The species is part of the Araceae family and is noted for its unique floral structure and ecological role in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
