Pheidochloa gracilis

Pheidochloa gracilis, commonly known as slender ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its drought tolerance and adaptability, it is considered an important forage plant in some regions.
