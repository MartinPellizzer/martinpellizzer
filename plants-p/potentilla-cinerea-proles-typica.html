Potentilla cinerea proles typica

Potentilla cinerea proles typica, commonly known as the gray cinquefoil, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
