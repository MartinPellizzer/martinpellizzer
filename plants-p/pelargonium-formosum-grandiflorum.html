Pelargonium formosum-grandiflorum

Pelargonium formosum-grandiflorum, commonly known as the Chinese rose, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with multiple stems and dense foliage. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
