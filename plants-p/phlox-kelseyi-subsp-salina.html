Phlox kelseyi subsp. salina

Phlox kelseyi subsp.

salina, commonly known as salt marsh phlox, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of the western United States. It thrives in saline and brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to high salt concentrations.

The plant typically grows in dense mats, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological restoration in salt-affected habitats.
