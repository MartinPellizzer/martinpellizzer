Phacelia distans subvar. leptostachya

Phacelia distans subvar. leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces elongated, branched flower clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
