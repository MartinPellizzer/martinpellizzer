Pilosocereus fulvilanatus subsp. vanheekianus

Pilosocereus fulvilanatus subsp.

vanheekianus, commonly known as the Vanheekian cactus, is a species native to parts of South America, particularly Argentina and Bolivia. It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale yellow spines.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or pale green, appearing in late spring or early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
