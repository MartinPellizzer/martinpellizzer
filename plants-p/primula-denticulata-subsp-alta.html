Primula denticulata subsp. alta

Primula denticulata subsp.

alta, commonly known as the highland primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
