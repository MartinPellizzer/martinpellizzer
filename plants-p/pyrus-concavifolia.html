Pyrus concavifolia

Pyrus concavifolia, commonly known as the concaveleaf pear, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive concave base. The tree produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and yellowish in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant form and seasonal interest.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
