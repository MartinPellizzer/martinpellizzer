Peperomia cundinamarcana

Peperomia cundinamarcana, commonly known as the Cundinamarca peperomia, is a species native to South America, specifically Colombia.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are borne on short, upright spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
