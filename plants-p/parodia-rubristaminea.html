Parodia rubristaminea

Parodia rubristaminea, commonly known as the red-stemmed pincushion cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a short, reddish stem and dense, spiny areoles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
