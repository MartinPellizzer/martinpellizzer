Pimela oleosa

Pimela oleosa, commonly known as African butter tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are rich in oils, which have been traditionally used for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
