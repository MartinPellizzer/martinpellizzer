Potentilla × scholziana

Potentilla × scholziana, commonly known as the Scholz's cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features dark green, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
