Pithecellobium caribaeum

Pithecellobium caribaeum, commonly known as the Carib bean or Carib pea, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential nutritional benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
