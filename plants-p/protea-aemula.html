Protea aemula

Protea aemula, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence that resembles a flower but is actually a composite of numerous small flowers. The plant features long, narrow bracts that give it a unique, almost fern-like appearance. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sunlight.

Protea aemula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also an important species in local ecosystems.
