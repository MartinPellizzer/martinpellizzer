Pavonia hastata f. intermedia

Pavonia hastata f. intermedia, commonly known as the purpletop verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas. The plant features tall, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or red. Its showy, clustered flowers range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
