Phyllanthus acinacifolius

Phyllanthus acinacifolius, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has a distinctive appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces edible fruits that are similar in appearance to dates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
