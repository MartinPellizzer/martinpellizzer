Polygonum romanum subsp. romanum

Polygonum romanum subsp.

romanum, commonly known as buckwheat, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Polygonum romanum subsp.

romanum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a valuable ground cover and a potential weed.
