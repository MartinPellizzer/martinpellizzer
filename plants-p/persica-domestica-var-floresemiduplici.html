Persica domestica var. floresemiduplici

Persica domestica var. floresemiduplici, commonly known as the double-flowered peach.

This variety is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its distinctive double flowers that consist of multiple petals instead of the usual single bloom. The botanical features include a robust root system and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth margin. It produces sweet, juicy fruits with a fuzzy skin, typically in shades of yellow or orange.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
