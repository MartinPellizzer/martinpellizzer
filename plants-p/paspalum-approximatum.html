Paspalum approximatum

Paspalum approximatum, commonly known as rough paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, which helps it thrive in various soil conditions.

Paspalum approximatum is often found in disturbed areas and is adaptable to both wet and dry environments.
