Pancratium latifolium

Pancratium latifolium, commonly known as the flatleaf yellow nutmeg, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its bulbous stem stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display and drought tolerance.
