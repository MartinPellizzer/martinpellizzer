Podocarpus lawrencii

Podocarpus lawrencii, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a slow-growing evergreen tree native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is well adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is also cultivated in other tropical regions.
