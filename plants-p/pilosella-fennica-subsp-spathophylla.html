Pilosella fennica subsp. spathophylla

Pilosella fennica subsp.

spathophylla, commonly known as the Finnish hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves and yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It typically thrives in moist, calcareous soils in woodland and meadow habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in several countries.
