Pisonia coriifolia

Pisonia coriifolia, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling coral in appearance. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Pisonia coriifolia is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
