Polypodium brasiliense var. subcordatum

Polypodium brasiliense var. subcordatum, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or rocky surfaces, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its fronds are usually dark green, with a leathery texture and a broad, rounded shape.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
