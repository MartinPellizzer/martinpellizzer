Protea sessilis

Protea sessilis, commonly known as the sessile protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in nutrient-poor soils and coastal regions. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive, elongated style that protrudes from the center.

Protea sessilis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
