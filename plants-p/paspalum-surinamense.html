Paspalum surinamense

Paspalum surinamense, commonly known as Surinam grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The leaves are narrow, flat, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
