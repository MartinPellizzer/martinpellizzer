Pavonia grewioides

Pavonia grewioides, commonly known as the redroot false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Pavonia grewioides thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.
