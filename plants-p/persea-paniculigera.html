Persea paniculigera

Persea paniculigera, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique, sweet fruit.
