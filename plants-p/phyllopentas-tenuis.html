Phyllopentas tenuis

Phyllopentas tenuis, commonly known as the slender leaf pentapent, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warm months. Its growth habit is low and spreading, making it well-suited for shaded, moist environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
