Phyllanthus ramillosus

Phyllanthus ramillosus, commonly known as the hairy leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Phyllanthus ramillosus is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
