Psidium glaziovianum

Psidium glaziovianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Glaziov's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Psidium glaziovianum is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its use in traditional medicine.
