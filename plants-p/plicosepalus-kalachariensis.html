Plicosepalus kalachariensis

Plicosepalus kalachariensis, commonly known as the Kalahari plicosepal, is a unique flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping sepals that form a cup-like structure around the flower. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its specialized morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
