Polyactium daucifolium

Polyactium daucifolium, commonly known as the meadow cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring temperate climates. It is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in Europe and parts of Asia.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator species for wetland and grassland habitats.
