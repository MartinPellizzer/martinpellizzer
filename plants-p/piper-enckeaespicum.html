Piper enckeaespicum

Piper enckeaespicum, commonly known as the Encke's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
