Pithecellobium pentzkeanum

Pithecellobium pentzkeanum, commonly known as the golden pod tree, is a tropical legume native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate or pinnate, and its distinctive yellow to orange pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests. It has small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in agroforestry systems.
