Primula acaulis var. sibthorpii

Primula acaulis var. sibthorpii, commonly known as the Sibthorpi's rock primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense, compact foliage composed of oval-shaped, dark green leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
