Phyllanthus loureiroi

Phyllanthus loureiroi, commonly known as the Java tea plant, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are tiny, spherical drupes that contain a single seed.

The plant is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine-free beverages.
