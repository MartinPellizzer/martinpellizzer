Plantago fiebrigii

Plantago fiebrigii, commonly known as the fever plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine.
