Pilosocereus purpusii

Pilosocereus purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or orange spiny stems and a striking white or yellow bloom. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in arid environments. It has a short, thick stem with areoles that bear spines and flowers.

Pilosocereus purpusii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
