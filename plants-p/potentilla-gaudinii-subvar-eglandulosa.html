Potentilla gaudinii subvar. eglandulosa

Potentilla gaudinii subvar. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and is characterized by its glandular-dotted leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
