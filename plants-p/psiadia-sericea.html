Psiadia sericea

Psiadia sericea, commonly known as silverleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves that reflect light, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. Psiadia sericea thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
