Pleurothallis leontoglossa

Pleurothallis leontoglossa, commonly known as the "lion's tongue orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on tree branches, relying on air and rain for nutrients rather than soil. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces intricate, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a prominent lip that resembles a lion's tongue, giving it its common name.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
