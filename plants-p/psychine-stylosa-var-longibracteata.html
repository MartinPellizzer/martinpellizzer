Psychine stylosa var. longibracteata

Psychine stylosa var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract psychine, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features elongated bracts that give it its common name, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
