Piper rivinoides f. rivinoides

Piper rivinoides f. rivinoides, commonly known as the river pepper or wild pepper, is a member of the Piperaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
