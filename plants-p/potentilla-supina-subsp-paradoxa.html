Potentilla supina subsp. paradoxa

Potentilla supina subsp.

paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxical prostrate cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to parts of North America. It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters along its stems.

The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
