Paphiopedilum barbigerum

Paphiopedilum barbigerum, commonly known as the beard lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-shaped flower that resembles a lip, with a distinctive beard-like structure on the lip's edge. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has hairy stems and leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Paphiopedilum barbigerum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
