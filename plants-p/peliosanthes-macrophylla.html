Peliosanthes macrophylla

Peliosanthes macrophylla, commonly known as the giant lily, is a striking plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching several feet in height. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green and glossy. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its dramatic floral display.
