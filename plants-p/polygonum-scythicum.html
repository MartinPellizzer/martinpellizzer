Polygonum scythicum

Polygonum scythicum, commonly known as Scythian knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, forming dense colonies.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
