Passiflora quetzal

Passiflora quetzal, commonly known as the quetzal passionflower, is a striking tropical vine native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and yellow hues, often with a crown of filaments resembling a bird's beak. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Passiflora quetzal thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
