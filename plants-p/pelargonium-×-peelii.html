Pelargonium × peelii

Pelargonium × peelii, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with upright stems and lobed leaves that are hairy and aromatic. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for containers and garden beds.

Pelargonium × peelii is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects, such as ladybugs and lacewings, to the garden.
