Phyllanthus casticum f. glaucescens

Phyllanthus casticum f. glaucescens, commonly known as the glaucous phyllanthus, is a variant of the phyllanthus casticum species.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is noted for its distinctive bluish-green color, which gives it its common name.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities.
