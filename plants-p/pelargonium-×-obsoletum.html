Pelargonium × obsoletum

Pelargonium × obsoletum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

Pelargonium × obsoletum is well-suited for hanging baskets and containers due to its compact form and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
