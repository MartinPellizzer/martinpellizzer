Ponerorchis chusua var. delavayi

Ponerorchis chusua var. delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive sympodial growth habit, characterized by its fleshy, rhizomatous stems. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with prominent sepals and petals, often arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
