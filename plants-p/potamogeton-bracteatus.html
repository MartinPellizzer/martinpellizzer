Potamogeton bracteatus

Potamogeton bracteatus, commonly known as the bracteate pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender stems that often branch and are covered with small, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving water. Its roots help stabilize sediment, contributing to water clarity and ecosystem health.

It is an important food source for various aquatic animals and is used in restoration projects to improve water quality.
