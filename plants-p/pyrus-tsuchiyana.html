Pyrus tsuchiyana

Pyrus tsuchiyana, commonly known as Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Pyrus tsuchiyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
