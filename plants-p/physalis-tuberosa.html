Physalis tuberosa

Physalis tuberosa, commonly known as the Chinese lantern or Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive orange, inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The fruit is a small, yellowish berry with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that develop into the familiar lantern-shaped husks.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a edible fruit in various cuisines.
