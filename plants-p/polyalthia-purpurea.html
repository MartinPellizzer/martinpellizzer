Polyalthia purpurea

Polyalthia purpurea, commonly known as the purple flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that display vibrant purple and red hues, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, fragrant flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Polyalthia purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.
