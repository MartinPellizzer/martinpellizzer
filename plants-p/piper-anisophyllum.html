Piper anisophyllum

Piper anisophyllum, commonly known as the pepper plant or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported antispasmodic and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
