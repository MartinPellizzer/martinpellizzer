Polygala molluginifolia f. rosea

Polygala molluginifolia f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pink to rose-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment. Its leaves are small and opposite, and the flowers are clustered in racemes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in well-drained soil conditions.
