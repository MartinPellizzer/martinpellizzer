Polygala nitida var. goliadensis

Polygala nitida var. goliadensis, commonly known as the Goliad milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States, particularly Texas.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, bloom in late spring and are pollinated by insects.
