Phyllanthus nobilis var. martii

Phyllanthus nobilis var. martii, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
