Pyrus achras f. microphylla

Pyrus achras f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese white pear, is a compact, ornamental tree or shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are densely packed, giving it a lush, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small pears. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
