Potentilla hookeriana var. furcata

Potentilla hookeriana var. furcata, commonly known as Hooker's cinquefoil, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, characterized by its cold, dry climate and well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. Its flowers are yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
