Paspalum paspalodes var. villosum

Paspalum paspalodes var. villosum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species for erosion control and habitat restoration.
