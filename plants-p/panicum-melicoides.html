Panicum melicoides

Panicum melicoides, commonly known as millet grass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its seeds are edible and have been used as a traditional food source in some regions.

Panicum melicoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
