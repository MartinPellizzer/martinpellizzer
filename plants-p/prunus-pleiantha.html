Prunus pleiantha

Prunus pleiantha, commonly known as the Chinese sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. Prunus pleiantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
