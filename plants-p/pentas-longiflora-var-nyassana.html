Pentas longiflora var. nyassana

Pentas longiflora var. nyassana, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its stems are often woody and branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
