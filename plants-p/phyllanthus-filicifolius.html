Phyllanthus filicifolius

Phyllanthus filicifolius, commonly known as the fern-leaved phyllanthus, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that resemble fern fronds. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
