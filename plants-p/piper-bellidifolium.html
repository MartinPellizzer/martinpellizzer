Piper bellidifolium

Piper bellidifolium, commonly known as the bellidifolium pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves with a glossy surface and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that transition from green to red as they mature. These berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Piper genus.
