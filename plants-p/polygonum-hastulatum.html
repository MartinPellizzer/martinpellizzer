Polygonum hastulatum

Polygonum hastulatum, commonly known as the spear-leaved knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a prominent midrib, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers in summer.

Polygonum hastulatum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
