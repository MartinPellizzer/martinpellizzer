Pilosella lactucella subsp. tricheilema

Pilosella lactucella subsp.

tricheilema, commonly known as the white hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, while the flowers are yellow with a white center, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator species for certain ecological conditions.
