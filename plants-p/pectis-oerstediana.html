Pectis oerstediana

Pectis oerstediana, commonly known as the yellow pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow ray florets surrounded by tubular disk florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Pectis oerstediana is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
