Piper phytolaccifolium f. peltobryon

Piper phytolaccifolium f. peltobryon, commonly known as the broad-leafed pepper plant, is a variant of the Piper phytolaccifolium species.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are peppery and used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in the Piper genus, contributing to its ecological and economic significance.
