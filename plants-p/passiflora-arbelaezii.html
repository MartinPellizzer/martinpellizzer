Passiflora arbelaezii

Passiflora arbelaezii, commonly known as the Colombian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues, along with intricate patterns resembling a crown. The plant produces edible, orange-colored berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in length.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
