Piper sneidernii

Piper sneidernii, commonly known as the sneidern's pepper vine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, three-lobed berries.

Piper sneidernii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm, humid climates.
