Prunus lusitanica var. variegata

Prunus lusitanica var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Portuguese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display white and green patterns, enhancing its ornamental value. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible red berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive foliage and seasonal interest, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
