Parthenocissus semicordata var. roylei

Parthenocissus semicordata var. roylei, commonly known as the Persian creeper, is a vigorous vine native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its climbing habit makes it suitable for trellises, walls, or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
