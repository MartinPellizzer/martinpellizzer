Phyllanthus decipiens var. genuinus

Phyllanthus decipiens var. genuinus, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, spherical fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
