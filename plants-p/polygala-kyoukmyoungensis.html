Polygala kyoukmyoungensis

Polygala kyoukmyoungensis, commonly known as the Korean milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, while the flowers are usually pink or white, with a unique, spurred structure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
