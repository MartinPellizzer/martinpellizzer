Phyllostachys cantoniensis

Phyllostachys cantoniensis, commonly known as the golden bamboo or Chinese golden bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow culms and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This fast-growing plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to create dense screens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it adaptable to various climates.

The plant is also valued for its role in erosion control and as a sustainable material for crafts and construction.
