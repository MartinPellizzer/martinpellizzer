Psydrax approximatus

Psydrax approximatus, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
