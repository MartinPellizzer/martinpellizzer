Potentilla wallichiana var. ternata

Potentilla wallichiana var. ternata, commonly known as the Tibetan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
