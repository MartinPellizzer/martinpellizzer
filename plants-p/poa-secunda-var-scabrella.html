Poa secunda var. scabrella

Poa secunda var. scabrella, commonly known as rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, rough to the touch, and have a distinctive hairy texture along their surfaces. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish spikelets.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils.
