Potentilla wimanniana var. argenteiformis

Potentilla wimanniana var. argenteiformis, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
