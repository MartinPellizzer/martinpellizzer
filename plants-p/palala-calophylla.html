Palala calophylla

Palala calophylla, commonly known as the Christmas tree fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is characterized by its tall, tree-like structure with a trunk covered in fibrous roots. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Palala calophylla is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
