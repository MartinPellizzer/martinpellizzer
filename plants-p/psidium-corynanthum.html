Psidium corynanthum

Psidium corynanthum, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to South Africa.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for rocky or well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

The leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing dense shade and habitat for local wildlife.
