Prunus duracina var. erythrocarpa

Prunus duracina var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique fruit color and seasonal beauty, it is valued in horticulture for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
