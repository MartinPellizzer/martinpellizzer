Piper pilibracteum

Piper pilibracteum, commonly known as the white-veined pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that contain the aromatic seeds used as a spice. Its botanical features include a pungent aroma and a preference for humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal properties.
