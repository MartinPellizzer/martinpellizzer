Phyllodoce × alpina

Phyllodoce × alpina, commonly known as the alpine wintergreen, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and alpine ecosystems. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer, which are usually white or pale pink.

Phyllodoce × alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed habitats.
