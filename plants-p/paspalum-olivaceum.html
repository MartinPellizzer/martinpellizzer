Paspalum olivaceum

Paspalum olivaceum, commonly known as silverleaf paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowering spikes are slender and often appear in summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Paspalum olivaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
