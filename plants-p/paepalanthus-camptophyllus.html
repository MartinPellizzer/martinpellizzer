Paepalanthus camptophyllus

Paepalanthus camptophyllus, commonly known as the zigzag pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, spiny, globular stems that often curve in a zigzag pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent tissues and minimal water requirements.

Due to its distinctive appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
