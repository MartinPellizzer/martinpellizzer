Pulicaria afghanica

Pulicaria afghanica, commonly known as Afghan groundsel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are usually grayish-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Pulicaria afghanica is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
