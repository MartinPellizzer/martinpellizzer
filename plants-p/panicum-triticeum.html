Panicum triticeum

Panicum triticeum, commonly known as slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas. It produces wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Panicum triticeum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
