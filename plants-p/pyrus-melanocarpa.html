Pyrus melanocarpa

Pyrus melanocarpa, commonly known as the black crabapple, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically edible but often tart. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape with serrated edges. It produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are white or pale pink.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
