Polypodium sucklingianum

Polypodium sucklingianum, commonly known as the Suckling's water fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds that are typically bi-lobed and have a distinctive, serrated margin. This plant is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. It prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil and is often found growing on rocks or tree trunks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
