Pelargonium caffrum

Pelargonium caffrum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The leaves are typically rounded with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium caffrum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
