Pyrrhocactus floccosus

Pyrrhocactus floccosus, commonly known as the woolly cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white spines that give it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant has a short, thick stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in spring.

Pyrrhocactus floccosus is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and distinctive member of the cactus family.
