Pieris japonica f. variegata

Pieris japonica f. variegata, commonly known as the Japanese pieris with variegation, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features a mix of white, yellow, and green hues on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale greenish-white flowers in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique leaf patterns and seasonal blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
