Potentilla sarmatica

Potentilla sarmatica, commonly known as the Sarmatian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Potentilla sarmatica is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
