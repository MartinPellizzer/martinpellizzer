Petroselinum sativum

Petroselinum sativum, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and dill. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are used both fresh and dried in cooking. Its roots are also edible and have a slightly peppery flavor.

Parsley is known for its high vitamin C content and is often used to add flavor and color to dishes.
