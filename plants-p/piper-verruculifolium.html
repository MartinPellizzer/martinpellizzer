Piper verruculifolium

Piper verruculifolium, commonly known as the warty-leaf pepper, is a climbing shrub native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, warty leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
