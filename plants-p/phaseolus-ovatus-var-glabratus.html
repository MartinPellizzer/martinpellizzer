Phaseolus ovatus var. glabratus

Phaseolus ovatus var. glabratus, commonly known as the hairy bean or glabrous hairy bean, is a variety of the common bean genus.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, though the "glabratus" variety has a smoother texture compared to other variants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and is typically cultivated for its nutritional value.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and resilience in various growing conditions.
