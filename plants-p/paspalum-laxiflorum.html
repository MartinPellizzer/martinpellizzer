Paspalum laxiflorum

Paspalum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Central America, where it thrives in wetland and marsh habitats. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Paspalum laxiflorum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
