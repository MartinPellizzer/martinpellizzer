Potentilla gordonii var. alpicola

Potentilla gordonii var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
