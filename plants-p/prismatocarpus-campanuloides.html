Prismatocarpus campanuloides

Prismatocarpus campanuloides, commonly known as the bell-shaped pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical regions.
