Persea catesbyana

Persea catesbyana, commonly known as the Florida persimmon, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its botanical features include a smooth, gray bark and a tendency to grow in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Native American cultures.
