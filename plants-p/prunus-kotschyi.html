Prunus kotschyi

Prunus kotschyi, commonly known as the Kotschyi cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. The plant is adapted to dry climates and can thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Prunus kotschyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
