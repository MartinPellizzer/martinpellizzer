Potentilla albipellis

Potentilla albipellis, commonly known as the white-haired cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Potentilla albipellis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and delicate floral display.
