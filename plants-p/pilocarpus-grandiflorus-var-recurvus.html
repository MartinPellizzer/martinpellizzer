Pilocarpus grandiflorus var. recurvus

Pilocarpus grandiflorus var. recurvus, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
