Pelargonium × grandidentatum

Pelargonium × grandidentatum, commonly known as the African marigold, is a hybrid geranium species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers and serrated, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium × grandidentatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
