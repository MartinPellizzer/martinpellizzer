Polystichum squamatum

Polystichum squamatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy fronds that are covered in fine, silvery scales, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its fronds are bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, upright growth habit.

Polystichum squamatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
