Potentilla brachyloba

Potentilla brachyloba, commonly known as the narrowleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with five to seven leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Potentilla brachyloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
