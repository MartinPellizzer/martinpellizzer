Poa pseudoconcinna

Poa pseudoconcinna, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 millimeters wide. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with flowers that are wind-pollinated and adapted to open habitats. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
