Pterachne

Pterachne, commonly known as the feather grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and delicate, feathery inflorescences. These plants are typically found in moist or wetland habitats, often growing in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Pterachne species are known for their ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and their resilience to periodic flooding.

The genus includes several species, such as Pterachne filiformis, which is native to parts of Africa and Asia.
