Ploca amentacea

Ploca amentacea, commonly known as the white willow, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches and bright yellow catkins that appear in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
