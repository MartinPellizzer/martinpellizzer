Psychotria ramuensis

Psychotria ramuensis, commonly known as the Amazonian psychotria, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It often grows as an understory plant in dense forest canopies, thriving in humid and shaded environments.

Psychotria ramuensis is also known for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of alkaloids such as dimethyltryptamine.
