Pavonia dimorphostemon

Pavonia dimorphostemon, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

Pavonia dimorphostemon is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
