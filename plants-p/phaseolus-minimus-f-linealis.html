Phaseolus minimus f. linealis

Phaseolus minimus f. linealis, commonly known as the slender bush bean, is a botanical variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, upright stems. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with small, rounded leaflets, and the plant produces small, elongated pods. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often cultivated for its edible beans.

This form is valued for its adaptability and use in both home gardens and small-scale farming.
