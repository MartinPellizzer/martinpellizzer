Prunus divaricata f. elegans

Prunus divaricata f. elegans, commonly known as the elegant cherry, is a compact, shrub-like form of the Japanese cherry.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically a glossy green and turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate beauty.

This cultivar is well-suited to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
