Panicum spretum

Panicum spretum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, reddish-brown inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Panicum spretum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
