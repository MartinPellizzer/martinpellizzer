Pandanus dauphinensis

Pandanus dauphinensis, commonly known as the Dauphin's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus, which is part of the palms family, Arecaceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its inflorescence consists of clusters of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is notable for its ecological and cultural significance in the region.
