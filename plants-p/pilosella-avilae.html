Pilosella avilae

Pilosella avilae, commonly known as the Avila's goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Pilosella avilae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
