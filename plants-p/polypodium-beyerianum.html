Polypodium beyerianum

Polypodium beyerianum, commonly known as the Beyer's cliff fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows on rocks or trees in humid, shaded environments, showcasing its adaptability to various substrates. The plant has a creeping rhizome with fronds that are pinnately compound, featuring a serrated or lobed margin. Its spores are produced in sori located on the undersides of the fronds, a characteristic feature of the Polypodiaceae family.

Polypodium beyerianum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
