Pentatropis spiralis var. longipetiolata

Pentatropis spiralis var. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled spiraled pentatropis, is a unique variant of the spiraled pentatropis species.

It is characterized by its elongated petioles, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant exhibits a climbing habit, often using its tendrils to support itself on structures or other plants. Its leaves are typically spirally arranged, contributing to its ornamental value.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, but the plant is valued for its foliage rather than its blooms.
