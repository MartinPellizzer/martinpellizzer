Pinus austriaca var. pendula

Pinus austriaca var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Austrian pine, is a striking coniferous tree characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

It is a variant of the Austrian pine, native to the Alps of Central Europe, and is valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture. The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green, and it produces cones that are cylindrical and reddish-brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated environments.
