Piper amplifolium

Piper amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its culinary use is less common compared to other pepper species.
