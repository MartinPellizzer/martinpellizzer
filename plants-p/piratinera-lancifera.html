Piratinera lancifera

Piratinera lancifera, commonly known as the sharp-leaved piratinera, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Piratinera lancifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
