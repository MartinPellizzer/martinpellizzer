Physalis aristata

Physalis aristata, commonly known as the golden berry or cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into the distinctive papery calyx surrounding the fruit. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is edible, often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.
