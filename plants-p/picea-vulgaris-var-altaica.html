Picea vulgaris var. altaica

Picea vulgaris var. altaica, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a variant of the white spruce native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Mongolia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. This species is adapted to harsh environments, featuring a thick bark that provides resistance to fire and insect damage.

It plays an important ecological role in boreal forests, supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
