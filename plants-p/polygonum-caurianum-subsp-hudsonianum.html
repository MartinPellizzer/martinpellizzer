Polygonum caurianum subsp. hudsonianum

Polygonum caurianum subsp.

hudsonianum, commonly known as the Hudson's polygonum, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
