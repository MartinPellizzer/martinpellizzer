Passiflora timoriana

Passiflora timoriana, commonly known as the Timor passionflower, is a climbing shrub native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces orange to red berries that are edible and contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
