Pulsatilla montana var. gavensis

Pulsatilla montana var. gavensis, commonly known as the Gaventine pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms of pale yellow to cream-colored flowers, which are surrounded by a dense ring of narrow, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in well-drained, sunny environments. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are covered in fine, silky hairs.

This variety is closely related to other members of the Ranunculaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
