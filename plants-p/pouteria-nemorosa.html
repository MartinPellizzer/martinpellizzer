Pouteria nemorosa

Pouteria nemorosa, commonly known as the wild mango or yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
