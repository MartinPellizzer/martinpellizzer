Pimpinella magna subsp. dissecta

Pimpinella magna subsp.

dissecta, commonly known as the divided cow-wheat, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
