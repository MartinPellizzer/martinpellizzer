Potentilla argentoidosa

Potentilla argentoidosa, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting foliage.
