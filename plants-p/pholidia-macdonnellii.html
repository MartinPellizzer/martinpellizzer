Pholidia macdonnellii

Pholidia macdonnellii, commonly known as the Macdonnell's pittosporum, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sandstone-derived soils along the coast.

Pholidia macdonnellii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
