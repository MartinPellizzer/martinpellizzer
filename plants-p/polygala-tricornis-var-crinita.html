Polygala tricornis var. crinita

Polygala tricornis var. crinita, commonly known as the hairy trident milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
