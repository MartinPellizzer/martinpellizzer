Phragmipedium longifolium f. minutum

Phragmipedium longifolium f. minutum, commonly known as the miniature phragmipedium, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for enthusiasts of miniature orchids.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
