Pichonia occidentalis

Pichonia occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Pichonia occidentalis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.
