Primula cuneifolia subsp. heterodonta

Primula cuneifolia subsp.

heterodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
