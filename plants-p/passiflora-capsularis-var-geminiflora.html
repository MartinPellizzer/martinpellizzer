Passiflora capsularis var. geminiflora

Passiflora capsularis var. geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
