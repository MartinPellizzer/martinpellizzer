Phyllocactus ackermannii

Phyllocactus ackermannii, commonly known as the ackermannii cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
