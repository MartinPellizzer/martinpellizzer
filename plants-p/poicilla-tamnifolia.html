Poicilla tamnifolia

Poicilla tamnifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf poicilla, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Poicilla tamnifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
