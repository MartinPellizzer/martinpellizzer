Potentilla collettiana

Potentilla collettiana, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are palmately divided, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla collettiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
