Polystachya xerophila

Polystachya xerophila, commonly known as the dryland sword fern, is a terrestrial orchid native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in a dense spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Polystachya xerophila is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
