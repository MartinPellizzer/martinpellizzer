Phyllanthus stultitiae

Phyllanthus stultitiae, commonly known as the "stupidity plant," is a rare and peculiar species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which consists of small, rounded leaves arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for taxonomists and botanists due to its unusual morphology.

Despite its common name, the plant has no known toxic properties and is not widely used in traditional medicine.
