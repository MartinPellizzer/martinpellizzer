Pyrus malus plana

Pyrus malus plana, commonly known as the flat apple tree, is a species of apple tree native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and moderate growth habit. The plant produces small, sour apples that are typically used for cider or cooking. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
