Potamogeton × attenuatus var. polygoniformis

Potamogeton × attenuatus var. polygoniformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its broad, flat leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern.

It is typically found in shallow, freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has slender, branching stems that can grow up to several meters in length, and it produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a distinct midrib, which helps in identifying the species.

This variety is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
