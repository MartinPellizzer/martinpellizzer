Phyllanthus obscurus

Phyllanthus obscurus, commonly known as the dark phyllanthus, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as an herb or shrub, often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its botanical features include smooth, often hairy stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia.

The plant is also noted for its use in traditional medicine, where various parts are employed for their purported health benefits.
