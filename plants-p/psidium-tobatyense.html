Psidium tobatyense

Psidium tobatyense, commonly known as the Toba pineapple or Toba guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, citrus-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is usually yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor.
