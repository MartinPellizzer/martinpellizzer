Pseudocymbidium lineare

Pseudocymbidium lineare, commonly known as the linear orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces elongated, pendulous flower spikes with small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

The species is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in orchids.
