Photinia subumbellata var. villosa

Photinia subumbellata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its common name. The foliage is typically dark green with a silvery or grayish underside, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, white flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by red or orange fruits that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
