Pandanus fetosus

Pandanus fetosus, commonly known as the small pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanus genus, characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of leaves. The plant produces large, cone-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. Its fibrous leaves are used in traditional crafts and thatching.

The species thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in brackish or saline soils.
