Polyscias ashtonii

Polyscias ashtonii, commonly known as the Ashton's sycamore, is a member of the Araceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, a common feature among arum family members. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
