Porphyrocoma pohliana

Porphyrocoma pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in mild climates.
