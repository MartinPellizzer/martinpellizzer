Polypompholyx

Polypompholyx, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively. It thrives in moist environments and is often found in the eastern United States and parts of Asia.

The plant has been used historically for its aromatic properties and in traditional medicine.
