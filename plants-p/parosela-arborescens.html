Parosela arborescens

Parosela arborescens, commonly known as the tree parosela, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Parosela arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
