Portulaca poellnitziana

Portulaca poellnitziana, commonly known as the Mexican portulaca, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Portulaca poellnitziana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
