Palmifolium strictum

Palmifolium strictum, commonly known as the rigid-leaf palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant's leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a distinctive visual appearance. Its growth habit is upright, making it suitable for both ornamental and ecological purposes in humid environments.

The fruit produces edible seeds, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
