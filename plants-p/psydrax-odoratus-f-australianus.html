Psydrax odoratus f. australianus

Psydrax odoratus f. australianus, commonly known as the Australian sweet pepper.

This plant is a variant of the sweet pepper species native to Australia, characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and a distinctive, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.
