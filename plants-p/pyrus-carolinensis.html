Pyrus carolinensis

Pyrus carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina apple, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 meters, featuring a rounded crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in cymes. The fruit is a small, sweet apple that ripens in the fall, often used for culinary purposes.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in American horticulture.
