Pterygocarpus abyssinicus

Pterygocarpus abyssinicus, commonly known as the Abyssinian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark brown wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume containing several seeds, and the tree is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional medicine.
