Potamogeton lucens var. foliosus

Potamogeton lucens var. foliosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats in North America.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter wide, arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth makes it both a valuable indicator species and a potential nuisance in certain water bodies.
