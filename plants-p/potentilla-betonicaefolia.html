Potentilla betonicaefolia

Potentilla betonicaefolia, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
