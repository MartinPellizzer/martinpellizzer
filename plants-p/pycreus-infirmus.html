Pycreus infirmus

Pycreus infirmus, commonly known as the slender pycreus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is a short-lived perennial that thrives in dry, well-drained conditions.
