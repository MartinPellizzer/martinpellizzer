Prunus ceylanica var. parvifolia

Prunus ceylanica var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved Ceylon cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Sri Lanka and parts of India. The plant is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small drupes, typically red or yellow when ripe, and are edible.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
