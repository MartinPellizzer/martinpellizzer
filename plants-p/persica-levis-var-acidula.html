Persica levis var. acidula

Persica levis var. acidula, commonly known as the sour persimmon, is a variant of the persimmon plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, tart fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly serrated edge. This variety is often cultivated for its unique flavor profile and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it distinct from other persimmon varieties, particularly in terms of fruit acidity and growth habits.
