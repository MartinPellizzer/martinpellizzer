Polycarpaea helichrysoides

Polycarpaea helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces numerous small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
