Pycreus filicinus

Pycreus filicinus, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks or in dense undergrowth. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

The botanical features of Pycreus filicinus make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to tropical gardens.
