Potamogeton spoliatus

Potamogeton spoliatus, commonly known as the spoliated pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly ribbed, adapted for efficient photosynthesis in water. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which develop into small, round fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton spoliatus is often found in still or slow-moving waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
