Podocoma reineckii

Podocoma reineckii, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration. The plant produces small, white or yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its thick, waxy cuticle and deep root system enable it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

Podocoma reineckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic landscapes.
