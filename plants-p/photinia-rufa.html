Photinia rufa

Photinia rufa, commonly known as the red tip photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish at the tips, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that attract birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
