Polygonifolia latifolia

Polygonifolia latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed polygon, is a herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive nodes and often edible parts. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
