Polygala calcarea f. grandis

Polygala calcarea f. grandis, commonly known as the large calcarea polygala, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its unique, often spiny stems and delicate, raceme-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, usually yellow or greenish flowers with distinctive, thread-like petals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
