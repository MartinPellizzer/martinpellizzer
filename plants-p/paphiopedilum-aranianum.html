Paphiopedilum aranianum

Paphiopedilum aranianum, commonly known as the Armenian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Armenia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, broad, elliptical leaf and produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive lip. The plant has a complex root system adapted to its rocky and mountainous habitat. It blooms in late summer, typically from July to August.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
