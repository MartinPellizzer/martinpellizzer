Plantago dombeyi

Plantago dombeyi, commonly known as the dombey's plantain, is a flowering plant species native to South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks.

Plantago dombeyi is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
