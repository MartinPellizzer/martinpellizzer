Ponthieva gracilis

Ponthieva gracilis, commonly known as the slender staghorn fern, is a species of epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that grow in a cascading pattern, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates to support its growth. Its rhizomes anchor it to trees or other structures, allowing it to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Ponthieva gracilis is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or mounted on driftwood due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
