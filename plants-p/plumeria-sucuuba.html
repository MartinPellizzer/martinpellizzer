Plumeria sucuuba

Plumeria sucuuba, commonly known as the sweet scented plumeria, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, waxy flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it produces small, round fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Plumeria sucuuba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
