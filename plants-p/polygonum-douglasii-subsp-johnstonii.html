Polygonum douglasii subsp. johnstonii

Polygonum douglasii subsp.

johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from red to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in stream banks and wet meadows.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other Polygonum species.
