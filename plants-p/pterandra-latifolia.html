Pterandra latifolia

Pterandra latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pterandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
