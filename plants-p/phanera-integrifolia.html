Phanera integrifolia

Phanera integrifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf phanera, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are usually alternate and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Phanera integrifolia is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
