Polypodium lorentzii

Polypodium lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees in humid, shaded forest environments. The plant has a rhizomatous structure with dense, overlapping fronds that form a compact rosette. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, shield-like shape, which helps it retain moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
