Polygala diffusa

Polygala diffusa, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and small, usually green or yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The flowers are clustered in spicate inflorescences and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
