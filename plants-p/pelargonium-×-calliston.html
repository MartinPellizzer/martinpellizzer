Pelargonium × calliston

Pelargonium × calliston, commonly known as the ivy-leaf geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.

Pelargonium × calliston is also popular for its adaptability to different light levels and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
