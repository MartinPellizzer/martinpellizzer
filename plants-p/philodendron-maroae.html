Philodendron maroae

Philodendron maroae, commonly known as the "Black Velvet Philodendron," is a striking tropical vine native to the rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves that range from deep green to almost black in color, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are not particularly showy. Its foliage is highly sought after in the horticultural trade for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Philodendron maroae thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and well-draining soil.
