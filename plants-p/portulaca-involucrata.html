Portulaca involucrata

Portulaca involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety coating. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

Portulaca involucrata is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
