Paphiopedilum mirabile

Paphiopedilum mirabile, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature in its pollination strategy. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green hues, with a prominent central pouch.

Paphiopedilum mirabile is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
