Pediocactus uncinatus var. wrightii

Pediocactus uncinatus var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's hooked cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining sandy or gravelly soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
