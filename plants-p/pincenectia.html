Pincenectia

Pincenectia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pincenectia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds.

It features slender, compound leaves with a finely divided structure, typical of many members of its family. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, a common floral arrangement in the carrot family. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its medicinal properties.

Pincenectia is often found in moist, temperate regions and is valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
