Pyrus hissarica

Pyrus hissarica, commonly known as the Hissar pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible pears that are typically sweet and aromatic. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The wood of Pyrus hissarica is also valued for its fine grain and durability.
