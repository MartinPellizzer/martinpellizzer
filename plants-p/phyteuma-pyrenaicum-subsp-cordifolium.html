Phyteuma pyrenaicum subsp. cordifolium

Phyteuma pyrenaicum subsp.

cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to pink.

The leaves are heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
