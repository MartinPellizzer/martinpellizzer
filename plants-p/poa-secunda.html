Poa secunda

Poa secunda, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. This plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Poa secunda produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
