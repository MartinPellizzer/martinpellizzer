Plexipus humbertii

Plexipus humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's plexipus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring dense, overlapping leaves that form a rosette structure to conserve moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that bloom during the brief summer months, relying on wind pollination due to the absence of pollinators at high elevations. Its root system is highly adapted to rocky soils, allowing it to anchor firmly and access limited water sources.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, Plexipus humbertii is considered a vulnerable species with limited distribution and ecological significance.
