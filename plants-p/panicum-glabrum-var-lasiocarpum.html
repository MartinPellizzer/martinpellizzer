Panicum glabrum var. lasiocarpum

Panicum glabrum var. lasiocarpum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and ecological role in stabilizing soil in various habitats.
