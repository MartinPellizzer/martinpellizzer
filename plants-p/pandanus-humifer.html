Pandanus humifer

Pandanus humifer, commonly known as the swamp pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves that often droop downward. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its roots are adapted to waterlogged soils, making it well-suited for swampy or wetland environments.

The plant is often used in traditional crafts and for its ornamental value.
