Phlox kelseyi subvar. minor

Phlox kelseyi subvar. minor, commonly known as the western phlox, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
