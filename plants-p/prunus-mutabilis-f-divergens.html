Prunus mutabilis f. divergens

Prunus mutabilis f. divergens, commonly known as the variegated cherry, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and pink variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, typically pink or white, which give way to small, edible fruits. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

This ornamental tree is prized for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
