Plantago perpusilla

Plantago perpusilla, commonly known as the small plantain, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
