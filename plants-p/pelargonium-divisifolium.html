Pelargonium divisifolium

Pelargonium divisifolium, commonly known as the leafy geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, deeply lobed leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
