Piptochaetium laevissimum

Piptochaetium laevissimum, commonly known as the smooth hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences, which are usually inconspicuous and clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential use in restoration projects.
