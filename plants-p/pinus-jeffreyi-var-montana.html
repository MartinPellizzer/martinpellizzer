Pinus jeffreyi var. montana

Pinus jeffreyi var. montana, commonly known as Jeffrey pine, is a variant of the Jeffrey pine native to the western United States.

It is a tall, coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure. The plant has a rugged, rugged appearance with a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Its cones are typically woody and contain seeds that are dispersed by wind.
