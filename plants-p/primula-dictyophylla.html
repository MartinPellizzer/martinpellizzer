Primula dictyophylla

Primula dictyophylla, commonly known as the mountain primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features hairy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
