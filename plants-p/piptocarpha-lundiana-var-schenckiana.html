Piptocarpha lundiana var. schenckiana

Piptocarpha lundiana var. schenckiana, commonly known as Schenck's bluebunch wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

This variety is valued for its erosion control capabilities and forage value for livestock.
