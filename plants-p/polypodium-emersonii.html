Polypodium emersonii

Polypodium emersonii, commonly known as the Emerson's shield fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a leathery texture and a deep green color. It produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori.

This plant is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in woodland ecosystems.
