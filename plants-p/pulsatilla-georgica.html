Pulsatilla georgica

Pulsatilla georgica, commonly known as the Georgian pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that are typically pale yellow to pink. The plant features a hairy stem that supports a single, large, cup-shaped flower with many showy stamens. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Pulsatilla georgica thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.
