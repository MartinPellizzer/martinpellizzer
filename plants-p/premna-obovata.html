Premna obovata

Premna obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed glorybower, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to broadly ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their potential health benefits.
