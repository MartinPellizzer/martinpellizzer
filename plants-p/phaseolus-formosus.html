Phaseolus formosus

Phaseolus formosus, commonly known as the sweet bean or the purple runner bean, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its hairy, purple-tinged pods and edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are heart-shaped and compound, with three leaflets.

The botanical features of Phaseolus formosus make it a valuable crop for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
