Paraprasophyllum limnetes

Paraprasophyllum limnetes, commonly known as the water lily of the limnetic zone, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It possesses broad, floating leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically showy, with a distinctive structure that includes a spadix and a crown-like calyx. The plant plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.

It is adapted to thrive in still or slow-moving waters, where it can anchor itself with its rhizomes.
