Physostegia virginiana var. parviflora

Physostegia virginiana var. parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved white beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
