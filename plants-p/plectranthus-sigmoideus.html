Plectranthus sigmoideus

Plectranthus sigmoideus, commonly known as the purple-leaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically purple or violet in color, with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it suitable for container planting or as a ground cover.
