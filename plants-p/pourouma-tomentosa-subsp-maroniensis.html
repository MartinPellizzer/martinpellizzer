Pourouma tomentosa subsp. maroniensis

Pourouma tomentosa subsp.

maroniensis, commonly known as the Maroniensis areca palm, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of South America. It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its pinnate leaves and clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its trunk is often covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
