Psorospermum lamianum

Psorospermum lamianum, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to round, green berries that turn orange-red when mature. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
