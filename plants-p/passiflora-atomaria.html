Passiflora atomaria

Passiflora atomaria, commonly known as the red passionflower, is a species of flowering plant in the family Passifloraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. Its fruit, known as a passionfruit, is edible and has a distinctive aroma.

Passiflora atomaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the flavor of its fruit.
