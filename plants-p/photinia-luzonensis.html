Photinia luzonensis

Photinia luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon photinia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy red flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

The flowers, which appear in clusters, are a key feature, often blooming in spring and attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.
