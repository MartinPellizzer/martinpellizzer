Pachyctenium mirabile

Pachyctenium mirabile, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, lantern-shaped inflorescences that resemble small, glowing orbs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Pachyctenium mirabile is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
