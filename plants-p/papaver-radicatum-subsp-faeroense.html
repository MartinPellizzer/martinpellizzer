Papaver radicatum subsp. faeroense

Papaver radicatum subsp.

faeroense, commonly known as the Faeroese poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Faeroe Islands. It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has a taproot system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often clustered at the base. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
