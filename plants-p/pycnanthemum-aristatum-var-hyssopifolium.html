Pycnanthemum aristatum var. hyssopifolium

Pycnanthemum aristatum var. hyssopifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often aromatic. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
