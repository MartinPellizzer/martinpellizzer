Pycreus diaphanus var. laxus

Pycreus diaphanus var. laxus, commonly known as the slender ghost orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green to white inflorescence and small, showy flowers that resemble those of its close relatives. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle, and relies on specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake. Its flowers are pollinated by moths, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air circulation.

Due to its restricted habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
