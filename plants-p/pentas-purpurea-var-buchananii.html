Pentas purpurea var. buchananii

Pentas purpurea var. buchananii, commonly known as the purple pentas, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from spring to autumn.
