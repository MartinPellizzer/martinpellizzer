Paspalum distichum var. indutum

Paspalum distichum var. indutum, commonly known as silky paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist or wetland environments, making it a common species in marshy areas. Its rhizomes and stolons allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

It is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its high biomass production.
