Pseudanthus vermicularis

Pseudanthus vermicularis, commonly known as the threadleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a composite head, giving it a sunflower-like appearance. The plant has linear, thread-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in grasslands and open areas.

Pseudanthus vermicularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
