Piper albescens

Piper albescens, commonly known as white pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white berries that are used to produce white pepper. The berries contain a high concentration of piperine, which gives the spice its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its culinary and medicinal uses.
