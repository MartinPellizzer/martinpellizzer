Porophyllum pringlei

Porophyllum pringlei, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its bright orange to red flower heads attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Porophyllum pringlei is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
