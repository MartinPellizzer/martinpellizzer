Paspalum clandestinum

Paspalum clandestinum, commonly known as the common paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is often found in both natural and agricultural environments.
