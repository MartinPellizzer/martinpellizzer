Phlomis alpina

Phlomis alpina, commonly known as Alpine betony, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pale purple or blue, blooming in summer.

Phlomis alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine environments.
