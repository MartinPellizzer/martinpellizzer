Phanera dasycarpa

Phanera dasycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited phanera, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, woody pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by thick, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
