Polypodium sericeum

Polypodium sericeum, commonly known as the silky polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often covered in a silvery, velvety coating. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks in humid environments. Its rhizome is creeping and produces multiple fertile and sterile fronds.

Polypodium sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
