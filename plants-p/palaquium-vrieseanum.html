Palaquium vrieseanum

Palaquium vrieseanum, commonly known as the Vrieseanum apple chestnut, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces edible, orange-red fruits that are similar in appearance to small apples or chestnuts. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
