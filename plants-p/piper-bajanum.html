Piper bajanum

Piper bajanum, commonly known as the Bajanum pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, thin fruits containing aromatic seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
