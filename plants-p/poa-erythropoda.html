Poa erythropoda

Poa erythropoda, commonly known as the redstem needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge at the base. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and is often found in association with other native grasses.

Poa erythropoda plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
