Protea auriculata

Protea auriculata, commonly known as the silver leaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous overlapping bracts that resemble petals. The plant features a stout, woody stem with broad, leathery leaves that have a silvery-gray appearance. Native to the Western and Eastern Cape regions, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a garden plant.
