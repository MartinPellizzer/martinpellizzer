Prunus aquifolioides

Prunus aquifolioides, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
