Pittosporum tenuifolium

Pittosporum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a mild, sweet taste. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Pittosporum tenuifolium is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
