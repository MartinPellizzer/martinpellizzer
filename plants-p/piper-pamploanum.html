Piper pamploanum

Piper pamploanum, commonly known as the Pamplean pepper, is a climbing shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used for their aromatic properties and are sometimes brewed into a tea.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
