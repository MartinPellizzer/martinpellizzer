Pilosella dolinensis

Pilosella dolinensis, commonly known as the Dolin Valley hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Eastern Carpathians.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at elevations between 1,200 and 2,200 meters. It produces yellow flowers with white ray florets, and its leaves are hairy and linear.

Pilosella dolinensis is an important component of high-altitude ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
