Pluchea squarrosa

Pluchea squarrosa, commonly known as the roughleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its hairy, often woolly leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
