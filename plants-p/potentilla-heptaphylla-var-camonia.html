Potentilla heptaphylla var. camonia

Potentilla heptaphylla var. camonia, commonly known as the Camonian cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in early summer. The plant has finely divided, serrated leaves that are often covered in a soft, woolly coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners interested in native and hardy plants.
