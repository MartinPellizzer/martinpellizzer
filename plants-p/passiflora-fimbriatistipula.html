Passiflora fimbriatistipula

Passiflora fimbriatistipula, commonly known as the fringed-stipule passionflower, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces tendrils that aid in climbing and supports its growth on trees or shrubs. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with finely serrated edges.

The fruit, which develops from the flower, is a small, edible berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.
