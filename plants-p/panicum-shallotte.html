Panicum shallotte

Panicum shallotte, commonly known as silver millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble downy plumes. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for dry climates and rocky soils. It is often used as a ornamental grass in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance.

The plant also serves as a forage crop for livestock in some regions.
