Phoradendron serpyllifolium var. aphyllum

Phoradendron serpyllifolium var. aphyllum, commonly known as the leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true leaves and instead has scale-like structures that perform photosynthesis. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers and red berries that are dispersed by birds. It is native to regions of the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for study in plant ecology and parasitism.
