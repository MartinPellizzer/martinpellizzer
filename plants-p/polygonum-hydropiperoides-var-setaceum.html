Polygonum hydropiperoides var. setaceum

Polygonum hydropiperoides var. setaceum, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features upright stems covered in fine, hairy glands, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is adapted to moist environments and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and marshy areas.
