Phacelia crenulata var. funera

Phacelia crenulata var. funera, commonly known as the funereal phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
