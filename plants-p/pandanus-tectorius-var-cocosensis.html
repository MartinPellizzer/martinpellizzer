Pandanus tectorius var. cocosensis

Pandanus tectorius var. cocosensis, commonly known as the coconut pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fibrous, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural fiber source.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments.
