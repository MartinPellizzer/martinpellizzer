Protea wendlandii

Protea wendlandii, commonly known as the Wendland's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical shape. The plant is native to the Western and Northern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces a unique, hollow stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, supporting its dense floral display.

Protea wendlandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
