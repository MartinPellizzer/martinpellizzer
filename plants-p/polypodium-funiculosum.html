Polypodium funiculosum

Polypodium funiculosum, commonly known as the hanging fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rock crevices. The plant has a creeping rhizome with long, slender fronds that drape downward, giving it its common name. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a delicate, feathery appearance.

Polypodium funiculosum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
