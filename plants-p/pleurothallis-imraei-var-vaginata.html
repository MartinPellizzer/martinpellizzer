Pleurothallis imraei var. vaginata

Pleurothallis imraei var. vaginata, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically produces small, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in moisture retention. Its flowers are usually borne on short, erect inflorescences, and they bloom during the cooler months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
