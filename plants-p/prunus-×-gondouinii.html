Prunus × gondouinii

Prunus × gondouinii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × gondouinii, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruit is a small, sweet drupe.

Prunus × gondouinii is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and seasonal blooms.
