Pernettya andina

Pernettya andina, commonly known as the Andean cowberry, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves and produces clusters of white flowers in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Pernettya andina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
