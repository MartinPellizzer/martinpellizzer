Phellodendron sinense

Phellodendron sinense, commonly known as the Chinese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark that exudes a yellowish sap. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged fruits, which are typical of the Rutaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the bark containing compounds used in traditional Chinese medicine.
