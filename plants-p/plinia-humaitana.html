Plinia humaitana

Plinia humaitana, commonly known as the Brazilian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, humid environments.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, making it a valued plant in local agriculture and cuisine.
