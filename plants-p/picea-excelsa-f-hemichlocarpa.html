Picea excelsa f. hemichlocarpa

Picea excelsa f. hemichlocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf white spruce, is a rare and distinctive variant of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are shorter and more clustered than those of the typical white spruce. This form is adapted to high-altitude environments, often found in mountainous regions with cold climates. The tree has a conical shape and produces small, winged seeds in cone-like structures.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
