Pleurogyne lloydiodes

Pleurogyne lloydiodes, commonly known as the slender pleurogyne, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy or seasonally flooded areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
