Primula edelbergii

Primula edelbergii, commonly known as the Edelberg's primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Primula edelbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in rock gardens and alpine displays.
