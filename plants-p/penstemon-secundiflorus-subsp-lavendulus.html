Penstemon secundiflorus subsp. lavendulus

Penstemon secundiflorus subsp.

lavendulus, commonly known as the lavender penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, lavender-pink to violet flowers.

The species features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
