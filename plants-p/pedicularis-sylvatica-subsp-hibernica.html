Pedicularis sylvatica subsp. hibernica

Pedicularis sylvatica subsp.

hibernica, commonly known as the winter lousewort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes or in open woods. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
