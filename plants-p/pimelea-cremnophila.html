Pimelea cremnophila

Pimelea cremnophila, commonly known as the silverleaf pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of whitish wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Pimelea cremnophila is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
