Paeonia corallina var. kesrouanensis

Paeonia corallina var. kesrouanensis, commonly known as the Kesrouan peony, is a rare and endemic species native to Lebanon.

It is characterized by its distinctive coral-pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has deeply lobed, compound leaves that are typically green with a slight hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
