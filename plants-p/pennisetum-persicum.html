Pennisetum persicum

Pennisetum persicum, commonly known as Persian grass, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Pennisetum persicum is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience to various environmental conditions.
