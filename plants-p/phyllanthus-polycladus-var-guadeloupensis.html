Phyllanthus polycladus var. guadeloupensis

Phyllanthus polycladus var. guadeloupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe phyllanthus, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
