Philodendron deflexum

Philodendron deflexum, commonly known as the drooping philodendron, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often curl downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Philodendron deflexum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
