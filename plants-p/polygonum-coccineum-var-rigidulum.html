Polygonum coccineum var. rigidulum

Polygonum coccineum var. rigidulum, commonly known as the rigid trailing knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink to red in color, blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
