Punica granatum var. macrocarpa

Punica granatum var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Persian pomegranate, is a variant of the pomegranate tree characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruits.

This cultivar is native to the Middle East and has been cultivated for centuries for its ornamental value and edible fruits. The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin, while the flowers are typically red or pink. The fruit develops in clusters and can weigh significantly more than the standard pomegranate, sometimes reaching up to 10 pounds.

It is valued for its vibrant foliage, ornamental appeal, and the potential for producing high-quality, sweet fruit.
