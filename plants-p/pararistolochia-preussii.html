Pararistolochia preussii

Pararistolochia preussii, commonly known as the preussii birthwort, is a climbing vine native to parts of Asia, including China and India.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, pipe-like flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
