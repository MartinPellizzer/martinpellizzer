Phalaris americana

Phalaris americana, commonly known as wild oat, is a grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear small, two-lobed flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Wild oat is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various wildlife.
