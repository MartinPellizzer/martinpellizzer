Polygonum ciliinode

Polygonum ciliinode, commonly known as the silky smartweed, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and have a distinctive ciliate margin, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or red.

The plant is often found in moist soils along streambanks and in wetlands, thriving in temperate climates.
