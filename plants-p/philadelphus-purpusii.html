Philadelphus purpusii

Philadelphus purpusii, commonly known as the Chinese mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often peeling in thin strips as the plant matures.
