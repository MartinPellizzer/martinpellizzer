Potentilla nivea subsp. chamissonis

Potentilla nivea subsp.

chamissonis, commonly known as white cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to alpine and arctic regions of North America. It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers, which typically bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in tundra and alpine meadows, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
