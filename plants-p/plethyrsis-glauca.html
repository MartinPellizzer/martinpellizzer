Plethyrsis glauca

Plethyrsis glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily in new areas.

Due to its invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
