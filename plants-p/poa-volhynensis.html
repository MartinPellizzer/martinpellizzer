Poa volhynensis

Poa volhynensis, commonly known as the Volhynian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and hairless, with a bluish-green hue.

Poa volhynensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
