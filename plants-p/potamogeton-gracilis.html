Potamogeton gracilis

Potamogeton gracilis, commonly known as slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by narrow, boat-shaped fruits. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Potamogeton gracilis is often found in slow-moving or still waters such as ponds, lakes, and wetlands.
