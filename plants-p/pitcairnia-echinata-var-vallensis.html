Pitcairnia echinata var. vallensis

Pitcairnia echinata var. vallensis, commonly known as the Valley pitcairnia, is a bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, spiky leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, inflorescences with vibrant orange or red flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
