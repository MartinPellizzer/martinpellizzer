Pinus densiflora f. brevifolia

Pinus densiflora f. brevifolia, commonly known as the Japanese black pine, is a rare and unique form of the Japanese white pine.

It is characterized by its shorter, more compact needles compared to the typical variety, giving it a denser appearance. This form is native to southern Japan and is often found in coastal areas where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces cones that are typically smaller than those of the standard species, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in the wild.
