Passiflora monadelpha

Passiflora monadelpha, commonly known as the silky passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a unique blend of blue, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are often consumed raw or used in jams. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Passiflora monadelpha is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential sedative properties.
