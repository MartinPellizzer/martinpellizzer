Peiranisia scopulorum

Peiranisia scopulorum, commonly known as the rock peiranisia, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in drought-tolerant gardens.
