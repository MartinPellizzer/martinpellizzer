Paspalum comasii

Paspalum comasii, commonly known as the clustered paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, dense foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Paspalum comasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
