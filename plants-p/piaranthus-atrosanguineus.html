Piaranthus atrosanguineus

Piaranthus atrosanguineus, commonly known as the black blood lily, is a striking member of the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dark red to almost black flowers, which bloom in late summer. It has strap-like, evergreen leaves that form a dense rosette.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
