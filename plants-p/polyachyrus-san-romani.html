Polyachyrus san-romani

Polyachyrus san-romani, commonly known as the San Romani cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming during the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
