Piper asperulibaccum

Piper asperulibaccum, commonly known as the rough-leaved pepper vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into green, fleshy berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and pharmacological studies.
