Pelargonium villiferum

Pelargonium villiferum, commonly known as the African geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
