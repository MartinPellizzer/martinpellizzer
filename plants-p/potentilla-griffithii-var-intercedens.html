Potentilla griffithii var. intercedens

Potentilla griffithii var. intercedens, commonly known as the Kashmir rose or Himalayan potentilla, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces showy, single or double flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens in temperate regions.
