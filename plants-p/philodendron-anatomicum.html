Philodendron anatomicum

Philodendron anatomicum, commonly known as the Anatomicum Philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. Its aerial roots allow it to climb trees and other structures, making it a common sight in humid, shaded environments.

Philodendron anatomicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
