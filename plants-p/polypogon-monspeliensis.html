Polypogon monspeliensis

Polypogon monspeliensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a bottlebrush, composed of tightly packed spikelets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
