Polygala matteiana

Polygala matteiana, commonly known as the Madagascan milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a hairy texture, while its flowers are often pink or purple in color.

Polygala matteiana is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native environment.
