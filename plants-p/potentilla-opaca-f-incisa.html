Potentilla opaca f. incisa

Potentilla opaca f. incisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and elliptical, with a distinctive serrated edge. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in sunny to partially shaded environments.
