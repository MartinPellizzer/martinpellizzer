Protea rudatisii

Protea rudatisii, commonly known as the Knysna protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to white bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
