Pandanus scopula

Pandanus scopula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spiky, branched structure that bears small, fragrant flowers.

The fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains a single seed, often used in local cuisines and crafts.
