Poa mucronata

Poa mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually borne at the tip of the stem.

Poa mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
