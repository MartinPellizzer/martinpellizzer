Primula septentrionalis var. subulifera

Primula septentrionalis var. subulifera, commonly known as the northern primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers in early spring. The leaves are typically lanceolate or subuliform, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
