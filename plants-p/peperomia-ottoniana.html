Peperomia ottoniana

Peperomia ottoniana, commonly known as the silver peperomia, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It features broad, waxy, silver-green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or red, which are followed by dark, elliptical berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
