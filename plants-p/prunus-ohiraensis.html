Prunus ohiraensis

Prunus ohiraensis, commonly known as Ohira's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a smooth margin, while its fruit is a small, dark red drupe.

Prunus ohiraensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
