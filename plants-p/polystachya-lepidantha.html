Polystachya lepidantha

Polystachya lepidantha, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, thriving on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, bloom in summer and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
