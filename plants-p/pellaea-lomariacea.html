Pellaea lomariacea

Pellaea lomariacea, commonly known as the silver fringed fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients efficiently.

The leaves are usually divided into small, linear segments, contributing to its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
