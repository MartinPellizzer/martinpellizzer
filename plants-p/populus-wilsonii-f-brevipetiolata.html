Populus wilsonii f. brevipetiolata

Populus wilsonii f. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled poplar, is a distinctive variant of the Wilson poplar.

It is characterized by its shorter petioles, which are the stalks connecting the leaves to the stem. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a serrated margin and a smooth upper surface. This cultivar is often valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
