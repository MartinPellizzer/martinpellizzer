Passiflora nigelliflora

Passiflora nigelliflora, commonly known as the black seed passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue petals, along with a crown of filaments. The plant produces dark, almost black, seeds that are enclosed within a fleshy, red or orange aril. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora nigelliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
