Piper augustum var. magnifolium

Piper augustum var. magnifolium, commonly known as the magnificent pepper plant, is a tropical species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
