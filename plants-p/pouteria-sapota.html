Pouteria sapota

Pouteria sapota, commonly known as the cherimoya, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a unique, creamy fruit with a texture similar to a hybrid of a banana and a strawberry. Its fruit contains a high amount of nutrients, including vitamins C and B-complex, as well as minerals like potassium and magnesium.

The plant is typically cultivated for its edible fruit, which is prized for its sweet, delicate flavor.
