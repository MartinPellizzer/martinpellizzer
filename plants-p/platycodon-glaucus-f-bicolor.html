Platycodon glaucus f. bicolor

Platycodon glaucus f. bicolor, commonly known as the blue bellflower or twin-color bellflower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically feature a blue or purple upper lip and a white or yellow lower lip. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blooms that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, adding to its visual appeal.
