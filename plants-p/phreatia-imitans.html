Phreatia imitans

Phreatia imitans, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests. The plant has a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its flowers are usually green or white with a distinctive, intricate pattern that resembles other orchid species.

Phreatia imitans is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of other orchids, a trait that may aid in pollination.
