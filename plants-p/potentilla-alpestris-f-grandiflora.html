Potentilla alpestris f. grandiflora

Potentilla alpestris f. grandiflora, commonly known as the alpine cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, bright yellow petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually divided into five lobes, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.".

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
