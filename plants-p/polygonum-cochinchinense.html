Polygonum cochinchinense

Polygonum cochinchinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinct nodes and often hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and often have a reddish or purple tinge.

The small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in late summer to early autumn.
