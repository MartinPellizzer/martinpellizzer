Poa thurmanniana

Poa thurmanniana, commonly known as Thurmann's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
