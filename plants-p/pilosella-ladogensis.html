Pilosella ladogensis

Pilosella ladogensis, commonly known as the Ladoga hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the region around Lake Ladoga in Russia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, yellowish to white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Pilosella ladogensis is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
