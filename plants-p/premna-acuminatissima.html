Premna acuminatissima

Premna acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved curlleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
