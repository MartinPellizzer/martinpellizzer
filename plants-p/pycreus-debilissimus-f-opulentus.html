Pycreus debilissimus f. opulentus

Pycreus debilissimus f. opulentus, commonly known as the opulent pycreus, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped with a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and vulnerability to habitat loss, it is considered a conservation concern.
