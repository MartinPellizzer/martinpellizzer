Pyrethrum ptarmiciflorum

Pyrethrum ptarmiciflorum, commonly known as the goldenroot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

However, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes due to its limited yield and specific growing conditions.
