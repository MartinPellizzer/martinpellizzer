Phyllanthus niruri var. amarus

Phyllanthus niruri var. amarus, commonly known as stonebreaker or wild coffee, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a small, shrub-like plant that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and its fruits are small, round, and often contain a single seed. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate harsh conditions and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The leaves are usually opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.
