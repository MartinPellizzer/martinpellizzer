Plectranthus hispidus

Plectranthus hispidus, commonly known as the rough plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus hispidus is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blossoms.
