Pleurothallis saltatoria

Pleurothallis saltatoria, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant features small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a unique structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern, allowing it to efficiently capture moisture and sunlight.

Pleurothallis saltatoria is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
