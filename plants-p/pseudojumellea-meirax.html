Pseudojumellea meirax

Pseudojumellea meirax, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shrubland and rocky areas, adapting well to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
