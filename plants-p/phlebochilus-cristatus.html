Phlebochilus cristatus

Phlebochilus cristatus, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy as well.

The species is notable for its distinctive crown-like appearance, with flowers clustered at the top of the stem.
