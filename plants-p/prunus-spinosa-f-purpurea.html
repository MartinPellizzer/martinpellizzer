Prunus spinosa f. purpurea

Prunus spinosa f. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered blackthorn, is a cultivar of the blackthorn plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has thorny branches and produces small, dark blue berries in the fall. Its foliage turns a striking red or orange in the autumn, adding ornamental value.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
