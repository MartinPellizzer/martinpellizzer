Potentilla topolkae

Potentilla topolkae, commonly known as the White Potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically divided into several lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.
