Phyllanthus rhamnifolius

Phyllanthus rhamnifolius, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy berries. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in various parts of South Asia.
