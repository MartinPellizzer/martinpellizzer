Physaria geyeri var. typica

Physaria geyeri var. typica, commonly known as the desert primerose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its leaves are linear and succulent, adapted to conserve water in harsh desert conditions.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a large number of seeds, contributing to its successful propagation in its native range.
