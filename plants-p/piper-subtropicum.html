Piper subtropicum

Piper subtropicum, commonly known as the Indian pepper vine, is a climbing plant belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, slender berries that turn red when mature. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
