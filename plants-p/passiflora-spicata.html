Passiflora spicata

Passiflora spicata, commonly known as the spiked passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces clusters of small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, often climbing or trailing along supports.

The botanical features of Passiflora spicata make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
