Pyrus takhtajanii

Pyrus takhtajanii, commonly known as the Takhtajan pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountains of Central Asia, particularly in regions of Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant is characterized by its deciduous foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, edible pomes that are similar to pears.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and its role in the study of pear evolution.
