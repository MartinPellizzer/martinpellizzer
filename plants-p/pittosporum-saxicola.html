Pittosporum saxicola

Pittosporum saxicola, commonly known as the rock pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its delicate blossoms.

Pittosporum saxicola is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
