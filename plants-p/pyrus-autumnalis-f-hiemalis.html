Pyrus autumnalis f. hiemalis

Pyrus autumnalis f. hiemalis, commonly known as the winter pear.

This ornamental plant is a cold-hardy cultivar of the common pear, characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, glossy dark green foliage. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

It is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
