Pelargonium trifoliatum

Pelargonium trifoliatum, commonly known as the three-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Pelargonium trifoliatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
