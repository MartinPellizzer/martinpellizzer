Pectis floribunda

Pectis floribunda, commonly known as the showy pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Pectis floribunda is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
