Portulaca vilmoriana

Portulaca vilmoriana, commonly known as the moss rose, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is a succulent species that thrives in hot, dry climates and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which bloom throughout the summer. Its ability to survive in poor soil conditions makes it a hardy and low-maintenance plant for gardens.

Portulaca vilmoriana is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
