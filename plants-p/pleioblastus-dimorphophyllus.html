Pleioblastus dimorphophyllus

Pleioblastus dimorphophyllus, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that often display a silvery or grayish hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a clumping growth habit and can form dense thickets, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The leaves are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
