Paspalum plicatulum var. oligostachyum

Paspalum plicatulum var. oligostachyum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. The plant is often found in wetland environments, including marshes and along riverbanks. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
