Pimpinella silvatica

Pimpinella silvatica, commonly known as the forest cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in woodland and shaded areas, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its fragrant foliage and delicate blooms make it a notable component of forest ecosystems.
