Psychotria quinquecuspis

Psychotria quinquecuspis, commonly known as the "Bushman's button" or "Cape sour orange," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and forest margins.

Psychotria quinquecuspis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.
