Polypodium sandwicense

Polypodium sandwicense, commonly known as the Hawaiian wall fern, is a species of fern native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that grow in a rosette formation. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky crevices or on stream banks. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a finely divided appearance that gives it a lacy texture.

This fern plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to the biodiversity of the unique Hawaiian flora.
