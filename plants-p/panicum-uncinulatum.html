Panicum uncinulatum

Panicum uncinulatum, commonly known as silky panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
