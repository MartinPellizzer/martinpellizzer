Pittosporum hirsutum

Pittosporum hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Pittosporum hirsutum is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects.
