Potentilla persica

Potentilla persica, commonly known as Persian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
