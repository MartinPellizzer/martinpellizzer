Pelargonium × megiston

Pelargonium × megiston, commonly known as the geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, often with a velvety texture. This plant is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for many garden settings.
