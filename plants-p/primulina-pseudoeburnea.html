Primulina pseudoeburnea

Primulina pseudoeburnea, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a rare and striking epiphytic plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a ghostly appearance, and its small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. It is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts due to its unique aesthetic and challenging cultivation requirements.

Due to its rarity and specific needs, it is considered a delicate and rare species in the world of horticulture.
