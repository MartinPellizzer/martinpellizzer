Polypodium vesicularipaleaceum

Polypodium vesicularipaleaceum, commonly known as the vesicularipaleaceum fern, is a tropical species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in the understory of tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of its leaves.

The species is noted for its unique ability to absorb and retain moisture, which aids in its survival in nutrient-poor soils.
