Plumeria mexicana

Plumeria mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican frangipani, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that belongs to the Apocynaceae family. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.
