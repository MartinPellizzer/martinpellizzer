Pelargonium × filifolium

Pelargonium × filifolium, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that resemble ivy leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
