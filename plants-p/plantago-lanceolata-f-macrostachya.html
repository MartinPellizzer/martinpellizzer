Plantago lanceolata f. macrostachya

Plantago lanceolata f. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive floral structure.

This plant is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, disturbed soils, often found along roadsides and in meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. The leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, providing a rough texture and aiding in water retention.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties and is also used as a natural remedy for various ailments.
