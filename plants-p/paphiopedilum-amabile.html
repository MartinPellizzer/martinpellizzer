Paphiopedilum amabile

Paphiopedilum amabile, commonly known as the sweet lady's slipper, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature that sets it apart from other orchid species. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white with a yellow throat, and it produces small, greenish-yellow fruits.

Paphiopedilum amabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
