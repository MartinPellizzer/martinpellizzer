Porterella eximia

Porterella eximia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Porterella eximia is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively, making it both a useful forage plant and a potential invasive species.
