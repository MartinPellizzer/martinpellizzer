Peltopsis

Peltopsis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plants typically grow as epiphytes or lithophytes, attaching themselves to trees or rocks for support. Their flowers are characterized by their intricate, often spider-like appearance, with long, slender petals and sepals.

Peltopsis species are valued for their ornamental beauty and are often cultivated in greenhouses or as houseplants.
