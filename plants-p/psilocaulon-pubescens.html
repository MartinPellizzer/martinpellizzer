Psilocaulon pubescens

Psilocaulon pubescens, commonly known as the hairy psilocaulon, is a unique species within the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, often glandular stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in whorled arrangements, and can display a range of colors from white to purple.

While not a hallucinogenic species like some other psilocybin-containing fungi, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitats.
