Picea vulgaris var. viminalis

Picea vulgaris var. viminalis, commonly known as the weeping spruce, is a variant of the white spruce that exhibits distinctive cascading foliage.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that create a unique, flowing appearance, making it a popular ornamental tree. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches. It thrives in cooler climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
