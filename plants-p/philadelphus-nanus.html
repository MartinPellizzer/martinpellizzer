Philadelphus nanus

Philadelphus nanus, commonly known as the dwarf mock orange, is a compact shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blossoms. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a foundation plant due to its hardy nature.

Philadelphus nanus is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
