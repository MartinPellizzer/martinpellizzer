Paphiopedilum epidendricum

Paphiopedilum epidendricum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, fleshy rhizome and a dense, hairy root system. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and grows in shaded, humid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
