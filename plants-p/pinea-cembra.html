Pinea cembra

Pinea cembra, commonly known as the Swiss pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Alps.

It is characterized by its slender, upward-curving needles and cone-like structures that hang from the branches. The tree thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in subalpine and alpine zones. Its wood is dense and durable, historically used for construction and furniture.

Pinea cembra plays a vital role in mountain ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
