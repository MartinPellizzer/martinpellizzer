Potentilla viridior

Potentilla viridior, commonly known as green potentilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring multiple stems that are often branched and covered in fine, grayish hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, or yellow, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Potentilla viridior thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
