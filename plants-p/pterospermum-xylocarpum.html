Pterospermum xylocarpum

Pterospermum xylocarpum, commonly known as the Chinese silk tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain flattened, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
