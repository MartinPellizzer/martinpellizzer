Picea koraiensis subsp. tonaiensis

Picea koraiensis subsp.

tonaiensis, commonly known as the Tonai fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Korean Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves.

The cones are typically small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. This subspecies thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the temperate forest ecosystems in its native habitat.
