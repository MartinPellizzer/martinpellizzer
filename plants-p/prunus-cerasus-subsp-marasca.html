Prunus cerasus subsp. marasca

Prunus cerasus subsp.

marasca, commonly known as the marasca cherry, is a small shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula. It produces small, yellowish-red fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe, usually in late summer.

The fruit is characterized by its high acidity and intense flavor, making it a key ingredient in traditional jams and liqueurs. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other cherry species.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
