Primula sempervivodes

Primula sempervivodes, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers typically range in color from white to pink and yellow, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy perennial that is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in cooler climates.
