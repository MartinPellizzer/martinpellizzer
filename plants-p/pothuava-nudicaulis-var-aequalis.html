Pothuava nudicaulis var. aequalis

Pothuava nudicaulis var. aequalis, commonly known as the slender pothuava, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
