Peperomia subglabra var. tuamotensis

Peperomia subglabra var. tuamotensis, commonly known as the Tuamotu peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia.

It features thick, fleshy, waxy leaves that are typically green with a subtle reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a desirable addition to succulent collections.
