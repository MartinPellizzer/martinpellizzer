Patagonium radicans

Patagonium radicans, commonly known as the creeping Patagonian plant, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of South America.

It features a sprawling growth habit with fibrous, rhizomatous stems that allow it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, and its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry climates.
