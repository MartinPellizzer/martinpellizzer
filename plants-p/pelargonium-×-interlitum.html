Pelargonium × interlitum

Pelargonium × interlitum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, slightly lobed leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
