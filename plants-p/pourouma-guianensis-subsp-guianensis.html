Pourouma guianensis subsp. guianensis

Pourouma guianensis subsp.

guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan areca, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, fibrous fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
