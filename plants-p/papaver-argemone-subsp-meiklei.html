Papaver argemone subsp. meiklei

Papaver argemone subsp.

meiklei, commonly known as the Meikle's poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features showy, bright yellow to orange flowers with prominent dark centers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its vibrant floral display.
