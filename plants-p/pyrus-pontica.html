Pyrus pontica

Pyrus pontica, commonly known as the Caucasian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in mountainous areas.

Pyrus pontica is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in horticulture.
