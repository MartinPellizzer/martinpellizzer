Polygonella gracilis

Polygonella gracilis, commonly known as the slender polygonella, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually less than 30 cm tall, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
