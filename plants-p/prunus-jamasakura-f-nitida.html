Prunus jamasakura f. nitida

Prunus jamasakura f. nitida, commonly known as the Shirofugen cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species, which is a type of sakura or cherry blossom.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that bloom in early spring, providing a stunning visual display. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with a compact, rounded form and dense foliage. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers are not only beautiful but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
