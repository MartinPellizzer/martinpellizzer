Pinus abies var. nordmanniana

Pinus abies var. nordmanniana, commonly known as the Nordmann fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in flat sprays. The tree produces cones that are cylindrical and often persist on the branches for several years. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Its wood is valued for its strength and is used in construction and furniture making.
