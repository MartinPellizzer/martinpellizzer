Psylliostachys koelzii

Psylliostachys koelzii, commonly known as the Koelz's bamboo, is a species of grass native to China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
