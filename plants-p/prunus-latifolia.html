Prunus latifolia

Prunus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet cherries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Prunus latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
