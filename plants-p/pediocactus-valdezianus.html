Pediocactus valdezianus

Pediocactus valdezianus, commonly known as the Valdez's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible fruits. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Pediocactus valdezianus is adapted to thrive in harsh desert environments with minimal rainfall.
