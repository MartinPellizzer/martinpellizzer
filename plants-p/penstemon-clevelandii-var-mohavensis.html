Penstemon clevelandii var. mohavensis

Penstemon clevelandii var. mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators, particularly bees and butterflies.
