Pentaschistis patula var. acuta

Pentaschistis patula var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed pentaschistis, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, acuminate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
