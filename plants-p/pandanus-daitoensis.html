Pandanus daitoensis

Pandanus daitoensis, commonly known as the Japanese pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, feather-like leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which eventually develop into woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and humid environments, often growing near the shoreline or in moist, well-drained soils.

Pandanus daitoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
