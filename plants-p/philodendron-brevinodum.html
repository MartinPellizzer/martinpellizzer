Philodendron brevinodum

Philodendron brevinodum, commonly known as the short-node philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green to variegated coloration. The plant has a slender, vine-like stem that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically borne in clusters, and are followed by distinctive, elongated berries.

Philodendron brevinodum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and humidity to flourish.
