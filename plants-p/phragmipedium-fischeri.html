Phragmipedium fischeri

Phragmipedium fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped petals and a distinctive, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white with a deep, velvety lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its rarity and unique appearance.
