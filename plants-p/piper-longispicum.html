Piper longispicum

Piper longispicum, commonly known as the long-styled pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature known as monoecious.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that ripens to a dark purple color, containing three seeds.
