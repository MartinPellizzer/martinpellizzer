Piptadenia anolidurus

Piptadenia anolidurus, commonly known as the Brazilian wild cashew, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, woody pods. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to seasonal rainfall patterns.

The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a hard, spiny shell.
