Piper plantaginifolium

Piper plantaginifolium, commonly known as the pepper plantain, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
