Pleioblastus linearis

Pleioblastus linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fuji grass, is a cold-hardy bamboo species native to East Asia.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green, growing in dense, compact clumps. The plant is characterized by its slow growth rate and ability to form thick mats, making it suitable for erosion control and ornamental use. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, offering seasonal variation in color and texture.
