Panicularia remota

Panicularia remota, commonly known as the remote panicular, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow to green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to thrive in sandy or rocky soils with low moisture availability.

Panicularia remota plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for pollinators in its native environment.
