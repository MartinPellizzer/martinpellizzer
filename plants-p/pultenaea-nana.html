Pultenaea nana

Pultenaea nana, commonly known as dwarf pultenaea, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia. It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Pultenaea nana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability.
