Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp. berchtoldii

Potamogeton berchtoldii subsp.

berchtoldii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 mm wide, and it produces small, green flowers in clusters.

The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and canals. Its rhizomes form dense underwater mats, which can influence water clarity and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

It is often found in shallow waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.
