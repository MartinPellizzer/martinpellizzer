Plantago syrtica

Plantago syrtica, commonly known as desert plantain, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which is typical of the Plantaginaceae family. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and healing properties.
