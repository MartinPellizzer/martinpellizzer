Phaca digitata

Phaca digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf or digitate leaf plant, is a species native to certain regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finger-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Phaca digitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
