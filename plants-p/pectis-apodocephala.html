Pectis apodocephala

Pectis apodocephala, commonly known as the narrowleaf pectis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
