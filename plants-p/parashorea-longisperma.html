Parashorea longisperma

Parashorea longisperma, commonly known as the longispersed talaud, is a tropical tree native to the Sunda Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Dipterocarpaceae family, which includes many valuable hardwood species. The plant is characterized by its tall stature, with some specimens reaching over 50 meters in height. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a leathery texture.

The tree produces small, fragrant flowers and is known for its durable, heavy wood that is used in construction and furniture.
