Pteridium aquilinum var. africanum

Pteridium aquilinum var. africanum, commonly known as the African bracken, is a fern species native to Africa.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in a variety of habitats, including grasslands, forests, and disturbed areas. The plant has fronds that are typically divided into pinnae, with a dark green, leathery texture. It reproduces via spores, which are released from the undersides of its sporangia.

This fern is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces, making it an important component of many ecosystems.
