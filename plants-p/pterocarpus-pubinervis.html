Pterocarpus pubinervis

Pterocarpus pubinervis, commonly known as the Malacca ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The plant has compound leaves with a hairy underside, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color.

The fruit is a pod that contains several flattened seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
