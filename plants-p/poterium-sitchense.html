Poterium sitchense

Poterium sitchense, commonly known as the Sichuan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

However, it is not widely known or commercially cultivated compared to other bellflower species.
