Pennantia endlicheri

Pennantia endlicheri, commonly known as the New Zealand flax.

It is a flowering plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne at the base of the leaves. Pennantia endlicheri is a member of the Pandanus family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
