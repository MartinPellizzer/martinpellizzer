Pyrus pourthiaea

Pyrus pourthiaea, commonly known as the Persian white pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, sweet, yellowish pear that ripens in late summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
