Phlox kelseyi subsp. variabilis

Phlox kelseyi subsp.

variabilis, commonly known as the variable phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners in arid and semi-arid regions.
