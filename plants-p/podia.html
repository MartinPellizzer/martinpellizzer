Podia

Podia, commonly known as the "false holly," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are shrubs or small trees native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plants are characterized by their simple, alternate leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. Podia species often have smooth, glossy bark and produce capsules as their fruiting structures.

They are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
