Pandanus prolixus

Pandanus prolixus, commonly known as the long-fruited pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces clustered, spadix-like inflorescences that bear small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Pandanus prolixus thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in swampy or brackish areas.
