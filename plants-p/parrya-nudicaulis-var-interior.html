Parrya nudicaulis var. interior

Parrya nudicaulis var. interior, commonly known as the Interior parrya, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, leafless stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in early spring. Its leaves, when present, are small and scale-like, often obscured by the dense mat of stems.

This variety is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a unique ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
