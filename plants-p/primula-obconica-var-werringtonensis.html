Primula obconica var. werringtonensis

Primula obconica var. werringtonensis, commonly known as the Werrington primrose, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a broad base that clasps the stem.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
