Pteronia camphorata

Pteronia camphorata, commonly known as the Cape geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant leaves and blooms in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Pteronia camphorata is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional medicine.
