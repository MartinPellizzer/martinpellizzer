Paepalanthus perpusillus

Paepalanthus perpusillus, commonly known as the dwarf palm or miniature palm, is a small, shrub-like plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes true palms, and is characterized by its compact size and unique leaf structure. The plant features a dense rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a tight cluster around a short, woody stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by clustered, edible berries.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ease of care, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
