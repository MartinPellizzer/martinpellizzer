Phyllanthus minor

Phyllanthus minor, commonly known as the lesser phyllanthus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or along stream banks. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, fleshy fruits.

Phyllanthus minor is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
