Phlomis maeotica

Phlomis maeotica, commonly known as the Crimean phlomis, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
