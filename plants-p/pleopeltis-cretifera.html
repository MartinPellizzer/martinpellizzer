Pleopeltis cretifera

Pleopeltis cretifera, commonly known as the crested rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often crested fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a unique, segmented appearance.

Pleopeltis cretifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
