Pelargonium undulifolium

Pelargonium undulifolium, commonly known as the zigzag geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating, and often zigzag-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in gardens around the world.
