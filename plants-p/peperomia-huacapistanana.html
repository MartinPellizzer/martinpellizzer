Peperomia huacapistanana

Peperomia huacapistanana, commonly known as the Huacapista peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
