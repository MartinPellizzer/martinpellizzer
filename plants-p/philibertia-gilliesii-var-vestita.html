Philibertia gilliesii var. vestita

Philibertia gilliesii var. vestita, commonly known as the silky filbert, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
