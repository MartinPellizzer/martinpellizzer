Persea gratissima var. drimyfolia

Persea gratissima var. drimyfolia, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to reddish fruits with a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor. Its fruit is highly valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
