Pulicaria bellidiflora

Pulicaria bellidiflora, commonly known as the bellflower centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes and have a distinctive bell-shaped appearance.

Pulicaria bellidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
