Penstemon alpinus subsp. brandegeei

Penstemon alpinus subsp.

brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it well-suited for alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators in its native habitat.
