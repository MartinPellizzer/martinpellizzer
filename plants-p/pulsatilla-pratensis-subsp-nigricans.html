Pulsatilla pratensis subsp. nigricans

Pulsatilla pratensis subsp.

nigricans, commonly known as the black crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dark purple to black, showy flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are palmately divided, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker flower color compared to other varieties of pulsatilla.
