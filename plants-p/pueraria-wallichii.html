Pueraria wallichii

Pueraria wallichii, commonly known as Indian Kudzu, is a climbing legume native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of purple or white flowers. The plant produces long, cylindrical pods containing several round, smooth seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Pueraria wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
