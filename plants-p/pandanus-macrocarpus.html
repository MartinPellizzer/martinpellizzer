Pandanus macrocarpus

Pandanus macrocarpus, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching.

The species is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
