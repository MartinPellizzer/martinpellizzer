Putoria brevifolia var. melillensis

Putoria brevifolia var. melillensis, commonly known as the Melillan rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and greyish, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its resilience and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
