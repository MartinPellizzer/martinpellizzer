Platycerium platylobum

Platycerium platylobum, commonly known as the shield fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped fronds that resemble the structure of a shield, giving it its common name. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs moisture and nutrients through its fronds from the air and rain. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
