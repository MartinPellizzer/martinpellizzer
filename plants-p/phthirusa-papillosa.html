Phthirusa papillosa

Phthirusa papillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The velvetleaf is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions.
