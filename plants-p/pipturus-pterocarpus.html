Pipturus pterocarpus

Pipturus pterocarpus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a broad, spreading canopy and produces flat, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are enclosed in woody pods, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.

Pipturus pterocarpus thrives in tropical climates and is often planted for its visual appeal and ability to provide shade.
