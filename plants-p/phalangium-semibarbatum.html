Phalangium semibarbatum

Phalangium semibarbatum, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate regions and is often found in woodland or disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
