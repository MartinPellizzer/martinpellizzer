Ptychococcus archboldianus

Ptychococcus archboldianus, commonly known as the St. Helena cypress, is a rare and unique conifer native to the island of St. Helena in the South Atlantic Ocean.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and conical shape, typical of many cypress species. The plant has a slow growth rate and is adapted to the unique climate and soil conditions of its endemic habitat. Due to its restricted distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for studies on plant adaptation and conservation.
