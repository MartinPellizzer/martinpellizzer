Ptilostemon diacanthus

Ptilostemon diacanthus, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
