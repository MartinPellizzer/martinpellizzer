Philibertella riparia

Philibertella riparia, commonly known as the riverbank bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands and riverbanks of eastern North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Philibertella riparia is typically found in riparian zones, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Its blooming period occurs from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
