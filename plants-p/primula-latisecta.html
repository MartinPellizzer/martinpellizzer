Primula latisecta

Primula latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive foliage appearance. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
