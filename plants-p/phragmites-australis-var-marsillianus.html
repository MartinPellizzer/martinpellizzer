Phragmites australis var. marsillianus

Phragmites australis var. marsillianus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland environments.

It is a tall, perennial grass that typically grows in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of feathery flowers that bloom in late summer. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
