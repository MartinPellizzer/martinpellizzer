Potentilla caulescens f. persicina

Potentilla caulescens f. persicina, commonly known as the Persian shrub cinquefoil, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has wiry, thorny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
