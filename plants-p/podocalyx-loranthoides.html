Podocalyx loranthoides

Podocalyx loranthoides, commonly known as the mistletoe tree, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Loranthaceae family.

It attaches itself to host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions, using haustoria to extract water and nutrients. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries, which are typically red or orange, contain seeds covered in a sticky substance to aid in dispersal by birds.

Despite its parasitic nature, Podocalyx loranthoides plays a role in ecosystem dynamics by providing food for various wildlife species.
