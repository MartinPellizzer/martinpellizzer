Pueraria montana var. chinensis

Pueraria montana var. chinensis, commonly known as kudzu, is a vigorous vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, trailing stems that can climb and entangle other vegetation. The plant produces purple or pink flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Kudzu is known for its rapid growth and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Botanically, it is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in various soil conditions.
