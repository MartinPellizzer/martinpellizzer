Pluchea dioscoridis var. pseudovalis

Pluchea dioscoridis var. pseudovalis, commonly known as false blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
