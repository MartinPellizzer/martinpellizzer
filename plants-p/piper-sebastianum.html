Piper sebastianum

Piper sebastianum, commonly known as the Sebastian's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into three-lobed, red berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a strong, peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
