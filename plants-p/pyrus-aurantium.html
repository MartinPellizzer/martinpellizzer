Pyrus aurantium

Pyrus aurantium, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its small, yellow, citrus-like fruits. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit. The leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the extraction of essential oils.
