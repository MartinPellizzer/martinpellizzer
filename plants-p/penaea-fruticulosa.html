Penaea fruticulosa

Penaea fruticulosa, commonly known as the silky broom.

It is a shrub native to South America, characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
