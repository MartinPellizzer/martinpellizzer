Pelargonium hurifolium

Pelargonium hurifolium, commonly known as the Cape marigold, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
