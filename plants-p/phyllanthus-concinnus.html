Phyllanthus concinnus

Phyllanthus concinnus, commonly known as the silverleaf phyllanthus, is a small, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces tiny, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Phyllanthus concinnus thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
