Phlegmariurus acutus

Phlegmariurus acutus, commonly known as the acute spleenwort, is a species of moss belonging to the family Pterobryaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy shoots and is typically found growing on rocks or soil in moist, shaded environments. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a distinct costa that extends to the apex. It reproduces through spores and is often found in temperate regions.

This moss is an important component of forest floor ecosystems, contributing to soil stability and moisture retention.
