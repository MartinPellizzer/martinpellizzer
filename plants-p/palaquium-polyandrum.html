Palaquium polyandrum

Palaquium polyandrum, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, segmented fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications due to its high antioxidant content.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
