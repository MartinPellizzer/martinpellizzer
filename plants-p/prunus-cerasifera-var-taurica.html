Prunus cerasifera var. taurica

Prunus cerasifera var. taurica, commonly known as the Turkish cherry or taurica cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, dark red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

The species is also cultivated for its hardy nature and adaptability to various soil types.
