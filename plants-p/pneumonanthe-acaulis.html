Pneumonanthe acaulis

Pneumonanthe acaulis, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Pneumonanthe acaulis is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
