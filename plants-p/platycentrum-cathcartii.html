Platycentrum cathcartii

Platycentrum cathcartii, commonly known as the Cathcart's flat-centre, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive, broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shady environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant collectors.
