Pitcairnia lopezii

Pitcairnia lopezii, commonly known as the Lopez's pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup to collect rainwater. The plant produces vibrant, showy inflorescences with colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Pitcairnia lopezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
