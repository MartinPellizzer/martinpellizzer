Piper andreanum

Piper andreanum, commonly known as the Surinam pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing numerous tiny seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
