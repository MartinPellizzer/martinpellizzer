Pharbitis nil var. japonica

Pharbitis nil var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese morning glory, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces large, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The seeds are edible and can be consumed raw or cooked.
