Pedalium busseanum

Pedalium busseanum, commonly known as the Indian tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its roots are also utilized in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ecological and economic significance in tropical regions.
