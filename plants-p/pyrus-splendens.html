Pyrus splendens

Pyrus splendens, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring bright yellow leaves that turn orange in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer. Pyrus splendens thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
