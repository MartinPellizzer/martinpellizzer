Plantago obversa

Plantago obversa, commonly known as narrow-leaved plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense spikes during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adaptable to various soil types.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in both sunny and partially shaded environments.
