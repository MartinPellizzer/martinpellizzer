Plectranthus galpinii

Plectranthus galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's plectranthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems.

Plectranthus galpinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habits.
