Polygala humbertii

Polygala humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, compound leaves. It is known for its unique seed pods that split open to release seeds, a feature common in many members of its family.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
