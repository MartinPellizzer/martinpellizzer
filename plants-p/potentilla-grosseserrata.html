Potentilla grosseserrata

Potentilla grosseserrata, commonly known as the roughleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.

Potentilla grosseserrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
