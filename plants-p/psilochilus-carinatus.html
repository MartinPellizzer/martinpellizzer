Psilochilus carinatus

Psilochilus carinatus, commonly known as the ridge-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that often have a distinct ridge or carina running along their length. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale yellow.

Psilochilus carinatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
