Picea abies var. abies

Picea abies var. abies, commonly known as the Norway spruce, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Europe and parts of northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender form and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree has needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and are arranged in spirals along the branches. Its cones are typically oval and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in forestry and as a source of timber.
