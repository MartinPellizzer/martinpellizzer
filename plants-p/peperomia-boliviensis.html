Peperomia boliviensis

Peperomia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native arid environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in collections of succulents and tropical foliage plants.
