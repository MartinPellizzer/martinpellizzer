Pavetta littorea

Pavetta littorea, commonly known as sea pavetta, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils along the shoreline. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences and are usually pale green or white in color.

Pavetta littorea plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
