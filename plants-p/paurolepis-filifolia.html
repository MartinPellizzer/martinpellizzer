Paurolepis filifolia

Paurolepis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
