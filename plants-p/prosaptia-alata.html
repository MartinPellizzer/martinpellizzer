Prosaptia alata

Prosaptia alata, commonly known as the winged sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often green or brown in color. The plant's most distinctive characteristic is its winged stems, which are covered in fine, hair-like structures. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

Prosaptia alata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
