Portulaca umbraticola subsp. coronata

Portulaca umbraticola subsp.

coronata, commonly known as the crown portulaca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is a low-growing, succulent annual that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features fleshy, thick leaves and produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its botanical characteristics include a branched stem, small alternate leaves, and a short flowering period during the warmer months.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and attractive appearance.
