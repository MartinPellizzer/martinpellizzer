Phaeopappus declinatus

Phaeopappus declinatus, commonly known as the rockweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, succulent leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in dry, well-drained soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Phaeopappus declinatus is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
