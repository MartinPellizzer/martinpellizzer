Phemeranthus calycinus

Phemeranthus calycinus, commonly known as the golden star or star flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces showy, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it has a short, fibrous root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, eye-catching blooms.
