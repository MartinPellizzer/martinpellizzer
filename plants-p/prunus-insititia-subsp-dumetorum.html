Prunus insititia subsp. dumetorum

Prunus insititia subsp.

dumetorum, commonly known as the blackcap cherry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, dark purple to black fruits.

The leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and a serrated margin. The plant produces white flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in jams and wines.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
