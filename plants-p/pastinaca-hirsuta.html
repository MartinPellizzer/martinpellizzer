Pastinaca hirsuta

Pastinaca hirsuta, commonly known as hairy burdock, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and are known for their medicinal properties.

Pastinaca hirsuta is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments.
