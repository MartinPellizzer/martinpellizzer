Pelargonium fissifolium

Pelargonium fissifolium, commonly known as the fissured-leaf geranium, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Pelargonium fissifolium is known for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
