Paludorchis robusta

Paludorchis robusta, commonly known as the robust marsh orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to wetland and marsh habitats in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils, and its leaves are typically broad and grass-like. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-rich environments.

Paludorchis robusta plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
