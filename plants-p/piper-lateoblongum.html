Piper lateoblongum

Piper lateoblongum, commonly known as the large-leafed pepper or *Piper lateoblongum*, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its seeds in traditional medicine.

The leaves and fruits of Piper lateoblongum are also used in some local cuisines for their distinctive flavor.
