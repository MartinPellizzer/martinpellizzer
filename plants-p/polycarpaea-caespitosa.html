Polycarpaea caespitosa

Polycarpaea caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted polycarpaea, is a perennial herb native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
