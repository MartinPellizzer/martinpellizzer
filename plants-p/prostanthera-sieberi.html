Prostanthera sieberi

Prostanthera sieberi, commonly known as Sieber's mintbush, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a minty scent, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Prostanthera sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
