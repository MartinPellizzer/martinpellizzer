Phyodina navicularis

Phyodina navicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf phyodina, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Phyodina navicularis is often found in rocky outcrops and along dry streambeds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
