Panicum perrieri

Panicum perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the wetlands and marshy areas of eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny grains. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, forming a mat-like structure that helps stabilize soil in its wetland habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Panicum perrieri plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its environment by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
