Pedilonum scabrifolium

Pedilonum scabrifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf pedilonum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms, it is a sought-after species among botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
