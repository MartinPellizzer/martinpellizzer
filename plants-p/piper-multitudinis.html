Piper multitudinis

Piper multitudinis, commonly known as the many-fruited pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and emit a mild peppery aroma.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
