Pseudotsuga sinensis subsp. brevifolia

Pseudotsuga sinensis subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to eastern China. It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are short and glossy.

The tree produces small, spherical cones that are typically retained on the branches for several years. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
