Poa pseudoattenuata

Poa pseudoattenuata, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often slightly bent at the base, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compressed flower clusters.

Poa pseudoattenuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
