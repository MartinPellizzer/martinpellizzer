Plectranthus gracillimus

Plectranthus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated in gardens across the world.
