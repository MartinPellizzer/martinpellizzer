Phylica cuspidata var. cuspidata

Phylica cuspidata var. cuspidata, commonly known as the Cape snowbrush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branched shrub form and produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The Cape snowbrush is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
