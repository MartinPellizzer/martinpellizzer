Piper gorgonae-insulae

Piper gorgonae-insulae, commonly known as the Gorgonae-Insulae pepper, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of the Indo-Pacific.

This climbing vine belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elongated, peppery fruits. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical significance lies in its potential medicinal properties and its role in local ecosystems.
