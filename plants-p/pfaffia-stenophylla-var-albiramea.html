Pfaffia stenophylla var. albiramea

Pfaffia stenophylla var. albiramea, commonly known as golden leaf senna, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody stem with a distinctive, branched structure. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the plant is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.

Pfaffia stenophylla var. albiramea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
