Phaleria glabra

Phaleria glabra, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single large seed. Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ornamental horticulture and traditional medicine.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
