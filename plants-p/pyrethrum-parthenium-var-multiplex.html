Pyrethrum parthenium var. multiplex

Pyrethrum parthenium var. multiplex, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-white composite flowers. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating migraines and inflammatory conditions. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, contain compounds such as parthenolide, which contribute to its therapeutic effects.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.
