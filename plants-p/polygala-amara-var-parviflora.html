Polygala amara var. parviflora

Polygala amara var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant features pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are clustered in spicate inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also of interest in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
