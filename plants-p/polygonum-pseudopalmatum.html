Polygonum pseudopalmatum

Polygonum pseudopalmatum, commonly known as the Japanese painted fern, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palm-like fronds that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and gold. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and moist, well-drained soil. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It produces small, greenish flowers in late summer, which are followed by reddish-brown seed heads.
