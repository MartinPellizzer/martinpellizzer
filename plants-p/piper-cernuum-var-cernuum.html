Piper cernuum var. cernuum

Piper cernuum var. cernuum, commonly known as the hanging pepper or weeping pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces tiny, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Native to the tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardens.
