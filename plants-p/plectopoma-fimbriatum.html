Plectopoma fimbriatum

Plectopoma fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed turban shell, is a marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Turbinellidae.

It is characterized by its large, spiral-shaped shell that is often adorned with delicate, fringed patterns along the outer lip. The shell is typically white or pale pink in color, with a smooth, glossy surface that can vary in thickness depending on the individual. This species is found in tropical and subtropical waters, often inhabiting shallow reefs and rocky substrates.

Its morphology and shell structure are well adapted for both protection and efficient movement through its marine environment.
