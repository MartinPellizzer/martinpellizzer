Phlox speciosa subsp. occidentalis

Phlox speciosa subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western garden phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to lavender, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
