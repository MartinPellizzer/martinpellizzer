Phaseolus vulgaris var. platyleucus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. platyleucus, commonly known as the white kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, round, white seeds that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant produces vining stems with trifoliate leaves and is cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and dietary fiber. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and nutritional benefits in traditional diets.
