Penducella erepsis

Penducella erepsis, commonly known as the silver leaf tree, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive silver-green foliage.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of silvery hairs, giving it its characteristic appearance. Penducella erepsis is known for its slow growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Its botanical structure includes a woody stem and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in clusters.
