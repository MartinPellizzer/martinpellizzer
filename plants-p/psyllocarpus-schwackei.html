Psyllocarpus schwackei

Psyllocarpus schwackei, commonly known as the yellow mangrove, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
