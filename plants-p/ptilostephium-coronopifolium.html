Ptilostephium coronopifolium

Ptilostephium coronopifolium, commonly known as the goldenrods, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
