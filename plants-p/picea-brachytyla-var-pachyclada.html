Picea brachytyla var. pachyclada

Picea brachytyla var. pachyclada, commonly known as the dwarf spruce, is a compact evergreen conifer native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its short, dense growth habit and dark green, needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
