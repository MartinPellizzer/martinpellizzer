Paeonia lactiflora

Paeonia lactiflora, commonly known as the peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and yellow. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fleshy, milky sap, which is responsible for its scientific name "lactiflora.". It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.

The peony is a member of the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is known for its long blooming period in late spring to early summer.
