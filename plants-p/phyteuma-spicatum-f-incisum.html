Phyteuma spicatum f. incisum

Phyteuma spicatum f. incisum, commonly known as the cut-leaved spicata phyteuma, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are distinctive for their finely cut, lobed appearance, which gives the plant its common name. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This cultivar is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
