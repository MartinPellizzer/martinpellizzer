Platanthera bifolia var. brachyphylla

Platanthera bifolia var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed lady's tresses, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers arranged in a loose, loose raceme. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
