Prunus serrulata f. tricolor

Prunus serrulata f. tricolor, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom with three colors, is a cultivar of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which display a striking combination of white, pink, and red hues during the spring blooming season. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 feet. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.

The foliage changes color throughout the seasons, adding to its aesthetic value.
