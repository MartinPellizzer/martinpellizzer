Persea gentlei

Persea gentlei, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange to reddish fruits that are edible when fully ripe. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Persea gentlei is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and its nutritious fruit.
