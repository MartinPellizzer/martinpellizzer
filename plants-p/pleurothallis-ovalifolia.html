Pleurothallis ovalifolia

Pleurothallis ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves that are often clustered at the base, forming a rosette-like structure. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found growing epiphytically on trees or rocks.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
