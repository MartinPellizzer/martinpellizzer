Piper phalangense

Piper phalangense, commonly known as the climbing pepper or long-leaf pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters, and it produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
