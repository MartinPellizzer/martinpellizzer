Protea abyssinica var. adolphi-friderici

Protea abyssinica var. adolphi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolphi-Friedrich protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers clustered around a central axis. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in the fynbos biome. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and vibrant floral display.
