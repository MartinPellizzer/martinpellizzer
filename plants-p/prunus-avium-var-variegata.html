Prunus avium var. variegata

Prunus avium var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cherry, is a cultivar of the sweet cherry tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and pink hues. The plant produces small, sweet, and juicy fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental and edible plant is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
