Polystachya pyramidalis

Polystachya pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal ginger, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect inflorescence bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
