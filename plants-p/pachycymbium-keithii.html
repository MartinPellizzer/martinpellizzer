Pachycymbium keithii

Pachycymbium keithii, commonly known as the Keith's thick-leaf orchid, is a unique orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, an adaptation to its often arid or seasonal habitat. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinct color pattern, typically featuring a combination of yellow and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sometimes endangered species in the wild.
