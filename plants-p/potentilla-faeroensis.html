Potentilla faeroensis

Potentilla faeroensis, commonly known as the Faeroese cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Faroe Islands.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and is adapted to the harsh climate of its native habitat. Its foliage consists of small, serrated leaves that are densely packed along its stems.

Potentilla faeroensis is notable for its resilience and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
