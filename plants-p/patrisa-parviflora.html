Patrisa parviflora

Patrisa parviflora, commonly known as the small-flower patrisa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Patrisa parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its subtle beauty.
