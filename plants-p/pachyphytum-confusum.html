Pachyphytum confusum

Pachyphytum confusum, commonly known as the "confused stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late spring or early summer.

Pachyphytum confusum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
