Pulicaria crispa var. candidissima

Pulicaria crispa var. candidissima, commonly known as the white cranesbill, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
