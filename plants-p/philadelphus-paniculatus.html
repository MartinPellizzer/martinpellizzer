Philadelphus paniculatus

Philadelphus paniculatus, commonly known as the panicled philadelphus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers that appear in panicles during late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
