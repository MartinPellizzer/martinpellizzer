Pachyrhizus teres

Pachyrhizus teres, commonly known as the round-rooted bean, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, tuberous roots that store nutrients, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Pachyrhizus teres is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various soil conditions.
