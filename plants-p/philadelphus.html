Philadelphus

Philadelphus, commonly known as the mock orange, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These shrubs are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North America. They are characterized by their fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a silvery or grayish underside.

Philadelphus species are valued in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
