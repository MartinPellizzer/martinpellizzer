Prunella vulgaris var. nemoralis

Prunella vulgaris var. nemoralis, commonly known as common selfheal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on both surfaces. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and clustered in spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
