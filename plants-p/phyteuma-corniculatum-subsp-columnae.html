Phyteuma corniculatum subsp. columnae

Phyteuma corniculatum subsp.

columnae, commonly known as the columnar phyteuma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features upright, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late summer.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive floral structure, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.
