Pilea falcata

Pilea falcata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, coin-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are borne on a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular indoor plant.

Pilea falcata is relatively low maintenance and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage rather than its flowers.
