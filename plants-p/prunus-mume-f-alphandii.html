Prunus mume f. alphandii

Prunus mume f. alphandii, commonly known as the Alphand's Japanese apricot, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Japanese apricot.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant is native to China and is cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens. It has a compact growth habit with smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves.

This variety is prized for its delicate blossoms and is often used in traditional Japanese and Chinese horticulture.
