Pyrus racemiflora

Pyrus racemiflora, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to pears but are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Pyrus racemiflora is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil conditions.
