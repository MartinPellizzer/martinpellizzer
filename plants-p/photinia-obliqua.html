Photinia obliqua

Photinia obliqua, commonly known as the redleaf photinia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the autumn season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

Photinia obliqua is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
