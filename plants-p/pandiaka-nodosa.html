Pandiaka nodosa

Pandiaka nodosa, commonly known as the nodding pandiaka, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding inflorescences that resemble small bells, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a mild, aromatic scent.

Pandiaka nodosa is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
