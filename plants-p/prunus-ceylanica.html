Prunus ceylanica

Prunus ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon cherry or Ceylon plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the wet tropical forests of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart red or yellow fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the specific ecological conditions of its native habitat.
