Pomaderris rosmarinifolia

Pomaderris rosmarinifolia, commonly known as silver wattle, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
