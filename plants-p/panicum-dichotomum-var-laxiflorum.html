Panicum dichotomum var. laxiflorum

Panicum dichotomum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered dropseed, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles that are often tinged with red or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, making it suitable for native plant gardens and erosion control.
