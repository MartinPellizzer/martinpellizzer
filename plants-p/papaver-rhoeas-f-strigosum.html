Papaver rhoeas f. strigosum

Papaver rhoeas f. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

This form exhibits a more robust and rugged appearance compared to the typical Papaver rhoeas. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. The flowers are usually bright red with a black center, similar to the standard species.

Botanically, it is noted for its hairy foliage and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
