Potamogeton balatonicus

Potamogeton balatonicus, commonly known as the Hungarian pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as lakes, rivers, and ponds. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and green, and it produces small, dark brown fruits.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various water organisms.
