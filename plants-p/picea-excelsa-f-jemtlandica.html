Picea excelsa f. jemtlandica

Picea excelsa f. jemtlandica, commonly known as the Jemtland spruce, is a naturally occurring form of the white spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often drooping cones, which are typically blue-green in color. The foliage is dense and needle-like, with a silvery-blue hue that gives the tree its striking appearance. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is native to the Scandinavian region.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its unique aesthetic.
