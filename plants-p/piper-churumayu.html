Piper churumayu

Piper churumayu, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of northern South America, particularly in Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its slender stems, heart-shaped leaves, and small, clustered flowers that produce peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both traditional medicine and ecological studies.
