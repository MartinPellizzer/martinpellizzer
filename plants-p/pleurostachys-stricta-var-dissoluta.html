Pleurostachys stricta var. dissoluta

Pleurostachys stricta var. dissoluta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yucca, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
