Pleurothallis rhodotantha

Pleurothallis rhodotantha, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its flowers are often adorned with intricate markings that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts and conservationists.
