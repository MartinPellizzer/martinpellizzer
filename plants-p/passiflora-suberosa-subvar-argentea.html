Passiflora suberosa subvar. argentea

Passiflora suberosa subvar. argentea, commonly known as the silverleaf passionflower, is a striking species native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. Its fruits are edible, turning from green to yellow or orange as they mature.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage.
