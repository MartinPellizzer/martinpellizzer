Pothos salicifolius

Pothos salicifolius, commonly known as the heartleaf pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly variegated appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-yellow berries, though it is primarily grown for its ornamental foliage. It thrives in low-light conditions and is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.

Its ability to climb and trail makes it versatile for hanging baskets or shelves.
