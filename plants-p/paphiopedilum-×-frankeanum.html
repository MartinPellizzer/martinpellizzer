Paphiopedilum × frankeanum

Paphiopedilum × frankeanum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Paphiopedilum niveum and Paphiopedilum suaveolens.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a distinctive appearance, featuring long, narrow leaves and a showy, lip-shaped flower. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
