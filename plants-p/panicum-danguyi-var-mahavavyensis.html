Panicum danguyi var. mahavavyensis

Panicum danguyi var. mahavavyensis, commonly known as the Mahavavyensis Panicum, is a grass species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with narrow, linear leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies.

This variety is of interest for its ecological role and potential horticultural value.
