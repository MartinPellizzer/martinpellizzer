Phyllanthus mannianus

Phyllanthus mannianus, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
