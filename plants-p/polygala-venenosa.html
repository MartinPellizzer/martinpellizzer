Polygala venenosa

Polygala venenosa, commonly known as the poison root or death's-head polygala, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often bizarre-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, and it thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

However, it is toxic to humans and animals, containing various alkaloids that can cause severe poisoning if ingested.
