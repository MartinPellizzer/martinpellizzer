Poa hypnoides var. glabra

Poa hypnoides var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, wiry stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its varietal name "glabra.". Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-rowed florets.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
