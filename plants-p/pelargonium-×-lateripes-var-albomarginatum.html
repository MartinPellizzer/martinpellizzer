Pelargonium × lateripes var. albomarginatum

Pelargonium × lateripes var. albomarginatum, commonly known as the white-margin geranium, is a hybrid pelargonium species characterized by its distinctive white margins on the leaves.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant features showy pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage is often variegated, with a combination of green and white or cream-colored veins, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
