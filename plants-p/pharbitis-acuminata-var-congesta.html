Pharbitis acuminata var. congesta

Pharbitis acuminata var. congesta, commonly known as red-rooted morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or red, which bloom in the morning and close by midday. Its roots are thick, fleshy, and often reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
