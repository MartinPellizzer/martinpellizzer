Pegaeophyton watsonii

Pegaeophyton watsonii, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

Pegaeophyton watsonii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
