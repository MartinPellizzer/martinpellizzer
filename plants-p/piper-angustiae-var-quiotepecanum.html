Piper angustiae var. quiotepecanum

Piper angustiae var. quiotepecanum, commonly known as the Quiotepec pepper, is a tropical vine native to southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by tiny, peppery fruits. Its botanical features include a pungent aroma and a mild to moderate level of capsaicin, making it distinct from other pepper species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional use in local cuisines.
