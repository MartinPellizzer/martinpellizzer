Pennisetum alopecuroides var. viridescens

Pennisetum alopecuroides var. viridescens, commonly known as green foxtail, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It features slender, hairless stems that produce dense clusters of greenish to yellowish spikelets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
