Passiflora stenosepala

Passiflora stenosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a central crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Passiflora stenosepala is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
