Pourouma digitata

Pourouma digitata, commonly known as the giant bamboo palm, is a striking plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant can grow up to 20 meters in height, making it one of the tallest palms in the world. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatch material by indigenous communities.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens.
