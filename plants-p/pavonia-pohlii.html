Pavonia pohlii

Pavonia pohlii, commonly known as the Pohli's hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Pavonia pohlii is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
