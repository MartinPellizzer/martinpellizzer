Potentilla argentea var. laciniata

Potentilla argentea var. laciniata, commonly known as silverleaf potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white underside on the leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces showy yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, typically arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice among gardeners.
