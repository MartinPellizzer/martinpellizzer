Polygala hemipterocarpa var. bracteata

Polygala hemipterocarpa var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple and opposite, often with a slight pubescence, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
