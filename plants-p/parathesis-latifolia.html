Parathesis latifolia

Parathesis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf parathesis, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in clusters, which are usually yellow or white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Parathesis latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
