Puya weberiana

Puya weberiana, commonly known as the Andean grass or the giant puya, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can reach heights of up to 10 feet. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Puya weberiana is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found growing in rocky or open areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
