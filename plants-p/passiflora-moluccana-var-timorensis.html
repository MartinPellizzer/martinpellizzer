Passiflora moluccana var. timorensis

Passiflora moluccana var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor passionflower, is a species native to the island of Timor in Indonesia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, showy flowers that feature a distinctive crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and often consumed by local communities. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture, and it thrives in tropical climates with high humidity.

This variety is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
