Pavonia elegans subsp. elegans

Pavonia elegans subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and relatively low-maintenance addition to native plant landscapes.
