Papaver somniferum convar. drogist

Papaver somniferum convar. drogist, commonly known as the opium poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and a capsule-like seed pod that matures into a latex-producing structure. The plant's botanical features include alternate, ovate leaves with prominent veins and a distinctive, erect stem. The latex contains alkaloids such as morphine and codeine, which are chemically extracted for medicinal and pharmaceutical purposes.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
