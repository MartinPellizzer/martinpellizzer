Prosopanche americana

Prosopanche americana, commonly known as the American hog peanut, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its growth habit and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
