Phytolacca costaricensis

Phytolacca costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican pokeweed, is a perennial shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Phytolaccaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into dark purple berries. Its roots and stems contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

Despite its toxicity, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
