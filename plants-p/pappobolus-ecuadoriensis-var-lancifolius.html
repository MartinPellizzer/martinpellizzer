Pappobolus ecuadoriensis var. lancifolius

Pappobolus ecuadoriensis var. lancifolius, commonly known as the Ecuadorian pappobolus, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and daisy-like appearance. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
