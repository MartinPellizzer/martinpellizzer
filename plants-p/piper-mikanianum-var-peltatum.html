Piper mikanianum var. peltatum

Piper mikanianum var. peltatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved pepper plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into elongated, cylindrical berries. These berries turn bright red when mature and are edible, though they have a mild, peppery flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine.
