Philodendron sanguineum

Philodendron sanguineum, commonly known as the red-veined philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, dark green leaves with striking red veins that stand out vividly against the foliage. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trained trellises.

Philodendron sanguineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
