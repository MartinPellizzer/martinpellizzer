Padus asiatica var. macrocarpa

Padus asiatica var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited Chinese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive, large, yellowish-orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
