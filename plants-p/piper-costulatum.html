Piper costulatum

Piper costulatum, commonly known as the waxy begonia or costleaf pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
