Picris japonica subsp. kamtschatica

Picris japonica subsp.

kamtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia. It typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features a basal rosette of leaves that are hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped form. Its yellow flowers are arranged in small clusters and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
