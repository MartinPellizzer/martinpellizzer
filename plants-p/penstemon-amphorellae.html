Penstemon amphorellae

Penstemon amphorellae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence and tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

Penstemon amphorellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting native pollinators.
