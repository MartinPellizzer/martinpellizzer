Pandanus tsaratananensis

Pandanus tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Tsaratanana pandanus, is a species of palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that grow in clusters at the base of the leaves. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
