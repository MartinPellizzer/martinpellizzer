Piptochaetium gibbum

Piptochaetium gibbum, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive turf.

Piptochaetium gibbum is also recognized for its resilience to drought and moderate grazing.
