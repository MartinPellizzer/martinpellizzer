Pelargonium × incarnatum

Pelargonium × incarnatum, commonly known as the Christmas rose or the Christmas carnation, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in winter, typically around December to January, giving it its common name. The plant features upright, branching stems and broad, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with five petals and a prominent central stigma.

Pelargonium × incarnatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its seasonal beauty and ease of care.
