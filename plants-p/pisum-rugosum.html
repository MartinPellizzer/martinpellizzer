Pisum rugosum

Pisum rugosum, commonly known as the rough vetch, is a leguminous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found growing in disturbed soils and is used in some agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological and agricultural studies.
