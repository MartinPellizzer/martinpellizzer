Phelypaea comosa

Phelypaea comosa, commonly known as the hairy water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help it adhere to water surfaces. The plant typically grows in still or slow-moving water, such as ponds and marshes, where it can thrive in shallow, nutrient-rich conditions. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often appearing in clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and asexually through rhizomes.

Phelypaea comosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
