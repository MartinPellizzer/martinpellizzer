Polygala verticillata var. dolichoptera

Polygala verticillata var. dolichoptera, commonly known as the long-stemmed milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often arranged in whorls, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
