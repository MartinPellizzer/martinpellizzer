Prunus sibirica var. pubescens

Prunus sibirica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It is adapted to harsh climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural studies.
