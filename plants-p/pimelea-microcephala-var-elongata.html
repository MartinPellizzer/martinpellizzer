Pimelea microcephala var. elongata

Pimelea microcephala var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color, which are produced in late summer to early autumn. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and dry forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
