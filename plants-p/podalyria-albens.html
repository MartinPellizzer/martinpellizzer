Podalyria albens

Podalyria albens, commonly known as the white thyme or alpine thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, aromatic, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent.

Podalyria albens thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and high-altitude environments.
