Prunus persica f. stellata

Prunus persica f. stellata, commonly known as the star peach, is a compact, ornamental variant of the peach tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, star-shaped flowers that appear in early spring, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to regular peaches, though they are often less flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for smaller gardens or container growing.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and mild fruit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and edible landscaping.
