Prunus deformis

Prunus deformis, commonly known as the Japanese white cherry, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has slender, spreading branches and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and parks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
