Phthirusa squamulosa

Phthirusa squamulosa, commonly known as the woolly mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, woolly gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
