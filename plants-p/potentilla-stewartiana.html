Potentilla stewartiana

Potentilla stewartiana, commonly known as Stewart's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in a fine layer of soft, white hairs.

Potentilla stewartiana thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
