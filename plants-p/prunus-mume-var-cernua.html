Prunus mume var. cernua

Prunus mume var. cernua, commonly known as the weeping Japanese plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading appearance.

It features oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn, and its flowers are typically pale pink to white, blooming in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are tart and often used in traditional Chinese and Japanese cuisines.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
