Pleomele thwaitesii

Pleomele thwaitesii, commonly known as the tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that give it a distinctive tropical appearance. The plant grows slowly and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the fronds above.

Pleomele thwaitesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments.
