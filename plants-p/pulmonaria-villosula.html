Pulmonaria villosula

Pulmonaria villosula, commonly known as the hairy lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spotted leaves and clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its foliage is covered in fine, hairy textures, which help to retain moisture and protect against frost.

Pulmonaria villosula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
