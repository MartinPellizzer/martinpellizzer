Persea pseudofasciculata

Persea pseudofasciculata, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible when fully ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various regions with a subtropical climate.
