Pittosporum cornifolium

Pittosporum cornifolium, commonly known as the horned pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Pittosporum cornifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
