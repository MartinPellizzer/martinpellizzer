Pisum sativum var. beckii

Pisum sativum var. beckii, commonly known as the Beck's pea, is a cultivated variety of the common pea.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain several seeds, similar to other pea varieties. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its potential in crop rotation and soil improvement.
