Poa chapmaniana

Poa chapmaniana, commonly known as Chapman's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny blue flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in native plant gardens.
