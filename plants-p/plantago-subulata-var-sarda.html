Plantago subulata var. sarda

Plantago subulata var. sarda, commonly known as the Sardian plantain, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, spiny leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
