Pimelea tasmanica

Pimelea tasmanica, commonly known as the Tasmanian devil's hair or Tasmanian bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, wiry stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract various pollinators. Its botanical characteristics include a woody structure and a preference for well-drained soils in sunny or partially shaded environments.

Pimelea tasmanica is also known for its resilience to harsh weather conditions, making it a hardy plant in its native habitat.
