Platanthera japonica

Platanthera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
