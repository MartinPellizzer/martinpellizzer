Polygonum bistorta var. griseum

Polygonum bistorta var. griseum, commonly known as the gray bistort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with heart-shaped leaves that have a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from pink to purple.

The roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported healing properties.
