Phrynium discolor

Phrynium discolor, commonly known as the golden torch plant, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a vibrant yellow or green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Phrynium discolor thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
