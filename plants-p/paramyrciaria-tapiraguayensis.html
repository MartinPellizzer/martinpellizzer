Paramyrciaria tapiraguayensis

Paramyrciaria tapiraguayensis, commonly known as the Tapiraguayensis myrciaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known species like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
