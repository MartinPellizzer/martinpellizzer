Paeonia anomala subsp. hybrida

Paeonia anomala subsp.

hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Paeoniaceae and is characterized by its large, showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant features deeply lobed, dark green leaves and has a rhizomatous root system. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
