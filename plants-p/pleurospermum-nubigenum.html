Pleurospermum nubigenum

Pleurospermum nubigenum, commonly known as the cloud-borne pleurospermum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine pubescence, while its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging soils.
