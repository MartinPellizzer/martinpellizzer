Pyrus magocsyana

Pyrus magocsyana, commonly known as the Magocsy pear, is a rare and lesser-known species of pear native to Hungary.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are broad and lobed, with a matte green surface and serrated edges.

Despite its ornamental potential, the species remains under-studied and is not widely cultivated.
