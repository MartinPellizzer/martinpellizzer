Piper silvimontanum

Piper silvimontanum, commonly known as the mountain pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, three-lobed berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Its aromatic leaves and fruits have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.
