Picris pyrenaica

Picris pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
