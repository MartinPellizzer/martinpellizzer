Persea leucoxylon

Persea leucoxylon, commonly known as the whiteheart mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and timber, though it is also used in traditional medicine.
