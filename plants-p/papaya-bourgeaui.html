Papaya bourgeaui

Papaya bourgeaui, commonly known as the giant papaya, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and a distinct, fleshy, orange-colored fruit. The plant produces a single, large fruit per plant, which can weigh up to 10 kilograms. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains the enzyme papain, which is used in food processing and medicine.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
