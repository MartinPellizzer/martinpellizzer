Phenianthus

Phenianthus, commonly known as the false bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, or white. They are native to various regions in the Americas, particularly in the southern United States and parts of Mexico. The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
