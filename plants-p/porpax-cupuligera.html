Porpax cupuligera

Porpax cupuligera, commonly known as the cupuliferous liverwort, is a small, mat-forming liverwort found in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Porellaceae and is characterized by its leafy shoots and small, cup-like structures called peristomes. It reproduces through spores released from the cup-like structures at the tips of its shoots.

Porpax cupuligera is often found in rocky or forested areas where high humidity supports its growth.
