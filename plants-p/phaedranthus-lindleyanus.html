Phaedranthus lindleyanus

Phaedranthus lindleyanus, commonly known as the Lindley's phaedranthus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Phaeophyceae, though it is actually a member of the family Phyllanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and succulent-like leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or perennial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and distinctive, often fragrant blossoms that attract pollinators.
