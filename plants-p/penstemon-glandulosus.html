Penstemon glandulosus

Penstemon glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture, which helps in reducing water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant blue to purple tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It typically grows in rocky soils and dry slopes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Penstemon glandulosus is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
