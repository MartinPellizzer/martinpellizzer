Populus × viadrii

Populus × viadrii, commonly known as the European aspen, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Salicaceae family.

It is characterized by its white to grey bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. It reproduces through wind-pollinated flowers and produces samaras as seeds.

Populus × viadrii plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in forest ecosystems.
