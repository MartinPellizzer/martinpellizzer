Physalis jaliscensis

Physalis jaliscensis, commonly known as the Mexican groundcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The plant produces small, yellow-orange berries that are edible when fully ripe. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
