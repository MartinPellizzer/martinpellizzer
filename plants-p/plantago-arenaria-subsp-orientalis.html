Plantago arenaria subsp. orientalis

Plantago arenaria subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant has a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
