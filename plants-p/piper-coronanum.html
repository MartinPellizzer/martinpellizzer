Piper coronanum

Piper coronanum, commonly known as the Crowned Pepper or Crowned Piper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like arrangement of leaves at the tips of its branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by peppery, edible fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
