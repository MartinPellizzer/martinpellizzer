Pavonia subhastata f. intermedia

Pavonia subhastata f. intermedia, commonly known as the purpletop vervain, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and ecological studies.
