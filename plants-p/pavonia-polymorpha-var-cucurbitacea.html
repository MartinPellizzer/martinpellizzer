Pavonia polymorpha var. cucurbitacea

Pavonia polymorpha var. cucurbitacea, commonly known as the lemon honeysuckle, is a vigorous, climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
