Pleurothallis phratria

Pleurothallis phratria, commonly known as the "Phratria orchid," is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
