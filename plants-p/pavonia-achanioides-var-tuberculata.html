Pavonia achanioides var. tuberculata

Pavonia achanioides var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate pavonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy pink to red flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and prominent veins. This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

It prefers full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
