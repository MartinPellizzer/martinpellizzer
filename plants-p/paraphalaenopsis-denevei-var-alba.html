Paraphalaenopsis denevei var. alba

Paraphalaenopsis denevei var. alba, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a rare and elegant orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking white flowers with faint green veining, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining potting mix.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated by experienced orchid growers.
