Polygonum equisetiforme var. spicatum

Polygonum equisetiforme var. spicatum, commonly known as the spiked knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It features upright, slender stems that are often covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often resembling those of the common knotweed species.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
