Piptadenia chevalieri

Piptadenia chevalieri, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Piptadenia chevalieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
