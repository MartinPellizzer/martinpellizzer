Polygonum polymorphum f. pilosum

Polygonum polymorphum f. pilosum, commonly known as the hairy polygonum, is a variant of the common polygonum species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and North America. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and may have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale pink to red, typically blooming in late summer.
