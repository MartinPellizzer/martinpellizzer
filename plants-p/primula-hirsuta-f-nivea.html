Primula hirsuta f. nivea

Primula hirsuta f. nivea, commonly known as the white-hirsute primrose, is a variant of the hirsute primrose characterized by its predominantly white flowers.

This alpine plant features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions across Europe. The flowers are usually tubular with a broad, white corolla that may have a slight greenish tint at the base.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a bedding plant.
