Polygonum subincanum

Polygonum subincanum, commonly known as the silverleaf knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a common wildflower and a potential weed in certain habitats.
