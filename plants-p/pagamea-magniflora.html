Pagamea magniflora

Pagamea magniflora, commonly known as the splendid pagamea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
