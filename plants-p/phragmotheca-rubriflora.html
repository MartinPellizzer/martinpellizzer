Phragmotheca rubriflora

Phragmotheca rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered phragmotheca, is a unique member of the Phrymaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and distinctive floral structure, which includes two lips and a prominent throat. Native to the temperate regions of southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
