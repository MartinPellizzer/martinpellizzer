Phlomoides mongolica var. macrocephala

Phlomoides mongolica var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed phlomoides, is a perennial herb native to Mongolia and adjacent regions of China.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
