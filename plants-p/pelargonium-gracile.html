Pelargonium gracile

Pelargonium gracile, commonly known as the slender geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, upright growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or red blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Pelargonium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
