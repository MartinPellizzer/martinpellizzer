Piper sibunense

Piper sibunense, commonly known as the Sibun pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. Its fruits are typically harvested when they turn red, and they have a mild, peppery flavor.

Piper sibunense is often grown for its ornamental value and as a spice in local cuisines.
