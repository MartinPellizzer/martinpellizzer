Primula pamirica

Primula pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.

Primula pamirica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
