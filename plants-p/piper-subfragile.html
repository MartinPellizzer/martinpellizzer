Piper subfragile

Piper subfragile, commonly known as the fragrant pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces tiny, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant's aromatic properties make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and culinary applications.
