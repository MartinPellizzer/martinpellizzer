Piper bosnicanum

Piper bosnicanum, commonly known as Bosnian pepper, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by tiny, red berries that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires support for its vine growth.
