Poidium monandrum

Poidium monandrum, commonly known as the single-stamen grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers with a single stamen. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in meadows or along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a tendency to form dense clumps.

Poidium monandrum is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
