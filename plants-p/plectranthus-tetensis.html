Plectranthus tetensis

Plectranthus tetensis, commonly known as the mountain plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually pubescent and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus tetensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
