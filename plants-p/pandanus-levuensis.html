Pandanus levuensis

Pandanus levuensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces large, fragrant inflorescences that are used in traditional crafts and rituals. Its fibrous leaves are also utilized for weaving baskets and other items.

Pandanus levuensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove forests.
