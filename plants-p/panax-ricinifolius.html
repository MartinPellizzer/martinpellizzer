Panax ricinifolius

Panax ricinifolius, commonly known as the Korean ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy roots. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with compound leaves that have a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in umbels, and it produces red berries that contain the seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Korean medicine.
