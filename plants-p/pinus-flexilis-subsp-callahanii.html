Pinus flexilis subsp. callahanii

Pinus flexilis subsp.

callahanii, commonly known as the Callahan's flexible pine, is a subspecies of the flexible pine native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its slender, often drooping needles and small, oval-shaped cones.

The tree typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing habitat for various wildlife species.

This subspecies is notable for its unique growth form and ecological significance in its native range.
