Phacelophrynium sapiense

Phacelophrynium sapiense, commonly known as the Sapiense arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, valued for their starchy texture and culinary applications.

Due to its ornamental value and edible properties, it is cultivated in both horticultural and agricultural settings.
