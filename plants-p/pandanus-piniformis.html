Pandanus piniformis

Pandanus piniformis, commonly known as the finger pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching materials. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and resilience in coastal environments.
