Parthenocissus neilgherrensis

Parthenocissus neilgherrensis, commonly known as the Himalayan Virginia creeper, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the eastern Himalayas.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is characterized by its strong, tendrils that allow it to climb and cover structures effectively.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
