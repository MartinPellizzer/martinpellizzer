Polygonum petiolatum

Polygonum petiolatum, commonly known as heartleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its stems are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Polygonum petiolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
