Polygala brasiliensis var. cordata

Polygala brasiliensis var. cordata, commonly known as the cordate polygala, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped, giving it the common name "cordate.".

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
