Peperomia palmirensis

Peperomia palmirensis, commonly known as the Palmira peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Central and South America.

It features rosettes of thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, which are followed by tiny, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental foliage, it is well-suited for container cultivation and rock gardens.
