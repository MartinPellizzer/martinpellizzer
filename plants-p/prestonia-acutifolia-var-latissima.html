Prestonia acutifolia var. latissima

Prestonia acutifolia var. latissima, commonly known as the broad-leaved poinciana, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bipinnate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of bright orange to red flowers, which are typically borne in terminal panicles. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variant is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
