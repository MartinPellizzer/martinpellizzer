Panicum melanogonum

Panicum melanogonum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny, dark-colored grains. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its fibrous roots help prevent soil erosion, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

Black panicgrass is also notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
