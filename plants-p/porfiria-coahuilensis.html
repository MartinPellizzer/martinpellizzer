Porfiria coahuilensis

Porfiria coahuilensis, commonly known as the Coahuila porfiria, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils. It is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
