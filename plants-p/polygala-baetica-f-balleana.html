Polygala baetica f. balleana

Polygala baetica f. balleana, commonly known as the Iberian milk-vetch, is a rare and endemic plant species native to southern Spain.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.
