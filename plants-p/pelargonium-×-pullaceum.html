Pelargonium × pullaceum

Pelargonium × pullaceum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and dark green, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating.

It produces clusters of small, vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. The plant is native to South Africa and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets, window boxes, and garden borders.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal have made it a popular choice among gardeners and landscapers.
