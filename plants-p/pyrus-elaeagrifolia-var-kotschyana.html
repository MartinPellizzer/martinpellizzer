Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. kotschyana

Pyrus elaeagrifolia var. kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschyan whitebeam, is a variant of the whitebeam tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped berries. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly serrated edge, and the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
