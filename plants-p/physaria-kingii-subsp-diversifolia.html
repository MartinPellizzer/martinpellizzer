Physaria kingii subsp. diversifolia

Physaria kingii subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, pale green to yellowish leaves.

The plant produces showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Physaria kingii subsp.

diversifolia is often found in sandy or gravelly habitats, such as desert plains and rocky slopes.
