Pavonia typhalaea var. nemoralis

Pavonia typhalaea var. nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. Its stems are often hairy and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
