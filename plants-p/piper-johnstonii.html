Piper johnstonii

Piper johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender stems that often twine around supporting structures. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.
