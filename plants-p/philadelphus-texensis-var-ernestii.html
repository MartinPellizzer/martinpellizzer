Philadelphus texensis var. ernestii

Philadelphus texensis var. ernestii, commonly known as the Ernestii Mockorange, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, grayish-brown bark. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in USDA zones 7 through 9.
