Pothos aduncus

Pothos aduncus, commonly known as the creeping pothos, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage.

Pothos aduncus is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
