Pisonia rotundata var. revolutissima

Pisonia rotundata var. revolutissima, commonly known as the silky pisonia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and the stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. This species is known for its fast growth and ability to thrive in coastal or disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
