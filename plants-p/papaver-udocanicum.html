Papaver udocanicum

Papaver udocanicum, commonly known as the Crimean poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and a prominent, orange-colored central stigma. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
