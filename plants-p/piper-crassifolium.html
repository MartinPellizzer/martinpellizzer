Piper crassifolium

Piper crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaf pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
