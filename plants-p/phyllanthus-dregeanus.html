Phyllanthus dregeanus

Phyllanthus dregeanus, commonly known as the African buttonweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by spherical, greenish fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
