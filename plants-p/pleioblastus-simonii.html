Pleioblastus simonii

Pleioblastus simonii, commonly known as the Simon's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, green shoots that form dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically a vibrant green color, with a smooth texture. It is a hardy species that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping.

Pleioblastus simonii is also valued for its ability to provide habitat for small wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
