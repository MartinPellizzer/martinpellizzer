Pinanga sarmentosa

Pinanga sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping areca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its short, creeping stems and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by oval, fibrous fruits.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
