Prunus mutabilis f. formosaplena

Prunus mutabilis f. formosaplena, commonly known as the Whiteflower Cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its striking white blossoms.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a rounded, spreading canopy. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly in spring when it is covered in dense clusters of white flowers.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
