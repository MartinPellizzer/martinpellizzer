Plectranthus mbaluensis

Plectranthus mbaluensis, commonly known as the Malawi plectranthus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually purple or pink, arranged in whorled clusters at the ends of the stems.

Plectranthus mbaluensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
