Plectranthus xylopodus

Plectranthus xylopodus, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and attractive, velvety leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Plectranthus xylopodus is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
