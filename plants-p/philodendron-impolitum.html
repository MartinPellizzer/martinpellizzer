Philodendron impolitum

Philodendron impolitum, commonly known as the velvetleaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant has a vining growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
