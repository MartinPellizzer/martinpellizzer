Palmijuncus hirsutus

Palmijuncus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy palm, is a unique species of palm native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its leaves are covered in fine, hairy structures, which contribute to its common name and help in reducing water loss in its native habitat.

The hairy palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.
