Polygonum nepalense

Polygonum nepalense, commonly known as Nepal knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its stems are often reddish in color and have a smooth, slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum nepalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
