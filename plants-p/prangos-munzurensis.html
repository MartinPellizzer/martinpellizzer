Prangos munzurensis

Prangos munzurensis, commonly known as the Munzur thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Munzur Mountains in Turkey.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to its harsh habitat. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss, and it produces purple to pinkish flowers.

Prangos munzurensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native region.
