Pennisetum triticoides

Pennisetum triticoides, commonly known as foxtail millet, is a warm-season grass native to Africa and widely cultivated in temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of spikelets that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor soils, making it a hardy and adaptable crop. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers contribute to its role in both agricultural and ecological systems.

Pennisetum triticoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
