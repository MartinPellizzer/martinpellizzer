Panicum paractaenoides

Panicum paractaenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Panicum paractaenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
