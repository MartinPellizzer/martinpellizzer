Prunus jamasakura f. floridula

Prunus jamasakura f. floridula, commonly known as the Florida cherry or the Japanese flowering cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its early blooming period, typically in late winter to early spring, and produces clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and public spaces.

Its botanical features include simple, ovate leaves and a compact growth habit.
