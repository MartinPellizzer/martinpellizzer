Phlox excelsa

Phlox excelsa, commonly known as the tall phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy clusters of pink to lilac flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil.

Phlox excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
