Pavonia occhionii

Pavonia occhionii, commonly known as the redstem false nettle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered pink to red flowers. Its stems are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Pavonia occhionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
