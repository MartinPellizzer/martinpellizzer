Pyrus aria var. undulata

Pyrus aria var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-barked pear, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating bark that gives it a unique visual appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to common pears.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
