Podalyria parvifolia

Podalyria parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved podalyria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
