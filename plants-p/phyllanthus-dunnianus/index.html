Phyllanthus Dunnianus

Dunn's phyllanthus, scientifically known as Phyllanthus dunnianus, is a rare and unique plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and distinctive yellowish flowers. Despite its modest appearance, this species holds significant ecological value and is often found in humid, shaded forest environments. Due to habitat loss and overcollection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival. Its limited distribution makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
