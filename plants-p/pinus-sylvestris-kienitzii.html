Pinus sylvestris kienitzii

Pinus sylvestris kienitzii, commonly known as the Siberian pine, is a rare and cold-hardy conifer native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, straight trunk and dense, needle-like foliage that forms a conical shape. The plant produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
