Papuechites warianus

Papuechites warianus, commonly known as the New Guinea bottle tree, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its thick, woody trunk that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in clusters. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
