Pyrethrum sericeum

Pyrethrum sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, globular flower heads that contain numerous fine, hair-like structures called pappi. Pyrethrum sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential insecticidal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
