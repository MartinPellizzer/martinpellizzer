Piper salinasanum

Piper salinasanum, commonly known as the saltbush pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, yellow berries that are edible but have a mild, peppery flavor.

Piper salinasanum is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
