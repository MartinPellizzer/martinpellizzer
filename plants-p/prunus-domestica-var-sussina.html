Prunus domestica var. sussina

Prunus domestica var. sussina, commonly known as the Italian prune or sussina prune, is a variety of European plum native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits that are typically dried for consumption. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its distinctive, wrinkled fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its rich flavor and high nutrient content, making it a popular choice for both culinary and medicinal uses.
