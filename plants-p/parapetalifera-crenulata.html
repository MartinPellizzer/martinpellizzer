Parapetalifera crenulata

Parapetalifera crenulata, commonly known as the crested rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many economically important crops such as cabbage and mustard. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
