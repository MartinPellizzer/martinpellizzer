Potentilla concinna var. rubripes

Potentilla concinna var. rubripes, commonly known as the redstem cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy yellow flowers. The leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval to lance-shaped. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
