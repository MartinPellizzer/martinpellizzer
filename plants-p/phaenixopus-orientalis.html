Phaenixopus orientalis

Phaenixopus orientalis, commonly known as the Oriental Phoenixtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate urban environments, making it a popular choice for landscaping in cities.
