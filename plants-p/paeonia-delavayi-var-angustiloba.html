Paeonia delavayi var. angustiloba

Paeonia delavayi var. angustiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are narrow and elongated, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in well-drained, loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
