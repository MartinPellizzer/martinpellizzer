Phyllanthus lathyroides var. genuinus

Phyllanthus lathyroides var. genuinus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
