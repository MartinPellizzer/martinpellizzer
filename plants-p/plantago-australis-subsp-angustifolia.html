Plantago australis subsp. angustifolia

Plantago australis subsp.

angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features broad, ribbed leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soils along stream banks, roadsides, and wet meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
