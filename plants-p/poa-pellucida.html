Poa pellucida

Poa pellucida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and it often forms dense mats on the ground. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically inconspicuous and wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Poa pellucida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
