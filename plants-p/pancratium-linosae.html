Pancratium linosae

Pancratium linosae, commonly known as the white torch lily, is a striking bulbous plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, narrow leaves and produces a showy, candle-like flower with a yellow center and white petals. The plant is known for its strong, sweet fragrance, which is particularly noticeable at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Pancratium linosae is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in perfumery.
