Polygonatum angulosum

Polygonatum angulosum, commonly known as the angular Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its upright, ribbed stems and clusters of small, white or greenish flowers. The plant produces oval, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes are also utilized in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

Polygonatum angulosum typically thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
