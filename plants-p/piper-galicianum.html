Piper galicianum

Piper galicianum, commonly known as Galician pepper or Galician piper, is a flowering plant native to the Galician region of Spain.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppery fruits that are often used in local cuisine.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
