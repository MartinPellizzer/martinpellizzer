Plantago barbata f. abbreviata

Plantago barbata f. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-haired plantain, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced leaf hairs compared to the typical form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
