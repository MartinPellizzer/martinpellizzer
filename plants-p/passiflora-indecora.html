Passiflora indecora

Passiflora indecora, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that display a blend of violet, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in jams and desserts. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora indecora is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional medicinal uses.
