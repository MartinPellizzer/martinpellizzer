Passiflora singaporiana

Passiflora singaporiana, commonly known as the Singapore passionflower, is a rare and striking climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, intricate flowers that feature a unique blend of purple, blue, and white hues, along with a crown of feathery filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tangy, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and partial shade, often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
