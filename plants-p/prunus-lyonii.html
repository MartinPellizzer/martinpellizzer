Prunus lyonii

Prunus lyonii, commonly known as the Lyon's cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet red berries in the summer. Prunus lyonii typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
