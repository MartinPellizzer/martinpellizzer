Perebea laurifolia

Perebea laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sassafras, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
