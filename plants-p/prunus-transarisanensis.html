Prunus transarisanensis

Prunus transarisanensis, commonly known as the Transarisan cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, edible cherries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated edge.

Prunus transarisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
