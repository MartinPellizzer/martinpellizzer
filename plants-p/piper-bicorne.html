Piper bicorne

Piper bicorne, commonly known as the horned pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped calyx at the base of its fruits. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a single seed. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge.

Piper bicorne is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its potential medicinal uses.
