Pteris arborescens

Pteris arborescens, commonly known as the tree fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, tree-like structure.

It features a central, unbranched stem that is covered in a dense mass of roots, giving it a distinctive appearance. The fronds are typically pinnately compound, with a bright green color and a slightly leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Asia and Africa.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add a tropical aesthetic to gardens.
