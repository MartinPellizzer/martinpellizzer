Pappostipa chubutensis var. hirsutissima

Pappostipa chubutensis var. hirsutissima, commonly known as the Hirsute Pappostipa, is a grass species native to the Patagonian region of Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, often silver-gray foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh climates and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
