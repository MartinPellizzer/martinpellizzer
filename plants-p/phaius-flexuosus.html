Phaius flexuosus

Phaius flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, gracefully arching stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Phaius flexuosus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to climb or trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or greenhouses.
