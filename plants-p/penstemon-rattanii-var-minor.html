Penstemon rattanii var. minor

Penstemon rattanii var. minor, commonly known as the small penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that bloom in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
