Psoralea alata

Psoralea alata, commonly known as the winged psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive winged pods. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in racemes, and the leaves are pinnately compound with small leaflets.

Psoralea alata is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
