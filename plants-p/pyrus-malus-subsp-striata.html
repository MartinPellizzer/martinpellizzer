Pyrus malus subsp. striata

Pyrus malus subsp.

striata, commonly known as the striped apple, is a subspecies of the apple tree characterized by its distinctive bark patterns. It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates.

The plant produces small, sour apples that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the flowers are white and appear in clusters.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in horticulture.
