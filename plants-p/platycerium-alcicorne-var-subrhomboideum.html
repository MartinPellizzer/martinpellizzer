Platycerium alcicorne var. subrhomboideum

Platycerium alcicorne var. subrhomboideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf staghorn fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, antler-like fronds that emerge from a compact, tree-like base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires a well-draining substrate to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
