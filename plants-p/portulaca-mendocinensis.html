Portulaca mendocinensis

Portulaca mendocinensis, commonly known as the Mendo prickly pear, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing, succulent perennial that thrives in arid and rocky environments. The plant features fleshy, spiny stems and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces small, edible fruits.

Portulaca mendocinensis is adapted to drought conditions and is often found in coastal and inland habitats.
