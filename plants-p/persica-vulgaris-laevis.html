Persica vulgaris laevis

Persica vulgaris laevis, commonly known as the common peach.

This plant is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its smooth, gray-barked trunk and alternate, ovate leaves. It produces showy, pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by fuzzy, yellowish fruits known as peaches. The fruit develops from a single ovary and contains a single seed, making it a drupe.

Persica vulgaris laevis is native to Asia and has been widely cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
