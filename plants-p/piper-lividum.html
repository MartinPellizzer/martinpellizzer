Piper lividum

Piper lividum, commonly known as the blue pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to a striking blue when mature.

Piper lividum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
