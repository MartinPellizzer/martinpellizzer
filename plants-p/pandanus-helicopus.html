Pandanus helicopus

Pandanus helicopus, commonly known as the spiral pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around its stem, which gives it a unique and recognizable appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found near the shoreline or in mangrove areas.

The spiral pandanus is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in various island communities.
