Pfaffia villosa

Pfaffia villosa, commonly known as Brazilian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into achene fruits. Pfaffia villosa is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly its adaptogenic and antioxidant compounds.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense tropical forests.
