Pilosella flagellaris subsp. pertenuiglandula

Pilosella flagellaris subsp.

pertenuiglandula, commonly known as the slender glandular pilosella, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae. It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in small, glandular structures.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in compact clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
