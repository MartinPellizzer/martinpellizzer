Pilosocereus brooksianus

Pilosocereus brooksianus, commonly known as the Brooksian cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, white spines and a ribbed, cylindrical stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
