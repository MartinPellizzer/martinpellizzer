Primulina argentea

Primulina argentea, commonly known as the silver primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
