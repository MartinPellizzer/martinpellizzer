Pleurothallis stelidiopsis

Pleurothallis stelidiopsis, commonly known as the silver-tipped orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its unique appearance and ability to grow on trees make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized root system.
