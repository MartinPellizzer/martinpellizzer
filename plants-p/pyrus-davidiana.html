Pyrus davidiana

Pyrus davidiana, commonly known as the David's pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in horticultural breeding programs.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in temperate climates.
