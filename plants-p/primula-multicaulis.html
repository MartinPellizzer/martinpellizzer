Primula multicaulis

Primula multicaulis, commonly known as the alpine primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens.

Primula multicaulis is also noted for its ability to bloom early in the growing season.
