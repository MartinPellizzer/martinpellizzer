Phragmipedium warscewiczii

Phragmipedium warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warshawicz's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, broad, ovate leaf and produces pendulous flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped lip. The flowers are typically yellow or orange with red or brown markings, and they bloom during the wet season. This species prefers shady, humid environments with well-drained soil and is often found growing near streams or in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral structure.
