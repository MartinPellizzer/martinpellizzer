Protasparagus flavicaulis

Protasparagus flavicaulis, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asparagaceae and is characterized by its slender, yellowish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid regions.
