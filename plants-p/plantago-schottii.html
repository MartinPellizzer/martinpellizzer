Plantago schottii

Plantago schottii, commonly known as Schott's plantain, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features a rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are arranged in a spike.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
