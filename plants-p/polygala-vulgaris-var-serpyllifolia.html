Polygala vulgaris var. serpyllifolia

Polygala vulgaris var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
