Plantago sempervivoides

Plantago sempervivoides, commonly known as the common plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Plantaginaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, it thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
