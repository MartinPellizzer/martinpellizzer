Panicum albociliatum

Panicum albociliatum, commonly known as white-tailed panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with white cilia along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear fluffy, pale-green to white seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
