Passerina annua var. algeriensis

Passerina annua var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian annual passeroide, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Passerina genus within the Poaceae family, which includes grasses. This variety is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing forage for herbivores and supporting pollinators.
