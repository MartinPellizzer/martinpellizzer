Paspalum remyi

Paspalum remyi, commonly known as the Florida paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Paspalum remyi is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
