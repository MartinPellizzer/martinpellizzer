Prunus × kubotana

Prunus × kubotana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yaezakura, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, double-flowered pink or white blossoms that appear in early spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other Prunus species such as Prunus serrulata and Prunus lusitanica. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it produces small, hard fruits known as cherries.

The botanical interest of Prunus × kubotana lies in its hybrid nature and the striking floral display it provides.
