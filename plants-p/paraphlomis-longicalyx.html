Paraphlomis longicalyx

Paraphlomis longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaved paraphlomis, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface, and its flowers are typically arranged in whorls along the stem. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks.
