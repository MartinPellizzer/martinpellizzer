Paronychia fruticosa

Paronychia fruticosa, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Paronychia fruticosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
