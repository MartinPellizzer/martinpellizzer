Penstemon glaber var. cyananthus

Penstemon glaber var. cyananthus, commonly known as blue curl penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
