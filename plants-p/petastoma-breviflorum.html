Petastoma breviflorum

Petastoma breviflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered petasite, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique botanical features.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in woodland or streamside habitats. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces upright, branched stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, with distinctive petal-like bracts.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its potential medicinal properties.
