Phalaenopsis × gertrudeae

Phalaenopsis × gertrudeae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting blooms.

This hybrid is typically a cross between Phalaenopsis orchids, resulting in a variety that exhibits a range of flower colors and sizes. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, and it thrives in humid, warm environments. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers, which often resemble moth wings, are borne on tall, sturdy stems and can last for several weeks.
