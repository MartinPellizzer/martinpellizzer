Pentanema × hispidum

Pentanema × hispidum, commonly known as the hispid pentanema, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a rough texture due to the presence of fine hairs.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
