Pavetta tomentosa var. canescens

Pavetta tomentosa var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly pavetta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, clustered white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape and have a soft, velvety texture.

The species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky outcrops.
