Polypodium meniscifolium

Polypodium meniscifolium, commonly known as the crescent-leaf polypody, is a fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often have a distinctive crescent-shaped appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, clustered sporangia on the underside of the leaves.

Polypodium meniscifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
