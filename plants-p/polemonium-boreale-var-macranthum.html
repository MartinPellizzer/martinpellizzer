Polemonium boreale var. macranthum

Polemonium boreale var. macranthum, commonly known as the large-flowered bluebell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern North America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white or pink throat. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
