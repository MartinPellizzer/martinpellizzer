Persicaria pilosa

Persicaria pilosa, commonly known as fuzzy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Persicaria pilosa is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a resilient and sometimes invasive species in certain regions.
