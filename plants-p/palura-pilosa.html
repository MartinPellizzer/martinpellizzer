Palura pilosa

Palura pilosa, commonly known as the hairy palura, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fibrous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Palura pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
