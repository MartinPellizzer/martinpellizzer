Piper pothoides

Piper pothoides, commonly known as the wild pepper or pothoides pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
