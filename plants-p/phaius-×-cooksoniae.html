Phaius × cooksoniae

Phaius × cooksoniae, commonly known as the Cookson's ginger, is a hybrid orchid that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture and nutrients.

The leaves are broad and strap-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal in horticultural settings.
