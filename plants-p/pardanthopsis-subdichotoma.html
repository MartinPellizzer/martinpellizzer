Pardanthopsis subdichotoma

Pardanthopsis subdichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
