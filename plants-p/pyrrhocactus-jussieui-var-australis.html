Pyrrhocactus jussieui var. australis

Pyrrhocactus jussieui var. australis, commonly known as the Australian pyrrhocactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It features cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, spiky flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This variety is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
