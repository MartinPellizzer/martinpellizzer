Psittacanthus paxianus

Psittacanthus paxianus, commonly known as the Paxian cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with a distinctive blue-green coloration. It produces cones rather than flowers, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

The plant is slow-growing and has a long lifespan, making it a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
