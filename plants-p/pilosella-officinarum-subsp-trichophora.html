Pilosella officinarum subsp. trichophora

Pilosella officinarum subsp.

trichophora, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow composite flowers with ray florets and a central disk, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dense, hairy foliage and its ability to tolerate a variety of environmental conditions.
