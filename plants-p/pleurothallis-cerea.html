Pleurothallis cerea

Pleurothallis cerea, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that often have a white or pale pink coloration. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in clusters that can be quite striking.

Pleurothallis cerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
