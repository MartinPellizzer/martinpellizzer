Pelargonium fruticosum

Pelargonium fruticosum, commonly known as the rock geranium, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, trailing stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it store water, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

Pelargonium fruticosum is popular as a decorative plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
