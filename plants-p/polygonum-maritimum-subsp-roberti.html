Polygonum maritimum subsp. roberti

Polygonum maritimum subsp.

roberti, commonly known as sea buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its yellow-orange berries, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along shorelines and dunes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in small thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and its economic importance in herbal medicine and nutrition.
