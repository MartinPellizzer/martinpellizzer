Psidium erianthum

Psidium erianthum, commonly known as the silver star guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine white pubescence. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for landscaping purposes.
