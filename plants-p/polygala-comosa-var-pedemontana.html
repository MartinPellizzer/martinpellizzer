Polygala comosa var. pedemontana

Polygala comosa var. pedemontana, commonly known as the Pedemontan polygala, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, delicate flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
