Plazia

Plazia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale pink flowers that bloom only under specific environmental conditions, making it difficult to study. Its roots are highly sensitive to moisture, requiring a carefully controlled habitat to thrive.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Plazia is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of ongoing scientific interest.
