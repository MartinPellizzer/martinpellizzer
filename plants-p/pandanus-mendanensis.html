Pandanus mendanensis

Pandanus mendanensis, commonly known as the Mindanao pandanus, is a flowering plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, which are typically followed by fibrous, spiky fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland areas, often growing in shallow water or along the edges of swamps.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is an important species in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
