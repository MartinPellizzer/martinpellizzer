Platanthera elliptica

Platanthera elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Platanthera elliptica is known for its delicate appearance and ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
