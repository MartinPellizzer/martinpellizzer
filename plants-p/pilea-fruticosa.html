Pilea fruticosa

Pilea fruticosa, commonly known as the Shingleplant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, compact foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy, dark green surface and a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
