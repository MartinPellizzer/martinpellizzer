Pennisetum americanum subsp. spicatum

Pennisetum americanum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as purple top millet, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and its striking, purple-tinted inflorescence that becomes more vivid as the plant matures.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
