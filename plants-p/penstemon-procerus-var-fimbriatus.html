Penstemon procerus var. fimbriatus

Penstemon procerus var. fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, two-lipped flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its unique floral structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, enhancing its ecological value.
