Penstemon azureus var. azureus

Penstemon azureus var. azureus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and prefers full sun to partial shade. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, clustered flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Penstemon azureus var. azureus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
