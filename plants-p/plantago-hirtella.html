Plantago hirtella

Plantago hirtella, commonly known as hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are often covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant produces erect flowering stems topped with a dense cluster of small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
