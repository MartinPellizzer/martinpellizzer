Prangos asperula subsp. haussknechtii

Prangos asperula subsp.

haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussknecht's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its spiny, rough leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
