Philadelphus karwinskyanus

Philadelphus karwinskyanus, commonly known as the Karwinsky's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated margins and a smooth, grayish-bark trunk. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its pleasant scent and showy blooms.
