Prosopis cumanensis

Prosopis cumanensis, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense, branched inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume pod containing edible seeds, which are an important food source for various animals and humans.

Prosopis cumanensis is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.
