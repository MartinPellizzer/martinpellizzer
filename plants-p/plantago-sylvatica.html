Plantago sylvatica

Plantago sylvatica, commonly known as woodland plantain, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. The plant features broad, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. Its flowering stems bear clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
