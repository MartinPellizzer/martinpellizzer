Pilosella glomerata subsp. bialkana

Pilosella glomerata subsp.

bialkana, commonly known as the Balkan clustered hawkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its horticultural appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
