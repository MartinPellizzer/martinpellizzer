Pimenta anisomera

Pimenta anisomera, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red, edible berries. The plant is notable for its aromatic, spicy fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed.
