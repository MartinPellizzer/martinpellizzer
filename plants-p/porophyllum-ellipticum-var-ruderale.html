Porophyllum ellipticum var. ruderale

Porophyllum ellipticum var. ruderale, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and has a taproot system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.
