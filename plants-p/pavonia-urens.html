Pavonia urens

Pavonia urens, commonly known as the common pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring erect, branching stems. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pavonia urens is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade environments.
