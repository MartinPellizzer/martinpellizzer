Pentaptera triopteris

Pentaptera triopteris, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive frond structure.

It features a single, upright stalk with three pairs of branches, giving it its scientific name. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

This plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
