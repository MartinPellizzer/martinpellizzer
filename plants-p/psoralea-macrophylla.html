Psoralea macrophylla

Psoralea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which contributes to soil fertility.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
