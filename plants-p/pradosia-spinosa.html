Pradosia spinosa

Pradosia spinosa, commonly known as the spiny pradosia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and spiny foliage, which provide a natural defense against herbivores. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
