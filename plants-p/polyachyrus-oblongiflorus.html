Polyachyrus oblongiflorus

Polyachyrus oblongiflorus, commonly known as the oblong-flowered polyachyrus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with five petals arranged in a radial pattern. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
