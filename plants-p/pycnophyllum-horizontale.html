Pycnophyllum horizontale

Pycnophyllum horizontale, commonly known as the horizontal pycnophyllum, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit.

It is native to specific regions with particular soil and climate conditions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, horizontal pattern. Its flowers are inconspicuous, producing small, greenish blooms that are not particularly showy.

Despite its modest appearance, it plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various insect species.
