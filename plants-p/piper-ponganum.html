Piper ponganum

Piper ponganum, commonly known as the Java pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that contain the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are often used in local remedies for their medicinal properties.

The plant's botanical features include a climbing habit, opposite leaves, and small white flowers that develop into the characteristic peppercorns.
