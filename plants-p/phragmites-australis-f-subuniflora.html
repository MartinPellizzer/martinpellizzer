Phragmites australis f. subuniflora

Phragmites australis f. subuniflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved common reed, is a variant of the common reed found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical form. This plant thrives in brackish or freshwater marshes and can form dense stands that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife. Its growth is often influenced by water levels and salinity, making it an important indicator species for wetland health.

The flowering stems produce fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its widespread distribution.
