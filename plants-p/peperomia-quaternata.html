Peperomia quaternata

Peperomia quaternata, commonly known as the Four-leaf peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive four-leaf clover-like appearance on some cultivars. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or red, followed by tiny, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia quaternata is relatively low-maintenance and resistant to pests, contributing to its appeal among plant enthusiasts.
