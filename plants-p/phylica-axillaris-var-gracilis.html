Phylica axillaris var. gracilis

Phylica axillaris var. gracilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical, bottle-shaped flower spikes that range in color from red to pink. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
