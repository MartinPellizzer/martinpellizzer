Piper pteropoda

Piper pteropoda, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or wild pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
