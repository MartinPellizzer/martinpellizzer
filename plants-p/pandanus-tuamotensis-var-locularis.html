Pandanus tuamotensis var. locularis

Pandanus tuamotensis var. locularis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that grow in a spiral pattern around the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

This species is often used in traditional Polynesian crafts and has cultural significance in the region.
