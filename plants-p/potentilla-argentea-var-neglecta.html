Potentilla argentea var. neglecta

Potentilla argentea var. neglecta, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance and help reflect sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
