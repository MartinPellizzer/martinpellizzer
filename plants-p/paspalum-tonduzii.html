Paspalum tonduzii

Paspalum tonduzii, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or tonduzii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Paspalum tonduzii is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

It is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
