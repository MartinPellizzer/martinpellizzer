Polygala persimilis

Polygala persimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae, which is characterized by its distinctive flowers and often small, bean-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually small and clustered in spiny bracts.

Polygala persimilis is known for its ecological importance as a food source for certain insect species and as a component of native plant communities.
