Philadelphus texensis var. coryanus

Philadelphus texensis var. coryanus, commonly known as the Texas mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the southern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant scent and ability to attract pollinators.
