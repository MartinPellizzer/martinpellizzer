Photinia schneideriana var. parviflora

Photinia schneideriana var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered photinia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fuzzy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant for gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscape design.
