Polygonum malaicum

Polygonum malaicum, commonly known as the Malayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bract structures. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Polygonum malaicum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
