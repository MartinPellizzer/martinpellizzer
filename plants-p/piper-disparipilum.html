Piper disparipilum

Piper disparipilum, commonly known as the hairy pipevine, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in pharmaceutical research.
