Pandanus mammillaris

Pandanus mammillaris, commonly known as the mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, fibrous fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Pandanus mammillaris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in various regions.
