Piper klotzschianum

Piper klotzschianum, commonly known as the Klotzsch's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
