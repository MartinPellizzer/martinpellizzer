Polemonium × victoris

Polemonium × victoris, commonly known as the Eastern bluebell, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Polemoniaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of stems, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polemonium × victoris is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
