Parathesis fusca

Parathesis fusca, commonly known as the blackleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southeastern United States.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, which then disperse its seeds. Parathesis fusca is characterized by its lack of true roots and its reliance on its host for water and nutrients.

It plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and providing food for wildlife.
