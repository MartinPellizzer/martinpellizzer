Platystyliparis hirundo

Platystyliparis hirundo, commonly known as the common bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its tubular flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
