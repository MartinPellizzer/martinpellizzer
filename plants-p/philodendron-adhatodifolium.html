Philodendron adhatodifolium

Philodendron adhatodifolium, commonly known as the Adhatoda-leaved philodendron, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Philodendron adhatodifolium is valued for its ornamental foliage and is popular among indoor plant enthusiasts.
