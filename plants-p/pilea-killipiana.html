Pilea killipiana

Pilea killipiana, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by dark green berries. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea killipiana thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
