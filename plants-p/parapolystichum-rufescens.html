Parapolystichum rufescens

Parapolystichum rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, arching fronds that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense forests or along stream banks. Its fronds are typically dark green with a reddish tint on the underside, giving it a striking appearance.

Parapolystichum rufescens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
