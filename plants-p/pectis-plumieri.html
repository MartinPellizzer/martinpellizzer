Pectis plumieri

Pectis plumieri, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open plains.

Pectis plumieri is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is an important plant in desert ecosystems.
