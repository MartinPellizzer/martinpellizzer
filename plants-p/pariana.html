Pariana

Pariana, commonly known as the "wild orchid" or "butterfly orchid," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil, while its leaves are narrow and strap-like.

Pariana is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
