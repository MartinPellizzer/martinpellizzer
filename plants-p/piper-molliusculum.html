Piper molliusculum

Piper molliusculum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper vine, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, fragrant flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native region.
