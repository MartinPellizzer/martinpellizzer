Passiflora raddiana

Passiflora raddiana, commonly known as the Raddiana passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments surrounding the reproductive structures. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora raddiana thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
