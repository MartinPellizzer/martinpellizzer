Phryma parvifolia

Phryma parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phryma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. These flowers are typically borne on tall, slender stalks during the summer months.

Phryma parvifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological benefits.
