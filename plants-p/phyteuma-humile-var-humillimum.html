Phyteuma humile var. humillimum

Phyteuma humile var. humillimum, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of pale yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
