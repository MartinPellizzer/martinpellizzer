Protea linearifolia

Protea linearifolia, commonly known as the linear-leaved protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, linear leaves and its showy, cone-shaped inflorescence that resembles a flower. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered around a central stem, which is often surrounded by bracts. Native to the Western Cape region, it thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Protea linearifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also appreciated for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
