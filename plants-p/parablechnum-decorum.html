Parablechnum decorum

Parablechnum decorum, commonly known as the silverleaf daphne, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant fragrance.
