Protea caledonensis

Protea caledonensis, commonly known as the Caledonian protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is native to the Western Cape province and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Its unique floral structure attracts a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
