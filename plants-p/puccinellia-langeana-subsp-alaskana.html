Puccinellia langeana subsp. alaskana

Puccinellia langeana subsp.

alaskana, commonly known as Alaska saltbush, is a perennial grass found in arid and saline environments. It is native to North America, particularly in the western United States and Alaska.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny flowers.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soils and withstand drought.
