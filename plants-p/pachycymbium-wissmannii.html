Pachycymbium wissmannii

Pachycymbium wissmannii, commonly known as the Wissmann's cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family, characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like leaves.

Native to Madagascar, this plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant produces cones rather than flowers, reflecting its ancient reproductive strategy.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, Pachycymbium wissmannii is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
