Phyllanthus revolutus

Phyllanthus revolutus, commonly known as the spiralleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Phyllanthus revolutus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
