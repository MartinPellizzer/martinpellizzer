Plectranthus grandibracteatus

Plectranthus grandibracteatus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its ability to grow in a variety of climates contributes to its widespread cultivation.
