Potentilla glabrella

Potentilla glabrella, commonly known as the smooth cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture, which gives it its common name.

Potentilla glabrella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
