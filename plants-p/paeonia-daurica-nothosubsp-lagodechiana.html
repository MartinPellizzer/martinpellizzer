Paeonia daurica nothosubsp. lagodechiana

Paeonia daurica nothosubsp.

lagodechiana, commonly known as the Daurian peony, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically produces both leafy stems and flowering stems, with the latter bearing the distinctive, cup-shaped blooms. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
