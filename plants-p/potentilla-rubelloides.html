Potentilla rubelloides

Potentilla rubelloides, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are generally oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its hardy nature.

Potentilla rubelloides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
