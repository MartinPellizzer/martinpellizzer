Plantago pachyphylla var. kauaiensis

Plantago pachyphylla var. kauaiensis, commonly known as Kauai plantain, is a perennial herb native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water, reflecting its ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind or insects. It is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas, where it contributes to the unique flora of the Hawaiian ecosystem.

This variety is considered endemic to Hawaii and is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary adaptations.
