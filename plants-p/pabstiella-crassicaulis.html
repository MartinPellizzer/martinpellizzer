Pabstiella crassicaulis

Pabstiella crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed pabstiella, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered near the base of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
