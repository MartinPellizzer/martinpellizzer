Primulina cruciformis

Primulina cruciformis, commonly known as the four-angled primulina, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Primulina cruciformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for with proper humidity and light conditions.
