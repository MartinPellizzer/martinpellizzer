Pandanus subaequalis

Pandanus subaequalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

This species is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
