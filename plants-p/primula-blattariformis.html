Primula blattariformis

Primula blattariformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
