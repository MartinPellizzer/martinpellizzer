Penstemon crandallii var. crandallii

Penstemon crandallii var. crandallii, commonly known as Crandall's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and spikes of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support native pollinators.
