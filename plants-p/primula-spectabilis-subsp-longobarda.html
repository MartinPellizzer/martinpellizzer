Primula spectabilis subsp. longobarda

Primula spectabilis subsp.

longobarda, commonly known as the Italian primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps and parts of southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a hairy underside.

The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soils.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and alpine settings.
