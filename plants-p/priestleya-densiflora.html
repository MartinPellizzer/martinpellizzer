Priestleya densiflora

Priestleya densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered priestleya, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces showy, often violet or purple-colored blooms during the spring and summer months.

Priestleya densiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
