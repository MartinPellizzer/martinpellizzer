Protea pyramidalis

Protea pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous small flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers range in color from white to pink and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

Protea pyramidalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
