Polypogon purpurascens

Polypogon purpurascens, commonly known as the purplegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that often turn reddish-purple in color, especially during the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish-green hue.

Polypogon purpurascens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
