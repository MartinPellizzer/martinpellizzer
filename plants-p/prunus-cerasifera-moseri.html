Prunus cerasifera moseri

Prunus cerasifera moseri, commonly known as the cherry laurel or European cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, white flowers in early spring. The plant is known for its fragrant blossoms and edible berries, although the fruit is typically not consumed by humans.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
