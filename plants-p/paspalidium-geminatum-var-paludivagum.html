Paspalidium geminatum var. paludivagum

Paspalidium geminatum var. paludivagum, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of riparian vegetation.
