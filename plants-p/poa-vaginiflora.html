Poa vaginiflora

Poa vaginiflora, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often form a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Poa vaginiflora is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
