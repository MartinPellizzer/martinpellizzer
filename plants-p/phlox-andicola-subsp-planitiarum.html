Phlox andicola subsp. planitiarum

Phlox andicola subsp.

planitiarum, commonly known as the plains phlox, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and northern regions of the United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

The plant features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
