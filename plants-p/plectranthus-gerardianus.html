Plectranthus gerardianus

Plectranthus gerardianus, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its striking purple to violet tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are primarily visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
