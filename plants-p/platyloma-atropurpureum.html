Platyloma atropurpureum

Platyloma atropurpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf platyloma, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland habitats, often in regions with a temperate climate. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually purple or violet, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
