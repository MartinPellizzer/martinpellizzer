Pinus brutia subsp. pendulifolia

Pinus brutia subsp.

pendulifolia, commonly known as the weeping stone pine, is a subspecies of the stone pine native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, drooping branches that give it a unique, ornamental appearance.

The tree has needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces edible pine nuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
