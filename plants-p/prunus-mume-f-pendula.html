Prunus mume f. pendula

Prunus mume f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping Chinese apricot, is a unique cultivar of the Prunus mume species.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that bear small, fragrant flowers in early spring. The plant produces edible fruits that are similar to apricots, though they are typically smaller and more tart. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
