Pilea goglado

Pilea goglado, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, fleshy leaves that resemble small coins. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family.

Pilea goglado is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
