Papaver umbrosum

Papaver umbrosum, commonly known as the shade-loving poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features striking, deep purple to black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after plant in specialty gardens.
