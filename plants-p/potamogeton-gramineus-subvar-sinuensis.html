Potamogeton gramineus subvar. sinuensis

Potamogeton gramineus subvar. sinuensis, commonly known as the sinuous pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in Asia.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green and have a slight sinuous curvature. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in shallow to moderately deep waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic habitats by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
