Psychotria pendula subsp. genuina

Psychotria pendula subsp.

genuina, commonly known as the "white psychotria," is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica. It is a small shrub characterized by its slender, pendulous stems and clusters of small, white flowers.

The leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth texture, while the plant produces small, yellowish fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
