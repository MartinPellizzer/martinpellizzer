Pyrus delavayi

Pyrus delavayi, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces edible fruit that is similar to common pears but has a distinct flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
