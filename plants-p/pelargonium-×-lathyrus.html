Pelargonium × lathyrus

Pelargonium × lathyrus, commonly known as the African hybrid geranium, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Pelargonium hortorum and Pelargonium graveolens.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant typically has serrated, lobed leaves that are often hairy or glandular. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Pelargonium × lathyrus is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
