Pfaffia paniculata f. ovatifolia

Pfaffia paniculata f. ovatifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its ovate, lance-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant is perennial, growing as a shrub or small tree, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
