Potentilla canadensis var. typica

Potentilla canadensis var. typica, commonly known as Canadian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs and produces clusters of bright yellow, five-petaled flowers. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets with serrated edges.

The species is often found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest edges.
