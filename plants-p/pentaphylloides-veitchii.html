Pentaphylloides veitchii

Pentaphylloides veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's five-leaf plant, is a unique species belonging to the family Pentaphylloideae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its distinctive five-leaf structure, which gives it its scientific name. The plant has small, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color, and it produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific growing conditions to thrive.
