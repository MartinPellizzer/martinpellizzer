Piper vallicola

Piper vallicola, commonly known as the mountain pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, dark berries.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
