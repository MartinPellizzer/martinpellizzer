Pinus lawsonii var. gracilis

Pinus lawsonii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Lawson cypress, is a rare and unique variant of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to a small region in the western United States, it thrives in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds, contributing to its reproductive cycle.

Its botanical features make it a valuable specimen for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
