Panicum vilvoides var. fluviatile

Panicum vilvoides var. fluviatile, commonly known as river panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found along riverbanks and in marshy areas. Its adaptability to waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
