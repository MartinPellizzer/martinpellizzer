Phaseolus viridissimus

Phaseolus viridissimus, commonly known as the green bean or yardlong bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It produces long, cylindrical pods that are typically green and edible when harvested young. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires regular watering.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and is cultivated for both food and ornamental purposes.
