Polypodium arcanum

Polypodium arcanum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and enigmatic member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green fronds that often appear translucent, giving it a ghostly appearance. This fern is typically found in shaded, moist environments such as tropical forests and rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are clustered on the underside of its fronds.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Polypodium arcanum remains a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
