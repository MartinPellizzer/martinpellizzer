Polygonum linearifolium

Polygonum linearifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of East Asia.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, making it both a hardy and potentially invasive species in certain environments.
