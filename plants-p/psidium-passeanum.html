Psidium passeanum

Psidium passeanum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or passion fruit, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a distinctive sweet-tart flavor. Its fruit is widely cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
