Papaver multiradiatum

Papaver multiradiatum, commonly known as the multiradiate poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display a variety of colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant features a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and arranged alternately along the stem.

Papaver multiradiatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
