Peperomia chilensis

Peperomia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean pepper plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant features oval, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Peperomia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements.
