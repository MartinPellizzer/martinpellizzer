Pisum arvense var. rufum

Pisum arvense var. rufum, commonly known as the red-field pea, is a wild legume native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and role in ecological succession.
