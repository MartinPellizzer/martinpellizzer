Penstemon virgatus var. asa-grayi

Penstemon virgatus var. asa-grayi, commonly known as the Asa-Gray beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of pink to purple tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
