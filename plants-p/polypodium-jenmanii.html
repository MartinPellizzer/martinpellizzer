Polypodium jenmanii

Polypodium jenmanii, commonly known as the Jenman's polypody, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are bi-lobed and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Polypodium jenmanii is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
