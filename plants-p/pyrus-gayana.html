Pyrus gayana

Pyrus gayana, commonly known as the African whitebeam, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by edible, subglobose fruits that are yellowish-green when ripe. It is often found in rocky outcrops and coastal areas, adapting well to arid and semi-arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
