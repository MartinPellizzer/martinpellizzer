Poa ensiformis

Poa ensiformis, commonly known as the swordgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Poa ensiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
