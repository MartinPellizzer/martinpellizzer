Primula fauriei var. samanimontana

Primula fauriei var. samanimontana, commonly known as the Samani Montane Primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It typically grows in cool, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. The plant features rosettes of broad, ovate leaves with a waxy texture and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is adapted to high-altitude conditions, showcasing resilience to cold and variable weather.
