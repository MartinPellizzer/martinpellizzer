Potentilla helvetica

Potentilla helvetica, commonly known as Swiss cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils.

Potentilla helvetica is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.
