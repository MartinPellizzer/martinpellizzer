Premna subcordata

Premna subcordata, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowroot or Indian arrowroot, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a distinctive cordate base. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
