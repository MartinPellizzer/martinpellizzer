Parsonsia lifuana

Parsonsia lifuana, commonly known as the Lifuana flame vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
