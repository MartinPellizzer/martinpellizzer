Potentilla × collina

Potentilla × collina, commonly known as the alpine rose or evergreen cinquefoil, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant features wiry, arching stems covered in small, dark green leaves that are often hairy. Its showy flowers, which range from white to pink or yellow, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Potentilla × collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
