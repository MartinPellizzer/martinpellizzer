Poa pardoana

Poa pardoana, commonly known as the Pardo's grass, is a species of perennial grass native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically a dull green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Poa pardoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
