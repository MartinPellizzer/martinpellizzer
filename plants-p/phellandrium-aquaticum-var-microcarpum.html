Phellandrium aquaticum var. microcarpum

Phellandrium aquaticum var. microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited swamp tea, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in swampy areas. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in spring and are followed by tiny, berry-like fruits.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to waterlogged conditions.
