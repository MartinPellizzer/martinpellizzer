Polygonum cliffortioides

Polygonum cliffortioides, commonly known as the cliffortioides knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in hue. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Polygonum cliffortioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
