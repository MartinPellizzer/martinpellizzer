Pitcairnia vargasii

Pitcairnia vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas Pitcher Plant, is a species of bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a feature common among many bromeliads. The plant produces striking pink or red inflorescences that emerge from the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
