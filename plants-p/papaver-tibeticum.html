Papaver tibeticum

Papaver tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan poppy, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool and arid conditions. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often studied for its adaptations to extreme climates.
