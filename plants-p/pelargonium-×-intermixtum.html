Pelargonium × intermixtum

Pelargonium × intermixtum, commonly known as the zonal geranium, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves with distinct color zones, typically in shades of red, purple, or rust. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
