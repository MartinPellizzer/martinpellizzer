Pyrus beauverdiana

Pyrus beauverdiana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus beauverdiana is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
