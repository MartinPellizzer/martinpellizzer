Piper valetudinarii

Piper valetudinarii, commonly known as the "spleenwort pepper," is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical characteristics include opposite, ovate leaves and a climbing habit with aerial roots.
