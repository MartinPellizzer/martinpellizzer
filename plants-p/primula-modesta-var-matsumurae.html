Primula modesta var. matsumurae

Primula modesta var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's primrose, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
