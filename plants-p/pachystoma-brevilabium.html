Pachystoma brevilabium

Pachystoma brevilabium, commonly known as the narrow-lipped stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and summer months. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptive characteristics make it a notable species in the Crassulaceae family.
