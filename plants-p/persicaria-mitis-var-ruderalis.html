Persicaria mitis var. ruderalis

Persicaria mitis var. ruderalis, commonly known as the slender smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a slightly rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This variant is closely related to other members of the Polygonaceae family, sharing similar botanical traits.
