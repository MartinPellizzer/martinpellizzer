Pleurothallis villosilabia

Pleurothallis villosilabia, commonly known as the hairy-labellum pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and its striking, hairy labellum, which gives it its common name. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular subject among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
