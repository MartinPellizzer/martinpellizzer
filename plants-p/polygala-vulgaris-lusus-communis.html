Polygala vulgaris lusus communis

Polygala vulgaris lusus communis, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small leaflets that help in reducing water loss.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
