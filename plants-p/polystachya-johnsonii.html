Polystachya johnsonii

Polystachya johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's spikegrass, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces clustered spikes of small, white or pale green flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance.

Polystachya johnsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
