Pahudia bella

Pahudia bella, commonly known as the white star orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a yellow center. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant blooms during the summer months.

Pahudia bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
