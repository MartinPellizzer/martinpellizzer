Potentilla demotica

Potentilla demotica, commonly known as the Demotica cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, hairy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. Its growth habit is compact, with multiple stems arising from a central rootstock.

Potentilla demotica is often found in open meadows and slopes at high elevations, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
