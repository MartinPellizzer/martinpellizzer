Polyscias hildebrandtii

Polyscias hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's gloryberry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
