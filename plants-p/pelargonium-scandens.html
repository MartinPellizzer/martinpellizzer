Pelargonium scandens

Pelargonium scandens, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that resemble ivy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

Pelargonium scandens is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to cascade attractively.
