Podocarpus macrocarpus

Podocarpus macrocarpus, commonly known as the Philippine cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, scale-like leaves and produces both male and female cones. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.
