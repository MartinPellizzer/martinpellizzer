Peltospermum paniculatum

Peltospermum paniculatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved peltospermum, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Peltospermataceae.

It is a small shrub characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are borne on slender stalks. Its seeds are unique, featuring a hard, lignified coat that allows them to remain viable for extended periods.

This species is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
