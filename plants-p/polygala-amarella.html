Polygala amarella

Polygala amarella, commonly known as the yellow milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The yellow milkvetch is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
