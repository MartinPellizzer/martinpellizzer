Polygonum fischeri

Polygonum fischeri, commonly known as the Fisher's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer, and its seed capsules split open to release tiny seeds.

Polygonum fischeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in challenging environments.
