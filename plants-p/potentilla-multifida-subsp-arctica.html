Potentilla multifida subsp. arctica

Potentilla multifida subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in extreme climates.
