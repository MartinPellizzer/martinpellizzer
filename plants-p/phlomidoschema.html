Phlomidoschema

Phlomidoschema, commonly known as the narrow-leaved phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a desirable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
