Polycodium Oblongum

Oblong polycodium, scientifically known as Polycodium oblongum, is a unique member of the Asteraceae family that thrives in temperate regions. This plant is characterized by its elongated leaves and distinctive inflorescence that resembles a small, fuzzy cluster. Native to parts of Asia and North America, it often grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its delicate structure and subtle beauty make it a favorite among botanists and gardeners alike. Despite its modest appearance, the oblong polycodium plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
