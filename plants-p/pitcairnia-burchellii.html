Pitcairnia burchellii

Pitcairnia burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant red and orange flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
