Phyllostegia parviflora

Phyllostegia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mistflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Phyllostegia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
