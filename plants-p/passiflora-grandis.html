Passiflora grandis

Passiflora grandis, commonly known as the giant passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a distinctive central corona. The plant produces edible, tropical berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a velvety texture.

Passiflora grandis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
