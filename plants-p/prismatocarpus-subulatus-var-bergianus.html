Prismatocarpus subulatus var. bergianus

Prismatocarpus subulatus var. bergianus, commonly known as the Bergian needleleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or brackish water areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local communities.
