Protea pulchella

Protea pulchella, commonly known as the "Cape Protea," is a striking plant native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is a member of the Proteaceae family, known for its showy, inflorescence-like structures called proteas. The plant features a tall, woody stem with dense clusters of bright pink to red flower heads, which are actually composed of many small, petal-like bracts. Protea pulchella thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
