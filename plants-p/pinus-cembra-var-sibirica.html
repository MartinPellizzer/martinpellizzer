Pinus cembra var. sibirica

Pinus cembra var. sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian stone pine, is a variant of the European stone pine native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Siberia.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slow growth and cold-hardy nature, thriving in high-altitude environments. The tree produces long, slender cones that release winged seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its needles are typically blue-green in color, and the bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against harsh climates.

This variety is valued for its timber and is often found in forest ecosystems where it plays a key role in soil stabilization and biodiversity.
