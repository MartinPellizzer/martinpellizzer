Psidium emarginatum

Psidium emarginatum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or emarginate guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, oval-shaped leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
