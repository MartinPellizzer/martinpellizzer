Protasparagus mollis

Protasparagus mollis, commonly known as the soft asparagus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant has slender, succulent stems and needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
