Premna oblongifolia

Premna oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf morning glory, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Premna oblongifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
