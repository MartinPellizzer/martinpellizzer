Potamogeton pectinatus f. laxus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. laxus, commonly known as the soft-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater habitats across Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its flexible, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences near the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and stable environmental conditions.
