Pilosella glaucescens subsp. amnoon

Pilosella glaucescens subsp.

amnoon, commonly known as the Amnoon bluehead, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, exhibiting a low, cushion-like growth form to withstand harsh weather conditions. Its blue to violet flower heads are surrounded by a dense, woolly coating of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and is often found in meadows and slopes at elevations above 3,000 meters.
