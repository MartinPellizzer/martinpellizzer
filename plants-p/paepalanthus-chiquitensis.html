Paepalanthus chiquitensis

Paepalanthus chiquitensis, commonly known as the Chiquitano palm, is a unique species native to the Chiquitos region of South America.

It is a type of palm characterized by its clustered growth habit and distinctive, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in dry, tropical climates and is adapted to seasonal droughts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
