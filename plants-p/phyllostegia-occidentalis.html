Phyllostegia occidentalis

Phyllostegia occidentalis, commonly known as the western steeplebush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of white to pale purple. These flowers are typically arranged in terminal spikes and bloom in the spring.

The plant is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
