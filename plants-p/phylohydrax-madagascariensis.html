Phylohydrax madagascariensis

Phylohydrax madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar water lily, is a unique aquatic plant native to the freshwater habitats of Madagascar.

It possesses broad, floating leaves that provide a habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant's roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the water, and its flowers are typically white or pale pink, attracting pollinators. It plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native wetlands.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
