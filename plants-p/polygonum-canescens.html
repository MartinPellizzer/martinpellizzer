Polygonum canescens

Polygonum canescens, commonly known as the grey knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems that often have a whitish or greyish bloom, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Polygonum canescens produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in late summer.
