Potentilla hispanica f. macropetala

Potentilla hispanica f. macropetala, commonly known as the Large-Flowered Spanish Cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features upright, woody stems with alternate, pinnately compound leaves composed of five oval to lance-shaped leaflets. The plant produces showy, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and long blooming period.
