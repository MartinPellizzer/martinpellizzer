Potentilla × mutabilis

Potentilla × mutabilis, commonly known as the sunset potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its showy, semi-double flowers that change color from white to pink to red as they progress through their bloom cycle. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has a compact, rounded form with silvery-gray foliage. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
