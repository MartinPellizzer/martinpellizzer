Pulicaria planifolia

Pulicaria planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf pulicaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Pulicaria planifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
