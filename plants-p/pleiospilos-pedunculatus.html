Pleiospilos pedunculatus

Pleiospilos pedunculatus, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to 30 cm in height, often adorned with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its swollen stem and leaves. It blooms during the summer months, producing vibrant, ephemeral flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a popular species among succulent enthusiasts and botanists.
