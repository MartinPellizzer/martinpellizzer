Polypodiopteris brachypodia

Polypodiopteris brachypodia, commonly known as the short-footed polypod, is a rare and delicate fern native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its small, intricately veined fronds that typically grow in shaded, moist environments. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or forest floor substrates. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal.

Due to habitat loss, this species is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
