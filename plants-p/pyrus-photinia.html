Pyrus photinia

Pyrus photinia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible by humans but are used in traditional medicine.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant fall foliage and attractive form.
