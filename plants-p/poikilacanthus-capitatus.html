Poikilacanthus capitatus

Poikilacanthus capitatus, commonly known as the zigzag goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems and bright yellow flowers. The plant features small, glandular hairs on its leaves, which contribute to its unique appearance and aid in water retention. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, making it an important species in its ecosystem.
