Polygonum rubriflorum

Polygonum rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves with a reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and streambanks, and is often found in temperate regions.

Polygonum rubriflorum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
