Pithecellobium pinetorum

Pithecellobium pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida pithecellobium, is a tropical shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces edible, pod-like fruits that are often consumed by wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
