Passiflora nipensis

Passiflora nipensis, commonly known as the Mexican passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant produces blue or violet berries that are edible and contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Passiflora nipensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional medicinal uses.
