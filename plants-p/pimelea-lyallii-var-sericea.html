Pimelea lyallii var. sericea

Pimelea lyallii var. sericea, commonly known as the silky pimelea, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and dense clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native insect populations.
