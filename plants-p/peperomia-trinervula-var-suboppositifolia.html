Peperomia trinervula var. suboppositifolia

Peperomia trinervula var. suboppositifolia, commonly known as the Opposite-leaf Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, ovate leaves that are opposite in arrangement, with a glossy, dark green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a desirable ornamental plant for collectors and enthusiasts.
