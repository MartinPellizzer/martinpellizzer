Protea susannae

Protea susannae, commonly known as the Susanna protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers, which are clustered around a central woody structure called a protea. The plant is native to the Western Cape region and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its rich biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
