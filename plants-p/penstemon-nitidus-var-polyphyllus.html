Penstemon nitidus var. polyphyllus

Penstemon nitidus var. polyphyllus, commonly known as the silver leaf penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and tall spikes of tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
