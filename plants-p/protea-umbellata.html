Protea umbellata

Protea umbellata, commonly known as the African daisies, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a flat or rounded shape. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers come in a range of colors, including white, pink, and red, and are often used in floral arrangements.

Protea umbellata is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for various pollinators.
