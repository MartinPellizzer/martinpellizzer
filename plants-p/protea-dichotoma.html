Protea dichotoma

Protea dichotoma, commonly known as the flat protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and its distinctive, cone-shaped inflorescences that are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and red, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Protea dichotoma is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and birds.
