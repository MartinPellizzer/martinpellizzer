Potentilla argentea var. canescens

Potentilla argentea var. canescens, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a soft, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
