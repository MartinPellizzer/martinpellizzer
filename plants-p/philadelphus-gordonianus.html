Philadelphus gordonianus

Philadelphus gordonianus, commonly known as Gordon's mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green above and lighter below. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Philadelphus gordonianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
