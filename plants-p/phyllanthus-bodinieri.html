Phyllanthus bodinieri

Phyllanthus bodinieri, commonly known as the African date palm, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles. Its fruit is a drupe that matures to a yellow or red color, containing a single seed.

Phyllanthus bodinieri is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
