Paltoria

Paltoria, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have basal rosettes of leaves and produce yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Paltoria species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

They are known for their ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making them persistent in various habitats.
