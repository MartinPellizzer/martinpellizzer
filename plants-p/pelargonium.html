Pelargonium

Pelargonium, commonly known as geranium, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Geraniaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are widely cultivated for their ornamental value. Pelargonium species are characterized by their showy flowers, which come in a variety of colors, and their aromatic leaves. They are typically grown as houseplants or in gardens, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Many species are also used in traditional medicine and as natural pest repellents.
