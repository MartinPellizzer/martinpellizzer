Protea acrocarpa

Protea acrocarpa, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, often found in mountainous regions. Its distinctive floral structure consists of a central cone surrounded by petal-like bracts, which are often mistaken for petals.

Protea acrocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
