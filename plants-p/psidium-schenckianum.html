Psidium schenckianum

Psidium schenckianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in local cuisines. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for various wildlife species.
