Poa tenuifolia var. rigida

Poa tenuifolia var. rigida, commonly known as rigid slender grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright, stiff growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, compact flower clusters.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
