Pandanus serrimarginalis

Pandanus serrimarginalis, commonly known as the hibiscus pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Pandanus serrimarginalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and practical uses in local communities.
