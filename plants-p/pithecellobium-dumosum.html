Pithecellobium dumosum

Pithecellobium dumosum, commonly known as the bushy acacia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woody growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are legumes that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines.

Pithecellobium dumosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
