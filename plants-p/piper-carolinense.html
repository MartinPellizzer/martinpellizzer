Piper carolinense

Piper carolinense, commonly known as the Carolina pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries that contain the aromatic seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a glossy surface and a distinctive, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of unique spices.
