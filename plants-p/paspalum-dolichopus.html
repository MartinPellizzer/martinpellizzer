Paspalum dolichopus

Paspalum dolichopus, commonly known as the long-awned paspalum, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, bristly awns on its spikelets. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Paspalum dolichopus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a component in native plant restoration projects.
