Panicum petiveri var. robustissimum

Panicum petiveri var. robustissimum, commonly known as the robust panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, fluffy panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are characteristic of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

Its adaptability and drought tolerance contribute to its widespread distribution in various ecosystems.
