Padus serotina neomontana

Padus serotina neomontana, commonly known as the mountain whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red or orange fruits called drupes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with moderate to high humidity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
