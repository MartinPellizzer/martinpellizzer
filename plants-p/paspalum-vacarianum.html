Paspalum vacarianum

Paspalum vacarianum, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or vacaria grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces spikelets that are often loosely arranged, with small, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum vacarianum is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
