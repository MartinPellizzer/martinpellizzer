Pentaphragma ellipticum

Pentaphragma ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Lentibacillaceae.

It is characterized by its submerged, leaf-like structures that are often mistaken for leaves but are actually specialized for gas exchange. The plant produces tiny, bladder-like structures called pneumatocysts, which help it float and access oxygen-rich water. It is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.

Its simple morphology and unique adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
