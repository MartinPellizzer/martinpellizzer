Phyllanthus oppositifolius

Phyllanthus oppositifolius, commonly known as the African spurge, is a shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments, though its botanical properties are still under scientific study.
