Palaquium warburgianum

Palaquium warburgianum, commonly known as the Warburg's wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed surrounded by a fleshy, edible pulp. The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
