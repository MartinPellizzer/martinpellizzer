Pulassarium pilosum

Pulassarium pilosum, commonly known as the silky pulassarium, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense clusters. Its root system helps stabilize the soil, making it ecologically significant in its habitat.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens.
