Potentilla biflora f. humilior

Potentilla biflora f. humilior, commonly known as the dwarf potentilla, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and white. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for alpine gardens and rockeries.

It is also valued for its hardiness and ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.
