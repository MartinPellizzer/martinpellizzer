Plinia coclensis

Plinia coclensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or yellow starapple, is a species of fruit-bearing plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Plinia coclensis is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
