Parodia mammulosa subsp. submammulosa

Parodia mammulosa subsp.

submammulosa, commonly known as the "Pincushion Cactus," is a compact, globular cactus native to southern Africa. It features a dense covering of white to pale green spines, which give it a pincushion-like appearance.

The plant has a short, cylindrical stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
