Paeonia × saundersii nothosubsp. majko

Paeonia × saundersii nothosubsp.

majko, commonly known as the Majko peony, is a hybrid plant that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics. It is a deciduous shrub with thick, aromatic stems and large, showy flowers that come in various colors, including red, pink, and white.

The plant's leaves are palmately compound, consisting of several ovate to lanceolate leaflets. It is native to the Balkan region and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
