Phaseolus vulgaris var. nigerrimus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. nigerrimus, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a variety of common bean native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy pods and seeds that are typically black or very dark brown in color. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It produces trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is valued in traditional cuisines and is also used in agricultural systems for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
