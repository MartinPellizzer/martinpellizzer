Pimelea haematostachya

Pimelea haematostachya, commonly known as the crimson devil or red-stemmed pimelea, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, tubular, red or orange-red flowers that appear in dense clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

Pimelea haematostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
