Pentacalia vulpinaris

Pentacalia vulpinaris, commonly known as the foxglove pentacalia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming habit.
