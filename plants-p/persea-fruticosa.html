Persea fruticosa

Persea fruticosa, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon or wild persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but typically have a bland or slightly bitter taste. Persea fruticosa thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
