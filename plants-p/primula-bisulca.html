Primula bisulca

Primula bisulca, commonly known as the two-lobed primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a slightly hairy texture.

Primula bisulca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
