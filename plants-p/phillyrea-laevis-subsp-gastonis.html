Phillyrea laevis subsp. gastonis

Phillyrea laevis subsp.

gastonis, commonly known as the Mediterranean laurel, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter, sometimes whitish underside.

The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
