Polygonatum nipponicum

Polygonatum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese Solomon's Seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its arching stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces dark blue or black berries that are edible but typically consumed in moderation. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its rhizomatous root system makes it a valuable plant for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
