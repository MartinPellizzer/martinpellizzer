Piper perhispidum

Piper perhispidum, commonly known as the rough pepper or hairy pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes numerous species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
