Panicum lindheimeri var. septentrionale

Panicum lindheimeri var. septentrionale, commonly known as the eastern panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
