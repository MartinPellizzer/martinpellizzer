Pulsatilla alpina subsp. cantabrica

Pulsatilla alpina subsp.

cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the plant produces a showy display in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
