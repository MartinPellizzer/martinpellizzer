Pyrus bohemica

Pyrus bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian pear, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, yellowish fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
