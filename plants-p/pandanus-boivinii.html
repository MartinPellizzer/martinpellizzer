Pandanus boivinii

Pandanus boivinii, commonly known as the Madagascar screw pine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that form a dense, rosette-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts, while its leaves are utilized in local cuisine and basket-making.

Pandanus boivinii thrives in coastal and wetland habitats, showcasing its adaptability to humid tropical environments.
