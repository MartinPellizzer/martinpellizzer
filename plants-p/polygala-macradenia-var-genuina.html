Polygala macradenia var. genuina

Polygala macradenia var. genuina, commonly known as the Cape mountain catchfly, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
