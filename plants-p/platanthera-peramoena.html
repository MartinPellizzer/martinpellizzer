Platanthera peramoena

Platanthera peramoena, commonly known as the purple twayblade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions and is often protected in conservation efforts.
