Polygonum glandulopilosum

Polygonum glandulopilosum, commonly known as the glandular hairy knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its stout, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it identifiable within the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel.
