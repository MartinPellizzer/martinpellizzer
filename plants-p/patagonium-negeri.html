Patagonium negeri

Patagonium negeri, commonly known as the black Patagonian plant, is a rare flowering species native to the high-altitude regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage and striking, violet-pink flowers that bloom during the short summer season. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of the Andes. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps retain moisture in the arid climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Patagonium negeri is considered a conservation concern.
