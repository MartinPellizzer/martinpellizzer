Persea fulva

Persea fulva, commonly known as the yellow avocado, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green bark and large, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a large, yellowish avocado with a smooth, leathery skin and a creamy, oily pulp.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
