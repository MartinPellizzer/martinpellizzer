Primulina sichuanensis var. pinnatipartita

Primulina sichuanensis var. pinnatipartita, commonly known as the Sichuan Primrose, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is a perennial herb with distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and exhibit a unique pinnately divided structure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.
