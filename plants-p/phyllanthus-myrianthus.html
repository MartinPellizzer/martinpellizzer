Phyllanthus myrianthus

Phyllanthus myrianthus, commonly known as the African star apple or wild star apple, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
