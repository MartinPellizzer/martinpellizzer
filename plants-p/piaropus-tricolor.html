Piaropus tricolor

Piaropus tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored piper, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which display three distinct colors—green, white, and red—on the same leaf blade. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-lobed berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
