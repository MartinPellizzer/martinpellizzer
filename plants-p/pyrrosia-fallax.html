Pyrrosia fallax

Pyrrosia fallax, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on rocks or trees. The plant features fronds that are typically leathery and have a distinctive, segmented appearance. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Pyrrosia fallax is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to indoor cultivation.
