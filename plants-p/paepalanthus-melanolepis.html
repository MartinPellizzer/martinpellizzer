Paepalanthus melanolepis

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that bears vibrant red or orange flowers. The plant has dark green, strap-like leaves that form a rosette, creating a dense, visually appealing structure. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
