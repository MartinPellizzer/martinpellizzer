Pourouma jaramilloi

Pourouma jaramilloi, commonly known as the Jaramillo's areca, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its elongated, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, branched inflorescences, followed by clustered, oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when mature.

Its rapid growth rate and ability to thrive in tropical conditions make it a popular choice for ornamental and horticultural purposes.
