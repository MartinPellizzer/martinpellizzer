Pyrus avalon

Pyrus avalon, commonly known as the Avalon pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the British Isles and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible fruit. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while the fruit is a pear-shaped berry with a yellowish or greenish skin.

The Avalon pear is valued for its aesthetic appeal and historical significance in British folklore.
