Plectranthus pharicus

Plectranthus pharicus, commonly known as Cape verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in dense spikes, usually in shades of blue or purple.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
