Platyopuntia corrugata

Platyopuntia corrugata, commonly known as the corrugated prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its flattened, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often adorned with yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating remarkable drought tolerance. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces edible fruits that are typically red or yellow in color.

The corrugated prickly pear plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
