Phalaris bulbosa

Phalaris bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous grass or narrow-leafed phalaris, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, ditches, and along streambanks.

Phalaris bulbosa is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
