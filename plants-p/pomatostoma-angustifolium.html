Pomatostoma angustifolium

Pomatostoma angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grey hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal conditions.

Pomatostoma angustifolium is an important component of coastal vegetation, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
