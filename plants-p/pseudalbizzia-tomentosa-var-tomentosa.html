Pseudalbizzia tomentosa var. tomentosa

Pseudalbizzia tomentosa var. tomentosa, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray pubescent foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval leaflets.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
