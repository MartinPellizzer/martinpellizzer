Parsonsia bartlensis

Parsonsia bartlensis, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a striking tropical shrub native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually opposite or alternate in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Parsonsia bartlensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
