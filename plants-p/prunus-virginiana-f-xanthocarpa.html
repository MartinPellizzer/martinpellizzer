Prunus virginiana f. xanthocarpa

Prunus virginiana f. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow crabapple, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the common crabapple.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruit, which contrasts strikingly with its green foliage. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy display.
