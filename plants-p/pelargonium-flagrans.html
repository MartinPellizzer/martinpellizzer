Pelargonium flagrans

Pelargonium flagrans, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Geraniaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and have a serrated edge, while the stems are hairy and often emit a scent when crushed. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers, which can range in color from pink to red.
