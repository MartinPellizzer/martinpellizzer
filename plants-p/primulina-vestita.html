Primulina vestita

Primulina vestita, commonly known as the velvet primula, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its velvety, waxy leaves that give it its common name, and its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family, which includes many ornamental plants prized for their foliage and blooms.

Primulina vestita is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
