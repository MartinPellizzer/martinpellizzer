Polygonum filiforme subsp. neofiliforme

Polygonum filiforme subsp.

neofiliforme, commonly known as the slender knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
