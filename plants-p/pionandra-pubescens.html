Pionandra pubescens

Pionandra pubescens, commonly known as the silky pionandra, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine pubescence.

Pionandra pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
