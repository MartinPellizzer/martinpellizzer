Papaver gariepinum

Papaver gariepinum, commonly known as the Gariep poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive black eye at the center. The plant features robust, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers bloom in spring and summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat.
