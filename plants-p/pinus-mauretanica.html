Pinus mauretanica

Pinus mauretanica, commonly known as the Mauretanian pine, is a species of conifer native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and needle-like leaves that are typically 4 to 8 centimeters long. The plant produces cones that are woody and can persist on the tree for several years. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its timber and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
