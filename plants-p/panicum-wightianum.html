Panicum wightianum

Panicum wightianum, commonly known as Wight's panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is a grass-like plant belonging to the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens or as a reed-like plant in aquatic settings.
