Prunus erecta

Prunus erecta, commonly known as the upright cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Prunus erecta typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
