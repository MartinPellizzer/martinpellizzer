Petasites tricholobus

Petasites tricholobus, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas.

Petasites tricholobus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
