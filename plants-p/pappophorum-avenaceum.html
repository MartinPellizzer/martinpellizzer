Pappophorum avenaceum

Pappophorum avenaceum, commonly known as the oat grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, temperate regions, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in achenes, which are often dispersed by wind due to the presence of a pappus, a feathery structure.

Pappophorum avenaceum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
