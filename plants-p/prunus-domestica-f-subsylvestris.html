Prunus domestica f. subsylvestris

Prunus domestica f. subsylvestris, commonly known as the wild cherry or sour cherry, is a subspecies of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its smaller, more acidic fruits compared to its cultivated relatives. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This subspecies is often found in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
