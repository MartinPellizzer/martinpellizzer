Parodia langsdorfii subsp. multiceps

Parodia langsdorfii subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a compact, globular cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It features a pale green to bluish-gray stem with distinct ribs and tubercles, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted foliage. It is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
