Polypodium subpubescens

Polypodium subpubescens, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its fronds that are covered with fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizome is creeping and produces sporangia on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.
