Polygonatum ensifolium

Polygonatum ensifolium, commonly known as the swordleaf Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that resemble a sword, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Polygonatum ensifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
