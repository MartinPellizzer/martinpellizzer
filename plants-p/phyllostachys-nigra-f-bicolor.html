Phyllostachys nigra f. bicolor

Phyllostachys nigra f. bicolor, commonly known as the black bamboo with variegation, is a variant of the black bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and sometimes pink, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is known for its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

This ornamental bamboo is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
