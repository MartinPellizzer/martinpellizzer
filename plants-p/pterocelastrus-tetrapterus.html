Pterocelastrus tetrapterus

Pterocelastrus tetrapterus, commonly known as the four-winged sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, four-winged fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitats.
