Pachygone cubensis var. angustifolia

Pachygone cubensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pachygone, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
