Palma oleosa

Palma oleosa, commonly known as the oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, straight trunk. The plant produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds, which are harvested for palm oil production. Its growth is highly dependent on warm, humid climates with ample sunlight.

Palma oleosa plays a significant role in both agriculture and the global economy due to its versatile oil yield.
