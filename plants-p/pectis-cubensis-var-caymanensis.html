Pectis cubensis var. caymanensis

Pectis cubensis var. caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman Islands pectis, is a flowering plant native to the Cayman Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
