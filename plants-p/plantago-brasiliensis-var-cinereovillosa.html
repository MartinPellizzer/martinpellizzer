Plantago brasiliensis var. cinereovillosa

Plantago brasiliensis var. cinereovillosa, commonly known as the cinereous hairy plantain, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically grayish-green in color, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the warmer months.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.
