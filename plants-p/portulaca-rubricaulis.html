Portulaca rubricaulis

Portulaca rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed portulaca, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the day and close at night. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a branching stem system that helps it conserve moisture.

Portulaca rubricaulis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive floral display.
