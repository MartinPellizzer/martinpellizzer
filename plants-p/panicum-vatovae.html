Panicum vatovae

Panicum vatovae, commonly known as the Vatova grass, is a flowering plant species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
