Polypodium tamandarei

Polypodium tamandarei, commonly known as the Tamandare huperzia, is a species of fern native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Huperziaceae and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Polypodium tamandarei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
