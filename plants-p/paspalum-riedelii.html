Paspalum riedelii

Paspalum riedelii, commonly known as Riedel's grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Paspalum riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
