Psydrax schimperianus subsp. occidentalis

Psydrax schimperianus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western false mallow, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa. It features hairy, ovate leaves with a serrated margin and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
