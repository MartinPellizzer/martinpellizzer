Polyspatha hirsuta var. togoensis

Polyspatha hirsuta var. togoensis, commonly known as the Togo velvet fern, is a rare and striking plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a unique textural appearance. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive look. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and ornamental value, it is a sought-after plant among botanists and horticulturists.
