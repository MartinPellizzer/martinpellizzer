Potentilla tiroliensis f. aprica

Potentilla tiroliensis f. aprica, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the European Alps.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in sunny and well-drained environments. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
