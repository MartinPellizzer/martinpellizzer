Polygala schoenlankii var. leptocaulis

Polygala schoenlankii var. leptocaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed milkvetch, is a rare and specialized plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate flowers that often appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions with minimal water requirements. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, which help minimize water loss.
