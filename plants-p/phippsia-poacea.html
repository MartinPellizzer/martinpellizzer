Phippsia poacea

Phippsia poacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf phippsia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
