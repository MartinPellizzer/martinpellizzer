Penstemon scapoides

Penstemon scapoides, commonly known as the scapoid beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon scapoides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
