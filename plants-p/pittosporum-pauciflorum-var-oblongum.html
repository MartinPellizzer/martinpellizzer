Pittosporum pauciflorum var. oblongum

Pittosporum pauciflorum var. oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pittosporum, is a shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which appear in spring. These flowers are followed by small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
