Peucedanum officinale var. italicum

Peucedanum officinale var. italicum, commonly known as sweet cicery, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and oval, with a distinct aroma, and are used in traditional medicine.
