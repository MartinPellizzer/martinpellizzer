Paullinia alata

Paullinia alata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapindaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white, waxy coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to small, round fruits resembling cherries. Its roots are the primary source of the starchy substance used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
