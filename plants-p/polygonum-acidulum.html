Polygonum acidulum

Polygonum acidulum, commonly known as sourdock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or disturbed soils and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant is known for its sour-tasting leaves, which have been used historically in traditional medicine. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants like buckwheat and knotweed.

Polygonum acidulum is often found in marshy areas and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
