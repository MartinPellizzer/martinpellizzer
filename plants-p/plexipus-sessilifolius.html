Plexipus sessilifolius

Plexipus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaved plexipus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically borne in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
