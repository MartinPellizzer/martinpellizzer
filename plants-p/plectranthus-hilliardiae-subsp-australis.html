Plectranthus hilliardiae subsp. australis

Plectranthus hilliardiae subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian plectranthus, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are often gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its fragrance and hardiness.
