Piper solutidrupum

Piper solutidrupum, commonly known as the golden pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its trailing vines and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.
