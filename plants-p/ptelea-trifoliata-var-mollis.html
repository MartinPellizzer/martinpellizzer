Ptelea trifoliata var. mollis

Ptelea trifoliata var. mollis, commonly known as the smoothleaf sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray-green bark and trifoliate leaves that are typically three-lobed. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscapes.
