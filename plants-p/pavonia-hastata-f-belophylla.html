Pavonia hastata f. belophylla

Pavonia hastata f. belophylla, commonly known as the showy pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, pink to red bracts that resemble petals. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, pea-like flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along streambanks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to garden settings.
