Potentilla bactriana var. bactriana

Potentilla bactriana var. bactriana, commonly known as the Afghan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
