Piper falconeri

Piper falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri pepper or the Madagascar pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, clustered, and often spiky fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and greenish-white in color.

The fruit, which is a berry, contains several seeds and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
