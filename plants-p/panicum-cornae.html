Panicum cornae

Panicum cornae, commonly known as cornflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Panicum cornae is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties.
