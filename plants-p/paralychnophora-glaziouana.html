Paralychnophora glaziouana

Paralychnophora glaziouana, commonly known as the Glaziouana mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of specific host trees.

It has small, scale-like leaves and produces white or pale yellow berries that are toxic to most animals. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing some photosynthesis. It is native to certain regions of South America and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its parasitic nature and toxic properties, it plays a unique role in its ecosystem.
