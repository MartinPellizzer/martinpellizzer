Paraprasophyllum concinnum

Paraprasophyllum concinnum, commonly known as the slender leek orchid, is an epiphytic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, leek-like leaves and small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows on decaying wood or tree trunks in moist, shaded environments. It has a short-lived rhizome and produces a single flower spike with several small blossoms.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
