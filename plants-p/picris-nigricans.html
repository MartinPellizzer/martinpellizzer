Picris nigricans

Picris nigricans, commonly known as blackweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread across different regions.
