Pilosella aurantiaca var. pseudoblyttii

Pilosella aurantiaca var. pseudoblyttii, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, giving the plant its scientific name "pilosella," which refers to the hairy texture.
