Penstemon kleei

Penstemon kleei, commonly known as the Klee's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Penstemon kleei is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
