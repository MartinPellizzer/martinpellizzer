Pyrus malus var. sinensis

Pyrus malus var. sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a variant of the common pear native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, white fruit with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree with broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in spring. Its fruit is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine and medicine.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
