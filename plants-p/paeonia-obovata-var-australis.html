Paeonia obovata var. australis

Paeonia obovata var. australis, commonly known as the Australian peony, is a variant of the peony plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with a fibrous root system.

The flowers are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and fragrance.
