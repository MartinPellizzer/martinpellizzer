Phlomoides lanatifolia

Phlomoides lanatifolia, commonly known as the woolly phlomoides, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and tall, slender stems. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
