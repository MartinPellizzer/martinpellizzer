Panicum palauense

Panicum palauense, commonly known as the Palau grass, is a flowering plant native to the island nation of Palau in the western Pacific.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland habitats, adapting to the tropical climate of its native region. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
