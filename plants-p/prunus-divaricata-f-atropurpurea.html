Prunus divaricata f. atropurpurea

Prunus divaricata f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purpleleaf cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus divaricata species, which is native to China.

This plant is characterized by its striking purple-tinged leaves, which provide ornamental value in gardens. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with a compact, rounded form. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves fully emerge.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
