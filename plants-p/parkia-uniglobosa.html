Parkia uniglobosa

Parkia uniglobosa, commonly known as the single-seeded legume or the African bean tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain a single large seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are bipinnately compound, and it has small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and its potential medicinal properties.
