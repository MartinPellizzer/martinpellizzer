Psidium acutangulum var. crassirame

Psidium acutangulum var. crassirame, commonly known as the rough-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery, and often rough-textured leaves, which are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to grow in partial shade.
