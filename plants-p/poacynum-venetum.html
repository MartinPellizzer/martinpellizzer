Poacynum venetum

Poacynum venetum, commonly known as the Venetian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Poacynum venetum is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
