Phelypaea arenaria var. euxina

Phelypaea arenaria var. euxina, commonly known as the European sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or yellowish flowers. It is adapted to withstand harsh, wind-swept conditions and has a low-growing habit.

This variety is often found in open, disturbed habitats and is considered a characteristic species of coastal vegetation.
