Polygala deflorata

Polygala deflorata, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers that resemble small, ghostly shapes. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
