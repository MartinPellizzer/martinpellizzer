Polyctenium fremontii var. typicum

Polyctenium fremontii var. typicum, commonly known as the Fremont's false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
