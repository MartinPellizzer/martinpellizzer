Peperomia peltata

Peperomia peltata, commonly known as the Shiny Cabbage Plant, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often heart-shaped or rounded, with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically greenish-white in color, which appear during the growing season. Its foliage is often variegated, featuring contrasting white or silver markings that enhance its ornamental appeal.

Peperomia peltata is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
