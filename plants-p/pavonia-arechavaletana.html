Pavonia arechavaletana

Pavonia arechavaletana, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's paver, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture that helps reduce water loss.

Pavonia arechavaletana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
