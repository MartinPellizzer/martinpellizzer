Persicaria fastigiatoramosa var. angustissima

Persicaria fastigiatoramosa var. angustissima, commonly known as narrowleaf hemp agrimony, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are often mistaken for grains due to their appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable species in its native habitats.
