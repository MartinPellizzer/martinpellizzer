Pleopeltis subconfluens

Pleopeltis subconfluens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on tree trunks and rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines along the undersides of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
