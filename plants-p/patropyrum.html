Patropyrum

Patropyrum, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Patropyrum is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
