Potentilla recta subsp. tenuisepala

Potentilla recta subsp.

tenuisepala, commonly known as narrow-lobed cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, branching stems and oblong, serrated leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and color.
