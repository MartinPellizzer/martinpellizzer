Prenanthes chondrilloides

Prenanthes chondrilloides, commonly known as the white burrweed, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, spadix-like flowers surrounded by a green, spathaceous bract. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its unique structure and habitat preferences make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
