Physaria geyeri subsp. purpurea

Physaria geyeri subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of purple and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
