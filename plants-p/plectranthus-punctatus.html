Plectranthus punctatus

Plectranthus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted plectranthus, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive white or silver speckled pattern on the upper surface. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Plectranthus punctatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
