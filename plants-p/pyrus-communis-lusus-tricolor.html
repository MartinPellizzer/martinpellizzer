Pyrus communis lusus tricolor

Pyrus communis lusus tricolor, commonly known as the three-color pear, is a cultivar of the common pear that exhibits a unique variegation pattern.

Its leaves display a striking combination of green, white, and pink, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically reaches a height of 4 to 8 meters. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to regular pears but may have a more delicate flavor.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its fruit production.
