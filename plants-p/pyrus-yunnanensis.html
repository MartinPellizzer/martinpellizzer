Pyrus yunnanensis

Pyrus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in its native habitat.

Pyrus yunnanensis is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.
