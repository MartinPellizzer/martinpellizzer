Panicum hemignostum

Panicum hemignostum, commonly known as the narrowleaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often narrow and slightly rolled at the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle, which is a loose, branched cluster of spikelets, characteristic of the grass family.

Panicum hemignostum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
