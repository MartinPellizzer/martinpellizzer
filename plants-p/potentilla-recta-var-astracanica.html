Potentilla recta var. astracanica

Potentilla recta var. astracanica, commonly known as the Astracanica cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its botanical features include stipitate stipules and a taproot system, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often found in steppe and semi-desert regions, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
