Potentilla assurgens subsp. thuringiaca

Potentilla assurgens subsp.

thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland edges and stream banks.

The plant features palmately divided leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
