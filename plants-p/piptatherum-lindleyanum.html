Piptatherum lindleyanum

Piptatherum lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Piptatherum lindleyanum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
