Pterocarpus tinctorius var. chrysothrix

Pterocarpus tinctorius var. chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and are highly fragrant. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its hard, durable wood. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small, feathery leaflets.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cultures.
