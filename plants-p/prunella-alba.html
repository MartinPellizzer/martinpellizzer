Prunella alba

Prunella alba, commonly known as self-heal, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of pale blue to violet flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Prunella alba is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
