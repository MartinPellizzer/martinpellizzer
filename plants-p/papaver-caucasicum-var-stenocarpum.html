Papaver caucasicum var. stenocarpum

Papaver caucasicum var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the Caucasian narrow-capsule poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers and elongated, narrow seed pods. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
