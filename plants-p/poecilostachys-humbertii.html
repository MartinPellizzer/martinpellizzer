Poecilostachys humbertii

Poecilostachys humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's panic grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
