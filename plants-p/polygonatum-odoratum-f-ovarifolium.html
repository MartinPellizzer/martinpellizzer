Polygonatum odoratum f. ovarifolium

Polygonatum odoratum f. ovarifolium, commonly known as the fragrant Solomon's seal, is a variant of the Solomon's seal plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces clusters of small, white to greenish-yellow flowers, which are followed by red berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique floral characteristics.
