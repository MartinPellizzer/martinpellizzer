Pavonia sepium subsp. macrocarpa

Pavonia sepium subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited wood rose, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries that persist into the winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
