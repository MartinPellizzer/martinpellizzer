Pelexia olivacea subsp. congesta

Pelexia olivacea subsp.

congesta, commonly known as the dense pelexia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
