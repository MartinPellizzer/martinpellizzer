Peliosanthes hypogyna

Peliosanthes hypogyna, commonly known as the Chinese yew, is a species of evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its conical shape and dark, shiny bark. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. Its foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are arranged spirally along the branches.

Peliosanthes hypogyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
