Passerina javanica

Passerina javanica, commonly known as the Javanese starling, is a small passerine bird native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sleek body, short wings, and distinctive black and white plumage. The bird has a slender beak adapted for feeding on insects and fruits. It typically inhabits forested areas and is often seen in mixed-species flocks.

Passerina javanica plays a role in seed dispersal and insect control within its ecosystem.
