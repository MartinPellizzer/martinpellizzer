Periploca capensis

Periploca capensis, commonly known as the Cape periploca, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It is often found in rocky areas and along coastal regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

The Cape periploca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
