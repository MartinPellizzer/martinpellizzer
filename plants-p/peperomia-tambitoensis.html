Peperomia tambitoensis

Peperomia tambitoensis, commonly known as the Tambito peperomia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and drought tolerance.

Peperomia tambitoensis is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
