Phaseolus reptans

Phaseolus reptans, commonly known as the creeping pole bean, is a herbaceous vine native to the Americas.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other members of the legume family. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and is characterized by its climbing habit through tendrils. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Botanically, it is classified within the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
