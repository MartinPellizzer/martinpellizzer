Prasophyllum wilsoniense

Prasophyllum wilsoniense, commonly known as Wilson's leek orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It is a perennial species that produces a single spike of flowers during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
