Phylica axillaris var. pedicellaris

Phylica axillaris var. pedicellaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
