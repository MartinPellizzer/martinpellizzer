Piper cumbotianum

Piper cumbotianum, commonly known as the Cumbre pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
