Potamogeton lucens subsp. zizii

Potamogeton lucens subsp.

zizii, commonly known as the golden pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, elongated leaves that are typically green to golden in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which float at the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving or still waters with moderate to high light exposure.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
