Passiflora suberosa var. hirsuta

Passiflora suberosa var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy passion vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems, which are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking, complex flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments around the reproductive structures. Its fruit is a woody, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
