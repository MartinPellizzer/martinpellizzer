Poliodendron

Poliodendron, commonly known as the giant polypody, is a fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize rocky or shaded areas effectively. Its spores are produced in specialized structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Poliodendron is often found in humid environments and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
