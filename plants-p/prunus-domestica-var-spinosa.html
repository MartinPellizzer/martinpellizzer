Prunus domestica var. spinosa

Prunus domestica var. spinosa, commonly known as the curly cherry or thorny cherry, is a variant of the common cherry tree.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, dark red fruits. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of oval, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
