Pycnocomon leucanthum

Pycnocomon leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered pycnocomon, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant's leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in tropical gardens.
