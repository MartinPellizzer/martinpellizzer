Pycreus sanguinolentus subsp. melanocephalus

Pycreus sanguinolentus subsp.

melanocephalus, commonly known as the black-headed pycreus, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black, inflorescences that contrast sharply with its pale green foliage.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators such as moths and beetles.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, this subspecies is considered endangered and is of particular interest to botanists and conservationists.
