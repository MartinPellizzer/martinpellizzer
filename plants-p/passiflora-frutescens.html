Passiflora frutescens

Passiflora frutescens, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and serrated edges.

Passiflora frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming properties.
