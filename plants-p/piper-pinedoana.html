Piper pinedoana

Piper pinedoana, commonly known as the Guatemalan pepper or Pinedoana pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in Guatemala and Mexico. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a glossy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that turn from yellow to red as they mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
