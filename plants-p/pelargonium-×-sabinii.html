Pelargonium × sabinii

Pelargonium × sabinii, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, slightly serrated leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Native to South Africa, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.
