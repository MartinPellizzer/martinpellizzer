Pandanus douglasii

Pandanus douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the top. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional crafts.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is often found in wet or swampy habitats.
