Phagnalon iminouakense

Phagnalon iminouakense, commonly known as the Moroccan phagnalon, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, featuring small, narrow leaves to reduce water loss. It produces yellow to orange composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
