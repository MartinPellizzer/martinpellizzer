Potamogeton × fluitans var. petiolatus

Potamogeton × fluitans var. petiolatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often broad at the base.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize sediments and improve water clarity. Its flowers are small and greenish, appearing in spike-like clusters, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for fish and invertebrates.
