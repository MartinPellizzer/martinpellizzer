Phoenicanthemum trianthum

Phoenicanthemum trianthum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically aromatic and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phoenicanthemum trianthum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
