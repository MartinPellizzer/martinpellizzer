Pogonachne

Pogonachne, commonly known as the beardgrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. Pogonachne species are characterized by their tufted growth habit and hairy or bristly foliage, which helps them conserve moisture. They produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Due to their hardy nature, they are often used in restoration projects and as ornamental plants in dry landscapes.
