Pectis elongata var. oerstediana

Pectis elongata var. oerstediana, commonly known as the Oersted's silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typical sun-like appearance. The plant features silver-gray, woolly foliage that helps reduce water loss in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, daisy-like clusters.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in full sun and dry conditions.
