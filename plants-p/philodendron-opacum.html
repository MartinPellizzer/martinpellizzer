Philodendron opacum

Philodendron opacum, commonly known as the velvet leaf philodendron, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface that reflects light, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic value.
