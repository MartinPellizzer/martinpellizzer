Phialanthus acunae

Phialanthus acunae, commonly known as the golden cup-mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small cups. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
