Pseudocalymma pachypus

Pseudocalymma pachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ghost flower, is a rare and striking member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and a compact growth habit, often forming a dense mat or low shrub. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
