Passiflora brachychlamys

Passiflora brachychlamys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in antioxidants. Its leaves are ovate with a rough texture and a distinctive midrib.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
