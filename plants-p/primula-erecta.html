Primula erecta

Primula erecta, commonly known as the upright primrose, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with flowering stems that rise above the basal leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of yellow, white, or pink, depending on the variety. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of down.

Primula erecta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
