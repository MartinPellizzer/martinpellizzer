Poiretia mattogrossensis

Poiretia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to the Amazon rainforest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, elongated spike bearing numerous small, spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of dense tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of spiders, a feature that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and is protected in many regions.
