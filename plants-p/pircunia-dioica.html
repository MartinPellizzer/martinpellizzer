Pircunia dioica

Pircunia dioica, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, upright stems above water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, aiding in photosynthesis. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the aquatic environment.

Pircunia dioica is also notable for its symbolic significance in various cultural and religious contexts.
