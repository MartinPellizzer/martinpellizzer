Photinia arguta var. parvifolia

Photinia arguta var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf photinia or small-leaved photinia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, globe-shaped fruits. It is well-suited for container gardening and as a foundation plant due to its dense growth habit and attractive foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in temperate climates.
