Pyrus femoriformis

Pyrus femoriformis, commonly known as the femoral pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Afghanistan and Pakistan. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and arranged in clusters.

Pyrus femoriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
