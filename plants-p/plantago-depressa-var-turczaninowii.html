Plantago depressa var. turczaninowii

Plantago depressa var. turczaninowii, commonly known as the Turczaninow's plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is widely distributed across temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
