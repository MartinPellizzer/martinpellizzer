Pentanema suaveolens

Pentanema suaveolens, commonly known as sweet pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a distinctive sweet scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest environments.
