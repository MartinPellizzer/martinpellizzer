Plantago barbata var. elongata

Plantago barbata var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a spike-like arrangement, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

This plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its healing benefits.
