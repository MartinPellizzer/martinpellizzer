Penstemon glandulosus var. glandulosus

Penstemon glandulosus var. glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, glandular hairs that give it its scientific name, and it is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
