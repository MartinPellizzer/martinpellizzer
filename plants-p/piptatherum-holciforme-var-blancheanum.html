Piptatherum holciforme var. blancheanum

Piptatherum holciforme var. blancheanum, commonly known as Blancheanum grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, glabrous leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
