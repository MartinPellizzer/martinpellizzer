Papaver alpinum subvar. citrinum

Papaver alpinum subvar. citrinum, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange petals, which contrast with its green, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to high-altitude landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
