Piper distigmatum

Piper distigmatum, commonly known as the slender pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by three-lobed berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
