Pelargonium × decoloratum

Pelargonium × decoloratum, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, glossy leaves that resemble ivy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its ornamental foliage and continuous flowering.

Pelargonium × decoloratum thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
