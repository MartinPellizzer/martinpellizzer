Pycnocoma sapinii

Pycnocoma sapinii, commonly known as the Alpine snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and is typically found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, aiding in water retention in cold, arid conditions.

Pycnocoma sapinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine collections.
