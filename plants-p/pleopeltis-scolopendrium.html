Pleopeltis scolopendrium

Pleopeltis scolopendrium, commonly known as the rock fern, is a species of epiphytic and lithophytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves that are often clustered in dense tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows on rocks or tree trunks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are usually dark green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pleopeltis scolopendrium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
