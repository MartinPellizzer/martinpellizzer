Pereskia aculeata

Pereskia aculeata, commonly known as the cactus apple or prickly pear, is a unique cactus species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers, typically blooming in the spring or summer. Its fruit, known as the "cactus apple," is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Cactaceae family and is notable for its ability to thrive in arid environments.
