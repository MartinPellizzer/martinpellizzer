Pterostylis oliveri

Pterostylis oliveri, commonly known as Oliver's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and undergrowth. The plant has a slender, underground rhizome and produces a single, erect stem with a single flower. The flower is characterized by its distinctive hood-like labellum and spurred lip, which are key features in its classification.

It is a rare and protected species, often found in specific microhabitats within its native range.
