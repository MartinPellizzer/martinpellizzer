Polemonium brandegeei subsp. typicum

Polemonium brandegeei subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the brandegee's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland environments, often found in the Appalachian region.

The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, with five petals and prominent veins. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in rich, loamy soils.
