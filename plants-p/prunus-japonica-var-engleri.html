Prunus japonica var. engleri

Prunus japonica var. engleri, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Japanese flowering cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and parks.

The fruit is a small, hard drupe that ripens in late summer.
