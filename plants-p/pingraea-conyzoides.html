Pingraea conyzoides

Pingraea conyzoides, commonly known as the tropical morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial plant characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Pingraea conyzoides is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
