Persicaria utriculosa

Persicaria utriculosa, commonly known as bladderroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a hairy texture and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its unique inflated seed pods, which resemble small bladders, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Persicaria utriculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
