Pavonia schwackei

Pavonia schwackei, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-pink bracts that surround a central cone-like structure, which is actually composed of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are primarily pollinated by insects.

Pavonia schwackei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
