Pseudognaphalium jaliscense

Pseudognaphalium jaliscense, commonly known as the Jalisco everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
