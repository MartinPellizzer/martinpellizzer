Polygala hyssopifolia

Polygala hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in a fine, blue-gray pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Polygala hyssopifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
