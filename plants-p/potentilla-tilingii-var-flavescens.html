Potentilla tilingii var. flavescens

Potentilla tilingii var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden potentilla, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in the summer. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.
