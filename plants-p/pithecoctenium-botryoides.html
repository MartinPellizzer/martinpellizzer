Pithecoctenium botryoides

Pithecoctenium botryoides, commonly known as the giant rat's-tail plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are often covered in a soft, woolly texture. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
