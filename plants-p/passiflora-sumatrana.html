Passiflora sumatrana

Passiflora sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran passionflower, is a climbing vine native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Passifloraceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a blend of red, yellow, and blue hues. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are covered in fine, persistent hairs.

Passiflora sumatrana thrives in tropical climates and requires ample sunlight and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
