Palaquium koratense

Palaquium koratense, commonly known as the Korat starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a tart, tangy flavor.

Palaquium koratense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
