Ptychosperma paradoxum

Ptychosperma paradoxum, commonly known as the golden palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are bright green with a distinctive golden hue on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellow, followed by fruit that contains a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
