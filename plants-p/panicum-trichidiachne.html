Panicum trichidiachne

Panicum trichidiachne, commonly known as the hairy panic grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, hairy foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
