Pachycladon wallii

Pachycladon wallii, commonly known as the Wallis pachycladon, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces woody capsules as its fruit, which contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Pachycladon wallii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
