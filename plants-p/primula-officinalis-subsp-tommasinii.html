Primula officinalis subsp. tommasinii

Primula officinalis subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Tommasinii primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
