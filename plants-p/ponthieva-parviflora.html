Ponthieva parviflora

Ponthieva parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered ponthieva, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a compact, evergreen palm characterized by its narrow, feather-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, making it suitable for indoor cultivation. Its foliage is dense and provides an elegant appearance, while its flowering occurs during the warmer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various growing conditions.
