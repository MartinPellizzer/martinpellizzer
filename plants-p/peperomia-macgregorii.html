Peperomia macgregorii

Peperomia macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's Peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features small, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture and a subtle sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, arranged in spikes along the stems. It is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in indirect light.

Peperomia macgregorii prefers well-draining soil and moderate humidity, making it suitable for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
