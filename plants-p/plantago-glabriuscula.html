Plantago glabriuscula

Plantago glabriuscula, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Narrowleaf plantain is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
