Polygala stenocarpa

Polygala stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala stenocarpa is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
