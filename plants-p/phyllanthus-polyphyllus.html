Phyllanthus polyphyllus

Phyllanthus polyphyllus, commonly known as the many-leaved phyllanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a varied leaf shape, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
