Pleopeltis thyssanolepis

Pleopeltis thyssanolepis, commonly known as the hairy pleopeltis, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, hairy fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air. Its fronds are usually lance-shaped with a serrated margin and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Pleopeltis thyssanolepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
