Potentilla recta f. vlasicensis

Potentilla recta f. vlasicensis, commonly known as the Vlasean cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers. The leaves are typically serrated and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and drought tolerance.
