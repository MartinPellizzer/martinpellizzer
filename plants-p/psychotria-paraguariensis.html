Psychotria paraguariensis

Psychotria paraguariensis, commonly known as ayahuasca, is a climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, particularly dimethyltryptamine (DMT), which contribute to its traditional use in spiritual and medicinal practices. It is typically grown in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

The plant's cultural significance is deeply rooted in the traditions of various indigenous communities in South America.
