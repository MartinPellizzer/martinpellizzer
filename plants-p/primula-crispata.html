Primula crispata

Primula crispata, commonly known as the wrinkled primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are ovate, hairy, and often have a crinkled texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or alpine habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
