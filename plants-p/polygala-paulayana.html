Polygala paulayana

Polygala paulayana, commonly known as the Malayan milk-vetch, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the genus Polygala. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
