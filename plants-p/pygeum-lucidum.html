Pygeum lucidum

Pygeum lucidum, commonly known as the shining African maple, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Anacardiaceae, which includes other well-known plants like the cashew and mango. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and palmate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties, particularly in supporting urinary and prostate health.
