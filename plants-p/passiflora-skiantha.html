Passiflora skiantha

Passiflora skiantha, commonly known as the scarlet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces orange-yellow berries that are edible and contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
