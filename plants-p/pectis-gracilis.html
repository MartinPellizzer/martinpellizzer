Pectis gracilis

Pectis gracilis, commonly known as the slender pectis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its slender, erect stems are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear with a slightly hairy texture.

Pectis gracilis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
