Pachycymbium sprengeri subsp. ogadense

Pachycymbium sprengeri subsp.

ogadense, commonly known as the Ogaden pachycymbium, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of East Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

The plant typically produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the wet season. It grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
