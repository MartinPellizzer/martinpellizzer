Potentilla chrysocraspeda

Potentilla chrysocraspeda, commonly known as the golden-haired cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches a height of about 15-30 centimeters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately divided into five pointed lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.
