Polygala vayredae

Polygala vayredae, commonly known as the Vayreda's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces distinctive, often pale yellow or greenish-white blooms that are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
