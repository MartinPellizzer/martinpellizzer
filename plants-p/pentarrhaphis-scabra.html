Pentarrhaphis scabra

Pentarrhaphis scabra, commonly known as the rough-leafed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Its resilience and adaptability make it a common species in various ecological and horticultural settings.
