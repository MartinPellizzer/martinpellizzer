Papaver argemonoides

Papaver argemonoides, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow petals and a distinctive, reddish-brown seed capsule that splits open when mature. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Papaveraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the opium poppy.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
