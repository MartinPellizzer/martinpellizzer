Poterium lucidum

Poterium lucidum, commonly known as the shining leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and waxy to the touch. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Poterium lucidum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests and along riverbanks.
