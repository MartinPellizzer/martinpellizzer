Podalyria glauca var. biflora

Podalyria glauca var. biflora, commonly known as the blue flax or mountain flax, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Podalyria glauca var. biflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
