Phaseolus luteolus

Phaseolus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow bean or yellow pinto bean, is a leguminous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, with yellow or green pods containing small, yellow or cream-colored seeds. The plant thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It also has a significant role in traditional agriculture and is used in various culinary practices across its native regions.

Additionally, it contributes to soil fertility through nitrogen fixation, making it a valuable crop in sustainable farming systems.
