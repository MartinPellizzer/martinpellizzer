Primula jesoana f. albiflora

Primula jesoana f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a rare and ornamental plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and the flowers are borne on short pedicels.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
