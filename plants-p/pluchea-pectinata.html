Pluchea pectinata

Pluchea pectinata, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a serrated margin.

The flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
