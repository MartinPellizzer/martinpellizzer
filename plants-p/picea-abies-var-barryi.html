Picea abies var. barryi

Picea abies var. barryi, commonly known as the Barry's fir, is a variant of the Norway spruce.

It is characterized by its distinctive needle coloration, which ranges from dark green to a striking blue-green. The cones of this variety are typically shorter and more cylindrical compared to those of the typical Norway spruce. It is native to specific regions in Europe and is often found in mountainous areas with cool, moist climates.

Due to its unique appearance, it is valued in horticulture for ornamental purposes.
