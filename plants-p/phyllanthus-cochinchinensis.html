Phyllanthus cochinchinensis

Phyllanthus cochinchinensis, commonly known as the broadleaf phyllanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its small yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
