Papaver lapponicum

Papaver lapponicum, commonly known as the Lapland poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a short, upright stem with palmately lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Its capsule fruit contains numerous tiny, dark seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Papaver lapponicum thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.
