Polygala forojulensis subsp. adriatica

Polygala forojulensis subsp.

adriatica, commonly known as the Adriatic polygala, is a flowering plant native to the Adriatic region of Southeast Europe. It is a member of the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
