Palaquium payenifolium

Palaquium payenifolium, commonly known as the Payeni's palaquium, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round berries that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local medicine.
