Podanthes lepida

Podanthes lepida, commonly known as the silverleaf podanthes, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Podanthes lepida is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
