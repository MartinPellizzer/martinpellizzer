Papaver opiiferum var. album

Papaver opiiferum var. album, commonly known as the white opium poppy, is a variant of the opium poppy native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its large, white, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces latex-containing capsules that contain alkaloids such as morphine and codeine. Its botanical features include smooth, lobed leaves and a tall, erect stem.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is subject to strict legal regulation in many countries.
