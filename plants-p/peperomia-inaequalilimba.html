Peperomia inaequalilimba

Peperomia inaequalilimba, commonly known as the unequal-leaf peperomia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a compact, low-growing plant with thick, fleshy leaves that are often variegated in color. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped, with a distinctive asymmetrical pattern that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually green or reddish in color.

This plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
