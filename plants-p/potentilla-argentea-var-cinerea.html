Potentilla argentea var. cinerea

Potentilla argentea var. cinerea, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs, and its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
