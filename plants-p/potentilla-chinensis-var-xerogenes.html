Potentilla chinensis var. xerogenes

Potentilla chinensis var. xerogenes, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in poor soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh environments.
