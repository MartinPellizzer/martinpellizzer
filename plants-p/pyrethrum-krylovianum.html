Pyrethrum krylovianum

Pyrethrum krylovianum, commonly known as the Krylovian pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant produces fine, hair-like structures called phloroglucinol, which are responsible for its insecticidal properties. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its natural pest-repelling abilities, it is sometimes cultivated for organic pest control.
