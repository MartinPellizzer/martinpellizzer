Prunus carolinae

Prunus carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina cherrybark oak, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its deep, fissured bark that resembles the color and texture of cherry bark. The tree produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark blue berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
