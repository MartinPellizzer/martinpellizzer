Petrophile arcuata

Petrophile arcuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr bush, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland regions. The plant produces small, pink or red flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which are characteristic of the genus Petrophile. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often appearing in whorls around the stems.

The species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
