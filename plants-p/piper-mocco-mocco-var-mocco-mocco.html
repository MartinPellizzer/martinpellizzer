Piper mocco-mocco var. mocco-mocco

Piper mocco-mocco var. mocco-mocco, commonly known as the Mocco-Mocco pepper, is a tropical vine native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species, and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy, peppery flavor. Its botanical structure includes a slender stem with adventitious roots and a system of tendrils for support.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its unique culinary and medicinal properties.
