Pavonia macdougallii

Pavonia macdougallii, commonly known as the desert broom.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.

Pavonia macdougallii is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
