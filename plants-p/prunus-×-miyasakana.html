Prunus × miyasakana

Prunus × miyasakana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom or sakura, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is celebrated for its ornamental value, particularly during its brief spring blooming period. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a stunning visual display. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
