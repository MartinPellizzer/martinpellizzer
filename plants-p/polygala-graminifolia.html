Polygala graminifolia

Polygala graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy milkwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, spicate inflorescences with small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in coastal and inland regions, thriving in well-drained environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
