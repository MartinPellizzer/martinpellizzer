Plantago lagopus var. glabrata

Plantago lagopus var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth plantain, is a variant of the common plantain native to temperate regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and hairless. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in roadsides, fields, and waste areas.

This variant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
