Potamogeton filiformis f. crinisimilis

Potamogeton filiformis f. crinisimilis, commonly known as the silky water milfoil, is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves that give it a feathery appearance.

It belongs to the family Potamogetonaceae and is native to freshwater environments in North America. The plant typically grows in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are pollinated by wind.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
