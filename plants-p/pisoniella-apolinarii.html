Pisoniella apolinarii

Pisoniella apolinarii, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it grows in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is typically found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for centuries.
