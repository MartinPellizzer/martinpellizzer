Pleurothyrium chrysophyllum

Pleurothyrium chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden-leafed tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
