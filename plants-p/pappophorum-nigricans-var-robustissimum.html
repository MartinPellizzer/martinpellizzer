Pappophorum nigricans var. robustissimum

Pappophorum nigricans var. robustissimum, commonly known as the robust blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, coarse-textured leaves and sturdy, erect growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically dark purple to black in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
