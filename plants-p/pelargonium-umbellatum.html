Pelargonium umbellatum

Pelargonium umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically rounded and have a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
