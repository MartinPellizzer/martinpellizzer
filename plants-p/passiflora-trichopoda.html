Passiflora trichopoda

Passiflora trichopoda, commonly known as the velvet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a crown of filaments and a central cluster of blue and white petals. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with a fine, velvety texture.

Passiflora trichopoda is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique and showy floral display.
