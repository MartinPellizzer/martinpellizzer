Pseudanthus ballingalliae

Pseudanthus ballingalliae, commonly known as the Ballingall's pseudanthus, is a unique plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a clustered inflorescence that gives the appearance of a single flower.

This species is noted for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
