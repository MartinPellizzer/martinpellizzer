Pancratium trichromum

Pancratium trichromum, commonly known as the three-colored yellow flag, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking yellow flowers with three distinct color zones. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a reddish or purple hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable water conditions.
