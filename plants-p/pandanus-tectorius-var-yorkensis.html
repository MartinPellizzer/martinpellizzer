Pandanus tectorius var. yorkensis

Pandanus tectorius var. yorkensis, commonly known as the York Island pandanus, is a variant of the screwpine plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-like leaves that grow in a rosette pattern, often reaching several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in brackish or saltwater conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
