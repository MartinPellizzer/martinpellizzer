Penstemon humilis subsp. brevifolius

Penstemon humilis subsp.

brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It features upright, branching stems that bear tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
