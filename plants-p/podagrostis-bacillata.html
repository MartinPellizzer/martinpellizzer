Podagrostis bacillata

Podagrostis bacillata, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Podagrostis bacillata is often found in temperate regions and is relatively uncommon in cultivated settings.
