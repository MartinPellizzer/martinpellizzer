Phlox multiflora

Phlox multiflora, commonly known as the common phlox, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Phlox multiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
