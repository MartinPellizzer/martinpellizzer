Panicum trichopiptum

Panicum trichopiptum, commonly known as the slender panicgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Panicum trichopiptum is often used for forage and soil stabilization in agricultural settings.
