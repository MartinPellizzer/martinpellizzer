Pitcairnia cubensis

Pitcairnia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban pitcairnia, is a species of bromeliad native to Cuba.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, a common feature among bromeliads. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
