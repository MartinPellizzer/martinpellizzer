Potentilla serpentini var. parvifrons

Potentilla serpentini var. parvifrons, commonly known as the small-leaved potentilla, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features wiry, many-branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a silvery-green hue due to dense pubescence. Its flowers are typically yellow or cream, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
