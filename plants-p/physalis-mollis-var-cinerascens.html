Physalis mollis var. cinerascens

Physalis mollis var. cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf physalis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of orange-red berries encased in a papery calyx. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow with five petals and bloom in the spring.

The fruit, which is edible when fully ripe, has a tangy, slightly sweet flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
