Poggeophyton aculeatum

Poggeophyton aculeatum, commonly known as the spiny river plant, is an extinct genus of early land plants from the Silurian period.

It is characterized by its simple, dichotomously branched stems and the presence of small, spiny structures called aculei. These aculei are believed to have provided structural support and possibly played a role in defense against herbivores. The plant likely thrived in shallow, aquatic environments, contributing to the early colonization of land by plants.

Fossil records of Poggeophyton aculeatum provide valuable insights into the evolutionary transition from aquatic to terrestrial plant life.
