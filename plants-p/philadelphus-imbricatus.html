Philadelphus imbricatus

Philadelphus imbricatus, commonly known as the common mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

The fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.
