Polygonum majus

Polygonum majus, commonly known as great knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring tall, slender stems and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, adding visual appeal to gardens.

The plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
