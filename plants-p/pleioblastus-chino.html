Pleioblastus chino

Pleioblastus chino, commonly known as Chinese bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, oval-shaped seeds.

Pleioblastus chino is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a decorative plant in gardens.
