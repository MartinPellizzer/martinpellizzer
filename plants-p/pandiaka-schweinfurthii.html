Pandiaka schweinfurthii

Pandiaka schweinfurthii, commonly known as the Schweinfurth's pandiaka, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a velvety texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pandiaka schweinfurthii is valued in horticulture for its unique floral display and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
