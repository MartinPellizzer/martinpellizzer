Penstemon subulatus

Penstemon subulatus, commonly known as the silver curl or Sierra penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, thriving in full sun. It is a perennial species that produces showy blooms from late spring to early summer.

Penstemon subulatus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
