Polygala nana var. humillima

Polygala nana var. humillima, commonly known as the dwarf milkvetch, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit, often reaching only a few inches in height, making it well-suited for rocky or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and rare species within its native range.
