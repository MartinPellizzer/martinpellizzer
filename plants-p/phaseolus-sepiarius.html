Phaseolus sepiarius

Phaseolus sepiarius, commonly known as the black bean plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing legume that produces elongated, dark green pods containing small, round black beans. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers. It is valued for its edible beans, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
