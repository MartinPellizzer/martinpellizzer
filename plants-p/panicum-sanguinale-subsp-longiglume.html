Panicum sanguinale subsp. longiglume

Panicum sanguinale subsp.

longiglume, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, reddish-brown spikelets that are often tinged with purple. Its inflorescence is typically a panicle, which is a branched cluster of flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed or open habitats.
