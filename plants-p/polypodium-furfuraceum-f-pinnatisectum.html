Polypodium furfuraceum f. pinnatisectum

Polypodium furfuraceum f. pinnatisectum, commonly known as the fern with finely divided leaves, is a variant of the common polypody.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinnatisect leaves that are finely divided, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often on rock surfaces or in forest understories. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
