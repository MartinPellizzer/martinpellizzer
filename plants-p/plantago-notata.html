Plantago notata

Plantago notata, commonly known as narrowleaf plantain, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Narrowleaf plantain is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
