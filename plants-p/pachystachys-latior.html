Pachystachys latior

Pachystachys latior, commonly known as the broadleaf torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling torches. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Pachystachys latior is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
