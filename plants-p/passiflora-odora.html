Passiflora odora

Passiflora odora, commonly known as the sweet passionflower, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature a central crown of filaments and a vibrant array of colors, often in shades of red, yellow, and blue. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a distinctive, fragrant aroma.

Passiflora odora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its culinary and medicinal uses.
