Pitcairnia caulescens

Pitcairnia caulescens, commonly known as the red-flowered pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup, typical of many bromeliads. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in a spiral arrangement, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its native dry climate.

Pitcairnia caulescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
