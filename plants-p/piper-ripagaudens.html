Piper ripagaudens

Piper ripagaudens, commonly known as the Guiana pepper, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces small, peppery berries that are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to the Amazon rainforest.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical research.
