Paepalanthus convexus var. strigosus

Paepalanthus convexus var. strigosus, commonly known as the woolly pincushion cactus, is a unique plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of white to grayish hairs that protect it from intense sunlight and moisture loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is relatively rare and requires specific care to cultivate successfully.
