Pogonolepis muelleriana

Pogonolepis muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's beard-plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and a basal rosette of leaves. Its flowering stems bear small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels.

The species is noted for its adaptability to harsh environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
