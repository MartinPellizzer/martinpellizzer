Prunus apetala var. iwozana

Prunus apetala var. iwozana, commonly known as the Iwozana cherry, is a rare and endemic species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a smooth margin and a glossy green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that matures in late summer, adding ornamental value to the plant.
