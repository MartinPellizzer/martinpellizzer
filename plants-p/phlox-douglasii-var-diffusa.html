Phlox douglasii var. diffusa

Phlox douglasii var. diffusa, commonly known as the Sierra phlox, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to sunny, well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The Sierra phlox is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
