Pyrus viridis

Pyrus viridis, commonly known as the European green apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it is characterized by its small, green, apple-like fruits. The plant produces white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. Pyrus viridis is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the autumn, and it can grow to a height of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
