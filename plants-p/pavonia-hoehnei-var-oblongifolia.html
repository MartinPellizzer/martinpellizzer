Pavonia hoehnei var. oblongifolia

Pavonia hoehnei var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pavonia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are ovate to oblong in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open woodland or disturbed areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
