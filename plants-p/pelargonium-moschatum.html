Pelargonium moschatum

Pelargonium moschatum, commonly known as the musk geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often clustered in umbels. Its leaves are typically rounded and have a distinctive musky fragrance, which gives it its common name.

Pelargonium moschatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its visual appeal and pleasant scent.
