Potamogeton perfoliatus var. lanceolatus

Potamogeton perfoliatus var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem, and it produces small, green flowers that are typically found in clusters. The plant is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape, which is more elongated compared to other varieties of pondweed. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

Its growth is often influenced by water clarity, nutrient levels, and light availability.
