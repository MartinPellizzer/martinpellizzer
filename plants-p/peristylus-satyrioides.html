Peristylus satyrioides

Peristylus satyrioides, commonly known as the satyr orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, butterfly-like flowers with a distinctive yellow and purple coloration. It typically blooms in spring and is often found in open grasslands and rocky soils.

Peristylus satyrioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
