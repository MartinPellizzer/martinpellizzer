Panicum kermesinum

Panicum kermesinum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a flowering plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a reddish tinge, especially under stress conditions.

Panicum kermesinum is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental grass in warmer climates.
