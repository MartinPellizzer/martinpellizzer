Piptocarpha organensis

Piptocarpha organensis, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish flowers. This plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
