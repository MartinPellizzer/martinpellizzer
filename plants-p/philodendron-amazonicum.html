Philodendron amazonicum

Philodendron amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach significant heights when provided with support. Its stems are fibrous and capable of producing aerial roots to anchor itself to trees or other structures.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
