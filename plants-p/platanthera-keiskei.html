Platanthera keiskei

Platanthera keiskei, commonly known as the Keiske's lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and a striking, fragrant flower spike that bears numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate floral structure.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
