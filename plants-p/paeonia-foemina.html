Paeonia foemina

Paeonia foemina, commonly known as the Persian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces palmately divided leaves and has a thick, fleshy rhizome that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Paeonia foemina is also valued for its historical use in traditional medicine.
