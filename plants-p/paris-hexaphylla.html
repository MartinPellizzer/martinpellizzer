Paris hexaphylla

Paris hexaphylla, commonly known as the six-leaf Paris, is a member of the Melanthiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with several whorled leaves, each of which is lanceolate and often tinged with reddish hues. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a loose umbel, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

The plant is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional medicine, though it is generally avoided due to its potential toxicity.
