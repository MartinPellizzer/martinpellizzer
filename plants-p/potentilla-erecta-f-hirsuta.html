Potentilla erecta f. hirsuta

Potentilla erecta f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its specific epithet "hirsuta.". The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered with fine hairs.

Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this species is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.
