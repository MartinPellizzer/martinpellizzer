Prestonia marginata

Prestonia marginata, commonly known as the marginated prestonia, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
