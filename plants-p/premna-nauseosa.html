Premna nauseosa

Premna nauseosa, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lamiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact. It produces small, clustered flowers with blue or purple petals, typically blooming in the spring.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
