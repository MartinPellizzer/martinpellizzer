Paxiodendron ulugurense

Paxiodendron ulugurense, commonly known as the Uluguru endemic tree, is a unique species native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, grey bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
