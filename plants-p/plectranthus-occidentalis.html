Plectranthus occidentalis

Plectranthus occidentalis, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, mint-scented leaves and clusters of small, tubular, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often grown as an ornamental or for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are used in traditional herbal remedies, and it is also a popular choice for culinary purposes.

Plectranthus occidentalis is relatively easy to cultivate and can be grown in containers or in garden beds.
