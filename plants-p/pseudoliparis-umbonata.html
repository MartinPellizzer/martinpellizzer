Pseudoliparis umbonata

Pseudoliparis umbonata, commonly known as the umbrella anemone, is a species of sea anemone found in the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like oral disc and long, slender tentacles. The polyps of Pseudoliparis umbonata are typically pale pink to white in color, and they are often found in shallow, sandy or rubble substrates. This anemone is known for its ability to host various species of anthozoans, including certain types of clownfish.

Its botanical features make it an important component of reef ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
