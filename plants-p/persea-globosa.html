Persea globosa

Persea globosa, commonly known as the globosa persimmon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a deep red or orange color. Its fruit is edible, though it may have astringent properties when unripe, and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
