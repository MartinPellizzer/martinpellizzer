Polygonum latum

Polygonum latum, commonly known as broadleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive nodes and often translucent leaf sheaths. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered flowers in late spring to early summer.

Polygonum latum is often found in disturbed soils and can spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in agricultural and wetland environments.
