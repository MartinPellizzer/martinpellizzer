Piper tomas-albertoi

Piper tomas-albertoi, commonly known as the Alberti's pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, peppery fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
