Phlox latifolia

Phlox latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf phlox, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
