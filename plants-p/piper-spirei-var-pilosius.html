Piper spirei var. pilosius

Piper spirei var. pilosius, commonly known as the velvet leaf pepper, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
