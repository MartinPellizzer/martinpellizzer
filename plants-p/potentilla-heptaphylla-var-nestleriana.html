Potentilla heptaphylla var. nestleriana

Potentilla heptaphylla var. nestleriana, commonly known as the Nestler's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, giving it the common name "cinquefoil.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
