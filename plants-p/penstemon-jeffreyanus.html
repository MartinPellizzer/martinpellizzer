Penstemon jeffreyanus

Penstemon jeffreyanus, commonly known as Jeffrey's beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of tubular, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Penstemon jeffreyanus is also valued for its ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
