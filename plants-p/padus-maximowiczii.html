Padus maximowiczii

Padus maximowiczii, commonly known as the Siberian whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that attract birds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Padus maximowiczii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
