Polygala myrtifolia var. angustifolia

Polygala myrtifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, often hairy stems that grow from a fibrous root system. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and usually have a distinctive, spurred shape.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
