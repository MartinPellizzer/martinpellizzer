Patagonium arachnipes

Patagonium arachnipes, commonly known as the spider-horned cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems that resemble the legs of a spider, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to harsh environments include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and a deep root system to access groundwater.

Patagonium arachnipes plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various desert species.
