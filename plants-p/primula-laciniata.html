Primula laciniata

Primula laciniata, commonly known as the Chinese primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is part of the Primulaceae family, which includes other well-known flowering plants.
