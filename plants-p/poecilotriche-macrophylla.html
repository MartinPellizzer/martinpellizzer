Poecilotriche macrophylla

Poecilotriche macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf poecilotriche, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insect species.
