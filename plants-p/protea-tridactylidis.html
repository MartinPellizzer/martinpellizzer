Protea tridactylidis

Protea tridactylidis, commonly known as the three-toed protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded head. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos biome. Its unique appearance makes it a popular ornamental plant, though it is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, a common trait among many protea species.
