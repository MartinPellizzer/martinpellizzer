Pterostylis rufa var. mitchellii

Pterostylis rufa var. mitchellii, commonly known as the red-stemmed helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and understoreys. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to reddish in color, with a distinctive, elongated spur.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
