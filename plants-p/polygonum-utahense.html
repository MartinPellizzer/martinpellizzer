Polygonum utahense

Polygonum utahense, commonly known as Utah knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
