Passerina filiformis

Passerina filiformis, commonly known as the slender pasqueflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to harsh, high-altitude conditions.

Passerina filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
