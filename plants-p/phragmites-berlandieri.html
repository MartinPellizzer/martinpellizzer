Phragmites berlandieri

Phragmites berlandieri, commonly known as the western common reed, is a tall, perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and tall, cylindrical flower spikes that produce a large amount of lightweight seeds. This plant is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along the shores of lakes and rivers. Its extensive root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Phragmites berlandieri plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
