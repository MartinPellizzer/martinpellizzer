Pithecellobium acuminatum

Pithecellobium acuminatum, commonly known as the sweet acacia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are compound, with multiple leaflets that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and for its seeds, which can be used in traditional medicine.
