Plectronia cordata

Plectronia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf plectronia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
