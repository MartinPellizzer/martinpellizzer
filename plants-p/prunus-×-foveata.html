Prunus × foveata

Prunus × foveata, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant typically has smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is a small, yellowish plum that ripens in late summer, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Prunus × foveata is native to the western United States and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
