Prismatomeris ovalifolia

Prismatomeris ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf prismatomeris, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
