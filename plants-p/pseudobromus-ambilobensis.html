Pseudobromus ambilobensis

Pseudobromus ambilobensis, commonly known as the Ambilobense needlegrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray sheen, which helps reduce water loss.

Pseudobromus ambilobensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
