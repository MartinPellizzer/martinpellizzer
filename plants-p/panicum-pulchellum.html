Panicum pulchellum

Panicum pulchellum, commonly known as the purple lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. The plant produces dense clusters of purple to pink flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fine texture that gives it a delicate appearance.

Panicum pulchellum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
