Panicum miliaceum var. corsinum

Panicum miliaceum var. corsinum, commonly known as Corsican millet, is a variant of the millet species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and historical use in traditional agriculture.
