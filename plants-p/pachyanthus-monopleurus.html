Pachyanthus monopleurus

Pachyanthus monopleurus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, bean-like fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
