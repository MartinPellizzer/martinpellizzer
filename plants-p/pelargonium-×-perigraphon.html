Pelargonium × perigraphon

Pelargonium × perigraphon, commonly known as the ivy-leaved geranium, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from various geranium species.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant for hanging baskets and containers due to its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Pelargonium × perigraphon is also valued for its adaptability to different light levels and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
