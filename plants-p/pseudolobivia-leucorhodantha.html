Pseudolobivia leucorhodantha

Pseudolobivia leucorhodantha, commonly known as the white-flowered lobivia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces showy, white to pale pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
