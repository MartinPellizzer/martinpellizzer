Philcoxia rhizomatosa

Philcoxia rhizomatosa, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy, leaf-like structures that are adapted for water storage. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often overlooked. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it thrives in environments with minimal rainfall and high sunlight exposure.
