Primula woodwardii

Primula woodwardii, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and streamside areas. Its leaves are broad and hairy, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Primula woodwardii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
