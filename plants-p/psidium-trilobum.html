Psidium trilobum

Psidium trilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is valued for its nutritional content.
