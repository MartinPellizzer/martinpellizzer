Popowia caesia

Popowia caesia, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with distinctive red veins. Its leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Popowia caesia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
