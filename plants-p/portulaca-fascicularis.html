Portulaca fascicularis

Portulaca fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered purslane, is a low-growing annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it common in disturbed areas and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaves and a distinctive cluster of flowers at the tips of the stems.
