Phalaris nodosa var. minor

Phalaris nodosa var. minor, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
