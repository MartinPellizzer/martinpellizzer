Pithecellobium leiocalyx

Pithecellobium leiocalyx, commonly known as the velvet bean tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in racemes. The plant is notable for its fruit, which resembles a bean and contains a single, edible seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of protein-rich seeds.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
