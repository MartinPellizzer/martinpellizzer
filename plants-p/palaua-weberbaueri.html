Palaua weberbaueri

Palaua weberbaueri, commonly known as the Pacific island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific islands, particularly Hawaii.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple throat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Palaua weberbaueri is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its aesthetic value in horticulture.
