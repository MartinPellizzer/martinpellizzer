Pittosporum croceum

Pittosporum croceum, commonly known as the golden pittosporum, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are typically yellow or white in color. These flowers are followed by bright orange or red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
