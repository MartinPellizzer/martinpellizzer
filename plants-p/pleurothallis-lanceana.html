Pleurothallis lanceana

Pleurothallis lanceana, commonly known as the lanceleaf orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
