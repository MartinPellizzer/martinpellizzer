Prunus × lannesiana f. versicolor

Prunus × lannesiana f. versicolor, commonly known as the variable cherry, is a botanical hybrid known for its striking color variation.

This cultivar belongs to the Rosaceae family and is a cross between Prunus lannesiana and Prunus serrulata. Its leaves exhibit a range of colors, from bright green to variegated yellow and white, making it visually distinctive. The plant is typically deciduous, with showy pink or white flowers in early spring.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
