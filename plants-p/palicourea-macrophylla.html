Palicourea macrophylla

Palicourea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant palicourea, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its stems are woody and often have a distinctive, slightly rough texture.

Palicourea macrophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
