Panicum longicaudum

Panicum longicaudum, commonly known as the long-tailed panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
