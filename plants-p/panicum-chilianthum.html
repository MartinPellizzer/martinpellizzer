Panicum chilianthum

Panicum chilianthum, commonly known as the Chilianthum grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
