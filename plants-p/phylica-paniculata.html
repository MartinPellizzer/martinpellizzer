Phylica paniculata

Phylica paniculata, commonly known as the silver tree, is a distinctive shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in panicles, which are visible during the winter months. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Phylica paniculata is also valued for its ecological role, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
