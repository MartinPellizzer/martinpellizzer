Physaria paysonii

Physaria paysonii, commonly known as the Payson bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are succulent, helping it conserve water, while its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

The plant is also known for its ability to self-pollinate, which aids in its reproduction in less favorable conditions.
