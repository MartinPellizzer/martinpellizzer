Pouteria tarapotensis

Pouteria tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean sapote, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly in the Andes region.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces a fruit that is similar in appearance to a persimmon, with a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, starchy pulp inside. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
