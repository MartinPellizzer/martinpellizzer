Piper pinoganense

Piper pinoganense, commonly known as the Mindanao pepper or Philippine black pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the provinces of Davao and Mindanao. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are followed by elongated, fleshy berries that turn dark when ripe.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine and has a distinct spicy flavor, similar to black pepper.
