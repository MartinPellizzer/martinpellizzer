Prunus nikaii

Prunus nikaii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn red in the autumn, and its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Prunus nikaii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
