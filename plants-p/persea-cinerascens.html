Persea cinerascens

Persea cinerascens, commonly known as the Mexican white sapote, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces a fruit with a thick, inedible rind and a sweet, creamy pulp that is edible. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it often has a distinctive white or ash-colored hue.

Persea cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
