Polypodium taeniatum

Polypodium taeniatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and its fronds are leathery with a distinctive, narrow shape.

Polypodium taeniatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
