Piper bredemeyeri f. escabridum

Piper bredemeyeri f. escabridum, commonly known as the Escabridum pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of aromatic foliage.
