Polygala rehmannii var. latipetala

Polygala rehmannii var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-petalled milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, often showy flowers that resemble small horns or spires. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, greenish or yellowish flowers clustered in racemes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
