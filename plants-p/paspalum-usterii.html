Paspalum usterii

Paspalum usterii, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
