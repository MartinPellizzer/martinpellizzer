Pennisetum alopecuroides f. viridescens

Pennisetum alopecuroides f. viridescens, commonly known as the green velvet fountain grass, is a variant of the fountain grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and its striking, fountain-like arrangement of narrow leaves. The foliage of this plant is typically a vibrant green, which gives it its common name. It produces plume-like inflorescences that range in color from silver to pink, adding visual interest in the garden.

This ornamental grass is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
