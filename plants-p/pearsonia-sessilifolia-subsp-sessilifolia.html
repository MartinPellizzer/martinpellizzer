Pearsonia sessilifolia subsp. sessilifolia

Pearsonia sessilifolia subsp.

sessilifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pearsonia, is a species native to New Zealand. It is a shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in coastal and lowland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
