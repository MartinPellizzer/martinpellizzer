Pavetta baconia var. tomentella

Pavetta baconia var. tomentella, commonly known as the tomentellum pavetta, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, soft texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
