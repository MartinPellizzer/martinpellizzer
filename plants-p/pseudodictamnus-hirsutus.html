Pseudodictamnus hirsutus

Pseudodictamnus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy false dittany, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, pungent odor when crushed.
