Polypodium sagitta

Polypodium sagitta, commonly known as the arrow fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in humid environments, often growing on trees or rocks in shaded areas. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique adaptation makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
