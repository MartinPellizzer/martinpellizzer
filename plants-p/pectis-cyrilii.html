Pectis cyrilii

Pectis cyrilii, commonly known as the Cyril's pectis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Pectis cyrilii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
