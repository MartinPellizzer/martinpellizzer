Pachystigma pteleoides

Pachystigma pteleoides, commonly known as the African snowball, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble snowballs, hence its common name. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers during the winter months, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
