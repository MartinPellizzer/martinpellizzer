Pithecellobium caesalpinioides

Pithecellobium caesalpinioides, commonly known as the yellow acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, woody pods and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of fodder.
