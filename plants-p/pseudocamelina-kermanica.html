Pseudocamelina kermanica

Pseudocamelina kermanica, commonly known as the Kermanica false camellia, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant features showy, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a slightly hairy texture.
