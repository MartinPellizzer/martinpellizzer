Passerina anthylloides var. glabrescens

Passerina anthylloides var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glabrous leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals and numerous stamens, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
