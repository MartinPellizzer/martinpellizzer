Phyllanthus nutans var. trojanus

Phyllanthus nutans var. trojanus, commonly known as the Trojan gooseberry, is a variant of the creeping woodrose.

It is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
