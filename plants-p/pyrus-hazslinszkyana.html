Pyrus hazslinszkyana

Pyrus hazslinszkyana, commonly known as the Hungarian pear, is a deciduous tree native to the Carpathian Basin.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring attractive yellowish-green leaves and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
