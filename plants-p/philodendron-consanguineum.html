Philodendron consanguineum

Philodendron consanguineum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Philodendron consanguineum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
