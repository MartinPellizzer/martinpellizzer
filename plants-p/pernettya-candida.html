Pernettya candida

Pernettya candida, commonly known as the white cowberry, is a species of shrub native to the boreal forests of northern Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. Pernettya candida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
