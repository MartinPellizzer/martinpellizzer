Parajubaea cocoides

Parajubaea cocoides, commonly known as the "cocoanut palm," is a unique species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 5 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, coconut-like fruits. Its botanical structure is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Despite its appearance, it is not closely related to true palms and belongs to the Arecaceae family.
