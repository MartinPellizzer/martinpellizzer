Pleurothallis gracilipedunculata

Pleurothallis gracilipedunculata, commonly known as the slender-stemmed pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
