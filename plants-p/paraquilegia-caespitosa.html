Paraquilegia caespitosa

Paraquilegia caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features basal rosettes of velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, violet to blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
