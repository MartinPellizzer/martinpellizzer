Panicum capillipes

Panicum capillipes, commonly known as the silky grass or slender panicgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of the Americas and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and fine, silky hairs that cover its leaves and inflorescence. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Panicum capillipes is a perennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
