Phyllanthus lycioides

Phyllanthus lycioides, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Phyllanthus lycioides is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value.
