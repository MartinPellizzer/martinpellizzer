Poa annua f. alpigena

Poa annua f. alpigena, commonly known as the alpine annual bluegrass, is a variant of the common annual bluegrass.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous plant characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender stems. This plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually bluish-green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Poa annua f. alpigena is adapted to harsh mountain conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
