Piper latifolium

Piper latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
