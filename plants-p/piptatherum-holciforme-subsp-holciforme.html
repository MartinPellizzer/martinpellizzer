Piptatherum holciforme subsp. holciforme

Piptatherum holciforme subsp.

holciforme, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a single, cylindrical spikelet that is often loosely arranged.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect and bird populations.
