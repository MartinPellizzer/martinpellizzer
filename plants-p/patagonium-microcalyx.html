Patagonium microcalyx

Patagonium microcalyx, commonly known as the Patagonian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh, cold climates of Patagonia. It has small, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.
