Polylobium micranthum

Polylobium micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered polylobium, is a member of the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It typically features palmate leaves with multiple lobes, which are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five stamens and a single pistil, typical of the family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
