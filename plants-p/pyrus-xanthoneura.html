Pyrus xanthoneura

Pyrus xanthoneura, commonly known as the golden-leafed pear, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which stands out during the growing season. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to common pears, though they are less widely cultivated. Native to regions of East Asia, it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in gardens for its striking appearance.
