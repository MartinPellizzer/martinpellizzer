Pavetta sepium var. merkeri

Pavetta sepium var. merkeri, commonly known as the Merker's pavetta, is a variant of the pavetta plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in small umbels, and are borne on short pedicels. The plant produces small, round berries that are often red when mature, containing several seeds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
