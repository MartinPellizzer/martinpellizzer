Piper gamboanum var. yapense

Piper gamboanum var. yapense, commonly known as the Yap pepper, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, slender berries. The berries are typically harvested when mature and are used in traditional cuisines for their pungent flavor.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
