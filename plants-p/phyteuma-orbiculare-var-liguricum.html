Phyteuma orbiculare var. liguricum

Phyteuma orbiculare var. liguricum, commonly known as the Ligurian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and its stems are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
