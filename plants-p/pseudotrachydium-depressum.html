Pseudotrachydium depressum

Pseudotrachydium depressum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy, succulent leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the cooler months, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its root system is extensive and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in harsh environments.

Overall, Pseudotrachydium depressum is a hardy, drought-tolerant species with distinctive botanical characteristics suited to its ecological niche.
