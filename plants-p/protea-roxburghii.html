Protea roxburghii

Protea roxburghii, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, white, fluffy inflorescences that resemble a cloud or a puff of smoke, giving it its common name. The plant is native to the Eastern Cape and is often found growing on rocky outcrops and mountain slopes. Its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions make it a popular ornamental plant.

Protea roxburghii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
