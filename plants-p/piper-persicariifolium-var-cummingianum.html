Piper persicariifolium var. cummingianum

Piper persicariifolium var. cummingianum, commonly known as the Cumming's pepper plant, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal remedies due to its aromatic compounds.
