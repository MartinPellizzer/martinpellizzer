Parasenecio hastatus var. tanakae

Parasenecio hastatus var. tanakae, commonly known as the Tanaka's bluebell, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural habitat.
