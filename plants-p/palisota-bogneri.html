Palisota bogneri

Palisota bogneri, commonly known as the Bogner's water plantain, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, featuring strap-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in its wetland environment.

Palisota bogneri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic life.
