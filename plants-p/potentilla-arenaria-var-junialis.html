Potentilla arenaria var. junialis

Potentilla arenaria var. junialis, commonly known as sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and rocky regions.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in dry environments. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or desert areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in landscaping.
