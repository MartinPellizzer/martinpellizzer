Polygonum novoascanicum

Polygonum novoascanicum, commonly known as the New Zealand knotweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes species known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are typically lanceolate with a prominent midrib. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygonum novoascanicum is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and forest margins.
