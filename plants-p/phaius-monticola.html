Phaius monticola

Phaius monticola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy orchid flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in well-drained, humus-rich soils. It is often found in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
