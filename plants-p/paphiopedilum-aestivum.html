Paphiopedilum aestivum

Paphiopedilum aestivum, commonly known as the summer orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lip-like petal that resembles a pouch, a feature unique to the genus Paphiopedilum. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink during the summer months. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

This orchid is known for its long blooming period and relatively easy care requirements.
