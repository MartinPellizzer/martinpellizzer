Polygonum serpyllaceum

Polygonum serpyllaceum, commonly known as common knotgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pink to reddish in color.

Polygonum serpyllaceum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural areas.
