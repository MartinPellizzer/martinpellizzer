Puccinellia phryganodes subsp. sibirica

Puccinellia phryganodes subsp.

sibirica, commonly known as Siberian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in steppes and deserts, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
