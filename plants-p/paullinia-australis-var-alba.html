Paullinia australis var. alba

Paullinia australis var. alba, commonly known as the white passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It belongs to the family Passifloraceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are covered in fine, soft hairs.
