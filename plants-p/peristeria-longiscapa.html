Peristeria longiscapa

Peristeria longiscapa, commonly known as the long-stemmed peristeria, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and green, often resembling a bird in flight, hence its common name. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that can reach several feet in length, making it a dramatic addition to any collection. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments by enthusiasts and botanists.
