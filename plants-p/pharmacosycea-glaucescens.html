Pharmacosycea glaucescens

Pharmacosycea glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant found in tropical regions.

It attaches to the branches of host trees, drawing water and nutrients through its haustoria. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green in color. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can have significant impacts on the health of its host trees.
