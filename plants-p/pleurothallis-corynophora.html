Pleurothallis corynophora

Pleurothallis corynophora, commonly known as the golden tunic orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the canopy of rainforests. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. Its growth habit involves attaching itself to trees or other plants without deriving nutrients from them.

Botanically, it belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate floral structure and specialized pollination mechanisms.
