Psoralea scabra

Psoralea scabra, commonly known as the rough psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in shrubland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Psoralea scabra is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
