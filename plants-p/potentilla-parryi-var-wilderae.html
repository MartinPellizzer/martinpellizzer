Potentilla parryi var. wilderae

Potentilla parryi var. wilderae, commonly known as the Sierra wild quince, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to harsh growing conditions.
