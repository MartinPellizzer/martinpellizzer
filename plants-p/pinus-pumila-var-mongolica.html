Pinus pumila var. mongolica

Pinus pumila var. mongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian dwarf pine, is a compact, low-growing conifer native to the cold, mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China.

This variant of the dwarf pine is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It thrives in harsh alpine and subarctic environments, tolerating extreme cold and poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, red-brown cones that release winged seeds, aiding in its dispersal across rocky and barren landscapes.

Its resilience makes it an important component of the ecological systems in its native range.
