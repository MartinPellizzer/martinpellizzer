Pleurothallis rupestris

Pleurothallis rupestris, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments on tree branches or rocky surfaces. The plant features delicate, linear leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its unique habitat and appearance, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
