Polygala sincorensis

Polygala sincorensis, commonly known as the Sincock's milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often hairy stems and clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Polygala sincorensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
