Primula veitchii

Primula veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's primrose, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces clusters of flowers in early spring.

Primula veitchii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
