Piper pubicatulum

Piper pubicatulum, commonly known as the pubescent pepper vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
