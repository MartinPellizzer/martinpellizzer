Psidium solisii

Psidium solisii, commonly known as the Solisii guava, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

The Solisii guava is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
