Prunus serrulata f. taizanfukun

Prunus serrulata f. taizanfukun, commonly known as the Japanese cherry tree, is a botanical variant of the Prunus serrulata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive double flowers, which are typically pink or white and appear in early spring. The plant is native to Japan and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value, particularly in gardens and public spaces. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The foliage turns vibrant red in the autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal throughout the year.
