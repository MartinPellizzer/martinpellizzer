Pyrrhocactus confinis

Pyrrhocactus confinis, commonly known as the Mexican red cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant red to orange-colored stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Pyrrhocactus confinis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
