Persica lancaefolia

Persica lancaefolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf peach.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and appear in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in suitable climates.
