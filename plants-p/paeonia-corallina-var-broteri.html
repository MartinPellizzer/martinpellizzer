Paeonia corallina var. broteri

Paeonia corallina var. broteri, commonly known as the coral peony, is a unique and rare variant of the coral peony species.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central America, particularly in Mexico and Guatemala. This plant is characterized by its striking, coral-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and red. The foliage consists of large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down.

It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
