Pithecellobium langlassei

Pithecellobium langlassei, commonly known as the yellow mombin, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with thorny branches, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
