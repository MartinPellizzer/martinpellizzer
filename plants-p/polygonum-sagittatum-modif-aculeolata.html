Polygonum sagittatum modif. aculeolata

Polygonum sagittatum modif.

aculeolata, commonly known as the arrow-leaved dock, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purple in color.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed soils.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family, known for its unique leaf structures and ecological adaptability.
