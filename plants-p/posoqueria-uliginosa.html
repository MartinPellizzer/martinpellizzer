Posoqueria uliginosa

Posoqueria uliginosa, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive greenish hue at the base. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and marshes.

Posoqueria uliginosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
