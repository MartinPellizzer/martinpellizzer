Polygonatum giganteum var. falcatum

Polygonatum giganteum var. falcatum, commonly known as the Chinese Solomon's Seal, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features long, arching stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers in late spring to early summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
