Platanthera concinna

Platanthera concinna, commonly known as the slender lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, dark green leaves. The plant produces delicate, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a loose spike, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

Platanthera concinna is a perennial herb that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
