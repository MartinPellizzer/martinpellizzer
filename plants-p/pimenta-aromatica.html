Pimenta aromatica

Pimenta aromatica, commonly known as the aromatic pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, peppery fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and fruits, are valued for their aromatic and therapeutic properties.

Pimenta aromatica is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
