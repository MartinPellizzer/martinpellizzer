Pulicaria glaucescens

Pulicaria glaucescens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are covered in a fine, whitish bloom that gives the plant its characteristic blue-gray appearance.

Pulicaria glaucescens is known for its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in Mediterranean climates.
