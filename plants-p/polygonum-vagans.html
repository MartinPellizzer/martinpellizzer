Polygonum vagans

Polygonum vagans, commonly known as wandering beggarticks, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint. Polygonum vagans is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.
