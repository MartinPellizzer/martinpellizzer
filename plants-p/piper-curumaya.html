Piper curumaya

Piper curumaya, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or curumaya pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing a spicy compound similar to capsaicin. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features include compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators.
