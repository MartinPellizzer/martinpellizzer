Philippia trimera subsp. keniensis

Philippia trimera subsp.

keniensis, commonly known as the Kenyan sandmat, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. It is a perennial herb characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
