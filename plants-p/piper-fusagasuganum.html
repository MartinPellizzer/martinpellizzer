Piper fusagasuganum

Piper fusagasuganum, commonly known as the Andean pepper or Escallonia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for both its ecological and cultural significance.
