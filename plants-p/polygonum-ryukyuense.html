Polygonum ryukyuense

Polygonum ryukyuense, commonly known as the Ryukyu knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive bracts and small flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a reddish tint, and it produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers.

Polygonum ryukyuense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
