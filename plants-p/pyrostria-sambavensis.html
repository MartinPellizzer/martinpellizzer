Pyrostria sambavensis

Pyrostria sambavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native habitat, thriving in well-drained soils and partial shade. Pyrostria sambavensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential uses in traditional medicine.
