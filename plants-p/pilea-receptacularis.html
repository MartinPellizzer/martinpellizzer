Pilea receptacularis

Pilea receptacularis, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its rosette of round, fleshy leaves that resemble coins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by a spadix that bears tiny berries. It is a popular houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

Pilea receptacularis thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor environments.
