Pulsatilla germanica

Pulsatilla germanica, commonly known as the German pasqueflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, stout stem with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine meadows.

Pulsatilla germanica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming period.
