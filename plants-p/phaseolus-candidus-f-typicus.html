Phaseolus candidus f. typicus

Phaseolus candidus f. typicus, commonly known as the white bean plant, is a climbing legume native to Central and South America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant bears pods that contain smooth, white to cream-colored seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various agricultural conditions.
