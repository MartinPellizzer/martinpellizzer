Pennisetum scoparium

Pennisetum scoparium, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of purple to pink bracts that resemble small, fluffy plumes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Pennisetum scoparium is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
