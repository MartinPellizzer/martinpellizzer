Polygala corymbosa

Polygala corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a hairy texture, while its stem is often branched and upright.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
