Polygonum filiforme var. smaragdinum

Polygonum filiforme var. smaragdinum, commonly known as the Emerald Threadplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like stems that are often tinged with a vibrant green color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or pink, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a valuable ground cover in natural habitats.
