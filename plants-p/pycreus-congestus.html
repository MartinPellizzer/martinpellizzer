Pycreus congestus

Pycreus congestus, commonly known as the clustered pycreus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

Pycreus congestus is known for its resilience to drought and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
