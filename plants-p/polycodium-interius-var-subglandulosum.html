Polycodium interius var. subglandulosum

Polycodium interius var. subglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular polycodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered with small, glandular hairs, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky outcrops.
