Piper rostratum

Piper rostratum, commonly known as the broadleaf pepper or rostratum pepper, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
