Philadelphus subcanus var. subcanus

Philadelphus subcanus var. subcanus, commonly known as the silver-leaf mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptical, with a silvery-gray underside that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature in temperate climates.
