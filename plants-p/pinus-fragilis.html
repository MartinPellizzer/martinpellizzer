Pinus fragilis

Pinus fragilis, commonly known as the Mexican pine, is a species of conifer native to Mexico.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The tree has slender, needle-like leaves that are usually arranged in bundles of five. Its cones are small and woody, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
