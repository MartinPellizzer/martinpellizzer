Passiflora edulis var. pomifera

Passiflora edulis var. pomifera, commonly known as the sweet passion fruit, is a tropical vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers and produces a fruit with a thick, leathery rind enclosing a juicy, aromatic pulp. The fruit contains numerous seeds and is rich in vitamins, particularly vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The plant is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible fruit.
