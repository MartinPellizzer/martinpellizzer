Polygonella brachystachya

Polygonella brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stemmed bottlebrush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and often edible seeds. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, and its leaves are arranged in a spiraled pattern along the stem. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Polygonella brachystachya is often found in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows.
