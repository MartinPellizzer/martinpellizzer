Platycodon grandiflorus f. monanthus

Platycodon grandiflorus f. monanthus, commonly known as the single-flowered bellflower, is a variant of the Chinese bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It is a perennial herbaceous plant with lance-shaped leaves and a robust root system.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden settings for its visual impact.
