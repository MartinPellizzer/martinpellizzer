Primulina rotundifolia

Primulina rotundifolia, commonly known as the rounded-leaf primula, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
