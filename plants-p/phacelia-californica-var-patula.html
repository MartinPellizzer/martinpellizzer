Phacelia californica var. patula

Phacelia californica var. patula, commonly known as the California phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
