Paramyrciaria delicatula

Paramyrciaria delicatula, commonly known as the delicate mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white, waxy flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant relies on its host for water and nutrients, while it provides nectar for pollinators like bees and birds. Its berries, which are dark red and fleshy, are consumed by various bird species.

This plant is often found in temperate forests and is an important part of the ecosystem's food web.
