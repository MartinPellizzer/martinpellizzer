Pteris dura

Pteris dura, commonly known as the hard fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Pteridaceae and is characterized by its robust, leathery fronds that are typically dark green in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, which are clustered in sori.

Pteris dura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
