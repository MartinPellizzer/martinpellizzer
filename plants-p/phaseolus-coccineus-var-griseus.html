Phaseolus coccineus var. griseus

Phaseolus coccineus var. griseus, commonly known as the purple runner bean, is a variant of the common scarlet runner bean.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and edible pods that turn green as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with compound leaves and is native to the Americas. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
