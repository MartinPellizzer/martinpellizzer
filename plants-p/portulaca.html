Portulaca

Portulaca, commonly known as moss rose, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Portulacaceae.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, yellow, and orange. These plants are native to Africa, Asia, and the Americas, and they thrive in hot, arid environments. Portulaca species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their drought tolerance and ease of care.

They have a short growing season, typically blooming in summer and going dormant in winter.
