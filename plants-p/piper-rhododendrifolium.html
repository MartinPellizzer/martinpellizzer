Piper rhododendrifolium

Piper rhododendrifolium, commonly known as the red-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Piperaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking red foliage, which contrasts with its small, green, oval-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant leaf color and unique appearance.
