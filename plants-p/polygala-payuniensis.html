Polygala payuniensis

Polygala payuniensis, commonly known as the Payun Mountain milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, often pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small and clustered, while its stems are often woody and branched.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
