Phragmipedium wallisii

Phragmipedium wallisii, commonly known as the Wallisii Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, large, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and green. The plant features a pseudobulb and produces aerial roots, which help it absorb moisture from the humid environment. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Phragmipedium wallisii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to cultivated conditions.
