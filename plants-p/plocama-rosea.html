Plocama rosea

Plocama rosea, commonly known as the pink willow, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in riparian zones.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
