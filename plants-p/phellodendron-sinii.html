Phellodendron sinii

Phellodendron sinii, commonly known as the Chinese yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark that exudes a strong, aromatic odor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its medicinal properties due to the presence of berberine in its bark.

Phellodendron sinii is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
