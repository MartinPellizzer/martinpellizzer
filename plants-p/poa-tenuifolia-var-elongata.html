Poa tenuifolia var. elongata

Poa tenuifolia var. elongata, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its fine, elongated leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
