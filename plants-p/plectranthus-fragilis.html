Plectranthus fragilis

Plectranthus fragilis, commonly known as creeping coleus or Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that ranges from purple to red, pink, and white. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal.

Plectranthus fragilis is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which emit a mint-like scent when crushed.
