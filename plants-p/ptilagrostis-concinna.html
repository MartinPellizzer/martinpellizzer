Ptilagrostis concinna

Ptilagrostis concinna, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss.
