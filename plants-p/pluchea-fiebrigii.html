Pluchea fiebrigii

Pluchea fiebrigii, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Pluchea fiebrigii is also valued for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for treating headaches and inflammation.
