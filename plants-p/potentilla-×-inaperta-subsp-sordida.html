Potentilla × inaperta subsp. sordida

Potentilla × inaperta subsp.

sordida, commonly known as the sordid cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States. It features small, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and adaptable plant suitable for xeriscaping and native garden landscapes.
