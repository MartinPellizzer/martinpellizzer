Pilosella caespitosa subsp. colliniformis

Pilosella caespitosa subsp.

colliniformis, commonly known as the hill pilosella, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense, mat-like clusters, often found in grassy areas and rocky soils.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
