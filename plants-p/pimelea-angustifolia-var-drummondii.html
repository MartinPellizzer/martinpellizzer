Pimelea angustifolia var. drummondii

Pimelea angustifolia var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's narrowleaf pimelea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
