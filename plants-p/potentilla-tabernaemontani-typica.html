Potentilla tabernaemontani typica

Potentilla tabernaemontani typica, commonly known as the alpine potentilla, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
