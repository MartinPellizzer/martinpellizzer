Panopsis perijensis

Panopsis perijensis, commonly known as the Perijan palm, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its unique, clustered growth habit and striking, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits containing black seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both researchers and horticulturists.
