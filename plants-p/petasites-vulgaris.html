Petasites vulgaris

Petasites vulgaris, commonly known as the buttercup saxifrage, is a perennial plant native to the alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Petasites vulgaris has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to tolerate cold climates.
