Portulaca caulerpoides

Portulaca caulerpoides, commonly known as the sea purslane, is a halophytic plant adapted to thrive in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other areas with high salinity. Its structure includes erect stems and a sprawling growth habit, allowing it to colonize various wetland habitats.

Portulaca caulerpoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
