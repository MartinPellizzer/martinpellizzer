Proustia cuneifolia subsp. cinerea

Proustia cuneifolia subsp.

cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf proustia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
