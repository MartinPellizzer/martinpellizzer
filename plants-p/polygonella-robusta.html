Polygonella robusta

Polygonella robusta, commonly known as the robust polygonella, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats. The plant is characterized by its upright, grass-like appearance with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically smooth and have a slightly waxy texture, adapting to the harsh alpine environment.

Polygonella robusta plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
