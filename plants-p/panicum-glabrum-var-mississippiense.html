Panicum glabrum var. mississippiense

Panicum glabrum var. mississippiense, commonly known as Mississippi panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces loose, panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-bearing flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
