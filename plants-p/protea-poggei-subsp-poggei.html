Protea poggei subsp. poggei

Protea poggei subsp.

poggei, commonly known as the Pogge's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flower heads.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially sunbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and long-lasting blooms.
