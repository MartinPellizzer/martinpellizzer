Philadelphus macranthus

Philadelphus macranthus, commonly known as the large-flowered mock orange, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and finely toothed. It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and produces small, black berries in the fall.

Philadelphus macranthus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial to full sun.
