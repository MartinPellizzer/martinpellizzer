Paspalum leptachne

Paspalum leptachne, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
