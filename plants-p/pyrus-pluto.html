Pyrus pluto

Pyrus pluto, commonly known as the Pluto pear, is a rare and ancient species of pear native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and fruit that are typically small and somewhat bitter. The plant is notable for its hardy nature, able to thrive in harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are dark green and oval-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge.

Due to its limited distribution and rarity, Pyrus pluto is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
