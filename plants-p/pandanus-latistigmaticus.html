Pandanus latistigmaticus

Pandanus latistigmaticus, commonly known as the broad-stigmatic pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and culinary applications. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense thickets in its native habitat.
