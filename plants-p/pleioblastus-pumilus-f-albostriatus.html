Pleioblastus pumilus f. albostriatus

Pleioblastus pumilus f. albostriatus, commonly known as the white-striped dwarf bamboo, is a compact, clump-forming grass that exhibits striking variegation.

Its foliage features a combination of light green and white streaks, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant is native to East Asia and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.

Due to its slow growth, it is ideal for small garden spaces or container gardening.
