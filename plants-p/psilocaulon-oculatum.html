Psilocaulon oculatum

Psilocaulon oculatum, commonly known as the eye-leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that feature a distinctive white or yellowish spot near the base, resembling an eye. The plant belongs to the Araceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

However, it is important to note that the plant contains calcium oxalate crystals, which can be toxic if ingested.
