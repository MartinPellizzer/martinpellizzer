Piper ramosii

Piper ramosii, commonly known as the creeping pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
