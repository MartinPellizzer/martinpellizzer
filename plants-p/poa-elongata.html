Poa elongata

Poa elongata, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a species of grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, often tufted inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Poa elongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in various ecological settings.
