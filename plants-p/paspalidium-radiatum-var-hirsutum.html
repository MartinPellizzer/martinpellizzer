Paspalidium radiatum var. hirsutum

Paspalidium radiatum var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This grass is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
