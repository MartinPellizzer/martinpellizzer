Plectranthus veronicifolius

Plectranthus veronicifolius, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from purple to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
