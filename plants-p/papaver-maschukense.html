Papaver maschukense

Papaver maschukense, commonly known as the Maschuk poppy, is a rare and striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange petals and a distinctive, elongated seed pod that develops after flowering. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a short, erect stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
