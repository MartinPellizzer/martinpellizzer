Prenanthes nitissima

Prenanthes nitissima, commonly known as the white rayed beggar's ticks, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Prenanthes nitissima is often found in clusters and is known for its distinctive, thread-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.
