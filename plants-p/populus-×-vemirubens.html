Populus × vemirubens

Populus × vemirubens, commonly known as the red-flowered poplar, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Populus balsamifera and Populus nigra.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a serrated margin and turn yellow in the autumn.

Populus × vemirubens is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
