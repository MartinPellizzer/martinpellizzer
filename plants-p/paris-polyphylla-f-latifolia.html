Paris polyphylla f. latifolia

Paris polyphylla f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved white adder's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its striking white flowers that resemble a snake's tongue. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and grows in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions at high altitudes.
