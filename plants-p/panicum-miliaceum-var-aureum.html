Panicum miliaceum var. aureum

Panicum miliaceum var. aureum, commonly known as golden millet, is a variant of the millet species native to warm climates.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, grass-like flowers that are typically green or red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

This plant is often used as a ornamental grass in gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic value and low maintenance requirements.
