Potentilla × diversifolia var. decurrens

Potentilla × diversifolia var. decurrens, commonly known as the spreading potentilla, is a hybrid shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, spreading growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a slightly hairy surface and a serrated margin. This plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms and hardy nature, suitable for USDA hardiness zones 4 to 8.
