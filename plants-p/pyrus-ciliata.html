Pyrus ciliata

Pyrus ciliata, commonly known as the hairy pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that are similar to pears. Pyrus ciliata thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
