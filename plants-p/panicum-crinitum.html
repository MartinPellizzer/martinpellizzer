Panicum crinitum

Panicum crinitum, commonly known as silky grass or silky panicum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetlands and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, hair-like leaves and produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton. Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Panicum crinitum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
