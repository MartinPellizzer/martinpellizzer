Pleurothallis infinita

Pleurothallis infinita, commonly known as the infinite pleurothallis, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

Pleurothallis infinita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
