Piper tarquiense

Piper tarquiense, commonly known as the Tarqui pepper or Andean pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are berries that turn from green to red when ripe, containing several seeds.

Piper tarquiense is valued both for its ornamental qualities and for its potential use in traditional medicine.
