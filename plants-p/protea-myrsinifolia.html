Protea myrsinifolia

Protea myrsinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Protea myrsinifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
