Potentilla coriandrifolia var. coriandrifolia

Potentilla coriandrifolia var. coriandrifolia, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, spreading stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
