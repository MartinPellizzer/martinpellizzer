Phacelia ramosissima var. eremophila

Phacelia ramosissima var. eremophila, commonly known as the desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects in arid landscapes.
