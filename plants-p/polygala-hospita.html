Polygala hospita

Polygala hospita, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Polygala hospita is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
