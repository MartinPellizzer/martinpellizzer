Penstemon laevigatus subsp. calycosus

Penstemon laevigatus subsp.

calycosus, commonly known as the Sierra penstemon, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to blue.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
