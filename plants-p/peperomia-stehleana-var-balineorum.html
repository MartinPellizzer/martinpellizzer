Peperomia stehleana var. balineorum

Peperomia stehleana var. balineorum, commonly known as the Balinese Peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are usually dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which are typically borne on short, erect spikes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.
