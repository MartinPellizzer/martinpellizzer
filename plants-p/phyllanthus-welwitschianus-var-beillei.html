Phyllanthus welwitschianus var. beillei

Phyllanthus welwitschianus var. beillei, commonly known as the African date palm, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its hardiness, it is also used in reforestation projects in arid regions.
