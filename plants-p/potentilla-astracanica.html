Potentilla astracanica

Potentilla astracanica, commonly known as the Astrakhan cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Potentilla astracanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
