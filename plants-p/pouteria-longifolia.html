Pouteria longifolia

Pouteria longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf pouteria, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elongated leaves and smooth, often aromatic bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that turn red when mature, containing a single large seed.
