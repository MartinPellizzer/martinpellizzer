Polygala echinosperma

Polygala echinosperma, commonly known as the spiny-fruit milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, milky substance. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
