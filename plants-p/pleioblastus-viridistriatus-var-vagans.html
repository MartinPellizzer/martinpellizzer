Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. vagans

Pleioblastus viridistriatus var. vagans, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a variant of the green bamboo species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are covered in a dense, silvery-white sheath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue that enhances its ornamental value. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is widely cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.
