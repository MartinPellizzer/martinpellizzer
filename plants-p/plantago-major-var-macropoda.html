Plantago major var. macropoda

Plantago major var. macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its larger, broader leaves.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as lawns, roadsides, and gardens. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a prominent midrib and a hairy underside, while the flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes. This plant is known for its ability to tolerate both full sun and partial shade, making it a resilient species in various environments.

Its botanical features make it easily identifiable and useful in ecological studies and herbal medicine.
