Polygonum blancheanum

Polygonum blancheanum, commonly known as the Blancheanum knotweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories or streambanks. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, and the leaves are covered in a fine, whitish pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
