Panax digitatus

Panax digitatus, commonly known as the Chinese ginseng, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish root system and its compound leaves with multiple leaflets. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, typically appearing in clusters.

Panax digitatus is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
