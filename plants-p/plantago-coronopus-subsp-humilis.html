Plantago coronopus subsp. humilis

Plantago coronopus subsp.

humilis, commonly known as lesser plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It features a basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadside verges, and wetlands.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
