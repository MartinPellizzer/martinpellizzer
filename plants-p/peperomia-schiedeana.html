Peperomia schiedeana

Peperomia schiedeana, commonly known as the Schiede's peperomia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptic in shape, with a deep green color and sometimes a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or reddish, emerging from the leaf axils. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Peperomia schiedeana is relatively low maintenance, requiring minimal watering and moderate humidity to flourish.
