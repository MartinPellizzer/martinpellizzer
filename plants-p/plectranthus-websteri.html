Plectranthus websteri

Plectranthus websteri, commonly known as the silver bush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, velvety silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a drought-tolerant, decorative plant.
