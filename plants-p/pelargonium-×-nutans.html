Pelargonium × nutans

Pelargonium × nutans, commonly known as the hanging geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features velvety, lobed leaves that are often variegated with contrasting colors. Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
