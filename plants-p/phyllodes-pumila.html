Phyllodes pumila

Phyllodes pumila, commonly known as the silky wattle, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the genus *Acacia* and is characterized by its narrow, feathery phyllodes that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and thrives in coastal or inland areas. Its flowers are usually yellow and appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Phyllodes pumila is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
