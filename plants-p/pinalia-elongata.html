Pinalia elongata

Pinalia elongata, commonly known as the slender pinalia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It features slender, elongated stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height, often branching at the upper portion. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pinalia elongata thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
