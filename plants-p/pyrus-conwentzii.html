Pyrus conwentzii

Pyrus conwentzii, commonly known as the Mexican white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Pyrus conwentzii is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas.
