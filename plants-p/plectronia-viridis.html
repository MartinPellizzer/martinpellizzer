Plectronia viridis

Plectronia viridis, commonly known as the green plectron, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Plectronia viridis thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
