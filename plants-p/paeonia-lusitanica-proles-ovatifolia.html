Paeonia lusitanica proles ovatifolia

Paeonia lusitanica proles ovatifolia, commonly known as the ovate-leaved peony, is a rare and ornamental species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant features ovate, serrated leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is often sought after by horticulturists and botanists alike.
