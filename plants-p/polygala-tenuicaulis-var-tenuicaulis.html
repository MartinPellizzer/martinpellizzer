Polygala tenuicaulis var. tenuicaulis

Polygala tenuicaulis var. tenuicaulis, commonly known as the slender spurge-plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features delicate, thread-like leaves and produces small, spurge-like flowers in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure.
