Phlox longifolia f. brevifolia

Phlox longifolia f. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-stemmed phlox, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a compact, herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a dense, upright growth habit.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun conditions.
