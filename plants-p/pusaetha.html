Pusaetha

Pusaetha, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, green, berry-like fruits that turn golden when ripe. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine for centuries.

Pusaetha thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal value.
