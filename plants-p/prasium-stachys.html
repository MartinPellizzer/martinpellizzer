Prasium stachys

Prasium stachys, commonly known as redstem filaree, is a flowering plant belonging to the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its slender, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Prasium stachys is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
