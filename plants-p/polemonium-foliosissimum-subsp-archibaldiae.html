Polemonium foliosissimum subsp. archibaldiae

Polemonium foliosissimum subsp.

archibaldiae, commonly known as Archibald's Jacob's ladder, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It belongs to the Polemoniaceae family and is characterized by its cascading clusters of blue to violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
