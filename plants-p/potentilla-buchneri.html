Potentilla buchneri

Potentilla buchneri, commonly known as the Buchner's cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its low-growing habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are typically basal, arranged in a rosette, and are covered with fine, soft hairs.

Potentilla buchneri is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments.
