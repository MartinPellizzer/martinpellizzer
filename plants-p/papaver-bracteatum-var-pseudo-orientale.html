Papaver bracteatum var. pseudo-orientale

Papaver bracteatum var. pseudo-orientale, commonly known as the Persian poppy, is a striking plant characterized by its vibrant red to orange flowers with a distinctive black center.

It is a herbaceous perennial native to regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant features finely cut, fern-like leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its showy appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.
