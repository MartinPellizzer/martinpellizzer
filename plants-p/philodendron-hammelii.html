Philodendron hammelii

Philodendron hammelii, commonly known as the hammel's philodendron, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
