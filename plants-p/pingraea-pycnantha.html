Pingraea pycnantha

Pingraea pycnantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed acacia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in compact clusters, which are followed by woody, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for soil stabilization.
