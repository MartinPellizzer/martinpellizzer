Piper caninum

Piper caninum, commonly known as the wild pepper or Indian pepper, is a tropical climbing shrub native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, vine-like stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into three-lobed, berry-like fruits. The fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
