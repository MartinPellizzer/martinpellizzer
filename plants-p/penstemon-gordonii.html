Penstemon gordonii

Penstemon gordonii, commonly known as Gordon's beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed.

Penstemon gordonii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
