Psorothamnus dentatus

Psorothamnus dentatus, commonly known as the toothbrush shrub, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties, allowing the plant to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant features small, compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and adaptability to poor soil conditions.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of its native desert ecosystems.
