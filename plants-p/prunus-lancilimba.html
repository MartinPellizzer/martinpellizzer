Prunus lancilimba

Prunus lancilimba, commonly known as the slender cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically lance-shaped, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Prunus lancilimba is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
