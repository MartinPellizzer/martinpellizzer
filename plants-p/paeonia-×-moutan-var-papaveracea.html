Paeonia × moutan var. papaveracea

Paeonia × moutan var. papaveracea, commonly known as the Japanese peony, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both the tree peony and the herbaceous peony.

It is native to East Asia and features showy, single or semi-double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has upright, branched stems covered with palmate leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and foliage.
