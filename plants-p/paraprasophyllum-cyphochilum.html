Paraprasophyllum cyphochilum

Paraprasophyllum cyphochilum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed paraprasophyllum, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
