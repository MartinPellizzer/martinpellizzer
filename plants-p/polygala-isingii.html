Polygala isingii

Polygala isingii, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spicate inflorescences and small, often violet or white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Polygala isingii is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
