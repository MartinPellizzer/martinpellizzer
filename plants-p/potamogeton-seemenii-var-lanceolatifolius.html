Potamogeton seemenii var. lanceolatifolius

Potamogeton seemenii var. lanceolatifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Asia.

It has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by cylindrical, spongy fruits. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
