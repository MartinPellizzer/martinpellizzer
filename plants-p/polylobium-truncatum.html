Polylobium truncatum

Polylobium truncatum, commonly known as the blunt-lobed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its distinctive fronds.

It features a compact rosette of leaves that are divided into broad, truncate lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are borne on short, erect stipes, and it produces spores in clusters.

The botanical features of Polylobium truncatum make it a valuable subject for studies in plant morphology and ecology.
