Peliostomum origanoides

Peliostomum origanoides, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland gardens.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
