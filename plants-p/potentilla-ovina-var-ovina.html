Potentilla ovina var. ovina

Potentilla ovina var. ovina, commonly known as silver cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
