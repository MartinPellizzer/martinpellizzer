Penstemon campanulatus

Penstemon campanulatus, commonly known as the bell penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Penstemon campanulatus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
