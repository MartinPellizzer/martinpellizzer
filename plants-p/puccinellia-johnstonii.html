Puccinellia johnstonii

Puccinellia johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in low-nutrient environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
