Passiflora cinerea

Passiflora cinerea, commonly known as the silver passionflower, is a climbing vine native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and distinctive, showy flowers that feature a unique arrangement of petals, sepals, and bracts. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Passiflora cinerea is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
