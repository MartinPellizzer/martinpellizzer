Poa pratensis subsp. angustiglumis

Poa pratensis subsp.

angustiglumis, commonly known as narrow-leaved meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit.

The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed florets. This subspecies is typically found in moist, nutrient-rich soils in meadows and along streambanks.

It plays an important role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
