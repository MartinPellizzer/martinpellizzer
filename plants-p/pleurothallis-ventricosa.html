Pleurothallis ventricosa

Pleurothallis ventricosa, commonly known as the "Hollow Orchid," is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, often hollow or chambered flower structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. Its flowers are usually small and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green, which help attract specific pollinators.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
