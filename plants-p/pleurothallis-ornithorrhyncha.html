Pleurothallis ornithorrhyncha

Pleurothallis ornithorrhyncha, commonly known as the bird-beak orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and drawing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique, elongated shape that resembles a bird's beak.

Pleurothallis ornithorrhyncha is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its rarity and distinctive appearance.
