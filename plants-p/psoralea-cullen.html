Psoralea cullen

Psoralea cullen, commonly known as the Cullen's psoralea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant typically produces small, yellow or greenish flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in nitrogen fixation.
