Potamogeton × henryi

Potamogeton × henryi, commonly known as the Henry's pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is typically found in freshwater habitats such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams, where it thrives in muddy or sandy substrates. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are often slightly notched at the edges. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, and it produces small, elongated fruits that are dispersed by water.

Potamogeton × henryi plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for invertebrates and serving as a food source for herbivorous fish and waterfowl.
