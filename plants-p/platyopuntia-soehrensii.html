Platyopuntia soehrensii

Platyopuntia soehrensii, commonly known as the Soehrens' prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, paddle-shaped stems that are covered in spines and are often ribbed. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to survive in harsh desert conditions.
