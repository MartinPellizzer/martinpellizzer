Pectocarya linearis subsp. ferocula

Pectocarya linearis subsp.

ferocula, commonly known as the linear pectocarya, is a species native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its morphology reflects its evolutionary response to drought and extreme temperatures.

It plays a minor role in local ecosystems but is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in arid environments.
