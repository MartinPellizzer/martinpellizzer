Poa nemoralis f. multiflora

Poa nemoralis f. multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered wood sedge, is a variant of the wood sedge species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces numerous small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
