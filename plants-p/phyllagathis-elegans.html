Phyllagathis elegans

Phyllagathis elegans, commonly known as the slender-leaved mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that thrives on the branches of host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers, followed by berries that are often consumed by birds. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its parasitic nature allows it to absorb water and nutrients from its host, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
