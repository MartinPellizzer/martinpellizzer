Polygala vulgaris var. angustifolia

Polygala vulgaris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, angular stems and small, clustered leaves that are often narrow and pointed. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish, arranged in spikelike inflorescences, and are adapted for insect pollination.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
