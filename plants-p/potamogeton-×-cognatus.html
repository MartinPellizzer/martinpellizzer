Potamogeton × cognatus

Potamogeton × cognatus, commonly known as the hybrid water plant, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Potamogeton crispus and Potamogeton praelatus.

It is an aquatic plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers. The plant has long, slender leaves that are often ribbon-like in appearance, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Potamogeton × cognatus is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions and can form dense vegetation in its habitat.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
