Phyllanthus pentaphyllus

Phyllanthus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leaf sandmat, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, sprawling growth habit and small, fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by small, round, pale green fruits that contain a single seed. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Phyllanthus pentaphyllus is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
