Piper rionechianum

Piper rionechianum, commonly known as the Rionecian pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its fruits are berry-like and contain a spicy, peppery flavor, making it valuable in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and unique characteristics, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
