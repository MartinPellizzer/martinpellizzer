Protea pendula

Protea pendula, commonly known as the hanging protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a pendulous, branched pattern. The plant is native to the coastal regions of the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted by their nectar.

Protea pendula is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also appreciated for its ecological significance in supporting local wildlife.
