Poa aegyptiaca

Poa aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green leaves and forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high temperatures and drought conditions.

Poa aegyptiaca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
