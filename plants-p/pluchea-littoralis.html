Pluchea littoralis

Pluchea littoralis, commonly known as coastal groundsel, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, and its leaves are usually alternate and covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to salt spray and tidal conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.

Pluchea littoralis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
