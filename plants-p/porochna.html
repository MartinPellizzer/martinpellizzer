Porochna

Porochna, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

Porochna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
