Poa pratensis var. subcaerulea

Poa pratensis var. subcaerulea, commonly known as blue meadowgrass, is a variant of the common meadowgrass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage, which is due to the presence of a bluish waxy coating on the leaves. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing turf and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

It is also used in some horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities and adaptability.
