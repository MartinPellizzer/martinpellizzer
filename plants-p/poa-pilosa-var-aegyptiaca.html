Poa pilosa var. aegyptiaca

Poa pilosa var. aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a dense, tufted growth habit, and it produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soils in marginal environments.
