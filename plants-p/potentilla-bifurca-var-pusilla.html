Potentilla bifurca var. pusilla

Potentilla bifurca var. pusilla, commonly known as dwarf cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with numerous stems that spread horizontally across the ground. Its leaves are compound, consisting of five oval-shaped leaflets, and are covered in fine, soft hairs.

This hardy species is well adapted to cold, rocky environments and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.
