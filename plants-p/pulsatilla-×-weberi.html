Pulsatilla × weberi

Pulsatilla × weberi, commonly known as the hybrid pasqueflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its showy, early spring blooms that range from white to pink. The plant produces a crown of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, thick rootstock. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific growing conditions to flourish.
