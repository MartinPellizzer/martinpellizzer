Potentilla crinita var. lemmonii

Potentilla crinita var. lemmonii, commonly known as the Lemmon's cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, wiry stems and hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
