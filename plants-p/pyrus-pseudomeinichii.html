Pyrus pseudomeinichii

Pyrus pseudomeinichii, commonly known as the Meineckia pear, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is a deciduous tree belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its small, elongated leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Due to its limited natural range and habitat destruction, it is highly vulnerable to extinction.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
