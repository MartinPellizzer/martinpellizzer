Pleurothallis weddelliana

Pleurothallis weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that often display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and green. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. It has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces fragrant blooms that attract various pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
