Piper pilosiusculum

Piper pilosiusculum, commonly known as the hairy small-leaved pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed berry that contains the aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its unique flavor profile.
