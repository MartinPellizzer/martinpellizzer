Pilosella hoppeana

Pilosella hoppeana, commonly known as the Hoppe's pearly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, which bloom in the summer months. Pilosella hoppeana is known for its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, and is often found in disturbed areas.
