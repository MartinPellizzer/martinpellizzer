Poa schimperiana

Poa schimperiana, commonly known as the Schimper's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and is often found in temperate regions with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually green or purplish in color.

Poa schimperiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
