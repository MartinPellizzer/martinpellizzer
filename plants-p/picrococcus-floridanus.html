Picrococcus floridanus

Picrococcus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida red mold, is a thermophilic fungus that thrives in high-temperature environments.

It is characterized by its ability to produce vivid red or orange spores, which are often found on decaying organic matter. This organism is notable for its role in biodegradation, particularly in the breakdown of complex organic compounds. It is also used in industrial applications, such as in the production of certain enzymes.

Its unique metabolic capabilities make it an important subject in microbiological research.
