Pinanga rumphiana

Pinanga rumphiana, commonly known as the Philippine bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and feathery foliage. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by triangular, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Pinanga rumphiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
