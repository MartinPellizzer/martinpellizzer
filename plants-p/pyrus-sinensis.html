Pyrus sinensis

Pyrus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese apple or sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is typically round, with a thin skin and a sweet, sometimes tart flavor. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
