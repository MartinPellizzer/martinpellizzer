Palicourea laniflora

Palicourea laniflora, commonly known as the woolly palicourea, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens.
