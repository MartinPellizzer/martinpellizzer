Perantha aurantiaca

Perantha aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hair. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Perantha aurantiaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
