Pyrethrum delicatulum

Pyrethrum delicatulum, commonly known as the delicate pyrethrum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant produces a yellowish-pink flower head that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its flowers contain natural pyrethrins, which are used in organic pest control.

Pyrethrum delicatulum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
