Polygala sparsifolia

Polygala sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Polygalaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and sparse, giving it its common name, and it often grows in arid or semi-arid environments.

Polygala sparsifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
