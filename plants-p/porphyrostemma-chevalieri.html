Porphyrostemma chevalieri

Porphyrostemma chevalieri, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, violet to purple flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate with a rough texture, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a source of bioactive compounds, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and studied for its medicinal properties.
