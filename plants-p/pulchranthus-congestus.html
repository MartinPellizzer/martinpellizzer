Pulchranthus congestus

Pulchranthus congestus, commonly known as the clustered beauty, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
