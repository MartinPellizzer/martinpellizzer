Palicourea costanensis

Palicourea costanensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican palicourea, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Palicourea costanensis is native to Central America and is often found in humid, lowland forest environments.
