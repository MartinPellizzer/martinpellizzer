Panicum rhachitrichum

Panicum rhachitrichum, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Panicum rhachitrichum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
