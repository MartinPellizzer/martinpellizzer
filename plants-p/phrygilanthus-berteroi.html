Phrygilanthus berteroi

Phrygilanthus berteroi, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The African flame tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
