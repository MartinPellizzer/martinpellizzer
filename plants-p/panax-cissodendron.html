Panax cissodendron

Panax cissodendron, commonly known as the red ginseng plant, is a rare and endangered species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araliaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots that are highly valued in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and has dark green, glossy leaves with a waxy texture. Its roots are harvested for their purported health benefits, including enhancing energy and immune function.

Due to overharvesting and habitat loss, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
