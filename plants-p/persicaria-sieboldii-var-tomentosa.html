Persicaria sieboldii var. tomentosa

Persicaria sieboldii var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a soft, woolly pubescence. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue when young. Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, appearing in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
