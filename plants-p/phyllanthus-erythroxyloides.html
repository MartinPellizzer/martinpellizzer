Phyllanthus erythroxyloides

Phyllanthus erythroxyloides, commonly known as the redwood phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
