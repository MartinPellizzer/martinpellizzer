Persea petenensis

Persea petenensis, commonly known as the Mexican persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit has a smooth, leathery skin and a sweet, sometimes astringent flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is also valued for its ornamental qualities.
