Potamogeton sinicus

Potamogeton sinicus, commonly known as the Chinese pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Asia.

It has long, slender leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures. It thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.

Potamogeton sinicus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
