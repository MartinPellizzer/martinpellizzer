Polemonium berryi

Polemonium berryi, commonly known as the eastern bluebell, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to blue or purple, depending on the variety. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The plant is part of the Polemoniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
