Pelargonium rosodorum

Pelargonium rosodorum, commonly known as the rose-scented geranium, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a strong, rose-like fragrance when crushed. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to red flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a velvety texture.

Pelargonium rosodorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its pleasant scent and decorative appearance.
