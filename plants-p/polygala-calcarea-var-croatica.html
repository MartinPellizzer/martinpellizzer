Polygala calcarea var. croatica

Polygala calcarea var. croatica, commonly known as the Croatian limestone milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
