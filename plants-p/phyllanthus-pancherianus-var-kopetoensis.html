Phyllanthus pancherianus var. kopetoensis

Phyllanthus pancherianus var. kopetoensis, commonly known as the Kopetdag phyllanthus, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
