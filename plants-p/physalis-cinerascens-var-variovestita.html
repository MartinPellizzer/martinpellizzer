Physalis cinerascens var. variovestita

Physalis cinerascens var. variovestita, commonly known as the variable-husked groundcherry, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It produces small, yellowish-orange fruits enclosed in a papery, inflated calyx. The plant features hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its edible fruits.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to both gardens and ecological studies.
