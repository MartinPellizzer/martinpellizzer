Polygonum nipponense f. albiflorum

Polygonum nipponense f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower Japanese knotweed, is a variant of the species Polygonum nipponense, characterized by its pale to white flowers.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered with a fine layer of hair, while the stems are often reddish in color.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
