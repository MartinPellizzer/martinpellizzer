Phlox × verna

Phlox × verna, commonly known as garden phlox, is a hybrid flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy clusters of pink, purple, or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
