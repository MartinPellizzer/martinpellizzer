Polygonum pedunculare

Polygonum pedunculare, commonly known as the red polygonum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically alternate, heart-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum pedunculare is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
