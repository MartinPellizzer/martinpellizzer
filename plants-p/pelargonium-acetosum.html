Pelargonium acetosum

Pelargonium acetosum, commonly known as the sour geranium, is a species of flowering plant in the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with serrated leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its unique scent and ornamental value make it a valued addition to both ornamental and medicinal horticulture.
