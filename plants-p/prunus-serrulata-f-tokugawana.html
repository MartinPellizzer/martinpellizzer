Prunus serrulata f. tokugawana

Prunus serrulata f. tokugawana, commonly known as the Tokugawa cherry, is a compact, ornamental form of the Japanese cherry.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and profuse display of white flowers in early spring. The plant features small, ovate leaves with finely serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

This variety is particularly prized for its ability to thrive in cooler climates and its historical significance in Japanese culture.
