Penstemon triflorus subsp. typicus

Penstemon triflorus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the three-flowered beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically blooms in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
