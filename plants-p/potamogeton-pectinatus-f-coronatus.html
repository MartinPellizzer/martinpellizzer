Potamogeton pectinatus f. coronatus

Potamogeton pectinatus f. coronatus, commonly known as the crowned pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that are often ribbed and have a smooth surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Its presence is an indicator of good water quality and is important for aquatic ecosystems.
