Pellionia japonica

Pellionia japonica, commonly known as Japanese nettle or velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a soft, velvety texture on the upper surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Pellionia japonica thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
