Pouzolzia neurocarpa

Pouzolzia neurocarpa, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Pouzolzia neurocarpa is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species in challenging conditions.
