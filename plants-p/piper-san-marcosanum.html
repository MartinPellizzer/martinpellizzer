Piper san-marcosanum

Piper san-marcosanum, commonly known as the San Marcos pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a bright red color, often used in local cuisines for their spicy flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance, while its stems are woody and climbing.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
