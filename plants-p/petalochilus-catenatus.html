Petalochilus catenatus

Petalochilus catenatus, commonly known as the chain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in a loose raceme. The flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a contrasting lip, and they emit a faintly fragrant aroma. This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a distinctive species within the Orchidaceae family.
