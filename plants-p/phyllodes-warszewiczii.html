Phyllodes warszewiczii

Phyllodes warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warzewicz's acacia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its long, flattened phyllodes that resemble leaves, which are actually modified stems. These phyllodes are typically green and serve as the primary photosynthetic structures of the plant. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters.

It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.
