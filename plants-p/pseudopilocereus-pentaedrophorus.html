Pseudopilocereus pentaedrophorus

Pseudopilocereus pentaedrophorus, commonly known as the five-edged night bloomer, is a cacti species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
