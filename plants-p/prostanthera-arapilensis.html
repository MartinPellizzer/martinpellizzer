Prostanthera arapilensis

Prostanthera arapilensis, commonly known as the Arapiles mint, is a small shrub native to the southeastern region of Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple in color. Prostanthera arapilensis is valued for its fragrant properties and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in coastal and dry forest habitats.
