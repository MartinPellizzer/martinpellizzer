Persicaria × pseudolapathum

Persicaria × pseudolapathum, commonly known as the hybrid persicaria or false loosestrife, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly known as buckwheats and sorrels. The plant features upright, branched stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive veining pattern. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer to early fall.

Persicaria × pseudolapathum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
