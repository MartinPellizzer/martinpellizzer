Phaca arida

Phaca arida, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Phaca arida is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its deep root system and succulent leaves.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in challenging climates.
