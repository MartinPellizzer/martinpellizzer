Piper cachimboense

Piper cachimboense, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or Cachimbo pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northern South America, particularly in regions of Venezuela, Colombia, and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit, a small, yellowish berry, contains the aromatic compound responsible for its spicy flavor.

Due to its unique chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and ethnobotrists for potential medicinal or culinary uses.
