Podochilus tortus

Podochilus tortus, commonly known as the twisted pod orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes and pseudobulbs that support its structure. Its flowers are usually small, with a unique twisted appearance, and are often borne in clusters.

The leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
