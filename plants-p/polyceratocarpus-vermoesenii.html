Polyceratocarpus vermoesenii

Polyceratocarpus vermoesenii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, shrub-like tree that typically grows in rocky or dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
