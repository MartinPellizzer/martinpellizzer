Polypodium cervicorne

Polypodium cervicorne, commonly known as the horned polypody, is a fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is often covered in a layer of brown, membranous tissue.

Polypodium cervicorne is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
