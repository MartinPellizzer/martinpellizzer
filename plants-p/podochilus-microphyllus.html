Podochilus microphyllus

Podochilus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blooms that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Podochilus microphyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
