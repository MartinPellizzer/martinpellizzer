Panicum adenorhachis

Panicum adenorhachis, commonly known as the golden panicum, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and clusters of golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
