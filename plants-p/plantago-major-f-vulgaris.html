Plantago major f. vulgaris

Plantago major f. vulgaris, commonly known as broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, oval leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, though it has been naturalized in many other regions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in spikes, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in lawns, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature and medicinal properties, it is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
