Pyrus sanguinea

Pyrus sanguinea, commonly known as the red gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, orange, or yellow berries that appear in late summer. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often reddish in color during the growing season. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters before the berries develop.

Pyrus sanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
