Packera bolanderi

Packera bolanderi, commonly known as Bolander's ragwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate to lanceolate in shape, often with hairy surfaces.

Bolander's ragwort produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
