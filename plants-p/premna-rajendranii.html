Premna rajendranii

Premna rajendranii, commonly known as the Indian nettle or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, with opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
