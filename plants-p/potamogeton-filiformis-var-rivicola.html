Potamogeton filiformis var. rivicola

Potamogeton filiformis var. rivicola, commonly known as the slender pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, thin, ribbon-like leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually arranged in clusters near the water surface. It thrives in slow-moving to still waters with soft, muddy or sandy substrates.

This variety is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands, contributing to aquatic habitat structure and water quality.
