Passiflora berteroana

Passiflora berteroana, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate structures, including a crown of filaments and a central corona. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

Passiflora berteroana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
