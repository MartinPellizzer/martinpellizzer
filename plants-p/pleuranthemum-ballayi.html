Pleuranthemum ballayi

Pleuranthemum ballayi, commonly known as the Ballay's ivy, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pleuranthemum ballayi is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and hanging baskets due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
