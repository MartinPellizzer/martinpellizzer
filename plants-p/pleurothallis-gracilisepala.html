Pleurothallis gracilisepala

Pleurothallis gracilisepala, commonly known as the slender-winged orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and pale, often displaying a subtle fragrance.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging species for cultivation.
