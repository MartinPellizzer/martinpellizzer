Paspalum papillosum

Paspalum papillosum, commonly known as the hairy paspalum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Paspalum papillosum is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and disturbed areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
