Pedicularis rubra

Pedicularis rubra, commonly known as the fiery poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and hairy.

Pedicularis rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
