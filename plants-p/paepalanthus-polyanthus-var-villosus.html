Paepalanthus polyanthus var. villosus

Paepalanthus polyanthus var. villosus, commonly known as the silky pincushion cactus, is a unique succulent native to South America.

It features a dense, spherical shape with numerous short, spiny stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its textured foliage. Its compact growth habit makes it well-suited for rock gardens or containers.

The hairy stems provide protection against intense sunlight and help reduce water loss in arid environments.
