Protea nivea

Protea nivea, commonly known as the white protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white, petal-like bracts. The plant typically grows in fynbos vegetation, thriving in nutrient-poor soils and coastal climates. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

Protea nivea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
