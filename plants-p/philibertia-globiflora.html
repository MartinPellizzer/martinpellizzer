Philibertia globiflora

Philibertia globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of broad, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. The plant produces striking clusters of small, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange, typically in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is also valued in traditional medicine and horticulture.
