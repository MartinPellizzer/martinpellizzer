Papaver lisae

Papaver lisae, commonly known as the Lisae poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of silky hairs.

Papaver lisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
