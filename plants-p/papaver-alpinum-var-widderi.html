Papaver alpinum var. widderi

Papaver alpinum var. widderi, commonly known as the Alpine poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange petals and a distinctive, saucer-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, thriving in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardiness.
