Padus serotina var. pendula

Padus serotina var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white crabsapple, is a cultivar of the species Padus serotina.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches that bear clusters of small, white flowers in early spring. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its graceful appearance and seasonal interest.
