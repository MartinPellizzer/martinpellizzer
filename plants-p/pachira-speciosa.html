Pachira speciosa

Pachira speciosa, commonly known as the golden pachira, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by distinctive, spiny fruit capsules. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its ability to adapt to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
