Pyrus raupingensis

Pyrus raupingensis, commonly known as the Raupingensis pear, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to South America, specifically found in parts of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small pear-shaped berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
