Piper schippianum

Piper schippianum, commonly known as the Schippia pepper or Schippianum pepper plant, is a member of the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into peppery fruits. It is often cultivated for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both ecological and horticultural studies.
