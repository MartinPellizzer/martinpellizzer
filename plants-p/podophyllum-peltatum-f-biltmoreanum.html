Podophyllum peltatum f. biltmoreanum

Podophyllum peltatum f. biltmoreanum, commonly known as the Biltmore variety of American bellflower, is a cultivar of the American bellflower plant.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and have a distinctive, slightly wavy margin. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a herbaceous perennial that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
