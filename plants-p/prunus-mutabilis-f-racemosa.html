Prunus mutabilis f. racemosa

Prunus mutabilis f. racemosa, commonly known as the racemosa cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus mutabilis species, which is native to China.

This plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink and then to red as the bloom progresses. The racemosa form is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and denser flower clusters compared to the typical form. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
