Paenula

Paenula, commonly known as the "lily of the valley," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Liliaceae.

It consists of several species, including Paenula alpina and Paenula sibirica, which are native to alpine and subarctic regions. The plants are characterized by their small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. They have a fibrous root system and linear, grass-like leaves.

Paenula species are often found in rocky or open habitats and are valued for their ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
