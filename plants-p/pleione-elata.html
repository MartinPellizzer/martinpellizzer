Pleione elata

Pleione elata, commonly known as the highland pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and small, showy flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in cool, shaded environments with high humidity, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Pleione elata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
