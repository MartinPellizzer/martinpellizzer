Psydrax parviflorus subsp. rubrocostatus

Psydrax parviflorus subsp.

rubrocostatus, commonly known as the red-costated psidium, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The leaves are ovate and have a glossy surface, while the stems are often reddish in color, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
