Polygala nicaeensis var. commutata

Polygala nicaeensis var. commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, bean-like pods that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
