Paspalum paniculatum var. minus

Paspalum paniculatum var. minus, commonly known as dwarf paspalum, is a low-growing grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, panicle-like inflorescences that are often inconspicuous. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover.

Botanically, it is valued for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
