Piper nigrum var. macrostachyum

Piper nigrum var. macrostachyum, commonly known as the large-stalked black pepper plant, is a variant of the black pepper species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, dark green leaves. The plant produces small white flowers that develop into the familiar black peppercorns used in culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its peppercorns, which are harvested and dried to produce the spice used globally.
