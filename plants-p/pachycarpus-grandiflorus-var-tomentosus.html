Pachycarpus grandiflorus var. tomentosus

Pachycarpus grandiflorus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly pachycarpus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
