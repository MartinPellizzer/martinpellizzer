Panicum scandens var. grandiflorum

Panicum scandens var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant panicgrass, is a vigorous perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks that can grow up to 6 feet in height, adorned with long, narrow leaves and large, feathery panicles of pale green to golden flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental use. It is drought-tolerant and adaptable to a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

The flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their delicate appearance and long-lasting nature.
