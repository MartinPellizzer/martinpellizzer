Polygonum subsessile f. typicum

Polygonum subsessile f. typicum, commonly known as the narrowleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescences and swollen nodes. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes and are pollinated primarily by insects.
