Persica vulgaris f. pyramidalis

Persica vulgaris f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the pyramidal peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its upright, tree-like growth habit.

It features a dense, pyramidal shape with closely spaced branches, making it suitable for small spaces or as a decorative plant. The foliage is typically dark green, and the plant produces sweet, juicy fruits similar to common peaches. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring regular watering and pruning to maintain its form.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible yield in suitable climates.
