Pandanus biciliatus

Pandanus biciliatus, commonly known as the double-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands and is characterized by its distinctive, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in two rows. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its roots and leaves are used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.
