Pultenaea gunnii subsp. tuberculata

Pultenaea gunnii subsp.

tuberculata, commonly known as the rough pultenaea, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough, tuberculate (knobbed) texture on the stems and leaves.
