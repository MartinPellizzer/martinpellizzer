Polygonum bargusinense

Polygonum bargusinense, commonly known as the Bargusin smartweed, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of Siberia and northern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as buckwheats and sorrels. The plant is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Polygonum bargusinense is adapted to grow in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tundra and wetland habitats.
