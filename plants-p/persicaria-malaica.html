Persicaria malaica

Persicaria malaica, commonly known as the Malayan knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems, heart-shaped leaves, and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Persicaria malaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
