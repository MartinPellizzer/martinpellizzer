Philodendron petraeum var. triangulare

Philodendron petraeum var. triangulare, commonly known as the Triangular Philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often a vibrant green with a slight variegation. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or shelves.

The varietal name "triangulare" refers to the distinctive triangular shape of its leaves.
