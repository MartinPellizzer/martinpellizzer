Penstemon richardsonii subsp. curtiflorus

Penstemon richardsonii subsp.

curtiflorus, commonly known as the narrow-flower beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
