Pogonia japonica

Pogonia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese bog orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils in forested areas, often in shaded environments. The plant features a slender, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant orchid scent.

Pogonia japonica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
