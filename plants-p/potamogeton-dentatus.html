Potamogeton dentatus

Potamogeton dentatus, commonly known as the toothed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem. The leaves are often serrated or toothed, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
