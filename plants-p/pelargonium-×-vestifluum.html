Pelargonium × vestifluum

Pelargonium × vestifluum, commonly known as the Christmas rose, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. It is native to South Africa and thrives in cool, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
