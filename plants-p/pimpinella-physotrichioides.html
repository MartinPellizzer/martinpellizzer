Pimpinella physotrichioides

Pimpinella physotrichioides, commonly known as the hairy cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a dry, two-lobed schizocarp, typical of the Apiaceae family.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in botanical and pharmacological studies.
