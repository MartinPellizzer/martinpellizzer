Pyrus chihsiniana

Pyrus chihsiniana, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits that ripen in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
