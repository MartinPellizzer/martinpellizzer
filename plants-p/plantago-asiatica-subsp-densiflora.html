Plantago asiatica subsp. densiflora

Plantago asiatica subsp.

densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower plantain, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features a basal rosette of broad, oval-shaped leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
