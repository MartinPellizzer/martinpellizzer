Piper eucalyptolimbum

Piper eucalyptolimbum, commonly known as the eucalyptus pepper vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
