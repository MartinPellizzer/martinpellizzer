Panicum sanguinale var. brevispicatum

Panicum sanguinale var. brevispicatum, commonly known as red panicgrass, is a variant of the panic grass genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, reddish or purplish spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it distinguishable from other panic grass species, particularly in its shorter inflorescences and coloration.
