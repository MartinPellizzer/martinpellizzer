Primula beesiana subsp. leucantha

Primula beesiana subsp.

leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and the plant prefers cool, moist environments. It is often found in woodland or shaded areas, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
