Perilla marathrosma

Perilla marathrosma, commonly known as wild perilla, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, clustered flowers. It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine and cooking, adding both flavor and potential health benefits.
