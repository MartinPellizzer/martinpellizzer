Pleurandra enervia

Pleurandra enervia, commonly known as the white-leaf arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are pale green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source, similar to taro.

Botanically, it belongs to the Araceae family, which includes many ornamental and edible plants.
