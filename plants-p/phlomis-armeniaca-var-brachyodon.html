Phlomis armeniaca var. brachyodon

Phlomis armeniaca var. brachyodon, commonly known as Armenian phlomis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in dense, grayish-blue hairy foliage. Its showy, pink to purple flower clusters bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
