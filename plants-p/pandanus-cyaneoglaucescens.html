Pandanus cyaneoglaucescens

Pandanus cyaneoglaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Polynesia and Micronesia. The plant is characterized by its stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, and it is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative plant.

The species is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
