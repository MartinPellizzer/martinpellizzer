Palaquium ravii

Palaquium ravii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, bright red fruits with a segmented, bumpy exterior. The fruit contains a white, juicy pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
