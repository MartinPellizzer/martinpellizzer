Pleioblastus varius

Pleioblastus varius, commonly known as the variable bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that often exhibit a variety of colors, including green, silver, and white. The plant is a member of the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Pleioblastus varius is also notable for its ability to form dense clumps, making it an effective ground cover in shaded areas.
