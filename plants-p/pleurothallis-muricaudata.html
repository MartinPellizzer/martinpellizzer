Pleurothallis muricaudata

Pleurothallis muricaudata, commonly known as the hairy-tailed orchid, is a species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features intricate, often hairy bracts that give it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and delicate, with a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Pleurothallis muricaudata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
