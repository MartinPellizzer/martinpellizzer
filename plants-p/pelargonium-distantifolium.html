Pelargonium distantifolium

Pelargonium distantifolium, commonly known as the distant-leaved geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, elongated leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
