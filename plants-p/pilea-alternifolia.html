Pilea alternifolia

Pilea alternifolia, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to China and is characterized by its round, coin-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by spathes, which are modified leaves that enclose the true flowers. It is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and attractive foliage.

Pilea alternifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
