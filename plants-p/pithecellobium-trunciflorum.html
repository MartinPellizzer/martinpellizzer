Pithecellobium trunciflorum

Pithecellobium trunciflorum, commonly known as the dwarf poinciana, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, edible pods containing seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
