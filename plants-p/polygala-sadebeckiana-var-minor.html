Polygala sadebeckiana var. minor

Polygala sadebeckiana var. minor, commonly known as the lesser sandwidge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in rocky, coastal habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
