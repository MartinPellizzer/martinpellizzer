Poa macrocalyx var. tatewakiana

Poa macrocalyx var. tatewakiana, commonly known as the Tatewakiana bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
