Persicaria scabra var. amurica

Persicaria scabra var. amurica, commonly known as Amur nettle or Amur hogweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that are covered in fine, stinging hairs, which can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.
