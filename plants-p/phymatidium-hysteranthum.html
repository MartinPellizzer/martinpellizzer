Phymatidium hysteranthum

Phymatidium hysteranthum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, and often bioluminescent flowers that appear at night. The plant has a complex root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme drought conditions. Its flowers emit a faint glow, which is believed to attract specific pollinators.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, it is considered a botanical curiosity and a subject of scientific interest.
