Polygala calcarea subsp. amarella

Polygala calcarea subsp.

amarella, commonly known as the orange-flowered milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as limestone soils.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to orange. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional medicine.
