Penstemon eriantherus var. redactus

Penstemon eriantherus var. redactus, commonly known as the red-hairy beardtongue, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from late spring through summer.
