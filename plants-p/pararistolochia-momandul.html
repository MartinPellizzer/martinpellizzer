Pararistolochia momandul

Pararistolochia momandul, commonly known as the narrow-leafed pipevine, is a climbing vine native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Aristolochiaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It contains toxic compounds, including aristolochic acids, which can be harmful to humans and animals.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated but requires caution due to its poisonous nature.
