Plantago texensis

Plantago texensis, commonly known as Texas plantain, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features a basal rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces spikes of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to withstand drought conditions.
