Papaver rhoeas var. hispidissimum

Papaver rhoeas var. hispidissimum, commonly known as the hispid scarlet poppy, is a variant of the common poppy characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves.

It produces striking, bright red flowers with a prominent central stigma, typical of the Papaveraceae family. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in disturbed soils, often found in fields and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
