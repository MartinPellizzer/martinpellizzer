Pinguicula vulgaris var. bohemica

Pinguicula vulgaris var. bohemica, commonly known as the Bohemian butterwort, is a carnivorous plant native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette of glandular, sticky leaves that trap and digest small insects. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires consistent humidity to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a fascinating subject for both horticultural and scientific study.
