Phryma leptostachya var. humilis

Phryma leptostachya var. humilis, commonly known as the low phryma, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Phrymaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom in late summer.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
