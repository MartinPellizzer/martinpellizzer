Pithecodendron argentinensis

Pithecodendron argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and spadix inflorescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by berries containing seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
