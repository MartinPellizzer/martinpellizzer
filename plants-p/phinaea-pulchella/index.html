Phinaea Pulchella

Phinaea orchid, scientifically known as Phinaea pulchella, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. This exquisite orchid is celebrated for its striking violet and white petals, which resemble delicate lace. Despite its beauty, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes, making it a vulnerable species in the wild. Its unique blooming cycle occurs during the short summer months, adding to its mystique. Conservation efforts are crucial to ensure its survival in its natural habitat.
