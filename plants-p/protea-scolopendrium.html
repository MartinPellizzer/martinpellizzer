Protea scolopendrium

Protea scolopendrium, commonly known as the mountain protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that consist of a central cone-like structure surrounded by colorful bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

Protea scolopendrium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
