Prunus espinozana

Prunus espinozana, commonly known as the Espinoza cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The tree produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in jams. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
