Phaseolus albicarminus

Phaseolus albicarminus, commonly known as the white-seeded bean, is a species of legume native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow seeds and hairy, upright stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and produces small, green, pod-like fruits. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in warm, tropical climates.

The plant is known for its potential nutritional value and is sometimes cultivated for its seeds.
