Paeonia saueri

Paeonia saueri, commonly known as the Caucasian peony, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the芍药科 (Paeoniaceae) family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant features palmately divided, dark green leaves and a short, thick rhizome. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, often growing in mountainous areas.

Paeonia saueri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, colorful blooms.
