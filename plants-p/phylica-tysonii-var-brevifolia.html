Phylica tysonii var. brevifolia

Phylica tysonii var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver bush, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance and texture.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
