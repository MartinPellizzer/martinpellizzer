Pygeum wilsonii var. macrophyllum

Pygeum wilsonii var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf African chinquapin, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically large and have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, spiny capsules containing the seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
