Philodendron riedelianum

Philodendron riedelianum, commonly known as the Riedel's philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Philodendron riedelianum is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
