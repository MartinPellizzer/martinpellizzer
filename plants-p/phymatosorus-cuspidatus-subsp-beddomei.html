Phymatosorus cuspidatus subsp. beddomei

Phymatosorus cuspidatus subsp.

beddomei, commonly known as the Beddome's cliff brake, is a fern native to the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia. It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices.

The plant has a distinctive frond structure with a narrow, elongated shape and serrated margins. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
