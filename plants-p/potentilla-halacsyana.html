Potentilla halacsyana

Potentilla halacsyana, commonly known as the Hungarian cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are compound, with five leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny locations.

Potentilla halacsyana is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and attractive floral display.
