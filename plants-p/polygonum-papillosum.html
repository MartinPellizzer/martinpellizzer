Polygonum papillosum

Polygonum papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf smartweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, slightly hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and along stream banks.

Polygonum papillosum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
