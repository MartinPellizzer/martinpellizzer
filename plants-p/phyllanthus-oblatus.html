Phyllanthus oblatus

Phyllanthus oblatus, commonly known as the African date palm, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth and small, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching structure that supports its dense foliage.

Phyllanthus oblatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties in various cultures.
