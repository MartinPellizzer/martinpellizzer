Pleurothallis muscosa

Pleurothallis muscosa, commonly known as the mossy orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of moss-like growth, contributing to its common name.

Pleurothallis muscosa is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
