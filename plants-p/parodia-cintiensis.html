Parodia cintiensis

Parodia cintiensis, commonly known as the Cintiensis Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 centimeters, with a compact and rounded shape. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Parodia cintiensis is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
