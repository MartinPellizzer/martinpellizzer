Phoradendron coloradense

Phoradendron coloradense, commonly known as the Colorado mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Viscaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds, facilitating its spread to new hosts. Its hemiparasitic nature means it derives some nutrients from its host but can photosynthesize independently.

The Colorado mistletoe plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for birds and insects.
