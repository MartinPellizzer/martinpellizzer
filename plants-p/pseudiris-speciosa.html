Pseudiris speciosa

Pseudiris speciosa, commonly known as the showy iris, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral structure, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic plants.
