Phyllodes mannii

Phyllodes mannii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound, narrow phyllodes that resemble leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in spikes, which bloom in spring and early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates.

The phyllodes are often used in horticulture for their ornamental value and drought tolerance.
