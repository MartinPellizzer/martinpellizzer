Pycnandra gordoniifolia

Pycnandra gordoniifolia, commonly known as the New Caledonian flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a distinctive, rugged trunk with thick, flaky bark, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped and glossy. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
