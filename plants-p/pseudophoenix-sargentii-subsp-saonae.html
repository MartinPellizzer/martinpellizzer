Pseudophoenix sargentii subsp. saonae

Pseudophoenix sargentii subsp.

saonae, commonly known as the Sargent's date palm, is a rare and endemic palm species native to the island of Socotra. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapted to the unique climate of Socotra.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
