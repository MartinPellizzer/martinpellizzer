Poa tenera

Poa tenera, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Poa tenera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
