Philodendron crassum

Philodendron crassum, commonly known as the broad-leaf philodendron, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a thick, woody stem that supports its climbing growth habit. Its leaves are typically dark green with a glossy texture and can vary in color depending on light conditions.

Philodendron crassum thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
