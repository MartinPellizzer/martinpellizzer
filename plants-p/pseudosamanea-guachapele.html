Pseudosamanea guachapele

Pseudosamanea guachapele, commonly known as the Guachapele tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its root system is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, lowland forests.
