Poa vaseyochloa

Poa vaseyochloa, commonly known as the Vasey's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native habitat.
