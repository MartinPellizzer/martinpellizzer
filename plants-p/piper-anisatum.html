Piper anisatum

Piper anisatum, commonly known as the ghost pepper or Indian star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used in culinary applications, particularly in Indian and Southeast Asian cuisines.

The fruit, which resembles a star, is also used in herbal remedies and as a spice.
