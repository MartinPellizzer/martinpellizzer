Potamogeton natans var. fluviatilis

Potamogeton natans var. fluviatilis, commonly known as the river pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it reproduces both sexually and vegetatively. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with muddy or sandy substrates.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
