Passiflora cheiroptera

Passiflora cheiroptera, commonly known as the little passion flower, is a climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that feature a unique blend of red, blue, and white petals, along with prominent filaments and a crown of stamens. The plant produces edible, orange-yellow berries that are rich in nutrients and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Passiflora cheiroptera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators like bats and hummingbirds.
