Prunus griffithii var. amygdaliflora

Prunus griffithii var. amygdaliflora, commonly known as the Chinese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
