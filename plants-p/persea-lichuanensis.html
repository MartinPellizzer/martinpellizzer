Persea lichuanensis

Persea lichuanensis, commonly known as the Lichuan persea, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the provinces of Guangxi and Yunnan, where it grows in humid, subtropical forests. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and have a glossy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in axillary inflorescences, and it produces edible fruits that are similar to avocados in appearance.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential for horticultural use.
