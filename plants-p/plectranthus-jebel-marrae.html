Plectranthus jebel-marrae

Plectranthus jebel-marrae, commonly known as the Jebel Marra plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Sudan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its hairy, square stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or blue in color, arranged in clusters at the leaf axils.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.
