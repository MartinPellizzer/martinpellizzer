Potentilla baileyi var. baileyi

Potentilla baileyi var. baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically hairy and serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
