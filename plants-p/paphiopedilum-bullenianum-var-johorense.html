Paphiopedilum bullenianum var. johorense

Paphiopedilum bullenianum var. johorense, commonly known as the Johor butterfly orchid, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Peninsular Malaysia.

It is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped petals and a distinctive, elongated lip that sets it apart from other orchid varieties. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial orchid, often found in shaded, humid environments with rich, well-draining soil. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and green, are highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts due to their beauty and rarity.

This variety is also noted for its slow growth and specific cultivation requirements, making it a challenging but rewarding species for advanced growers.
