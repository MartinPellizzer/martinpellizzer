Parthenium hispidum var. typicum

Parthenium hispidum var. typicum, commonly known as the hispid rat's-tail, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are hairy and often have a distinctive, rough texture. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
