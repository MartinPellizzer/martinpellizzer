Pyrus nummularia

Pyrus nummularia, commonly known as the Japanese whitebeam, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It features small, rounded leaves that are typically a glossy green on top and grayish-white underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet berries in late summer. It is adapted to temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
