Phaseoloides ferrugineum

Phaseoloides ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty phaseoloid, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features trifoliate leaves with reddish-brown veins and produces small, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several elliptical seeds. It is adapted to grow in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
