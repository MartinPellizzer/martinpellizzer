Populus nigra subsp. caudina

Populus nigra subsp.

caudina, commonly known as the Weeping Black Poplar, is a subspecies of the black poplar native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading branches that create a dramatic, drooping appearance.

The leaves are broadly ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near waterways.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional landscapes.
