Pandorea amboinensis

Pandorea amboinensis, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with elliptical leaflets and produces woody, cylindrical stems that can climb using tendrils. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and green spaces in suitable climates.
