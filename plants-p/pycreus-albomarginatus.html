Pycreus albomarginatus

Pycreus albomarginatus, commonly known as the white-margin orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically have a white margin with a contrasting violet or purple center, making it visually distinctive. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
