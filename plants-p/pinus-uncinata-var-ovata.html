Pinus uncinata var. ovata

Pinus uncinata var. ovata, commonly known as the Spanish stone pine, is a variant of the stone pine native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen conifer characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The cones of this variety are typically small and woody, containing edible seeds known as pignons. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional landscapes.
