Persicaria minor subsp. decipiens

Persicaria minor subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the false knotweed, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive leaf shapes and flowering structures.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and its ornamental value in garden settings.
