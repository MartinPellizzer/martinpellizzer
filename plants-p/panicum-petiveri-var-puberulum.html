Panicum petiveri var. puberulum

Panicum petiveri var. puberulum, commonly known as the silky foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and inflorescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle, with slender, bract-like structures.

This variant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
