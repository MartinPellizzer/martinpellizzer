Primula viscosa f. cynoglossifolia

Primula viscosa f. cynoglossifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed primrose, is a botanical variant of the common primrose, characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
