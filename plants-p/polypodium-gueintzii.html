Polypodium gueintzii

Polypodium gueintzii, commonly known as the Gueintz's shield fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its shield-shaped fronds that are typically dark green and leathery in texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, and it relies on spore dispersal for propagation.

Polypodium gueintzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
