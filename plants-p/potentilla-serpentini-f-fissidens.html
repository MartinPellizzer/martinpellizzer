Potentilla serpentini f. fissidens

Potentilla serpentini f. fissidens, commonly known as the fissured potentilla, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to specific regions in the mountainous areas of Europe. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a unique appearance. Its flowers are typically small, yellowish, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in challenging environments.
