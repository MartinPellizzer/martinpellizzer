Paphiopedilum rothschildianum var. elliottianum

Paphiopedilum rothschildianum var. elliottianum, commonly known as the Elliott's lady's slipper, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper, a feature typical of the orchid family. The plant produces large, showy flowers with a mix of pink, white, and yellow hues, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles.
