Pilosella iserana subsp. subfasciculata

Pilosella iserana subsp.

subfasciculata, commonly known as the Alpine pearly everlasting, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long-lasting blooms.
