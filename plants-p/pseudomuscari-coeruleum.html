Pseudomuscari coeruleum

Pseudomuscari coeruleum, commonly known as the blue muscari, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
