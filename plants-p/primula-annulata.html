Primula annulata

Primula annulata, commonly known as the ringed primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that often display a striking contrast between the inner and outer petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are usually oval and hairy, providing a textured appearance.

Primula annulata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
