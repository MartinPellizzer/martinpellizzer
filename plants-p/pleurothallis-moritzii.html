Pleurothallis moritzii

Pleurothallis moritzii, commonly known as the Moritz's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with intricate patterns on the petals. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Pleurothallis moritzii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
