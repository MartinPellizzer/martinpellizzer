Plocama calabrica var. alba

Plocama calabrica var. alba, commonly known as white plocama, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
