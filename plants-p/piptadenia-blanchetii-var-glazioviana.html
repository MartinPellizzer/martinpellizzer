Piptadenia blanchetii var. glazioviana

Piptadenia blanchetii var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
