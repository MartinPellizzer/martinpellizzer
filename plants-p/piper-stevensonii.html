Piper stevensonii

Piper stevensonii, commonly known as Stevenson's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
