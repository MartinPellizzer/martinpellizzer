Pterophyllus salisburiensis aurea

Pterophyllus salisburiensis aurea, commonly known as the golden Salisbury's pterophyllus, is a rare and ornamental plant characterized by its striking golden foliage.

It belongs to the family Pterophyllaceae and is native to a specific region in South America. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy, vibrant yellow hue that intensifies in sunlight. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
