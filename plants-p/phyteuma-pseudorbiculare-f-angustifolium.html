Phyteuma pseudorbiculare f. angustifolium

Phyteuma pseudorbiculare f. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved physa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive bilabiate structure. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Campanulaceae family.
