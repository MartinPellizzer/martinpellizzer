Prunus jamasakura f. diversipetala

Prunus jamasakura f. diversipetala, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom variant with diverse petals, is a cultivar of the Prunus jamasakura species.

It is characterized by its unique floral morphology, featuring petals that vary in color and shape, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in early spring, producing a striking display of white, pink, and red hues.

The leaves are ovate with a smooth margin, and the plant is well adapted to temperate climates.
