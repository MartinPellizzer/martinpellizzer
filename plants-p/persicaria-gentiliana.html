Persicaria gentiliana

Persicaria gentiliana, commonly known as white knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Persicaria gentiliana is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along stream banks.
