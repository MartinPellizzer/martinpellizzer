Prunus laurifolia

Prunus laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel cherry or sweet laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that ripens in the fall.

Prunus laurifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
