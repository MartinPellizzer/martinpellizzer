Polypodium melanotrichium

Polypodium melanotrichium, commonly known as the black-haired fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with dark, wiry fronds that have a distinctive blackish hue on the underside. Its sporangia are clustered in sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
