Piper macapaense

Piper macapaense, commonly known as the Macapa pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature.

The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
