Pachystigma stenophylla

Pachystigma stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pachystigma, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
