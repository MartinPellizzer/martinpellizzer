Piper hahnii

Piper hahnii, commonly known as the Java pepper or hahnii pepper plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes various pepper species. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, peppercorn-like fruits that are typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Piper hahnii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
