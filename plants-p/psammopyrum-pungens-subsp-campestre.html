Psammopyrum pungens subsp. campestre

Psammopyrum pungens subsp.

campestre, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
