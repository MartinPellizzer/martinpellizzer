Penstemon jamesii subsp. ophianthus

Penstemon jamesii subsp.

ophianthus, commonly known as the glacier penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, thriving in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to cold climates and is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
