Phoradendron schottii

Phoradendron schottii, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on various host trees in the tropical regions of South America.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb water and nutrients. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are important food sources for birds and other animals. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and often have a silvery or grayish appearance due to dense pubescence.

Phoradendron schottii plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by influencing host tree growth and supporting local wildlife.
