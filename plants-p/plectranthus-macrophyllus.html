Plectranthus macrophyllus

Plectranthus macrophyllus, commonly known as Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, slightly minty scent and its clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has broad, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Plectranthus macrophyllus is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and analgesic effects.
