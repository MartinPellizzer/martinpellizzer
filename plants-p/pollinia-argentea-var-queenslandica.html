Pollinia argentea var. queenslandica

Pollinia argentea var. queenslandica, commonly known as the Queensland silver pollinia, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-white petals and a unique structure of pollinia, which are tightly clustered masses of pollen. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments within rainforests, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
