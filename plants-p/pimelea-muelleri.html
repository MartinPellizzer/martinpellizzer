Pimelea muelleri

Pimelea muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in heathlands and rocky soils. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value.

Pimelea muelleri is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
