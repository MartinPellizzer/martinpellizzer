Peruvocereus albicephalus

Peruvocereus albicephalus, commonly known as the white-headed cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
