Phaseolus saccharatus

Phaseolus saccharatus, commonly known as the sweet bean or sugar bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its climbing habit, with tendrils that help it ascend supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The species is valued for its nutritional content, particularly its high carbohydrate and protein levels, making it an important crop in some regions.
