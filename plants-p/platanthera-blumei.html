Platanthera blumei

Platanthera blumei, commonly known as the Blume's lady's tresses, is a rare and delicate orchid native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a dense, fragrant inflorescence of small, white to pale pink flowers that resemble a tresses of hair. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often growing in shaded, rocky or forested areas. It has a tuberous root system and is pollinated primarily by specific species of bees.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest in botanical conservation efforts.
