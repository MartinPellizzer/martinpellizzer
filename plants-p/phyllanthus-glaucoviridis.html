Phyllanthus glaucoviridis

Phyllanthus glaucoviridis, commonly known as the green-leaf phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a waxy, greenish coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by greenish, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodlands or rocky areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
