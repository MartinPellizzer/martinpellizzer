Prunus taplejungnica

Prunus taplejungnica, commonly known as the Nepal plum.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India. The plant produces small, sweet, yellow or red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in the region.
