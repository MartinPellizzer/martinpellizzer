Phyllanthus barbadensis

Phyllanthus barbadensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Caribbean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red or orange aril, which is a distinctive feature of the species.

Phyllanthus barbadensis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
