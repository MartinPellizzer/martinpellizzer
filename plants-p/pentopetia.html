Pentopetia

Pentopetia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit, which resembles a small fig, is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor.

Pentopetia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to rocky or arid environments.
