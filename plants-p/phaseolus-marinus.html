Phaseolus marinus

Phaseolus marinus, commonly known as the sea bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments, making it a hardy species. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers.

Phaseolus marinus is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds and has historical significance in traditional medicine.
