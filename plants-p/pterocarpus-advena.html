Pterocarpus advena

Pterocarpus advena, commonly known as the Indian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.
