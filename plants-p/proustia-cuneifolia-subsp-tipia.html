Proustia cuneifolia subsp. tipia

Proustia cuneifolia subsp.

tipia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved proustia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
