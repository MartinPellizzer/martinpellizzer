Pyrus pyraster f. longicaulis

Pyrus pyraster f. longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed crabapple, is a cultivar of the common crabapple tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color. This variety produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible but often tart.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and historical significance.
