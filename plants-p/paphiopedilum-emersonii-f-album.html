Paphiopedilum emersonii f. album

Paphiopedilum emersonii f. album, commonly known as the White Emerson's Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high altitudes of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with intricate patterns and a unique lip structure that distinguishes it from other orchid species. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid, thriving in cool, shaded environments with high humidity. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to retain moisture.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and is often cultivated in specialized greenhouses.
