Pelargonium × dependens

Pelargonium × dependens, commonly known as the trailing geranium, is a hybrid species within the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant features velvety, lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Pelargonium × dependens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
