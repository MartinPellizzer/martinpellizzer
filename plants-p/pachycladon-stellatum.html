Pachycladon stellatum

Pachycladon stellatum, commonly known as the star-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped leaves, which are arranged in a compact, cushion-like form. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Pachycladon stellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
