Pouteria longipes

Pouteria longipes, commonly known as the longipes sapote, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated leaves and produces yellow or orange fruits that are edible. The plant belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses. Its flowers are small and typically green or yellow, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
