Phenax sonneratii var. angustifolius

Phenax sonneratii var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf phenax, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow or white, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
