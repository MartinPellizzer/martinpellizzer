Persepolium salicornioides

Persepolium salicornioides, commonly known as the Persian glasswort, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline and coastal environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, and its structure allows it to tolerate high salt concentrations. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellow, and it plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.

Persepolium salicornioides is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
