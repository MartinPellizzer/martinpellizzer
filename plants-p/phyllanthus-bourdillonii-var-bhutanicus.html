Phyllanthus bourdillonii var. bhutanicus

Phyllanthus bourdillonii var. bhutanicus, commonly known as the Bhutanese spurge, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
