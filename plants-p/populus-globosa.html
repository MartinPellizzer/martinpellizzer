Populus globosa

Populus globosa, commonly known as the globe poplar, is a deciduous tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical-shaped fruits, which are covered in fine, silky hairs. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and a bluish-green underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
