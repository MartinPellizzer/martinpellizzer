Piper gamblei

Piper gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier pepper or Gambier vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy surface.

The small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in spikes, and the plant produces berries that are typically used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
