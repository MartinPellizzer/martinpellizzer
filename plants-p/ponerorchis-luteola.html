Ponerorchis luteola

Ponerorchis luteola, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ponerorchis luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
