Pseudoeriocoma acuta

Pseudoeriocoma acuta, commonly known as the sharp-tipped ericoid, is a shrub native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive, pointed shape, which helps it conserve water in arid environments.

Pseudoeriocoma acuta is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and mountainous regions.
