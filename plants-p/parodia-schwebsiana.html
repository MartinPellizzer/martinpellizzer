Parodia schwebsiana

Parodia schwebsiana, commonly known as the Schwebes' cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is often covered in white or pale-colored spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
