Phaeiris hexagona

Phaeiris hexagona, commonly known as the hexagon leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hexagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in spikes, and can be white, yellow, or greenish in color.

Phaeiris hexagona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
