Polygala goetzei

Polygala goetzei, commonly known as the Goetz's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that resembles a small, twisted horn.

Polygala goetzei is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
