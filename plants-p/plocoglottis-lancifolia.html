Plocoglottis lancifolia

Plocoglottis lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved plocoglottis, is a rare and striking member of the fern family.

It is characterized by its long, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply divided, giving it a delicate, lacy appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique morphology makes it a sought-after species among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
