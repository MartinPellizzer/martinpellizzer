Panicum capillare var. minus

Panicum capillare var. minus, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes. The plant typically thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas. Its botanical features include slender stems and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in coastal or sandy environments.
