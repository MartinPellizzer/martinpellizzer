Primula carnea

Primula carnea, commonly known as the common cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features fleshy, hairy stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming from late winter to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in natural habitats.
