Polygonum hesperium

Polygonum hesperium, commonly known as the desert knotweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and often reddish stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface.

Polygonum hesperium is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.
