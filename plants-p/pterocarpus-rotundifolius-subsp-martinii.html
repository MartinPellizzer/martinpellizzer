Pterocarpus rotundifolius subsp. martinii

Pterocarpus rotundifolius subsp.

martinii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Western Ghats of India. It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.

The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small and yellowish-green. It produces woody pods that contain flat, dark seeds.

This species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
