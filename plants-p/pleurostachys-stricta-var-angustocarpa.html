Pleurostachys stricta var. angustocarpa

Pleurostachys stricta var. angustocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited stiff grass, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its upright, slender growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a rough, textured surface.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.
