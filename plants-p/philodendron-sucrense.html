Philodendron sucrense

Philodendron sucrense, commonly known as the Suyari Philodendron, is a striking tropical climbing plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a deep green color and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by the development of red, fleshy fruits. Philodendron sucrense thrives in high humidity and warm temperatures, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a valuable addition to botanical collections and green spaces.
