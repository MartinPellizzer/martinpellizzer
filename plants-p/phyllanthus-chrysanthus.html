Phyllanthus chrysanthus

Phyllanthus chrysanthus, commonly known as golden phyllanthus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry to moderately moist conditions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
