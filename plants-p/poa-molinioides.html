Poa molinioides

Poa molinioides, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-ranked spikelets.

Poa molinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
