Pedilochilus montanus

Pedilochilus montanus, commonly known as the mountain pedilochilus, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool and moist conditions. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a few leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the thin soil of its mountainous habitat.

Pedilochilus montanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
