Primula cusickiana var. nevadensis

Primula cusickiana var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on erect stems. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in meadows and along streambanks at high elevations. Its leaves are broadly ovate with a hairy texture, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
