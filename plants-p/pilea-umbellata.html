Pilea umbellata

Pilea umbellata, commonly known as the Chinese money plant, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular houseplant.

Pilea umbellata is known for its ability to propagate through leaf cuttings, which is a key feature in its cultivation.
