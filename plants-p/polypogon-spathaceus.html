Polypogon spathaceus

Polypogon spathaceus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spathegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged on slender stalks, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Polypogon spathaceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
