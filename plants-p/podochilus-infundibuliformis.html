Podochilus infundibuliformis

Podochilus infundibuliformis, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, hood-like floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
