Prinodia bumelioides

Prinodia bumelioides, commonly known as the eastern prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Prinodia bumelioides plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various animals.
