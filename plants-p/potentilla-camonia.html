Potentilla camonia

Potentilla camonia, commonly known as the yellow cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features upright stems covered with fine hairs and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Potentilla camonia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
