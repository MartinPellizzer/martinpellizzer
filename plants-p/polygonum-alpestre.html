Polygonum alpestre

Polygonum alpestre, commonly known as alpine knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive nodes and bracteate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are accompanied by leaf-like bracts.

Polygonum alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
