Pteronia erythrochaeta

Pteronia erythrochaeta, commonly known as the red-stemmed pteronia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often reddish in color, giving it its common name.

Pteronia erythrochaeta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
