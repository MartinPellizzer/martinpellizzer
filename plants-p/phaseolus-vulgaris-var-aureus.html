Phaseolus vulgaris var. aureus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. aureus, commonly known as the golden bean or yellow kidney bean, is a variant of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its bright yellow pods and seeds, which are distinct from the typical green or red varieties. The plant is a climbing legume with compound leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some culinary applications.
