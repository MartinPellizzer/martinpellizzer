Persica vulgaris var. atropurpurea

Persica vulgaris var. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf peach, is a cultivar of the peach tree characterized by its striking purple foliage.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value, with leaves that turn deep purple in the spring and summer. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding to its visual appeal. It produces small, sweet fruits similar to those of common peaches, though the fruit may be less abundant than in other varieties.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
