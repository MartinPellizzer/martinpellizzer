Pycnocycla glauca

Pycnocycla glauca, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its striking blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Pycnocycla glauca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
