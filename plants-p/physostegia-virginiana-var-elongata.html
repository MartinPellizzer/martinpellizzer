Physostegia virginiana var. elongata

Physostegia virginiana var. elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed spikeflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, two-lipped flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.
