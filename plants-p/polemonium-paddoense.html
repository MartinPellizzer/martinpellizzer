Polemonium paddoense

Polemonium paddoense, commonly known as the New Zealand bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream sides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
