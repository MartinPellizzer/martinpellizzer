Pedicularis cernua

Pedicularis cernua, commonly known as the downy monkeyflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its drooping, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae and is known for its attractive, delicate appearance.

Pedicularis cernua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits to pollinators.
