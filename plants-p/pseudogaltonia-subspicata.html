Pseudogaltonia subspicata

Pseudogaltonia subspicata, commonly known as the Natal onion or Natal lily, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded bulb with a fibrous root system and produces grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant blooms with clusters of white or pale pink, bell-shaped flowers that are often fragrant. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Pseudogaltonia subspicata is a hardy, drought-tolerant species often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
