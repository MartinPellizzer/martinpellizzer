Pinus atrovirens

Pinus atrovirens, commonly known as the dark-green pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically 2 to 4 centimeters long. The tree produces cones that are typically 5 to 8 centimeters in length, containing edible seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in forested areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological and ornamental purposes.
