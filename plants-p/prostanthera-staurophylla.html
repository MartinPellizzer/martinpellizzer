Prostanthera staurophylla

Prostanthera staurophylla, commonly known as the star-flowered mintbush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stems, giving it its common name. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a strong mint-like fragrance.

Prostanthera staurophylla is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and semi-arid environments.
