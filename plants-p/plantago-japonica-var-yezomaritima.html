Plantago japonica var. yezomaritima

Plantago japonica var. yezomaritima, commonly known as Yezo plantain, is a variant of the common plantain species native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
