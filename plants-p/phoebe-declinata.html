Phoebe declinata

Phoebe declinata, commonly known as the rock phoebe, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas or on limestone outcrops. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
