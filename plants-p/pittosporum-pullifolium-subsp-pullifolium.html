Pittosporum pullifolium subsp. pullifolium

Pittosporum pullifolium subsp.

pullifolium, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is typically dark green and leathery.

The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native habitats.
