Papaver armeniacum var. fugax

Papaver armeniacum var. fugax, commonly known as Armenian poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep crimson, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal medicine.
