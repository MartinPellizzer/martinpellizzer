Polypodium persicariifolium

Polypodium persicariifolium, commonly known as the Persian shield fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its dense, overlapping fronds that form a compact rosette. The leaves are typically dark green with a velvety texture and often have a distinctive pattern of veins. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
