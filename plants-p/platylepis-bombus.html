Platylepis bombus

Platylepis bombus, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with five lobes and a prominent central tube. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
