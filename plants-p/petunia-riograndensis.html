Petunia riograndensis

Petunia riograndensis, commonly known as the Rio Grande petunia, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. This species is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of about 15 to 30 centimeters.

Its foliage consists of ovate, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.
