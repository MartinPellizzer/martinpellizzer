Pothos crassipedunculatus

Pothos crassipedunculatus, commonly known as the marble queen pothos, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of cream and green, which adds to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, though it is primarily grown for its foliage rather than its blooms. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Pothos crassipedunculatus is a popular houseplant due to its ease of care and ability to purify indoor air.
