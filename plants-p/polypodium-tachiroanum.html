Polypodium tachiroanum

Polypodium tachiroanum, commonly known as the Tachiroan fern, is a species of polypody found in the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that typically grow in dense clusters. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually light green and have a leathery texture, adapting to low-light conditions.

Polypodium tachiroanum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in both natural and cultivated settings.
