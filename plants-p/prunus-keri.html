Prunus keri

Prunus keri, commonly known as the Keri cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible cherries that are typically red when mature. Prunus keri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
