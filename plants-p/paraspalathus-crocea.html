Paraspalathus crocea

Paraspalathus crocea, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Paraspalathus crocea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
