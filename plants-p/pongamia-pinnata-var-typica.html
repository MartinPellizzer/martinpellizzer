Pongamia pinnata var. typica

Pongamia pinnata var. typica, commonly known as the Indian beech or pongam tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing seeds that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

Its botanical features include a taproot system and the ability to fix nitrogen, making it adaptable to various soil conditions.
