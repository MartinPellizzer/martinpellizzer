Polygonum parryi

Polygonum parryi, commonly known as Parry's knotweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Polygonum parryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
