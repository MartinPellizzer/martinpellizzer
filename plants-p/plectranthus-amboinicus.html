Plectranthus amboinicus

Plectranthus amboinicus, commonly known as holy basil or tulsi, is a herbaceous plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, opposite leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and religious practices. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Plectranthus amboinicus is a perennial shrub that can grow up to 1 meter in height under optimal conditions.
