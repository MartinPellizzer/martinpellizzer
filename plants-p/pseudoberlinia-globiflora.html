Pseudoberlinia globiflora

Pseudoberlinia globiflora, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It is often found in moist, wooded areas and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
