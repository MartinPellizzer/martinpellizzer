Polygala butyracea var. carunculata

Polygala butyracea var. carunculata, commonly known as the red-flowered milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Polygala. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a hairy texture.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
