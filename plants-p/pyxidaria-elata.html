Pyxidaria elata

Pyxidaria elata, commonly known as the tall pyxidaria, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
