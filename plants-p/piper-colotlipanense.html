Piper colotlipanense

Piper colotlipanense, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a species of flowering vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper plants such as black pepper. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by elliptical, winged fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate with a glossy surface and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is characterized by its climbing habit and is often found in humid, tropical environments.
