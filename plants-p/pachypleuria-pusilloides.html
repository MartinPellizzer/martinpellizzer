Pachypleuria pusilloides

Pachypleuria pusilloides, commonly known as the dwarf pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has slender, wiry stems. Its flowers are usually small and pale, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its delicate nature, it requires careful cultivation to thrive in captivity.
