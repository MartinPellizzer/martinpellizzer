Pratia hederacea var. odorata

Pratia hederacea var. odorata, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweetbriar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves emit a sweet, aromatic scent, which is released when crushed, giving it its common name.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
