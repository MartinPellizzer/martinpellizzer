Pimenta yumana

Pimenta yumana, commonly known as Yuman pimento, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small, red, fleshy berry, is edible and has a sweet, spicy flavor.

Pimenta yumana is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to its aromatic properties.
