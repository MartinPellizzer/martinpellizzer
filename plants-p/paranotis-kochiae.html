Paranotis kochiae

Paranotis kochiae, commonly known as the Kochia silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in dense, spherical clusters during the summer months. Paranotis kochiae is a short-lived perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
