Phleum subulatum subsp. ciliatum

Phleum subulatum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit.

The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, narrow clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
