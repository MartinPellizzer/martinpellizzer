Populus laurifolia var. simonii

Populus laurifolia var. simonii, commonly known as Chinese balm, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
