Primula sikuensis

Primula sikuensis, commonly known as the Siku's primrose, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Primula sikuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
