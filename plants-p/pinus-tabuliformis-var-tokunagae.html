Pinus tabuliformis var. tokunagae

Pinus tabuliformis var. tokunagae, commonly known as the Tokunagae stone pine, is a variant of the Chinese stone pine native to Japan.

It is characterized by its unique, thick, and often flaky bark that resembles stone, giving it its common name. The tree produces large, edible pine nuts, which are a significant feature of its botanical profile. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and contain several seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance and hardiness.
