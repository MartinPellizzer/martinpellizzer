Phaseolus vulgaris var. lividus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. lividus, commonly known as the dark bean or black bean variety, is a cultivar of the common bean plant.

It is characterized by its deep, dark green foliage and elongated, slender pods that mature to a dark brown or black color. The plant is a climbing legume that produces small, white or pale flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its high protein content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
