Pellaea ribae

Pellaea ribae, commonly known as the ribbed maidenhair fern, is a species of fern native to South America.

It belongs to the family Pellaeaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Pellaea ribae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
