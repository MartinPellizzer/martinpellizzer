Pimelea erecta

Pimelea erecta, commonly known as the erect bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Pimelea erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
