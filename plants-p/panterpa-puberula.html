Panterpa puberula

Panterpa puberula, commonly known as the hairy panther orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests or high-altitude grasslands. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, Panterpa puberula is considered vulnerable, highlighting the need for its protection.
