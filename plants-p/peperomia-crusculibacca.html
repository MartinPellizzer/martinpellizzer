Peperomia crusculibacca

Peperomia crusculibacca, commonly known as the coral peperomia, is a compact, low-growing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge, especially under bright light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, which are not particularly showy. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Peperomia crusculibacca is valued for its attractive foliage and is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
