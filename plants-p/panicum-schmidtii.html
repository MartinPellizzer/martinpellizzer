Panicum schmidtii

Panicum schmidtii, commonly known as Schmidt's Panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often visible during the summer months. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can be beneficial for soil stabilization.

Panicum schmidtii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological value.
