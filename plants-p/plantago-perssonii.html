Plantago perssonii

Plantago perssonii, commonly known as the Persson's plantain, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to rocky and arid soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
