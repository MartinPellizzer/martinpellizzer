Petunga longifolia

Petunga longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf petunga, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Petunga longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
