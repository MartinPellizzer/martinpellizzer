Pleurothallis variegata

Pleurothallis variegata, commonly known as the variegated pleurothallis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This species is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern around the pseudobulbs, which help store water and nutrients. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
