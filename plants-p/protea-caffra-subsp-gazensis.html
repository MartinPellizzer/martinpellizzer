Protea caffra subsp. gazensis

Protea caffra subsp.

gazensis, commonly known as the Gazenstroom protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous brightly colored bracts that surround the small, white flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the fynbos vegetation of the Western Cape. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, with leathery, lance-shaped leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and is a symbol of South African biodiversity.
