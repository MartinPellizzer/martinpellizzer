Polygonum babingtonii

Polygonum babingtonii, commonly known as the Babington's knotweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes plants known for their distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to red, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

The leaves are characterized by their smooth, glossy surface and serrated edges.
