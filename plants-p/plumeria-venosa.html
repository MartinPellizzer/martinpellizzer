Plumeria venosa

Plumeria venosa, commonly known as the variegated frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive veined pattern. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves with a waxy texture and a pleasant, sweet scent. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Plumeria venosa is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.
