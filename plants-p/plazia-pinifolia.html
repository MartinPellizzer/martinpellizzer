Plazia pinifolia

Plazia pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf plazia, is a unique plant species characterized by its needle-like foliage.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to survive in harsh environmental conditions. The plant's leaves are typically small, thick, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Plazia pinifolia produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the plant.

Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.
