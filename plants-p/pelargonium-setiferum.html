Pelargonium setiferum

Pelargonium setiferum, commonly known as the woolly geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. It is often grown as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Pelargonium setiferum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
