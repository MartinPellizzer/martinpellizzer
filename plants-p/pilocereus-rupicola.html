Pilocereus rupicola

Pilocereus rupicola, commonly known as the rock cactus or stone cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
