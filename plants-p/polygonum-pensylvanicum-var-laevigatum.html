Polygonum pensylvanicum var. laevigatum

Polygonum pensylvanicum var. laevigatum, commonly known as smooth knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces small, three-lobed berries that are dispersed by water.

Smooth knotweed is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed or wetland environments, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
