Papilionanthe teres f. candida

Papilionanthe teres f. candida, commonly known as the White-veined Orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique white and green veined leaves, which give it a distinct appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and green, often with a subtle pinkish tinge. It is a low-growing epiphyte that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
