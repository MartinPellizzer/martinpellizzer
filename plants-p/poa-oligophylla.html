Poa oligophylla

Poa oligophylla, commonly known as the slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that often form dense mats. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is usually concealed within the foliage, making it less conspicuous.

Poa oligophylla plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
