Pterocephalus hookeri

Pterocephalus hookeri, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, adapting well to harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces distinctive blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Pterocephalus hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance.
