Pittosporum ledoides

Pittosporum ledoides, commonly known as the New Zealand pittosporum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter, hairy underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Pittosporum ledoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
