Passiflora glaberrima var. cumbalensis

Passiflora glaberrima var. cumbalensis, commonly known as the Cumbal passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, showy flowers with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are typically edible and contain a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
