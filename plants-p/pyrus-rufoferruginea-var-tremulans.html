Pyrus rufoferruginea var. tremulans

Pyrus rufoferruginea var. tremulans, commonly known as the red-ironwood pear, is a variant of the pear tree characterized by its distinctive bark and foliage.

It is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features alternate, simple leaves that are often hairy and have a reddish-brown underside. Its small, yellow flowers appear in spring, followed by edible fruits that are typically small and yellow or brown in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
