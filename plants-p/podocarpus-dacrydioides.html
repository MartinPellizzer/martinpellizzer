Podocarpus dacrydioides

Podocarpus dacrydioides, commonly known as the silver pine or white pine, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters of three, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in horticulture and reforestation efforts.
