Passiflora hassleriana var. paraguariensis

Passiflora hassleriana var. paraguariensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that feature a complex structure with five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible, tropical fruits known as passionfruits, which are prized for their aromatic pulp and high vitamin C content. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
