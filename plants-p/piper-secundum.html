Piper secundum

Piper secundum, commonly known as the secondary pepper or creeping pepper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, fleshy berries.

Piper secundum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
