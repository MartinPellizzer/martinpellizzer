Primula pantlingii

Primula pantlingii, commonly known as the Pantling's primrose, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. Primula pantlingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
