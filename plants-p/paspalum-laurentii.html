Paspalum laurentii

Paspalum laurentii, commonly known as the slender paspalum, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Paspalum laurentii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
