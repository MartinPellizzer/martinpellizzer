Paphinia subclausa

Paphinia subclausa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved paphiopedilum, is a rare and intriguing orchid species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety flowers that are typically a blend of purple and white. The plant produces showy, lip-shaped petals that are highly specialized for pollination by specific insects. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, Paphinia subclausa is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
