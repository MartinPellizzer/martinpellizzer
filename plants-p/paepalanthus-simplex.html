Paepalanthus simplex

Paepalanthus simplex, commonly known as the simple pincushion cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is a small, compact cactus with a dense cluster of short, cylindrical stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Paepalanthus simplex is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
