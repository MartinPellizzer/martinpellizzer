Potentilla leuconota

Potentilla leuconota, commonly known as the white cinquefoil, is a perennial shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five leaflets, which are typically serrated and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Potentilla leuconota is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
