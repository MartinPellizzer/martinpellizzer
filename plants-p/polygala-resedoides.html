Polygala resedoides

Polygala resedoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are usually pink or purple.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
