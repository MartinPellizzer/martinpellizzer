Petalochilus chamaephyllus

Petalochilus chamaephyllus, commonly known as the low-growing orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant features linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
