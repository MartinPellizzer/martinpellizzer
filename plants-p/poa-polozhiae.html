Poa polozhiae

Poa polozhiae, commonly known as the Khabarovsk grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Russia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
