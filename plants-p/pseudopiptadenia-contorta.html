Pseudopiptadenia contorta

Pseudopiptadenia contorta, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds by wind.
