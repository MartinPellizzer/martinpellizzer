Prunus myrobalana roseaplena

Prunus myrobalana roseaplena, commonly known as the Persian lilac or Persian apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and delicate foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
