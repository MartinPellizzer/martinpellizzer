Piper cambodianum

Piper cambodianum, commonly known as Cambodian pepper or long pepper, is a flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are used as a spice and traditional medicine. Its leaves are also used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
