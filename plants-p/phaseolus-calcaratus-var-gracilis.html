Phaseolus calcaratus var. gracilis

Phaseolus calcaratus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender scarlet runner bean, is a climbing legume native to the Andean regions of South America.

It produces vibrant red or orange flowers and elongated, edible pods that contain small, round seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and hairy stems, typical of the Phaseolus genus. It thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional cuisines.
