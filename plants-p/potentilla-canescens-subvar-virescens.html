Potentilla canescens subvar. virescens

Potentilla canescens subvar. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically green and have a slightly rough texture, with five leaflets arranged oppositely along the stems. This subvariety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
