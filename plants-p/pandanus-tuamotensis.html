Pandanus tuamotensis

Pandanus tuamotensis, commonly known as the Tahitian pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Tuamotu Archipelago in French Polynesia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and broad, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for making thatch.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in its native habitat.
