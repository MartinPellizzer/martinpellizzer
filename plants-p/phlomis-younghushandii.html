Phlomis younghushandii

Phlomis younghushandii, commonly known as the Tibetan blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Phlomis younghushandii is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.
