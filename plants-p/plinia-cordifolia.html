Plinia cordifolia

Plinia cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Plinia cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
