Packera scalaris var. scalaris

Packera scalaris var. scalaris, commonly known as the scalycup aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and basal rosette of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream banks, preferring cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in a disk-like cluster.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
