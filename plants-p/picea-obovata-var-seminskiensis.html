Picea obovata var. seminskiensis

Picea obovata var. seminskiensis, commonly known as the Semsinskii fir, is a rare subspecies of the Japanese fir.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central and northern Japan, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The tree has slender, conical crowns and dark green, needle-like leaves that are slightly curved. Its cones are typically reddish-brown and hang downward, releasing seeds in the autumn.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
