Primula hirsuta subsp. brevipilosa

Primula hirsuta subsp.

brevipilosa, commonly known as the short-hair primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or shaded habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
