Piper bipunctatum

Piper bipunctatum, commonly known as the two-spotted pepper plant, is a tropical climbing vine belonging to the Piperaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild peppery flavor in its leaves.

Due to its rapid growth and climbing habit, it is suitable for trellises or as a ground cover in humid environments.
