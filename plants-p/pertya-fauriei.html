Pertya fauriei

Pertya fauriei, commonly known as the golden star fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called sori, which are typically located on the underside of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, moist soil.

Pertya fauriei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive, lacy appearance.
