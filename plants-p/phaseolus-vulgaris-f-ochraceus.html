Phaseolus vulgaris f. ochraceus

Phaseolus vulgaris f. ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow bean.

This variant is a cultivated form of the common bean, characterized by its distinctive yellow pod and seed color. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with climbing vines, producing pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it a notable variety for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
