Podalyria calyptrata var. styracifolia

Podalyria calyptrata var. styracifolia, commonly known as the sticky willow or styracifolia willow, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often arching stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, sticky, glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated margin.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
