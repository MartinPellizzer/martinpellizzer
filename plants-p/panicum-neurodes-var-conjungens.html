Panicum neurodes var. conjungens

Panicum neurodes var. conjungens, commonly known as the jointed panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender growth habit and produces panicles that are often loosely arranged. The plant typically thrives in wetland or marshy environments, where it can tolerate waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms with small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
