Petunia phoenicea

Petunia phoenicea, commonly known as the purple petunia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are tubular with five petals.

Petunia phoenicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
