Pithecellobium borneense

Pithecellobium borneense, commonly known as the Bornean burr legume, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive fruit structures. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural studies.
