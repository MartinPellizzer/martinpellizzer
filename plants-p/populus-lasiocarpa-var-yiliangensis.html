Populus lasiocarpa var. yiliangensis

Populus lasiocarpa var. yiliangensis, commonly known as the Yiliang poplar, is a rare variant of the poplar species native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-brown bark and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered with fine, silky hairs. This variety thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. The plant plays a significant role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanical research and conservation efforts.
