Polygonum sieboldii var. pratense

Polygonum sieboldii var. pratense, commonly known as the meadow knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with small, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
