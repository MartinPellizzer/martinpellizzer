Pisonia amplifolia

Pisonia amplifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved pisonia, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy texture. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Pisonia amplifolia thrives in coastal areas and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
