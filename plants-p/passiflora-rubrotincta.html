Passiflora rubrotincta

Passiflora rubrotincta, commonly known as the red-stained passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature a vibrant mix of red, purple, and white hues. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface.

Passiflora rubrotincta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms and ecological value to pollinators.
