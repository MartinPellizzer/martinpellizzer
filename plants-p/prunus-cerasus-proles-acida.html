Prunus cerasus proles acida

Prunus cerasus proles acida, commonly known as the sour cherry, is a species of Prunus native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that produces small, tart cherries that are widely used in cooking and baking. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces fruits that are typically harvested in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit's use in jams, sauces, and as a flavoring agent.
