Pyrus browiczii

Pyrus browiczii, commonly known as the Browiczii pear, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces edible fruits, though they are not as widely cultivated as other pear varieties.

It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in mountainous environments with well-drained soils.
