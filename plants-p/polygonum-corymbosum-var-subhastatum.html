Polygonum corymbosum var. subhastatum

Polygonum corymbosum var. subhastatum, commonly known as the redstem filaree, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its prostrate to ascending growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive reddish tint, especially along the veins.

It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
