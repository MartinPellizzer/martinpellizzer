Panicum amabile

Panicum amabile, commonly known as sweet bluestem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a warm-season grass that typically grows in wetland areas and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink hues. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that becomes more pronounced as the plant matures.

Panicum amabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in moist environments.
