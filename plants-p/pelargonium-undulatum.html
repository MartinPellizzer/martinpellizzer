Pelargonium undulatum

Pelargonium undulatum, commonly known as the lemon-scented geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor cultivation.

Pelargonium undulatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in aromatherapy and as a natural pest repellent.
