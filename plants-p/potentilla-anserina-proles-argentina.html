Potentilla anserina proles argentina

Potentilla anserina proles argentina, commonly known as the Argentine silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
