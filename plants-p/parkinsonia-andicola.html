Parkinsonia andicola

Parkinsonia andicola, commonly known as the Andean palo de rosa, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bipinnate leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rocky or sandy soils. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
