Pandanus mayotteensis

Pandanus mayotteensis, commonly known as the Mayotte pandanus, is a unique species native to the Comoros Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments. Its inflorescences produce small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
