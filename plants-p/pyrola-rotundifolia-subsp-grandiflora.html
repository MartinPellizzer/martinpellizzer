Pyrola rotundifolia subsp. grandiflora

Pyrola rotundifolia subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered wintergreen, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its rosette of broad, dark green, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped white or pink flowers in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, moist soils and is often found in coniferous forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
