Prunus ballota

Prunus ballota, commonly known as the Spanish cherry or the ballota cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, red or black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are pink or white.

Prunus ballota is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
