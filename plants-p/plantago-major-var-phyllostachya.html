Plantago major var. phyllostachya

Plantago major var. phyllostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and Africa, typically growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense spikes, which are clustered at the ends of its stems. Its leaves are broad and ribbed, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This variant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
