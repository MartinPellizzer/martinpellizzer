Podocarpus pilgeri var. thailandensis

Podocarpus pilgeri var. thailandensis, commonly known as the Thai podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, scale-like leaves. The tree typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in mountainous areas with high humidity. It produces small, woody cones that contain edible seeds, which are sometimes used in local cuisines.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some parts of its native range.
