Populus ciliata var. weixi

Populus ciliata var. weixi, commonly known as the Weixi poplar, is a variant of the poplar tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features serrated margins and a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in river valleys and lowland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
