Pouteria subsessilifolia

Pouteria subsessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing the edible seeds known as Brazil nuts. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and has a dense canopy that provides habitat for various wildlife species.

Its fruit is an important part of the local ecosystem and is also harvested for its valuable seeds.
