Prunus communis lusus purpurea

Prunus communis lusus purpurea, commonly known as the purple leaf cherry, is a cultivar of the Prunus genus, characterized by its striking purplish foliage.

This ornamental plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 6 to 10 feet. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface that turns a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible fruits that are similar to cherries.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for gardens and urban landscapes.
