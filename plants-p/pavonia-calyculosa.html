Pavonia calyculosa

Pavonia calyculosa, commonly known as the scarlet pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, scarlet to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a velvety texture.

Pavonia calyculosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
