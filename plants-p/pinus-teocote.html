Pinus teocote

Pinus teocote, commonly known as the Mexican bristlecone pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in bundles of five. The plant produces cones that are woody and contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, red aril. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The tree is known for its slow growth and can live for several centuries, making it a resilient and long-lived species.
