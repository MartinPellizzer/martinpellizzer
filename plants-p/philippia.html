Philippia

Philippia, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble bells. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and birds.

Philippia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
