Portulaca pilosa var. paraguayensis

Portulaca pilosa var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay sandmat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
