Phaseolus vulgaris var. fragarinus

Phaseolus vulgaris var. fragarinus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers and elongated, edible pods that turn yellow as they mature. The plant is a climbing vine with trifoliate leaves and produces beans that are often used in culinary applications. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to the Americas.

Its genetic diversity contributes to its adaptability in various climates and soils.
