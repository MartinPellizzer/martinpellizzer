Pitcairnia brongniartiana var. latifolia

Pitcairnia brongniartiana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved pitcairnia, is a striking bromeliad native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention. The plant produces striking inflorescences with vibrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and enthusiasts.
