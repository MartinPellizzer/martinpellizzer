Prunus salicifolia var. acutifolia

Prunus salicifolia var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like leaves that are elliptical and pointed, giving it its specific epithet "acutifolia.". The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, dark red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural practices.
