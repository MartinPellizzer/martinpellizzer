Phaseolus nakashimae var. borealis

Phaseolus nakashimae var. borealis, commonly known as the northern lima bean, is a lesser-known variety of the common bean genus.

It is native to the highlands of central Mexico and is characterized by its small, elongated, green seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with indeterminate vines that can reach several meters in length. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to cooler climates and is often cultivated in temperate regions for its nutritious seeds.
