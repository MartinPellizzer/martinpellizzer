Potamogeton compressus var. dimidius

Potamogeton compressus var. dimidius, commonly known as the narrowleaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged spirally along its stem, which can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spike-like structures, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various waterfowl and invertebrates.

Its dense growth can also influence water clarity and nutrient cycling in lakes and ponds.
