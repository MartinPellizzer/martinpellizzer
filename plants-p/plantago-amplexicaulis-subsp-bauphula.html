Plantago amplexicaulis subsp. bauphula

Plantago amplexicaulis subsp.

bauphula, commonly known as the broadleaf plantain, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Africa. It features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in a spike, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks.

Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
