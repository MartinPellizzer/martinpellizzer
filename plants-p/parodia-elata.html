Parodia elata

Parodia elata, commonly known as the "Candelabra Cactus," is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive branching pattern that resembles a candlestick. The plant features pale green to bluish-gray stems that are ribbed and covered in white spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters at the tips of the branches.

Parodia elata is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
