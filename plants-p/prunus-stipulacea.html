Prunus stipulacea

Prunus stipulacea, commonly known as the stipule cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to the eastern United States. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe, which is typically eaten by birds and small mammals.

The plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
