Phymatopsis roseomarginata

Phymatopsis roseomarginata, commonly known as the rose-margin sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers with distinctive pink margins, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Phymatopsis roseomarginata is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
