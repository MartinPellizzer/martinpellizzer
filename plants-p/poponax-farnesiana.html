Poponax farnesiana

Poponax farnesiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces a distinctive resinous scent, which is often used in traditional medicine and perfumery. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Poponax farnesiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
