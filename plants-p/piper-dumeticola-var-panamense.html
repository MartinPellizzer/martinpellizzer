Piper dumeticola var. panamense

Piper dumeticola var. panamense, commonly known as the Panama pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into elongated, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
