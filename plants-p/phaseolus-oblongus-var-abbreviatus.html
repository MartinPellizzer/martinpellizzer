Phaseolus oblongus var. abbreviatus

Phaseolus oblongus var. abbreviatus, commonly known as the dwarf bean, is a variant of the common bean species.

It is a climbing legume characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to typical Phaseolus species. The plant produces small, oval-shaped pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and nutritional content, making it a useful crop in agricultural settings.
