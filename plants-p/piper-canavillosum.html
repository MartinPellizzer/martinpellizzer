Piper canavillosum

Piper canavillosum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Piper genus in the family Piperaceae, which includes many species of tropical flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that appear during the warm season. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Piper canavillosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
