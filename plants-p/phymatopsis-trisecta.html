Phymatopsis trisecta

Phymatopsis trisecta, commonly known as the three-lobed phymatopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate to elliptic leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

Phymatopsis trisecta is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is considered a member of the Apiaceae family.
