Piper falcifolium

Piper falcifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pepper vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species. The plant is characterized by its long, slender, and often narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by berries that mature to a bright red color.

Piper falcifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
