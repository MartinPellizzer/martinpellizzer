Pandanus militaris var. malayanus

Pandanus militaris var. malayanus, commonly known as the Malayan pandanus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline environments. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that attract various pollinators.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and cultural significance in local communities.
