Parasenecio × shirouma-montanus

Parasenecio × shirouma-montanus, commonly known as the Shirouma-montanus hybrid, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Parasenecio shirouma* and *Parasenecio montanus*.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions of East Asia. It has a fibrous root system and exhibits a clumping growth habit.

Due to its hybrid origin, it displays a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flower size.
