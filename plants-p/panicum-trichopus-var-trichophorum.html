Panicum trichopus var. trichophorum

Panicum trichopus var. trichophorum, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
