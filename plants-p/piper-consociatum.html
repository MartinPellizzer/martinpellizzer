Piper consociatum

Piper consociatum, commonly known as the yellow pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly peppery aroma.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of rainforests.
