Papaver pyrenaicum subsp. aurantiacum

Papaver pyrenaicum subsp.

aurantiacum, commonly known as the orange poppy, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pyrenees region. It features striking orange-red flowers with a distinctive saucer-shaped corolla, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
