Panicum pilcomayense

Panicum pilcomayense, commonly known as the Andean bluestem, is a flowering grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate, pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
