Phragmites communis var. altissimus

Phragmites communis var. altissimus, commonly known as the tall reed, is a perennial grass that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. It produces dense clusters of small, fluffy flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variety is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along the shores of lakes and rivers.
