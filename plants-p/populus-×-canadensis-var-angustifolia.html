Populus × canadensis var. angustifolia

Populus × canadensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf poplar, is a hybrid species within the Populus genus.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of its parent species. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along riverbanks. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is valued for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in reforestation and erosion control projects.
