Pteridium latiusculum subsp. japonicum

Pteridium latiusculum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese bracken, is a fern species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its large, triangular fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This species is known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense colonies, which can dominate certain ecosystems.
