Plectranthus manganjensis

Plectranthus manganjensis, commonly known as the Manganjen plectranthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs and shrubs.

Plectranthus manganjensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
