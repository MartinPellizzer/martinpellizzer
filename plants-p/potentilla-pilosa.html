Potentilla pilosa

Potentilla pilosa, commonly known as the fuzzy cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or stream banks. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native gardens.
