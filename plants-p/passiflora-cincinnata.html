Passiflora cincinnata

Passiflora cincinnata, commonly known as the slender passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, palmate leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers that resemble a crown. The flowers are typically blue or purple with intricate patterns, and they emit a faint fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
