Pinanga albescens var. sarawakensis

Pinanga albescens var. sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak bamboo palm, is a unique variant of the genus Pinanga native to Borneo.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit and long, narrow leaves that resemble those of a bamboo plant. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
