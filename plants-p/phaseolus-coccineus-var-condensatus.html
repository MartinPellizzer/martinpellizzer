Phaseolus coccineus var. condensatus

Phaseolus coccineus var. condensatus, commonly known as the scarlet runner bean, is a cultivated variant of the common scarlet runner bean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers and edible, bean-like pods that develop after flowering. The plant is a climbing legume with compound leaves and is native to the Americas. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value and as a source of edible beans.
