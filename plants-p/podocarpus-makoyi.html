Podocarpus makoyi

Podocarpus makoyi, commonly known as the Makoy's podocarp, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Podocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, cone-like structures. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically leathery and arranged in whorls, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
