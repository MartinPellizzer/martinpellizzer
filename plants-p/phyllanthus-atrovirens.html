Phyllanthus atrovirens

Phyllanthus atrovirens, commonly known as black-eyed Susan, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with dark centers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
