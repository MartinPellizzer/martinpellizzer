Polypodium stigmaticum

Polypodium stigmaticum, commonly known as the stigmatic holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green and have a leathery texture. This fern is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including countries like India, China, and Japan. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant's spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
