Piper longepedunculatum

Piper longepedunculatum, commonly known as the long pedunculate pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. It produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
