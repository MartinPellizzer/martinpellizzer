Pandanus kariangensis

Pandanus kariangensis, commonly known as the Kariangensis pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and large, feather-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiky, woody fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be used for making ropes and mats.

Pandanus kariangensis thrives in coastal and wetland areas, adapting well to humid and saline environments.
