Polygonatum odoratum var. maximowiczii

Polygonatum odoratum var. maximowiczii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes in gardens and natural habitats.
