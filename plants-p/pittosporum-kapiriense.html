Pittosporum kapiriense

Pittosporum kapiriense, commonly known as the Kapiriense Pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pittosporaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in terminal panicles.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
