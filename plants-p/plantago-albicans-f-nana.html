Plantago albicans f. nana

Plantago albicans f. nana, commonly known as the dwarf plantain, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features rosettes of broad, oval leaves that are typically hairy and have a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used for their soothing and anti-inflammatory effects.
