Protea micans subsp. micans

Protea micans subsp.

micans, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family. It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white to pale pink flowers arranged in a spherical or conical shape.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its foliage consists of long, narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and elegant appearance.
