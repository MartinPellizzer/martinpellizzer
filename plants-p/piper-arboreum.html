Piper arboreum

Piper arboreum, commonly known as the sacred pepper or tree pepper, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit, often growing as a vine or small tree. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into red, peppercorn-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Piper arboreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in various cultures.
