Polypodium dulce

Polypodium dulce, commonly known as the sweet fern, is a type of fern that belongs to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
