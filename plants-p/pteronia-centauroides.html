Pteronia centauroides

Pteronia centauroides, commonly known as the Cape umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is well-suited for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens.
