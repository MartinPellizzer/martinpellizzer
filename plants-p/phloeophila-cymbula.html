Phloeophila cymbula

Phloeophila cymbula, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the stems are often hairy.

Phloeophila cymbula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
