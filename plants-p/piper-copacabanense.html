Piper copacabanense

Piper copacabanense, commonly known as the Copacabana pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in regions of Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its climbing habit, with long, slender stems that support its compound leaves. Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
