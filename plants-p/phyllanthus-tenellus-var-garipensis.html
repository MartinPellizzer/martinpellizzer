Phyllanthus tenellus var. garipensis

Phyllanthus tenellus var. garipensis, commonly known as the African spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is native to parts of Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
