Protea angolensis f. trichanthera

Protea angolensis f. trichanthera, commonly known as the Angolan protea, is a unique variant of the protea family native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking, cone-shaped inflorescences that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in mountainous regions with a cool, moist climate. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by birds, particularly sunbirds, which are attracted to its nectar.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
