Phlox maculata var. suaveolens

Phlox maculata var. suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet willie blume, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
