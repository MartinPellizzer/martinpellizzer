Pandanus pseudosyncarpus

Pandanus pseudosyncarpus, commonly known as the false screw pine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and thatching. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a spiky, multiple-seeded structure that develops from the flowering stems.

Pandanus pseudosyncarpus is well adapted to coastal and saline environments, making it a hardy species in tropical climates.
