Plectranthus luteus

Plectranthus luteus, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant thrives in warm, sunny conditions and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators.

Plectranthus luteus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
