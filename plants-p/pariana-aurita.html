Pariana aurita

Pariana aurita, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Pariana aurita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
