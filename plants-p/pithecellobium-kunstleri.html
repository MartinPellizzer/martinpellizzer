Pithecellobium kunstleri

Pithecellobium kunstleri, commonly known as the yellow velvet tamarind, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
