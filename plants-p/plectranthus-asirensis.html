Plectranthus asirensis

Plectranthus asirensis, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are velvety and often have a distinct, minty fragrance.

Plectranthus asirensis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
