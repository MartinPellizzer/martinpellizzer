Philotheca australis var. parviflora

Philotheca australis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered mountain tea, is a perennial shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
