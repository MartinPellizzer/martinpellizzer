Primula kingii subsp. gageana

Primula kingii subsp.

gageana, commonly known as the Gageana primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to New Zealand. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring.

The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are oval and slightly pointed, arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
