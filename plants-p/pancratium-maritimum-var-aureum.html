Pancratium maritimum var. aureum

Pancratium maritimum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden crown flower, is a striking coastal plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, demonstrating tolerance to salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
