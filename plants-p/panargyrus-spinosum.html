Panargyrus spinosum

Panargyrus spinosum, commonly known as the spiny panargyrus, is a unique succulent plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular shape with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss and deter herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
