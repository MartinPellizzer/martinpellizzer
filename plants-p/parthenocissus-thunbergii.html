Parthenocissus thunbergii

Parthenocissus thunbergii, commonly known as Boston ivy, is a woody vine native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.

Its tendrils allow it to climb and cover walls or trellises effectively.
