Pelargonium apiifolium

Pelargonium apiifolium, commonly known as the leafy geranium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate climates.
