Pseuderanthemum albiflorum

Pseuderanthemum albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower pseuderanthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles and bloom during the warmer months.

Pseuderanthemum albiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
