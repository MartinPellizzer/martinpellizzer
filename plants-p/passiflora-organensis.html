Passiflora organensis

Passiflora organensis, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, complex flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tropical, slightly tart flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Passiflora organensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has cultural significance in several indigenous communities.
