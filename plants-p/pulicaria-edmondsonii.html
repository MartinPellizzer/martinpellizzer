Pulicaria edmondsonii

Pulicaria edmondsonii, commonly known as the Edmondson's groundsel, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves with a fuzzy texture and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
