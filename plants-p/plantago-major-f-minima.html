Plantago major f. minima

Plantago major f. minima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf plantain, is a variant of the common plantain characterized by its smaller, narrower leaves.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along paths. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in a spike-like inflorescence, which are wind-pollinated. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in urban and rural settings alike.
