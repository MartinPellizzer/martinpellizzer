Piper frostii

Piper frostii, commonly known as the Frost's pepper, is a tropical climbing vine native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes over 2,000 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant, and often spicy berries that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Piper frostii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
