Pavonia sepium var. balansae

Pavonia sepium var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae false mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant features showy, pink to red bract-like flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
