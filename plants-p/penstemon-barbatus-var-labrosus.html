Penstemon barbatus var. labrosus

Penstemon barbatus var. labrosus, commonly known as the hummingbird penstemon, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds, enhancing its ecological value.
