Pepinia armata

Pepinia armata, commonly known as the rock lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and limestone soils, showcasing its adaptability to extreme conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
