Poa badachschanica

Poa badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakshan grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in mountainous areas of countries like Afghanistan and Tajikistan.

Due to its ecological significance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
