Pteris fraseri

Pteris fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's water fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, delicate fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance. The plant prefers moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce roots that help anchor it in damp soil.

Pteris fraseri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
