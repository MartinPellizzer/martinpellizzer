Persica levis var. aurantia

Persica levis var. aurantia, commonly known as the golden peach.

This variant is a cultivar of the peach tree, characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange fruit skin. The fruit is typically smaller than standard peaches and has a sweeter, more aromatic flavor. It is prized for its ornamental value as well as its edible qualities.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sunlight.
