Phyllanthus pynaertii

Phyllanthus pynaertii, commonly known as the African buttonwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Phyllanthus pynaertii is also noted for its ability to thrive in coastal and arid environments.
