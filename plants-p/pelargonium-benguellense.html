Pelargonium benguellense

Pelargonium benguellense, commonly known as the Benguella geranium, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink to red flowers, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to gardens, though it is relatively rare in cultivation.
