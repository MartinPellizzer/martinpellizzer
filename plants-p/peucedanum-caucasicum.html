Peucedanum caucasicum

Peucedanum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian cowparsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescences and aromatic foliage.

It is native to the Caucasus region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its small white or greenish flowers appear in clustered umbels, and the seeds are enclosed in small, hairy fruits.

The plant contains various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and herbal medicine.
