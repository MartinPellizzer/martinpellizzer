Physostegia leptophylla

Physostegia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved false dragonhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces spikes of showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of violet or blue, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
