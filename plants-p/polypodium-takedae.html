Polypodium takedae

Polypodium takedae, commonly known as the Takeda's sword fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its rhizome is creeping, and it produces sporangia on the underside of its fronds.

Polypodium takedae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
