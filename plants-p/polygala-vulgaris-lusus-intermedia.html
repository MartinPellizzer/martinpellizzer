Polygala vulgaris lusus intermedia

Polygala vulgaris lusus intermedia, commonly known as the common milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which are usually pale yellow or greenish, are adapted for insect pollination, enhancing its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
