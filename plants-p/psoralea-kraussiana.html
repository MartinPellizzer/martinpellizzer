Psoralea kraussiana

Psoralea kraussiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow or pale green flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the genus Psoralea. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
