Piper bonthainense

Piper bonthainense, commonly known as the Bonthainese pepper, is a climbing vine native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes other pepper species such as black pepper. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when mature, and the fruits are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
