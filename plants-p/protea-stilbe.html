Protea stilbe

Protea stilbe, commonly known as the silver protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble a cluster of small flowers, with a distinctive silver-tinged appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces hard, woody seed pods that open to release seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
