Pseudrachicallis

Pseudrachicallis, commonly known as the false rachis grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, which are small clusters of florets. These plants are often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Pseudrachicallis species are generally low-growing and have a clumping habit, making them suitable for ornamental use in gardens.
