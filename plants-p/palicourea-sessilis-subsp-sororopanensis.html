Palicourea sessilis subsp. sororopanensis

Palicourea sessilis subsp.

sororopanensis, commonly known as the Panamanian blueberry. It is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America, particularly Panama.

The plant features opposite, elliptic leaves with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, blue-black berry that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.
