Polygonella myriophylla

Polygonella myriophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved mossy grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often densely packed, giving it a moss-like appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, characteristic of the grass family.

Polygonella myriophylla is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
