Pabstiella marinsiensis

Pabstiella marinsiensis, commonly known as the Marinsiensis lichen, is a rare and specialized symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and a green alga or cyanobacterium.

It thrives in specific microhabitats, typically on rock surfaces in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant's structure consists of a thallus, which is a composite body formed by the mutualistic relationship between its fungal and algal components. Its botanical significance lies in its ecological role as an indicator species for environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to researchers studying biodiversity and climate change impacts.
