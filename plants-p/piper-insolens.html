Piper insolens

Piper insolens, commonly known as the bitter apple or wild pepper, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries. The fruit is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
