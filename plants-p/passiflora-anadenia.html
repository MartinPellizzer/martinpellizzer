Passiflora anadenia

Passiflora anadenia, commonly known as the Brazilian passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often have a bland taste. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a rough texture and are often used in traditional medicine.

Passiflora anadenia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring full sun and well-drained soil.
