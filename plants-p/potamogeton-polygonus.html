Potamogeton polygonus

Potamogeton polygonus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater ecosystems in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in shallow, slow-moving waters. Potamogeton polygonus plays a vital role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter and food for various aquatic organisms.
