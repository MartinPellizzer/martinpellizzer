Pseudoroegneria cognata subsp. shingoensis

Pseudoroegneria cognata subsp.

shingoensis, commonly known as the Shingo grass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings.
