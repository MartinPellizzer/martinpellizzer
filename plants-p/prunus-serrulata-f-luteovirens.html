Prunus serrulata f. luteovirens

Prunus serrulata f. luteovirens, commonly known as the goldenleaf cherry, is a botanical variant of the Japanese cherry tree.

It is characterized by its unique foliage, featuring a striking combination of yellow and green leaves that change with the seasons. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in spring, which are followed by red or black berries in summer. It is typically found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This form is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces for its aesthetic beauty and seasonal interest.
