Pilea spinulosa

Pilea spinulosa, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a low-growing perennial herb native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
