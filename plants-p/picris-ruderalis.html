Picris ruderalis

Picris ruderalis, commonly known as the field chamomile, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow composite flowers that resemble daisies. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in other regions. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Picris ruderalis is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
