Piper hispidiseptum

Piper hispidiseptum, commonly known as the rough-septum pepper, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are typically berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Piper hispidiseptum make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological studies.
