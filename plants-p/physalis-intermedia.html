Physalis intermedia

Physalis intermedia, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It produces vibrant orange, lantern-shaped fruits that enclose a cluster of small, edible berries. The plant features hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in both ornamental and culinary contexts.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has a tangy, sweet flavor.
