Panicum atrofuscum

Panicum atrofuscum, commonly known as black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or black. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Its ornamental value is limited, but it is sometimes grown as a forage crop for livestock.
