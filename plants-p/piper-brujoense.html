Piper brujoense

Piper brujoense, commonly known as the devil's pepper, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small, round berry, turns red when mature and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant's name reflects its association with mystical and sometimes feared properties in local cultures.
