Psoralea rubicunda

Psoralea rubicunda, commonly known as the scarlet psoralea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its seed pods are slender and contain several small, hard seeds.

Psoralea rubicunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
