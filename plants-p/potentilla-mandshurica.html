Potentilla mandshurica

Potentilla mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian cinquefoil, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with multiple stems. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically arranged in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Potentilla mandshurica is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
