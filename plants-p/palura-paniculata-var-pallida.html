Palura paniculata var. pallida

Palura paniculata var. pallida, commonly known as the pale palura or pale panicled palura, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
