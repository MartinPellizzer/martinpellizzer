Ptilopteris triptera

Ptilopteris triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed huperzia, is a species of fern belonging to the family Huperziaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that are often divided into three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizome is thick and fleshy, allowing it to store water and nutrients, which helps it survive in less hospitable conditions.

The spores are produced in clusters on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
