Polygala leptostachys

Polygala leptostachys, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base.

It is valued in traditional medicine and is also cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
