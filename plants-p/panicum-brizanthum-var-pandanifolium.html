Panicum brizanthum var. pandanifolium

Panicum brizanthum var. pandanifolium, commonly known as the pandan-leaf panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves that resemble pandan leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays for its ornamental value.

It produces small, delicate flowers that are arranged in panicles, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
