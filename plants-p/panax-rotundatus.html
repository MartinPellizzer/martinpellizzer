Panax rotundatus

Panax rotundatus, commonly known as the Japanese ginseng, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, yellowish roots and its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant belongs to the Araliaceae family and is closely related to Panax ginseng. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The roots are used in traditional medicine to enhance vitality and support immune function.
