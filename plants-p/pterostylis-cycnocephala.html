Pterostylis cycnocephala

Pterostylis cycnocephala, commonly known as the black-tongued duck orchid, is an epiphytic orchid native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive black-tipped lip. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches in moist, shaded environments. It blooms during the spring and summer months, producing a unique floral structure that resembles a duck's bill.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
