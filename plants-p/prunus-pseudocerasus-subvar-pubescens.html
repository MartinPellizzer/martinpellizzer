Prunus pseudocerasus subvar. pubescens

Prunus pseudocerasus subvar. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often hairy bark and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The hairy pubescence on its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
