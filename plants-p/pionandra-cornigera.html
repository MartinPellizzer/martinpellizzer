Pionandra cornigera

Pionandra cornigera, commonly known as the yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Pionandra cornigera is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in horticultural displays.
