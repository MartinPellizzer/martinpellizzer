Paphinia seegeri var. seegeri

Paphinia seegeri var. seegeri, commonly known as the Seeger's Paphinia, is a rare and unique orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant features broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a challenging but rewarding species for experienced orchid growers.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and by dedicated horticulturists.
