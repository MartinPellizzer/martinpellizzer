Peruvocereus albispinus

Peruvocereus albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered with white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in cactus collections.
