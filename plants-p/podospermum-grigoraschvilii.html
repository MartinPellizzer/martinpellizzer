Podospermum grigoraschvilii

Podospermum grigoraschvilii, commonly known as the Georgian velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Podospermum grigoraschvilii is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
