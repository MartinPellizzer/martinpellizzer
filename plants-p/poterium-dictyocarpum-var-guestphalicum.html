Poterium dictyocarpum var. guestphalicum

Poterium dictyocarpum var. guestphalicum, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a ghostly appearance, hence its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in dense forest undergrowth. Its spore-producing structures are clustered in small, compact clusters called sorus.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique morphology and ecological niche within its habitat.
