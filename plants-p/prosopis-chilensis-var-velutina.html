Prosopis chilensis var. velutina

Prosopis chilensis var. velutina, commonly known as the silky mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark, which becomes fissured with age, and its compound leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it has been used traditionally for forage and timber.
