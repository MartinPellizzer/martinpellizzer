Passiflora cristalina

Passiflora cristalina, commonly known as the crystalline passionflower, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its striking, intricate flowers that feature five sepals, five petals, and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a velvety texture, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

Passiflora cristalina is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
