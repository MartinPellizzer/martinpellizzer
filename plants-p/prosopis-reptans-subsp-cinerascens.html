Prosopis reptans subsp. cinerascens

Prosopis reptans subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silverleaf mesquite, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by long, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds. It has deep roots that allow it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

This subspecies is often used for erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
