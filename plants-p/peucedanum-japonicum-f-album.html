Peucedanum japonicum f. album

Peucedanum japonicum f. album, commonly known as Japanese angelica or white angelica, is a variant of the angelica plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical green or purple hues of the species. The plant features tall, slender stems with finely divided, feather-like leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
