Passiflora hyacinthiflora

Passiflora hyacinthiflora, commonly known as the hyacinth-flowered passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hyacinth-like flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
