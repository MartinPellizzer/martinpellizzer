Pera barbinervis

Pera barbinervis, commonly known as the slender-veined pera, is a rare and intriguing plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves with fine, parallel veins that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or white, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible but not widely consumed.

Due to habitat loss, Pera barbinervis is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
