Penstemon pachyphyllus

Penstemon pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaf beardtongue, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
