Pleurothallis testifolia

Pleurothallis testifolia, commonly known as the testicle orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees in humid, shaded environments. The plant features delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for both botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
