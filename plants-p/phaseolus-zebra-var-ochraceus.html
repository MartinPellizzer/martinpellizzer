Phaseolus zebra var. ochraceus

Phaseolus zebra var. ochraceus, commonly known as the orange-seeded zebra bean, is a cultivated variant of the common bean species.

It is characterized by its striking, striped pods and vibrant orange seeds that stand out from the typical green or brown varieties. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in warm climates and well-drained soil. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets, and it produces erect stems that support its bean pods.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.
