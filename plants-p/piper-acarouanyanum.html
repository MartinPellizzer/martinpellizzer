Piper acarouanyanum

Piper acarouanyanum, commonly known as the Amazonian pepper or wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many species of pepper plants. The plant produces small, green berries that turn red when ripe, containing the spicy compound piperine. It is often found growing as an epiphyte on trees, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the tropical ecosystem and a source of traditional medicinal use.
