Penstemon pseudospectabilis var. pseudospectabilis

Penstemon pseudospectabilis var. pseudospectabilis, commonly known as the purple penstemon, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from lavender to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
