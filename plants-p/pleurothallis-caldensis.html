Pleurothallis caldensis

Pleurothallis caldensis, commonly known as the Caldensis Orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Ecuador.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that thrives in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant features delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
