Portulaca oleracea var. grandiflora

Portulaca oleracea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the moss rose or large-flowered portulaca, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the morning and close in the heat of the day. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it both a decorative and resilient plant in horticulture.
