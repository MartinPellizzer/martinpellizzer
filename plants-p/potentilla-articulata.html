Potentilla articulata

Potentilla articulata, commonly known as the jointed cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features wiry, branching stems with small, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. Its flowers are yellow, consisting of five petals, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is often found in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows.
