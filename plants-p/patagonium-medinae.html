Patagonium medinae

Patagonium medinae, commonly known as the Medina's Patagonian lichen, is a unique fungal-lichen composite found in the extreme southern regions of South America.

It thrives in harsh, cold environments, typically on rocky substrates in Patagonia. The organism exhibits a symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga, enabling it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions. Its structure consists of a dense, crust-like thallus that can vary in color from pale green to dark gray.

Patagonium medinae plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and providing microhabitats for other organisms in its ecosystem.
