Papaver nudicaule var. rubroaurantiacum

Papaver nudicaule var. rubroaurantiacum, commonly known as the red-orange poppy, is a striking variant of the common poppy.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange petals that bloom in early summer. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its delicate, nodding flowers are surrounded by narrow, green leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
