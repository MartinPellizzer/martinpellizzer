Polygala alfredi

Polygala alfredi, commonly known as the Cape Flora, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Polygalaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
