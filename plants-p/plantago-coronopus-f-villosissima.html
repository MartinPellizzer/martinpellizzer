Plantago coronopus f. villosissima

Plantago coronopus f. villosissima, commonly known as the hairy plantain, is a variant of the common plantain, characterized by its dense, velvety pubescence.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
