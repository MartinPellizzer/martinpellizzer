Pervillaea lanata

Pervillaea lanata, commonly known as the woolly pervillaea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Pervillaea lanata is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a member of the Asteraceae family.
