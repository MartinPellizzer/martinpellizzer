Piper macropunctatum

Piper macropunctatum, commonly known as the giant pepper plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family, which includes many pepper species. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruits are initially green and turn red when mature, containing small, black seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
