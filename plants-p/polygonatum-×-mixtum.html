Polygonatum × mixtum

Polygonatum × mixtum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Solomon's seal, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Polygonatum sibiricum and Polygonatum multiflorum.

It is characterized by its arching stems that bear clusters of yellowish-green flowers, followed by bright red berries. The plant is native to eastern Asia and thrives in woodland habitats with partial shade. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and form a dense rosette at the base.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
