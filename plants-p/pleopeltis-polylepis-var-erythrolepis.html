Pleopeltis polylepis var. erythrolepis

Pleopeltis polylepis var. erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-veined rock fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leathery, dark green leaves with prominent reddish veins. The fronds are typically pinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, oblong pinnae. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
