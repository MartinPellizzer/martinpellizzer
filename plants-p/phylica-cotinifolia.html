Phylica cotinifolia

Phylica cotinifolia, commonly known as the silver cotoneaster, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, red berries that are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

Phylica cotinifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its resilience to drought conditions.
