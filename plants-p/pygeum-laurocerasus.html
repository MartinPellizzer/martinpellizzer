Pygeum laurocerasus

Pygeum laurocerasus, commonly known as the common laurel.

It is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and appear in clusters, followed by red berries that contain one or two seeds.

The bark is thick and deeply furrowed, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
