Pycnandra coriacea

Pycnandra coriacea, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and fragrant, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain hard, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been traditionally used in incense and as a source of timber.
