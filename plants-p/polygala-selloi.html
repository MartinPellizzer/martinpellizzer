Polygala selloi

Polygala selloi, commonly known as the Sello's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Polygalaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. The plant has a slender, branching stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
