Pavonia kunthii var. cordifolia

Pavonia kunthii var. cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf pavonia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
