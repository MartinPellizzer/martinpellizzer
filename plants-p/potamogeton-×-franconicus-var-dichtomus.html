Potamogeton × franconicus var. dichtomus

Potamogeton × franconicus var. dichtomus, commonly known as the branched pondweed, is a hybrid aquatic plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, branched stems that often grow in shallow waters. The leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

This plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
