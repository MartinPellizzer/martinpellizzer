Primula zahlbrukneri

Primula zahlbrukneri, commonly known as the Himalayan primrose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, brightly colored flowers, typically in shades of yellow and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are broad and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Primula zahlbrukneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
