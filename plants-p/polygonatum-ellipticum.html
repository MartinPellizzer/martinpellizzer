Polygonatum ellipticum

Polygonatum ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic Solomon's seal, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features upright, clustered stems that bear lance-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black, containing several seeds. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Polygonatum ellipticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
