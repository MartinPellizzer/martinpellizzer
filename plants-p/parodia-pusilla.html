Parodia pusilla

Parodia pusilla, commonly known as the miniature moon cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to Argentina.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive green or blue-green color and a white or pinkish bloom. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically small and appear in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.

Parodia pusilla is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance and compact size.
