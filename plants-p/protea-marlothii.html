Protea marlothii

Protea marlothii, commonly known as the Marloth's protea, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Proteaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy inflorescence composed of numerous white to pinkish flowers arranged in a rounded head. The plant is native to the Western and Eastern Cape provinces, where it thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Protea marlothii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.
