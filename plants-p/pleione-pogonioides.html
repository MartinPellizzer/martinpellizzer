Pleione pogonioides

Pleione pogonioides, commonly known as the golden pleione, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of darker markings. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions with partial shade. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its alpine habitat.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
