Prunus serrulata hatazakura

Prunus serrulata hatazakura, commonly known as the Japanese cherry blossom, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is celebrated for its delicate, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a fleeting yet spectacular floral display. The plant belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and smooth, bark-like trunk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also play a significant role in cultural and ecological contexts.
