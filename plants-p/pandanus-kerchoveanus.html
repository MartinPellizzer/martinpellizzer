Pandanus kerchoveanus

Pandanus kerchoveanus, commonly known as the New Caledonia screw pine, is a tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous trunk and long, spirally arranged leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its roots system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.

Pandanus kerchoveanus plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
