Potamogeton laticaulis

Potamogeton laticaulis, commonly known as broad-stemmed pondweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, ribbon-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found in clusters near the water's surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in lakes, ponds, and wetlands.

Potamogeton laticaulis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
