Piper guineense var. congolense

Piper guineense var. congolense, commonly known as African pepper or Congolese pepper, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Piperaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature, and the plant is often cultivated for its medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
