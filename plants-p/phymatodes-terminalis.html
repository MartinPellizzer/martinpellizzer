Phymatodes terminalis

Phymatodes terminalis, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and open woodlands. The plant has a deep taproot system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers, aiding in its survival in drought conditions. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and borne in a single terminal head.

The plant is adapted to tolerate high temperatures and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh desert ecosystems.
