Prangos denticulata

Prangos denticulata, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, toothed margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
