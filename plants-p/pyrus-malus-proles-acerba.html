Pyrus malus proles acerba

Pyrus malus proles acerba, commonly known as the bitter apple, is a species of apple tree characterized by its sharp, acidic flavor.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while the flowers are white and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a pome, usually small and tart, with a thick, rough skin.
