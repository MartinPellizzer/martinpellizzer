Primula reticulata

Primula reticulata, commonly known as the Japanese primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to Japan.

It features upright, hairy stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers typically bloom in early spring, adding a splash of color to woodland gardens.
