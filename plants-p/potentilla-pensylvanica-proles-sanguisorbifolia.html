Potentilla pensylvanica proles sanguisorbifolia

Potentilla pensylvanica proles sanguisorbifolia, commonly known as the Pennsylvania cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks. It has serrated leaves and produces small, persistent fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
